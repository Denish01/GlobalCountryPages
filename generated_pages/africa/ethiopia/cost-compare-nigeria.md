[SECTION] Overview: Ethiopia vs Nigeria [/SECTION]
Ethiopia and Nigeria are two of the most populous countries in Africa, with distinct economic profiles. Ethiopia has a rapidly growing economy, driven by investments in infrastructure, agriculture, and manufacturing, while Nigeria's economy is largely driven by its oil and gas sector. In terms of cost of living, Ethiopia is generally cheaper than Nigeria, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. The cost of living in Ethiopia is around 15-20% lower than in Nigeria, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Ethiopia has a relatively stable economy, with a growing middle class and a strong focus on development. Nigeria, on the other hand, has faced economic challenges in recent years, including a decline in oil prices and a shortage of foreign exchange. As a result, the cost of living in Nigeria has increased, making it more expensive than Ethiopia. Overall, Ethiopia offers a more affordable lifestyle, with a lower cost of living and a more stable economy.
