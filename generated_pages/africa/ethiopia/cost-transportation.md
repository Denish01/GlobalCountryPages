[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Ethiopia has a diverse range of transportation modes, including buses, taxis, ride-hailing services, and trains. The quality of public transit varies greatly between cities, with Addis Ababa having a more developed system than other urban areas. Most people in Ethiopia get around using **minibuses** or **shared taxis**, which are affordable and widely available. However, the roads can be congested, and traffic accidents are common, so it's essential to exercise caution when traveling.

The transportation network in Ethiopia is still developing, and the government is investing in infrastructure projects to improve the quality of roads and public transportation. Despite the challenges, Ethiopia has a vibrant culture of transportation, with many **traditional modes of transport**, such as horse-drawn carriages and donkey carts, still in use. In addition, the country has a growing **ride-hailing industry**, with several apps available, including Ride, Feres, and ZayRide.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use **minibuses** or **shared taxis** for affordable and convenient travel within cities.
- Negotiate **taxi fares** before starting your journey to avoid overcharging.
- Download **ride-hailing apps** like Ride, Feres, or ZayRide for easy and convenient transportation.
- Be cautious of **traffic accidents** and **congestion** when traveling by road.
- Consider **domestic flights** for long-distance travel, but be prepared for limited routes and higher costs.
- Always **wear a seatbelt** and follow local **traffic rules** to ensure your safety on the road.
