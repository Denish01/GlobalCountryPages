[SECTION] Overview: Ethiopia vs Somalia [/SECTION]
Ethiopia and Somalia are two neighboring countries in the Horn of Africa, with distinct economic contexts and cost of living profiles. Ethiopia has a more diversified economy, with a growing manufacturing sector and a large agricultural industry, which contributes to its relatively higher GDP per capita. In contrast, Somalia's economy is still recovering from years of conflict and instability, with a smaller and more fragile economy. As a result, Ethiopia tends to have a lower cost of living, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation generally lower than in Somalia.

The cost of living difference between the two countries is significant, with Ethiopia offering better value for money for expats, students, and retirees. However, Somalia has its own unique advantages, such as a lower cost of living in certain categories, like transportation and internet. Overall, the cost of living in Ethiopia is approximately 20-30% lower than in Somalia, making it a more attractive destination for those looking for an affordable lifestyle in the region.
