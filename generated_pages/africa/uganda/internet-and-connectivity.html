Internet Overview

The internet in Uganda has improved significantly over the years, with the average internet speed being around 10 Mbps download. However, there is still a significant urban-rural divide, with internet services being more reliable and faster in cities like Kampala and Entebbe compared to rural areas. In urban areas, you can expect to find 4G connectivity, while in rural areas, 2G and 3G networks are more common. The reliability of the internet can also be affected by power outages and network maintenance, which can be frequent in some areas.

Despite these challenges, the Ugandan government has been working to improve the country's internet infrastructure, with several initiatives aimed at increasing access to the internet, particularly in rural areas. This has led to an increase in the number of internet users in the country, with more people now having access to online services and information. However, the cost of internet services can still be a barrier for many people, particularly in rural areas where incomes are generally lower.

Getting a SIM Card

Getting a SIM card in Uganda is relatively easy, with several mobile carriers offering tourist-friendly plans. You can buy a SIM card at the airport, or at any of the carrier's shops or authorized dealers in major towns. To purchase a SIM card, you will need to provide your passport and fill out a registration form. The cost of a SIM card is around 5 USD, and you can top up your account with airtime and data bundles at any time. The main carriers in Uganda are MTN Uganda, Airtel Uganda, and Uganda Telecom, all of which offer a range of data plans and packages.

MTN Uganda and Airtel Uganda are the most popular carriers among tourists, offering a range of data plans and packages that cater to different needs and budgets. For example, MTN Uganda offers a daily data bundle for around 1 USD, while Airtel Uganda offers a weekly data bundle for around 5 USD. It's recommended to research the different carriers and their offers before purchasing a SIM card to ensure that you get the best deal for your needs.

WiFi Availability

  • Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels and lodges offering free WiFi to their guests.
  • Cafe WiFi culture is growing in Uganda, with many cafes and restaurants in major towns offering free WiFi to customers.
  • Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in Uganda, particularly in Kampala, with many offering reliable and fast WiFi.
  • Public WiFi is limited in Uganda, but you can find it in some public areas such as airports and shopping malls.
  • Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while on the go.

For Remote Workers

  • Coworking spaces such as The Hub and Outbox are popular among remote workers, offering reliable and fast WiFi, as well as other amenities such as meeting rooms and printing services.
  • Reliable cafe chains such as Java House and Cafe Javas offer free WiFi and a comfortable working environment, making them popular among remote workers.
  • Backup internet options such as mobile hotspots and portable WiFi routers are available, but can be expensive and may not always be reliable.
  • VPNs are recommended for remote workers, particularly those who need to access sensitive information or use public WiFi networks.
  • Shared offices and virtual offices are also available, offering a range of services and amenities, including reliable WiFi and meeting rooms.

Tips & Warnings

  • Internet censorship is a concern in Uganda, with some websites and social media platforms being blocked or restricted.
  • Blocked sites include some news websites and social media platforms, so it's a good idea to use a VPN to access these sites.
  • VPN recommendations include ExpressVPN and NordVPN, both of which offer reliable and secure connections.
  • Roaming alternatives such as purchasing a local SIM card or using a portable WiFi router can be more cost-effective than roaming with your home carrier.
  • eSIM options are limited in Uganda, but some carriers such as MTN Uganda offer eSIM plans for certain devices.
  • It's also a good idea to research the best data plans and packages for your needs, and to purchase a SIM card or portable WiFi router as soon as possible to avoid high roaming charges.
