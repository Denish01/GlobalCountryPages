# Where to Stay in Uganda — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Uganda, the best places to stay are in the capital city of Kampala or in the nearby towns of Entebbe and Jinja, which offer a range of accommodation options to suit different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Kololo** (Kampala)
Best For: Luxury travelers and business travelers
Vibe: Kololo is a upscale neighborhood with a relaxed atmosphere, known for its beautiful views of Lake Victoria and its proximity to the city center. The area is home to many embassies and international organizations, giving it a diplomatic and secure feel.
Price Range: $100-500/night
Pros: Kololo offers easy access to the city's main attractions, a range of high-end restaurants and bars, and a secure environment. The area is also close to the Kampala Golf Course and the Uganda Museum.
Cons: Kololo can be quite expensive, and the area is not as vibrant as other parts of the city.

**Entebbe** (Entebbe)
Best For: Families and travelers looking for a relaxed beach vacation
Vibe: Entebbe is a charming town located on the shores of Lake Victoria, with a laid-back atmosphere and a beautiful beach. The area is known for its water sports, fishing, and boat cruises.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Entebbe offers a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, a beautiful beach, and a range of outdoor activities. The area is also close to the Entebbe International Airport and the Uganda Wildlife Education Centre.
Cons: Entebbe can be quite quiet, and the area is not as developed as other parts of the country.

**Kabale** (Kabale)
Best For: Backpackers and adventure travelers
Vibe: Kabale is a bustling town located in the southwestern part of the country, known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities. The area is close to Lake Bunyonyi and the Bwindi Impenetrable National Park.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Kabale offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and boat cruises. The area is also close to several national parks and game reserves.
Cons: Kabale can be quite crowded, and the area is not as developed as other parts of the country.

**Nakasero** (Kampala)
Best For: Business travelers and luxury travelers
Vibe: Nakasero is a upscale neighborhood located in the heart of Kampala, known for its beautiful views of the city and its proximity to the main business district. The area is home to many hotels, restaurants, and bars.
Price Range: $100-500/night
Pros: Nakasero offers easy access to the city's main attractions, a range of high-end restaurants and bars, and a secure environment. The area is also close to the Kampala Golf Course and the Uganda Museum.
Cons: Nakasero can be quite expensive, and the area is not as vibrant as other parts of the city.

**Jinja** (Jinja)
Best For: Adventure travelers and backpackers
Vibe: Jinja is a bustling town located on the shores of Lake Victoria, known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities. The area is close to the source of the Nile River and the Bujagali Falls.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Jinja offers a range of outdoor activities, including white-water rafting, kayaking, and boat cruises. The area is also close to several national parks and game reserves.
Cons: Jinja can be quite crowded, and the area is not as developed as other parts of the country.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book your accommodation in advance, especially during peak season (June to September and December to February).
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, and Airbnb to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider booking a room with a view, especially in areas like Kololo and Entebbe.
- Negotiate the price, especially if you're booking a room for an extended period.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during peak season.
- Read reviews and check the cancellation policy before booking.
- Use a credit card or PayPal to book your accommodation, as these offer more protection than debit cards or cash.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas like the Old Taxi Park in Kampala, which can be crowded and chaotic.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas.
- Use a reputable taxi or ride-hailing service, and always agree on the price before you start your journey.
- Keep your valuables secure, and consider using a hotel safe or a money belt.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events.
- Stay up-to-date with local news and events, and follow government travel advisories.
