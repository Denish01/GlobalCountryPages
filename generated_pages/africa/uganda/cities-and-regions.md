# Uganda Cities & Regions Guide

Uganda is divided into 134 districts, which are grouped into four main administrative regions, with the main cities and regions being Kampala, the capital, and other major urban centers like Kira, Mbarara, and Entebbe, as well as key regions such as Buganda, Western Uganda, and Northern Uganda.

[SECTION] How Uganda Is Divided [/SECTION]
Uganda's administrative structure is divided into four main regions: the Central Region, the Eastern Region, the Northern Region, and the Western Region. These regions are further divided into 134 districts, which are the second-level administrative divisions. The districts are then subdivided into counties, sub-counties, parishes, and villages. This structure is designed to provide a framework for the delivery of public services, as well as to facilitate local governance and administration.

The four main regions of Uganda are distinct in terms of their geography, culture, and economy. The Central Region, which includes the capital city of Kampala, is the most populous and economically developed region. The Eastern Region is known for its agricultural production, while the Northern Region is home to several national parks and game reserves. The Western Region is known for its natural beauty, with several lakes, rivers, and mountains.

[SECTION] Major Cities [/SECTION]
**Kampala** (1.6 million)
Role: capital, economic hub
Known For: Kampala is known for its vibrant cultural scene, with numerous museums, galleries, and performance venues, as well as its bustling markets and nightlife. The city is also home to several important landmarks, including the Uganda Museum and the Kasubi Tombs.
Best For: visitors interested in history, culture, and urban life

**Kira** (317,000)
Role: satellite city
Known For: Kira is a rapidly growing city located just outside of Kampala, known for its modern amenities and infrastructure. The city is home to several shopping centers, restaurants, and entertainment venues.
Best For: visitors looking for a more laid-back and suburban experience

**Mbarara** (195,000)
Role: regional hub
Known For: Mbarara is a major city in western Uganda, known for its agricultural production and trade. The city is also home to several important landmarks, including the Mbarara University and the Mbarara Regional Referral Hospital.
Best For: visitors interested in agriculture, trade, and regional culture

**Entebbe** (79,000)
Role: port city
Known For: Entebbe is a coastal city located on the shores of Lake Victoria, known for its beautiful beaches and water sports. The city is also home to the Entebbe International Airport and the Uganda Wildlife Education Centre.
Best For: visitors interested in water sports, beach life, and wildlife

**Jinja** (72,000)
Role: industrial center
Known For: Jinja is a major industrial city located in eastern Uganda, known for its manufacturing and trade. The city is also home to several important landmarks, including the Jinja Museum and the Bujagali Falls.
Best For: visitors interested in industry, trade, and adventure sports

[SECTION] Regional Differences [/SECTION]
- The regions of Uganda differ significantly in terms of culture, with the Buganda Region being known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional villages, while the Northern Region is home to several distinct ethnic groups with their own languages and customs.
- The economy of Uganda's regions also varies, with the Central Region being the most economically developed, while the Eastern and Northern Regions are more agricultural and rural.
- The climate of Uganda's regions differs, with the Western Region being known for its mild and temperate climate, while the Northern Region is hotter and drier.
- The language spoken in Uganda's regions also varies, with English and Swahili being widely spoken in the Central Region, while local languages such as Luganda and Lusoga are more commonly spoken in the Eastern and Northern Regions.

[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
Uganda has a relatively well-developed transportation network, with several domestic airports, a railway line, and an extensive network of roads. The most common mode of transportation is by bus or taxi, with several private companies operating routes between major cities and towns. Domestic flights are also available, with several airlines operating flights between Entebbe International Airport and other major airports in the country. The railway line connects Kampala to the port city of Mombasa in Kenya, and is primarily used for freight transportation. Driving conditions in Uganda can be challenging, especially during the rainy season, and visitors are advised to exercise caution when driving on rural roads.

Overall, Uganda's transportation network provides a range of options for visitors to get around the country, from domestic flights to bus and taxi services. With some planning and research, visitors can easily navigate the country and explore its many attractions.
