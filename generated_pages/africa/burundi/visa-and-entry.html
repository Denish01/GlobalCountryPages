Visa Policy Overview

Burundi has a moderate visa policy, requiring citizens from most countries to obtain a visa before entering the country. However, a few countries are exempt from visa requirements, including citizens of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Kenya, Rwanda, Tanzania, and Uganda, who can enter Burundi visa-free for a limited period. The country issues various types of visas, including tourist visas, business visas, and transit visas, each with its own set of requirements and regulations.

The visa policy in Burundi is designed to regulate the flow of visitors and ensure national security. The government has implemented measures to facilitate the visa application process, including the introduction of an electronic visa system. However, the visa requirements and regulations can change frequently, and it is essential to verify the current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Burundi before traveling.

Business Travelers: Business travelers must obtain a business visa, which requires an invitation letter from a Burundian company or organization. The visa is typically valid for 30 days, but can be extended for an additional 30 days. Business travelers must provide proof of their business activities and may be required to register with the local authorities.
Students: Students must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a Burundian educational institution. The visa is typically valid for the duration of the study program, and students must provide proof of sufficient funds to support their stay in the country.

The main entry points to Burundi include the Melchior Ndadaye International Airport in Bujumbura and the land borders with the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Rwanda, and Tanzania. Upon arrival, visitors must present their travel documents and complete an arrival card. The immigration authorities will then issue a visa (if required) and stamp the visitor's passport. Visitors must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country and comply with customs regulations.
The entry procedures in Burundi can be straightforward, but visitors must ensure they have all the necessary documents and comply with the regulations. The immigration authorities may ask questions about the purpose of the visit, the length of stay, and the accommodation arrangements. Visitors must also be prepared to provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds to support their stay in the country.
