[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Cameroon has a diverse range of transportation modes, including buses, taxis, minivans, and motorcycles. The quality of public transit varies greatly between cities, with larger cities like Yaounde and Douala having more developed systems. Most people in Cameroon get around using **informal transportation** methods, such as shared taxis and minivans, which are often cheaper but less reliable than formal public transportation. The country's transportation network is also affected by its **geography**, with mountainous regions making it difficult to build and maintain roads.

In general, the transportation system in Cameroon is **underdeveloped**, with a lack of investment in infrastructure and maintenance. This can lead to **congestion** and **delays**, especially in urban areas. However, the government has been working to improve the transportation network, with plans to build new roads and expand public transportation systems. Despite these challenges, Cameroon's transportation system is **affordable**, with many options available for both short and long-distance travel.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use **ride-hailing apps** like Yango or Bolt for convenient and affordable transportation in major cities.
- **Negotiate taxi fares** before starting your journey, as meters are not always available.
- Consider **carpooling** or **sharing taxis** to save money on transportation costs.
- Be aware of **traffic congestion** and **road conditions**, especially during peak hours or in rural areas.
- Use **reputable bus companies** for intercity travel, and book tickets in advance to ensure availability.
- Always **wear a helmet** when riding a motorcycle or scooter, and follow local traffic laws and regulations.
