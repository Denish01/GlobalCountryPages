[SECTION] Overview: Cameroon vs Chad [/SECTION]
Cameroon and Chad are two neighboring countries in Central Africa with distinct economic profiles. Cameroon has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, mining, and manufacturing, while Chad's economy is primarily driven by oil exports. This economic difference is reflected in the cost of living, with Cameroon generally offering lower prices for everyday items and services. The overall cost of living in Cameroon is around 15-20% lower than in Chad, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Cameroon has a more developed infrastructure, with better roads, transportation, and communication networks, which can reduce costs for individuals and businesses. In contrast, Chad's infrastructure is less developed, which can increase costs for transportation, logistics, and other services. Additionally, Cameroon has a more stable economy, with a lower inflation rate, which can help to keep prices stable and predictable.
