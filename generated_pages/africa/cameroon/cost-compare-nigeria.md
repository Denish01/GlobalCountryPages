[SECTION] Overview: Cameroon vs Nigeria [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Cameroon and Nigeria varies significantly, with Cameroon being the more affordable option. The economic context of both countries plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Cameroon has a relatively stable economy, with a growing service sector and a strong agricultural industry. Nigeria, on the other hand, has a more volatile economy, with a significant dependence on oil exports. As a result, Nigeria's economy is more prone to fluctuations in global oil prices, which can impact the cost of living. Overall, Cameroon offers a more stable and affordable environment for expats, students, and retirees.

The cost of living in Cameroon is generally lower due to several factors, including lower housing costs, food prices, and transportation expenses. Additionally, Cameroon has a more developed tourism infrastructure, which can make it easier for foreigners to navigate and find affordable options. Nigeria, on the other hand, has a larger and more diverse economy, with a wider range of job opportunities and a more vibrant cultural scene. However, the higher cost of living in Nigeria can be a significant challenge for those on a budget. Overall, the cost of living in Cameroon is approximately 15-20% lower than in Nigeria, making it a more attractive option for those looking for an affordable lifestyle.
