Visa Policy Overview

Cameroon has a moderate visa policy, requiring visitors from most countries to obtain a visa before entering the country. However, citizens of certain countries, such as those in the Central African Economic and Monetary Community (CEMAC), are exempt from visa requirements. Approximately 20 countries have visa-free access to Cameroon, while others can obtain a visa on arrival or through an eVisa system. The country's visa policy aims to balance the need to attract tourists and business travelers with the need to maintain national security and control over immigration.

The visa policy of Cameroon is subject to change, and new regulations may be introduced at short notice. It is essential for travelers to check the latest requirements before planning their trip. The country's immigration authorities are also working to improve the efficiency and transparency of the visa application process, including the introduction of online application systems and expanded eligibility for visa on arrival.

Tourists: Tourists visiting Cameroon can typically stay for up to 90 days, and may be required to provide proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and health insurance. Extensions of stay are possible, but must be applied for at the immigration authorities in Yaounde. Tourists are also required to register with the local authorities within 48 hours of arrival.
Business Travelers: Business travelers must obtain a business visa, which requires an invitation letter from a Cameroonian company, as well as proof of sufficient funds and health insurance. Business travelers may be required to attend an interview at the embassy or consulate, and must also register with the local authorities within 48 hours of arrival.
Students: Students must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment at a Cameroonian educational institution, as well as sufficient funds and health insurance. Students must also register with the local authorities within 48 hours of arrival, and may be required to attend an interview at the embassy or consulate.
Workers: Workers must obtain a work visa, which requires sponsorship by a Cameroonian employer, as well as proof of qualifications and experience. Workers must also register with the local authorities within 48 hours of arrival, and may be required to attend an interview at the embassy or consulate.

The main entry points for Cameroon are the international airports in Yaounde (NSI) and Douala (DLA), as well as the land borders with neighboring countries. Upon arrival, visitors must present their passport, visa (if required), and other required documents to the immigration authorities. The immigration process typically involves the completion of a declaration form, and the payment of a small fee for the issuance of an entry stamp. Visitors may also be required to undergo a medical examination or provide proof of vaccination against certain diseases.
Cameroon has a number of land borders with neighboring countries, including Nigeria, Chad, Central African Republic, Republic of Congo, Gabon, and Equatorial Guinea. The border crossing procedures vary depending on the country and the type of visa held, but generally involve the presentation of a valid passport and visa, as well as the completion of a declaration form.

- Failure to obtain the necessary vaccinations or health certificates before arrival
