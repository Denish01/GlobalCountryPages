What Is Cameroon?

Cameroon is a sovereign country located in Central Africa, bordered by six countries: Nigeria, Chad, Central African Republic, Republic of Congo, Gabon, and Equatorial Guinea. With a diverse geography, Cameroon is often referred to as "Africa in miniature" due to its varied landscape, which includes mountains, deserts, rainforests, and coastal plains. The country has a rich cultural heritage, with over 200 ethnic groups and a mix of French and English languages, reflecting its history as a former French and British colony.

Cameroon is situated on the Gulf of Guinea, with a coastline along the Atlantic Ocean. The country's geography is characterized by a range of climates, from the tropical south to the semi-arid north. The Cameroon Highlands, which include the Bamenda Highlands and the Adamawa Plateau, are home to several active and dormant volcanoes, including Mount Cameroon, the country's highest peak. The country's diverse geography and climate support a wide range of flora and fauna, making it a popular destination for eco-tourism and wildlife enthusiasts.

Cameroon is known for its vibrant culture, with a mix of traditional and modern influences. The country is home to a thriving music scene, with popular genres such as makossa and bikutsi. The capital city, Yaounde, is a hub of cultural and economic activity, with a range of museums, galleries, and markets showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage. Cameroon is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes popular dishes such as ndole (a stew made with bitter leaves and meat or fish) and jollof rice.

Key History

  1. 1884 - Cameroon becomes a German colony, known as Kamerun, after the German explorer Gustav Nachtigal signs a treaty with local chiefs.
  2. 1911 - Cameroon is divided between France and Britain, with France receiving the larger share of the territory.
  3. 1960 - Cameroon gains independence from France, with Ahmadou Ahidjo as its first president.
  4. 1961 - The British colony of Southern Cameroons joins Cameroon, following a referendum in which the majority of the population votes to join the newly independent country.
  5. 1982 - Paul Biya becomes president of Cameroon, a position he still holds today, making him one of the longest-serving heads of state in Africa.
  6. 1992 - Cameroon introduces a multi-party system, allowing for greater political freedom and opposition to the ruling party.
  7. 2014 - Cameroon passes a law making bilingualism (French and English) an official policy, in an effort to promote national unity and address linguistic and cultural tensions.

What Makes Cameroon Unique

  • Cameroon is home to over 200 ethnic groups, each with their own language and cultural traditions.
  • The country has a unique geological feature, the Lake Nyos, which is a deep lake that is home to a large deposit of carbon dioxide.
  • Cameroon is one of the most biodiverse countries in Africa, with a wide range of flora and fauna, including lowland gorillas, chimpanzees, and forest elephants.
  • The country has a thriving music scene, with popular genres such as makossa and bikutsi.
  • Cameroon is home to the Waza National Park, which is one of the largest and most important conservation areas in West Africa.

Quick Summary

Cameroon is a sovereign country in Central Africa, known for its diverse geography, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant music scene, with a unique blend of French and English languages and a history as a former French and British colony.
