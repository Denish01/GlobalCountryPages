[SECTION] Overview: Cameroon vs Equatorial Guinea [/SECTION]
Cameroon and Equatorial Guinea are two neighboring countries in Central Africa, with distinct economic profiles. Cameroon has a more diverse economy, with a strong agricultural sector, while Equatorial Guinea is heavily reliant on its oil and gas reserves. As a result, Equatorial Guinea has a higher GDP per capita, but also a higher cost of living. The main difference in costs between the two countries lies in the prices of accommodation, food, and transportation. Cameroon offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for rent, meals, and transportation, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Cameroon has a larger population and a more developed economy, which has led to a more competitive market and lower prices. In contrast, Equatorial Guinea's economy is smaller and more dependent on oil exports, which has resulted in higher prices for goods and services. Overall, Cameroon is the more affordable option, with a cost of living that is around 30-40% lower than Equatorial Guinea. This makes it an attractive destination for those looking for a more affordable lifestyle in Central Africa.
