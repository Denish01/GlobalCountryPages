# Education Costs in Republic of Congo

The cost of education in Republic of Congo varies greatly depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Republic of Congo is divided into public and private sectors, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, but often lacking in quality and resources. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a higher quality of education, but at a significantly higher cost. The language of instruction is **French**, and education is compulsory for children between the ages of 6 and 16. The school system is modeled after the French system, with a focus on core subjects such as mathematics, science, and language.

The availability of international schools in Republic of Congo is limited, with most options being found in the capital city of **Brazzaville**. These schools offer a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the British curriculum, and are popular among expat families. However, the cost of attending an international school can be prohibitively expensive, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Tips [/SECTION]
- The quality of education in Republic of Congo is generally lower compared to other countries in the region, due to limited resources and infrastructure.
- Expats often opt for international schools, which offer a higher quality of education and a range of curricula.
- Scholarship opportunities are limited, but available for students who excel in their studies.
- Online learning options are becoming increasingly popular, with many universities and institutions offering online courses and degree programs.
- The **Marien Ngouabi University** is one of the top-ranked universities in the country, offering a range of programs, including business and engineering.
- It is recommended that students and families research and visit schools before making a decision, to get a sense of the quality of education and the school community.
