[SECTION] Economic Overview [/SECTION]
Reunion's economy is classified as an upper-middle-income economy, with a high standard of living compared to other African islands. The island's economic development is heavily influenced by its relationship with France, which provides significant financial support and investment. Reunion's economy has experienced steady growth over the years, driven by a combination of agriculture, tourism, and services. The island's unique cultural heritage and natural beauty make it an attractive destination for tourists, while its agricultural sector produces a range of crops, including sugarcane, vanilla, and geranium.

Reunion's economic trajectory is expected to continue on a positive path, driven by investments in infrastructure, tourism, and renewable energy. The island's government has implemented various initiatives to diversify the economy and reduce dependence on French support. However, the economy still faces challenges, including a high unemployment rate and a reliance on imported goods. The island's remote location and limited natural resources also pose challenges for economic development.

Reunion's economy is also characterized by a high level of social benefits and public services, which are funded by France. The island's residents enjoy access to high-quality healthcare, education, and social security, which contributes to a high standard of living. However, the economy's dependence on French support also limits its ability to develop a more independent and self-sustaining economy.
