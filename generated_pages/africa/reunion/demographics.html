Population Overview

Reunion's population size is relatively small compared to other islands in the region, but its growth rate is steady, with a 0.8% increase per annum. The island's urbanization rate is high, with approximately 97% of the population residing in urban areas, primarily in the capital city of Saint-Denis and other major towns. This trend is driven by economic opportunities, education, and access to healthcare, which are more readily available in urban centers. Migration patterns in Reunion are influenced by its historical ties with France, as well as its proximity to other Indian Ocean islands, such as Madagascar and Mauritius.

The population of Reunion is projected to continue growing, albeit at a slow rate, due to a combination of natural increase and net migration. The island's population is relatively young, with a median age of 34 years, which has implications for the labor market, education, and healthcare systems. Reunion's population density is relatively high, with an average of 357 people per square kilometer, which can put pressure on the island's natural resources and infrastructure.

Reunion's population is also characterized by a high life expectancy, with an average of 78 years, which is comparable to other developed countries. The fertility rate in Reunion is approximately 2.2 children per woman, which is slightly above the replacement rate, indicating a stable population growth rate. Overall, Reunion's population is diverse, urbanized, and relatively young, with a high life expectancy and a stable growth rate.

The ethnic diversity of Reunion is a result of its strategic location in the Indian Ocean, which has made it a hub for trade, migration, and cultural exchange. The various ethnic groups have contributed to the island's unique cultural identity, with a blend of African, European, Asian, and Middle Eastern influences. While there are some tensions between ethnic groups, Reunion is generally considered a harmonious and multicultural society, with a strong sense of Creole identity and a shared history.

Languages

  • French is the official language, used in government, education, and formal settings
  • Creole, also known as Reunion Creole, is widely spoken as a vernacular language
  • Tamil and other Indian languages are spoken by the Indian community
  • Mandarin Chinese is spoken by the Chinese community
  • Other languages, such as Arabic and Malagasy, are spoken by smaller communities

Urbanization & Major Cities

  • Reunion has a highly urbanized population, with approximately 97% of the population residing in urban areas
  • Saint-Denis is the capital and largest city, with a population of over 150,000
  • Saint-Pierre is the second-largest city, with a population of over 80,000
  • Saint-Andre and Saint-Benoit are other major urban centers, with significant economic and cultural activities
  • The urban-rural split is pronounced, with a high concentration of population and economic activity in urban areas, and a more dispersed population in rural areas.
