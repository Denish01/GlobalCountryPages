[SECTION] What Is Reunion? [/SECTION]
Reunion is a **French overseas department and region** located in the Indian Ocean, off the coast of East Africa. It is situated about 200 kilometers southwest of Mauritius and 700 kilometers east of Madagascar. The island has a total area of approximately 2,512 square kilometers and is home to a diverse population of around 0.9 million people. Reunion is known for its unique cultural heritage, which is a blend of French, African, and Asian influences. The island's economy is primarily driven by **sugar cane production**, **tourism**, and **agriculture**.

Geographically, Reunion is a volcanic island with a rugged terrain, featuring numerous mountains, forests, and waterfalls. The island's highest peak, **Piton des Neiges**, stands at an elevation of 3,070 meters above sea level. The climate is tropical, with two main seasons: a hot and humid summer from November to April, and a cooler winter from May to October. Reunion's strategic location in the Indian Ocean makes it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region.

Reunion's sovereignty status is that of an **overseas department and region of France**, which means that it is an integral part of France and is subject to French law and jurisdiction. The island has its own regional assembly and is represented in the French National Assembly and Senate. Reunion's residents are French citizens and enjoy the same rights and privileges as citizens in mainland France.
