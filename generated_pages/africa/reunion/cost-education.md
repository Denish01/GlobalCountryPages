# Education Costs in Reunion

The cost of education in Reunion varies depending on the type of institution and the level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge tuition fees ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Reunion is based on the French model, with a strong emphasis on public education. Public schools in Reunion are generally considered to be of good quality, with a high literacy rate and a comprehensive curriculum. The language of instruction is French, and students typically attend school from the age of 6 to 16. Compulsory education lasts for 10 years, and students can then choose to pursue further education at a lycée or a university. Private schools are also available, and these often offer a more specialized or bilingual education.

International schools are available in Reunion, particularly in the capital city of Saint-Denis. These schools cater to expatriate families and offer a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the French baccalauréat. The quality of education in Reunion is generally considered to be high, with a strong focus on academic achievement and a wide range of extracurricular activities. However, the education system can be quite rigid, and students may have limited flexibility in terms of course selection and career paths.
