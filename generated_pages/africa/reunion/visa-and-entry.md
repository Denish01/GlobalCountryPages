# Reunion Visa & Entry Requirements

As a territory of France, Reunion's visa requirements are governed by French regulations, and citizens of certain countries may not need a visa to enter Reunion, while others may need to obtain a visa prior to arrival.

Reunion has a moderate visa policy, with citizens of many countries eligible for visa-free entry or able to obtain a visa on arrival. As a French territory, Reunion's visa rules are aligned with those of France, and the territory is part of the Schengen Area. Citizens of countries that are part of the European Union, as well as some other countries, can enter Reunion without a visa and stay for up to 90 days. However, citizens of other countries may need to obtain a visa prior to arrival, and the requirements can vary depending on the nationality and purpose of the visit.

Reunion's visa policy is designed to balance the need to control immigration with the need to promote tourism and economic development. The territory's unique location in the Indian Ocean, with its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, makes it an attractive destination for tourists and business travelers alike. As a result, the visa policy is designed to be flexible and welcoming, while also ensuring the security and integrity of the territory.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Reunion without a visa if they are citizens of eligible countries, and can stay for up to 90 days. They will need to show proof of onward travel and sufficient funds to support their stay. Tourists can also apply for an extension of their stay, but this must be done prior to the expiration of their initial stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to obtain a visa prior to arrival, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They will need to show an invitation letter from a Reunion-based company, as well as proof of their business activities. Business travelers can stay for up to 90 days, but may need to apply for an extension if they need to stay longer.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in Reunion will need to obtain a student visa prior to arrival. They will need to show proof of enrollment in a Reunion-based educational institution, as well as proof of sufficient funds to support their stay. Student visas are typically valid for the duration of the student's studies.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Reunion will need to obtain a work visa prior to arrival. They will need to show a job offer from a Reunion-based employer, as well as proof of their qualifications and experience. Work visas are typically valid for the duration of the worker's employment contract.
