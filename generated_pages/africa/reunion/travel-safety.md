# Reunion Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Reunion is generally a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a strong focus on protecting visitors, but as with any travel, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Reunion, an overseas department of France, boasts a reputation for being a secure destination, with crime rates significantly lower than in many other parts of the world. The island's strong economy, good governance, and high standard of living contribute to its safety. However, as with any popular tourist destination, petty crimes such as pickpocketing and theft can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Visitors should remain vigilant and take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

The risk of violent crime in Reunion is relatively low, but it's not entirely absent. There have been instances of burglary, robbery, and assault, although these are relatively rare. The local authorities take the safety of tourists seriously, and there is a dedicated tourist police force to handle any issues that may arise. Additionally, Reunion is prone to natural disasters such as cyclones and earthquakes, which can pose a risk to travelers. It's essential to stay informed about weather conditions and follow local advice in the event of an emergency.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as overpriced souvenirs or fake guided tours
- **Credit card skimming** and ATM fraud
- **cyclone** and earthquake risks, particularly during the summer months
- **water-borne illnesses** from consuming untreated water
- **sunburn** and heat exhaustion from not taking adequate precautions
- **road accidents** due to reckless driving or poor road conditions

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and typhoid are recommended for travel to Reunion
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is recommended for sensitive stomachs
- **Medical Facilities:** Reunion has good-quality medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, but medical evacuation to France may be necessary for serious conditions
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, and many medications are easily accessible

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or a money belt
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or debit cards instead
- Stay informed about weather conditions and follow local advice in the event of an emergency
- Wear protective clothing and sunscreen to prevent sunburn and heat exhaustion
- Drive defensively and follow local traffic laws
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, even when you're in the room

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Reunion is generally a safe destination for solo and women travelers, but it's still essential to take normal precautions to protect yourself
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night, and consider joining a guided tour or group activity
- Dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events
- Keep your hotel room number and personal belongings secure, and consider using a hotel safe or a money belt
