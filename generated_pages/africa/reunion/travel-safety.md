# Reunion Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Reunion is generally a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a strong emphasis on visitor safety, but as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Reunion, a French territory in the Indian Ocean, boasts a reputation for being a secure and welcoming destination. The island's strong economy, low unemployment rate, and high standard of living contribute to its safety. However, as with any popular tourist destination, petty crimes such as pickpocketing and theft can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Visitors should remain vigilant and take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

The Reunionese authorities take visitor safety seriously, and the island has a dedicated tourist police force that patrols popular areas and provides assistance to visitors. Additionally, the island's compact size and well-developed infrastructure make it easy for tourists to get around and find help when needed. However, it's essential to be aware of the potential risks associated with natural disasters, such as tropical cyclones and volcanic eruptions, which can affect the island.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake gemstone sales and overpriced souvenirs
- Traffic accidents, particularly when driving on the island's winding roads
- Natural disasters, such as tropical cyclones and volcanic eruptions
- Petty crime in rural areas, such as theft and vandalism
- Overcharging by some taxi drivers and vendors
- Beach safety, with strong currents and riptides at some beaches

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for travelers to Reunion
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended for visitors
- **Medical Facilities:** Reunion has well-equipped hospitals and medical facilities, particularly in Saint-Denis and Saint-Pierre, but visitors may need to pay upfront for treatment
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, particularly in urban areas, and many pharmacies are open late or 24 hours

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or a money belt
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Be cautious when driving, particularly on the island's winding roads
- Follow local advice and instructions in the event of a natural disaster
- Stay hydrated and protected from the sun, particularly when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities
- Learn some basic French phrases, such as "bonjour" and "merci," to help you navigate and interact with locals

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Reunion is generally a safe destination for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a welcoming atmosphere
- However, visitors should still take necessary precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and being mindful of their surroundings
- Consider joining a guided tour or group activity to meet other travelers and stay safe
- Be respectful of local customs and dress modestly, particularly when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events
