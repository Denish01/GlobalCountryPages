[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Amphitheatre of El Jem**, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is one of the most impressive Roman amphitheaters in Africa, and hosts the annual **International Festival of Symphony Music**.
- The **Bardo Museum** in Tunis is home to a vast collection of **Roman mosaics**, which are considered some of the finest in the world.
- The **Medina of Tunis**, a historic neighborhood in the capital city, is a treasure trove of **Islamic architecture**, with its narrow streets, bustling markets, and historic mosques.
- The **Festival of Carthage**, a annual cultural festival, celebrates Tunisian music, dance, and theater, and features performances by local and international artists.
- The **Traditional Tunisian Cuisine**, which is a unique blend of **Arabic**, **Mediterranean**, and **African** flavors, is renowned for its delicious dishes, such as **couscous** and **harissa**.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Ibn Khaldun**, a Tunisian historian and philosopher, is considered one of the greatest thinkers of the **Islamic Golden Age**, and his works have had a profound impact on the development of **sociology** and **economics**.
- **Tunisian diaspora**, with communities in countries such as **France**, **Canada**, and **United States**, have made significant contributions to their adopted countries, in fields such as **science**, **art**, and **politics**.
- **Tunisian women**, who have made significant progress in recent years, have become a driving force for **social change**, with many women holding key positions in **government**, **business**, and **civil society**.
- **Mohamed Bouazizi**, a Tunisian street vendor, whose self-immolation in 2010 sparked the **Arab Spring**, is remembered as a symbol of resistance against **oppression** and **corruption**.
