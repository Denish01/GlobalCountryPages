# Cost of Living in Tunisia - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Tunisia is generally affordable, with a daily budget of $30-50 USD for backpackers, $50-100 USD for mid-range travelers, and $150-250 USD for comfort seekers.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $30-50 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $50-100 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $150-250 USD
Currency: TND
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 5-10% in tourist areas
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Tunisia? [/SECTION]
Tunisia is considered one of the more affordable countries in North Africa, with a cost of living that is lower than its neighbors, Algeria and Libya. The country offers a unique blend of Mediterranean and Arabic cultures, rich history, and beautiful landscapes, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. Compared to global averages, Tunisia is relatively cheap, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation being significantly lower than in Western Europe or North America.

The cost of living in Tunisia can vary depending on the location, with cities like Tunis and Sousse being more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. However, overall, Tunisia is a great value destination, offering a high quality of life at a relatively low cost. Whether you're a backpacker, a mid-range traveler, or a comfort seeker, Tunisia has something to offer, from affordable accommodation and delicious street food to luxurious resorts and fine dining restaurants.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel, which can be significantly cheaper than mid-range or luxury hotels.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls, which offer delicious and affordable meals.
- Use public transportation, such as buses or metros, which are cheap and efficient.
- Avoid drinking tap water and opt for bottled water instead, which is relatively cheap.
- Haggling is expected in markets and souks, so be prepared to negotiate prices.
- Learn some basic Arabic phrases, such as "shukraan" (thank you) and "as-salamu alaykum" (hello), which can go a long way in showing respect for the local culture and getting better prices.
