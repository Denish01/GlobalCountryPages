[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Tunisia has a well-developed transportation network, with a range of options available for getting around, including buses, taxis, and trains. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent bus services and a reliable train network. Most people in Tunisia get around using public transportation, with buses being the most popular mode of transport. The country's roads are also well-maintained, making it easy to get around by car or taxi.

In larger cities like Tunis and Sfax, the public transportation system is more developed, with a greater frequency of buses and trains. However, in smaller towns and rural areas, the options may be more limited, and taxis or private cars may be the only viable option. Additionally, the country's transportation network is relatively affordable, with the cost of a monthly transit pass being around $25 USD.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a monthly transit pass, which can be used on buses and trains.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Bolt to get around larger cities, as they are often cheaper than metered taxis.
- Always agree on the fare before starting a taxi ride, and be prepared to negotiate.
- Consider renting a car for long-distance journeys, as it can be more cost-effective than taking a domestic flight or train.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas like bus stations and markets.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps to ensure your safety, especially at night.
