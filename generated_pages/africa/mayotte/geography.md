# Mayotte Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

Mayotte is an overseas department and region of France located in the Indian Ocean, off the coast of East Africa, and is part of the Comoros archipelago.

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Mayotte is situated in the northern part of the Mozambique Channel, approximately 200 miles northwest of Madagascar. The territory is part of the Comoros archipelago, which also includes the islands of Grande Comore, Anjouan, and Moheli. Mayotte is bordered by the Comoros to the east and southeast, and by Madagascar to the west and southwest. The island is surrounded by the Indian Ocean, with the Mozambique Channel to the west and the Comoros Basin to the east.

The strategic location of Mayotte in the Indian Ocean makes it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region. The territory's proximity to the African mainland and its membership in the European Union through France also make it an attractive destination for tourists and investors. The borders of Mayotte are defined by its maritime boundaries with the Comoros and Madagascar, and the territory has a total coastline of approximately 185 kilometers.
