Political System

The government of Mayotte is based on the French system, with the President of France serving as the head of state and the Prefect of Mayotte as the head of government. The Departmental Council of Mayotte, composed of 26 members, is the legislative body responsible for making laws and decisions for the territory. Mayotte's political system is characterized by a unitary system, where the central government in France has significant control over the territory's affairs. The separation of powers is also present, with the executive, legislative, and judicial branches each having distinct roles and responsibilities. The executive branch is responsible for enforcing laws, the legislative branch for making laws, and the judicial branch for interpreting laws.

The political system in Mayotte is also influenced by its history and cultural context. The territory has a unique cultural identity, with a mix of French, African, and Islamic influences. This diversity is reflected in the territory's language, with French being the official language, but many residents also speaking Mahorais, a local dialect. The currency used in Mayotte is the Euro, which is the same as in France.

Mayotte's relationship with France is also an important aspect of its political system. As an overseas department and region of France, Mayotte is represented in the French National Assembly and Senate, and its residents are French citizens. This relationship provides Mayotte with access to French funding, infrastructure, and services, but it also means that the territory has limited autonomy and decision-making power.

Elections

  • The electoral system in Mayotte is based on universal suffrage, with all citizens aged 18 and above eligible to vote.
  • Elections for the Departmental Council of Mayotte are held every six years, with the most recent election taking place in 2021.
  • The last presidential election in France, which also applies to Mayotte, was held in 2022, with Emmanuel Macron winning a second term.
  • Voter participation in Mayotte is generally high, with turnout rates often exceeding 80% in local and national elections.

Political Stability & Challenges

  • The current political climate in Mayotte is relatively stable, with a strong presence of French institutions and a growing economy.
  • Key issues facing the territory include unemployment, which affects over 20% of the population, and poverty, which is estimated to affect around 30% of the population.
  • Regional dynamics, including the relationship with the Comoros and Madagascar, are also important considerations for Mayotte's government.
  • The territory's freedom indices are generally positive, with Mayotte ranking 24th out of 180 countries on the Human Freedom Index.
  • Mayotte also faces challenges related to climate change, including rising sea levels and increased frequency of natural disasters.
  • The territory's biodiversity is also an important consideration, with Mayotte being home to a unique and fragile ecosystem.

Foreign Relations

  • Mayotte is a member of several international organizations, including the United Nations, the European Union, and the Indian Ocean Commission.
  • The territory has a close relationship with France, which provides significant financial and military support.
  • Mayotte also has diplomatic relations with several countries in the region, including the Comoros, Madagascar, and Mauritius.
  • The territory's international stance is generally aligned with that of France, with a focus on promoting peace, stability, and human rights in the region.
  • Mayotte is also a member of the African, Caribbean and Pacific Group of States, which aims to promote economic cooperation and development among its member states.
