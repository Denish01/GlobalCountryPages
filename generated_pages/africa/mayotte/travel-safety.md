Mayotte is generally a safe destination to visit, with low crime rates compared to other territories in the region, but tourists should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings to ensure a trouble-free trip.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Mayotte, an overseas department of France, boasts a relatively low crime rate compared to other African territories. The island's strong economy and stable governance contribute to its safety, making it an attractive destination for tourists. However, as with any travel destination, it is essential to be mindful of one's surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. Petty crimes, such as pickpocketing and theft, can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, but violent crimes are rare. The local authorities and tourist police are generally helpful and responsive to visitors' needs.

The island's natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly locals make it an ideal destination for those seeking a unique and relaxing experience. Nevertheless, visitors should be aware of the potential risks associated with traveling to a tropical island, such as natural disasters like cyclones and health risks like mosquito-borne diseases. By taking necessary precautions and staying informed, tourists can minimize their risk and have a safe and enjoyable trip to Mayotte.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Petty theft and pickpocketing in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as overpriced services and fake products
- Credit card skimming and ATM scams
- Mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and chikungunya
- Strong currents and rip tides at beaches
- Cyclones and tropical storms during the rainy season
- Limited access to medical facilities and emergency services in rural areas

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to Mayotte
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors are advised to use bottled or filtered water
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are generally good in Mamoudzou and other major towns, but limited in rural areas
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are available in major towns, but visitors may need to travel to Mamoudzou for specialized medical care

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas
- Keep doors and windows locked, especially in coastal areas
- Be cautious of strong currents and rip tides when swimming
- Use sunscreen and insect repellent to prevent sunburn and mosquito-borne diseases
- Stay informed about local weather conditions and cyclone warnings

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, especially at night
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary and whereabouts
