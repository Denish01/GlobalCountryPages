Mayotte, an overseas department of France, is notable for its remarkable achievements in various fields, including its unique cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and significant contributions to the global community.

Cultural Treasures

  • The Dzaoudzi Mosque, a historic mosque that dates back to the 19th century, is a notable example of the island's rich cultural heritage.
  • The Mayotte Museum, located in Mamoudzou, showcases the island's history, culture, and traditions, and features a range of exhibits and artifacts.
  • The Shimaore Language, which is spoken by the majority of the population, is an important part of Mayotte's cultural identity and is recognized as an official language.
  • The Mayotte Carnival, which takes place annually, is a vibrant and colorful celebration of the island's music, dance, and culture.
  • The Traditional Mayotte House, known as the Tzimagaria, is a unique and distinctive form of architecture that reflects the island's cultural and historical heritage.

People & Global Impact

  • Abdourahamane Soilihi, a Mayotte-born politician, has played a key role in promoting the island's interests at the national and international levels.
  • The Mayotte Diaspora, which is estimated to number over 100,000 people, has made significant contributions to the economic and cultural development of the island, and has helped to promote Mayotte's interests abroad.
  • Youssouf Elamine, a Mayotte-born athlete, has won several international awards and has helped to promote the island's sporting reputation.
  • The Mayotte Community, which is known for its warm hospitality and welcoming nature, has helped to make the island a popular destination for tourists and visitors.

What Visitors Love

  • The Beaches of Mayotte, which are known for their crystal-clear waters and white sand, are a major draw for tourists and offer a range of activities, including swimming, snorkeling, and diving.
  • The Local Cuisine, which combines African, Arab, and French influences, is a unique and delicious reflection of the island's cultural heritage, and features a range of dishes, including Poulet au coco and Brochettes de poisson.
  • The Scenic Landscapes, which include the Mont Choungui and the Baie de Boueni, offer breathtaking views and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and trekking.
  • The Hospitality of the Mayotte People, which is known for its warmth and generosity, has helped to make the island a popular destination for tourists and visitors.
  • The Rich Cultural Heritage, which is reflected in the island's music, dance, and traditions, is a major draw for visitors and offers a unique and enriching cultural experience.
  • The Water Sports, which include sailing, diving, and snorkeling, are a major attraction for visitors and offer a range of exciting and challenging activities.

Hidden Gems

  • The Island of Pamandzi, which is located off the coast of Mayotte, is a hidden gem that offers a range of beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning scenery.
  • The Mayotte Lagoon, which is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, is a unique and diverse ecosystem that supports a range of marine life and offers a range of outdoor activities, including snorkeling and diving.
  • The Mont Choungui, which is the highest point on the island, offers breathtaking views and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and trekking.
