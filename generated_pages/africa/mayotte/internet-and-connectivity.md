The internet in Mayotte is generally good, with reliable connections available in urban areas, but can be slower and less consistent in rural regions, making it essential to plan ahead and research the best options for staying connected during your visit.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Mayotte is relatively well-developed, considering its status as an overseas department of France. The average internet speed is around 10 Mbps download, which is sufficient for basic browsing, email, and social media. However, the quality of the internet can vary greatly depending on the location, with urban areas like Mamoudzou and Koungou generally having faster and more reliable connections than rural areas. The mobile network coverage is good, with the main carriers Orange, SFR, and Free providing decent coverage across the island.

The reliability of the internet in Mayotte can be a concern, especially during peak hours or in areas with high demand. Power outages and network maintenance can also cause disruptions to internet services. However, overall, the internet infrastructure in Mayotte is relatively stable, and most users can expect to have a decent connection most of the time. It's worth noting that the internet quality can be affected by the weather, especially during the rainy season, and users may experience slower speeds or dropped connections.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Mayotte, you can visit one of the main carrier's stores, such as Orange or SFR, and provide a valid passport and proof of address. The cost of a tourist SIM card is around 10 USD, and it usually comes with a set amount of data, voice minutes, and texts. The recommended carriers for tourists are Orange and SFR, as they offer a range of plans and have good coverage across the island. You can also purchase a SIM card at the airport or at some retail stores, but it's best to check with the carrier for the most up-to-date plans and pricing.
