[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
The utility infrastructure in Mayotte is relatively well-developed, with access to **electricity**, **water**, and **gas** available to most of the population. However, the costs of these utilities can fluctuate due to seasonal variation, with higher demand during the summer months. The **French** government, which oversees the territory, has implemented measures to stabilize prices and ensure a reliable supply of energy and water. Despite these efforts, residents may still experience occasional power outages and water shortages, particularly during periods of high demand.

The cost of utilities in Mayotte is generally higher than in other parts of Africa, due to the territory's remote location and reliance on imported fuel. However, the **French** government has implemented subsidies and other measures to help reduce the burden of utility costs on low-income households. As a result, the cost of utilities in Mayotte is relatively stable, with minimal fluctuations from year to year. It's worth noting that the cost of utilities can vary depending on the location, with urban areas tend to have higher costs than rural areas.
