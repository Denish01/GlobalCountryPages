[SECTION] How Mayotte Is Divided [/SECTION]
Mayotte is an overseas department of France, located in the Indian Ocean, and is divided into 17 communes, which are similar to municipalities. The communes are further grouped into two main departments: the Petite-Terre and the Grande-Terre. The Petite-Terre is home to the capital city of Mamoudzou, while the Grande-Terre is the larger of the two islands and is home to several other major towns and cities. The administrative structure of Mayotte is based on the French system, with a prefect and a general council responsible for governing the island.

The 17 communes of Mayotte are responsible for providing local services such as education, healthcare, and sanitation, while the departmental government is responsible for matters such as infrastructure, transportation, and economic development. The communes are also responsible for managing their own budgets and making decisions on local issues. Overall, the administrative structure of Mayotte is designed to provide a high level of autonomy to the local communities, while also ensuring that the island is well-integrated into the French system.
