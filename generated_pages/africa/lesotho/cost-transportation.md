[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Lesotho's transportation network is relatively underdeveloped, with a lack of modern infrastructure and limited public transportation options. The main modes of transportation in Lesotho are **minibuses**, **taxis**, and **private vehicles**, with most people relying on these options to get around. The quality of public transit is generally poor, with limited coverage and unreliable schedules, making it difficult for people to access basic services and amenities. As a result, many people in Lesotho rely on **walking** or **cycling** as their primary mode of transportation.

The lack of a well-developed public transportation system in Lesotho makes it challenging for people to move around, especially in rural areas where **off-road vehicles** are often the only option. The government has been working to improve the transportation network, with plans to upgrade roads and introduce new public transportation systems. However, for now, the transportation options in Lesotho remain limited, and people often have to rely on **informal transportation** options, such as hitchhiking or using unlicensed taxis.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using **minibuses** or **shared taxis**, which are often cheaper than metered taxis.
- When using taxis, always **negotiate the fare** before starting your journey to avoid being overcharged.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially when using public transportation.
- Consider **renting a car** if you plan to travel extensively, as it can be more cost-effective than using taxis or public transportation.
- Always **wear a seatbelt** and follow local traffic rules to ensure your safety on the road.
- Download **maps** or **GPS apps** to help navigate Lesotho's roads and find the best routes.
