Location & Borders

Lesotho is a sovereign country located in the southern part of the African continent. It is completely surrounded by South Africa, making it one of the three countries in the world that are entirely enclaved by a single country. Lesotho is situated in the Drakensberg Mountains and the Maluti Mountains, which provide a unique and breathtaking landscape. The country's location in the southern hemisphere also means that its seasons are reversed compared to those in the northern hemisphere.

Lesotho's border with South Africa stretches for approximately 909 kilometers, making it one of the longest international borders in southern Africa. The country's location in the highveld region of southern Africa also means that it has a unique cultural and historical heritage. Lesotho has a long history of interaction with its neighboring country, South Africa, and the two countries have a complex and multifaceted relationship. The border between Lesotho and South Africa is also home to several border crossings, which facilitate the movement of people and goods between the two countries.

Terrain & Landscape

Lesotho's terrain is characterized by mountains and highlands, with the Drakensberg Mountains and the Maluti Mountains being the most prominent features. The country's landscape is also dotted with rivers, valleys, and plateaus, which provide a unique and diverse environment. The Orange River, which forms part of the border with South Africa, is one of the most significant rivers in the country. Lesotho's terrain is also home to several waterfalls, including the Maletsunyane Falls, which is one of the highest waterfalls in southern Africa.

The country's mountainous terrain also means that it has a range of climatic zones, from temperate to alpine. The highlands are generally cooler and more temperate, while the lowlands are warmer and more humid. Lesotho's terrain is also home to a range of geological features, including caves, rock formations, and fossils. The country's unique landscape and geology also make it a popular destination for hiking, trekking, and rock climbing.

Lesotho's landscape is also characterized by grasslands and savannas, which provide a habitat for a range of wildlife, including antelopes, zebras, and birds. The country's national parks and game reserves are also home to a range of wildlife, including lions, leopards, and elephants. Lesotho's unique landscape and wildlife make it a popular destination for tourists and adventure seekers.
