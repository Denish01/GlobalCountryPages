Visa Policy Overview

Lesotho has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others require a visa on arrival or in advance. The country is a member of the Southern African Development Community (SADC) and has relaxed visa requirements for citizens of SADC member states. Currently, citizens of over 50 countries can enter Lesotho visa-free, including the United States, the United Kingdom, and many European countries. However, it is essential to check the latest visa requirements before traveling to Lesotho, as these can change frequently.

Lesotho's visa policy aims to promote tourism and economic development while ensuring national security. The country offers various types of visas, including tourist, business, student, and work visas, each with its own set of requirements and regulations. Visitors can stay in Lesotho for a maximum period of 14 days, which can be extended for an additional 14 days with permission from the immigration authorities.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists can enter Lesotho visa-free for a maximum stay of 14 days, which can be extended for an additional 14 days with permission from the immigration authorities. They must provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds to support their stay.

Business Travelers: Business travelers may require a visa on arrival or in advance, depending on their nationality. They must provide an invitation letter from a Lesotho-based company and proof of sufficient funds to support their stay.

Students: Students must apply for a student visa in advance through the Lesotho embassy or consulate. They must provide proof of enrollment at a Lesotho-based educational institution and sufficient funds to support their stay.

Workers: Workers must apply for a work visa in advance through the Lesotho embassy or consulate. They must provide a sponsorship letter from a Lesotho-based employer and meet specific requirements, including proof of qualifications and experience.

Entry Points & Procedures

Lesotho has two main airports: Moshoeshoe I International Airport (MSU) and Matekane Air Strip (MTK). The country also shares land borders with South Africa, and visitors can enter by road through several border posts. Upon arrival, visitors must present their travel documents and complete an arrival/departure card. They will then proceed to immigration, where their documents will be checked and their entry will be stamped. It is essential to ensure that all documents are in order and that visitors have the necessary visas and permits before arriving in Lesotho.

Lesotho's immigration procedures can be straightforward, but it is crucial to be prepared and to have all necessary documents ready. Visitors should also be aware of any specific requirements, such as proof of onward travel or sufficient funds, to avoid any issues during the entry process.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity

  • Not completing the visa application form correctly (if required)
  • Not having the necessary documents, such as an invitation letter or sponsorship letter
  • Not being aware of any specific requirements, such as health certificates or proof of qualifications and experience
