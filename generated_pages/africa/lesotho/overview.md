[SECTION] What Is Lesotho? [/SECTION]
Lesotho is a **sovereign** country located in Southern Africa, completely surrounded by the country of South Africa. It is a small country with a total area of approximately 30,355 square kilometers, making it one of the smallest countries in Africa. Lesotho is known for its stunning natural beauty, with a unique landscape of mountains, valleys, and rivers. The country has a diverse geography, with a range of climates and ecosystems, from the **Maluti Mountains** in the east to the **Lowlands** in the west.

Lesotho has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong tradition of **Basotho** culture and history. The country is home to a number of important historical sites, including the **Thaba Bosiu** National Monument, which is the former capital of the Kingdom of Lesotho. Lesotho is also known for its traditional crafts, such as **weaving** and **pottery**, which are an important part of the country's cultural identity. The country has a growing economy, with a mix of traditional industries such as **agriculture** and **mining**, as well as a developing **tourism** sector.

Lesotho is a member of the **Commonwealth of Nations** and the **Southern African Development Community (SADC)**, and has a close relationship with its neighboring countries. The country has a **constitutional monarchy** system of government, with a **king** as head of state and a **prime minister** as head of government. Lesotho is a **democratic** country, with regular elections and a strong tradition of **human rights**.
