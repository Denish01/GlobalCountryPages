# Lesotho Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Lesotho is generally moderate, with improving mobile network coverage and increasing access to **WiFi** in urban areas, but still limited in rural areas, making it essential to plan ahead for staying connected during your visit.

[FACTBOX]
Average Internet Speed: 5 Mbps download
Mobile Network Coverage: Moderate
Main Carriers: Vodacom Lesotho, Econet Telecom Lesotho, Orange Lesotho
SIM Card Cost: 20 USD for tourist SIM
WiFi Availability: Limited
5G Available: No
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet quality in Lesotho varies greatly between urban and rural areas, with the capital city Maseru and other major towns having relatively better **internet speeds** and reliability compared to rural areas. However, the overall internet quality is still considered moderate, with average download speeds of around 5 Mbps. The reliability of the internet can also be affected by power outages and network congestion, especially during peak hours. Despite these challenges, the Lesotho government has been working to improve the country's **ICT infrastructure**, including the rollout of **fiber optic cables** and **mobile broadband** services.

In recent years, there has been a significant increase in **mobile internet** penetration, with many Basotho people using their mobile phones to access the internet. However, the lack of **fixed-line broadband** infrastructure in many areas means that mobile internet is often the only option for many people. This has led to a growing demand for **mobile data** services, with many mobile operators offering a range of **data plans** and **promotions** to attract customers. Despite these developments, the internet in Lesotho still lags behind many other countries in terms of speed and reliability, making it essential for visitors to plan ahead and research their options carefully.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Lesotho is relatively straightforward, with many mobile operators offering **tourist SIM cards** and **prepaid plans**. The two main carriers, Vodacom Lesotho and Econet Telecom Lesotho, have a wide range of plans and offers, including **data-only SIMs** and **voice and data bundles**. To purchase a SIM card, you will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and the cost is around 20 USD for a tourist SIM. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from an authorized dealer or the carrier's official store to avoid any potential issues with the SIM card or service.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with many hotels and lodges offering **free WiFi** to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is not very common in Lesotho, but some cafes and restaurants in Maseru and other major towns do offer **WiFi** to their customers.
- Coworking spaces are limited, but some **innovation hubs** and **business centers** do offer **WiFi** and other amenities to remote workers and entrepreneurs.
- Public WiFi is not widely available, but some **government institutions** and **public libraries** do offer **free WiFi** to the public.
- Some **shopping malls** and **tourist information centers** also offer **WiFi** to visitors.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces such as the **Lesotho Innovation Hub** and **Maseru Business Center** offer **WiFi** and other amenities to remote workers and entrepreneurs.
- Reliable cafe chains such as **Mugg & Bean** and **Vida e Caffe** offer **WiFi** and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options such as **mobile hotspots** and **portable WiFi routers** are available for purchase or rent.
- VPNs are recommended to ensure **online security** and **data protection** when using public WiFi or mobile networks.
- Some **accommodation options** such as **guesthouses** and **self-catering apartments** offer **WiFi** and other amenities to remote workers and digital nomads.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major issue in Lesotho, but some **online content** may be blocked or restricted.
- **VPN** use is recommended to ensure **online security** and **data protection** when using public WiFi or mobile networks.
- Roaming alternatives such as **local SIM cards** and **mobile hotspots** are available for visitors who want to avoid **roaming charges**.
- eSIM options are not widely available in Lesotho, but some **mobile operators** do offer **eSIM plans** and **data packages**.
- **Power outages** and **network congestion** can affect internet reliability, especially in rural areas.
- **Cybercrime** and **online scams** are a growing concern in Lesotho, and visitors should take **online security** precautions to protect themselves and their devices.
