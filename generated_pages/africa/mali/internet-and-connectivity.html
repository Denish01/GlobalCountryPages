Internet Overview

The internet in Mali is a growing sector, with a significant increase in the number of internet users over the past decade. However, the quality of internet services remains a challenge, particularly in rural areas where the infrastructure is still underdeveloped. In urban areas like Bamako, the capital city, internet speeds are generally faster and more reliable, with many internet service providers offering a range of plans and packages. Despite this, the overall internet penetration rate in Mali remains low, with only a small percentage of the population having access to the internet.

The urban-rural divide is a significant issue in Mali, with internet access and quality varying greatly between cities and rural areas. In rural areas, internet access is often limited, and speeds are generally slower. This can make it difficult for people in these areas to access online services, communicate with others, and access information. However, efforts are being made to improve internet access and quality in rural areas, with some mobile network operators investing in infrastructure development and network expansion.

Getting a SIM Card

To get a SIM card in Mali, you can visit one of the many mobile network operator shops or authorized retailers in Bamako or other major cities. You will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and the process is generally straightforward. The main carriers in Mali, including Orange Mali, Malitel, and Telecel, offer a range of SIM card options and data plans, including tourist-friendly plans with affordable rates. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable retailer to avoid any issues with activation or billing.

You can also purchase a SIM card at the airport or at some hotels, but the selection may be limited, and prices may be higher. It's a good idea to research the different carriers and plans before purchasing a SIM card to find the best option for your needs and budget.

WiFi Availability

  • Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with many hotels offering free WiFi to their guests.
  • Cafe WiFi culture is not as prevalent in Mali as in some other countries, but some cafes and restaurants in Bamako and other major cities do offer WiFi.
  • Coworking spaces are becoming more popular in Bamako, with some offering high-speed internet and other amenities.
  • Public WiFi is limited, but some internet cafes and cybercafes offer WiFi access for a fee.
  • Some restaurants and bars also offer WiFi, but the quality and speed can vary.

For Remote Workers

  • Coworking spaces like Bamako Hub and Mali Tech offer a range of amenities, including high-speed internet, meeting rooms, and networking opportunities.
  • Reliable cafe chains like Cafe de Rome and Le Loft offer good WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
  • Backup internet options like mobile hotspots and portable WiFi routers can be useful in case of internet outages or slow speeds.
  • Using a VPN can help to ensure online security and data protection when working remotely.
  • Some coliving spaces and hostels also offer WiFi and other amenities for remote workers.

Tips & Warnings

  • Internet censorship is not a major issue in Mali, but some websites and online services may be blocked or restricted.
  • Using a VPN can help to bypass any restrictions and ensure online security.
  • Roaming alternatives like eSIM and international SIM cards can be useful for travelers who need to stay connected.
  • Be aware of scams and phishing attempts when using public WiFi or accessing online services.
  • Researching local internet laws and regulations can help to avoid any issues or fines.
  • Using strong passwords and two-factor authentication can help to protect online accounts and personal data.
