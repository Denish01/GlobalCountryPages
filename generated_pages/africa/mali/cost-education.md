# Education Costs in Mali

The cost of education in Mali varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Mali's education system is primarily based on the French model, with **French** being the language of instruction. The system is divided into primary, secondary, and higher education. Primary education is compulsory for 6 years, followed by 3 years of lower secondary education. Public education is generally free or subsidized, but the quality can vary depending on the region and the availability of resources. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a higher quality of education, but at a higher cost. International schools are also available in major cities, offering a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the British curriculum.

The quality of education in Mali is a significant concern, with many schools lacking basic infrastructure and resources. However, there are efforts being made to improve the education system, including the introduction of new curricula and teacher training programs. For expatriates and international students, there are several options available, including international schools and private institutions. These schools offer a higher quality of education and a range of extracurricular activities, but at a higher cost.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Tips [/SECTION]
- The **University of Bamako** is the top-ranked university in Mali, offering a range of programs in French and local languages.
- **Scholarships** are available for local and international students, but are limited and competitive.
- **Online learning** options are becoming increasingly available, but are limited by internet access and infrastructure.
- Education quality in Mali is generally lower than in other West African countries, but is improving with investment and reform efforts.
- **International schools** are the best option for expatriates and international students, offering a high quality of education and a range of extracurricular activities.
- **Language skills** are essential for navigating the education system and daily life in Mali, with French being the primary language of instruction.
