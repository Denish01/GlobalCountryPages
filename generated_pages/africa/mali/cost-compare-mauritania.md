[SECTION] Overview: Mali vs Mauritania [/SECTION]
Mali and Mauritania are two neighboring countries in West Africa, with distinct economic profiles and cost of living indices. Mali has a relatively lower cost of living, driven by its larger population, lower GDP per capita, and a more informal economy. In contrast, Mauritania has a smaller population, higher GDP per capita, and a more formalized economy, which contributes to its higher cost of living. The economic context of the two countries is also influenced by their natural resources, with Mauritania being rich in iron ore and fish, while Mali has significant gold and cotton reserves.

The cost of living difference between Mali and Mauritania is noticeable, with Mali being around 15-20% cheaper than its neighbor. This is reflected in various aspects of daily life, including housing, food, transportation, and utilities. Expats, students, and retirees may find Mali to be a more affordable option, while those with higher incomes or specific lifestyle requirements may prefer Mauritania. However, it's essential to note that both countries have their unique charms and challenges, and the decision to choose one over the other ultimately depends on individual preferences and priorities.
