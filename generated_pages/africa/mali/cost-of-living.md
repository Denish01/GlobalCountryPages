[SECTION] How Expensive Is Mali? [/SECTION]
Mali is considered an affordable country to visit or live in, with a cost of living that is lower than many other countries in West Africa. The capital city, Bamako, is the most expensive place to live, but even there, prices are relatively low compared to other major cities in the region. Compared to its neighbors, Mali is more expensive than Niger and Burkina Faso, but less expensive than Senegal and Mauritania. Overall, Mali offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for travelers and expats.

The cost of living in Mali is influenced by the country's economy, which is primarily based on agriculture and mining. [SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs
- Eat at local restaurants and street food stalls to save on food costs
- Use public transportation or walk/bike instead of taking taxis to save on transport costs
- Avoid traveling during peak season (December to February) to save on accommodation and tour costs
- Learn some basic French phrases to help you navigate and negotiate prices
- Consider buying local products and handicrafts instead of imported goods to save on shopping costs

Overall, Mali is a great destination for travelers and expats who want to experience the rich culture and history of West Africa without breaking the bank. With some basic knowledge of the local costs and customs, visitors can have a comfortable and enjoyable stay in Mali without overspending.
