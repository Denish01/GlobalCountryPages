# Top Things to Do in Mali — Attractions & Experiences

Mali offers a rich cultural heritage and diverse landscapes, with top attractions including the Great Mosque of Djenné, the Dogon Country, and the Niger River, making it a fascinating destination for travelers to explore.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **pirogue** ride along the Niger River and experience the local fishing culture
- Attend a traditional **Dogon mask dance** performance in the Dogon Country
- Visit a **local market** in Bamako or other towns to experience the vibrant colors and sounds of Malian culture
- Go on a **camel safari** in the desert regions of northern Mali
- Learn about the **ancient trade routes** that once connected Mali to other parts of Africa and the world
- Participate in a **traditional tea ceremony** with a local family
- Explore the **underground tunnels** and passageways of the ancient city of Timbuktu

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Bandiagara Escarpment**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the Dogon people
- Take a boat ride on **Lake Debo**, a beautiful and peaceful spot for birdwatching and relaxation
- Visit the **Niger River**, which runs through the heart of Mali and offers stunning scenery and wildlife
- Go hiking in the **Fouta Djallon highlands**, a beautiful and rugged region with stunning views
- Explore the **desert landscapes** of northern Mali, including the **Sahara Desert** and the **Adrar des Iforas** mountain range

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum of Mali** in Bamako to learn about the country's history and culture
- Explore the **Great Mosque of Djenné**, one of the most famous landmarks in Mali
- Attend a **traditional festival**, such as the **Festival au Desert** or the **Festival sur le Niger**
- Take a **cooking class** and learn how to prepare traditional Malian dishes
- Visit the **Maison des Artisans** in Bamako, a center for local crafts and artisans
- Explore the **historical city of Timbuktu**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a center of Islamic learning and culture

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **town of Ségou**, a former capital of the Bamana Empire and a center of historical and cultural significance
- Explore the **village of San**, known for its beautiful adobe architecture and traditional crafts
- Take a **boat ride** on the **Bani River**, a peaceful and scenic spot for relaxation
- Visit the **Kati** region, known for its beautiful scenery and historical significance
- Explore the **markets** of **Mopti**, a bustling and vibrant town with a rich cultural heritage

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip from **Bamako** to the **Fouta Djallon highlands**, a beautiful and rugged region with stunning views (distance: 200 km, travel time: 3-4 hours)
- Visit the **town of Djenné** from **Mopti**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the famous Great Mosque (distance: 100 km, travel time: 2-3 hours)
- Explore the **Niger River** from **Timbuktu**, a scenic and peaceful spot for boat rides and relaxation (distance: 20 km, travel time: 1 hour)
- Take a day trip from **Ségou** to the **village of San**, known for its beautiful adobe architecture and traditional crafts (distance: 50 km, travel time: 1-2 hours)
- Visit the **Bandiagara Escarpment** from **Mopti**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the Dogon people (distance: 150 km, travel time: 3-4 hours)
