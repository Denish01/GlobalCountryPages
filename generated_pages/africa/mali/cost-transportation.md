[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Mali has a relatively underdeveloped transportation system, with most people relying on **minibuses**, **taxis**, and **motorcycles** to get around. The quality of public transit is generally poor, with overcrowding and lack of maintenance being common issues. In the capital city of Bamako, the public transportation system is more developed, with a network of buses and taxis that cover most areas of the city. However, in rural areas, transportation options are limited, and many people rely on **bicycles** or **walking** to get around.

The main transportation modes in Mali are road-based, with a network of paved and unpaved roads that connect major cities and towns. The country's geography, with the **Niger River** running through it, also makes **river transport** a viable option for cargo and passengers. However, the transportation system is often affected by **potholes**, **traffic congestion**, and **security concerns**, which can make traveling in Mali challenging.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using **minibuses** or **shared taxis**, which are often cheaper than metered taxis.
- When using taxis, it's common to **negotiate the fare** before starting the journey.
- Be aware of **traffic congestion** and **potholes**, which can make traveling in Mali challenging.
- Consider using **local taxi apps**, which can help you find and book taxis more easily.
- Always **wear a helmet** when riding a motorcycle or bicycle, as road safety can be a concern in Mali.
- Be mindful of **security concerns**, particularly when traveling at night or in rural areas.
