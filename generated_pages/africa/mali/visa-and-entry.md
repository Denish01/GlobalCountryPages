# Mali Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Mali before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Mali has a moderate visa policy, requiring citizens from most countries to obtain a visa before entering the country. However, citizens from a few countries, such as those in the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS), are eligible for visa-free access. The visa policy is designed to balance the need to control immigration with the desire to promote tourism and economic development. Visitors can obtain a visa from a Malian embassy or consulate, or in some cases, upon arrival at the airport.

The visa policy of Mali is subject to change, and it is essential to check with the official embassy or consulate of Mali for the most up-to-date information. The country has a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, making it an attractive destination for tourists, business travelers, and students. The government of Mali aims to facilitate the entry of visitors while ensuring national security and public safety.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** To enter Mali as a tourist, visitors must obtain a tourist visa, which is usually valid for 90 days. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, but this can be extended for an additional 90 days. Tourists must provide proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and health insurance.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers must obtain a business visa, which requires an invitation letter from a Malian company or organization. The requirements for business travelers are similar to those for tourists, but they must also provide proof of their business activities and may need to register with the local authorities.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in Mali must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a Malian educational institution. The student visa is usually valid for the duration of the study program, and students must provide proof of financial support and health insurance.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Mali must obtain a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a Malian employer. The work visa is usually valid for the duration of the employment contract, and workers must provide proof of their qualifications and experience.
