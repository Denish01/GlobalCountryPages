# Where to Stay in Saint Helena — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Saint Helena, the best areas to stay are in and around the capital city of Jamestown, which offers a range of accommodations and easy access to the island's main attractions. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**Jamestown** (Jamestown)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: Jamestown is the heart of Saint Helena, with a rich history and culture, offering a unique and charming atmosphere, surrounded by historic buildings and stunning scenery. The town has a relaxed and friendly vibe, making it an ideal base for exploring the island.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Close proximity to the island's main attractions, a range of restaurants and shops, and a variety of accommodation options. The town also has a good selection of amenities, including a hospital, a post office, and a few shops.
Cons: Can be quite crowded during peak season, and some areas can be noisy due to the town's central location.

**Half Tree Hollow** (Half Tree Hollow)
Best For: Family / Backpacker
Vibe: Half Tree Hollow is a residential area located just outside of Jamestown, offering a more laid-back and peaceful atmosphere, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The area is popular with families and backpackers due to its affordable accommodation options and easy access to the town.
Price Range: $80-$200/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, stunning views, and a peaceful atmosphere. The area is also close to the island's main attractions, making it an ideal base for exploring.
Cons: Limited amenities and services in the immediate area, and can be quite hilly, making it difficult for those with mobility issues.

**St Paul's** (St Paul's)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: St Paul's is a picturesque village located on the west coast of the island, offering a tranquil and secluded atmosphere, surrounded by stunning scenery and beautiful beaches. The village is popular with families and luxury travelers due to its high-end accommodation options and range of amenities.
Price Range: $200-$400/night
Pros: Stunning scenery, beautiful beaches, and a range of high-end accommodation options. The village also has a good selection of amenities, including a few restaurants and shops.
Cons: Can be quite isolated, and limited access to public transportation, making it necessary to have a car or rely on taxis.

**Longwood** (Longwood)
Best For: Backpacker / Budget
Vibe: Longwood is a rural area located in the center of the island, offering a peaceful and natural atmosphere, surrounded by stunning countryside and scenic views. The area is popular with backpackers and budget travelers due to its affordable accommodation options and easy access to the island's main attractions.
Price Range: $50-$150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, peaceful atmosphere, and stunning scenery. The area is also close to the island's main attractions, making it an ideal base for exploring.
Cons: Limited amenities and services in the immediate area, and can be quite remote, making it difficult to access public transportation.

**Alarm Forest** (Alarm Forest)
Best For: Nature Lover / Adventure Seeker
Vibe: Alarm Forest is a rural area located on the east coast of the island, offering a unique and rugged atmosphere, surrounded by stunning natural scenery and scenic views. The area is popular with nature lovers and adventure seekers due to its range of outdoor activities and stunning scenery.
Price Range: $100-$250/night
Pros: Stunning natural scenery, range of outdoor activities, and a unique and rugged atmosphere. The area is also close to the island's main attractions, making it an ideal base for exploring.
Cons: Can be quite remote, and limited access to public transportation, making it necessary to have a car or rely on taxis.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas, and keep an eye on your belongings.
- Jamestown can be quite hilly, so be prepared for some steep walks and uneven terrain.
- Consider renting a car or using taxis to get around the island, as public transportation can be limited.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas.
- Keep an eye on the weather, as the island can be prone to strong winds and rough seas.
- Stay hydrated and bring sun protection, as the sun can be strong in the tropical climate.
