[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Heart-Shaped Waterfall**, a unique natural wonder, is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of the island's natural beauty.
- The **Saint Helena National Trust** works to preserve the island's cultural heritage, including its historic buildings, museums, and archaeological sites.
- The **Jamestown Festival** is an annual celebration of the island's music, dance, and art, featuring local performers and artisans.
- The **Saint Helena Museum** showcases the island's history and culture, with exhibits on topics such as the island's geological history, its role in the British Empire, and the exile of Napoleon Bonaparte.
- The **Diana's Peak** nature reserve is a protected area that preserves the island's unique flora and fauna, and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Napoleon Bonaparte**, the former French emperor, was exiled to Saint Helena, where he died in 1821, leaving a lasting legacy on the island.
- **Saint Helena diaspora** communities can be found in countries such as the United Kingdom, South Africa, and Australia, where they have made significant contributions to their adopted cultures.
- The **Saint Helena community** is known for its warm hospitality and strong sense of community, with many residents actively involved in volunteer work and community organizations.
- **Jonathan the tortoise**, a resident of the island, is believed to be the oldest living land animal on record, and has become a symbol of the island's unique wildlife.
