[SECTION] What Is Saint Helena? [/SECTION]
Saint Helena is a British overseas territory located in the South Atlantic Ocean, off the coast of Africa. The territory consists of three main islands: Saint Helena, Ascension Island, and Tristan da Cunha. With a population of approximately 5,000 people, Saint Helena is one of the most remote islands in the world, with a unique cultural and historical identity shaped by its **colonial past** and **isolation**. The island's capital, Jamestown, is a small town with a rich history, featuring old buildings, museums, and historical landmarks.

Saint Helena is situated about 1,200 miles west of the coast of Angola, and its strategic location has made it an important stopover for ships traveling between Europe and Asia. The island's economy is primarily driven by **fishing**, **tourism**, and **financial services**, with a growing focus on **sustainable development** and **environmental conservation**. The territory's unique biodiversity and natural beauty make it an attractive destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

The island's history is deeply connected to its **British heritage**, with many historical sites and landmarks reflecting its **colonial past**. The most famous historical figure associated with Saint Helena is **Napoleon Bonaparte**, who was exiled to the island in 1815 and died there in 1821. Today, Saint Helena is a popular destination for history buffs, nature enthusiasts, and those seeking a unique and off-the-beaten-path travel experience.
