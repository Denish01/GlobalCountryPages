Food Costs Overview

The cost of food in Saint Helena is influenced by the island's geographical isolation, which makes it difficult to produce and transport goods locally. As a result, many food items are imported from the UK or other countries, driving up prices. Eating out is not a common practice in Saint Helena, and most residents prefer to cook at home using local ingredients. However, there are a few restaurants and cafes in Jamestown that offer a range of dishes, from traditional Saint Helenian cuisine to international fare. The prices of meals in these establishments vary, but a meal at an inexpensive restaurant can cost around $15-$20 USD per person.

The high cost of living in Saint Helena is also reflected in the prices of groceries, which are generally higher than in other parts of Africa. A monthly grocery budget for one person can range from $300 to $500 USD, depending on individual preferences and dietary needs. The prices of basic items such as milk, bread, and eggs are relatively high, and the cost of meat and fresh produce can be even higher due to the need for imports. Despite these challenges, the Saint Helenian government has implemented initiatives to promote local food production and reduce reliance on imports, which may help to bring down food prices in the long term.

Supermarket vs Local Market

The prices of groceries in Saint Helena can vary depending on whether you shop at a supermarket or a local market. Supermarkets tend to have a wider range of products, including imported goods, but the prices can be higher than at local markets. Local markets, on the other hand, offer fresh produce and other locally sourced products at lower prices. However, the selection may be limited, and the quality of the products can vary. It's a good idea to shop at both supermarkets and local markets to get the best value for your money.

In general, it's best to buy fresh produce, meat, and dairy products at local markets, where you can find a wide range of locally sourced goods at competitive prices. For imported goods, such as canned foods, pasta, and rice, supermarkets may be a better option. Bargaining is not a common practice in Saint Helena, and prices are generally fixed. However, you may be able to negotiate the price of fresh produce or other items at local markets, especially if you're buying in bulk.

Local Food Tips

  • The cheapest way to eat in Saint Helena is to buy fresh produce and other locally sourced products at local markets and cook at home.
  • Local staples such as fish, potatoes, and onions are good value and can be used to make a range of dishes.
  • Avoid buying imported goods, such as meat and dairy products, which can be expensive.
  • Try traditional Saint Helenian dishes, such as fish cakes and coconut rice, which are made with local ingredients and are often cheaper than international cuisine.
  • Consider buying in bulk and freezing items such as meat and fish to save money.
  • Visit the local market in Jamestown to sample fresh produce and other locally sourced products, and to get a feel for the local food culture.
