# Rent & Housing Prices in Burkina Faso

The cost of rent in Burkina Faso varies depending on the location, with the capital city Ouagadougou being the most expensive, and prices ranging from $100 to $500 per month for a one-bedroom apartment.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
Burkina Faso's rental market is relatively affordable, with a wide range of options available in the capital city Ouagadougou and other major urban centers. Typical lease terms are for one to two years, with a deposit requirement of one to three months' rent. Tenant rights are generally well-protected, with the **Burkinabé government** implementing policies to regulate the rental market and prevent exploitation. Compared to its regional neighbors, Burkina Faso's housing costs are relatively low, with prices in Ouagadougou being lower than in cities like Accra, Ghana, or Abidjan, Ivory Coast.

The housing market in Burkina Faso is also influenced by the country's economic conditions, with the **inflation rate** of 4.2% in 2024 affecting the cost of living and housing prices. The **GDP per capita** of $2,896 also plays a role in determining the affordability of housing, with many Burkinabé relying on informal housing arrangements or shared accommodations. The government has implemented initiatives to improve access to affordable housing, including the development of new residential areas and the provision of subsidies for low-income households.
