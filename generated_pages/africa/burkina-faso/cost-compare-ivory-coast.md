[SECTION] Overview: Burkina Faso vs Ivory Coast [/SECTION]
Burkina Faso and Ivory Coast are two neighboring countries in West Africa, with distinct economic profiles. Burkina Faso has a relatively low GDP per capita and a high poverty rate, which contributes to its lower cost of living. In contrast, Ivory Coast has a more diversified economy and a higher GDP per capita, resulting in a slightly higher cost of living. The overall cost of living in Burkina Faso is around 15-20% lower than in Ivory Coast, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable destination.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Burkina Faso's economy is largely based on agriculture, with cotton, gold, and livestock being major exports. Ivory Coast, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on services, manufacturing, and agriculture. The difference in economic profiles is reflected in the cost of living, with Burkina Faso being generally cheaper for accommodation, food, and transportation. However, Ivory Coast has a more developed infrastructure and a wider range of amenities, which may make it more appealing to some visitors.
