[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Mauritania has a limited but developing transportation network, with most people relying on taxis, buses, and minibuses to get around. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with the capital city of Nouakchott having a more extensive network of buses and taxis compared to other cities. In rural areas, transportation options are limited, and many people rely on walking or using donkeys and camels to travel short distances.

The main transportation modes in Mauritania include taxis, buses, and minibuses, which are widely available in cities and towns. However, the quality of the roads and the condition of the vehicles can vary greatly, making travel times unpredictable. Many people also use ride-hailing apps like Careem and Bolt, which are available in major cities and offer a convenient and affordable way to get around.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use ride-hailing apps like Careem and Bolt for convenient and affordable transportation in major cities.
- Negotiate taxi fares before starting your journey, as meters are not always used.
- Be prepared for unpredictable travel times due to road conditions and traffic.
- Consider renting a 4x4 vehicle for travel outside of cities, as road conditions can be challenging.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps for airport transfers to ensure a safe and reliable journey.
- Always wear a seatbelt and follow local traffic rules to ensure a safe journey.
