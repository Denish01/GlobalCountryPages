# Monthly Budget Guide for Mauritania

To live in Mauritania, you would need around $400-700 per month for a budget lifestyle, $800-1,200 per month for a mid-range lifestyle, and $1,500-2,500 per month for a comfortable lifestyle.

[FACTBOX]
GDP per Capita (PPP): $7,369
Budget Living (monthly): $400-700
Mid-Range Living (monthly): $800-1,200
Comfortable Living (monthly): $1,500-2,500
Average Local Salary: $200-300 USD/month, approximate
Currency: MRU
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Cost of Living Overview [/SECTION]
Mauritania's cost of living is relatively low compared to other countries in the region, with a GDP per capita PPP of $7,369. This means that the average person in Mauritania has a relatively modest disposable income, which is reflected in the overall affordability of goods and services. For a budget-conscious individual, $400-700 per month can go a long way, covering basic expenses such as rent, food, and transportation. However, for those who want to enjoy a more comfortable lifestyle, with amenities such as air conditioning, internet, and dining out, $1,500-2,500 per month would be more suitable. Compared to neighboring countries such as Senegal and Mali, Mauritania's cost of living is relatively low, making it an attractive option for expats and travelers.

The cost of living in Mauritania can vary greatly depending on the location, with the capital city Nouakchott being the most expensive. However, even in Nouakchott, the cost of living is still relatively low compared to other major cities in the region. For example, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center can cost between $200-500 per month, which is relatively affordable compared to other cities in West Africa. Additionally, the cost of food and transportation is also relatively low, making it possible to live comfortably on a modest budget.

[SECTION] Budget Tier Lifestyles [/SECTION]
For those living on a budget of $400-700 per month, daily life in Mauritania can be quite simple. You would likely share an apartment with others outside of the city center, cook most of your meals, and rely on public transportation. You would also have to be mindful of your expenses on entertainment and social activities, as these can quickly add up. However, with some careful planning, it is possible to enjoy some of the local culture and cuisine on a budget. For example, you could try some of the local street food, such as grilled meat or fish, or visit some of the local markets to buy fresh produce.

For those with a mid-range budget of $800-1,200 per month, life in Mauritania can be quite comfortable. You could afford to rent a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, eat out occasionally, and enjoy some of the local entertainment options, such as going to the cinema or playing sports. You would also have more flexibility to travel within the country and explore some of the local attractions, such as the coastal towns or the desert landscapes. However, you would still need to be mindful of your expenses and make some compromises on luxuries such as air conditioning or a car.

For those with a comfortable budget of $1,500-2,500 per month, life in Mauritania can be quite luxurious. You could afford to rent a nice two-bedroom apartment in a desirable neighborhood, eat out frequently, and enjoy some of the high-end entertainment options, such as going to a nightclub or taking a weekend trip to a nearby city. You would also have the flexibility to hire a car and driver, or to take taxis whenever you need to. Additionally, you could afford to invest in some of the local amenities, such as a gym membership or a private healthcare plan.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Strategies [/SECTION]
- Learn some basic Arabic phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or cars
- Cook your own meals instead of eating out
- Buy local produce and spices at the market instead of at a supermarket
- Avoid traveling during peak season (December to February) to save on accommodation and transportation costs
- Consider sharing an apartment with others to split the rent
- Take advantage of the local culture and entertainment options, such as attending a traditional wedding or visiting a local festival
- Learn about the local customs and traditions to avoid unintentionally offending someone and to build stronger relationships with the community

[SECTION] Is Mauritania Affordable? [/SECTION]
Mauritania can be an affordable destination for digital nomads, retirees, and students who are looking for a low-cost lifestyle. The cost of living is relatively low, and the country offers a unique cultural experience and a chance to explore the Sahara Desert and the Atlantic coast. However, for families or individuals who are used to a high standard of living, Mauritania may not be the most affordable option. The country's infrastructure and amenities are still developing, and some luxuries such as air conditioning, internet, and healthcare may be limited or expensive. Additionally, the country's remote location and limited transportation options may make it difficult to travel to and from Mauritania, which could add to the overall cost of living.

Overall, Mauritania is an affordable destination for those who are flexible and adaptable, and who are willing to make some compromises on their lifestyle. With some careful planning and research, it is possible to live comfortably in Mauritania on a modest budget. However, for those who are looking for a high standard of living and a wide range of amenities, Mauritania may not be the best option. It is essential to weigh the pros and cons and consider factors such as lifestyle, budget, and personal preferences before making a decision.
