# Mauritania Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Mauritania before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Mauritania has a moderate visa policy, with citizens from certain countries eligible for visa-free access or visas on arrival. The country is working to improve its tourism infrastructure and attract more visitors, but its visa requirements can be complex. Currently, citizens from about 10 countries, including some African nations, can enter Mauritania without a visa. However, most nationalities require a visa to enter the country, which can be obtained in advance or, in some cases, upon arrival.

Mauritania's visa policy is designed to balance the need to control immigration with the desire to promote tourism and economic development. The country has a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, with attractions such as the Sahara Desert, the Atlantic coast, and the historic city of Chinguetti. With the right documentation and planning, visitors can have a rewarding and safe experience in Mauritania.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists typically require a visa to enter Mauritania, which can be obtained in advance or, in some cases, upon arrival. The maximum stay for tourists is usually 90 days, and visitors must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require an invitation letter from a Mauritanian company or organization, as well as a visa. The requirements for business travelers can be more complex, and it is recommended to consult with the Mauritanian embassy or consulate in advance. **Students:** Students who wish to study in Mauritania must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a Mauritanian educational institution and sufficient funds to support themselves. **Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Mauritania must obtain a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a Mauritanian employer and a labor contract.

[SECTION] Documents Checklist [/SECTION]
1. A valid passport with at least six months' validity
2. A completed visa application form
3. One recent passport-sized photo
4. Proof of onward travel or return ticket
5. Proof of sufficient funds to support yourself during your stay
6. Health insurance that covers you for the duration of your stay
7. A hotel reservation or proof of accommodation
8. A letter of invitation or sponsorship, if required
9. Proof of vaccination against yellow fever, if traveling from a high-risk country
10. A copy of your birth certificate, if required

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airports in Mauritania are Nouakchott-Oumtounsy International Airport and Nouadhibou International Airport. When arriving at the airport, visitors must go through immigration and customs, where they will be required to present their passport, visa, and other documents. The process can be slow, and visitors should be prepared to wait. Land borders are also open, and visitors can enter Mauritania by road from neighboring countries such as Senegal and Mali. When entering by land, visitors must stop at the border crossing and present their documents to the immigration officer.

At immigration, visitors will be required to fill out a declaration form and present their passport, visa, and other documents. The officer may ask questions about the purpose of your visit, your accommodation, and your onward travel plans. It is essential to have all the necessary documents and to be honest and cooperative with the immigration officer. If you have any issues or concerns, it is recommended to contact the Mauritanian embassy or consulate in advance for guidance.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not checking the validity of your passport before traveling
- Not obtaining the necessary visa or documentation before arriving in Mauritania
- Not having sufficient funds to support yourself during your stay
- Not having proof of onward travel or return ticket
- Not declaring all items in your luggage, including gifts and souvenirs
- Not respecting local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or other cultural sites
