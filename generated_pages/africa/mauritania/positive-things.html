Notable Achievements

CategoryAchievementDetails
ScienceDesertification ResearchMauritania has made significant contributions to desertification research, with a focus on combating the effects of the Sahara Desert on the country's ecosystem and agricultural productivity.
SportsFootball SuccessThe Mauritania national football team has shown impressive performance in recent years, qualifying for the Africa Cup of Nations for the first time in 2019.
ArtsRich Musical HeritageMauritania has a unique and diverse musical tradition, with popular genres such as Moors music and Saharan blues.
DevelopmentPoverty ReductionMauritania has made notable progress in reducing poverty, with the poverty rate declining from 51.4% in 2000 to 31.2% in 2019, according to the World Bank.
InnovationRenewable EnergyMauritania has invested heavily in renewable energy, particularly wind and solar power, with the goal of increasing the share of renewables in the energy mix to 50% by 2030.
Global ContributionsPeacekeeping MissionsMauritania has contributed troops to several United Nations peacekeeping missions, including in the Democratic Republic of Congo and the Central African Republic.

Cultural Treasures

  • The Banc d'Arguin National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a vital breeding ground for marine life and a key stopover for migratory birds.
  • The Mauritanian Moorish architecture, characterized by its use of mud and thatch, is a unique and distinctive style that reflects the country's rich cultural heritage.
  • The Tea Culture, an integral part of Mauritanian tradition, is a symbol of hospitality and friendship, with tea being an essential part of social gatherings and ceremonies.
  • The Eid al-Fitr celebrations, marking the end of Ramadan, are an important part of Mauritanian culture, with families and friends coming together to share meals and exchange gifts.
  • The Saharan Festival, held annually in the town of Nouadhibou, showcases the country's rich musical and cultural heritage, with performances by local and international artists.

People & Global Impact

  • Mohamed Ould Cheikh El Ghazouani, the current President of Mauritania, has played a key role in promoting economic development and regional cooperation in West Africa.
  • The Mauritanian diaspora, with significant communities in France, Spain, and other European countries, has made important contributions to the development of the country, particularly in the areas of education and healthcare.
  • Fatma Ebtehaj, a renowned Mauritanian writer and poet, has written extensively on themes such as identity, culture, and social justice, and has been recognized for her contributions to African literature.
  • The Mauritanian values of hospitality, respect, and solidarity are highly prized in the country, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome they receive from the local population.

  • The exciting outdoor activities, such as camel trekking, fishing, and birdwatching, offer a range of exciting experiences for adventure-seekers.

Hidden Gems

  • The Parc National du Diawling, a protected area located on the Senegal River, is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts, with a wide range of bird species and other animals.
  • The town of Tichitt**, with its well-preserved traditional architecture, is a hidden gem that offers a glimpse into Mauritania's rich cultural heritage.
  • The Mauritanian Saharan nomads, who continue to live a traditional lifestyle, offer a unique opportunity for visitors to experience the country's rich cultural diversity.
  • The annual Nouadhibou International Film Festival**, which showcases the best of African and international cinema, is a highlight of the country's cultural calendar.
