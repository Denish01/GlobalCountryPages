# Rent & Housing Prices in Mauritania

The cost of rent in Mauritania can range from $200 to $1,000 per month for a one-bedroom apartment, depending on the location and quality of the housing.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
Mauritania's housing market is characterized by a mix of traditional and modern housing, with a growing demand for rental properties in urban areas. The rental market is relatively unregulated, with typical lease terms ranging from 6 to 12 months. Deposits are usually required, equivalent to 1-2 months' rent, and tenant rights are limited. Compared to its regional neighbors, Mauritania's housing costs are relatively moderate, with prices lower than in countries like Senegal but higher than in Mali. The government has implemented initiatives to increase access to affordable housing, but the market remains largely informal.

The housing market in Mauritania is also influenced by the country's economic conditions, with a GDP per capita of $7,369 and an inflation rate of 2.5%. The education and health sectors also play a role in shaping the housing market, with 4.7% of GDP spent on education and $92 per capita spent on health. The out-of-pocket health expenditure is 43.8%, which may impact the affordability of housing for some individuals. Overall, Mauritania's housing market offers a range of options for renters and buyers, from modern apartments in the city center to traditional houses in the suburbs.
