[SECTION] Overview: Mauritania vs Western Sahara [/SECTION]
Mauritania and Western Sahara are two neighboring countries in Northwest Africa, with distinct economic contexts. Mauritania has a relatively more diversified economy, with a mix of agriculture, mining, and fishing industries, while Western Sahara's economy is largely based on phosphate mining and fishing. The cost of living in Mauritania is generally lower, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being more affordable. On the other hand, Western Sahara has a more limited market, which can drive up prices for certain goods and services.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Mauritania has a more developed infrastructure, with better roads, ports, and communication networks, which facilitates trade and commerce. In contrast, Western Sahara's infrastructure is more limited, which can make it more expensive to import goods and services. Overall, Mauritania offers a more affordable lifestyle, with a cost of living that is around 15-20% lower than Western Sahara.
