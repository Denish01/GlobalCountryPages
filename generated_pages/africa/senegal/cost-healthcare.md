# Healthcare Costs in Senegal

The cost of healthcare in Senegal can vary significantly depending on whether you choose public or private healthcare services, with public services generally being more affordable but often lacking in quality and resources, while private services can be more expensive but offer better facilities and care.

[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
Senegal's healthcare system is a mixed model, combining both public and private healthcare services. The public healthcare system is generally underfunded and understaffed, with limited resources and long wait times, while the private sector offers more modern facilities and better-trained staff, but at a higher cost. For locals, the public healthcare system is often the only affordable option, while foreigners and expats may prefer to use private healthcare services. It is recommended to have health insurance in Senegal, as the cost of medical care can be high, especially in private facilities.

The healthcare system in Senegal is also divided between urban and rural areas, with more facilities and better-equipped hospitals located in cities like Dakar, while rural areas often have limited access to healthcare services. This can make it difficult for people living in rural areas to access medical care, especially in emergency situations. Additionally, the quality of care can vary significantly depending on the facility and the doctor, so it's essential to research and choose a reputable hospital or clinic.

[SECTION] Emergency Care [/SECTION]
In case of a medical emergency, it's essential to call the local emergency number, 15 or 18, or go to the nearest hospital. The best hospitals for foreigners and expats in Senegal are generally located in Dakar, such as the Dakar University Hospital or the Clinique des Nations. These hospitals have better-equipped facilities and more trained staff, but may be more expensive than public hospitals. It's also recommended to have a plan in place for emergency situations, such as having a list of emergency contacts and knowing the location of the nearest hospital.
