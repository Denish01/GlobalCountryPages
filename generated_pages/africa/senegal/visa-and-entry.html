Visa Policy Overview

Senegal has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Citizens of around 125 countries can enter Senegal without a visa or obtain a visa on arrival, while others need to apply for a visa in advance. The country aims to promote tourism and economic development, and its visa policy reflects this goal. Senegal offers visa-free access to citizens of several countries, including all ECOWAS member states, for stays of up to 90 days.

The visa policy of Senegal is designed to facilitate travel and trade while ensuring national security and public health. The country has introduced an e-visa system, which allows travelers to apply for a visa online and receive it via email. This system has streamlined the visa application process and made it more convenient for travelers. Overall, Senegal's visa policy is designed to balance the need for security and control with the need to promote tourism and economic development.

Tourists: Tourists can enter Senegal without a visa for stays of up to 90 days. They must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay. Tourists can also obtain a visa on arrival at the airport or border crossing. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, but extensions are possible. To extend their stay, tourists must apply to the immigration authorities and provide required documents.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may need to obtain a visa in advance, depending on their nationality. They must also provide an invitation letter from a Senegalese company, proof of business activities, and other required documents. Business travelers may be required to register with the local authorities and obtain a work permit.
Students: Students must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a Senegalese educational institution, a valid passport, and other required documents. The duration of the student visa depends on the length of the study program. Students must also register with the local authorities and obtain a residence permit.
Workers: Workers must obtain a work visa, which requires a sponsorship from a Senegalese employer, a valid passport, and other required documents. The work visa is typically issued for a specific period, and workers must register with the local authorities and obtain a residence permit.

The main airports in Senegal are Blaise Diagne International Airport (DSS) in Dakar and Saint-Louis Airport (XLS) in Saint-Louis. Land borders are also open, with major border crossings at Rosso (Mauritania), Kidira (Mali), and Diofior (Gambia). When arriving in Senegal, travelers must present their travel documents, including their passport, visa (if required), and proof of onward travel. Immigration procedures typically involve filling out a declaration form, presenting travel documents, and answering questions from immigration officers. Travelers may also be required to pay a fee for a visa on arrival or other services.
At the airport, travelers can expect to go through immigration and customs procedures, which may include baggage screening and questioning by immigration officers. At land borders, travelers may need to present their documents to immigration officers and pay any required fees. It is essential to ensure that all travel documents are in order and that travelers have the required documents, including a valid passport, visa (if required), and proof of onward travel.
- Not checking the validity of your passport before traveling
- Not obtaining a visa in advance when required
- Not having proof of onward travel
- Not declaring goods or currency at customs
- Not registering with the local authorities (for students and workers)
- Not obtaining a residence permit (for students and workers)
