# Nigeria History — Key Events & Timeline

Nigeria has a rich and diverse history that spans over two millennia, from the ancient **Nok civilization** to the modern-day federal republic.

[SECTION] Historical Overview [/SECTION]
The history of Nigeria dates back to the ancient **Nok civilization**, which flourished from around 1000 BC to 300 AD. This civilization is known for its distinctive terracotta sculptures and is considered one of the earliest known civilizations in West Africa. The region was later inhabited by various ethnic groups, including the **Hausa**, **Yoruba**, and **Igbo**, each with their own distinct culture and traditions. In the 15th century, the **Portuguese** arrived in Nigeria, marking the beginning of European involvement in the region. The **British** later colonized Nigeria in the 19th century, and the country gained independence in 1960.

The pre-colonial period in Nigeria was marked by the rise and fall of various empires and kingdoms, including the **Kanem-Bornu Empire** and the **Oyo Empire**. These empires played a significant role in shaping the region's culture, politics, and economy. The colonial period, on the other hand, was marked by the exploitation of Nigeria's natural resources and the imposition of European values and institutions. The struggle for independence was led by nationalist leaders such as **Nnamdi Azikiwe** and **Obafemi Awolowo**, who fought for Nigeria's freedom from British rule.

In the modern era, Nigeria has faced numerous challenges, including **civil war**, **military rule**, and **economic instability**. Despite these challenges, the country has made significant progress in recent years, with a growing economy and a thriving democracy. Today, Nigeria is the most populous country in Africa and a major player in regional and global affairs. The country continues to evolve, with a diverse population and a complex history that shapes its identity and informs its future.

Nigeria's history is a testament to the resilience and determination of its people, who have overcome numerous challenges to build a modern nation-state. From the ancient **Nok civilization** to the modern-day federal republic, Nigeria's history is a rich and complex tapestry that continues to unfold.

6. **1957** - The **Western Region** becomes self-governing, with **Obafemi Awolowo** as its premier. Awolowo plays a significant role in shaping Nigeria's post-independence politics and economy.
