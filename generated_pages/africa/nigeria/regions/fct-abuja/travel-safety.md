[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
FCT Abuja, being the capital territory of Nigeria, has a relatively stable security environment compared to other parts of the country. However, visitors should still be cautious of petty crimes such as pickpocketing, scams, and burglary. The region has a strong police presence, and tourist areas are generally well-patrolled. Nevertheless, it's crucial to stay informed about local conditions and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. As with any major city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid traveling alone at night.

The Nigerian government has implemented various security measures to ensure the safety of visitors, including the deployment of security personnel and the establishment of a tourist police unit. However, despite these efforts, crimes can still occur, and visitors should remain vigilant. It's also important to note that FCT Abuja is a diverse region with a mix of urban and rural areas, and the safety conditions can vary depending on the location. By taking necessary precautions and staying informed, visitors can minimize their risk and have a safe and enjoyable experience in FCT Abuja.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **scams** targeting tourists, such as fake police officers or officials demanding bribes
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas such as markets or bus stations
- Avoid traveling alone at night and use reputable transportation services
- Be cautious of **traffic accidents** and take necessary precautions when crossing the road
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Be aware of **health risks** such as malaria and take necessary precautions

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever vaccination is required for travel to Nigeria, and hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are also recommended
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in FCT Abuja are generally of good quality, but visitors may need to pay upfront for treatment
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but visitors should be cautious of counterfeit medications

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a copy of your passport and important documents
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or debit cards instead
- Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuables
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Stay informed about local conditions and follow local news and advice

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **harassment** and take necessary precautions to stay safe, such as avoiding walking alone at night
- Dress modestly and avoid displaying signs of wealth
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary and whereabouts
