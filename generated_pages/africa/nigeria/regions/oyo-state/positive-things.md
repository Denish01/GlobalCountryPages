[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Oyo National Museum** showcases the state's rich cultural heritage, with a collection of artifacts and exhibits that highlight its history and traditions.
- The **Old Oyo National Park** is a UNESCO-designated site, featuring ancient monuments, palaces, and other historical structures that reflect the state's cultural and historical significance.
- The **Yoruba Traditional Arts** are an integral part of Oyo State's cultural identity, with various art forms like wood carvings, textile weaving, and pottery being practiced and preserved by local artisans.
- The **Egungun Festival** is a significant cultural event in Oyo State, honoring the ancestors and showcasing the state's rich cultural heritage through music, dance, and traditional performances.
- The **Ijala Festival** is another important cultural event, celebrating the state's agricultural prosperity and featuring traditional music, dance, and masquerade performances.

- The **natural scenery**, including the **Old Oyo National Park** and the **Agodi Gardens**, provides a serene and picturesque environment for relaxation and recreation.
- The **cultural festivals**, such as the Egungun and Ijala festivals, offer a unique glimpse into the state's rich cultural heritage and traditions.
- The **historic landmarks**, including the **Oyo National Museum** and the **Mapo Hall**, provide a fascinating insight into the state's history and cultural significance.
- The **vibrant markets**, such as the **Bodija Market** and the **Dugbe Market**, offer a wide range of local products, including handicrafts, textiles, and food items.
