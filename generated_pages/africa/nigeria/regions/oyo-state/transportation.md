# Getting Around Oyo State — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around Oyo State, you can use various transportation options, including taxis, buses, and ride-hailing services, as well as domestic flights and intercity buses to travel to and from the state.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Oyo State is located in the southwestern part of Nigeria, and the main airport serving the state is the Ibadan Airport, which is a domestic airport. The airport receives flights from major cities in Nigeria, including Lagos and Abuja. If you are traveling from outside Nigeria, you will need to fly into a major international airport, such as the Murtala Muhammed International Airport in Lagos, and then take a domestic flight or other transportation to Oyo State. Overland entry points into Oyo State include the Oyo-Osun border and the Oyo-Ogun border, which can be accessed by bus or car.

The state is also accessible by road, with major highways connecting it to other parts of Nigeria. The Oyo-Ibadan road and the Oyo-Lagos road are two of the main highways that pass through the state. You can take a bus or taxi from neighboring states or cities to get to Oyo State. Alternatively, you can hire a car and driver to take you to your destination.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights are available from Ibadan Airport to other major cities in Nigeria, including Lagos and Abuja. The main domestic airlines operating in Nigeria include Arik Air, Air Peace, and Dana Air. You can book flights online or through a travel agent, and prices vary depending on the airline and time of booking. A one-way ticket from Ibadan to Lagos, for example, can cost around $50-$100. It is recommended to book flights in advance to get the best prices.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
There is no rail network in Oyo State, and train travel is not an option. However, there are plans to develop a rail network in Nigeria, which may include a line passing through Oyo State. For now, other modes of transportation, such as buses and taxis, are the primary options for getting around the state.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus companies, such as ABC Transport and God is Good Motors, operate in Oyo State, providing transportation to and from neighboring states and cities. The buses are generally comfortable and affordable, with prices starting from around $5-$10 for a one-way ticket. The main bus terminals in Oyo State are located in Ibadan, the state capital, and in other major towns.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Oyo State, you will need a valid driver's license, either from Nigeria or your home country.
- Road conditions in Oyo State are generally moderate, with some paved roads and some unpaved roads, especially in rural areas.
- Fuel costs in Nigeria are relatively low, with a liter of petrol costing around $0.40-$0.50.
- Car rental companies are available in Ibadan and other major towns, with prices starting from around $20-$50 per day.
- When driving in Oyo State, be aware of hazards such as potholes, traffic congestion, and aggressive driving.
- It is also important to be aware of local traffic laws and regulations, such as the requirement to wear a seatbelt and not to use a mobile phone while driving.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be cautious when using taxis or okadas, as some drivers may not be licensed or insured.
- Always agree on a fare before you start your journey, especially when using taxis or okadas.
- Use reputable bus companies and avoid traveling at night, especially if you are not familiar with the area.
- Keep your valuables safe and secure, especially when using public transportation.
- Be aware of your surroundings and avoid traveling alone in isolated areas.
- Download ride-hailing apps, such as Bolt, to make it easier to get around.
- Learn some basic Yoruba phrases, such as "hello" (bawo ni) and "thank you" (ese), to help you communicate with locals.
- Be patient and flexible when traveling in Oyo State, as traffic congestion and other delays can occur.
