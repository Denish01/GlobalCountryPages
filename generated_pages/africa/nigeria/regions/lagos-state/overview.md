[SECTION] What Is Lagos State? [/SECTION]
Lagos State is a **federated state** in Nigeria, located on the coast of West Africa. It is situated on the **Bight of Benin** and is bordered by Ogun State to the east and north, and the **Atlantic Ocean** to the south. Lagos State is the most populous state in Nigeria, with a population of over 21 million people, and is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and economic significance. The state is home to the **Lagos Lagoon**, which is a major waterway that connects the state to the Atlantic Ocean.

Lagos State has a long history dating back to the 15th century, when it was a major trading center for the **Portuguese** and other European colonizers. The state has a diverse economy, with major industries including **manufacturing**, **finance**, and **entertainment**. Lagos State is also home to many cultural attractions, including the **National Museum**, the **Lagos State House of Assembly**, and the **Eko Atlantic City**.

The state is also known for its **traffic congestion**, which is one of the worst in the world. Despite this, Lagos State remains a major hub for business, entertainment, and culture in West Africa.
