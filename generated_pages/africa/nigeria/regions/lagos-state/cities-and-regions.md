[SECTION] How Lagos State Is Divided [/SECTION]
Lagos State is one of the 36 states in Nigeria and is divided into 20 Local Government Areas (LGAs) and 37 Local Council Development Areas (LCDAs). The state is further divided into five administrative divisions, namely Ikeja, Badagry, Ikorodu, Lagos, and Epe. Each of these divisions has its own unique character and plays a significant role in the state's economy and governance. The administrative structure of Lagos State is designed to ensure effective governance and delivery of services to the citizens.

The 20 Local Government Areas in Lagos State are the primary administrative units, and each has its own council and chairman. The Local Council Development Areas, on the other hand, are smaller units that are created to bring governance closer to the people. The administrative divisions in Lagos State are designed to promote economic growth, social development, and environmental sustainability.

[SECTION] Major Cities [/SECTION]
**Lagos** (21,000,000)
Role: Economic hub, cultural center, and port city
Known For: Lagos is the economic nerve center of Nigeria and is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and stunning architecture. The city is home to many notable landmarks, including the National Museum, the Lagos State House of Assembly, and the Eko Atlantic City.
Best For: Business travelers, cultural enthusiasts, and adventure seekers

**Ikeja** (500,000)
Role: State capital and administrative center
Known For: Ikeja is the capital of Lagos State and is known for its modern infrastructure, shopping malls, and entertainment centers. The city is home to the Murtala Muhammed International Airport and the Lagos State Secretariat.
Best For: Government officials, business travelers, and shoppers

**Badagry** (200,000)
Role: Historic city and tourist center
Known For: Badagry is a historic city that is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning beaches. The city is home to many notable landmarks, including the Badagry Heritage Museum, the Point of No Return, and the Coconut Beach.
Best For: History buffs, cultural enthusiasts, and beach lovers

**Ikorodu** (400,000)
Role: Industrial center and commercial hub
Known For: Ikorodu is a major industrial center and commercial hub in Lagos State. The city is home to many factories, warehouses, and markets, and is known for its vibrant economy and rich cultural heritage.
Best For: Industrialists, traders, and entrepreneurs

**Epe** (200,000)
Role: Agricultural center and tourist destination
Known For: Epe is a major agricultural center in Lagos State and is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The city is home to many tourist attractions, including the Epe Fish Market, the Epe Marina, and the Awolowo Museum.
Best For: Nature lovers, agricultural enthusiasts, and cultural tourists

Lagos State has a well-developed transportation system, with many options available for getting around.
