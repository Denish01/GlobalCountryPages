# Getting Around Lagos State — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Lagos State, you can use a combination of transportation modes including taxis, buses, ride-hailing apps, and private cars, although the state's infrastructure and traffic conditions can be challenging.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Lagos State is easily accessible by air through the Murtala Muhammed International Airport, which is located in Ikeja, about 20 kilometers northwest of Lagos city. The airport serves as a major hub for domestic and international flights, with direct connections to several cities in Africa, Europe, and the Middle East. Some of the major airlines that operate flights to Lagos include British Airways, Emirates, Lufthansa, and Turkish Airlines. If you're traveling from within Nigeria, you can also take a bus or drive to Lagos State, although the roads can be congested and poorly maintained.

For overland entry points, Lagos State is connected to other parts of Nigeria by a network of roads, including the Lagos-Ibadan Expressway and the Lagos-Abeokuta Expressway. The state is also connected to the neighboring country of Benin by a border crossing at Seme. However, traveling by road can be challenging due to the poor condition of the roads and the risk of robbery and other crimes. It's generally recommended to avoid traveling by road at night and to use a reputable bus company or private car service.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
If you need to travel within Nigeria, there are several domestic airlines that operate flights from Lagos to other parts of the country. Some of the major domestic airlines include Arik Air, Air Peace, and Dana Air. These airlines operate flights to several destinations, including Abuja, Port Harcourt, and Kano. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through a travel agent. The cost of domestic flights can vary depending on the route and the time of booking, but you can expect to pay around $100-$200 for a one-way ticket. It's generally a good idea to book in advance to get the best prices.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Lagos State has a limited rail network, with only a few routes operating within the state. The Nigerian Railway Corporation operates passenger trains from Lagos to other parts of the country, including Ibadan and Kano. However, the trains are often slow and unreliable, and the routes are limited. There are no high-speed train options available in Lagos State. If you want to take the train, you can book tickets at the train station or through a ticketing agent. The cost of train tickets can vary depending on the route and the class of service, but you can expect to pay around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several bus companies that operate intercity bus services from Lagos to other parts of Nigeria. Some of the major bus companies include ABC Transport, God is Good Motors, and Young Shall Grow Motors. These companies operate buses to several destinations, including Abuja, Port Harcourt, and Benin City. The buses are often comfortable and air-conditioned, and the prices are relatively affordable. You can expect to pay around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket, depending on the route and the class of service. It's generally a good idea to book in advance to get the best prices and to ensure availability.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Lagos State, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The roads in Lagos State can be poorly maintained and congested, so it's essential to be patient and cautious when driving.
- Fuel costs can be high, with prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 per liter, depending on the location and the type of fuel.
- If you plan to rent a car, be sure to choose a reputable company and inspect the vehicle carefully before driving.
- Be aware of the traffic laws and regulations, including the requirement to wear a seatbelt and not use a mobile phone while driving.
- Hazards on the road can include potholes, pedestrians, and other vehicles, so it's essential to be alert and focused when driving.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or when using public transportation.
- When using a taxi or ride-hailing service, make sure to agree on the fare before you start your journey.
- Avoid traveling by road at night, as the roads can be poorly lit and the risk of robbery or other crimes is higher.
- Consider downloading a ride-hailing app or a mapping app to help you navigate the city.
- Be prepared for heavy traffic and congestion, especially during peak hours.
- Don't carry large amounts of cash, and use a reputable currency exchange service if you need to exchange money.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, and avoid engaging in arguments or disputes with locals.
