Lagos State is a thriving region in Nigeria that boasts numerous positive achievements and highlights, including its vibrant cultural scene, innovative economy, and stunning natural beauty.

Cultural Treasures

  • The National Museum in Lagos is a treasure trove of Nigerian history and culture, featuring a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits.
  • The Eyo Festival is a traditional festival that takes place in Lagos, showcasing the city's rich cultural heritage through music, dance, and theater performances.
  • The New Afrika Shrine is a popular cultural center that hosts various events and concerts, including the annual Felabration festival, which celebrates the life and music of Fela Kuti.
  • The Lagos Book and Art Festival is an annual event that brings together writers, artists, and intellectuals to discuss and showcase the latest developments in Nigerian literature and art.
  • The Osun Osogbo Sacred Grove is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located near Lagos, which is dedicated to the worship of the goddess Osun and features various shrines and sculptures.

People & Global Impact

  • Wole Soyinka, a Nobel laureate and renowned writer, is from Lagos State and has made significant contributions to Nigerian literature and culture.
  • The Lagos diaspora community has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Lagosians living and working abroad and sending remittances back home.
  • Fela Kuti, a legendary musician and activist, was from Lagos and used his music to speak out against social injustice and corruption.
  • The Lagos business community has played a key role in shaping the Nigerian economy, with many entrepreneurs and business leaders from Lagos State making significant contributions to various sectors.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Lagos people is often praised by visitors, who comment on the warm and welcoming nature of the city's residents.
  • The food in Lagos is a major highlight, with popular dishes like jollof rice, suya, and egusi soup being favorites among visitors.
  • The beaches in Lagos, such as Bar Beach and Elegushi Beach, are popular spots for relaxation and recreation.
  • The Nightlife in Lagos is vibrant and exciting, with many bars, clubs, and lounges to choose from.
  • The markets in Lagos, such as the Balogun Market and the Lekki Market, offer a wide range of goods and souvenirs.
  • The scenery in Lagos, including the Lagos Lagoon and the Atlantic Ocean, provides a beautiful backdrop for photography and sightseeing.

Hidden Gems

  • The Lekki Conservation Centre is a hidden gem that offers a unique opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Lagos State, with its mangrove forests and wildlife.
  • The Badagry Heritage Museum is a lesser-known museum that showcases the history and culture of the Badagry people, who were instrumental in the transatlantic slave trade.
  • The Epe Fish Market is a bustling marketplace that offers a wide range of fresh seafood and provides a glimpse into the daily lives of Lagosians.
  • The Ikorodu Festival is a traditional festival that takes place in the Ikorodu area of Lagos State, featuring music, dance, and theater performances that showcase the local culture.
