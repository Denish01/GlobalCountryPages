[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Lagos State, a region in Nigeria, is a bustling metropolis with a high population density, which can contribute to safety concerns. The state is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and economic opportunities, but it also faces challenges such as traffic congestion, poverty, and crime. Visitors to Lagos State should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night, and take necessary precautions to stay safe. The Nigerian government has implemented various measures to improve security, but the risk of crime and scams remains high.

While Lagos State can be a challenging destination, many visitors have a safe and enjoyable experience. The key to a successful trip is to be informed, plan carefully, and take necessary precautions. Visitors should research their destinations, stay up-to-date with local news and events, and follow advice from local authorities and their government's travel advisory. Additionally, visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and avoid displaying signs of wealth or engaging in behaviors that may attract unwanted attention.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Pickpocketing** and **purse snatching** are common in crowded areas and public transportation
- **Scams** involving **ATM** and **credit card** fraud are prevalent, visitors should be cautious when using ATMs or credit cards
- **Traffic accidents** can occur due to reckless driving and poor road conditions
- **Kidnapping** and **armed robbery** can occur, especially at night or in isolated areas
- **Street harassment** and **catcalling** can be a problem for female visitors
- **Bribery** and **corruption** are common, visitors should be aware of their rights and avoid engaging in corrupt practices
- **Health risks** such as **malaria** and **typhoid fever** are present, visitors should take necessary precautions

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever vaccination is required, and visitors should also consider vaccinations for hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and typhoid fever
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, visitors should use bottled or filtered water
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are available, but quality and availability can vary, visitors should have adequate health insurance
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but visitors should be cautious when purchasing medications and ensure they are from reputable sources

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night
- Keep valuables secure and out of sight
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth or engaging in behaviors that may attract unwanted attention
- Use reputable transportation services and avoid hitchhiking
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe
- Be cautious when using ATMs or credit cards
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night
- Stay up-to-date with local news and events

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious when interacting with strangers, especially men
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night
- Dress modestly and avoid displaying signs of wealth
- Consider hiring a reputable driver or joining a guided tour to reduce risks
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary and whereabouts
