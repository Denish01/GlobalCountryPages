# Getting Around Rivers State — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Rivers State, you can use a combination of domestic flights, buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services, as the region has a moderate transportation network with various options available.

[FACTBOX]
Main International Airport(s): Port Harcourt International Airport (PHC)
Domestic Air Network: moderate
Rail Network: none
Drives On: right
Road Quality: poor
Ride-Hailing Apps: Bolt, local alternatives
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Rivers State is easily accessible by air, with the Port Harcourt International Airport (PHC) being the main entry point. The airport receives direct flights from major Nigerian cities like Lagos and Abuja, as well as international flights from destinations like Dubai and Amsterdam. If you're traveling from within Nigeria, you can also take a bus or drive to Rivers State, with the city of Port Harcourt being a major transportation hub. The state is bordered by several other Nigerian states, including Imo, Abia, and Bayelsa, making it a key stopover point for overland travelers.

For those traveling from outside Nigeria, the easiest way to get to Rivers State is to fly into Port Harcourt International Airport, which is located about 30 kilometers from the city center. From the airport, you can take a taxi or ride-hailing service to get to your destination. Alternatively, you can also fly into another major Nigerian city like Lagos or Abuja and then take a domestic flight to Port Harcourt. Overland entry points into Rivers State include the cities of Onitsha and Uyo, which are connected to Port Harcourt by major highways.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights are a convenient way to get to Rivers State, with several airlines operating flights from major Nigerian cities. Some of the main domestic airlines that operate in Rivers State include Arik Air, Dana Air, and Air Peace. These airlines offer flights from cities like Lagos, Abuja, and Kano to Port Harcourt, with prices starting from around $50 for a one-way ticket. To book a domestic flight, you can visit the website of the airline or use a local travel agent. It's also a good idea to book in advance to get the best prices, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Rivers State does not have a rail network, making it impossible to travel by train within the state or to neighboring states. However, there are plans to develop a rail network in the region, which would connect Port Harcourt to other major Nigerian cities. For now, travelers have to rely on other modes of transportation like buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services to get around.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Buses and coaches are a popular way to travel within Rivers State and to neighboring states. Several bus companies operate in the region, including major operators like ABC Transport and God is Good Motors. These companies offer daily services to cities like Lagos, Abuja, and Onitsha, with prices starting from around $10 for a one-way ticket. The quality of the buses varies, but most operators use modern vehicles with air conditioning and comfortable seating. To book a bus ticket, you can visit the website of the bus company or go to their terminal in person.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Rivers State, you need a valid Nigerian driver's license or an international driving permit.
- The road quality in Rivers State is generally poor, with many roads being unpaved or in disrepair.
- Fuel costs in Rivers State are relatively high, with a liter of gasoline costing around $0.80.
- Car rental services are available in Port Harcourt, but the options are limited and the prices are relatively high.
- When driving in Rivers State, be aware of the potential hazards like potholes, traffic congestion, and armed robbery.
- It's also a good idea to have a basic knowledge of the local traffic laws and regulations to avoid any problems with the authorities.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be careful when using taxis or ride-hailing services, as some drivers may try to overcharge or take you on a longer route than necessary.
- Always agree on the fare before you start your journey, and make sure the driver uses a meter if available.
- When traveling by bus, be sure to arrive at the terminal early to secure a good seat and avoid any last-minute delays.
- Download local transportation apps like Bolt to book ride-hailing services and get around the city more easily.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas like bus terminals and markets.
- Avoid traveling at night, especially if you're not familiar with the area, as the roads can be poorly lit and the risk of robbery is higher.
- Consider hiring a reputable driver or tour guide if you're not familiar with the local roads and transportation system.
