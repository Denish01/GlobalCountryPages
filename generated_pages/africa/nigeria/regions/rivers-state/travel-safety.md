[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Rivers State, located in the Niger Delta region of Nigeria, is a complex and challenging destination for travelers. The state has a history of civil unrest, crime, and kidnapping, which can make it difficult for visitors to feel safe. While the state government has taken steps to improve security, the risk of crime and violence remains high, particularly in certain areas. Travelers should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe, including avoiding traveling alone at night and being cautious of suspicious activity.

The safety situation in Rivers State can vary depending on the location and time of day. Some areas, such as the capital city of Port Harcourt, are considered safer than others, while areas near the oil-producing regions are often more volatile. Travelers should stay informed about local conditions and follow the advice of their government's travel advisory. It's also essential to research and understand the local culture and customs to avoid unintentionally offending locals or attracting unwanted attention.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Armed robbery and kidnapping are common crimes in Rivers State, particularly in areas near the oil-producing regions.
- Scams involving fake investment schemes and business opportunities are prevalent, targeting unsuspecting tourists and locals alike.
- Petty theft and pickpocketing are common in crowded areas and markets.
- Traffic accidents are a significant risk due to poor road conditions and reckless driving.
- Piracy and armed robbery are risks for travelers using water transportation.
- Local gangs and cults often engage in violent clashes, which can affect travelers.
- Corruption is widespread, and travelers may be targeted by corrupt officials seeking bribes.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for travel to Rivers State.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in Rivers State are limited, and travelers may need to seek medical attention in nearby cities or countries.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacy access is limited, and travelers should bring essential medications and supplies.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Research and stay informed about local conditions and travel advisories.
- Avoid traveling alone at night and use reputable transportation services.
- Be cautious of suspicious activity and avoid displaying signs of wealth.
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or locker.
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or mobile payments instead.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions to avoid unintentionally offending locals.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Traveling alone as a woman can be particularly challenging in Rivers State, and solo female travelers should take extra precautions to stay safe.
- Avoid traveling at night and use reputable transportation services, such as licensed taxis or ride-hailing services.
- Dress modestly and avoid displaying signs of wealth to minimize attention.
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary, including your accommodations and planned activities.
