# Getting Around Kano State — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around Kano State, you can use a combination of domestic flights, buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services, as well as drive yourself with a valid license and rental car.

[FACTBOX]
Main International Airport(s): Mallam Aminu Kano International Airport (KAN)
Domestic Air Network: moderate
Rail Network: limited
Drives On: right
Road Quality: moderate
Ride-Hailing Apps: Bolt, local alternatives
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Kano State is accessible by air through the Mallam Aminu Kano International Airport (KAN), which receives flights from major Nigerian cities like Lagos and Abuja, as well as international flights from nearby countries. The airport is located about 10 kilometers northwest of the city center and offers basic facilities such as currency exchange, restaurants, and car rental services. For travelers who prefer to enter Kano State overland, there are several entry points along the state's borders with neighboring states, including Jigawa, Kaduna, and Bauchi. The most common overland route is by bus or taxi from major cities like Lagos, Abuja, or Kaduna.

The main entry points into Kano State are through the cities of Kano, Jigawa, and Kaduna, which are connected by major highways. The journey by road can be long and tiring, but it offers a glimpse into the country's diverse landscapes and cultures. Several bus companies, including luxury and economy options, operate daily services from major cities to Kano State, with fares ranging from a few thousand to tens of thousands of naira, depending on the route and class of service. Additionally, travelers can take taxis or ride-hailing services from nearby cities to get to Kano State.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
For travelers who prefer to fly, there are several domestic airlines that operate flights to and from Kano State, including **Arik Air**, **Air Peace**, and **Dana Air**. These airlines offer regular flights from major cities like Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt, with fares ranging from 20,000 to 100,000 naira or more, depending on the route, class of service, and time of booking. It is recommended to book flights in advance to secure the best rates, and to check with the airlines for any travel restrictions or requirements, such as **COVID-19 protocols**. Travelers can book flights online through the airlines' websites or through local travel agents.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Kano State has a limited rail network, with only a few trains operating on the **Lagos-Kano** and **Port Harcourt-Kano** routes. The trains are operated by the **Nigerian Railway Corporation** and offer a relatively affordable and comfortable way to travel, with fares ranging from 5,000 to 20,000 naira or more, depending on the route and class of service. However, the trains are not always reliable, and the journey can be slow and bumpy. It is recommended to check with the railway corporation for schedules and fares before planning a trip.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several bus companies operate intercity services to and from Kano State, including **ABC Transport**, **Chisco Transport**, and **God is Good Motors**. These companies offer a range of services, from luxury to economy, with fares ranging from 5,000 to 20,000 naira or more, depending on the route and class of service. The buses are generally comfortable and well-maintained, but the journey can be long and tiring, especially on rough roads. Travelers can book tickets online or at the bus terminals, and it is recommended to check with the companies for schedules and fares before planning a trip.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Kano State, you need a valid **driver's license**, which can be obtained from the Nigerian Federal Road Safety Commission (FRSC) or exchanged from a foreign license.
- The **road conditions** in Kano State are generally moderate, with some paved highways and urban roads, but also many rough and unpaved rural roads.
- **Fuel costs** are relatively affordable, with prices ranging from 150 to 200 naira per liter, depending on the location and type of fuel.
- **Car rental** services are available in major cities, with prices ranging from 10,000 to 50,000 naira per day, depending on the type of vehicle and rental company.
- **Hazards** to watch out for include poor road conditions, traffic congestion, and reckless driving, especially at night or in rural areas.
- It is recommended to **drive defensively** and follow local traffic rules and regulations to avoid accidents and fines.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **scams** and **fraud** when using public transport or dealing with strangers, and always verify the authenticity of services and providers.
- **Payment methods** may vary, with some services accepting only cash, while others accept credit or debit cards, so it is recommended to have a combination of both.
- Download **ride-hailing apps** like Bolt or local alternatives to get around easily and conveniently.
- **Safety** is a major concern, especially at night or in isolated areas, so it is recommended to avoid traveling alone or with strangers, and to keep valuables secure.
- **Respect local customs** and traditions, especially when interacting with locals or visiting cultural sites.
- **Learn some Hausa phrases**, such as "sannu" (hello) and "na gode" (thank you), to show respect and appreciation for the local culture.
- **Stay informed** about local events and news, especially during periods of unrest or instability, to plan your trip accordingly.
- **Bring a map** or use a GPS device to navigate the city and surrounding areas, as signage may be limited or unclear.
