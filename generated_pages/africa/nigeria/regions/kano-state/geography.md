The state is landlocked, with no direct access to the ocean, and is located about 965 kilometers northwest of the Nigerian capital, Abuja. Kano State is also strategically located near the **Sahel region**, a transitional zone between the Sahara Desert to the north and the savannas to the south.

The state's location in the northern part of Nigeria makes it an important hub for trade and commerce, with several major roads and highways passing through it. The city of Kano, the state capital, is a major urban center and a key cultural and economic hub in the region. The state's borders with neighboring countries are not directly adjacent, but it is close to the border with the Republic of Niger to the north. The **Niger River**, which flows through several West African countries, is also located nearby, although it does not pass directly through Kano State.
