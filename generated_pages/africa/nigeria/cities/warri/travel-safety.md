[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Warri, a city in the Niger Delta region of Nigeria, has a complex safety landscape. On one hand, the city has a rich cultural heritage and a vibrant economy, making it an attractive destination for tourists and business travelers alike. However, Warri also experiences a high level of petty crime, including pickpocketing, scams, and armed robbery. Additionally, the city has been affected by communal clashes and protests in the past, which can sometimes turn violent. To stay safe, visitors should be aware of their surroundings, avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and keep valuables secure.

Despite these challenges, many areas of Warri are considered safe, and the city has a strong sense of community. With some basic precautions and an understanding of local conditions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Warri. It's essential to research your destination thoroughly, stay up-to-date with local news, and follow advice from local authorities and your government's travel advisory. By taking these steps, you can minimize your risk and have a positive experience in Warri.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Advance Fee Scams**: Scammers may target tourists with promises of easy money or investment opportunities, but these are often scams that can result in financial loss.
- **Pickpocketing**: Petty thieves often operate in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations, so it's essential to keep valuables secure.
- **Armed Robbery**: There is a risk of armed robbery in some areas of Warri, particularly at night, so it's best to avoid carrying large amounts of cash and to use reputable transportation services.
- **Kidnapping**: There have been cases of kidnapping in Warri, particularly in areas with a high crime rate, so it's essential to be aware of your surroundings and to avoid traveling alone at night.
- **Scams at ATMs**: Scammers may target tourists at ATMs, so it's best to use reputable machines and to cover the keypad when entering your PIN.
- **Overcharging**: Some vendors and taxi drivers may try to overcharge tourists, so it's essential to agree on a price before making a purchase or using a service.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: The Nigerian government recommends that visitors be vaccinated against **Yellow Fever**, **Hepatitis A**, and **Typhoid** before traveling to Warri.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is not considered safe to drink in Warri, so it's best to stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities**: Warri has some good quality medical facilities, including the **Delta State University Teaching Hospital**, but these may not be up to international standards. It's essential to have adequate health insurance that covers medical evacuation if necessary.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacies are widely available in Warri, but the quality of medications can vary, so it's best to stick to reputable pharmacies and to bring any essential medications with you from home.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas, and keep valuables secure.
- Use reputable transportation services, such as licensed taxis or ride-hailing apps, and avoid traveling alone at night.
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuables.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or debit cards instead.
- Stay informed about local conditions and follow advice from local authorities and your government's travel advisory.
- Learn some basic **Nigerian Pidgin** phrases, such as "hello" (kóyo) and "thank you" (imela), to help you communicate with locals.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious when interacting with strangers, especially men, and avoid traveling alone at night.
- Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events.
- Consider hiring a reputable guide or joining a tour group to help you navigate the city and stay safe.
- Keep your hotel room number and contact information private, and avoid sharing your itinerary with strangers.
