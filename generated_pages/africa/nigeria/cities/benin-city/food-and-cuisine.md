# Benin City Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Benin City, you should try the local specialties such as **Akara**, **Egusi Soup**, and **Puff-Puff**, which showcase the city's rich culinary heritage.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: yams, cassava, plantains, rice
National Dish: Akara, a fried bean cake made from mashed beans and spices
Meal Times: 7-10 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 6-9 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: halal and vegetarian options are available, but it's best to ask your server for clarification
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Benin City's cuisine is defined by its rich cultural heritage, with influences from the Edo people, who are the indigenous inhabitants of the city. The city's location in the southern part of Nigeria also means that its cuisine has been shaped by the country's diverse regional flavors. Benin City's cuisine is characterized by the use of local ingredients such as yams, cassava, and plantains, which are often combined with spices and herbs to create flavorful dishes. The city's cuisine has also been influenced by its history as a major trading center, with trade routes that connected it to other parts of West Africa and beyond.

The Edo people have a strong tradition of cooking and food preparation, and their cuisine is known for its bold flavors and spices. One of the key influences on Benin City's cuisine is the use of **ogbono seeds**, which are used to thicken soups and stews. The city's cuisine has also been shaped by its proximity to the Niger Delta, which provides a rich source of fresh seafood. The combination of these influences has resulted in a unique and flavorful cuisine that is distinct from other parts of Nigeria.

Benin City's cuisine is also known for its regional variations, with different areas of the city specializing in different types of dishes. For example, the **Ogba** area is known for its **Akara**, while the **Ring Road** area is known for its **Suya**, a type of grilled meat that is popular throughout Nigeria. These regional variations add to the richness and diversity of Benin City's cuisine, and provide visitors with a wide range of options to choose from.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Eating with your hands**: it is customary to eat with your hands in Benin City, especially when eating traditional dishes like **Akara** and **Puff-Puff**.
- **Tipping**: tipping is not mandatory, but it is appreciated for good service, 5-10% of the total bill.
- **Reservation**: it is not necessary to make a reservation at most restaurants, but it is recommended for large groups or special occasions.
- **Dress code**: the dress code is casual and relaxed, but it is recommended to dress modestly when visiting traditional restaurants or attending cultural events.
- **Eating etiquette**: it is customary to wait for the host to start eating before you begin, and to use your right hand when eating with your hands.
