[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Lagos, the largest city in Nigeria, is a hub of economic and cultural activity, but it also poses significant safety challenges for visitors. The city is known for its high crime rate, with petty theft, scams, and violent crimes being common occurrences. However, with the right mindset and precautions, visitors can minimize their risks and have a safe and enjoyable trip. It is essential to be aware of the local conditions and take necessary measures to protect oneself, such as avoiding traveling alone at night, using reputable transportation services, and being cautious of suspicious activities.

The Nigerian government has taken steps to improve security in Lagos, including the deployment of police and military personnel to key areas. Additionally, many businesses and organizations have implemented their own security measures, such as armed guards and CCTV cameras. Despite these efforts, crime remains a significant concern, and visitors should remain vigilant and take necessary precautions to stay safe. It is also important to note that some areas of the city are considered safer than others, and visitors should research and plan their itinerary accordingly.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Scams: visitors should be cautious of scams, such as the "help me" scam, where locals approach tourists and ask for help with a fake problem.
- Petty theft: pickpocketing and bag snatching are common in crowded areas, visitors should be mindful of their belongings.
- Traffic accidents: Lagos is known for its chaotic traffic, visitors should be cautious when crossing the road and use reputable transportation services.
- Kidnapping: there is a risk of kidnapping in Lagos, visitors should avoid traveling alone at night and be cautious of suspicious activities.
- Armed robbery: armed robbery is a significant concern in Lagos, visitors should avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use secure transportation services.
- Credit card scams: visitors should be cautious when using credit cards, as scams are common in Lagos.
- Black market activities: visitors should avoid engaging in black market activities, such as buying counterfeit goods or exchanging currency on the street.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** visitors should ensure they have all the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Lagos, including yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid.
- **Water Safety:** tap water is not safe to drink, visitors should use bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities:** medical facilities in Lagos are limited, and visitors may need to be evacuated to a nearby country for serious medical conditions. It is essential to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.
- **Pharmacy Access:** pharmacies are widely available in Lagos, but visitors should be cautious of counterfeit medications.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Research and plan your itinerary carefully, avoiding areas with high crime rates.
- Use reputable transportation services, such as taxis or ride-hailing apps.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and use secure transportation services.
- Be mindful of your belongings, and use a money belt or a secure bag.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or mobile payments instead.
- Be cautious of scams, and avoid engaging with suspicious individuals.
- Stay informed about local conditions, and follow local news and government advisories.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Solo female travelers should be cautious when traveling alone at night, and use secure transportation services.
- Dress modestly, and avoid drawing attention to yourself.
- Avoid engaging with suspicious individuals, and be cautious of scams.
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home, and keep them informed of your itinerary and whereabouts.
