[SECTION] What Is Lagos? [/SECTION]
Lagos is a city-state located in the southwestern part of Nigeria, bordering the Gulf of Guinea. As the former capital of Nigeria, Lagos is now the country's economic and cultural hub, with a population of over 15 million people, making it one of the largest cities in Africa. The city is situated on the coast of West Africa, with a geographic position that has made it an important center for trade and commerce.

Lagos is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and diverse economy. The city is home to many museums, galleries, and cultural institutions, showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage. Lagos is also a major economic center, with a thriving financial sector, a busy port, and a growing technology industry. The city's economy is driven by a mix of industries, including manufacturing, construction, and services.

The city's history dates back to the 15th century, when it was a major trading post for the Portuguese. Over the years, Lagos has been influenced by various cultures, including the British, who colonized Nigeria in the late 19th century. Today, Lagos is a melting pot of different cultures, with a unique blend of traditional and modern architecture, music, and art.
