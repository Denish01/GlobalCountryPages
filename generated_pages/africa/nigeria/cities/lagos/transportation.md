# Getting Around Lagos — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Lagos, you can use a variety of transportation options including taxis, ride-hailing apps, buses, and ferries, although the city's traffic congestion can make travel time unpredictable.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Lagos is a major city in Nigeria and has a well-connected international airport, Murtala Muhammed International Airport (LOS), which receives flights from many major airlines around the world. The airport is located about 14 miles northwest of the city center and offers direct flights to several destinations in Africa, Europe, and the Middle East. Some of the major airlines that operate direct flights to Lagos include British Airways, Emirates, Lufthansa, and Turkish Airlines. Additionally, there are several overland entry points into Lagos, including the border crossings with the neighboring countries of Benin and Cameroon, although these routes are not commonly used by tourists.

For those traveling overland, there are several bus companies that operate services from neighboring countries to Lagos, including ABC Transport and God is Good Motors. These buses typically depart from major cities such as Accra in Ghana and Cotonou in Benin, and the journey can take several hours or even days, depending on the route and the condition of the roads. It's also possible to enter Lagos by sea, with several ferry services operating from nearby ports, including the Lagos Ferry Service, which connects the city to nearby islands and coastal towns.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
For domestic travel within Nigeria, there are several airlines that operate flights from Lagos to other major cities, including Abuja, Kano, and Port Harcourt. Some of the main domestic airlines include Arik Air, Air Peace, and Dana Air, which offer frequent flights to many destinations around the country. The cost of domestic flights can vary depending on the route and the time of year, but approximate costs range from $50 to $200 for a one-way ticket. It's generally recommended to book flights in advance to secure the best prices, and many airlines offer online booking and payment options.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Lagos has a limited rail network, with only a few routes operating within the city and to nearby towns. The Nigerian Railway Corporation (NRC) operates passenger trains from Lagos to several destinations, including Ibadan and Abeokuta, but the service is not frequent and the trains are often slow and unreliable. There are no high-speed rail options available in Lagos, and the booking process can be complicated, with many tickets only available for purchase at the train station. However, the NRC is currently working to upgrade the rail network and improve services, so it's worth checking for updates before traveling.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several bus companies that operate intercity services from Lagos to other major cities in Nigeria, including ABC Transport, God is Good Motors, and Young Shall Grow Motors. These buses typically depart from major bus stations, such as the Jibowu Bus Stop or the Ojota Bus Stop, and offer a range of services, including air-conditioned coaches and economy buses. The cost of bus travel can vary depending on the route and the quality of the bus, but approximate costs range from $10 to $50 for a one-way ticket. The journey times can be long, especially during peak traffic hours, but the buses are generally a reliable and affordable option for traveling around the country.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Lagos, you will need to obtain a Nigerian driver's license, which can be applied for at the Federal Road Safety Commission (FRSC) office.
- The road conditions in Lagos are often poor, with many potholes and uneven surfaces, so it's essential to drive carefully and slowly.
- Fuel costs are relatively high in Nigeria, with gasoline prices averaging around $0.80 per liter.
- Car rental companies are available in Lagos, including major international brands like Hertz and Avis, but the prices can be high, and the quality of the vehicles may vary.
- One of the main hazards of driving in Lagos is the chaotic traffic, with many drivers ignoring traffic rules and regulations, so it's essential to stay alert and patient.
- Additionally, there is a risk of carjacking and robbery, especially at night, so it's recommended to avoid driving alone in isolated areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of the **scams** that target tourists, including fake taxi drivers and touts, and always use reputable transportation services.
- **Payment methods** for transportation in Lagos include cash, credit cards, and mobile payments, but it's always a good idea to have some local currency, the Naira, handy.
- Download **transport apps**, such as Uber or Bolt, to book rides and track your journey.
- **Safety** is a major concern in Lagos, especially at night, so it's recommended to avoid traveling alone and to use reputable transportation services.
- **Traffic congestion** is a major problem in Lagos, so it's essential to plan your journey in advance and allow plenty of time to reach your destination.
- **Road conditions** can be poor, especially during the rainy season, so it's essential to drive carefully and slowly.
- **Local knowledge** is essential for navigating the city, so it's a good idea to hire a local driver or guide, or to use a reputable transportation service.
- **Respect local customs** and traditions, especially when interacting with local drivers or transportation staff, and always be patient and polite.
