# Where to Stay in Lagos — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Lagos, the best areas to stay include Victoria Island, Ikoyi, Lekki, Ikeja, Surulere, and Apapa, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's what you can expect:
**Victoria Island** (Lagos)
Best For: Luxury/Business
Vibe: Victoria Island is known for its upscale atmosphere, with many high-end restaurants, bars, and hotels, making it a great spot for business travelers and those seeking luxury. The area is also home to many embassies and consulates, adding to its international feel.
Price Range: $150-500/night
Pros: Close proximity to the business district, many dining and entertainment options, easy access to the airport.
Cons: Traffic can be heavy, and the area can feel quite commercial.

**Ikoyi** (Lagos)
Best For: Luxury/Family
Vibe: Ikoyi is a affluent neighborhood with a more relaxed atmosphere, featuring tree-lined streets, parks, and a variety of restaurants and shops. It's a popular area for families and those seeking a more laid-back experience.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Quiet and safe, plenty of green spaces, close to the lagoon.
Cons: Limited nightlife options.

**Lekki** (Lagos)
Best For: Business/Luxury
Vibe: Lekki is a fast-growing area with a mix of residential and commercial developments, featuring many new hotels, restaurants, and shopping centers. It's a great spot for business travelers and those seeking modern amenities.
Price Range: $80-250/night
Pros: Many new and modern facilities, easy access to the expressway, growing entertainment scene.
Cons: Traffic can be heavy, and the area is still developing.

**Ikeja** (Lagos)
Best For: Budget/Family
Vibe: Ikeja is a bustling area with a more local feel, featuring many markets, shops, and restaurants. It's a great spot for budget travelers and families seeking an authentic experience.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable prices, many local amenities, close to the airport.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded.

**Surulere** (Lagos)
Best For: Budget/Backpacker
Vibe: Surulere is a lively area with a mix of residential and commercial developments, featuring many bars, clubs, and restaurants. It's a great spot for budget travelers and backpackers seeking a vibrant atmosphere.
Price Range: $15-50/night
Pros: Affordable prices, many entertainment options, close to the national stadium.
Cons: Can be noisy, and the area can feel quite crowded.

**Apapa** (Lagos)
Best For: Business
Vibe: Apapa is a commercial area with a focus on industry and trade, featuring many warehouses, ports, and shipping facilities. It's a great spot for business travelers seeking proximity to the ports and industrial areas.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Close to the ports and industrial areas, many business facilities.
Cons: Traffic can be heavy, and the area can feel quite industrial.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to February).
- Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or during off-peak season.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices often higher during holidays and lower during the off-season.
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs.
- Use travel agencies or local tour operators to find the best deals and get insider knowledge.
- Be cautious of scams and always use reputable booking platforms.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas like Ajegunle and Mushin, known for higher crime rates.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night.
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or a secure bag.
- Be mindful of traffic and road conditions, and plan your route accordingly.
