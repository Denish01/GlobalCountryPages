[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **stilt village tour** in Makoko, a unique fishing community built on stilts over the water
- Attend a **traditional Yoruba festival**, such as the Osun Osogbo festival, to experience the local culture and traditions
- Visit the **Lekki Market**, a bustling marketplace where you can buy local handicrafts and souvenirs
- Take a **cooking class** to learn how to make traditional Nigerian dishes, such as jollof rice and egusi soup
- Go on a **street art tour** to see the city's vibrant street art scene and learn about the local artists
- Attend a **music performance** at the New Afrika Shrine, a legendary music venue that showcases the best of Nigerian music
- Take a **yoga class** on the beach, a unique and relaxing way to experience the city's beautiful coastline

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Lekki Conservation Centre**, a nature reserve with a canopy walk and a chance to see local wildlife, such as monkeys and birds
- Go to the **Bar Beach**, a popular beach for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports
- Explore the **Badagry Creek**, a scenic waterway that offers boat tours and a chance to see local fishermen at work
- Visit the **Elegushi Royal Beach**, a private beach with a range of amenities, including restaurants and water sports
- Go on a **hiking trip** to the **Ogba Forest Reserve**, a scenic area with hiking trails and a chance to see local wildlife

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum Lagos**, a museum that showcases Nigeria's history and culture
- Attend a **traditional dance performance**, such as the Egungun festival, to experience the local culture and traditions
- Take a **tour of the Brazilian Quarter**, a historic neighborhood with a unique architectural style and a rich cultural heritage
- Visit the **Nike Art Gallery**, a gallery that showcases a collection of Nigerian art
- Attend a **music performance** at the **Muson Centre**, a cultural center that showcases the best of Nigerian music and art
- Visit the **Kalakuta Museum**, a museum that showcases the life and legacy of Fela Kuti, a legendary Nigerian musician

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Jhalobia Recreation Park**, a scenic park with a range of amenities, including restaurants and a playground
- Explore the **Ikoyi Cemetery**, a historic cemetery with a range of interesting gravestones and a chance to learn about the city's history
- Go to the **Bogobiri House**, a cultural center with a range of amenities, including a restaurant and a bar
- Attend a **book reading** at the **Jazzhole**, a bookstore and cultural center that showcases the best of Nigerian literature
- Visit the **Alara Concept Store**, a unique store that sells a range of local handicrafts and souvenirs

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Badagry**, a historic town with a range of attractions, including the Badagry Heritage Museum and the Point of No Return (distance: 60 km, travel time: 1 hour)
- Visit **Abeokuta**, a town with a range of attractions, including the Olumo Rock and the Abeokuta Museum (distance: 100 km, travel time: 2 hours)
- Go to **Ijebu Ode**, a town with a range of attractions, including the Ijebu Ode Museum and the Osogbo Sacred Grove (distance: 120 km, travel time: 2.5 hours)
- Take a day trip to **Lekki**, a town with a range of attractions, including the Lekki Conservation Centre and the Lekki Market (distance: 30 km, travel time: 30 minutes)
- Visit **Epe**, a town with a range of attractions, including the Epe Fish Market and the Epe Beach (distance: 50 km, travel time: 1 hour)
