# Getting Around Kano — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Kano, you can use a combination of taxis, buses, and ride-hailing services, as well as domestic flights and trains for longer journeys.

Main International Airport(s): Mallam Aminu Kano International Airport (KAN)

Rail Network: limited
Drives On: right
Road Quality: poor
Ride-Hailing Apps: Bolt, local alternatives
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Kano is a major city in Nigeria, and the Mallam Aminu Kano International Airport (KAN) is the main entry point for international travelers. The airport receives direct flights from several African and European cities, including Cairo, Dubai, and London. If you are traveling from within Nigeria, you can also take a domestic flight from major cities like Lagos and Abuja. Overland entry points include the border crossings with Niger and Benin, although these routes are not commonly used by tourists.

For travelers from other parts of Nigeria, Kano is also accessible by road, with several bus companies operating daily services from major cities. The journey can be long and arduous, especially during the rainy season, but it is a more affordable option than flying. The city is also connected to the Nigerian rail network, although the services are limited and not always reliable. The main train station in Kano is the Kano Railway Station, which receives trains from Lagos and other major cities.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights are a convenient way to travel within Nigeria, and several airlines operate from Kano to other major cities. The main domestic airlines in Nigeria include Arik Air, Air Peace, and Dana Air, which offer frequent flights to destinations like Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as prices can be high. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Kano start from around $100 for a one-way ticket to Lagos, depending on the airline and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
The rail network in Nigeria is limited, and the services are not always reliable. However, the Nigerian Railway Corporation operates trains from Kano to other major cities like Lagos and Kaduna. The journey can be long, taking around 24 hours from Kano to Lagos, but it is a more scenic route than flying. There are no high-speed train options in Nigeria, and the booking process can be cumbersome. It is recommended to book in advance and to check the train schedule before traveling.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several bus companies operate from Kano to other major cities in Nigeria, offering a more affordable option than flying. The main bus companies include ABC Transport, God is Good Motors, and Young Shall Grow Motors, which operate daily services from Kano to destinations like Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt. The quality of the buses varies, but most companies offer comfortable and air-conditioned vehicles. Costs start from around $10 for a one-way ticket to nearby cities, depending on the company and route.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Nigeria, you need a valid driver's license, which can be obtained locally or in your home country.
- Road conditions in Kano are generally poor, with many potholes and uneven surfaces.
- Fuel costs are relatively low in Nigeria, with gasoline priced at around $0.40 per liter.
- Car rental companies are available in Kano, but the options are limited, and prices may be high.
- Hazards on the road include aggressive driving, poor road conditions, and the risk of carjacking or robbery.
- It is recommended to drive with a local guide or to use a reputable taxi service, especially at night.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of scams and touts at the airport and bus stations, and always use authorized taxi or ride-hailing services.
- Payment methods include cash, credit cards, and mobile payments, but cash is still the preferred method in many places.
- Download the Bolt app or other local ride-hailing services to get around the city.
- Always negotiate the price before taking a taxi or bus, and be clear about your destination.
- Avoid traveling at night, especially by road, due to the risk of robbery or carjacking.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when interacting with taxi drivers or other transport providers.
- Keep your valuables safe and secure, especially in crowded areas or public transport.
- Learn some basic Hausa phrases, such as "hello" (assalamu alaikum) and "thank you" (na gode), to interact with locals and transport providers.
