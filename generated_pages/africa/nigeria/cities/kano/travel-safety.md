[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Kano, as a major city in Nigeria, has a complex security situation that requires visitors to exercise caution. The city has experienced instances of violent crime, including armed robbery and kidnapping, although these are not typically targeted at tourists. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and scams, is more common and can be a significant concern for visitors. Additionally, the city's crowded markets and streets can be overwhelming, and visitors should be mindful of their surroundings to avoid becoming victims of crime.

It's essential for visitors to Kano to stay informed about local conditions and take necessary precautions to stay safe. This includes avoiding traveling alone at night, using reputable transportation services, and being cautious when dealing with strangers. The Nigerian government has taken steps to improve security in the city, including the deployment of security personnel to high-risk areas. However, visitors should still exercise caution and avoid areas that are considered high-risk.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **scams** involving fake police officers or officials who may demand bribes or try to extort money from visitors.
- **Pickpocketing** is common in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations, so visitors should keep a close eye on their belongings.
- **Armed robbery** is a risk, particularly at night, so visitors should avoid traveling alone and use reputable transportation services.
- **Kidnapping** is a rare but serious risk, and visitors should avoid traveling to high-risk areas and keep a low profile.
- **Traffic accidents** are common in Kano, so visitors should exercise caution when crossing roads and avoid traveling in overcrowded or poorly maintained vehicles.
- **Food and water poisoning** is a risk, particularly from street food or untreated water, so visitors should stick to reputable restaurants and drink bottled or filtered water.
- **ATM scams** are common, so visitors should use reputable ATMs and keep their PIN numbers secure.
- **Touts and con artists** may approach visitors in tourist areas, offering fake services or trying to scam them out of money.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Visitors to Kano should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations against **yellow fever**, **Hepatitis A**, and **typhoid**.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to **bottled or filtered water**.
- **Medical Facilities:** Kano has some good quality medical facilities, but visitors may need to pay upfront for treatment, and **travel insurance** is highly recommended.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available in Kano, but visitors should be cautious when purchasing **medications** and ensure they are from reputable sources.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **reputable map** or use a GPS device to navigate the city.
- **Avoid traveling alone** at night and use reputable transportation services.
- Keep **valuables secure** and be mindful of your surroundings in crowded areas.
- **Dress modestly** and respect local customs to avoid drawing attention to yourself.
- **Learn some basic Hausa phrases**, such as greetings and directions, to help you navigate the city.
- **Stay informed** about local conditions and security alerts through your government's travel advisory website.
- **Keep your hotel room door locked** and use the hotel safe to store valuables.
- **Be cautious when using ATMs** and keep your PIN number secure.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- **Dress conservatively** and avoid drawing attention to yourself, particularly in rural areas.
- **Avoid traveling alone** at night and use reputable transportation services.
- **Stay in touch** with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary.
- **Be mindful of your surroundings** and avoid walking alone in isolated areas.
