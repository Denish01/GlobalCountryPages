[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Abuja is situated in the Federal Capital Territory, which is an independent administrative division that is not part of any state in Nigeria. The city is located in the center of the country, approximately 800 kilometers northwest of Lagos, the commercial capital. Abuja is bordered by several states, including Niger State to the west, Kaduna State to the north, Nasarawa State to the east, and Kogi State to the south. The city's location was chosen to be equidistant from the major cities in Nigeria, making it a central and accessible hub for the country.

The Federal Capital Territory, where Abuja is located, is a vast area of over 7,000 square kilometers, with Abuja city being the main urban center. The territory is surrounded by rolling hills, savannas, and grasslands, with several rivers, including the Gurara River, flowing through it. The city's location in the center of the country has made it an important transportation hub, with major highways and rail lines connecting it to other parts of Nigeria.

The terrain is generally flat, with some undulating hills and valleys, making it suitable for urban development. The city's landscape is dominated by the **savanna** ecosystem, with grasses, shrubs, and trees being the main vegetation types.

The city's terrain and landscape have been modified by human activity, with urban development, agriculture, and infrastructure development changing the natural environment.

The climate and geology of the area have also shaped the terrain and landscape of Abuja.
