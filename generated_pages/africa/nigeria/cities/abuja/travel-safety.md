[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Abuja is a relatively safe city compared to other major cities in Nigeria, with a strong presence of security personnel and a growing economy. However, as with any major city, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, scams, and armed robberies can occur, particularly in crowded areas and at night. Visitors should be cautious when using ATMs, exchanging money, or carrying large amounts of cash, as these activities can attract unwanted attention. Additionally, the city's traffic can be chaotic, and visitors should exercise caution when crossing roads or using public transportation.

Despite these risks, Abuja is a vibrant and welcoming city, with a rich cultural heritage and a strong sense of community. With proper planning, precautions, and an understanding of local customs and conditions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Abuja. It is essential to stay informed about local conditions, follow local advice, and take necessary precautions to minimize risks. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings, keep valuables secure, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or displaying signs of wealth.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Scams: visitors should be cautious of scams, particularly those involving fake investment schemes, phishing, or advance fee fraud
- Pickpocketing: crowded areas such as markets and bus stations are hotspots for pickpocketing
- Armed robberies: visitors should be aware of their surroundings, particularly at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash
- Kidnappings: visitors should avoid traveling alone at night and be cautious of strangers who approach them
- Traffic accidents: visitors should exercise caution when crossing roads or using public transportation
- ATM scams: visitors should be cautious when using ATMs, particularly in isolated areas
- Fake police: visitors should be wary of individuals posing as police officers, particularly if they request bribes or try to arrest them

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** visitors should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations for hepatitis A, typhoid, and yellow fever, as well as take precautions against malaria
- **Water Safety:** tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water
- **Medical Facilities:** Abuja has several high-quality medical facilities, including the National Hospital and the University of Abuja Teaching Hospital, but visitors may need to pay upfront for treatment
- **Pharmacy Access:** pharmacies are widely available, but visitors should ensure they purchase medications from reputable sources

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas or at night
- Keep valuables secure, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and avoid traveling alone at night
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Stay informed about local conditions, and follow local advice
- Keep your hotel room door locked, and use the hotel safe to store valuables
- Avoid using ATMs in isolated areas, and be cautious when using public Wi-Fi

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious when interacting with strangers, particularly men, and avoid traveling alone at night
- Dress modestly, and avoid displaying signs of wealth or affluence
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Keep your hotel room door locked, and use the hotel safe to store valuables, and consider staying in a reputable hotel with good security
