Overall Safety Assessment

Kaduna, like many other cities in Nigeria, faces challenges related to crime and safety. The city has experienced instances of violent crime, including armed robbery and kidnapping, as well as petty crime like pickpocketing and scams. However, it's essential to note that most crimes are opportunistic and can be mitigated with proper precautions and awareness. Visitors should be cautious, especially at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables. Additionally, the city has a strong cultural heritage and a rich history, making it an attractive destination for tourists who are willing to take necessary safety measures.

The Nigerian government has taken steps to improve security in Kaduna, including increasing police presence and implementing curfews. However, the situation can change rapidly, and tourists should stay informed about local conditions and follow their government's travel advisories. By being aware of the potential risks and taking necessary precautions, visitors can minimize their exposure to danger and have a safe and enjoyable experience in Kaduna.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of advance fee scams, where individuals promise large sums of money or investments in exchange for a small upfront fee.
  • Beware of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and markets.
  • Be aware of traffic scams, where individuals may pose as police officers or traffic officials to extort money from drivers.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use reputable banks and ATMs to minimize the risk of robbery.
  • Be cautious of unlicensed taxis and okadas (motorcycle taxis), which can be a safety risk.
  • Beware of kidnapping and armed robbery, which can occur in certain areas of the city.
  • Be aware of cult activities, which can lead to violence and unrest in certain areas.

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for travel to Kaduna.
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended.
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities in Kaduna are limited, and visitors may need to travel to larger cities like Abuja or Lagos for quality medical care. It's essential to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are available in Kaduna, but the quality and availability of medications can vary.

Practical Safety Tips

  • Avoid traveling at night, especially in areas with high crime rates.
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
  • Keep valuables secure and out of sight, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and markets.
  • Avoid engaging with touts or scammers, and be cautious of strangers who approach you with unsolicited offers or advice.
  • Stay informed about local conditions and follow your government's travel advisories.
  • Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel's safe or security box to store valuables.

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be cautious of harassment and catcalling, especially in crowded areas and markets.
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps instead.
  • Dress modestly and avoid drawing attention to yourself, especially in conservative areas.
  • Stay in touch with family and friends back home, and keep them informed of your itinerary and whereabouts.
