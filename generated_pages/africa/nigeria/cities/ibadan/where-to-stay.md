# Where to Stay in Ibadan — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Ibadan, you should stay in areas like Agodi, Jericho, or Bodija, which offer a range of accommodations and easy access to the city's main attractions. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas/neighborhoods in Ibadan, consider the following options:
**Agodi** (Ibadan)
Best For: Luxury/Business
Vibe: Agodi is a upscale neighborhood with a mix of modern and traditional architecture, offering a sophisticated and elegant atmosphere. It is home to several government buildings and embassies, making it a hub for business and diplomacy.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Close proximity to government offices, high-end amenities, and a secure environment. 
Cons: Traffic congestion during peak hours, limited budget-friendly options.

**Jericho** (Ibadan)
Best For: Family/Backpacker
Vibe: Jericho is a lively and vibrant neighborhood with a mix of residential and commercial areas, offering a unique blend of traditional and modern culture. It is known for its bustling markets and street food stalls.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable accommodations, easy access to local markets, and a wide range of dining options. 
Cons: Noise pollution from the busy streets, limited parking options.

**Bodija** (Ibadan)
Best For: Business/Luxury
Vibe: Bodija is a modern and upscale neighborhood with a focus on commerce and industry, offering a sleek and contemporary atmosphere. It is home to several major companies and office buildings.
Price Range: $40-150/night
Pros: Close proximity to major businesses, high-end amenities, and a secure environment. 
Cons: Limited budget-friendly options, traffic congestion during peak hours.

**Oke-Ado** (Ibadan)
Best For: Family/Backpacker
Vibe: Oke-Ado is a residential neighborhood with a mix of traditional and modern homes, offering a warm and welcoming atmosphere. It is known for its quiet streets and family-friendly environment.
Price Range: $15-80/night
Pros: Affordable accommodations, easy access to local markets, and a safe environment. 
Cons: Limited high-end amenities, traffic congestion during peak hours.

**Mokola** (Ibadan)
Best For: Luxury/Business
Vibe: Mokola is an upscale neighborhood with a focus on commerce and entertainment, offering a sophisticated and elegant atmosphere. It is home to several major hotels and restaurants.
Price Range: $60-250/night
Pros: Close proximity to major businesses, high-end amenities, and a secure environment. 
Cons: Limited budget-friendly options, noise pollution from the busy streets.
