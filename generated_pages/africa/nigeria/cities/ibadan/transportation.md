# Getting Around Ibadan — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around Ibadan, you can use a variety of transportation options including buses, taxis, ride-hailing apps, and private car rentals, with the city's road network being the primary mode of transportation.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Ibadan is a major city in Nigeria, and while it has its own airport, most international visitors will likely arrive at the Murtala Muhammed International Airport in Lagos, which is about 130 kilometers away. From Lagos, you can take a domestic flight, bus, or taxi to get to Ibadan. The Ibadan Airport, also known as Alakia Airport, receives limited domestic flights from other major cities in Nigeria, including Lagos and Abuja. If you're traveling overland, Ibadan is connected to other major cities in Nigeria through a network of highways, including the Lagos-Ibadan Expressway and the Ibadan-Ilorin Expressway.

For those traveling from neighboring countries, the closest international border is with the Republic of Benin, which is about 200 kilometers away. However, there are no direct bus or train services from Benin to Ibadan, so you'll need to take a taxi or private car to get to the border and then transfer to another vehicle to continue your journey. Alternatively, you can fly into Lagos and then take a domestic flight or bus to Ibadan.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights in Nigeria are limited, and Ibadan Airport receives only a few flights per day from other major cities. The main domestic airlines that operate flights to Ibadan include Aero Contractors, Arik Air, and Dana Air. The most common routes are from Lagos and Abuja, with flights typically taking around an hour. Booking flights in advance is recommended, as prices can be high and availability may be limited. Approximate costs for a one-way ticket from Lagos to Ibadan can range from $50 to $200, depending on the airline and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Unfortunately, there is no rail network in Ibadan or in Nigeria as a whole, so train travel is not an option. The Nigerian government has proposed several rail development projects in recent years, but none have been completed yet. As a result, road transportation remains the primary mode of getting around the city and the country.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several bus companies operate intercity services from Ibadan to other major cities in Nigeria, including Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt. Some of the most popular bus companies include ABC Transport, Ifeoluwa Transport, and Oyo Line. The quality of the buses can vary, but most are air-conditioned and relatively comfortable. Prices are generally affordable, with a one-way ticket from Ibadan to Lagos costing around $10 to $20. The journey can take around 2-3 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Nigeria, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- Road conditions in Ibadan can be challenging, with many potholes and uneven surfaces.
- Fuel costs are relatively low, with a liter of gasoline costing around $0.50.
- Car rental companies are available in Ibadan, but prices can be high, with a daily rate of around $50-$100.
- Driving in Nigeria can be hazardous, with many aggressive drivers and poor road conditions.
- It's recommended to hire a driver or use a reputable car rental company to avoid any potential risks.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be careful when using taxis or ride-hailing apps, as some drivers may try to overcharge you or take a longer route to increase the fare.
- Always negotiate the price before you start your journey, and make sure you have a clear understanding of the route and the cost.
- Avoid using okadas (motorcycles) or keke napeps (tricycles) as they can be unsafe and unreliable.
- Download the Bolt app or other local ride-hailing apps to book a ride in advance and avoid any potential scams.
- Always carry some cash with you, as not all transportation providers may accept credit or debit cards.
- Be prepared for traffic congestion, especially during peak hours, and plan your journey accordingly.
- Consider hiring a reputable driver or car rental company for the day to avoid any potential risks or hassles.
- Always wear your seatbelt and follow all traffic rules to stay safe on the road.
