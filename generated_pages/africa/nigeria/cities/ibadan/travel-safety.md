Ibadan is generally a safe city to visit, but as with any major city, there are certain precautions and safety considerations that tourists should be aware of to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Ibadan, the largest city in Nigeria, is a bustling metropolis with a rich cultural heritage and a strong sense of community. While the city is generally safe, tourists should be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and scams are common, and tourists should be cautious when dealing with strangers or using public transportation. Additionally, the city's infrastructure and traffic can be challenging, and tourists should be prepared for crowded streets and chaotic traffic conditions.

Overall, Ibadan is a city that is full of life and energy, and with some basic precautions and awareness, tourists can have a safe and enjoyable experience. It's essential to stay informed about local conditions, respect local customs and traditions, and take necessary measures to protect oneself from potential risks. By doing so, tourists can immerse themselves in the city's vibrant culture, try local cuisine, and explore the many historical and cultural landmarks that Ibadan has to offer.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of ** Advance Fee Fraud** scams, where individuals promise large sums of money in exchange for upfront fees.
- Beware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas and public transportation.
- Be aware of **traffic scams**, where individuals may pose as traffic officials to extort money from tourists.
- Do not carry large amounts of cash, and use **ATMs** or credit cards instead.
- Be cautious of **strangers** who approach you with unsolicited offers or requests.
- Beware of **fake police officers** who may stop you and demand bribes.
- Be aware of **protests** and demonstrations, which can turn violent.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever vaccination is required, and hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are recommended.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are available, but quality and availability can vary, and medical insurance is recommended.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but the quality and authenticity of medications can vary.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **map** or use a GPS device to navigate the city.
- Be aware of your **surroundings** and keep an eye on your belongings.
- Use **licensed taxis** or ride-hailing services instead of hitchhiking or using unlicensed taxis.
- Keep your **hotel room door** locked and use the hotel safe to store valuables.
- Be cautious of **strangers** who approach you with unsolicited offers or requests.
- Stay **informed** about local conditions and events that may affect your safety.
- Make **copies** of important documents, such as your passport and driver's license.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be **cautious** when walking alone at night, and try to stay in well-lit areas.
- **Dress modestly** and respect local customs and traditions.
- Be aware of your **surroundings** and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
- Consider **staying in a hotel** or guesthouse that has good security and is located in a safe area.
