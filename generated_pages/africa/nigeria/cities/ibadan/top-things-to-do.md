[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **stilt walking** lesson and learn about the traditional Yoruba art form
- Attend a **traditional Yoruba wedding** ceremony to experience the local culture
- Learn how to make **Adire** textile, a traditional Yoruba fabric
- Go on a **cocoa farm tour** to see how cocoa is harvested and processed
- Take a **drumming lesson** and learn about the significance of drums in Yoruba culture
- Visit a **local market**, such as the Dugbe Market, to see the variety of goods and food on offer
- Learn about **Yoruba mythology** and the stories behind the gods and goddesses

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Agodi Gardens**, a peaceful park with a lake and walking trails
- Visit the **Ibadan Zoo**, home to a variety of animals, including lions, monkeys, and snakes
- Hike up **Oke-Ado Mountain** for stunning views of the city
- Take a **boat ride** on the **Ogunpa River** to see the city from a different perspective
- Visit the **University of Ibadan Botanical Garden**, a peaceful oasis in the heart of the city

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum of Nigeria** to learn about Nigerian history and culture
- Explore the **Ibadan National Museum of Natural History** to see exhibits on Nigeria's natural history
- Attend a **traditional Yoruba festival**, such as the Osun Osogbo Festival
- Take a **cooking class** and learn how to make traditional Yoruba dishes
- Visit the **Cocoa House**, a historic building that showcases Nigeria's cocoa industry
- Attend a **theater performance** at the **University of Ibadan Theater**

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Ibadan Arts Council**, a hub for local artists and artisans
- Explore the **Ibadan City Mall**, a modern shopping center with a variety of restaurants and shops
- Take a **walking tour** of the city to see the historic buildings and landmarks
- Visit the **Ibadan Golf Club**, a scenic golf course with stunning views of the city
- Attend a **local music event**, such as a concert or festival, to experience the city's vibrant music scene

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Lagos**, the former capital city of Nigeria, to see the National Museum and the Eko Atlantic City (120 km, 2 hours)
- Visit **Abeokuta**, the capital city of Ogun State, to see the Olumo Rock and the National Museum (100 km, 1.5 hours)
- Explore **Oshogbo**, a city known for its natural springs and traditional festivals (100 km, 1.5 hours)
- Take a day trip to **Ifẹ**, a city known for its ancient bronze artifacts and traditional festivals (200 km, 3 hours)
- Visit **Ijebu-Ode**, a city known for its historic buildings and traditional festivals (150 km, 2.5 hours)
