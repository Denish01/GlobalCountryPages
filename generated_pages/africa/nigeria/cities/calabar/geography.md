Nigeria, the parent country of Calabar, is bordered by several countries, including Benin to the west, Cameroon to the southeast, Chad to the northeast, and Niger to the north. The Gulf of Guinea, part of the Atlantic Ocean, lies to the south of Calabar. The city's strategic location has made it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region.

The location of Calabar has played a significant role in shaping its history and culture.
