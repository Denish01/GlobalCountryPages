# Enugu Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Enugu is generally considered a safe city to visit, but as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of the local conditions and take necessary precautions to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Enugu, a city in southeastern Nigeria, has a relatively low crime rate compared to other major cities in the country. However, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, scams, and theft can still occur, especially in crowded areas and markets. Visitors should be cautious when walking around the city, especially at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables. The city has a strong police presence, and the authorities are generally responsive to reports of crime.

Despite the overall safety of Enugu, there are some areas that are best avoided, particularly at night. The city's outskirts and some of the more rural areas can be prone to crime and banditry, and visitors should exercise caution when traveling to these areas. Additionally, the city's traffic can be chaotic, and visitors should be careful when crossing the road or using public transportation. With some basic precautions and an awareness of the local conditions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Enugu.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Scams targeting foreigners, particularly those involving fake investment schemes or requests for money transfers
- Pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and markets
- Traffic accidents and congestion, particularly when using public transportation
- Petty crimes such as burglary and robbery, particularly in areas with low security
- **Advance fee fraud**, where scammers promise large sums of money in exchange for an upfront payment
- **Fake police officers**, who may stop and search visitors or demand bribes
- **Overcharging**, particularly by taxi drivers and market vendors
- **Food and waterborne illnesses**, particularly when eating from street vendors or consuming untreated water

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Visitors should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations for **Hepatitis A**, **Typhoid**, and **Yellow Fever**
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water
- **Medical Facilities:** Enugu has some good quality medical facilities, including the **University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital**, but visitors may still need to travel to Lagos or Abuja for more specialized care
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but visitors should be cautious when purchasing medication from street vendors or unlicensed pharmacies

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a valid form of identification and keep a copy of your passport and other important documents in a safe place
- Be cautious when using public transportation, and always use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables, and use a hotel safe or secure bag to store your belongings
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel's safe or a secure bag to store your valuables
- Avoid walking alone at night, and use a reputable taxi service or ride-sharing app to get around
- Be aware of your surroundings, and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas and markets
- Learn some basic **Nigerian Pidgin** phrases, such as "hello" (kedu) and "thank you" (imaolu), to help you communicate with locals

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Solo female travelers should be cautious when walking alone at night, and use a reputable taxi service or ride-sharing app to get around
- Women should dress modestly, particularly when visiting mosques or churches, and avoid wearing revealing clothing
- Solo travelers should stay in touch with family and friends back home, and keep them informed of their itinerary and whereabouts
- Women should be cautious when interacting with strangers, and avoid accepting rides or invitations from unknown individuals
