Getting There

Enugu is a city located in the southeastern part of Nigeria, and it can be accessed through Akanu Ibiam International Airport, which receives flights from major Nigerian cities such as Lagos and Abuja. The city is also connected to other parts of the country by road, with several bus companies operating daily services from Enugu to other cities. For international travelers, the closest major airport is the Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport in Abuja, which is about a 4-hour drive from Enugu.

To enter Enugu overland, you can use the Enugu-Port Harcourt expressway, which connects the city to other major cities in southern Nigeria. There are also several border crossings with neighboring countries, including Cameroon and Benin, although these may not be the most convenient or efficient way to enter the city. It's worth noting that road travel in Nigeria can be challenging due to poor road conditions and security concerns, so it's essential to plan carefully and stay informed about local conditions before embarking on a journey.

Domestic Flights

Domestic flights are a convenient way to get to Enugu from other parts of Nigeria, with several airlines operating daily services from major cities such as Lagos and Abuja. The main domestic airlines in Nigeria include Arik Air, Air Peace, and Dana Air, which offer a range of flights to Enugu and other destinations across the country. To book a domestic flight, you can visit the airline's website or use a local travel agency, and prices start from around $50 for a one-way ticket, depending on the airline and time of booking.

It's worth noting that domestic flights in Nigeria can be prone to delays and cancellations, so it's essential to stay flexible and plan for contingencies when booking a flight. Additionally, some airlines may have specific requirements or restrictions for passengers, such as check-in times or baggage limits, so it's a good idea to check with the airline before your flight.

Trains & Rail

Nigeria's rail network is still in the process of development, and while there are some train services available, they are not always reliable or efficient. The Nigerian Railway Corporation operates a few train routes, including a service from Enugu to Port Harcourt, but these trains are often delayed or cancelled, and the journey can be slow and uncomfortable. If you do decide to take the train, it's a good idea to book in advance and plan for contingencies, as the train schedule can be unpredictable.

Buses & Coaches

Bus travel is a popular way to get around in Nigeria, with several companies operating daily services from Enugu to other cities. Some of the main bus companies in Nigeria include ABC Transport, Young Shall Grow, and Ifesinachi, which offer a range of routes and prices to suit different budgets and preferences. The quality of bus services can vary, but some companies offer comfortable and reliable services, with prices starting from around $10 for a one-way ticket.

Driving

  • To drive in Nigeria, you will need to obtain a local driver's license or use an international driver's license, which is valid for up to 3 months.
  • Road conditions in Nigeria can be poor, with many roads in disrepair and prone to traffic congestion.
  • Fuel costs in Nigeria can be high, with prices starting from around $0.50 per liter.
  • Car rental companies are available in Enugu, but prices can be high, and the quality of vehicles may vary.
  • When driving in Nigeria, it's essential to be aware of local hazards such as potholes, traffic congestion, and security risks, and to plan your journey carefully to avoid these hazards.
  • Additionally, it's a good idea to use a reputable car rental company and to check the condition of the vehicle before renting it, to ensure that you have a safe and reliable ride.

Transport Tips

  • When using taxis or ride-hailing services, it's a good idea to agree on the price before you start your journey, to avoid any disputes or scams.
  • Be aware of local scams and fraudsters, who may target travelers and tourists, and take steps to protect yourself and your belongings.
  • Use reputable bus companies and taxi services, and avoid traveling alone at night, to minimize your risk of crime or accidents.
  • Download local transportation apps such as Bolt or Traffik, which can help you navigate the city and find the best transportation options.
  • Keep your valuables secure and be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or public transportation.
  • Be prepared for traffic congestion and delays, and plan your journey accordingly to avoid any inconvenience or stress.
  • Use cash or mobile payments to pay for transportation, as credit card facilities may not always be available.
  • Stay informed about local events and festivals, which may affect transportation and traffic in the city, and plan your journey accordingly to avoid any disruptions.
