# Getting Around Port Harcourt — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Port Harcourt, you can use a combination of taxis, ride-hailing apps, buses, and private cars, as the city has a relatively well-developed transportation network.

[FACTBOX]
Main International Airport(s): Port Harcourt International Airport (PHC)
Domestic Air Network: moderate
Rail Network: none
Drives On: right
Road Quality: moderate
Ride-Hailing Apps: Bolt, local alternatives
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Port Harcourt International Airport (PHC) is the main entry point for international travelers, with direct flights to major hubs like Lagos, Abuja, and Amsterdam. The airport is located about 30 kilometers from the city center, and you can get to your destination using taxis or ride-hailing apps. If you're traveling overland, you can enter Port Harcourt from neighboring cities like Lagos, Abuja, or Benin City via the East-West Road or the Port Harcourt-Aba Road.

For those traveling from within Nigeria, there are several overland entry points, including the popular Aba Road and the Ikwerre Road. You can use buses or private cars to get to Port Harcourt from these entry points. The journey can take several hours, depending on the traffic and road conditions. It's essential to plan your trip in advance and check the latest travel advisories before embarking on your journey.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Port Harcourt has a moderate domestic air network, with flights to major cities like Lagos, Abuja, and Kano. Domestic airlines like Air Peace, Arik Air, and Dana Air operate from Port Harcourt International Airport, offering several flights per day to these destinations. You can book your flights online or through a travel agent, and approximate costs range from $50 to $200, depending on the airline and time of booking. It's recommended to book your flights in advance to get the best deals.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Port Harcourt does not have a rail network, so train travel is not an option. However, there are plans to develop a rail system in the future, which may improve transportation options in the city. For now, travelers rely on buses, taxis, and private cars to get around.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several intercity bus companies operate in Port Harcourt, offering services to neighboring cities like Lagos, Abuja, and Benin City. Companies like ABC Transport, Ifesinachi Transport, and Rivers Transport offer daily services, with prices ranging from $10 to $50, depending on the destination and type of service. The quality of the buses varies, but most companies offer comfortable and safe transportation options.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Port Harcourt, you need a valid **driver's license**, which can be obtained from the Federal Road Safety Commission.
- Road conditions in Port Harcourt are moderate, with some **potholes** and **traffic congestion**.
- Fuel costs are relatively high, with **premium gasoline** costing around $0.80 per liter.
- Car rental companies like **Hertz** and **Avis** operate in Port Harcourt, offering a range of vehicles for rent.
- When driving in Port Harcourt, be aware of **traffic hazards** like potholes, pedestrians, and **okada** (motorcycle) riders.
- It's essential to have a **valid vehicle insurance** and **roadworthiness certificate** to avoid fines and penalties.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **scams** and **touts** at the airport and bus stations, and always use authorized transportation services.
- Use **cash** or **mobile payment** options like **OPay** or **Paystack** to pay for transportation services.
- Download **ride-hailing apps** like **Bolt** or **Uber** to get around the city.
- Always **negotiate** the price before taking a taxi or tricycle.
- Be aware of **traffic rules** and **regulations**, and always wear a **seatbelt** when driving or riding in a vehicle.
- Avoid traveling at **night** or during **peak hours** to avoid traffic congestion and **robbery**.
- Use **reputable** transportation companies and **licensed** drivers to ensure your safety.
