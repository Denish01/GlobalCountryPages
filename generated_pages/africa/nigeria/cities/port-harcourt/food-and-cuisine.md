# Port Harcourt Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

To experience the best of Port Harcourt's cuisine, you should try dishes like Afang Soup, Edikaikong Soup, and Puff-Puff, which showcase the city's rich cultural heritage and diverse culinary influences.

Meal Times: 7-10 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 6-9 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: Port Harcourt's cuisine is not strictly halal or kosher, but vegetarian-friendly options are available

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Port Harcourt's cuisine is a unique blend of traditional Nigerian dishes, influenced by the city's cultural diversity and its location in the Niger Delta region. The city's food culture is characterized by the use of fresh seafood, locally-grown produce, and traditional spices. The cuisine is also shaped by the city's history of trade and cultural exchange with other parts of Nigeria and West Africa. As a result, Port Harcourt's cuisine offers a wide range of flavors and dishes, from spicy soups to savory stews and fragrant jollof rice.

The key influences on Port Harcourt's cuisine include the indigenous Kalabari and Ikwerre peoples, as well as the city's history of trade and cultural exchange with other parts of Nigeria and West Africa. The city's cuisine is also shaped by its location in the Niger Delta region, which provides an abundance of fresh seafood and other aquatic resources. Regional variations in the cuisine are also evident, with different neighborhoods and communities having their own unique dishes and cooking styles.

Port Harcourt's food culture is also known for its vibrant markets and street food scene, which offers a wide range of delicious and affordable options. From spicy suya and fragrant jollof rice to fresh fruits and vegetables, the city's markets and street food stalls are a great place to experience the local cuisine. Whether you're looking for a quick snack or a hearty meal, Port Harcourt's food culture has something to offer.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- It is customary to greet the host or server with a handshake or a bow before taking a seat
- It is considered polite to wash your hands before eating
- It is customary to eat with your right hand, as the left hand is considered unclean
- It is considered impolite to leave the table until everyone has finished eating
- Tipping is not mandatory, but it is appreciated for good service
- Reservations are not always necessary, but they are recommended for large groups or special occasions
