# Port Harcourt Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Port Harcourt is generally considered a high-risk destination due to its history of crime, kidnappings, and social unrest, but with proper precautions and knowledge, visitors can minimize their risks and have a safe trip.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Port Harcourt, a major city in Nigeria, poses significant safety challenges to visitors. The city has experienced a high rate of crime, including armed robbery, kidnappings, and carjacking. Additionally, social unrest, protests, and demonstrations can occur without warning, making it essential to stay informed about local conditions. While the Nigerian government has taken steps to improve security, the risk of crime and violence remains high, and visitors should exercise extreme caution when traveling to Port Harcourt.

Despite the safety concerns, many visitors have successfully traveled to Port Harcourt without incident. To minimize risks, it is crucial to research and understand the local environment, stay informed about current events, and take necessary precautions to ensure personal safety. This includes being aware of one's surroundings, avoiding traveling alone at night, and using reputable transportation services. By taking a proactive and informed approach to safety, visitors can reduce their risk of encountering problems and have a successful trip to Port Harcourt.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Armed robbery and carjacking are common, especially at night or in isolated areas
- Kidnappings for ransom can occur, especially against foreigners or high-profile individuals
- Scams and confidence tricks are prevalent, including fake investment schemes and advance fee fraud
- Pickpocketing and theft are common in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations
- Traffic accidents are frequent due to poor road conditions and reckless driving
- **Water-borne illnesses** can be a risk due to poor sanitation and water quality
- **Malaria** and other mosquito-borne diseases are prevalent in Port Harcourt, take necessary precautions

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid vaccinations are recommended
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, bottled or filtered water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Port Harcourt has some good-quality medical facilities, but they can be limited and may require payment upfront
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are available, but the quality and availability of medications can vary

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Research and understand the local environment and current events
- Stay informed about local conditions through government advisories and reputable sources
- Use reputable transportation services, such as licensed taxis or ride-sharing services
- Avoid traveling alone at night or in isolated areas
- Keep valuables secure and be mindful of pickpocketing and scams
- **Stay healthy** by taking necessary precautions against water-borne illnesses and mosquito-borne diseases
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on local conditions

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Solo female travelers may face additional challenges, including harassment and unwanted attention
- Dress modestly and be mindful of local customs and traditions
- Avoid traveling alone at night or in isolated areas, use reputable transportation services instead
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary and plans
