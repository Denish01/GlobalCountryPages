[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Osun Osogbo Sacred Grove**, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a sacred forest and home to numerous shrines and artifacts of the Yoruba people.
- The **Durbar Festival** is a traditional festival celebrated in Kano, showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage and featuring horse riding, music, and dance.
- The **Benin City National Museum** is a museum showcasing the art and history of the Benin Empire, with a collection of over 1,000 artifacts.
- The **Hausa-Fulani** people have a rich tradition of **woodcarving** and **leatherwork**, with intricate designs and patterns.
- The **Yoruba** people have a strong tradition of **textile production**, with colorful and intricate **Adire** and **Aso Oke** fabrics.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Chinua Achebe**, a Nigerian novelist and poet, is widely regarded as one of the most influential African writers of the 20th century, with his novel **Things Fall Apart** being a classic of modern African literature.
- **Wole Soyinka**, a Nigerian playwright and poet, was awarded the **Nobel Prize in Literature** in 1986, recognizing his contributions to literature and culture.
- The Nigerian **diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Nigerians holding key positions in international organizations and companies.
- **Fela Kuti**, a Nigerian musician and activist, is widely regarded as one of the most influential musicians of the 20th century, with his music and message continuing to inspire people around the world.
