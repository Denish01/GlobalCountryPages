How Nigeria Is Divided

Nigeria is a federal republic divided into 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory, Abuja. The country is further divided into 774 local government areas, which are the third-tier administrative divisions. The 36 states are grouped into six geopolitical zones: North Central, North East, North West, South East, South South, and South West. Each state has its own government and is responsible for providing certain services such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure.

The administrative structure of Nigeria is designed to promote decentralization and regional development. The federal government is responsible for matters such as national defense, foreign policy, and interstate commerce, while the state governments have jurisdiction over matters such as education, healthcare, and local infrastructure. The local government areas are responsible for providing basic services such as sanitation, water supply, and primary education.

Major Cities

Lagos (13.4M)

Role: Economic hub

Known For: Lagos is a bustling metropolis and the economic center of Nigeria, known for its vibrant culture, beautiful beaches, and bustling markets. The city is home to many industries, including finance, technology, and entertainment.

Best For: Business travelers, entrepreneurs, and culture enthusiasts

Abuja (3.2M)

Role: Capital

Known For: Abuja is a planned city and the capital of Nigeria, known for its modern architecture, beautiful parks, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is home to many government institutions, embassies, and international organizations.

Best For: Diplomats, government officials, and international visitors

Kano (3.6M)

Role: Cultural center

Known For: Kano is a historic city and a major cultural center in northern Nigeria, known for its ancient walls, bustling markets, and traditional crafts. The city is home to many museums, galleries, and cultural institutions.

Best For: History buffs, cultural enthusiasts, and adventure seekers

Ibadan (3.1M)

Role: Economic hub

Known For: Ibadan is a major city in southwestern Nigeria, known for its vibrant markets, beautiful parks, and rich cultural heritage. The city is home to many industries, including agriculture, manufacturing, and technology.

Best For: Business travelers, entrepreneurs, and students

Port Harcourt (2.3M)

Role: Port city

Known For: Port Harcourt is a major port city in southern Nigeria, known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and rich oil and gas reserves. The city is home to many industries, including energy, manufacturing, and logistics.

Best For: Oil and gas professionals, logistics experts, and beach lovers

Regional Differences

  • The north and south of Nigeria have distinct cultural and linguistic differences, with the north being predominantly Muslim and the south being predominantly Christian.
  • The eastern and western regions have different economic profiles, with the east being more industrialized and the west being more agricultural.
  • The northern regions are generally drier and hotter than the southern regions, which are more humid and tropical.
  • The major cities in Nigeria have distinct characters and identities, with Lagos being the economic hub, Abuja being the capital, and Kano being the cultural center.

Getting Around

Nigeria has a relatively well-developed transportation network, with many domestic flights, trains, and buses connecting the major cities. The country has a total of 32 airports, with the Murtala Muhammed International Airport in Lagos being the busiest. The Nigerian Railway Corporation operates a network of trains that connect the major cities, including Lagos, Abuja, and Kano. The roads in Nigeria are generally in good condition, but driving can be challenging due to traffic congestion and poor road discipline. Many Nigerians rely on buses and taxis for transportation, but there are also many private car owners and ride-hailing services.
