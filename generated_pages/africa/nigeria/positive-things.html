Cultural Treasures

  • The Osun Osogbo Sacred Grove, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a sacred forest and home to numerous shrines and artifacts of the Yoruba people.
  • The Durbar Festival is a traditional festival celebrated in Kano, showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage and featuring horse riding, music, and dance.
  • The Benin City National Museum is a museum showcasing the art and history of the Benin Empire, with a collection of over 1,000 artifacts.
  • The Hausa-Fulani people have a rich tradition of woodcarving and leatherwork, with intricate designs and patterns.
  • The Yoruba people have a strong tradition of textile production, with colorful and intricate Adire and Aso Oke fabrics.

People & Global Impact

  • Chinua Achebe, a Nigerian novelist and poet, is widely regarded as one of the most influential African writers of the 20th century, with his novel Things Fall Apart being a classic of modern African literature.
  • Wole Soyinka, a Nigerian playwright and poet, was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1986, recognizing his contributions to literature and culture.
  • The Nigerian diaspora has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Nigerians holding key positions in international organizations and companies.
  • Fela Kuti, a Nigerian musician and activist, is widely regarded as one of the most influential musicians of the 20th century, with his music and message continuing to inspire people around the world.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Nigerian people is often praised by visitors, with many commenting on the warm and welcoming nature of the locals.
  • The Jollof rice is a popular dish in Nigeria, with many visitors enjoying the flavorful and spicy taste of this traditional West African dish.
  • The Osun Osogbo Sacred Grove is a popular tourist destination, with many visitors drawn to the natural beauty and spiritual significance of the site.
  • The National Museum in Lagos is a must-visit for anyone interested in Nigerian history and culture, with a collection of over 10,000 artifacts and exhibits.
  • The Eko Hotel and Suites in Lagos is a popular destination for business and leisure travelers, with its modern amenities and stunning views of the city.
  • The Obudu Plateau is a scenic destination in eastern Nigeria, with its rolling hills and natural beauty making it a popular spot for hiking and relaxation.

Hidden Gems

  • The Kainji National Park is a lesser-known national park in Nigeria, with its stunning natural beauty and diverse wildlife making it a hidden gem for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
  • The Tarkwa Bay Beach in Lagos is a secluded and picturesque beach, with its calm waters and stunning views making it a popular spot for relaxation and recreation.
  • The Nike Art Gallery in Lagos is a hidden gem for art lovers, with its collection of over 8,000 artworks and exhibits showcasing the best of Nigerian and African art.
  • The Gurara Waterfalls in Niger State is a stunning natural wonder, with its picturesque scenery and tranquil atmosphere making it a must-visit for anyone interested in exploring Nigeria's natural beauty.
