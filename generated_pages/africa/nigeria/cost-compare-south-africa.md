[SECTION] Overview: Nigeria vs South Africa [/SECTION]
Nigeria and South Africa are two of the largest economies in Africa, but they have distinct economic contexts that affect their cost of living. Nigeria has a large and growing population, with a relatively low GDP per capita, which contributes to its lower cost of living. In contrast, South Africa has a more developed economy, with a higher GDP per capita and a more diverse range of industries, which drives up its cost of living. Overall, Nigeria is generally cheaper than South Africa, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation.

The cost of living difference between Nigeria and South Africa is significant, with Nigeria being approximately 30-40% cheaper. This makes Nigeria an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees who are looking for a affordable place to live. However, it's worth noting that the cost of living in Nigeria can vary greatly depending on the location, with cities like Lagos and Abuja being more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. In South Africa, the cost of living is more consistent across the country, but it's still possible to find affordable options in smaller towns and cities.
