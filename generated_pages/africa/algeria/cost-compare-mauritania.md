[SECTION] Overview: Algeria vs Mauritania [/SECTION]
Algeria and Mauritania are two neighboring countries in North Africa with distinct economic profiles. Algeria has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on oil and gas production, while Mauritania's economy is primarily driven by mining and fishing. The cost of living in Algeria is generally lower than in Mauritania, with prices for food, housing, and transportation being relatively cheaper. However, the cost of living in Mauritania can vary greatly depending on the location, with the capital city of Nouakchott being more expensive than other parts of the country.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Algeria has a more developed infrastructure and a larger economy, which contributes to its lower prices. On the other hand, Mauritania's economy is smaller and more vulnerable to external shocks, which can lead to higher prices and a more unpredictable cost of living. Overall, Algeria is generally around 15-20% cheaper than Mauritania, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a affordable place to live in North Africa.
