[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Algeria is a complex and diverse country with varying safety conditions. While the capital city of Algiers and other major tourist areas are generally considered safe, there are areas of concern, particularly in the eastern and southern regions near the borders with Mali and Libya. The risk of petty crime, such as pickpocketing and scams, is moderate, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The risk of violent crime is also moderate, with occasional reports of armed robbery and terrorism. However, the Algerian government has taken steps to improve security, and the country has experienced a significant decrease in terrorist attacks in recent years.

Despite the challenges, Algeria is a rewarding destination for travelers who are willing to take necessary precautions. The country has a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a warm and welcoming population. With proper planning, research, and awareness of potential risks, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Algeria. It is essential to stay informed about local conditions, respect local customs and traditions, and take necessary precautions to minimize risks.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Petty theft**: Pickpocketing and bag snatching are common in crowded areas and public transportation.
- **Scams**: Visitors may be targeted by scammers, particularly in tourist areas, with false promises of guided tours or souvenirs.
- **Road accidents**: Algeria has a high rate of road accidents, visitors should be cautious when driving or crossing the road.
- **Terrorism**: Although rare, terrorist attacks can occur, visitors should stay informed about local conditions and avoid areas of conflict.
- **Banditry**: Visitors should be cautious when traveling in remote areas, particularly in the eastern and southern regions.
- **ATM scams**: Visitors should be cautious when using ATMs, as scams and credit card skimming are common.
- **Overcharging**: Visitors may be overcharged for goods and services, particularly in tourist areas.
- **Protests and demonstrations**: Visitors should avoid areas of protests and demonstrations, as they can turn violent.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: Visitors should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations, including hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is not safe to drink, visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities**: Medical facilities are generally good in major cities, but limited in rural areas. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical evacuation.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacies are widely available, but visitors may need to show a prescription for certain medications.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas and public transportation.
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt.
- Avoid traveling alone in remote areas, particularly at night.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps.
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches.
- Stay informed about local conditions and follow local advice.
- Learn some basic Arabic phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you".
- Respect local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting mosques or other religious sites.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- **Dress modestly**: Women should dress conservatively, covering their shoulders and knees, particularly when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
- **Avoid traveling alone at night**: Solo travelers, particularly women, should avoid traveling alone at night and use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps.
- **Be prepared for attention**: Women may receive unwanted attention from men, and should be prepared to politely but firmly decline any advances.
- **Stay in touch with family and friends**: Solo travelers should stay in touch with family and friends back home, and consider registering with their government's travel advisory program.
