How Algeria Is Divided

Algeria is divided into 58 provinces, also known as wilayas, which are further divided into 547 districts and 1,541 municipalities. The country's administrative structure is based on a centralized system, with the capital city of Algiers serving as the seat of government. Each province has its own governor and council, which are responsible for managing local affairs and implementing national policies.

The provinces of Algeria are grouped into several regions, including the coastal region, the Tell Atlas region, and the Sahara desert region. Each region has its own unique character and identity, shaped by its history, culture, and geography. The coastal region, for example, is home to many of the country's major cities, including Algiers, Oran, and Annaba, and is known for its beautiful beaches and vibrant cultural scene. In contrast, the Sahara desert region is a vast and sparsely populated area, known for its extreme climate and natural resources.

Major Cities

Algiers (3.4M)

Role: capital, economic hub, cultural center

Known For: Algiers is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city, known for its stunning architecture, beautiful beaches, and rich cultural heritage. The city is home to many museums, galleries, and cultural institutions, and hosts a number of festivals and events throughout the year.

Best For: visitors interested in history, culture, and urban exploration

Oran (800,000)

Role: economic hub, port city

Known For: Oran is a major commercial center and port city, known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and rich cultural heritage. The city is home to many historic landmarks, including the Kasbah and the Great Mosque.

Best For: visitors interested in commerce, industry, and urban entertainment

Constantine (500,000)

Regional Differences

  • The coastal region is more urbanized and industrialized, with a strong focus on commerce and tourism.
  • The Sahara desert region is more sparsely populated and focused on oil and gas production, mining, and other extractive industries.
  • The Tell Atlas region is known for its fertile valleys and is a major center for agriculture and forestry.
  • The Kabylie region is home to a distinct Berber culture and is known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Getting Around

Algeria has a well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis available. The country's main airport is Houari Boumedienne Airport in Algiers, which offers flights to major cities in Europe, Africa, and the Middle East. The train network is also well-developed, with lines connecting major cities such as Algiers, Oran, and Constantine. Buses and taxis are widely available, and offer a convenient and affordable way to get around. Driving conditions can be challenging, especially in rural areas, but the country's main roads are generally well-maintained.
