[SECTION] Overview: Algeria vs Mali [/SECTION]
Algeria and Mali are two neighboring countries in North Africa with distinct economic profiles. Algeria has a more diversified economy, with a strong oil and gas sector, while Mali's economy is primarily driven by agriculture and mining. The cost of living in Algeria is generally lower than in Mali, mainly due to the availability of subsidized housing, food, and transportation. Additionally, Algeria's larger economy and more developed infrastructure contribute to lower prices for goods and services.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Algeria's economy is more industrialized, with a larger manufacturing sector, which leads to lower production costs and subsequently lower prices for consumers. In contrast, Mali's economy is more reliant on imports, which can drive up prices due to transportation costs and tariffs. Overall, Algeria is around 20-30% cheaper than Mali, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle.
