[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Algeria has made significant progress in developing its infrastructure over the past few decades, with a focus on improving the quality of life for its citizens and supporting economic growth. The country has invested heavily in its **energy sector**, with a particular emphasis on developing its natural gas and oil resources. However, despite these efforts, Algeria still faces significant challenges in terms of providing access to basic services such as **electricity**, **water**, and **sanitation**, particularly in rural areas. The government has launched several initiatives aimed at addressing these disparities and improving the overall quality of infrastructure in the country.

Recent investments in Algeria's infrastructure have focused on upgrading its **transport networks**, including the construction of new highways and the expansion of its **rail network**. The country has also made significant progress in developing its **telecommunications sector**, with a focus on increasing **internet penetration** and improving mobile coverage. However, despite these efforts, Algeria's infrastructure still lags behind that of many other countries in the region, and the government faces significant challenges in terms of maintaining and upgrading its existing systems.

The urban-rural gap in terms of infrastructure development is a significant challenge facing Algeria, with many rural areas lacking access to basic services such as **electricity**, **water**, and **sanitation**. The government has launched several initiatives aimed at addressing these disparities, including the construction of new roads and the expansion of its **energy** and **water** networks. However, despite these efforts, much work remains to be done to ensure that all citizens have access to the services they need to thrive.
