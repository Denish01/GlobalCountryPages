[SECTION] Overview: Algeria vs Niger [/SECTION]
Algeria and Niger are two neighboring countries in North Africa with distinct economic profiles. Algeria has a relatively high GDP per capita and a diverse economy driven by oil and gas exports, while Niger is one of the poorest countries in the world, with a largely agrarian economy. The cost of living in Algeria is generally lower than in Niger, due to a combination of factors including lower housing costs, cheaper food, and lower transportation expenses. Overall, Algeria offers a more affordable lifestyle for expats, students, and families, with a cost of living that is around 20-30% lower than in Niger.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Algeria's economy is more diversified, with a strong manufacturing sector and a growing services sector, which has helped to keep prices relatively low. In contrast, Niger's economy is heavily reliant on agriculture, which can lead to price volatility and higher costs for basic necessities. Additionally, Algeria's larger size and more developed infrastructure have helped to reduce transportation costs and increase access to affordable goods and services.
