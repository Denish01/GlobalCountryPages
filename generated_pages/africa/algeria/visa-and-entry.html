Visa Policy Overview

Algeria has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Citizens of around 10 countries, including Malaysia, Morocco, and Tunisia, can enter Algeria visa-free for up to 90 days. However, most nationalities require a visa to enter the country. The Algerian government has introduced an e-visa system to simplify the application process for eligible nationalities.

The visa policy is designed to balance the country's security concerns with the need to promote tourism and economic development. Visitors can apply for a visa at an Algerian embassy or consulate, or through the e-visa portal. The processing time and requirements vary depending on the type of visa and nationality. It is essential to check the official government website or consult with the embassy or consulate for the latest information on visa requirements and application procedures.

Tourists: Tourists can apply for a single-entry or multiple-entry visa, which is usually valid for 90 days. The typical stay is 30-60 days, but this can be extended for an additional 30-60 days. To extend their stay, tourists must apply at the local police station or immigration office.
Business Travelers: Business travelers require an invitation letter from an Algerian company, which must be approved by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. They can apply for a business visa, which is usually valid for 90 days. The requirements and process differ from those for tourists, and business travelers may need to provide additional documentation, such as a letter from their employer and proof of financial resources.
Students: Students must provide proof of enrollment at an Algerian university and apply for a student visa. The visa is usually valid for the duration of their studies, and students must register with the local authorities within 30 days of arrival.
Workers: Workers require a sponsorship letter from their Algerian employer and must apply for a work visa. The process involves obtaining a work permit from the Ministry of Labor and applying for a residence visa.
