[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Botswana is a landlocked country located in southern Africa, situated between the **Kalihari Desert** and the **Okavango Delta**. It shares borders with four countries: South Africa to the south and southeast, Namibia to the west and north, Zimbabwe to the northeast, and Zambia to the north. Botswana is a significant country in terms of its geography, with a diverse range of landscapes and ecosystems. The country's location in the center of southern Africa makes it a crucial hub for trade and tourism in the region.

The country's borders are defined by a combination of natural and artificial boundaries. The Molopo River forms part of the border with South Africa, while the Nossob River forms part of the border with Namibia. The border with Zimbabwe is defined by a combination of rivers and artificial boundaries, while the border with Zambia is relatively short and defined by a series of hills and valleys. Botswana's location in the heart of southern Africa makes it an ideal destination for tourists and travelers looking to experience the region's unique culture, history, and natural beauty.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Botswana's terrain is characterized by a mix of **deserts**, **grasslands**, and **wetlands**. The country is dominated by the **Kalihari Desert**, which covers much of the central and western regions. The Kalihari is a vast expanse of sand dunes, grasslands, and thorny scrub, and is home to a wide range of wildlife, including **lions**, **leopards**, and **cheetahs**. In contrast, the **Okavango Delta** is a lush and fertile region, characterized by winding waterways, **islands**, and **lagoons**. The delta is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to a staggering array of wildlife, including **elephants**, **hippos**, and **crocodiles**.

The country's landscape is also defined by a number of significant **rivers**, including the **Chobe River**, the **Limpopo River**, and the **Shashe River**. These rivers play a crucial role in supporting the country's ecosystem, and provide a source of water for both humans and animals. In addition to its rivers, Botswana is also home to a number of **hills** and **mountains**, including the **Tsodilo Hills** and the **Tuli Block**. These hills and mountains provide a habitat for a wide range of flora and fauna, and offer stunning views and scenery for tourists and travelers.

The **Makgadikgadi Pans** are another notable feature of Botswana's landscape. These vast, flat expanses of salt and clay are the remains of an ancient lake, and are home to a wide range of wildlife, including **zebras**, **wildebeest**, and **giraffes**. The pans are also known for their unique **baobab trees**, which are found nowhere else in the world. Overall, Botswana's terrain and landscape are incredibly diverse, and offer a wide range of opportunities for outdoor activities, including **safari**, **hiking**, and **birdwatching**.

[SECTION] Natural Highlights [/SECTION]
- **Chobe National Park**: A world-famous park known for its large herds of elephants and buffaloes.
- **Okavango Delta**: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most biologically diverse places on the planet.
- **Makgadikgadi Pans**: A vast, flat expanse of salt and clay that is home to a wide range of wildlife.
- **Tsodilo Hills**: A range of hills that are home to over 4,000 ancient rock paintings.
- **Moremi Game Reserve**: A protected area that is known for its diverse wildlife and beautiful scenery.
- **Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park**: A large park that spans the border with South Africa, and is home to a wide range of wildlife, including lions, leopards, and cheetahs.
