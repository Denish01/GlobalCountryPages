# Education Costs in Botswana

The cost of education in Botswana varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Botswana is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the largest. Public schools are generally considered to be of good quality, but may lack resources and infrastructure compared to private schools. The language of instruction is English, with Setswana being taught as a secondary language. Compulsory education in Botswana lasts for 10 years, from primary school to junior secondary school. International schools are also available, offering a range of curricula including British, American, and International Baccalaureate.

The quality of education in Botswana is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement. However, there are some challenges facing the education system, including a shortage of qualified teachers and limited resources in rural areas. Despite these challenges, Botswana has made significant progress in increasing access to education, with a high literacy rate and a growing number of students pursuing higher education. Private schools and international schools offer a range of options for students, including boarding facilities and extracurricular activities.
