[SECTION] Overview: Botswana vs Zimbabwe [/SECTION]
Botswana and Zimbabwe are two neighboring countries in Southern Africa with distinct economic profiles. Botswana has a relatively high GDP per capita, driven by its rich natural resources, including diamonds, copper, and nickel. In contrast, Zimbabwe has faced significant economic challenges, including hyperinflation and a decline in its manufacturing sector. As a result, the cost of living in Botswana is generally higher than in Zimbabwe. However, Zimbabwe's economy has been showing signs of recovery in recent years, which may impact the cost of living in the country.

The overall cost of living in Botswana is around 20-30% higher than in Zimbabwe, making Zimbabwe a more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees. However, the cost of living in Botswana can vary depending on the location, with cities like Gaborone and Maun tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. In Zimbabwe, the cost of living in cities like Harare and Bulawayo is relatively high, while smaller towns and rural areas are more affordable.
