Visa Policy Overview

Botswana has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens from over 80 countries to enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival. The country aims to balance tourism and economic development with security and immigration control. Citizens from eligible countries can stay in Botswana for up to 90 days, depending on their nationality. The visa policy is designed to facilitate travel and trade while ensuring the safety and security of both citizens and visitors.

The visa policy is divided into several categories, including visa-free, visa on arrival, and visa required. Citizens from countries such as the United States, the United Kingdom, and Canada can enter Botswana visa-free, while citizens from countries such as India and China require a visa. The visa requirements and regulations can change, so it is essential to check with the official embassy or consulate of Botswana for the latest information.

Tourists: Tourists can enter Botswana visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival, depending on their nationality. The typical stay for tourists is 90 days, and extensions can be applied for at the immigration office. Tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may require a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must provide an invitation letter from a registered company in Botswana, proof of business registration, and other supporting documents. Business travelers can stay in Botswana for up to 90 days, and extensions can be applied for.
Students: Students who wish to study in Botswana must apply for a student visa. They must provide proof of enrollment at a recognized institution, a valid passport, and sufficient funds for their stay. The student visa is usually valid for the duration of the course.
Workers: Workers who wish to work in Botswana must apply for a work visa. They must have a job offer from a registered company in Botswana, a valid passport, and sufficient qualifications and experience for the job. The work visa is usually valid for the duration of the employment contract.

The main entry points to Botswana are Sir Seretse Khama International Airport in Gaborone, Kasane Airport, and Maun Airport. Land borders are also available, including the border with South Africa at Ramatlabama and the border with Namibia at Mamuno. Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration desk, where they will be required to present their travel documents and complete a declaration form. The immigration officer will then stamp the passport and issue a visa (if required). Visitors must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country, including gifts, souvenirs, and food items.
The entry procedures in Botswana are generally straightforward, but visitors must ensure they have all the required documents and follow the correct procedures to avoid any issues. The immigration officers are usually helpful and efficient, but it is essential to be patient and cooperative during the entry process.
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not having proof of onward travel
- Not having sufficient funds for the stay
- Not declaring goods brought into the country
- Not having the required vaccinations (such as yellow fever)
- Not registering with the immigration office if staying for more than 90 days
- Not having a valid visa (if required)
