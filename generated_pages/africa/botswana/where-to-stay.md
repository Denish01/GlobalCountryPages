# Where to Stay in Botswana — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

For travelers looking to explore the natural beauty and wildlife of Botswana, the best places to stay are in and around the major cities of Gaborone, Maun, and Kasane, as well as within the national parks and game reserves. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, we have identified the best fit for different types of travelers:
**Gaborone City** (Gaborone)
Best For: business travelers and families
Vibe: Gaborone City is a bustling metropolis with a mix of modern and traditional architecture, offering a unique blend of urban and African culture. The city has a relatively laid-back atmosphere, with many restaurants, shops, and entertainment options.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: proximity to government offices and major businesses, variety of accommodation options, access to amenities like restaurants and shops
Cons: traffic congestion, limited nightlife

**Maun** (Maun)
Best For: adventurers and backpackers
Vibe: Maun is a charming town located on the edge of the Okavango Delta, with a relaxed and rustic atmosphere. The town is a popular base for safaris and outdoor activities, with many tour operators and adventure companies.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: access to the Okavango Delta, variety of outdoor activities, relatively affordable accommodation options
Cons: limited amenities and services, can be dusty and chaotic

**Kasane** (Kasane)
Best For: luxury travelers and those visiting Chobe National Park
Vibe: Kasane is a small town located on the banks of the Chobe River, with a tranquil and natural atmosphere. The town is a popular base for game viewing and safaris, with many luxury lodges and resorts.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: proximity to Chobe National Park, stunning natural scenery, luxury accommodation options
Cons: can be expensive, limited amenities and services outside of lodges and resorts

**Chobe National Park** (Chobe National Park)
Best For: wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers
Vibe: Chobe National Park is a vast and diverse wilderness area, with a rugged and untouched atmosphere. The park is home to a wide range of wildlife, including elephants, lions, and giraffes.
Price Range: $150-400/night
Pros: incredible wildlife viewing opportunities, scenic landscapes, variety of accommodation options
Cons: can be hot and dusty, limited amenities and services

**Okavango Delta** (Okavango Delta)
Best For: adventure seekers and nature lovers
Vibe: The Okavango Delta is a unique and breathtakingly beautiful region, with a serene and peaceful atmosphere. The delta is a popular destination for safaris, boat tours, and outdoor activities.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: stunning natural scenery, variety of outdoor activities, opportunity to see wildlife in their natural habitat
Cons: can be expensive, limited amenities and services, requires planning and organization

**Francistown** (Francistown)
Best For: budget travelers and those looking for a more urban experience
Vibe: Francistown is a bustling city with a mix of modern and traditional architecture, offering a unique blend of urban and African culture. The city has a relatively laid-back atmosphere, with many restaurants, shops, and entertainment options.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: relatively affordable accommodation options, variety of amenities and services, access to nearby natural attractions
Cons: limited nightlife, can be dusty and chaotic
