# Mauritius Education — Schools, Universities & Student Guide

The education system in Mauritius is well-structured and offers a wide range of opportunities for students, with a strong emphasis on primary, secondary, and tertiary education, as well as international schools and universities.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Mauritius is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 5 and 11, and is provided free of charge by the government. Secondary education is also compulsory, and students typically attend school between the ages of 12 and 16. Tertiary education is provided by universities and other institutions, and is highly competitive. The quality of education in Mauritius is generally high, with a strong emphasis on **science**, **technology**, **engineering**, and **mathematics (STEM)** subjects. Public schools are free and widely available, but private schools are also popular, especially among the wealthy.

The education system in Mauritius is overseen by the Ministry of Education, which is responsible for setting curriculum standards, managing schools, and providing resources and support to teachers and students. The ministry has implemented a number of initiatives in recent years to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curriculum materials and the provision of training and professional development opportunities for teachers. Despite these efforts, the education system in Mauritius still faces a number of challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of resources in some schools.

Overall, the education system in Mauritius is well-structured and offers a wide range of opportunities for students. The country has a highly literate population, and education is highly valued by the government and by society as a whole. With a strong emphasis on STEM subjects and a highly competitive tertiary education system, Mauritius is an attractive destination for students from around the world.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- International schools are widely available in Mauritius, with a range of curricula offered, including **International Baccalaureate (IB)**, **British**, and **American** systems.
- Many international schools are located in the northern part of the island, particularly in the areas around Port Louis and Grand Baie.
- Tuition fees for international schools can be high, ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per year, depending on the school and the curriculum.
- Some popular international schools in Mauritius include the International Preparatory School, the Mauritius International School, and the Le Bocage International School.
- International schools in Mauritius often have a diverse student body, with students from a range of countries and cultural backgrounds.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Mauritius, foreign students typically need to obtain a **student visa**, which can be applied for through the Mauritian embassy or consulate in their home country.
- English is the primary language of instruction in most universities and schools in Mauritius, but some institutions may also offer courses in **French** or **Creole**.
- Foreign students may be eligible for **scholarships** or other forms of financial aid, depending on their country of origin and their academic qualifications.
- The cost of living in Mauritius can be relatively high, particularly in the northern part of the island, but there are also many affordable options available for students.
- Foreign students should research the **health insurance** options available to them, as some universities and schools may require proof of health insurance as a condition of enrollment.
- The **Mauritian government** offers a range of resources and support services for foreign students, including help with finding accommodation and getting settled in the country.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the major strengths of the education system in Mauritius is its emphasis on **STEM subjects**, which has helped to drive economic growth and development in the country.
- However, the education system in Mauritius also faces a number of challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of resources in some schools.
- The **Ministry of Education** has implemented a number of initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curriculum materials and the provision of training and professional development opportunities for teachers.
- Despite these efforts, there are still significant disparities in educational outcomes between different regions and communities in Mauritius, with some areas experiencing much higher levels of **poverty** and **inequality**.
- The education system in Mauritius is also facing the challenge of **brain drain**, as many of the country's most talented and educated citizens emigrate to other countries in search of better job opportunities.
