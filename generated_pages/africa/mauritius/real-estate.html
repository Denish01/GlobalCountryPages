Market Overview

The property market in Mauritius has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by a combination of factors including government initiatives, tourism development, and foreign investment. The market is characterized by a mix of luxury developments, residential properties, and commercial spaces, with a strong focus on the coastal regions. The current market conditions are favorable, with prices trending upwards and a high demand for properties, particularly in urban areas. The government has implemented various measures to attract foreign investment, including the introduction of the Integrated Resort Scheme (IRS) and the Real Estate Scheme (RES), which offer a range of benefits and incentives for foreign buyers.

The price trajectory in Mauritius has been steadily increasing, with prices in the capital city of Port Louis and other urban areas experiencing significant growth. The urban areas are the most sought-after, with Grand Baie, Flic-en-Flac, and Pereybere being popular destinations for both locals and foreigners. In contrast, the rural areas are less developed, but still offer opportunities for investment and growth. The market is expected to continue growing, driven by the country's strong economy, political stability, and attractive business environment.

The government has also implemented measures to promote the development of the real estate sector, including the creation of the Mauritius Tourism Promotion Authority (MTPA) and the Board of Investment (BOI). These initiatives aim to promote the country as a tourist destination and to attract foreign investment, which is expected to have a positive impact on the property market. Overall, the market in Mauritius offers a range of opportunities for investors, from luxury developments to residential properties, and is expected to continue growing in the coming years.

Foreign Ownership Rules

  • Foreigners can buy property in Mauritius, but there are certain restrictions and requirements that must be met.
  • Foreign buyers must obtain a residence permit or a work permit to be eligible to purchase a property.
  • Foreigners can also purchase property through a company structure, which can provide additional benefits and incentives.
  • The Board of Investment (BOI) is responsible for approving foreign investment in the real estate sector, and must be consulted before making a purchase.
  • Foreign buyers must also comply with the Non-Citizens (Property Restriction) Act, which regulates the purchase of property by non-citizens.

Buying Process

  1. Research and select a property: The first step is to research and select a property that meets your needs and budget.
  2. Appoint a real estate agent: It is recommended to appoint a reputable real estate agent to assist with the purchase process.
  3. Conduct a property inspection: A thorough inspection of the property should be conducted to identify any defects or issues.
  4. Obtain a purchase agreement: A purchase agreement must be signed by both the buyer and seller, outlining the terms and conditions of the sale.
  5. Apply for a residence permit: Foreign buyers must apply for a residence permit or a work permit to be eligible to purchase a property.
  6. Open a bank account: A bank account must be opened in Mauritius to facilitate the payment of the purchase price.
  7. Pay the deposit: A deposit of 10% of the purchase price must be paid to secure the property.
  8. Complete the transfer: The transfer of ownership must be completed, and the property must be registered in the buyer's name.
  9. Obtain a certificate of ownership: A certificate of ownership must be obtained from the Ministry of Housing and Lands.
  10. Register with the local authorities: The buyer must register with the local authorities, including the Municipal Council and the Central Electricity Board.

Rental Market

  • The rental market in Mauritius is highly competitive, with a high demand for properties, particularly in urban areas.
  • The typical lease term is 12 months, with a security deposit of 2-3 months' rent.
  • Rent is usually paid monthly, and can be negotiated with the landlord.
  • Properties can be furnished or unfurnished, depending on the agreement between the landlord and tenant.
  • Tenants have certain rights, including the right to a fair rent and the right to quiet enjoyment of the property.

Investment Tips

  • Research thoroughly: It is essential to research the market and the property thoroughly before making an investment.
  • Consider the location: The location of the property is crucial, with urban areas being more sought-after than rural areas.
  • Look for emerging areas: Emerging areas, such as Beau Bassin and Rose Hill, offer opportunities for growth and investment.
  • Understand the risks: There are risks associated with investing in property, including market fluctuations and regulatory changes.
  • Seek professional advice: It is recommended to seek professional advice from a reputable real estate agent or lawyer to ensure a smooth and successful investment.
  • Consider property management: Property management is crucial to ensure that the property is well-maintained and that rental income is maximized.
