The cost of education in Mauritius varies depending on the type of institution and the level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge tuition fees ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Mauritius is generally of high quality, with a strong emphasis on compulsory education for all children between the ages of 5 and 16. Public schools are free or heavily subsidized, and the language of instruction is English, with French and Creole also being widely spoken. The public education system is well-regarded, but private schools and international institutions offer a higher level of quality and are often preferred by expats and those seeking a more elite education. Compulsory education in Mauritius consists of primary education, which lasts for 6 years, and secondary education, which lasts for 5 years.

The education system in Mauritius is also influenced by the country's cultural and linguistic diversity, with a mix of European, African, and Asian traditions. International schools are available in Mauritius, catering to the needs of expat families and offering a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate, British, and French systems. These schools offer a high level of quality and are often preferred by those seeking a more global education.
