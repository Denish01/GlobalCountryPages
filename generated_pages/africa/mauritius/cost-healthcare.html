Healthcare System Overview

Mauritius has a mixed healthcare system, comprising both public and private sectors. The public sector provides universal access to healthcare, with most services being free or low-cost for citizens. However, the quality of public healthcare can vary, and waiting times can be long. Private healthcare, on the other hand, offers high-quality services, but at a higher cost. For foreigners, it is recommended to have health insurance that covers them in Mauritius, as they may not be eligible for free public healthcare. The healthcare system is generally well-equipped to handle common medical conditions, but for more complex or specialized care, patients may need to seek treatment abroad.

The healthcare system in Mauritius is well-organized, with a network of hospitals, clinics, and health centers throughout the island. The public sector is managed by the Ministry of Health, while the private sector is made up of independent hospitals and clinics. For locals, the public healthcare system is generally sufficient, but for foreigners, private healthcare is often the preferred option due to the higher quality of care and shorter waiting times. It is worth noting that some private hospitals and clinics in Mauritius have international accreditation, making them a popular choice for medical tourism.

Pharmacy & Medication Costs

  • Common medications such as painkillers and antibiotics are widely available at pharmacies and relatively affordable, with prices starting from $5-$10.
  • Prescription medications may require a doctor's prescription, which can cost $10-$20.
  • Pharmacies are widely available in urban areas, but may be less accessible in rural areas.
  • Over-the-counter medications are widely available, but may require a consultation with a pharmacist.
  • Some medications may be subsidized by the government, making them more affordable for locals.
  • It is recommended to check the prices and availability of medications before traveling to Mauritius.

Emergency Care

In the event of a medical emergency, it is recommended to call the emergency number 114 or visit the nearest hospital. Ambulance services are available, but may not be as widespread as in other countries. For foreigners, it is recommended to have a list of emergency contact numbers, including the embassy and insurance provider.
