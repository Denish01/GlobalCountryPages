The cost of rent in Mauritius can range from $400 to $1,200 USD per month for a one-bedroom apartment, depending on the location and quality of the accommodation.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
Mauritius has a relatively **stable** housing market, with a mix of modern and traditional accommodations available for rent and purchase. The rental market is **competitive**, with many apartments and houses available, especially in the city center and surrounding suburbs. Typical lease terms range from 6 to 12 months, with a **security deposit** equivalent to 1-2 months' rent required. Tenant rights are generally well-protected, with the **Mauritius Housing Authority** regulating the market and providing support for tenants. Compared to its regional neighbors, Mauritius has a relatively **high** cost of living, but its housing costs are still lower than those in Reunion and other island nations in the Indian Ocean.

The housing market in Mauritius is also influenced by the country's **tourism industry**, with many apartments and houses being rented out to tourists and expats. This has driven up prices in popular areas, but there are still **affordable** options available for those who are willing to look outside the city center. Overall, Mauritius offers a unique blend of **island living** and modern amenities, making it an attractive destination for expats and tourists alike.
