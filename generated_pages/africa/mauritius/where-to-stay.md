# Where to Stay in Mauritius — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Mauritius, consider the island's diverse regions, each offering a unique blend of luxury, nature, and culture, with top areas including Grand Baie, Flic-en-Flac, and Belle Mare.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Grand Baie** (Grand Baie)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families
Vibe: Grand Baie is a bustling tourist hub with a vibrant nightlife, shopping centers, and a beautiful beach, offering a mix of relaxation and activity. The area is known for its lively atmosphere and wide range of amenities.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Easy access to water sports, a variety of restaurants and bars, and a beautiful beach. The area also offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to apartments.
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, especially at night.

**Flic-en-Flac** (Flic-en-Flac)
Best For: Families and budget travelers
Vibe: Flic-en-Flac is a laid-back town with a beautiful beach, a variety of restaurants, and a range of outdoor activities, including snorkeling and hiking. The area is known for its relaxed atmosphere and natural beauty.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, a beautiful beach, and a range of outdoor activities. The area is also close to the capital city, Port Louis.
Cons: Limited nightlife and shopping options.

**Belle Mare** (Belle Mare)
Best For: Luxury travelers and honeymooners
Vibe: Belle Mare is a picturesque coastal town with a stunning beach, crystal-clear waters, and a range of luxury resorts, offering a tranquil and romantic atmosphere. The area is known for its natural beauty and high-end amenities.
Price Range: $300-$600/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, luxury resorts, and a range of water sports. The area is also close to the Ile aux Cerfs island, a popular spot for snorkeling and swimming.
Cons: Can be expensive, and limited options for budget travelers.

**Port Louis** (Port Louis)
Best For: Business travelers and culture enthusiasts
Vibe: Port Louis is the capital city of Mauritius, with a rich history, cultural attractions, and a range of business facilities, offering a unique blend of culture and commerce. The area is known for its vibrant markets, historic buildings, and traditional cuisine.
Price Range: $150-$400/night
Pros: Close to business facilities, cultural attractions, and historic landmarks. The area is also home to a range of restaurants and markets.
Cons: Can be busy and noisy, especially during rush hour.

**Trou aux Biches** (Trou aux Biches)
Best For: Families and nature lovers
Vibe: Trou aux Biches is a charming coastal town with a beautiful beach, a range of outdoor activities, and a relaxed atmosphere, offering a tranquil and natural setting. The area is known for its stunning beach, coral reef, and range of water sports.
Price Range: $150-$350/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, outdoor activities, and a range of accommodation options. The area is also close to the Mont Choisy Le Golf course.
Cons: Limited nightlife and shopping options.

**Le Morne** (Le Morne)
Best For: Adventure seekers and nature lovers
Vibe: Le Morne is a picturesque peninsula with a stunning beach, a range of outdoor activities, and a historic landmark, offering a unique blend of nature and culture. The area is known for its beautiful beach, coral reef, and range of water sports.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, outdoor activities, and a range of accommodation options. The area is also home to the Le Morne Brabant mountain, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Cons: Limited amenities and services.
