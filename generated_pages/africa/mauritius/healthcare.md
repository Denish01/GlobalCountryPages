# Mauritius Healthcare — Hospitals, Insurance & Medical Guide

Mauritius offers a well-structured healthcare system with a mix of public and private facilities, providing a high standard of medical care to its population and visitors.

[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
Mauritius has a mixed healthcare system, combining public and private providers to offer a wide range of medical services. The public sector is primarily responsible for providing **primary healthcare** to the population, with a network of community health centers and hospitals. The private sector, on the other hand, offers more specialized and advanced medical care, often catering to foreigners and locals who can afford it. The quality of care in Mauritius is generally high, with many medical professionals trained abroad, particularly in the UK and France. However, there may be some disparities in access to healthcare between urban and rural areas, with more facilities and specialists concentrated in cities like Port Louis and Curepipe.

The Mauritian government has made significant efforts to improve the healthcare system, investing in infrastructure and human resources. As a result, the country has achieved considerable success in controlling and preventing diseases, such as **malaria** and **tuberculosis**. The healthcare system is also supported by a network of **non-governmental organizations (NGOs)** and **community-based initiatives**, which provide additional services and support to vulnerable populations.

Despite these achievements, there are still some challenges facing the healthcare system in Mauritius, including a shortage of certain medical specialists and a lack of access to healthcare services in some rural areas. However, overall, the healthcare system in Mauritius is well-organized and provides a high standard of care to its population and visitors.

[SECTION] Health Insurance [/SECTION]
- **Private health insurance** is recommended for visitors and expats, as it provides access to better-equipped private hospitals and a wider range of medical services.
- **Local insurance options** are available, including the Mauritius National Insurance Scheme, which provides coverage for certain medical expenses.
- **Expatriate health insurance** is also available, offering comprehensive coverage for medical expenses, including evacuation and repatriation.
- The cost of health insurance in Mauritius varies depending on the provider, level of coverage, and individual circumstances.
- **Travel insurance** is also recommended for visitors, as it provides coverage for unexpected medical expenses and emergencies.
- Some employers in Mauritius offer **group health insurance** as a benefit to their employees, which can provide comprehensive coverage for medical expenses.

[SECTION] Pharmacies & Medications [/SECTION]
- **Pharmacies** are widespread in Mauritius, with many locations in urban and tourist areas.
- A **prescription** is required for most medications, which can be obtained from a licensed medical practitioner.
- **Common medications** are widely available, including those for **pain relief**, **antibiotics**, and **allergies**.
- **Brand-name medications** are also available, although **generic alternatives** may be more affordable.
- The cost of medications in Mauritius is generally reasonable, with many pharmacies offering competitive prices.

[SECTION] Vaccinations & Health Risks [/SECTION]
- **Hepatitis A** and **typhoid** vaccinations are recommended for all travelers to Mauritius.
- **Hepatitis B** and **rabies** vaccinations are also recommended for certain individuals, such as healthcare workers and adventure travelers.
- **Malaria** is present in some areas of Mauritius, particularly in the southeast, and **insect-borne disease** precautions are recommended.
- **Dengue fever** and **chikungunya** are also present in Mauritius, and **mosquito-borne disease** precautions are recommended.
- **Water-borne diseases**, such as **cholera** and **giardiasis**, can be present in some areas, and **water safety** precautions are recommended.
- **Food safety** is generally good in Mauritius, but **food-borne illnesses** can still occur, and **food handling** precautions are recommended.

[SECTION] Medical Tourism [/SECTION]
- Mauritius is a popular destination for **medical tourism**, offering a range of medical services, including **cosmetic surgery**, **dentistry**, and **fertility treatments**.
- **Accredited hospitals**, such as Fortis Clinique Darne and Apollo Bramwell Hospital, offer high-quality medical care and specialized services.
- **Cost savings** can be significant, with many medical procedures costing less in Mauritius than in other countries.
- **Travel packages** are available, which can include accommodation, transportation, and medical care, making it easier for medical tourists to plan their trip.

[SECTION] Emergency Procedures [/SECTION]
- In the event of a **medical emergency**, call the emergency number, 114, or go to the nearest hospital.
- **Ambulance services** are available, but may not be as widespread as in other countries.
- **Nearest hospitals** can be found in urban areas, such as Port Louis and Curepipe.
- **Insurance claims** can be made through the insurance provider, and it is recommended to have all necessary documents and information ready.
- **Emergency medical evacuation** may be necessary in some cases, and **travel insurance** can provide coverage for this.
