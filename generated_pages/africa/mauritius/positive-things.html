Cultural Treasures

  • The Aapravasi Ghat, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a historic immigration depot that showcases the history of indentured labor in Mauritius.
  • The Mauritius Museum of Art, located in the capital city of Port Louis, features a collection of contemporary and traditional Mauritian art.
  • The Sega Tipik music and dance tradition is a unique and vibrant expression of Mauritian culture, characterized by its soulful melodies and energetic rhythms.
  • The Ganesh Chaturthi festival, celebrated by the Hindu community in Mauritius, is a colorful and vibrant celebration of the birth of Lord Ganesha.
  • The Mauritius Film Development Corporation supports the development of the local film industry, producing a range of films that showcase Mauritian culture and talent.

People & Global Impact

  • Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam, the first prime minister of Mauritius, played a key role in the country's independence movement and is remembered as a national hero.
  • The Mauritian diaspora has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Mauritians living and working abroad, particularly in Europe and North America.
  • Dr. Navinchandra Ramgoolam, a renowned cardiologist, has made significant contributions to the field of medicine, including the development of new treatments for heart disease.
  • The Mauritian values of diversity, tolerance, and resilience have been recognized as key factors in the country's success, and are highly regarded by visitors and international partners.

What Visitors Love

  • The beautiful beaches of Mauritius, including Grand Baie and Pereybere, are a major draw for tourists, offering a range of water sports and activities.
  • The delicious cuisine of Mauritius, which combines African, European, and Asian influences, is a highlight of any visit, with popular dishes including biriyani and vindaye.
  • The warm hospitality of the Mauritian people is legendary, with visitors often commenting on the friendly and welcoming nature of the locals.
  • The unique wildlife of Mauritius, including the pink pigeon and the Mauritius kestrel, is a major attraction for nature lovers and birdwatchers.
  • The range of outdoor activities, including hiking, diving, and fishing, makes Mauritius a popular destination for adventure seekers.
  • The vibrant culture of Mauritius, which includes a range of festivals and celebrations, is a highlight of any visit, with the Mauritius International Jazz Festival being a particular standout.

Hidden Gems

  • The Ile aux Aigrettes, a small island located off the coast of Mauritius, is a nature reserve that is home to a range of unique and endemic species.
  • The Mauritius National Library, located in the capital city of Port Louis, is a treasure trove of information on the history and culture of Mauritius.
  • The Ganga Talao, a sacred lake located in the center of the island, is a pilgrimage site for Hindus and a popular tourist destination.
  • The Mauritius Glass Gallery, located in the town of Vacoas, is a unique museum that showcases a range of glass art and sculpture.
