# Mauritius Politics & Government — System, Leaders & Stability

Mauritius is governed as a **parliamentary democracy** with a president serving as head of state and a prime minister as head of government.

[SECTION] Political System [/SECTION]
Mauritius has a **parliamentary system**, where the government is accountable to the legislature, and the president serves as the head of state with ceremonial and symbolic duties. The prime minister, as the head of government, is responsible for advising the president and overseeing the administration of the country. The **separation of powers** is maintained through the independence of the judiciary, the legislature, and the executive. Mauritius has a **unitary system**, with a centralized government and no federal divisions. The government is divided into various ministries, each responsible for a specific portfolio, such as education, health, and finance.

The **National Assembly** is the unicameral legislature of Mauritius, composed of 70 members elected by popular vote for a five-year term. The assembly is responsible for making laws, approving the budget, and exercising parliamentary control over the government. The **opposition** plays a crucial role in the political system, providing a check on the government and ensuring that the interests of all citizens are represented.

The **judiciary** is independent and impartial, with the **Supreme Court** as the highest court of appeal. The judiciary is responsible for interpreting the laws and ensuring that they are consistent with the **constitution**. The **constitution** is the supreme law of the land, outlining the fundamental rights and freedoms of citizens, as well as the structure and powers of the government.

[SECTION] Elections [/SECTION]
- The **electoral system** in Mauritius is based on a **first-past-the-post** system, where voters cast ballots for individual candidates.
- **General elections** are held every five years, with the most recent election taking place in 2019.
- The **last election** saw the Alliance Morisien winning a majority of seats in the National Assembly.
- The **next election** is scheduled for 2024, with voter participation expected to be high, as it was in the previous election, with a turnout of over 80%.

[SECTION] Political Stability & Challenges [/SECTION]
- The current **political climate** in Mauritius is stable, with a strong and independent judiciary, a free press, and a vibrant civil society.
- **Key issues** facing the government include managing the economy, improving education and healthcare, and addressing environmental concerns.
- **Regional dynamics** play a significant role in Mauritian politics, with the country being a member of the **African Union** and the **Southern African Development Community**.
- Mauritius has a high level of **press freedom**, with a ranking of 14th out of 180 countries in the Reporters Without Borders Press Freedom Index.
- The country also has a high level of **human development**, with a ranking of 66th out of 189 countries in the Human Development Index.

[SECTION] Foreign Relations [/SECTION]
- Mauritius is a member of the **United Nations**, the **African Union**, and the **Commonwealth of Nations**.
- The country has **diplomatic relations** with over 150 countries, including the United States, China, and India.
- Mauritius is a **key player** in regional organizations, such as the **Indian Ocean Commission** and the **Southern African Development Community**.
- The country has a **strategic location**, with a significant portion of international trade passing through its ports.
- Mauritius has a **strong stance** on issues such as climate change, sustainable development, and human rights, and is an active participant in international forums and negotiations.
