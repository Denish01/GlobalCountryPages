# Zimbabwe Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

When traveling to Zimbabwe, you can expect to find a moderately developed internet infrastructure with varying levels of connectivity depending on your location, with the overall internet quality being generally good in urban areas but less reliable in rural regions.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Zimbabwe has undergone significant improvements in recent years, with a notable increase in the number of internet users and a growing demand for online services. However, the **urban-rural divide** remains a significant challenge, with urban areas like Harare and Bulawayo enjoying better internet connectivity and speeds compared to rural areas. The reliability of the internet can also be affected by **power outages** and **network maintenance**, which can be frequent in some areas. Despite these challenges, Zimbabwe's internet infrastructure has made significant progress, with many internet service providers offering a range of services, including **fiber optic connections**, **mobile broadband**, and **satellite internet**.

The Zimbabwean government has also implemented initiatives to improve internet access and affordability, such as the **National Broadband Strategy**, which aims to increase broadband penetration and reduce costs. Additionally, many **internet service providers** are investing in infrastructure development, including the deployment of **4G networks** and **fiber optic cables**. These efforts have contributed to an increase in internet penetration, with more Zimbabweans having access to the internet than ever before. However, there is still a long way to go, particularly in rural areas, where internet access remains limited and often unreliable.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Zimbabwe, you can visit any of the major mobile network operators, such as **Econet Wireless**, **NetOne**, or **Telecel**, at their retail shops or authorized dealers. You will need to provide a valid **passport** and **proof of residence** to register for a SIM card. The cost of a tourist SIM card is approximately 10 USD, and you can purchase data bundles and airtime at various outlets, including retail shops, supermarkets, and street vendors. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable dealer to avoid any issues with **network coverage** and **data speeds**.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels offering **free WiFi** to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is growing, with many cafes and restaurants offering **free WiFi** to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular, with many offering **high-speed internet** and other amenities.
- Public WiFi is limited, but some **public hotspots** are available in major cities like Harare and Bulawayo.
- Some **shopping malls** and **airports** also offer free WiFi to visitors.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like **Hub Zimbabwe** and **Bulawayo Coworking Space** offer reliable internet and a productive work environment.
- Reliable cafe chains like **Cafe Noma** and **The Coffee Shop** offer good WiFi and a comfortable work space.
- Backup internet options like **mobile hotspots** and **portable WiFi routers** are available for purchase or rent.
- VPNs are recommended to ensure **online security** and **data protection**.
- Some **coliving spaces** and **hostels** also offer coworking facilities and reliable internet.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is a concern in Zimbabwe, with some **websites** and **online services** being blocked.
- **Social media** and **messaging apps** may be restricted or blocked during times of **civil unrest** or **elections**.
- Using a **VPN** is recommended to ensure **online anonymity** and **data protection**.
- **Roaming alternatives** like **eSIMs** and **portable WiFi routers** can be a cost-effective option for travelers.
- **Power outages** and **network maintenance** can affect internet reliability, so it's essential to have a **backup plan**.
- **Cybercrime** is a growing concern in Zimbabwe, so it's crucial to take **online security** measures seriously.
