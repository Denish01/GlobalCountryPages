# Cost of Living in Zimbabwe - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Zimbabwe can vary greatly depending on your lifestyle, but on average, a daily budget of $30-100 USD per day can cover basic expenses for a visitor or expat.

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Zimbabwe? [/SECTION]
Zimbabwe is generally considered to be an affordable country to live in or visit, especially when compared to its neighboring countries such as South Africa. The cost of living in Zimbabwe is relatively low, with affordable accommodation options, a wide range of local food choices, and reasonable transportation costs. However, the country has experienced periods of high inflation in the past, which can affect the purchasing power of the local currency. Overall, Zimbabwe offers a great balance between affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike.

Compared to other countries in the region, Zimbabwe is more affordable than South Africa, but slightly more expensive than Zambia and Mozambique. The cost of living in Zimbabwe is also influenced by the country's economic conditions, with prices tend to be higher in major cities like Harare and Bulawayo, and lower in smaller towns and rural areas. Visitors and expats can take advantage of the country's natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly people, while enjoying a relatively low cost of living.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in budget-friendly hostels or guesthouses, especially in smaller towns and rural areas.
- Eat local food at markets or street stalls, which is not only affordable but also delicious.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on fuel costs.
- Avoid exchanging money at airports or hotels, which often have poor exchange rates.
- Shop at local markets for fresh produce and other essentials, rather than relying on supermarkets.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost activities, such as visiting national parks or attending local festivals.
