# Transportation Costs in Zimbabwe

The cost of transportation in Zimbabwe varies widely, with a monthly transit pass costing around $10-$20 USD, depending on the city and mode of transport.

[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Zimbabwe has a relatively underdeveloped public transportation system, with most people relying on **informal** modes of transport such as **minibus taxis** and **commuter omnibuses**. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with Harare having a more comprehensive network than other urban areas. However, the majority of the population relies on **walking** or **cycling** as their primary mode of transport, especially in rural areas.

The main transportation modes in Zimbabwe include **road transport**, with a network of **paved** and **unpaved roads** connecting major cities and towns. The country also has a **railway network**, although it is not well-maintained and is mostly used for **freight transport**. The lack of a well-developed public transportation system has led to the growth of **informal** transport services, including **taxis** and **ride-hailing apps**.
