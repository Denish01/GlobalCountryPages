# Positive Things About Zimbabwe - Achievements & Culture

Zimbabwe is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and impressive achievements in various fields, making it a fascinating country to explore and discover.

[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Matobo Hills**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a unique cultural and natural landscape featuring ancient rock art and impressive granite formations.
- The **Great Zimbabwe Ruins**, another UNESCO World Heritage Site, are the remains of an ancient city that was once the capital of the Kingdom of Zimbabwe.
- The **Zimbabwe International Film Festival**, held annually in Harare, showcases a diverse range of local and international films, promoting film production and appreciation in the country.
- The **National Gallery of Zimbabwe** in Harare is a premier art institution that promotes and preserves Zimbabwean art and culture, with a collection of over 6,000 works of art.
- The **Chimurenga Music**, a genre of Zimbabwean music, is a unique blend of traditional and modern styles, with notable musicians like **Thomas Mapfumo** and **Oliver Mtukudzi**.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Morgan Tsvangirai**, a prominent Zimbabwean politician, was a key figure in the country's struggle for democracy and human rights.
- **Tsitsi Dangarembga**, a renowned Zimbabwean author, has written several critically acclaimed novels, including **Nervous Conditions**, which explores the experiences of women in Zimbabwe.
- The **Zimbabwean diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global community, with many Zimbabweans excelling in fields like medicine, law, and engineering.
- **Strive Masiyiwa**, a successful Zimbabwean entrepreneur, is the founder of **Econet Wireless**, a leading telecommunications company in Africa.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **Victoria Falls**, one of the largest waterfalls in the world, is a breathtaking natural wonder that attracts millions of visitors each year.
- The **Zimbabwean hospitality**, known for its warmth and friendliness, makes visitors feel welcome and at home.
- The **local cuisine**, which includes dishes like **sadza** and **braai**, is a delicious and unique blend of traditional and modern flavors.
- The **game reserves**, such as **Hwange National Park**, offer exciting opportunities for **safari** adventures and wildlife viewing.
- The **scenic landscapes**, including the **Eastern Highlands** and the **Matobo Hills**, provide stunning views and opportunities for hiking and exploration.
- The **vibrant markets**, like the **Harare Market**, offer a colorful and lively atmosphere, with a wide range of local handicrafts and souvenirs on sale.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Gonarezhou National Park**, located in the southeastern part of the country, is a lesser-known but equally impressive game reserve, with a wide range of wildlife and scenic landscapes.
- The **Chinhoyi Caves**, a series of limestone caves and rock formations, are a unique and fascinating natural wonder, with stunning views and exciting opportunities for exploration.
- The **Zimbabwe Museum of Human Sciences**, located in Harare, is a small but fascinating museum that showcases the country's rich cultural and scientific heritage.
- The **Bulawayo Railway Museum**, located in Bulawayo, is a unique and interesting museum that explores the history of railways in Zimbabwe and the region.
