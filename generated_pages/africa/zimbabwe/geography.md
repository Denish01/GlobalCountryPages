[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Zimbabwe is situated in the southern part of the African continent, and its location is characterized by its landlocked position, with no direct access to the ocean. The country is bordered by four countries: Zambia to the northwest, Mozambique to the east, South Africa to the south, and Botswana to the southwest. Zimbabwe's location in the interior of the continent has a significant impact on its climate, as it is far from the moderating influence of the ocean.

The country's borders are defined by a combination of natural and artificial boundaries, including rivers, mountains, and straight lines. The Zambezi River forms part of the border with Zambia, while the Limpopo River forms part of the border with South Africa. The border with Mozambique is defined by a combination of rivers and straight lines, while the border with Botswana is defined by a straight line. Zimbabwe's location and borders have played a significant role in shaping its history, culture, and economy.
