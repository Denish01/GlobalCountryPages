Education System Overview

The education system in Eritrea is structured into three main levels: primary education, secondary education, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory and lasts for 7 years, starting from the age of 7. Secondary education is divided into two cycles: the first cycle lasts for 4 years and the second cycle lasts for 2 years. Tertiary education is provided by universities, colleges, and institutes, with a strong focus on technical and vocational training. The quality of education in Eritrea has improved significantly in recent years, with a strong emphasis on teacher training and curriculum development. However, the education system still faces challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers, inadequate infrastructure, and limited resources.

The public education system in Eritrea is free and accessible to all citizens, but the quality of education can vary significantly depending on the location and the school. Private schools are also available, but they are relatively expensive and cater to a small minority of students. The government has implemented various initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curriculum and teaching methods, as well as the provision of scholarships and grants to students from disadvantaged backgrounds.

Despite the challenges, the education system in Eritrea has made significant progress in recent years, with a strong focus on increasing access to education and improving the quality of education. The government has also implemented various initiatives to promote education for all, including the introduction of free education for all citizens and the provision of financial support to students from disadvantaged backgrounds.

International Schools

  • International schools are limited in Eritrea, but some schools offer International Baccalaureate (IB) and British curricula.
  • The Asmara International Community School is one of the few international schools in the country, offering American and British curricula.
  • The Eritrea International School is another option, offering IB and British curricula.
  • The cost of attending an international school in Eritrea can range from $5,000 to $10,000 per year.
  • International schools are mainly located in the capital city, Asmara, and cater to the children of expatriates and diplomats.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Eritrea, foreign students need to obtain a student visa, which can be obtained from the Eritrean embassy or consulate in their home country.
  • Foreign students are required to have a good command of English or Tigrinya, the official languages of instruction in Eritrean universities.
  • Scholarships are available to foreign students, including the Eritrean Government Scholarship and the African Union Scholarship.
  • The cost of living in Eritrea is relatively low, with rent starting from $100 per month and food starting from $3 per meal.
  • Foreign students can also apply for part-time jobs to support their living expenses, but work permits are required.
  • The Eritrean government has introduced various initiatives to promote internationalization of education, including the introduction of foreign language courses and cultural exchange programs.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • The education system in Eritrea faces challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers, inadequate infrastructure, and limited resources.
  • The teacher-student ratio is high, with an average of 1 teacher to 50 students.
  • The dropout rate is also high, with many students dropping out of school due to poverty and lack of access to education.
  • Despite the challenges, the education system in Eritrea has made significant progress in recent years, with a strong focus on increasing access to education and improving the quality of education.
  • The government has introduced various initiatives to promote education for all, including the introduction of free education for all citizens and the provision of financial support to students from disadvantaged backgrounds.
