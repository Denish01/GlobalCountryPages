# Where to Stay in Eritrea — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

For travelers planning to visit Eritrea, the best places to stay are in the capital city Asmara and its surrounding areas, which offer a mix of modern amenities and traditional Eritrean culture.

**Asmara City Center** (Asmara)
Best For: Luxury, Business
Vibe: The city center is a bustling area with a mix of Italian colonial architecture and modern buildings, offering a unique blend of old and new. The area is also home to many restaurants, cafes, and shops.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Close proximity to major attractions, easy access to public transportation, and a wide range of dining and shopping options. 
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, and prices may be higher than in other areas.

**Teseney** (Teseney)
Best For: Backpacker, Family
Vibe: Teseney is a small town located near the Sudanese border, known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities such as hiking and camping. The town has a relaxed atmosphere and is a great place to experience rural Eritrean life.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: Affordable prices, opportunities for outdoor activities, and a chance to experience rural Eritrean culture.
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and may require a longer commute to major attractions.

**Massawa** (Massawa)
Best For: Luxury, Family
Vibe: Massawa is a coastal city located on the Red Sea, known for its beautiful beaches and historic architecture. The city has a relaxed atmosphere and is a great place to unwind and enjoy the sun and sea.
Price Range: $30-100/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, historic architecture, and a range of dining and shopping options.
Cons: Can be hot and humid, and prices may be higher than in other areas.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.
- Research and compare prices to find the best deals.
- Consider staying in guesthouses or hostels for a more local experience.
- Be prepared to negotiate prices, especially when booking directly with hotel owners or managers.
- Check for any additional fees or taxes that may be added to the room rate.
- Read reviews and check the location of the accommodation before booking.
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Expedia, to find and book accommodations.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Research the area and choose accommodations in safe and secure locations.
- Avoid areas near the borders with Sudan and Ethiopia, as they may be considered sensitive or restricted.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
- Use reputable taxi services or public transportation, and avoid walking alone at night.
- Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation, such as Asmara City Center.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, and dress modestly when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events.
