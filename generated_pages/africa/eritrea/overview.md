[SECTION] What Is Eritrea? [/SECTION]
Eritrea is a **sovereign** country located in the Horn of Africa, bordered by Sudan to the west, Ethiopia to the south, and Djibouti to the southeast. The country has a strategic position along the Red Sea, with a coastline of approximately 1,200 kilometers. Eritrea is known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse geography, and resilient people. The country has a total area of around 124,320 square kilometers, with a mix of highlands, lowlands, and coastal plains.

Eritrea gained its independence from Ethiopia in 1993, after a long struggle for self-determination. The country has a diverse population, with nine ethnic groups, each with their own language and culture. The two main languages spoken in Eritrea are **Tigrinya** and **Arabic**, with English and Italian also widely spoken. The capital city, **Asmara**, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its well-preserved Italian colonial architecture and vibrant cultural scene.

Eritrea's economy is primarily based on agriculture, with the country producing crops such as cotton, sorghum, and wheat. The country is also rich in natural resources, including copper, gold, and marble. However, Eritrea faces significant development challenges, including poverty, limited infrastructure, and a lack of access to basic services such as healthcare and education.

1. 1890 - Eritrea becomes an Italian colony, known as Italian Eritrea, and remains under Italian rule until the end of World War II.
2. 1952 - Eritrea is federated with Ethiopia, under a UN-sponsored agreement, but the federation is short-lived and Eritrea's autonomy is gradually eroded.
3. 1961 - The Eritrean Liberation Front (ELF) is formed, marking the beginning of Eritrea's long struggle for independence from Ethiopia.
4. 1991 - The Eritrean People's Liberation Front (EPLF) captures the capital city, Asmara, and the country gains de facto independence from Ethiopia.
5. 1993 - Eritrea holds a referendum on independence, in which the majority of the population votes in favor of independence, and the country is officially recognized as a sovereign state.
6. 1998 - Eritrea and Ethiopia engage in a border conflict, which lasts for two years and results in significant human suffering and economic losses.
7. 2018 - Eritrea and Ethiopia sign a peace agreement, marking a new era of cooperation and diplomacy between the two countries.
