[SECTION] Overview: Morocco vs Tunisia [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Morocco and Tunisia can vary significantly, with Morocco being the more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees. Morocco's lower cost of living is largely due to its lower housing costs, with rent prices in cities like Marrakech and Fez being significantly lower than in Tunis, the capital of Tunisia. Additionally, Morocco's diverse economy, which includes a strong agricultural sector, helps to keep food prices relatively low. In contrast, Tunisia's economy is more focused on industry and services, which can drive up costs for everyday items.

In terms of overall cost of living, Morocco is around 15-20% cheaper than Tunisia, with the biggest differences being in housing and transportation costs. However, Tunisia has a more developed economy and a higher standard of living, which can make it a more attractive option for some expats and travelers. Tunisia also has a more modern infrastructure, with better roads and public transportation, which can make it easier to get around. Overall, the choice between Morocco and Tunisia will depend on an individual's priorities and lifestyle, with Morocco offering a more affordable and traditional experience, and Tunisia offering a more modern and developed one.
