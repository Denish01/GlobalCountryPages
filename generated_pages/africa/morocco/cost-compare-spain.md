[SECTION] Overview: Morocco vs Spain [/SECTION]
Morocco and Spain are two neighboring countries with distinct economic contexts. Morocco, a sovereign country in North Africa, has a lower GDP per capita and a more developing economy compared to Spain, a developed European country. The cost of living in Morocco is significantly lower than in Spain, making it an attractive destination for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle. The overall cost difference between the two countries is around 30-40%, with Morocco being the more affordable option.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Morocco's economy is driven by industries such as textiles, phosphates, and tourism, while Spain's economy is more diversified, with a strong service sector and a high standard of living. The lower cost of living in Morocco is reflected in the prices of everyday items, such as food, housing, and transportation. However, it's essential to note that the cost of living in Morocco can vary greatly depending on the location, with cities like Marrakech and Fez tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas.

[SECTION] Bottom Line [/SECTION]
