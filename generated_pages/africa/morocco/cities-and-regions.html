How Morocco Is Divided

Morocco is divided into 12 regions, each with its own governor and regional council. These regions are further divided into provinces and prefectures, which are then divided into municipalities and rural communes. Major Cities

Casablanca (3.7M)

Role: economic hub, port city

Known For: its vibrant culture, historic architecture, and bustling streets. Casablanca is a melting pot of traditional and modern Morocco, with a mix of French colonial and Arabic influences.

Best For: business travelers, shoppers, and those interested in history and culture.

Rabat (2.1M)

Role: capital, cultural center

Known For: its historic landmarks, such as the Kasbah of the Udayas and the Royal Palace, as well as its vibrant arts and cultural scene.

Best For: history buffs, families, and those interested in politics and government.

Marrakech (1.7M)

Role: cultural center, tourist hub

Known For: its vibrant souks, historic architecture, and lively nightlife. Marrakech is a city of stunning beauty, with a mix of Arabic, Berber, and French influences.

Best For: tourists, shoppers, and those interested in history and culture.

Fes (1.1M)

Role: cultural center, historic city

Known For: its well-preserved medieval architecture, vibrant souks, and rich cultural heritage. Fes is a city of great historical significance, with a mix of Arabic, Berber, and Jewish influences.

Best For: history buffs, cultural enthusiasts, and those interested in traditional crafts and cuisine.

Regional Differences

  • Culture: Morocco's regions have distinct cultural identities, with different influences from Arabic, Berber, French, and Spanish cultures.
  • Economy: The regions vary in terms of economic development, with some regions having a strong focus on industry and tourism, while others are more rural and agricultural.
  • Climate: Morocco's climate varies from region to region, with the north being generally cooler and more temperate, while the south is hotter and more desert-like.
  • Language: While Arabic is the official language, Berber languages are also widely spoken in some regions, particularly in the north and east.

Getting Around

Morocco has a well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis available. The country's main airport is Mohammed V International Airport in Casablanca, which offers flights to most major cities in Morocco. Trains are a convenient way to travel between cities, with the national rail network covering most of the country. Buses are also widely available, with both public and private companies operating routes between cities and towns. Driving in Morocco can be challenging, particularly in rural areas, but the country's road network is generally well-maintained.
