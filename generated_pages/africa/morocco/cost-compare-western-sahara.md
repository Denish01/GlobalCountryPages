[SECTION] Overview: Morocco vs Western Sahara [/SECTION]
Morocco and Western Sahara are two neighboring countries in North Africa with distinct economic profiles. Morocco has a relatively diversified economy, with a strong focus on manufacturing, tourism, and agriculture, which contributes to its higher GDP per capita. In contrast, Western Sahara has a smaller and more limited economy, with a significant dependence on phosphate mining and fishing. As a result, Morocco tends to have a lower cost of living, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation generally lower than in Western Sahara. The overall cost of living in Morocco is around 20-30% lower than in Western Sahara, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Morocco has a more developed infrastructure, with better roads, public transportation, and amenities, which contributes to its lower costs. In contrast, Western Sahara has a more limited infrastructure, with higher costs for transportation, communication, and other services. Additionally, Morocco has a more competitive market, with a wider range of products and services available, which helps to keep prices low. Overall, Morocco's stronger economy and more developed infrastructure make it a more affordable option for those looking to live in the region.
