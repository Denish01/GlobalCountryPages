# Where to Stay in Morocco — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Morocco, consider the vibrant cities of Marrakech, Fez, and Rabat, as well as the coastal towns of Essaouira and Taghazout, each offering a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, we've highlighted the best fit for different types of travelers, along with the character, price range, pros, and cons of each neighborhood.

**Medina** (Marrakech)
Best For: Backpacker / Family
Vibe: The Medina is a historic and vibrant area filled with narrow streets, bustling souks, and stunning architecture, offering an immersive experience of Moroccan culture. The area is always lively, with a mix of locals and tourists.
Price Range: $20-$100/night
Pros: Rich cultural experience, plenty of dining options, close to major attractions
Cons: Can be overwhelming and noisy

**Hivernage** (Marrakech)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: Hivernage is a modern and upscale neighborhood with wide avenues, luxurious hotels, and trendy restaurants, providing a comfortable and relaxing stay. The area is calm and serene, with plenty of amenities.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Modern amenities, close to golf courses, easy access to the city center
Cons: Less authentic Moroccan experience

**Fez Medina** (Fez)
Best For: Family / Cultural
Vibe: The Fez Medina is one of the oldest and most well-preserved medieval cities in the world, with a rich history and stunning architecture. The area is quiet and peaceful, with a strong sense of community.
Price Range: $30-$150/night
Pros: Historic significance, authentic Moroccan experience, plenty of cultural attractions
Cons: Narrow streets can be confusing to navigate

**Kasbah** (Tangier)
Best For: Luxury / Romantic
Vibe: The Kasbah is a historic and picturesque neighborhood with stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea, offering a romantic and relaxing atmosphere. The area is calm and peaceful, with plenty of amenities.
Price Range: $80-$250/night
Pros: Breathtaking views, historic significance, close to the city center
Cons: Steep streets can be challenging to navigate

**Essaouira Medina** (Essaouira)
Best For: Backpacker / Surfer
Vibe: The Essaouira Medina is a charming and laid-back neighborhood with a strong surf culture, offering a relaxed and friendly atmosphere. The area is always lively, with a mix of locals and tourists.
Price Range: $20-$80/night
Pros: Relaxed atmosphere, plenty of surf schools, close to the beach
Cons: Limited dining options

**Gueliz** (Marrakech)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: Gueliz is a modern and upscale neighborhood with wide avenues, luxurious hotels, and trendy restaurants, providing a comfortable and relaxing stay. The area is calm and serene, with plenty of amenities.
Price Range: $80-$200/night
Pros: Modern amenities, close to the city center, easy access to transportation
Cons: Less authentic Moroccan experience

**Rabat Medina** (Rabat)
Best For: Family / Cultural
Vibe: The Rabat Medina is a historic and vibrant area filled with narrow streets, bustling souks, and stunning architecture, offering an immersive experience of Moroccan culture. The area is always lively, with a mix of locals and tourists.
Price Range: $30-$120/night
Pros: Rich cultural experience, plenty of dining options, close to major attractions
Cons: Can be overwhelming and noisy

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to August and December)
- Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, and Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or during low season
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during peak season and festivals
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs
- Look for hotels and guesthouses with traditional Moroccan decor and architecture for an authentic experience
- Consider booking a riad, a traditional Moroccan house with a courtyard, for a unique and peaceful stay

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night
- Be mindful of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and markets
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in busy tourist areas
- Consider staying in a neighborhood with a strong police presence, like the Medina in Marrakech
- Look for accommodations with 24-hour reception and security
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on local news and events to avoid any potential safety issues
- Consider hiring a guide or joining a tour group to explore the city and its surroundings safely and efficiently
