# Togo Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Togo can be a relatively safe destination for tourists, but petty crime, scams, and health risks are present, and visitors should exercise caution and stay informed to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Togo is a West African country with a mix of urban and rural areas, each with its own set of safety concerns. While the capital city Lome is generally considered safe, with a strong police presence, petty crime and scams are common, particularly in crowded areas and markets. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The country has experienced periods of political unrest and demonstrations in the past, but these are typically limited to specific areas and can be avoided with proper planning and information.

In general, Togo is a welcoming country, and tourists are often treated with hospitality and respect. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to be mindful of local customs, traditions, and safety guidelines to ensure a positive experience. By taking the necessary precautions and staying informed, visitors can minimize their risk and enjoy the country's rich culture, beautiful landscapes, and friendly people.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Petty theft and pickpocketing are common in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations
- Scams targeting tourists, including fake gemstone sales and overpriced souvenirs, are prevalent in popular tourist areas
- Traffic accidents are a significant risk, particularly when traveling by car or motorcycle
- Muggings and armed robberies can occur, especially at night or in isolated areas
- Credit card scams and ATM fraud are becoming increasingly common, use reputable banks and keep an eye on your accounts
- Local guides or taxi drivers may demand extra fees or tips, agree on prices beforehand and be firm but polite

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid vaccinations are recommended, and malaria prophylaxis is necessary for travel to certain areas
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are limited, especially outside of Lome, and visitors may need to travel to neighboring countries for specialized care
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are available in major cities, but the quality and availability of medications can vary

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas, and keep a close eye on your belongings
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always agree on prices beforehand
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or mobile payments when possible
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel safe to store valuables
- Avoid traveling alone or at night, especially in rural areas
- Stay informed about local conditions and demonstrations, and avoid areas of unrest
- Learn some basic French phrases, such as "bonjour" and "merci," to show respect and appreciation for the local culture

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Solo female travelers should exercise extra caution, especially at night, and consider joining a group tour or using reputable taxi services
- Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events
- Avoid traveling alone in rural areas, and consider hiring a local guide or joining a group tour for added safety and support
- Be prepared for attention and harassment, and stay calm and firm but polite in your interactions with locals
