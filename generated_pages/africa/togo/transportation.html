To get around in Togo, you can utilize a range of transportation options, including taxis, buses, and rental cars, as the country has a limited domestic air network and no rail system, but a relatively well-developed road network.

Getting There

Togo's main international airport is GnassingbÃ© EyadÃ©ma International Airport, located about 35 kilometers northeast of the capital city Lome. The airport receives flights from major airlines, including Air France, Turkish Airlines, and Royal Air Maroc, with direct connections to European and African hubs such as Paris, Istanbul, and Casablanca. Overland entry points are also available, with border crossings from neighboring countries Ghana, Burkina Faso, and Benin. The most popular border crossing is the one from Ghana, which is a major transit point for goods and people.

For those entering Togo by land, there are several bus companies that operate from neighboring countries, including Ghana and Benin. The journey can be long and arduous, but it is a more affordable option for those on a budget. Additionally, there are also private car services and taxis available for hire at the border crossings. It is essential to note that border crossings can be chaotic, and it is recommended to have all necessary documents and paperwork in order to avoid any delays or issues.

Domestic Flights

Togo has a limited domestic air network, with only a few airlines operating within the country. The main domestic airline is ASKY Airlines, which offers flights from Lome to other major cities such as Kara and Niamtougou. Booking domestic flights can be done through the airline's website or at local travel agencies. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $100 to $200 USD, depending on the route and time of booking. It is recommended to book in advance to secure the best rates.

Trains & Rail

Togo has no rail network, making it one of the few countries in Africa without a railway system. As a result, trains are not an option for getting around the country. However, there are plans to develop a rail network in the future, which would significantly improve transportation options and connectivity within the country. In the meantime, travelers must rely on buses, taxis, and rental cars to get around.

Buses & Coaches

There are several bus companies that operate in Togo, offering intercity services to major towns and cities. The most popular bus companies include STIF and UTC, which operate modern and comfortable buses. The quality of the buses varies, but most are well-maintained and offer a safe and reliable way to travel. Routes cover most parts of the country, including the capital city Lome and other major towns such as Kara and Sokode. Costs are relatively affordable, ranging from $5 to $20 USD, depending on the route and class of service.

The city of Lome has a limited public transportation system, with city buses operating on fixed routes. Taxis are widely available and can be hired for short trips or longer journeys. Ride-hailing apps are also available in Lome, but the availability of cars is limited. Tuk-tuks and rickshaws are also available in some parts of the city, offering a cheap and convenient way to get around.

Driving

  • To drive in Togo, you will need a valid international driver's license or a local driver's license.
  • Road conditions in Togo are moderate, with some paved roads and many unpaved roads, especially in rural areas.
  • Fuel costs are relatively high, with petrol prices ranging from $1.20 to $1.50 USD per liter.
  • Car rental companies are available in Lome and other major towns, offering a range of vehicles, including sedans, SUVs, and trucks.
  • When driving in Togo, be aware of the local traffic rules and regulations, including speed limits and right-of-way rules.
  • Hazards on the road include potholes, pedestrians, and livestock, so drivers need to be vigilant and cautious when driving.

Transport Tips

  • Be aware of scams and touts at bus stations and airports, and always negotiate prices before departure.
  • Payment methods include cash, credit cards, and mobile money, but cash is the most widely accepted.
  • Download local apps such as Jumia Travel or HotelNow to book hotels and flights.
  • Be cautious when using taxis or ride-hailing services, and always check the driver's ID and license before departure.
  • Avoid traveling at night, especially on rural roads, due to poor lighting and road conditions.
  • Respect local traffic rules and regulations, and always wear a seatbelt when driving or riding in a vehicle.
  • Learn some basic French phrases to help you navigate and communicate with locals.
  • Be prepared for delays and cancellations, especially during peak travel seasons or in cases of bad weather.
