To enter Togo, citizens of most countries require a visa, which can be obtained on arrival or in advance, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit.

Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Togo before traveling.

Visa Policy Overview

Togo has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival, while others must apply in advance. Citizens of countries such as Benin, Burkina Faso, and Ghana can enter Togo visa-free, while citizens of countries like the United States, Canada, and the European Union can obtain a visa on arrival. The Togolese government has implemented various measures to facilitate travel to the country, including the introduction of an electronic visa system.

The visa policy of Togo is designed to promote tourism and economic development, while also ensuring the security and integrity of the country's borders. The government has established a list of countries whose citizens are eligible for visa-free entry, visa on arrival, or require a visa in advance. It is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it is always best to verify the current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Togo before traveling.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists can obtain a visa on arrival, which is valid for a maximum stay of 7-15 days, depending on their nationality. The typical stay for tourists is 7-10 days, and extensions are possible up to 90 days. To apply for a tourist visa, visitors must provide proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and health insurance.

Business Travelers: Business travelers require a visa, which can be obtained on arrival or in advance. They must provide an invitation letter from a Togolese company, proof of business activities, and other relevant documents. The requirements for business travelers differ from those for tourists, and they may need to provide additional documentation, such as a letter of introduction from their employer.

Students: Students require a student visa, which can be obtained in advance. They must provide proof of enrollment in a Togolese educational institution, a letter of acceptance, and other relevant documents. The duration of stay for students is typically one year, and they must register with the local authorities within 30 days of arrival.

Workers: Workers require a work visa, which can be obtained in advance. They must provide a sponsorship letter from a Togolese employer, proof of qualifications, and other relevant documents. The process for obtaining a work visa can be lengthy, and workers must register with the local authorities within 30 days of arrival.

Documents Checklist

  9. Sponsorship letter from a Togolese employer (for workers)
  10. Other relevant documents, such as a letter of introduction or a certificate of good conduct

Entry Points & Procedures

The main airport in Togo is the LomÃ©-Tokoin International Airport, which receives flights from major airlines. The airport is located about 5 kilometers from the city center and offers various facilities, including currency exchange, restaurants, and car rental services. Land borders are also open, and visitors can enter Togo by road from neighboring countries such as Ghana, Burkina Faso, and Benin. At immigration, visitors will be required to present their passport, visa (if required), and other relevant documents. They will also be asked to complete a declaration form and may be subject to questioning by immigration officers.

Upon arrival, visitors will be required to undergo customs and immigration procedures, which can take some time. It is essential to ensure that all documents are in order and that visitors have the necessary visas and permits. The immigration officers may also ask for proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and health insurance. Visitors should be prepared to provide this information and to answer any questions that the immigration officers may have.

  • Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity

  • Not registering with the local authorities within the required timeframe
