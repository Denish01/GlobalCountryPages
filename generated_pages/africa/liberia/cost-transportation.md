[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Liberia's transportation system is largely underdeveloped, with a lack of **efficient public transportation** options. The majority of the population relies on **minibuses** and **shared taxis** to get around, which can be **inexpensive** but often **unreliable**. The roads in Liberia are generally in poor condition, especially during the **rainy season**, which can make traveling by road challenging. In Monrovia, the capital city, traffic congestion is a significant problem, and **commuting times** can be lengthy.

The quality of public transit in Liberia is **limited**, with few options available for those who cannot afford to use **private transportation**. However, the government has made efforts to **improve the transportation infrastructure**, including the construction of new roads and the introduction of **new bus services**. Despite these efforts, the transportation system in Liberia still **lacks organization** and **reliability**, making it difficult for people to get around, especially in rural areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using **minibuses** or **shared taxis**, which are often **cheaper** than taking a **private taxi**.
- When using a **taxi**, always **negotiate the fare** before you start your journey to avoid being overcharged.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your **belongings**, especially in crowded areas or when using **public transportation**.
- Consider using a **local ride-hailing service**, which can be a **convenient** and **affordable** option.
- Always **wear a seatbelt** when traveling by car, and avoid traveling at **night** if possible.
- Learn some **basic phrases** in the local language, such as "how much" and "where is", to help you navigate and communicate with **transportation providers**.
