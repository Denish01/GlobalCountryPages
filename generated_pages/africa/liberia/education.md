The education system in Liberia is structured to provide students with a comprehensive education, with a focus on primary, secondary, and tertiary education, although it faces challenges in terms of quality and accessibility.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Liberia is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 11, and is designed to provide students with a basic education in subjects such as **English**, **mathematics**, and **science**. Secondary education is not compulsory, but is available to students who complete their primary education and pass a national examination. Tertiary education is available at universities and colleges, and provides students with the opportunity to pursue higher education in a variety of fields. The quality of education in Liberia is a major concern, with many schools lacking **qualified teachers**, **textbooks**, and other essential resources.

The education system in Liberia is also characterized by a significant divide between public and private schools. Public schools are generally underfunded and understaffed, while private schools are often better equipped and staffed, but are also more expensive. This has resulted in a situation where many students are unable to access quality education, particularly in rural areas. Despite these challenges, the government of Liberia has made efforts to improve the education system, including the introduction of **free primary education** and the establishment of a **national education policy**.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- International schools are available in Liberia, particularly in the capital city of Monrovia, and offer a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate (IB)**, **British**, and **American**.
- These schools are generally private and expensive, but provide students with a high-quality education and the opportunity to pursue higher education abroad.
- Some international schools in Liberia also offer **boarding facilities** for students from other parts of the country or from abroad.
- The cost of attending an international school in Liberia can range from $1,000 to $5,000 per year, depending on the school and the level of education.
- International schools in Liberia are often staffed by **qualified teachers** from around the world, and offer a range of **extracurricular activities** and **sports**.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Liberia as a foreigner, students must obtain a **student visa**, which requires a valid passport, proof of admission to a Liberian university, and proof of financial support.
- English is the **official language** of Liberia, and is the language of instruction at all levels of education, so foreign students must have a good command of English to study in Liberia.
- There are several **scholarships** available to foreign students who wish to study in Liberia, including scholarships offered by the Liberian government and by international organizations.
- The cost of living in Liberia is relatively low, but foreign students may still need to budget around $500 to $1,000 per month for **accommodation**, **food**, and **transportation**.
- Foreign students who wish to study in Liberia must also undergo a **medical examination** and provide proof of **health insurance**.
- [CALLOUT] It is essential for foreign students to research the current security situation in Liberia before making a decision to study in the country.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the major challenges facing the education system in Liberia is the lack of **qualified teachers**, particularly in rural areas.
- The education system in Liberia also suffers from a lack of **infrastructure**, including **classrooms**, **libraries**, and **laboratories**.
- Despite these challenges, the government of Liberia has made efforts to improve the education system, including the introduction of **free primary education** and the establishment of a **national education policy**.
- The education system in Liberia also faces challenges related to **equity** and **access**, with many students in rural areas unable to access quality education.
- [RATING] Education Quality: 2/5 
- Recent reforms aimed at improving the education system in Liberia include the introduction of **new curriculum** and the establishment of **teacher training programs**.
