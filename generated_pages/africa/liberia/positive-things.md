[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **National Museum of Liberia** in Monrovia is home to a collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the country's history and culture.
- The **Kpatawee Waterfall** is a popular tourist destination and a significant cultural site, with traditional ceremonies and rituals taking place there.
- The **Liberian National Cultural Festival** is an annual event that celebrates the country's music, dance, and art, with performances and exhibitions by local and international artists.
- The **Bassa Cultural Festival** is a unique celebration of the Bassa people's traditions and customs, with music, dance, and food.
- The **Kru Mask** is an important cultural symbol in Liberia, used in traditional ceremonies and rituals to represent the country's rich cultural heritage.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Ellen Johnson Sirleaf**, the first female president of Liberia, was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2011 for her efforts to promote peace and stability in the country.
- The Liberian diaspora community has made significant contributions to the country's development, with many individuals returning to Liberia to start businesses and invest in the economy.
- **Winston Tubman**, a Liberian politician and lawyer, has played a key role in promoting human rights and the rule of law in Liberia.
- The values of **hospitality** and **community** are highly valued in Liberian culture, with many people placing a strong emphasis on family and social relationships.
