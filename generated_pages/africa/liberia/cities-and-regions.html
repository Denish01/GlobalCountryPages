How Liberia Is Divided

Liberia is divided into 15 counties, each with its own administrative structure and local government. These counties are further subdivided into districts, which are the second-level administrative divisions. The counties are: Bomi, Bong, Gbarpolu, Grand Bassa, Grand Cape Mount, Grand Gedeh, Grand Kru, Lofa, Margibi, Maryland, Montserrado, Nimba, River Cess, River Gee, and Sinoe. Each county has its own unique character, with some being more urbanized and others being more rural.

The administrative structure of Liberia is designed to promote decentralization and local governance, with each county having a significant degree of autonomy. The counties are responsible for providing basic services such as healthcare, education, and infrastructure, while the central government is responsible for national policies and programs. This division of labor allows for more effective governance and development at the local level, and has helped to promote economic growth and stability in Liberia.

Major Cities

Monrovia (1.1M)

Role: capital, economic hub, cultural center

Known For: Monrovia is the largest city in Liberia and is home to many of the country's major institutions, including the University of Liberia and the National Museum. The city has a vibrant cultural scene, with many markets, restaurants, and entertainment venues.

Best For: visitors interested in history, culture, and urban life

Buchanan (300,000)

Role: port city, economic hub

Known For: Buchanan is Liberia's second-largest city and is an important center for trade and commerce. The city has a rich history, with many colonial-era buildings and landmarks.

Best For: visitors interested in history, trade, and industry

Gbarnga (150,000)

Role: regional hub, cultural center

Known For: Gbarnga is the capital of Bong County and is an important center for culture and education. The city is home to Cuttington University, one of Liberia's premier institutions of higher learning.

Best For: visitors interested in education, culture, and rural life

Kakata (100,000)

Role: regional hub, commercial center

Known For: Kakata is a major commercial center in Margibi County and is known for its vibrant markets and trade. The city is also home to many historical landmarks, including the Kakata Museum.

Best For: visitors interested in history, trade, and commerce

Voinjama (50,000)

Role: regional hub, cultural center

Known For: Voinjama is the capital of Lofa County and is an important center for culture and tradition. The city is home to many historical landmarks, including the Voinjama Museum.

Best For: visitors interested in culture, tradition, and rural life

Regional Differences

  • The regions of Liberia differ significantly in terms of culture, with some areas being more traditional and others being more urbanized.
  • The economy of Liberia varies by region, with some areas being more industrialized and others being more agricultural.
  • The climate of Liberia also differs by region, with some areas being more tropical and others being more temperate.
  • The language spoken in Liberia also varies by region, with some areas speaking more English and others speaking more local languages.

Getting Around

Liberia has a relatively underdeveloped transportation network, with few paved roads and limited public transportation options. The country has no domestic flights, and the road network is often in poor condition. However, there are many private buses and taxis available, and many people also use motorcycles or bicycles to get around. The main roads are generally in good condition, but the secondary roads can be rough and may require a 4x4 vehicle. Visitors should be prepared for long journey times and rough road conditions, and should always wear a seatbelt and drive defensively.

For visitors, the best way to get around Liberia is by private car or taxi, as this allows for more flexibility and convenience. However, there are also many bus companies that operate in Liberia, including private companies and government-owned buses. These buses are often crowded and may not be very comfortable, but they are a cheap and convenient way to get around. Visitors should always be careful when using public transportation, and should never carry large amounts of cash or valuables. It is also a good idea to hire a driver or guide, as they can help navigate the roads and provide valuable insights into the local culture.
