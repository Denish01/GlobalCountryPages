Cultural Treasures

  • The National Museum of Liberia in Monrovia is home to a collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the country's history and culture.
  • The Kpatawee Waterfall is a popular tourist destination and a significant cultural site, with traditional ceremonies and rituals taking place there.
  • The Liberian National Cultural Festival is an annual event that celebrates the country's music, dance, and art, with performances and exhibitions by local and international artists.
  • The Bassa Cultural Festival is a unique celebration of the Bassa people's traditions and customs, with music, dance, and food.
  • The Kru Mask is an important cultural symbol in Liberia, used in traditional ceremonies and rituals to represent the country's rich cultural heritage.

People & Global Impact

  • Ellen Johnson Sirleaf, the first female president of Liberia, was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2011 for her efforts to promote peace and stability in the country.
  • The Liberian diaspora community has made significant contributions to the country's development, with many individuals returning to Liberia to start businesses and invest in the economy.
  • Winston Tubman, a Liberian politician and lawyer, has played a key role in promoting human rights and the rule of law in Liberia.
  • The values of hospitality and community are highly valued in Liberian culture, with many people placing a strong emphasis on family and social relationships.

What Visitors Love

  • The warm and welcoming hospitality of the Liberian people, with many visitors commenting on the friendly and generous nature of the locals.
  • The delicious and varied food, with popular dishes including jollof rice, fufu, and pepper pot soup.
  • The stunning natural scenery, with Liberia's coastline, mountains, and forests offering plenty of opportunities for hiking, surfing, and wildlife spotting.
  • The vibrant and lively markets, with colorful stalls selling everything from fresh produce to handicrafts and souvenirs.
  • The historic landmarks, including the Liberia National Museum and the Centennial Pavilion, which offer a glimpse into the country's rich history and culture.
  • The exciting and energetic nightlife, with many bars, clubs, and restaurants staying open late into the night.

Hidden Gems

  • The Liberian National Forest is a hidden gem, with its lush vegetation and diverse wildlife offering a unique and unforgettable experience for visitors.
  • The Lake Piso is a beautiful and tranquil spot, with its calm waters and stunning scenery making it a popular destination for boat trips and fishing.
  • The Gola Forest is a protected area that is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including many endangered species, and offers a unique opportunity for hiking and wildlife spotting.
  • The Cape Mesurado is a historic peninsula that is home to the Liberian National Museum and offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and the surrounding coastline.
