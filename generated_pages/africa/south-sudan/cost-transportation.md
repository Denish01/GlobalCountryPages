[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
South Sudan has a limited public transportation system, with most people relying on **minibuses** and **shared taxis** to get around. The quality of public transit varies greatly between cities, with Juba, the capital, having a slightly more developed system than other urban areas. In rural areas, transportation options are scarce, and many people walk or use **bicycle taxis**. The lack of infrastructure and high inflation rate of 91.4% make transportation costs volatile and subject to change.

The main transportation modes in South Sudan are **road-based**, with a network of roads connecting major cities and towns. However, many of these roads are in poor condition, especially during the rainy season, which can make travel difficult and expensive. Despite these challenges, the government is working to improve the transportation network, with plans to upgrade roads and introduce new public transportation systems.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using **minibuses** or **shared taxis**, which are often cheaper than metered taxis.
- When using taxis, always **negotiate the fare** before starting your journey, as meters are not always used.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid traveling alone in **unmarked vehicles**.
- Download local transportation apps, such as **Mula**, to help you navigate the city and find affordable transportation options.
- Consider hiring a **private driver** for long-distance trips, as this can be safer and more convenient than using public transportation.
- Always **check the condition** of your vehicle before renting, as road conditions in South Sudan can be challenging.
