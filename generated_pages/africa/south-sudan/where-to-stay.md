# Where to Stay in South Sudan — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting South Sudan, the best areas to stay are in the capital city of Juba, which offers a range of accommodations and amenities for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Juba, here's what you can expect:
**Juba Town** (Juba)
Best For: business travelers
Vibe: Juba Town is the central business district, with a mix of old and new buildings, and a bustling atmosphere, especially during the day. The area is also home to many government offices and international organizations.
Price Range: $50-$200/night
Pros: proximity to government offices, easy access to transportation, variety of restaurants and shops
Cons: traffic congestion, noise pollution

**Konyokonyo** (Juba)
Best For: luxury travelers
Vibe: Konyokonyo is an upscale neighborhood with modern houses and apartments, known for its tranquility and security. The area is popular with expats and wealthy locals.
Price Range: $100-$500/night
Pros: modern amenities, secure environment, scenic views of the Nile River
Cons: limited access to public transportation, high prices

**Munuki** (Juba)
Best For: family travelers
Vibe: Munuki is a residential area with a mix of old and new houses, known for its quiet and peaceful atmosphere. The area is popular with families and locals.
Price Range: $30-$150/night
Pros: affordable prices, family-friendly environment, proximity to schools and shops
Cons: limited access to amenities, traffic congestion during peak hours

**Tongping** (Juba)
Best For: backpackers
Vibe: Tongping is a lively neighborhood with a mix of shops, restaurants, and bars, known for its vibrant atmosphere and affordable prices. The area is popular with young travelers and locals.
Price Range: $10-$50/night
Pros: affordable prices, lively atmosphere, variety of food options
Cons: noise pollution, limited access to amenities

** Jebel** (Juba)
Best For: budget travelers
Vibe: Jebel is a hilly neighborhood with a mix of old and new houses, known for its stunning views of the city and affordable prices. The area is popular with budget travelers and locals.
Price Range: $20-$100/night
Pros: affordable prices, scenic views, proximity to markets and shops
Cons: steep hills, limited access to amenities

**Gudele** (Juba)
Best For: mid-range travelers
Vibe: Gudele is a residential area with a mix of old and new houses, known for its quiet and peaceful atmosphere. The area is popular with mid-range travelers and locals.
Price Range: $40-$200/night
Pros: affordable prices, family-friendly environment, proximity to schools and shops
Cons: limited access to amenities, traffic congestion during peak hours

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (December to February)
- Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and options
- Negotiate prices with hotel owners or managers, especially for long-term stays
- Consider booking apartments or guesthouses for longer stays or with families
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during peak season
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure safety and proximity to amenities
- Contact the accommodation provider directly to confirm booking and ask about any additional fees or services

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas near the border with Sudan, due to ongoing conflict and instability
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas
- Keep valuables secure and be cautious of petty theft or scams
- Consider hiring a local guide or driver to navigate the city and surrounding areas
- Stay informed about local events and demonstrations, which can affect traffic and safety
- Keep emergency contact numbers handy, such as the local police or embassy, in case of an emergency
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when interacting with locals or visiting cultural sites
