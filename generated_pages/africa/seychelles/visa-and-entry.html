Visa Policy Overview

Seychelles has a relatively liberal visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival. The country's visa stance is designed to promote tourism and facilitate travel for business and other purposes. Currently, citizens of over 140 countries can enter Seychelles without a visa, while others can obtain a visa on arrival or through an eVisa system. The visa policy is subject to change, so it's essential to check the latest requirements before planning a trip to Seychelles.

The Seychelles government has implemented a visa policy that aims to balance the need to facilitate travel with the need to maintain national security and control over immigration. The country's visa-free access policy is one of the most liberal in the region, making it an attractive destination for tourists and business travelers alike. Whether you're planning a short trip or an extended stay, it's essential to understand the visa requirements and regulations that apply to your nationality and purpose of visit.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists can enter Seychelles visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival, depending on their nationality. The typical stay for tourists is 90 days, but this can be extended for an additional 90 days with approval from the immigration authorities. To extend their stay, tourists must apply for an extension at least 24 hours before their current visa expires.

Business Travelers: Business travelers may require a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must provide an invitation letter from a Seychelles-based company, proof of sufficient funds, and a valid passport. Business travelers may also require a work permit, which can be obtained through the Seychelles Ministry of Employment.

Students: Students must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a Seychelles-based educational institution, a valid passport, and sufficient funds. The duration of the student visa is typically one year, but it can be extended for an additional year with approval from the immigration authorities.

Workers: Workers must obtain a work permit, which requires sponsorship from a Seychelles-based employer. The work permit is typically valid for one year, but it can be extended for an additional year with approval from the immigration authorities.

Documents Checklist

  1. A valid passport with at least six months' validity
  2. Proof of onward travel, such as a return ticket or a reservation for onward travel
  3. Proof of sufficient funds, such as cash, traveler's checks, or credit cards
  4. A completed arrival/departure card, which will be provided by the airline or at the port of entry
  5. A health certificate, if required by the Seychelles authorities
  6. A yellow fever vaccination certificate, if traveling from a country with a risk of yellow fever transmission
  7. A police certificate, if required by the Seychelles authorities
  8. A copy of your travel itinerary, including your flight itinerary and hotel reservation

Entry Points & Procedures

The main entry points to Seychelles are the Seychelles International Airport (SEZ) and the port of Victoria. Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and complete an arrival/departure card. The immigration officer will then stamp their passport and provide a visa, if required. Visitors must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country, including gifts, souvenirs, and other items.

The entry procedures in Seychelles are relatively straightforward, but visitors must ensure that they have all the required documents and follow the correct procedures to avoid any delays or issues. It's also essential to be aware of the customs regulations and declare any restricted or prohibited items to avoid any penalties or fines.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
  • Not having proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
  • Not completing the arrival/departure card correctly
  • Not declaring any goods or gifts that are subject to customs regulations
  • Not having the required vaccinations or health certificates
  • Not checking the latest visa requirements and regulations before traveling to Seychelles
