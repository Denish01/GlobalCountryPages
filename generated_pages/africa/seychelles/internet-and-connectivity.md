# Seychelles Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Seychelles is generally of good quality, with reliable connections available in urban areas, making it easy for tourists and remote workers to stay connected.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Seychelles is relatively well-developed, with a good average internet speed of 10 Mbps download, making it suitable for general browsing, streaming, and online work. However, there is a noticeable urban vs rural divide, with faster and more reliable connections available in urban areas like Victoria, while rural areas may experience slower speeds and less reliable connections. Despite this, the overall internet infrastructure is relatively reliable, with minimal downtime and outages, making it a good destination for remote workers and digital nomads.

In terms of internet quality, Seychelles has made significant progress in recent years, with investments in fiber-optic cables and mobile network upgrades. This has resulted in improved speeds and coverage, especially in tourist areas and major towns. However, as with many island nations, there may be occasional outages or slow speeds due to weather conditions or maintenance, but overall, the internet in Seychelles is suitable for most needs.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Seychelles, tourists can visit one of the main carrier's stores, such as Cable & Wireless Seychelles or Airtel Seychelles, and purchase a prepaid SIM card. The process is relatively straightforward, requiring a valid passport and a small fee, approximately 10 USD. The recommended carriers offer a range of data plans, including tourist-friendly options with generous data allowances and affordable prices. It's also possible to purchase SIM cards at the airport or at local shops, but the main carrier stores usually offer the best deals and support.

When purchasing a SIM card, it's essential to check the coverage and data speeds in the areas you plan to visit, as well as the cost of data roaming and any additional fees. Some carriers may also offer special promotions or discounts for tourists, so it's worth inquiring about these when purchasing your SIM card.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels and resorts offering free WiFi to their guests, often with speeds suitable for streaming and online work.
- Cafe WiFi culture is also well-established, with many cafes and restaurants offering free WiFi to customers, making it easy to find a spot to work or relax with a coffee.
- Coworking spaces are available in Victoria and other major towns, offering a productive environment and reliable internet connections.
- Public WiFi is also available in some areas, such as airports, shopping centers, and tourist information offices, although speeds and reliability may vary.
- Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while enjoying the local cuisine and nightlife.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like the Seychelles Coworking Space in Victoria offer a productive environment and reliable internet connections, making them ideal for remote workers.
- Reliable cafe chains like Cafe del Mar and La Boussole offer good WiFi and a comfortable working environment, making them popular spots for digital nomads.
- Backup internet options like mobile hotspots or portable WiFi routers can be useful in case of outages or slow speeds, especially in rural areas.
- VPNs are not strictly necessary, but they can be useful for accessing geo-restricted content or ensuring secure connections, especially when using public WiFi.
- Some hotels and resorts also offer coworking facilities or business centers, which can be a good option for remote workers who need a more formal working environment.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a significant concern in Seychelles, but some sites may be blocked due to local laws or regulations.
- Using a VPN can help access geo-restricted content and ensure secure connections, especially when using public WiFi.
- Roaming alternatives like purchasing a local SIM card or using a portable WiFi router can be more cost-effective than relying on international roaming.
- eSIM options are not widely available, but some carriers may offer eSIM plans for compatible devices.
- It's essential to check the terms and conditions of your data plan, including any data limits or speed restrictions, to avoid unexpected charges or throttling.
- Public WiFi networks may not always be secure, so it's a good idea to use a VPN or take other precautions to protect your device and data.
