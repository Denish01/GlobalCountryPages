# Where to Stay in Seychelles — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To find the best place to stay in Seychelles, consider the top areas of Mahe, Praslin, and La Digue, which offer a range of accommodations catering to different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Beau Vallon** (Mahe)
Best For: Families/Luxury
Vibe: Beau Vallon is a picturesque bay with a long stretch of sandy beach, offering a relaxed and serene atmosphere, perfect for families and luxury seekers, with a range of high-end resorts and hotels.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, range of water sports, and proximity to Victoria, the capital city, make it an ideal location for families and those seeking luxury.
Cons: Can be crowded during peak season, and prices may be steep for budget travelers.

**Anse Georgette** (Praslin)
Best For: Couples/Luxury
Vibe: Anse Georgette is a secluded and picturesque beach with crystal-clear waters, offering a romantic and intimate atmosphere, perfect for couples and honeymooners.
Price Range: $300-$600/night
Pros: Stunning beach, luxurious resorts, and proximity to the Vallée de Mai nature reserve, make it an ideal location for couples and nature lovers.
Cons: Limited dining and entertainment options, and can be difficult to access without a car.

**La Digue** (La Digue)
Best For: Backpackers/Adventure
Vibe: La Digue is a charming and laid-back island with a strong emphasis on eco-tourism, offering a unique and authentic experience for backpackers and adventure seekers.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Affordable accommodations, beautiful beaches, and a range of outdoor activities, such as biking and hiking, make it an ideal location for budget travelers and adventure seekers.
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and can be difficult to access without a ferry.

**Victoria** (Mahe)
Best For: Business/Luxury
Vibe: Victoria is the capital city of Seychelles, offering a bustling and vibrant atmosphere, with a range of high-end hotels and amenities, perfect for business travelers and luxury seekers.
Price Range: $250-$500/night
Pros: Range of amenities, including restaurants, bars, and shops, proximity to the port and airport, and a range of business facilities, make it an ideal location for business travelers.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, and prices may be steep for budget travelers.

**Anse Source d'Argent** (La Digue)
Best For: Families/Adventure
Vibe: Anse Source d'Argent is a stunning beach with unique granite formations, offering a relaxed and adventurous atmosphere, perfect for families and nature lovers.
Price Range: $150-$350/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, range of outdoor activities, and proximity to the La Digue village, make it an ideal location for families and adventure seekers.
Cons: Limited dining and entertainment options, and can be difficult to access without a car.

**Anse Volbert** (Praslin)
Best For: Luxury/Families
Vibe: Anse Volbert is a picturesque bay with a long stretch of sandy beach, offering a relaxed and luxurious atmosphere, perfect for families and luxury seekers.
Price Range: $250-$500/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, range of water sports, and proximity to the Praslin airport, make it an ideal location for families and those seeking luxury.
Cons: Can be crowded during peak season, and prices may be steep for budget travelers.
