[SECTION] Supermarket vs Local Market [/SECTION]
The prices of groceries in Seychelles can vary significantly depending on whether you shop at a supermarket or a local market. Supermarkets tend to be more expensive, especially for imported goods, but they often offer a wider selection and more convenient shopping experience. Local markets, on the other hand, offer fresh produce and a more authentic shopping experience, but the prices can be lower, especially for **local staples** like fruits, vegetables, and seafood. It's worth noting that bargaining is not typically expected at supermarkets, but it is more common at local markets, especially for bulk purchases.

When shopping at local markets, it's a good idea to **haggle** politely and to be respectful of the vendors. You can often get a better price by buying in bulk or by shopping during the off-season. Additionally, local markets often offer a wider selection of **fresh produce**, including fruits, vegetables, and seafood, which can be a great way to experience the local cuisine and to support the local economy. Overall, a combination of supermarket and local market shopping can offer the best of both worlds, with a wide selection of goods and a chance to experience the local culture.

- The **cheapest way to eat** in Seychelles is to buy **street food** or to eat at **local eateries**, which offer a range of affordable and delicious options.

- **Fresh seafood** is abundant in Seychelles and can be a great way to experience the local cuisine, especially when bought directly from the fishermen or at local markets.
- It's a good idea to **avoid buying imported goods**, especially if you're on a budget, as they can be expensive and may not be as fresh as local alternatives.
