Overall Safety Assessment

Seychelles is considered a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates compared to other countries. The island nation has a strong focus on tourism, and as such, the government has implemented various measures to ensure visitor safety. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to minimize risks. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and theft can occur, particularly in crowded areas or tourist hotspots. Additionally, Seychelles is prone to natural disasters like cyclones and floods, which can impact travel plans.

The Seychellois government has taken steps to address safety concerns, including the establishment of a tourist police unit. This specialized unit is dedicated to assisting visitors and providing guidance on safety matters. Moreover, the local community is generally welcoming and friendly, with many Seychellois people speaking English, French, or Seychellois Creole, making it relatively easy for tourists to communicate and navigate the islands. Overall, Seychelles is a wonderful destination for travelers, offering a unique blend of stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations
  • Credit card scams can occur, so it's essential to monitor your accounts and keep your cards secure
  • Overcharging by taxi drivers or vendors can happen, so it's crucial to agree on prices beforehand
  • Snorkeling and diving accidents can occur due to strong currents or inadequate equipment, so choose reputable operators
  • Cyclones and floods can impact travel plans, so stay informed about weather conditions
  • Road accidents can happen, especially when driving at night or on unlit roads, so exercise caution
  • Food poisoning can occur from consuming undercooked or contaminated food, so choose reputable restaurants and food stalls

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended
  • Water Safety: Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Seychelles has well-equipped medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, but medical evacuation may be necessary for serious conditions
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are readily available in major towns, but some medications may be in short supply

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always lock your accommodation and secure your valuables
  • Use reputable taxi services or licensed tour operators
  • Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas and keep an eye on your drinks
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or attending cultural events
  • Stay informed about weather conditions and natural disaster warnings
  • Choose licensed diving and snorkeling operators to minimize the risk of accidents
  • Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuables

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Seychelles is generally a safe destination for solo female travelers, but it's still essential to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting temples or attending cultural events
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in isolated areas, and use reputable taxi services instead
  • Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary and plans
