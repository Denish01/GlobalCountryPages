[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Seychelles is divided into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. **Primary education** is compulsory for all children between the ages of 5 and 11 and is provided free of charge by the government. The primary education curriculum includes subjects such as mathematics, science, English, French, and Seychellois Creole. **Secondary education** is also compulsory and is provided for students between the ages of 12 and 16. The secondary education curriculum includes a wide range of subjects, including mathematics, science, English, French, and social studies. **Tertiary education** is provided by the University of Seychelles and the Seychelles Polytechnic, which offer a range of academic programs in fields such as business, education, and health sciences.

The quality of education in Seychelles is generally high, with a strong emphasis on **academic achievement** and **personal development**. The government has invested heavily in education, with a significant proportion of the national budget allocated to education. As a result, the education system in Seychelles is well-equipped with modern facilities and technology, and students have access to a wide range of resources and support services. However, there are some challenges facing the education system, including a shortage of qualified teachers in certain subjects and a lack of opportunities for **vocational training**.

The education system in Seychelles is predominantly public, with the government providing free education to all citizens. However, there are also a number of private schools and institutions that offer alternative educational programs. These private institutions are often **fee-paying** and may offer specialized programs or courses that are not available in the public system.
