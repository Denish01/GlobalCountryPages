[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Gambia is generally a safe country to visit, with low rates of violent crime. However, as with any travel destination, there are certain precautions that visitors should take to ensure their safety. The country has a relatively stable government and a strong focus on tourism, which has contributed to its overall safety. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, can be a problem in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Despite the overall safety of Gambia, there are some areas that visitors should avoid, particularly at night. The country has a high unemployment rate, and this can lead to petty crime and scams in some areas. Additionally, the country's roads can be hazardous, particularly at night, and visitors should exercise caution when traveling by road. Overall, Gambia is a safe destination for visitors who take normal precautions and are aware of their surroundings.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **scams** involving friendly locals who offer to show you around or sell you souvenirs, as these can be overpriced or non-existent.
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas, particularly in markets and tourist hotspots.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use reputable **taxi** services or **tour operators**.
- Be cautious of **ATM scams**, and use machines located in secure areas such as banks or hotels.
- Be aware of **petty crime** in areas such as Serrekunda and Brikama, and take normal precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.
- Avoid **walking alone at night**, particularly in areas with poor lighting or high crime rates.
- Be cautious of **over-friendly locals** who may be trying to scam or rob you.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for travel to Gambia.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in Gambia are limited, and visitors may need to be evacuated to a nearby country for serious medical treatment. It is recommended that visitors have **travel insurance** that covers medical evacuation.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacy access is limited in Gambia, and visitors should bring any necessary medications with them.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas or at night.
- Use reputable taxi services or tour operators, and always agree on a price before you start your journey.
- Keep valuables secure, and use a hotel safe or a money belt to protect your belongings.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead.
- Be cautious of scams and petty crime, and trust your instincts if something seems suspicious.
- Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen, as the sun can be strong in Gambia.
- Learn some basic Wolof phrases, such as "ba beneen" (hello) and "jërëjëf" (thank you), to help you communicate with locals.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
