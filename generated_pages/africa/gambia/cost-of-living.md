[SECTION] How Expensive Is Gambia? [/SECTION]
Gambia is considered one of the most affordable countries in West Africa, offering a great balance of low costs and exciting experiences for travelers. Compared to its neighboring country, Senegal, Gambia has a lower cost of living, making it an attractive destination for budget-conscious travelers. The country's economy is primarily driven by agriculture, tourism, and remittances, which contributes to its relatively low prices. Overall, Gambia is an excellent choice for travelers looking to explore Africa without breaking the bank.

In terms of global averages, Gambia's cost of living is significantly lower than in many Western countries. However, prices can vary depending on the location, with the capital city Banjul and the tourist areas tend to be more expensive than the rest of the country. Despite this, Gambia remains an affordable destination, offering a unique and enriching experience for visitors.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in budget-friendly accommodations, such as hostels or guesthouses, to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls to experience the local cuisine at affordable prices.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transport costs.
- Avoid exchanging money at airports or hotels, as the rates tend to be unfavorable.
- Learn some basic Wolof phrases, such as "ba beneen" (hello), to show respect for the local culture and potentially receive better prices.
- Bargain at markets and from vendors, as it's a common practice in Gambia, to get the best deals on souvenirs and other items.
