# Where to Stay in Gambia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To find the best place to stay in Gambia, consider the coastal areas of Kololi, Kotu, and Cape Point, which offer a mix of relaxation, culture, and entertainment options for various types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Gambia, the unique character and advantages make them suitable for different types of travelers. 
**Kololi** (Kololi)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families
Vibe: Kololi is known for its vibrant nightlife, beautiful beaches, and wide range of restaurants and bars, making it an ideal spot for those looking for a lively atmosphere. The area is also home to several high-end resorts and hotels, catering to luxury travelers.
Price Range: $150-$500/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, wide range of dining options
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded during peak season

**Kotu** (Kotu)
Best For: Backpackers and budget travelers
Vibe: Kotu is a laid-back and affordable area, popular among backpackers and budget travelers, with several guesthouses and hostels available. The area is also close to the Kotu Stream, which is home to a variety of birdlife.
Price Range: $20-$100/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, close to nature reserves, relaxed atmosphere
Cons: Limited nightlife and dining options

**Cape Point** (Cape Point)
Best For: Families and those looking for relaxation
Vibe: Cape Point is a quiet and peaceful area, perfect for families and those looking to unwind. The area is home to several beautiful beaches and a few restaurants and bars.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, peaceful atmosphere, family-friendly
Cons: Limited nightlife and dining options

**Bakau** (Bakau)
Best For: Business travelers and those looking for a local experience
Vibe: Bakau is a bustling town with a mix of local markets, restaurants, and bars. The area is also home to several business centers and conference facilities, making it suitable for business travelers.
Price Range: $50-$200/night
Pros: Local markets and restaurants, business facilities, close to Banjul
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy

**Serrekunda** (Serrekunda)
Best For: Budget travelers and those looking for a local experience
Vibe: Serrekunda is a large and vibrant town, with a mix of local markets, restaurants, and bars. The area is also home to several affordable accommodation options, making it popular among budget travelers.
Price Range: $20-$100/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, local markets and restaurants, vibrant atmosphere
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy

**Fajara** (Fajara)
Best For: Luxury travelers and those looking for a relaxing atmosphere
Vibe: Fajara is a quiet and peaceful area, with several high-end resorts and hotels available. The area is also home to a beautiful beach and a few restaurants and bars.
Price Range: $150-$500/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, peaceful atmosphere, luxury accommodation options
Cons: Limited nightlife and dining options

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to April), to ensure availability and the best rates.
- Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider negotiating the price, especially for longer stays or during the off-season.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with rates increasing during peak season and decreasing during the off-season.
- Read reviews and check the location of the accommodation before booking.
- Look for accommodation options that offer free Wi-Fi, breakfast, and other amenities to enhance your stay.
- Contact the accommodation provider directly to inquire about any special offers or discounts.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid walking alone at night, especially in isolated areas, and use licensed taxis or transport services.
- Keep valuables secure and be mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas.
- Be aware of your surroundings and avoid displaying signs of wealth.
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night.
- Consider staying in areas with 24-hour security and reception.
- Research the location of your accommodation and its proximity to local amenities, transport, and attractions before booking.
