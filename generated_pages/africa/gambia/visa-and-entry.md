# Gambia Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Gambia before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Gambia has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival. The country offers visa-free access to citizens of over 100 countries, including the European Union, the United States, and several African nations. This liberal approach aims to promote tourism and economic growth in the country. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it is crucial to verify the current requirements before traveling to Gambia.

Gambia's visa policy is designed to balance the need to facilitate travel and tourism with the need to maintain national security and regulate immigration. The country has a relatively straightforward visa application process, and visitors can obtain a visa on arrival at the airport or through the embassy or consulate of Gambia in their home country. The visa policy is subject to change, and it is recommended to check with the official embassy or consulate of Gambia for the latest information.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can obtain a visa on arrival at the airport, which is valid for up to 90 days. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, and extensions can be obtained through the immigration department. Tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a **Business Visa**, which must be obtained in advance through the embassy or consulate of Gambia. They must provide an invitation letter from a Gambian company, proof of business activities, and other supporting documents.
**Students:** Students must obtain a **Student Visa**, which requires proof of enrollment at a Gambian educational institution, a letter of acceptance, and other supporting documents. The duration of the student visa is typically one year, and it can be renewed annually.
**Workers:** Workers must obtain a **Work Permit**, which requires sponsorship from a Gambian employer. The employer must demonstrate that the worker has the necessary skills and qualifications for the job, and the worker must provide proof of qualifications and experience.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry point to Gambia is the Banjul International Airport, which receives flights from major European and African cities. Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration desk, where they will be required to present their passport, visa (if required), and other supporting documents. The immigration officer will review the documents and may ask questions about the purpose of the visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements. Visitors must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country, including gifts, souvenirs, and electronic devices.

Land borders are also available, and visitors can enter Gambia through the border with Senegal. However, it is recommended to use the official border crossing points, as some areas may be restricted or require special permits. At the border, visitors will be required to present their passport, visa (if required), and other supporting documents, and may be subject to customs and immigration checks.

- Failing to verify the current visa requirements before traveling to Gambia
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not providing sufficient proof of onward travel or accommodation arrangements
- Not declaring goods or gifts at the border or airport
- Not obtaining the necessary vaccinations, including yellow fever
- Not having travel insurance documents or proof of sufficient funds to support stay
