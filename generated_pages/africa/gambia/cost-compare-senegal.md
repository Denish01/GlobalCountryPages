[SECTION] Overview: Gambia vs Senegal [/SECTION]
Gambia and Senegal are two neighboring countries in West Africa, with distinct economic profiles and cost structures. Gambia has a smaller economy and a lower GDP per capita, which contributes to its lower cost of living. The country's economy is primarily driven by agriculture, tourism, and remittances, which has led to a relatively low cost of living. In contrast, Senegal has a more diversified economy, with a stronger industrial sector and a higher GDP per capita, resulting in a slightly higher cost of living. Overall, Gambia is a more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees, while Senegal may offer more opportunities for business and career development.

The cost of living in Gambia is generally lower due to the country's smaller economy and lower demand for goods and services. Housing, food, and transportation costs are significantly lower in Gambia, making it an attractive option for those looking for an affordable lifestyle. However, Senegal offers more modern amenities and a wider range of services, which may be appealing to those who value convenience and comfort. The difference in cost of living between the two countries is around 15-20%, with Gambia being the more affordable option.
