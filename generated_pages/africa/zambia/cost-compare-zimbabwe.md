[SECTION] Overview: Zambia vs Zimbabwe [/SECTION]
Zambia and Zimbabwe are two neighboring countries in southern Africa, both with unique economies and cost structures. Zambia has a more stable economy, driven by its rich natural resources, including copper, cobalt, and gold. Zimbabwe, on the other hand, has faced significant economic challenges in recent years, including hyperinflation and a decline in its manufacturing sector. As a result, Zambia tends to be more expensive in terms of luxury goods and services, while Zimbabwe is generally cheaper for basic necessities.

The cost of living in Zambia is around 15-20% lower than in Zimbabwe, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees. However, Zimbabwe has a more developed tourism infrastructure, with more options for accommodation, dining, and entertainment. Additionally, Zimbabwe has a more vibrant cultural scene, with a rich history and diverse wildlife. Overall, the choice between Zambia and Zimbabwe depends on individual priorities and lifestyle preferences.
