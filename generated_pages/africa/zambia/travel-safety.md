# Zambia Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Zambia is generally considered a safe country to visit, with most tourists experiencing a trouble-free trip, but as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Zambia is a vast and diverse country, with a range of safety conditions depending on the location and activity. While most tourists visit Zambia without incident, there are some areas and situations that require caution. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, is a concern in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, while violent crime is relatively rare. Additionally, Zambia has a high risk of road accidents, particularly at night, due to poor road conditions and inadequate lighting. However, with some basic precautions and awareness, visitors can minimize their risk and have a safe and enjoyable trip.

Overall, Zambia is considered a relatively safe country, with a strong emphasis on hospitality and welcoming visitors. The Zambian people are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and most tourists find the country to be a warm and inviting place to visit. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions to stay safe. This includes being mindful of your surroundings, keeping valuables secure, and avoiding traveling alone at night.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **ATM scams**, where thieves use card skimming devices to steal your card information
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations
- Be mindful of **overcharging** by taxi drivers and vendors, and always agree on a price before starting a journey or making a purchase
- Be cautious of **wildlife**, particularly in national parks and game reserves, and follow all instructions from guides and rangers
- Be aware of **road accidents**, particularly at night, and always wear a seatbelt and drive defensively
- Be cautious of **drink spiking**, particularly in bars and nightclubs, and never leave your drink unattended
- Be mindful of **border crossings**, particularly with neighboring countries, and be aware of any requirements or restrictions

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid are recommended, while malaria prophylaxis is also necessary in some areas
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are generally good in major towns, but can be limited in rural areas, and medical insurance is recommended
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are available in major towns, but can be limited in rural areas, and it's recommended to bring any necessary medications with you

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **copy of your passport** and leave the original in a safe place
- Be mindful of your **surroundings**, particularly in crowded areas, and keep valuables secure
- Always **use a reputable taxi service** or ride-sharing app, and agree on a price before starting a journey
- Be cautious of **strangers** who approach you, particularly in tourist areas, and never carry large amounts of cash
- Always **follow local advice** and instructions, particularly in national parks and game reserves
- Be mindful of **local customs** and traditions, particularly in rural areas, and dress modestly and respect local norms
- Always **keep your hotel room door locked** and use the hotel safe to store valuables

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of **local customs** and traditions, particularly in rural areas, and dress modestly and respect local norms
- Be cautious of **harassment**, particularly in crowded areas, and avoid traveling alone at night
- Always **stay in touch** with family and friends back home, and keep them informed of your itinerary and whereabouts
- Be aware of **local support services**, such as women's organizations and crisis centers, and know how to access them if needed
