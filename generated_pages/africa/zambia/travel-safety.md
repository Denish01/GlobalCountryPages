# Zambia Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Zambia is generally a safe country to visit, with low to moderate crime rates, but tourists should still be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Zambia is a vast and diverse country, with a mix of urban and rural areas, each with its own unique safety profile. While the country has a reputation for being relatively safe, there are still areas where tourists should exercise caution, particularly at night. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, is common in crowded areas like markets and bus stations, and tourists should be mindful of their belongings. Additionally, there have been reports of scams targeting tourists, particularly in popular destinations like Victoria Falls.

Overall, Zambia is a great destination for tourists, with many wonderful experiences to be had, from safaris and wildlife viewing to cultural exploration and adventure activities. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions to stay safe. By being informed and taking common-sense precautions, tourists can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Zambia.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be wary of **scams** targeting tourists, particularly in popular destinations like Victoria Falls, where touts may offer fake tours or activities.
- **Pickpocketing** and **theft** are common in crowded areas like markets and bus stations, so keep a close eye on your belongings.
- **Traffic accidents** are a significant risk in Zambia, particularly on rural roads, so be cautious when driving or taking taxis.
- **Wildlife** can be a hazard in national parks and game reserves, so follow park rules and stay with guides.
- **Muggings** can occur in urban areas, particularly at night, so avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas.
- **Border crossings** can be chaotic and prone to scams, so be cautious when crossing into or out of Zambia.
- **ATM scams** have been reported in some areas, so be careful when using ATMs and cover the keypad when entering your PIN.
- **Food and waterborne illnesses** can be a risk, particularly when eating at street vendors or drinking tap water, so take necessary precautions to stay healthy.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Recommended vaccinations include **Hepatitis A**, **Typhoid**, and **Meningitis**, and required vaccinations include **Yellow Fever** if traveling from or through an infected area.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are limited in rural areas, but major cities like Lusaka and Livingstone have decent hospitals and medical facilities. Travel insurance is highly recommended.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are available in major cities, but access to certain medications may be limited.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas, and keep a close eye on your belongings.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-sharing services, and always agree on a fare before starting your journey.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead.
- Follow park rules and stay with guides when visiting national parks and game reserves.
- Be cautious when crossing roads, as traffic can be chaotic in urban areas.
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel safe if available.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, particularly in rural areas.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious when traveling alone, particularly at night, and consider joining a group tour or using a reputable taxi service.
- Dress modestly and be respectful of local customs, particularly in rural areas.
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas, and stick to well-lit and populated areas.
- Keep in touch with family and friends back home, and let them know your itinerary and whereabouts.
