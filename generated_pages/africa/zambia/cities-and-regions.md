[SECTION] How Zambia Is Divided [/SECTION]
Zambia is a sovereign country located in southern Africa, and its administrative structure is divided into 10 provinces, each with its own provincial capital. These provinces are further divided into districts, which are then subdivided into wards and villages. The 10 provinces of Zambia are: Central, Copperbelt, Eastern, Luapula, Lusaka, Muchinga, Northern, North-Western, Southern, and Western. This division allows for effective governance and management of the country's resources and population.

The provinces of Zambia are diverse in terms of their geography, economy, and culture. Some provinces, such as the Copperbelt, are heavily industrialized and urbanized, while others, such as the Eastern Province, are more rural and agricultural. [SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
Zambia has a well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis available. However, the road network in Zambia can be challenging, especially during the rainy season, and visitors are advised to exercise caution when driving.
