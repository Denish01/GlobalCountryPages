[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Mosi-oa-Tunya** (Victoria Falls) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World, attracting millions of visitors each year.
- The **Kazembe** tradition is an important part of Zambian cultural heritage, with a focus on traditional music, dance, and storytelling.
- The **Tonga** people are known for their skilled craftsmanship, particularly in woodcarving and basket-weaving.
- The **Lusaka National Museum** showcases Zambian art and culture, with a collection of traditional artifacts and contemporary art pieces.
- The **Kapiri Mposhi** district is home to a number of important cultural sites, including the **Kapiri Mposhi** rock art site.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Kenneth Kaunda**, the first president of Zambia, played a key role in the country's independence movement and was a prominent figure in African politics.
- **Slade Mulungu**, a Zambian-born entrepreneur, has made significant contributions to the development of the African tech industry.
- The Zambian diaspora community has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Zambians working in fields such as medicine, engineering, and finance.
- **Inonge Wina**, the first female vice-president of Zambia, has been a strong advocate for women's rights and education.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **Zambian people** are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature, making visitors feel at home.
- The **Zambian cuisine** is a unique blend of traditional and modern flavors, with popular dishes such as **nshima** and **kapenta**.
- The **South Luangwa National Park** is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Zambia, with its stunning scenery and diverse wildlife.
- The **Victoria Falls** is a must-see attraction, with its breathtaking views and exciting adventure activities.
- The **Lusaka** city is a hub of cultural and economic activity, with a range of museums, markets, and restaurants to explore.
- The **Kafue National Park** is one of the largest national parks in Zambia, with a diverse range of wildlife and scenic landscapes.
