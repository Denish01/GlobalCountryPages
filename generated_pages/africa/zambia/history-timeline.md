The history of Zambia is a rich and complex narrative that spans thousands of years, from the earliest known human habitation to the modern era, with significant events and periods shaping the country into the sovereign nation it is today.

The history of Zambia begins with the earliest known human habitation, dating back to the **Stone Age**, with archaeological evidence showing that the region was inhabited by various groups of people, including the **Khoisan** and **Bantu** tribes. The pre-colonial period was marked by the rise and fall of various kingdoms and empires, including the **Kingdom of Luba** and the **Kingdom of Kazembe**, which played important roles in the region's trade and cultural development. The colonial period, which began in the late 19th century, saw the arrival of European colonizers, including the British, who established the territory of **Northern Rhodesia**, which would eventually become Zambia.

The colonial period was marked by significant economic and social changes, including the exploitation of the country's natural resources and the introduction of new technologies and infrastructure. The **Scramble for Africa**, which occurred in the late 19th century, saw European powers competing for control of the continent, with the British ultimately establishing control over the region. The colonial period also saw the emergence of a **nationalist movement**, which sought to challenge British rule and achieve independence for the territory. The movement, led by figures such as **Kenneth Kaunda**, played a crucial role in the country's transition to independence.
