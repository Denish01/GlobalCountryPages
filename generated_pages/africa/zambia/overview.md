[SECTION] What Is Zambia? [/SECTION]
Zambia is a **sovereign** country located in southern Africa, bordered by eight countries: DR Congo, Tanzania, Malawi, Mozambique, Zimbabwe, Botswana, Namibia, and Angola. It is a landlocked country, with a diverse geography that includes savannas, forests, and mountains. Zambia is known for its rich natural resources, including copper, cobalt, and gold, as well as its vibrant culture and stunning natural beauty. The country has a long history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Stone Age.

Zambia is a popular tourist destination, with many attractions such as the **Victoria Falls**, one of the largest waterfalls in the world, and the **South Luangwa National Park**, which is known for its abundant wildlife and stunning scenery. The country has a diverse economy, with a mix of agriculture, mining, and manufacturing. The official language is **English**, which is widely spoken, and the currency is the **Zambian kwacha** (ZMW).

Zambia has a rich cultural heritage, with over 70 ethnic groups and a strong tradition of music, dance, and art. The country has a relatively stable government, with a **presidential system** and a **multi-party democracy**. Zambia is a member of various international organizations, including the **United Nations**, the **African Union**, and the **Commonwealth of Nations**.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1924 - Zambia becomes a British protectorate, known as Northern Rhodesia.
2. 1953 - Northern Rhodesia is merged with Southern Rhodesia and Nyasaland to form the Federation of Rhodesia and Nyasaland.
3. 1964 - Zambia gains independence from Britain, with Kenneth Kaunda as its first president.
4. 1972 - Zambia becomes a one-party state, with the United National Independence Party (UNIP) as the only legal party.
5. 1991 - Zambia adopts a new constitution, which introduces a multi-party system and holds democratic elections.
6. 2011 - Zambia experiences a peaceful transition of power, with Michael Sata succeeding Rupiah Banda as president.
7. 2015 - Edgar Lungu becomes the sixth president of Zambia, following the death of Michael Sata.

[SECTION] What Makes Zambia Unique [/SECTION]
- Zambia is home to one of the largest waterfalls in the world, **Victoria Falls**, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
- The country has a diverse wildlife, with many national parks and game reserves, including the **South Luangwa National Park** and the **Kafue National Park**.
- Zambia has a rich cultural heritage, with over 70 ethnic groups and a strong tradition of music, dance, and art.
- The country is a major producer of **copper**, with the mining industry being a significant contributor to the economy.
- Zambia has a unique **traditional cuisine**, which includes dishes such as nshima (a staple made from cornmeal), stew, and braai (barbecued meat).
