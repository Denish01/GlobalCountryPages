# Zambia Visa & Entry Requirements

To enter Zambia, citizens of most countries require a visa, which can be obtained on arrival or in advance, depending on their nationality and purpose of visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Zambia before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Zambia has a moderate visa policy, with citizens of over 40 countries eligible for visa-free access or visa on arrival. The country aims to promote tourism and business while maintaining national security. Citizens of countries that are not eligible for visa-free access or visa on arrival must apply for a visa in advance at a Zambian embassy or consulate. Zambia offers various types of visas, including tourist, business, student, and work visas, each with its own set of requirements and restrictions.

The visa policy is designed to facilitate travel to Zambia while ensuring that visitors comply with the country's laws and regulations. Visitors can expect a relatively straightforward process when applying for a visa, with most applications being processed within a few days. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it is crucial to verify the current requirements before traveling to Zambia.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can obtain a visa on arrival or in advance, depending on their nationality. The typical stay for tourists is 90 days, which can be extended for an additional 90 days. Tourists must provide proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and health insurance. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require an invitation letter from a Zambian company or organization, as well as proof of their business activities. They may be eligible for a multiple-entry visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. **Students:** Students must provide proof of enrollment in a Zambian educational institution, as well as a letter of acceptance. They may be eligible for a student visa, which can be valid for up to 12 months. **Workers:** Workers must have a sponsorship letter from a Zambian employer, as well as a valid employment contract. They may be eligible for a work visa, which can be valid for up to 24 months.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airports in Zambia are Kenneth Kaunda International Airport in Lusaka and Harry Mwanga Nkumbula International Airport in Livingstone. Visitors can also enter Zambia through land borders with neighboring countries. Upon arrival, visitors must present their travel documents, including their passport, visa (if required), and proof of onward travel. Immigration officers may ask questions about the purpose and duration of the visit, as well as the visitor's accommodation arrangements. Visitors must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country, including gifts, souvenirs, and electronic devices.

Visitors can expect a relatively straightforward process when entering Zambia, with most procedures taking around 30 minutes to an hour. However, it is essential to note that immigration procedures can be delayed during peak travel seasons, and visitors should plan accordingly. Additionally, visitors must ensure that they comply with all customs regulations, including declaring any restricted or prohibited items.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not having the required visa or documentation
- Not declaring goods or gifts when entering the country
- Not having proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
- Not having health insurance or a medical certificate
- Not registering with the local authorities within the required timeframe (for long-term visitors)
