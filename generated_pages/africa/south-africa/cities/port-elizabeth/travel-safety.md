[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Port Elizabeth, also known as the "Friendly City," is a popular tourist destination in South Africa, known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant cultural scene, and rich history. The city has a relatively low crime rate compared to other major cities in the country, and most visitors can expect a safe and enjoyable experience. However, as with any city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing, theft, and burglary are the most common types of crime in Port Elizabeth, and visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night.

Overall, Port Elizabeth is a great destination for tourists, with a wide range of activities and attractions to suit all interests. The city has a strong focus on tourism, and visitors can expect a warm welcome from the friendly locals. However, it's essential to be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions to stay safe. By being informed and taking normal precautions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Port Elizabeth.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas, especially at the beachfront and in the Central Business District.
- **Burglars** often target tourist accommodation, so make sure to secure your room and valuables.
- **Scams** are common in Port Elizabeth, including **ATM scams** and **credit card scams**, so be cautious when using ATMs and credit cards.
- **Traffic accidents** are a risk in Port Elizabeth, especially at night, so be careful when crossing roads and driving.
- **Protests** and **demonstrations** can occur in Port Elizabeth, so avoid areas where crowds are gathering.
- **Muggings** can occur in isolated areas, especially at night, so avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas.
- **Credit card skimming** is a common scam in Port Elizabeth, so be cautious when using credit cards at restaurants and shops.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended for travel to South Africa.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink in Port Elizabeth, but bottled water is recommended for visitors.
- **Medical Facilities:** Port Elizabeth has several good-quality hospitals and medical facilities, including the Port Elizabeth Provincial Hospital and the Life St George's Hospital.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available in Port Elizabeth, and most medications are easily accessible.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always **carry a map** and know the location of your accommodation and nearby attractions.
- **Keep valuables secure** and consider using a safe or lockbox to store important documents and belongings.
- **Be aware of your surroundings**, especially in crowded areas and at night.
- **Use reputable taxi services** or **ride-sharing apps** to get around the city.
- **Avoid walking alone** in isolated areas, especially at night.
- **Keep your hotel room door locked** and use the hotel's safe or lockbox to store valuables.
- **Be cautious when using ATMs** and **credit cards**, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
- **Stay informed** about local conditions and events that may affect your safety.
