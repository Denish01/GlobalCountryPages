# Where to Stay in Pretoria — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Pretoria, the best areas to stay include the city center, Hatfield, Menlo Park, and Waterkloof, which offer a range of accommodation options to suit different types of travelers and budgets. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, Pretoria has something unique to offer:
**Arcadia** (Pretoria)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families
Vibe: Arcadia is known for its beautiful parks, embassies, and historic buildings, offering a tranquil and secure environment, with a mix of old and new architecture that creates a unique and interesting vibe.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Close proximity to the city center, beautiful parks, and a variety of restaurants, Arcadia offers a range of amenities and activities for visitors, including the Pretoria National Botanical Garden and the Union Buildings.
Cons: Traffic can be heavy during peak hours, and some areas can be quite hilly.

**Hatfield** (Pretoria)
Best For: Backpackers and students
Vibe: Hatfield is a vibrant and lively area, known for its nightlife, restaurants, and shops, with a mix of old and new buildings that creates a dynamic and energetic vibe.
Price Range: $20-80/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, close to the University of Pretoria, and a variety of entertainment options, Hatfield is a great place for young travelers and students to meet new people and experience the local culture.
Cons: Can be noisy at night, and some areas can be quite crowded.

**Menlo Park** (Pretoria)
Best For: Families and business travelers
Vibe: Menlo Park is a safe and secure area, known for its beautiful homes, parks, and restaurants, offering a relaxed and welcoming environment, with a mix of modern and traditional architecture that creates a sense of community.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Close to the city center, beautiful parks, and a variety of restaurants, Menlo Park offers a range of amenities and activities for visitors, including the Menlo Park Shopping Centre and the Pretoria Country Club.
Cons: Can be quite suburban, and some areas can be quite far from public transport.

**Waterkloof** (Pretoria)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families
Vibe: Waterkloof is an upmarket area, known for its beautiful homes, parks, and views of the city, offering a tranquil and secure environment, with a mix of modern and traditional architecture that creates a sense of luxury.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Close to the city center, beautiful views, and a variety of restaurants, Waterkloof offers a range of amenities and activities for visitors, including the Waterkloof Golf Club and the Pretoria Country Club.
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and some areas can be quite hilly.

**Brooklyn** (Pretoria)
Best For: Business travelers and families
Vibe: Brooklyn is a safe and secure area, known for its beautiful homes, parks, and restaurants, offering a relaxed and welcoming environment, with a mix of modern and traditional architecture that creates a sense of community.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Close to the city center, beautiful parks, and a variety of restaurants, Brooklyn offers a range of amenities and activities for visitors, including the Brooklyn Mall and the Pretoria Country Club.
Cons: Can be quite suburban, and some areas can be quite far from public transport.

**Pretoria Central** (Pretoria)
Best For: Backpackers and budget travelers
Vibe: Pretoria Central is a vibrant and lively area, known for its historic buildings, museums, and markets, with a mix of old and new architecture that creates a dynamic and energetic vibe.
Price Range: $20-60/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, close to the city's main attractions, and a variety of entertainment options, Pretoria Central is a great place for travelers to experience the local culture and meet new people.
Cons: Can be quite crowded, and some areas can be quite noisy.
