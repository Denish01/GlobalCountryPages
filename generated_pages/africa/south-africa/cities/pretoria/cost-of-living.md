# Cost of Living in Pretoria - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Pretoria is generally moderate, with a daily budget of $30-60 USD for backpackers, $60-120 USD for mid-range travelers, and $120-250 USD for those seeking a more comfortable experience.

Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $120-250 USD
Currency: ZAR
Tipping Custom: 10-15% expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Pretoria? [/SECTION]
Pretoria, the administrative capital of South Africa, offers a unique blend of urban and natural attractions, with a cost of living that is generally lower than in other major cities in the country, such as Cape Town or Johannesburg. Compared to global averages, Pretoria is relatively affordable, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation being lower than in many Western countries. However, prices can vary depending on the location, with areas like the city center and suburbs tend to be more expensive than outer neighborhoods.

In terms of regional comparison, Pretoria is generally more affordable than its neighboring cities, such as Johannesburg, but more expensive than smaller towns in the surrounding provinces. The city's economy is driven by the public sector, education, and research, which contributes to a relatively stable and moderate cost of living. Overall, Pretoria offers a great balance between affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists, students, and expats alike.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in shared accommodation or outside the city center to reduce rent costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls, which offer affordable and delicious meals.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transport costs.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost attractions, such as the Pretoria National Botanical Garden or the Voortrekker Monument.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets for groceries and other essentials.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (December to February) to save on accommodation and tourism costs.
