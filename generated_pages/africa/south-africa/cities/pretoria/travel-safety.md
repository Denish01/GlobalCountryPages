[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Pretoria, the administrative capital of South Africa, is a city with a mix of modern and historic attractions, but it also faces challenges related to crime and safety. While most tourists visit Pretoria without incident, petty crimes such as theft, pickpocketing, and scams are common, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Violent crime is less common but can occur, especially at night or in areas with high crime rates. It's essential to take necessary precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings, keeping valuables secure, and avoiding walking alone in isolated areas at night.

To stay safe in Pretoria, it's crucial to research your destination, stay informed about local conditions, and plan accordingly. This includes choosing reputable transportation services, avoiding displaying signs of wealth, and keeping hotel rooms and cars locked. Additionally, being mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas, and avoiding carrying large amounts of cash can help minimize the risk of petty crime. By taking these precautions and staying informed, you can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Pretoria.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **ATM scams**, where thieves use card skimmers or cameras to steal your PIN and card information.
- Avoid **hitchhiking**, as it's not a safe or recommended way to travel in Pretoria.
- Be wary of **street vendors** who may try to sell you counterfeit goods or overpriced items.
- Keep an eye out for **pickpocketing** in crowded areas, such as bus stations and markets.
- Don't leave **valuables** unattended, especially in public areas or tourist hotspots.
- Be cautious of **scams** involving fake police officers or traffic officials, and always ask to see identification.
- Avoid **walking alone** at night, especially in isolated areas or areas with high crime rates.
- Be aware of **protests** or **demonstrations**, which can sometimes turn violent or affect traffic.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Consult your doctor or a travel clinic to determine the necessary vaccinations for travel to South Africa, such as hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink in Pretoria, but it's recommended to stick to bottled or filtered water to be cautious.
- **Medical Facilities:** Pretoria has a range of medical facilities, including public and private hospitals, but the quality of care can vary. It's recommended to have medical insurance that covers you for emergencies.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available in Pretoria, and many are open 24 hours or have extended hours.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always **lock your car** and keep valuables out of sight.
- Be **aware of your surroundings**, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.
- Avoid **displaying signs of wealth**, such as expensive jewelry or large amounts of cash.
- Keep your **hotel room door locked** and use the hotel safe to store valuables.
- Avoid **walking alone** at night, especially in isolated areas or areas with high crime rates.
- Use **reputable transportation services**, such as licensed taxis or ride-sharing apps.
- Keep your **phone charged** and with you at all times, in case of emergencies.
- Stay **informed** about local conditions and events that may affect your safety.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be **cautious** when interacting with strangers, especially men, and avoid engaging with aggressive or persistent individuals.
- Dress **modestly** and avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or clothing.
- Avoid **walking alone** at night, especially in isolated areas or areas with high crime rates, and use reputable transportation services instead.
- Stay **informed** about local conditions and events that may affect your safety, and consider joining a group tour or using a reputable guide service.
