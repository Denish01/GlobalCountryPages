[SECTION] What Is Johannesburg? [/SECTION]
Johannesburg is a major city located in the Gauteng province of South Africa, and is the country's economic hub. The city is situated on the Highveld plateau, at an elevation of approximately 1,750 meters above sea level. Johannesburg is known for its rich history, which dates back to the late 19th century when it was founded as a mining town, and has since grown into a thriving metropolis with a diverse population of over 4.9 million people. The city is a significant cultural and economic center, with a strong focus on finance, commerce, and industry.

Johannesburg is also a city of great contrasts, with both wealthy and impoverished areas, and a mix of modern and historic architecture. The city is home to many world-class attractions, including the Apartheid Museum, the Johannesburg Art Gallery, and the famous Gold Reef City theme park. Johannesburg is also a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous parks, gardens, and nature reserves, such as the Johannesburg Botanical Gardens and the Klipriviersberg Nature Reserve.

The city's strategic location makes it an important transportation hub, with two international airports, the OR Tambo International Airport and the Lanseria International Airport, and a comprehensive network of roads and highways. Johannesburg is also a major center for education and research, with several universities and institutions of higher learning, including the University of the Witwatersrand and the University of Johannesburg.
