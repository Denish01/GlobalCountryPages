# Cost of Living in Johannesburg - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Johannesburg can vary greatly depending on lifestyle and accommodation choices, but overall, it is considered a moderately priced city with a daily budget range of $30-100 USD for backpackers, $60-200 USD for mid-range travelers, and $150-500 USD for comfort seekers.

Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $30-60 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $60-150 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $150-300 USD
Currency: ZAR
Tipping Custom: expected (10-15% in restaurants and bars)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Johannesburg? [/SECTION]
Johannesburg is a major city in South Africa, and its cost of living is relatively moderate compared to other global cities. The cost of living in Johannesburg is lower than in many Western countries, but higher than in some other African cities. Compared to its neighboring cities, Johannesburg is more expensive than Pretoria but less expensive than Cape Town. The city offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, and a vibrant food scene with a variety of local and international cuisine.

The cost of living in Johannesburg can vary greatly depending on lifestyle and accommodation choices. For example, renting a one-bedroom apartment in the city center can cost between $400-800 USD per month, while a similar apartment in a suburban area can cost between $200-500 USD per month. Food prices also vary, with a meal at a local eatery costing around $5-10 USD, while a meal at a mid-range restaurant can cost between $15-30 USD.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel instead of a luxury hotel to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls instead of mid-range or fine dining restaurants to save on food costs.
- Use public transport instead of taxis or rental cars to save on transport costs.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (December to February) to save on accommodation and flight costs.
- Take advantage of free walking tours and outdoor activities instead of paying for guided tours or expensive attractions.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets instead of convenience stores or tourist shops to save on food and souvenir costs.

Overall, Johannesburg offers a unique blend of urban and natural attractions, and with some planning and research, it is possible to have a great experience in the city without breaking the bank. By being mindful of accommodation choices, food options, and transport costs, visitors can enjoy all that Johannesburg has to offer while staying within their budget.
