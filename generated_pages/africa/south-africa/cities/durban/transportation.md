# Getting Around Durban — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Durban, you can use a combination of taxis, buses, and rental cars, as well as ride-hailing apps, which provide a convenient and relatively affordable way to navigate the city and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Durban is easily accessible by air, with King Shaka International Airport (DUR) serving as the main gateway to the city. The airport is located about 35 kilometers north of Durban's city center and offers direct flights to several major hubs, including Johannesburg, Cape Town, and international destinations such as Dubai and Mauritius. If you're coming from within South Africa, you can also take a bus or drive to Durban, which is connected to the rest of the country by a network of highways and roads. The city is also a popular stop on the **Garden Route**, a scenic driving route that runs along the coast from Cape Town to Port Elizabeth.

If you're entering Durban overland, you can cross the border from neighboring countries such as Mozambique, Swaziland, and Lesotho. The city is also a major port, with cruise ships and cargo vessels regularly calling at the Durban Harbour. From the airport, you can take a taxi or bus to get to your destination, or rent a car to explore the city and its surroundings. The **N3 highway** connects Durban to Johannesburg, while the **N2 highway** runs along the coast to Cape Town.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Durban has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to and from the city. **South African Airways**, **Mango**, and **Kulula** are some of the major airlines that offer domestic flights to Durban. You can fly to Durban from major cities such as Johannesburg, Cape Town, and Port Elizabeth, as well as smaller towns and cities. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as prices can be high. Approximate costs for domestic flights to Durban range from $100 to $300, depending on the airline, route, and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Durban has a limited rail network, with **Shongololo Express** and **Premier Classe** offering luxury train services to and from the city. The **Durban-Johannesburg** route is one of the most popular, with trains running regularly between the two cities. However, the rail network is not as extensive as in other countries, and buses and taxis are often a more convenient and affordable option. You can book train tickets online or at the train station, and prices range from $50 to $200, depending on the class of service and route.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several bus companies operate in Durban, including **Greyhound**, **Intercape**, and **Eldo Coaches**. These companies offer intercity bus services to and from Durban, with routes connecting the city to major towns and cities across South Africa. The buses are generally comfortable and well-maintained, with onboard amenities such as air conditioning and reclining seats. Prices range from $20 to $100, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Durban, you need a valid **driver's license** from your home country or an **International Driving Permit**.
- The road quality in Durban is generally good, with well-maintained highways and roads.
- Fuel costs in Durban are relatively high, with prices ranging from $1 to $2 per liter.
- Car rental companies such as **Avis**, **Hertz**, and **Europcar** operate in Durban, with prices starting from $40 per day.
- When driving in Durban, be aware of **potholes** and **speed bumps**, especially on rural roads.
- It's also important to be mindful of **traffic congestion**, especially during peak hours, and to follow **traffic rules** and regulations.

- Be aware of **scams** and **petty theft** when using public transport in Durban, and keep a close eye on your belongings.
- Use **reputable taxi companies** or **ride-hailing apps** to ensure your safety.
- Download **transport apps** such as **Uber** and **Bolt** to book rides and track your journey.
- Always **wear a seatbelt** when driving or riding in a car, and follow **traffic rules** and regulations.
- Be mindful of **traffic congestion** and **road closures**, especially during peak hours or construction periods.
- Consider **renting a car** with a **GPS system** to help navigate the city and its surroundings.
- Learn some **basic Zulu phrases**, such as "**Sawubona**" (hello) and "**Ngiyabonga**" (thank you), to interact with locals and enhance your travel experience.
