[SECTION] What Is Durban? [/SECTION]
Durban is a coastal city situated in the eastern part of South Africa, in the province of KwaZulu-Natal. It is the third-most populous city in the country and is known for its mild climate, beautiful beaches, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is home to a diverse population, with a mix of Zulu, Xhosa, Afrikaans, and English-speaking people, making it a unique and fascinating place to visit. Durban is also an important economic hub, with a thriving port and a strong focus on tourism, manufacturing, and agriculture.

Geographically, Durban is situated on the Indian Ocean coast, with the city center located on a narrow coastal plain. The surrounding landscape is dominated by the Drakensberg Mountains to the west and the Indian Ocean to the east. The city's climate is subtropical, with warm summers and mild winters, making it an attractive destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts. Durban is also home to a number of natural attractions, including the Golden Mile beachfront, the Durban Botanic Gardens, and the nearby Valley of a Thousand Hills.

The city has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of African, European, and Asian influences. The city is home to a number of museums, galleries, and cultural institutions, including the Durban Art Gallery, the KwaZulu-Natal Museum, and the BAT Centre. Durban is also known for its vibrant music and arts scene, with a number of festivals and events taking place throughout the year, including the Durban International Film Festival and the Essence Festival.
