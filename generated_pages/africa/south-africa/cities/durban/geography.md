Durban is situated in the KwaZulu-Natal province of South Africa, on the eastern coast of the country. It is bounded by the Indian Ocean to the east, the Drakensberg Mountains to the west, and the rolling hills of the Natal Midlands to the north. The city is located approximately 500 kilometers southeast of Johannesburg, the country's largest city, and is a major port and tourist destination. The coastline of Durban is characterized by a mix of sandy beaches, rocky shores, and mangrove swamps, providing a unique and diverse range of ecosystems.

The city's location on the Indian Ocean makes it an important hub for international trade and commerce, with the Port of Durban being one of the busiest ports in Africa. The surrounding province of KwaZulu-Natal is home to a diverse range of cultures, languages, and landscapes, including the **Zulu** and **Xhosa** peoples, who have a rich and vibrant cultural heritage. The province is also home to several major cities, including Pietermaritzburg, the provincial capital, and Richards Bay, a major industrial center.
