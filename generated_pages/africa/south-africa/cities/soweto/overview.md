[SECTION] What Is Soweto? [/SECTION]
Soweto is an urban settlement located about 15 kilometers southwest of Johannesburg, the economic hub of South Africa. The name Soweto is an acronym for "South Western Townships," which was given to the area due to its location. Soweto is not a sovereign state but rather a metropolitan municipality within the Gauteng province of South Africa. It is known for its rich cultural heritage and its significant role in the country's history, particularly during the apartheid era. Soweto is home to a diverse population of over 1.3 million people, with various ethnic groups and languages spoken, including Zulu, Xhosa, Afrikaans, and English.

Soweto's geographic position makes it an important part of the larger Johannesburg metropolitan area. The city is situated in a valley, surrounded by hills and mountains, and is divided into several townships, each with its own unique character. Soweto is known for its vibrant culture, which is reflected in its music, dance, art, and cuisine. The city is also home to several historic landmarks, including the Hector Pieterson Museum and the Regina Mundi Church, which played a significant role in the anti-apartheid movement.

Soweto is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world who come to experience its rich culture and history. The city offers a range of activities and attractions, including guided tours, cultural performances, and visits to historic landmarks. Soweto is also a major economic hub, with several industries, including manufacturing, mining, and services. The city is well-connected to the rest of South Africa, with several major highways and public transportation systems.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1904 - The area that is now Soweto was first settled by African people who were forcibly removed from their homes in Johannesburg to make way for white settlers.
2. 1948 - The National Party came to power in South Africa, implementing the policy of apartheid, which led to further segregation and marginalization of the black population in Soweto.
3. 1976 - The Soweto Uprising took place, in which thousands of students protested against the imposition of Afrikaans as a language of instruction in schools, leading to clashes with the police and the deaths of several students.
4. 1990 - Nelson Mandela, the leader of the African National Congress (ANC), was released from prison, marking a significant turning point in the struggle against apartheid.
5. 1994 - South Africa held its first democratic elections, in which the ANC won a majority of the votes, and Nelson Mandela became the country's first black president.
6. 2002 - The City of Soweto was established as a metropolitan municipality, giving the city more autonomy and control over its affairs.
7. 2010 - Soweto hosted several matches during the FIFA World Cup, which was held in South Africa, bringing international attention and investment to the city.
