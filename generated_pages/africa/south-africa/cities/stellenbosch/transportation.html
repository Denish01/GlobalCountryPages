Getting There

Stellenbosch is located in the Western Cape province of South Africa, and the closest international airport is Cape Town International Airport (CPT), which is about 35 kilometers from Stellenbosch. Cape Town International Airport is a major hub with direct flights to several destinations in Africa, Europe, and Asia, including Johannesburg, London, and Dubai. You can also enter Stellenbosch overland from other parts of South Africa, using the N1 highway, which connects Cape Town to Johannesburg, or the N2 highway, which connects Cape Town to Port Elizabeth.

If you are traveling from other parts of South Africa, you can take a domestic flight to Cape Town International Airport, and then take a taxi, bus, or rental car to get to Stellenbosch. Alternatively, you can take a bus or drive from other parts of the country, using the national highways. Stellenbosch is also a popular tourist destination, and many tour operators offer shuttle services from Cape Town International Airport to Stellenbosch.

Domestic Flights

If you need to travel to other parts of South Africa, you can take a domestic flight from Cape Town International Airport, which is served by several airlines, including South African Airways, British Airways, and Mango Airlines. Domestic flights are available to several destinations, including Johannesburg, Durban, and Port Elizabeth. To book a domestic flight, you can visit the website of the airline or use a travel booking website, such as Expedia or Booking.com. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the route and airline, but you can expect to pay around $100-$200 for a one-way ticket.

Trains & Rail

The rail network in South Africa is limited, and there are no direct trains to Stellenbosch. However, you can take a train from Cape Town to other parts of the country, using the Shongololo Express or the Blue Train, which offer luxury train travel to several destinations, including Johannesburg and Pretoria. The trains are comfortable and offer scenic views of the countryside, but the journey can be long and may not be the most convenient option. To book a train ticket, you can visit the website of the train operator or use a travel booking website.

Buses & Coaches

Several bus companies operate in South Africa, including Intercape, Greyhound, and Baz Bus, which offer services to several destinations, including Stellenbosch. The buses are comfortable and offer a convenient and affordable way to travel, with approximate costs ranging from $20-$50 for a one-way ticket. You can book a bus ticket online or at the bus station, and the journey can take several hours, depending on the route and traffic conditions.

Driving

  • To drive in South Africa, you need a valid driver's license from your home country, which must be translated into English if it is not already in English.
  • The road conditions in South Africa are generally good, with well-maintained highways and roads, but you may encounter potholes and gravel roads in rural areas.
  • Fuel costs in South Africa are relatively low, with petrol prices ranging from $0.50-$1.00 per liter, depending on the location and type of fuel.
  • Car rental companies are available at Cape Town International Airport and in Stellenbosch, with prices starting from around $40-$60 per day, depending on the type of vehicle and rental company.
  • When driving in South Africa, be aware of the speed limits, which range from 60-120 km/h, depending on the road and location, and always wear a seatbelt.
  • Be cautious of pedestrians and animals on the road, especially in rural areas, and avoid driving at night if possible, as the roads can be poorly lit and there may be pedestrians and animals on the road.

Transport Tips

  • When using a taxi or ride-hailing app, always check the driver's identification and license before getting in the vehicle.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas and public transport.
  • Use a reputable car rental company and check the vehicle before renting it, to ensure it is in good condition.
  • Download the Uber or Bolt app to book a ride, and use a credit card or mobile payment method to pay for your fare.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use a credit card or debit card instead, to minimize the risk of theft or loss.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, and avoid driving or walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night.
  • Keep your vehicle doors and windows locked, and be cautious of strangers approaching your vehicle, to minimize the risk of theft or hijacking.
