# South Africa Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of South Africa before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
South Africa has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Citizens of approximately 75 countries can enter South Africa without a visa, while others may need to apply for a visa before their trip or obtain one upon arrival. The country's visa policy is designed to balance the need to facilitate tourism and business travel with the need to maintain national security and control over immigration.

The South African government has introduced various visa facilitation measures, such as the eVisa system, to make it easier for visitors to obtain the necessary documents. However, the requirements and regulations can still be complex, and it's essential to understand the specific requirements for your nationality and purpose of visit. The country's visa policy is subject to change, so it's crucial to stay up-to-date with the latest information before planning your trip.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists from eligible countries can enter South Africa without a visa and stay for up to 90 days. The typical stay is 14-30 days, and extensions are possible in exceptional circumstances. Tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must provide an invitation letter from a South African company, proof of business activities, and other supporting documents. The requirements and regulations for business travelers are more complex than for tourists.
**Students:** Students from eligible countries can enter South Africa on a study visa, which is valid for the duration of their course. They must provide proof of enrollment at a South African educational institution, sufficient funds, and medical insurance.
**Workers:** Workers from eligible countries can enter South Africa on a work visa, which is valid for the duration of their employment contract. They must provide a job offer from a South African employer, proof of qualifications and experience, and other supporting documents.

[SECTION] Documents Checklist [/SECTION]
1. Valid passport with at least 6 months' validity
2. Completed visa application form (if required)
3. Recent passport-sized photos
4. Proof of onward travel
5. Sufficient funds for the duration of stay
6. Medical insurance that covers the applicant for the duration of stay
7. Proof of accommodation or a confirmed hotel booking
8. Invitation letter or proof of business activities (for business travelers)
9. Proof of enrollment at a South African educational institution (for students)
10. Job offer or employment contract (for workers)

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airports in South Africa are OR Tambo International Airport in Johannesburg, Cape Town International Airport, and King Shaka International Airport in Durban. Land borders are also an option, with major border crossings at Beitbridge (Zimbabwe), Groblersbrug (Botswana), and Komatipoort (Mozambique). Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and answer questions about their stay. The immigration officer may also request additional information or documentation, depending on the purpose and duration of the visit.

The entry procedures in South Africa are generally straightforward, but it's essential to ensure that all required documents are in order to avoid any delays or issues. Visitors should also be prepared to answer questions about their itinerary, accommodation, and activities during their stay.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not having a valid passport with at least 6 months' validity
- Not providing sufficient proof of onward travel
- Not having enough funds for the duration of stay
- Not having medical insurance that covers the applicant for the duration of stay
- Not providing required supporting documents, such as an invitation letter or proof of business activities
- Not declaring all goods and currency upon arrival, which can lead to fines or penalties
