# Eastern Cape Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting the Eastern Cape, be sure to try the region's unique blend of traditional Xhosa and Afrikaans cuisine, which is characterized by a mix of seafood, meat, and vegetable dishes, often flavored with local spices and herbs.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: maize meal, beef, mutton, seafood
National Dish: Umngqusho, a traditional Xhosa dish made from samp and beans
Meal Times: breakfast 7-10 am, lunch 12-2 pm, dinner 6-8 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 10-15% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: many restaurants offer halal and vegetarian options, but it's best to check ahead of time
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
The Eastern Cape's cuisine is defined by its rich cultural heritage, with influences from the indigenous Xhosa people, as well as Dutch, British, and African settlers. The region's unique blend of traditional and modern flavors is reflected in its use of local ingredients, such as seafood, beef, and mutton, which are often combined with African spices and herbs. The Eastern Cape is also known for its traditional Xhosa dishes, such as Umngqusho and Injera bread, which are often served at social gatherings and special occasions.

The region's cuisine also varies depending on the location, with the coastal areas offering a wide range of seafood dishes, while the inland areas are known for their meat and vegetable dishes. The Eastern Cape is also home to a number of wine farms and breweries, which produce a range of wines and beers that are perfect for pairing with the region's cuisine.

In addition to its traditional cuisine, the Eastern Cape is also home to a number of modern restaurants and cafes, which offer a range of international dishes, from Italian and Chinese to Indian and Mexican. These restaurants often use local ingredients and flavors to create unique and innovative dishes that reflect the region's cultural diversity.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- It is customary to greet the host or server with a handshake or a smile
- Table manners are generally informal, but it is considered polite to keep your hands visible and not to eat with your left hand
- Tipping is expected, but not mandatory, and is usually around 10-15% of the total bill
- Reservations are usually not necessary, but it's a good idea to book ahead for popular restaurants
- Dress codes are generally casual, but it's a good idea to dress smartly for more formal restaurants
- It is considered impolite to leave the table until everyone has finished eating
