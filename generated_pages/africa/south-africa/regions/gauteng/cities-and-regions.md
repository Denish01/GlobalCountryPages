# Gauteng Cities & Regions Guide

Gauteng is a province in South Africa that is divided into several cities and regions, with the main cities including Johannesburg, Pretoria, and Ekurhuleni, and the regions varying in character, economy, and climate.

[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Gauteng Is Divided [/SECTION]
Gauteng is divided into five municipalities, each with its own unique character and role to play in the province's economy and culture. The City of Johannesburg is the largest and most populous municipality, and is home to many of the province's major businesses and cultural attractions. The City of Tshwane, which includes the city of Pretoria, is the administrative capital of South Africa and is known for its historic landmarks and academic institutions. The other three municipalities, Ekurhuleni, Sedibeng, and West Rand, are smaller but still play important roles in the province's economy and culture.

The municipalities in Gauteng are further divided into wards, which are represented by local councillors who are responsible for addressing the needs and concerns of their constituents. This system of local government allows for a more direct connection between citizens and their government, and helps to ensure that the needs of all communities are taken into account. Overall, the administrative divisions in Gauteng are designed to promote efficient and effective governance, and to support the social and economic development of the province.

Gauteng has a well-developed transportation network, with many domestic flights, trains, and buses connecting the major cities and regions. However, traffic congestion can be a problem in the major cities, particularly during rush hour. Overall, getting around Gauteng is relatively easy, with many options available to suit different budgets and preferences.

The public transportation system in Gauteng is also well-developed, with many buses and trains connecting the major cities and regions. The Gautrain is a high-speed rail link that connects Johannesburg, Pretoria, and OR Tambo International Airport, and is a convenient and efficient way to get around the province. There are also many taxi services and ride-hailing apps available, making it easy to get around without a car.
