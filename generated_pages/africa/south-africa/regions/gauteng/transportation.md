# Getting Around Gauteng — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Gauteng, you can use a variety of transportation options including airports, domestic flights, trains, buses, city transport, and driving, each with its own advantages and disadvantages.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Gauteng is easily accessible by air, with two major international airports, OR Tambo International Airport and Lanseria International Airport, serving as hubs for domestic and international flights. OR Tambo International Airport is the primary airport, located about 20 kilometers east of Johannesburg, and offers direct flights to major cities around the world, including London, New York, and Sydney. Lanseria International Airport, on the other hand, is a smaller airport located about 30 kilometers northwest of Johannesburg and serves as a hub for domestic and regional flights.

For those traveling overland, Gauteng is connected to the rest of South Africa and neighboring countries via a network of highways and roads. The main overland entry points include the N1 highway from Cape Town, the N3 highway from Durban, and the N4 highway from Maputo, Mozambique. There are also several bus companies that operate from neighboring countries, including Intercape and Greyhound, which offer daily services to and from Gauteng.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Gauteng has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating from OR Tambo International Airport and Lanseria International Airport to other major cities in South Africa. The main domestic airlines include South African Airways, British Airways, and Mango, which offer frequent flights to cities such as Cape Town, Durban, and Port Elizabeth. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, and prices can range from around $50 to $200 or more, depending on the route and class of travel.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Gauteng has a moderate rail network, with several train routes connecting the province to other parts of South Africa. The main rail operator is the Passenger Rail Agency of South Africa (PRASA), which offers daily services to and from Johannesburg and Pretoria. The most popular route is the Gautrain, a high-speed train that connects Johannesburg, Pretoria, and OR Tambo International Airport. The Gautrain is a convenient and efficient way to travel, with frequent departures and comfortable seats, and tickets can be booked online or at the train station.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several bus companies that operate in Gauteng, offering intercity services to and from major cities in South Africa. The main bus companies include Intercape, Greyhound, and Translux, which offer daily services to cities such as Cape Town, Durban, and Port Elizabeth. The buses are generally comfortable and well-maintained, with onboard amenities such as air conditioning and toilets. Prices can range from around $20 to $100 or more, depending on the route and class of travel.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Gauteng, you will need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The roads in Gauteng are generally in good condition, but be aware of potholes and uneven surfaces, especially on rural roads.
- Fuel costs can range from around $1 to $2 per liter, depending on the type of fuel and location.
- Car rental companies such as Avis, Hertz, and Europcar are widely available at airports and city centers, and prices can range from around $40 to $100 per day, depending on the type of vehicle and rental period.
- Be aware of hazards such as pedestrian traffic, cyclists, and animals on the road, especially at night or in rural areas.
- It is recommended to drive defensively and follow local traffic rules and regulations to avoid fines or accidents.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of scams and touts at airports and bus stations, and only use reputable transport services.
- Use a reputable taxi company or ride-hailing app to avoid overcharging or scams.
- Download the Gautrain app or the South African transport app to plan your journey and book tickets in advance.
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas or public transport, and keep an eye on your luggage at all times.
- Use a credit card or cash to pay for transport services, as not all providers accept foreign currency or debit cards.
- Be prepared for traffic congestion, especially during peak hours or in construction areas, and plan your journey accordingly.
- Consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot to stay connected and access transport information on the go.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when interacting with transport providers or other locals.
