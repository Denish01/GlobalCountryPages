[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in South Africa is relatively well-developed, with a good average internet speed and mobile network coverage that spans across the country. However, there is a notable urban vs rural divide, with faster and more reliable internet connections available in urban areas such as **Johannesburg** and **Cape Town**, while rural areas often experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Despite this, the overall internet quality in South Africa is considered to be among the best in Africa, making it a great destination for digital nomads and remote workers.

The reliability of the internet in South Africa can vary depending on the location and the service provider. In general, the major carriers such as **Vodacom** and **MTN** offer reliable connections, but outages and slow speeds can occur, especially during peak hours or in areas with high demand. Additionally, the country's internet infrastructure is still developing, and issues such as **load shedding** (planned power outages) can affect internet availability. However, overall, the internet in South Africa is considered to be relatively stable and reliable, making it a great option for those who need to stay connected.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in South Africa is relatively easy, with a range of options available from the major carriers. You can purchase a SIM card at the airport, or at one of the many carrier stores or kiosks located throughout the country. To purchase a SIM card, you will typically need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and the cost will range from 10-20 USD for a tourist SIM. The major carriers offer a range of data plans, including prepaid and postpaid options, and it's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers such as **Vodacom** or **MTN** for the best coverage and speeds.

It's also worth noting that some carriers offer **tourist-friendly plans** that include data, voice, and SMS, and these plans can be a great option for those who need to stay connected during their stay in South Africa. Additionally, many carriers offer **data-only plans** that can be a more affordable option for those who only need data access.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality in South Africa is generally good, with many hotels offering fast and reliable connections.
- Cafe WiFi culture is also widespread, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in South Africa, especially in cities such as **Johannesburg** and **Cape Town**, and many offer fast and reliable WiFi connections.
- Public WiFi is also available in many areas, including airports, shopping malls, and public libraries.
- Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi to customers, making it easy to stay connected while on the go.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces such as **Neighbourgoods Market** and **The Workspace** offer fast and reliable WiFi connections, as well as a range of other amenities such as meeting rooms and networking events.
- Reliable cafe chains such as **Vida e Caffè** and **Seattle Coffee Company** offer good WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options such as **mobile hotspots** and **portable WiFi routers** can be a good idea, especially for those who need to work remotely in rural areas.
- VPNs can be useful for remote workers who need to access certain websites or services that may be blocked in South Africa.
- Many **coliving spaces** are also available in South Africa, offering a range of amenities including fast and reliable WiFi, as well as a community of like-minded individuals.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major issue in South Africa, but some websites and services may be blocked or restricted.
- Blocked sites can include certain **torrenting sites** and **adult content** websites.
- VPN recommendations include **ExpressVPN** and **NordVPN**, which can help to bypass restrictions and keep your internet activity private.
- Roaming alternatives such as **eSIMs** and **portable WiFi routers** can be a good idea for those who need to stay connected while traveling.
- It's also a good idea to be aware of **data limits** and **out-of-bundle rates**, as these can be expensive and unexpected.
- Additionally, it's a good idea to use **strong passwords** and **two-factor authentication** to keep your online accounts and devices secure.
