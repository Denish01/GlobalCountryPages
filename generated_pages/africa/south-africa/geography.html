Location & Borders

South Africa is located at the southern tip of the African continent, bordered by several countries and surrounded by the Atlantic and Indian Oceans. To the north, it shares borders with Namibia, Botswana, Zimbabwe, and Mozambique, while to the east, it borders Eswatini and Lesotho. The country's unique geography is characterized by its coastal plains, which stretch along the Atlantic and Indian Ocean coastlines, and its mountainous interior, which includes the famous Drakensberg Mountains.

The country's borders are a result of its complex history, with the Great Escarpment, a mountain range that runs along the eastern edge of the plateau, forming a natural border with Lesotho. The Orange River, which flows through the northern part of the country, forms a border with Namibia, while the Limpopo River forms a border with Zimbabwe and Mozambique. South Africa's coastline is approximately 2,798 kilometers long, with the Atlantic Ocean to the west and the Indian Ocean to the east.

Terrain & Landscape

South Africa's terrain is characterized by its diverse and varied landscape, which includes mountains, deserts, forests, and coastlines. The country's interior is dominated by the Highveld, a vast plateau that covers much of the central and eastern parts of the country. The Highveld is surrounded by several mountain ranges, including the Drakensberg Mountains, which are the highest in the country. The Drakensberg Mountains are a popular destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts, with their scenic valleys and towering peaks.

The western part of the country is dominated by the Kalahari Desert, a vast and arid region that covers much of the Northern Cape province. The Kalahari Desert is known for its red sand dunes and unique wildlife, including the famous lions and cheetahs. The eastern part of the country is characterized by a subtropical climate, with tropical forests and coastal plains that are home to a wide variety of wildlife and marine life. The coastline of South Africa is also home to several islands, including Robben Island, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The country's rivers, including the Orange, Vaal, and Limpopo, play an important role in shaping its landscape and supporting its agriculture and industry. The rivers also support a wide variety of wildlife, including fish, birds, and mammals. South Africa's landscape is also characterized by its caves, including the famous Cango Caves, which are a popular tourist destination.

Best Time To Visit

Best months: September to November and March to May. Why: The weather is mild and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, surfing, and wildlife viewing. Avoid: June to August, as the weather can be cool and rainy, and December to February, as the weather can be hot and humid.

Natural Highlights

  • Table Mountain is a flat-topped mountain that overlooks the city of Cape Town and is a popular destination for hikers and tourists.
  • Kruger National Park is one of Africa's largest game reserves, covering an area of approximately 19,485 square kilometers and home to a wide variety of wildlife, including lions, elephants, and giraffes.
  • Garden Route is a scenic stretch of coastline that runs along the southern coast of South Africa, known for its beautiful beaches, lakes, and forests.
  • Cango Caves are a series of caves that are located in the Swartberg Mountains and are a popular destination for tourists and adventure seekers.
  • Blyde River Canyon is a large canyon that is located in the Drakensberg Mountains and is one of the largest green canyons in the world.
  • iSimangaliso Wetland Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is located on the eastern coast of South Africa and is known for its diverse wildlife and unique ecosystem.
