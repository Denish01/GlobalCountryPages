# Where to Stay in Comoros — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Comoros, the best areas to stay include Moroni, Fomboni, Moutsamoudou, Domoni, and Mitsamiouli, which offer a range of options for different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's what you can expect:
**Moroni** (Moroni)
Best For: Luxury, Business
Vibe: Moroni is the capital and largest city of Comoros, offering a vibrant and bustling atmosphere with a mix of modern and traditional architecture. The city has a rich cultural scene, with many historical landmarks and museums to explore.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Moroni has a wide range of amenities, including restaurants, shops, and entertainment options, and is a great base for exploring the rest of the island. The city also has a beautiful beach and a scenic coastal promenade. Additionally, Moroni is home to many of the country's top hotels and resorts.
Cons: Moroni can be quite crowded and noisy, especially in the city center, and traffic can be a problem.

**Fomboni** (Fomboni)
Best For: Backpacker, Family
Vibe: Fomboni is a charming town on the island of Moheli, known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs. The town has a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, making it an ideal destination for families and backpackers.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: Fomboni is a great spot for water sports and activities, such as snorkeling, diving, and fishing. The town also has a range of affordable accommodation options, including guesthouses and hostels. Additionally, Fomboni is home to many traditional Comorian villages, offering a glimpse into the local culture.
Cons: Fomboni is a small town, and amenities can be limited, especially for luxury travelers.

**Moutsamoudou** (Anjouan)
Best For: Luxury, Business
Vibe: Moutsamoudou is the largest town on the island of Anjouan, and is known for its stunning natural beauty, with lush forests, waterfalls, and scenic coastal views. The town has a sophisticated and upscale atmosphere, with many high-end hotels and resorts.
Price Range: $150-400/night
Pros: Moutsamoudou has a range of amenities, including restaurants, shops, and entertainment options, and is a great base for exploring the island. The town also has a beautiful beach and a scenic coastal promenade. Additionally, Moutsamoudou is home to many of the country's top hotels and resorts.
Cons: Moutsamoudou can be quite expensive, especially for luxury travelers, and the town can be quite crowded during peak season.

**Domoni** (Anjouan)
Best For: Family, Backpacker
Vibe: Domoni is a charming town on the island of Anjouan, known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs. The town has a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, making it an ideal destination for families and backpackers.
Price Range: $30-70/night
Pros: Domoni is a great spot for water sports and activities, such as snorkeling, diving, and fishing. Additionally, Domoni is home to many traditional Comorian villages, offering a glimpse into the local culture.
Cons: Domoni is a small town, and amenities can be limited, especially for luxury travelers.

**Mitsamiouli** (Grand Comore)
Best For: Luxury, Business
Vibe: Mitsamiouli is a charming town on the island of Grand Comore, known for its stunning natural beauty, with lush forests, waterfalls, and scenic coastal views. The town has a sophisticated and upscale atmosphere, with many high-end hotels and resorts.
