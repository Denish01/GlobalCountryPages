Overall Safety Assessment

Comoros is a sovereign island nation located off the coast of East Africa, with a unique cultural blend of African, Arab, and French influences. While Comoros can be a fascinating and rewarding destination for travelers, it does come with some safety concerns. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, is a moderate risk, particularly in crowded areas and markets. Additionally, the country is prone to natural disasters like cyclones and floods, which can impact travel plans and safety.

However, with some basic precautions and awareness, visitors can minimize their risks and have a safe and enjoyable trip. The Comorian government has taken steps to improve security, including the establishment of a tourist police force. Furthermore, the local population is generally friendly and welcoming to tourists. It's essential to stay informed about local conditions, respect local customs, and take necessary precautions to ensure a smooth and safe journey.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Petty theft and pickpocketing in crowded areas and markets
  • Scams targeting tourists, including overpriced goods and services
  • Traffic accidents due to poor road conditions and reckless driving
  • Cyclones and floods, which can impact travel plans and safety
  • Foodborne illnesses from consuming undercooked or contaminated food
  • Waterborne illnesses from drinking untreated water
  • Demonstrations and protests, which can turn violent
  • Corruption and bribery, particularly when interacting with local authorities

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are limited, particularly outside of Moroni, and may not meet international standards
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacy access is limited, and it's recommended to bring essential medications and supplies

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, particularly in rural areas
  • Respect local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting mosques or attending cultural events
  • Stay informed about local weather conditions and potential natural disasters
  • Bring essential medications and supplies, including sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Learn basic Comorian phrases, such as greetings and directions
  • Stay hydrated and bring bottled or filtered water

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be mindful of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs, particularly when visiting mosques or attending cultural events
  • Avoid engaging with strangers or accepting rides from unknown individuals
  • Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary and plans
