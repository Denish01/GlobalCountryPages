[SECTION] Overview: Comoros vs Mozambique [/SECTION]
Comoros and Mozambique are two African countries with distinct economic contexts. Comoros, an archipelago located off the coast of East Africa, has a relatively small economy with a GDP per capita of 1,449 USD. Mozambique, on the other hand, has a larger economy with a GDP per capita of 1,266 USD. Despite having a higher GDP per capita, Comoros has a lower cost of living compared to Mozambique. The inflation rate in Comoros is also lower, at 2.5% compared to Mozambique's 3.5%. This suggests that Comoros may offer better value for money, especially for expats and tourists.

The cost of living in Comoros is generally lower due to several factors, including lower housing costs, food prices, and transportation expenses. However, it's essential to note that both countries have their unique characteristics, and the cost of living can vary depending on individual lifestyle choices. For example, while Comoros may be cheaper for housing and food, Mozambique may offer better value for entertainment and leisure activities. Overall, Comoros appears to be the more affordable option, but it's crucial to consider various factors before making a decision.
