The best time to visit Guinea-Bissau is during the dry season, from November to May, when the weather is dry and comfortable, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the country's natural beauty.

Best months to visit Guinea-Bissau: November to May. Peak season: December to February. Budget season: June to October. Avoid: March to May, due to the intense heat and humidity, and the risk of malaria and other diseases.

Seasons Overview

Guinea-Bissau has a tropical savanna climate, with two main seasons: the dry season and the wet season. The dry season, which runs from November to May, is characterized by dry and comfortable weather, with average temperatures ranging from 20Â°C to 30Â°C (68Â°F to 86Â°F). This is the best time to visit Guinea-Bissau, as the weather is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting. The wet season, which runs from June to October, is marked by heavy rainfall and high humidity, making it more challenging to travel and engage in outdoor activities.

The seasons in Guinea-Bissau have a significant impact on travel, as the wet season can make roads impassable and increase the risk of malaria and other diseases. During the dry season, the roads are more accessible, and the weather is more comfortable, making it easier to explore the country's national parks, beaches, and cultural attractions. However, the dry season is also the peak tourist season, which means that prices for accommodations and tourist services may be higher.

The climate in Guinea-Bissau varies by region, with the coastal areas tend to be more humid and hot than the interior regions. The Fouta Djallon highlands, located in the northeast of the country, have a cooler climate than the rest of the country, making them a popular destination for trekking and hiking.
