# Education Costs in Guinea-Bissau

The cost of education in Guinea-Bissau varies greatly depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Guinea-Bissau is still developing, with a strong focus on public education. However, the quality of public education can be inconsistent, with a lack of resources and infrastructure in some areas. Private schools, on the other hand, tend to offer higher quality education, but at a significantly higher cost. The language of instruction is **Portuguese**, which can be a barrier for some students. Compulsory education lasts for 6 years, from ages 6 to 12, and is free for all citizens. International schools are available in the capital city of Bissau, offering a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **British Curriculum**.

The education system in Guinea-Bissau faces several challenges, including a lack of qualified teachers, inadequate infrastructure, and limited resources. Despite these challenges, the government is working to improve the education system, with a focus on increasing access to education, particularly for girls and rural communities. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a range of programs, from **primary** to **secondary** education, and often have better facilities and more qualified teachers. However, these schools are often expensive and out of reach for many families.
