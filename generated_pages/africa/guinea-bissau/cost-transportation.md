[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Guinea-Bissau has a limited transportation network, with most people relying on **minibuses** and **taxis** to get around. The quality of public transit is generally poor, with few options available outside of the capital city, Bissau. In rural areas, **bicycles** and **motorcycles** are common modes of transportation, while in urban areas, **walking** is a popular option due to the lack of reliable public transportation.

The transportation infrastructure in Guinea-Bissau is underdeveloped, with few paved roads and limited access to **car rentals** and **ride-hailing services**. However, the country is working to improve its transportation network, with plans to upgrade its roads and expand its public transportation options. Despite the challenges, visitors can still get around Guinea-Bissau with some planning and flexibility, using a combination of **taxis**, **minibuses**, and **private cars**.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using **minibuses** or **shared taxis**, which are often cheaper than hiring a private taxi.
- When taking a taxi, always **negotiate the fare** before you start your journey to avoid overcharging.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded **minibus** or **taxi** situations.
- Consider hiring a **private car** and driver for longer trips or if you prefer a more comfortable and convenient option.
- Always wear a **seatbelt** and follow local traffic rules to ensure your safety on the road.
- Download **maps** or **transportation apps** to help navigate the limited transportation network in Guinea-Bissau.
