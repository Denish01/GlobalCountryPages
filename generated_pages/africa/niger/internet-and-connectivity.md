# Niger Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Niger is relatively underdeveloped, with limited availability and slow speeds, especially outside of the capital city Niamey, but it is still possible to get connected with a SIM card or WiFi.

[FACTBOX]
Average Internet Speed: 2.5 Mbps
Mobile Network Coverage: Moderate
Main Carriers: Airtel, Orange, Moov
SIM Card Cost: 5 USD
WiFi Availability: Limited
5G Available: No
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Niger is still in its early stages of development, with a significant urban-rural divide in terms of availability and quality. In Niamey, the capital city, internet speeds are relatively faster, with some areas having access to **fiber optic** connections. However, outside of Niamey, internet speeds are generally slower, and **mobile network coverage** can be spotty. The reliability of the internet is also a concern, with frequent **outages** and **disconnections**. Despite these challenges, the government has been working to improve the country's internet infrastructure, with plans to expand **mobile network coverage** and increase **internet speeds**.

The internet quality in Niger is also affected by the lack of **infrastructure**, particularly in rural areas. Many areas have limited access to **electricity**, which can make it difficult to stay connected. Additionally, the cost of **internet plans** can be prohibitively expensive for many Nigeriens, making it difficult for them to access the internet. However, there are some **initiatives** underway to improve internet access and affordability, such as the development of **community networks** and **low-cost internet plans**.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Niger, you can visit one of the main carriers, such as Airtel, Orange, or Moov. You will need to provide some **identification**, such as a passport, and pay for the SIM card, which typically costs around 5 USD. You can also purchase **prepaid plans** with data, which can range from 1-10 USD per month, depending on the carrier and the amount of data you need. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable carrier, as some smaller carriers may have limited coverage and poor quality.

To purchase a SIM card, you can visit a carrier's store or a **retail outlet**. You will need to provide your **passport** and fill out a **registration form**. The process is relatively straightforward, but it may take some time to complete. It is also a good idea to **test your SIM card** before leaving the store to make sure it is working properly.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally **good**, with many hotels offering **free WiFi** to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is not very developed, but some **coffee shops** and **restaurants** in Niamey offer **free WiFi**.
- Coworking spaces are limited, but some **startup hubs** and **incubators** offer **shared workspaces** with **reliable WiFi**.
- Public WiFi is not widely available, but some **internet cafes** and **cybercafes** offer **paid WiFi**.
- Some **universities** and **research institutions** also offer **public WiFi**.

- Reliable cafe chains like **Cafe de la Paix** and **Cafe du Sahel** offer **free WiFi** and a **comfortable work environment**.
