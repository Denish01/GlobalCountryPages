# Namibia Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Namibia before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Namibia has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and prior approval required for different nationalities. Citizens of over 50 countries can enter Namibia visa-free, while others may need to obtain a visa on arrival or through prior application. The country aims to balance tourism promotion with security and immigration control. The visa policy is designed to facilitate travel for legitimate purposes, such as tourism, business, and study, while ensuring that the country's laws and regulations are respected.

The visa-free access policy applies to citizens of countries with which Namibia has a reciprocal agreement, such as the United States, the United Kingdom, and many European Union member states. For other nationalities, the requirements and procedures vary, and it is essential to check the latest information before planning a trip to Namibia. The country's visa policy is subject to change, and it is crucial to verify the current requirements to avoid any issues during travel.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Namibia visa-free if they are citizens of eligible countries, or they can obtain a visa on arrival for a maximum stay of 90 days. The typical stay for tourists is 14-30 days, and extensions are possible but require prior approval. Tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to obtain a visa prior to arrival, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must provide an invitation letter from a Namibian company, proof of business activities, and other supporting documents. Business travelers may be required to register with the Namibian authorities and comply with local regulations.
**Students:** Students must obtain a study visa, which requires proof of enrollment at a Namibian educational institution, a medical certificate, and other supporting documents. The study visa is usually valid for the duration of the study program, and students must comply with the conditions of their visa.
**Workers:** Workers must obtain a work visa, which requires sponsorship by a Namibian employer, proof of qualifications and experience, and other supporting documents. The work visa is usually valid for a specific period, and workers must comply with the conditions of their visa and Namibian labor laws.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Namibia are the Hosea Kutako International Airport (WDH) near Windhoek, the Walvis Bay International Airport (WVB), and several land borders with neighboring countries. Upon arrival, travelers must present their travel documents, including their passport, visa (if required), and proof of onward travel. Immigration officers may ask questions about the purpose of the visit, the length of stay, and other details. Travelers must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country, including gifts, souvenirs, and food items.

At land borders, travelers must stop at the border post and present their documents to the immigration officer. The process may take longer than at airports, and travelers should be prepared to wait and provide additional information if required. It is essential to ensure that all travel documents are in order and that travelers have the necessary visas and permits before attempting to enter Namibia.

- Not checking the latest visa requirements before traveling

- Not providing sufficient proof of onward travel or return ticket
- Not declaring goods or gifts at the border
- Not complying with the conditions of the visa or permit
- Not registering with the Namibian authorities (if required)
