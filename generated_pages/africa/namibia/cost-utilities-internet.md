# Utility & Internet Costs in Namibia

The cost of utilities and internet in Namibia can range from $100 to $300 per month for a medium-sized apartment, depending on usage and service providers.

Monthly Utilities (85 sqm apt): ($200-$300 USD)
Internet (60 Mbps): ($50-$70 USD/month)

Currency: NAD

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Namibia's utility infrastructure is relatively well-developed, with a high percentage of the population having access to **electricity**, **water**, and **sanitation**. However, the cost of utilities can fluctuate due to seasonal variations in demand and supply. During the summer months (December to February), electricity costs tend to be higher due to increased demand for air conditioning and cooling. In contrast, during the winter months (June to August), electricity costs tend to be lower due to reduced demand for heating. Overall, the cost of utilities in Namibia is relatively stable, with a moderate **inflation rate** of 4.2% in 2024.

The quality of utility services in Namibia is generally good, with a high level of **reliability** and **availability**. However, there may be occasional **outages** or **disruptions** due to maintenance or technical issues. To mitigate these risks, it is essential to choose a reputable service provider and to have a backup plan in place, such as a **generator** or **uninterruptible power supply (UPS)**.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities in Namibia, it is essential to contact the relevant service providers (e.g. **Telecom Namibia**, **NamPower**) and provide the necessary documentation (e.g. **identification**, **proof of address**).
- Prepaid **electricity** meters are widely available in Namibia, allowing users to control their energy usage and avoid unexpected bills.
- When choosing an **internet service provider**, consider factors such as **speed**, **reliability**, and **customer support**.
- To save on utility bills, consider using **energy-efficient appliances**, **turning off lights and electronics** when not in use, and **using public transport** or **carpooling**.
- Regularly review and adjust your utility usage to avoid **waste** and **inefficiency**, and consider investing in **renewable energy sources** (e.g. **solar panels**) to reduce your reliance on the grid.
