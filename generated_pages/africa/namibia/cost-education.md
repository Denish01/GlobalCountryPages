# Education Costs in Namibia

The cost of education in Namibia varies depending on the type of institution and level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Namibia's education system is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the largest. The **language of instruction** is English, which is the country's official language. The education system consists of 13 years of compulsory education, starting from pre-primary to secondary school. The public education sector is generally considered to be of good quality, but it can be overcrowded and underfunded in some areas. On the other hand, private schools are often better equipped and have smaller class sizes, but they can be expensive. International schools are also available in Namibia, mainly in the capital city of Windhoek, and they offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Examinations**.

The Namibian education system is designed to provide students with a well-rounded education, with a focus on **academic achievement**, as well as **extracurricular activities** such as sports and music. The system is also designed to promote **critical thinking** and **problem-solving skills**, which are essential for success in the modern world. However, the education system in Namibia still faces some challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of resources in some schools. Despite these challenges, the education system in Namibia is considered to be one of the best in the region, and it provides students with a solid foundation for future success.
