[SECTION] What Is Namibia? [/SECTION]
Namibia is a **sovereign** country located in southern Africa, bordered by Angola to the north, Zambia and Botswana to the east, and South Africa to the south and east. With a population of 2.6 million people, Namibia is a relatively small country, but it is known for its vast and diverse landscapes, which range from the **Namib Desert** in the west to the **Kalihari Desert** in the east. The country has a unique cultural heritage, with a mix of African, European, and indigenous influences.

Namibia is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world with its stunning natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. The country is home to several **national parks** and **game reserves**, including the famous **Etosha National Park**, which is known for its abundant wildlife and unique salt pans. The capital city, Windhoek, is a bustling metropolis with a blend of modern and colonial architecture, and a vibrant cultural scene.

Namibia has a complex and fascinating history, with a mix of African, European, and indigenous influences. The country was a German colony from the late 19th century until the end of World War I, and it was then mandated to South Africa by the League of Nations. Namibia gained its independence in 1990, after a long and bloody struggle against South African rule.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1884 - Germany establishes a colony in Namibia, which it names German South West Africa.
2. 1915 - South African troops defeat the German colonial forces, and Namibia is occupied by South Africa.
3. 1920 - The League of Nations mandates Namibia to South Africa, which administers the territory as a province.
4. 1966 - The United Nations General Assembly revokes the mandate, and Namibia becomes a UN trust territory.
5. 1990 - Namibia gains its independence from South Africa, with Sam Nujoma as its first president.
6. 1994 - The first democratic elections are held in Namibia, with the South West Africa People's Organization (SWAPO) winning a majority of the votes.
7. 2015 - Namibia celebrates its 25th anniversary of independence, with a series of national events and celebrations.

- Namibia is home to the **world's oldest desert**, the Namib Desert, which is estimated to be over 55 million years old.
- The country has a unique **geological feature**, the Fish River Canyon, which is one of the largest canyons in the world.
- Namibia is known for its **abundant wildlife**, with a wide range of species, including elephants, lions, and cheetahs.
- The country has a **rich cultural heritage**, with a mix of African, European, and indigenous influences.
- Namibia is home to several **ancient rock art sites**, including the famous Tsodilo Hills, which are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
