Cultural Treasures

  • The Cape Coast Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a historic fort that serves as a reminder of Ghana's complex past and its role in the transatlantic slave trade.
  • The Asante Gold Regalia, a collection of gold jewelry and ornaments, is a priceless cultural treasure that showcases the rich heritage of the Asante people.
  • The Ewe Kente Cloth Weaving tradition is a vibrant and colorful art form that has been passed down through generations, with each piece telling a unique story and symbolizing the community's history and values.
  • The Homowo Festival, celebrated by the Ga people, is a traditional harvest festival that honors the gods and ancestors, featuring music, dance, and traditional food.
  • The Bantama Highlife Music style, which originated in the 1920s, is a unique blend of traditional Akan music and Western influences, and has become an integral part of Ghanaian popular culture.

People & Global Impact

  • Kofi Annan, the seventh Secretary-General of the United Nations, was a Ghanaian diplomat who played a key role in promoting global peace and development.
  • The Ghanaian diaspora has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Ghanaians living abroad remitting funds back to their families and communities.
  • Dr. Ama Ata Aidoo, a renowned Ghanaian author and playwright, has written extensively on the experiences of African women and the impact of colonialism on traditional societies.
  • The values of community and respect for elders are deeply ingrained in Ghanaian culture, with many Ghanaians prioritizing family and social relationships above personal achievements.

What Visitors Love

  • The warm hospitality of the Ghanaian people, who are known for their welcoming nature and generosity towards visitors.
  • The delicious local cuisine, which features dishes such as jollof rice, fufu, and banku, often served with a variety of spicy sauces and stews.
  • The stunning natural scenery, including the scenic coastline, the rolling hills of the Volta Region, and the vibrant markets of Accra and Kumasi.
  • The rich cultural heritage, which is evident in the many museums, historical sites, and traditional festivals that take place throughout the year.
  • The vibrant music and dance scene, which features a wide range of traditional and contemporary styles, from Highlife to Azonto.
  • The thriving arts and crafts industry, which offers a wide range of unique and colorful textiles, wood carvings, and other handicrafts.

Hidden Gems

  • The Kakum National Park, which is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including monkeys, birds, and butterflies, and offers a unique canopy walkway for visitors to explore.
  • The Wli Waterfalls, which are located in the Volta Region and offer a stunning natural wonder, with the largest waterfall in West Africa.
  • The Bui National Park, which is a protected area that is home to a wide range of wildlife, including elephants, lions, and antelopes, and offers a unique opportunity for visitors to experience the natural beauty of Ghana.
  • The Akuapem Hills, which offer a scenic and peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside and a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking and birdwatching.
