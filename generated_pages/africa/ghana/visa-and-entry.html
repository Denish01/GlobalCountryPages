Visa Policy Overview

Ghana has a moderate visa policy, with citizens from over 30 countries eligible for visa-free entry or visa on arrival. The country aims to promote tourism and economic development, while also maintaining national security and public health. Citizens from eligible countries can stay in Ghana for a specified period, usually up to 30 or 90 days, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit.

Ghana's visa policy is designed to facilitate travel for legitimate purposes, such as tourism, business, education, and work. The country has introduced an electronic visa (eVisa) system to simplify the application process and reduce processing times. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and travelers should always check with the official embassy or consulate of Ghana for the latest information.

Tourists: Tourists can stay in Ghana for up to 30 or 90 days, depending on their nationality. They must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay. Tourists can also apply for an extension of their stay, but this must be done before their initial visa expires.
Business Travelers: Business travelers must have a valid passport, a letter of invitation from a Ghanaian company, and proof of sufficient funds. They may also need to register with the Ghana Investment Promotion Centre (GIPC) and obtain a business permit.
Students: Students must have a valid passport, a letter of admission from a Ghanaian university, and proof of sufficient funds. They must also register with the National Immigration Authority and obtain a student permit.
Workers: Workers must have a valid passport, a job offer from a Ghanaian employer, and a work permit. They must also register with the National Immigration Authority and obtain a residence permit.

Ghana has several entry points, including the Kotoka International Airport in Accra, the Tamale Airport in Tamale, and several land borders with neighboring countries. Upon arrival, travelers must present their passport, visa (if required), and other supporting documents to the immigration officer. They will be asked to fill out a disembarkation card and may be required to pay a fee for their visa or other services. Travelers must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country and comply with customs regulations.
The entry process can take some time, especially during peak travel seasons. Travelers should be patient and ensure they have all the necessary documents to avoid any delays or issues. It is also recommended to have a confirmed onward travel arrangement and sufficient funds to cover their stay in Ghana.
- Not checking the validity of their passport before traveling
- Not having the required visa or documentation
- Not declaring goods or currency at customs
- Not registering with the National Immigration Authority (for students or workers)
- Not obtaining the necessary permits or licenses (for business travelers or workers)
- Not having proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
