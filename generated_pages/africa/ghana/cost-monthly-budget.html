Cost of Living Overview

Ghana is considered to be a relatively affordable country, with a GDP per capita (PPP) of $8,020. The cost of living in Ghana is lower compared to many other countries in the world, making it an attractive destination for expats, digital nomads, and tourists. However, the cost of living can vary greatly depending on the lifestyle and location. For example, a budget lifestyle in a smaller city like Tamale or Kumasi can be very affordable, while a mid-range lifestyle in the capital city Accra can be relatively expensive. Compared to neighboring countries like Ivory Coast and Togo, Ghana has a similar cost of living, but it is generally more expensive than Burkina Faso.

The different budgets can go a long way in Ghana, depending on the lifestyle and choices. A budget of $400-600 per month can provide a simple but comfortable life, with shared accommodation, cooking at home, and using public transportation. A mid-range budget of $800-1,200 per month can provide a more comfortable life, with a one-bedroom apartment in a central location, eating out occasionally, and using a mix of public and private transportation. A comfortable budget of $1,500-2,500 per month can provide a luxurious life, with a nice two-bedroom apartment, eating out often, and using a taxi or car.

Budget Tier Lifestyles

A budget lifestyle in Ghana can provide a simple but comfortable life. With a budget of $400-600 per month, you can afford to share a room in a house or apartment, cook most of your meals at home, and use public transportation. You can also afford to go out occasionally, but it would be limited to local restaurants and bars. You would not be able to afford to eat out at mid-range or high-end restaurants, and you would have to be careful with your expenses to stay within your budget.

A mid-range lifestyle in Ghana can provide a more comfortable life. With a budget of $800-1,200 per month, you can afford to rent a one-bedroom apartment in a central location, eat out occasionally, and use a mix of public and private transportation. You can also afford to go out more often, and enjoy some of the city's nightlife and cultural events. However, you would still have to be mindful of your expenses, and you would not be able to afford to eat out at high-end restaurants or travel frequently.

A comfortable lifestyle in Ghana can provide a luxurious life. With a budget of $1,500-2,500 per month, you can afford to rent a nice two-bedroom apartment, eat out often, and use a taxi or car. You can also afford to travel frequently, and enjoy some of the country's best tourist attractions. You would have a high level of comfort and flexibility, and you would be able to enjoy many of the luxuries that Ghana has to offer.

Money-Saving Strategies

  • Learn to cook local dishes to save money on food
  • Use public transportation to save money on transportation costs
  • Shop at local markets to save money on groceries and other items
  • Avoid eating out at high-end restaurants
  • Use a prepaid phone plan to save money on phone bills
  • Consider sharing an apartment or house with others to split the cost of rent
  • Take advantage of free or low-cost cultural events and activities
  • Use a water filter or purification tablets to save money on bottled water

Is Ghana Affordable?

Ghana can be an affordable destination for digital nomads, retirees, and students who are looking for a low-cost lifestyle. The cost of living in Ghana is relatively low, and the country offers a unique cultural experience and a warm climate. However, Ghana may not be affordable for everyone, particularly those who are used to a high standard of living. The country's infrastructure and amenities can be limited, particularly outside of the major cities, and the cost of living can vary greatly depending on the lifestyle and location. Additionally, the country's economy can be volatile, and the exchange rate can fluctuate, which can affect the cost of living.

Overall, Ghana can be a great destination for those who are looking for an affordable and unique cultural experience. However, it is essential to do your research and plan carefully to ensure that you have enough money to cover your living expenses and enjoy your time in the country. With a budget of $400-600 per month, you can have a simple but comfortable life, while a budget of $1,500-2,500 per month can provide a luxurious life. It is crucial to consider your lifestyle, location, and personal preferences when determining your budget and making the most of your time in Ghana.
