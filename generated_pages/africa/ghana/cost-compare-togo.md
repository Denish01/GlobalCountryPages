[SECTION] Overview: Ghana vs Togo [/SECTION]
Ghana and Togo are two neighboring countries in West Africa, with distinct economic profiles. Ghana has a relatively strong economy, driven by its natural resources, agriculture, and services sector, which contributes to a higher GDP per capita. In contrast, Togo's economy is smaller and more vulnerable to external shocks, resulting in a lower GDP per capita. The cost of living in Ghana is generally lower than in Togo, with the exception of a few categories such as transportation and utilities. The overall cost of living difference between the two countries is around 15-20%, making Ghana a more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of both countries plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Ghana's economy is more diversified, with a strong services sector, which contributes to higher prices in certain categories. On the other hand, Togo's economy is more reliant on agriculture and manufacturing, resulting in lower prices for certain goods and services. However, the overall cost of living in Ghana is still lower due to its larger economy and more competitive markets. For example, the cost of housing, food, and entertainment in Ghana is generally lower than in Togo, making it a more attractive option for those looking for an affordable lifestyle.
