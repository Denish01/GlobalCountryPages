[SECTION] Overview: Madagascar vs Mauritius [/SECTION]
Madagascar and Mauritius are two island nations located in the Indian Ocean, off the coast of East Africa. While both countries share some cultural and historical similarities, they have distinct economic profiles. Madagascar is one of the poorest countries in the world, with a largely agrarian economy and limited infrastructure. In contrast, Mauritius is a more developed economy with a strong tourism industry and a diverse range of sectors, including manufacturing, finance, and technology. As a result, the cost of living in Madagascar is significantly lower than in Mauritius, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation being substantially cheaper.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Madagascar's economy is largely driven by agriculture, with a focus on crops such as rice, maize, and coffee. The country also has significant mineral resources, including nickel, iron, and cobalt. In contrast, Mauritius has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on tourism, manufacturing, and financial services. The country's strategic location in the Indian Ocean has also made it an important hub for international trade and commerce. Overall, the cost of living in Madagascar is approximately 30-40% lower than in Mauritius, making it an attractive option for those looking for an affordable lifestyle.
