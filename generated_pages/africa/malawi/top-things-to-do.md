# Top Things to Do in Malawi — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Malawi, you should not miss the stunning **Lake Malawi**, the vibrant city of **Lilongwe**, and the breathtaking **Mount Mulanje**, which offer a mix of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and adventure activities that showcase the best of what this African country has to offer.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **dhow safari** on Lake Malawi to explore the lake's many islands and coves
- Visit a **local village** to experience the traditional way of life and learn about the customs and practices of the Malawian people
- Go **kayaking** or **paddleboarding** on Lake Malawi to explore the lake's stunning coastline
- Take a **coffee tour** in the **Mulanje District** to learn about the production of Malawi's famous coffee
- Attend a **traditional dance performance** to experience the vibrant culture and music of Malawi
- Go **stargazing** in the **Nyika National Park** to take in the breathtaking night sky
- Take a **safari** in the **Liwonde National Park** to see a wide range of wildlife, including elephants, lions, and leopards

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Nyika National Park**: known for its scenic landscapes and diverse wildlife
- **Mount Mulanje**: offers hiking and trekking opportunities with breathtaking views
- **Lake Malawi**: stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming and water sports
- **Likoma Island**: beautiful beaches and historic sites
- **Vwaza Marsh Wildlife Reserve**: diverse wildlife and scenic wetlands
- **Dzalanyama Forest Reserve**: lush forests and diverse birdlife

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Lilongwe Museum**: insights into the history and culture of Malawi
- **Mzuzu Museum**: insights into the history and culture of northern Malawi
- **Kungoni Centre of Culture and Art**: a showcase of Malawian art and culture
- **St. Peter's Cathedral**: a historic church with stunning architecture
- **Chongoni Rock Art Area**: a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring ancient rock art
- **Malawi National Museum**: a comprehensive museum showcasing the history and culture of Malawi

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **Zomba Plateau**: a scenic plateau with stunning views and hiking opportunities
- **Nkhata Bay**: a charming town on the shores of Lake Malawi with a rich history
- **Thyolo**: a scenic town in the **Shire Highlands** known for its tea plantations
- **Dedza**: a town in the **Central Region** known for its pottery and crafts
- **Ntchisi Forest Reserve**: a lush forest reserve with diverse wildlife and hiking opportunities

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
1. **Lilongwe to Dzalanyama Forest Reserve**: 100 km, 2 hours, scenic forest reserve with diverse birdlife
2. **Blantyre to Mount Mulanje**: 60 km, 1.5 hours, hiking and trekking opportunities with breathtaking views
3. **Mzuzu to Nyika National Park**: 180 km, 3.5 hours, scenic landscapes and diverse wildlife
4. **Salima to Lake Malawi**: 20 km, 30 minutes, stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters
5. **Zomba to Ku Chawe Inn**: 10 km, 30 minutes, historic hotel with stunning views of the surrounding landscape
