[SECTION] Overview: Malawi vs Tanzania [/SECTION]
Malawi and Tanzania are two neighboring countries in southeastern Africa, both offering a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. In terms of cost of living, Malawi tends to be the more affordable option, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. The economic context of the two countries is also different, with Malawi having a more diversified economy and Tanzania being more reliant on agriculture and mining. As a result, Malawi's cost of living is generally around 15-20% lower than Tanzania's, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees on a budget.

The cost of living in Malawi and Tanzania can vary depending on the location, with urban areas like Lilongwe and Dar es Salaam tend to be more expensive than rural areas. However, overall, Malawi offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for everyday items like food, housing, and transportation. This makes it an attractive option for those looking to experience African culture without breaking the bank. In contrast, Tanzania offers a more developed tourism infrastructure, with more options for accommodation, dining, and activities, but at a higher cost.
