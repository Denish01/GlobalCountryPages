[SECTION] What Is Malawi? [/SECTION]
Malawi is a **sovereign** country located in southeastern Africa. It is a landlocked nation, bordered by Mozambique to the east, south, and west, Tanzania to the north, and Zambia to the northwest. Malawi is known for its stunning natural beauty, with a diverse geography that includes mountains, forests, and a large lake, Lake Malawi, which is a popular destination for tourists and a vital source of food and income for the local population.

Malawi is a relatively small country, with a total area of approximately 118,000 square kilometers. The country has a tropical climate, with warm temperatures and high humidity levels throughout the year. The capital city, Lilongwe, is located in the central region of the country and is the largest city in Malawi. The country has a diverse culture, with over 15 ethnic groups, each with their own language and customs.

Malawi is a member of the Commonwealth of Nations and the United Nations, and has a growing economy, with a focus on agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism. The country has a rich history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Stone Age. Malawi has a complex and fascinating cultural heritage, with a blend of traditional and modern influences.
