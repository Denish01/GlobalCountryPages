# Malawi Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Malawi before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Malawi has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens from over 50 countries to enter without a visa or with a visa on arrival. The country aims to promote tourism and facilitate travel for business and other purposes. Citizens from visa-exempt countries can stay in Malawi for up to 30 days, while others may need to obtain a visa in advance or upon arrival. The visa policy is subject to change, and it is essential to check with the Malawian embassy or consulate for the latest information.

Malawi's visa policy is designed to balance the need to facilitate travel with the need to maintain national security and control. The country has a long history of welcoming visitors, and its visa policy reflects this. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly people, Malawi is an attractive destination for tourists, business travelers, and students. The country's visa policy is intended to make it easy for visitors to enter and stay in the country, while also ensuring that national interests are protected.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Malawi with a visa on arrival, which is valid for up to 30 days. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, but this can be extended for an additional 30 days. To extend their stay, tourists must apply to the Malawian immigration authorities. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to provide an invitation letter from a Malawian company or organization, as well as proof of their business activities. They may also need to obtain a business visa, which can be valid for up to 6 months. **Students:** Students must provide proof of enrollment at a Malawian educational institution, as well as proof of financial support. They may need to obtain a student visa, which can be valid for up to 12 months. **Workers:** Workers must have a job offer from a Malawian employer and obtain a work permit, which can be valid for up to 2 years. They may also need to obtain a residence permit.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Malawi are Kamuzu International Airport in Lilongwe and Chileka International Airport in Blantyre. There are also several land border crossings, including those with Mozambique, Tanzania, and Zambia. Upon arrival, visitors must present their travel documents and complete an arrival form. They will also need to undergo immigration and customs procedures, which may include a health screening and baggage inspection. Visitors should ensure that they have all the necessary documents and comply with immigration and customs regulations to avoid any issues.

The immigration process in Malawi is generally straightforward, but visitors should be prepared to provide detailed information about their stay, including their accommodation and travel plans. They should also be aware of any specific requirements, such as the need for a yellow fever vaccination certificate or a health certificate. Visitors who are unsure about the requirements or procedures should consult with the Malawian embassy or consulate in their home country or seek advice from a reputable travel agent.
