# Where to Stay in Djibouti — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Djibouti, consider the capital city of Djibouti as the central hub, with various neighborhoods catering to different types of travelers, from backpackers to luxury seekers.

For each of the top areas, the following characteristics are notable:
**Haramous** (Djibouti)
Best For: Luxury/Business
Vibe: Located in the heart of the city, Haramous offers a blend of modern amenities and traditional Djiboutian culture, with upscale restaurants and shops nearby. The area is known for its vibrant nightlife and lively atmosphere.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to the city center, easy access to major attractions, and a wide range of dining options. 
Cons: Can be noisy due to the nightlife, and prices may be higher than in other areas.

**Plateau du Serpent** (Djibouti)
Best For: Family/Backpacker
Vibe: This historic neighborhood offers a unique blend of French colonial architecture and African culture, with a relaxed atmosphere and stunning views of the Gulf of Tadjourah. The area is known for its quiet streets and local markets.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable prices, rich cultural heritage, and a variety of local eateries. 
Cons: Limited amenities and services compared to other areas.

**Herón** (Djibouti)
Best For: Business/Luxury
Vibe: As a modern and upscale area, Herón boasts sleek skyscrapers, high-end shopping centers, and world-class restaurants, making it an ideal choice for business travelers and those seeking luxury. The area is known for its professional atmosphere and stunning views of the city.
Price Range: $150-400/night
Pros: State-of-the-art amenities, excellent transportation links, and a wide range of entertainment options. 
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and the area may lack the traditional charm of other neighborhoods.

**Tadjourah** (Tadjourah)
Best For: Backpacker/Adventure
Vibe: Located on the coast, Tadjourah is a charming town with a rich history, stunning beaches, and a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for those seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. The area is known for its natural beauty and water sports opportunities.
Price Range: $10-50/night
Pros: Affordable prices, beautiful beaches, and a variety of outdoor activities. 
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and the area may be affected by seasonal weather conditions.

**Ali Sabieh** (Ali Sabieh)
Best For: Family/Backpacker
Vibe: As a smaller town, Ali Sabieh offers a peaceful atmosphere, stunning natural scenery, and a range of outdoor activities, making it an ideal choice for families and backpackers. The area is known for its friendly locals and traditional markets.
Price Range: $15-70/night
Pros: Affordable prices, beautiful scenery, and a variety of local experiences. 
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and the area may be affected by seasonal weather conditions.

**Dikhil** (Dikhil)
Best For: Adventure/Backpacker
Vibe: Located in the western part of the country, Dikhil is a small town surrounded by stunning natural scenery, including mountains, forests, and lakes, making it perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. The area is known for its natural beauty and trekking opportunities.
Price Range: $10-40/night
Pros: Affordable prices, beautiful scenery, and a variety of outdoor activities. 
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and the area may be affected by seasonal weather conditions.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak travel seasons (December to February and June to August).
- Research and compare prices on multiple booking platforms, such as Booking.com, Expedia, and Hotels.com.
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or during off-peak seasons.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during peak seasons.
- Read reviews and check the location of the accommodation before booking.
- Look for accommodations that offer free Wi-Fi, breakfast, and other amenities.
- Consider booking a room with a view, especially in coastal areas.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded markets and public areas.
- Keep valuables secure, and consider using a hotel safe or a money belt.
- Stay hydrated, especially during outdoor activities, and bring sunscreen and a hat.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or other cultural sites.
- Consider hiring a guide or joining a guided tour to explore the country and its attractions.
