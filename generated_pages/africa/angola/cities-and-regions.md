[SECTION] How Angola Is Divided [/SECTION]
Angola is divided into 18 provinces, each with its own capital and administrative structure. The provinces are further divided into municipalities, communes, and villages. The country's administrative divisions are based on a mix of geographical, cultural, and historical factors. The provinces are responsible for providing basic services such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure to their residents.

The 18 provinces of Angola are: Bengo, Benguela, Bié, Cabinda, Cuando Cubango, Cuanza Norte, Cuanza Sul, Cunene, Huambo, Huíla, Luanda, Lunda Norte, Lunda Sul, Malanje, Moxico, Namibe, Uíge, and Zaire. Each province has its own unique character and contributes to the country's diverse cultural and economic landscape.

[SECTION] Regional Differences [/SECTION]
- The northern regions of Angola, such as Zaire and Uíge, have a more tropical climate and are known for their dense rainforests and abundant wildlife.
- The central highlands, including provinces such as Huambo and Bié, have a more temperate climate and are known for their stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.
- The southern regions, including provinces such as Namibe and Cunene, have a more arid climate and are known for their beautiful beaches and vibrant markets.
- The eastern regions, including provinces such as Cuando Cubango and Moxico, have a more rural character and are known for their vast savannas and traditional villages.

[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
Angola has a relatively well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis available. The country's main airport is located in Luanda, and there are several smaller airports throughout the country. The road network is also well-developed, with several major highways connecting the provinces. However, driving conditions can be challenging, especially in rural areas, due to poor road maintenance and lack of infrastructure.

Domestic flights are a convenient way to travel between provinces, with several airlines operating regular flights between major cities. Trains are also available, although the network is limited and services can be unreliable. Buses are a popular mode of transportation, with several companies operating regular services between cities and towns. Taxis are also widely available, although prices can be high and services can be unpredictable.
