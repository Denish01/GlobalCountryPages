# Top Things to Do in Angola — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Angola, some of the top things to do include exploring the historic city of Luanda, visiting the stunning natural beauty of the Namib Desert, and experiencing the unique cultural heritage of the country, with its blend of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **sunset cruise** along the coast of Luanda and enjoy the stunning views of the city skyline
- Experience the unique **Afro-Portuguese cuisine** of Angola, with its blend of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences
- Visit a **traditional market** in Luanda and browse the colorful stalls selling local handicrafts and souvenirs
- Attend a **traditional music performance**, such as a concert of semba music, which is a unique blend of African and Portuguese rhythms
- Take a **desert safari** in the Namib Desert and experience the stunning natural beauty of the desert landscape
- Visit a **local coffee plantation** and learn about the production of coffee in Angola
- Go on a **whale watching tour** and see the humpback whales that migrate to the coast of Angola each year

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit **Iona National Park**, which is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, lions, and giraffes
- Explore the **Namib Desert**, which is one of the most unique and stunning desert landscapes in the world
- Go **hiking** in the **Serra da Leba** mountains, which offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape
- Relax on the **beaches** of **Luanda**, which are known for their stunning natural beauty and vibrant nightlife
- Visit the **Kissama National Park**, which is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, lions, and giraffes
- Take a **boat tour** of the **Kuene River**, which offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and opportunities for wildlife viewing

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Museu Nacional de História Natural**, which offers insight into Angola's natural history and cultural heritage
- Explore the **historic city of Luanda**, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and features a unique blend of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences
- Attend a **traditional festival**, such as the **Festival de Música de Luanda**, which celebrates the unique cultural heritage of Angola
- Visit the **Palácio de Ferro**, which is a historic building that features unique architecture and offers stunning views of the city
- Take a **cooking class** and learn about the unique **Afro-Portuguese cuisine** of Angola
- Visit the **Muxima Church**, which is a historic church that features stunning architecture and offers insight into the cultural heritage of Angola
