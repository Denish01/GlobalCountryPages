What Is Angola?

Angola is a sovereign country located in southern Africa, bordered by the Democratic Republic of the Congo, the Republic of Congo, Zambia, and Namibia. With a population of 35.6 million people, Angola is the seventh most populous country in Africa. The country has a diverse geography, with a long coastline along the Atlantic Ocean, and a varied landscape that includes savannas, forests, and mountains. Angola is known for its rich natural resources, including oil and diamonds, which have played a significant role in the country's economy and history.

Angola has a complex and tumultuous past, with a history of colonization, civil war, and political instability. However, in recent years, the country has made significant progress in rebuilding its economy and infrastructure, and is now one of the fastest-growing economies in Africa. The capital city, Luanda, is a major urban center and a hub for commerce, culture, and entertainment. Angola is also home to a diverse range of cultures, with over 40 ethnic groups and a mix of traditional and modern influences.

Angola's strategic location on the Atlantic coast has made it an important center for trade and commerce, with a long history of interaction with European and other African countries. The country's Portuguese heritage is still evident in its language, architecture, and cuisine, and Angola remains a member of the Portuguese-speaking community of nations. Despite its challenges, Angola has a lot to offer visitors, from its stunning natural beauty to its vibrant culture and rich history.

Key History

  1. 1575 - The Portuguese establish a settlement at Luanda, which becomes a major center for the slave trade.
  2. 1975 - Angola gains independence from Portugal, after a long and bloody struggle.
  3. 1975-2002 - Angola is embroiled in a civil war, fought between the government and the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola (UNITA).
  4. 1992 - The first multiparty elections are held in Angola, but the results are disputed and the civil war continues.
  5. 2002 - The civil war ends with the death of UNITA leader Jonas Savimbi, and the government begins to rebuild the country.
