[SECTION] Overview: Angola vs DR Congo [/SECTION]
Angola and DR Congo are two neighboring countries in Africa with distinct economic profiles. Angola has a relatively high GDP per capita due to its significant oil reserves, while DR Congo has a much lower GDP per capita due to its struggling economy. The cost of living in Angola is generally higher than in DR Congo, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation being significantly higher in Luanda, the capital city of Angola. The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living, with Angola's oil-driven economy leading to higher prices for goods and services.

The overall cost of living in Angola is around 20-30% higher than in DR Congo, making DR Congo a more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees. However, the cost of living in Angola can vary greatly depending on the lifestyle and location, with Luanda being one of the most expensive cities in Africa. In contrast, DR Congo offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. Despite the lower cost of living, DR Congo still faces significant economic challenges, including high inflation and a lack of infrastructure.
