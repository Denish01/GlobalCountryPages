# Education Costs in Angola

The cost of education in Angola varies significantly depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally more affordable than private schools, and international schools being the most expensive.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Angola is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being the largest. However, the quality of education in public schools is often lower than in private schools, due to a lack of resources and infrastructure. The language of instruction is **Portuguese**, and there are 9 years of compulsory education. International schools are available in major cities, catering to expatriate children and offering curricula such as the **International Baccalaureate**.

The public education system in Angola faces several challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers, inadequate infrastructure, and limited resources. In contrast, private schools in Angola offer a higher quality of education, with better facilities and more qualified teachers. However, these schools are often expensive and out of reach for many Angolans. International schools in Angola offer a range of curricula, including the **British Curriculum** and the **American Curriculum**, and are often preferred by expatriate families.
