Visa Policy Overview

Angola has a moderate visa policy, requiring citizens from most countries to obtain a visa before entering the country. Currently, only a few countries have visa-free access to Angola, including Portugal, Mozambique, and a few other African nations. Citizens from other countries can obtain a visa on arrival or through the Angolan embassy or consulate in their home country.

The Angolan government has been working to improve its visa system, introducing an electronic visa (eVisa) system for citizens from certain countries. This has made it easier for tourists and business travelers to obtain a visa and visit the country. However, the requirements and regulations can still be complex, and it's essential to check with the official authorities for the latest information.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists can apply for a tourist visa, which is typically valid for 30 days. The visa can be obtained on arrival at certain airports or through the Angolan embassy or consulate in their home country. Tourists can extend their stay for an additional 30 days, but this must be done through the immigration authorities in Angola.

Business Travelers: Business travelers require a business visa, which must be obtained through the Angolan embassy or consulate in their home country. They will need to provide an invitation letter from an Angolan company and proof of their business activities. The visa is typically valid for 30 days, but can be extended for up to 6 months.

Students: Students require a student visa, which must be obtained through the Angolan embassy or consulate in their home country. They will need to provide proof of enrollment at an Angolan university and a letter of acceptance from the university. The visa is typically valid for the duration of their studies.

Workers: Workers require a work visa, which must be obtained through the Angolan embassy or consulate in their home country. They will need to provide a sponsorship letter from their employer and proof of their qualifications. The visa is typically valid for up to 2 years, but can be extended.

Entry Points & Procedures

The main entry points for Angola are the Luanda International Airport (LAD) and the Catumbela Airport (CBT). Visitors can also enter the country through land borders with neighboring countries, including the Democratic Republic of Congo, the Republic of Congo, Zambia, and Namibia. Upon arrival, visitors will need to go through immigration and customs, where they will need to present their visa, passport, and other required documents. The immigration authorities may also ask questions about the purpose of the visit, the length of stay, and the accommodation arrangements.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not checking the visa requirements before traveling to Angola
  • Not having all the required documents, including a valid passport and proof of onward travel
  • Not applying for the correct type of visa, such as a tourist visa or business visa
  • Not providing sufficient proof of funds for the duration of the stay
  • Not registering with the immigration authorities within 3 days of arrival
  • Not extending the visa or stay permit before it expires
