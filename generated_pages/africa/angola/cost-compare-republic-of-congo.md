[SECTION] Overview: Angola vs Republic of Congo [/SECTION]
Angola and the Republic of Congo are two neighboring countries in Central Africa with distinct economic profiles. Angola has a more developed economy, driven by its oil industry, which contributes to its higher cost of living. In contrast, the Republic of Congo has a more diversified economy, with a stronger focus on agriculture and manufacturing. As a result, the cost of living in the Republic of Congo is generally lower than in Angola. The difference in cost of living between the two countries can be attributed to various factors, including the cost of housing, food, transportation, and utilities.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Angola's economy has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by its oil industry, which has led to an increase in the cost of living. On the other hand, the Republic of Congo's economy has been more stable, with a slower pace of growth, which has helped to keep the cost of living relatively low. Overall, the Republic of Congo offers a more affordable lifestyle, with a lower cost of living index, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees.
