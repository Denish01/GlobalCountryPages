# Moving to Angola — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Angola, you will need to navigate the country's complex visa and residency requirements, understand the local culture and customs, and be prepared for the challenges that come with living in a developing country.

[SECTION] Why People Move to Angola [/SECTION]
Angola is a country with a rich history and culture, and many people are drawn to its unique blend of African and Portuguese influences. The country has a growing economy, with a strong focus on the oil and gas industry, which attracts many expats and foreign investors. Additionally, Angola's natural beauty, with its stunning coastline, mountains, and savannas, makes it an attractive destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The quality of life in Angola can be high, with modern amenities and a vibrant cultural scene available in the capital city of Luanda. However, the cost of living can be relatively high, and the country still faces many development challenges, including poverty, corruption, and infrastructure issues.

Expats in Angola are typically professionals working in the oil and gas industry, as well as diplomats, aid workers, and entrepreneurs. The expat community is relatively small, but it is growing, and there are many opportunities to connect with like-minded individuals. The demographics of the expat community are diverse, with people from all over the world, including Europe, North America, Africa, and Asia. Many expats choose to live in Luanda, which offers a relatively high standard of living and access to modern amenities.

Despite the challenges, many people find that the warm and welcoming nature of the Angolan people, combined with the country's rich culture and natural beauty, make it a great place to live and work. With the right mindset and preparation, expats can thrive in Angola and enjoy a unique and rewarding experience. The country is also investing in its infrastructure, including roads, schools, and healthcare, which is improving the overall quality of life for residents.

- Credit cards are not widely accepted, and it is recommended to have a local bank account and debit card.
- The cost of living in Angola can be high, and it is recommended to budget carefully and plan for unexpected expenses.
