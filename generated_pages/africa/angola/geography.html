Location & Borders

Angola is located in southern Africa, bordering the Atlantic Ocean to the west. It is situated between the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Namibia, with the Republic of Congo and Zambia to the north and east, respectively. The country's unique position allows it to have a diverse geography, with a long coastline along the Atlantic Ocean and a varied landscape that includes mountains, forests, and deserts. The capital city, Luanda, is located on the coast and serves as the country's main port and commercial center.

The borders of Angola are defined by its neighboring countries, with the Democratic Republic of the Congo to the north, the Republic of Congo to the north and east, Zambia to the east, and Namibia to the south. The country's coastline along the Atlantic Ocean provides a significant source of revenue through fishing and oil production. The geography of Angola is also characterized by several major rivers, including the Cuanza, Kuando, and Okavango rivers, which flow through the country and provide water and fertile soil for agriculture.

Terrain & Landscape

The terrain of Angola is varied and complex, with a range of different landscapes and ecosystems. The desert regions of Angola are located in the south and southwest of the country, where the Namib Desert stretches along the coast. This desert is known for its unique landscape of sand dunes, rocky outcrops, and dry riverbeds.
