# Doing Business in Angola — Investment, FDI & Opportunities

Doing business in Angola can be challenging due to its complex bureaucracy and corruption, but the country also offers significant investment opportunities in key sectors such as oil and gas, agriculture, and mining.

[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
The business environment in Angola is characterized by a complex and often slow-moving bureaucracy, which can make it difficult for foreign investors to navigate. The government has implemented various reforms aimed at improving the business climate, but corruption remains a significant challenge. The government's attitude towards foreign investment is generally positive, with a focus on attracting investment in key sectors such as oil and gas, agriculture, and mining. However, the level of bureaucracy and corruption can deter some investors.

The government has established several agencies to promote investment and support businesses, including the Angola Investment Promotion Agency (APIEX) and the National Agency for Private Investment (ANIP). These agencies provide a range of services, including investment promotion, business registration, and support for foreign investors. Despite these efforts, the business environment in Angola remains challenging, and investors need to be prepared to navigate complex regulatory requirements and bureaucratic procedures.

The level of corruption in Angola is a significant concern for foreign investors. Corruption can occur at various levels, from petty bribery to large-scale corruption involving government officials and state-owned enterprises. Investors need to be aware of these risks and take steps to mitigate them, such as implementing robust anti-corruption policies and procedures.

[SECTION] Foreign Direct Investment [/SECTION]
- Foreign direct investment (FDI) in Angola has been increasing in recent years, driven by investments in the oil and gas sector.
- The main investing countries in Angola are China, Portugal, and Brazil.
- The government offers various incentives to attract FDI, including tax exemptions, subsidies, and investment promotion agencies.
- However, some sectors are restricted to foreign investment, including defense, security, and certain areas of the oil and gas sector.
- The government is implementing various reforms to improve the business climate and attract more FDI.

[SECTION] Legal & Regulatory Framework [/SECTION]
- The legal and regulatory framework in Angola is based on **Portuguese law**, with some modifications to reflect the country's specific needs.
- **Business laws** are governed by the Commercial Code, which outlines the rules and regulations for businesses in Angola.
- **Intellectual property protection** is provided by the Industrial Property Law, which protects patents, trademarks, and copyrights.
- **Labor laws** are governed by the Labor Code, which outlines the rights and obligations of employees and employers.
- **Dispute resolution** is provided by the courts and arbitration tribunals, which can be slow and unpredictable.
- The government is implementing various reforms to improve the legal and regulatory framework, including the creation of a **commercial court** to handle business disputes.

[SECTION] Challenges & Risks [/SECTION]
- **Corruption** is a significant challenge in Angola, with corruption occurring at various levels, from petty bribery to large-scale corruption involving government officials and state-owned enterprises.
- **Bureaucratic procedures** can be slow and complex, making it difficult for businesses to navigate the regulatory environment.
- **Infrastructure** is limited in some areas, particularly outside of Luanda, making it difficult to transport goods and people.
- **Currency risks** are significant, with the **kwanza** (AOA) being a volatile currency.
- **Political risk** is also a concern, with the government facing various challenges, including corruption, poverty, and inequality.
- **Market limitations** are a challenge, with the market being relatively small and underdeveloped.

[SECTION] Free Zones & Incentives [/SECTION]
- Angola has established several **special economic zones** (SEZs) to attract investment and promote economic growth, including the Luanda-Bengo SEZ and the Cabinda SEZ.
- The government offers various **tax incentives** to attract investment, including tax exemptions and subsidies.
- **Investment promotion agencies**, such as APIEX and ANIP, provide support to investors, including investment promotion, business registration, and support for foreign investors.
- The government is implementing various **reforms** to improve the business climate and attract more investment, including the creation of a **one-stop shop** for investors.
