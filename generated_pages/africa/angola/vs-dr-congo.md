# Angola vs DR Congo

When deciding between Angola and DR Congo, consider factors like your travel style, interests, and priorities to choose the destination that best fits your needs.

[SECTION] Why People Compare Them [/SECTION]
Angola and DR Congo are often compared due to their geographical proximity and shared cultural heritage as African nations. Both countries have a rich history, diverse natural landscapes, and a blend of traditional and modern influences, making them appealing to travelers and adventure-seekers. The comparison between Angola and DR Congo is also driven by their unique characteristics, such as Angola's coastal scenery and DR Congo's vast wilderness areas, which cater to different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Key Differences [/SECTION]
- **Population**: Angola has a significantly smaller population than DR Congo, with approximately 36 million people compared to over 102 million.
- **Language**: The official language of Angola is Portuguese, while DR Congo has multiple official languages, including French, Lingala, Kiswahili, and others.
- **Currency**: Angola uses the Angolan Kwanza (AOA), whereas DR Congo uses the Congolese Franc (CDF).
- **Climate**: Angola has a tropical climate with a dry season, while DR Congo experiences a tropical climate with both wet and dry seasons.
- **Cost Level**: Angola is generally more expensive than DR Congo, with higher prices for accommodations, food, and activities.
- **Safety Level**: DR Congo is considered less safe than Angola, with higher crime rates and instability in certain regions.

[SECTION] Key Similarities [/SECTION]
- **Natural Resources**: Both Angola and DR Congo are rich in natural resources, including oil, minerals, and timber.
- **Cultural Heritage**: Both countries have a vibrant cultural scene, with a mix of traditional and modern influences, including music, dance, and art.
- **Wildlife**: Both countries are home to diverse and abundant wildlife, including elephants, lions, and gorillas.
- **Colonial History**: Both Angola and DR Congo were colonized by European powers, with Angola being a former Portuguese colony and DR Congo being a former Belgian colony.
