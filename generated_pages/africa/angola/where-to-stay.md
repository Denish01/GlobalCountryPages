# Where to Stay in Angola — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

If you're planning to visit Angola, the best areas to stay are in the capital city Luanda, with neighborhoods like **Miramar** and **Maianga** offering a range of accommodation options for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**Miramar** (Luanda)
Best For: Luxury
Vibe: Known for its stunning ocean views and modern architecture, Miramar is a upscale neighborhood with a relaxed atmosphere. It's home to many embassies and international organizations, giving it a cosmopolitan feel.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: Close proximity to the city center, a variety of high-end restaurants and bars, and easy access to the beach. 
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and traffic in the area can be heavy during rush hour.

**Maianga** (Luanda)
Best For: Business
Vibe: As one of the oldest neighborhoods in Luanda, Maianga has a unique blend of colonial and modern architecture, with a lively atmosphere and a mix of local and international businesses. 
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Centrally located, with many shops, restaurants, and services nearby, making it an ideal location for business travelers. 
Cons: Can be noisy, especially at night, and traffic congestion is common.

**Alvalade** (Luanda)
Best For: Family
Vibe: A residential neighborhood with a more laid-back atmosphere, Alvalade is known for its wide streets, parks, and gardens, making it a great area for families to stay. 
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: A quieter area with plenty of space for kids to play, and easy access to local amenities and services. 
Cons: Not as many dining or entertainment options as other areas, and can feel a bit far from the city center.

**Marginal** (Luanda)
Best For: Backpacker
Vibe: A coastal neighborhood with a lively atmosphere, Marginal is a popular area for young travelers and backpackers, with many budget-friendly accommodation options and a vibrant nightlife. 
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable prices, a variety of bars and clubs, and stunning ocean views. 
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, especially at night, and some areas can feel a bit run-down.

**Talatona** (Luanda)
Best For: Luxury
Vibe: An upscale neighborhood with modern architecture and a relaxed atmosphere, Talatona is a popular area for luxury travelers, with many high-end amenities and services nearby. 
Price Range: $250-600/night
Pros: Close proximity to the city center, a variety of high-end restaurants and bars, and easy access to the beach. 
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and some areas can feel a bit isolated.
