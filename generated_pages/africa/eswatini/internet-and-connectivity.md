The internet in Eswatini is relatively well-developed, with a growing number of internet users and a decent average internet speed, making it possible for tourists and remote workers to stay connected during their stay.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Eswatini has seen significant improvements in recent years, with the average internet speed increasing to 10 Mbps download. However, there is still a noticeable urban-rural divide, with faster and more reliable internet connections available in urban areas such as Mbabane and Manzini, while rural areas often experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. The internet quality in Eswatini is generally good, with most users able to access **broadband** connections, but the reliability can be affected by factors such as weather conditions and aging infrastructure.

Despite these challenges, the internet has become an essential tool for many people in Eswatini, with a growing number of internet users and a increasing demand for online services. The government has also launched initiatives to improve the country's **information and communication technology (ICT)** infrastructure, including the development of a national **fiber optic network**. These efforts aim to increase internet penetration, improve connectivity, and enhance the overall quality of the internet in Eswatini.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Eswatini, tourists can visit one of the main carriers' stores, such as MTN or Eswatini Mobile, and purchase a prepaid SIM card for approximately 10 USD. The process typically requires a valid passport and proof of address, and the SIM card will be activated immediately. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable carrier, as they often offer better coverage and more competitive data plans. Some popular data plans include MTN's **Swazi Bundle**, which offers 1 GB of data for 5 USD, and Eswatini Mobile's **Data Bundle**, which offers 2 GB of data for 10 USD.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels and lodges offering free WiFi to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is growing, with many cafes and restaurants in urban areas offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are limited, but some cities like Mbabane have a few options that offer reliable WiFi and a productive work environment.
- Public WiFi is available in some areas, such as shopping malls and public libraries, but the speed and reliability can vary.
- Some restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi, but the speed and availability can be limited.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like the **Mbabane Hub** offer a productive work environment with reliable WiFi and other amenities.
- Reliable cafe chains like **The Coffee Shop** offer good WiFi and a comfortable work space.
- Backup internet options like **mobile hotspots** can be useful in case of internet outages or slow speeds.
- Some hotels and lodges offer **dedicated workspaces** with reliable WiFi and other amenities.
- Using a **virtual private network (VPN)** can help remote workers stay secure and private while working online.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major concern in Eswatini, but some websites may be blocked due to **content restrictions**.
- Using a VPN can help bypass **geo-restrictions** and access blocked websites.
- Roaming alternatives like **eSIM** can be a convenient option for tourists who want to stay connected without changing their physical SIM card.
- Public WiFi networks can be **vulnerable to hacking**, so it's essential to use a VPN and keep software up to date.
- Some internet service providers may have **data caps** or **throttling**, so it's essential to check the terms and conditions before purchasing a data plan.
- Regular **software updates** can help keep devices secure and ensure a smooth internet experience.
