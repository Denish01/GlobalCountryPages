[SECTION] Overview: Eswatini vs Mozambique [/SECTION]
Eswatini and Mozambique are two neighboring countries in Southern Africa with distinct economic profiles. Eswatini, a small, landlocked country, has a higher GDP per capita and a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism. Mozambique, on the other hand, is a larger country with a lower GDP per capita and a more limited economy, heavily reliant on natural resources and agriculture. In terms of cost of living, Mozambique is generally cheaper than Eswatini, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Eswatini has a more stable economy, with a strong currency and a relatively low inflation rate, which contributes to its higher cost of living. Mozambique, on the other hand, has a more volatile economy, with a weaker currency and a higher inflation rate, which makes it more affordable for expats and tourists. Overall, Mozambique is approximately 20-30% cheaper than Eswatini, making it a more attractive option for those looking for a lower cost of living.
