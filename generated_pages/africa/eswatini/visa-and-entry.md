# Eswatini Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Eswatini before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Eswatini has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others may obtain a visa on arrival or require a visa in advance. The country aims to facilitate tourism and business travel while maintaining national security. Citizens of over 90 countries can enter Eswatini without a visa, including many African, European, and American nations. However, it is essential to check the specific requirements for your nationality to ensure a smooth entry process.

Eswatini's visa policy is designed to attract visitors and investors, promoting economic growth and cultural exchange. The country offers various types of visas, including tourist, business, student, and work visas, each with its own set of requirements and regulations. Understanding the visa policy and requirements is crucial to avoid any complications or delays during your trip.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Eswatini visa-free for up to 30 days, provided they have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, and extensions can be granted for an additional 30 days. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may enter Eswatini visa-free for up to 30 days, but they must provide a letter of invitation from a registered Eswatini company and proof of their business activities. **Students:** Students must apply for a student visa in advance, providing proof of enrollment at a recognized Eswatini institution and sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. The duration of the student visa is typically one year, renewable for the duration of the course. **Workers:** Workers must apply for a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a registered Eswatini employer. The process involves submitting various documents, including a contract, qualifications, and medical certificate.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Eswatini are the King Mswati III International Airport (SHO) and the Matsapha International Airport (MTS), as well as several land borders with South Africa and Mozambique. Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and answer questions about their stay. The immigration process is generally straightforward, but it is essential to ensure that all documents are in order to avoid any delays or complications. Visitors may also be required to provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Failing to check the visa requirements for your nationality
- Not having a valid passport with at least 6 months' validity
- Insufficient proof of onward travel or funds
- Not having the required documents for business, student, or work visas
- Not applying for a visa in advance when required
- Not registering with the local authorities within the required timeframe (for longer stays)
