The earliest known history of Eswatini dates back to the Stone Age, with evidence of human habitation found in the form of rock art and stone tools. The area was later inhabited by Bantu-speaking peoples, who migrated to the region from Central Africa. The kingdom was known for its strong monarchy and traditional customs, which continue to play an important role in Eswatini's culture today. In the late 19th century, Eswatini became a British protectorate, and later a colony, which had a significant impact on the country's development.
