# Education Costs in Eswatini

The cost of education in Eswatini varies from free or subsidized for public schools to several thousand dollars per year for private and international schools.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Eswatini is divided into public and private schools, with the public schools generally considered to be of lower quality due to limited resources and infrastructure. The language of instruction in public schools is primarily **Swazi**, while private schools often use **English** as the primary language of instruction. Compulsory education in Eswatini is 7 years, and students typically attend school from the age of 6 to 13. International schools are also available, primarily in the capital city of **Mbabane**, and offer a range of curricula including **British**, **American**, and **International Baccalaureate**.

The quality of education in Eswatini is generally considered to be lower than in other countries in the region, due in part to limited resources and infrastructure. However, there are some high-quality private schools and international schools that offer a more comprehensive education. These schools often have better facilities, more qualified teachers, and a more rigorous curriculum, making them a popular choice for expats and those who can afford the higher fees.
