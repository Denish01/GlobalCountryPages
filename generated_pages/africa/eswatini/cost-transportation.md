[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Eswatini has a relatively underdeveloped public transportation system, with most people relying on **minibuses** and **shared taxis** to get around. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with Mbabane and Manzini having more frequent and reliable services. However, the lack of a comprehensive public transportation network means that many people rely on private vehicles or **ride-hailing apps** to get around. The country's road network is generally in good condition, but traffic congestion can be a problem in urban areas.

The main transportation modes in Eswatini are **minibuses**, **shared taxis**, and **private vehicles**. Minibuses and shared taxis are the most affordable options, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable. Private vehicles are more convenient, but they can be expensive to maintain and fuel. The government has been working to improve the public transportation system, but there is still a long way to go. Despite the challenges, Eswatini's transportation system is relatively affordable, with the cost of fuel and transportation services comparable to other countries in the region.
