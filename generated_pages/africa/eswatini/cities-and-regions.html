How Eswatini Is Divided

Eswatini is a small, landlocked country in southern Africa, divided into four administrative regions. These regions are Hhohho, Lubombo, Manzini, and Shiselweni. Each region has its own unique character, with varying landscapes, climates, and economic activities. The regions are further divided into smaller districts, known as "tinkhundla", which are used for local administration and governance.

The administrative structure of Eswatini is designed to promote decentralization and community participation in decision-making. The four regions are represented in the national government, and each region has its own regional administration, which is responsible for delivering services and implementing national policies at the local level. This system helps to ensure that the needs and concerns of local communities are taken into account in national decision-making.

Major Cities

Mbabane (95,000)

Role: capital city, administrative center

Known For: Mbabane is a small, picturesque city surrounded by mountains, with a mix of traditional and modern architecture. It is the seat of government and the commercial center of Eswatini.

Best For: visitors interested in history, culture, and government, as well as those looking for a relaxed, small-town atmosphere.

Manzini (110,000)

Role: economic hub, commercial center

Known For: Manzini is the largest city in Eswatini and the country's main economic hub, with a busy market and a range of industries, including textiles and food processing. It is a bustling, vibrant city with a mix of traditional and modern culture.

Best For: visitors interested in business, commerce, and industry, as well as those looking for a lively, urban atmosphere.

Big Bend (10,000)

Role: rural town, agricultural center

Known For: Big Bend is a small town located in the southeastern part of Eswatini, in the Lubombo region. It is surrounded by fertile agricultural land and is known for its sugar cane and cotton production.

Best For: visitors interested in rural life, agriculture, and outdoor activities, such as hiking and birdwatching.

Nhlangano (7,000)

Role: rural town, administrative center

Known For: Nhlangano is a small town located in the southern part of Eswatini, in the Shiselweni region. It is the administrative center of the region and is known for its natural beauty, with surrounding mountains and forests.

Best For: visitors interested in rural life, nature, and outdoor activities, such as hiking and fishing.

Piggs Peak (5,000)

Role: rural town, tourist center

Known For: Piggs Peak is a small town located in the northwestern part of Eswatini, in the Hhohho region. It is known for its natural beauty, with surrounding mountains and forests, and is a popular destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts.

Best For: visitors interested in nature, outdoor activities, and adventure sports, such as hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.

Regional Differences

  • The Hhohho region is known for its mountainous terrain and natural beauty, with a strong focus on tourism and outdoor activities.
  • The Lubombo region is characterized by its lowveld landscape and agricultural production, with a focus on sugar cane and cotton.
  • The Manzini region is urban and commercial, with a focus on industry and economic development.
  • The Shiselweni region is rural and traditional, with a strong focus on cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Getting Around

Eswatini has a relatively well-developed transportation network, with a range of options for getting around the country. The main roads are paved and well-maintained, making it easy to travel by car or bus. There are also domestic flights available between the major towns, as well as a limited rail network. The country has a number of bus companies that operate scheduled services between the main towns, and taxis are also widely available. For visitors, renting a car is a popular option, as it provides flexibility and freedom to explore the country at your own pace. However, driving conditions can be challenging, particularly on rural roads, and visitors should be aware of the local traffic laws and regulations.

Overall, Eswatini is a small and easily accessible country, with a range of transportation options available to suit different needs and budgets. With its beautiful landscapes, rich culture, and friendly people, Eswatini is a great destination for visitors looking to experience the best of Africa.
