[SECTION] Overview: Eswatini vs Lesotho [/SECTION]
Eswatini and Lesotho are two neighboring countries in Southern Africa, both with unique cultural and economic contexts. Eswatini has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism, while Lesotho's economy is primarily driven by textiles and mining. In terms of cost of living, Eswatini is generally cheaper than Lesotho, with a lower overall cost of living index. This is reflected in the prices of everyday items, such as food, housing, and transportation. However, Lesotho has a lower cost of living in certain categories, such as utilities and internet.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Eswatini has a more stable economy, with a lower inflation rate and a higher GDP per capita. This stability is reflected in the country's lower prices for goods and services. Lesotho, on the other hand, has a higher inflation rate and a lower GDP per capita, which can make it more challenging for residents to afford everyday items. Overall, Eswatini is the cheaper option for most expats and travelers, but Lesotho has its own unique advantages and attractions.
