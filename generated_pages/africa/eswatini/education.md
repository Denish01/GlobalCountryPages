[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Eswatini is divided into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 13, and is provided free of charge by the government. Secondary education is also provided by the government, as well as by private schools, and typically lasts for 5 years. Tertiary education is available at universities and colleges, and provides students with a range of academic and professional programs to choose from. The quality of education in Eswatini is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on **academic achievement** and **vocational training**. However, there are some challenges facing the education system, including a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of resources in some schools.

The education system in Eswatini is overseen by the Ministry of Education and Training, which is responsible for setting education policy and standards. The ministry works closely with schools and universities to ensure that students receive a high-quality education that prepares them for success in their future careers. In addition to public schools, there are also a number of private schools in Eswatini that offer a range of academic programs and **extracurricular activities**. These schools are often preferred by parents who can afford to pay the tuition fees, as they are seen as providing a higher quality education than public schools.

Overall, the education system in Eswatini provides students with a range of opportunities to pursue their academic and professional goals. While there are some challenges facing the system, the government and other stakeholders are working to address these issues and improve the quality of education in the country.
