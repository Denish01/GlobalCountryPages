# Monthly Budget Guide for Eswatini

To live in Eswatini, you would need approximately $600-1,200 per month for a comfortable lifestyle, depending on your personal preferences and habits.

[FACTBOX]

Mid-Range Living (monthly): $700 USD
Comfortable Living (monthly): $1,000 USD
Average Local Salary: $250 USD/month, approximate
Currency: SZL
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Cost of Living Overview [/SECTION]
Eswatini offers a unique blend of traditional and modern lifestyles, with a relatively low cost of living compared to other countries in the region. The GDP per capita (PPP) of $11,799 suggests that the country has a relatively high standard of living, and this is reflected in the cost of living. A budget of $400 per month can provide a basic but comfortable lifestyle, while $700 per month can offer a mid-range lifestyle with some luxuries. A comfortable lifestyle, on the other hand, would require around $1,000 per month. Compared to neighboring countries, Eswatini is generally more affordable than South Africa but more expensive than Mozambique.

The cost of living in Eswatini varies depending on the location, with urban areas like Mbabane and Manzini tend to be more expensive than rural areas. However, even in the cities, the cost of living is relatively low compared to other major cities in the region. For example, a meal at a mid-range restaurant can cost around $5-10 per person, while a one-bedroom apartment in the city center can cost around $200-300 per month. Overall, Eswatini offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists, expats, and digital nomads.

[SECTION] Budget Tier Lifestyles [/SECTION]
At the budget level of $400 per month, daily life in Eswatini can be quite basic but comfortable. You can expect to share an apartment with others, cook most of your meals, and use public transportation. You can still enjoy some of the local culture and scenery, but you may have to be careful with your expenses and prioritize your spending. For example, you can visit the local markets and try some of the traditional food, but you may not be able to afford to eat out at restaurants often.

At the mid-range level of $700 per month, you can enjoy a more comfortable lifestyle in Eswatini. You can afford to rent a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, eat out at mid-range restaurants, and use a combination of public and private transportation. You can also enjoy some of the local entertainment options, such as going to the movies or attending cultural events. However, you may still have to be mindful of your expenses and make some compromises, such as cooking at home most nights instead of eating out.

At the comfortable level of $1,000 per month, you can enjoy a high standard of living in Eswatini. You can afford to rent a nice two-bedroom apartment, eat out at high-end restaurants, and use a car or taxi for transportation. You can also enjoy a wide range of entertainment options, such as going to the gym, taking classes, or attending luxury events. You can also travel within the country and enjoy some of the local tourist attractions, such as the Hlane Royal National Park or the Milwane Wildlife Sanctuary.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Strategies [/SECTION]
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or cars to get around
- Cook most of your meals at home instead of eating out
- Shop at local markets for fresh produce and other essentials
- Avoid eating at touristy restaurants and instead opt for local eateries
- Use cash instead of credit cards to avoid high fees
- Negotiate prices when shopping at markets or from street vendors
- Consider sharing an apartment with others to split the rent
- Take advantage of free or low-cost entertainment options, such as hiking or visiting local parks

[SECTION] Is Eswatini Affordable? [/SECTION]
Eswatini can be an affordable destination for digital nomads, retirees, and students, depending on their individual circumstances and lifestyle preferences. The cost of living is relatively low, and the country offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life. However, for families or individuals with high expectations for luxury and comfort, Eswatini may not be the most affordable option. Additionally, the country's economy is still developing, and some amenities and services may not be as readily available or affordable as in more developed countries.

Overall, Eswatini is a great option for those who are looking for a unique and affordable African experience. The country offers a rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and a relatively low cost of living, making it an attractive destination for tourists, expats, and digital nomads. However, it's essential to have realistic expectations and to research thoroughly before making any decisions about moving to or visiting Eswatini. With some planning and flexibility, you can enjoy a comfortable and affordable lifestyle in this beautiful African country.
