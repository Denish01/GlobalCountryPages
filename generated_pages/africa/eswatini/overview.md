[SECTION] What Is Eswatini? [/SECTION]
Eswatini, formerly known as Swaziland, is a **sovereign** country located in southern Africa. It is bordered by South Africa to the north, south, and west, and Mozambique to the east. With a population of 1.2 million people, Eswatini is one of the smallest countries in Africa, but it is known for its diverse geography, which ranges from mountains to savannas. The country has a unique cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on traditional practices and customs.

Eswatini is a **monarchy**, with the king serving as the head of state. The country has a diverse economy, with a mix of agriculture, mining, and manufacturing. The capital city, Mbabane, is the largest city in the country and is the center of government and commerce. Eswatini is also known for its natural beauty, with several national parks and game reserves that are home to a wide range of wildlife, including lions, elephants, and giraffes.

The country has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on traditional music, dance, and art. The Swazi people are known for their colorful traditional clothing and their vibrant festivals, which showcase their music, dance, and crafts. Eswatini is also home to several important historical sites, including the **Ngwenya Mine**, which is one of the oldest mines in the world.
