# Moving to Eswatini — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Eswatini, you should start by researching the country's visa and residency options, understanding the cost of living, and planning your relocation carefully, as the process can be complex and time-consuming. 

[SECTION] Why People Move to Eswatini [/SECTION]
Eswatini, formerly known as Swaziland, is a small, landlocked country in southern Africa that offers a unique blend of traditional culture and modern amenities, attracting expats and tourists alike. The country's stunning natural beauty, with its mountains, forests, and wildlife reserves, makes it an attractive destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Additionally, Eswatini has a growing economy, with a mix of industries such as agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism, providing opportunities for **foreign investment** and **career development**.

The expat community in Eswatini is relatively small, but diverse, with people from various countries and backgrounds, including South Africa, the United Kingdom, and the United States. The country's **quality of life** is generally good, with a low cost of living, a warm climate, and a relatively safe environment. However, the country still faces challenges such as poverty, unemployment, and limited access to healthcare and education, which can be a concern for some expats.

Despite these challenges, many expats find Eswatini to be a welcoming and **cultural rich** country, with a strong sense of community and tradition. The country's **Swazi culture** is known for its vibrant music, dance, and art, and expats can experience this firsthand by attending traditional festivals and events. Overall, Eswatini offers a unique and rewarding experience for expats who are looking for a new adventure and a chance to make a positive impact in a developing country.
