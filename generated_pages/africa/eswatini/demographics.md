# Eswatini Demographics — Population, Ethnicity & Religion

The population of Eswatini is comprised of approximately 1.2 million people, with a diverse mix of ethnic groups, languages, and religions, showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage.

[SECTION] Population Overview [/SECTION]
The population of Eswatini is relatively small compared to other countries in Africa, with a total of approximately 1.2 million people. The population growth rate is around 1.1%, which is relatively low compared to other countries in the region. The median age of the population is 21.7 years, indicating a relatively young population. Urbanization is also a significant trend in Eswatini, with around 24% of the population living in urban areas. The population density is approximately 71 people per square kilometer, which is relatively low compared to other countries in the region.

In terms of growth trends, Eswatini's population is expected to continue growing, albeit at a slow rate. The country's population is also experiencing a shift towards urbanization, with many people moving from rural areas to urban centers in search of better economic opportunities. Migration patterns in Eswatini are also significant, with many people moving to neighboring countries such as South Africa in search of work.

The population of Eswatini is also affected by various social and economic factors, including high levels of poverty and unemployment. The country also has a significant HIV/AIDS epidemic, which has had a major impact on the population, particularly in terms of life expectancy and mortality rates. Despite these challenges, the government of Eswatini is working to improve the living standards of its citizens, including through the provision of basic services such as healthcare and education.
