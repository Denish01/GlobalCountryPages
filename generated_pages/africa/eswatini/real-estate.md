[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The property market in Eswatini is relatively small and developing, with a mix of modern and traditional homes, as well as commercial properties. The market is driven by a combination of local demand and foreign investment, with a growing trend towards **urbanization** and development of **tourism infrastructure**. Prices have been steadily increasing over the past few years, with a notable surge in demand for properties in the capital city, Mbabane, and other urban areas. However, the market is still relatively **affordable** compared to other countries in the region, making it an attractive option for investors and homebuyers.

The market can be broadly divided into urban and rural areas, with the former offering a range of modern amenities and services, while the latter provides a more traditional and **rural lifestyle**. The urban areas, such as Mbabane and Manzini, are hubs for **commercial activity** and offer a range of properties, from apartments to houses and commercial buildings. In contrast, the rural areas are largely **agricultural** and offer a more relaxed pace of life, with opportunities for **farming** and **ecotourism**.

The government of Eswatini has implemented various initiatives to promote **foreign investment** and stimulate economic growth, including the establishment of **special economic zones** and **investment incentives**. These efforts have contributed to an increase in foreign investment in the property sector, with many investors attracted to the country's **strategic location** and **natural resources**.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners can buy property in Eswatini, but they must obtain a **permit** from the Ministry of Housing and Urban Development.
- Foreigners are restricted from buying **agricultural land**, which is reserved for Swazi citizens.
- Foreigners can lease land for a period of up to **50 years**, with the option to renew.
- Foreign companies can own property in Eswatini, but they must be registered with the **Companies Registry**.
- Foreigners must also comply with **exchange control regulations** and obtain approval from the Central Bank of Eswatini.

[SECTION] Buying Process [/SECTION]
1. **Search** for a property that meets your requirements and budget.
2. **Appoint** a real estate agent or lawyer to assist with the purchase process.
3. **Conduct** a **title search** to ensure the property has a clear title.
4. **Negotiate** the price and terms of the sale with the seller.
5. **Sign** a **sale agreement** and pay a **deposit**.
6. **Obtain** a **permit** from the Ministry of Housing and Urban Development.
7. **Register** the property with the **Deeds Registry**.
8. **Pay** the **balance** of the purchase price.
9. **Transfer** the property into your name.
10. **Obtain** a **certificate of ownership** from the Deeds Registry.

[SECTION] Rental Market [/SECTION]
- **Tenant rights** are protected under the **Rent Control Act**, which regulates rent increases and eviction procedures.
- **Typical lease terms** range from 6 to 12 months, with a 2-3 month **notice period**.
- **Deposit norms** require a deposit equivalent to 1-2 months' rent.
- **Furnished** properties are more common in urban areas, while **unfurnished** properties are more common in rural areas.
- **Rentals** can be managed by a **property management company** or by the owner themselves.
