# Eswatini vs Lesotho

When deciding between Eswatini and Lesotho, consider your priorities in terms of culture, nature, and travel style, as both countries offer unique experiences despite their similarities.

[SECTION] Why People Compare Them [/SECTION]
Eswatini and Lesotho are often compared because they are both small, landlocked countries located in Southern Africa, surrounded by South Africa, and share similar cultural and historical backgrounds. They are also both known for their stunning natural beauty, with mountains, valleys, and wildlife reserves, making them attractive destinations for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

The comparison between Eswatini and Lesotho is also driven by their proximity and the fact that they are both relatively off-the-beaten-path destinations, which can make them appealing to travelers looking for unique and authentic experiences. Additionally, both countries have a strong focus on traditional culture and heritage, with a mix of African, European, and colonial influences, which can be fascinating for visitors interested in history and cultural diversity.

[SECTION] Key Differences [/SECTION]
- **Population**: Eswatini has a smaller population compared to Lesotho, with approximately 1.2 million people, while Lesotho has around 2.1 million inhabitants.
- **Size**: Lesotho is significantly larger than Eswatini, covering an area of 30,355 km², while Eswatini covers 17,364 km².
- **Language**: While both countries have English as an official language, Eswatini's primary language is Swazi, and Lesotho's is Sesotho.
- **Currency**: Eswatini uses the Swazi lilangeni (SZL), and Lesotho uses the Lesotho loti (LSL).
- **Climate**: Eswatini has a subtropical climate, while Lesotho has a temperate climate, with colder winters and milder summers.
- **Cost Level**: Lesotho is generally considered to be more budget-friendly than Eswatini, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and activities.

[SECTION] Key Similarities [/SECTION]
- **Culture**: Both countries have a rich cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on traditional customs, music, and dance.
- **Nature**: Both Eswatini and Lesotho are known for their stunning natural beauty, with mountains, valleys, and wildlife reserves.
- **History**: Both countries have a complex history, with a mix of African, European, and colonial influences.
- **Hospitality**: Both Eswatini and Lesotho are known for their warm and welcoming people, making visitors feel at home.
