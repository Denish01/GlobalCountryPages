Overall Safety Assessment

Benin is generally a safe country to visit, with low levels of violent crime. However, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, purse snatching, and scams are common in tourist areas and crowded markets. Visitors should be cautious when walking alone at night, especially in poorly lit areas, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash. The country has a relatively stable political climate, but demonstrations and protests can occur, and visitors should avoid areas where large crowds are gathering.

As with any travel destination, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to stay safe in Benin. This includes being aware of your surroundings, keeping valuables secure, and avoiding traveling alone at night. The Beninese government has taken steps to improve security, including the establishment of a tourist police force, which can provide assistance to visitors. Despite the potential risks, many tourists visit Benin each year without incident, and with proper planning and precautions, you can have a safe and enjoyable trip.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of scams involving ATM skimming and credit card fraud
  • Be aware of pickpocketing and purse snatching in crowded markets and tourist areas
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use reputable exchange offices or banks
  • Be mindful of scams involving fake police officers or officials
  • Be cautious when using taxis, and always agree on a price before starting your journey
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in poorly lit areas
  • Be aware of demonstrations and protests, and avoid areas where large crowds are gathering
  • Be cautious of food and water poisoning, and take necessary precautions to stay healthy

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are limited, especially outside of major cities, and medical evacuation may be necessary in serious cases
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are available in major cities, but access to medication may be limited in rural areas

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always carry a copy of your passport and important documents
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
  • Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe
  • Stay informed about local conditions and events that may affect your safety
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Learn some basic French phrases, which can help you communicate with locals
  • Stay hydrated and take necessary precautions to avoid heat exhaustion

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas
  • Dress modestly, and be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and consider using reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Stay in touch with family and friends back home, and consider registering with your government's travel advisory program
