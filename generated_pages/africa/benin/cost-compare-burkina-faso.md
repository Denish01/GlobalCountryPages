[SECTION] Overview: Benin vs Burkina Faso [/SECTION]
Benin and Burkina Faso are two neighboring countries in West Africa, both belonging to the **Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS)**. While they share some cultural and economic similarities, there are notable differences in their cost of living. Benin has a slightly higher GDP per capita and a lower inflation rate compared to Burkina Faso, which contributes to its relatively lower cost of living. The overall cost of living in Benin is around 10-15% lower than in Burkina Faso, making it a more affordable option for expats, students, and travelers.

The economic context of both countries is largely driven by agriculture, with Benin having a more diversified economy and a stronger **service sector**. This diversity contributes to a more stable and affordable cost of living in Benin. In contrast, Burkina Faso's economy is more reliant on **gold mining** and **agriculture**, which can lead to fluctuations in the cost of living. Overall, Benin offers a more stable and affordable cost of living, making it an attractive option for those looking to live or travel in West Africa.
