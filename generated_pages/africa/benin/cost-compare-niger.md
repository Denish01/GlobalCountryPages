[SECTION] Overview: Benin vs Niger [/SECTION]
Benin and Niger are two neighboring countries in West Africa with distinct economic profiles. Benin has a relatively more diversified economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, trade, and services, whereas Niger's economy is heavily reliant on agriculture and mining. In terms of cost of living, Benin tends to be more affordable than Niger, particularly when it comes to housing and food. The overall cost of living in Benin is around 10-15% lower than in Niger, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Benin has a more stable economy, with a lower inflation rate and a higher GDP per capita, which contributes to its relatively lower cost of living. In contrast, Niger's economy is more vulnerable to fluctuations in global commodity prices, which can lead to higher inflation and a decrease in purchasing power. As a result, Niger tends to be more expensive than Benin, particularly when it comes to imported goods and services.
