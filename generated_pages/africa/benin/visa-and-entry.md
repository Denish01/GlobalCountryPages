# Benin Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Benin before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Benin has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival. The country offers visa-free access to citizens of several African countries, as well as a few other nations. In total, around 20 countries have visa-free access to Benin, while many others can obtain a visa on arrival or through an e-visa system. This policy aims to promote tourism and economic development in the country.

Benin's visa policy is designed to balance the need for security and control with the desire to attract visitors and investors. The country has made efforts to simplify its visa application process and reduce processing times, making it easier for travelers to plan their trips. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it is crucial to verify the current requirements before traveling to Benin.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can typically stay in Benin for up to 30 days, and they may be required to provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds. Extensions are possible but must be applied for at the immigration office in Porto-Novo. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to provide an invitation letter from a Beninese company, as well as proof of their business activities. They may also be required to register with the local authorities. **Students:** Students must provide proof of enrollment in a Beninese educational institution, as well as a valid passport and health insurance. The duration of their stay will depend on the length of their studies. **Workers:** Workers must have a valid work permit, which can be obtained through their employer. They will also need to provide proof of qualifications and experience.

[SECTION] Documents Checklist [/SECTION]
1. A valid passport with at least six months' validity
2. A completed visa application form (if required)
3. One recent passport-sized photo
4. Proof of onward travel or return ticket
5. Proof of sufficient funds (at least 100,000 XOF per person)
6. Health insurance that covers the duration of stay
7. A hotel reservation or proof of accommodation
8. A yellow fever vaccination certificate (if traveling from a high-risk area)
9. A police certificate (for workers or students)
10. A copy of the invitation letter (for business travelers or students)

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airport in Benin is the Cotonou Cadjehoun Airport (COO), which receives international flights from several African and European cities. There are also several land borders, including those with Togo, Burkina Faso, Niger, and Nigeria. When arriving in Benin, travelers will need to go through immigration and customs procedures, which may include filling out a declaration form and presenting their passport and visa (if required). It is essential to have all required documents ready and to be prepared to answer questions from immigration officials.

Travelers can also enter Benin by land or sea, but they will still need to go through the necessary procedures and present their documents to the authorities. It is recommended to check with the relevant authorities or a travel agent to ensure that all requirements are met before attempting to enter the country.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Failing to verify the current visa requirements before traveling
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not providing proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
- Not having the required vaccinations, including yellow fever
- Not declaring all goods and valuables when going through customs
- Not registering with the local authorities (for business travelers or workers)
