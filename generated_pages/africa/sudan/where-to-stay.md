# Where to Stay in Sudan — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To determine where to stay in Sudan, consider the country's capital city Khartoum and its surrounding neighborhoods, which offer a mix of modern amenities, cultural experiences, and historical landmarks.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For travelers visiting Sudan, the following areas are highly recommended:
**Khartoum Downtown** (Khartoum)
Best For: luxury / business
Vibe: Khartoum Downtown is the heart of the city, with modern buildings, shopping centers, and restaurants, offering a cosmopolitan atmosphere. The area is also close to many historical landmarks, including the National Museum of Sudan.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: convenient location, modern amenities, access to public transportation
Cons: traffic congestion, limited budget options

**Omdurman** (Omdurman)
Best For: family / culture
Vibe: Omdurman is a historic city located just across the Nile River from Khartoum, known for its vibrant markets, traditional restaurants, and cultural landmarks like the Khalifa's House. The area offers a unique glimpse into Sudanese culture and history.
Price Range: $50-150/night
Pros: rich cultural heritage, traditional cuisine, affordable prices
Cons: limited modern amenities, crowded streets

**Khartoum North** (Khartoum)
Best For: backpacker / budget
Vibe: Khartoum North is a more laid-back area, with a mix of old and new buildings, and a thriving expat community. The area is close to the Nile River and offers stunning views, as well as a range of affordable accommodation options.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: affordable prices, expat community, scenic views
Cons: limited amenities, traffic congestion

**Port Sudan** (Port Sudan)
Best For: luxury / relaxation
Vibe: Port Sudan is a coastal city located in the northeastern part of the country, known for its beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and water sports. The area is ideal for those seeking relaxation and leisure activities.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: stunning beaches, water sports, modern amenities
Cons: limited cultural experiences, high prices

**Bahri** (Khartoum)
Best For: family / mid-range
Vibe: Bahri is a suburban area located just north of Khartoum, known for its quiet streets, parks, and family-friendly atmosphere. The area is close to the city center and offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to mid-range hotels.
Price Range: $30-100/night
Pros: family-friendly atmosphere, affordable prices, convenient location
Cons: limited nightlife, traffic congestion

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to February)
- Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for long-term stays or off-season bookings
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during holidays and festivals
- Check for any travel restrictions or requirements, such as visas or vaccinations
- Read reviews and check the location before booking, to ensure it meets your needs and preferences
- Use a credit card or PayPal to secure your booking, in case of any issues or cancellations

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid traveling to areas near the borders with South Sudan, Libya, or the Central African Republic, due to security concerns
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or markets, and keep valuables secure
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and avoid traveling alone at night
- Stay hydrated and protected from the sun, especially during outdoor activities
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or cultural landmarks
- Keep your accommodation address and contact information handy, in case of emergencies
