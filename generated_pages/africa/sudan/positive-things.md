[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Pyramids of Meroe** are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most impressive archaeological sites in Africa.
- The **National Museum of Sudan** in Khartoum is home to a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage.
- The **Khartoum Festival** is a annual celebration of music, dance, and theater that brings together artists and performers from all over the country.
- The **Sudanese tradition of hospitality** is renowned for its warmth and generosity, with visitors often being greeted with **ahwa** (coffee) and **gurassa** (flatbread).
- The ** ancient city of Nuri** is a historic site located on the west bank of the Nile River, with several **pyramids** and **temples** dating back to the **Kushite period**.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Mo Ibrahim**, a Sudanese-British businessman and philanthropist, is the founder of the **Mo Ibrahim Foundation**, which promotes good governance and leadership in Africa.
- **Francis Deng**, a Sudanese diplomat and scholar, is a renowned expert on **conflict resolution** and **human rights**, and has served as the **UN Secretary-General's Special Adviser** on the **Prevention of Genocide**.
- The **Sudanese diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global community, with many Sudanese individuals holding prominent positions in **politics**, **business**, and **academia**.
- **Dr. Halima Mohamed Abdel-Rahman**, a Sudanese doctor and scientist, has made groundbreaking research on **malaria** and **tuberculosis**, and has received several international awards for her work.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **Nile River** is a major tourist attraction, with many visitors taking **boat cruises** or **fishing trips** along its banks.
- The **Sudanese cuisine** is a unique blend of **African**, **Arabic**, and **Mediterranean** flavors, with popular dishes like **gurassa** and **molokhiya**.
- The **Khartoum souks** (markets) are a treasure trove of **local handicrafts**, **spices**, and **textiles**, with many visitors shopping for **souvenirs**.
- The **Sudanese people** are known for their **warm hospitality**, with visitors often being greeted with **smiles** and **open arms**.
- The **desert landscapes** of Sudan are a popular destination for **trekking** and **safari** adventures, with many visitors exploring the **Bayuda Desert** and the **Nubian Desert**.
- The **Red Sea coast** of Sudan is a popular destination for **scuba diving** and **snorkeling**, with many visitors exploring the **coral reefs** and **marine life** of the **Port Sudan** area.
