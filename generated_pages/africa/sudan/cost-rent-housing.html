Housing Market Overview

Sudan's housing market is characterized by a high demand for rental properties, particularly in the capital city of Khartoum. The rental market is largely unregulated, which can make it challenging for tenants to find affordable and secure housing. Typical lease terms range from 6-12 months, with a deposit requirement of 1-2 months' rent. Tenant rights are limited, and disputes are often resolved through informal negotiations. Compared to regional neighbors, Sudan's housing costs are relatively high, particularly in the city center, due to the country's growing economy and limited housing supply.

The high inflation rate of 138.8% in 2022 has also contributed to the increasing cost of living in Sudan, including housing prices. Additionally, the limited availability of mortgage financing and the high cost of construction materials have driven up property prices. However, the Sudanese government has implemented initiatives to increase the supply of affordable housing, including the development of new residential areas and the provision of subsidized housing for low-income families.

Best Neighborhoods for Expats

  • Khartoum 2: A popular neighborhood for expats, with rents ranging from $300-$600 USD per month for a one-bedroom apartment, offering a mix of modern amenities and traditional Sudanese culture.
  • Amarat: A upscale neighborhood with rents ranging from $500-$1,000 USD per month for a two-bedroom apartment, featuring luxury amenities and a secure environment.
  • Riyadh: A family-friendly neighborhood with rents ranging from $300-$600 USD per month for a three-bedroom apartment, offering spacious living and easy access to schools and amenities.
  • Khartoum North: A rapidly developing neighborhood with rents ranging from $200-$400 USD per month for a one-bedroom apartment, featuring new amenities and investment opportunities.
  • Omdurman: A historic neighborhood with rents ranging from $150-$300 USD per month for a one-bedroom apartment, offering traditional architecture and cultural experiences.

Renter Tips for Sudan

  • Research neighborhoods thoroughly to find affordable options and secure environments.
  • Work with a reputable real estate agent to find suitable properties and navigate the rental process.
  • Be prepared to negotiate prices and terms with landlords.
  • Be aware of scams and fraudulent activities in the rental market, and always verify the ownership and legitimacy of properties.
  • Utilities such as electricity and water are usually not included in the rent, so factor these costs into your budget.
  • Furnished apartments are common, but unfurnished options are also available, so clarify the furnishing status before signing a lease.
