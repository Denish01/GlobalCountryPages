[SECTION] Overview: DR Congo vs Zambia [/SECTION]
The cost of living in DR Congo and Zambia differs significantly due to various economic factors. DR Congo has a larger population and a more complex economy, with a mix of French and African influences, while Zambia has a smaller population and a more stable economy. The cost of living in Zambia is generally lower, with a more stable inflation rate and a stronger currency. In contrast, DR Congo has a higher inflation rate, which affects the purchasing power of its citizens.

The economic context of both countries plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. DR Congo has a more diverse economy, with a strong focus on mining and agriculture, while Zambia's economy is more dependent on copper mining. The difference in economic structures and stability contributes to the variation in cost of living between the two countries. On average, Zambia is 15-20% cheaper than DR Congo, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a affordable lifestyle.
