The cost of utilities and internet in DR Congo can vary depending on factors such as location, usage, and provider, but on average, a monthly utility bill for an 85 sqm apartment can range from $100 to $200.

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
The utility infrastructure in DR Congo is still developing, with many areas lacking access to reliable electricity and clean water. As a result, utility costs can be relatively high, especially for those who rely on private providers for services such as electricity and water. The cost of utilities in DR Congo can also be subject to fluctuations, with prices changing frequently due to factors such as fuel shortages and currency exchange rates. Additionally, there can be seasonal variations in utility costs, with prices tend to be higher during the dry season when water is scarce.

The quality of utility infrastructure in DR Congo varies greatly depending on the location, with urban areas such as Kinshasa tend to have better access to reliable utilities than rural areas. However, even in urban areas, power outages and water shortages are common, and residents often have to rely on backup generators and water storage systems. The government has been working to improve the utility infrastructure, but progress has been slow, and the cost of utilities remains a significant burden for many households.
