[SECTION] Overview: DR Congo vs Nigeria [/SECTION]
The cost of living in DR Congo and Nigeria varies significantly, with DR Congo being generally cheaper. This is largely due to the economic context of both countries, with DR Congo having a smaller economy and lower average income. However, the cost of living in both countries can be high, especially in major cities like Kinshasa and Lagos. The main difference in cost is in the areas of housing, food, and transportation, with DR Congo being around 20-30% cheaper in these categories.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. DR Congo has a largely informal economy, with many people engaged in small-scale trade and agriculture. Nigeria, on the other hand, has a larger and more diversified economy, with a strong focus on oil and gas production. This has led to a higher cost of living in Nigeria, especially in areas like housing and transportation. However, the cost of living in both countries can be managed with careful planning and budgeting, and there are many affordable options available for those who are willing to look.
