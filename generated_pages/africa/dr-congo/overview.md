[SECTION] What Is DR Congo? [/SECTION]
The Democratic Republic of the Congo, commonly known as DR Congo, is a **sovereign** country located in central Africa. It is the second-largest country on the continent, with a diverse geography that includes tropical rainforests, mountains, and savannas. DR Congo is known for its rich natural resources, including **copper**, **cobalt**, and **diamonds**, which have played a significant role in the country's history and economy.

DR Congo shares borders with nine countries, including the Republic of Congo, Central African Republic, South Sudan, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, Tanzania, Zambia, and Angola. The country has a long and complex history, with over 450 ethnic groups and more than 700 languages spoken. Despite its challenges, DR Congo is a country with immense cultural and natural wealth, and its people are known for their resilience and hospitality.

The capital city, Kinshasa, is a bustling metropolis with a population of over 12 million people. It is a major cultural and economic hub, with a vibrant music scene and a mix of traditional and modern architecture. DR Congo is also home to several national parks and wildlife reserves, including the famous **Virunga National Park**, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1885 - The Congo Free State is established by King Leopold II of Belgium, marking the beginning of European colonization in the region.
2. 1908 - The Congo Free State is annexed by Belgium, becoming a Belgian colony known as the Belgian Congo.
3. 1960 - The Belgian Congo gains independence, becoming the Republic of the Congo.
4. 1971 - The country is renamed Zaire by President Mobutu Sese Seko, who rules the country with an authoritarian regime.
5. 1997 - The country is renamed the Democratic Republic of the Congo, following the overthrow of President Mobutu by Laurent Kabila.
6. 2003 - The country adopts a new constitution, marking a transition towards democracy and stability.
7. 2019 - Felix Tshisekedi is elected president, becoming the first opposition candidate to win the presidency in the country's history.

[SECTION] What Makes DR Congo Unique [/SECTION]
- DR Congo is home to the **second-largest tropical rainforest** in the world, covering over 100 million hectares.
- The country has the **largest population** of lowland gorillas in the world, with over 10,000 individuals.
- DR Congo is the **world's largest producer** of cobalt, a key mineral used in the production of batteries and electronics.
- The country has a **rich cultural heritage**, with over 450 ethnic groups and more than 700 languages spoken.
- DR Congo is home to the **Inga Dam**, one of the largest hydroelectric dams in the world, which provides electricity to millions of people.
