Getting There

The Democratic Republic of the Congo has several international airports, with N'Djili Airport in Kinshasa being the main entry point for most travelers. Direct flights to Kinshasa are available from major European and African cities, including Brussels, Paris, and Johannesburg. Alternatively, you can also enter the country overland from neighboring countries such as Uganda, Rwanda, and Tanzania, although this can be a challenging and time-consuming process due to the poor state of the roads and border crossings. If you do plan to enter the country overland, it's essential to research the current border crossing requirements and regulations, as well as to ensure that you have all the necessary documents and visas.

For those traveling from within Africa, there are also several regional flight hubs, including Addis Ababa and Nairobi, which offer connections to Kinshasa and other major cities in the DR Congo. Additionally, you can also take a ferry or a boat from neighboring countries such as the Republic of Congo and Tanzania to reach the DR Congo. However, this option can be slow and unpredictable, and it's not recommended for most travelers.

Domestic Flights

Domestic flights are available in the DR Congo, although the network is limited and often unreliable. To book a domestic flight, you can either visit the airline's website or go to the airport in person, although it's recommended to book in advance to ensure availability. The cost of a domestic flight can vary greatly depending on the route and airline, but you can expect to pay around $100-$200 for a one-way ticket. It's also worth noting that domestic flights can be subject to delays and cancellations, so it's essential to be flexible and plan accordingly.

Trains & Rail

The rail network in the DR Congo is limited and in poor condition, with only a few routes available, including the line from Kinshasa to Matadi and the line from Lubumbashi to Sakania. The trains are often slow and uncomfortable, and the schedules can be unpredictable. However, taking the train can be a unique and adventurous experience, and it's a good way to see the countryside. To book a train ticket, you can either go to the train station in person or buy a ticket from a local travel agent. The cost of a train ticket can vary depending on the route and class, but you can expect to pay around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket.

Buses & Coaches

Buses and coaches are a popular mode of transportation in the DR Congo, with several companies operating intercity routes. The quality of the buses can vary greatly, ranging from modern and comfortable to old and overcrowded. Some of the main bus companies include Malaika and Kasango, which offer routes between major cities such as Kinshasa, Lubumbashi, and Kisangani. The cost of a bus ticket can vary depending on the route and company, but you can expect to pay around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket. It's also worth noting that bus travel can be slow and unpredictable, due to the poor state of the roads and traffic conditions.

Driving

  • To drive in the DR Congo, you will need to obtain a local driver's license or use an international driver's license, although it's not always enforced.
  • The road quality in the DR Congo is generally poor, with many roads being unpaved and in disrepair.
  • Fuel costs can be high, especially outside of major cities, and it's essential to plan ahead and research the current fuel prices.
  • Car rental companies are available in major cities such as Kinshasa and Lubumbashi, although the selection of cars can be limited and the prices can be high.
  • Driving in the DR Congo can be hazardous, due to the poor state of the roads and the unpredictable behavior of other drivers.
  • It's essential to exercise caution and drive defensively, especially at night or in areas with poor visibility.

Transport Tips

  • Be aware of scams and overcharging, especially when using taxis or ride-hailing services, and always agree on the price before you start your journey.
  • Use reputable and licensed transportation companies, and avoid using unlicensed or unofficial taxis or buses.
  • Download local transportation apps, such as Taxis Verts, to help you navigate and find transportation options.
  • Always carry a map and a GPS device, as the roads and transportation networks can be unpredictable and confusing.
  • Be prepared for delays and cancellations, especially when using public transportation, and plan ahead to avoid inconvenience.
  • Use cash instead of credit cards, as many transportation companies and drivers may not accept credit cards.
  • Avoid traveling at night, especially in areas with poor security or road conditions, and use reputable and secure transportation options instead.
