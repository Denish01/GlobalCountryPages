[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Cabo Verde is generally a safe country to visit, with low levels of violent crime and a strong emphasis on tourism. The country has a stable government and a growing economy, which has contributed to its safe and welcoming environment. However, as with any travel destination, there are some safety concerns that visitors should be aware of. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, can be a problem in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, there have been reports of scams targeting tourists, particularly in the areas of Sal and Boa Vista.

Visitors to Cabo Verde should also be aware of the potential for natural disasters, such as hurricanes and droughts, which can affect the country's infrastructure and economy. However, these events are relatively rare and the government has measures in place to mitigate their impact. Overall, Cabo Verde is a great destination for travelers who are looking for a unique and exciting experience, with a rich culture and beautiful landscapes. With some basic precautions and an understanding of the local environment, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **ATM scams**, where thieves use card skimmers to steal your information
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- **Scams** targeting tourists are common, particularly in Sal and Boa Vista, and can include fake tour operators and overpriced services
- **Petty crime** can be a problem in some areas, particularly at night
- Be cautious of **unlicensed taxis**, which can be a risk to your safety and security
- **Credit card fraud** can be a problem, so be sure to keep your card information secure
- **Beach theft** can be a problem, particularly in crowded areas, so be sure to keep a close eye on your belongings

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended, while rabies and yellow fever are required for some travelers
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are limited, particularly outside of Praia, and may not meet international standards
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacy access is limited, particularly outside of urban areas, and may not have a wide range of medications available

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night
- Keep a close eye on your belongings, particularly in tourist hotspots
- Use **reputable taxi services** or **ride-sharing apps** to get around
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use **credit cards** or **ATMs** instead
- Be cautious of **strangers** who approach you, particularly in tourist areas
- Keep your **hotel room door** locked and secure, and use the **hotel safe** to store valuables
- Be aware of **local customs** and **traditions**, and respect the local way of life

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Cabo Verde is generally a safe destination for solo and women travelers, but **common sense** and **caution** should still be exercised
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night, and avoid walking alone in **isolated areas**
- Use **reputable taxi services** or **ride-sharing apps** to get around, and avoid **hitchhiking**
- Keep a close eye on your **drinks** and **belongings**, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Be respectful of **local customs** and **traditions**, and dress **modestly** to avoid drawing attention to yourself
