# Where to Stay in Cabo Verde — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Cabo Verde, the best places to stay are in the cities of Sal, Boa Vista, and Praia, which offer a range of accommodations and activities for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas/neighborhoods in Cabo Verde, here's a brief overview:
**Santa Maria** (Sal)
Best For: Luxury, family
Vibe: Santa Maria is a bustling beach town with a vibrant atmosphere, known for its beautiful beaches and water sports.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Excellent beaches, wide range of accommodations, plenty of restaurants and bars
Cons: Can be crowded during peak season

**Vila do Maio** (Maio)
Best For: Backpacker, off-the-beaten-path
Vibe: Vila do Maio is a small, laid-back town with a rich history and stunning natural surroundings.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: Affordable, authentic local experience, beautiful beaches
Cons: Limited amenities and activities

**Praia** (Praia)
Best For: Business, culture
Vibe: Praia is the capital city of Cabo Verde, offering a mix of urban and cultural experiences.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Rich cultural heritage, vibrant nightlife, plenty of restaurants and shops
Cons: Traffic and noise can be a problem

**Mindelo** (São Vicente)
Best For: Luxury, culture
Vibe: Mindelo is a charming city with a rich cultural scene, known for its colonial architecture and vibrant music scene.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Rich cultural heritage, beautiful architecture, plenty of restaurants and bars
Cons: Can be expensive

**Espargos** (Sal)
Best For: Family, budget
Vibe: Espargos is a small town in the center of Sal Island, offering a relaxed atmosphere and affordable accommodations.
Price Range: $30-80/night
Pros: Affordable, family-friendly, close to beaches
Cons: Limited amenities and activities

**Boa Vista** (Boa Vista)
Best For: Nature lovers, off-the-beaten-path
Vibe: Boa Vista is a peaceful island with stunning natural beauty, known for its beaches, dunes, and wildlife.
Price Range: $50-150/night
Pros: Unspoiled natural beauty, relaxed atmosphere, plenty of outdoor activities
Cons: Limited amenities and infrastructure

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to April)
- Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for long stays or off-season bookings
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with higher rates during holidays and festivals
- Read reviews and check the location before booking
- Look for packages that include activities or meals to save money
- Be cautious of hidden fees or charges

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night
- Keep valuables secure and be mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas
- Choose accommodations in safe and central locations, close to amenities and transport
- Research the local area before booking, considering factors like noise, traffic, and proximity to beaches or attractions
- Be aware of local customs and traditions, respecting the local culture and environment
- Stay informed about local events and festivals, which can affect accommodation prices and availability
