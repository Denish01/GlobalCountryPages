Education System Overview

The education system in Cabo Verde is structured into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 12, and is provided free of charge by the government. Secondary education, which lasts for 3 years, is also compulsory, and students can choose to pursue a technical or vocational track. Tertiary education is provided by a number of universities and institutes, which offer a range of programs in fields such as engineering, business, and health sciences. The quality of education in Cabo Verde is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on academic rigor and critical thinking. However, there are some challenges, particularly in terms of infrastructure and resources, which can vary significantly between public and private institutions.

The public education system in Cabo Verde is free and open to all citizens, but it can be overcrowded and underfunded. In contrast, private schools and universities offer a more exclusive and expensive education, with better facilities and more qualified teachers. Despite these challenges, the government has made significant investments in education reform, including the introduction of new curricula and teaching methods, and the establishment of scholarship programs to support disadvantaged students.

Overall, the education system in Cabo Verde is well-structured and provides a solid foundation for students to pursue their academic and professional goals. However, there are still some challenges to be addressed, particularly in terms of equity and access to quality education.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Cabo Verde, international students need to obtain a student visa, which can be applied for at the Cabo Verdean embassy or consulate in their home country.
  • International students are required to have a good command of Portuguese, which is the official language of instruction in most universities.
  • There are several scholarship programs available to international students, including the Cabo Verdean Government Scholarship and the European Union's Erasmus Mundus Scholarship.
  • The cost of living in Cabo Verde is relatively low, with accommodation options ranging from $200 to $500 per month, and food options ranging from $3 to $10 per meal.
  • International students can also apply for part-time jobs to support themselves during their studies, with average hourly wages ranging from $5 to $10.
  • The Cabo Verdean Ministry of Education provides support services for international students, including language courses and cultural orientation programs.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • One of the major strengths of the education system in Cabo Verde is its emphasis on academic rigor and critical thinking, which prepares students well for higher education and the job market.
  • However, the system also faces some challenges, including infrastructure and resource constraints, particularly in rural areas.
  • The government has introduced reforms aimed at improving the quality and equity of education, including the introduction of new curricula and teaching methods.
  • Despite these efforts, there are still some challenges to be addressed, including inequalities in access to quality education, particularly for disadvantaged groups.
  • The Cabo Verdean education system has also been affected by brain drain, with many qualified teachers and graduates emigrating to other countries in search of better opportunities.
