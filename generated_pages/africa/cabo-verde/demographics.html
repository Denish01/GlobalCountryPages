Population Overview

Cabo Verde's population size is relatively small compared to other African countries, but it has been growing steadily over the years. The population growth rate is approximately 1.2%, which is relatively low compared to other countries in the region. The country's urban population accounts for about 65% of the total population, with the majority living in the capital city of Praia and other urban centers. Cabo Verde has experienced significant migration patterns, with many of its citizens emigrating to other countries, particularly in Europe and the United States, in search of better economic opportunities. However, the country has also been a recipient of immigrants from other African countries, adding to its cultural diversity.

The country's population has been influenced by its history as a former Portuguese colony, which has left a lasting impact on its demographics. The population is relatively young, with a median age of 25.5 years, and has a relatively high life expectancy of 73 years. The fertility rate is approximately 2.2 children per woman, which is relatively low compared to other countries in the region. Overall, Cabo Verde's population is characterized by a mix of traditional and modern lifestyles, with a strong emphasis on family and community ties.

Cabo Verde's population has also been shaped by its geography, with the country comprising a group of islands off the coast of West Africa. The country's population is concentrated in the urban centers, with the majority living in the capital city of Praia and other urban areas. The rural areas are relatively sparsely populated, with many people engaged in agriculture and fishing.
