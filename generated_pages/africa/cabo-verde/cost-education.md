# Education Costs in Cabo Verde

The cost of education in Cabo Verde varies depending on the type of institution and the level of education, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge tuition fees ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Cabo Verde is divided into pre-primary, primary, secondary, and tertiary levels, with **Portuguese** being the primary language of instruction. Public education is generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on basic education, while private schools offer a more diverse range of subjects and extracurricular activities. Compulsory education in Cabo Verde lasts for 12 years, from the age of 6 to 18, and is free for all citizens. International schools are also available, primarily in the capital city of **Praia**, offering a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **British** systems.

The quality of public education in Cabo Verde is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on basic education, while private schools offer a more diverse range of subjects and extracurricular activities. However, there are some challenges facing the education system, including a shortage of qualified teachers and inadequate infrastructure in some areas. Despite these challenges, Cabo Verde has made significant progress in increasing access to education, with a literacy rate of around 87% and a net enrollment rate of over 90% for primary education.
