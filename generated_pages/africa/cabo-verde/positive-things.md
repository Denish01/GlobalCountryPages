[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Historic Centre of Ribeira Grande**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a testament to the country's rich cultural heritage and colonial history.
- The **Cabo Verdean Carnival**, held annually in the capital city of Praia, is a vibrant celebration of music, dance, and culture.
- The **Museu Nacional de Cabo Verde**, located in Praia, is a museum that showcases the country's history, art, and culture.
- The **Fortaleza de São Filipe**, a 16th-century fort, is a historic landmark that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
- The **Tabanka Music and Dance**, a traditional form of music and dance, is an important part of Cabo Verdean cultural identity.

- The **Cabo Verdean diaspora**, which is estimated to be over 1 million people, has made significant contributions to the country's economy and culture, and has helped to promote Cabo Verdean identity abroad.
- **Maria de Barros**, a renowned singer and songwriter, has helped to popularize Cabo Verdean music and culture around the world.
- The **Cabo Verdean community** in the United States, particularly in the city of Boston, has established several cultural organizations and events to promote Cabo Verdean culture and identity.
