[SECTION] Overview: Cabo Verde vs Sao Tome and Principe [/SECTION]
Cabo Verde and Sao Tome and Principe are two island nations located off the coast of West Africa, with distinct economic contexts and cost of living profiles. Cabo Verde has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on tourism, remittances, and foreign investment, which has contributed to its higher GDP per capita. In contrast, Sao Tome and Principe has a smaller economy, heavily reliant on agriculture and fishing, which has resulted in a lower GDP per capita. The cost of living in Cabo Verde is generally lower, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation being more affordable.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Cabo Verde has a more stable economy, with a lower inflation rate and a stronger currency, which has helped to keep prices under control. In contrast, Sao Tome and Principe has experienced higher inflation rates, which has led to increased prices for goods and services. Overall, Cabo Verde is around 15-20% cheaper than Sao Tome and Principe, making it a more attractive option for expats, tourists, and investors looking for affordable living costs.
