Visa Policy Overview

Cabo Verde has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens from over 50 countries to enter visa-free or with a visa on arrival. The country is a popular tourist destination, and its visa policy reflects this by making it relatively easy for visitors to enter. Citizens of countries such as the European Union, the United States, and the United Kingdom can enter Cabo Verde visa-free for up to 30 days, while citizens of other countries may need to obtain a visa on arrival or in advance.

The visa policy of Cabo Verde is designed to promote tourism and economic development while maintaining national security. The country has a relatively simple and streamlined visa application process, making it easy for visitors to plan their trips. However, it's essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it's always best to check with the official embassy or consulate of Cabo Verde for the latest information.

Tourists: Tourists can enter Cabo Verde visa-free for up to 30 days, and their stay can be extended for an additional 30 days. To enter the country, tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may need to obtain a visa in advance, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They must also provide an invitation letter from a Cabo Verde company, proof of business activities, and other relevant documents.
Students: Students who wish to study in Cabo Verde must obtain a student visa in advance. They must provide proof of enrollment at a recognized institution, a valid passport, and other relevant documents.
Workers: Workers who wish to work in Cabo Verde must obtain a work visa in advance. They must provide a sponsorship letter from a Cabo Verde employer, proof of qualifications and experience, and other relevant documents.
