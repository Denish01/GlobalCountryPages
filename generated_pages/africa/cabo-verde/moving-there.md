# Moving to Cabo Verde — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Cabo Verde, you will need to research and understand the various visa options, relocation processes, and cultural nuances that will help you navigate this beautiful island nation and make the most of your expat experience.

[SECTION] Why People Move to Cabo Verde [/SECTION]
Cabo Verde, also known as Cape Verde, is a collection of islands off the coast of West Africa that has become a popular destination for expats and digital nomads due to its unique blend of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian cultures. The country offers a high **quality of life**, with a warm tropical climate, stunning beaches, and a growing economy. Many expats are drawn to Cabo Verde's **laid-back lifestyle**, which is characterized by a mix of relaxation, outdoor activities, and vibrant cultural events. The expat community in Cabo Verde is diverse, with people from all over the world, including Europe, Africa, and the Americas, and it is estimated that there are around 10,000 to 20,000 expats living in the country.

The **cost of living** in Cabo Verde is relatively low compared to other island nations, making it an attractive destination for retirees, digital nomads, and families. The country also offers a range of **outdoor activities**, including surfing, kitesurfing, and hiking, which makes it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Additionally, Cabo Verde has a **growing tourism industry**, which provides opportunities for entrepreneurs and business owners to start their own ventures. The country's **strategic location** near the midpoint between Europe, Africa, and the Americas also makes it an important hub for international trade and commerce.

Cabo Verde's **cultural diversity** is another major draw for expats, with a unique blend of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences. The country has a **rich cultural heritage**, with a mix of traditional music, dance, and art, as well as modern amenities and infrastructure. The **language** spoken in Cabo Verde is Portuguese, but many people also speak Creole, and some English is spoken in tourist areas. Overall, Cabo Verde offers a unique and exciting expat experience that combines relaxation, adventure, and cultural immersion.

[SECTION] Expat Communities [/SECTION]
- The majority of expats live in the capital city of **Praia**, which offers a range of amenities and services.
- The town of **Santa Maria** on the island of Sal is another popular destination for expats, with a growing community of digital nomads and entrepreneurs.
- Online groups such as **Expats in Cabo Verde** and **Cabo Verde Expats** provide a platform for expats to connect and share information.
- Social networks like **Facebook** and **LinkedIn** have groups dedicated to expats in Cabo Verde, which can be a great way to meet new people and get advice.
- Meetup groups like **Cabo Verde Expats Meetup** and **Praia Expats** organize regular events and activities for expats to socialize and get involved in the local community.

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- Opening a bank account in Cabo Verde can be a straightforward process, with several banks offering services in English.
- Money transfers can be made through banks or online services like **Western Union** and **PayPal**.
- The local currency is the **Cabo Verdean escudo (CVE)**, but euros and US dollars are widely accepted.
- Credit cards like **Visa** and **Mastercard** are accepted in many places, but it's still a good idea to have some cash on hand.
- [CALLOUT] Be aware that banking fees and exchange rates can be high, so it's a good idea to research and compare rates before making any transactions.

[SECTION] Challenges & Honest Advice [/SECTION]
- **Culture shock** can be a challenge for some expats, especially those who are not used to the laid-back pace of life in Cabo Verde.
- **Bureaucracy** can be slow and frustrating, especially when dealing with government agencies or official paperwork.
- **Language barriers** can be a problem, especially for those who do not speak Portuguese or Creole.
- **Infrastructure** can be limited in some areas, especially outside of the capital city.
