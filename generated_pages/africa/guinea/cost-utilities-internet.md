# Utility & Internet Costs in Guinea

The cost of utilities and internet in Guinea can vary depending on factors such as location, usage, and provider, but on average, a monthly utility bill for an 85 sqm apartment can range from $100 to $200 USD.

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Guinea's utility infrastructure is still developing, with frequent power outages and water shortages affecting many areas. The cost of utilities can fluctuate due to seasonal variation, with higher demand for electricity and water during the dry season. Additionally, the country's high inflation rate of 8.1% can lead to price increases for utilities and other essential services. As a result, it is essential to factor in these fluctuations when budgeting for utilities in Guinea.

The quality of utility infrastructure in Guinea varies depending on the location, with urban areas like Conakry tend to have more reliable access to electricity and water compared to rural areas. However, even in urban areas, power outages and water shortages can occur, making it necessary to have backup plans such as generators or water storage tanks. The government is working to improve the utility infrastructure, but progress is slow, and the costs of utilities remain relatively high compared to other countries in the region.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities, it is recommended to work with a local real estate agent or property manager who can assist with the process.
- Prepaid electricity meters are available and can help manage costs, but it is essential to budget for regular top-ups.
- The best internet providers in Guinea are Orange and MTN, which offer reliable and fast connections.
- To save on utility bills, consider using energy-efficient appliances and turning off lights and electronics when not in use.
- It is also a good idea to invest in a backup generator or water storage tank to mitigate the impact of power outages and water shortages.
