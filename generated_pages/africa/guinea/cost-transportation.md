[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Guinea has a limited but developing transportation network, with most people relying on **minibuses**, **taxis**, and **motorcycles** to get around. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with Conakry having a more comprehensive network than other urban areas. However, the lack of a well-organized public transportation system means that many people rely on informal modes of transport, such as **shared taxis** and **bush taxis**. The road network is also limited, with many roads in poor condition, especially during the rainy season.

The main transportation modes in Guinea are road-based, with a few **ferry services** operating on the coast and rivers. The country's geography, with its mountainous terrain and limited infrastructure, makes it challenging to develop a comprehensive transportation network. However, efforts are being made to improve the road network and expand public transportation services, including the introduction of **new bus services** and **ride-hailing apps**. Despite these challenges, Guinea's transportation sector is growing, with an increasing number of **private transport operators** offering services, including **car rentals** and **tourist transportation**.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use **ride-hailing apps** like Africab or Allo Taxi for convenient and affordable transportation.
- **Negotiate taxi fares** before starting your journey to avoid overcharging.
- **Be cautious** when using public transportation, especially at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
- Consider **renting a car** for long-distance travel or for exploring rural areas.
- **Check the weather forecast** before embarking on a journey, as roads can become impassable during the rainy season.
- Use **local currency (GNF)** for transactions, as not all businesses accept foreign currency.
