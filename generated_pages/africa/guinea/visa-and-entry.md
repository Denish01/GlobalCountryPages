# Guinea Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Guinea before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Guinea has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Citizens of certain African countries, such as ECOWAS member states, can enter Guinea visa-free, while others may need to obtain a visa in advance or on arrival. The country's visa policy aims to balance the need to facilitate travel and trade with the need to maintain national security and control over immigration.

Guinea offers visa-free access to citizens of several countries, including ECOWAS member states, such as Senegal, Mali, and Sierra Leone. However, citizens of most other countries, including the US, UK, and EU member states, require a visa to enter Guinea. The country's visa policy is subject to change, and it is essential to check with the official embassy or consulate of Guinea for the latest requirements.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists require a valid passport, a return ticket, and proof of sufficient funds to enter Guinea. The typical stay for tourists is 90 days, and extensions are possible but require an application to the Guinean immigration authorities. Tourists can obtain a visa on arrival at Conakry International Airport, but it is recommended to apply in advance to avoid any issues.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers require a valid passport, a return ticket, proof of sufficient funds, and an invitation letter from a Guinean company or organization. The invitation letter must be approved by the Guinean Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Business travelers can obtain a multiple-entry visa, which is valid for up to one year.
**Students:** Students require a valid passport, a return ticket, proof of sufficient funds, and a letter of enrollment from a Guinean educational institution. The letter of enrollment must be approved by the Guinean Ministry of Education. Students can obtain a student visa, which is valid for the duration of their studies.
**Workers:** Workers require a valid passport, a return ticket, proof of sufficient funds, and a sponsorship letter from a Guinean employer. The sponsorship letter must be approved by the Guinean Ministry of Labor. Workers can obtain a work visa, which is valid for up to two years.

[SECTION] Documents Checklist [/SECTION]
1. A valid passport with at least six months' validity
2. A completed visa application form
3. Two recent passport-sized photographs
4. A return ticket or proof of onward travel
5. Proof of sufficient funds (at least $100 per day)
6. A hotel reservation or proof of accommodation
7. A yellow fever vaccination certificate
8. A letter of invitation or sponsorship (for business travelers, students, or workers)
9. A letter of enrollment (for students)
10. A copy of the company's registration certificate (for business travelers)

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Guinea are Conakry International Airport, which receives flights from major African and European cities, and the land borders with neighboring countries. Upon arrival, travelers must present their passport, visa (if required), and other documents to the immigration authorities. The immigration process can be slow, and travelers may need to wait in line for several hours. It is essential to have all the required documents and to be patient and respectful when dealing with the immigration authorities.

Travelers can also enter Guinea by land through the borders with Guinea-Bissau, Senegal, Mali, Ivory Coast, Liberia, and Sierra Leone. The land borders are generally less busy than the airport, but travelers may still need to wait in line and present their documents to the immigration authorities.

- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not having a completed visa application form
- Not having proof of sufficient funds
- Not having a return ticket or proof of onward travel
- Not having a yellow fever vaccination certificate
- Not having a letter of invitation or sponsorship (for business travelers, students, or workers)
