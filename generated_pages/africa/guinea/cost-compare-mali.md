[SECTION] Overview: Guinea vs Mali [/SECTION]
Guinea and Mali are two West African countries with distinct economic profiles. Guinea has a higher GDP per capita and a more diversified economy, with a strong mining sector, while Mali's economy is more reliant on agriculture. Despite these differences, both countries have a relatively low cost of living compared to other countries in the region. However, Guinea is generally cheaper than Mali, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. The cost of living in Guinea is around 15% lower than in Mali, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Guinea's economy has been growing steadily in recent years, driven by investments in the mining sector, while Mali's economy has been affected by security concerns and political instability. As a result, Guinea has a more developed infrastructure and a wider range of goods and services available, which can affect the cost of living. However, Mali's economy is more reliant on informal trade and traditional industries, which can make it more challenging to compare prices and costs between the two countries.
