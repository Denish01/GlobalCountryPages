# Where to Stay in Somalia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When traveling to Somalia, it's essential to choose your accommodation wisely, considering factors such as safety, location, and amenities, with the best areas to stay including Mogadishu, Hargeisa, and Bosaso.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, we've outlined the best options for different types of travelers:
**Mogadishu Hamar Weyne** (Mogadishu)
Best For: Business travelers
Vibe: This historic neighborhood is known for its bustling markets and ancient architecture, offering a unique glimpse into Somali culture. It's a great place to experience the local way of life, with many restaurants and shops nearby.
Price Range: $50-100/night
Pros: Central location, easy access to markets and restaurants, historic significance
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited amenities

**Hargeisa Downtown** (Hargeisa)
Best For: Families
Vibe: This charming neighborhood is a great place to experience the local culture, with many family-friendly restaurants and shops nearby. It's also close to several parks and recreational areas, making it ideal for families.
Price Range: $30-70/night
Pros: Family-friendly, plenty of restaurants and shops, close to parks
Cons: Limited nightlife, can be quiet in the evenings

**Bosaso Port** (Bosaso)
Best For: Backpackers
Vibe: This vibrant neighborhood is a great place to meet other travelers, with many budget-friendly accommodations and restaurants nearby. It's also close to the port, making it easy to access boat tours and other water activities.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: Affordable, social atmosphere, easy access to water activities
Cons: Can be noisy, limited amenities

**Mogadishu Waberi** (Mogadishu)
Best For: Luxury travelers
Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is known for its modern amenities and high-end restaurants, making it a great place to relax and unwind. It's also close to several embassies and government buildings, making it a safe and secure area.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: Modern amenities, high-end restaurants, safe and secure
Cons: Limited local culture, can be expensive

**Galkayo Downtown** (Galkayo)
Best For: Business travelers
Vibe: This bustling neighborhood is a great place to conduct business, with many offices and conference centers nearby. It's also close to several restaurants and shops, making it easy to entertain clients.
Price Range: $50-100/night
Pros: Central location, easy access to offices and conference centers, plenty of restaurants and shops
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited amenities

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons (June to September)
- Use online platforms such as Booking.com or Airbnb to find and book accommodations
- Negotiate prices, especially for longer stays or during off-peak seasons
- Consider working with a local tour operator or travel agent to find the best options
- Read reviews and check the location before booking
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices often higher during peak seasons
- Use a credit card or other secure payment method to book and pay for accommodations

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas near the border with Ethiopia or Kenya, due to security concerns
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure
- Use a reputable taxi or transportation service, or walk instead of driving
- Stay in touch with local authorities and follow their advice on safety and security
- Consider hiring a local guide or security escort, especially in rural areas
