Visa Policy Overview

Somalia has a relatively strict visa policy, with only a few countries having visa-free access. The country is cautious about who it allows to enter, due to security concerns and the need to regulate the flow of people. Citizens of neighboring countries, such as Djibouti and Kenya, may have easier access, but most other nationalities will need to obtain a visa before arriving in Somalia.

The Somali government has been working to improve its visa process, including introducing an online application system for certain types of visas. However, the process can still be complex and time-consuming, and it is essential to plan ahead and allow plenty of time for your application to be processed. The Somali government has also introduced a range of security measures to ensure the safety of visitors, including screening and vetting procedures.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: To visit Somalia as a tourist, you will need to obtain a visa before arriving in the country. The typical stay for tourists is 30 days, but this can be extended with permission from the Somali government. You will need to provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds to support yourself during your stay.

Business Travelers: Business travelers will need to obtain a special permission visa, which can take several weeks to process. You will need to provide a letter of invitation from a Somali company, as well as proof of your business activities and intentions.

Students: Students who wish to study in Somalia will need to obtain a student visa, which can take several months to process. You will need to provide proof of enrollment in a Somali educational institution, as well as proof of sufficient funds to support yourself during your stay.

Workers: Workers who wish to work in Somalia will need to obtain a work permit, which can take several months to process. You will need to provide proof of sponsorship from a Somali employer, as well as proof of your qualifications and experience.

Entry Points & Procedures

The main airports in Somalia are Mogadishu International Airport and Hargeisa International Airport. When arriving at the airport, you will need to go through immigration and customs procedures, which can be time-consuming. You will need to present your passport, visa (if required), and other documents to the immigration officer. You may also be required to undergo security screening and vetting procedures. Land borders are also available, but these can be more challenging to navigate, and it is recommended to use official border crossings.

When arriving in Somalia, you should be prepared to provide detailed information about your trip, including your itinerary, accommodation, and contacts. You may also be required to register with the local authorities and obtain a residence permit. It is essential to follow all instructions and regulations carefully to avoid any issues or delays.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not allowing enough time for your visa application to be processed
  • Not providing all required documents, including proof of onward travel and sufficient funds
  • Not registering with the local authorities and obtaining a residence permit
  • Not following all instructions and regulations carefully
  • Not being aware of the local security situation and taking necessary precautions
  • Not having a valid and recognized vaccination certificate, if required
