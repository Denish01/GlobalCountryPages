How Somalia Is Divided

Somalia is divided into 18 regions, also known as gobol, which are further divided into districts. The country has a federal system of government, with a central government and regional administrations. The 18 regions are grouped into several states, including Somaliland, Puntland, Jubaland, South West State, Hirshabelle, and Galmudug. Each state has its own government and a degree of autonomy, although the central government in Mogadishu is responsible for national security, foreign policy, and other key areas.

The division of Somalia into regions and states is based on a combination of historical, cultural, and geographical factors. The country has a long history of clan-based politics, and many of the regions and states are dominated by specific clans. The federal system of government is designed to accommodate these clan-based differences and provide a framework for regional autonomy and self-governance.

Regional Differences

  • The northern regions, such as Somaliland and Puntland, have a distinct cultural and linguistic identity, with a strong sense of autonomy and self-governance.
  • The southern regions, such as Jubaland and South West State, have a more diverse cultural and linguistic identity, with a stronger influence from neighboring countries.
  • The central regions, such as Hirshabelle and Galmudug, have a mix of cultural and linguistic identities, with a strong sense of nomadic and pastoral traditions.
  • The coastal regions, such as Kismayo and Berbera, have a distinct economic and cultural identity, with a strong focus on trade and commerce.

Getting Around

Somalia has a limited transportation network, with few paved roads and limited public transportation options. Domestic flights are available between major cities, such as Mogadishu, Hargeisa, and Kismayo. Buses and taxis are also available, although the road conditions can be challenging. Driving conditions are hazardous, with few paved roads and a high risk of banditry and terrorism. It is recommended that visitors use reputable transportation services and exercise caution when traveling, especially in rural areas.
