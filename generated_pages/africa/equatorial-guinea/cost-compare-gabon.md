[SECTION] Overview: Equatorial Guinea vs Gabon [/SECTION]
Equatorial Guinea and Gabon are two neighboring countries in Central Africa, with distinct economic profiles. Equatorial Guinea has a high-income economy, driven by its oil and gas sector, while Gabon has a more diversified economy with a strong focus on timber, mining, and agriculture. The cost of living in Equatorial Guinea is generally higher due to its limited market and high demand for goods and services. In contrast, Gabon has a more competitive market, which helps keep prices lower. Overall, Gabon is the more affordable option, with a cost of living that is around 15-20% lower than Equatorial Guinea.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Equatorial Guinea's economy is heavily reliant on oil exports, which can lead to fluctuations in the cost of living. In contrast, Gabon's diversified economy provides a more stable environment, with less volatility in prices. Additionally, Gabon has a more developed infrastructure, including a better transportation system and more affordable housing options. As a result, Gabon is generally the more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle in Central Africa.
