Overall Safety Assessment

Equatorial Guinea is a country with a complex safety profile. While it is possible to have a safe and enjoyable trip, visitors should be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions. The country has experienced periods of instability and unrest, and crime rates are higher than in many other African nations. Petty theft, scams, and corruption are common, and visitors may encounter harassment or demands for bribes from officials. However, with proper planning, awareness, and caution, it is possible to minimize the risks and have a positive experience in Equatorial Guinea.

The government has taken steps to improve security in recent years, including increasing the presence of police and military personnel in key areas. However, the effectiveness of these measures can vary, and visitors should still exercise caution, especially at night or in isolated areas. It is also important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, as Equatorial Guinea is a conservative country with strong cultural norms. By being mindful of these factors and taking necessary precautions, visitors can have a safe and rewarding experience in this unique and fascinating country.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of scams involving fake police or officials demanding bribes
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable currency exchange services
  • Be aware of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas
  • Do not leave valuables unattended, especially in public areas
  • Be cautious of corrupt officials demanding bribes or favors
  • Avoid traveling at night, especially in isolated areas
  • Be aware of protests and demonstrations, which can turn violent
  • Do not engage with street vendors or unofficial guides, as they may be scams

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid are recommended
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Limited and often of poor quality, with a lack of medical supplies and equipment
  • Pharmacy Access: Limited, and often with a lack of essential medications

Practical Safety Tips

  • Research and stay informed about local conditions and travel advisories
  • Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
  • Avoid displaying wealth or carrying large amounts of cash
  • Use reputable transportation services, such as licensed taxis or ride-sharing services
  • Be cautious of strangers approaching you, especially if they seem overly friendly or aggressive
  • Keep valuable documents and important phone numbers in a safe place
  • Stay in contact with your embassy or consulate in case of an emergency
  • Learn some basic Spanish or French phrases to help you communicate with locals

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be aware of cultural norms and traditional values, and dress modestly
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and use reputable transportation services
  • Be cautious of harassment or unwanted attention, and avoid engaging with aggressive individuals
  • Stay in well-lit and populated areas, and avoid isolated or deserted areas
