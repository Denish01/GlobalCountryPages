[SECTION] Overview: Mozambique vs Zambia [/SECTION]
Mozambique and Zambia are two neighboring countries in Southern Africa, both with unique cultural and economic contexts. Mozambique has a lower GDP per capita and a more stable inflation rate, which contributes to its overall lower cost of living. Zambia, on the other hand, has a more developed economy and a higher standard of living, but also higher prices for goods and services. In general, Mozambique is a more affordable destination for daily expenses, accommodation, and transportation, while Zambia offers a more modern and developed infrastructure.

The economic context of both countries plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Mozambique's economy is primarily driven by agriculture, while Zambia's economy is more diversified, with a strong focus on mining and manufacturing. This diversification has led to higher prices for goods and services in Zambia, making it more expensive than Mozambique. However, Zambia's more developed infrastructure and higher standard of living may make it a more attractive destination for some expats and travelers.
