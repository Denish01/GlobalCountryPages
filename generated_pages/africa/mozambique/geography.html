Location & Borders

Mozambique is a sovereign country located in southeastern Africa, bordering the Indian Ocean to the east. It shares its borders with several countries, including Tanzania to the north, Malawi, Zambia, Zimbabwe, and South Africa to the west, and Eswatini to the southwest. The country's unique location allows it to have a diverse geography, with a long coastline along the Indian Ocean and a variety of landscapes, from mountains to savannas.

Mozambique's borders are defined by a combination of natural and artificial boundaries. The country's northern border with Tanzania is marked by the Ruvuma River, while its western border with Zambia, Zimbabwe, and South Africa is defined by a combination of rivers, mountains, and artificial boundaries. The country's southern border with South Africa and Eswatini is also marked by a combination of natural and artificial boundaries. Mozambique's coastline along the Indian Ocean provides a significant source of revenue for the country, with major ports located in the cities of Maputo, Beira, and Nacala.

Terrain & Landscape

Mozambique's terrain is diverse, with a range of landscapes that include mountains, plateaus, and coastal plains. The country's highest point, Monte Binga, is located in the western part of the country, near the border with Zimbabwe. The mountain range stretches for several hundred kilometers, with peaks reaching elevations of over 2,000 meters. The country's central region is characterized by a plateau, with elevations ranging from 200 to 1,000 meters. The plateau is dotted with numerous rivers, including the Zambezi River, which flows through the country and provides a source of water and transportation.

Mozambique's coastal region is characterized by a narrow coastal plain, with sandy beaches, dunes, and mangrove swamps. The coastal plain is home to a variety of wildlife, including hippos, crocodiles, and numerous species of birds. The country's rivers, including the Zambezi, Limpopo, and Save, provide a source of water and support a wide range of aquatic life. Mozambique's landscape is also home to several national parks and game reserves, including the Gorongosa National Park, which is known for its diverse wildlife and scenic beauty.

The country's terrain and landscape have played a significant role in shaping its history and culture. The mountains and plateaus have provided a natural barrier against invaders, while the coastal plain has facilitated trade and commerce with other countries. The country's rivers have also played a crucial role in the development of its agriculture and industry.

Best Time To Visit

Best months: September to November and April to May. Why: These months offer mild temperatures and minimal rainfall, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, wildlife viewing, and beach activities. Avoid: December to March, as the summer months can be hot and humid, with frequent rainfall.

Natural Highlights

  • Gorongosa National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Africa's most biodiverse parks, with a wide range of wildlife, including lions, elephants, and hippos.
  • The Bazaruto Archipelago is a group of six islands located off the coast of Mozambique, known for their beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and diverse marine life.
  • The Zambezi River is one of Africa's longest and most iconic rivers, stretching over 2,500 kilometers and supporting a wide range of aquatic life.
  • The Monte Binga mountain range is a popular destination for hikers and trekkers, with its rugged terrain and scenic beauty.
  • The Quirimbas National Park is a marine and terrestrial park, with a wide range of wildlife, including dolphins, whales, and numerous species of birds.
  • The Maputo Elephant Reserve is a protected area located near the capital city of Maputo, providing a habitat for elephants and other wildlife.
