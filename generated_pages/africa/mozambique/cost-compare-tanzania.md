[SECTION] Overview: Mozambique vs Tanzania [/SECTION]
Mozambique and Tanzania are two neighboring countries in Southeast Africa, each with its unique culture, history, and economy. In terms of cost of living, Mozambique is generally cheaper than Tanzania, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living, with Mozambique having a more developed economy and a stronger currency. According to the World Bank, Mozambique's GDP per capita (PPP) is around 1,266 USD, while Tanzania's is around 1,104 USD.

The cost of living difference between Mozambique and Tanzania can be attributed to various factors, including the cost of accommodation, food, and transportation. On average, Mozambique is around 15-20% cheaper than Tanzania, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle. However, it's essential to note that prices can vary significantly depending on the location, with urban areas tend to be more expensive than rural areas. For example, the capital city of Maputo in Mozambique and Dar es Salaam in Tanzania tend to have higher prices compared to other parts of the country.
