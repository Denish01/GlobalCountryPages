Notable Achievements

CategoryAchievementDetails
ScienceConservation EffortsMozambique has made significant strides in conservation, with the establishment of the Gorongosa National Park, which has become a model for sustainable conservation and community development.
SportsFootball SuccessThe Mozambican national football team has achieved notable success, including winning the COSAFA Cup in 2004 and 2007.
ArtsTraditional MusicMozambique is home to a rich cultural heritage, with traditional music genres such as marabenta and marrabenta being recognized and celebrated globally.
DevelopmentPoverty ReductionMozambique has made significant progress in reducing poverty, with the percentage of the population living below the poverty line decreasing from 69% in 1990 to 46% in 2015.
InnovationRenewable EnergyMozambique has invested heavily in renewable energy, with the Mphanda Nkuwa hydroelectric dam being one of the largest in Africa, and the Gorongosa solar power plant being a significant contributor to the national grid.
Global ContributionsPeacekeeping MissionsMozambique has contributed to several UN peacekeeping missions, including in the Democratic Republic of Congo, Liberia, and Sudan.
EducationLiteracy RateMozambique has made significant progress in increasing its literacy rate, from 40% in 1990 to 60% in 2015, with a focus on primary education and adult literacy programs.
Economic GrowthGDP GrowthMozambique has experienced significant economic growth, with its GDP growing at an average rate of 7% per annum from 2000 to 2015, driven by agriculture, mining, and manufacturing.

Cultural Treasures

  • The Island of Mozambique, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a historic island town that showcases the country's rich cultural heritage and architectural diversity.
  • The Gorongosa National Park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, lions, and hippos, and is considered one of the most biodiverse places on the planet.
  • The Maputo Cathedral, a stunning example of Portuguese colonial architecture, is a prominent landmark in the capital city and a symbol of the country's rich cultural heritage.
  • The Mozambican National Museum of Art, located in Maputo, showcases the country's vibrant art scene, with a collection of contemporary and traditional art pieces.

People & Global Impact

  • Samora Machel, the country's first president after independence, played a significant role in shaping Mozambique's history and development.
  • The Mozambican diaspora, with significant communities in Portugal, South Africa, and the United States, has made significant contributions to the country's economic development and cultural exchange.
  • Graca Machel, a renowned humanitarian and wife of Nelson Mandela, has been a vocal advocate for women's rights and education, and has worked tirelessly to promote social justice and human rights.
  • The Mozambican values of ubuntu (humanity towards others) and communalism are reflected in the country's strong sense of community and social cohesion.

What Visitors Love

  • The warm hospitality of the Mozambican people, who are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, makes visitors feel at home.
  • The delicious cuisine, which features a unique blend of African, Portuguese, and Indian flavors, is a highlight of any visit to Mozambique.
  • The stunning natural beauty, with its vast coastlines, coral reefs, and mountain ranges, offers a wide range of outdoor activities and breathtaking scenery.
  • The vibrant markets, such as the Maputo Market, offer a glimpse into the country's rich cultural heritage and provide a platform for local artisans to showcase their wares.
  • The relaxed atmosphere, with its laid-back pace of life, makes Mozambique an ideal destination for those seeking a tranquil and rejuvenating experience.
  • The opportunities for outdoor adventure, including snorkeling, diving, and hiking, make Mozambique a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Hidden Gems

  • The Quirimbas Archipelago, a chain of islands off the northern coast, is a hidden gem that offers a unique opportunity to experience the country's rich marine biodiversity and stunning natural beauty.
  • The Mount Namuli, a rugged and scenic mountain range, is a popular destination for hiking and trekking, with breathtaking views and a wide range of flora and fauna.
  • The Lake Malawi, which forms part of the border with Malawi, is a stunning lake that offers a wide range of water sports and activities, including fishing, boating, and swimming.
  • The town of Ilha de Mozambique, a historic town on the island of the same name, is a charming and atmospheric destination that offers a glimpse into the country's rich cultural heritage and history.
