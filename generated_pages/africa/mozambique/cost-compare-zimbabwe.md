[SECTION] Overview: Mozambique vs Zimbabwe [/SECTION]
Mozambique and Zimbabwe are two neighboring countries in Southern Africa with distinct economic profiles. Mozambique has a growing economy, driven by its natural resources, agriculture, and tourism sectors, while Zimbabwe's economy has been facing challenges, including hyperinflation and a decline in its manufacturing sector. The cost of living in Mozambique is generally lower than in Zimbabwe, with accommodation, food, and transportation costs being significantly cheaper. However, Zimbabwe has a more developed infrastructure and a wider range of amenities, which can make it a more attractive option for some expats and tourists.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Mozambique has a relatively stable economy, with a growing middle class and a increasing demand for goods and services. In contrast, Zimbabwe's economy has been facing significant challenges, including a severe cash crisis and a decline in the value of its currency. As a result, the cost of living in Zimbabwe can be volatile, with prices fluctuating rapidly in response to changes in the exchange rate and inflation. Overall, Mozambique is generally the cheaper option, with a cost of living that is around 20-30% lower than in Zimbabwe.
