# Top Things to Do in Mozambique — Attractions & Experiences

Mozambique offers a diverse range of exciting activities and experiences, from exploring the historic city of Maputo to relaxing on the stunning beaches of the Bazaruto Archipelago, making it a must-visit destination for any traveler.

[SECTION] Top Attractions [/SECTION]
[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Fortaleza de São Tiago | Mozambique Island | Historic Fort | UNESCO World Heritage Site and former Portuguese trading post |
| Gorongosa National Park | Sofala Province | National Park | Abundant wildlife, including elephants, lions, and hippos |
| Bazaruto Archipelago | Inhambane Province | Island Chain | Pristine beaches, coral reefs, and excellent snorkeling and diving opportunities |
| Maputo Central Market | Maputo | Market | Vibrant marketplace offering a wide range of local goods and cuisine |
| Ilha de Mozambique | Nampula Province | Historic Island | Rich history, beautiful architecture, and stunning beaches |
| Chimanimani Mountains | Manica Province | Mountain Range | Scenic hiking trails, waterfalls, and traditional villages |
| Inhambane Old Town | Inhambane Province | Historic Town | Well-preserved colonial architecture and charming atmosphere |
| Niassa Reserve | Niassa Province | Wildlife Reserve | Remote and untouched wilderness area with abundant wildlife |
| Lake Malawi | Niassa Province | Lake | Shared border with Malawi, offering stunning scenery and water activities |
| Ponta do Ouro | Maputo Province | Beach Town | Popular spot for surfing, snorkeling, and dolphin watching |
[/TABLE]

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **dhow** sailing trip to explore the coastline and nearby islands
- Visit a traditional **Ronga** village to learn about local customs and way of life
- Go on a **horseback safari** in the Gorongosa National Park for a unique wildlife experience
- Attend a **Capoeira** performance, a traditional Afro-Brazilian martial art
- Take a **cooking class** to learn about Mozambican cuisine and local ingredients
- Go **kiteboarding** or **windsurfing** in the strong winds of the Bazaruto Archipelago
- Explore the **street art** scene in Maputo, featuring vibrant murals and graffiti

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Hike to the **Mount Gorongosa** peak for stunning views of the surrounding landscape
- Visit the **Bazaruto National Park** for snorkeling, diving, and marine life spotting
- Explore the **Chimanimani Mountains** for scenic trails, waterfalls, and traditional villages
- Go on a **safari** in the **Niassa Reserve** for a chance to spot rare and endangered species
- Relax on the **Tofo Beach**, known for its stunning scenery and vibrant nightlife
- Take a **boat tour** of the **Lake Malawi** to explore the surrounding villages and scenic shoreline

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Mozambique National Museum** to learn about the country's history and culture
- Explore the **Fortaleza de São Tiago**, a historic fort and former Portuguese trading post
- Attend a traditional **Makonde** mask-carving workshop to learn about local craftsmanship
- Visit the **Maputo Central Market** to experience the sights, sounds, and smells of local commerce
- Take a **tour of the historic city** of Maputo, featuring colonial architecture and vibrant street life
- Attend a **traditional music and dance performance** to experience Mozambican arts and culture

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Quirimbas National Park**, a remote and untouched wilderness area
- Explore the **town of Ibo**, known for its historic architecture and stunning scenery
- Take a **boat tour** of the **Pemba Bay**, featuring stunning coastline and marine life
- Visit the **Nacala-a-Velha**, a historic town with well-preserved colonial architecture
- Go **trekking** in the **Chimanimani Mountains**, offering scenic trails and stunning views

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip from Maputo to the **Ponta do Ouro** (120 km, 2 hours), known for its stunning beaches and surfing opportunities
- Visit the **Gorongosa National Park** (400 km, 6 hours from Maputo), featuring abundant wildlife and scenic landscapes
- Explore the **Inhambane Old Town** (460 km, 7 hours from Maputo), with its well-preserved colonial architecture and charming atmosphere
- Take a day trip from Nampula to the **Ilha de Mozambique** (200 km, 3 hours), a historic island with stunning beaches and rich history
- Visit the **Chimanimani Mountains** (300 km, 5 hours from Maputo), offering scenic hiking trails and stunning views
