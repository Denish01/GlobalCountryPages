[SECTION] What Is Mozambique? [/SECTION]
Mozambique is a **sovereign** country located in southeastern Africa, bordered by the Indian Ocean to the east, Tanzania to the north, Malawi, Zambia, Zimbabwe, South Africa, and Eswatini to the west, and Madagascar to the east across the Mozambique Channel. With a population of over 33.9 million people, Mozambique is a country with a rich cultural heritage and a diverse geography, featuring a long coastline, mountains, and savannas. The country is known for its beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and vibrant cities, making it a popular destination for tourists and investors alike.

Mozambique has a complex history, with various empires and colonial powers vying for control over the region. The country gained independence from Portugal in 1975, after a long and bloody struggle. Today, Mozambique is a member of the Southern African Development Community (SADC) and the African Union, and is working to develop its economy and improve the living standards of its citizens. The country has a diverse economy, with major industries including agriculture, mining, and manufacturing.

Mozambique is also home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including many species of birds, mammals, and fish. The country's natural beauty and rich cultural heritage make it an attractive destination for tourists, with popular attractions including the **Gorongosa National Park**, the **Bazaruto Archipelago**, and the historic city of **Ilha de Mozambique**.
