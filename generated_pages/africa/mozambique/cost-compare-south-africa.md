[SECTION] Overview: Mozambique vs South Africa [/SECTION]
Mozambique and South Africa are two neighboring countries in Southern Africa, with distinct economic profiles and cost of living standards. Mozambique has a lower GDP per capita and a smaller economy compared to South Africa, which is one of the most developed countries on the continent. However, Mozambique's lower cost of living makes it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle. The country's economy is largely driven by agriculture, mining, and tourism, while South Africa has a more diversified economy with a strong services sector.

The cost of living in Mozambique is generally lower compared to South Africa, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being significantly cheaper. However, the quality of infrastructure and services in Mozambique can be lower compared to South Africa, which may affect the overall quality of life. For example, while renting a one-bedroom apartment in Maputo, the capital city of Mozambique, can cost around $200-300 per month, a similar apartment in Johannesburg, South Africa can cost around $400-600 per month. Overall, Mozambique offers a more affordable lifestyle, but with some trade-offs in terms of infrastructure and services.
