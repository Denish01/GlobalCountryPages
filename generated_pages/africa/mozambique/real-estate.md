[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The property market in Mozambique is growing, driven by foreign investment, urbanization, and government efforts to improve infrastructure and business conditions. The market is still relatively underdeveloped, offering opportunities for investors to acquire properties at competitive prices. However, the market is also subject to fluctuations, and prices can vary depending on the location, with urban areas like Maputo and Beira tend to be more expensive than rural areas. The price trajectory has been steadily increasing over the past few years, with some areas experiencing higher growth rates than others.

The urban-rural divide is significant in Mozambique, with urban areas offering better infrastructure, amenities, and economic opportunities. However, rural areas also offer investment opportunities, particularly in the agriculture and tourism sectors. The government has implemented policies to encourage investment in rural areas, such as tax incentives and subsidies for agricultural development. Despite these efforts, the rural property market remains relatively underdeveloped, and investors may face challenges such as limited access to financing and infrastructure.

The current market conditions are favorable for investors, with a range of properties available, from luxury apartments to commercial developments and rural land. However, investors should be aware of the risks associated with investing in a developing market, such as fluctuations in the currency and potential changes in government policies. Overall, the property market in Mozambique offers opportunities for investors looking to tap into the country's growing economy and urbanization trends.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners can buy property in Mozambique, but they must obtain a residence permit or a work visa to do so.
- Foreigners can also buy property through a company, which can provide additional benefits such as tax incentives and easier ownership transfer.
- However, foreigners are restricted from buying property in certain areas, such as border zones and areas designated for national security.
- Foreigners must also obtain a permit from the Ministry of Justice to buy property, which can take several months to process.
- Foreigners can lease property for up to 99 years, which can provide a workaround for those who do not want to buy property outright.

[SECTION] Buying Process [/SECTION]
1. Research the market and identify a property to buy.
2. Contact a real estate agent or lawyer to assist with the purchase.
3. Obtain a residence permit or work visa if necessary.
4. Apply for a permit from the Ministry of Justice to buy property.
5. Conduct due diligence on the property, including a title search and inspection.
6. Negotiate the price and terms of the sale.
7. Sign a sales agreement and pay a deposit.
8. Transfer the ownership of the property and pay the remaining balance.
9. Register the property with the relevant authorities.
10. Obtain a **certificate of ownership**, which is required to sell or lease the property in the future.

[SECTION] Rental Market [/SECTION]
- The rental market in Mozambique is relatively small, but it is growing, particularly in urban areas.
- Tenants have **rights** under the law, including the right to a written lease and protection from eviction.
- Typical lease terms are 6-12 months, and deposits are usually equivalent to 1-2 months' rent.
- Furnished properties are more common in urban areas, while unfurnished properties are more common in rural areas.
- Rent prices are relatively high in urban areas, particularly in Maputo and Beira, where demand is high and supply is limited.

- Be aware of the **risks** associated with investing in a developing market, such as fluctuations in the currency and potential changes in government policies.
- Consider **property management** options, such as hiring a local company to manage the property and handle tenant relations.
- Research **tax incentives** and other benefits available to foreign investors, such as subsidies for agricultural development.
- Work with a **reputable real estate agent** or lawyer to assist with the purchase and ensure that the transaction is smooth and secure.
- Consider **long-term investment** strategies, such as buying and holding property for several years to ride out market fluctuations.
