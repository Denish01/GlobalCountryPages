Visa Policy Overview

Gabon has a moderate visa policy, requiring visitors from many countries to obtain a visa before entering the country. However, citizens of certain countries are eligible for visa-free access or can obtain a visa on arrival. Gabon's visa policy is designed to facilitate travel while ensuring national security and public health. The country offers various types of visas, including tourist, business, and student visas, each with its own set of requirements and regulations.

Gabon's visa policy is not overly restrictive, but it does require visitors to meet certain criteria before entering the country. Citizens of around 20 countries, including France, Germany, and Italy, are eligible for visa-free access, while citizens of other countries can obtain a visa on arrival or through the e-visa system. The e-visa system, introduced in 2015, has made it easier for visitors to obtain a visa and has helped to increase tourism in the country.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists can obtain a visa on arrival or through the e-visa system, which is usually valid for a maximum stay of 90 days. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, and extensions are possible but must be applied for before the initial visa expires. Tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay in Gabon.

Business Travelers: Business travelers must have an invitation letter from a Gabonese company, as well as a valid passport and proof of onward travel. The business visa is usually valid for a maximum stay of 90 days, and extensions are possible but must be applied for before the initial visa expires. Business travelers may also be required to provide additional documentation, such as a certificate of incorporation or a letter from their employer.

Students: Students must have a valid passport, proof of enrollment at a Gabonese university, and sufficient funds to support their stay in Gabon. The student visa is usually valid for a maximum stay of one year, and extensions are possible but must be applied for before the initial visa expires. Students may also be required to provide additional documentation, such as a medical certificate or a police certificate.

Workers: Workers must have a valid passport, a sponsorship letter from a Gabonese employer, and sufficient funds to support their stay in Gabon. The work visa is usually valid for a maximum stay of one year, and extensions are possible but must be applied for before the initial visa expires. Workers may also be required to provide additional documentation, such as a certificate of qualification or a letter from their employer.

Entry Points & Procedures

The main entry points for Gabon are Libreville International Airport (LBV) and the land borders with Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, and the Republic of Congo. Upon arrival, visitors must present their travel documents, including their passport, visa (if required), and proof of onward travel. Visitors may also be required to provide additional documentation, such as a health certificate or a police certificate. The immigration process typically takes around 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the number of passengers and the complexity of the application.

At Libreville International Airport, visitors can expect to go through a standard immigration procedure, which includes presenting their travel documents, answering questions from the immigration officer, and having their fingerprints taken. Visitors may also be required to pay a fee for their visa, which can be paid in cash or by credit card. The airport also offers a range of facilities, including currency exchange, baggage claim, and transportation services.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not applying for a visa in advance, if required
  • Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
  • Not having proof of onward travel
  • Not having sufficient funds to support their stay in Gabon
  • Not having the required documentation, such as an invitation letter or enrollment proof
  • Not declaring goods or currency upon arrival, which can result in fines or penalties
