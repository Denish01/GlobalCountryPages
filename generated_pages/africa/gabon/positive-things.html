Cultural Treasures

  • The Ivindo National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to a stunning array of biodiversity, including lowland gorillas and forest elephants.
  • The Mvet is a traditional instrument and an important part of Gabonese cultural heritage, used in music, dance, and storytelling.
  • The Bwiti initiation ritual is an important tradition in Gabonese culture, marking the transition from childhood to adulthood.
  • The Gabonese mask-making tradition is a renowned art form, with intricate designs and symbolism.
  • The Libreville Museum is a cultural treasure trove, showcasing the history and art of Gabon.

People & Global Impact

  • Omar Bongo, the former President of Gabon, played a key role in the country's development and was a prominent figure in African politics.
  • Ali Bongo Ondimba, the current President of Gabon, has continued to promote the country's economic development and international cooperation.
  • The Gabonese diaspora has made significant contributions to the global community, with notable figures in fields such as music, art, and literature.
  • The Gabonese values of hospitality, respect, and community are highly regarded and reflected in the country's welcoming culture.

What Visitors Love

  • The beautiful beaches of Libreville, such as the Gulf of Guinea, are a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.
  • The vibrant markets of Gabon, such as the Grand Marche in Libreville, offer a colorful and lively shopping experience.
  • The delicious cuisine of Gabon, which includes popular dishes like Poulet Nyembwe and Liboke, is a highlight of any visit.
  • The warm hospitality of the Gabonese people is renowned, with visitors often commenting on the friendly and welcoming nature of the locals.
  • The stunning natural scenery of Gabon, including the Crystal Mountains and the Ivindo River, is a major draw for tourists and adventure seekers.
  • The exciting outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, and surfing, make Gabon an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hidden Gems

  • The Kongou Falls, located in the Ivindo National Park, are a breathtaking sight and a must-see for any visitor to Gabon.
  • The Lope National Park is a hidden gem, with its unique blend of savannah and forest ecosystems and stunning scenery.
  • The Gabonese traditional medicine is a lesser-known aspect of the country's cultural heritage, with a rich tradition of using plants and natural remedies to promote health and wellbeing.
  • The Gabonese craft industry is a thriving sector, with skilled artisans producing beautiful and intricate crafts, such as woodcarvings and textiles.
