[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Gabon's transportation network is relatively underdeveloped, with most people relying on **taxis**, **minibuses**, and **private cars** to get around. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with Libreville having a more comprehensive network than other urban areas. The lack of a well-organized public transportation system makes it challenging for visitors to navigate, especially outside of major cities. However, the country's scenic roads and picturesque landscapes make driving a pleasant experience, with many tourists opting to rent cars to explore Gabon's natural beauty.

In urban areas, **public transportation** is limited, and many residents rely on **informal taxis** and **shared minibuses** to commute. The roads in Gabon are generally in good condition, but traffic congestion can be a problem in major cities, especially during peak hours. To get around, many locals use **motorcycles** or **bicycles**, which are affordable and convenient modes of transportation. Additionally, **walking** is a common mode of transportation, especially in smaller towns and villages.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using **public buses** or **minibuses**, which are affordable and widely available.
- Use **ride-hailing apps** like Uber or Bolt to get around cities, as they are convenient and relatively affordable.
- Always **negotiate taxi fares** before starting your journey, as meters are not always used.
- Be aware of **traffic congestion** in major cities, especially during peak hours, and plan your route accordingly.
- Consider **renting a car** to explore Gabon's natural beauty, but be aware of **fuel costs** and **parking challenges**.
- Use **local currency (XAF)** to pay for transportation, as it is widely accepted and can help you avoid exchange rate issues.
