# Gabon Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Gabon is relatively well-developed in urban areas, but the quality and availability can vary significantly depending on the location, with **average internet speeds** and **mobile network coverage** being a concern in rural areas. 

[FACTBOX]
Average Internet Speed: 5 Mbps download

Main Carriers: Gabon Telecom, Moov, Airtel
SIM Card Cost: 10 USD for tourist SIM
WiFi Availability: Limited
5G Available: No
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Gabon is characterized by a significant urban-rural divide, with **faster internet speeds** and more reliable connections available in cities like Libreville and Port-Gentil, while rural areas often struggle with **slower speeds** and **less reliable connections**. This divide is largely due to the limited infrastructure and lack of investment in rural areas, making it challenging for residents and visitors to access **quality internet services**. Despite these challenges, the internet has become an essential tool for many Gabonese, with a growing number of **internet users** and a increasing demand for **online services**.

The reliability of the internet in Gabon can also be a concern, with **frequent outages** and **slow speeds** reported by users, particularly during peak hours or in areas with high demand. However, efforts are being made to improve the **internet infrastructure**, including the deployment of **fiber optic cables** and the expansion of **mobile networks**, which are expected to enhance the overall **quality of service** and provide more **reliable connections** to users.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Gabon, visitors can purchase one from a local **mobile operator**, such as Gabon Telecom, Moov, or Airtel, at their stores or authorized retailers. To buy a SIM card, users will typically need to provide a **valid passport** and **proof of address**, and the process can be completed in a few minutes. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable carrier, as they offer **better coverage** and **more reliable services**. Data plans are available, with prices varying depending on the carrier and the amount of **data included**.

For example, Gabon Telecom offers a **tourist plan** with 1GB of data for around 10 USD, while Moov offers a similar plan with 2GB of data for around 15 USD. It is essential to check the **coverage and prices** before purchasing a SIM card, as they can vary significantly depending on the carrier and the location.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally **good**, with many hotels offering **free WiFi** to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is not very developed in Gabon, but some **modern cafes** in urban areas offer **free WiFi** to customers.
- Coworking spaces are available in major cities, offering **reliable WiFi** and **other amenities** to remote workers and entrepreneurs.
- Public WiFi is **limited**, but some **public hotspots** are available in airports, hotels, and other public areas.
- Many **restaurants and bars** also offer **free WiFi** to customers, making it easier to stay connected while exploring the country.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like **Gabon Coworking** and **Libreville Coworking** offer **reliable WiFi** and **other amenities** to remote workers and entrepreneurs.
- Reliable cafe chains like **Cafe de la Gare** and **Cafe du Port** offer **free WiFi** and a **comfortable working environment**.
- Backup internet options like **mobile hotspots** and **satellite internet** are available, but can be **expensive** and **less reliable**.
- VPNs are not strictly necessary, but can be useful for **secure browsing** and **accessing blocked sites**.
- **eSIM options** are not widely available, but can be a **convenient alternative** to traditional SIM cards for some users.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a significant concern in Gabon, but some **websites may be blocked** due to **copyright or licensing issues**.
- **VPN recommendations** include using a reputable VPN provider to **secure browsing** and **protect user data**.
- Roaming alternatives like **local SIM cards** and **mobile hotspots** can be **more cost-effective** than roaming with a **foreign carrier**.
- **eSIM options** can be a **convenient alternative** to traditional SIM cards, but may not be widely supported by all carriers.
- **Public WiFi** can be **insecure**, so it is essential to use **caution** when accessing sensitive information or **using public hotspots**.
- **Regular software updates** and **antivirus protection** are essential to **protect user devices** from **malware and other threats**.
