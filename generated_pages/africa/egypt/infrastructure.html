Infrastructure Overview

Egypt's infrastructure development level is moderate, with significant investments made in recent years to improve the country's transportation, energy, and telecommunications sectors. The government has implemented various projects to upgrade the country's infrastructure, including the construction of new roads, bridges, and tunnels, as well as the expansion of the rail network and the development of new airports and seaports. However, there is still a significant urban-rural gap in terms of infrastructure development, with rural areas often lacking access to basic services such as electricity, water, and sanitation.

The Egyptian government has made significant efforts to address the infrastructure gap, with a focus on improving the business environment and attracting foreign investment. The country has also made significant progress in developing its energy sector, with a focus on increasing the use of renewable energy sources such as wind and solar power. Despite these efforts, however, Egypt's infrastructure still faces significant challenges, including congestion, pollution, and a lack of maintenance.

Energy & Electricity

  • Egypt's power generation mix is dominated by natural gas, which accounts for around 70% of the country's electricity production.
  • The country has made significant progress in increasing the use of renewable energy sources, with a focus on wind and solar power.
  • Egypt's electricity grid is generally reliable, with some occasional power outages in certain areas.
  • The government has implemented various projects to improve the efficiency and reliability of the country's energy sector, including the development of new power plants and the expansion of the grid.
  • Egypt has set a target of generating 20% of its electricity from renewable sources by 2022, and is working to increase the use of solar power and wind power in the country's energy mix.
