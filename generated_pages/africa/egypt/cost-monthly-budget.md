# Monthly Budget Guide for Egypt

To live in Egypt, you would need approximately $400-700 per month for a budget lifestyle, $700-1,200 per month for a mid-range lifestyle, and $1,200-2,000 per month for a comfortable lifestyle.

Mid-Range Living (monthly): $900
Comfortable Living (monthly): $1,600
Average Local Salary: $250/month, approximate
Currency: EGP
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Cost of Living Overview [/SECTION]
Egypt is considered a relatively affordable country to live in, with a GDP per capita PPP of $19,094. The cost of living in Egypt can vary greatly depending on the lifestyle and location. A budget of $400 per month can provide a modest lifestyle, while $900 per month can offer a more comfortable mid-range lifestyle. In comparison to neighboring countries, Egypt is more affordable than Israel and Palestine, but more expensive than Sudan and Libya. The high inflation rate of 28.3% can affect the purchasing power of the local currency, EGP.

The different budgets can go a long way in Egypt, with $400 per month allowing for a basic lifestyle, $900 per month providing a more comfortable lifestyle, and $1,600 per month offering a high level of comfort. For example, a budget of $400 per month can cover the cost of a shared apartment outside of the city center, basic food and groceries, and public transportation. On the other hand, a mid-range budget of $900 per month can provide a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, a mix of cooking and eating out, and a combination of public and private transportation.

[SECTION] Budget Tier Lifestyles [/SECTION]
At the budget level of $400 per month, daily life in Egypt can be quite modest. You can afford to share a small apartment outside of the city center, cook most of your meals, and use public transportation. You can also afford to visit local markets and try some street food, but eating out at mid-range restaurants would be a rare treat. You would need to be mindful of your expenses and prioritize your spending. For example, you might need to choose between taking a taxi or using public transportation, or between buying new clothes or second-hand ones.

At the mid-range level of $900 per month, daily life in Egypt can be quite comfortable. You can afford to rent a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, eat out at mid-range restaurants a few times a week, and use a combination of public and private transportation. You can also afford to join a gym, take a few hobbies, and go out with friends. You would have more freedom to choose how you spend your money, but you would still need to be mindful of your expenses. For example, you might need to choose between traveling within Egypt or taking a weekend trip to a nearby country.

At the comfortable level of $1,600 per month, daily life in Egypt can be quite luxurious. You can afford to rent a nice two-bedroom apartment in a desirable neighborhood, eat out at high-end restaurants, and use a taxi or car for transportation. You can also afford to travel within Egypt, take weekend trips to nearby countries, and enjoy a wide range of hobbies and activities. You would have a high level of freedom to choose how you spend your money, and you would be able to enjoy many of the comforts and luxuries that Egypt has to offer.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Strategies [/SECTION]
- Learn to cook Egyptian dishes and eat at home instead of eating out
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or cars
- Shop at local markets instead of supermarkets
- Avoid traveling during peak season
- Consider sharing an apartment with roommates
- Take advantage of free or low-cost cultural events and activities
- Learn some Arabic phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals
- Use cash instead of credit cards to avoid high interest rates

[SECTION] Is Egypt Affordable? [/SECTION]
Egypt can be an affordable destination for digital nomads, retirees, and students who are looking for a low-cost lifestyle. The cost of living in Egypt is relatively low, and the country offers a wide range of affordable accommodation options, food, and transportation. However, for families or individuals who are used to a high standard of living, Egypt may not be the most affordable destination. The high inflation rate and limited job opportunities can make it challenging for some people to make ends meet. Additionally, the country's infrastructure and services can be unpredictable, which can be frustrating for some people.

Overall, Egypt is a great destination for people who are looking for a unique cultural experience, a low-cost lifestyle, and a warm climate. With some planning and research, it is possible to live comfortably in Egypt on a budget of $400-1,600 per month. However, it's essential to be mindful of the local economy, culture, and customs to make the most of your experience in Egypt. **Budget-conscious travelers** can find many ways to save money and enjoy the country's many attractions, but **high-end travelers** may find that Egypt is not the most affordable destination for their lifestyle.
