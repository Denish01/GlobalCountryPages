Visa Policy Overview

Egypt has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and prior visa requirements for different nationalities. Citizens of around 10 countries, including Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates, can enter Egypt visa-free for a limited period. Other nationalities may be eligible for a visa on arrival or may need to obtain a prior visa from an Egyptian embassy or consulate.

The Egyptian government has introduced an electronic visa system to simplify the process for tourists and business travelers. The e-visa is available for citizens of over 40 countries and can be obtained online prior to travel. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and travelers should always check the latest information before planning their trip to Egypt.

Tourists: Tourists can typically stay in Egypt for up to 30 days, and their visa can be obtained on arrival at major airports or land borders. The visa is usually valid for a single entry, but multiple-entry visas can be obtained for a longer period.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may need to provide additional documentation, such as an invitation letter from an Egyptian company or a letter explaining the purpose of their visit. They may also need to register with the Egyptian authorities.
Students: Students who wish to study in Egypt must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment at an Egyptian educational institution. The visa is usually valid for the duration of the course.
Workers: Workers who wish to work in Egypt must obtain a work visa, which requires sponsorship from an Egyptian employer. The visa is usually valid for the duration of the employment contract.

The main airports in Egypt are Cairo International Airport, Hurghada International Airport, and Sharm El-Sheikh International Airport. Land borders are also open, with major crossings at Rafah (with Gaza), Taba (with Israel), and Salloum (with Libya). Upon arrival, travelers will need to proceed to the immigration counter, where they will need to present their passport, visa (if required), and completed arrival/departure card. The immigration officer will review the documents and may ask questions about the purpose of the visit and the length of stay.
Travelers should be prepared to provide additional information or documentation, such as proof of onward travel or sufficient funds. It is also essential to ensure that the passport is stamped correctly, as this will be required for departure. In case of any issues or discrepancies, travelers may be required to pay a fine or face further questioning.
- Failing to check the validity of the passport before traveling
- Not obtaining the correct type of visa for the purpose of the visit
- Not registering with the Egyptian authorities (if required)
- Not carrying sufficient funds or proof of onward travel
- Not having a valid health insurance certificate (if required)
- Not declaring all items in the luggage, including gifts or souvenirs
