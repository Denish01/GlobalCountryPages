[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Egypt is a fascinating country with a rich history and culture, attracting millions of tourists every year. While it is generally considered safe to visit, there are some safety concerns that travelers should be aware of. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing, scams, and harassment can occur, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, the country has experienced periods of unrest and protests in recent years, which can affect traveler safety. However, with proper precautions and an understanding of the local environment, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Egypt.

It is essential to stay informed about local conditions and follow government advisories. The Egyptian government has taken steps to improve security, including increasing the presence of tourist police and implementing security measures at popular tourist sites. Nevertheless, it is crucial for travelers to remain vigilant and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. This includes being aware of their surroundings, keeping valuables secure, and avoiding traveling alone at night. By taking these precautions and staying informed, visitors can minimize their risk and have a safe and rewarding experience in Egypt.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **scams** involving pyramid tours, where locals may offer to guide you around the pyramids and then demand money.
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas, such as markets and public transportation.
- Be mindful of **aggressive vendors** who may try to sell you goods or services at inflated prices.
- Be cautious of **traffic accidents**, as driving in Egypt can be chaotic and unpredictable.
- Be aware of **harassment**, especially for solo female travelers, and take necessary precautions to stay safe.
- Be cautious of **food and waterborne illnesses**, and take necessary precautions to stay healthy.
- Be aware of **crowded areas** and **demonstrations**, which can turn violent and affect traveler safety.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended for travel to Egypt.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are generally good in major cities, but may be limited in rural areas. Travelers may need to pay upfront for medical services, and insurance is recommended.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but the quality of medications may vary.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.
- Be cautious of **scams** and **aggressive vendors**.
- Learn some basic Arabic phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you".
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
- Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen, as the sun can be intense in Egypt.
- Consider hiring a reputable guide or joining a guided tour to stay safe and get the most out of your trip.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Take necessary precautions to stay safe, such as avoiding traveling alone at night and being aware of your surroundings.
- Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
- Be prepared for **harassment**, and take necessary steps to stay safe, such as avoiding eye contact and not engaging with aggressive individuals.
- Consider joining a guided tour or hiring a reputable guide to stay safe and get the most out of your trip.
