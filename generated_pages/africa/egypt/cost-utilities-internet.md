The cost of utilities and internet in Egypt can vary depending on the location, usage, and provider, but on average, a monthly utility bill for an 85 sqm apartment can range from $100 to $200 USD.

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Egypt's utility infrastructure has undergone significant improvements in recent years, with a focus on increasing efficiency and reducing costs. However, the country still faces challenges in providing reliable and consistent services, particularly in rural areas. The cost of utilities in Egypt can be subject to fluctuations, mainly due to changes in global energy prices and the country's economic conditions. The inflation rate in Egypt is currently at 28.3%, which can impact the cost of utilities and other essential services.

The cost of utilities in Egypt can also vary depending on the season, with higher consumption of electricity and water during the summer months. For example, the cost of electricity can increase by up to 20% during the peak summer months, while the cost of water can remain relatively stable throughout the year. It's essential to consider these seasonal variations when planning and budgeting for utility costs in Egypt.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities in Egypt, it's essential to have a valid residence permit and a local address, and to contact the relevant utility providers to schedule an installation.
- Prepaid electricity meters are widely used in Egypt, allowing residents to pay for their electricity usage in advance and avoid unexpected bills.
- The best internet providers in Egypt include Vodafone, Orange, and Etisalat, which offer a range of plans and services to suit different needs and budgets.
- To save on utility bills, residents can consider using energy-efficient appliances, reducing their water consumption, and taking advantage of off-peak electricity rates.
- It's also a good idea to regularly review and compare utility providers and plans to ensure that you're getting the best deal for your money.
