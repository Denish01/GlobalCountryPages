[SECTION] Overview: Egypt vs Palestine [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Egypt and Palestine varies significantly, with Egypt being the more affordable option. Egypt has a large and diverse economy, with a strong focus on industry, agriculture, and tourism, which contributes to its relatively low cost of living. In contrast, Palestine has a smaller and more limited economy, which is heavily reliant on international aid and has a higher cost of living as a result. On average, Egypt is around 20-30% cheaper than Palestine, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable place to live.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Egypt has a large population and a relatively stable economy, which has helped to keep prices low. In contrast, Palestine has a smaller population and a more fragile economy, which has led to higher prices for many goods and services. Additionally, the political situation in Palestine has also contributed to a higher cost of living, with many goods and services being subject to taxes and tariffs. Overall, Egypt is the clear winner when it comes to affordability, with a lower cost of living and a more stable economy.
