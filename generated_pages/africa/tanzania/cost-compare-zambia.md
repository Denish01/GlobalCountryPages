[SECTION] Overview: Tanzania vs Zambia [/SECTION]
Tanzania and Zambia are two neighboring countries in East Africa with distinct economic profiles. Tanzania has a larger population and a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, mining, and tourism. Zambia, on the other hand, has a smaller population and an economy that is heavily reliant on copper mining. In terms of cost of living, Tanzania is generally cheaper than Zambia, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. However, Zambia has a more developed infrastructure and a higher standard of living, which can make it more attractive to expats and tourists.

The cost of living difference between Tanzania and Zambia is largely driven by the exchange rate and the local economy. The Tanzanian shilling is relatively stable, while the Zambian kwacha has experienced significant fluctuations in recent years. Additionally, Tanzania has a larger informal economy, which can make it easier to find cheap accommodation and food options. However, Zambia has a more developed formal economy, with better-paying jobs and a higher standard of living. Overall, Tanzania is a better option for budget-conscious travelers and expats, while Zambia is more suitable for those who value a higher standard of living and are willing to pay a premium for it.
