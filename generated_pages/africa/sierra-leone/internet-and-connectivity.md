# Sierra Leone Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Sierra Leone is relatively slow and unreliable, with average download speeds of around 5 Mbps, but it is steadily improving with the introduction of new mobile network providers and internet services.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Sierra Leone is still in the process of development, with **mobile networks** being the primary means of access for most people. The **urban vs rural divide** is significant, with faster and more reliable internet connections available in cities like Freetown, while rural areas often struggle with slow and inconsistent connections. The **reliability** of internet connections can also be a challenge, with frequent **outages** and **disconnections** reported by users. Despite these challenges, the internet is playing an increasingly important role in the country's economy and daily life, with many businesses and individuals relying on it for communication, information, and commerce.

The **quality of internet services** in Sierra Leone varies depending on the provider and the location. Some areas have access to **fiber-optic connections**, which offer faster and more reliable internet speeds, while others are limited to **mobile broadband** or **satellite internet**, which can be slower and more expensive. The **cost of internet services** is also a significant factor, with many users struggling to afford the monthly fees for internet access. However, there are efforts underway to improve the affordability and accessibility of internet services, including the introduction of **low-cost internet plans** and **community-based internet initiatives**.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Sierra Leone, visitors can purchase one from a **mobile network provider** such as Orange, Africell, or Sierratel. These providers have **retail outlets** and **authorized dealers** throughout the country, including at airports and border crossings. To purchase a SIM card, visitors will need to provide **identification documents**, such as a passport, and may also need to register their SIM card with the provider. The **cost of a SIM card** is around 10 USD, and data plans are available starting from around 1 USD per day.

Recommended carriers for tourists include Orange and Africell, which offer a range of **prepaid plans** and **data packages**. These plans can be purchased at retail outlets or online, and can be **topped up** with additional credit as needed. It's also possible to purchase a **local SIM card** with a **data plan** that includes **international roaming**, which can be useful for visitors who need to stay connected while traveling outside of Sierra Leone.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi is generally available in **mid-range and luxury hotels**, but the quality can be **variable**.
- Cafe WiFi culture is not well-established in Sierra Leone, but some **modern cafes** and **restaurants** in Freetown and other major cities offer free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming more common in Freetown, offering **reliable WiFi** and other amenities to remote workers and entrepreneurs.
- Public WiFi is limited, but some **government buildings** and **public institutions** offer free WiFi to visitors.
- Some **internet cafes** and **cyber cafes** offer paid WiFi access, but these can be **slow** and **unreliable**.
