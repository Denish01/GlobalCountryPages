[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Sierra Leone is a country with a complex safety profile, where visitors can experience a mix of warm hospitality and potential security risks. While the country has made significant progress since the civil war, petty crime, scams, and health risks remain concerns for tourists. The capital city, Freetown, is generally considered safe, with a strong police presence, but visitors should still exercise caution, especially at night. The country's rural areas, however, can be more challenging to navigate, with limited infrastructure and a higher risk of crime.

It is essential to approach Sierra Leone with a balanced perspective, recognizing both the potential dangers and the many rewards that the country has to offer. With proper planning, research, and precautions, visitors can have a safe and enriching experience in Sierra Leone. However, it is crucial to stay informed about local conditions, respect local customs, and take necessary precautions to minimize risks. By doing so, tourists can enjoy the country's stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality while maintaining a safe and healthy journey.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas and markets
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, use **credit cards** or **traveler's checks** instead
- Be aware of **scams** targeting tourists, such as fake **gemstone** or **gold** sales
- Avoid **unlicensed taxis** and use reputable transportation services
- Be cautious of **strangers** approaching you with **unsolicited offers** or **requests**
- Avoid **displaying signs of wealth**, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Be aware of **traffic accidents**, which can be common due to poor road conditions

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid are recommended
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, use **bottled** or **filtered** water instead
- **Medical Facilities:** Limited, especially in rural areas, and often require **cash payment** or **travel insurance**
- **Pharmacy Access:** Limited, especially outside Freetown, and may not have a wide range of medications

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Stay informed about local conditions and **security updates**
- Avoid traveling alone, especially at night
- Use **reputable transportation** services, such as licensed taxis or shuttle buses
- Keep **valuable items** secure and out of sight
- Avoid **displaying signs of wealth**, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Be respectful of **local customs** and traditions
- Stay **hydrated** and take **regular breaks** in hot weather
- Use **insect repellent** to prevent **malaria** and other insect-borne diseases

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **catcalling** and **harassment**, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid traveling alone at night, use **reputable transportation** services instead
- Dress **conservatively**, respecting local customs and traditions
- Stay in **well-lit** and **populated areas**, avoiding isolated or dimly lit areas
