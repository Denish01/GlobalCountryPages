Sierra Leone is a sovereign country located in West Africa, bordered by Guinea to the north and east, and Liberia to the southeast. The country has a coastline along the Atlantic Ocean to the west, with the Sierra Leone Peninsula forming part of the coastline. The capital city, Freetown, is situated on the peninsula and serves as the country's main port and commercial center. Sierra Leone's strategic location on the Atlantic coast made it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region.

The country's borders with Guinea and Liberia are defined by a combination of natural and artificial boundaries. The border with Guinea is largely defined by the Koindou and Makona rivers, while the border with Liberia is marked by the Mano River. Sierra Leone's coastline is approximately 402 kilometers long, with a number of bays, inlets, and islands, including the Sierra Leone Peninsula and the Turtle Islands. The country's unique geography has played a significant role in shaping its history, culture, and economy.
