[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
The business environment in Sierra Leone is characterized by a mix of opportunities and challenges. The government has implemented various reforms to improve the business climate, including the establishment of a **Business Registration Office** to simplify the registration process and the introduction of a **National Investment Policy** to attract foreign investment. However, the country still faces significant challenges, including a high level of **bureaucracy** and **corruption**, which can hinder business operations and increase costs. The government's attitude towards foreign investment is generally positive, with a focus on promoting **private sector development** and increasing **foreign direct investment**.

Despite these challenges, Sierra Leone has made significant progress in recent years, with the country's economy growing at an average rate of 6% per annum. The government has also implemented various measures to improve the business environment, including the establishment of a **Commercial Court** to resolve business disputes and the introduction of a **Tax Reform** to simplify the tax system. However, more needs to be done to address the underlying challenges and create a more favorable business environment.

The country's **infrastructure** is also a significant challenge, with poor roads, limited access to electricity, and inadequate telecommunications. However, the government has invested heavily in infrastructure development, including the construction of new roads and the expansion of the **port of Freetown**. These investments are expected to improve the business environment and increase access to markets.
