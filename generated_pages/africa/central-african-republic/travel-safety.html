Overall Safety Assessment

The Central African Republic is a challenging and unstable country to visit, with high levels of crime, violence, and health risks. The country has been plagued by conflict, poverty, and corruption, which has led to a breakdown in law and order. As a result, tourists are advised to exercise extreme caution when traveling to the Central African Republic. The capital city, Bangui, is generally considered to be the safest area, but even here, crime and violence can occur. The countryside and border regions are particularly hazardous, with armed groups and bandits operating in many areas.

Despite the risks, the Central African Republic is a beautiful country with a rich culture and stunning natural scenery. With careful planning, research, and precautions, it is possible to have a safe and enjoyable trip. However, tourists must be aware of the potential dangers and take necessary steps to mitigate them. This includes staying informed about local conditions, avoiding travel to high-risk areas, and taking necessary security precautions. It is also essential to respect local customs and traditions, and to be mindful of the country's fragile security situation.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Armed robbery and banditry are common in many areas, particularly in the countryside and border regions
  • Scams and extortion by officials and police are possible, particularly at checkpoints and border crossings
  • Kidnapping and hostage-taking have occurred in the past, particularly in areas controlled by armed groups
  • Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, is common in urban areas
  • Corruption is widespread, and tourists may be asked for bribes or "fines" by officials
  • Protests and demonstrations can turn violent, and tourists should avoid areas where crowds are gathering
  • Road accidents are common, particularly on rural roads, due to poor road conditions and reckless driving

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid vaccinations are recommended, and in some cases required
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are limited, particularly in rural areas, and tourists may need to be evacuated to another country for serious medical treatment
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacy access is limited, particularly in rural areas, and tourists should bring any necessary medications with them

Practical Safety Tips

  • Research local conditions and stay informed about security developments
  • Avoid travel to high-risk areas, particularly at night
  • Use reputable and licensed taxi services, and avoid hitchhiking or traveling alone
  • Keep valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas
  • Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
  • Respect local customs and traditions, and avoid taking photographs of sensitive or restricted areas
  • Stay in touch with your government's travel advisory department and register with their travel registration service

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Solo travelers, particularly women, should exercise extreme caution when traveling to the Central African Republic
  • Avoid traveling alone, particularly at night, and stay in well-lit and populated areas
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
  • Avoid engaging with strangers or accepting rides from unknown individuals
