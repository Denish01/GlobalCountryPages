Location & Borders

The Central African Republic is a landlocked country located in the heart of Africa. It is situated in the central part of the continent, and its borders are shared with six neighboring countries: Cameroon to the west, Chad to the north, Sudan to the northeast, South Sudan to the east, the Democratic Republic of the Congo to the south, and the Republic of the Congo to the southwest. The country's unique location makes it a crossroads of different cultures and ecosystems.

The Central African Republic is not bounded by any major bodies of water, but several important rivers flow through the country, including the Oubangui River, the Kotto River, and the Mbomou River. These rivers provide a source of freshwater and support a variety of aquatic life. The country's location in the tropics and its landlocked position contribute to its distinct climate and geography.

Terrain & Landscape

The Central African Republic's terrain is characterized by a vast plateau, with an average elevation of about 600 meters above sea level. The plateau is dotted with hills and mountains, including the Yade Massif and the Bongos Massif. The country's highest point, Mount Ngaoui, is located in the northwest and rises to an elevation of 1,420 meters. The terrain is also marked by numerous valleys and river basins, which support a diverse range of flora and fauna.

The country's landscape is dominated by tropical forests, which cover about 30% of the country's land area. These forests are home to a wide variety of tree species, including mahogany, ebony, and teak. The forests are also inhabited by a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, giraffes, and chimpanzees. In addition to the forests, the country's landscape also features grasslands and savannas, which are found in the northern and eastern parts of the country.

The Central African Republic is also home to several national parks and wildlife reserves, which provide a protected habitat for the country's diverse wildlife. These parks and reserves include the Manovo-Gounda St. Floris National Park, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the Dzanga-Sangha National Park, which is known for its lowland gorillas and forest elephants.

Best Time To Visit

Best months: December to May. Why: The dry season offers the best conditions for traveling and wildlife viewing, with low humidity and comfortable temperatures. Avoid: June to November, due to the heavy rainfall and high humidity, which can make travel difficult and uncomfortable.
