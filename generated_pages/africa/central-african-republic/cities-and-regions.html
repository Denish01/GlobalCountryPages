How Central African Republic Is Divided

The Central African Republic is divided into 16 prefectures, 1 economic prefecture, and 1 commune. The prefectures are further divided into 71 sub-prefectures, which are then divided into communes. The country's administrative structure is designed to provide a framework for governance and development at the local level. The prefectures are responsible for providing basic services such as healthcare, education, and infrastructure, while the sub-prefectures and communes focus on community development and local governance.

The country's administrative divisions are also influenced by its geography and climate. The northern regions are generally drier and more sparsely populated, while the southern regions are more fertile and densely populated. Regional Differences

  • The northern regions are generally drier and more sparsely populated, with a stronger influence from neighboring countries such as Chad and Sudan.
  • The southern regions are more fertile and densely populated, with a stronger focus on agricultural production and cultural festivals.
  • The western regions are known for their rich natural resources, including gold and diamonds, and are home to many mining communities.
  • The eastern regions are more remote and sparsely populated, with a stronger focus on traditional crafts and local culture.

Getting Around

The Central African Republic has a limited transportation network, with few paved roads and limited access to domestic flights. The country's main airport is located in Bangui, and offers flights to several regional destinations, including Brazzaville, Douala, and Kinshasa. There are also several bus companies that operate throughout the country, offering transportation to major cities and towns. However, the road conditions are often poor, and travel can be slow and uncomfortable. For visitors who prefer to drive, car rental companies are available in Bangui, but driving conditions can be challenging, especially during the rainy season.
