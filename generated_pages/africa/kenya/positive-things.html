Cultural Treasures

  • The Fort Jesus historic site in Mombasa is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a testament to Kenya's rich cultural and architectural heritage.
  • The Maasai people are known for their vibrant traditional clothing and jewelry, which is an important part of Kenyan cultural identity.
  • The Lamu Island is a cultural treasure trove, with its narrow streets, historic buildings, and traditional Swahili architecture.
  • The Bomas of Kenya cultural center in Nairobi showcases the traditional music, dance, and crafts of Kenya's different ethnic groups.
  • The Kisumu Museum is a repository of Kenyan history and culture, with a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the country's past.

People & Global Impact

  • Wangari Maathai, a Kenyan environmentalist and women's rights activist, was the first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize in 2004.
  • The Kenyan diaspora has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Kenyans living and working abroad sending remittances back home to support their families and communities.
  • Barack Obama, the 44th President of the United States, has Kenyan roots, with his father Barack Obama Sr. hailing from the village of Kogelo in western Kenya.
  • Dr. Patrick Njiru, a Kenyan-born doctor, has made significant contributions to the field of medicine, including the development of a vaccine for the River Blindness disease.

What Visitors Love

  • The warm hospitality of the Kenyan people, who are known for their welcoming nature and friendly smiles.
  • The delicious cuisine, which includes popular dishes like ugali, sukuma wiki, and nyama choma.
  • The stunning natural scenery, from the snow-capped peaks of Mount Kenya to the sun-kissed beaches of the Indian Ocean.
  • The exciting wildlife experiences, including safaris and game drives in Kenya's many national parks and reserves.
  • The vibrant culture, which is a unique blend of traditional and modern influences, with a thriving music and arts scene.
  • The modern infrastructure, including the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport and the Nairobi-Mombasa highway, which make it easy to get around the country.

Hidden Gems

  • The Lake Turkana region, which is home to a unique and fragile ecosystem, with a variety of wildlife and traditional communities.
  • The Kakamega Forest, which is a lush and vibrant ecosystem, with a wide variety of plant and animal species.
  • The Hell's Gate National Park, which is a scenic and picturesque park, with stunning rock formations and a variety of wildlife.
  • The Rusinga Island, which is a peaceful and serene destination, with beautiful beaches and a rich cultural heritage.
