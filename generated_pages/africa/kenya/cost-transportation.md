[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
In Kenya, the main modes of transportation include **matatus** (minibuses), city buses, and taxis. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with Nairobi having a more comprehensive network compared to other cities. Most people in Kenya get around using matatus or city buses, which are affordable and widely available. However, the roads in Kenya can be congested, especially in major cities like Nairobi and Mombasa, which can lead to increased travel times.

The transportation network in Kenya is also influenced by the country's geography, with many rural areas being inaccessible by public transportation. In these areas, people often rely on **boda bodas** (motorcycle taxis) or walking to get around. The government has been investing in improving the transportation infrastructure, including the construction of new roads and the expansion of public transportation systems.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using matatus or city buses instead of taxis or ride-hailing apps.
- Use reputable ride-hailing apps like Uber or Bolt to ensure safe and reliable transportation.
- Always negotiate the price with taxi drivers before starting your journey to avoid overcharging.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded public transportation.
- Consider renting a car for long-distance travel or intercity trips to have more flexibility and comfort.
- Download transportation apps like **Mondo Ride** or **Little Ride** to book rides and track your journey in real-time.
