Why People Move to Kenya

Kenya is a popular destination for expats, with its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and growing economy attracting people from all over the world. Many expats are drawn to Kenya's vibrant cities, such as Nairobi and Mombasa, which offer a unique blend of traditional and modern amenities. The country's expat demographics are diverse, with a mix of professionals, entrepreneurs, retirees, and students, and the quality of life in Kenya is generally considered to be high, with a warm climate, beautiful landscapes, and a wide range of outdoor activities.

One of the main reasons people move to Kenya is for work opportunities, with many international companies and organizations having a presence in the country. Others are attracted to Kenya's tourism industry, with its stunning national parks, beautiful beaches, and vibrant culture. The country is also a popular destination for digital nomads, with its relatively low cost of living and good internet connectivity. Overall, Kenya offers a unique and exciting experience for expats, with its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and growing economy.

Kenya's quality of life is generally considered to be high, with a wide range of amenities and services available, including good healthcare, education, and shopping. The country has a tropical climate, with warm temperatures and high humidity throughout the year, and the cost of living is relatively low compared to other countries in the region. However, as with any country, there are also some challenges to living in Kenya, including traffic congestion, bureaucracy, and security concerns.

Relocation Checklist

  1. Research visa options and requirements
  2. Gather necessary documents, including passport, birth certificate, and qualifications
  3. Apply for a visa and wait for approval
  4. Book flights and arrange accommodation
  5. Register with the Kenyan authorities and obtain a residence permit
  6. Open a bank account and transfer funds
  7. Find a job or start a business
  8. Arrange for healthcare and health insurance
  9. Learn about Kenyan culture and customs
  10. Join expat communities and social networks
  11. Find a school or university for children
  12. Plan for taxes and financial obligations

Expat Communities

  • Nairobi Expats is a popular online group for expats living in Nairobi
  • The Kenya Expats Facebook group is a great place to connect with other expats and ask questions
  • The International Association of Nairobi offers social events and activities for expats
  • The Mombasa Expats group is a good resource for expats living in Mombasa
  • The Kenya Expat Forum is a online forum where expats can ask questions and share advice

Banking & Finances

  • Opening a bank account in Kenya is relatively straightforward, with many banks offering services for expats
  • Money transfers can be made through banks or online services such as PayPal or TransferWise
  • Currency considerations include the Kenyan shilling (KES), which is the local currency, and US dollars, which are widely accepted
  • Taxes in Kenya include income tax, which is payable on earnings above a certain threshold
  • Financial planning is important for expats, with considerations including savings, investments, and retirement planning

Challenges & Honest Advice

  • Culture shock can be a challenge for expats, with Kenya's vibrant culture and customs often being very different from those in Western countries
  • Bureaucracy can be frustrating, with many processes and procedures being slow and inefficient
  • Language barriers can be a challenge, with Swahili and English being the main languages spoken in Kenya
  • Security concerns are a reality in Kenya, with petty crime and terrorism being a risk in some areas
  • Traffic congestion is a major problem in Nairobi and other cities, with roads often being congested and chaotic
  • Healthcare can be a challenge, with some medical facilities and services being of a lower standard than those in Western countries
