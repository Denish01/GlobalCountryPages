Location & Borders

Kenya is a country located in East Africa, bordering the Indian Ocean to the southeast. It is situated between the equator and the Tropic of Capricorn, with its terrain varying from coastal plains to mountainous highlands. Kenya shares borders with five countries: Ethiopia to the north, Somalia to the east, Tanzania to the south, Uganda to the west, and South Sudan to the northwest. The country's strategic location allows it to serve as a gateway to the African continent, with the Port of Mombasa being a major shipping hub.

Kenya's geography is also characterized by its proximity to several bodies of water, including Lake Victoria to the west and Lake Turkana to the north. The country's coastline along the Indian Ocean provides a scenic and economically important region, with popular tourist destinations like Mombasa and Malindi. The capital city, Nairobi, is located in the central highlands, about 100 km from the eastern edge of the Rift Valley. Kenya's diverse geography and climate support a wide range of ecosystems, from tropical forests to arid deserts, and from snow-capped mountains to coral reefs.

Terrain & Landscape

Kenya's terrain is characterized by a diverse range of landscapes, including mountains, rivers, deserts, and forests. The country can be divided into several distinct regions, each with its unique geography and climate. The Rift Valley, which runs from north to south, is a prominent geological feature that has shaped the country's landscape. The valley is flanked by mountain ranges, including the Aberdare Mountains and the Mount Kenya massif, which is the highest peak in the country and the second-highest in Africa.

The coastal region is characterized by a narrow strip of low-lying plains, with the Nyika Plateau forming a scenic and fertile area. The Savannah grasslands, which cover much of the country, are home to a wide range of wildlife, including the famous Maasai Mara National Reserve. Kenya's deserts, including the Chalbi Desert, cover a significant portion of the country, particularly in the north and east. The country's forests, such as the Arabuko-Sokoke Forest, are important habitats for a wide range of flora and fauna, and play a crucial role in maintaining the country's biodiversity.

The country's rivers, including the Tana River and the Ewaso Nyiro River, support agriculture, industry, and urban centers, and provide water for irrigation and drinking. Kenya's lakes, including Lake Victoria and Lake Turkana, are important sources of fish and other aquatic resources. The country's diverse terrain and landscape support a wide range of ecosystems, from tropical forests to arid deserts, and from snow-capped mountains to coral reefs.

Best Time To Visit

Best months: June to September. Why: The dry season offers ideal weather for safaris and outdoor activities, with clear skies and mild temperatures. Avoid: March to May, when the long rains can make roads impassable and disrupt outdoor activities.

Natural Highlights

  • Maasai Mara National Reserve: a world-famous wildlife reserve known for its abundant lion, leopard, and cheetah populations.
  • Mount Kenya National Park: a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the highest peak in the country, with glaciers, lakes, and diverse wildlife.
  • Lake Nakuru National Park: a protected area known for its vibrant flamingo populations and diverse birdlife.
  • Tsavo National Park: a large and diverse park, home to elephants, lions, and other wildlife, with unique geological features like the Yatta Plateau.
  • Amboseli National Park: a scenic park located at the foot of Mount Kilimanjaro, with abundant wildlife and stunning scenery.
  • Hell's Gate National Park: a unique park featuring geothermal activity, with hot springs, geysers, and a diverse range of flora and fauna.
