[SECTION] Overview: Chad vs Nigeria [/SECTION]
Chad and Nigeria are two neighboring countries in West Africa with distinct economic profiles. Chad has a largely agricultural economy, while Nigeria has a more diversified economy with a strong oil and gas sector. In terms of cost of living, Chad is generally cheaper than Nigeria, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. The overall cost of living in Chad is around 15-20% lower than in Nigeria, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees on a budget. The economic context of both countries is also different, with Chad having a smaller economy and lower GDP per capita than Nigeria.

The cost of living difference between Chad and Nigeria can be attributed to several factors, including the cost of housing, food, and transportation. In Chad, the cost of living is lower due to the availability of affordable housing options, lower food prices, and a relatively low cost of transportation. In contrast, Nigeria has a higher cost of living due to the high demand for housing, particularly in urban areas, and the relatively high cost of food and transportation. Overall, Chad offers a more affordable lifestyle, making it an attractive option for those looking to live in West Africa without breaking the bank.
