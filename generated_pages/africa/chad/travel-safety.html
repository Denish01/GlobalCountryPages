Overall Safety Assessment

Chad is a challenging destination for travelers, with a range of safety concerns that require careful consideration. The country has experienced periods of instability, including rebel activity and banditry, particularly in the northern and eastern regions. While the capital city, N'Djamena, is generally considered safer than other parts of the country, petty crime and scams are common. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and markets, and take necessary precautions to secure their belongings.

Despite the safety concerns, many travelers visit Chad without incident, and the country has a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty to offer. With proper planning, research, and caution, visitors can minimize their risk and have a rewarding experience in Chad. It's essential to stay informed about local conditions, follow local advice, and be prepared for unexpected events. The Chadian government has taken steps to improve security, including the deployment of security forces in key areas, but the situation remains volatile.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and theft are common in crowded areas and markets
  • Scams targeting tourists, including fake police and officials, are frequent
  • Banditry and carjacking are risks, particularly in rural areas
  • Rebel activity is a concern in northern and eastern regions
  • Protests and demonstrations can turn violent, avoid areas of unrest
  • Traffic accidents are common due to poor road conditions and reckless driving
  • Petty crime is prevalent, including burglary and theft
  • Corruption is widespread, be prepared for requests for bribes

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid are recommended
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Limited and of variable quality, medical evacuation may be necessary
  • Pharmacy Access: Limited, it's essential to bring a personal supply of medications

Practical Safety Tips

  • Research and stay informed about local conditions and events
  • Avoid traveling at night, especially in rural areas
  • Use reputable and licensed taxis or transportation services
  • Keep valuables secure and be mindful of your surroundings
  • Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as jewelry or expensive watches
  • Learn some basic French and Arabic phrases to communicate with locals
  • Stay in touch with your government's travel advisory department

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be prepared for conservative attitudes and dress modestly
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas
  • Use reputable and licensed transportation services, and avoid hitchhiking
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and keep valuables secure, as petty crime can be a problem
  • Consider hiring a guide or joining a group tour to minimize risks and gain local insight
