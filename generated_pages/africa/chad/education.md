# Chad Education — Schools, Universities & Student Guide

The education system in Chad is characterized by a mix of public and private institutions, with a strong emphasis on primary education, although the country still faces significant challenges in terms of access, quality, and funding.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Chad is structured into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education, which is compulsory, lasts for six years and is followed by three years of lower secondary education. Upper secondary education, which is not compulsory, lasts for two years and leads to a baccalaureate diploma. The quality of education in Chad varies significantly, with public schools often facing challenges such as overcrowding, lack of resources, and poorly trained teachers. Private schools, on the other hand, tend to offer better facilities and more qualified teachers, but are often expensive and inaccessible to many Chadians.

The public education system in Chad is free, but many families still struggle to afford the costs of uniforms, books, and other supplies. As a result, many children, particularly in rural areas, do not attend school or drop out at an early age. The government has been working to improve the education system, including increasing access to primary education and improving teacher training. However, significant challenges remain, including a shortage of qualified teachers, inadequate infrastructure, and a lack of resources.

Despite these challenges, there are some positive trends in the education system in Chad. For example, the country has made significant progress in increasing access to primary education, particularly for girls. Additionally, the government has introduced initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curricula and teacher training programs.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- International schools in Chad are limited, but some options are available in the capital city of N'Djamena, including the International Community School and the American International School.
- These schools offer a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate (IB), British, and American programs.
- Tuition fees for international schools in Chad can be expensive, ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per year.
- Some international schools also offer boarding facilities for students from outside N'Djamena.
- The availability of international schools in other parts of the country is limited, and many expatriate families choose to send their children to school in other countries, such as France or the United Kingdom.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Chad, foreign students must obtain a student visa, which can be obtained through the Chadian embassy or consulate in their home country.
- Foreign students must also demonstrate proficiency in French, which is the language of instruction in most Chadian universities.
- Some universities in Chad offer scholarships to international students, particularly those from other African countries.
- Living costs for students in Chad can be relatively low, with accommodation options available for $100-300 per month.
- Foreign students must also register with the local authorities and obtain a residence permit.
- Additionally, foreign students may need to take a language proficiency test, such as the Test de Français International (TFI), to demonstrate their language skills.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the major challenges facing the education system in Chad is the lack of qualified teachers, particularly in rural areas.
- The country also faces significant infrastructure challenges, including a lack of classrooms, desks, and other basic equipment.
- Despite these challenges, the education system in Chad has made significant progress in recent years, including an increase in access to primary education and improvements in teacher training.
- The government has also introduced initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curricula and teacher training programs.
- However, the education system in Chad still faces significant challenges, including a lack of funding, inadequate infrastructure, and a shortage of qualified teachers.
