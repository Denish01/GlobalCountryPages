Location & Borders

Rwanda is a sovereign country located in the eastern part of the African continent. It is situated in the African Great Lakes region, and its borders are shared with four countries: Uganda to the north, Tanzania to the east, Burundi to the south, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo to the west. Rwanda is a landlocked country, meaning it does not have any direct access to the ocean. However, it is surrounded by several lakes, including Lake Kivu, which is the largest lake in the country.

Rwanda's strategic location in the heart of Africa makes it an important hub for trade and commerce. The country's borders are relatively small, with a total length of approximately 893 kilometers. Despite its small size, Rwanda has a diverse geography, with a range of landscapes and climates that support a wide variety of flora and fauna. The country's location near the equator also means that it has a relatively consistent climate throughout the year, with two main seasons: a dry season and a wet season.

Terrain & Landscape

Rwanda's terrain is characterized by a mix of mountains, valleys, and plains. The country is often referred to as the "Land of a Thousand Hills" due to its rugged terrain, which is dominated by the Albertine Rift mountain range. The highest point in Rwanda is Mount Karisimbi, which is located in the Virunga Mountains and stands at an elevation of 4,507 meters. The Virunga Mountains are also home to several other notable peaks, including Mount Bisoke and Mount Muhabura.

In addition to its mountainous terrain, Rwanda is also home to several rivers and lakes. The country's longest river is the Kagera River, which forms part of the border with Tanzania. Rwanda is also home to several national parks, including the Volcanoes National Park, which is located in the Virunga Mountains and is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including mountain gorillas. The country's diverse landscape and climate support a wide range of flora and fauna, making it a popular destination for ecotourism.

Rwanda's terrain and landscape have also played a significant role in shaping the country's history and culture. The country's rugged terrain made it difficult for colonial powers to penetrate, which allowed Rwanda to maintain its independence for longer than many other African countries. Today, Rwanda's unique landscape and culture make it a popular destination for tourists and travelers.

Natural Highlights

  • Volcanoes National Park: a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to mountain gorillas, golden monkeys, and other wildlife.
  • Lake Kivu: a large lake that forms part of the border with the Democratic Republic of the Congo and offers stunning scenery and water sports.
  • Nyungwe Forest National Park: a dense forest that is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including chimpanzees and other primates.
  • Akagera National Park: a savannah park that is home to a wide range of wildlife, including lions, leopards, and elephants.
  • Mount Karisimbi: the highest peak in Rwanda, which offers stunning views and hiking opportunities.
  • Gishwati-Mukura National Park: a new national park that is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including chimpanzees and other primates.
