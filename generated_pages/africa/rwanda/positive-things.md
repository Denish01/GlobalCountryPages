[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Kigali Genocide Memorial Centre** is a poignant reminder of the country's history and a tribute to the victims of the 1994 genocide.
- The **Intore Cultural Dance Troupe** is a renowned group that showcases traditional Rwandan dance and music.
- The **Rwanda National Museum** in Butare is a treasure trove of Rwandan history and culture, with a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the country's past.
- The **Nyamata Church** is a historic site and a place of worship that commemorates the victims of the genocide.
- The **Imigongo** traditional art form is a unique and colorful expression of Rwandan culture, characterized by intricate designs and patterns.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Paul Kagame**, the current President of Rwanda, has been recognized for his efforts in promoting economic development and stability in the country.
- The **Rwandan diaspora** has made significant contributions to the country's development, with many individuals returning to Rwanda to start businesses and invest in the economy.
- **Diane Rwigara**, a Rwandan women's rights activist, has been a vocal advocate for women's empowerment and gender equality in the country.
- The **Rwandan values** of **Ubuntu** (community) and **Agaciro** (self-pride) are core principles that guide the country's social and cultural fabric.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **warm hospitality** of the Rwandan people is consistently praised by visitors, who are often greeted with open arms and a warm welcome.
- The **delicious food**, including dishes like **umutsima** (a dish made from cassava leaves and peas) and **brochettes** (skewers of marinated meat), is a highlight of any visit to Rwanda.
- The **stunning scenery**, including the **Volcanoes National Park** and the **Lake Kivu** shoreline, is a major draw for tourists and adventure seekers.
- The **vibrant markets**, such as the **Kimisagara Market** in Kigali, offer a colorful and lively glimpse into Rwandan culture and daily life.
- The **guided tours**, including **gorilla trekking** and **birdwatching**, provide a unique and unforgettable experience for visitors.
- The **traditional crafts**, such as **basket weaving** and **woodcarving**, are a great way to learn about Rwandan culture and take home a unique souvenir.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Muhanga Caves** are a lesser-known natural wonder, featuring a series of limestone caves and rock formations that are perfect for hiking and exploration.
- The **Rulindo District** is a rural area that is often overlooked by tourists, but offers a glimpse into traditional Rwandan life and stunning natural scenery.
- The **Kivu Belt** is a region that is home to a number of **coffee plantations**, which offer a unique opportunity to learn about Rwanda's coffee industry and sample some of the country's famous coffee.
- The **Rusumo Falls** are a beautiful and tranquil spot, located on the border with Tanzania, which offer a chance to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
