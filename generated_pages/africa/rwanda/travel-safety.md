[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Rwanda is generally a safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other African countries. The government has made significant efforts to improve security and stability, making it an attractive destination for tourists. However, as with any travel, it's essential to take normal precautions to ensure your safety. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night. Additionally, Rwanda has a complex history, and visitors should be respectful of the local culture and traditions.

Rwanda has made significant progress in rebuilding and recovering from its past conflicts, and the country is now considered one of the most stable and secure in the region. The capital city, Kigali, is particularly safe, with a strong police presence and good street lighting. However, as with any city, petty theft and scams can occur, and visitors should take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Overall, Rwanda is a great destination for travelers who want to experience African culture, nature, and hospitality in a relatively safe and secure environment.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **scams** targeting tourists, especially in crowded areas and markets.
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in tourist areas and public transportation.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use **credit cards** or **traveler's checks** instead.
- Be cautious of **overcharging** by taxi drivers and vendors.
- Avoid **drinking tap water** and opt for bottled or filtered water instead.
- Be aware of **wild animals** when trekking or hiking in national parks.
- Be respectful of **local customs** and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid vaccinations are recommended.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are limited, especially outside of Kigali, and visitors may need to be evacuated to neighboring countries for serious medical conditions.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacy access is limited, and visitors may need to bring their own medications.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night and in crowded areas.
- Use **reputable taxi services** or **ride-hailing apps** instead of flagging down taxis on the street.
- Keep **valuable items** secure and consider using a **hotel safe** or **lockbox**.
- Avoid **traveling alone** at night and use **group tours** or **guided tours** instead.
- Be **respectful of local customs** and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas.
- Keep **important phone numbers** handy, such as the emergency number and tourist police.
- Consider **registering** with your government's travel advisory program to receive important safety and security updates.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be **cautious of catcalling** and **harassment**, especially in crowded areas.
- Use **reputable taxi services** or **ride-hailing apps** instead of flagging down taxis on the street.
- Avoid **traveling alone** at night and use **group tours** or **guided tours** instead.
- Consider **staying in **women-only** accommodations or **hostels** for added safety and security.
