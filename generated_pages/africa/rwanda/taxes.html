Tax System Overview

Rwanda has a territorial taxation system, where only income earned within the country is subject to tax. However, residents are taxed on their worldwide income, with foreign-earned income subject to tax in Rwanda. To be considered a tax resident in Rwanda, an individual must have a permanent home in the country, or have been present in the country for at least 183 days in a calendar year. The tax system in Rwanda is administered by the Rwanda Revenue Authority (RRA), which is responsible for collecting taxes, customs, and other levies.

The tax system in Rwanda is designed to promote economic growth and development, while also ensuring that individuals and businesses contribute to the country's revenue. The government has implemented various tax incentives and exemptions to encourage investment and entrepreneurship, particularly in key sectors such as agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism. For example, the government offers a tax holiday of up to 7 years for investors in certain sectors, and a reduced tax rate of 15% for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).

Corporate & Business Tax

  • The corporate tax rate in Rwanda is 30%, with a reduced rate of 15% for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) with an annual turnover of less than RWF 50 million.
  • Small businesses with an annual turnover of less than RWF 10 million are exempt from corporate tax, but are required to pay a presumptive tax of 3% of their turnover.
  • Companies operating in free zones are exempt from corporate tax for a period of up to 10 years, and are also exempt from VAT and customs duties.
  • Businesses are required to register with the RRA and obtain a taxpayer identification number (TIN) in order to operate in Rwanda.
  • Companies are also required to file their tax returns by the end of June each year, and are subject to audits by the RRA to ensure compliance with tax laws.

VAT / Sales Tax

  • The standard VAT rate in Rwanda is 18%, with a reduced rate of 0% for basic necessities such as food, medicine, and education services.
  • Exemptions from VAT include financial services, insurance services, and rental income.
  • A tourist refund scheme is available for foreign tourists who purchase goods and services in Rwanda, allowing them to claim a refund of VAT paid on their purchases.
  • Businesses are required to register for VAT if their annual turnover exceeds RWF 50 million, and are required to file their VAT returns on a quarterly basis.

For Expats & Foreign Workers

  • Expatriates who are resident in Rwanda for more than 183 days in a calendar year are considered tax residents and are subject to tax on their worldwide income.
  • Rwanda has double taxation treaties with 16 countries, including the United Kingdom, the United States, and South Africa, to avoid double taxation of income.
  • Expatriates are required to obtain a work permit and register with the RRA in order to work in Rwanda.
  • Social security contributions are mandatory for all employees in Rwanda, including expatriates, with a contribution rate of 10% of gross salary.
  • Expatriates are allowed to remit their earnings abroad, subject to certain restrictions and requirements.
  • Expatriates may also be eligible for a tax credit in their home country for taxes paid in Rwanda, depending on the terms of the double taxation treaty.

Crypto & Investment Income

  • Investment income, including dividends, interest, and rental income, is subject to tax in Rwanda, with a withholding tax rate of 15% for non-residents.
  • Cryptocurrency transactions are subject to tax in Rwanda, with gains from the sale of cryptocurrency considered capital gains and subject to tax at a rate of 30%.
  • Stock exchange transactions are subject to a transaction tax of 0.2% of the transaction value.
  • Real estate investments are subject to a property tax of 0.5% of the property value, as well as a capital gains tax of 30% on the sale of property.
