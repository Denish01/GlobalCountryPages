[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Basilique de Notre Dame de la Paix de Yamoussoukro** is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest churches in the world.
- The **Festival des Masques** is a traditional festival where masks are worn to represent ancestral spirits and to communicate with the dead.
- The **Djembe** is a traditional drum that originated in Ivory Coast and is an important part of Ivorian music and culture.
- The **Baule people** are known for their skilled goldwork and intricate wood carvings.
- The **Grand Mosquée du Plateau** is a beautiful mosque located in the heart of Abidjan, showcasing Islamic architecture and design.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Djedje Mady** is a renowned Ivorian musician who has won numerous awards for his music, including the prestigious **Kora Award**.
- **Youssouf Bakayoko** is a former Ivorian football player who played for several European clubs, including Paris Saint-Germain and Olympique de Marseille.
- The **Ivorian diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Ivorians working in fields such as finance, medicine, and technology.
- **Aicha Koné** is a prominent Ivorian businesswoman who has founded several companies, including a successful textile manufacturing firm.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **warm hospitality** of the Ivorian people, who are known for their welcoming nature and friendly smiles.
- The **delicious cuisine**, which includes popular dishes such as **fufu** and **groundnut stew**.
- The **beautiful beaches** along the Atlantic coast, particularly in the town of **Assinie**.
- The **vibrant markets**, such as the **Grand Marché** in Abidjan, where visitors can find a wide range of goods, including traditional clothing and handicrafts.
- The **stunning scenery**, including the **Man Mountains** and the **Sassandra River**.
- The **exciting nightlife**, with numerous bars, clubs, and live music venues to choose from, particularly in the city of **Abidjan**.
