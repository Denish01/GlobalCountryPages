Visa Policy Overview

Ivory Coast has a moderate visa policy, requiring citizens from most countries to obtain a visa before entering the country. However, citizens from a few countries are eligible for visa-free access or can obtain a visa on arrival. The country's visa policy is designed to promote tourism and business while ensuring national security. Currently, citizens from about 20 countries, including some African and European nations, can enter Ivory Coast without a visa.

The Ivory Coast government has implemented an eVisa system to facilitate the visa application process for eligible citizens. This system allows applicants to submit their visa applications online and receive their visas via email. The eVisa is typically processed within 48 hours, and it is valid for a single entry into the country. Visitors who plan to stay in Ivory Coast for an extended period or for purposes other than tourism or business may need to apply for a different type of visa.

Tourists: Tourists can apply for an eVisa or a tourist visa, which is typically valid for 90 days. The maximum stay is 90 days, and extensions are possible but require approval from the immigration authorities. Tourists must provide proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and health insurance.
Business Travelers: Business travelers require an invitation letter from a registered company in Ivory Coast and must apply for a business visa. The business visa is typically valid for 90 days, and the applicant must provide proof of business activities and sufficient funds.
Students: Students must apply for a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a recognized educational institution in Ivory Coast. The student visa is typically valid for the duration of the study program, and the applicant must provide proof of sufficient funds and health insurance.
Workers: Workers must apply for a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a registered company in Ivory Coast. The work visa is typically valid for the duration of the employment contract, and the applicant must provide proof of qualifications, experience, and sufficient funds.

Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration desk, where they will be required to present their travel documents, including their passport, visa (if required), and proof of onward travel. Visitors may also be asked to provide proof of sufficient funds and health insurance. Land borders are also available, and visitors can enter Ivory Coast by road from neighboring countries such as Ghana, Liberia, and Guinea.
At the immigration desk, visitors will be required to complete an arrival/departure card, which will be stamped by the immigration officer. The card must be retained and presented upon departure from the country. Visitors who arrive without the required documents or with incomplete documentation may be denied entry or required to pay a fine.
- Failing to apply for a visa before arrival, if required
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not providing proof of onward travel
- Not having sufficient funds (at least 100,000 XOF)
- Not having health insurance
- Not completing the arrival/departure card correctly
- Not retaining the arrival/departure card for presentation upon departure
