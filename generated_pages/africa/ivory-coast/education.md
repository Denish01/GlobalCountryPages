# Ivory Coast Education — Schools, Universities & Student Guide

The education system in Ivory Coast is a complex and multifaceted network that provides students with a range of opportunities, from primary education to higher learning, with a strong emphasis on **French language instruction** and a growing focus on **internationalization**.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Ivory Coast is divided into three main levels: **primary**, **secondary**, and **tertiary**. Primary education, which is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 11, is provided by **public schools** and **private schools**, with a focus on basic subjects such as mathematics, reading, and writing. Secondary education, which is also compulsory, is provided by **lycées** and **collèges**, and offers a range of subjects, including science, technology, and languages. Tertiary education is provided by **universities** and **institutes**, which offer a range of programs, including bachelor's and master's degrees.

The quality of education in Ivory Coast varies, with **public schools** often struggling with limited resources and **private schools** offering higher-quality education. However, the government has been working to improve the education system, with a focus on increasing access to education, particularly for girls and rural communities. Despite these efforts, the education system in Ivory Coast still faces a number of challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers and inadequate infrastructure.

The education system in Ivory Coast is overseen by the **Ministry of National Education and Technical Education**, which is responsible for setting education policies and standards. The ministry has implemented a number of reforms in recent years, including the introduction of a new curriculum and the establishment of a national examination system. These reforms aim to improve the quality of education and increase access to education, particularly for disadvantaged groups.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- International schools are available in major cities, including Abidjan and Yamoussoukro, and offer a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate (IB)**, **British**, and **American**.
- The **International Community School** in Abidjan offers an IB curriculum and has a diverse student body from over 50 countries.
- The **Lycée Français d'Abidjan** offers a French curriculum and is accredited by the French Ministry of Education.
- International schools in Ivory Coast can be expensive, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per year.
- Many international schools offer **extracurricular activities**, including sports, music, and art programs.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Ivory Coast, foreign students must obtain a **student visa**, which can be obtained through the Ivorian embassy or consulate in their home country.
- Foreign students must also demonstrate **proficiency in French**, which is the language of instruction in most Ivorian universities.
- **Scholarships** are available for foreign students, including those offered by the Ivorian government and international organizations.
- Living costs for students in Ivory Coast are relatively low, with a monthly budget of around $200-500 covering accommodation, food, and other expenses.
- Foreign students must also register with the **Ivorian authorities** and obtain a **residence permit**.
- Health insurance is also required for foreign students, and can be obtained through the **Ivorian social security system**.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- The education system in Ivory Coast faces a number of challenges, including a shortage of **qualified teachers** and inadequate **infrastructure**.
- Despite these challenges, the education system in Ivory Coast has made significant progress in recent years, with an increase in **enrollment rates** and **literacy rates**.
- The government has implemented a number of reforms to improve the education system, including the introduction of a new **curriculum** and the establishment of a **national examination system**.
- The education system in Ivory Coast also faces challenges related to **equity and inclusion**, with disadvantaged groups, including girls and rural communities, often struggling to access education.
- The government has also implemented initiatives to promote **vocational training** and **technical education**, which are seen as key to addressing the country's **youth unemployment** challenge.
