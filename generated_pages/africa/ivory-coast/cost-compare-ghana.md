[SECTION] Overview: Ivory Coast vs Ghana [/SECTION]
Ivory Coast and Ghana are two neighboring countries in West Africa with distinct economic profiles. Ivory Coast has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, mining, and manufacturing, while Ghana's economy is primarily driven by agriculture and natural resources. In terms of cost of living, Ivory Coast tends to be more affordable than Ghana, particularly when it comes to housing, food, and transportation. The overall cost of living in Ivory Coast is around 15-20% lower than in Ghana, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a affordable lifestyle.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Ivory Coast has a more stable economy, with a lower inflation rate and a stronger currency, which helps to keep prices stable. In contrast, Ghana's economy has experienced higher inflation rates in recent years, which has driven up the cost of living. Additionally, the cost of housing, food, and transportation in Ghana is generally higher than in Ivory Coast, making it a more expensive option for those looking to live and work in the region.
