# Rent & Housing Prices in Ivory Coast

The cost of rent in Ivory Coast can vary depending on the location, with average monthly rents for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center ranging from $200-$500 USD (approximately 120,000-300,000 XOF) and $100-$300 USD (approximately 60,000-180,000 XOF) outside the city center.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
Ivory Coast's housing market is characterized by a mix of modern and traditional housing units, with a growing demand for affordable housing. The rental market is relatively competitive, with tenants having a range of options to choose from, including apartments, houses, and shared accommodations. Typical lease terms in Ivory Coast range from 6-12 months, with a deposit requirement of 1-2 months' rent. Tenant rights are protected by law, with the government implementing measures to regulate the rental market and prevent exploitation. Compared to regional neighbors, Ivory Coast's housing costs are relatively moderate, with prices lower than in Ghana but higher than in neighboring Liberia.

The housing market in Ivory Coast is also influenced by the country's economic growth, with the GDP per capita (PPP) of $7,669 indicating a relatively stable economy. However, the **inflation rate** of 3.5% and **out-of-pocket health spend** of 32.0% can impact the affordability of housing. The government has implemented measures to increase access to affordable housing, including the development of new housing projects and the provision of subsidies for low-income households.
