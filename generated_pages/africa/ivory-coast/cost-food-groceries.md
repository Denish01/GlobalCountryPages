[SECTION] Food Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Food prices in Ivory Coast are generally lower compared to the regional average, with the country's GDP per capita PPP of $7,669 and an inflation rate of 3.5% contributing to a relatively stable food market. The cost of food is driven by factors such as transportation, storage, and import costs, with **local produce** and **staple foods** like rice, beans, and corn being more affordable than imported goods. Eating out is a popular culture in Ivory Coast, particularly in urban areas, with many restaurants and street food stalls offering a wide range of **traditional dishes** at affordable prices. However, home cooking is also common, with many people preferring to buy ingredients from local markets and prepare meals at home.

The food prices in Ivory Coast can fluctuate depending on the season, with **fresh produce** being more expensive during the dry season. Additionally, the cost of food can vary depending on the location, with cities like Abidjan and Yamoussoukro tend to have higher prices than smaller towns and rural areas. Overall, Ivory Coast offers a wide range of affordable food options, making it a great destination for travelers and locals alike.

[SECTION] Supermarket vs Local Market [/SECTION]
The prices of groceries can vary significantly between supermarkets and local markets in Ivory Coast. Generally, supermarkets tend to have higher prices than local markets, particularly for **imported goods**. However, supermarkets often offer a wider range of products, including **fresh produce**, **meat**, and **dairy products**. Local markets, on the other hand, offer a wide range of **fresh produce**, **grains**, and **staple foods** at lower prices. It's recommended to buy **fresh produce** and **staple foods** from local markets, while **imported goods** and **specialty items** may be found at supermarkets.

Bargaining is a common practice at local markets in Ivory Coast, particularly for **fresh produce** and **grains**. Vendors often expect customers to negotiate prices, and it's not uncommon to get a discount of 10-20% off the initial price. When shopping at local markets, it's a good idea to **learn some basic French phrases**, such as "combien ça coûte?" (how much does it cost?), and to **be respectful and polite** when negotiating prices.

[SECTION] Local Food Tips [/SECTION]
- Eating at **street food stalls** or **local eateries** is a great way to try **traditional dishes** at affordable prices.
- **Fufu** and **attiéké** are staple foods in Ivory Coast and are often served with **stews** or **sauces**.
- **Fresh produce** is widely available and affordable, with **mangos**, **pineapples**, and **bananas** being popular fruits.
- **Local cheese** and **yogurt** are good value and can be found at most markets.
- **Imported goods**, such as **apples** and **beef**, tend to be more expensive and may not be as fresh as local alternatives.
- **Bargaining** is a common practice at local markets, so be prepared to negotiate prices and **be respectful** of vendors.
