[SECTION] Overview: Ivory Coast vs Senegal [/SECTION]
Ivory Coast and Senegal are two West African countries with distinct economic profiles. Ivory Coast has a relatively high GDP per capita, driven by its strong agricultural sector, while Senegal's economy is more diversified, with a growing service sector. In terms of cost of living, Ivory Coast tends to be cheaper than Senegal, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. However, Senegal has a more developed tourist infrastructure, which can drive up prices for certain goods and services. Overall, Ivory Coast offers a more affordable lifestyle, with a cost of living that is around 15-20% lower than in Senegal.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in shaping their cost of living. Ivory Coast has a larger economy and a more developed industrial sector, which can lead to higher wages and a lower cost of living. Senegal, on the other hand, has a smaller economy and a more limited industrial sector, which can result in higher prices for certain goods and services. Additionally, the exchange rate between the two countries' currencies, the West African CFA franc (XOF), can also impact the cost of living. Currently, the exchange rate is relatively stable, but fluctuations can occur, affecting the cost of living in both countries.
