Overall Safety Assessment

Ivory Coast is a country with a complex safety situation. While it has made significant progress in recent years, the country still faces challenges related to crime, corruption, and social unrest. As a tourist, you should be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing, bag snatching, and scams are common in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, there have been reports of armed robberies and violent crimes in some parts of the country. However, with proper planning, awareness, and caution, you can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Ivory Coast.

It's essential to note that the country has a relatively high crime rate, particularly in urban areas like Abidjan and Yamoussoukro. However, most crimes are opportunistic, and tourists are not typically targeted. The government has taken steps to improve security, including increasing police presence in tourist areas and implementing measures to combat corruption. Despite these efforts, it's crucial to remain vigilant and take necessary precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of scams involving fake police officers or officials asking for bribes
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable ATMs and banks
  • Be aware of pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas
  • Avoid traveling alone at night and use reputable taxis or transportation services
  • Be cautious of armed robberies and violent crimes in some parts of the country
  • Be aware of traffic accidents and road conditions, particularly when traveling by car
  • Avoid eating or drinking from street vendors or unreliable sources
  • Be cautious of corrupt officials and bribery attempts

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid fever are recommended
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are limited, particularly in rural areas, and medical evacuation may be necessary in emergency situations
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are available in major cities, but access to medication may be limited in rural areas

Practical Safety Tips

  • Research your destination and stay informed about local conditions
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid traveling alone at night
  • Use reputable transportation services and avoid hitchhiking
  • Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe
  • Avoid displaying signs of wealth and keep cash and valuables secure
  • Learn some basic French phrases to help you communicate with locals
  • Respect local customs and traditions to avoid offending locals

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be cautious when traveling alone at night and use reputable transportation services
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas and stick to well-lit and populated areas
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, particularly when interacting with local men
  • Consider joining a group tour or traveling with a reliable guide to increase safety and security
