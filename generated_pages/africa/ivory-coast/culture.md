# Ivory Coast Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Ivory Coast, also known as Côte d'Ivoire, is a culturally rich and diverse country located in West Africa. The country's culture is defined by its **multi-ethnic** makeup, with over 60 ethnic groups, each with their own distinct traditions and customs. The key influences on Ivorian culture include **French colonialism**, **African traditions**, and **Islamic** and **Christian** practices. The official language is **French**, but many local languages, such as Baoulé and Dioula, are also widely spoken. The ethnic and linguistic diversity of Ivory Coast has created a unique cultural identity that is a blend of traditional and modern practices.

The country's cultural identity is also reflected in its **traditional clothing**, which is a mix of colorful prints and patterns. The **Baoulé people**, who are one of the largest ethnic groups, are known for their **intricate masks** and **wood carvings**. The **Dioula people**, on the other hand, are known for their **rich textile traditions**, including **hand-woven cotton** and **indigo-dyed** fabrics. The cultural diversity of Ivory Coast is also reflected in its **cuisine**, which is a blend of traditional African, French, and Islamic influences.

Ivory Coast's cultural identity is also shaped by its **history**, which has been marked by periods of **colonization**, **independence**, and **conflict**. The country's cultural heritage is reflected in its **museums**, **monuments**, and **historical sites**, which showcase the country's rich history and cultural diversity. The Ivorian people are known for their **hospitality** and **warmth**, and visitors to the country are often greeted with open arms and a welcoming smile.

The Ivorian people are also known for their **love of spices**, which are used to add flavor to a variety of dishes. Visitors to the country are often invited to share meals with locals, which is a great way to experience the country's **hospitality** and **cuisine**.

[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Greet people with a handshake or a bow, as a sign of respect
- Use your right hand when eating or giving or receiving something, as the left hand is considered unclean
- Remove your shoes before entering a mosque or a traditional home
- Dress modestly, especially when visiting rural areas or attending traditional ceremonies
- Learn a few words of French or a local language, such as "bonjour" (hello) or "merci" (thank you)
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially during festivals and celebrations

**Don't:**
- Point with your left hand, as this is considered rude
- Eat with your left hand, as this is considered unclean
- Publicly display affection, as this is considered taboo
- Discuss politics or sensitive topics, as this can be considered impolite
- Refuse an offer of food or drink, as this can be considered rude
- Take pictures of people without their permission, as this can be considered invasive

- **Wood carvings**: The country is famous for its intricate wood carvings, which are often used to decorate masks and other traditional objects.
- **Textile traditions**: Ivory Coast is known for its rich textile traditions, which include **hand-woven cotton** and **indigo-dyed** fabrics.
- **Literature**: The country has a thriving literary scene, which includes writers such as **Bernard Dadié** and **Ahmadou Kourouma**.
