# Doing Business in Ivory Coast — Investment, FDI & Opportunities

Doing business in Ivory Coast is relatively challenging due to bureaucratic hurdles and corruption, but the country offers significant investment opportunities in key sectors such as agriculture, infrastructure, and energy.

Currency Stability: Moderate

[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
The business environment in Ivory Coast is characterized by a mix of opportunities and challenges. The government has implemented various reforms to improve the business climate, including the creation of a one-stop shop for business registration and the simplification of tax procedures. However, corruption remains a significant issue, and bureaucratic hurdles can slow down business operations. The government's attitude towards foreign investment is generally positive, with a focus on promoting economic growth and development. Despite this, the country's business environment is still affected by a relatively high level of corruption and a complex regulatory framework.

The level of bureaucracy in Ivory Coast is relatively high, with many procedures requiring multiple steps and approvals. This can make it difficult for businesses to operate efficiently, especially for small and medium-sized enterprises. Additionally, the country's infrastructure, including transportation and energy systems, is still developing and can be a challenge for businesses. However, the government is investing heavily in infrastructure development, which is expected to improve the business environment in the long term.

Overall, the business environment in Ivory Coast presents both opportunities and challenges for investors. While the country offers significant potential for growth and development, businesses must be prepared to navigate a complex regulatory framework and overcome bureaucratic hurdles. With the right approach and support, however, Ivory Coast can be a rewarding and profitable destination for investors.

[SECTION] Foreign Direct Investment [/SECTION]
- Foreign direct investment (FDI) in Ivory Coast has been increasing in recent years, with a focus on the agriculture, infrastructure, and energy sectors.
- The main investing countries in Ivory Coast are France, China, and the Netherlands.
- The government offers various incentives to attract FDI, including tax breaks, investment subsidies, and streamlined registration procedures.
- Restricted sectors for FDI include defense, security, and certain areas of the energy sector.

[SECTION] Legal & Regulatory Framework [/SECTION]
- The business laws in Ivory Coast are based on the French civil law system, with a focus on protecting the rights of investors and promoting economic development.
- Intellectual property protection is provided through the Intellectual Property Law, which covers trademarks, patents, and copyrights.
- Labor laws in Ivory Coast are relatively strict, with a focus on protecting the rights of workers and promoting fair labor practices.
- Dispute resolution is typically done through the courts, although arbitration and mediation are also available.
- The government is working to improve the regulatory framework, including the creation of a one-stop shop for business registration and the simplification of tax procedures.

[SECTION] Challenges & Risks [/SECTION]
- Corruption is a significant challenge in Ivory Coast, with a high risk of bribery and extortion.
- The business environment is affected by a relatively high level of bureaucracy, with many procedures requiring multiple steps and approvals.
- Infrastructure challenges, including transportation and energy systems, can be a significant obstacle for businesses.
- Currency risks are moderate, with the West African CFA franc (XOF) pegged to the euro.
- Political risks are relatively low, with a stable government and a focus on promoting economic development.

[SECTION] Free Zones & Incentives [/SECTION]
- Ivory Coast has two special economic zones (SEZs), which offer tax breaks, investment subsidies, and streamlined registration procedures to investors.
- The government offers various incentives to attract investment, including tax breaks, investment subsidies, and low-interest loans.
- The Investment Promotion Agency (API) provides support to investors, including assistance with registration, licensing, and tax procedures.
- The government is working to improve the business environment, including the creation of a one-stop shop for business registration and the simplification of tax procedures.
