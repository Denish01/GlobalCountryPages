# Ivory Coast Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Ivory Coast is relatively well-developed, with a growing number of users and improving infrastructure, making it possible for tourists and remote workers to stay connected during their stay.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet quality in Ivory Coast is generally good, especially in urban areas such as Abidjan and Yamoussoukro, where **fiber optic** connections and **4G** networks are widely available. However, the urban-rural divide is still significant, with rural areas often experiencing slower speeds and less reliable connections. The internet infrastructure in Ivory Coast has been improving in recent years, with investments in **undersea cables** and **cellular networks**, but outages and slow speeds can still occur, especially during peak hours or in areas with high demand.

Despite these challenges, the internet has become an essential tool for many Ivorians, with a growing number of users accessing the internet for **education**, **entertainment**, and **business** purposes. The government has also launched initiatives to improve internet access and affordability, including the development of **community networks** and **public WiFi hotspots**. However, more needs to be done to address the urban-rural divide and ensure that all citizens have access to reliable and affordable internet services.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Ivory Coast, tourists can visit the offices of one of the main carriers, such as **MTN**, **Orange**, or **Moov**, and provide a valid **passport** and **proof of address**. The SIM card cost is approximately 5 USD, and data plans are available for as low as 1 USD per day. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a authorized dealer or carrier office to avoid any issues with **activation** or **coverage**. The documents needed to buy a SIM card include a valid passport, proof of address, and a completed registration form.
