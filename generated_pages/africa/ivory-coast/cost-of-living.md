[SECTION] How Expensive Is Ivory Coast? [/SECTION]
Ivory Coast is considered an affordable country to visit or live in, with a cost of living that is relatively low compared to other countries in West Africa. The country's economy is driven by agriculture, with a growing tourism industry, and this is reflected in the prices of accommodations, food, and other services. Compared to its neighbors, such as Ghana and Liberia, Ivory Coast is slightly more expensive, but still offers a great value for money. The official currency is the West African CFA franc (XOF), which is pegged to the Euro.

The cost of living in Ivory Coast can vary depending on the location, with cities like Abidjan and Yamoussoukro tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. However, overall, the country offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. With a daily budget of $20-40 USD, backpackers can easily find affordable accommodations and food, while mid-range travelers can enjoy a more comfortable lifestyle with a daily budget of $40-80 USD.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs
- Eat at local restaurants or street food stalls to enjoy affordable and delicious meals
- Use public transportation or walk instead of taking taxis to get around
- Learn some basic French phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals
- Avoid traveling during peak season (December to February) to save on flight and accommodation costs
- Consider purchasing local products and handicrafts instead of imported goods to support the local economy and save money
