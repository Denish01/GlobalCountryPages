Visa Policy Overview

Sao Tome and Principe has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Citizens of around 15 countries, including Portugal, Brazil, and some African nations, can enter visa-free for a specified period. Other nationalities may be eligible for a visa on arrival or an e-visa, while some require a visa in advance from the embassy or consulate. The country's visa policy aims to balance tourism and economic development with security and immigration control.

The visa policy of Sao Tome and Principe is designed to facilitate travel and investment while ensuring the security and integrity of the country's borders. With a growing tourism industry and a strategic location in the Gulf of Guinea, Sao Tome and Principe is becoming an increasingly popular destination for travelers and businesspeople alike. Understanding the visa requirements and regulations is essential for a smooth and successful trip.
