Cultural Identity

Sao Tome and Principe's culture is a unique blend of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences. The country's history as a Portuguese colony has had a profound impact on its language, customs, and traditions. The majority of the population is of African descent, with many people having ancestral ties to various ethnic groups from the African mainland. The official language, Portuguese, is widely spoken, and the country's cultural identity is deeply rooted in its Lusophone heritage.

The ethnic makeup of Sao Tome and Principe is diverse, with various groups contributing to the country's cultural landscape. The Forros, descendants of African slaves and Portuguese colonizers, make up a significant portion of the population. Other groups, such as the Angolares and Service, also play important roles in shaping the country's cultural identity. The cultural diversity of Sao Tome and Principe is reflected in its vibrant music, dance, and art, which are all influenced by African, Portuguese, and Brazilian traditions.

Sao Tome and Principe's cultural identity is also characterized by its strong sense of community and hospitality. The country's small population and isolated location have fostered a close-knit society, where traditional values and customs are still highly valued. Visitors to the country can experience this unique cultural identity firsthand by attending local festivals, trying traditional cuisine, and engaging with the friendly and welcoming locals.

Do:
- Greet people with a handshake or a hug, as physical touch is an important part of the culture
- Respect traditional customs and festivals, and participate if invited
- Learn a few basic phrases in Portuguese, such as obrigado (thank you) and por favor (please)
- Remove your shoes when entering a home or mosque
- Bring a gift, such as a bottle of wine or a box of chocolates, when visiting someone's home
- Engage in conversation and show interest in the local culture and traditions
Don't:
- Publicly display affection, as this is considered impolite in Sao Tome and Principe
- Refuse an offer of food or drink, as this is considered rude
- Touch or point at someone with your left hand, as the left hand is considered unclean
- Discuss politics or sensitive topics, as these can be divisive and uncomfortable
- Take pictures of people without their permission, as this is considered impolite
- Litter or dispose of trash improperly, as this is considered disrespectful to the environment and the community
