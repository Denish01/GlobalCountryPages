# Sao Tome and Principe Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

In Sao Tome and Principe, you should eat dishes that blend African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences, such as **Calulu**, **Arroz de Pescado**, and **Feijoada**, which showcase the country's unique cultural heritage.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: rice, beans, cassava, fish
National Dish: Calulu, a stew made with fish or meat, vegetables, and spices
Meal Times: breakfast 7-10 am, lunch 12-2 pm, dinner 7-10 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: limited vegetarian options, some halal and kosher options available
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Sao Tome and Principe's cuisine is a fusion of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian flavors, reflecting the country's history as a colonial outpost. The use of **coconut milk**, **palm oil**, and **spices** is characteristic of the local cooking style. The country's geographic location off the coast of West Africa also influences its cuisine, with **seafood** playing a significant role in many dishes. Regional variations exist, with the island of Principe having its own unique culinary traditions.

The Portuguese influence is evident in the use of **olive oil**, **garlic**, and **onions**, while African ingredients like **cassava** and **yams** are also staple components. Brazilian cuisine has also had an impact, particularly in the use of **beans** and **rice**. The result is a distinctive and flavorful cuisine that is both familiar and exotic.

Sao Tome and Principe's food culture is also shaped by its history as a **sugar** and **cocoa** producer. Many traditional dishes feature these ingredients, and the country's cuisine is known for its rich, sweet flavors. Overall, the food in Sao Tome and Principe is a delicious reflection of the country's complex cultural heritage.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Mealtimes**: eat with your right hand, as the left hand is considered unclean
- **Table manners**: wait for the host to invite you to sit and start eating
- **Tipping**: 5-10% of the total bill
- **Reservations**: not always necessary, but recommended for popular restaurants
- **Dress code**: casual, but modest clothing is recommended
- **Eating with locals**: try to learn some basic Portuguese phrases, like **"obrigado"** (thank you)
