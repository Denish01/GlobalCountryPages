[SECTION] How Expensive Is Sao Tome and Principe? [/SECTION]
Sao Tome and Principe is an island nation located in the Gulf of Guinea, off the coast of West Africa. The country has a relatively low cost of living compared to other African nations. The cost of living in Sao Tome and Principe is generally affordable, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation being relatively low. However, the country's remote location and limited infrastructure can make some goods and services more expensive. Compared to its neighbors, Gabon and Equatorial Guinea, Sao Tome and Principe is a more affordable destination for tourists and expats.

The country's economy is largely driven by agriculture, with a focus on cocoa, coffee, and copra production. The tourism industry is also growing, with visitors drawn to the country's beautiful beaches, lush forests, and vibrant culture. Despite its affordability, Sao Tome and Principe still offers a unique and rewarding experience for visitors, with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a warm and welcoming population.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in guesthouses or small hotels, which are often cheaper than larger hotels.
- Eat at local restaurants or street food stalls, which offer delicious and affordable meals.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible, as taxis can be expensive.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (June to September), when prices for accommodation and flights are higher.
- Learn some basic Portuguese phrases, which can help you negotiate prices and interact with locals.
- Be mindful of your water and energy consumption, as utilities can be expensive in Sao Tome and Principe.
