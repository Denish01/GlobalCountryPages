When visiting Belgium, the best places to stay are in the city centers of Brussels, Bruges, and Antwerp, which offer a mix of historic charm, cultural attractions, and modern amenities.

Best Areas to Stay

For each of the top 7 areas/neighborhoods:

Grand Place (Brussels)

Best For: luxury / business

Vibe: The Grand Place is the heart of Brussels, surrounded by stunning Gothic architecture and lively streets filled with cafes and restaurants. At night, the area comes alive with elegant lighting and a sophisticated atmosphere.

Price Range: $150-300/night

Pros: central location, historic buildings, high-end shopping and dining

Cons: can be crowded and noisy

Saint-Gilles (Brussels)

Best For: luxury / families

Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is known for its beautiful parks, elegant architecture, and vibrant nightlife. It's a popular area for families and those seeking a more refined experience.

Price Range: $120-250/night

Pros: safe and clean, plenty of restaurants and shops, easy access to public transport

Cons: limited budget options

Bruges Historic Center (Bruges)

Best For: couples / families

Vibe: The historic center of Bruges is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, filled with medieval charm, picturesque canals, and stunning architecture. It's a romantic and family-friendly area that's perfect for exploring on foot or by bike.

Price Range: $100-200/night

Pros: unique and historic atmosphere, plenty of cultural attractions, scenic canals

Cons: can be touristy and crowded

Antwerp City Center (Antwerp)

Best For: backpackers / luxury

Vibe: Antwerp's city center is a bustling area filled with historic landmarks, trendy bars, and world-class shopping. It's a great area for both budget-conscious travelers and those seeking a more luxurious experience.

Price Range: $80-200/night

Pros: central location, vibrant nightlife, cultural attractions

Cons: some areas can be seedy at night

Leopold Quarter (Brussels)

Best For: business / luxury

Vibe: The Leopold Quarter is a modern and sophisticated area, home to the European Parliament and many international organizations. It's a great area for business travelers and those seeking a more contemporary experience.

Price Range: $150-300/night

Pros: modern amenities, easy access to public transport, international atmosphere

Cons: limited historic charm

Ghent City Center (Ghent)

Best For: couples / families

Vibe: Ghent's city center is a beautiful and historic area, filled with medieval architecture, picturesque canals, and a vibrant cultural scene. It's a great area for couples and families seeking a unique and romantic experience.

Price Range: $80-180/night

Pros: historic atmosphere, cultural attractions, scenic canals

Cons: some areas can be crowded and touristy

Matonge (Brussels)

Best For: backpackers / budget

Vibe: Matonge is a vibrant and multicultural area, known for its African and Asian cuisine, lively markets, and affordable accommodations. It's a great area for budget-conscious travelers seeking a unique and eclectic experience.

Price Range: $40-100/night

Pros: affordable prices, multicultural atmosphere, lively markets

Cons: limited amenities and services

Booking Tips

  • Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (June to August) and major holidays.
  • Compare prices across multiple booking platforms, such as Booking.com, Expedia, and Airbnb.
  • Consider staying in a guesthouse or hostel for a more local and budget-friendly experience.
  • Look for accommodations with free Wi-Fi and breakfast included.
  • Be aware of any additional fees or taxes, such as the city tax in Brussels.
  • Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs.
  • Negotiate prices for long-term stays or off-season bookings.

Safety & Location Tips

  • Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
  • Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in busy train stations and airports.
  • Consider staying in areas with good public transport links, such as the city centers of Brussels, Bruges, and Antwerp.
  • Research areas to avoid, such as the northern parts of Brussels and some neighborhoods in Antwerp.
  • Stay informed about local events and demonstrations, which can affect traffic and safety.
  • Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel safe for valuables.
