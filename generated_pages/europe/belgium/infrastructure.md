[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Belgium has a highly developed infrastructure, with significant investments in recent years to improve its transportation networks, energy systems, and telecommunications. The country's strategic location in the heart of Europe makes it an important hub for trade and commerce. However, there is a notable urban-rural gap in terms of infrastructure development, with cities like Brussels and Antwerp having more advanced facilities than rural areas. The Belgian government has been working to address this issue, with initiatives aimed at improving connectivity and access to services in rural regions.

The country's infrastructure is also characterized by a strong focus on sustainability, with efforts to reduce carbon emissions and increase the use of renewable energy sources. This is reflected in the development of green transportation systems, such as electric vehicle charging stations and bike-sharing schemes. Additionally, Belgium has implemented various initiatives to improve energy efficiency, such as building insulation and smart grid systems.

Overall, Belgium's infrastructure is well-equipped to support the country's economy and population, with a strong emphasis on innovation and sustainability. The government continues to invest in infrastructure development, with a focus on improving connectivity, reducing carbon emissions, and promoting economic growth.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]
- **Power generation**: Belgium's energy mix is dominated by nuclear power, which accounts for around 40% of total electricity generation, followed by natural gas and renewable energy sources.
- **Renewable energy**: The country has set ambitious targets to increase the share of renewable energy in its energy mix, with a focus on wind power, solar energy, and biomass.
- **Energy efficiency**: Belgium has implemented various initiatives to improve energy efficiency, including building insulation, smart grid systems, and energy-efficient lighting.
- **Reliability**: The country's energy grid is considered reliable, with minimal power outages and a strong focus on maintenance and upgrades.
- **Sustainability**: Belgium is committed to reducing its carbon footprint, with plans to phase out nuclear power and increase the use of renewable energy sources.
