[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Belgium is generally a safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other European countries. The country has a strong police presence, and tourist areas are well-patrolled. However, as with any popular tourist destination, petty crime and scams can occur. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, and take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The country's overall safety rating is 4 out of 5, indicating a low to moderate risk of crime and violence.

Belgium's safety assessment is influenced by its location in Western Europe, where crime rates are generally lower compared to other parts of the world. The country has a stable government and a strong economy, which contributes to its overall safety and security. However, as with any country, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should be aware of these differences to make informed decisions about their travel plans. By taking necessary precautions and being aware of their surroundings, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Belgium.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as the "gold ring scam" or the "friendly local" scam
- Bike theft, especially in larger cities like Brussels and Antwerp
- Overcharging by taxi drivers or restaurants
- Petty crime in areas with high foot traffic, such as train stations and tourist areas
- Scams involving ATMs or credit card machines
- Aggressive begging or panhandling in some areas

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Belgium, but it is recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Belgium.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are of high quality and widely available, especially in larger cities like Brussels and Antwerp. Health insurance is not always required, but it is recommended to have some form of coverage.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are easily accessible and well-stocked, with many locations throughout the country.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or ATMs instead
- Keep hotel rooms and accommodations secure, using safes or locks when available
- Be cautious of overly friendly locals or strangers who approach you with unsolicited offers or advice
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas, especially at night
- Keep your passport and important documents secure, making copies and leaving them with a trusted friend or family member

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Consider joining a guided tour or group to explore the country and meet other travelers
- Be cautious of overly friendly locals or strangers who approach you with unsolicited offers or advice
- Keep your hotel room or accommodations secure, using safes or locks when available, and consider staying in well-lit and populated areas
