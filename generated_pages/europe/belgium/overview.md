[SECTION] What Is Belgium? [/SECTION]
Belgium is a **sovereign** country located in Western Europe, bordered by France, Germany, Luxembourg, and the Netherlands. With a population of approximately 11.7 million people, it is a relatively small country with a rich history and cultural heritage. Belgium is known for its stunning medieval architecture, vibrant cities, and world-class cuisine, including its famous **chocolate** and **beer**.

Geographically, Belgium is situated in a strategic location, with the North Sea to the northwest and the Ardennes forest to the southeast. The country is divided into three main regions: Flanders, Wallonia, and the Brussels-Capital Region, each with its own distinct culture and language. The capital city, Brussels, is a hub for international politics and business, hosting the European Union's headquarters and the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO).

Belgium's unique blend of cultures, languages, and history has created a fascinating and diverse country that is worth exploring. From the cobblestone streets of Bruges to the modern architecture of Antwerp, Belgium has something to offer for every interest and taste. Whether you're interested in history, art, food, or outdoor activities, Belgium is a destination that is sure to captivate and inspire.

7. 2016 - The Brussels terrorist attacks occur, resulting in significant changes to the country's security measures.
