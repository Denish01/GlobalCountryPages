Cultural Treasures

  • The Grand Place in Brussels, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a masterpiece of Gothic architecture and a testament to the city's rich history.
  • The Rubens House in Antwerp is a museum showcasing the works and life of the famous Flemish painter Peter Paul Rubens.
  • The Binche Carnival, a UNESCO-recognized Masterpiece of the Intangible Heritage of Humanity, is a unique and colorful celebration that takes place every year in the city of Binche.
  • The Flemish Primitives, a group of painters from the 15th and 16th centuries, are renowned for their detailed and realistic works of art, which can be found in museums throughout Belgium.
  • The Atomium, a iconic landmark in Brussels, is a symbol of Belgian engineering and design, and offers stunning views of the city.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Belgian people, who are known for their warm welcome and friendly nature, makes visitors feel at home.
  • The chocolate, which is renowned for its high quality and delicious taste, is a must-try for anyone visiting Belgium.
  • The beer, with over 1,000 different varieties, is a major part of Belgian culture, and visitors can sample many different types at bars and breweries throughout the country.
  • The scenery, with its rolling hills, picturesque villages, and stunning coastline, is a major draw for tourists, who come to enjoy the country's natural beauty.
  • The festivals, such as the Tomorrowland music festival and the Ghent Festival, which showcase the country's vibrant culture and entertainment scene.
  • The cuisine, which includes popular dishes such as moules-frites and waffles, is a major part of the Belgian experience, and visitors can sample many different types of food at restaurants and cafes throughout the country.

Hidden Gems

  • The Castle of Gaasbeek, a medieval castle located just outside of Brussels, is a lesser-known gem that offers stunning views and a glimpse into the country's rich history.
  • The Hoge Kempen National Park, a unique and diverse natural area, is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, and offers many opportunities for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • The Museum of Fine Arts in Tournai, which features a collection of art and artifacts from the Middle Ages to the present day, is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by tourists, but is well worth a visit.
  • The Town of Durbuy, which is known as the "smallest town in the world", is a charming and picturesque destination that offers a unique and unforgettable experience for visitors.
