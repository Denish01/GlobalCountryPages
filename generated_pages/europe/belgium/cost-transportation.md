[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Belgium has a well-developed transportation system, with a range of options available to get around the country. The main modes of transportation include buses, trams, metro lines, and commuter trains, which are efficient and reliable. The quality of public transit is high, with frequent services and affordable prices. Most people in Belgium get around using public transportation, with many cities having comprehensive networks of buses and trams. In larger cities like Brussels and Antwerp, metro lines and commuter trains are also available, making it easy to get around without a car.

The transportation system in Belgium is well-integrated, with many cities having a single ticketing system that can be used across different modes of transport. This makes it easy to get around, even for visitors who are not familiar with the system. Additionally, many cities in Belgium are relatively small and compact, making it easy to get around on foot or by bike. In fact, cycling is a popular mode of transport in Belgium, with many cities having dedicated bike lanes and bike-sharing schemes.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use a **MOBIB** card for convenient travel on public transportation in Brussels and other cities.
- Download the **NMBS/SNCB** app for train schedules and tickets.
- Use **Uber** or **Bolt** for ride-hailing services, available in most cities.
- Consider buying a **monthly transit pass** for unlimited travel on public transportation.
- Be aware of **traffic restrictions** in city centers, especially in Brussels and Antwerp.
- Use a **bike-sharing scheme** or rent a bike for a day to explore cities like Brussels and Bruges.
