[SECTION] Overview: Belgium vs Netherlands [/SECTION]
Belgium and the Netherlands are two neighboring countries in Western Europe, known for their rich history, cultural heritage, and high standard of living. In terms of cost of living, both countries are relatively expensive, but Belgium tends to be slightly cheaper. The cost difference between the two countries can be attributed to various factors, including the cost of housing, food, transportation, and utilities. On average, Belgium is around 10-20% cheaper than the Netherlands, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a affordable yet high-quality lifestyle.

The economic context of both countries is similar, with a strong focus on services, industry, and trade. However, the Netherlands has a more diversified economy, with a strong presence of multinational companies, while Belgium's economy is more focused on manufacturing and logistics. This difference in economic structure can affect the cost of living in both countries, with the Netherlands tend to have higher prices for certain goods and services. Overall, while both countries offer a high quality of life, Belgium is generally the more affordable option, making it an attractive choice for those looking to balance cost and quality.
