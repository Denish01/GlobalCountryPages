Population Overview

The population of Cyprus is relatively small, with a total of around 1.25 million people, according to the latest estimates. The population growth rate is approximately 0.7%, which is relatively low compared to other European countries. The majority of the population, around 70%, lives in urban areas, with the capital city Nicosia being the largest urban center. The population of Cyprus has been shaped by various migration patterns, including the influx of Greek and Turkish Cypriots, as well as immigrants from other European countries. The country's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa has made it an attractive destination for migrants and asylum seekers.

The population of Cyprus has been growing steadily over the years, with a significant increase in the urban population. The country's urbanization rate is relatively high, with many people moving from rural areas to cities in search of better job opportunities and living standards. This trend has led to the growth of cities such as Nicosia, Limassol, and Larnaca, which are now major economic and cultural centers. The population of Cyprus is also aging, with a median age of 37 years, which is relatively high compared to other European countries.

The population of Cyprus is also characterized by a significant diaspora community, with many Cypriots living abroad, particularly in the United Kingdom, Australia, and the United States. This diaspora community has played an important role in shaping the country's culture, economy, and politics, and has helped to maintain strong ties between Cyprus and other countries.

The ethnic composition of Cyprus is characterized by a diverse mix of groups, with Greek Cypriots making up the majority of the population, at around 78%. Turkish Cypriots, who are predominantly Muslim, make up around 18% of the population and live mainly in the north of the island. The country is also home to smaller ethnic groups, such as Armenians, Maronites, and Latins, who are predominantly Christian. The ethnic relations in Cyprus are complex, with a long history of conflict between the Greek and Turkish Cypriot communities. However, in recent years, there have been efforts to promote reconciliation and cooperation between the two communities.

Languages

  • Greek is the official language of the Republic of Cyprus and is spoken by the majority of the population.
  • Turkish is also an official language and is spoken by the Turkish Cypriot community.
  • English is widely spoken as a second language, particularly in the tourism and business sectors.
  • Armenian and other minority languages are also spoken by smaller communities.
  • The lingua franca of Cyprus is English, which is widely used for communication between people of different linguistic backgrounds.

Urbanization & Major Cities

  • The urban population of Cyprus makes up around 70% of the total population, with the majority living in the capital city Nicosia and other urban centers.
  • Nicosia is the largest city in Cyprus, with a population of around 200,000 people, and is the country's economic, cultural, and administrative center.
  • Limassol is the second-largest city, with a population of around 100,000 people, and is a major port and tourist center.
  • The fastest-growing cities in Cyprus are those in the coastal regions, such as Larnaca and Paphos, which are popular tourist destinations and have seen significant investment in recent years.
  • The diaspora community of Cyprus is significant, with many Cypriots living abroad, particularly in the United Kingdom, Australia, and the United States, and maintaining strong ties with their country of origin.
