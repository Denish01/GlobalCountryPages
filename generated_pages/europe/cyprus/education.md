[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Cyprus is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 5 and 11, and is provided free of charge by the government. Secondary education is also compulsory, and lasts for six years, from ages 11 to 17. The quality of education in Cyprus is generally high, with a strong emphasis on core subjects such as mathematics, science, and language. Public schools are free and widely available, but there are also a number of private schools that offer alternative curricula and teaching methods. The public education system is well-funded and well-regarded, but there are some concerns about the quality of education in certain areas, particularly in rural regions.

The tertiary education system in Cyprus is also well-developed, with a number of universities and colleges offering a wide range of degree programs. The University of Cyprus is the largest and most prestigious university in the country, and is ranked among the top 1000 universities globally. There are also a number of private universities and colleges that offer specialized programs in fields such as business, engineering, and hospitality management. The cost of higher education in Cyprus is relatively low compared to other European countries, making it an attractive option for international students.

Overall, the education system in Cyprus is well-structured and offers a wide range of options for students. While there are some challenges, particularly in certain areas of the country, the system is generally of high quality and provides students with a solid foundation for future success.
