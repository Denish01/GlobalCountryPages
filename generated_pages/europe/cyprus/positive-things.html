Cultural Treasures

  • The Kourion Archaeological Site, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, features ancient ruins and mosaics that date back to the Roman and Byzantine periods.
  • The Byzantine Museum in Nicosia showcases a collection of Byzantine art and artifacts, including icons, manuscripts, and sculptures.
  • The Cyprus Museum is the largest and oldest archaeological museum in Cyprus, with a collection of artifacts that tell the story of the island's history and culture.
  • The Famagusta Walled City is a historic city that is home to several cultural and historical landmarks, including the Lala Mustafa Pasha Mosque and the Famagusta Cathedral.
  • The Troodos Mountains are home to several traditional villages, including Omodos and Lofou, which are known for their well-preserved architecture and cultural heritage.

People & Global Impact

  • Makarios III, the first president of Cyprus, played a key role in the country's independence movement and was a prominent figure in international politics.
  • The Cypriot diaspora has made significant contributions to the economy and culture of countries like the United States, the United Kingdom, and Australia.
  • Nicos Anastasiades, the current president of Cyprus, has been instrumental in promoting economic development and stability in the country.
  • The Cypriot community is known for its strong values of hospitality, respect for tradition, and commitment to family and social ties.

What Visitors Love

  • The beautiful beaches of Cyprus, such as Nissi Beach and Fig Tree Bay, are consistently ranked among the best in the world.
  • The delicious cuisine of Cyprus, which features dishes like halloumi cheese and souvlaki, is a favorite among visitors.
  • The historic city of Nicosia, with its well-preserved architecture and rich cultural heritage, is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history and culture.
  • The natural beauty of the Troodos Mountains, which offer hiking trails, scenic villages, and stunning vistas, is a major draw for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • The vibrant nightlife of cities like Ayia Napa and Larnaca, which feature a range of bars, clubs, and restaurants, is popular among young travelers.
  • The friendly and welcoming locals, who are known for their hospitality and warm reception of visitors, make Cyprus a great destination for tourists.

Hidden Gems

  • The Akhna Dam, a scenic spot that is home to a variety of bird species and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Konnos Bay, a picturesque beach that is located near the town of Ayia Napa and is known for its crystal-clear waters and scenic hiking trails.
  • The Vouni Palace, a ancient ruin that dates back to the Roman period and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Lefkara Village, a traditional village that is known for its well-preserved architecture, scenic views, and local handicrafts, such as lace and silverwork.
