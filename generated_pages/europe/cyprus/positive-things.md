[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Kourion Archaeological Site**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, features ancient ruins and mosaics that date back to the Roman and Byzantine periods.
- The **Byzantine Museum** in Nicosia showcases a collection of Byzantine art and artifacts, including icons, manuscripts, and sculptures.
- The **Cyprus Museum** is the largest and oldest archaeological museum in Cyprus, with a collection of artifacts that tell the story of the island's history and culture.
- The **Famagusta Walled City** is a historic city that is home to several cultural and historical landmarks, including the **Lala Mustafa Pasha Mosque** and the **Famagusta Cathedral**.
- The **Troodos Mountains** are home to several traditional villages, including **Omodos** and **Lofou**, which are known for their well-preserved architecture and cultural heritage.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Makarios III**, the first president of Cyprus, played a key role in the country's independence movement and was a prominent figure in international politics.
- The **Cypriot diaspora** has made significant contributions to the economy and culture of countries like the United States, the United Kingdom, and Australia.
- **Nicos Anastasiades**, the current president of Cyprus, has been instrumental in promoting economic development and stability in the country.
- The **Cypriot community** is known for its strong values of hospitality, respect for tradition, and commitment to family and social ties.
