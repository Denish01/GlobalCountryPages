# Education Costs in Cyprus

The cost of education in Cyprus varies depending on the type of institution and the level of education, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from $2,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Cyprus is generally of high quality, with both public and private institutions offering a range of programs. Public education is free or subsidized for local students, and the language of instruction is primarily Greek, although some schools may offer Turkish or English as a secondary language. The compulsory education years are from age 5 to 18, and students can attend public or private schools. Private schools in Cyprus are known for their high standards and are often preferred by expat families, although they can be expensive. International schools are also available, offering a range of curricula, including British, American, and International Baccalaureate.

The Cypriot education system is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary levels, with students typically attending primary school from age 5 to 11, and secondary school from age 12 to 18. The academic year typically runs from September to June, with a 10-month calendar. The education system in Cyprus is overseen by the Ministry of Education and Culture, which sets the curriculum and standards for all schools. The ministry also provides funding for public schools and regulates the private education sector. In addition to traditional schools, Cyprus also has a number of vocational and technical schools, which offer training in areas such as engineering, hospitality, and healthcare.
