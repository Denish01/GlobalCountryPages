Overall Safety Assessment

Cyprus is generally a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other European countries. The island nation has a strong sense of community, and visitors often comment on the friendly and welcoming nature of the Cypriots. However, as with any popular tourist destination, there are some areas and situations that require caution. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag snatching, can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, while scams and confidence tricks are also present. Additionally, the ongoing dispute between the Greek and Turkish Cypriot communities means that there are some areas that are best avoided.

Despite these precautions, Cyprus is a wonderful destination for travelers, with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant cultural scene. The country has a well-developed tourist infrastructure, with many resources available to help visitors stay safe and have a enjoyable trip. By taking some basic precautions and being aware of the local situation, travelers can have a safe and rewarding experience in Cyprus. With its beautiful beaches, ancient ruins, and delicious cuisine, Cyprus is an ideal destination for families, couples, and solo travelers alike.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Be aware of scams and confidence tricks, such as the "gold ring scam" or the "friendly local" scam
  • Be careful when using ATMs and credit card machines, as card skimming and fraud can occur
  • Be wary of overly friendly strangers who approach you with unsolicited offers or advice
  • Be cautious when driving, as road conditions can be poor and drivers can be aggressive
  • Be aware of the risk of heat stroke and dehydration, especially during the summer months
  • Be careful when swimming, as some beaches can have strong currents or rough seas

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Cyprus, but it is recommended that travelers are up to date on all routine vaccinations
  • Water Safety: Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended for sensitive stomachs
  • Medical Facilities: Cyprus has a well-developed healthcare system, with modern hospitals and medical facilities available in major towns and cities
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available and well-stocked, with many open late or 24 hours a day

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always carry a valid form of ID and a copy of your passport
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
  • Use reputable taxi companies or ride-sharing services, and always agree on the fare before you start your journey
  • Keep your hotel room door locked and use the safe to store valuables
  • Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi or accessing sensitive information online
  • Stay hydrated and protected from the sun, especially during the summer months
  • Learn some basic Greek and Turkish phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you", to show respect for the local culture

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Cyprus is generally a very safe country for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong focus on visitor safety
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially at night or in crowded areas
  • Use reputable taxi companies or ride-sharing services, and always agree on the fare before you start your journey
  • Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
