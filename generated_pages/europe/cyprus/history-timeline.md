# Cyprus History — Key Events & Timeline

The history of Cyprus is a complex and multifaceted narrative that spans thousands of years, from the earliest known human habitation to the present day, with significant events and periods shaping the island nation into what it is today.

[SECTION] Historical Overview [/SECTION]
Cyprus has a rich and diverse history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the **Neolithic period**, around 10,000 years ago. The island was strategically located at the crossroads of the ancient world, making it an attractive location for various civilizations, including the **Myceenaens**, **Assyrians**, **Persians**, and **Romans**. Cyprus was an important center of trade and culture, with the city of **Kition** serving as a major commercial hub. The island was also a significant center of early Christianity, with the **Apostle Paul** and **Saint Barnabas** visiting the island in the 1st century AD.

In the **Middle Ages**, Cyprus was conquered by the **Byzantine Empire**, followed by the **Lusignan** and **Venetian** periods, which had a profound impact on the island's culture, architecture, and politics. The **Ottoman Empire** conquered Cyprus in 1571, and the island remained under Ottoman rule for nearly three centuries, during which time the Turkish language and culture became an integral part of the island's identity. The **British Empire** took control of Cyprus in 1878, and the island became a British colony, with the British introducing modern infrastructure, education, and administration.

The 20th century saw a significant increase in **nationalist** sentiment, with both Greek and Turkish Cypriots seeking independence and self-determination. The island gained independence from Britain in 1960, but the **intercommunal violence** and **partition** of the island in 1974 have had a lasting impact on the country's politics and society. Today, Cyprus is a **sovereign state**, with a **presidential system** and a **market-based economy**, and is a member of the **European Union** and the **United Nations**.

The modern era has seen significant economic growth and development, with tourism, finance, and shipping playing important roles in the country's economy. However, the ongoing **Cyprus dispute** and the **partition** of the island remain major challenges, with efforts towards **reunification** and **peace** ongoing.

[SECTION] Founding & Formation [/SECTION]
Cyprus became the entity it is today through a complex and often tumultuous process, with the island's strategic location and rich natural resources making it an attractive location for various civilizations and empires. The **Ottoman Empire** played a significant role in shaping the island's modern identity, with the Turkish language and culture becoming an integral part of the island's heritage. The **British Empire** also had a profound impact on the island, introducing modern infrastructure, education, and administration, and laying the foundations for the country's modern economy and politics.

The **independence movement**, led by Archbishop Makarios and the Greek Cypriot nationalist movement, played a crucial role in the country's founding and formation, with the island gaining independence from Britain in 1960. However, the **partition** of the island in 1974 and the ongoing **Cyprus dispute** have had a lasting impact on the country's politics and society, with efforts towards **reunification** and **peace** ongoing.

[SECTION] Colonial & Independence Period [/SECTION]
The colonial period in Cyprus, which lasted from 1878 to 1960, was marked by significant economic and social change, with the British introducing modern infrastructure, education, and administration. The **British colonial authorities** also promoted the development of the island's natural resources, including its **copper** and **asbestos** mines, and encouraged the growth of **agriculture** and **tourism**. However, the British also imposed their own language, culture, and laws on the island, which had a profound impact on the island's identity and sense of self.

The **independence movement**, led by Archbishop Makarios and the Greek Cypriot nationalist movement, began to gain momentum in the 1950s, with the island's Greek Cypriot community seeking union with Greece. The **Turkish Cypriot community**, however, preferred to maintain the island's independence and neutrality, leading to **intercommunal tensions** and **violence**. The island gained independence from Britain in 1960, with Archbishop Makarios as the country's first president, but the **partition** of the island in 1974 and the ongoing **Cyprus dispute** have had a lasting impact on the country's politics and society.

The **independence movement** was marked by significant **violence** and **bloodshed**, with the **EOKA** (National Organisation of Cypriot Fighters) and the **TMT** (Turkish Resistance Organisation) engaging in a bitter and often brutal struggle for control of the island. The **British colonial authorities** responded with force, imposing **curfews** and **martial law**, and **deporting** many of the island's Greek Cypriot leaders.

[SECTION] Modern Era [/SECTION]
The modern era in Cyprus has been marked by significant economic growth and development, with **tourism**, **finance**, and **shipping** playing important roles in the country's economy. The country has also become a major **energy producer**, with significant offshore gas reserves discovered in recent years. However, the ongoing **Cyprus dispute** and the **partition** of the island remain major challenges, with efforts towards **reunification** and **peace** ongoing.

The **European Union** has played a significant role in promoting **peace** and **stability** in Cyprus, with the country joining the EU in 2004. The **EU** has also provided significant **financial** and **technical** assistance to the country, helping to promote **economic development** and **social cohesion**. However, the **global financial crisis** has had a significant impact on Cyprus's economy, with the country requiring a **bailout** from the EU and IMF in 2013.

The Cypriot government has announced its intention to develop a **national strategy** for the exploitation of its natural resources, with a focus on **sustainability** and **environmental protection**. The country has also launched a number of initiatives aimed at promoting **reconciliation** and **peace**, including the **Committee on Missing Persons** and the **Technical Committee on Cultural Heritage**.
