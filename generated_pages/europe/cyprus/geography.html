Location & Borders

Cyprus is an island nation located in the eastern part of the Mediterranean Sea, situated about 80 km south of Turkey, 105 km west of Syria, and 380 km north of Egypt. The country is bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the south, west, and north, and it is separated from Turkey by the Kyrenia Mountains. Cyprus is also close to Greece, with the Greek island of Rhodes being about 380 km to the west. The country's strategic location has made it an important hub for trade and cultural exchange throughout history.

Cyprus is situated at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa, making it a unique cultural and geographical melting pot. The country's borders are surrounded by several bodies of water, including the Mediterranean Sea to the south and west, and the Levantine Sea to the east. The Akamas Peninsula is a notable geographical feature, located on the northwest coast of Cyprus and known for its natural beauty and diverse wildlife. The country's borders with neighboring countries are primarily maritime, with the exception of the United Nations Buffer Zone, which separates the internationally recognized Republic of Cyprus from the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus.

Terrain & Landscape

Cyprus is characterized by a diverse range of landscapes, including mountains, plains, and coastlines. The Troodos Mountains are the largest mountain range in Cyprus, covering much of the western and central parts of the island. The highest point in Cyprus, Mount Olympus, is located in the Troodos Mountains and reaches an elevation of 1,952 meters. The mountains are home to a variety of flora and fauna, including forests of pine, cedar, and cypress trees.

The Mesaoria Plain is a large, fertile plain that covers much of the eastern part of Cyprus. The plain is home to several major cities, including Nicosia, the capital city of Cyprus, and is an important agricultural region. The coastline of Cyprus is approximately 648 km long and features a variety of beaches, bays, and peninsulas. The Karpas Peninsula is a notable geographical feature, located on the northeastern coast of Cyprus and known for its natural beauty and diverse wildlife.

The island's terrain is also characterized by several rivers, including the Pedieos River, which flows through the city of Nicosia and is the longest river in Cyprus. The country's landscape has been shaped by its geological history, with volcanic and tectonic activity having played a significant role in forming the island's terrain. The result is a diverse and complex landscape, with a wide range of ecosystems and habitats supporting a rich variety of plant and animal life.
