[SECTION] Overview: Albania vs Greece [/SECTION]
Albania and Greece are two neighboring countries in Southeastern Europe, with distinct economic profiles and cost of living. Albania has a lower GDP per capita and a smaller economy, which translates to lower prices for goods and services. Greece, on the other hand, has a more developed economy and a higher standard of living, which comes with higher costs. Overall, Albania offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for housing, food, transportation, and utilities. However, Greece offers a more developed infrastructure, a richer cultural heritage, and a more vibrant tourism industry, which may be worth the extra cost for some individuals.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Albania has a growing economy, with a focus on tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing, while Greece has a more diversified economy, with a strong services sector and a significant tourism industry. The cost of living in Albania is also influenced by its proximity to Italy and other European countries, which has led to an increase in trade and investment. In contrast, Greece has a more developed economy, with a strong focus on services and tourism, which has driven up prices for goods and services.
