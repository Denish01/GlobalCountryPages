[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Albania is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education lasts for 9 years, from age 6 to 15, and is compulsory for all children. Secondary education, which lasts for 3 years, is also compulsory and provides students with a general education or vocational training. Tertiary education, which includes universities and colleges, provides students with a range of academic and professional programs. The quality of education in Albania is generally good, with a strong emphasis on theoretical knowledge and a growing focus on practical skills.

The education system in Albania is predominantly public, with the majority of schools and universities funded by the government. However, there is a growing private sector, with many private schools and universities offering a range of programs and courses. The public education system is generally considered to be of high quality, with well-qualified teachers and a comprehensive curriculum. However, there are some challenges, including limited resources and infrastructure in some areas, particularly in rural regions.

Overall, the education system in Albania provides students with a solid foundation for future academic and professional success. With a strong emphasis on public education and a growing private sector, students have a range of options to pursue their goals and interests. The government has also implemented various reforms to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curricula and teaching methods.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- International schools are available in major cities, including Tirana and Vlorë, offering a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate (IB)**, **British**, and **American** programs.
- These schools cater to the children of expatriates and Albanian families who want to provide their children with an international education.
- The cost of attending an international school in Albania can range from $5,000 to $15,000 per year, depending on the school and the program.
- Some international schools also offer boarding facilities for students from outside the city or country.
- The availability of international schools is limited in rural areas, but there are some online and distance learning options available.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Albania, foreign students need to obtain a **student visa**, which can be applied for at the Albanian embassy or consulate in their home country.
- Students may need to demonstrate proficiency in **Albanian** or **English**, depending on the program and institution they are applying to.
- There are various **scholarships** available for foreign students, including government-funded and private scholarships.
- The cost of living in Albania is relatively low, with **accommodation** options starting from $200 per month and **food** options starting from $3 per meal.
- Foreign students can also work part-time to support themselves while studying in Albania, with many universities offering **part-time job** opportunities on campus.
- The **Albanian government** also offers some **language courses** and **orientation programs** to help foreign students adjust to life in Albania.
