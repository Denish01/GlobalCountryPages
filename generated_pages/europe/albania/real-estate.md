[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The Albanian property market has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by the country's economic development and increasing tourism industry. The market is characterized by a mix of old and new properties, with a trend towards modernization and renovation of existing buildings. The demand for properties in urban areas, particularly in the capital city of Tirana, has been high, with prices increasing steadily over the past few years. In contrast, rural areas have seen slower growth, with prices remaining relatively stable. The **coastal regions**, such as Vlorë and Sarandë, have also become popular destinations for foreign buyers, attracted by the beautiful beaches and scenic views.

The Albanian government has implemented various initiatives to stimulate the property market, including the introduction of **tax incentives** for foreign investors and the development of new infrastructure projects. However, the market still faces challenges, such as a lack of transparency and corruption, which can make it difficult for foreign buyers to navigate the system. Despite these challenges, the Albanian property market offers attractive opportunities for investors, with relatively low prices and a growing demand for rental properties.

The current market conditions are characterized by a high demand for properties in the **residential sector**, particularly in the capital city of Tirana. The demand for **commercial properties**, such as office spaces and retail units, has also been increasing, driven by the growth of the service sector and the expansion of international companies in the country. Overall, the Albanian property market is expected to continue growing, driven by the country's economic development and increasing foreign investment.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners can buy property in Albania, but they must obtain a **permission from the Ministry of Justice**.
- Foreign companies can also buy property in Albania, but they must be registered with the **Albanian Trade Register**.
- There are no restrictions on the type of property that can be bought by foreigners, but they must comply with the **zoning regulations** and **urban planning rules**.
- Foreigners can also lease property in Albania, with a **minimum lease period of 5 years**.
- To avoid **double taxation**, foreign buyers should consult with a tax advisor to ensure compliance with the Albanian tax laws.

- The **deposit** is usually equivalent to 1-2 months' rent, and it is refundable at the end of the lease period.
- **Furnished apartments** are more common in urban areas, particularly in Tirana, while **unfurnished apartments** are more common in rural areas.
