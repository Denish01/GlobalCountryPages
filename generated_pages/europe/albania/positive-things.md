[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Butrint Ancient City**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a must-visit attraction, featuring ancient ruins and artifacts from the Greek and Roman periods.
- The **Et'hem Bey Mosque** in Tirana is a stunning example of Ottoman architecture, with intricate frescoes and ornate decorations.
- The **Gjirokastër Castle** is a historic fortress that dates back to the 12th century, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
- The **Iso-Polyphony** is a unique form of traditional Albanian music, recognized by UNESCO as a **Masterpiece of the Intangible Heritage of Humanity**.
- The **National Museum of Albania** in Tirana showcases the country's rich history and cultural heritage, with a collection of artifacts and exhibits from prehistoric times to the present day.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Ismail Kadare**, a renowned Albanian writer and poet, has been recognized internationally for his literary works, including the **Prince of Asturias Award** for Literature.
- **Ferid Murad**, an Albanian-American physician and pharmacologist, was awarded the **Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine** in 1998 for his discovery of the role of nitric oxide in the cardiovascular system.
- The **Albanian diaspora** has made significant contributions to the cultural and economic development of countries around the world, including the United States, Italy, and Greece.
- **Mother Teresa**, an Albanian-born Catholic nun, is revered for her humanitarian work and dedication to helping the poor and marginalized, and was awarded the **Nobel Peace Prize** in 1979.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The warm **hospitality** of the Albanian people, who are known for their welcoming nature and generosity towards guests.
- The delicious **traditional cuisine**, which features a range of dishes, including **qofte** (meatballs), **fërgesë** (a corn bread), and **baklava** (a sweet pastry).
- The stunning **natural scenery**, including the **Accursed Mountains**, the **Albanian Riviera**, and the **Lake Ohrid**.
- The rich **cultural heritage**, with numerous historical sites, museums, and festivals to explore.
- The vibrant **city life**, with a range of cultural events, nightlife, and entertainment options in cities like Tirana and Vlorë.
- The **affordability** of the country, with low prices for accommodations, food, and activities, making it an attractive destination for budget travelers.
