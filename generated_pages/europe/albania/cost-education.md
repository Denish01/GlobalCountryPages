# Education Costs in Albania

The cost of education in Albania varies depending on the type of institution and the level of education, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge tuition fees ranging from $1,000 to $5,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Albania is divided into public and private institutions, with the public sector being the dominant provider of education. Public schools in Albania are generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on the **Albanian language** and **mathematics**. However, private schools are also popular, particularly among families who can afford to pay for them, as they often offer smaller class sizes and more individualized attention. Compulsory education in Albania lasts for 12 years, from age 6 to 18, and is divided into primary, secondary, and high school levels. International schools are also available in Albania, particularly in the capital city of Tirana, and offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Examinations**.

The language of instruction in Albanian public schools is **Albanian**, although some schools may also offer instruction in **English** or other foreign languages. Private schools, on the other hand, may offer instruction in a range of languages, including **English**, **Italian**, and **Turkish**. International schools in Albania typically offer instruction in **English**, and may also offer instruction in other languages, such as **French** or **German**. The academic year in Albania typically runs from September to June, with a break in the summer months.
