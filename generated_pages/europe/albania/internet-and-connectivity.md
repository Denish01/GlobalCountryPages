[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Albania has undergone significant improvements in recent years, with the average internet speed reaching 25 Mbps download. However, there is still a notable urban-rural divide, with faster speeds and better coverage available in cities like Tirana, compared to rural areas. Despite this, the overall reliability of the internet in Albania is good, with few reports of major outages or disruptions. This makes it suitable for general use, such as browsing, social media, and streaming.

In terms of internet infrastructure, Albania has a well-developed network of fiber-optic cables, which provides a solid foundation for fast and reliable internet connections. The country's internet service providers (ISPs) also offer a range of packages and plans, catering to different needs and budgets. However, it's worth noting that the quality of internet services can vary depending on the provider and the location, so it's a good idea to research and compare different options before choosing an ISP.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Albania is a relatively straightforward process, with several mobile carriers offering tourist-friendly plans. The main carriers, such as Vodafone, Telekom, and Eagle, have stores and kiosks at major airports, cities, and towns, where you can purchase a SIM card. To buy a SIM card, you will typically need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, although some carriers may have additional requirements. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from a official carrier store or a reputable reseller, as this will ensure that you get a genuine SIM card and avoid any potential scams.

The cost of a SIM card in Albania is relatively low, with tourist SIMs starting from around 10 USD. Data plans are also affordable, with prices starting from around 5 USD per GB. The following table provides a summary of the tourist plans offered by the main carriers in Albania:

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality in Albania is generally good, with many hotels and resorts offering free and fast WiFi to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is also widespread, with many cafes and restaurants offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in Albania, particularly in Tirana, and often offer high-speed WiFi and other amenities.
- Public WiFi is available in many public areas, such as airports, bus stations, and city centers.
- Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while dining out.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like Tirana Coworking and Albanian Innovation Hub offer reliable and fast WiFi, as well as other amenities like meeting rooms and networking events.
- Cafe chains like Mulliri i Vjeter and Komiteti Kafe offer good WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options like mobile hotspots and portable WiFi routers are available for purchase or rent, providing a reliable backup in case of internet outages.
- VPNs are recommended for remote workers, as they provide an additional layer of security and protection for sensitive data.
- Many cafes and restaurants also offer power outlets and comfortable seating, making them suitable for remote work.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major issue in Albania, but some websites may be blocked due to copyright or other restrictions.
- Using a VPN is recommended to ensure secure and private browsing, particularly when using public WiFi networks.
- Roaming alternatives like eSIMs and portable WiFi routers are available, offering a convenient and cost-effective way to stay connected while traveling.
- It's a good idea to research and compare different internet service providers and plans before choosing one, to ensure that you get the best value for your money.
- Be aware of data limits and roaming charges when using your mobile phone or other devices, as these can add up quickly.
