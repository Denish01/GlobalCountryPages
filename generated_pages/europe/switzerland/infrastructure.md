# Switzerland Infrastructure — Roads, Airports, Electricity & Water

Switzerland has a highly developed infrastructure, characterized by a comprehensive and well-maintained network of roads, railways, airports, and telecommunications, which supports its strong economy and high standard of living.

[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Switzerland's infrastructure is considered one of the best in the world, with significant investments in recent years to maintain and upgrade its networks. The country's strategic location at the heart of Europe makes it an important hub for international trade and transportation. The infrastructure development level is high, with a strong focus on sustainability, energy efficiency, and environmental protection. However, there is a notable urban-rural gap, with cities like Zurich and Geneva enjoying superior infrastructure, while rural areas may face challenges in accessing certain services. Recent investments have aimed to bridge this gap, with initiatives such as the expansion of broadband internet and mobile phone coverage to remote areas.

The Swiss government has implemented various policies to promote sustainable infrastructure development, including the use of renewable energy sources, reduction of greenhouse gas emissions, and improvement of public transportation. These efforts have contributed to Switzerland's high ranking in terms of infrastructure quality and environmental sustainability. The country's infrastructure is also characterized by a high level of private sector involvement, with many companies playing a crucial role in the development and maintenance of transportation networks, energy systems, and telecommunications.

The infrastructure in Switzerland is well-integrated, with different modes of transportation and services working together seamlessly. For example, the country's rail network is connected to its road network, making it easy to travel from one place to another. The infrastructure is also designed to be highly efficient, with a strong focus on reducing energy consumption and minimizing environmental impact. Overall, Switzerland's infrastructure is a key factor in its economic success and high standard of living.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]
- Switzerland generates most of its electricity from **hydropower**, which accounts for around 56% of the country's power generation.
- The country has a highly reliable electricity grid, with an average of less than 20 minutes of power outages per year.
- Switzerland has set ambitious targets to increase its use of **renewable energy sources**, aiming to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 50% by 2030.
- The country has a well-developed **electric vehicle** charging infrastructure, with over 1,000 public charging stations.
- Switzerland is a net exporter of electricity, with a significant portion of its power generation being exported to neighboring countries.

[SECTION] Water & Sanitation [/SECTION]
- Switzerland has a highly developed **water supply system**, with over 99% of the population having access to safe drinking water.
- The country's **sanitation system** is also well-developed, with over 95% of the population having access to improved sanitation facilities.
- Switzerland has a strong focus on **water conservation**, with measures such as water-saving appliances and efficient irrigation systems.
- The country's **wastewater treatment** plants are highly efficient, with over 90% of wastewater being treated before being released into the environment.
- Switzerland has a well-developed **flood protection** system, with measures such as levees, dams, and flood-control reservoirs to protect against flooding.
