[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Getting around Switzerland is relatively easy, with a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses, trains, and trams. The quality of public transit is excellent, with frequent and reliable services connecting most parts of the country. Most people in Switzerland get around using public transportation, with many cities having a comprehensive network of buses and trams. The country's geography, with mountains and valleys, can make driving challenging, but the road network is well-maintained, and many people also use cars to get around.

The public transportation system in Switzerland is highly integrated, with a single ticket or pass allowing travel on multiple modes of transport. The **Swiss Travel Pass** is a popular option for tourists, offering unlimited travel on public transportation, including trains, buses, and boats. The pass can be purchased for a specific number of days, ranging from 3 to 15 days, and is available in different classes, including first and second class.
