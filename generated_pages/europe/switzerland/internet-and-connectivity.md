[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Switzerland boasts an excellent internet infrastructure, with an average internet speed of 211 Mbps download, making it one of the fastest countries in the world. The urban-rural divide is relatively small, with most areas having access to reliable and fast internet, although speeds may be slower in very rural areas. The country's rugged terrain can sometimes pose a challenge to internet connectivity, but overall, the internet quality is high and reliable, with minimal downtime or disruptions.

The Swiss internet market is highly competitive, with several major carriers offering a range of plans and services. This competition has driven innovation and investment in the sector, resulting in a high-quality internet experience for users. Additionally, the country's strong economy and high standard of living have contributed to a high level of internet penetration, with over 90% of the population having access to the internet.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Switzerland, you can visit one of the many retail stores of the main carriers, such as Swisscom, Salt, or Sunrise, or purchase one at a airport or train station. You will typically need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, although some carriers may have additional requirements. The cost of a tourist SIM card is around 20 USD, and data plans start from around 10 USD per day. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds.

Swisscom, Salt, and Sunrise are the top three carriers in Switzerland, and they all offer a range of plans and services tailored to tourists and travelers. These plans often include data, voice, and text, and can be purchased for a set period of time, such as a day, week, or month. Some carriers also offer prepaid plans, which can be a cost-effective option for short-term visitors.
