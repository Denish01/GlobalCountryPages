[SECTION] Overview: Malta vs Italy [/SECTION]
Malta and Italy are two European countries with distinct economic profiles. Malta, a small island nation, has a relatively low cost of living compared to its larger neighbor, Italy. The GDP per capita in Malta is lower than in Italy, which contributes to the overall lower cost of living. Additionally, Malta has a more favorable tax environment, which attracts expats and businesses. In terms of cost levels, Malta is generally cheaper than Italy, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being lower. However, the cost difference between the two countries can vary greatly depending on the specific location and lifestyle.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Malta has a growing economy, driven by tourism, finance, and technology, which has led to an increase in demand for housing and other services. Italy, on the other hand, has a more mature economy, with a stronger focus on manufacturing and industry. The cost of living in Italy can vary greatly depending on the region, with cities like Rome and Milan being more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. Overall, Malta offers a more affordable lifestyle, with a lower cost of living that is around 10-20% lower than in Italy.
