[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Malta's infrastructure has undergone significant development in recent years, with investments in transportation, energy, and telecommunications. The country's strategic location in the Mediterranean has made it an important hub for trade and tourism, driving the need for modern and efficient infrastructure. However, there is still a noticeable gap between urban and rural areas, with the latter often experiencing slower internet speeds and less reliable public transportation. Despite this, Malta's infrastructure is generally of high quality, with a strong emphasis on **sustainability** and **environmental protection**.

Malta's government has implemented various initiatives to improve the country's infrastructure, including investments in **renewable energy** and **energy efficiency**. The country aims to reduce its dependence on fossil fuels and increase its use of solar and wind power. Additionally, Malta has made significant progress in developing its **information and communication technology (ICT)** sector, with a focus on **e-government** and **digitalization**.

The Maltese government has also launched several projects to improve the country's **transportation infrastructure**, including the development of a new **public transportation system** and the upgrading of existing **roads** and **highways**. These initiatives aim to reduce congestion, improve air quality, and enhance the overall quality of life for residents and visitors.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]
- Malta's power generation is primarily based on **natural gas** and **diesel**, with a growing focus on **renewable energy sources** such as **solar** and **wind power**.
- The country's energy sector is dominated by the state-owned **Enemalta** corporation, which is responsible for the generation, transmission, and distribution of electricity.
- Malta has made significant progress in increasing its **energy efficiency**, with a focus on reducing energy consumption and promoting the use of **energy-efficient technologies**.
- The country aims to generate at least **10% of its electricity from renewable sources** by 2025, with a long-term goal of becoming **carbon neutral**.
- Malta is also exploring the potential of **electric vehicles**, with several initiatives aimed at promoting the adoption of **electric cars** and **hybrid vehicles**.

[SECTION] Water & Sanitation [/SECTION]
- Malta's **tap water** is safe to drink, with a high level of **water quality** maintained by the country's **water services corporation**.
- The country has a well-developed **sanitation system**, with a high level of **wastewater treatment** and **sewage collection**.
- Malta has made significant progress in reducing **water pollution**, with a focus on protecting the country's **marine environment** and **water resources**.
- The country is also promoting **water conservation**, with initiatives aimed at reducing **water waste** and promoting the use of **water-efficient technologies**.
- Malta's **rural areas** often experience **water scarcity** during the summer months, highlighting the need for more **sustainable water management** practices.
