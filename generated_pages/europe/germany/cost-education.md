# Education Costs in Germany

The cost of education in Germany varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education generally being free or subsidized for local students, while international students and those attending private institutions may face higher fees.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The German education system is highly regarded for its quality, with a strong emphasis on **science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM)** fields. Public education in Germany is generally free or subsidized, with most students attending public schools. However, there are also private schools and international schools available, which may offer a more tailored education experience, but at a higher cost. The language of instruction in German schools is primarily **German**, although some international schools may offer instruction in other languages, such as **English** or **French**. Compulsory education in Germany typically lasts for 12-13 years, starting at the age of 6 and ending at the age of 18.

The quality of public and private education in Germany is generally high, with many schools offering a range of **extracurricular activities** and **language courses**. International schools are also available in major cities, such as **Berlin** and **Munich**, which cater to the needs of expat students and offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate (IB)**. However, the availability and quality of international schools can vary depending on the location, so it's essential to research and visit schools before making a decision.
