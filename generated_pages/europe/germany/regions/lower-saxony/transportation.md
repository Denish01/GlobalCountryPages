[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Lower Saxony has two main international airports, Hannover Airport (HAJ) and Bremen Airport (BRE), which offer flights to various destinations in Europe and beyond. Hannover Airport is a major hub with connections to many European cities, including Amsterdam, Berlin, and London, while Bremen Airport serves as a secondary hub with flights to destinations such as Munich, Frankfurt, and Paris. In addition to air travel, Lower Saxony can also be reached by car or bus via the European highway network, with major routes such as the A1, A2, and A7 passing through the region. The region is also accessible by train, with frequent services from neighboring countries and other parts of Germany.

For those traveling overland, there are several entry points along the borders with neighboring countries, including the Netherlands and Denmark. The region's proximity to the North Sea and the Baltic Sea also makes it a popular destination for those arriving by ferry or cruise ship. With its well-developed transportation infrastructure, getting to Lower Saxony is relatively easy, whether you're coming from within Germany or from further afield. The region's central location in northern Europe also makes it an ideal base for exploring the surrounding area, with many major cities and attractions within easy reach.

If you need to travel within Germany, there are several domestic airlines that operate flights from Hannover Airport and Bremen Airport to other major cities, including Berlin, Munich, and Frankfurt. [SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Lower Saxony has an extensive rail network, with frequent services to major cities and towns throughout the region. The high-speed ICE (InterCity Express) trains are the fastest way to travel, with journeys from Hannover to Berlin taking around 1.5 hours. The regional trains, on the other hand, are a more affordable option and offer a scenic route through the countryside.
