# Berlin Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Berlin, be sure to try the city's unique blend of traditional German cuisine with international influences, featuring popular dishes like Currywurst, Schnitzel, and Sauerbraten.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: pork, beef, potatoes, bread
National Dish: Sauerbraten, a pot roast dish marinated in vinegar, water, and spices
Meal Times: typical breakfast times are between 7-10 am, lunch from 12-2 pm, and dinner from 6-10 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: it is customary to round up the bill to the nearest euro or leave 5-10% of the total
Dietary Notes: Berlin has a growing number of vegetarian and vegan options, and many restaurants offer halal and kosher dishes
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Berlin's cuisine is shaped by its history as a cultural melting pot, with influences from German, Turkish, Italian, and Asian cuisines. The city's food scene is known for its hearty meat dishes, bread, and potatoes, as well as its vibrant street food culture. Berlin's culinary identity is also defined by its love of beer, with many local brews available in restaurants and bars. The city's food culture is a reflection of its history, from the traditional German dishes like Sauerbraten and Schnitzel to the modern, international flavors of its street food scene.

Berlin's regional variations are also worth exploring, with different neighborhoods offering unique culinary experiences. The Kreuzberg district, for example, is known for its Turkish cuisine, while the Mitte district is home to many traditional German restaurants. The city's food scene is also influenced by its seasonal produce, with many restaurants offering dishes made with fresh, local ingredients.

In recent years, Berlin has become a hub for food innovation, with many young chefs and restaurateurs opening new, avant-garde restaurants. The city's food scene is now more diverse than ever, with a wide range of options to suit every taste and budget. From traditional German cuisine to modern, international flavors, Berlin has something to offer every food lover.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- it is customary to wait to be seated by the host or hostess
- table manners are formal, with an emphasis on using utensils and keeping elbows off the table
- tipping is expected, with 5-10% of the total bill being standard
- reservations are recommended for popular restaurants, especially on weekends
- dress codes vary, but smart casual is generally acceptable
- **Prost**, or cheers, is a common toast, often made with beer or wine
