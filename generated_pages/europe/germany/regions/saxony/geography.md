Saxony is situated in the eastern part of Germany, bordering the countries of Poland and the Czech Republic to the east and south, respectively. The region is also surrounded by the German states of Brandenburg to the north, Saxony-Anhalt to the west, Thuringia to the southwest, and Bavaria to the south. Saxony is a landlocked region, with no direct access to the sea, but it is drained by several major rivers, including the Elbe, Mulde, and Spree.

The location of Saxony has played a significant role in shaping its history and culture, with the region having been an important center of trade and commerce in the past.
