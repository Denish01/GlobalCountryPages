[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Greet people with a firm handshake and a friendly "Guten Tag" (good day)
- Use formal titles such as "Herr" or "Frau" when addressing older people or those in positions of authority
- Try to learn a few basic German phrases, such as "Danke" (thank you) and "Bitte" (please)
- Respect traditional customs and festivals, and participate if invited to do so
- Tip your server in restaurants and bars, around 10% is standard
- Be punctual and respectful of other people's time

**Don't:**
- Don't be late for appointments or social engagements
- Don't ignore traditional customs and festivals, as they are an important part of the region's cultural heritage
- Don't tip too much, as this can be seen as impolite
- Don't speak too loudly in public, as Germans tend to value quiet and reserve
- Don't ignore the rules of the road, such as speed limits and pedestrian crossings
- Don't be afraid to ask for help or directions, as the locals are generally friendly and willing to assist
