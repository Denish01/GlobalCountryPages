[SECTION] Overview: Germany vs Switzerland [/SECTION]
Germany and Switzerland are two of the most economically stable countries in Europe, with high standards of living and strong economies. However, the cost of living in these countries differs significantly. Germany has a more affordable cost of living, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. In contrast, Switzerland is known for being one of the most expensive countries in the world, with high prices for housing, food, and other living expenses. On average, the cost of living in Germany is around 20-30% lower than in Switzerland.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in the cost of living. Germany has a larger economy and a more diverse industry base, which helps to keep prices lower. Switzerland, on the other hand, has a smaller economy and a high demand for housing and other living expenses, which drives up prices. Overall, Germany is the more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees, while Switzerland may be more suitable for those with high incomes or a high level of financial security.
