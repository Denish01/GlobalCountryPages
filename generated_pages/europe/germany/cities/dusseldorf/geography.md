# Dusseldorf Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

Dusseldorf is a city located in the western part of Germany, situated on the banks of the Rhine River, and is the capital of the state of North Rhine-Westphalia.

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Dusseldorf is situated in the heart of the Rhine-Ruhr region, one of the most densely populated areas in Europe. The city is bordered by the cities of Neuss, Meerbusch, and Ratingen, and is approximately 35 kilometers northwest of Cologne. Dusseldorf is also located near the Dutch border, with the city of Arnhem being about 70 kilometers to the northwest.

The city's strategic location on the Rhine River has made it an important hub for trade and commerce for centuries. The Rhine River, one of the most important waterways in Europe, flows through the city and provides a connection to the North Sea and other major European cities. Dusseldorf is also close to several major highways and rail lines, making it easily accessible by car, train, or bus.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Dusseldorf's terrain is relatively flat, with the city being situated in the Rhine Valley. The city's highest point, the Sandberg, is only 165 meters above sea level, and the lowest point is the Rhine River, which flows through the city at an elevation of 28 meters. The surrounding countryside is characterized by rolling hills and forests, with the Bergisches Land region to the east and the Lower Rhine region to the west.

The city itself is divided into several districts, each with its own unique character and landscape. The Altstadt, or old town, is a charming area with narrow streets and historic buildings, while the Medienhafen, a former industrial area, has been transformed into a modern hub for media and technology companies. The city also has several parks and green spaces, including the Hofgarten, a large park that dates back to the 18th century.

The Rhine River plays a major role in shaping the city's landscape, with the riverbank being lined with parks, promenades, and restaurants. The city also has several bridges that span the river, including the famous Oberkassel Bridge, which offers stunning views of the city and the river.
