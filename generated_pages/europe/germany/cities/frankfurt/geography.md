[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Frankfurt is situated in the state of Hesse, in the central-west part of Germany. The city is nestled in the Rhine-Main region, approximately 30 kilometers from the Rhine River. Frankfurt is a vital transportation hub, with its international airport being one of the busiest in Europe. The city is bordered by several neighboring cities and districts, including Offenbach am Main, Bad Homburg, and the Main-Taunus-Kreis district. Frankfurt's strategic location has made it an important center for trade, commerce, and culture in the region.

The city's proximity to the Rhine River and the Main River has played a significant role in its development and growth throughout history. The Rhine-Main region is a densely populated area, with several major cities and urban centers, including Wiesbaden, Mainz, and Darmstadt. Frankfurt's location at the crossroads of several major transportation routes has made it an attractive location for businesses, tourists, and investors. The city's international airport and its extensive network of highways and railways connect it to major cities in Europe and around the world.
