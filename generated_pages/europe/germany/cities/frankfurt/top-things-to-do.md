# Top Things to Do in Frankfurt — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Frankfurt, you should not miss exploring the Römerberg square, visiting the Städel Museum, and taking a stroll along the Main River, as these attractions offer a glimpse into the city's rich history, cultural significance, and natural beauty. 

[SECTION] Top Attractions [/SECTION]
[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Römerberg | Altstadt, Frankfurt | Historic Square | Historic heart of Frankfurt with colorful half-timbered houses |
| Städel Museum | Schaumainkai, Frankfurt | Art Museum | One of Germany's most famous art museums with a collection of 3,000 paintings |
| Main Tower | Neue Mainzer Straße, Frankfurt | Observation Deck | Panoramic views of the city from its 200-meter high observation deck |
| Frankfurt Cathedral | Domplatz, Frankfurt | Cathedral | A stunning example of Gothic architecture with impressive stone carvings |
| Museumsufer | Schaumainkai, Frankfurt | Museum District | A collection of museums along the Main River, including the Städel Museum and the German Film Museum |
| Palmengarten | Siesmayerstraße, Frankfurt | Botanical Garden | A beautiful green oasis in the heart of the city with a stunning collection of plants |
| Frankfurt Zoo | Bernhard-Grzimek-Allee, Frankfurt | Zoo | Home to over 4,500 animals from around the world, including elephants, giraffes, and penguins |
| Eiserner Steg | Mainkai, Frankfurt | Pedestrian Bridge | A historic pedestrian bridge with stunning views of the Main River and the city skyline |
| Senckenberg Natural History Museum | Senckenberganlage, Frankfurt | Natural History Museum | One of the largest natural history museums in Europe with a collection of over 30 million specimens |
| Goethe House | Großer Hirschgraben, Frankfurt | Historic House | The birthplace of famous German writer Johann Wolfgang von Goethe, now a museum showcasing his life and work |
[/TABLE]

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Frankfurt City Tour** to explore the city's hidden corners and learn about its history and culture
- Visit the **Frankfurt Christmas Market** to experience the city's festive atmosphere and try traditional German food and drinks
- Go on a **Main River Cruise** to see the city from a different perspective and learn about its history and landmarks
- Attend a performance at the **Alte Oper**, a stunning concert hall with excellent acoustics
- Take a **Food Tour** to sample the city's culinary delights, including traditional German dishes and international cuisine
- Visit the **Frankfurter Bücherfest**, a book festival that celebrates literature and authors from around the world

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Palmengarten**, a beautiful botanical garden with a stunning collection of plants
- **Frankfurter Stadtwald**, a large forest area with hiking and biking trails
- **Main River**, a scenic river that runs through the heart of the city and offers stunning views and boat tours
- **Grüneburgpark**, a beautiful park with gardens, walking trails, and a stunning view of the city skyline
- **Nidda River**, a scenic river that runs through the outskirts of the city and offers hiking and biking trails

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Städel Museum**, one of Germany's most famous art museums with a collection of 3,000 paintings
- **German Film Museum**, a museum that showcases the history of German cinema and film production
- **Museum of World Cultures**, a museum that showcases a collection of artifacts from around the world
- **Frankfurt Cathedral**, a stunning example of Gothic architecture with impressive stone carvings
- **Alte Oper**, a stunning concert hall with excellent acoustics and a variety of performances
- **Schirn Kunsthalle**, a modern art museum with a collection of contemporary art
