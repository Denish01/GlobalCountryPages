# Stuttgart - Overview & Essential Facts

Stuttgart is the capital and largest city of the state of Baden-Württemberg in southwest Germany, known for its rich cultural heritage, automotive industry, and picturesque location in the Swabian Jura mountain range.

[SECTION] What Is Stuttgart? [/SECTION]
Stuttgart is a city located in the southwest of Germany, in the state of Baden-Württemberg. It is situated in a picturesque valley, surrounded by the Swabian Jura mountain range, and is known for its rich cultural heritage, including numerous museums, theaters, and music venues. The city has a population of around 630,000 people and is a major economic center, with a strong focus on the automotive industry, as it is home to companies such as Mercedes-Benz and Porsche. Stuttgart is also a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its unique blend of traditional and modern architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and stunning natural surroundings.

Stuttgart's history dates back to the 10th century, and over the years, the city has developed a distinct character, shaped by its strategic location at the crossroads of Europe. The city has been an important center of trade and commerce, and its cultural and economic significance has grown steadily over the centuries. Today, Stuttgart is a thriving metropolis, offering a high quality of life, excellent education and research opportunities, and a wide range of cultural and leisure activities.

The city's stunning natural surroundings, with the Swabian Jura mountain range to the south and the Neckar river running through the city, make it an attractive destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, including hiking, cycling, and water sports, and the city's many parks and gardens offer a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of urban life.
