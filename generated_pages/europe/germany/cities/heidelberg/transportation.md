# Getting Around Heidelberg — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Heidelberg, you can use a variety of transportation options, including trains, buses, and taxis, as well as walk or bike through the city's scenic streets.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Heidelberg is located in the state of Baden-Württemberg in southwestern Germany, and it is easily accessible by air, land, or train. The closest international airport is Frankfurt Airport (FRA), which is about 80 kilometers (50 miles) from Heidelberg. From the airport, you can take a train or bus to Heidelberg, or rent a car and drive. There are also several overland entry points, including the autobahn highways A5 and A65, which connect Heidelberg to other major cities in Germany and Europe.

If you prefer to travel by train, you can take a direct train from Frankfurt Airport to Heidelberg's main train station, which is located in the city center. The train ride takes about an hour, and trains run frequently throughout the day. You can also take a bus from the airport to Heidelberg, which takes about an hour and a half. Several bus companies, including FlixBus and Eurolines, operate from Frankfurt Airport to Heidelberg, with multiple departures throughout the day.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
While Heidelberg does not have its own airport, you can fly into Frankfurt Airport (FRA) and then take a train or bus to Heidelberg. Domestic flights within Germany are limited, but you can fly from major cities like Berlin, Munich, and Hamburg to Frankfurt Airport. Several airlines, including Lufthansa and Eurowings, operate domestic flights within Germany. To book a domestic flight, you can use the airlines' websites or a travel booking website like Expedia or Booking.com. Approximate costs for domestic flights within Germany range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Heidelberg has an extensive rail network, with frequent trains connecting the city to other major cities in Germany and Europe. The city's main train station is a major hub, with trains running to destinations like Frankfurt, Stuttgart, and Munich. You can take a high-speed train, such as the ICE (InterCity Express), to travel quickly to other cities. To book a train ticket, you can use the Deutsche Bahn website or a ticket machine at the train station. Approximate costs for train tickets range from $10 to $100, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several bus companies, including FlixBus and Eurolines, operate from Heidelberg to other cities in Germany and Europe. The buses are a convenient and affordable way to travel, with prices starting from around $5. The bus station is located near the train station, and you can book tickets online or at the station. The buses run frequently, with multiple departures throughout the day, and offer a range of amenities, including Wi-Fi and comfortable seating.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Germany, you need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The road conditions in Heidelberg are generally good, with well-maintained highways and streets.
- Fuel costs are around $1.50 per liter for gasoline and $1.20 per liter for diesel.
- Car rental companies like Hertz, Avis, and Europcar have offices in Heidelberg, and you can book a car online or at the office.
- When driving in Heidelberg, be aware of the city's many bike lanes and pedestrian zones, and watch out for trams and buses.
- Parking can be challenging in the city center, but there are several parking garages and lots available.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of pickpocketing and scams at train stations and tourist areas.
- Use a ticket machine or the Deutsche Bahn website to book train tickets, as this is often cheaper than buying at the counter.
- Download the DB Navigator app for train schedules and ticket booking.
- Use a credit card or cash to pay for transportation, as not all providers accept foreign currencies.
- Be mindful of bike lanes and pedestrian zones when driving or walking.
- Take a **StadtRad** (city bike) for a day to explore the city's scenic bike paths.
- Avoid driving in the city center, as traffic can be heavy and parking is limited.
- Use a taxi or ride-hailing service at night, as public transportation may be limited.
