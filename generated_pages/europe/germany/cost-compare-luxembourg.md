[SECTION] Overview: Germany vs Luxembourg [/SECTION]
Germany and Luxembourg are two European countries with distinct economic profiles. Germany has a large and diverse economy, with a strong manufacturing sector and a high standard of living. Luxembourg, on the other hand, is a small country with a highly developed financial sector and a high GDP per capita. Despite these differences, both countries have a high cost of living, but Germany is generally cheaper than Luxembourg. The cost of living in Germany is around 20-30% lower than in Luxembourg, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of both countries also plays a role in their cost of living. Germany has a large and competitive market, which helps to keep prices low. Luxembourg, on the other hand, has a small and highly specialized economy, which can drive up prices for certain goods and services. Additionally, Luxembourg has a highly developed financial sector, which can attract high-income individuals and drive up prices for housing and other services. Overall, Germany offers a more affordable lifestyle than Luxembourg, but both countries have their unique advantages and disadvantages.
