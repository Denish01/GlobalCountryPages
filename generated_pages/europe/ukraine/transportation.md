# Getting Around Ukraine — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Ukraine, you can use a variety of transportation options, including international and domestic flights, an extensive rail network, buses, and city transport systems, as well as drive on your own or use ride-hailing services.

Rail Network: extensive
Drives On: right
Road Quality: moderate
Ride-Hailing Apps: Uber, Bolt, local alternatives
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Ukraine has several international airports, with Boryspil International Airport (KBP) being the largest and busiest, serving as a major hub for both domestic and international flights. Kyiv International Airport (IEV) is another significant airport, offering flights to various European destinations. You can also enter Ukraine by land, with major overland entry points at the borders with Poland, Slovakia, Hungary, Romania, and Moldova. Direct flight hubs to Ukraine include Amsterdam, Berlin, Frankfurt, London, and Paris, among others.

If you prefer to travel by land, you can take buses or trains from neighboring countries, such as Poland, Belarus, or Russia. The main overland entry points are located at the borders with these countries, and you can find bus and train stations nearby. Additionally, you can also take a ferry from Turkey or other Black Sea countries to Ukraine's ports, such as Odessa or Mykolaiv. However, this option may take longer and may not be as frequent as flights or overland transport.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Ukraine has a moderate domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights between major cities, including Kyiv, Lviv, Odessa, and Kharkiv. The main domestic airlines are Ukraine International Airlines, Windrose Airlines, and Motorsich Airlines. You can book flights online or through a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the route and time of year. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $20 to $100 USD per one-way ticket. It's recommended to book in advance to secure the best prices and availability.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Ukraine has an extensive rail network, with trains connecting major cities and towns across the country. The quality of the rail network varies, with some routes offering modern and comfortable trains, while others may be older and less comfortable. Key routes include Kyiv to Lviv, Odessa, and Kharkiv, as well as international connections to neighboring countries. You can book train tickets online or at the train station, and prices vary depending on the class and route. High-speed options are available on some routes, such as the Kyiv to Kharkiv line. The booking process can be done through the Ukrainian Railways website or through a ticket office at the train station.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Ukraine has a well-developed network of intercity buses and coaches, with several companies operating routes across the country. Some of the main bus companies include Autolux, Gunsel, and Ukrbus, offering connections between major cities and towns. The quality of the buses varies, but most offer comfortable seating and affordable prices. Routes and costs vary depending on the company and destination, but you can expect to pay between $5 to $20 USD for a one-way ticket. You can book tickets online or at the bus station, and it's recommended to book in advance to secure the best prices and availability.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Ukraine, you'll need a valid international driving permit and a passport.
- Road conditions vary, with some highways being well-maintained, while others may be in poor condition.
- Fuel costs are relatively low, with gasoline prices around $0.50 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies are available in major cities, but be sure to research and choose a reputable company.
- Be aware of hazards such as potholes, aggressive drivers, and limited road signs.
- It's recommended to avoid driving at night, especially on rural roads, due to poor lighting and road conditions.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of scams, especially when using taxis or ride-hailing services, and always agree on a price before starting your journey.
- Payment methods vary, but most transportation services accept cash, and some accept credit or debit cards.
- Download the Ukrainian Railways app or the Citymobil app for convenient ticket booking and navigation.
- Learn some basic Ukrainian phrases, such as "добрий день" (dobryi den', or "good day"), to help you navigate and communicate with locals.
- Be prepared for crowds and long lines, especially during peak travel seasons.
- Consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot for convenient internet access and navigation.
- Always carry a map or guidebook, and be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, and try to avoid traveling during rush hour or peak tourist season.
