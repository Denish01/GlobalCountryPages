Internet Overview

The internet in Ukraine has undergone significant improvements in recent years, with a notable increase in speed and coverage. However, there is still a noticeable urban-rural divide, with faster and more reliable internet connections available in major cities like Kyiv, Lviv, and Odessa, while rural areas may experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Despite these challenges, Ukraine's internet infrastructure is generally reliable, with most carriers offering a stable connection and a range of data plans to suit different needs and budgets.

In terms of internet quality, Ukraine ranks among the top countries in Eastern Europe, with an average download speed of 25 Mbps, which is sufficient for streaming, online gaming, and other data-intensive activities. However, it's worth noting that internet speeds can vary depending on the location and the carrier, so it's a good idea to research and compare different options before choosing a provider. Additionally, Ukraine has made significant progress in expanding its fiber-optic network, which has improved internet speeds and reliability in many areas.

Getting a SIM Card

Getting a SIM card in Ukraine is relatively straightforward, with a range of options available at airports, mobile shops, and kiosks. To purchase a SIM card, you will typically need to provide a passport and proof of address, although some carriers may have additional requirements. The cost of a tourist SIM card can range from 5-10 USD, depending on the carrier and the data plan. Recommended carriers include Kyivstar, Vodafone Ukraine, and Lifecell, which offer a range of data plans and have good coverage throughout the country.

When choosing a SIM card, it's a good idea to consider your data needs and budget, as well as the coverage and speed of the carrier. Some carriers offer unlimited data plans, while others may have data caps or restrictions on certain types of traffic. It's also worth noting that some carriers may offer additional services, such as international roaming or free access to certain apps and services.

WiFi Availability

  • Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most mid-range and luxury hotels offering fast and reliable connections
  • Cafe WiFi culture is widespread, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers
  • Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in major cities, offering high-speed internet and a range of amenities for remote workers
  • Public WiFi is available in many cities, although it may not always be secure or reliable
  • Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected on the go

For Remote Workers

  • Coworking spaces like Kyiv's iHub and Lviv's Startup Depot offer high-speed internet and a range of amenities for remote workers
  • Reliable cafe chains like Coffee Shop Company and Coffeemania offer fast and free WiFi, making them a great option for remote work
  • Backup internet options like portable WiFi hotspots and mobile SIM cards can provide a reliable connection in case of an outage
  • VPNs are recommended for remote workers, as they can help to protect against cyber threats and ensure secure access to sensitive data
  • Many libraries and universities also offer free WiFi and a quiet workspace, making them a great option for remote workers

Tips & Warnings

  • Internet censorship is a concern in Ukraine, with some sites and services blocked or restricted
  • Blocked sites may include certain social media platforms, news sites, and online services
  • VPNs are recommended to help bypass censorship and ensure secure access to blocked sites
  • Roaming alternatives like portable WiFi hotspots and local SIM cards can be a cost-effective option for tourists and remote workers
  • eSIM options are becoming increasingly available, offering a convenient and flexible way to stay connected
  • When using public WiFi, it's a good idea to use a VPN and keep your device and data secure to protect against cyber threats.
