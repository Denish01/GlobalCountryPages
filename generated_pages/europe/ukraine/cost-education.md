# Education Costs in Ukraine

The cost of education in Ukraine varies depending on the type of institution and the level of study, with public education being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Ukraine is divided into public and private institutions, with the public sector being the largest and most widely accessible. Public schools in Ukraine are generally considered to provide a good quality education, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and a wide range of subjects taught. The language of instruction is Ukrainian, although some schools may also offer instruction in Russian or other minority languages. Compulsory education in Ukraine lasts for 11 years, from age 6 to 17, and is divided into primary, secondary, and upper secondary levels. International schools are also available in major cities such as Kyiv and Odessa, offering a range of curricula including British, American, and International Baccalaureate.

In terms of quality, public education in Ukraine is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and a wide range of subjects taught. However, some private schools may offer additional benefits such as smaller class sizes, more extracurricular activities, and better facilities. International schools in Ukraine typically follow a foreign curriculum and may have a more diverse student body, making them a popular choice for expat families. The availability of international schools in Ukraine is limited, but they can be found in major cities such as Kyiv, Odessa, and Lviv.
