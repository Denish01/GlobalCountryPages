# Education Costs in United Kingdom

The cost of education in the United Kingdom varies significantly depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from $5,000 to $20,000 or more per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The United Kingdom has a well-established education system, with a strong emphasis on public education, which is generally free or subsidized for local students. The quality of public education in the UK is considered to be high, with many schools achieving excellent academic results. However, private schools, also known as independent schools, offer an alternative for families who can afford the tuition fees, and often have smaller class sizes and more resources. The language of instruction in the UK is English, and compulsory education lasts for 12 years, from age 5 to 16. International schools are also available, particularly in larger cities such as London, offering a range of curricula including the International Baccalaureate and American high school diplomas.

The UK education system is divided into several stages, including primary education (ages 5-11), secondary education (ages 11-16), and further education (ages 16-18). Students can then choose to pursue higher education at a university or college. The UK is home to many world-class universities, including the University of Oxford and the University of Cambridge, which are consistently ranked among the top universities globally. The education system in the UK is highly regarded, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement, and many students going on to pursue higher education and successful careers.
