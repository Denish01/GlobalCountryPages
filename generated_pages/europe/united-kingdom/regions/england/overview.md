[SECTION] What Is England? [/SECTION]
England is a country that is part of the United Kingdom, which also includes Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. Geographically, England is located on the island of Great Britain, bordering the North Sea to the east, the English Channel to the south, and the Atlantic Ocean to the west. It shares a land border with Scotland to the north and Wales to the west. England is known for its vibrant cities, historic landmarks, and picturesque countryside, making it a popular destination for tourists and a hub for international business and culture.

England's history dates back to the Roman era, and it has been shaped by various invasions, dynasties, and empires over the centuries. The country has a diverse landscape, ranging from the rolling hills of the Cotswolds to the rugged mountains of the Lake District. England is also home to a wide range of cultural attractions, including world-class museums, theaters, and music venues. From the bustling streets of London to the historic cities of York and Bath, England has a unique charm that attracts visitors from around the world.

England's sovereignty is tied to the United Kingdom, with the UK Parliament in London being the supreme legislative body. However, England has its own regional government and a degree of autonomy, with the ability to make decisions on certain domestic matters. The country is also a member of various international organizations, including the United Nations, the European Union (until 2020), and the Commonwealth.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 55 BC - The Roman conquest of England begins, with Julius Caesar's invasion of the island.
2. 1066 - The Norman Conquest of England takes place, with William the Conqueror defeating the English army at the Battle of Hastings.
3. 1215 - The Magna Carta is signed, establishing the principle of rule of law and limiting the power of the monarch.
4. 1534 - The English Reformation takes place, with the establishment of the Church of England and the separation from the Roman Catholic Church.
5. 1801 - The Kingdom of Great Britain and the Kingdom of Ireland are merged to form the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland.
6. 1945 - England plays a major role in the Allied victory in World War II, with Winston Churchill serving as Prime Minister.
7. 1973 - The United Kingdom, including England, joins the European Economic Community (EEC), a precursor to the European Union.
