# Where to Stay in Bath — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Bath, the best areas to stay are in the city center, with top neighborhoods including the **City Centre**, **Royal Crescent**, **Pulteney Road**, **Lansdown**, **Walcot**, **Bathwick**, and **Newbridge**, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**City Centre** (Bath)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: The City Centre is the heart of Bath, with its stunning Georgian architecture, lively atmosphere, and proximity to major attractions like the Roman Baths and Bath Abbey. It's a great place to experience the city's vibrant culture and nightlife.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Central location, easy access to attractions, wide range of restaurants and bars. 
Cons: Can be noisy, limited parking options.

**Royal Crescent** (Bath)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: The Royal Crescent is a picturesque area with beautiful Georgian architecture, tranquil atmosphere, and stunning views of the Royal Victoria Park. It's perfect for those seeking a relaxing and upscale experience.
Price Range: $200-400/night
Pros: Scenic views, historic architecture, proximity to Royal Victoria Park. 
Cons: Limited dining options, can be expensive.

**Pulteney Road** (Bath)
Best For: Family / Business
Vibe: Pulteney Road is a charming area with a mix of Georgian and modern architecture, a vibrant atmosphere, and easy access to the city center. It's a great place for families and business travelers alike.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Central location, variety of restaurants, easy access to attractions. 
Cons: Can be busy, limited parking options.

**Lansdown** (Bath)
Best For: Backpacker / Budget
Vibe: Lansdown is a lively area with a mix of student accommodation, bars, and restaurants, offering a more affordable and vibrant experience. It's perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.
Price Range: $50-150/night
Pros: Affordable, lively atmosphere, easy access to city center. 
Cons: Can be noisy, limited amenities.

**Walcot** (Bath)
Best For: Family / Local
Vibe: Walcot is a charming area with a village-like atmosphere, historic architecture, and a strong sense of community. It's a great place for families and those seeking a more local experience.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Quiet atmosphere, historic architecture, local amenities. 
Cons: Limited dining options, can be far from city center.

**Bathwick** (Bath)
Best For: Luxury / Relaxation
Vibe: Bathwick is a tranquil area with stunning views of the River Avon, beautiful architecture, and easy access to the city center. It's perfect for those seeking a relaxing and luxurious experience.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Scenic views, peaceful atmosphere, easy access to city center. 
Cons: Limited amenities, can be expensive.

**Newbridge** (Bath)
Best For: Budget / Long stays
Vibe: Newbridge is a quiet area with a mix of residential and commercial properties, offering a more affordable and peaceful experience. It's a great place for budget travelers and those on longer stays.
Price Range: $40-120/night
Pros: Affordable, quiet atmosphere, easy access to city center. 
Cons: Limited amenities, can be far from attractions.
