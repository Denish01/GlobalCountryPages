# Getting Around Cardiff — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Cardiff, you can use a variety of transportation options, including buses, trains, and taxis, as well as ride-hailing apps and car rentals, making it easy to explore the city and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Cardiff is easily accessible by air, with Cardiff Airport (CWL) being the main international airport serving the city. The airport is located about 12 miles west of the city center and offers direct flights to several European destinations, including Amsterdam, Barcelona, and Paris. You can also fly into Bristol Airport (BRS) or London Heathrow Airport (LHR) and take a bus or train to Cardiff. The city is also connected to the rest of the UK by a network of roads and motorways, making it easy to drive to Cardiff from other parts of the country.

If you're coming from outside the UK, you can fly into one of the London airports, such as Heathrow or Gatwick, and then take a bus or train to Cardiff. The city is also accessible by sea, with ferry services operating from Ireland and other parts of the UK. The port of Cardiff is located in the Bay area and offers stunning views of the city. Overland entry points include the M4 motorway, which connects Cardiff to London and other major cities in the UK.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
While Cardiff Airport has a limited domestic air network, you can fly to other parts of the UK from the airport. The main domestic airlines operating from Cardiff include **Flybe** and **KLM**, with flights to destinations such as Edinburgh, Glasgow, and Newcastle. You can book flights online through the airlines' websites or through a travel agent. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Cardiff start from around $50-$100 for a one-way ticket, depending on the destination and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Cardiff has an extensive rail network, with regular trains operating to other parts of the UK. The city's main railway station is **Cardiff Central**, which is located in the city center and offers connections to London, Birmingham, and other major cities. You can book train tickets online through the National Rail website or at the station. The rail network in Cardiff is operated by **Transport for Wales** and **Great Western Railway**, with frequent trains running throughout the day. High-speed options are available on some routes, including the **Great Western Railway** service to London Paddington.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Cardiff has a comprehensive network of buses and coaches, with services operating to other parts of the UK and within the city. The main bus station is **Cardiff Central Bus Station**, which is located in the city center and offers connections to destinations such as London, Bristol, and Swansea. You can book bus tickets online through the **National Express** website or at the station. The bus network in Cardiff is operated by **National Express** and **Stagecoach**, with frequent buses running throughout the day.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Cardiff, you'll need a valid **driver's license** from your home country or an **International Driving Permit**.
- The road quality in Cardiff is generally good, with well-maintained roads and motorways.
- Fuel costs in Cardiff are relatively high, with prices starting from around $1.50 per liter.
- Car rental companies such as **Europcar** and **Hertz** operate in Cardiff, with a range of vehicles available for rent.
- When driving in Cardiff, be aware of the city's **one-way systems** and **pedestrianized areas**, and always follow local traffic rules and regulations.
- Parking in Cardiff can be limited, especially in the city center, so be prepared to pay for parking or use a **park and ride** service.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When using public transport in Cardiff, be sure to **validate your ticket** before boarding the bus or train.
- The **Transport for Wales** app is a useful resource for planning your journey and tracking bus and train times.
- Be aware of **peak hour** traffic in Cardiff, which can cause delays and congestion.
- When using a taxi or ride-hailing service, always **check the driver's ID** and **agree on the fare** before starting your journey.
- Cardiff is a relatively safe city, but be aware of **pickpocketing** and **bag snatching** in crowded areas.
- The **Visit Cardiff** website is a useful resource for planning your trip and finding out more about the city's transport options.
- When driving in Cardiff, be aware of the city's **20mph zones** and **cycle lanes**, and always follow local traffic rules and regulations.
