[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Cardiff is a vibrant and welcoming city, with a strong sense of community and a low crime rate. Visitors can feel comfortable exploring the city's many attractions, from the historic castle to the modern bay area, without undue concern for their safety. As with any city, however, it's still important to take common-sense precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night, and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in popular tourist spots.

Overall, Cardiff is a great destination for travelers, with a wide range of activities and attractions to suit all interests. From cultural events to outdoor pursuits, there's always something to do in this vibrant and friendly city. With its low crime rate and strong sense of community, Cardiff is an excellent choice for solo travelers, families, and groups of all ages. Whether you're interested in history, culture, or entertainment, Cardiff has something to offer, and its safe and welcoming atmosphere makes it an ideal destination for visitors from around the world.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas, especially around tourist attractions
- Scams targeting tourists, such as overpriced taxis or fake ticket sellers
- Drink spiking and petty theft in bars and clubs, especially in the city centre
- Aggressive begging and panhandling in some areas, especially around the castle
- Traffic congestion and pedestrian accidents, especially in the city centre
- Cybercrime and ATM scams, especially in tourist areas
- Rowdy behaviour and noise pollution in student areas, especially at night

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** None required, but standard vaccinations such as MMR and flu shots are recommended
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available
- **Medical Facilities:** High-quality medical facilities are available, including the University Hospital of Wales
- **Pharmacy Access:** Easy, with many pharmacies and chemists throughout the city

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in popular tourist spots
- Use reputable taxi services or public transport, and always check the fare before you start your journey
- Avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night, especially in areas with low safety levels
- Keep your valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or locker
- Be cautious of aggressive beggars and panhandlers, and avoid engaging with them
- Follow local advice and instructions in case of an emergency

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Cardiff is generally a safe and welcoming city for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in deserted areas
- Consider joining a group tour or using a reputable taxi service to get around the city
- Keep in touch with family and friends back home, and consider sharing your itinerary with them
