# Brighton Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

Brighton's culture is a unique blend of traditional British heritage and modern, alternative influences, shaped by its history as a seaside resort and its current status as a thriving arts and cultural hub.

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Brighton's cultural identity is defined by its rich history, stunning architecture, and vibrant arts scene. The city has a long tradition of attracting visitors from all over the world, and this has contributed to its diverse and inclusive atmosphere. Brighton is known for its **LGBTQ+ friendly** community, and its annual Pride parade is one of the largest and most popular in the UK. The city's cultural influences are predominantly British, but there are also significant **European** and **international** influences, reflecting its history as a major port and tourist destination.

The ethnic and linguistic makeup of Brighton is predominantly **English**, but there are also significant **minority communities**, including people of **Asian**, **African**, and **European** descent. The city is home to a thriving **student population**, with two universities and a number of language schools, which adds to its diverse and cosmopolitan atmosphere. Brighton's cultural scene is also shaped by its **seaside location**, with many festivals and events celebrating its maritime heritage and natural beauty.

Brighton's cultural identity is perhaps best summed up by its **bohemian** and **alternative** vibe, which reflects its history as a haven for artists, musicians, and writers. The city is home to a thriving **independent scene**, with many unique shops, cafes, and venues that showcase its creative and eclectic spirit. From its stunning **Regency architecture** to its vibrant **street art**, Brighton is a city that is proud of its heritage and its individuality.
