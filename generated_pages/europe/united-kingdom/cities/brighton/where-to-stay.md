# Where to Stay in Brighton — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Brighton, you should stay in one of its vibrant neighborhoods, such as the Lanes, North Laine, or Hove, which offer a range of accommodations to suit different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, Brighton offers a unique experience for travelers. 
**The Lanes** (Brighton) 
Best For: Luxury travelers and shoppers
Vibe: The Lanes is a historic and upscale neighborhood filled with narrow streets, trendy boutiques, and fine dining restaurants, offering a sophisticated and elegant atmosphere. 
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Central location, high-end shopping, and dining options, proximity to the seafront
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, expensive

**North Laine** (Brighton) 
Best For: Backpackers and young travelers
Vibe: North Laine is a vibrant and bohemian neighborhood known for its street art, independent shops, and lively atmosphere, making it perfect for those looking for a unique and eclectic experience. 
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: Affordable, lively atmosphere, and a variety of restaurants and bars
Cons: Can be noisy, limited parking options

**Hove** (Brighton) 
Best For: Families and those seeking a more relaxed atmosphere
Vibe: Hove is a charming and laid-back neighborhood with a beautiful beach, parks, and a range of family-friendly activities, offering a more subdued and peaceful experience. 
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: Relaxed atmosphere, beautiful beach, and plenty of green spaces
Cons: Less central than other areas, limited nightlife options

**Kemptown** (Brighton) 
Best For: LGBTQ+ travelers and those looking for a vibrant nightlife
Vibe: Kemptown is a lively and diverse neighborhood known for its LGBTQ+ community, bars, and clubs, offering a fun and energetic atmosphere. 
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: Vibrant nightlife, diverse community, and proximity to the seafront
Cons: Can be noisy, limited family-friendly activities

**Montpelier** (Brighton) 
Best For: Business travelers and those seeking a more upscale experience
Vibe: Montpelier is an elegant and refined neighborhood with beautiful architecture, parks, and a range of high-end amenities, offering a sophisticated and professional atmosphere. 
Price Range: $120-250/night
Pros: Upscale amenities, beautiful parks, and proximity to the city center
Cons: Can be expensive, limited nightlife options

**Prestonville** (Brighton) 
Best For: Families and those seeking a more suburban experience
Vibe: Prestonville is a charming and family-friendly neighborhood with a range of parks, schools, and local amenities, offering a more relaxed and peaceful atmosphere. 
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: Relaxed atmosphere, plenty of green spaces, and family-friendly activities
Cons: Less central than other areas, limited nightlife options
