[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Brighton is situated on the southeastern coast of England, approximately 76 km south of London. The city is nestled between the South Downs National Park to the north and the English Channel to the south. To the east lies the town of Peacehaven, while the city of Hove borders Brighton to the west. The city's strategic location on the coast has made it an important center for trade and tourism throughout its history. Brighton is also a popular destination for day-trippers and weekend visitors from London, thanks to its excellent rail connections and proximity to the capital.

The city's borders are defined by its surrounding districts and natural features. To the north, the South Downs National Park provides a scenic backdrop to the city, with its rolling hills and picturesque villages. The park is a popular destination for hikers, cyclists, and nature lovers, with its stunning scenery and diverse wildlife. To the south, the English Channel provides a dramatic coastline, with its rocky shores and sandy beaches. The city's coastline is dotted with piers, marinas, and other waterfront attractions, making it a popular destination for sailors, fishermen, and beachgoers.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Brighton's terrain is characterized by its coastal location and proximity to the South Downs National Park. The city is situated on a mixture of chalk and clay soils, with the South Downs rising to the north. The downs are a range of hills that stretch across southeastern England, providing a scenic backdrop to the city. The hills are dotted with woodlands, heathlands, and grasslands, and are home to a diverse range of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and birds of prey. The city itself is relatively flat, with some gentle slopes and hills rising from the seafront.

The coastline at Brighton is a mix of sandy beaches, rocky shores, and cliffs. The city's famous **beach** is a popular destination for sunbathers, swimmers, and water sports enthusiasts. The beach is lined with a promenade, which offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding coastline. To the east of the city, the coastline becomes more rugged, with cliffs and rocky shores dominating the landscape. This area is popular with walkers and nature lovers, who come to enjoy the stunning scenery and diverse wildlife of the area.

The city's landscape has been shaped by its history and geography. The old town is a maze of narrow streets and alleys, with many historic buildings and landmarks. The city's **pier**, which dates back to the 19th century, is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. The pier is home to a range of amusement arcades, restaurants, and other attractions, and offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding coastline.
