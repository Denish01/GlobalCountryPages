# Brighton Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Brighton is generally a safe city to visit, with low crime rates compared to other major cities in the United Kingdom.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Brighton is a popular tourist destination known for its vibrant culture, beautiful beaches, and friendly atmosphere. The city has a strong police presence, and visitors can feel safe walking around the city center and major tourist areas. However, as with any city, there are some areas that are considered safer than others, and tourists should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Overall, Brighton is a great destination for solo travelers, families, and couples, with plenty of activities and attractions to enjoy.

Despite its overall safety, Brighton is not immune to crime, and visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. The city has a lively nightlife scene, and some areas can become crowded and rowdy, especially on weekends. Additionally, as with any popular tourist destination, there are some scams and petty crimes that target visitors, such as pickpocketing and street performers who may be aggressive in seeking tips. However, these incidents are relatively rare, and visitors can minimize their risk by taking common-sense precautions and being mindful of their belongings.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Aggressive street performers and beggars in the city center and seafront
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake charity collectors or people posing as police officers
- Drink spiking and theft in bars and clubs, especially in the Kemptown area
- Cycle theft, with many bikes left unlocked in the city center
- Traffic accidents, with many pedestrians and cyclists using the city's busy roads
- Rowdy behavior and anti-social behavior in some areas, especially at night

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Brighton, but visitors should ensure they are up to date with routine vaccinations such as MMR and tetanus.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Brighton, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it.
- **Medical Facilities:** Brighton has a range of high-quality medical facilities, including the Royal Sussex County Hospital and several private clinics. Visitors from the EU can access medical care using their EHIC card, while others may need to pay for treatment or have travel insurance.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available in Brighton, with many open late or 24 hours a day.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night
- Keep an eye on your drinks and avoid leaving them unattended
- Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Be aware of your surroundings and avoid distractions, such as using your phone in crowded areas
- Follow local advice and instructions from police or other authorities

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Brighton is generally a safe and welcoming city for solo and women travelers, with many female-friendly bars and restaurants
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, such as the city center or seafront
- Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in deserted areas, especially at night
- Keep in touch with friends and family back home and let them know your itinerary and plans
