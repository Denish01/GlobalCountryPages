[SECTION] How Expensive Is Cambridge? [/SECTION]
Cambridge is a city in the United Kingdom, known for its prestigious university and historic architecture. Compared to other major cities in the UK, Cambridge is relatively expensive, but still more affordable than London. The cost of living in Cambridge is influenced by its popularity among tourists and students, which drives up prices for accommodation and food. However, the city offers a unique blend of history, culture, and entertainment, making it a worthwhile destination for visitors. Overall, Cambridge is a moderately expensive city, with prices comparable to other major university towns in the UK.

In comparison to other European cities, Cambridge is more expensive than many Eastern European destinations, but less expensive than major Western European cities like Paris or Amsterdam. The city's prices are also influenced by its proximity to London, with many residents commuting to the capital for work or leisure. As a result, Cambridge has a relatively high cost of living, but still offers a range of affordable options for visitors and residents.

- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls, rather than mid-range restaurants
- Avoid visiting during peak tourist season (June-August) to save on accommodation and tourist attractions
- Look for discounts and promotions at local shops and restaurants, especially for students and locals
