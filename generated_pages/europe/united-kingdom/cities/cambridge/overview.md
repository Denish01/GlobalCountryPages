[SECTION] What Is Cambridge? [/SECTION]
Cambridge is a city situated in the county of Cambridgeshire, England, approximately 50 miles north of London. It is a non-metropolitan district and the administrative center of the county, with a rich history dating back to the Roman era. Cambridge is perhaps most famous for being the home of the **University of Cambridge**, one of the world's oldest and most respected institutions of higher learning. The city's scenic riverside, historic colleges, and quaint streets make it a popular tourist destination.

Geographically, Cambridge is positioned in the eastern part of England, near the River Cam, from which it gets its name. The city has a relatively small population, with a diverse economy based on education, technology, and tourism. Cambridge is also known for its vibrant cultural scene, with numerous museums, galleries, and festivals throughout the year. The city's unique blend of history, academia, and innovation makes it a fascinating place to visit or live.

As a city within the United Kingdom, Cambridge is subject to the UK's parliamentary system and is represented in the UK Parliament. The city has its own local government, responsible for providing various services to residents, including transportation, education, and healthcare. Cambridge is also a popular destination for international students, researchers, and tourists, drawn by its world-class academic institutions, cultural attractions, and historic landmarks.
