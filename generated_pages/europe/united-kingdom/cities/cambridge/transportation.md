# Getting Around Cambridge — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Cambridge, you can use a variety of transportation options, including buses, trains, taxis, and ride-hailing services, as well as walk or cycle through the city's historic streets.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Cambridge is easily accessible by air, with several international airports located within a few hours' drive of the city. London Stansted Airport (STN) is the closest major airport, located about 30 miles south of Cambridge, and offers flights to destinations across Europe and beyond. London Heathrow Airport (LHR) is also within reach, located about 60 miles southwest of Cambridge, and offers a wider range of international flights. From both airports, you can take a bus or train to Cambridge, or hire a taxi or ride-hailing service.

If you're traveling overland, Cambridge is connected to the rest of the UK by a network of roads and railways. The M11 motorway runs north-south just west of the city, while the A14 and A10 roads provide connections to the east and west. By train, Cambridge is served by regular services from London King's Cross and Liverpool Street stations, as well as from other major cities across the UK. You can also take a bus or coach from other cities in the UK, with companies such as National Express operating regular services to Cambridge.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
While Cambridge has its own small airport, it does not offer scheduled domestic flights. However, you can fly from other airports in the UK to London Stansted or London Heathrow, and then take a bus or train to Cambridge. If you need to travel within the UK, the train network is generally a more convenient and efficient option, with frequent services connecting Cambridge to other major cities. If you do need to take a domestic flight, you can book with airlines such as British Airways or EasyJet, with prices starting from around $50-$100 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Cambridge has an extensive rail network, with regular services connecting the city to London, Birmingham, and other major cities across the UK. The city's main train station is Cambridge Station, which is located just south of the city center and offers frequent services to London King's Cross and Liverpool Street stations. You can also take a train to other destinations in the UK, including Manchester, Leeds, and Newcastle. The train network is generally reliable and efficient, with booking available online or at the station. Prices vary depending on the route and time of travel, but you can expect to pay around $20-$50 for a one-way ticket to London.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Cambridge is also served by a network of bus and coach services, with companies such as National Express and Stagecoach operating regular services to other cities in the UK. You can take a bus or coach from Cambridge to destinations such as London, Birmingham, and Manchester, with prices starting from around $10-$30 for a one-way ticket. The bus station is located in the city center, and you can book tickets online or at the station. While the bus network is not as extensive as the train network, it can be a more affordable option for shorter journeys.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in the UK, you'll need to have a valid driver's license and insurance, and be at least 17 years old.
- The road quality in Cambridge is generally good, but be aware of narrow streets and pedestrianized areas in the city center.
- Fuel costs are around $1.50-$2.00 per liter, depending on the type of fuel and location.
- Car rental is available in Cambridge, with companies such as Europcar and Hertz operating in the city.
- Be aware of traffic congestion in the city center, particularly during rush hour.
- Parking can be limited in the city center, with many areas restricted to residents or pay-and-display parking.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of peak hours and plan your journey accordingly to avoid congestion.
- Use a **contactless payment card** or mobile payment method to pay for public transport fares.
- Download the **National Rail** or **Transport for London** apps to plan your journey and book tickets.
- Be mindful of **cyclists and pedestrians** when driving, and use designated bike lanes and pedestrian crossings.
- Avoid using **unlicensed taxis** or ride-hailing services, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Consider purchasing a **Cambridge PlusBus** ticket for unlimited bus travel in the city.
- Be prepared for **road closures** and diversions, particularly during peak construction seasons.
- Use **cashless payment methods** whenever possible to avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
