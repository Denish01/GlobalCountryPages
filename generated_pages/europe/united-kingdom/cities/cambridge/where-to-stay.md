# Where to Stay in Cambridge — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Cambridge, the best areas to stay are the city center, Cambridge North, and surrounding neighborhoods like Chesterton and Trumpington, which offer a range of accommodations and easy access to the city's main attractions.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top 5-7 areas/neighborhoods:

**City Centre** (Cambridge)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: The city centre is the heart of Cambridge, with its historic colleges, picturesque riverside, and bustling streets lined with shops and restaurants, offering a unique and unforgettable experience. The area is perfect for those who want to be in the midst of the action and have easy access to the city's main attractions.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to the University of Cambridge, easy access to public transport, and a wide range of dining and entertainment options. The city centre is also home to many of Cambridge's famous colleges, including King's College and Trinity College.
Cons: Can be quite crowded and noisy, especially during peak season, and parking can be a challenge.

**Cambridge North** (Cambridge)
Best For: Business / Families
Vibe: Cambridge North is a vibrant and modern area, with a mix of residential and commercial spaces, including the Cambridge North railway station, which provides easy access to London and other parts of the country. The area is also home to many parks and green spaces, making it a great option for families.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Conveniently located near the railway station, with easy access to the city centre and surrounding areas, and a range of amenities, including shops, restaurants, and leisure facilities. The area is also home to the Cambridge Business Park, making it a great option for business travelers.
Cons: Limited nightlife options, and some areas can be quite industrial.

**Chesterton** (Cambridge)
Best For: Families / Backpackers
Vibe: Chesterton is a charming and laid-back neighborhood, with a mix of old and new buildings, including historic pubs and modern cafes, and a strong sense of community. The area is located just north of the city centre, making it a great option for those who want to be close to the action but also want a more relaxed atmosphere.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, easy access to the city centre, and a range of local amenities, including parks, shops, and restaurants. The area is also home to the River Cam, making it a great option for those who enjoy walking and cycling.
Cons: Limited public transport options, and some areas can be quite residential.

**Trumpington** (Cambridge)
Best For: Families / Luxury
Vibe: Trumpington is a picturesque and affluent neighborhood, with a mix of old and new buildings, including historic mansions and modern apartments, and a strong sense of community. The area is located just south of the city centre, making it a great option for those who want to be close to the action but also want a more relaxed atmosphere.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: High-quality accommodation options, easy access to the city centre, and a range of local amenities, including parks, shops, and restaurants. The area is also home to the Trumpington Meadows, making it a great option for those who enjoy walking and cycling.
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and some areas can be quite residential.

**Mill Road** (Cambridge)
Best For: Backpackers / Students
Vibe: Mill Road is a vibrant and eclectic neighborhood, with a mix of independent shops, cafes, and restaurants, and a strong sense of community. The area is located just east of the city centre, making it a great option for those who want to be close to the action but also want a more bohemian atmosphere.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, easy access to the city centre, and a range of local amenities, including independent shops and cafes. The area is also home to the Mill Road Cemetery, making it a great option for those who enjoy history and culture.
Cons: Can be quite noisy, especially at night, and some areas can be quite run-down.
