[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Cambridge is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year, and it has a reputation for being a safe and welcoming city. The city has a strong police presence, with a dedicated team of officers who patrol the streets and ensure that visitors and locals alike feel safe. However, as with any city, there are some areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take some basic precautions to ensure their safety, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables secure.

Overall, Cambridge is a very safe city, with low crime rates compared to other cities of similar size. Visitors can feel confident walking around the city, even at night, and there are plenty of amenities and services available to support tourists. The city also has a strong sense of community, with many locals happy to offer advice and assistance to visitors. However, it's always important to be aware of your surroundings and take some basic precautions to ensure your safety, such as avoiding walking alone in isolated areas at night and keeping valuables secure.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas, such as the city centre and tourist hotspots
- Bike theft, with many bikes left unlocked in the city
- Scams targeting students, such as fake accommodation or job offers
- Rowdy behaviour and noise pollution in areas with high concentrations of bars and clubs
- Traffic congestion and pedestrian safety, particularly in areas with limited pedestrian crossings
- Cyclist safety, with many cyclists on the roads and some areas with limited bike lanes
- Begging and panhandling in the city centre, with some individuals being aggressive or persistent

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Cambridge, but it's always a good idea to ensure you're up to date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Cambridge, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it
- **Medical Facilities:** Cambridge has a range of high-quality medical facilities, including Addenbrooke's Hospital and several smaller clinics and surgeries
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are plentiful in Cambridge, with many located in the city centre and surrounding areas

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas or at night
- Keep valuables secure, such as using a hotel safe or a money belt
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-sharing services
- Keep your bike locked and secure, and consider using a bike lock with a good reputation
- Be mindful of traffic and pedestrian safety, particularly in areas with limited pedestrian crossings
- Keep your belongings close and secure in crowded areas, such as the city centre and tourist hotspots

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Cambridge is generally a very safe city for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong police presence
- However, it's still important to take some basic precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings and keeping valuables secure
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, particularly at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Many bars and clubs in Cambridge have a strong focus on safety and security, with measures in place to protect solo and women travelers, such as secure entry and exit systems and staff trained to deal with incidents of harassment or assault
