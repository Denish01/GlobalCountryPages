The historic city of Cambridge offers a plethora of exciting activities and attractions to explore, from world-renowned universities and museums to picturesque riverside walks and traditional English pubs.

Unique Experiences

  • Take a punting tour along the River Cam to see the city's famous colleges and bridges from a unique perspective
  • Attend a formal hall dinner at one of the universities to experience traditional Cambridge dining
  • Explore the Cambridge University Botanic Garden, a tranquil oasis in the heart of the city
  • Visit the Orchard Tea Gardens, a charming spot for afternoon tea and delicious cakes
  • Go on a ghost tour of Cambridge to discover the city's spooky side and haunted history
  • Take a stargazing evening at the Cambridge University Observatory to explore the night sky

Outdoor & Nature

  • Grantchester Meadows, a beautiful spot for walking and picnicking along the River Cam
  • Cambridge University Botanic Garden, home to over 8,000 plant species and a peaceful escape from the city
  • Wandlebury Country Park, a scenic area for hiking and exploring the countryside
  • Milton Country Park, a former quarry turned nature reserve with walking trails and wildlife spotting opportunities
  • River Great Ouse, a tranquil spot for boat trips and fishing

Cultural Experiences

  • Cambridge Philharmonic Orchestra, a world-class orchestra performing at various venues around the city
  • Cambridge Arts Theatre, a popular spot for live performances, including theater, dance, and music
  • Museum of Classical Archaeology, a unique collection of plaster casts of ancient Greek and Roman sculptures
  • Kettle's Yard, a historic house turned art gallery showcasing an impressive collection of modern and contemporary art
  • The Polar Museum, a fascinating museum dedicated to the history of polar exploration and the British Antarctic Territory

Hidden Gems

  • The Round Church, a historic church with a unique circular design and stunning stained-glass windows
  • The Leper Chapel, a medieval chapel with a fascinating history and peaceful atmosphere
  • The Cambridge American Cemetery, a moving memorial to the American soldiers who lost their lives in World War II
  • The Fitzwilliam Museum's Courtauld Gallery, a lesser-known collection of Impressionist and modern art
  • The Cambridge University Library's manuscripts room, a treasure trove of rare books and historical documents

Day Trips

  • London, 64 miles, 1 hour by train, visit iconic landmarks like Buckingham Palace and the British Museum
  • Ely, 16 miles, 30 minutes by train, explore the historic cathedral city and its picturesque riverside
  • Oxford, 128 miles, 2.5 hours by train, visit the famous university city and its stunning architecture
  • Peterborough, 37 miles, 45 minutes by train, discover the city's rich history and cultural attractions
  • Newmarket, 20 miles, 30 minutes by train, visit the famous horse racing town and its beautiful countryside

Planning Tips

Suggested itineraries:

- 3-day highlights: explore the city centre, visit King's College Chapel, and take a punting tour

- 7-day comprehensive: add visits to the Fitzwilliam Museum, Cambridge University Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology, and a day trip to Ely

- 14-day deep exploration: include visits to the surrounding countryside, Wandlebury Country Park, and the Cambridge American Cemetery, and take time to stroll along the River Cam and explore the city's many colleges and gardens

Key tips: book accommodations and popular attractions in advance, especially during peak season, and prioritize your must-see sights to make the most of your time in Cambridge.
