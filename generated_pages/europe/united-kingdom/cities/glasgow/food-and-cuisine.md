# Glasgow Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Glasgow, be sure to try the city's unique blend of traditional Scottish cuisine with modern twists, featuring popular dishes such as **Cullen Skink**, **Scotch Pie**, and **Aberdeen Angus Steak**.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: potatoes, seafood, beef, lamb
National Dish: **Haggis**, a savory pudding made from sheep's heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with onions, oatmeal, and spices
Meal Times: 7-10 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 5-8 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 10-15% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: vegetarian-friendly options available, some restaurants offer halal and kosher options
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Glasgow's cuisine is shaped by its Scottish heritage, with a focus on local ingredients such as seafood, beef, and lamb. The city's proximity to the sea and its rich farmland have contributed to the development of a distinct food culture, with popular dishes often featuring fresh seafood, high-quality meats, and hearty vegetables. Glasgow's cuisine has also been influenced by its cultural exchanges with other parts of the United Kingdom, as well as international cuisines, resulting in a diverse range of dining options.

The city's food scene is characterized by its warm and welcoming atmosphere, with many restaurants and cafes serving traditional Scottish dishes with a modern twist. From classic **Full Scottish Breakfast** to elegant **High Tea**, Glasgow offers a unique dining experience that showcases the best of Scottish cuisine. The city is also home to a number of food festivals and events throughout the year, celebrating its rich culinary heritage and promoting local food producers.

Glasgow's regional variations are also worth exploring, with the city's West End and East End offering distinct culinary experiences. The West End is known for its upscale restaurants and bars, while the East End is home to a number of traditional pubs and cafes serving classic Scottish fare. Visitors can also explore the city's many markets and food halls, which offer a wide range of local specialties and artisanal products.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Tipping**: it is customary to tip 10-15% of the total bill in restaurants and bars
- **Table Manners**: it is considered polite to keep your hands visible on the table, and to not start eating until everyone has been served
- **Reservation Culture**: it is recommended to make a reservation in advance, especially for popular restaurants
- **Dress Code**: the dress code in Glasgow is generally casual, but it is recommended to dress up for nicer restaurants
- **Eating Etiquette**: it is considered polite to try a little of each dish, and to not leave the table until everyone has finished eating
- **Payment**: it is common to pay with credit or debit card, but it is also acceptable to pay with cash
