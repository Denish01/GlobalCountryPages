# London Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting London, be sure to try the city's iconic dishes, such as **Fish and Chips**, **Bangers and Mash**, and **Full English Breakfast**, which showcase the city's rich culinary heritage. 

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: beef, lamb, fish, potatoes
National Dish: Fish and Chips, battered and fried fish served with crispy chips (fries) and often accompanied by mushy peas
Meal Times: 7-10 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 6-9 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 10-15% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: London offers a wide range of halal, kosher, and vegetarian options, with many restaurants clearly labeling their menus
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
London's cuisine is a unique blend of traditional British dishes, influenced by the city's history of immigration and colonialism. The city's food scene is shaped by its **multicultural** identity, with a wide range of international cuisines, from Indian and Chinese to Italian and Greek, available throughout the city. London's culinary landscape is also defined by its **regional variations**, with different areas of the city specializing in specific types of cuisine, such as **Brick Lane** for Indian food and **Chinatown** for Chinese cuisine. The city's food culture is also characterized by its **pub culture**, with many traditional pubs serving classic British dishes, such as **Sunday Roast** and **Ploughman's Lunch**.

London's cuisine has been influenced by its **historical trade routes**, with the city's proximity to the sea making it an important hub for the importation of spices, tea, and other exotic ingredients. This has resulted in the development of unique **fusion dishes**, such as **Chicken Tikka Masala**, which combines Indian spices with British chicken. The city's food scene is also shaped by its **seasonal produce**, with many restaurants and markets showcasing the best of British ingredients, such as **asparagus** and **strawberries**.

In recent years, London's food scene has undergone a significant transformation, with the rise of **food trucks** and **street food markets**. These have brought a new level of diversity and creativity to the city's culinary landscape, with many innovative and experimental dishes on offer. From **traditional pies** to **modern vegan cuisine**, London's food scene has something to offer every taste and budget.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Table Manners**: it is customary to keep your hands visible on the table and to not leave the table until everyone has finished eating
- **Tipping**: it is customary to tip 10-15% of the total bill in restaurants and bars
- **Reservations**: it is recommended to make reservations in advance, especially for popular restaurants
- **Dress Code**: the dress code varies depending on the restaurant, but it is generally recommended to dress smartly for evening meals
- **Eating Etiquette**: it is customary to wait for the host to indicate where to sit and to not start eating until everyone has been served
- **Paying the Bill**: it is customary to pay the bill at the end of the meal, and to not split the bill unless agreed upon in advance
