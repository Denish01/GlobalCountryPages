# Top Things to Do in Edinburgh — Attractions & Experiences

Edinburgh, the capital city of Scotland, is a must-visit destination that offers a wide range of historical, cultural, and entertainment activities, and some of the top things to do include visiting the famous Edinburgh Castle, exploring the Royal Mile, and attending the Edinburgh Festival Fringe.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Ghost Tour** of the city's haunted underground vaults and alleyways
- Visit the **Royal Edinburgh Military Tattoo**, a unique display of military precision and pageantry
- Explore the **Mary King's Close**, a hidden network of 17th-century streets and homes
- Attend a **Traditional Scottish Ceilidh**, a lively evening of music, dance, and storytelling
- Go on a **Whisky Tasting Tour**, sampling some of Scotland's finest spirits
- Take a stroll through the **Princes Street Gardens**, a beautiful public park in the heart of the city
- Visit the **Scottish Storytelling Centre**, a hub for traditional storytelling and live performances

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Holyrood Park**, a large public park featuring several hills, lochs, and walking trails
- **Arthur's Seat**, a dormant volcano offering breathtaking views of the city
- **The Water of Leith**, a scenic river walk that runs through the heart of the city
- **The Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh**, a beautiful botanical garden featuring a vast array of plant species
- **The Pentland Hills**, a range of hills just outside the city, perfect for hiking and outdoor activities
- **The Firth of Forth**, a scenic estuary that offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding coastline

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **The National Museum of Scotland**, featuring a vast collection of Scottish and international artifacts
- **The Scottish National Gallery**, housing an impressive collection of Scottish and international art
- **The Museum of Childhood**, showcasing a collection of toys, games, and childhood memorabilia
- **The Royal Lyceum Theatre**, a historic theater that hosts a variety of productions throughout the year
- **The Usher Hall**, a stunning concert hall that hosts a range of musical performances
- **The Scottish Storytelling Centre**, a hub for traditional storytelling and live performances

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **The Summerhall Arts Venue**, a former veterinary school turned arts center, featuring a range of exhibitions and performances
- **The Georgian House**, a beautifully restored 18th-century townhouse that offers a glimpse into Edinburgh's past
- **The Museum of Edinburgh**, a small museum that showcases the city's history and culture
- **The Gilmerton Cove**, a network of underground tunnels and chambers that date back to the 18th century
- **The Stockbridge Market**, a bustling marketplace that offers a range of fresh produce, crafts, and street food

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- **St. Andrews**, a historic town located about 50 miles from Edinburgh, famous for its stunning cathedral and world-class golf courses (distance: 50 miles, travel time: 1 hour)
- **The Scottish Highlands**, a scenic region located about 100 miles from Edinburgh, featuring stunning landscapes, lochs, and glens (distance: 100 miles, travel time: 2 hours)
- **The Isle of May**, a small island located off the coast of Edinburgh, featuring a stunning lighthouse and a range of wildlife (distance: 10 miles, travel time: 1 hour by boat)
- **The Rosslyn Chapel**, a historic chapel located about 10 miles from Edinburgh, famous for its stunning architecture and connections to the Knights Templar (distance: 10 miles, travel time: 30 minutes)
- **The North Berwick**, a charming seaside town located about 30 miles from Edinburgh, featuring a range of beaches, golf courses, and outdoor activities (distance: 30 miles, travel time: 1 hour)
