# Bristol Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Bristol, be sure to try some of the city's famous dishes, such as the **Bristol Cream** and the **Pie and Mash**, which showcase the city's unique blend of traditional British cuisine with international influences.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: potatoes, fish, meat, vegetables
National Dish: Sunday Roast with roasted meat, vegetables, and gravy
Meal Times: 7-10 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 6-9 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 10-15% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: vegetarian-friendly, gluten-free options available

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Bristol's cuisine is defined by its rich maritime history and cultural diversity, with a focus on fresh, locally-sourced ingredients. The city's food scene is heavily influenced by its **West Country** roots, with traditional dishes such as **Cream Teas** and **Pasties** still popular today. Bristol is also known for its vibrant street food scene, with a variety of international cuisines, including **Indian**, **Chinese**, and **Mexican**. The city's proximity to the sea also means that fresh seafood is a staple of many Bristol restaurants.

Bristol's food culture is also shaped by its history as a major port city, with many international trade routes passing through the city. This has resulted in a unique blend of culinary influences, with dishes such as **Jamaican Jerk Chicken** and **Thai Green Curry** sitting alongside traditional British fare. The city is also home to a number of **food festivals** throughout the year, celebrating everything from **local produce** to **craft beer**.

From traditional **pub grub** to high-end **fine dining**, Bristol's food scene has something to offer every taste and budget. Whether you're looking for a hearty **Sunday Roast** or a quick **street food snack**, the city's diverse range of eateries is sure to satisfy.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Tipping**: it is customary to tip 10-15% of the total bill in restaurants and bars
- **Eating Etiquette**: it is considered polite to wait for the host to indicate where to sit and to keep your hands visible on the table
- **Reservation Culture**: it is a good idea to book a table in advance, especially on weekends or during peak holiday seasons
- **Dress Code**: dress codes vary depending on the restaurant, but it is generally a good idea to dress smartly for evening meals
- **Table Manners**: it is considered impolite to eat with your hands or to leave the table without excusing yourself
- **Payment**: it is customary to pay the bill at the end of the meal, rather than as you go
