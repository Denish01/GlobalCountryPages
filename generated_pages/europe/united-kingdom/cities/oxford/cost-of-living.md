[SECTION] How Expensive Is Oxford? [/SECTION]
Oxford is a city in the United Kingdom known for its prestigious university and high standard of living, which contributes to its moderate to high cost of living. Compared to other major cities in the UK, such as London, Oxford is slightly more affordable, but still more expensive than many other cities in the country. The cost of living in Oxford is driven by factors such as housing, food, and transportation, which can be relatively high. However, the city's unique cultural and academic attractions, as well as its proximity to London, make it an attractive destination for tourists and students alike.

In terms of global averages, Oxford is more expensive than many cities in Europe, but less expensive than some of the major cities in Western Europe, such as Paris or Zurich. The city's cost of living is also influenced by its popularity among tourists, which can drive up prices for accommodations and other services during peak travel seasons.
