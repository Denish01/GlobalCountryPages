[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
The United Kingdom has a well-developed transportation network, with a wide range of options available to get around, including public transportation, taxis, ride-hailing services, and car rentals. The quality of public transit is generally high, with efficient and reliable services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in the UK get around using public transportation, with buses and trains being the most popular modes of transport. However, the cost of transportation can vary significantly depending on the location, with cities like London tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas.

The UK's transportation network is also well-connected to other European countries, with frequent flights, trains, and buses available to major destinations. The country's road network is also well-developed, with a comprehensive system of motorways, highways, and local roads. However, driving in the UK can be challenging, especially for visitors who are not used to driving on the left-hand side of the road. Additionally, traffic congestion is a common problem in major cities, especially during rush hour.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider purchasing a **visitor oyster card** for convenient and discounted travel on public transportation in London.
- Use **ride-hailing apps** like Uber or Bolt for convenient and affordable transportation, especially at night or in areas with limited public transportation.
- **Negotiate taxi fares** before you start your journey, especially for longer distances or airport transfers.
- Take advantage of **discounted train tickets** by booking in advance or using a **railcard** for frequent train travel.
- Be aware of **traffic congestion** and plan your journey accordingly, especially during rush hour or in major cities.
- Use **parking apps** to find affordable and convenient parking options in cities, and consider using **park and ride** services for easier access to public transportation.
