# Education Costs in Belarus

The cost of education in Belarus varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools charge tuition fees ranging from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Belarus is divided into public and private institutions, with the public sector being the dominant provider of education. Public schools in Belarus are generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on **mathematics**, **science**, and **language**. The language of instruction is primarily **Belarusian**, but **Russian** is also widely used, especially in urban areas. Compulsory education in Belarus lasts for 11 years, starting from the age of 6 and ending at the age of 17. International schools are available in major cities, such as Minsk, and offer a range of curricula, including **IB**, **British**, and **American** programs.

The quality of education in Belarus is generally considered to be good, with a strong focus on academic achievement. However, the education system has been criticized for being overly centralized and lacking in flexibility. Private schools in Belarus offer an alternative to public education, with many providing a more **liberal** and **student-centered** approach to learning. International schools in Belarus cater to the needs of expatriate families and offer a range of curricula and programs. Despite the availability of international schools, the majority of students in Belarus attend public schools, which are generally considered to be of good quality.
