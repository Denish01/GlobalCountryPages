[SECTION] Overview: Belarus vs Poland [/SECTION]
Belarus and Poland are two neighboring countries in Eastern Europe with distinct economic profiles. Belarus has a relatively low GDP per capita and a higher inflation rate compared to Poland, which has a more developed economy and a higher standard of living. Despite these differences, Belarus offers a lower cost of living, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle. The cost of living in Belarus is around 20-30% lower than in Poland, with significant savings on rent, food, and transportation.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in their cost of living. Belarus has a more centralized economy, with a strong emphasis on state-owned enterprises, while Poland has a more liberalized economy with a growing private sector. This difference in economic structure contributes to the variation in prices and costs between the two countries. Overall, Belarus offers a more affordable lifestyle, but Poland provides a more developed infrastructure and a higher standard of living.
