[SECTION] Overview: Belarus vs Russia [/SECTION]
Belarus and Russia are two neighboring countries with distinct economic profiles. Belarus has a smaller economy with a GDP per capita of $20,100, while Russia has a larger economy with a GDP per capita of $27,400. The inflation rate in Belarus is slightly higher, at 5.5%, compared to Russia's 3.8%. In terms of cost of living, Belarus is generally cheaper than Russia, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. The overall cost of living in Belarus is approximately 15-20% lower than in Russia, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Belarus has a more controlled economy, with a strong emphasis on state-owned enterprises, while Russia has a more diversified economy with a mix of state-owned and private enterprises. This difference in economic structure contributes to the varying costs of living in the two countries. Additionally, the exchange rates between the Belarusian ruble and the Russian ruble can also impact the cost of living, especially for foreigners. Overall, Belarus offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower costs for everyday expenses, making it an attractive option for those looking to stretch their budget.
