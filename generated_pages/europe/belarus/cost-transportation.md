The cost of transportation in Belarus varies, but on average, a monthly transit pass can cost around $15-$20 USD, while a taxi start rate can range from $1-$2 USD, and fuel prices are approximately $0.60-$0.80 USD per liter.

[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Getting around Belarus is relatively easy, with a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses, minivans, and commuter trains. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent departures and relatively low fares. Most people in Belarus use public transportation to get around, with buses and minivans being the most popular modes of transport. The country's capital, Minsk, has a comprehensive public transportation system that includes a metro system, making it easy to navigate the city.

In addition to public transportation, taxis and ride-hailing services are widely available in Belarus, with Yandex Taxi and Uber being the most popular options. The country's roads are generally in good condition, making it easy to get around by car, although parking can be a challenge in larger cities. Overall, Belarus has a well-developed transportation system that makes it easy to get around, whether you're using public transportation, taxis, or driving a car.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
