[SECTION] Overview: Belarus vs Lithuania [/SECTION]
Belarus and Lithuania are two neighboring countries in Eastern Europe, with distinct economic contexts and cost of living profiles. Belarus has a lower GDP per capita and a higher inflation rate compared to Lithuania, which translates to lower prices for goods and services. The overall cost of living in Belarus is around 20-30% lower than in Lithuania, making it an attractive option for those looking for an affordable European lifestyle. The economic context of the two countries is also different, with Belarus having a more state-controlled economy and Lithuania being a member of the European Union and the Eurozone.

The cost of living difference between Belarus and Lithuania is noticeable in various aspects, including rent, food, transportation, and utilities. Belarus offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation, making it an ideal destination for expats, students, and retirees. However, Lithuania offers a more modern and Western-style lifestyle, with better infrastructure and a more developed economy. The choice between the two countries ultimately depends on individual preferences and priorities, but for those looking for an affordable and authentic European experience, Belarus may be the better option.
