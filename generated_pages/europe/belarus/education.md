[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Belarus is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, also known as basic education, lasts for four years and is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 10. Secondary education, which includes general secondary education and vocational training, lasts for seven years and is also compulsory. Tertiary education, which includes universities and colleges, provides students with a wide range of academic programs and specializations. The quality of education in Belarus is generally considered to be high, with a strong emphasis on **theoretical knowledge** and **practical skills**. Most schools in Belarus are public, but there are also a number of private schools and universities that offer a range of academic programs.

The education system in Belarus is highly centralized, with the Ministry of Education responsible for overseeing the entire education system. This has both positive and negative effects, as it ensures a high level of consistency and quality across the country, but also limits the autonomy of individual schools and universities. In recent years, there has been a growing trend towards **internationalization** and **modernization** of the education system, with a focus on developing **critical thinking** and **problem-solving skills**.

The public education system in Belarus is generally considered to be of high quality, with well-trained teachers and modern facilities. However, there are some challenges, including a lack of **diversity** and **flexibility** in the curriculum, as well as limited opportunities for **student mobility** and **international exchange**. Despite these challenges, the education system in Belarus remains one of the strongest in the region, with a high level of **academic achievement** and a strong reputation for producing well-qualified graduates.
