# Liechtenstein Tax Guide — Income, Corporate & VAT Rates

The tax rates in Liechtenstein range from 1% to 8% for income tax, with a corporate tax rate of 12.5% and a value-added tax (VAT) rate of 7.7%.

[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
Liechtenstein has a **territorial taxation** system, meaning that only income derived from sources within the country is subject to tax. However, individuals who are considered tax residents of Liechtenstein are subject to **worldwide taxation**, meaning that their global income is taxable in Liechtenstein. To be considered a tax resident, an individual must have their **domicile** or **habitual abode** in Liechtenstein. The country has a relatively low tax environment, making it an attractive location for individuals and businesses.

The tax system in Liechtenstein is designed to be efficient and business-friendly, with a focus on attracting foreign investment and promoting economic growth. The country has a network of **double taxation treaties** with over 60 countries, which helps to prevent double taxation and fiscal evasion. Additionally, Liechtenstein has a **flat corporate tax rate** of 12.5%, which applies to all companies, regardless of their size or industry.

[SECTION] Corporate & Business Tax [/SECTION]
- The **corporate tax rate** in Liechtenstein is 12.5%, which applies to all companies, regardless of their size or industry.
- Small businesses with annual turnover of less than CHF 100,000 are exempt from **value-added tax (VAT)**.
- Liechtenstein has several **free zones**, including the Vaduz Free Zone and the Balzers Free Zone, which offer tax exemptions and other incentives to companies that locate there.
- Companies that establish a presence in Liechtenstein must **register with the Commercial Register** and obtain a **tax identification number**.
- Companies that are considered **foreign-controlled** may be subject to **withholding tax** on dividends, interest, and royalties.

[SECTION] VAT / Sales Tax [/SECTION]
- The **standard VAT rate** in Liechtenstein is 7.7%, which applies to most goods and services.
- A **reduced VAT rate** of 2.5% applies to certain goods and services, including food, books, and hotel accommodations.
- **Exemptions** from VAT apply to certain goods and services, including healthcare, education, and financial services.
- Liechtenstein has a **tourist refund scheme**, which allows tourists to claim a refund of VAT on certain purchases.

- Liechtenstein has a network of **double taxation treaties** with over 60 countries, which helps to prevent double taxation and fiscal evasion.
- Expats and foreign workers who are tax residents of Liechtenstein are required to **register with the tax authorities** and obtain a **tax identification number**.

- Expats and foreign workers who are not tax residents of Liechtenstein may be subject to **withholding tax** on income derived from Liechtenstein sources.
- Expats and foreign workers who are tax residents of Liechtenstein may be required to **file a tax return** and report their worldwide income.
