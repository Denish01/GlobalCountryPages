[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Liechtenstein is a small, sovereign country located in Central Europe, nestled between Austria and Switzerland. It sits in the **Rhine Valley**, where the Rhine River flows and forms part of its western border. The country's unique location, surrounded by the **Alps** to the east and the **Swiss Plateau** to the west, makes it a fascinating destination for travelers. With a total area of approximately 160 square kilometers, Liechtenstein is one of the smallest countries in the world, both in terms of population and land area.

The country's borders are shared with two neighboring countries: Austria to the east and Switzerland to the west. The border with Austria stretches for about 34 kilometers, while the border with Switzerland is approximately 41 kilometers long. Liechtenstein's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe makes it an important hub for trade and commerce. The country's proximity to major European cities, such as Zurich and Innsbruck, also makes it an attractive destination for tourists and business travelers alike.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Liechtenstein's terrain is characterized by a mix of **mountains**, **hills**, and **valleys**. The country's landscape is dominated by the **Rhine Valley**, which runs along its western border, and the **Alps**, which rise up to the east. The **Grauspitz**, the country's highest point, is located in the **Rätikon** mountain range, which forms part of the **Alps**. The **Rhine River**, which flows through the valley, provides a source of water and has played a crucial role in shaping the country's landscape over time.

The country's landscape is also dotted with **forests**, which cover about 43% of its total area. These forests, which are primarily composed of **coniferous trees**, provide a habitat for a wide range of **wildlife**, including **deer**, **wild boar**, and **birds of prey**. In addition to its natural beauty, Liechtenstein's landscape has also been shaped by human activity, with many **castles**, **fortresses**, and **villages** scattered throughout the country.

The **climate** and **geology** of Liechtenstein have also played a significant role in shaping its landscape. The country's **temperate climate**, with cold winters and warm summers, has created a diverse range of **ecosystems**, from **alpine meadows** to **deciduous forests**. The country's **geology**, which is characterized by a mix of **limestone**, **sandstone**, and **granite**, has also created a range of **landforms**, including **caves**, **gorges**, and **waterfalls**.
