[SECTION] What Is Liechtenstein? [/SECTION]
Liechtenstein is a **sovereign** country located in Central Europe, bordered by Austria to the east and Switzerland to the west. It is a small, independent nation with a unique history and culture. The country is nestled in the Rhine Valley, with the Rhine River forming its western border. Liechtenstein is known for its stunning natural beauty, with mountains, forests, and picturesque villages. The country has a strong economy, with a high standard of living and a thriving financial sector.

Liechtenstein is a **constitutional monarchy**, with the Prince of Liechtenstein serving as head of state. The country has a parliamentary system, with a unicameral parliament known as the Landtag. The capital city, Vaduz, is the seat of government and the largest city in the country. Liechtenstein is a member of the European Free Trade Association (EFTA) and has close ties with neighboring countries, particularly Switzerland.

Despite its small size, Liechtenstein has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on **art**, **music**, and **literature**. The country is home to several museums, galleries, and festivals, showcasing the work of local and international artists. The Liechtenstein National Museum, located in Vaduz, features a collection of artifacts and exhibits on the country's history, culture, and natural environment.
