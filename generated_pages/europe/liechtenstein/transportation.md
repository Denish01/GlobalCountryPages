# Getting Around Liechtenstein — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Liechtenstein, you can use a combination of buses, trains, and walking, as the country has a well-developed public transportation system, with the added option of driving or using ride-hailing services.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Liechtenstein does not have its own international airport, but the closest major airport is Zurich Airport (ZRH) in Switzerland, which is about 115 kilometers from Vaduz, the capital of Liechtenstein. From Zurich Airport, you can take a train or bus to Liechtenstein. Another option is to fly into Friedrichshafen Airport (FDH) in Germany, which is about 140 kilometers from Vaduz. Direct flights to Zurich Airport are available from major European cities, and from there, you can take a train or bus to Liechtenstein. Overland entry points to Liechtenstein include borders with Austria and Switzerland, where you can drive or take a bus.

The main entry points by bus are the border crossings with Austria and Switzerland, where you can take a bus from cities like Vienna, Zurich, or St. Gallen. The bus journey from Zurich Airport to Vaduz takes around 1-2 hours, depending on traffic. You can also take a train from Zurich Airport to Sargans or Buchs in Switzerland, and then transfer to a bus to Vaduz. The train journey from Zurich Airport to Sargans takes around 1 hour, and from there, you can take a bus to Vaduz, which takes around 30-40 minutes.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Since Liechtenstein is a small country with a limited domestic air network, there are no domestic flights available. However, you can take a helicopter tour over the country, which offers a unique perspective on the landscape. These tours are usually booked in advance and can be quite expensive, with prices starting from around $200 per person for a short tour.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Liechtenstein has a moderate rail network, with connections to Austria and Switzerland. The main train station is in Schaan, which is the largest town in Liechtenstein, and from there, you can take trains to Vienna, Zurich, or other destinations in the region. The train network is operated by the Österreichische Bundesbahnen (ÖBB) and the Schweizerische Bundesbahnen (SBB), and you can book tickets online or at the train station. The train journey from Schaan to Vienna takes around 4-5 hours, and to Zurich takes around 1-2 hours. High-speed trains are available from Zurich to other destinations in Europe.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
The bus network in Liechtenstein is well-developed, with connections to Austria and Switzerland. The main bus company is LIEmobil, which operates buses within Liechtenstein and to neighboring countries. You can book tickets online or at the bus station, and the prices are generally affordable, with a single ticket costing around $2-5. The bus journey from Vaduz to Zurich takes around 1-2 hours, and to Vienna takes around 4-5 hours.
