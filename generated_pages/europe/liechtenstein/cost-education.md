The cost of education in Liechtenstein varies depending on the type of school and institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Liechtenstein is highly regarded and offers a high-quality education to its students. The system is divided into public and private schools, with public schools being free or subsidized for all students, regardless of their nationality. The language of instruction is German, but many schools also offer classes in English and other languages. Compulsory education lasts for 11 years, from age 6 to 17, and is divided into primary and secondary education. International schools are also available in Liechtenstein, offering a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the British curriculum.

The quality of education in Liechtenstein is generally high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and vocational training. Public schools are well-funded and offer a wide range of subjects and extracurricular activities. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a more personalized education and often have smaller class sizes. International schools cater to the needs of expat families and offer a range of curricula and languages. Overall, the education system in Liechtenstein provides students with a solid foundation for future academic and professional success.
