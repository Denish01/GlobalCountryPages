[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Liechtenstein is a small country with a well-developed public transportation system, making it easy to get around without a car. The main transportation modes in Liechtenstein are buses and trains, with an extensive network of routes covering the entire country. The quality of public transit is high, with frequent and reliable services. Most people in Liechtenstein use public transportation or walk and cycle, as the country is relatively small and compact.

The capital city, Vaduz, has a comprehensive bus network with several routes covering the city and its surroundings. The buses are modern and comfortable, with frequent departures throughout the day. In addition to buses, Liechtenstein also has a commuter train service that connects the country to neighboring Switzerland and Austria. The train service is efficient and convenient, with regular departures from the main stations in Vaduz and other towns.
