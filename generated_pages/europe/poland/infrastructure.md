[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Poland's infrastructure has undergone significant development in recent years, with major investments in transportation, energy, and telecommunications. The country has made significant progress in modernizing its infrastructure, with a focus on improving the quality of roads, railways, and airports. However, there is still a notable gap between urban and rural areas, with rural areas often lacking access to high-quality infrastructure. The government has implemented various initiatives to address this issue, including investments in rural road networks and broadband internet access.

Poland's infrastructure development is driven by its strategic location at the heart of Europe, making it an important hub for trade and commerce. The country has invested heavily in its transportation infrastructure, including the construction of new highways, railways, and airports. This has improved connectivity with neighboring countries and facilitated the growth of international trade. Additionally, Poland has made significant progress in developing its energy sector, with a focus on increasing the use of renewable energy sources and reducing dependence on fossil fuels.

The country's infrastructure development is also driven by its growing economy, which has led to an increase in demand for high-quality infrastructure. Poland has attracted significant foreign investment in recent years, particularly in the areas of manufacturing, logistics, and technology. This has created a need for modern and efficient infrastructure, including transportation networks, energy systems, and telecommunications.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]
- Poland's energy sector is dominated by **coal**, which accounts for approximately 70% of the country's electricity generation.
- The country is working to reduce its dependence on fossil fuels and increase the use of **renewable energy sources**, such as wind and solar power.
- Poland's energy infrastructure is relatively reliable, with a low frequency of power outages.
- The country is investing in the development of new energy technologies, including **nuclear power** and **biomass**.
- Poland is also working to improve energy efficiency, with a focus on reducing energy consumption and promoting the use of **energy-efficient technologies**.

[SECTION] Water & Sanitation [/SECTION]

- Sanitation coverage is high, with approximately 95% of the population having access to modern sanitation facilities.
