Cultural Treasures

  • The Mont Orgueil Castle is a 13th-century fortress that now serves as a museum and cultural center, showcasing the island's rich history and heritage.
  • The Jersey Museum and Art Gallery features a collection of artifacts and artworks that tell the story of the island's history and culture, including the Gerald Durrell Wildlife Conservation Trust.
  • The Battle of Flowers is a popular annual festival that celebrates the island's floral heritage, with elaborate floats and displays of flowers and foliage.
  • The Jersey Folk Lore tradition is kept alive through music, dance, and storytelling, with the Jersey Folk Lore Society working to preserve and promote the island's unique cultural heritage.
  • The St. Brelade's Church is a historic parish church that dates back to the 12th century and features stunning stained-glass windows and intricate stone carvings.

  • The island's vibrant events calendar, which includes festivals like the Jersey Live Music Festival and the Jersey International Air Display, offers something for everyone.
  • The range of outdoor activities, including hiking, cycling, and water sports, makes Jersey an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
