[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Jersey is a small island with a well-developed public transportation system, making it easy to get around without a car. The main transportation modes on the island include buses, taxis, and bicycles, with most people relying on buses as their primary mode of transport. The quality of public transit in Jersey is generally good, with frequent bus services connecting major towns and villages. However, the island's compact size and scenic roads also make it an ideal destination for cyclists and walkers.

The public transportation system in Jersey is operated by LibertyBus, which offers a comprehensive network of bus routes covering most parts of the island. The buses are modern, comfortable, and frequent, making it easy to get around without a car. In addition to buses, taxis are also widely available, with many taxi companies operating on the island. For those who prefer to drive, Jersey has a well-maintained road network, and car rental companies are available at the airport and in major towns.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]

- Download the LibertyBus app to plan your bus journey and track bus times in real-time.
- Be aware that parking spaces are limited in Saint Helier, and parking fees can be high, so consider using public transport or parking outside the city center.
- When using taxis, always check the meter is switched on and agree on the fare before starting your journey.
- Consider renting a bicycle or electric bike to explore the island, which can be a cost-effective and environmentally friendly way to get around.
- Be mindful of Jersey's narrow roads and scenic routes, especially when driving or cycling, and always follow local traffic rules and regulations.
