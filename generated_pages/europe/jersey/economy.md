[SECTION] Economic Overview [/SECTION]
Jersey's economy is classified as a high-income economy, with a high standard of living and a strong focus on financial services. The island's economy has developed significantly over the years, with a shift from traditional industries such as agriculture and manufacturing to more service-oriented sectors. The financial services sector, which includes banking, insurance, and investment, is a significant contributor to the island's economy, with many international companies having a presence on the island. The economy is also driven by a highly skilled and educated workforce, with a strong emphasis on innovation and entrepreneurship.

The island's economic development is overseen by the States of Jersey, which has implemented policies to promote economic growth, stability, and diversification. The government has also invested in infrastructure, including transportation networks, energy, and telecommunications, to support business and economic activity. Jersey's economy is also closely linked to the UK economy, with the island using the British pound as its currency and having a customs union with the UK.

Jersey's economy has a strong track record of stability and low unemployment, with a highly skilled and adaptable workforce. The island has a favorable business environment, with a low tax regime and a range of incentives to attract businesses and investment. The government has also implemented policies to promote innovation and entrepreneurship, including support for start-ups and small businesses.
