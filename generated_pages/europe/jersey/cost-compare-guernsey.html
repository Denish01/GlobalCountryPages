Overview: Jersey vs Guernsey

Jersey and Guernsey are two British Crown dependencies in the English Channel, known for their stunning coastlines, mild climate, and high standard of living. While both islands offer a unique blend of British and French culture, they have distinct economic profiles. Jersey has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on financial services, tourism, and e-commerce, which contributes to its higher cost of living. Guernsey, on the other hand, has a more traditional economy, with a focus on fishing, agriculture, and light industry. As a result, Guernsey tends to be more affordable than Jersey, with lower prices for rent, food, and transportation.

The cost of living difference between Jersey and Guernsey is significant, with Jersey being around 10-15% more expensive. This is largely due to Jersey's higher rent prices, which can range from $1,500 to $3,000 per month for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center. Guernsey, on the other hand, offers more affordable rent options, with prices ranging from $1,000 to $2,000 per month for a similar apartment. Additionally, Jersey's higher transportation costs, including fuel and taxi fares, contribute to its higher overall cost of living. However, both islands offer a high quality of life, with excellent healthcare, education, and recreational facilities, making them attractive destinations for expats, students, and retirees.

Where Each Country Wins

Bottom Line

For expats, students, and retirees, Guernsey may be a better choice due to its lower cost of living and more relaxed pace of life.
