What Is Jersey?

Jersey is a British Crown dependency located in the English Channel, off the coast of France. It is the largest of the Channel Islands, with a population of approximately 0.11 million people. The island is known for its stunning beaches, mild climate, and rich history, which dates back to the Neolithic period. Jersey is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its unique blend of British and French culture.

Geographically, Jersey is situated about 14 miles off the coast of Normandy, France, and 85 miles south of the English coast. The island is approximately 9 miles long and 5 miles wide, with a total area of around 45 square miles. Jersey's strategic location has made it an important center for trade and commerce throughout history, with the island's capital, Saint Helier, serving as a major port.

Jersey's sovereignty status is that of a British Crown dependency, meaning that it is not part of the United Kingdom but is under the British monarch's jurisdiction. The island has its own government, with a lieutenant governor representing the monarch and a chief minister heading the local government. Jersey is also a member of the British-Irish Council, which aims to promote cooperation between the UK, Ireland, and the Crown dependencies.

Key History

  1. 1204 - Jersey is separated from the Duchy of Normandy and becomes a British Crown dependency.
  2. 1348 - The Black Death devastates the island, killing a significant portion of the population.
  3. 1406 - The Jersey militia is formed to defend the island against French attacks.
  4. 1781 - The Battle of Jersey takes place, in which the British defeat a French invasion force.
  5. 1945 - Jersey is liberated from German occupation, which lasted from 1940 to 1945.
  6. 2005 - The Jersey Financial Services Commission is established to regulate the island's financial sector.
  7. 2013 - The Jersey States Assembly is formed, replacing the previous States of Jersey government.
