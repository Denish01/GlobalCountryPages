[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in the Isle of Man is based on the British model, with some modifications to suit the island's unique needs and culture. The system is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary education, with 12 years of compulsory education. Primary education starts at the age of 5 and lasts for 6 years, followed by 5 years of secondary education. Students then have the option to pursue **A-levels** or **BTECs** (Business and Technology Education Council qualifications) at the age of 16. The quality of education in the Isle of Man is generally high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and **extracurricular activities**. The majority of schools are public, but there are also some private schools, including **independent schools** and **international schools**.

The Isle of Man education system is overseen by the **Department of Education, Sport and Culture**, which is responsible for setting education policies, managing schools, and providing support services to students and teachers. The department works closely with schools, teachers, and other stakeholders to ensure that the education system is meeting the needs of students and the community. The education system in the Isle of Man is also supported by a range of **external agencies**, including the **UK National Curriculum** and the **British Examination system**.

One of the key strengths of the education system in the Isle of Man is its ability to provide students with a wide range of academic and **vocational programs**, including **apprenticeships** and **work-based learning**. This allows students to develop a range of skills and knowledge, and to pursue their interests and career goals. The education system also places a strong emphasis on **student support**, with a range of services available to help students with their academic and personal development.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- The Isle of Man has a range of international schools, offering **British**, **American**, and **International Baccalaureate (IB)** curricula.
- The **King William's College** is a popular international school, offering a British-style education to students from around the world.
- The **St. Ninian's High School** is another well-known international school, offering a range of academic and extracurricular programs.
- International schools in the Isle of Man are generally **expensive**, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per year.
- Many international schools in the Isle of Man offer **boarding facilities**, allowing students to live on campus and experience the island's culture and way of life.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in the Isle of Man, international students need to obtain a **student visa**, which requires proof of acceptance by a recognized educational institution, proof of financial support, and proof of English language proficiency.
- English language proficiency is a requirement for most courses, with **IELTS** or **TOEFL** exams commonly accepted.
- The Isle of Man government offers a range of **scholarships** to international students, including the **Isle of Man Scholarship** and the **Manx Scholarship**.
- Living costs for students in the Isle of Man are generally **high**, with rent, food, and transportation costs similar to those in the UK.
- International students can work part-time while studying in the Isle of Man, with a range of **part-time jobs** available in the service and hospitality sectors.
- The Isle of Man has a range of **student support services**, including academic support, counseling, and career advice.
