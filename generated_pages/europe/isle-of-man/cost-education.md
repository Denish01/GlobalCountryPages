# Education Costs in Isle of Man

The cost of education in the Isle of Man varies, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for residents, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to $20,000 or more per year, depending on the institution and level of study.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in the Isle of Man is generally of high quality, with a strong emphasis on literacy and numeracy skills. Public schools, also known as state schools, are free or subsidized for residents, and the language of instruction is English, although **Manx**, the indigenous language, is also taught in some schools. Compulsory education lasts for 11 years, from the age of 5 to 16, and students typically attend primary school from age 5 to 11, followed by secondary school from age 11 to 16. The Isle of Man also has a number of private schools, including international schools, which offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **GCSE**.

The quality of education in the Isle of Man is generally considered to be high, with good teacher-to-pupil ratios and a strong focus on academic achievement. However, some private schools may offer a more diverse range of subjects and extracurricular activities, which may appeal to expat families or those seeking a more specialized education. International schools, in particular, may offer a more global perspective and a wider range of curricula, which can be beneficial for students who plan to study or work abroad. The Isle of Man's education system is overseen by the **Department of Education, Sport and Culture**, which is responsible for setting educational policies and standards.
