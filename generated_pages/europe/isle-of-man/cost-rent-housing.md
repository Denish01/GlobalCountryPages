[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
The rental market in Isle of Man is relatively small and competitive, with a high demand for properties in the capital city of Douglas. Typical lease terms are 6-12 months, and tenants are usually required to pay a deposit of one to two months' rent. The Isle of Man government has implemented laws to protect tenant rights, including the requirement for landlords to provide a written tenancy agreement and to register their properties with the government. Compared to its regional neighbors, the housing costs in Isle of Man are relatively high, similar to those in the United Kingdom.

The housing market in Isle of Man is also influenced by the island's unique economic and demographic factors, including its low unemployment rate and high standard of living. The demand for housing is driven by a mix of locals and expats, including professionals and retirees who are attracted to the island's **tax-friendly** environment and **high quality of life**. As a result, the housing market can be competitive, especially in the most desirable areas, and tenants may need to act quickly to secure a property.
