[SECTION] Economic Overview [/SECTION]
The Isle of Man is a self-governing British Crown dependency with a highly developed economy. The island's economic classification is high-income, with a GDP per capita of over $88,000, making it one of the wealthiest territories in the world. The economy has experienced steady growth over the years, driven by a combination of factors, including its strategic location, favorable business environment, and highly skilled workforce. The Isle of Man has a mixed economy, with a strong public sector and a thriving private sector, which contributes to its economic development and stability.

The island's economy has undergone significant transformation over the years, shifting from a primarily agricultural and manufacturing-based economy to a more service-oriented one. Today, the financial services sector is a major driver of the economy, with many international banks and financial institutions having a presence on the island. The Isle of Man has also become a popular destination for e-gaming companies, with many major players in the industry setting up operations on the island.

The Isle of Man's economic development is also driven by its favorable business environment, which includes a low-tax regime, a highly skilled workforce, and a well-developed infrastructure.
