[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
The Isle of Man has a relatively well-developed public transportation system, with buses being the primary mode of transport. The **Isle of Man Bus and Rail** network provides comprehensive coverage of the island, with frequent services connecting major towns and villages. Most people get around the island using buses, while some prefer to drive or use taxis. The quality of public transit is generally good, with modern buses and a reliable schedule.

The island's compact size and scenic roads make it an ideal destination for drivers, with many visitors choosing to rent cars to explore the island. However, parking in Douglas and other major towns can be limited, and drivers should be aware of the island's narrow roads and **speed limits**. Cyclists and pedestrians are also catered for, with dedicated cycle lanes and footpaths in many areas.
