[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
The Isle of Man has a mixed healthcare system, which combines elements of public and private healthcare. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of high quality, with comprehensive coverage for locals, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and other medical procedures. However, foreigners may not be eligible for public healthcare and may need to rely on private healthcare options, which can be more expensive. The system works on a residency-based model, where locals are entitled to free or low-cost healthcare, while foreigners may need to pay out-of-pocket or have private insurance to cover their medical expenses.

The quality of public and private healthcare in the Isle of Man is generally high, with well-trained medical professionals and modern facilities. However, there may be some differences in the quality of care and the range of services available between public and private providers. Locals can access public healthcare services free of charge or at a low cost, while foreigners may need to pay for private healthcare or have insurance to cover their medical expenses. It is recommended that foreigners have some form of health insurance to cover their medical expenses, as the cost of healthcare in the Isle of Man can be high.
