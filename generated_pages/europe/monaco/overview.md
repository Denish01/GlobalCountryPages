[SECTION] What Is Monaco? [/SECTION]
Monaco, officially known as the Principality of Monaco, is a **sovereign** city-state located on the French Riviera in Western Europe. It is surrounded by France on three sides and bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the east. With a population of just over 39,000 people, Monaco is the second-smallest country in the world, after the Vatican City. Despite its small size, Monaco is known for its stunning scenery, mild climate, and luxurious lifestyle, making it a popular tourist destination.

Monaco's unique geographic position has played a significant role in its history and development. The country's strategic location on the Mediterranean Sea has made it an important center for trade and commerce, while its natural harbor has provided a safe haven for ships and sailors. Today, Monaco is a major financial center and a popular destination for wealthy individuals and tourists alike.

Monaco's sovereignty status is a result of its long history of independence, which dates back to the 13th century. The country has been ruled by the Grimaldi family since 1297, making it one of the oldest monarchies in the world. Despite being surrounded by France, Monaco has maintained its independence and is a member of various international organizations, including the United Nations and the Council of Europe.
