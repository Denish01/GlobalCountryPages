# Education Costs in Monaco

The cost of education in Monaco varies significantly depending on the type of institution, with public schools being free or heavily subsidized for residents, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to over $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Monaco is highly regarded, with a strong emphasis on the **French language** and culture. Public schools in Monaco are free for residents, and the quality of education is generally high, with a focus on core subjects such as mathematics, science, and literature. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a more exclusive and often bilingual education, with some institutions offering **International Baccalaureate** programs. Compulsory education in Monaco lasts for 10 years, from the age of 6 to 16, and is divided into primary and secondary education.

The availability of international schools in Monaco is limited, but there are a few institutions that cater to the expat community, offering **English-language** instruction and **international curricula**. These schools can be quite expensive, but they provide a unique opportunity for students to receive a high-quality education in a multicultural environment. The language of instruction in public schools is French, while private and international schools may offer instruction in multiple languages, including English, French, and Italian.
