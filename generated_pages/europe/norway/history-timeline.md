# Norway History — Key Events & Timeline

The history of Norway is a complex and fascinating narrative that spans over 10,000 years, from the earliest known Stone Age settlements to the modern era, with key events including the Viking Age, the union with Denmark, and the country's eventual independence.

[SECTION] Historical Overview [/SECTION]
The earliest known history of Norway dates back to the **Stone Age**, around 10,000 years ago, when the first humans migrated to the region after the last ice age. The **Viking Age**, which lasted from the late 8th century to the mid-11th century, was a period of significant expansion and exploration for the Norwegian people, with Viking raids and trading expeditions taking place throughout Europe. During this time, Norway was divided into several small kingdoms, with the most powerful being the Kingdom of Vestfold. In the 14th century, Norway entered into a union with Denmark, which would last for over 400 years and have a profound impact on the country's language, culture, and politics. The **Reformation** in the 16th century also had a significant impact on Norway, with the country adopting **Lutheranism** as its official religion.

In the 19th century, Norway began to assert its independence from Denmark, with the country adopting its own constitution in 1814. However, it wasn't until 1905 that Norway finally gained its independence, with **Haakon VII** being crowned the country's first king. During **World War II**, Norway was occupied by Germany, with the country playing an important role in the resistance movement. In the post-war period, Norway experienced significant economic growth, driven by the country's **oil and gas industry**, which was discovered in the 1960s. Today, Norway is a **sovereign state** with a strong economy and a high standard of living.

The country's history has been shaped by its geography, with the **fjords** and **mountains** of western Norway creating a unique cultural and linguistic landscape. The **Sami people**, who are the indigenous people of northern Norway, have also played an important role in the country's history, with their own distinct culture and traditions. Throughout its history, Norway has been influenced by its neighbors, including **Sweden** and **Denmark**, with the country adopting many of their customs and practices. However, Norway has also maintained its own unique identity, with a strong sense of national pride and a commitment to **democracy** and **human rights**.

Norway's history has also been marked by significant **cultural** and **scientific** achievements, including the works of **Henrik Ibsen** and **Edvard Grieg**, who are considered two of the country's most important cultural figures. The country has also produced several notable scientists, including **Vilhelm Bjerknes**, who made significant contributions to the field of **meteorology**. Today, Norway is a **modern** and **progressive** country, with a strong economy and a high standard of living.
