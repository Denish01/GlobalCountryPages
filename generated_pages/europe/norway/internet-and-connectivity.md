# Norway Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

In Norway, you can expect to find high-quality internet with widespread coverage, making it easy to stay connected throughout your visit.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Norway boasts an excellent internet infrastructure, with fast and reliable connections available in most parts of the country. The average internet speed is 23.5 Mbps, which is more than sufficient for streaming, online browsing, and other daily activities. However, there is a noticeable urban-rural divide, with rural areas having slower internet speeds and less reliable connections compared to urban centers like **Oslo** and **Bergen**. Despite this, the overall internet quality in Norway is high, making it an ideal destination for digital nomads and remote workers.

The reliability of the internet in Norway is also noteworthy, with minimal downtime and a robust network that can handle high traffic. This is due in part to the country's investment in its **fiber-optic network**, which provides a fast and stable connection to most households and businesses. Additionally, Norway's **mobile network** is extensive, covering over 99% of the population, making it easy to stay connected even in remote areas.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Norway, you can visit one of the main carriers' stores, such as **Telenor**, **NetCom**, or **Tele2**, which can be found in most shopping malls and city centers. You will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address to purchase a SIM card. The cost of a tourist SIM card is approximately 10-20 USD, and it usually comes with a set amount of data and minutes. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds.

The process of buying a SIM card is relatively straightforward, and most carriers offer **tourist-friendly plans** that include a set amount of data, minutes, and texts. These plans can be purchased online or in-store, and they usually require a minimum stay of 30 days. It's also possible to purchase a **prepaid SIM card**, which can be refilled with credit as needed.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally high, with most hotels offering **free WiFi** to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is widespread, with most cafes and coffee shops offering **free WiFi** to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular, with many offering **high-speed WiFi** and other amenities.
- Public WiFi is available in many cities, including **Oslo** and **Bergen**, with many **public hotspots** available throughout the city.
- Many **museums** and **libraries** also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while exploring the country's cultural attractions.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like **Oslo Coworking** and **Bergen Coworking** offer high-speed WiFi and other amenities, making them ideal for remote workers.
- Reliable cafe chains like **Kaffebrenneriet** and **Stockfleths** offer free WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options like **mobile hotspots** and **portable WiFi routers** are available for purchase or rent, providing a reliable connection in case of an emergency.
- VPNs are not strictly necessary, but they can provide an added layer of security when working online, especially when using public WiFi networks.
- Many **co-living spaces** and **hostels** also offer high-speed WiFi and other amenities, making them a great option for remote workers.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is minimal in Norway, but some websites may be blocked due to **copyright restrictions**.
- Blocked sites include some **file-sharing platforms** and **pirate websites**, but most popular websites are accessible.
- VPNs like **ExpressVPN** and **NordVPN** can provide an added layer of security when working online, especially when using public WiFi networks.
- Roaming alternatives like **eSIMs** and **international SIM cards** are available for purchase, providing a convenient and cost-effective way to stay connected while traveling.
- It's recommended to purchase a **local SIM card** or **eSIM** instead of relying on roaming, as it can be more cost-effective and provide better coverage.
