[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]

- The **Norwegian Folk Museum** in Oslo showcases the country's rich cultural heritage, with a collection of over 150,000 artifacts and exhibits on traditional Norwegian architecture, clothing, and crafts.
- The **Edvard Munch Museum** in Oslo is dedicated to the life and work of the famous Norwegian painter, with a collection of over 1,100 of his paintings, prints, and sculptures.
- The **Sami Culture** is an important part of Norway's cultural heritage, with a rich tradition of music, dance, and handicrafts.
- The **Midsummer Festival** is a traditional Norwegian celebration that takes place on the summer solstice, with music, dancing, and feasting around the country.

- **Gro Harlem Brundtland**, a Norwegian politician and physician, was the first woman to serve as Prime Minister of Norway and was a key figure in the development of the **United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals**.
- The **Norwegian diaspora** has had a significant impact on the development of many countries around the world, including the United States, Canada, and Australia, with many Norwegian immigrants playing important roles in shaping their adopted countries.
