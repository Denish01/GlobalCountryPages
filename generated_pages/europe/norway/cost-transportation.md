[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Norway has a well-developed transportation system, with a range of options available for getting around, including public transportation, taxis, ride-hailing services, and car rentals. The quality of public transit in Norway is generally high, with efficient and reliable bus, train, and metro services available in most cities. Most people in Norway use a combination of public transportation and cycling or walking to get around, with many cities having dedicated bike lanes and pedestrianized areas.

The country's rugged terrain and harsh climate can make transportation challenging, particularly in rural areas, but the government has invested heavily in infrastructure to improve road and rail links. As a result, Norway has a comprehensive network of roads, buses, and trains that make it easy to get around, even in remote areas. In addition, many cities in Norway have introduced **congestion charges** and **low-emission zones** to reduce traffic and encourage the use of public transportation.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider purchasing a **rechargeable public transportation card** to save money on single fares.
- Use **ride-hailing apps** like Uber or Bolt to get around cities, as they are often cheaper than taxis.
- Be aware of **congestion charges** and **low-emission zones** in cities, which may affect your transportation costs.
- Negotiate the fare with taxi drivers before you start your journey, especially for longer trips.
- Use **parking apps** to find and pay for parking in cities, as it can be difficult to find parking spots.
- Consider **car sharing** or **car rental** for longer trips or rural areas, as public transportation may be limited.
