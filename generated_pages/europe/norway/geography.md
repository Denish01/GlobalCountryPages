[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Norway is a **sovereign** country located in the northern part of Europe, bordering the North Sea and the Arctic Circle. It shares borders with Sweden to the east, Finland to the northeast, and Russia to the northeast. The country is surrounded by the Norwegian Sea to the west, the North Sea to the southwest, and the Barents Sea to the north. Norway's strategic location allows it to control the **Skagerrak** and **Kattegat** straits, which connect the North Sea to the Baltic Sea.

The country's borders with its neighboring countries are well-defined, with the border with Sweden being the longest at approximately 1,619 kilometers. The border with Finland is about 736 kilometers long, while the border with Russia is relatively short at around 196 kilometers. Norway's unique geography, with its many **fjords** and islands, makes it a popular destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts. The country's coastline is also home to many **lighthouses**, which have played a crucial role in guiding ships through the rocky and treacherous waters.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Norway's terrain is characterized by its many **mountains**, **fjords**, and **waterfalls**. The country's landscape is dominated by the **Scandinavian Mountains**, which stretch from the southern tip of Norway to the northernmost parts of the country. The highest peak, **Galdhøpiggen**, is located in the Jotunheimen mountain range and stands at an impressive 2,469 meters above sea level. The country's many fjords, such as the **Sognefjord** and the **Hardangerfjord**, are popular tourist destinations and offer breathtaking scenery and a wide range of outdoor activities.

Norway's landscape is also characterized by its many **forests**, which cover about 30% of the country's land area. The country's forests are dominated by **coniferous** trees, such as spruce and pine, and are home to a wide range of wildlife, including **elk**, **reindeer**, and **brown bears**. The country's many **rivers**, such as the **Glomma** and the **Tana**, are popular for **fishing** and **rafting**, and offer stunning scenery and exciting outdoor activities. Norway's unique geography and diverse landscape make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

The country's **coastline** is also an important part of its geography, with many **islands** and **archipelagos** located off the coast. The **Lofoten Islands** and the **Vesteralen Islands** are popular tourist destinations, known for their stunning scenery and unique wildlife. Norway's coastline is also home to many **fishing villages**, which have played a crucial role in the country's economy and culture for centuries.
