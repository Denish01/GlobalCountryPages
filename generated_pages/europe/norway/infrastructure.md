[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Norway's infrastructure is characterized by a high level of development, with significant investments in recent years to improve and expand its transportation networks, energy systems, and telecommunications. The country's infrastructure is designed to support its economy, which is heavily reliant on **oil and gas production**, as well as **fishing and forestry**. However, there is a noticeable urban-rural gap in terms of infrastructure development, with cities like Oslo and Bergen having better access to amenities and services compared to rural areas. The Norwegian government has been working to address this gap through targeted investments and initiatives.

Norway's infrastructure development is also driven by its commitment to **sustainability and environmental protection**. The country aims to become carbon neutral by 2030 and has implemented various measures to reduce its greenhouse gas emissions, such as investing in **renewable energy sources** and promoting **electric vehicles**. The government has also launched initiatives to improve energy efficiency, reduce waste, and promote sustainable transportation.

The country's infrastructure is well-maintained, with a strong focus on **innovation and technology**. Norway has a highly developed **information and communication technology (ICT) sector**, which provides a solid foundation for its economy and society. The country is also a leader in **digitalization**, with a high level of internet penetration and a strong digital infrastructure.

- The country has set ambitious targets to reduce its greenhouse gas emissions and become carbon neutral by 2030
- Norway is also a major exporter of **oil and gas**, with a well-developed infrastructure for production, transportation, and storage
