Cultural Treasures

  • The Bryggen Wharf in Bergen is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the oldest and most well-preserved medieval trading ports in Europe.
  • The Norwegian Folk Museum in Oslo showcases the country's rich cultural heritage, with a collection of over 150,000 artifacts and exhibits on traditional Norwegian architecture, clothing, and crafts.
  • The Edvard Munch Museum in Oslo is dedicated to the life and work of the famous Norwegian painter, with a collection of over 1,100 of his paintings, prints, and sculptures.
  • The Sami Culture is an important part of Norway's cultural heritage, with a rich tradition of music, dance, and handicrafts.
  • The Midsummer Festival is a traditional Norwegian celebration that takes place on the summer solstice, with music, dancing, and feasting around the country.

People & Global Impact

  • Erik the Red, a Norwegian explorer, founded the first Norse settlement in Greenland in the 10th century, which had a significant impact on the history of the North Atlantic region.
  • Fridtjof Nansen, a Norwegian explorer and diplomat, played a key role in the development of the League of Nations and was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1922 for his work on behalf of refugees.
  • Gro Harlem Brundtland, a Norwegian politician and physician, was the first woman to serve as Prime Minister of Norway and was a key figure in the development of the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals.
  • The Norwegian diaspora has had a significant impact on the development of many countries around the world, including the United States, Canada, and Australia, with many Norwegian immigrants playing important roles in shaping their adopted countries.

What Visitors Love

  • The natural scenery of Norway, with its fjords, mountains, and waterfalls, is a major draw for tourists, with many visitors taking hiking and cycling trips to explore the countryside.
  • The hospitality of the Norwegian people is legendary, with many visitors commenting on the warm welcome they receive from locals.
  • The traditional Norwegian cuisine, with its focus on seafood, meatballs, and brown cheese, is a highlight for many visitors, with many restaurants serving up delicious and authentic dishes.
  • The Northern Lights are a major attraction for many visitors, with Norway being one of the best places in the world to see this natural phenomenon.
  • The cultural festivals of Norway, such as the Oslo Jazz Festival and the Bergen International Festival, are a great way to experience the country's vibrant cultural scene.
  • The winter sports facilities of Norway, with its many ski resorts and ice rinks, are a major draw for sports enthusiasts, with many visitors coming to the country to enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and ice hockey.

Hidden Gems

  • The Lofoten Islands are a hidden gem of Norway, with their stunning natural scenery, picturesque villages, and vibrant cultural scene.
  • The Trolltunga rock formation is a unique and breathtaking natural wonder, with its challenging hike and stunning views making it a must-visit for many outdoor enthusiasts.
  • The Sognefjord is the longest and deepest fjord in Norway, with its stunning scenery and picturesque villages making it a great destination for boating and hiking trips.
  • The Northern Norway region is a hidden gem, with its stunning natural scenery, vibrant cultural scene, and unique Sami culture making it a great destination for visitors looking to experience the authentic Norway.
