Location & Borders

Norway is a sovereign country located in the northern part of Europe, bordering the North Sea and the Arctic Circle. It shares borders with Sweden to the east, Finland to the northeast, and Russia to the northeast. The country is surrounded by the Norwegian Sea to the west, the North Sea to the southwest, and the Barents Sea to the north. Norway's strategic location allows it to control the Skagerrak and Kattegat straits, which connect the North Sea to the Baltic Sea.

The country's borders with its neighboring countries are well-defined, with the border with Sweden being the longest at approximately 1,619 kilometers. The border with Finland is about 736 kilometers long, while the border with Russia is relatively short at around 196 kilometers. Norway's unique geography, with its many fjords and islands, makes it a popular destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts. The country's coastline is also home to many lighthouses, which have played a crucial role in guiding ships through the rocky and treacherous waters.
