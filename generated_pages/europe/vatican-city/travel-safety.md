[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Vatican City is a unique and safe destination, with a population of less than 1,000 people, making it one of the smallest countries in the world. The city-state is surrounded by high walls and has a strong police presence, which contributes to its low crime rate. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around the city, even at night, and can expect a warm welcome from the locals. However, as with any popular tourist destination, there are some petty crimes and scams to be aware of, such as pickpocketing and overpriced souvenirs.

The Vatican City's safety record is also due to its strict security measures, including metal detectors and bag checks at entrances to popular attractions like St. Peter's Basilica. Additionally, the city-state has a well-organized and efficient emergency services system, with a hospital and several medical clinics on site. Overall, Vatican City is a very safe destination, and visitors can focus on enjoying the city's stunning architecture, beautiful art, and rich history without worrying about their safety.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas like St. Peter's Square
- Overpriced souvenirs and food at tourist traps
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake charity collectors or unofficial tour guides
- Crowds and long lines at popular attractions, which can increase the risk of pickpocketing
- Limited English spoken by some locals, which can lead to misunderstandings
- High prices for accommodations and food, especially during peak season
- Petty theft and vandalism in areas with limited security, such as parking lots

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Vatican City, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended for visitors.
- **Medical Facilities:** The Vatican City has a well-equipped hospital and several medical clinics, with high-quality medical care available.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are available and well-stocked, with a wide range of medications and medical supplies.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas like St. Peter's Square
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Be cautious of strangers approaching you, especially if they're trying to sell you something
- Stay hydrated and take breaks in the shade, especially during hot summer months
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting churches or other places of worship
- Learn some basic Italian phrases, such as "grazie" (thank you) and "per favore" (please)

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Vatican City is a very safe destination for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong police presence
- Consider visiting popular attractions during the day, when they're more crowded and safer
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas
- Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting churches or other places of worship
