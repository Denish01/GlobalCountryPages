[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Vatican City is the smallest country in the world, both by area and population, and as such, it has a very limited transportation system. The city is completely surrounded by Rome, Italy, and most people walk or use public transportation to get around. The quality of public transit is excellent, with efficient and reliable bus and metro systems connecting Vatican City to Rome. Most people get around on foot, as the city is very small and pedestrian-friendly.

The lack of traffic and parking problems makes Vatican City a unique and pleasant place to visit or live. The city is also very safe, with low crime rates and a strong police presence. Visitors can easily walk to most attractions, including the Vatican Museums, St. Peter's Basilica, and the Sistine Chapel. For longer trips, public transportation is readily available, with bus and metro lines connecting Vatican City to Rome and other surrounding cities.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider purchasing a **Roma Pass**, which includes free public transportation and discounted entry to attractions.
- Use the **MyTaxi** or **Uber** apps to book ride-hailing services, as they are widely available and convenient.
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **scams** in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
- **Negotiate taxi fares** before starting your journey, especially for longer trips.
- Consider **walking or biking** for shorter trips, as Vatican City is very pedestrian-friendly and safe.
- Use **public transportation** for longer trips, as it is efficient and reliable.
