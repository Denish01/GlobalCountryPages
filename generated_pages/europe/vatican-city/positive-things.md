[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Sistine Chapel**, famous for its **Michelangelo**-painted ceiling, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most visited cultural attractions in the world.
- The **Vatican Museums** house an impressive collection of art and artifacts, including Egyptian and Etruscan antiquities, as well as modern and contemporary art.
- The **Vatican Library** is one of the oldest and largest libraries in the world, with a collection of over 1.6 million rare books and manuscripts.
- The **Pontifical Villas of Castel Gandolfo**, a summer residence of the Pope, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination.
- The **Vatican's Necropolis**, a ancient Roman burial ground, is a significant archaeological site and a testament to the city's rich history.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Pope Francis**, the current head of the Catholic Church, has been a prominent figure in global affairs, advocating for social justice, environmental protection, and interfaith dialogue.
- The **Vatican's diplomatic corps** plays a significant role in international relations, with diplomatic relations with over 180 countries worldwide.
- **Mother Teresa**, a Catholic nun and Nobel laureate, was a prominent figure in the Vatican's humanitarian efforts, and her legacy continues to inspire charitable work around the world.
- The **Vatican's charitable organizations**, such as **Caritas Internationalis** and the **Pontifical Council for the Pastoral Care of Migrants and Itinerant People**, provide aid and support to vulnerable communities worldwide.
