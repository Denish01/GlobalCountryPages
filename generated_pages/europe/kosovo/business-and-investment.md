# Doing Business in Kosovo — Investment, FDI & Opportunities

Doing business in Kosovo can be challenging due to its complex political and economic environment, but the country also offers various investment opportunities, particularly in the energy, agriculture, and tourism sectors.

[FACTBOX]

Currency Stability: Moderate
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
Kosovo's business environment is characterized by a complex and sometimes unpredictable **bureaucratic system**. The government has made efforts to improve the business climate, but **corruption** remains a significant issue. The country's **disputed status** also creates uncertainty for investors, as it is not recognized by all countries, including some European Union member states. However, the government has implemented various reforms to enhance the business environment, including the establishment of a **one-stop shop** for businesses and the introduction of **e-government services**. Despite these efforts, the business environment in Kosovo remains challenging, and investors need to be aware of the potential **risks and obstacles**.

The government's attitude toward foreign investment is generally positive, and it has introduced various incentives to attract **foreign direct investment (FDI)**. However, the investment process can be slow due to the complex **regulatory framework** and the need for multiple **licenses and permits**. Additionally, the country's **infrastructure** is still underdeveloped, particularly in the areas of transportation and energy. To overcome these challenges, investors need to be well-prepared and have a good understanding of the local business environment.

Kosovo's **economic growth** has been steady in recent years, driven primarily by the **service sector** and **remittances** from abroad. The country has also made progress in **EU integration**, which is expected to improve the business environment and attract more foreign investment. However, the **political situation** remains fragile, and investors need to be aware of the potential **risks and uncertainties** associated with the country's **disputed status**.

[SECTION] Foreign Direct Investment [/SECTION]
- Kosovo has attracted significant FDI in recent years, particularly in the **energy** and **telecommunications** sectors.
- The main investing countries are **Turkey**, **Austria**, and **Slovenia**.
- The government offers various incentives for FDI, including **tax exemptions** and **subsidies** for investment in **priority sectors**.
- However, FDI is restricted in certain sectors, such as **defense** and **security**.
- The government has established a **one-stop shop** for FDI, which provides assistance with the investment process and **regulatory compliance**.

[SECTION] Legal & Regulatory Framework [/SECTION]
- Kosovo has a **civil law system**, based on the **German** and **Austrian** models.
- The country has a **constitution** that guarantees **property rights** and **freedom of contract**.
- The **Commercial Law** regulates business activities, including **company formation** and **contract law**.
- The **Labor Law** regulates employment relationships, including **minimum wage** and **working conditions**.
- The country has a **dispute resolution** system, which includes **courts** and **arbitration**.
- **Intellectual property protection** is provided by the **Law on Intellectual Property**, which includes **copyright**, **trademark**, and **patent** protection.

[SECTION] Challenges & Risks [/SECTION]
- Kosovo's **disputed status** creates uncertainty for investors, particularly in terms of **recognition** and **access to international markets**.
- The country's **corruption** and **bureaucratic system** can create obstacles for businesses, particularly in terms of **regulatory compliance** and **access to public services**.
- The **infrastructure** is underdeveloped, particularly in the areas of **transportation** and **energy**.
- The **political situation** is fragile, and investors need to be aware of the potential **risks and uncertainties** associated with the country's **disputed status**.
- The **currency risk** is moderate, as the **euro** is widely used, but the country's **monetary policy** is limited.
- The **market size** is small, which can limit the growth potential of businesses.

- The **Kosovo Investment and Enterprise Support Agency (KIESA)** provides assistance with the investment process and **regulatory compliance**.
