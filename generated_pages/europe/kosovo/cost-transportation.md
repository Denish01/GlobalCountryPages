[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Kosovo has a relatively underdeveloped transportation network, with limited public transportation options and a reliance on private vehicles and taxis. The main modes of transportation in Kosovo are buses, taxis, and private cars, with many people opting to drive their own vehicles due to the lack of efficient public transportation. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with Pristina having the most developed network, while smaller towns and rural areas often have limited options.

The majority of people in Kosovo get around by driving their own cars or using taxis, with many also relying on ride-hailing apps such as Free Now and Bolt. The roads in Kosovo are generally in good condition, but traffic can be heavy, especially in Pristina. The country is also working to improve its public transportation network, with new bus routes and services being introduced in recent years.
