# Getting Around Florence — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around Florence, you can use a variety of transportation options including walking, buses, trains, and taxis, as well as ride-hailing apps, making it relatively easy to navigate the city and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Florence is easily accessible by air, with two nearby international airports: Florence Airport (FLR) and Pisa International Airport (PSA). Florence Airport is the closest, located about 4 kilometers from the city center, and offers flights to several European destinations. Pisa International Airport, on the other hand, is about 80 kilometers west of Florence and offers a wider range of international flights. You can also reach Florence by train or bus from other major Italian cities, such as Rome, Venice, and Milan. Overland entry points include the A1 and A11 highways, which connect Florence to the rest of Italy and Europe.

If you're planning to arrive in Florence by air, you can take a taxi or bus from the airport to the city center. The journey takes about 15-30 minutes from Florence Airport, depending on traffic, and about 1-2 hours from Pisa International Airport. Alternatively, you can take a train from Pisa Central Station, which is connected to the airport by a shuttle bus. The train journey from Pisa to Florence takes about 1 hour.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Italy has a moderate domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights within the country. If you're planning to travel to other parts of Italy, you can take a domestic flight from Florence Airport or Pisa International Airport. The main domestic airlines in Italy are Alitalia, Ryanair, and EasyJet, which operate flights to destinations such as Rome, Venice, Milan, and Naples. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through a travel agency. Approximate costs for domestic flights in Italy range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Italy has an extensive rail network, with frequent trains connecting major cities. Florence is a major rail hub, with trains running to destinations such as Rome, Venice, Milan, and Naples. The high-speed rail network, known as **Frecciarossa**, connects Florence to Rome, Venice, and other major cities, with journey times of under 2 hours. You can book train tickets online through the Trenitalia website or at the train station. The booking process is relatively straightforward, and you can choose from a range of ticket options, including economy, premium, and business class.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus companies such as FlixBus and Eurolines operate in Italy, offering affordable and convenient travel options. Buses are a good alternative to trains, especially for shorter journeys or for those on a budget. The bus network in Italy is relatively extensive, with frequent services connecting major cities. You can book bus tickets online or at the bus station, and approximate costs range from $10 to $50, depending on the route and time of booking.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Italy, you'll need a valid **driver's license** from your home country or an International Driving Permit.
- Road conditions in Italy are generally good, but be aware of narrow streets and limited parking in city centers.
- Fuel costs in Italy are relatively high, with gasoline prices ranging from $1.50 to $2.50 per liter.
- Car rental companies such as Hertz, Avis, and Europcar have offices in Florence and at the airports.
- Be aware of **ZTL (Limited Traffic Zones)** in city centers, which restrict access to certain areas.
- Parking in Florence can be challenging, especially in the city center, so be prepared to pay for parking or use park-and-ride facilities.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **scams** in tourist areas and on public transportation.
- Use **cashless payment methods** such as credit cards or contactless payments for convenience and security.
- Download **transport apps** such as Citymapper or Google Maps to help navigate the city.
- Validate your **train ticket** before boarding the train to avoid fines.
- Be prepared for **traffic congestion** in city centers, especially during peak hours.
- Use **licensed taxis** or ride-hailing apps to ensure a safe and reliable journey.
- Respect **local traffic rules** and pedestrian areas to avoid fines or accidents.
- Consider purchasing a **Firenze Card**, which grants access to public transportation and discounts on attractions.
