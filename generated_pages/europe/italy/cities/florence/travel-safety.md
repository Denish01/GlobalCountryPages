Florence is generally a safe city to visit, with low crime rates compared to other major cities in Europe.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Florence is a popular tourist destination, and as such, it has a strong police presence, particularly in areas frequented by visitors. The city's historic center is generally well-lit and safe, with many pedestrians and police officers on the streets. However, as with any city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag snatching, can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, and visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and keep a close eye on their belongings.

Overall, Florence is a safe city, and most visitors have a trouble-free trip. The city has a strong sense of community, and locals are generally friendly and welcoming to tourists. However, as with any city, it's essential to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe. Visitors should avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night, and should keep their valuables secure. By taking these precautions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Florence.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake charity collectors or street performers
- Overcharging by taxi drivers or restaurant owners
- Pickpocketing on buses and trains, particularly during peak hours
- Theft of bicycles or motorbikes
- Scams involving fake or stolen goods, such as leather products or jewelry
- Aggressive panhandling or begging in tourist areas

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Florence, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Florence, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it.
- **Medical Facilities:** Florence has high-quality medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, and many medical staff speak English.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available and easily accessible, and many pharmacists speak English.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep your valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe for valuables
- Be cautious when using ATMs and avoid using them in isolated areas
- Keep your purse or backpack in front of you, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Florence is generally a safe city for solo and women travelers, but it's still important to take normal precautions to stay safe
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night, and consider using a taxi or public transportation instead
- Be mindful of your surroundings and keep your valuables secure, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Consider joining a group tour or finding a travel buddy for added safety and companionship
