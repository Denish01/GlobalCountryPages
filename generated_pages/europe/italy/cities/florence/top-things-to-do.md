# Top Things to Do in Florence — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Florence, you should not miss the iconic **Duomo**, the Uffizi Gallery, and the Ponte Vecchio, which are just a few of the many top attractions that make this city a must-see destination.

[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore (Duomo) | Piazza del Duomo | Landmark | Iconic cathedral complex in the heart of Florence |
| Uffizi Gallery | Piazzale degli Uffizi | Museum | World-renowned collection of Renaissance art |
| Ponte Vecchio | Lungarno degli Archibusieri | Bridge | Medieval stone bridge lined with shops and boutiques |
| Accademia Gallery | Via Ricasoli | Museum | Home to Michelangelo's famous sculpture, David |
| Palazzo Pitti | Piazza Pitti | Palace | Grand palace that was once the residence of the Medici family |

| Palazzo Vecchio | Piazza della Signoria | Palace | Historic town hall with impressive architecture and artwork |
| Santa Croce Church | Piazza Santa Croce | Church | Beautiful church with impressive tombs and artwork |
[/TABLE]

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **gondola ride** down the Arno River to see the city from a different perspective
- Visit a local **leather workshop** to learn about the traditional craftsmanship of Florence
- Explore the **Oltrarno neighborhood** and get lost in its charming streets and piazzas
- Attend a **wine tasting** at a local vineyard to sample some of the region's famous wines
- Take a **cooking class** to learn how to make traditional Tuscan dishes
- Go **hiking in the Tuscan countryside** to see the rolling hills and picturesque villages

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Boboli Gardens** for stunning views of the city and beautiful gardens
- Explore the **Tuscan countryside** and its rolling hills and picturesque villages
- Go **hiking in the Apennine Mountains** for breathtaking scenery and outdoor adventure
- Visit the **Arno River** and take a stroll along its banks
- Explore the **Parco delle Cascine** for a peaceful escape from the city

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Uffizi Gallery** to see some of the world's most famous artworks
- Explore the **Accademia Gallery** and see Michelangelo's famous sculpture, David
- Attend a **performance at the Teatro della Pergola** to see some of the city's best theater and music
- Visit the **Palazzo Pitti** and its impressive collection of artwork and artifacts
- Explore the **Museo Galileo** to learn about the history of science and technology

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **San Felice in Piazza** church for its beautiful artwork and peaceful atmosphere
- Explore the **Santo Spirito neighborhood** for its charming streets and local shops
- Visit the **La Specola Museum** to see its impressive collection of natural history specimens
- Take a stroll along the **Lungarno delle Grazie** for beautiful views of the Arno River

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Siena** (distance: 70 km, travel time: 1 hour) to explore its medieval streets and stunning piazzas
- Visit **Pisa** (distance: 85 km, travel time: 1.5 hours) to see the famous Leaning Tower
- Explore the **Chianti wine region** (distance: 30 km, travel time: 30 minutes) to sample some of the world's best wines
- Take a day trip to **Lucca** (distance: 80 km, travel time: 1.5 hours) to explore its charming streets and historic architecture
- Visit the **Tuscan hill town of San Gimignano** (distance: 60 km, travel time: 1 hour) to see its stunning scenery and medieval architecture

It is recommended to visit Florence during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and pleasant, and to avoid visiting during the peak tourist season in the summer. Additionally, consider purchasing a **Firenze Card** to get free admission to many of the city's museums and landmarks, and to avoid long lines and wait times.
