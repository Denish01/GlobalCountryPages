[SECTION] What Is Florence? [/SECTION]
Florence is a city located in the central region of Italy, in the Tuscany province. It is situated in the valley of the Arno River and is surrounded by the Tuscan hills. The city has a long and complex history, dating back to the Roman era, and has been an important cultural and economic center in Italy for centuries. Florence is known for its stunning architecture, including the iconic Duomo, a terracotta-tiled cathedral complex in the heart of the city, and the Ponte Vecchio, a medieval stone bridge lined with shops.

The city is also famous for its rich cultural heritage, including numerous museums and art galleries, such as the Uffizi Gallery, which houses some of the world's most famous paintings, including Botticelli's "The Birth of Venus". Florence is also the birthplace of the Italian Renaissance, a cultural and artistic movement that emerged in the 14th century and had a profound impact on Western art and culture. The city has a population of around 350,000 people and is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year.

Florence is a unique and fascinating city that offers a wealth of cultural, artistic, and historical attractions. From its stunning architecture to its world-class museums and galleries, Florence is a city that has something to offer everyone. Whether you're interested in history, art, food, or fashion, Florence is a city that is sure to captivate and inspire.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 59 BC - The Romans founded the city of Florence, which they named Florentia.
2. 1297 - The Medici family, who would go on to become one of the most powerful families in Florence, established their bank in the city.
3. 1348 - The Black Death, a pandemic that swept through Europe, devastated the city of Florence, killing over half of its population.
4. 1401 - The competition for the bronze doors of the Florence Cathedral was held, which marked the beginning of the Renaissance in Florence.
5. 1865 - Florence became the capital of the Kingdom of Italy, a position it held until 1871.
6. 1966 - The Arno River flooded the city, causing widespread damage and destruction to many of its historic buildings and landmarks.
7. 1982 - The historic center of Florence was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural and historical significance.
