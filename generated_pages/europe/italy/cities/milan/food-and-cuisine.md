# Milan Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Milan, be sure to try the city's renowned **Risotto alla Milanese**, **Ossobuco**, and **Panettone**, which showcase the city's rich culinary heritage and are must-try dishes that define Milan's unique food culture.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: rice, polenta, pasta, butter
National Dish: Risotto alla Milanese, a saffron-infused risotto dish typically served with ossobuco
Meal Times: 7:30-10:00 am for breakfast, 12:30-2:30 pm for lunch, 7:30-10:00 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: vegetarian-friendly, with many traditional dishes featuring vegetables and cheese
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Milan's cuisine is characterized by its use of rich and flavorful ingredients, such as butter, cheese, and meat, which reflect the city's history as a major trading center. The city's location in the Lombardy region has also influenced its cuisine, with many traditional dishes featuring rice, polenta, and pasta. Milanese cuisine has been shaped by various cultural and historical influences, including the Mediterranean, Alpine, and European traditions. As a result, the city's cuisine is known for its unique blend of flavors and ingredients, with a focus on hearty and comforting dishes.

The city's culinary scene is also defined by its use of high-quality ingredients, such as fresh vegetables, meats, and cheeses. Many traditional Milanese dishes feature local ingredients, such as **asparagi** (asparagus) and **zucca** (pumpkin), which are often combined with rich sauces and seasonings. The city's cuisine has also been influenced by its history as a major cultural and economic center, with many traditional dishes reflecting the city's wealth and sophistication.

In addition to its traditional cuisine, Milan is also known for its modern and innovative food scene, with many restaurants and cafes offering creative and experimental dishes. The city is home to a number of Michelin-starred restaurants, as well as a variety of casual eateries and street food vendors. Whether you're looking for traditional Milanese cuisine or something new and exciting, the city has a wide range of options to suit every taste and budget.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- Table manners are formal, with an emphasis on using utensils and keeping your hands visible
- Tipping is expected, but not excessive, with 5-10% of the total bill being standard
- Reservations are recommended for high-end restaurants, especially on weekends and during peak travel seasons
- Dress codes are often formal, especially in upscale restaurants, with a focus on elegant and refined attire
- Mealtimes are typically later than in other European cities, with lunch served from 12:30-2:30 pm and dinner served from 7:30-10:00 pm
- It's customary to greet the server with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek, and to address them with formal titles such as "signore" or "signora"
