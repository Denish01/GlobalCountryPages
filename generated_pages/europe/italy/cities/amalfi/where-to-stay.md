# Where to Stay in Amalfi — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Amalfi, consider the top neighborhoods of Amalfi Town, Positano, Ravello, Conca dei Marini, Praiano, Minori, and Maiori, each offering unique experiences for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a detailed breakdown:

**Amalfi Town** (Amalfi)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Amalfi Town offers a vibrant and picturesque atmosphere, with colorful buildings, historic landmarks, and a stunning cathedral. The town's central location makes it an ideal base for exploring the Amalfi Coast.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Central location, stunning cathedral, vibrant atmosphere
Cons: Can be crowded, limited parking

**Positano** (Positano)
Best For: Luxury / Honeymoon
Vibe: Positano is a charming and romantic town, known for its colorful houses, steep streets, and beautiful beaches. The town's laid-back atmosphere and stunning scenery make it a perfect destination for couples and honeymooners.
Price Range: $250-$600/night
Pros: Romantic atmosphere, stunning scenery, beautiful beaches
Cons: Steep streets, limited parking

**Ravello** (Ravello)
Best For: Luxury / Culture
Vibe: Ravello is a historic and cultural town, perched on top of a hill, offering breathtaking views of the Amalfi Coast. The town's stunning villas, beautiful gardens, and rich cultural heritage make it a must-visit destination for art lovers and history buffs.
Price Range: $200-$400/night
Pros: Breathtaking views, stunning villas, rich cultural heritage
Cons: Limited nightlife, steep streets

**Conca dei Marini** (Conca dei Marini)
Best For: Family / Beach
Vibe: Conca dei Marini is a small and charming town, located on the Amalfi Coast, known for its beautiful beaches and stunning scenery. The town's relaxed atmosphere and family-friendly amenities make it an ideal destination for families.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, stunning scenery, family-friendly
Cons: Limited nightlife, limited restaurants

**Praiano** (Praiano)
Best For: Backpacker / Budget
Vibe: Praiano is a small and laid-back town, located on the Amalfi Coast, offering a more affordable and relaxed alternative to the other towns. The town's stunning scenery, beautiful beaches, and lively nightlife make it a popular destination for backpackers and budget travelers.
Price Range: $80-$200/night
Pros: Affordable, stunning scenery, beautiful beaches
Cons: Limited amenities, steep streets

**Minori** (Minori)
Best For: Family / Beach
Vibe: Minori is a small and charming town, located on the Amalfi Coast, known for its beautiful beaches and stunning scenery. The town's relaxed atmosphere and family-friendly amenities make it an ideal destination for families.
Price Range: $100-$250/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, stunning scenery, family-friendly
Cons: Limited nightlife, limited restaurants

**Maiori** (Maiori)
Best For: Luxury / Beach
Vibe: Maiori is a large and vibrant town, located on the Amalfi Coast, offering a wide range of amenities and activities. The town's stunning scenery, beautiful beaches, and lively nightlife make it a popular destination for luxury travelers.
Price Range: $200-$400/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, stunning scenery, lively nightlife
Cons: Can be crowded, limited parking

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September)
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Airbnb, to compare prices and find deals
- Consider visiting during the shoulder season (April to May or September to October) for lower prices and smaller crowds
- Negotiate prices, especially when booking directly with hotels or guesthouses
- Look for packages and special offers, which can include meals, activities, or transportation
- Read reviews and check the location before booking, to ensure it meets your needs and expectations
- Use travel agents or local tour operators to book accommodations and activities, for a more personalized experience

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Use reputable taxi services or public transportation, to avoid scams and overcharging
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night, especially in smaller towns
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially on beaches and in crowded areas
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting churches or historical sites
- Learn some basic Italian phrases, to help navigate and communicate with locals
- Consider purchasing travel insurance, to cover unexpected medical or travel-related expenses
