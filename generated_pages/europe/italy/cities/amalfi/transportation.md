# Getting Around Amalfi — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Amalfi, you can use a combination of public transportation, including buses and ferries, as well as private transportation options like taxis and car rentals, which provide a range of choices for exploring this beautiful Italian city.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
The closest international airport to Amalfi is Naples International Airport (NAP), which is located about 60 kilometers north of the city. From the airport, you can take a taxi, bus, or rent a car to get to Amalfi. There are also direct flights to Naples from many major European cities, as well as some international flights from outside of Europe. If you prefer to travel by land, you can take a train or bus to Naples and then transfer to a local bus or ferry to get to Amalfi.

Overland entry points to Amalfi include the A3 highway, which connects the city to Naples and other major cities in southern Italy. You can also take a ferry from nearby towns like Positano or Sorrento to get to Amalfi. The city is also connected to the rest of Italy by a network of buses and trains, although the frequency and quality of these services can vary depending on the time of year and other factors. It's worth noting that Amalfi is a popular tourist destination, and the roads and public transportation systems can get crowded during peak season.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights within Italy are limited, but you can fly from Naples to other major cities like Rome, Florence, and Milan. The main domestic airlines in Italy are Alitalia, Ryanair, and EasyJet, and they offer a range of flights to different parts of the country. To book a domestic flight, you can use the airlines' websites or a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the route and time of year. A one-way ticket from Naples to Rome, for example, can cost anywhere from $50 to $200, depending on the airline and the time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
The rail network in Italy is extensive, but the quality and frequency of trains can vary depending on the region. In Amalfi, the closest train station is in Naples, and from there you can take a local train or bus to get to the city. The main train operator in Italy is Trenitalia, and they offer a range of trains, including high-speed trains like the Frecciarossa and Italo. To book a train ticket, you can use the Trenitalia website or a ticket machine at the train station, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several bus companies that operate in Amalfi, including SITA and CTP, which offer routes to nearby towns and cities. The buses are generally comfortable and reliable, although the frequency of services can vary depending on the time of year and other factors. You can buy tickets at the bus station or on the bus, and prices vary depending on the route and distance. A one-way ticket from Amalfi to Positano, for example, can cost around $2 to $5.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Italy, you need a valid driver's license from your home country or an International Driving Permit.
- The road conditions in Amalfi can be narrow and winding, especially in the historic center, and parking spaces are limited.
- Fuel costs in Italy are generally higher than in other parts of Europe, with prices ranging from $1.50 to $2.50 per liter.
- Car rental companies like Hertz, Avis, and Europcar have offices in Amalfi and Naples, and prices vary depending on the type of car and rental period.
- Driving in Amalfi can be challenging, especially during peak season, and you should be aware of the local traffic rules and regulations.
- It's also a good idea to have a GPS or map with you, as the roads in Amalfi can be confusing, especially for first-time visitors.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of the traffic rules and regulations in Italy, including the requirement to wear a seatbelt and the prohibition on using a phone while driving.
- Use a reputable taxi company or ride-hailing app to avoid scams and overcharging.
- Buy a **Biglietto** (ticket) before boarding a bus or train, as you may be fined if you don't have one.
- Download the **Trenitalia** or **Moovit** app to plan your journey and buy tickets.
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas like bus and train stations.
- Consider purchasing a **Campania Artecard**, which gives you free entry to many museums and public transportation in the region.
- Learn some basic **Italian** phrases, such as "dove posso trovare..." (where can I find...) or "quanto costa?" (how much does it cost?), to help you navigate the transportation system.
