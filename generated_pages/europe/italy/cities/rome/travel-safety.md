# Rome Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Rome is generally a safe city to visit, with low to moderate crime rates and a strong police presence, but as with any major tourist destination, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Rome is a bustling city with a rich history and culture, and millions of tourists visit each year without incident. However, as with any major city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and scams, can be a problem in areas like the Colosseum, the Pantheon, and the Trevi Fountain, where large crowds of tourists gather. Additionally, visitors should be cautious when using public transportation, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas.

Overall, Rome is a safe city, but visitors should still take necessary precautions to stay safe. This includes being aware of their surroundings, keeping a close eye on their belongings, and avoiding carrying large amounts of cash. Visitors should also be cautious when interacting with strangers, especially those who approach them with unsolicited offers or requests. By taking these precautions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Rome.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing in crowded areas, such as the Colosseum and the Pantheon
- Scams at tourist hotspots, such as the Trevi Fountain and the Spanish Steps
- Overcharging by taxi drivers, especially at night
- Street vendors selling counterfeit goods, such as fake designer handbags and watches
- Thieves targeting tourists on public transportation, especially at night
- Scams involving fake police officers or utility workers, who may ask to inspect your apartment or hotel room
- Pickpocketing in crowded markets, such as the Campo de' Fiori

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Rome, but visitors should be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Rome, but visitors may prefer to drink bottled water.
- **Medical Facilities:** Rome has high-quality medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, and many doctors speak English.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available in Rome, and many are open late or 24 hours.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep a close eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas and on public transportation
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night
- Use reputable taxi companies, and always check the meter before you start your journey
- Be cautious when interacting with strangers, especially those who approach you with unsolicited offers or requests
- Keep your hotel room door locked, and use the hotel safe to store valuables
