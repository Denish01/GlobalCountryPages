# Where to Stay in Rome — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Rome, consider the city's historic center and surrounding neighborhoods, which offer a range of options to suit different types of travelers and budgets. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, Rome has a unique character that can enhance your travel experience. 
**Monti** (City) 
Best For: backpacker / family 
Vibe: Monti is a charming neighborhood with narrow streets, picturesque piazzas, and a lively atmosphere, making it ideal for those who want to experience the authentic side of Rome. The area is also close to the Roman Forum and Colosseum, making it a great base for exploring ancient Rome. 
Price Range: $80-200/night 
Pros: central location, plenty of restaurants and bars, historic charm 
Cons: can be noisy, limited parking 

**Trastevere** (City) 
Best For: luxury / family 
Vibe: Trastevere is a upscale neighborhood located on the other side of the Tiber River, known for its beautiful piazzas, stunning views, and vibrant nightlife. The area is also home to many high-end restaurants and boutiques, making it a great choice for those who want to experience the best of Rome. 
Price Range: $150-300/night 
Pros: beautiful views, upscale amenities, lively nightlife 
Cons: can be expensive, limited budget options 

**Centro Storico** (City) 
Best For: business / luxury 
Vibe: The historic center of Rome is a bustling area filled with ancient landmarks, high-end boutiques, and world-class restaurants. The area is also close to many business centers and conference venues, making it a great choice for business travelers. 
Price Range: $100-250/night 
Pros: central location, historic landmarks, high-end amenities 
Cons: can be crowded, limited parking 

**Testaccio** (City) 
Best For: family / budget 
Vibe: Testaccio is a charming neighborhood located near the city center, known for its authentic atmosphere, delicious food, and lively nightlife. The area is also home to many budget-friendly accommodations and restaurants, making it a great choice for families and budget travelers. 
Price Range: $60-150/night 
Pros: authentic atmosphere, budget-friendly options, delicious food 
Cons: limited high-end amenities, can be noisy 

**Prati** (City) 
Best For: business / family 
Vibe: Prati is a upscale neighborhood located near the Vatican City, known for its beautiful parks, stunning views, and high-end amenities. The area is also close to many business centers and conference venues, making it a great choice for business travelers and families. 
Price Range: $120-280/night 
Pros: beautiful parks, high-end amenities, close to the Vatican 
Cons: can be expensive, limited budget options 

**Parioli** (City) 
Best For: luxury / family 
Vibe: Parioli is a upscale neighborhood located near the city center, known for its beautiful parks, stunning views, and high-end amenities. The area is also home to many high-end restaurants and boutiques, making it a great choice for those who want to experience the best of Rome. 
Price Range: $180-350/night 
Pros: beautiful parks, high-end amenities, stunning views 
Cons: can be expensive, limited budget options 

**San Giovanni** (City) 
Best For: budget / family 
Vibe: San Giovanni is a charming neighborhood located near the city center, known for its authentic atmosphere, delicious food, and lively nightlife. The area is also home to many budget-friendly accommodations and restaurants, making it a great choice for families and budget travelers. 
Price Range: $50-120/night 
Pros: authentic atmosphere, budget-friendly options, delicious food 
Cons: limited high-end amenities, can be noisy

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (June to August) 
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals 
- Consider staying in a neighborhood that is close to public transportation to make it easy to get around the city 
- Look for accommodations that offer free Wi-Fi and breakfast to save money 
- Be aware of any additional fees or charges, such as city taxes or cleaning fees 
- Read reviews and check the cancellation policy before booking 
- Negotiate the price if you are booking directly with a hotel or guesthouse 

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots 
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night 
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas like train stations and bus terminals 
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps to get around the city 
- Consider staying in a neighborhood that is close to the city center, but not directly in the historic center, to avoid crowds and noise 
- Use a money belt or a secure bag to carry your valuables 
- Be mindful of traffic and pedestrian rules, and use crosswalks and pedestrian signals to stay safe
