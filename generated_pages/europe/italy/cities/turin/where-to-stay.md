# Where to Stay in Turin — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Turin, the best areas to stay are in the city center, where you can easily access historic landmarks, cultural attractions, and entertainment options, with top neighborhoods including the **City Center**, **Vittorio Veneto**, and **Crocetta**.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**City Center** (Turin)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: The City Center is the heart of Turin, with a vibrant and sophisticated atmosphere, surrounded by stunning baroque architecture and world-class museums.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to major attractions, high-end shopping, and fine dining, with excellent public transportation links.
Cons: Can be quite crowded and noisy, with limited budget-friendly options.

**Vittorio Veneto** (Turin)
Best For: Backpacker / Family
Vibe: This lively neighborhood is known for its beautiful parks, historic cafes, and bustling streets, with a mix of affordable and mid-range accommodation options.
Price Range: $80-180/night
Pros: Affordable prices, plenty of restaurants and bars, and easy access to the city center.
Cons: Can be noisy at night, with some areas showing signs of wear and tear.

**Crocetta** (Turin)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is famous for its beautiful architecture, high-end boutiques, and exclusive restaurants, with a refined and elegant atmosphere.
Price Range: $200-400/night
Pros: Elegant surroundings, excellent dining options, and close proximity to the city center.
Cons: Expensive prices, with limited budget-friendly options.

**Aurora** (Turin)
Best For: Family / Mid-range
Vibe: This charming neighborhood is located just outside the city center, with a relaxed and family-friendly atmosphere, surrounded by parks and green spaces.
Price Range: $100-220/night
Pros: Affordable prices, plenty of family-friendly activities, and easy access to the city center.
Cons: Limited nightlife options, with some areas showing signs of urban decay.

**San Salvario** (Turin)
Best For: Backpacker / Budget
Vibe: This vibrant neighborhood is known for its bohemian vibe, with a mix of street art, independent shops, and multicultural restaurants, and a lively atmosphere.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Affordable prices, plenty of cultural attractions, and a lively nightlife scene.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, with some areas showing signs of urban decay.
