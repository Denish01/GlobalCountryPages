[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Genoa, a historic port city in northwest Italy, is considered a relatively safe destination for tourists. The city has a strong police presence, and visitors can feel comfortable exploring its streets and landmarks. However, as with any major city, there are areas that are safer than others, and tourists should be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag snatching, can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, but violent crime is rare.

Genoa's old town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is generally safe, with narrow streets and historic buildings that attract tourists and locals alike. The city's waterfront area, known as the Porto Antico, is also a popular spot, with many restaurants, bars, and shops. While Genoa is a safe city, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Be mindful of your surroundings, keep an eye on your belongings, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake petitions or charitable donations
- Overcharging by taxi drivers or restaurants in tourist areas
- Petty theft in busy markets or shopping streets
- Aggressive panhandling or begging in popular areas
- Counterfeit goods or souvenirs being sold in markets or streets
- Drink spiking or theft in bars or clubs, especially at night
- ATM scams or credit card skimming in tourist areas

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Genoa, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Genoa, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it
- **Medical Facilities:** Genoa has good-quality medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, with English-speaking staff available
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available and easily accessible, with many open late or 24 hours

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the meter is running
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Be cautious when using ATMs or credit cards in tourist areas, and cover the keypad when entering your PIN
- Stay informed about local conditions and events that may affect your safety

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Genoa is generally a safe city for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong police presence
- Avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night and use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
- Be mindful of your surroundings and keep an eye on your drinks in bars or clubs, especially at night
- Consider joining a guided tour or group to explore the city and meet other travelers
