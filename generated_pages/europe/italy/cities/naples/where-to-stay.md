# Where to Stay in Naples — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Naples, the best areas to stay are in the historic center, near the waterfront, or in the upscale neighborhoods of Chiaia and Posillipo, which offer a mix of historic charm, stunning views, and modern amenities. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**Historic Center** (Naples)
Best For: Backpackers, families, and history buffs
Vibe: The historic center is a bustling and vibrant area filled with narrow streets, historic landmarks, and a plethora of restaurants and shops, offering an immersive experience of Naples' rich history and culture.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Close proximity to major attractions, lively atmosphere, and a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, especially at night.

**Chiaia** (Naples)
Best For: Luxury travelers, couples, and business travelers
Vibe: Chiaia is an upscale neighborhood known for its high-end shopping, trendy restaurants, and beautiful waterfront promenade, offering a more refined and sophisticated experience of Naples.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Upscale amenities, stunning views of the Bay of Naples, and a more relaxed atmosphere.
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and some areas may feel too touristy.

**Posillipo** (Naples)
Best For: Families, couples, and those seeking a more relaxed atmosphere
Vibe: Posillipo is a charming hilltop neighborhood offering breathtaking views of the bay, a more laid-back atmosphere, and a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking and swimming.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Panoramic views, a more relaxed atmosphere, and a range of outdoor activities.
Cons: Can be quite hilly, and some areas may feel isolated.

**Waterfront** (Naples)
Best For: Business travelers, couples, and those seeking easy access to transportation
Vibe: The waterfront area is a bustling and modern neighborhood with a range of amenities, including restaurants, bars, and shops, as well as easy access to the port and other transportation hubs.
Price Range: $120-280/night
Pros: Easy access to transportation, modern amenities, and stunning views of the bay.
Cons: Can be quite noisy, especially at night, and some areas may feel too commercial.

**Vomero** (Naples)
Best For: Families, budget travelers, and those seeking a more local experience
Vibe: Vomero is a charming and historic neighborhood located on a hilltop, offering stunning views of the city, a range of local restaurants and shops, and a more laid-back atmosphere.
Price Range: $60-180/night
Pros: Affordable prices, stunning views, and a more local experience.
Cons: Can be quite hilly, and some areas may feel isolated.

**Pizzofalcone** (Naples)
Best For: Backpackers, students, and those seeking a more bohemian atmosphere
Vibe: Pizzofalcone is a charming and historic neighborhood known for its narrow streets, local markets, and a range of affordable restaurants and bars, offering a more bohemian and laid-back experience of Naples.
Price Range: $40-120/night
Pros: Affordable prices, lively atmosphere, and a range of local amenities.
Cons: Can be quite noisy, especially at night, and some areas may feel too crowded.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September), to secure the best rates and availability.
- Consider visiting during the shoulder season (April to May or September to October) for lower prices and smaller crowds.
- Look for discounts and promotions, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings.
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Expedia, to compare prices and find the best deals.
- Negotiate with hotel staff or property owners, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during peak season and lower during the off-season.
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs and preferences.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas with high crime rates, such as the Sanità and Forcella neighborhoods.
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas, such as the historic center and train stations.
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in busy areas, and consider using a money belt or a secure bag.
- Use licensed taxis or public transportation, and avoid hitchhiking or accepting rides from strangers.
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas.
- Consider staying in a hotel or hostel with a 24-hour reception and security personnel for added safety and convenience.
