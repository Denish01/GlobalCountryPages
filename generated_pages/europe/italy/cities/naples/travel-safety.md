Naples is generally a safe city to visit, with low to moderate crime rates, but tourists should be aware of petty crimes, scams, and areas to avoid.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Naples, a bustling city in southern Italy, is a popular tourist destination known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and vibrant cultural scene. While the city is generally safe, tourists should be mindful of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. As with any major city, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, bag snatching, and scams are present, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. However, violent crimes are relatively rare, and the city has a strong police presence.

Despite its safety, Naples has a reputation for being a bit rough around the edges, with a mix of elegant historic neighborhoods and run-down areas. The city's streets can be chaotic, with narrow alleys, crowded markets, and aggressive drivers. However, with some basic precautions and an understanding of the local culture, visitors can navigate the city safely and enjoy all that Naples has to offer. It's essential to stay informed, be aware of your surroundings, and take necessary precautions to minimize the risk of becoming a victim of crime.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas, such as train stations, buses, and tourist hotspots.
- Beware of **scams** at the port, such as fake ferry ticket sellers or luggage handlers demanding tips.
- Watch out for **aggressive street vendors** in popular tourist areas, who may try to sell you overpriced goods or services.
- Be aware of **motorcycle thieves**, who may target tourists with expensive cameras or jewelry.
- Don't leave valuables unattended, as **bag snatching** is common in crowded areas.
- Be cautious of **ATM scams**, where thieves may use card skimmers or fake ATMs to steal your money.
- Beware of **overcharging** by taxi drivers or restaurants, especially in tourist areas.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Naples, but make sure you're up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Naples has good-quality medical facilities, including public and private hospitals, with many English-speaking doctors and staff.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are easily accessible, with many located in central areas and open late.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas, and keep valuables secure.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the meter is running.
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches, especially in crowded areas.
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the safe to store valuables.
- Be cautious when crossing streets, as Naples is known for its chaotic traffic.
- Learn some basic Italian phrases, such as "help" (aiuto) and "where is..." (dov'è...), to help you navigate the city.
- Stay informed about local events and demonstrations, which may affect your travel plans.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be confident and assertive, especially when dealing with aggressive street vendors or catcalls.
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night, and stick to well-lit streets and popular areas.
- Dress modestly, especially when visiting churches or cultural sites, to avoid drawing attention to yourself.
- Consider joining a guided tour or group to explore the city, especially if you're new to Naples or traveling alone.
