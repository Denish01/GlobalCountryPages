[SECTION] Overview: Italy vs Vatican City [/SECTION]
Italy and Vatican City are two neighboring countries with distinct economic contexts. Italy is a large, sovereign nation with a diverse economy, while Vatican City is a small, independent city-state with a unique economy driven by its religious and tourist activities. The cost of living in Italy is generally lower compared to Vatican City, mainly due to the latter's limited space, high demand, and exclusive tourism industry. In terms of overall expenses, Italy offers a more affordable lifestyle, with prices ranging from 20-30% lower than those in Vatican City.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Italy's economy is driven by various sectors, including manufacturing, services, and agriculture, which contributes to its relatively lower prices. On the other hand, Vatican City's economy is largely dependent on the sales of postage stamps, coins, and souvenirs, as well as investments and donations, resulting in higher prices for goods and services. Overall, Italy is a more affordable destination for expats, students, and tourists, while Vatican City is geared towards high-end tourism and exclusive experiences.
