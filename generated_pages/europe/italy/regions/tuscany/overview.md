[SECTION] What Is Tuscany? [/SECTION]
Tuscany is a **region** located in central Italy, bordered by the regions of Liguria to the northwest, Emilia-Romagna to the north, Umbria to the east, and Lazio to the southeast. The region has a long and complex history, with various civilizations and empires vying for control over the area. Today, Tuscany is a popular tourist destination, known for its **rolling hills**, **vineyards**, and **medieval towns**. The region is also home to a number of **UNESCO World Heritage Sites**, including the historic centers of Florence, Siena, and San Gimignano.

Tuscany is situated in a strategic location, with the **Apennine Mountains** to the east and the **Tyrrhenian Sea** to the west. The region's diverse landscape is characterized by **hills**, **mountains**, and **coastal plains**, making it an ideal destination for outdoor activities such as **hiking**, **cycling**, and **beachgoing**. Tuscany is also known for its **cuisine**, which is based on local ingredients such as **olive oil**, **wine**, and **fresh produce**.

The region has a strong sense of identity and a distinct **culture**, which is reflected in its **art**, **architecture**, and **traditions**. Tuscany is home to a number of **famous artists**, including **Michelangelo**, **Leonardo da Vinci**, and **Galileo Galilei**, who were all born in the region. Today, Tuscany continues to be a hub of **creative activity**, with numerous **festivals**, **museums**, and **galleries** showcasing the region's rich cultural heritage.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1000 BC - The **Etruscan civilization** emerges in Tuscany, leaving behind a legacy of **art**, **architecture**, and **engineering**.
2. 351 AD - The **Roman Empire** conquers Tuscany, introducing **Christianity** and **Latin** to the region.
3. 1200 AD - The **Medici family** rises to power in Florence, becoming one of the most influential **dynasties** in Tuscan history.
4. 1500 AD - The **Renaissance** flourishes in Tuscany, with **artists**, **writers**, and **scientists** making significant contributions to Western culture.
5. 1861 AD - Tuscany becomes part of the newly unified **Kingdom of Italy**, with Florence serving as the capital city.
6. 1944 AD - Tuscany is **liberated** from German occupation during World War II, with the region playing a significant role in the **Italian Resistance**.
7. 1982 AD - The **European Union** is established, with Tuscany becoming an integral part of the **European economy** and **cultural landscape**.

- Tuscany has a **rich cultural heritage**, with numerous **museums**, **galleries**, and **festivals** showcasing the region's **art**, **history**, and **traditions**.

- Tuscany is a popular destination for **outdoor activities**, including **hiking**, **cycling**, and **beachgoing**, with numerous **national parks** and **nature reserves** to explore.
