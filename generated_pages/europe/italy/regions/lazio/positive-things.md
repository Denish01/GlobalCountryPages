[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Colosseum**, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most iconic landmarks in Rome, is a testament to the region's rich history and architectural heritage.
- The **Vatican City**, an independent city-state within Rome, is home to numerous iconic art and architectural treasures, including St. Peter's Basilica and the Sistine Chapel.
- The **Roman Forum**, a sprawling archaeological site, showcases the ancient history and cultural significance of the region.
- The **Hadrian's Villa**, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a remarkable example of ancient Roman architecture and engineering.
- The **Festa dei Noantri**, a traditional festival in Rome, celebrates the region's cultural heritage and traditions.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Enrico Fermi**, a Nobel laureate and physicist, was born in Rome and made significant contributions to the development of nuclear physics.
- **Andrea Bocelli**, a world-renowned opera singer, was born in Tuscany but has strong ties to Lazio, where he has performed numerous times.
- The **Italian diaspora** has had a significant impact on global cuisine, with many Italian restaurants and food establishments around the world serving traditional dishes from Lazio, such as carbonara and amatriciana.
- **Don Luigi Sturzo**, a Catholic priest and politician, was born in Caltagirone, Sicily, but played a crucial role in the development of Christian democracy in Lazio and Italy as a whole.
