[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Lazio is easily accessible by air, with two main international airports, Leonardo da Vinci–Fiumicino Airport (FCO) and Ciampino–G. B. Pastine International Airport (CIA), both located near Rome. FCO is one of the busiest airports in Europe and offers direct flights to many major cities around the world, including New York, London, and Tokyo. CIA is a smaller airport that handles mostly budget and charter flights. If you prefer to travel by land, you can enter Lazio by car or bus from neighboring regions, such as Umbria, Marche, and Campania. The region is also connected to the rest of Italy by a comprehensive network of highways and roads.

In addition to air travel, Lazio can also be reached by train, with Rome's Termini station being one of the main hubs for high-speed trains in Italy. From Termini, you can take trains to other major cities in Italy, such as Milan, Florence, and Naples. If you prefer to travel by sea, you can also arrive in Lazio by ferry, with the port city of Civitavecchia being a major entry point for cruise ships and ferries from other parts of the Mediterranean.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
If you need to travel within Italy, you can use the domestic air network, which is extensive and convenient. Domestic airlines such as Alitalia, Ryanair, and EasyJet operate flights from Rome's airports to other major cities in Italy, including Milan, Venice, and Naples. You can book domestic flights online or through a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the airline, route, and time of year. On average, a one-way domestic flight can cost between $50 and $200, depending on the route and airline.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Lazio has an extensive rail network, with Rome being a major hub for trains in Italy. The region is connected to the rest of Italy by high-speed trains, such as the Frecciarossa and Italo, which can take you to other major cities like Milan, Florence, and Naples in just a few hours. You can book train tickets online or at the train station, and prices vary depending on the route, class, and time of year. On average, a one-way high-speed train ticket can cost between $60 and $200, depending on the route and class.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
If you prefer to travel by bus, Lazio has an extensive network of intercity buses that connect the region to other parts of Italy. Companies such as FlixBus and MarinoBus operate buses from Rome to other major cities, including Milan, Florence, and Naples. You can book bus tickets online or at the bus station, and prices vary depending on the route and time of year. On average, a one-way bus ticket can cost between $10 and $50, depending on the route and company.
