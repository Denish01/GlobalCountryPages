[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel, especially for shorter trips, to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local trattorias or osterias, which offer affordable and authentic Italian cuisine.
- Buy a **B.I.G.** (Roma's public transportation card) for convenient and discounted travel on buses, trams, and metro lines.
- Take advantage of free admission to museums and historical sites on the first Sunday of each month.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets for fresh produce and groceries, rather than relying on tourist-oriented stores.
- Learn some basic Italian phrases to navigate everyday situations and avoid tourist-oriented prices.
- Explore the surrounding countryside and smaller towns in Lazio, which can offer a more affordable and authentic experience.
- Consider visiting during the shoulder season (April to May or September to October), when prices for accommodations and tourist services tend to be lower. 
Additionally, visitors can save money by purchasing a **Roma Pass**, which grants free entry to several attractions and discounts on public transportation.
