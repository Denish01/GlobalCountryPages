# Cost of Living in Campania - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Campania is generally moderate, with prices comparable to other regions in southern Italy, making it an affordable destination for visitors and expats alike. 

[FACTBOX]

Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $100-200 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $200-350 USD
Currency: EUR
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 1-2 euros for good service

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Campania? [/SECTION]
Campania, a region in southern Italy, offers a unique blend of rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture, all at a relatively moderate cost. Compared to other European destinations, Campania is generally more affordable, with prices similar to those found in other parts of southern Italy. However, prices can vary depending on the location, with popular tourist areas like the Amalfi Coast and Naples tend to be more expensive than lesser-known towns and rural areas. Overall, Campania provides a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for visitors and expats.

In terms of global averages, Campania's cost of living is lower than in many Western European countries, but higher than in some Eastern European and Asian destinations. The region's economy is driven by tourism, agriculture, and industry, which contributes to its moderate cost of living. Visitors can expect to find a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts, and a diverse range of dining options, from traditional trattorias to high-end restaurants.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting during the off-season (April-May or September-October) to avoid peak tourist prices.
- Look for accommodation options outside of city centers, such as in nearby towns or rural areas, to save on rent.
- Eat at local trattorias or street food stalls, which offer delicious and affordable meals.
- Use public transportation, such as buses and metro lines, to get around, rather than taxis or rental cars.
- Take advantage of free walking tours and visit local markets to experience the region's culture and history without breaking the bank.
- Consider purchasing a Campania Artecard, which grants access to many of the region's museums, historical sites, and public transportation, at a discounted rate.
