# Campania Cities & Regions Guide

Campania, a region in southern Italy, is divided into five provinces, with Naples as its capital, and features a diverse range of cities and regions that offer a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

[SECTION] How Campania Is Divided [/SECTION]
Campania is divided into five provinces, each with its own distinct character and attractions. The provinces are Naples, Salerno, Caserta, Benevento, and Avellino. The region is also home to several smaller towns and villages, many of which are perched on hills or nestled in valleys. The administrative structure of Campania is designed to promote regional development and provide services to the local population. The provinces are further divided into communes, which are the basic administrative units of the region.

The division of Campania into provinces and communes reflects the region's complex history and geography. The provinces are often centered on major cities, such as Naples and Salerno, which serve as economic and cultural hubs. The communes, on the other hand, are often smaller and more rural, with their own unique traditions and customs. Overall, the administrative structure of Campania is designed to balance the needs of the region's urban and rural areas.

[SECTION] Major Cities [/SECTION]
**Naples** (967,000)
Role: capital, economic hub, cultural center
Known For: Naples is known for its rich history, vibrant cultural scene, and stunning natural beauty, with landmarks like the Castel dell'Ovo and the National Archaeological Museum.
Best For: visitors interested in history, culture, and food, as Naples is famous for its pizza and other culinary delights.

**Salerno** (135,000)
Role: economic hub, port city
Known For: Salerno is known for its stunning coastal location, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene, with landmarks like the Salerno Cathedral and the Castello di Arechi.
Best For: visitors interested in history, culture, and outdoor activities, as Salerno is close to the Amalfi Coast and other natural attractions.

**Caserta** (76,000)
Role: economic hub, cultural center
Known For: Caserta is known for its stunning royal palace, the Reggia di Caserta, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest palaces in the world.
Best For: visitors interested in history, architecture, and culture, as Caserta is home to several museums and cultural institutions.

**Benevento** (59,000)
Role: economic hub, cultural center
Known For: Benevento is known for its rich history, with landmarks like the Benevento Cathedral and the Roman Theatre, and its stunning natural beauty, with the Apennine Mountains nearby.
Best For: visitors interested in history, culture, and outdoor activities, as Benevento is close to several national parks and nature reserves.

**Avellino** (53,000)
Role: economic hub, cultural center
