# Getting Around Campania — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Campania, a region in southern Italy, you can use a variety of transportation options including trains, buses, ferries, and car rentals, which provide easy access to its main cities, towns, and tourist attractions.

Road Quality: moderate
Ride-Hailing Apps: Uber, local alternatives

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Campania is easily accessible by air, with Naples International Airport being the main gateway to the region. The airport is located about 3.7 miles northeast of Naples city center and offers direct flights to many European cities, as well as connections to other Italian cities. You can also reach Campania by train, with Naples being a major hub on the Italian rail network, or by bus, with many companies operating services from other Italian cities. Additionally, Campania has several ports, including the Port of Naples, which offers ferry connections to nearby islands and other coastal towns.

If you're traveling overland from other parts of Italy, you can reach Campania by car or bus, with the A1 and A3 highways connecting the region to Rome and other major cities. You can also take the train, with frequent services from Rome, Florence, and other Italian cities. Alternatively, you can take a bus or ferry from nearby regions, such as Lazio or Calabria. The region's strategic location makes it easy to explore the surrounding areas, including the Amalfi Coast, Pompeii, and the islands of Capri and Ischia.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
While Campania's domestic air network is limited, you can still fly to other Italian cities from Naples International Airport. The main domestic airlines operating in Italy are Alitalia, Ryanair, and EasyJet, which offer flights to cities such as Rome, Milan, and Venice. You can book flights online or through a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the season and availability. A one-way ticket from Naples to Rome, for example, can cost between $50 and $200, depending on the airline and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Campania has an extensive rail network, with trains being a convenient way to get around the region. The main train operator is Trenitalia, which offers frequent services to cities such as Naples, Salerno, and Caserta. You can also take high-speed trains, such as the Frecciarossa, which connects Naples to Rome and other major Italian cities. Train tickets can be booked online or at train stations, and prices vary depending on the type of train and class of service. A one-way ticket from Naples to Rome, for example, can cost between $60 and $150, depending on the train and class.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Buses and coaches are another way to get around Campania, with many companies operating services between cities and towns. The main bus companies operating in the region are SITA, FlixBus, and MarinoBus, which offer routes to cities such as Naples, Salerno, and Sorrento. Bus tickets can be booked online or at bus stations, and prices vary depending on the route and company. A one-way ticket from Naples to Salerno, for example, can cost between $10 and $30, depending on the company and time of booking.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Campania, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an International Driving Permit.
- The road quality in Campania is generally moderate, with some roads being narrow and winding, especially in rural areas.
- Fuel costs are around $6 per gallon, and you can find gas stations throughout the region.
- Car rental companies such as Hertz, Avis, and Europcar operate in Campania, and you can book a car online or at the airport.
- When driving in Campania, be aware of the region's congested roads, especially in Naples, and be prepared for aggressive driving from locals.
- Parking can be challenging in city centers, and you may need to use parking garages or street parking with a meter.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When taking a taxi, make sure to use a licensed taxi and agree on the fare before you start your journey.
- Be aware of pickpocketing and scams in crowded areas, such as train stations and tourist hotspots.
- Download the **Trenitalia** app to book train tickets and check schedules.
- Use a **rechargeable ticket** for public transport in Naples, which can be bought at newsstands or ticket machines.
- Be prepared for **traffic jams** and road closures, especially during peak season.
- Consider purchasing a **Campania Artecard**, which offers discounts and free entry to many museums and attractions.
- When driving, use a **GPS** or map to navigate the region's roads, and be aware of **toll roads** and **parking restrictions**.
- Be respectful of local **traffic rules** and **customs**, and enjoy your time exploring Campania.
