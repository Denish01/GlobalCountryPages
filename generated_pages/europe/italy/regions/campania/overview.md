[SECTION] What Is Campania? [/SECTION]
Campania is a region in southern Italy, bordered by the regions of Lazio to the north, Molise to the northeast, Apulia to the east, and Calabria to the southeast. The region has a long coastline along the Tyrrhenian Sea to the west and the Gulf of Salerno to the south. Campania is known for its stunning natural beauty, with a mix of mountains, hills, and coastal plains, as well as its rich cultural heritage, including ancient ruins, historic cities, and vibrant festivals.

The region is home to three active volcanoes: Mount Vesuvius, Mount Epomeo, and the Phlegraean Fields, which have shaped the landscape and influenced the local culture. The Campania region is also famous for its delicious cuisine, which includes dishes such as pizza, seafood, and buffalo mozzarella cheese. The region's capital, Naples, is a major city and a hub for culture, economy, and transportation.

Campania has a long and complex history, with various civilizations and empires having ruled the region over the centuries. The region has been an important center for trade, culture, and learning, and its strategic location has made it a prized possession for many rulers. Today, Campania is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.
