Sardinia is the second-largest island in the Mediterranean Sea, located west of the Italian Peninsula. It is an autonomous region of Italy, with its own regional government and a distinct cultural identity. The island is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and rugged landscape, making it a popular tourist destination. Sardinia is also home to a rich history, with evidence of human settlement dating back to the Neolithic era.

Today, Sardinia is a thriving region, with a strong sense of identity and a unique cultural heritage.
