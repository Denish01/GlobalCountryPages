Sardinia's culture is a unique blend of Italian, Mediterranean, and indigenous influences, shaped by its strategic location in the western Mediterranean and its history of invasions and migrations.

The Sardinian language has several dialects, including Campidanese, Logudorese, and Sassarese, which are spoken in different parts of the island.

Each region has its own unique cultural and linguistic characteristics, with the coastal areas being more influenced by external cultures and the mountains being more isolated and traditional.
