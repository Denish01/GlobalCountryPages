[SECTION] How Sardinia Is Divided [/SECTION]
Sardinia is an autonomous region of Italy, divided into eight provinces, each with its own unique character and attractions. The provinces are further divided into communes, which are the basic administrative units of the region. The eight provinces of Sardinia are Cagliari, Carbonia-Iglesias, Medio Campidano, Nuoro, Ogliastra, Olbia-Tempio, Oristano, and Sassari. This administrative structure allows for a degree of autonomy and self-governance, which is reflected in the region's distinct cultural and linguistic identity.

The provinces of Sardinia are diverse, with varying landscapes, economies, and cultural traditions. From the rugged mountains of Nuoro to the coastal towns of Olbia-Tempio, each province has its own unique charm and attractions. The region's capital, Cagliari, is located in the southern part of the island and serves as the administrative and economic hub of Sardinia.
