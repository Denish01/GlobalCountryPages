# Top Things to Do in Sardinia — Attractions & Experiences

Sardinia, an autonomous region in Italy, offers a wide range of exciting activities and attractions for visitors to explore, from its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters to its rich history and unique cultural heritage.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **nuragic** tour to explore the ancient stone structures and learn about the history and culture of the nuragic civilization
- Attend a traditional **Sardinian festival**, such as the Sartiglia di Oristano or the Festa dei Noantri, to experience the local music, dance, and food
- Go on a **wine tasting tour** to sample some of the region's excellent wines, such as Cannonau and Vermentino
- Take a **boat tour** of the La Maddalena archipelago to explore the stunning islands and crystal-clear waters
- Learn about the traditional **Sardinian crafts**, such as weaving and pottery, at a local workshop or market
- Attend a **traditional Sardinian dinner** to sample local specialties, such as suckling pig and seafood risotto

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Gennargentu National Park**, a stunning park with rugged mountains, forests, and wildlife
- Hike the **Selvaggio Blu** trail, a challenging but rewarding hike with stunning views of the Mediterranean
- Visit the **Cala Goloritzé** beach, a picturesque beach with crystal-clear waters and stunning rock formations
- Go **scuba diving** or **snorkeling** in the crystal-clear waters of the Costa Smeralda to explore the marine life and coral reefs
- Take a **bike tour** of the countryside to explore the rolling hills, scenic roads, and picturesque villages

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum of Archaeology** in Cagliari to learn about the history and culture of Sardinia
- Explore the **Cagliari Cathedral**, a beautiful cathedral with stunning architecture and historic significance
- Attend a **traditional Sardinian music** performance to experience the local folk music and dance
- Take a **cooking class** to learn about traditional Sardinian cuisine and ingredients
- Visit the **Museo del Coltellino**, a museum showcasing the history and production of traditional Sardinian knives

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Isola di San Pietro**, a charming island with a picturesque village, stunning beaches, and crystal-clear waters
- Explore the **Grotta del Bue Marino**, a stunning sea cave with unique rock formations and crystal-clear waters
- Take a **hike** in the **Supramonte** mountains, a rugged and scenic range with stunning views of the Mediterranean
- Visit the **Castello di Acquafredda**, a medieval castle with stunning views of the surrounding countryside
- Attend a **local market**, such as the Mercato di Cagliari, to sample local food, wine, and handicrafts

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to the **La Maddalena archipelago** from Palau, a 30-minute ferry ride, to explore the stunning islands and crystal-clear waters
- Visit the **Tharros** archaeological site from San Giovanni di Sinis, a 10-minute drive, to explore the ancient ruins and stunning views of the Mediterranean
- Take a day trip to the **Gennargentu National Park** from Dorgali, a 30-minute drive, to explore the stunning park and wildlife
- Visit the **Cala Goloritzé** beach from Dorgali, a 30-minute drive, to relax on the picturesque beach and swim in the crystal-clear waters
- Take a day trip to the **Isola di San Pietro** from Carloforte, a 30-minute ferry ride, to explore the charming island and stunning beaches
