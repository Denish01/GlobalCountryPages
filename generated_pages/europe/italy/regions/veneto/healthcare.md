[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
The healthcare system in Veneto, a region in northern Italy, is part of the country's national health service, which provides universal coverage to all citizens and residents. The system is primarily funded through taxes and social security contributions, with a small portion coming from out-of-pocket payments. Veneto's healthcare system is known for its high quality, with well-equipped hospitals, skilled medical professionals, and a wide range of specialized services. While public healthcare is generally of high quality, private healthcare options are also available for those who prefer more personalized care or shorter wait times. In urban areas, access to healthcare is generally excellent, with many hospitals and clinics located throughout the region's major cities, including Venice, Verona, and Padua. In rural areas, access to healthcare may be more limited, but the region has made efforts to improve services in these areas, including the use of telemedicine and mobile health units.

The public healthcare system in Veneto is free or low-cost for most medical services, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and prescriptions. However, some services, such as dental care and cosmetic procedures, may require out-of-pocket payments. The region's healthcare system is also known for its emphasis on preventive care, with regular check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations available to all residents. Overall, Veneto's healthcare system is well-regarded and provides high-quality care to its residents and visitors.

Veneto's healthcare system is also characterized by a strong network of healthcare professionals, including doctors, nurses, and other medical specialists. The region is home to several major medical schools and research institutions, which helps to attract top talent and drive innovation in the field. Additionally, Veneto's healthcare system is known for its use of advanced technology, including electronic health records and telemedicine, which helps to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of care.

- **Private health insurance**: available for those who prefer more personalized care or shorter wait times, with a range of options available from local and international providers
- **Expatriate health insurance**: recommended for foreign residents, with many local and international providers offering coverage options
- **EU Health Insurance Card**: available to EU citizens, providing access to public healthcare services in Veneto
- **Travel health insurance**: recommended for visitors, providing coverage for medical emergencies and other unexpected expenses
- **Supplemental insurance**: available for services not covered by the national health service, such as dental care and cosmetic procedures
