[SECTION] Overview: Italy vs San Marino [/SECTION]
Italy and San Marino are two neighboring countries in Europe, with distinct economic profiles. Italy is a large and diverse country with a population of over 59 million people, while San Marino is a small, independent republic with a population of just over 34,000. The cost of living in Italy is generally lower than in San Marino, thanks to the larger country's more competitive markets and lower prices for many everyday items. However, San Marino's unique location and tax regime make it an attractive destination for some expats and businesses.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Italy has a more developed and diversified economy, with a strong manufacturing sector and a large service industry. San Marino, on the other hand, has a more limited economy, with a focus on financial services, tourism, and light industry. As a result, San Marino tends to have higher prices for many goods and services, particularly in the areas of housing, food, and transportation. Overall, Italy is the more affordable option for most people, with a cost of living that is around 10-20% lower than in San Marino.
