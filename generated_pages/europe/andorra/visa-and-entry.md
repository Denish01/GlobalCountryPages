# Andorra Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Andorra before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Andorra has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others require a visa. The country is not a member of the European Union (EU), but it has a special relationship with the EU, which allows for a relatively liberal visa policy. Citizens of over 100 countries, including the US, Canada, and Australia, can enter Andorra visa-free for tourism and business purposes. However, citizens of other countries, including India and China, require a visa to enter Andorra.

Andorra's visa policy is designed to promote tourism and economic development, while also ensuring the security and integrity of the country's borders. The country has a relatively simple visa application process, and most visas are issued within a few days. However, it's essential to check the current visa requirements before traveling to Andorra, as visa rules can change frequently.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Andorra visa-free for up to 90 days, provided they have a valid passport and proof of onward travel. The typical stay for tourists is 7-10 days, and extensions are possible but require documentation and approval from the authorities.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must provide an invitation letter from a local company, proof of business activities, and other supporting documents.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in Andorra must provide proof of enrollment in a local educational institution, a valid passport, and health insurance. The duration of stay is typically limited to the duration of the study program.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Andorra must have a valid work permit, which is typically sponsored by a local employer. The application process involves submitting various documents, including a contract, proof of qualifications, and health insurance.

[SECTION] Documents Checklist [/SECTION]
1. Valid passport with at least six months' validity

3. Proof of sufficient funds (e.g., cash, credit cards, or traveler's checks)

6. Proof of enrollment (for students)
7. Work permit (for workers)
8. Contract or employment agreement (for workers)
