Andorra's education system is known for its high quality, with a strong emphasis on the **Catalan language** and culture. Private schools, on the other hand, offer an alternative for families who prefer a more personalized education or a specific curriculum, such as **International Baccalaureate** or **French** programs. Compulsory education in Andorra lasts for 10 years, from the age of 6 to 16, and is divided into primary and secondary education. International schools are also available in Andorra, catering to the needs of expat families and offering a range of curricula, including **British**, **American**, and **International** programs.

The language of instruction in Andorra's public schools is **Catalan**, although some private schools may offer instruction in other languages, such as **Spanish**, **French**, or **English**. The education system in Andorra is generally considered to be of high quality, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and **vocational training**. The country has a number of agreements with neighboring countries, such as **France** and **Spain**, which allow students to pursue higher education opportunities abroad. Additionally, Andorra has a number of international schools that offer a range of curricula and programs, making it an attractive destination for expat families.
