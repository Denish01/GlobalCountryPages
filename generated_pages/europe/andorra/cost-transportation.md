[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Andorra is a small, mountainous country with a well-developed transportation system, making it easy to get around without a car. The main transportation modes in Andorra include buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services, with many residents and tourists also opting to walk or cycle. The quality of public transit in Andorra is generally good, with frequent bus services connecting the capital, Andorra la Vella, to other towns and villages. Most people get around Andorra by using the public bus system or by walking, as the country is relatively small and many destinations are within walking distance.

The public transportation system in Andorra is operated by private companies, with buses running regularly throughout the day. The country's terrain can make driving challenging, especially during the winter months, but the roads are generally well-maintained. Many tourists also visit Andorra for its ski resorts, which are accessible by bus or car. Overall, Andorra's transportation system is convenient and relatively affordable, making it easy to explore the country without breaking the bank.
