[SECTION] Overview: Andorra vs Spain [/SECTION]
Andorra and Spain are two neighboring countries in Europe with distinct economic profiles. Andorra, a small sovereign state located in the Pyrenees mountains, has a high standard of living and a strong economy driven by tourism, finance, and trade. Spain, on the other hand, is a larger country with a more diversified economy and a lower cost of living. The cost of living in Andorra is generally higher than in Spain, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being significantly higher in Andorra. However, Andorra's lower taxes and higher salaries can make it an attractive option for some expats and businesses.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Andorra has a highly developed economy with a strong focus on services, while Spain has a more mixed economy with a significant manufacturing sector. The cost of living in Andorra is also influenced by its unique tax regime, which includes low taxes on income and wealth. In contrast, Spain has a more traditional tax system with higher taxes on income and wealth. Overall, while Andorra is generally more expensive than Spain, the specific costs and lifestyle can vary greatly, and individuals should carefully consider their own needs and circumstances when deciding which country to choose.
