[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Andorra is considered a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates and a strong emphasis on tourism. The country's small size and isolated location in the Pyrenees mountains contribute to its safe and peaceful atmosphere. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around the capital city, Andorra la Vella, and exploring the surrounding countryside without worrying about their personal safety. However, as with any popular tourist destination, there are some petty crimes and scams that visitors should be aware of, such as pickpocketing and overcharging by some vendors.

Overall, Andorra is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience a unique and safe European country. The country's stunning natural beauty, modern amenities, and friendly locals make it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, shoppers, and families. With some basic precautions and an understanding of the local culture, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Andorra. It's also worth noting that Andorra has a well-organized and efficient emergency services system, with a single emergency number (112) that can be used to contact the police, ambulance, or fire department in case of an emergency.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Overcharging by some vendors, particularly in markets and souvenir shops
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake police officers or street performers
- Petty theft in hotels and apartments, particularly if doors and windows are left unlocked
- Traffic accidents, particularly on mountain roads
- Slippery roads and sidewalks during winter months
- Altitude sickness in high-altitude areas
- Sunburn and heat exhaustion during summer months

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Andorra, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Andorra, but bottled water is widely available
- **Medical Facilities:** Andorra has a well-developed healthcare system, with modern hospitals and medical facilities available in the capital city and larger towns
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available and well-stocked, with many open late or 24 hours

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a hotel safe
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Be cautious when using ATMs and avoid using them in isolated areas
- Keep hotel doors and windows locked, particularly in apartments or rooms with balconies
- Wear warm clothing and sturdy shoes during winter months
- Bring sunscreen and a hat during summer months

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Andorra is a very safe country for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong emphasis on tourism
- Women travelers can feel comfortable walking alone in the capital city and surrounding areas, but should still take basic precautions such as avoiding isolated areas at night
- Solo travelers can consider joining a guided tour or group to meet other travelers and explore the country
- Women travelers should be aware of their surroundings and avoid engaging with aggressive or persistent vendors or panhandlers
