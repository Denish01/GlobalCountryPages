[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Slovakia is a landlocked country situated in the heart of Europe, nestled in the **Carpathian Mountains**. It shares borders with five countries: the Czech Republic to the northwest, Poland to the north, Ukraine to the east, Hungary to the south, and Austria to the southwest. The country's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe has made it an important hub for trade and cultural exchange throughout history. The **Danube River**, which forms part of the border with Hungary and Austria, is a significant waterway that has played a crucial role in shaping the country's economy and culture.

Slovakia's borders are entirely land-based, with no direct access to the sea. However, the country's proximity to the **Black Sea** and the **Baltic Sea** has facilitated trade and cultural exchange with other European countries. The country's terrain is varied, with mountains, forests, and rivers that have created a unique landscape. The **Tatra Mountains**, which form part of the Carpathian range, are a popular destination for hiking and skiing, while the **Slovak Paradise** national park is known for its stunning scenery and diverse wildlife.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Slovakia's terrain is characterized by a mix of mountains, forests, and lowlands. The **Carpathian Mountains** cover much of the country, with the **Tatra Mountains** being the highest range. The Tatra Mountains are a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with their rugged peaks, alpine lakes, and picturesque villages. The **Fatra Mountains** and the **Slovak Ore Mountains** are other notable ranges that offer opportunities for hiking, skiing, and exploration. The country's mountains are also home to a number of **caves**, including the famous **Demänovská Cave of Liberty**, which is one of the longest cave systems in Europe.

The country's forests cover about 40% of the land area, with a mix of deciduous and coniferous trees. The **Slovak Paradise** national park is a protected area that is known for its stunning scenery and diverse wildlife, including **brown bears**, **wolves**, and **lynx**. The park's unique landscape features a mix of mountains, forests, and canyons, with numerous hiking trails and scenic viewpoints. The country's rivers, including the **Danube**, **Váh**, and **Hron**, flow through the valleys and lowlands, providing a source of water and supporting a range of aquatic life.

Slovakia's landscape has been shaped by its geological history, with the country's mountains and valleys formed through a combination of tectonic activity and erosion. The country's **soil** is varied, with a mix of fertile lowlands and poorer soils in the mountains. The country's **mineral resources**, including **coal**, **iron**, and **copper**, have been exploited for centuries, with mining playing an important role in the country's economy.
