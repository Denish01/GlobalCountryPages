# Education Costs in Slovakia

The cost of education in Slovakia varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Slovakia is divided into public and private institutions, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for local students. The quality of public education is considered to be good, with a focus on the **Slovak language** and culture. However, some public schools may lack resources and infrastructure compared to private institutions. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a more diverse range of programs and languages, including **English**, **German**, and **French**. Compulsory education in Slovakia lasts for 10 years, starting from the age of 6 and ending at 16. International schools are also available in major cities like **Bratislava**, offering a range of curricula, including **IB**, **Cambridge**, and **American** programs.

The education system in Slovakia is overseen by the **Ministry of Education, Science, Research and Sport**, which sets the curriculum and standards for all schools. The academic year typically runs from September to June, with a winter break in February and a summer break in July and August. Students can choose from a range of programs, including vocational training, technical schools, and **gymnasiums**, which prepare students for university studies. Overall, the education system in Slovakia provides students with a solid foundation in academics, as well as opportunities for personal development and growth.
