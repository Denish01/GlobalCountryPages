Visa Policy Overview

Slovakia has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter visa-free for short-term stays. As a member of the European Union and the Schengen Area, Slovakia's visa policy is aligned with other EU countries. Citizens of over 60 countries can enter Slovakia without a visa for up to 90 days within a 180-day period. This includes citizens of the US, Canada, Australia, and the UK. However, it's essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it's always best to check with the Slovak embassy or consulate for the most up-to-date information.

Slovakia's visa policy is designed to facilitate travel and trade while ensuring the security and integrity of the country's borders. The country has a well-developed infrastructure for processing visa applications, and most applications are processed within a few days. However, it's recommended to apply for a visa well in advance of your planned travel date to avoid any delays or complications.

Tourists: Tourists can enter Slovakia without a visa for up to 90 days within a 180-day period. The typical stay for tourists is 7-10 days, and extensions are possible in exceptional cases. Tourists must have a valid passport, travel insurance, and proof of sufficient funds to support their stay.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may enter Slovakia without a visa for up to 90 days within a 180-day period. They must have a valid passport, an invitation letter from a Slovak company, and proof of business activities. Business travelers may also need to register with the Slovak authorities.
Students: Students must have a valid passport, a student visa, and proof of enrollment at a Slovak university. The student visa is usually issued for the duration of the study program, and students must register with the Slovak authorities within 3 days of arrival.
Workers: Workers must have a valid passport, a work permit, and a contract with a Slovak employer. The work permit is usually issued for a specific period, and workers must register with the Slovak authorities within 3 days of arrival.
