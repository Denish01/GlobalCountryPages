Foreign Ownership Rules

  • Foreigners can buy property in Slovakia without any restrictions, but they may need to obtain a permit from the Ministry of Justice in certain cases.
  • Foreign companies can also buy property in Slovakia, but they may be subject to additional regulations and requirements.
  • Foreigners who are not residents of Slovakia may need to appoint a power of attorney to represent them in the buying process.
  • Foreigners who buy property in Slovakia may be subject to certain tax implications, including capital gains tax and income tax on rental income.
  • Foreigners who are not residents of Slovakia may need to obtain a tax identification number in order to buy property and pay taxes.

  1. Research and find a property: This can be done through a real estate agent, online listings, or by driving around and looking for properties that are for sale.
  2. Appoint a power of attorney: If the buyer is not a resident of Slovakia, they may need to appoint a power of attorney to represent them in the buying process.
  3. Obtain a permit: In certain cases, the buyer may need to obtain a permit from the Ministry of Justice in order to buy the property.
  4. Sign a reservation agreement: Once the buyer has found a property, they can sign a reservation agreement to secure the property and prevent it from being sold to someone else.
  5. Conduct due diligence: The buyer should conduct due diligence on the property, including checking the ownership documents and ensuring that there are no outstanding debts or liabilities.
  6. Sign a purchase agreement: Once the buyer is satisfied with the property, they can sign a purchase agreement with the seller.
  7. Pay a deposit: The buyer will typically need to pay a deposit of around 10% of the purchase price to secure the property.
  8. Complete the purchase: The buyer will need to complete the purchase by paying the remaining balance of the purchase price and transferring the ownership documents.
  9. Register the property: The buyer will need to register the property with the local authorities and obtain a new title deed.
  10. Obtain a tax identification number: The buyer may need to obtain a tax identification number in order to pay taxes on the property.
