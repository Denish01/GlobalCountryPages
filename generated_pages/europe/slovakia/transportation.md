# Getting Around Slovakia — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Slovakia, you can use a combination of public transportation options, including buses, trains, and taxis, as well as ride-hailing apps and car rentals, with the country's well-developed infrastructure making it easy to navigate.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Slovakia is easily accessible by air, with two main international airports: M. R. Štefánik Airport in Bratislava and Košice International Airport in Košice. Several airlines offer direct flights to Slovakia from major European cities, including Vienna, Prague, and Warsaw. You can also enter the country by land, with several border crossings from neighboring countries such as the Czech Republic, Poland, Ukraine, Hungary, and Austria. The main overland entry points are located near the cities of Bratislava, Žilina, and Košice, with regular bus and train services connecting Slovakia to its neighboring countries.

If you prefer to travel by land, you can take a bus or train from nearby cities, such as Vienna, Prague, or Budapest. Several bus companies, including FlixBus and Eurolines, operate regular services to Slovakia, while train operators like Czech Railways and Hungarian State Railways offer connections to major Slovak cities. Additionally, you can also enter Slovakia by car, with several highway connections to neighboring countries, including the D1 highway from the Czech Republic and the M1 highway from Hungary.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Slovakia has a limited domestic air network, with only a few flights operating between Bratislava and Košice. The main domestic airline is **Danube Wings**, which offers regular flights between the two cities. You can book flights online through the airline's website or through a travel agency. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $50 to $100 USD, depending on the time of booking and the season. It's worth noting that domestic flights are not always the most convenient option, as train and bus services are often more frequent and affordable.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Slovakia has an extensive rail network, with regular train services connecting major cities like Bratislava, Žilina, and Košice. The main train operator is **Železničná spoločnosť Slovensko** (ZSSK), which offers a range of services, including high-speed trains and regional trains. You can book train tickets online or at train stations, with prices starting from around $10 USD for regional trains and $30 USD for high-speed trains. The rail network is well-developed, with comfortable and clean trains, making it a convenient option for traveling around the country.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Slovakia has a well-developed bus network, with several companies operating intercity services. The main bus companies are **SAD Žilina** and **Eurolines**, which offer regular services to major cities and towns. Bus tickets can be booked online or at bus stations, with prices starting from around $5 USD for regional buses and $20 USD for long-distance buses. The bus network is extensive, with frequent services and comfortable buses, making it a popular option for travelers.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Slovakia, you need a valid **driver's license** from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The road quality in Slovakia is generally good, with well-maintained highways and roads.
- Fuel costs are relatively low, with prices starting from around $1.20 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies like **Europcar** and **Hertz** are available at major airports and cities, with prices starting from around $40 USD per day.
- When driving in Slovakia, be aware of **speed limits**, which are 130 km/h on highways and 50 km/h in urban areas.
- Be careful when driving on **narrow roads** and **mountain passes**, as they can be challenging, especially during winter months.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When using public transportation, make sure to **validate your ticket** before boarding, as fines can be imposed for unvalidated tickets.
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
- Download the **ŽSSK** or **SAD Žilina** app to book train or bus tickets in advance.
- Use a **reputable taxi company** or ride-hailing app to avoid scams.
- Always **check the price** before taking a taxi or using a ride-hailing service.
- Be mindful of **traffic rules** and **speed limits**, as Slovakia has strict traffic laws.
- Consider purchasing a **public transportation card** for convenient travel in cities.
- Learn some basic **Slovak phrases**, such as "hello" (ah-ho) and "thank you" (dh-kuh), to help you navigate the country.
