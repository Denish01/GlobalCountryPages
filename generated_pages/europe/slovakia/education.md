[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Slovakia is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, also known as **basic education**, is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 15 and lasts for 9 years. Secondary education, which includes **general secondary schools** and **vocational schools**, typically lasts for 2-4 years and provides students with a range of academic and practical skills. Tertiary education, which includes **universities** and **colleges**, offers a wide range of academic programs and is generally open to students who have completed their secondary education.

The quality of education in Slovakia is generally high, with a strong emphasis on **science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM)** subjects. The education system is largely public, with the majority of schools and universities funded by the government. However, there are also a growing number of private schools and universities, which offer a range of alternative educational programs.

The education system in Slovakia is also known for its **affordability**, with tuition fees for public universities generally lower than those in other European countries. This, combined with the country's **rich cultural heritage** and **friendly people**, makes Slovakia an attractive destination for international students.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- There are a number of international schools in Slovakia, particularly in Bratislava and other major cities, which offer a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate (IB)**, **British**, and **American** programs.
- These schools cater to the needs of expatriate families and offer a high-quality education that is recognized internationally.
- The costs of attending an international school in Slovakia can vary, but are generally higher than those of public schools, with tuition fees ranging from 5,000 to 15,000 EUR per year.
- Some of the most popular international schools in Slovakia include the **British International School** in Bratislava and the **American International School** in Košice.
- Many international schools in Slovakia also offer a range of extracurricular activities, including sports, music, and art programs.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Slovakia, international students will need to obtain a **student visa**, which can be applied for at the Slovak embassy or consulate in their home country.
- Students will also need to demonstrate a **good knowledge of the Slovak language**, although many universities offer programs taught in English and other languages.
- There are a range of **scholarships** available to international students, including government-funded scholarships and those offered by individual universities.
- The **cost of living** in Slovakia is generally lower than in other European countries, with accommodation, food, and other living expenses relatively affordable.
- International students will also need to have **health insurance** that covers them for the duration of their stay in Slovakia.
- Many universities in Slovakia also offer **language support** and **orientation programs** to help international students settle in and adjust to life in the country.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the strengths of the education system in Slovakia is its **strong emphasis on STEM subjects**, which has helped to drive innovation and economic growth in the country.
- However, the system also faces a number of **challenges**, including a shortage of qualified teachers in certain subjects and a need for greater investment in educational infrastructure.
- In recent years, the Slovak government has implemented a number of **reforms** aimed at improving the quality of education, including the introduction of new curricula and teaching methods.
- The education system in Slovakia is also **highly centralized**, which can make it difficult for schools and universities to respond to local needs and priorities.
- Despite these challenges, the education system in Slovakia remains **highly respected** and is recognized for its **academic excellence** and **research quality**.
