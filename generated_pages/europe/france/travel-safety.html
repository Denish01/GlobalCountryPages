Overall Safety Assessment

France is considered a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other popular tourist destinations. The country has a strong police presence, and most visitors can feel comfortable walking around during the day and night. However, as with any popular tourist destination, petty crimes such as pickpocketing and scams can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as keeping a close eye on their luggage and being mindful of their surroundings.

It's worth noting that France has experienced some high-profile terrorist attacks in recent years, but the country has taken significant steps to increase security and prevent future attacks. Visitors can expect to see a visible police presence, particularly in areas with high foot traffic, and should follow any instructions or guidance provided by local authorities. Overall, France is a safe and enjoyable destination for visitors, and with some basic precautions, most people can have a trouble-free and enjoyable trip.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and bag snatching are common in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, particularly in Paris and other major cities.
  • Scams targeting tourists, such as the "gold ring" scam, where a person drops a ring and asks for money, are common in popular areas.
  • Be aware of ATM and credit card scams, particularly in tourist areas, and always cover the keypad when entering your PIN.
  • Be cautious of overly friendly strangers, particularly in bars and clubs, and never leave your drink unattended.
  • Be aware of traffic and crowds in popular areas, and take necessary precautions to stay safe.
  • Be mindful of demonstrations and protests, which can occur in major cities, and avoid areas where they are taking place.
  • Be aware of bike theft, particularly in major cities, and always lock your bike securely.

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to France, but it's always a good idea to make sure your routine vaccinations are up to date.
  • Water Safety: Tap water is safe to drink in France, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it.
  • Medical Facilities: France has a high-quality healthcare system, with well-equipped hospitals and medical facilities available throughout the country. Visitors from the EU can access medical care using their EHIC card, while visitors from outside the EU should have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available in France, and visitors can easily access prescription medications and other healthcare products.

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always be mindful of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
  • Keep a close eye on your luggage and belongings, particularly in areas with high foot traffic.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead.
  • Be aware of your drink and food at all times, and never leave them unattended.
  • Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas, particularly at night.
  • Keep your hotel room door locked, and use the hotel safe to store valuable items.
  • Be aware of local customs and traditions, particularly in rural areas, and respect them.

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • France is generally a safe country for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community.
  • However, visitors should still take normal precautions to stay safe, such as avoiding walking alone in isolated areas at night and being mindful of their surroundings.
  • Be aware of local customs and traditions, particularly in rural areas, and respect them.
  • Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, particularly at night, and avoid engaging with overly friendly strangers.
