# Cost of Living in Nantes - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Nantes is generally considered to be moderate, with daily expenses ranging from $60-120 USD per day for a backpacker to $200-350 USD per day for a mid-range traveler.

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Nantes? [/SECTION]
Nantes is a city located in the western part of France, and its cost of living is relatively lower compared to other major cities in the country, such as Paris. However, it is still considered to be one of the more expensive cities in France, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation being higher than in some other parts of the country. Compared to global averages, Nantes is moderately priced, with a cost of living index score of around 75, which is lower than cities like New York or London, but higher than cities like Berlin or Budapest.

In terms of neighboring cities, Nantes is more expensive than some of the smaller towns and cities in the surrounding region, but less expensive than larger cities like Paris or Lyon. The city's moderate cost of living makes it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike, offering a great balance between quality of life and affordability. The city's economy is also relatively strong, with a diverse range of industries, including technology, healthcare, and education, which helps to drive up demand for accommodation and other services, and consequently, prices.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel, which can be significantly cheaper than mid-range or luxury options.

- Take advantage of free or low-cost attractions, such as museums or parks, which can be a great way to explore the city without breaking the bank.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets, which often offer fresh produce and other essentials at lower prices than convenience stores or tourist areas.
- Look for discounts or promotions, such as happy hour deals or special offers, which can help to reduce costs and make the most of your budget.
