Location & Borders

Nantes is situated in the western part of France, approximately 340 kilometers west of Paris, and is the capital of the Loire-Atlantique department in the Pays de la Loire region. The city is nestled in the Loire Valley, where the Erdre and Sèvre rivers converge, and is surrounded by a diverse range of landscapes, from the Atlantic coast to the rolling hills and forests of the interior. The city's strategic location has made it an important center for trade and commerce throughout history, with the nearby port city of Saint-Nazaire providing access to the Atlantic Ocean.

The city of Nantes is bordered by several other communes, including Rezé, Vertou, and Saint-Sébastien-sur-Loire, and is situated near the Brittany region, which is known for its unique cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. The nearby Loire River, which flows through the city, has played a significant role in shaping the history and development of Nantes, and the city's picturesque riverbanks and canals are now popular tourist attractions. The region's mild climate and rich soil have also made it an ideal location for agriculture, with the surrounding countryside producing a wide range of crops, including wheat, corn, and grapes.

The terrain and landscape of Nantes are characterized by a mix of urban and natural environments, with the city's historic center featuring a range of architectural styles, from medieval to modern. The city's urban landscape is also characterized by a range of parks and gardens, including the Jardin des Plantes, which features a wide range of plant species and a beautiful rose garden.
