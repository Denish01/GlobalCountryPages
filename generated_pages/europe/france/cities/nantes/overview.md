[SECTION] What Is Nantes? [/SECTION]
Nantes is a city in the Loire-Atlantique department in the Pays de la Loire region of western France. It is situated on the Loire River, approximately 50 kilometers from the Atlantic coast. The city has a long and complex history, having been an important center of trade and culture since the Middle Ages. Today, Nantes is a thriving metropolis with a strong economy, a rich cultural scene, and a high quality of life.

Nantes is known for its beautiful architecture, which reflects its history as a major port city. The city's old town features many historic buildings, including the stunning Nantes Cathedral and the Château des Ducs de Bretagne, a medieval fortress that now serves as a museum. The city is also home to several parks and gardens, including the Jardin des Plantes, a beautiful botanical garden that dates back to the 19th century.

In addition to its cultural and historical attractions, Nantes is also a hub for business and innovation. The city is home to several major companies, including the aerospace firm Airbus, and has a strong reputation for research and development. The city is also a popular destination for tourists, with over 2 million visitors per year.
