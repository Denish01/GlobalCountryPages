# Lyon Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Lyon, be sure to try the city's renowned **salade lyonnaise**, **quenelles**, and **tarte aux pralines**, which are just a few examples of the rich and diverse culinary delights that Lyon has to offer.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: pork, beef, chicken, and freshwater fish
National Dish: salade lyonnaise, a salad made with lettuce, bacon, and a poached egg
Meal Times: typical breakfast times are between 7:30 and 10:00 am, lunch between 12:00 and 2:00 pm, and dinner between 7:30 and 10:00 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: it is customary to leave a 5-10% tip
Dietary Notes: Lyon's cuisine is not particularly halal or kosher-friendly, but there are many vegetarian-friendly options available
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Lyon's cuisine is known for its rich flavors and hearty portions, and is heavily influenced by the city's history as a major trade center. The city's unique cultural identity is reflected in its culinary traditions, which combine elements of French, Italian, and Mediterranean cuisine. One of the key influences on Lyonnaise cuisine is the city's proximity to the countryside, which provides an abundance of fresh produce, meat, and dairy products. The city's chefs make use of these local ingredients to create a wide range of dishes, from simple salads and soups to complex meat and fish dishes.

Lyon's culinary scene is also characterized by its many **bouchons**, which are small, informal restaurants that serve traditional Lyonnaise cuisine. These restaurants are often family-owned and operated, and offer a cozy and welcoming atmosphere in which to enjoy a meal. In addition to the bouchons, Lyon is also home to many **mères**, which are restaurants that specialize in traditional Lyonnaise cuisine. These restaurants are often more formal than the bouchons, and offer a wide range of dishes made with fresh, local ingredients.

Lyon's cuisine is also known for its many regional variations, which reflect the city's location at the crossroads of several different culinary traditions. The city's cuisine has been influenced by the cuisine of the surrounding regions, including the **Dauphiné** and **Savoie**. This has resulted in a wide range of dishes that are unique to Lyon and the surrounding area.

- it is customary to keep your hands visible on the table, rather than in your lap
- it is customary to not leave the table until everyone is finished eating
