# Top Things to Do in Lyon — Attractions & Experiences

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **gastronomic tour** of Lyon's famous cuisine, including traditional dishes like salade lyonnaise and quenelles
- Explore the **traboules**, narrow passageways that connect buildings and streets in the historic district
- Visit the **Lyon Festival of Lights**, a annual event that showcases stunning light installations and performances
- Take a **boat tour** of the Rhône and Saône rivers, which offer stunning views of the city
- Attend a **football match** at the Groupama Stadium, home of Olympique Lyonnais
- Visit the **Lyon Silk Museum**, which showcases the city's rich history of silk production and textile design
- Explore the **Croix-Rousse neighborhood**, a historic district known for its bohemian vibe and street art

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Parc de la Tête d'Or**, a large urban park with a lake, botanical garden, and zoo
- **Rhône River**, which offers scenic views and boat tours
- **Saône River**, which flows through the heart of the city and offers stunning views
- **Lyon Mountains**, a range of hills and mountains that offer hiking and skiing opportunities
- **Lac de Bourget**, a large lake that offers boating and water sports
- **Pilat Regional Natural Park**, a protected area that offers hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Musée des Beaux-Arts de Lyon**, a museum that features an extensive collection of art and artifacts
- **Lyon Cathedral**, a beautiful Gothic church with stunning stained-glass windows
- **Roman Theatres and Odeon of Fourvière**, ancient Roman ruins that offer stunning views of the city
- **Maison de la Danse**, a theater that features contemporary dance performances and workshops
- **Institut Lumière**, a museum that showcases the history of cinema and the Lumière brothers
- **Théâtre de la Croix-Rousse**, a historic theater that features performances and concerts

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **Le Village des Créateurs**, a neighborhood that features independent boutiques and art galleries
- **La Maison des Canuts**, a museum that showcases the history of silk production and textile design
- **Le Jardin des Plantes**, a botanical garden that features a wide range of plant species
- **La Place du Change**, a historic square that features a beautiful fountain and cafes
- **Le Musée des Arts Décoratifs**, a museum that features a collection of decorative arts and design

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- **Annecy**, a charming town located about 140 km from Lyon, known for its canals and old town (2 hours by car or train)
- **Chambéry**, a historic town located about 100 km from Lyon, known for its medieval castle and stunning views of the Alps (1.5 hours by car or train)
- **Grenoble**, a city located about 100 km from Lyon, known for its stunning mountain views and outdoor activities (1.5 hours by car or train)
- **Beaujolais**, a wine region located about 50 km from Lyon, known for its vineyards and wine tastings (1 hour by car)
- **Perouges**, a medieval village located about 30 km from Lyon, known for its narrow streets and historic architecture (45 minutes by car)

With its rich history, cultural significance, and natural beauty, Lyon is a city that has something to offer for every type of traveler.
