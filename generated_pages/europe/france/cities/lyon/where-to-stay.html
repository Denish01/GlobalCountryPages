To find the best place to stay in Lyon, consider the top neighborhoods of Vieux Lyon, Croix-Rousse, Presqu'île, Part-Dieu, Bellecour, Guillotière, and Confluence, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers.

Best Areas to Stay

Vieux Lyon (Lyon)

Best For: Luxury / History Buffs

Vibe: This historic neighborhood is known for its cobblestone streets, medieval architecture, and vibrant cultural scene, offering a unique and immersive experience. Its narrow streets are filled with boutique shops, restaurants, and bars, making it perfect for those who want to be in the heart of the action.

Price Range: $150-300/night

Pros: Rich history, cultural attractions, and a wide range of dining options, Vieux Lyon is a great choice for those who want to experience the authentic Lyon. It is also close to many major landmarks and public transportation. The neighborhood is relatively safe and well-lit at night.

Cons: It can be quite touristy and expensive, and some areas can be quite crowded.

Croix-Rousse (Lyon)

Best For: Backpacker / Artists

Vibe: This bohemian neighborhood is known for its street art, independent shops, and lively atmosphere, making it a great choice for young travelers and artists. The neighborhood is also home to many bars, clubs, and live music venues, making it a great spot for those who want to experience Lyon's nightlife.

Price Range: $80-150/night

Pros: Affordable, vibrant atmosphere, and a wide range of cultural attractions, Croix-Rousse is a great choice for those who want to experience the alternative side of Lyon. The neighborhood is also relatively safe and has a strong sense of community.

Cons: It can be quite noisy and crowded, especially at night.

Presqu'île (Lyon)

Best For: Business / Families

Vibe: This peninsula neighborhood is known for its beautiful parks, historic landmarks, and modern amenities, making it a great choice for families and business travelers. The neighborhood is also home to many restaurants, cafes, and shops, making it a great spot for those who want to experience the modern side of Lyon.

Price Range: $100-250/night

Pros: Central location, modern amenities, and a wide range of dining options, Presqu'île is a great choice for those who want to experience the best of Lyon. The neighborhood is also relatively safe and well-connected to public transportation.

Cons: It can be quite expensive and crowded, especially during peak season.

Part-Dieu (Lyon)

Best For: Business / Luxury

Vibe: This modern neighborhood is known for its sleek skyscrapers, upscale shopping centers, and vibrant business scene, making it a great choice for business travelers and those who want to experience the luxurious side of Lyon. The neighborhood is also home to many restaurants, bars, and entertainment venues, making it a great spot for those who want to experience the modern nightlife of Lyon.

Price Range: $150-300/night

Pros: Modern amenities, upscale shopping, and a wide range of dining options, Part-Dieu is a great choice for those who want to experience the best of Lyon's business and luxury scene. The neighborhood is also relatively safe and well-connected to public transportation.

Cons: It can be quite expensive and formal, and some areas can be quite crowded.

Bellecour (Lyon)

Best For: Families / Tourists

Vibe: This charming neighborhood is known for its beautiful squares, historic landmarks, and lively atmosphere, making it a great choice for families and tourists. The neighborhood is also home to many restaurants, cafes, and shops, making it a great spot for those who want to experience the authentic Lyon.

Price Range: $80-200/night

Pros: Central location, historic landmarks, and a wide range of dining options, Bellecour is a great choice for those who want to experience the best of Lyon. The neighborhood is also relatively safe and well-connected to public transportation.

Cons: It can be quite crowded and touristy, and some areas can be quite noisy.

Guillotière (Lyon)

Best For: Budget / Students

Vibe: This vibrant neighborhood is known for its street art, independent shops, and lively atmosphere, making it a great choice for budget travelers and students. The neighborhood is also home to many bars, clubs, and live music venues, making it a great spot for those who want to experience Lyon's nightlife.

Price Range: $50-100/night

Pros: Affordable, vibrant atmosphere, and a wide range of cultural attractions, Guillotière is a great choice for those who want to experience the alternative side of Lyon. The neighborhood is also relatively safe and has a strong sense of community.

Cons: It can be quite noisy and crowded, especially at night.

Confluence (Lyon)

Best For: Luxury / Modern

Vibe: This modern neighborhood is known for its sleek architecture, upscale shopping centers, and vibrant business scene, making it a great choice for luxury travelers and those who want to experience the modern side of Lyon. The neighborhood is also home to many restaurants, bars, and entertainment venues, making it a great spot for those who want to experience the modern nightlife of Lyon.

Price Range: $150-300/night

Pros: Modern amenities, upscale shopping, and a wide range of dining options, Confluence is a great choice for those who want to experience the best of Lyon's luxury scene. The neighborhood is also relatively safe and well-connected to public transportation.

Cons: It can be quite expensive and formal, and some areas can be quite crowded.

Booking Tips

  • Book in advance to get the best prices and availability, especially during peak season.
  • Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find the best deals.
  • Consider staying in a neighborhood that is close to public transportation to make it easier to get around the city.
  • Look for hotels or apartments that offer free Wi-Fi and other amenities to make your stay more comfortable.
  • Read reviews and check the ratings of the hotel or apartment before booking to make sure it meets your needs and expectations.
  • Be aware of any additional fees or charges, such as cleaning fees or tourist taxes, before booking.
  • Consider booking a hotel or apartment that offers a cancellation policy in case your plans change.

Safety & Location Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
  • Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure, and consider using a hotel safe or a money belt.
  • Be mindful of pickpocketing and petty theft, especially in crowded areas and public transportation.
  • Consider staying in a neighborhood that is close to public transportation and has a strong police presence.
  • Be aware of any local events or demonstrations that may affect your safety or travel plans.
  • Keep an eye on the weather and be prepared for any extreme weather conditions, such as heatwaves or flooding.
