# Where to Stay in Montpellier — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Montpellier, consider the city's historic center, **Ecusson**, and its surrounding neighborhoods, which offer a range of accommodations to suit different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, Montpellier has a unique character that caters to various preferences:
**Ecusson** (Montpellier) 
Best For: Luxury / History buffs / Families
Vibe: The historic center of Montpellier, **Ecusson**, is a maze of narrow streets filled with medieval architecture, boutique shops, and restaurants, offering a unique and authentic experience. Its vibrant atmosphere is perfect for those looking to immerse themselves in the city's rich history and culture.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Central location, historic charm, proximity to main attractions
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited parking options

**Antigone** (Montpellier) 
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: This modern neighborhood is known for its sleek architecture, upscale shopping centers, and vibrant nightlife, making it an ideal spot for business travelers and those seeking a more contemporary experience. 
Price Range: $120-300/night
Pros: Modern amenities, close to business district, variety of dining options
Cons: Less historic charm, can be expensive

**Les Beaux-Arts** (Montpellier) 
Best For: Backpackers / Students
Vibe: This lively neighborhood is home to the University of Montpellier and the famous **Beaux-Arts** museum, offering a youthful and artistic atmosphere. It's perfect for backpackers and students looking for an affordable and dynamic environment.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Affordable, vibrant atmosphere, close to university and museum
Cons: Can be noisy, limited luxury options

**Port Marianne** (Montpellier) 
Best For: Families / Relaxation
Vibe: Located near the Lez River, **Port Marianne** is a tranquil neighborhood with a mix of modern and historic buildings, offering a peaceful retreat from the city's hustle and bustle. Its parks and waterfront promenade make it an excellent choice for families and those seeking relaxation.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Scenic views, peaceful atmosphere, family-friendly
Cons: Limited nightlife, somewhat distant from city center

**Saint-Roch** (Montpellier) 
Best For: Nightlife / Young travelers
Vibe: This neighborhood is known for its lively nightlife, with numerous bars, clubs, and restaurants, making it an ideal spot for young travelers and those looking to experience Montpellier's vibrant nightlife.
Price Range: $70-180/night
Pros: Vibrant nightlife, close to city center, affordable
Cons: Can be noisy, limited family-friendly options

**Mosson** (Montpellier) 
Best For: Sports / Families
Vibe: Located near the Mosson Stadium, this neighborhood is perfect for sports enthusiasts and families, offering a range of outdoor activities and a more laid-back atmosphere.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Sports facilities, family-friendly, affordable
Cons: Limited nightlife, somewhat distant from city center
