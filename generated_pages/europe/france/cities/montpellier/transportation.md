# Getting Around Montpellier — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Montpellier, you can use a variety of transportation options including trains, buses, metro, and taxis, as well as ride-hailing apps and cycling, making it easy to explore the city and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Montpellier is easily accessible by air, with the Montpellier-Méditerranée Airport located about 7 kilometers southeast of the city center. The airport offers direct flights to several European cities, including Paris, London, and Amsterdam, as well as connections to other French cities. If you prefer to travel by land, you can take a train or bus from nearby cities like Marseille, Lyon, or Toulouse. The city is also connected to the rest of Europe by a network of highways, making it accessible by car or bus.

For those traveling from outside of Europe, the nearest major international airports are in Paris, Marseille, and Nice, which offer connections to many global destinations. From these airports, you can take a domestic flight or train to Montpellier. Alternatively, you can take a bus or drive from other European cities, depending on your starting location and preferences. Montpellier is a popular tourist destination, and its transportation links make it easy to get to and from the city.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
If you need to travel within France, you can take a domestic flight from Montpellier-Méditerranée Airport to several destinations, including Paris, Lyon, and Nice. The main domestic airlines operating in France are Air France, Transavia, and Hop!, which offer frequent flights to many cities. You can book your flights online or through a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the route, time of year, and how far in advance you book. On average, a one-way domestic flight can cost between $50 and $200, depending on the route and airline.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Montpellier has an extensive rail network, with frequent trains connecting the city to other parts of France and Europe. The city's main train station, Montpellier Saint-Roch, is a major hub for the French rail network, with trains operated by SNCF, the national rail company. You can take high-speed trains, known as TGVs, to destinations like Paris, Lyon, and Marseille, as well as regional trains to smaller towns and cities. The booking process is straightforward, and you can reserve your tickets online or at the train station. Prices vary depending on the route and type of train, but you can expect to pay between $20 and $100 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus companies like FlixBus, Eurolines, and Ouibus operate in Montpellier, offering connections to other French cities and European destinations. The buses are generally comfortable and affordable, with prices starting from around $10 for a one-way ticket. You can book your tickets online or at the bus station, and the journey times vary depending on the route and traffic conditions. The main bus station in Montpellier is located near the train station, making it easy to transfer between buses and trains.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in France, you need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The road quality in Montpellier and surrounding areas is generally good, with well-maintained highways and urban roads.
- Fuel costs are around $1.50 per liter for gasoline and $1.20 per liter for diesel.
- Car rental companies like Europcar, Hertz, and Avis have offices in Montpellier, and you can book your car online or at the airport.
- Be aware of parking restrictions and fees in the city center, as well as potential traffic congestion during peak hours.
- The city has a bike-sharing system and pedestrianized areas, so be mindful of cyclists and pedestrians when driving.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of pickpocketing and scams in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, and keep your valuables secure.
- You can pay for public transportation tickets with cash or by card, but it's a good idea to have some local currency, euros, for small purchases.
- Download the Montpellier city guide app or a transit app like Citymapper to help you navigate the city's transportation network.
- Be prepared for potential strikes or disruptions to public transportation services, and check for updates before you travel.

- Consider purchasing a **Montpellier City Pass**, which includes public transportation, attractions, and discounts.
