[SECTION] What Is Strasbourg? [/SECTION]
Strasbourg is a city in France, situated on the Ill River, where it forms part of the Rhine river valley. The city is the capital of the Grand Est region and the Bas-Rhin department, and it is known for its well-preserved medieval architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and significant historical landmarks. As the seat of several European institutions, including the European Parliament and the Council of Europe, Strasbourg plays an important role in European politics and international relations.

Geographically, Strasbourg is located near the French-German border, which has contributed to its unique cultural identity and historical significance. The city has been an important center of trade and commerce for centuries, and its strategic location has made it a prized possession for various European powers throughout history. Today, Strasbourg is a thriving city with a strong economy, a rich cultural heritage, and a high standard of living.

Strasbourg is also known for its stunning architecture, which reflects its complex history and cultural diversity. The city's old town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring a unique blend of medieval, Renaissance, and Baroque styles. The iconic Gothic cathedral, the Petite France district, and the Palais Rohan are just a few examples of the city's many architectural treasures.
