# Strasbourg Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

Strasbourg is a city located in the Grand Est region of northeastern France, situated on the Ill River, where it forms part of the Rhine-Meuse basin.

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Strasbourg is situated in the heart of the Rhine Valley, approximately 480 kilometers east of Paris and 320 kilometers west of Stuttgart, Germany. The city is nestled in a strategic location, bordering Germany to the east and north, and surrounded by the rest of France to the west and south. The Rhine River, which forms the border between France and Germany, is just a few kilometers east of the city. The proximity to the Rhine River and the border with Germany has played a significant role in shaping Strasbourg's history, culture, and economy.

The city's location at the crossroads of Europe has made it an important hub for trade, commerce, and tourism. Strasbourg is connected to several major European cities, including Paris, Berlin, and Zurich, by high-speed rail networks, making it easily accessible by train. The city is also close to several international airports, including the Strasbourg International Airport, which offers flights to destinations across Europe and beyond. The unique blend of French and German influences has contributed to Strasbourg's rich cultural heritage, making it a fascinating destination for visitors from around the world.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Strasbourg's terrain is characterized by a mix of flat plains and gentle slopes, with the Ill River flowing through the city. The surrounding landscape is dominated by the **Vosges Mountains** to the west and the **Black Forest** to the east. The city's terrain is generally flat, with an average elevation of around 140 meters above sea level. The highest point in Strasbourg is the Council of Europe Building, which stands at 122 meters, while the lowest point is the Ill River, which flows through the city at an elevation of 132 meters.

The city's landscape is also marked by several **canals** and **waterways**, including the Ill River, which flows through the historic center of Strasbourg. The canals and waterways have played a significant role in the city's history, providing a means of transportation and trade. Today, they are a popular attraction for tourists, offering scenic boat tours and picturesque views of the city. The surrounding countryside is characterized by rolling hills, forests, and farmland, making it a popular destination for hiking, cycling, and other outdoor activities.

The city's terrain and landscape have also been shaped by its history and culture. The **Petite France** neighborhood, for example, is a charming area with narrow streets, canals, and half-timbered houses, which reflect the city's medieval past. The **Orangerie Park**, on the other hand, is a beautiful green space with gardens, fountains, and walking trails, which offers a peaceful escape from the city.
