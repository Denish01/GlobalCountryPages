# Where to Stay in Nice — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Nice, the best areas to stay are typically found in the city center and along the famous Promenade des Anglais, offering a range of options for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Nice, the characteristics and advantages are as follows:
**Vieux Nice** (City)
Best For: backpacker / family
Vibe: Vieux Nice, or Old Town, is a historic and charming area filled with narrow streets, local shops, and restaurants, offering a unique and authentic experience. The area is bustling with activity, especially at night, but still manages to maintain a warm and welcoming atmosphere.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Rich in history and culture, plenty of dining options, close to the beach
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited parking options

**Promenade des Anglais** (City)
Best For: luxury / business
Vibe: The Promenade des Anglais is a iconic and upscale area, known for its stunning views of the Baie des Anges and luxurious hotels and restaurants. The area is perfect for those looking to experience the best of Nice's high-end offerings.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: Breathtaking views, high-end amenities, central location
Cons: Expensive, can be quite touristy

**Cours Saleya** (City)
Best For: family / foodies
Vibe: The Cours Saleya area is a charming and vibrant neighborhood, famous for its daily market and flower stalls. The area is filled with local restaurants and cafes, making it perfect for food lovers.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Lively atmosphere, plenty of dining options, close to the beach
Cons: Can be noisy, limited parking options

**Mont Boron** (City)
Best For: luxury / families
Vibe: Mont Boron is an upscale and residential area, offering stunning views of the city and the sea. The area is perfect for those looking for a more relaxed and peaceful experience.
Price Range: $250-600/night
Pros: Breathtaking views, peaceful atmosphere, luxurious amenities
Cons: Limited dining options, far from the city center

**Liberation** (City)
Best For: budget / backpacker
Vibe: The Liberation area is a lively and cosmopolitan neighborhood, known for its street art, local shops, and multicultural restaurants. The area is perfect for budget-conscious travelers looking for a unique experience.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Affordable, vibrant atmosphere, close to the city center
Cons: Can be noisy, limited high-end amenities

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September), to ensure availability and the best prices.
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider staying in a nearby town, like Villefranche-sur-Mer or Beaulieu-sur-Mer, for a more affordable and peaceful experience.
- Look for apartments or guesthouses outside of the city center for better value and a more local experience.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during peak season and decreasing during the off-season.
- Negotiate prices, especially when booking directly with a hotel or guesthouse.
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs and expectations.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas, like the Promenade des Anglais and Vieux Nice.
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night, especially in the Liberation neighborhood.
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially on public transportation and in tourist areas.
- Stay close to the city center and main attractions to ensure easy access to public transportation and amenities.
- Consider staying in a hotel or guesthouse with a 24-hour reception for added security and assistance.
- Be mindful of traffic and pedestrian safety, especially when crossing the busy streets of Nice.
