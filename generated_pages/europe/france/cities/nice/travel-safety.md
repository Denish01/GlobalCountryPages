[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Nice, a popular tourist destination on the French Riviera, boasts a reputation for being a safe and welcoming city. With its stunning beaches, historic architecture, and vibrant cultural scene, Nice attracts millions of visitors each year. While it is considered a safe city, petty crimes such as pickpocketing and bag snatching can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially at night, and take necessary precautions to ensure their personal safety.

As with any major city, there are areas in Nice that are considered safer than others. The city's main tourist areas, such as the Promenade des Anglais and the Old Town, are generally well-patrolled and safe, even at night. However, visitors should still be mindful of their belongings and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas. The French authorities take the safety of tourists seriously, and there is a dedicated tourist police force in place to assist visitors with any issues they may encounter.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake petitions or charity collectors
- Overpriced restaurants and cafes in tourist areas
- Aggressive beggars and panhandlers in some areas
- Bike theft, particularly in the Old Town and Promenade des Anglais
- Opportunistic crime, such as theft from cars or apartments
- ATMs and credit card skimming scams

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Nice, but it is recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Nice, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it.
- **Medical Facilities:** Nice has a well-equipped hospital, the University Hospital of Nice, which offers high-quality medical care. Many doctors and medical staff speak English, and some medical facilities cater specifically to tourists.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are plentiful and easily accessible in Nice, with many open late or 24 hours.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas at night
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or a secure bag
- Be cautious of overly friendly or aggressive strangers
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, even when you're in the room
- Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Research your accommodation and choose a safe and reputable hotel or hostel
- Be confident and assertive, but also aware of your surroundings and potential risks
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night and consider using a taxi or public transport instead
- Keep in touch with family and friends back home and let them know your itinerary and plans
