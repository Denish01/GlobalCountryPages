To get around in Nice, you can use a variety of transportation options, including buses, trains, taxis, and ride-hailing services, as well as explore the city on foot or by bike.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Nice Côte d'Azur Airport, also known as Nice Airport, is the main international airport serving the city of Nice. Located about 6 kilometers southwest of the city center, it offers direct flights to many European cities, as well as some international destinations. The airport is a hub for Air France and has connections to major European cities like Paris, London, and Amsterdam. If you're coming from outside Europe, you may need to transfer at a major hub like Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport. The city is also accessible by train, with the Nice-Ville station offering connections to other French cities and neighboring countries.

If you prefer to arrive by land, Nice is connected to the rest of France and Europe by a network of highways and roads. The A8 highway connects Nice to Marseille, Lyon, and other major French cities, while the A54 highway links Nice to Italy. There are also several bus companies that operate from Nice to other European cities, including FlixBus and Eurolines. Additionally, you can take a ferry from the port of Nice to other destinations in the Mediterranean, such as Corsica and Sardinia.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
For domestic flights within France, you can use airlines like Air France, EasyJet, and Ryanair. These airlines operate from Nice Côte d'Azur Airport to other major French cities, including Paris, Lyon, and Bordeaux. You can book flights online through the airlines' websites or through a travel agency. Approximate costs for domestic flights within France can range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and time of booking. It's recommended to book in advance to get the best prices.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Nice has an extensive rail network, with the Nice-Ville station offering connections to other French cities and neighboring countries. The rail network is operated by SNCF, the French national railway company. You can take high-speed trains like the TGV to destinations like Paris, Lyon, and Marseille. The booking process is relatively straightforward, and you can book tickets online through the SNCF website or at the train station. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
For intercity bus travel, you can use companies like FlixBus, Eurolines, and Ouibus. These companies operate from Nice to other major French cities and European destinations. The buses are generally comfortable and affordable, with prices starting from around $10. You can book tickets online through the companies' websites or at the bus station. The quality of the buses and coaches is generally good, with most offering Wi-Fi and onboard amenities.
