# Where to Stay in Bordeaux — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Bordeaux, consider the city center and its surrounding neighborhoods, which offer a range of accommodations for different types of travelers, from budget-friendly options to luxury hotels. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top 5-7 areas/neighborhoods:

**Chartrons** (Bordeaux)
Best For: Families / Business
Vibe: Chartrons is a charming neighborhood with a mix of old and new buildings, offering a relaxed atmosphere and easy access to the city center. The area is known for its picturesque streets, quaint shops, and restaurants.
Price Range: $80-$200/night
Pros: Close proximity to the Garonne River, plenty of restaurants and cafes, and a variety of shops. 
Cons: Can be quite crowded, especially during peak season.

**Saint-Pierre** (Bordeaux)
Best For: Luxury / Couples
Vibe: Saint-Pierre is the historic heart of Bordeaux, with beautiful 18th-century architecture, upscale shops, and fine dining restaurants. The area is vibrant and lively, with a sophisticated atmosphere.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Central location, stunning architecture, and high-end amenities. 
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and parking can be a challenge.

**Mériadeck** (Bordeaux)
Best For: Business / Solo travelers
Vibe: Mériadeck is a modern neighborhood with a focus on business and commerce, offering easy access to the city's main train station and a variety of restaurants and cafes. The area is convenient and practical, with a more subdued atmosphere.
Price Range: $60-$150/night
Pros: Close to the train station, plenty of dining options, and a range of accommodation types. 
Cons: Can feel a bit soulless, with a lack of historic charm.

**Bacalan** (Bordeaux)
Best For: Backpackers / Budget travelers
Vibe: Bacalan is a trendy neighborhood with a mix of street art, independent shops, and affordable restaurants. The area is lively and eclectic, with a youthful atmosphere.
Price Range: $30-$80/night
Pros: Affordable prices, vibrant atmosphere, and a range of budget-friendly dining options. 
Cons: Can be quite noisy, especially at night.

**Caudéran** (Bordeaux)
Best For: Families / Long-stay travelers
Vibe: Caudéran is a residential neighborhood with a relaxed atmosphere, offering easy access to parks and green spaces. The area is quiet and peaceful, with a range of amenities for families.
Price Range: $40-$120/night
Pros: Affordable prices, plenty of parks and green spaces, and a range of family-friendly restaurants. 
Cons: Can be quite far from the city center, with limited public transportation options.
