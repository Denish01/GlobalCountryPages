[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Bordeaux is considered one of the safest cities in France, with a strong emphasis on maintaining a secure environment for tourists and locals alike. The city's low crime rates and efficient law enforcement contribute to its high safety rating. Visitors can feel comfortable exploring the city's historic center, wine regions, and surrounding areas without undue concern for their personal safety. However, as with any popular tourist destination, there are areas to be aware of and precautions to take to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

Bordeaux's safety is also reflected in its well-lit streets, clean public spaces, and vibrant cultural scene. The city's strong sense of community and welcoming atmosphere make it an ideal destination for solo travelers, families, and groups. While petty crimes such as pickpocketing and scams can occur, they are relatively rare and can be easily avoided with basic precautions. By taking common-sense measures to protect oneself and staying informed about local conditions, visitors can have a safe and memorable experience in Bordeaux.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting wine tourists, such as fake wine tastings or overpriced wine purchases
- Bag snatching in busy streets and markets
- ATM skimming and credit card fraud
- Overcharging by some taxi drivers or tour operators
- Bike theft in unlocked or unattended areas
- Panhandling and aggressive begging in some neighborhoods

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Bordeaux, but it is recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Bordeaux, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it.
- **Medical Facilities:** Bordeaux has high-quality medical facilities and hospitals, including the University Hospital of Bordeaux, which offers emergency services and specialized care.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are easily accessible throughout the city, with many open late or 24 hours.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas and keep a close eye on personal belongings
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or lockbox
- Be cautious when using ATMs or credit cards, and monitor accounts for suspicious activity
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth or carrying large amounts of cash
- Stay informed about local events and demonstrations, which can sometimes affect tourist areas

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Bordeaux is generally a safe and welcoming city for solo and women travelers, with many women-friendly cafes, restaurants, and cultural events
- Consider joining a guided tour or group activity to meet other travelers and explore the city
- Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts, especially at night or in unfamiliar areas
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas or engaging with aggressive panhandlers or street performers
