[SECTION] Overview: France vs Italy [/SECTION]
France and Italy are two of the most popular countries in Europe, known for their rich history, culture, and natural beauty. When it comes to the cost of living, both countries have their own unique characteristics. France is known for being one of the more expensive countries in Europe, with a high cost of living in cities like Paris. Italy, on the other hand, has a more varied cost of living, with prices ranging from affordable in the south to more expensive in the north. Overall, Italy is generally cheaper than France, with an overall cost of living that is around 15-20% lower.

The economic context of both countries also plays a role in their cost of living. France has a strong economy with a high GDP per capita, which contributes to its higher cost of living. Italy, on the other hand, has a more mixed economy, with a lower GDP per capita and higher unemployment rates. This has led to a more affordable cost of living in many parts of the country. However, it's worth noting that both countries have a high standard of living, with good healthcare, education, and infrastructure.
