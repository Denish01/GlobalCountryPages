[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Palace of Versailles**, a former royal palace with opulent decorations and gardens, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most popular tourist destinations in France.
- The **Louvre Museum**, which houses an impressive collection of art and artifacts from around the world, including the Mona Lisa, is one of the largest and most visited museums in the world.
- The **French Riviera**, also known as the Côte d'Azur, is a popular destination for its stunning coastline, beautiful beaches, and vibrant cities such as Nice and Cannes.
- The **Notre-Dame Cathedral**, a beautiful and historic Catholic cathedral in Paris, is one of the most famous landmarks in France and a symbol of the country's rich cultural heritage.
- The **Ballet de l'Opéra national de Paris**, one of the most prestigious ballet companies in the world, is known for its technical precision and artistic expression.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Marie Curie**, a Polish-born physicist and chemist who lived and worked in France, was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and is widely recognized as one of the most important scientists of the 20th century.
- The **French diaspora**, which includes people of French descent living in countries such as the United States, Canada, and Australia, has made significant contributions to the arts, sciences, and politics.
- **Simone de Beauvoir**, a French philosopher and feminist, is widely regarded as one of the most important feminist thinkers of the 20th century and has had a profound impact on modern feminist theory.
- The **values of liberté, égalité, fraternité**, which are enshrined in the French Constitution, have inspired democratic movements around the world and are a cornerstone of French identity.
