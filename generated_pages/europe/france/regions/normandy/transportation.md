# Getting Around Normandy — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Normandy, you can use a variety of transportation options, including buses, trains, and cars, as well as walking and cycling in the region's charming towns and cities.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Normandy is easily accessible by air, with two international airports, Caen Carpiquet Airport and Deauville-Normandie Airport, offering flights to and from various European cities. Direct flights are available from hubs such as Paris, London, and Amsterdam, making it convenient for travelers to reach the region. Additionally, Normandy can be reached by car or bus from neighboring regions in France, such as Brittany and the Loire Valley, as well as from the UK via ferry or the Channel Tunnel. The region's strategic location makes it an ideal destination for travelers exploring northern France.

For those traveling from outside Europe, the closest major international airport is Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport, which offers connections to Normandy via domestic flights or high-speed trains. The airport is about a two-hour drive from Normandy, and trains and buses are also available from the airport to various destinations in the region. Overland entry points include the port city of Le Havre, which receives ferries from the UK, and the A28 and A84 highways, which connect Normandy to the rest of France.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights within Normandy are limited, but airlines such as Air France and Hop! offer connections between the region's airports and Paris. The most common domestic route is between Caen and Paris, with multiple daily flights available. Booking in advance is recommended, as prices can vary depending on the time of year and demand. Approximate costs for a one-way domestic flight range from $50 to $200, depending on the airline and time of booking. It's also worth noting that trains are often a more convenient and affordable option for traveling within Normandy, with frequent departures and scenic routes.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Normandy has an extensive rail network, with frequent trains connecting the region's major cities, including Caen, Rouen, and Le Havre. The rail network is operated by SNCF, the French national railway company, which offers a range of train types, including high-speed TGV trains and regional TER trains. Key routes include the Paris-Rouen-Le Havre line, which connects Normandy to the French capital, and the Caen-Cherbourg line, which runs along the coast. Booking trains in advance is recommended, especially for high-speed trains, as prices can vary depending on demand. The booking process is straightforward, with tickets available online or at train stations.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus companies such as FlixBus and Eurolines operate in Normandy, offering affordable and convenient travel between cities. The buses are comfortable and well-maintained, with onboard amenities such as Wi-Fi and restrooms. Routes include connections between Normandy's major cities, as well as to neighboring regions in France. Costs are generally lower than trains, with prices starting from around $10 for a one-way ticket. However, journey times can be longer, and frequencies may be less frequent than trains.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- A valid driver's license is required to rent a car in Normandy, with some rental companies requiring an international driving permit.
- Road conditions in Normandy are generally good, with well-maintained highways and scenic coastal roads.
- Fuel costs are around $1.50 per liter for gasoline and $1.20 per liter for diesel.
- Car rental companies such as Europcar and Hertz have branches at airports and in major cities, with prices starting from around $40 per day.
- Hazards to watch out for include narrow rural roads and pedestrianized city centers, where parking can be limited.
- It's recommended to rent a car with a GPS system, as signage can be limited in rural areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** in crowded train stations and tourist areas.
- Use **cash** or **credit cards** to pay for tickets and transportation, as not all vendors accept foreign currency.
- Download the **SNCF** app to book trains and check schedules.
- Use **Google Maps** to navigate Normandy's roads and cities.
- Be mindful of **road signs** and **speed limits**, as traffic laws are strictly enforced in France.
- Consider purchasing a **Normandy Pass**, which offers discounts and free entry to attractions and public transportation.
- Be prepared for **traffic congestion** in urban areas, especially during rush hour.
- Use **reputable taxi companies** or **ride-hailing apps** to ensure a safe and reliable journey.
