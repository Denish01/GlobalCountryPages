[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Cité de Carcassonne**, a medieval walled city, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most visited attractions in France.
- The **Toulouse-Lautrec Museum** in Albi showcases an extensive collection of artworks by the famous Post-Impressionist artist **Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec**.
- The **Cathédrale Saint-Étienne** in Toulouse is a stunning example of Gothic architecture and features an impressive **rose window**.
- The **Festival de Radio France et Montpellier** is an annual music festival that celebrates classical music and features performances by world-renowned artists.
- The **Musée des Augustins** in Toulouse is a museum of fine arts that houses an impressive collection of paintings and sculptures from the Middle Ages to the 20th century.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec**, the famous Post-Impressionist artist, was born in Albi, Occitanie, and his works continue to inspire artists worldwide.
- **Jean Jaurès**, a prominent French socialist leader, was born in Castres, Occitanie, and played a significant role in shaping French politics.
- The **Occitan diaspora** has contributed significantly to the cultural and economic development of countries like the United States, Canada, and Australia.
- **Frédéric Mistral**, a French writer and poet, was awarded the **Nobel Prize in Literature** in 1904 for his contributions to the **Occitan language** and culture.
