# Top Things to Do in Corsica — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Corsica, visit the **Bonifacio** citadel, explore the **Scandola Nature Reserve**, and hike to the **Monte Cinto** peak, which offer a mix of history, culture, and breathtaking natural beauty that define this French region in the Mediterranean.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **boat tour** of the **Gulf of Valinco** to explore its secluded beaches and coves.
- Attend a **Corsican polyphonic singing** performance, a unique and traditional form of music.
- Visit a **local vineyard** to taste some of Corsica's renowned wines, such as **Patrimonio** and **Ajaccio**.
- Learn about **Corsican cuisine** through a cooking class, focusing on dishes like **aziminu** and **brocciu**.
- Explore the **Corsican craft** of **knife-making** in a workshop, learning about this traditional artisanal skill.
- Participate in the **Festa di u Mundu**, a celebration of Corsican culture and traditions.
- Go on a **donkey trek** through the **Corsican mountains**, offering a unique perspective on the landscape.

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Hike the **GR20 trail**, one of Europe's most challenging and scenic long-distance hiking trails.
- Visit the **Parc Naturel Régional de Corse**, a protected area showcasing Corsica's diverse flora and fauna.
- Explore the **Lac de l'Ospedale**, a picturesque lake surrounded by mountains and forests.
- Relax on the **Plage de Palombaggia**, one of Corsica's most beautiful and popular beaches.
- Discover the **Gorges de la Restonica**, a scenic canyon with crystal-clear waters and hiking trails.

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Musée Fesch** in Ajaccio, featuring an impressive collection of Italian Renaissance art.
- Explore the **Village de Sartène**, known for its narrow streets, historic buildings, and traditional Corsican architecture.
- Attend a **Corsican festival**, such as the **Festival de Musique de Chambre** in Bonifacio.
- Learn about **Corsican history** at the **Musée de la Corse** in Corte.
- Visit the **Cathédrale Saint-Jean-Baptiste** in Ajaccio, a beautiful example of Corsican architecture.
- Discover the **Maison de la Corse** in Ajaccio, a cultural center showcasing Corsican traditions and crafts.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Village de Piana**, perched on a cliffside and offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
- The **Plage de Tamaricciu**, a secluded beach accessible only by foot or boat.
- The **Chapelle de la Trinité** in Quenza, a small, historic chapel with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
- The **Musée de l'Alta Rocca** in Levie, featuring a collection of Corsican artifacts and exhibits on local history and culture.
- The **Jardin des Plantes** in Ajaccio, a beautiful botanical garden with a variety of plant species.

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- From **Ajaccio**, visit the **Sanguinaires Islands** (15 km, 30 minutes), known for their stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters.
- From **Bonifacio**, explore the **Lavezzi Islands** (10 km, 1 hour by boat), featuring beautiful beaches and scenic hiking trails.
- From **Corte**, hike to the **Lac de Melo** (10 km, 2 hours), a picturesque lake surrounded by mountains and forests.
- From **Calvi**, visit the **Réserve Naturelle de Scandola** (30 km, 1 hour), a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring unique wildlife and landscapes.
- From **Bastia**, explore the **Cap Corse** (30 km, 1 hour), a scenic peninsula with picturesque villages and stunning views.
