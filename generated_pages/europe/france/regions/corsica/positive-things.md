[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Calanches de Piana**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a breathtaking natural wonder featuring towering granite cliffs and crystal-clear waters.
- The **Cathedral of Saint-Jean-Baptiste** in Ajaccio is a stunning example of Baroque architecture and a significant cultural landmark.
- The **Musee Fesch** in Ajaccio is a renowned art museum that houses an impressive collection of Italian and French art, including works by **Tintoretto** and **Titian**.
- The **Festival de Musique de Corsica** is a popular cultural event that showcases the island's rich musical heritage, featuring traditional folk music and dance performances.
- The **Corsican Polyphonic Song**, a traditional form of music that dates back to the 16th century, is an integral part of the island's cultural identity and has been recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]

- **Pasquale Paoli**, a Corsican general and statesman, played a key role in the island's struggle for independence and is remembered as a national hero.

- The **Corsican values** of hospitality, respect, and community are highly regarded and have been recognized as an integral part of the island's cultural identity.
