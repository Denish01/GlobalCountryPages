# Cost of Living in Corsica - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Corsica is generally considered to be moderate to expensive, with prices comparable to those found in other popular European destinations. 

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Expensive
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $80-120 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $150-250 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $300-500 USD
Currency: EUR
Tipping Custom: expected (5-10%)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Corsica? [/SECTION]
Corsica, a French island in the Mediterranean, is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture. The cost of living in Corsica is generally higher than in other parts of France, due to its island location and limited availability of certain goods. Compared to other European destinations, Corsica is more expensive than countries like Portugal or Spain, but less expensive than popular destinations like Monaco or the French Riviera. The cost of accommodation, food, and transportation in Corsica can be relatively high, especially during the peak tourist season. However, with some planning and research, it is possible to find affordable options and enjoy all that the island has to offer.

Overall, Corsica is a great destination for those who are looking for a unique and authentic European experience, but may not be the best fit for budget-conscious travelers. The island's stunning beaches, rugged mountains, and charming villages make it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, foodies, and culture lovers. With its rich history and vibrant culture, Corsica has something to offer for everyone, from hiking and water sports to historical sites and local festivals.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting during the off-season (April-May or September-October) to avoid peak tourist prices.
- Look for accommodation options outside of the main cities, such as in coastal towns or villages.
- Eat at local eateries or try street food to save money on meals.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transport costs.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost activities, such as hiking or visiting local markets.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets for groceries and other essentials, rather than relying on tourist-oriented shops.
