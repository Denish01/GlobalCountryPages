# Getting Around Ile-de-France — Transport & Travel Guide

Getting around Ile-de-France is relatively easy, thanks to its well-developed public transportation system, which includes an extensive rail network, buses, and a comprehensive metro system in the capital city of Paris.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Ile-de-France is easily accessible by air, with two major international airports, Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) and Paris Orly Airport (ORY), offering direct flights to numerous destinations worldwide. Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport is one of the busiest airports in Europe and serves as a hub for Air France, while Paris Orly Airport is a popular destination for low-cost carriers. Both airports are well-connected to the city center by public transportation, including trains, buses, and taxis. For those traveling overland, Ile-de-France is also accessible by train, bus, or car, with several major highways and rail lines connecting the region to the rest of France and Europe.

In addition to its airports, Ile-de-France is also a major hub for international train travel, with the Gare du Nord in Paris serving as a terminus for the Eurostar train, which connects the city to London, Brussels, and other European destinations. The region is also accessible by bus, with several companies offering routes to and from other European cities. For those driving, Ile-de-France is easily accessible by car, with several major highways, including the A1, A4, and A6, connecting the region to the rest of France and Europe.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Ile-de-France has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines offering flights to destinations throughout France. Air France, the national carrier, operates numerous flights from Paris to other major cities in France, including Nice, Lyon, and Bordeaux. Other airlines, such as Transavia and Hop!, also offer domestic flights to and from Paris. Booking domestic flights in advance is recommended, as prices can be high, especially during peak travel seasons. Approximate costs for domestic flights can range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and airline.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Ile-de-France has an extensive rail network, with several major train stations in Paris, including the Gare du Nord, Gare de Lyon, and Gare Montparnasse. The region is served by several types of trains, including high-speed TGV trains, which connect Paris to other major cities in France, as well as international destinations. The rail network is operated by SNCF, the national railway company, which offers a range of ticket options, including advance-purchase tickets and rail passes. Booking train tickets in advance is recommended, as prices can be high, especially for high-speed trains. The booking process is relatively straightforward, with tickets available online or at train stations.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Ile-de-France has a comprehensive network of buses and coaches, with several companies offering intercity routes to and from Paris. Companies such as FlixBus, Eurolines, and Ouibus operate buses to destinations throughout France and Europe, with prices starting from around $10. The bus network is a good option for those on a budget or for shorter journeys, with many buses offering comfortable seating and onboard amenities. However, journey times can be longer than by train, and the frequency of services may be limited.
