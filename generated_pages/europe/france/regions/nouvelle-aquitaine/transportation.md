To get around in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, you can use a combination of transportation options including trains, buses, domestic flights, and car rentals, which provide extensive coverage of the region.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Nouvelle-Aquitaine is easily accessible by air, with several international airports including Bordeaux-Merignac Airport, Biarritz Airport, and Limoges Airport, which receive direct flights from major European cities. Bordeaux-Merignac Airport is the largest airport in the region and offers connections to many international destinations. The region can also be reached by car or bus from neighboring countries, with several major highways and roads crossing the border from Spain and other parts of France.

The region's strategic location makes it a popular destination for tourists and business travelers alike, with many overland entry points along its borders. The **A10** and **A63** highways connect Nouvelle-Aquitaine to the rest of France, while the **A64** and **A65** highways provide links to Spain and other parts of southwestern Europe. The region is also accessible by train, with **TGV** (Train à Grande Vitesse) and **Intercités** trains connecting major cities like Bordeaux, Limoges, and Poitiers to Paris and other parts of France.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Nouvelle-Aquitaine has a moderate domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights between the region's airports and other parts of France. **Air France** and **Ryanair** are two of the main airlines operating in the region, with flights connecting Bordeaux, Biarritz, and Limoges to destinations like Paris, Lyon, and Marseille. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as prices can be high. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Nouvelle-Aquitaine has an extensive rail network, with **SNCF** (Société Nationale des Chemins de Fer Français) operating trains throughout the region. The **TGV** (Train à Grande Vitesse) is the fastest way to travel, with trains connecting major cities like Bordeaux, Limoges, and Poitiers to Paris and other parts of France. The **Intercités** and **TER** (Transport Express Régional) trains provide more frequent stops and are a good option for traveling to smaller towns and villages. Booking can be done online or at train stations, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several intercity bus companies operate in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, including **FlixBus**, **Eurolines**, and **Isilines**. These companies offer affordable fares and connect major cities like Bordeaux, Limoges, and Poitiers to other parts of France and Europe. The quality of the buses is generally good, with comfortable seating and onboard amenities like Wi-Fi and restrooms. Prices vary depending on the route and time of year, but approximate costs range from $10 to $50 for a one-way ticket.
