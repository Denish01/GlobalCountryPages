[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
Latvia has a business-friendly environment, with a relatively low level of bureaucracy and a government that is open to foreign investment. The country has made significant progress in recent years in simplifying its regulatory framework and reducing administrative barriers. However, corruption remains a concern, and companies may encounter some difficulties when dealing with government agencies. The overall climate is competitive, with a highly skilled workforce and a favorable geographic location. Latvia is a member of the European Union and the eurozone, which provides a high level of economic and political stability.

The government of Latvia has implemented various measures to attract foreign investment, including the creation of special economic zones and the provision of tax incentives. The country has also established a number of business support organizations, such as the Investment and Development Agency of Latvia, which provides assistance to foreign investors. Despite these efforts, the level of bureaucracy can still be a challenge, particularly for small and medium-sized enterprises.

The business environment in Latvia is also characterized by a high level of transparency and accountability, with a strong emphasis on the rule of law and the protection of property rights. The country has a well-developed financial system, with a number of banks and other financial institutions that offer a range of services to businesses.

[SECTION] Foreign Direct Investment [/SECTION]
- Latvia has attracted significant amounts of foreign direct investment (FDI) in recent years, particularly from countries such as Sweden, Estonia, and Lithuania.
- The main investing countries in Latvia are those with which it shares a border or has historical ties.
- The government of Latvia offers a number of incentives to attract FDI, including tax breaks and investment grants.
- Restricted sectors for FDI in Latvia include defense and security, as well as certain areas of the economy that are considered strategic, such as energy and transportation.

[SECTION] Legal & Regulatory Framework [/SECTION]
- Latvia has a well-developed legal and regulatory framework, with a strong emphasis on the rule of law and the protection of property rights.
- The country has a number of business laws, including the Commercial Law and the Law on Companies, which provide a framework for the establishment and operation of businesses.
- Latvia has a relatively high level of intellectual property protection, with laws that protect patents, trademarks, and copyrights.
- The country has a well-developed system of labor laws, which provide protections for workers and regulate employment relationships.
- Dispute resolution in Latvia is typically through the court system, although alternative dispute resolution methods such as arbitration and mediation are also available.

[SECTION] Challenges & Risks [/SECTION]
- One of the main challenges facing businesses in Latvia is the relatively small size of the market, which can limit opportunities for growth and expansion.
- The country is also vulnerable to economic shocks from its main trading partners, particularly Russia and the European Union.
- Corruption remains a concern in Latvia, particularly in certain areas of government and the economy.
- The business environment in Latvia can be bureaucratic, particularly for small and medium-sized enterprises.
- The country is also subject to a number of external risks, including changes in the global economy and geopolitical tensions in the region.
