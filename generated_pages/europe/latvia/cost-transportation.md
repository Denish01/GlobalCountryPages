[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Latvia has a well-developed transportation system, with a range of options available for getting around, including public transportation, taxis, ride-hailing services, and rental cars. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent and reliable bus and train services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in Latvia get around using public transportation, with buses being the most popular mode of transport, followed by trains and taxis.

The country's capital, Riga, has a comprehensive public transportation system, including buses, trams, and trolleybuses, making it easy to get around without a car. Outside of Riga, public transportation options may be less frequent, but still reliable and affordable. Latvia is also a relatively small country, making it easy to explore by car or bike, with scenic routes and bike paths connecting major cities and towns.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use public transportation for short distances, as it is affordable and reliable.
- Consider purchasing a monthly transit pass for frequent travel.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Bolt or Yandex.Taxi for convenient and affordable door-to-door services.
- Always negotiate the fare with taxi drivers before starting your journey.
- Be aware of peak hour traffic in major cities, and plan your journey accordingly.
- Consider renting a car for longer trips or exploring rural areas, as public transportation options may be limited.
