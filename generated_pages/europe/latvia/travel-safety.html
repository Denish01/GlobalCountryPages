Overall Safety Assessment

Latvia is generally a safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other European countries. The capital city, Riga, is a popular tourist destination, and most visitors have a trouble-free experience. However, as with any country, there are some areas and situations that require caution. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and theft can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, while violent crimes are relatively rare. The country has a well-organized police force and a strong emphasis on law and order, which contributes to its overall safety.

The risk of scams is moderate, with some reports of taxi drivers overcharging tourists and scams targeting visitors at airports and bus stations. Additionally, there are some areas in Riga and other cities that are best avoided, particularly at night, due to high levels of crime and anti-social behavior. Nevertheless, with some basic precautions and an awareness of potential risks, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Latvia. The country's stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly people make it a great destination for travelers.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Taxi scams: be wary of drivers who refuse to use meters or overcharge
  • Pickpocketing: crowded areas and tourist hotspots are common targets
  • Street harassment: some areas can have high levels of anti-social behavior
  • ATM scams: be cautious when using ATMs, especially at night
  • Overpriced tourist services: research and compare prices before booking
  • Drunk and disorderly behavior: some areas can have high levels of intoxication, especially at night

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: no specific vaccinations are required for travel to Latvia, but make sure you are up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
  • Water Safety: tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available
  • Medical Facilities: medical facilities are of a high standard, especially in Riga, but some areas may have limited access to specialized care
  • Pharmacy Access: pharmacies are widely available and well-stocked, with many staff speaking English

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
  • Keep valuables secure and use hotel safes or lockers
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Keep your hotel room door locked and secure
  • Be cautious when using ATMs, especially at night
  • Learn some basic Latvian phrases, such as "hello" (sveika) and "thank you" (paldies)

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Latvia is generally a safe country for solo and women travelers, but take normal precautions to stay safe
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night and stick to well-lit streets
  • Be cautious of overly friendly strangers, especially in bars and clubs
  • Keep your hotel room number and personal belongings secure and private
