# Education Costs in Latvia

The cost of education in Latvia varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools charge tuition fees ranging from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Latvia is generally considered to be of high quality, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and language skills. Public education is free or subsidized for local students, and the language of instruction is Latvian, although some schools may offer instruction in other languages, such as English or Russian. Compulsory education in Latvia lasts for 12 years, from age 7 to 19, and is divided into primary, secondary, and upper secondary education. Private and international schools are also available, offering a range of curricula and languages of instruction, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Examinations**.

The quality of education in Latvia is generally high, with a strong emphasis on **science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM)** subjects, as well as languages and social sciences. However, some schools may struggle with resources and infrastructure, particularly in rural areas. International schools in Latvia offer a range of curricula and languages of instruction, making them a popular choice for expat families. These schools often have smaller class sizes and more individualized attention, but can be expensive, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per year.
