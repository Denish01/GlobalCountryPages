[SECTION] Overview: Latvia vs Lithuania [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Latvia and Lithuania can vary significantly, with Latvia being the more expensive of the two countries. This is largely due to Latvia's stronger economy and higher standard of living, with a GDP per capita of over $31,000 compared to Lithuania's $29,922. The inflation rates in both countries are relatively low, with Latvia's inflation rate at 2.5% and Lithuania's at 2.1%. Overall, Lithuania is the cheaper option, with a cost of living that is around 10-15% lower than Latvia's.

In terms of economic context, both countries have experienced significant growth in recent years, with Latvia's economy growing by over 4% in 2020 and Lithuania's economy growing by over 3%. However, Latvia's economy is more diversified, with a strong focus on manufacturing, IT, and tourism, while Lithuania's economy is more focused on manufacturing and agriculture. This diversification has contributed to Latvia's higher cost of living, as well as its higher standard of living.
