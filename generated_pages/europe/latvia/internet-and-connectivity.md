[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Latvia's internet infrastructure is one of the best in the European Union, with a high level of penetration and excellent connectivity. The average internet speed in Latvia is 24.4 Mbps download, which is relatively fast compared to other European countries. The urban areas, such as the capital Riga, have a well-developed internet infrastructure, with many internet service providers offering high-speed connections. However, there is a noticeable urban-rural divide, with rural areas having slower internet speeds and less reliable connections. Despite this, the overall reliability of the internet in Latvia is good, with minimal downtime and few connectivity issues.

The Latvian government has made significant efforts to improve the country's internet infrastructure, including the development of a national broadband network and the introduction of 5G technology. This has made Latvia an attractive destination for digital nomads and remote workers, who can take advantage of the fast and reliable internet connections to stay productive. Additionally, the widespread availability of **WiFi** and **mobile networks** makes it easy to stay connected on the go.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Latvia is relatively easy, with many mobile operators offering tourist-friendly plans. The main carriers, such as Latvian Mobile Telephone (LMT), Tele2, and Bite, offer a range of plans and packages that cater to different needs and budgets. To get a SIM card, you will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and you can purchase a SIM card at most mobile operator stores, kiosks, or online. The cost of a tourist SIM card is approximately 5-10 USD, and data plans start from around 10-20 USD per month.

It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds. Additionally, many mobile operators offer **pay-as-you-go** plans, which can be a good option for short-term visitors.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with many hotels offering fast and reliable connections.
- Cafe WiFi culture is widespread, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular, with many offering high-speed internet and other amenities.
- Public WiFi is available in many parks and public areas, although the speeds can be slower than in cafes or hotels.
- Many shopping malls and airports also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected on the go.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces, such as TechHub and The Mill, offer high-speed internet and other amenities, making them a great option for remote workers.
- Reliable cafe chains, such as Double Coffee and Coffee Inn, offer fast WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options, such as portable **WiFi hotspots**, can be useful in case of an emergency or when working in rural areas.
- **VPN** services can be useful for remote workers who need to access sensitive information or connect to company networks.
- Many libraries and universities also offer free WiFi and a quiet working environment, making them a great option for remote workers.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is relatively low in Latvia, but some websites may be blocked due to copyright or other issues.
- **VPN** services can be useful for accessing blocked websites or protecting sensitive information.
- Roaming alternatives, such as portable WiFi hotspots or local SIM cards, can be a cost-effective option for travelers.
- eSIM options are becoming increasingly popular, and many mobile operators offer eSIM plans for tourists and remote workers.
- When using public WiFi, it is recommended to use a **VPN** and to be cautious when entering sensitive information.
- It is also recommended to check the terms and conditions of your mobile operator before using your phone or SIM card abroad.
