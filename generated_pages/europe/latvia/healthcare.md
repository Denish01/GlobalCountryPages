# Latvia Healthcare — Hospitals, Insurance & Medical Guide

Healthcare in Latvia is a well-organized system that provides high-quality medical services to its citizens and visitors, with a strong focus on public healthcare and a growing private sector.

[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
Latvia's healthcare system is primarily publicly funded, with a strong emphasis on preventive care and a wide range of medical services available to all citizens. The public healthcare system is generally of good quality, with well-equipped hospitals and clinics, especially in urban areas like Riga. However, there may be some disparities in the quality of care between urban and rural areas, with rural areas often having fewer medical facilities and specialized services. Private healthcare is also available, offering additional options for those who can afford it, and is often preferred by foreigners and those seeking specialized care.

The public healthcare system in Latvia is funded through a combination of government funding and **social health insurance**, which is mandatory for all citizens. This system provides access to a wide range of medical services, including doctor's visits, hospital stays, and **prescription medications**. However, there may be some **out-of-pocket costs** associated with certain services, such as dental care and **cosmetic procedures**.

Overall, Latvia's healthcare system is well-regarded and provides good quality care, especially in urban areas. However, there may be some challenges in accessing specialized care or **emergency services** in rural areas. Despite these challenges, Latvia's healthcare system is generally considered to be of high quality, with a strong focus on **preventive care** and **public health** initiatives.

[SECTION] Health Insurance [/SECTION]
- **Private health insurance** is recommended for foreigners and those who want additional coverage beyond the public system
- **Local health insurance options** are available, including those offered by private companies and the public healthcare system
- **Expats** may be required to purchase private health insurance, depending on their visa status and employment situation
- **Costs** for private health insurance vary depending on the provider and level of coverage, but are generally relatively affordable
- **Public health insurance** is mandatory for all citizens, and provides access to a wide range of medical services
- **Supplementary insurance** is also available, offering additional coverage for services like **dental care** and **vision care**

[SECTION] Pharmacies & Medications [/SECTION]
- **Prescription medications** are widely available at pharmacies throughout Latvia
- **Over-the-counter medications** are also widely available, and can be purchased without a prescription
- **Common brands** of medications are available, including international brands and local generics
- **Costs** for medications vary depending on the type and brand, but are generally relatively affordable
- **Prescription requirements** vary depending on the medication, but are generally similar to those in other European countries

[SECTION] Vaccinations & Health Risks [/SECTION]
- **Routine vaccinations** are recommended for all travelers, including **MMR** and **DTaP**
- **Hepatitis A** and **hepatitis B** vaccinations are also recommended, especially for those who will be working in healthcare or with children
- **Rabies** vaccination is recommended for those who will be working with animals or spending time in rural areas
- **Tick-borne encephalitis** is a risk in some areas of Latvia, especially during the summer months
- **Water safety** is generally good, but it's still important to take precautions when drinking tap water or swimming in lakes and rivers
- **Food safety** is also generally good, but it's still important to take precautions when eating at restaurants or buying food from street vendors

[SECTION] Medical Tourism [/SECTION]
- **Medical tourism** is a growing industry in Latvia, with many private hospitals and clinics offering specialized services like **cosmetic surgery** and **dental care**
- **Popular procedures** include **orthopedic surgery**, **cardiology**, and **fertility treatments**
- **Accredited hospitals** include the Riga East University Hospital and the Pauls Stradins Clinical University Hospital
- **Cost savings** can be significant, especially for procedures like **dental care** and **cosmetic surgery**

[SECTION] Emergency Procedures [/SECTION]
- **Emergency services** are available 24/7, and can be accessed by calling the **emergency number** 112
- **Ambulance reliability** is generally good, but may be limited in rural areas
- **Nearest hospitals** can be found by asking locals or using a mapping app like Google Maps
- **Insurance claims** can be filed with private insurance companies or the public healthcare system, depending on the type of coverage
- **Medical evacuation** may be necessary in some cases, especially for serious injuries or illnesses that require specialized care.
