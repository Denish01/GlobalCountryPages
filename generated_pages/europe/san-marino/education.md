The education system in San Marino is structured into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education, also known as **scuola primaria**, is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 11 and provides a foundation in subjects such as Italian, mathematics, and science. Secondary education, or **scuola secondaria**, is divided into two stages: lower secondary, which lasts for three years, and upper secondary, which lasts for three to five years. Tertiary education is provided by the **University of the Republic of San Marino**, which offers a range of undergraduate and graduate programs. The quality of education in San Marino is generally high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and a low student-to-teacher ratio. Most schools in San Marino are public, but there are also a number of private schools, including international schools, which offer alternative curricula and teaching methods.

In addition, San Marino has a number of **specialized schools**, including schools for students with special needs and schools that focus on specific subjects such as music or art.

Overall, the education system in San Marino is well-regarded and provides students with a solid foundation for future academic and professional success.
