# San Marino History — Key Events & Timeline

The history of San Marino is a rich and complex narrative spanning over 1,700 years, from its founding by Saint Marinus in 301 AD to its current status as a sovereign state.

[SECTION] Historical Overview [/SECTION]
San Marino's history began in the 4th century AD when **Saint Marinus**, a Christian stonemason, escaped persecution from the Roman Emperor Diocletian and settled on the top of **Mount Titano**. Over time, a community formed around him, and in 301 AD, he founded the city of San Marino. The early history of San Marino was marked by its unique location, which provided natural protection from invaders. In the **Middle Ages**, San Marino developed a system of government that would eventually become a **republic**, with a council of **Capitani Reggenti** (Captains Regent) at its helm. Throughout its history, San Marino has maintained its independence, despite being surrounded by powerful states such as the **Roman Empire**, the **Holy Roman Empire**, and the **Italian city-states**.

In the **Renaissance period**, San Marino's strategic location made it an important stopover for merchants and travelers, and the city-state developed a thriving economy. However, San Marino's independence was repeatedly threatened by neighboring powers, including the **Papal States** and the **Duchy of Urbino**. Despite these challenges, San Marino managed to maintain its sovereignty and developed a unique system of government that has endured to this day. In the **modern era**, San Marino has continued to evolve, adopting a **constitution** in 1600 and becoming a member of the **United Nations** in 1992.

San Marino's history has been marked by its ability to adapt to changing circumstances while maintaining its independence and unique system of government. From its founding by Saint Marinus to the present day, San Marino has remained a sovereign state, with a distinct culture and identity. Today, San Marino is a thriving **tourist destination**, attracting visitors from around the world with its stunning architecture, rich history, and breathtaking scenery.
