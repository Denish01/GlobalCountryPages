[SECTION] Budget Tier Lifestyles [/SECTION]
With a budget of $2,500 per month, you can live a modest life in San Marino. You would likely share an apartment with others, cook most of your meals, and rely on public transportation. You could afford to eat out occasionally, but it would be a rare treat. You would have to be mindful of your expenses and make sacrifices in order to stay within your budget. For example, you might have to skip going out to the movies or attending cultural events.

A mid-range budget of $3,500 per month provides a more comfortable lifestyle. You could afford a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, eat out occasionally, and enjoy some of the country's cultural attractions. You would still have to be mindful of your expenses, but you would have more freedom to enjoy the things you like. For example, you could take a weekend trip to a nearby city or attend a concert or festival.

With a comfortable budget of $6,000 per month, you can live a high standard of life in San Marino. You could afford a nice two-bedroom apartment, dine out frequently, and enjoy all of the country's cultural attractions. You would have the freedom to pursue your interests and hobbies, and you could afford to travel regularly. For example, you could take a trip to Italy or another European country, or attend a major sporting event.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Strategies [/SECTION]
- Consider sharing an apartment with others to reduce rent costs
- Cook most of your meals instead of eating out
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or cars
- Take advantage of free or low-cost cultural attractions, such as museums and historical sites
- Shop at local markets or grocery stores instead of tourist areas
- Avoid eating at restaurants in tourist areas, which tend to be more expensive
- Consider purchasing a monthly transportation pass instead of individual tickets
- Use cash instead of credit cards to avoid foreign transaction fees
