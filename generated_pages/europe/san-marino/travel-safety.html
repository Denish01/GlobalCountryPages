Overall Safety Assessment

San Marino is considered one of the safest countries in the world, with low crime rates and a strong emphasis on public safety. Visitors can feel comfortable exploring the country's historic center, scenic countryside, and popular tourist attractions without undue concern for their personal safety. The country's small size, lack of major cities, and tight-knit community contribute to its safe and welcoming atmosphere. As with any popular tourist destination, however, it's still important to take common-sense precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

San Marino's safety record is reflected in its low rates of petty and violent crime. The country has a well-organized and effective law enforcement system, with a strong police presence in tourist areas. Additionally, the country's geographic location, nestled in the Apennine Mountains, makes it relatively isolated from external threats. Overall, San Marino is an excellent choice for travelers seeking a safe and relaxing vacation experience. With its stunning scenery, rich history, and friendly locals, San Marino is a hidden gem that is sure to delight visitors from around the world.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded tourist areas, such as the historic center and popular attractions
  • Overpriced souvenirs and tourist traps, particularly in areas with high foot traffic
  • Aggressive street vendors and panhandlers, although rare
  • Traffic accidents, particularly on narrow mountain roads and in rural areas
  • Slippery roads and sidewalks, especially during winter months
  • Limited English spoken by some locals, which can lead to communication difficulties
  • Higher prices for food and accommodations during peak tourist season

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to San Marino, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
  • Water Safety: Tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it
  • Medical Facilities: San Marino has a well-equipped hospital and several medical clinics, with high-quality care and English-speaking staff
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are readily available, with a wide range of medications and knowledgeable staff

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Use reputable taxi services or public transportation, and avoid hitchhiking
  • Keep valuables secure and out of sight, especially when walking or hiking in rural areas
  • Follow local advice and warning signs, particularly in areas with steep drop-offs or inclement weather
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases, such as "grazie" (thank you) and "per favore" (please)
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting historic sites or attending cultural events
  • Stay informed about local events and demonstrations, which can occasionally affect tourist areas

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • San Marino is generally a very safe destination for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a welcoming atmosphere
  • Take normal precautions to protect yourself and your belongings, such as avoiding walking alone in isolated areas at night
  • Consider visiting during the day, when popular tourist areas are more crowded and well-lit
  • Don't hesitate to ask for help or assistance from local authorities or tourist information offices if you need it
