[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
San Marino is a small country with a well-developed public transportation system, making it easy to get around without a car. The most common mode of transportation is by bus, with an extensive network of routes covering the entire country. The quality of public transit is high, with frequent and reliable services. Most people get around San Marino by using the public bus system or by walking, as the country is relatively small and pedestrian-friendly.

The country's unique geography, with steep hills and narrow streets, makes it challenging to navigate by car. However, the public transportation system is well-suited to the country's terrain, with buses and taxis able to navigate the winding roads with ease. Visitors can easily get around San Marino without speaking Italian, as many signs and announcements are translated into English, and many locals speak some English, particularly in the tourism industry.
