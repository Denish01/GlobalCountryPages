Cultural Treasures

  • The Historic Centre of San Marino, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a testament to the country's rich history and cultural heritage, with its well-preserved medieval architecture and stunning views.
  • The Three Towers of San Marino, a iconic symbol of the country, offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and are a must-visit attraction for tourists.
  • The San Marino Museum of Fine Arts features a collection of artworks by local and international artists, showcasing the country's vibrant cultural scene.
  • The San Marino National Museum provides insights into the country's history, culture, and traditions, with a range of exhibits and artifacts on display.
  • The Festa di San Marino, a traditional festival held annually, celebrates the country's patron saint and features music, dance, and food, showcasing the local culture and traditions.

People & Global Impact

  • Titanio, a San Marino-born artist, has gained international recognition for his unique sculptures and installations, which have been exhibited in museums and galleries around the world.
  • The San Marino diaspora has made significant contributions to the global community, with many San Marino-born individuals holding prominent positions in fields such as politics, business, and academia.
  • Captain Reginaldo Giardi, a San Marino-born aviator, was the first person to fly a plane over the Apennine Mountains, and his achievement is still celebrated today.
  • The San Marino values of freedom, democracy, and human rights are reflected in the country's strong commitment to international cooperation and global governance.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the San Marino people, who are known for their warm welcome and friendly nature, makes visitors feel at home.
  • The local cuisine, which features a range of traditional dishes such as pasta, pizza, and risotto, is a highlight of any visit to San Marino.
  • The scenic views of the Apennine Mountains and the surrounding landscape are breathtaking, and offer ample opportunities for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • The historic centre of San Marino, with its narrow streets and picturesque piazzas, is a charming and atmospheric place to explore.
  • The shopping in San Marino, which features a range of local boutiques and specialty stores, offers a unique and authentic shopping experience.
  • The festivals and events, such as the San Marino Grand Prix and the San Marino International Music Festival, are a great way to experience the local culture and entertainment.

Hidden Gems

  • The Parco Naturale del Monte Titano, a protected area located on the slopes of Mount Titano, offers stunning views and opportunities for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • The Castello di Fiorentino, a medieval castle located in the countryside, is a lesser-known but fascinating attraction that offers insights into the country's history and architecture.
