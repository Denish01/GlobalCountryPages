# Where to Stay in San Marino — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in San Marino, consider the historic center of San Marino City, which offers a range of accommodations and easy access to the country's main attractions.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For a unique and memorable experience, San Marino has several areas to choose from, each with its own character and advantages. 
**San Marino City** (San Marino)
Best For: luxury, family, business
Vibe: San Marino City is the historic and cultural heart of the country, with narrow streets, medieval architecture, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It offers a range of high-end hotels, restaurants, and shops, as well as a variety of cultural attractions and events.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: central location, historic architecture, stunning views
Cons: can be crowded and noisy, limited parking
**Borgo Maggiore** (Borgo Maggiore)
Best For: backpacker, budget
Vibe: Borgo Maggiore is a charming and lively neighborhood located just below San Marino City, with a range of affordable accommodations, restaurants, and bars. It's a great place to experience the local culture and meet other travelers.
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: affordable, lively atmosphere, easy access to San Marino City
Cons: limited amenities, can be noisy
**Serravalle** (Serravalle)
Best For: family, business
Vibe: Serravalle is a modern and convenient neighborhood located near the border with Italy, with a range of hotels, restaurants, and shops. It's a great place to stay for those who want to explore the surrounding countryside and visit nearby cities.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: modern amenities, easy access to Italy, convenient location
Cons: lacks historic charm, can be busy
**Fiorentino** (Fiorentino)
Best For: luxury, relaxation
Vibe: Fiorentino is a picturesque and tranquil neighborhood located in the countryside, with a range of high-end hotels and resorts. It's a great place to unwind and enjoy the natural beauty of San Marino.
Price Range: $200-350/night
Pros: stunning views, peaceful atmosphere, luxurious amenities
Cons: remote location, limited access to public transportation
**Acquaviva** (Acquaviva)
Best For: backpacker, budget
Vibe: Acquaviva is a charming and historic neighborhood located in the countryside, with a range of affordable accommodations and a lively atmosphere. It's a great place to experience the local culture and meet other travelers.
Price Range: $60-120/night
Pros: affordable, historic architecture, lively atmosphere
Cons: limited amenities, can be noisy
**Domagnano** (Domagnano)
Best For: family, business
Vibe: Domagnano is a modern and convenient neighborhood located near the border with Italy, with a range of hotels, restaurants, and shops. It's a great place to stay for those who want to explore the surrounding countryside and visit nearby cities.
Price Range: $80-180/night
Pros: modern amenities, easy access to Italy, convenient location
Cons: lacks historic charm, can be busy

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to August)
- Use online platforms such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider staying in a guesthouse or hostel for a more authentic and budget-friendly experience
- Look for accommodations with free parking and Wi-Fi
- Negotiate the price, especially if you're staying for an extended period
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during peak season
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs

- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in busy areas

- Be aware of the country's unique laws and regulations, including those related to customs and immigration
- Keep your accommodation address and contact information handy in case of an emergency
