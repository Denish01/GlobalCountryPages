# Monthly Budget Guide for North Macedonia

To live in North Macedonia, you would need approximately $400-700 per month for a budget lifestyle, $700-1,200 per month for a mid-range lifestyle, and $1,200-2,000 per month for a comfortable lifestyle.

[FACTBOX]
GDP per Capita (PPP): $26,987
Budget Living (monthly): $400-700
Mid-Range Living (monthly): $700-1,200
Comfortable Living (monthly): $1,200-2,000
Average Local Salary: $500-700 USD/month, approximate
Currency: MKD
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Budget Tier Lifestyles [/SECTION]
At the budget tier, daily life in North Macedonia is simple but comfortable. You can afford to rent a shared apartment or a small studio outside of the city center, and cook most of your meals at home. You can use public transportation to get around, and enjoy some free or low-cost entertainment options like hiking or visiting local markets. However, you may need to make some sacrifices, such as limiting your dining out or skipping some social events. For example, you can expect to pay around $3-5 for a meal at a local restaurant, and $1-2 for a beer.

At the mid-range tier, daily life in North Macedonia is more relaxed and enjoyable. You can afford to rent a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, and enjoy some luxuries like dining out or taking a taxi. You can also afford to join a gym or take a hobby class, and enjoy some social events like concerts or festivals. However, you may still need to be mindful of your expenses, and make some choices about how to allocate your budget. For example, you can expect to pay around $10-20 for a meal at a mid-range restaurant, and $5-10 for a beer.

At the comfortable tier, daily life in North Macedonia is luxurious and stress-free. You can afford to rent a large apartment or house, and enjoy all the amenities and services you need. You can dine out at high-end restaurants, take taxis or drive a car, and enjoy premium entertainment options like theater or opera performances. You can also afford to travel and explore the region, and enjoy some of the best that North Macedonia has to offer. For example, you can expect to pay around $20-50 for a meal at a high-end restaurant, and $10-20 for a beer.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Strategies [/SECTION]
- Consider sharing an apartment with roommates to reduce rent costs
- Cook at home instead of eating out to save on food costs
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or cars to save on transportation costs
- Take advantage of free or low-cost entertainment options like hiking or visiting local markets
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets instead of high-end stores to save on groceries
- Avoid eating at touristy restaurants and opt for local eateries instead
- Consider buying a monthly public transportation pass to save on transportation costs
- Take advantage of discounts or promotions offered by local businesses or services
