[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Stobi Archaeological Site** is a well-preserved ancient city with impressive ruins and artifacts from the Roman and Byzantine periods.
- The **Lake Ohrid region** is home to numerous **Orthodox churches and monasteries**, showcasing unique Byzantine architecture and frescoes.
- The **Macedonian folk music and dance** traditions are an integral part of the country's cultural heritage, with various festivals and events celebrating these art forms.
- The **Old Bazaar in Skopje** is a historic marketplace filled with Ottoman-era architecture, shops, and restaurants, offering a glimpse into the city's rich history.
- The **Radovish Carnival** is a colorful and lively event that takes place every summer, featuring music, dance, and costumes.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Mother Teresa**, the Nobel Peace Prize laureate, was born in Skopje in 1910 and spent her early life in North Macedonia before dedicating her life to humanitarian work.
- The **Macedonian diaspora** has made significant contributions to the fields of science, art, and literature, with notable figures such as **Milčo Mančevski**, a film director and screenwriter.
- **Sts. Cyril and Methodius**, two Byzantine brothers and saints, created the **Glagolitic alphabet** and played a crucial role in the development of Slavic literature and culture.
- The values of **hospitality and respect for tradition** are deeply ingrained in Macedonian culture, with strong emphasis on family, community, and social bonds.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **warm hospitality** of the Macedonian people, who are known for their welcoming nature and eagerness to share their culture and traditions.
- The **delicious cuisine**, which features a mix of Mediterranean and Balkan flavors, with popular dishes such as ** Shopska salad**, ** Moussaka**, and **Burek**.
- The **breathtaking natural scenery**, including **Lake Ohrid**, **Lake Prespa**, and the **Šar Mountains**, which offer opportunities for hiking, skiing, and water sports.
- The **rich cultural heritage**, with numerous historical sites, museums, and festivals that showcase the country's complex history and cultural diversity.
- The **vibrant city life** in Skopje, with its lively **Old Bazaar**, **Kale Fortress**, and **Macedonia Square**, which offer a unique blend of history, architecture, and entertainment.
- The **affordability and value for money**, with North Macedonia being one of the most budget-friendly destinations in Europe.
