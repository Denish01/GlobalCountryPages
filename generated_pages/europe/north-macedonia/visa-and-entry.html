Visa Policy Overview

North Macedonia has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of over 50 countries to enter visa-free. The country is a member of the European Union's visa liberalization program, which enables citizens of certain countries to enter and stay in North Macedonia for up to 90 days within a 180-day period without a visa. The visa policy is designed to promote tourism, trade, and cultural exchange while maintaining national security and immigration control.

The visa policy of North Macedonia is relatively liberal, with a focus on facilitating travel and tourism. Citizens of countries that do not require a visa can enter North Macedonia with a valid passport and stay for up to 90 days. However, citizens of countries that require a visa must apply for a visa at the embassy or consulate of North Macedonia before traveling. The country also offers various types of visas, including tourist, business, student, and work visas, each with its own set of requirements and regulations.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: To enter North Macedonia as a tourist, visitors must have a valid passport, proof of sufficient funds, and health insurance. The typical stay for tourists is up to 90 days, and extensions are possible but require application at the local police station. Business Travelers: Business travelers must have a valid passport, an invitation letter from a North Macedonian company, and proof of sufficient funds. They may also require a work permit or a business visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. Students: Students must have a valid passport, proof of enrollment at a North Macedonian university, and sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. The duration of stay for students is typically up to one year, and they may require a student visa. Workers: Workers must have a valid passport, a work permit, and a sponsorship from a North Macedonian employer. The process of obtaining a work visa can be complex and requires application at the embassy or consulate of North Macedonia.

Documents Checklist

  1. Valid passport with at least six months' validity
  2. Completed visa application form (if required)
  3. Recent passport-sized photograph
  4. Proof of sufficient funds (cash, traveler's checks, or credit cards)
  5. Health insurance documents
  6. Invitation letter from a North Macedonian company (for business travelers)
  7. Proof of enrollment at a North Macedonian university (for students)
  8. Work permit and sponsorship documents (for workers)
  9. Hotel reservation or confirmation of accommodation
  10. Return or onward ticket

Entry Points & Procedures

The main airports in North Macedonia are Skopje International Airport and Ohrid St. Paul the Apostle Airport. The country also has several land borders with neighboring countries, including Kosovo, Serbia, Bulgaria, Greece, and Albania. When entering North Macedonia, visitors must present their passport and other required documents to the immigration officer. They may also be asked to provide information about their stay, including their accommodation and purpose of visit. The immigration procedure is typically straightforward, but visitors should be prepared to answer questions and provide documentation as required.

Upon arrival, visitors may also be required to register with the local police station within 24 hours of their arrival. This is a mandatory requirement for all foreign visitors, and failure to register may result in fines or other penalties. The registration process is typically simple and requires presentation of a valid passport and other identification documents.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not checking the validity of your passport before traveling
  • Failing to register with the local police station within 24 hours of arrival
  • Not having sufficient funds or health insurance
  • Not having a return or onward ticket
  • Not providing required documentation, such as an invitation letter or proof of enrollment
  • Not applying for a visa or work permit when required
