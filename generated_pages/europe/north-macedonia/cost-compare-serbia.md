[SECTION] Overview: North Macedonia vs Serbia [/SECTION]
North Macedonia and Serbia are two neighboring countries in the Balkan region, with similar cultural and historical backgrounds. However, they have distinct economic profiles, which affect their cost of living. North Macedonia has a smaller economy and a lower GDP per capita compared to Serbia, which translates to lower prices for goods and services. The country's inflation rate is also relatively low, which helps maintain the purchasing power of its citizens. In contrast, Serbia has a more developed economy, with a higher GDP per capita and a slightly higher inflation rate. As a result, Serbia tends to be more expensive than North Macedonia, especially when it comes to accommodation, food, and transportation.

The cost of living difference between North Macedonia and Serbia is noticeable, with North Macedonia being around 15-20% cheaper. This discrepancy is largely due to the difference in economic development and the resulting price levels. However, both countries offer a unique blend of traditional and modern lifestyles, making them attractive to expats, students, and tourists alike. While Serbia's more developed economy and larger cities may appeal to some, North Macedonia's lower cost of living and stunning natural beauty make it an excellent choice for those looking for an affordable and relaxing experience.
