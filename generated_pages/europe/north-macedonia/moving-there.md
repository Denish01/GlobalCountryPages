# Moving to North Macedonia — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to North Macedonia, you should research the country's visa and residency options, understand the cost of living, and plan for the challenges of adapting to a new culture and bureaucracy. 

[SECTION] Why People Move to North Macedonia [/SECTION]
North Macedonia is a hidden gem in the heart of the Balkans, attracting expats and digital nomads with its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and affordable cost of living. The country offers a unique blend of traditional and modern culture, with a growing expat community in the capital city of Skopje. Many people move to North Macedonia for work, study, or retirement, drawn by the country's strategic location, mild climate, and welcoming people. The quality of life in North Macedonia is generally good, with a low cost of living, modern amenities, and a wide range of outdoor activities, from hiking and skiing to swimming and fishing.

The expat demographics in North Macedonia are diverse, with a mix of young professionals, families, and retirees from Europe, the Americas, and Asia. Many expats are drawn to the country's vibrant cultural scene, with numerous festivals, concerts, and events throughout the year. The country's education system is also a major draw, with several international schools and universities offering programs in English and other languages. Overall, North Macedonia offers a great balance of traditional and modern lifestyle, making it an attractive destination for people looking for a new adventure.

The cost of living in North Macedonia is relatively low, with affordable housing, food, and transportation options. The country has a growing economy, with a mix of traditional industries such as agriculture and manufacturing, and modern sectors such as IT and tourism. The government is also actively promoting foreign investment and entrepreneurship, making it easier for expats to start their own businesses or work remotely. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and affordable cost of living, North Macedonia is an excellent choice for people looking to relocate to a new country.

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- Opening a bank account in North Macedonia is relatively easy, with several international banks operating in the country
- Money transfers can be done through online platforms or traditional banking services
- The local currency is the Macedonian denar (MKD), although euros and US dollars are widely accepted
- Credit cards are widely accepted in major cities, but cash is still the preferred payment method
- Taxation in North Macedonia is relatively low, with a flat rate of 10% for individuals and companies

- Language barriers can be a problem, especially outside of Skopje
- The country's infrastructure is still developing, with occasional power outages and internet disruptions
- Corruption is still a problem in some areas, requiring caution and due diligence
