[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
North Macedonia has a relatively well-developed transportation network, with buses and taxis being the primary modes of public transportation. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with Skopje, the capital, having a more comprehensive network of buses and taxis. Most people in North Macedonia get around by using public transportation, walking, or driving their own vehicles. The country's geography, with mountains and valleys, can make transportation challenging, especially in rural areas.

The main transportation modes in North Macedonia include buses, taxis, and minivans, which connect major cities and towns. The country's road network is generally in good condition, although some rural roads may be poorly maintained. The public transportation system is relatively affordable, with a single bus fare costing around $0.50-1.00 USD. However, the frequency and coverage of public transportation services can vary significantly between cities and rural areas.
