[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
North Macedonia's infrastructure development level is moderate, with a notable gap between urban and rural areas. The country has made significant progress in recent years, with investments in transportation, energy, and telecommunications. However, challenges remain, particularly in rural areas where access to basic services such as **water**, **sanitation**, and **electricity** can be limited. The government has implemented various initiatives to address these disparities and improve the overall infrastructure, including the development of a **national infrastructure plan**. This plan aims to enhance the country's competitiveness, promote economic growth, and improve the quality of life for its citizens.

The urban-rural gap in infrastructure development is a significant challenge for North Macedonia. While cities like **Skopje** and **Ohrid** have relatively modern infrastructure, rural areas often lack access to basic services. The government has launched initiatives to address this issue, including the development of **rural infrastructure projects** that focus on improving access to **water**, **sanitation**, and **electricity**. Additionally, efforts are being made to enhance **transportation links** between urban and rural areas, including the construction of new **roads** and the upgrade of existing **railway lines**.

Despite the challenges, North Macedonia has made significant progress in recent years, with investments in infrastructure reaching record highs. The country has also made significant strides in **European integration**, with the goal of joining the **European Union** and **NATO**. This has led to increased investments in infrastructure, particularly in the areas of **transportation** and **energy**. The country has also become a key player in regional infrastructure projects, including the development of **corridors** and **hubs** that connect Europe and the Middle East.

- The majority of North Macedonia's **power generation** comes from **thermal power plants**, with a significant share from **hydroelectric power**.
- The country has made significant progress in increasing its **renewable energy** capacity, with a focus on **solar** and **wind power**.
- **Energy efficiency** is a key area of focus, with efforts to reduce energy consumption and promote the use of **energy-efficient technologies**.
- The **electricity grid** is generally reliable, with minimal **outages** and a high level of **access** to electricity across the country.
- North Macedonia is also exploring the potential for **regional energy cooperation**, including the development of **regional energy markets** and **cross-border energy trade**.
