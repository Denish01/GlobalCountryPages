[SECTION] Overview: Bulgaria vs Greece [/SECTION]
Bulgaria and Greece are two neighboring countries in Southeastern Europe that offer a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. However, when it comes to the cost of living, Bulgaria is generally the more affordable option. With a lower GDP per capita and inflation rate, Bulgaria offers a more budget-friendly lifestyle, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation that are significantly lower than in Greece. According to various studies, Bulgaria's cost of living is around 20-30% lower than Greece's, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking to stretch their budget.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Bulgaria has a growing economy, with a strong focus on technology and manufacturing, while Greece is still recovering from the economic crisis of 2008. As a result, Bulgaria has seen significant investment in infrastructure and tourism, making it a more attractive destination for foreigners. Additionally, the cost of living in Bulgaria is not only lower than in Greece but also compared to other European countries, making it an excellent choice for those looking for an affordable and high-quality lifestyle.
