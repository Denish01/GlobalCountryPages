[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Bulgaria is generally a safe country to visit, with low levels of violent crime and a strong emphasis on tourism. The capital city, Sofia, is a hub of activity and culture, with a vibrant nightlife and plenty of attractions to explore. However, as with any country, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Bulgaria has a relatively low crime rate compared to other European countries, but petty crimes such as pickpocketing and scams can still occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

Overall, Bulgaria is a great destination for travelers who want to experience a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning mountains, picturesque villages, and vibrant cities, Bulgaria has something to offer for every type of traveler. By taking some basic precautions and being aware of their surroundings, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Bulgaria. The country's tourism infrastructure is well-developed, with many hotels, restaurants, and attractions catering specifically to international visitors. Additionally, the Bulgarian people are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature, making visitors feel at home.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable taxi services
- Be wary of overly friendly locals who may be trying to scam you
- Keep an eye on your drinks in bars and nightclubs to avoid drink spiking
- Be careful when using ATMs and avoid those in isolated areas
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night, especially in poorly lit areas

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Bulgaria, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended for sensitive stomachs
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in Bulgaria are generally of a good standard, with many hospitals and clinics catering to international patients
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available and well-stocked, with many medications available over-the-counter

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a copy of your passport and leave the original in a safe place
- Keep your valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in poorly lit areas
- Use reputable taxi services and always check the meter is working before you start your journey
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the safe if available
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting churches or monasteries

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Bulgaria is generally a safe country for solo and women travelers, with low levels of harassment and crime
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid walking alone at night, especially in poorly lit areas, and consider using a reputable taxi service
- Dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting churches or monasteries
