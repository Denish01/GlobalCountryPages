# Education Costs in Bulgaria

The cost of education in Bulgaria varies depending on the type of institution and the level of study, with public education being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Bulgaria is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the dominant provider of education. Public schools in Bulgaria are generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on **mathematics**, **science**, and **language**. The language of instruction is Bulgarian, but many schools also offer classes in foreign languages such as English, German, and French. Compulsory education in Bulgaria lasts for 12 years, from age 6 to 18, and is divided into primary, secondary, and vocational education. International schools are also available in Bulgaria, particularly in larger cities such as Sofia and Plovdiv, which offer a range of curricula including the **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Examinations**.

The quality of education in Bulgaria is generally considered to be good, with many schools having well-qualified teachers and modern facilities. However, there can be significant differences in the quality of education between public and private schools, as well as between urban and rural areas. Private schools in Bulgaria tend to have smaller class sizes and more resources, but can be expensive. International schools, on the other hand, offer a range of curricula and are often preferred by expats and international students. These schools can be quite expensive, but offer a high-quality education and a range of extracurricular activities.
