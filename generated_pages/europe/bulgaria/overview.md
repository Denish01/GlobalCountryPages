[SECTION] What Is Bulgaria? [/SECTION]
Bulgaria is a **sovereign** country located in southeastern Europe, bordering the Black Sea to the east. It is situated in the Balkan Peninsula, surrounded by Romania to the north, Serbia and North Macedonia to the west, Greece to the south, and Turkey to the southeast. With a rich history dating back to ancient times, Bulgaria is known for its unique cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and friendly people.

Bulgaria has a diverse geography, with mountains, forests, and coastal areas along the Black Sea. The country has a **temperate climate**, with cold winters and warm summers. The capital city, Sofia, is the largest city in Bulgaria and is home to many historical landmarks, museums, and cultural institutions. Bulgaria is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its beautiful beaches, ski resorts, and historic sites.

Bulgaria has a strong sense of national identity, with a distinct language, **Bulgarian**, which is the official language. The country has a long history of traditions and customs, with many festivals and celebrations throughout the year. Bulgaria is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes popular dishes such as **shopska salad**, **banitsa**, and **kebapche**.

2. 1018 - Bulgaria was conquered by the Byzantine Empire and remained under its rule for nearly two centuries.
3. 1185 - The Second Bulgarian Empire was established, with Ivan Asen I as its first ruler.
4. 1396 - Bulgaria was conquered by the Ottoman Empire and remained under its rule for nearly five centuries.
5. 1878 - Bulgaria gained independence from the Ottoman Empire, with the Treaty of San Stefano.
6. 1946 - Bulgaria became a **communist state**, with the Bulgarian Communist Party in power.
7. 1990 - Bulgaria transitioned to a **democratic government**, with the first free elections held in the country.
