[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Bulgaria offers a range of transportation options, including public buses, trams, trolleybuses, and metro lines in larger cities like Sofia. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent departures and affordable fares. Most people in Bulgaria get around using public transportation, with buses being the most popular mode of transport. In smaller towns and villages, minibuses and shared taxis are often the primary means of transportation.

The country's transportation network is well-developed, with many cities having their own public transportation systems. However, the quality of roads can vary, especially in rural areas. Bulgaria has a comprehensive network of highways and major roads, making it easy to travel between cities by car or bus. The country is also investing in its transportation infrastructure, with new roads and public transportation systems being built.
