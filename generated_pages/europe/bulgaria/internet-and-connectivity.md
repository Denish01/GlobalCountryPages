[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Bulgaria is relatively fast and reliable, with an average download speed of 25 Mbps, which is comparable to other European countries. The urban areas, such as Sofia, Plovdiv, and Varna, have excellent internet coverage, with many cafes, restaurants, and hotels offering free WiFi. However, the rural areas have slower internet speeds and more limited coverage, which can make it challenging for remote workers to stay connected. Despite this, the overall internet quality in Bulgaria is good, and the country has made significant investments in recent years to improve its digital infrastructure.

The reliability of the internet in Bulgaria is also good, with few reports of outages or downtime. The country has a well-developed telecommunications network, with multiple mobile carriers and internet service providers offering a range of plans and services. This competition has driven prices down and quality up, making it easy for tourists and residents to access fast and reliable internet. However, as with any country, there can be some variations in internet quality depending on the location and service provider, so it's always a good idea to research and compare different options before choosing an internet plan.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Bulgaria is relatively easy, with many mobile carriers offering tourist-friendly plans and SIM cards available for purchase at airports, train stations, and mobile carrier stores. To buy a SIM card, you will typically need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, although some carriers may have additional requirements. The main carriers in Bulgaria, such as Mtel, Telenor, and Vivacom, offer a range of plans and services, including prepaid and postpaid options, data-only plans, and bundles with voice and text. It's a good idea to compare the different plans and prices before choosing a carrier, as some may offer better value or coverage in certain areas.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality in Bulgaria is generally good, with many hotels offering free WiFi to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is also widespread, with many cafes and restaurants offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in Bulgaria, particularly in larger cities like Sofia and Plovdiv, and often offer high-speed WiFi and other amenities.
- Public WiFi is also available in many cities and towns, although the quality and coverage can vary.
- Many shopping malls and airports also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while on the go.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like Sofia Coworking and Plovdiv Coworking offer high-speed WiFi, desks, and other amenities for remote workers.
- Reliable cafe chains like Costa Coffee and Starbucks offer free WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options like mobile hotspots and portable WiFi routers can be useful in case of an outage or when working in rural areas.
- VPNs can be useful for remote workers who need to access sensitive information or work with clients in other countries.
- Many libraries and universities also offer free WiFi and a quiet working environment, making them a good option for remote workers.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is relatively limited in Bulgaria, but some websites may be blocked due to copyright or other issues.
- Blocked sites can often be accessed using a VPN, which can also help to protect your online security and privacy.
- Roaming alternatives like eSIMs and portable WiFi routers can be a good option for tourists and remote workers who need to stay connected while traveling.
- Some mobile carriers may have roaming restrictions or limits, so it's a good idea to check with your carrier before traveling.

- Bulgaria has a relatively high level of internet freedom, but it's still important to be aware of your online activities and take steps to protect your security and privacy.
