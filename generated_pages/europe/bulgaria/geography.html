Location & Borders

Bulgaria is a country located in southeastern Europe, situated in the Balkan Peninsula. It is bordered by several countries, including Romania to the north, Serbia and North Macedonia to the west, Greece to the south, and Turkey to the southeast. Bulgaria also has a coastline along the Black Sea to the east, which provides access to the Atlantic Ocean via the Mediterranean Sea. The country's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe and Asia has made it an important center for trade and cultural exchange throughout history.

The country's borders are marked by several major rivers, including the Danube River to the north, which forms the border with Romania, and the Maritsa River to the south, which forms the border with Greece. The border with Turkey is marked by the Rezovska River, while the border with Serbia and North Macedonia is marked by several smaller rivers and mountain ranges. Bulgaria's diverse geography and location have made it a popular destination for tourists and traders alike, with its rich history, culture, and natural beauty attracting visitors from around the world.

Bulgaria's terrain is characterized by a mix of mountains, valleys, and coastal plains. The country can be divided into several distinct regions, including the Danube Plain to the north, the Balkan Mountains, which stretch across the center of the country, and the Rhodope Mountains to the south. The highest point in the country is Musala, which is located in the Rila Mountains and is the highest peak in the Balkans.
