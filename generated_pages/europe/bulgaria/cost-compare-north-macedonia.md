[SECTION] Overview: Bulgaria vs North Macedonia [/SECTION]
Bulgaria and North Macedonia are two neighboring countries in Southeastern Europe that offer a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. In terms of cost of living, Bulgaria is generally considered to be cheaper than North Macedonia, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. The economic context of the two countries is also different, with Bulgaria having a more developed economy and a higher GDP per capita. However, North Macedonia has been growing rapidly in recent years, with a strong focus on tourism and foreign investment.

The cost of living in Bulgaria is around 10-20% lower than in North Macedonia, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees. The main reason for this difference is the lower cost of accommodation in Bulgaria, particularly in larger cities like Sofia. Additionally, Bulgaria has a more developed public transportation system, which makes it easier to get around without a car. On the other hand, North Macedonia has a more vibrant cultural scene, with a rich history and a strong sense of community. The country is also home to a number of beautiful lakes, mountains, and national parks, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
