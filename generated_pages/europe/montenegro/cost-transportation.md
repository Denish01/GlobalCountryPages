[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Montenegro has a relatively well-developed transportation system, with buses being the primary mode of public transport. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent bus services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in Montenegro get around by using public buses or minivans, which are affordable and convenient. However, the country's mountainous terrain can make road travel challenging, especially during winter months. The capital city, Podgorica, has a comprehensive bus network, while other cities like Kotor and Budva rely on a combination of buses and taxis.

The country's scenic coastline and picturesque towns make it a popular destination for tourists, and many visitors choose to explore Montenegro by car or on foot. The roads in Montenegro are generally in good condition, but driving can be challenging due to the country's rugged terrain and narrow roads. Alternatively, visitors can use taxis or ride-hailing services, which are widely available in major cities. With a GDP per capita PPP of $34,807 (2024) and an inflation rate of 3.3% (2024), Montenegro offers a relatively affordable transportation system compared to other European countries.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a monthly transit pass or using ride-hailing services.
- Download the Bolt or TaxiPodgorica app to book taxis or ride-hailing services in advance.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.
- When using taxis, make sure to agree on the fare before starting your journey or insist on using the meter.
- Consider renting a car for longer trips or exploring the countryside, but be aware of the country's challenging road conditions.
- Always wear a seatbelt and follow local traffic rules to ensure a safe and enjoyable journey.
