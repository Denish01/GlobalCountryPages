# Where to Stay in Montenegro — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Montenegro, consider the coastal towns of Kotor, Budva, and Ulcinj, as well as the capital city Podgorica, which offer a range of accommodation options for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, Montenegro has something unique to offer:
**Kotor** (Kotor)
Best For: Luxury travelers and those interested in history
Vibe: Kotor is a charming, historic town with a beautiful old town and stunning bay, offering a sophisticated and cultural experience. The town has a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for those looking to unwind.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Unique historic architecture, stunning natural beauty, and a range of cultural attractions. The old town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the bay is perfect for boat tours and other water activities.
Cons: Can be crowded during peak season, and parking can be challenging.

**Budva** (Budva)
Best For: Families and young travelers
Vibe: Budva is a lively, coastal town with a beautiful old town and a range of beaches, offering a fun and energetic atmosphere. The town has a vibrant nightlife, with many bars and restaurants to choose from.
Price Range: $80-$250/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, historic old town, and a range of accommodation options. The town is also close to other popular destinations, such as Sveti Stefan and Petrovac.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded during peak season.

**Ulcinj** (Ulcinj)
Best For: Backpackers and budget travelers
Vibe: Ulcinj is a laid-back, coastal town with a beautiful old town and a range of beaches, offering a relaxed and affordable experience. The town has a strong sense of community, and is perfect for those looking to meet other travelers.
Price Range: $30-$150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, beautiful beaches, and a range of outdoor activities. The town is also close to the border with Albania, making it a great base for exploring the region.
Cons: Limited cultural attractions, and the town can be quiet during the off-season.

**Podgorica** (Podgorica)
Best For: Business travelers and those interested in urban culture
Vibe: Podgorica is a modern, bustling city with a range of cultural attractions and a vibrant atmosphere. The city has a strong sense of energy, and is perfect for those looking to experience the urban side of Montenegro.
Price Range: $60-$200/night
Pros: Modern amenities, cultural attractions, and a range of accommodation options. The city is also close to the airport, making it a convenient base for exploring the country.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, and the city lacks the historic charm of other towns.

**Tivat** (Tivat)
Best For: Luxury travelers and those interested in sailing
Vibe: Tivat is a sophisticated, coastal town with a beautiful marina and a range of high-end amenities, offering a luxurious and exclusive experience. The town has a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for those looking to unwind.
Price Range: $150-$400/night
Pros: Luxury accommodation options, beautiful marina, and a range of sailing and water activities. The town is also close to the airport, making it a convenient base for exploring the Bay of Kotor.
Cons: Can be expensive, and the town lacks the historic charm of other destinations.

**Perast** (Perast)
Best For: Couples and honeymooners
Vibe: Perast is a charming, historic town with a beautiful old town and stunning views of the Bay of Kotor, offering a romantic and intimate experience. The town has a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for those looking to unwind.
Price Range: $80-$250/night
Pros: Unique historic architecture, stunning natural beauty, and a range of romantic amenities. The town is also close to other popular destinations, such as Kotor and Budva.
Cons: Limited accommodation options, and the town can be quiet during the off-season.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June-September), to ensure availability and the best prices.
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Airbnb, to compare prices and find the best deals.
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or during the off-season.
- Look for discounts or package deals, which can include amenities such as breakfast or car rental.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices often higher during peak season and lower during the off-season.
- Read reviews and check the location before booking, to ensure the accommodation meets your needs.
- Consider booking a room with a view, especially in coastal towns, to make the most of the stunning natural beauty.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas with high crime rates, such as some parts of Podgorica, and take normal precautions to ensure personal safety.
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas, such as bus stations and tourist hotspots.
- Keep valuables secure, especially in coastal towns where theft can be more common.
- Consider staying in areas with good public transportation links, to make it easy to get around.
- Be aware of the proximity to beaches, historic sites, and other attractions, to make the most of your stay.
- Research the local area before booking, to ensure it meets your needs and is suitable for your type of travel.
