# Education Costs in Montenegro

The cost of education in Montenegro varies depending on the type of institution and the level of study, with public education being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Montenegro is divided into preschool, primary, and secondary education, with a strong emphasis on public education. Public schools are generally considered to be of good quality, with a focus on the **Montenegrin language** and culture. However, private schools and international schools are also available, offering a range of curricula, including **IB** and **Cambridge**, and catering to the needs of expat families and international students. Compulsory education in Montenegro lasts for 9 years, from the age of 6 to 15, and is free for all citizens.

The quality of education in Montenegro is generally considered to be good, with a strong focus on **STEM subjects** and a range of extracurricular activities. However, there are some differences in the quality of education between public and private schools, with private schools often having more resources and better facilities. International schools in Montenegro offer a range of curricula and are a popular choice for expat families, with many schools offering **English language instruction** and a range of extracurricular activities.
