[SECTION] Overview: Montenegro vs Kosovo [/SECTION]
Montenegro and Kosovo are two neighboring countries in the Balkan region, with distinct economic profiles and cost of living standards. Montenegro has a higher GDP per capita and a more developed tourism industry, which drives up prices for accommodation, food, and other services. In contrast, Kosovo has a lower GDP per capita and a more limited economy, resulting in lower prices for most goods and services. Overall, Kosovo is generally cheaper than Montenegro, with a cost of living difference of around 20-30% for most expenses.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in shaping their cost of living. Montenegro has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on tourism, manufacturing, and services, while Kosovo's economy is primarily driven by agriculture and remittances from abroad. This difference in economic structure contributes to the variation in prices between the two countries. Additionally, Montenegro's proximity to Western Europe and its aspirations to join the European Union have led to increased investment and economic activity, which has driven up prices in the country.
