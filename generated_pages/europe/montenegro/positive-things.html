Cultural Treasures

  • The Bay of Kotor is a stunning fjord-like bay and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its breathtaking natural beauty and historic towns such as Kotor and Perast.
  • The Durmitor Mountains are a popular destination for hiking and outdoor activities, with their rugged landscape and picturesque villages.
  • The National Museum of Montenegro in Cetinje showcases the country's rich history and cultural heritage, with a collection of artifacts and exhibits that date back to the medieval period.
  • The Kotor Cathedral is a beautiful Catholic cathedral that dates back to the 12th century, featuring stunning Romanesque architecture and intricate stone carvings.
  • The Budva Old Town is a charming historic town with narrow streets, picturesque piazzas, and a range of cultural attractions, including the Budva Citadel and the Church of St. John.

People & Global Impact

  • Novak Djokovic, the world-renowned tennis player, has Montenegrin roots and has been involved in various philanthropic initiatives in the country.
  • The Montenegrin diaspora has made significant contributions to the development of the country, with many expatriates returning to invest in businesses, education, and healthcare.
  • Miodrag Bulatovic, a prominent Montenegrin writer, has won numerous literary awards for his works, including the NIN Award for his novel The War Was Better.
  • The Montenegrin values of hospitality, respect for tradition, and community solidarity are highly regarded and have been recognized as an important part of the country's cultural identity.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Montenegrin people is consistently praised by visitors, who appreciate the warm welcome and generous spirit of the locals.
  • The local cuisine is a highlight of any visit to Montenegro, with popular dishes such as cevapi, janjetina, and palacinke showcasing the country's rich gastronomic heritage.
  • The scenic beauty of Montenegro's coastline, mountains, and national parks is a major draw for tourists, with many visitors drawn to the country's stunning natural landscapes.
  • The historic towns of Kotor, Budva, and Cetinje are popular destinations, with their narrow streets, picturesque piazzas, and range of cultural attractions.
  • The summer festivals in Montenegro, such as the Kotor Festival of Theatre and the Budva Summer Festival, offer a range of cultural and entertainment options for visitors.
  • The outdoor activities in Montenegro, such as hiking, biking, and water sports, provide plenty of opportunities for adventure and exploration.

Hidden Gems

  • The Biogradsko Lake is a beautiful and tranquil lake located in the Biogradska Gora National Park, offering opportunities for hiking, fishing, and relaxation.
  • The Ostrog Monastery is a stunning Orthodox monastery that dates back to the 17th century, featuring breathtaking architecture and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Perast Old Town is a charming historic town located on the Bay of Kotor, known for its beautiful baroque architecture, picturesque streets, and stunning views of the bay.
  • The Lovcen National Park is a scenic national park located in the Lovcen Mountains, offering opportunities for hiking, biking, and exploration of the country's natural beauty.
