[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The property market in Montenegro has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by the country's stunning natural beauty, rich history, and **economic stability**. The market is characterized by a mix of modern and traditional properties, with a focus on **tourism** and **recreational** developments. Prices have been increasing steadily, particularly in popular areas such as the **Bay of Kotor** and the **Budva Riviera**. However, the market remains relatively affordable compared to other European destinations, making it an attractive option for investors and buyers. Urban areas tend to be more expensive than rural areas, but the latter offers a more relaxed pace of life and a closer connection to nature.

The current market conditions are favorable for buyers, with a wide range of properties available, from **apartments** and **villas** to **land** and **commercial** spaces. The price trajectory is expected to continue its upward trend, driven by the country's growing popularity as a **tourist destination** and its increasing appeal to international investors. The government has also implemented various initiatives to stimulate the market, including **investment incentives** and **tax breaks**.

The property market in Montenegro is also characterized by a strong **seasonal** component, with prices and demand varying depending on the time of year. The peak season, which runs from June to September, sees a significant increase in demand and prices, while the off-season is generally quieter and more affordable. This seasonal pattern is particularly pronounced in popular tourist areas, where **rental yields** can be higher during the peak season.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners can buy property in Montenegro without any significant restrictions, but they must obtain a **permit** from the Ministry of Interior.
- Foreign companies can also buy property, but they must be registered in Montenegro and have a **tax identification number**.
- There are no restrictions on the type of property that can be bought, but **agricultural land** can only be purchased by companies that are registered for agricultural activities.
- Foreigners can also lease property, with **leasehold** agreements available for up to 99 years.
- To buy property, foreigners must open a **bank account** in Montenegro and obtain a **tax identification number**.

[SECTION] Rental Market [/SECTION]
- The rental market in Montenegro is relatively **unregulated**, but tenants have certain rights, including the right to a **written contract**.
- **Lease terms** are typically one to three years, with a **security deposit** equivalent to one to three months' rent.
- **Rent** is usually paid in **advance**, with a **minimum** of one month's notice required for termination.
- **Furnished** properties are more common in tourist areas, while **unfurnished** properties are more common in urban areas.
- **Property management** companies are available to assist with **rental management** and **maintenance**.

[SECTION] Investment Tips [/SECTION]
- **Emerging areas** such as **Ulcinj** and **Bar** offer attractive investment opportunities, with lower prices and potential for growth.
- **Tourist areas** such as **Budva** and **Kotor** offer higher **rental yields**, but prices are also higher.
- **Property management** is crucial to maximizing **rental income** and minimizing **maintenance costs**.
- **Legal considerations** such as **taxation** and **regulations** must be carefully considered when investing in property.
- **Due diligence** is essential when buying property, including **checking** the **ownership** and **checking** for any **outstanding debts**.
- **Local knowledge** is essential when navigating the property market in Montenegro, so it's recommended to work with a **reputable real estate agent** or **property consultant**.
