[SECTION] What Is Montenegro? [/SECTION]
Montenegro is a **sovereign** country located in southeastern Europe, bordered by Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Serbia, Kosovo, and Albania. With a population of approximately 0.62 million people, Montenegro is a relatively small country, but it has a rich history and culture. The country's name, which translates to "Black Mountain," refers to the dark forests that cover much of its terrain. Montenegro is known for its stunning natural beauty, with a mix of rugged mountains, pristine beaches, and picturesque villages.

Geographically, Montenegro is situated on the Adriatic coast, with a diverse landscape that includes mountains, forests, and a coastline along the Adriatic Sea. The country's capital, Podgorica, is located in the central region, while other major cities include Kotor, Budva, and Herceg Novi. Montenegro has a Mediterranean climate, with warm summers and mild winters, making it an attractive destination for tourists.

Montenegro declared its independence from Serbia and Montenegro in 2006, becoming a **sovereign state**. Since then, the country has been working to develop its economy, infrastructure, and tourism industry. Today, Montenegro is a popular destination for travelers, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.
