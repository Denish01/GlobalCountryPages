Education System Overview

Estonia's education system is highly regarded, with a strong emphasis on public education, which is generally free or subsidized for local students. The language of instruction is primarily Estonian, although some schools may offer instruction in Russian or English. Compulsory education in Estonia lasts for 12 years, starting from the age of 7 and ending at the age of 18. Public education is generally considered to be of high quality, with well-equipped schools and qualified teachers. Private education is also available, although it is not as widespread as public education. International schools are available in larger cities, such as Tallinn, and offer a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the British curriculum.

In addition to public and private schools, Estonia also has a number of international schools that cater to the needs of expat families and international students. These schools offer a range of curricula and are often staffed by experienced international teachers. Estonia's education system is highly developed, with a strong emphasis on technology and innovation. The country has a highly developed e-government system, and many schools use digital tools and platforms to support learning. This has helped to establish Estonia as a leader in education technology, and the country is often cited as an example of best practice in the use of technology to support learning.
