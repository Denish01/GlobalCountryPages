[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Estonia is considered a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other European countries. The overall atmosphere is welcoming and friendly, making it an excellent destination for tourists. However, as with any foreign travel, it's essential to take normal precautions to ensure your safety. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and bag snatching can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, but these are relatively rare. Violent crimes are even less common, and Estonia has a strong reputation for being a secure and stable country.

The capital city, Tallinn, is particularly safe, with a strong police presence and well-lit streets. The city's old town is a popular tourist area, and visitors can feel comfortable walking around, even at night. However, it's always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas. The countryside and smaller towns are also generally safe, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. Overall, Estonia is an excellent choice for travelers who want to experience a unique and safe cultural experience.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as overpriced taxi rides or fake tour operators
- Drunk and disorderly behavior, particularly in Tallinn's old town on weekends
- Credit card skimming and ATM fraud, especially in tourist areas
- Bike theft, particularly in urban areas
- Online scams and phishing attacks, especially when using public Wi-Fi
- Border crossing scams, particularly when traveling to or from Russia

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Estonia, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Estonia, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in Estonia are of high quality and widely available, especially in urban areas. However, some medical procedures may require payment upfront, so it's essential to have travel insurance.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are easily accessible and well-stocked, with many prescription medications available over-the-counter.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or public transportation
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-sharing services, and always check the meter is on
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or debit cards instead
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the safe if available
- Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi, especially when accessing sensitive information
- Follow local advice and instructions, especially in areas with restricted access

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Estonia is generally a safe country for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a welcoming atmosphere
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-sharing services, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Keep in touch with family and friends back home, and let them know your itinerary and plans
