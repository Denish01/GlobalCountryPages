Cultural Treasures

  • The Historic Centre of Tallinn, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a well-preserved medieval town with a rich cultural heritage.
  • The Estonian National Museum in Tartu is a leading cultural institution, showcasing the country's history, art, and traditions.
  • The Song Festival, a traditional Estonian music festival, has been held every five years since 1869, featuring thousands of singers and musicians.
  • The Kadriorg Park and Palace, a beautiful Baroque palace and garden complex, was built in the 18th century for the Russian royal family.
  • The Estonian Folk Dance Festival, a biennial event, showcases traditional Estonian folk dances and music.

What Visitors Love

  • The medieval architecture of Tallinn's Old Town, with its well-preserved walls, towers, and churches, is a major tourist attraction.
  • The beautiful coastline of Estonia, with its many beaches, bays, and islands, offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, sailing, and fishing.
  • The traditional Estonian cuisine, with its emphasis on local ingredients such as meat, fish, and berries, is a highlight of many visitors' trips.
  • The friendly and welcoming locals, with their high level of English language proficiency, make visitors feel at home.
  • The vibrant cultural scene, with its many festivals, concerts, and exhibitions, offers something for every interest and taste.
  • The excellent value for money, with its relatively low prices for accommodation, food, and activities, makes Estonia an attractive destination for budget-conscious travelers.

Hidden Gems

  • The Hiiumaa Island, located off the northwest coast of Estonia, is a peaceful and picturesque destination, with its beautiful beaches, forests, and traditional villages.
  • The Soomaa National Park, a protected area of wetlands and forests, is home to a wide range of wildlife, including bears, wolves, and eagles.
  • The Tartu University, one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in Estonia, is a hub of academic and cultural activity, with a rich history and beautiful architecture.
  • The Estonian Open-Air Museum, located in Tallinn, showcases traditional Estonian architecture and way of life, with its many historic buildings and exhibits.
