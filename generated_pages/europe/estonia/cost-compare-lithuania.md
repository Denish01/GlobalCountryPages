[SECTION] Overview: Estonia vs Lithuania [/SECTION]
Estonia and Lithuania are two neighboring countries in the Baltic region of Europe, both offering unique cultural and economic experiences. Estonia, with its highly developed economy and strong tech industry, tends to have higher prices for goods and services compared to Lithuania. The GDP per capita in Estonia is around $31,400, slightly higher than Lithuania's $29,900. This economic difference is reflected in the cost of living, with Estonia being around 10-20% more expensive than Lithuania. However, both countries offer a high standard of living and a range of amenities for expats, students, and tourists.

The economic context of both countries is characterized by a strong service sector, with a growing focus on technology and innovation. Estonia, in particular, has gained a reputation as a hub for startups and digital innovation, which has driven up demand for housing and services in cities like Tallinn. Lithuania, on the other hand, has a more diverse economy with a strong manufacturing sector, which has kept prices relatively lower. Overall, while both countries offer a great quality of life, Lithuania is generally the more affordable option, with prices around 10-20% lower than in Estonia.
