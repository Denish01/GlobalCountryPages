# Estonia Infrastructure — Roads, Airports, Electricity & Water

Estonia's infrastructure is well-developed, with a strong focus on digitalization, sustainability, and connectivity, making it one of the most advanced countries in the region.

[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Estonia's infrastructure development level is high, with significant investments made in recent years to enhance its digital, transport, and energy sectors. The country has been actively working to reduce the urban-rural gap in infrastructure development, with a focus on improving access to high-speed internet, healthcare, and education in rural areas. Estonia's strategic location in Northern Europe, bordering the Baltic Sea, has also made it an important transit country for trade between Europe and Russia. The government has been investing heavily in infrastructure development, with a focus on sustainability, innovation, and digitalization.

Estonia's infrastructure development is also driven by its strong economy, which has been growing steadily over the past decade. The country has a highly developed IT sector, with many international companies having operations in Estonia. This has created a demand for high-quality infrastructure, including data centers, transportation networks, and logistics facilities. The government has been working to create a business-friendly environment, with a focus on innovation, entrepreneurship, and foreign investment.

The urban-rural gap in infrastructure development remains a challenge in Estonia, with many rural areas still lacking access to high-quality infrastructure, including roads, internet, and healthcare facilities. However, the government has been working to address this issue, with a focus on investing in rural infrastructure development and promoting digitalization and innovation in rural areas.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]
- Estonia's power generation is mostly based on **oil shale**, with a significant portion also coming from **wind power** and **biomass**.
- The country has been working to reduce its dependence on oil shale, with a focus on increasing the use of **renewable energy sources**.
- Estonia's electricity grid is well-developed, with a high level of reliability and minimal power outages.
- The country has also been investing in **energy efficiency** measures, including the development of smart grids and green buildings.
- Estonia is connected to the **Nordic energy market**, allowing for the import and export of electricity.
