[SECTION] What Is Estonia? [/SECTION]
Estonia is a **sovereign** country located in **Northern Europe**, bordering the Baltic Sea and the Gulf of Finland. It is situated between Latvia to the south and Russia to the east, with Finland to the north across the Gulf of Finland. Estonia is known for its stunning natural beauty, with over 2,000 islands and a diverse landscape of forests, bogs, and coastal cliffs. The country has a rich cultural heritage, with a unique blend of Nordic and Baltic influences.

Estonia is a relatively small country, with a population of just over 1.3 million people. Despite its small size, Estonia has a strong sense of national identity and a distinct language, **Estonian**, which is a Finno-Ugric language closely related to Finnish. The country has a high standard of living and a strong economy, with a focus on technology, innovation, and entrepreneurship. Estonia is also a popular tourist destination, with its capital city **Tallinn** being a major draw for visitors from around the world.

Estonia's strategic location has made it an important hub for trade and commerce throughout history. The country has been ruled by various powers, including the Danes, Germans, Swedes, and Russians, each leaving their mark on the country's culture, architecture, and language. Today, Estonia is a **democratic** country and a member of the European Union, the NATO alliance, and other international organizations.

1. 1219 - Estonia is conquered by the Danes, marking the beginning of foreign rule in the country.
2. 1561 - Estonia is divided between Sweden and Poland, with the northern part of the country becoming a Swedish province.
3. 1721 - Estonia is ceded to Russia, and remains under Russian rule for nearly two centuries.
4. 1918 - Estonia declares its independence from Russia, following the Russian Revolution.
5. 1940 - Estonia is occupied by the Soviet Union, and becomes a Soviet republic.
6. 1991 - Estonia regains its independence, following the collapse of the Soviet Union.
7. 2004 - Estonia joins the European Union and NATO, marking a new era of cooperation and integration with Western Europe.
