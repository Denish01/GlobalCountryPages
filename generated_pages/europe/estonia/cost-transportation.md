[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Estonia has a well-developed public transportation system, with buses and trains being the primary modes of transport. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent and reliable services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in Estonia get around using public transportation, with a significant number also opting for cycling and walking, especially in urban areas. The country's compact size and relatively low population density make it easy to navigate, and the use of technology, such as mobile apps and online ticketing systems, has further improved the transportation experience.

The capital city, Tallinn, has a particularly well-developed public transportation system, with an extensive network of buses and trams. The city is also home to a number of bike-sharing schemes and car-sharing services, making it easy for residents and visitors to get around without relying on personal vehicles. Outside of Tallinn, public transportation is less frequent, but still reliable, with buses and trains connecting major towns and cities. The use of **ride-hailing apps** and **taxi services** is also widespread, especially in urban areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use public transportation whenever possible, as it is generally affordable and reliable.
- Consider purchasing a **rechargeable public transportation card**, which can be used on buses and trains throughout the country.
- Use **ride-hailing apps**, such as Bolt and Uber, for convenient and affordable transportation in urban areas.
- Be aware of **traffic rules and regulations**, especially when driving in urban areas.
- Consider **cycling or walking** for shorter journeys, especially in urban areas, to save money and reduce your carbon footprint.
- Always **negotiate the fare** before getting into a taxi, especially if you're not using a metered taxi.
