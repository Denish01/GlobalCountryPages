Location & Borders

Luxembourg is a small, landlocked country located in Western Europe. It is situated in a region where the Ardenne Mountains and the Moselle River valley meet. The country shares borders with three neighboring countries: Belgium to the west and north, France to the south, and Germany to the east. Luxembourg's strategic location has made it an important cultural and economic crossroads throughout history.

The country's borders are marked by natural features such as rivers and hills, with the Moselle River forming a significant part of its border with Germany. The landscape is characterized by a mix of forests, farmland, and urban areas, with the capital city Luxembourg City situated in the south-central part of the country. Despite its small size, Luxembourg has a diverse geography, with different regions offering unique landscapes and attractions.

Terrain & Landscape

Luxembourg's terrain is varied, with a mix of mountains, valleys, and plains. The Ardenne Mountains cover about one-third of the country, with the highest point being Kneiff at 560 meters. The mountains are covered in dense forests, with many hiking trails and scenic viewpoints. The Moselle River valley is another notable feature, with steep cliffs and scenic vineyards.

The country's landscape is also characterized by a number of rivers, including the Sauer, Our, and Alzette rivers. These rivers have carved out valleys and gorges, creating a diverse range of landscapes and ecosystems. The Gutland region, which covers the central and eastern parts of the country, is a plateau region with a mix of farmland, forests, and urban areas.

Luxembourg's landscape has been shaped by its geological history, with the country being located in a region of significant tectonic activity. The country's rocks are predominantly limestone and sandstone, with many caves and rock formations throughout the country. The unique landscape and geology of Luxembourg have created a diverse range of ecosystems, with many rare and endangered species found in the country.
