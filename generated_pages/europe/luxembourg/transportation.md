# Getting Around Luxembourg — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Luxembourg, you can choose from a variety of transportation options, including an extensive rail network, a well-developed road system, and a range of public transportation services, making it easy to explore the country and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Luxembourg is easily accessible by air, with Luxembourg Airport (LUX) being the main international airport, offering direct flights to many European cities, including London, Paris, and Frankfurt. The airport is located about 6 kilometers east of Luxembourg City and is connected to the city center by bus and taxi services. If you're traveling from neighboring countries, you can also enter Luxembourg by car or train, with several overland entry points along the borders with Belgium, France, and Germany.

Luxembourg's strategic location in the heart of Europe makes it a convenient hub for travelers, with many major cities within a few hours' reach by car or train. The country's well-developed road network and extensive rail system also make it easy to get around once you arrive. Whether you're traveling for business or leisure, Luxembourg's transportation options are designed to make your journey as smooth and efficient as possible.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic air travel in Luxembourg is limited, as the country is relatively small and most destinations can be reached by car or train within a short time. However, there are some domestic flights available, mainly connecting Luxembourg Airport with smaller regional airports. Luxembourg's national carrier, Luxair, operates a limited number of domestic flights, and you can book tickets through their website or at a local travel agency. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the route and time of booking, but you can expect to pay around $100-$200 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Luxembourg has an extensive rail network, with frequent train services connecting the country to its neighbors and major European cities. The national rail company, Chemins de Fer Luxembourgeois (CFL), operates a range of train services, including high-speed trains to destinations like Paris, Brussels, and Frankfurt. You can book train tickets online or at local train stations, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service. A one-way ticket from Luxembourg City to Brussels, for example, can cost around $20-$50.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Luxembourg has a well-developed network of bus and coach services, connecting major towns and cities across the country. Several private companies, including AVL and RGTR, operate bus services, offering frequent departures and affordable prices. You can book tickets online or at local bus stations, and prices vary depending on the route and time of booking. A one-way ticket from Luxembourg City to Esch-sur-Alzette, for example, can cost around $2-$5.
