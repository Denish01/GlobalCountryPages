# Healthcare Costs in Luxembourg

The cost of healthcare in Luxembourg can range from $50 to $500 per visit, depending on the type of service and whether you choose public or private healthcare, with an average health expenditure per capita of $8,170 per year.

[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
Luxembourg has a mixed healthcare system, combining both public and private services, with a strong emphasis on providing high-quality care to all citizens and residents. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of high quality, with well-equipped hospitals and skilled medical professionals, while private healthcare offers more personalized services and shorter waiting times. For locals, the healthcare system is largely subsidized by the government, with most medical expenses reimbursed by the state. For foreigners, it is recommended to have health insurance, as medical costs can be high, and not all services are covered by the public system.

The healthcare system in Luxembourg is designed to provide comprehensive coverage to all residents, with a range of services available, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and specialist consultations. However, the system can be complex, and navigating it can be challenging, especially for foreigners. It is essential to have a good understanding of the system and to have adequate health insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses. In Luxembourg, health insurance is mandatory for all residents, and there are various options available, including basic local insurance, comprehensive private insurance, and international insurance plans.

[SECTION] Pharmacy & Medication Costs [/SECTION]
- Prescription medication prices vary from $5 to $50 per prescription, depending on the type of medication and the pharmacy.
- Over-the-counter medication prices range from $5 to $20 per item, depending on the type of medication and the pharmacy.
- Prescription rules are strict, and most medication requires a prescription from a licensed doctor.
- Pharmacies are widely available, with many locations throughout the country, including in major cities and towns.
- Medication prices are generally higher in private pharmacies than in public pharmacies.
- Some medication may require a special import permit, which can increase the cost and delay the delivery.

[SECTION] Emergency Care [/SECTION]
In the event of a medical emergency, it is essential to call the emergency number, 112, which is available 24/7. Ambulance services are widely available, and emergency responders are trained to provide high-quality care. For foreigners, it is recommended to visit one of the major hospitals in Luxembourg City, such as the **Centre Hospitalier de Luxembourg** or the **Clinique Sainte-Elisabeth**, which offer a range of medical services, including emergency care, and have staff who speak multiple languages. In a medical emergency, it is crucial to remain calm and to provide as much information as possible to the emergency responders, including your location, the nature of the emergency, and any relevant medical history.
