[SECTION] What Is Luxembourg? [/SECTION]
Luxembourg is a **sovereign** country located in Western Europe, bordered by Belgium, France, and Germany. With a population of approximately 0.66 million people, it is one of the smallest countries in the European Union. Despite its small size, Luxembourg has a unique cultural identity shaped by its strategic geographic position and complex history. The country is known for its stunning natural beauty, with dense forests, rolling hills, and picturesque valleys.

Luxembourg's history has been marked by its position at the crossroads of Europe, with various empires and dynasties vying for control over the region. Today, the country is a **constitutional monarchy** with a parliamentary system, and it has developed a strong economy based on **steel production**, **finance**, and **technology**. Luxembourg City, the capital, is a popular tourist destination, with its historic old town, scenic valleys, and modern architecture.

The country's linguistic diversity is another notable aspect of its culture, with **Luxembourgish**, **French**, and **German** all being official languages. This multilingualism reflects the country's history and its position at the heart of Europe. Luxembourg is also a member of several international organizations, including the European Union, the United Nations, and NATO, and it plays an active role in European and global affairs.

1. 963 - The County of Luxembourg is established, marking the beginning of the country's history as a separate entity.
2. 1354 - Luxembourg becomes a Duchy, with the ruling family holding significant power and influence in the region.
3. 1795 - Luxembourg is annexed by France during the French Revolution, and it remains under French control until 1815.
4. 1815 - The Congress of Vienna establishes Luxembourg as a Grand Duchy, with the Dutch King William I as its ruler.
5. 1867 - The Treaty of London establishes Luxembourg's independence and neutrality, with the country becoming a **sovereign state**.
6. 1914-1918 - Luxembourg is occupied by Germany during World War I, and it suffers significant economic and human losses.
7. 1945 - Luxembourg becomes a founding member of the United Nations and begins to play an active role in European and global affairs.
