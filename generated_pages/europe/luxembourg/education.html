Education System Overview

The education system in Luxembourg is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, which is compulsory for all children between the ages of 4 and 11, focuses on developing basic skills in languages, mathematics, and social sciences. Secondary education, which lasts for seven years, offers a range of academic and vocational programs, including technical secondary education and classical secondary education. Tertiary education, which includes universities and university colleges, offers a wide range of academic programs, including bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees. The quality of education in Luxembourg is generally high, with a strong emphasis on multilingualism and cultural diversity.

The education system in Luxembourg is predominantly public, with most schools and universities being state-funded. However, there are also a number of private schools and institutions, including international schools and private universities, which offer alternative educational programs and curricula. The public education system is generally considered to be of high quality, with well-qualified teachers and modern infrastructure. However, there are some challenges, including overcrowding in some schools and limited resources for certain subjects.

In recent years, the Luxembourg government has implemented a number of reforms aimed at improving the quality and accessibility of education, including the introduction of free education for all students and the development of new vocational training programs. These reforms have helped to make education more accessible and affordable for all, regardless of background or socio-economic status.

International Schools

  • There are a number of international schools in Luxembourg, offering a range of curricula, including IB, British, and American programs.
  • These schools cater to the needs of international students and families, providing a high-quality education in a multilingual and multicultural environment.
  • Some of the most popular international schools in Luxembourg include the International School of Luxembourg and the St. George's International School.
  • Tuition fees for international schools in Luxembourg can be high, ranging from 10,000 to 30,000 EUR per year.
  • Many international schools in Luxembourg offer scholarships and financial aid to students who cannot afford the full tuition fee.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Luxembourg, international students need to obtain a student visa, which can be applied for at the Luxembourg embassy or consulate in their home country.
  • Language requirements for studying in Luxembourg vary depending on the institution and program, but most universities require proficiency in French, German, or English.
  • There are a number of scholarships available to international students, including the Luxembourg government scholarship and the European Union's Erasmus Mundus scholarship.
  • Living costs for students in Luxembourg can be high, with rent, food, and transportation costs averaging around 1,500 EUR per month.
  • International students can work part-time in Luxembourg, but need to obtain a work permit and register with the local authorities.
  • Many universities in Luxembourg offer language support and orientation programs for international students, to help them adjust to life in Luxembourg.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • The education system in Luxembourg is known for its high quality and multilingual approach, with a strong emphasis on academic excellence and cultural diversity.
  • However, there are some challenges, including overcrowding in some schools and limited resources for certain subjects, such as science and technology.
  • The Luxembourg government has implemented a number of reforms aimed at improving the quality and accessibility of education, including the introduction of free education for all students and the development of new vocational training programs.
  • One of the main strengths of the education system in Luxembourg is its flexibility, with a range of academic and vocational programs available to students, including apprenticeships and distance learning programs.
  • Despite these strengths, there are some weaknesses, including inequality and disadvantage, with some students from low-income backgrounds facing barriers to accessing education and achieving their full potential.
