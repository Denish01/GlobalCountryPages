[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Luxembourg has a well-developed public transportation system, with an extensive network of buses and trains connecting the city to the surrounding areas. The quality of public transit is generally high, with frequent and reliable services. Most people in Luxembourg get around using public transportation, with the majority of residents holding a monthly transit pass. The country's compact size and scenic landscapes also make it an ideal destination for cycling and walking, with many bike paths and pedestrian zones throughout the city.

The public transportation system in Luxembourg is managed by the **Verkéiersverbond**, which offers a range of services including buses, trains, and trams. The city is also well-connected to the surrounding countries, with regular train services to Belgium, France, and Germany. For those who prefer to drive, Luxembourg has a well-maintained road network, although traffic can be heavy during peak hours. Parking in the city can be challenging, but there are several parking garages and lots available.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider purchasing a **Mobilitéitskaart**, a monthly transit pass that offers unlimited travel on public transportation in Luxembourg.
- Use the **mobiliteit.lu** app to plan your journey and track public transportation in real-time.
- Be aware that traffic in Luxembourg City can be heavy during peak hours, and consider using alternative modes of transport such as cycling or walking.
- When taking a taxi, make sure to check the meter is running and agree on the fare before starting your journey.
- For longer journeys, consider using the train or bus, which can be more cost-effective and scenic than driving.
- Always follow local traffic rules and regulations, and be mindful of pedestrians and cyclists when driving in the city.
