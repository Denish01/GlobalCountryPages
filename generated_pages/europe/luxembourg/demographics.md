[SECTION] Population Overview [/SECTION]
The population of Luxembourg is relatively small, with a total of 681,973 people, according to the latest estimates. The country has experienced steady population growth, with a rate of 2.1% per annum, driven by a combination of natural increase and net migration. The population is predominantly urban, with approximately 92.3% of the population residing in urban areas, such as Luxembourg City, Esch-sur-Alzette, and Differdange. The country's population is also characterized by a high level of **multiculturalism**, with a significant proportion of foreign-born residents and a diverse mix of languages and cultures.

The population growth trend in Luxembourg is expected to continue, driven by a combination of factors, including a strong economy, high standard of living, and a favorable business environment. The country's **urbanization** rate is also expected to increase, with more people moving to urban areas in search of better job opportunities and higher living standards. However, this trend also poses challenges, such as housing shortages, traffic congestion, and pressure on public services.

The migration pattern in Luxembourg is characterized by a significant influx of foreign workers, particularly from other European countries, such as Portugal, France, and Belgium. The country's **migration policy** is designed to attract highly skilled workers and entrepreneurs, while also providing a safe and welcoming environment for refugees and asylum seekers. The government has implemented various initiatives to promote **integration** and **social cohesion**, including language training programs, cultural events, and community outreach activities.
