[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Slovenia has a high-quality internet infrastructure, with fast and reliable connections available in most parts of the country. The average internet speed is around 74 Mbps download, which is comparable to other European countries. However, there is a notable urban-rural divide in terms of internet quality, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in urban areas such as Ljubljana and Maribor. In rural areas, internet speeds can be slower and less reliable, but overall, the internet infrastructure in Slovenia is well-developed and suitable for most needs.

The reliability of the internet in Slovenia is also a major advantage, with most connections remaining stable and uninterrupted. This makes it an attractive destination for remote workers and digital nomads who require a reliable internet connection to stay productive. Additionally, Slovenia's internet infrastructure is constantly being upgraded and improved, with the rollout of 5G networks in recent years. This has further enhanced the country's internet capabilities and provides a solid foundation for future growth and development.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Slovenia is a straightforward process, with several mobile carriers offering tourist-friendly plans. The main carriers, Telekom Slovenije, A1 Slovenija, and Telemach, all offer a range of plans and options, including prepaid SIM cards that can be purchased at airports, train stations, and mobile carrier stores. To buy a SIM card, you will typically need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, although some carriers may have additional requirements. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and most reliable connections.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality in Slovenia is generally good, with most hotels offering fast and reliable connections.
- Cafe WiFi culture is also widespread, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in Slovenia, with many offering high-speed internet and other amenities.
- Public WiFi is available in many cities and towns, including Ljubljana and Maribor, although the quality and reliability can vary.
- Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while dining out.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces such as Poligon and Kino Šiška offer high-speed internet and other amenities, making them ideal for remote workers.
- Reliable cafe chains such as Costa Coffee and Starbucks offer fast and reliable WiFi, making them good backup options.
- Backup internet options such as mobile hotspots and portable WiFi routers are also available, although they can be more expensive.
- VPNs are not strictly necessary in Slovenia, but they can provide an added layer of security and protection when using public WiFi networks.
- Many libraries and community centers also offer free WiFi, making them a good option for remote workers who need a quiet and productive space.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major issue in Slovenia, although some websites may be blocked due to copyright or other restrictions.
- Blocked sites can often be accessed using a VPN, which can provide a secure and private connection.
- VPN recommendations include ExpressVPN and NordVPN, which offer fast and reliable connections.
- Roaming alternatives such as eSIMs and portable WiFi routers can provide a convenient and cost-effective way to stay connected while traveling.
- It is always a good idea to check the terms and conditions of your SIM card or mobile plan before using it, as some carriers may have restrictions or limitations on data usage.
