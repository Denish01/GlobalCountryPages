# Getting Around Gibraltar — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Gibraltar, you can use a combination of public transportation, taxis, and rental cars, as the territory has a well-developed network of roads and a range of transport options.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Gibraltar International Airport (GIB) is the main entry point for visitors, with flights from various European cities, including London, Manchester, and Barcelona. You can also enter Gibraltar by land from Spain, with the border crossing at La Línea de la Concepción being the most convenient option. The airport is located near the city center, and you can easily get to your accommodation by taxi or bus. If you're coming from Spain, you can take a bus or drive to Gibraltar, but be aware that there may be queues at the border, especially during peak travel seasons.

The main direct flight hubs to Gibraltar include London Heathrow, London Gatwick, and Manchester Airport, with British Airways and EasyJet operating regular flights. You can also fly into Malaga Airport in Spain and then take a bus or taxi to Gibraltar, which is about a two-hour journey. Overland entry points from Spain include the border crossing at La Línea de la Concepción, which is open 24 hours a day, and the eastern border crossing at San Roque, which is open from 9 am to 8 pm.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Gibraltar has a limited domestic air network, with no scheduled flights within the territory. However, you can take a helicopter tour of the Rock of Gibraltar, which offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding countryside. If you're looking to explore the surrounding region, you can fly from Gibraltar to other destinations in Europe, such as Morocco or Portugal, with several airlines operating flights from the airport. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, and you can expect to pay around $200-$500 for a one-way ticket, depending on the destination and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Gibraltar has no rail network, so you won't be able to take a train to get around the territory. However, you can take a bus or taxi to the nearby Spanish town of San Roque, which has a train station with connections to other cities in Spain, including Madrid and Seville. If you're planning to explore the surrounding region, you can take a train from San Roque to other destinations in Spain, with Renfe operating regular services.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Gibraltar has a well-developed bus network, with several companies operating services within the territory and to nearby towns in Spain. The main bus company is Gibraltar Bus Company, which operates services to all parts of the territory, including the city center, the airport, and the border with Spain. You can pay for your ticket on the bus, and fares are around $2-$5, depending on the distance. There are also several private bus companies that operate services to other destinations in Spain, including Malaga and Seville.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Gibraltar, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country, and you must be at least 18 years old.
- The road quality in Gibraltar is generally good, with well-maintained roads and minimal traffic congestion.
- Fuel costs are around $1.50 per liter, which is slightly higher than in Spain.
- Car rental companies are available at the airport and in the city center, with prices starting from around $40 per day.
- When driving in Gibraltar, you should be aware of the narrow streets and tight corners, especially in the old town.
- Parking can be a challenge, especially in the city center, so it's a good idea to use a parking garage or park outside the city walls.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of the border crossing from Spain, which can be busy, especially during peak travel seasons.
- Use a reputable taxi company or ride-hailing app to avoid scams.
- Pay for your bus ticket on the bus, and make sure you have the correct change.
- Download the Gibraltar Bus Company app to plan your route and track your bus in real-time.
- Be mindful of the narrow streets and pedestrianized areas in the old town, and watch out for cyclists and pedestrians.
- Use a parking garage or park outside the city walls to avoid congestion and parking fines.
- Consider purchasing a Gibraltar Tourist Travel Pass, which includes unlimited bus travel and discounts on attractions and activities.
- Be prepared for queues at the border crossing, especially during peak travel seasons, and make sure you have all the necessary documents, including your passport and driver's license.
