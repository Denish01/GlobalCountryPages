[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Gibraltar is a small territory with a well-developed public transportation system, making it easy to get around without a car. The main transportation modes in Gibraltar include buses, taxis, and walking, as the territory is relatively small and pedestrian-friendly. The quality of public transit in Gibraltar is good, with frequent bus services connecting the city center to outlying areas. Most people in Gibraltar get around on foot or by bus, as the territory is compact and traffic can be heavy at times.

The public transportation system in Gibraltar is efficient and reliable, with buses running frequently throughout the day. The buses are operated by Gibraltar Bus Company, which offers a range of routes covering the entire territory. Taxis are also widely available, but can be more expensive than buses. Walking is a popular mode of transport in Gibraltar, as the territory is small and many attractions are within walking distance. Cycling is not a common mode of transport in Gibraltar, due to the territory's hilly terrain and narrow roads.
