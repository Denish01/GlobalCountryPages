Education System Overview

The education system in Gibraltar is structured into primary, secondary, and tertiary levels, with primary education being compulsory for children between the ages of 4 and 11, and secondary education being compulsory for students between the ages of 11 and 16. The system is modeled after the British education system, with students taking GCSE and A-level exams at the end of their secondary and post-secondary education, respectively. The quality of education in Gibraltar is generally considered to be high, with a strong focus on academic achievement and extracurricular activities. Public schools in Gibraltar are free and open to all residents, while private schools offer an alternative option for those who can afford it.

The public education system in Gibraltar is well-funded and well-organized, with a range of resources and support available to students. The private education system, on the other hand, offers a more personalized and specialized approach to education, with smaller class sizes and a greater emphasis on individual attention. Both public and private schools in Gibraltar offer a range of extracurricular activities, including sports, music, and drama, which are an integral part of the education system.

In terms of tertiary education, Gibraltar has a range of options available, including the University of Gibraltar, which offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in fields such as business, education, and health. The university is known for its small class sizes and personalized approach to education, and is a popular choice for students from Gibraltar and around the world.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Gibraltar, international students will need to apply for a student visa, which can be obtained through the Gibraltar Immigration Department.
  • English language proficiency is a requirement for most programs, and students will need to demonstrate their English language skills through a recognized language proficiency test, such as IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Scholarships are available for international students, including the Gibraltar Government Scholarship and the University of Gibraltar Scholarship.
  • Living costs in Gibraltar can be high, with rent, food, and transportation costs ranging from $1,000 to $3,000 per month.
  • International students will need to have health insurance to cover any medical expenses while studying in Gibraltar.
  • The Gibraltar Student Union offers a range of support services for international students, including academic support, housing assistance, and social activities.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • The education system in Gibraltar is known for its high standards and strong focus on academic achievement.
  • One of the challenges facing the education system in Gibraltar is the limited range of programs available, particularly at the tertiary level.
  • The education system in Gibraltar is also heavily reliant on British-style curricula, which can limit the range of options available to students.
  • Recent reforms have aimed to increase access to education and improve the quality of teaching, particularly in the areas of mathematics and science.
  • The University of Gibraltar has implemented a range of initiatives to improve student satisfaction and graduate employability, including work placement programs and career counseling services.
