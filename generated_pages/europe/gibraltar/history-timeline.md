# Gibraltar History — Key Events & Timeline

The history of Gibraltar is a complex and fascinating narrative that spans over 2,000 years, from its earliest known inhabitants to its current status as a British Overseas Territory.

[SECTION] Historical Overview [/SECTION]
Gibraltar's strategic location at the southern tip of the Iberian Peninsula has made it a prized possession for various civilizations throughout history. The earliest known inhabitants of Gibraltar were the **Phoenicians**, who established a settlement in the 12th century BC. The **Romans** later conquered the region, and it became an important stopping point for trade and commerce. Following the decline of the Roman Empire, Gibraltar was ruled by the **Moors**, who introduced their language, culture, and architecture to the region. In 1704, Gibraltar was captured by a combined Anglo-Dutch fleet, and it has remained under British control ever since.

The **Treaty of Utrecht** in 1713 formally ceded Gibraltar to Britain, and it has since been a British Overseas Territory. Gibraltar's unique cultural identity has been shaped by its history, with a blend of British, Spanish, and Moorish influences. The territory has played a significant role in various military conflicts, including the **Napoleonic Wars** and **World War II**. In recent years, Gibraltar has developed a thriving economy, with a strong focus on financial services, tourism, and online gaming.

Gibraltar's history has been marked by periods of tension with neighboring Spain, which has long claimed sovereignty over the territory. The **Spanish Civil War** had a significant impact on Gibraltar, as it became a key base for the British military. In the 1960s and 1970s, Gibraltar's **constitution** was established, and the territory began to develop its own system of government. Today, Gibraltar is a self-governing territory, with its own parliament and a high degree of autonomy.

The territory's history has also been shaped by its **geography**, with the famous **Rock of Gibraltar** dominating the landscape. The Rock has been a key factor in Gibraltar's strategic importance, and it has been the site of numerous military installations and defenses over the centuries. Gibraltar's unique history and culture have made it a fascinating destination for tourists, with a rich heritage of historical landmarks, museums, and cultural events.
