Healthcare Overview

Gibraltar's healthcare system is a universal system, with all residents and citizens entitled to free or low-cost medical care. The system is primarily funded by the government, with some additional funding from the National Health Service (NHS). The quality of care in Gibraltar is generally excellent, with well-trained medical professionals and modern facilities. In terms of access, Gibraltar's small size means that urban and rural areas are not a significant concern, as all areas are within a short distance of medical facilities. The healthcare system is also well-equipped to handle the needs of tourists and visitors, with many medical professionals speaking English as a primary language.

The public healthcare system in Gibraltar is the primary provider of medical services, with a range of facilities and services available, including hospitals, clinics, and community health centers. Private healthcare options are also available, although these are not as widely used as the public system. The quality of care in both public and private facilities is high, with a strong focus on patient care and safety.

Gibraltar's healthcare system is also well-integrated with the UK's NHS, with many medical professionals trained in the UK and a high level of cooperation between the two systems. This means that residents and visitors from the UK can often receive seamless care in Gibraltar, with minimal disruption to their medical treatment.

Health Insurance

  • Private health insurance is recommended for visitors and residents, as it can provide additional coverage and flexibility in terms of medical care.
  • Local insurance options are available, including plans from companies such as Bupa and AXA.
  • Coverage for expats is widely available, with many international insurance companies offering plans that include coverage in Gibraltar.
  • Costs for health insurance in Gibraltar can vary widely, depending on the type and level of coverage, as well as the age and health status of the individual.
  • Group insurance plans are often available for employers, providing a cost-effective way to offer health insurance to employees.
  • Travel insurance is also recommended for visitors, as it can provide additional coverage for unexpected medical expenses.

Pharmacies & Medications

  • Availability of medications is generally good in Gibraltar, with a wide range of prescription and over-the-counter medications available.
  • Prescription requirements are similar to those in the UK, with a prescription from a licensed medical professional required for most medications.
  • Common brands of medications are widely available, including international brands such as Pfizer and GlaxoSmithKline.
  • Costs for medications in Gibraltar can vary, depending on the type and brand of medication, as well as the individual's insurance coverage.
  • Pharmacy access is widespread, with several pharmacies located throughout Gibraltar, including in the city center and near major medical facilities.

Vaccinations & Health Risks

  • Required vaccinations for visitors to Gibraltar are generally the same as those for the UK, with recommendations including MMR, DTaP, and influenza.
  • Recommended vaccinations may include additional vaccinations, such as hepatitis A and B, depending on the individual's health status and travel plans.
  • Endemic diseases in Gibraltar are similar to those in the UK, with a low risk of infectious diseases such as tuberculosis and HIV.
  • Water safety is generally good in Gibraltar, with a well-maintained water supply system and minimal risk of water-borne illnesses.
  • Food safety is also generally good, with a high standard of food handling and preparation in restaurants and food establishments.
  • Sun protection is recommended, as Gibraltar's sunny climate can increase the risk of skin cancer and other sun-related health problems.

Medical Tourism

  • Popular procedures for medical tourism in Gibraltar include cosmetic surgery, dental care, and orthopedic surgery.
  • Accredited hospitals in Gibraltar include St Bernard's Hospital and the King Edward VII Hospital, both of which have international accreditation.
  • Cost savings can be significant for medical tourism in Gibraltar, with costs often lower than in the UK or other European countries.
  • English-speaking medical professionals are widely available, making it easy for international patients to communicate and receive care.
