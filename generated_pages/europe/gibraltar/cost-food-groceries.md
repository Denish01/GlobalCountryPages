# Food & Grocery Prices in Gibraltar

The cost of food in Gibraltar is relatively high compared to other European territories, with a meal at an inexpensive restaurant costing around $15-$20 USD and a monthly grocery budget for one person ranging from $500-$700 USD.

[SECTION] Food Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Gibraltar's food prices are influenced by its unique location, with the territory relying heavily on imports from the UK and other European countries. As a result, prices for certain items, such as meat and dairy products, can be higher than in neighboring Spain. However, the territory's low tax rates and lack of VAT (Value-Added Tax) help to keep prices relatively competitive. Eating out is a popular pastime in Gibraltar, with a wide range of restaurants serving everything from traditional British fare to international cuisine. Despite this, home cooking remains a staple for many residents, with many locals opting to prepare meals at home using fresh ingredients from local markets and supermarkets.

The eating-out culture in Gibraltar is vibrant, with many restaurants and cafes offering a range of options to suit all budgets. However, for those on a tight budget, home cooking is often the more affordable option. With a little planning and creativity, it's possible to prepare delicious and nutritious meals at home using ingredients from local markets and supermarkets. Additionally, many residents take advantage of the territory's proximity to Spain, where prices for certain items, such as fruit and vegetables, can be significantly lower.

[SECTION] Supermarket vs Local Market [/SECTION]
When it comes to shopping for groceries, Gibraltar residents have a choice between supermarkets and local markets. While supermarkets tend to offer a wider range of products, including imported items, local markets often provide fresher produce at lower prices. For example, a kilogram of apples may cost $3.00-$5.00 USD at a supermarket, while the same item may be available for $2.00-$3.00 USD at a local market. Additionally, local markets often offer a more personalized shopping experience, with vendors happy to provide advice and recommendations on the best products to buy.

In general, it's a good idea to buy fresh produce, such as fruit and vegetables, at local markets, while supermarkets may be a better option for packaged goods and household essentials. Bargaining is not typically expected at either supermarkets or local markets, although vendors may be willing to offer discounts for bulk purchases or regular customers. By shopping at a combination of both supermarkets and local markets, residents can take advantage of the best prices and products available.
