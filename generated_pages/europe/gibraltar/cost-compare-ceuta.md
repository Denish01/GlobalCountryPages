[SECTION] Overview: Gibraltar vs Ceuta [/SECTION]
Gibraltar and Ceuta are two European territories with distinct economic contexts. Gibraltar, a British Overseas Territory, has a strong financial sector and a high standard of living, which contributes to its relatively high cost of living. Ceuta, a Spanish autonomous city, has a more modest economy and a lower cost of living. The main difference in cost levels between the two territories lies in the prices of housing, food, and transportation. Gibraltar's proximity to the UK and its status as a financial hub drive up prices, making it more expensive than Ceuta.

The economic context of the two territories also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Gibraltar's economy is driven by financial services, tourism, and online gaming, which attracts high-income individuals and drives up prices. Ceuta, on the other hand, has a more diverse economy with a mix of manufacturing, tourism, and trade, which keeps prices relatively lower. Overall, Ceuta is around 20-30% cheaper than Gibraltar, making it a more affordable option for those looking to live in a European territory.
