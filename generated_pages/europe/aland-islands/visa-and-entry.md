# Aland Islands Visa & Entry Requirements

As a territory of Finland, the Aland Islands have a relatively liberal visa policy, and citizens of many countries can enter the islands without a visa, but it's essential to verify the specific requirements for your nationality before traveling.

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
The Aland Islands have a moderate visa policy, with citizens of over 60 countries able to enter the islands without a visa. The visa policy is largely aligned with that of Finland, and the islands are part of the Schengen Area, which allows for passport-free travel between certain European countries. Citizens of countries that are part of the Schengen Area or have a visa-free agreement with Finland can typically enter the Aland Islands without a visa, while citizens of other countries may need to apply for a visa in advance.

The Aland Islands are a popular tourist destination, and the visa policy is designed to facilitate travel to the islands while maintaining the security and integrity of the Schengen Area. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to apply for a visa in advance, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit.
