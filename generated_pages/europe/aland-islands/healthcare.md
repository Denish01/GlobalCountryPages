[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
The Aland Islands' healthcare system is a universal, publicly-funded system, providing comprehensive medical care to all residents, including expatriates and tourists. The system is primarily funded through taxes and government subsidies, with some out-of-pocket expenses for certain services. The quality of care is generally excellent, with well-trained medical professionals and modern facilities. Urban areas, such as the capital Mariehamn, have better access to healthcare services compared to rural areas, but the difference is not significant. The Aland Islands' healthcare system is closely tied to the Finnish healthcare system, and residents often travel to Finland for specialized care.

The public healthcare system in the Aland Islands provides a wide range of services, including primary care, hospital care, and specialized care. Private healthcare services are also available, but they are not as widespread as public services. The Aland Islands have a high standard of living and a strong economy, which contributes to the overall quality of the healthcare system. The healthcare system is also well-integrated with other social services, such as social welfare and education, to provide comprehensive support to residents.

The healthcare system in the Aland Islands is also known for its emphasis on preventive care and health promotion. The authorities have implemented various initiatives to promote healthy lifestyles, prevent diseases, and improve overall well-being. These initiatives include public health campaigns, health screenings, and education programs. The Aland Islands' healthcare system is a model for other countries, with its focus on universal access, high-quality care, and preventive care.

[SECTION] Health Insurance [/SECTION]
- The Aland Islands' healthcare system is primarily funded through taxes and government subsidies, but private health insurance is recommended for non-EU citizens and expatriates.
- The **Finnish Social Insurance Institution (KELA)** provides health insurance coverage for Finnish citizens, including those living in the Aland Islands.
- **Private health insurance** is available from various providers, including Finnish and international insurance companies.
- **Travel insurance** is recommended for tourists and visitors, as it provides coverage for unexpected medical expenses.
- **Expatriate health insurance** is available for foreign nationals living and working in the Aland Islands, providing comprehensive coverage for medical expenses.
- **Supplementary insurance** is available for residents who want to supplement their public healthcare coverage with additional services, such as private hospital care.

[SECTION] Pharmacies & Medications [/SECTION]
- **Prescription medications** are widely available at pharmacies throughout the Aland Islands, but a prescription from a licensed doctor is required.
- **Over-the-counter medications** are available at pharmacies and some retail stores, but may require a prescription for certain medications.
- **Common brands** of medications are available, including international brands and Finnish brands.
- **Costs** of medications vary, but are generally lower than in other European countries.
- **Pharmacy services** are widely available, with many pharmacies offering extended hours and emergency services.

[SECTION] Vaccinations & Health Risks [/SECTION]
- **Required vaccinations** for travel to the Aland Islands include measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), as well as tetanus and diphtheria.
- **Recommended vaccinations** include hepatitis A and B, and influenza.
- **Endemic diseases** include tick-borne encephalitis and Lyme disease, which are prevalent in the Aland Islands' forests and grasslands.
- **Water safety** is generally good, but it is recommended to drink tap water or bottled water to avoid waterborne illnesses.
- **Food safety** is also good, but it is recommended to follow proper food handling and cooking practices to avoid foodborne illnesses.
- **Insect-borne illnesses** such as mosquito-borne illnesses are rare in the Aland Islands, but it is still recommended to take precautions against insect bites.
