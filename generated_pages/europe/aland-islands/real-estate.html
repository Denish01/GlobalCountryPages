Market Overview

The Aland Islands property market is characterized by a mix of modern and traditional homes, with a strong emphasis on sustainable living and energy efficiency. The current market conditions are relatively stable, with a steady demand for properties, particularly in the capital city of Mariehamn. The price trajectory has been steadily increasing over the past few years, with an average annual increase of 3-5%. The urban areas, such as Mariehamn, tend to have higher property prices compared to the rural areas, where prices are generally lower. However, the rural areas offer a more relaxed pace of life and a strong sense of community.

The Aland Islands' unique location in the Baltic Sea, between Finland and Sweden, makes it an attractive destination for tourists and investors alike. The territory's autonomous status within Finland also provides a unique set of rules and regulations that govern the property market. The market is also influenced by the seasonal fluctuations in tourism, with a peak season during the summer months.

The property market in Aland Islands is also characterized by a strong sense of community involvement, with many local initiatives and projects aimed at preserving the territory's natural beauty and cultural heritage. This has led to a growing demand for eco-friendly properties and sustainable developments, which are becoming increasingly popular among buyers and investors.

Foreign Ownership Rules

  • Foreigners are allowed to buy property in Aland Islands, but they need to obtain a permit from the Finnish government.
  • The permit is usually granted if the buyer is a citizen of an EU or EEA country, or if the buyer has a residence permit in Finland.
  • Non-EU citizens may face restrictions on buying property, but they can still buy through a Finnish company or a trust.
  • Foreign buyers should also be aware of the tax implications of buying property in Aland Islands, including the payment of capital gains tax.
  • It is recommended that foreign buyers consult a lawyer or a real estate agent to ensure they comply with all the necessary regulations and procedures.

Buying Process

  1. Research the market and find a property that suits your needs and budget.
  2. Contact a real estate agent to help you with the buying process.
  3. View properties and select the one you want to buy.
  4. Make an offer and negotiate the price.
  5. Sign a purchase agreement and pay a deposit.
  6. Apply for a permit if you are a foreign buyer.
  7. Conduct a property inspection** to ensure the property is in good condition.
  8. Finalize the purchase and transfer the ownership.
  9. Register the property with the local authorities.
  10. Obtain a certificate of ownership** to confirm your ownership of the property.

Rental Market

  • The rental market in Aland Islands is relatively small, but it is growing in popularity among tourists and expats.
  • Tenant rights are protected by law, and tenants have the right to rent control and security of tenure.
  • Typical lease terms are 6-12 months, but they can be longer or shorter depending on the agreement.
  • The deposit is usually equivalent to one or two months' rent.
  • Furnished and unfurnished properties are available, but furnished properties are more common in the rental market.
