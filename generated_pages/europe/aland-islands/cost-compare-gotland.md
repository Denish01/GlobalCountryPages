[SECTION] Overview: Aland Islands vs Gotland [/SECTION]
The Aland Islands and Gotland are two autonomous regions in the Baltic Sea, with distinct cultural and economic profiles. The Aland Islands, a territory of Finland, have a higher GDP per capita and a more diverse economy, driven by services, tourism, and shipping. Gotland, a part of Sweden, has a more homogeneous economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, tourism, and manufacturing. In terms of cost of living, the Aland Islands tend to be more expensive, particularly when it comes to housing, food, and transportation.

The difference in cost of living between the two regions can be attributed to various factors, including the Aland Islands' unique tax status, which attracts high-income earners and entrepreneurs. Additionally, the Aland Islands' remote location and limited connectivity to the mainland contribute to higher transportation costs and a more limited range of affordable housing options. In contrast, Gotland's economy is more integrated with the Swedish mainland, which helps to keep costs lower. Overall, Gotland is generally cheaper than the Aland Islands, with a difference of around 10-20% in overall cost of living.
