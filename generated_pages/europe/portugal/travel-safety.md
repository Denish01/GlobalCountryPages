[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Portugal is considered a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other European countries. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around cities and towns, even at night, with minimal risk of encountering violent crime. However, as with any popular tourist destination, petty crimes such as pickpocketing and bag snatching can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, scams targeting tourists are not uncommon, so it's essential to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions.

Overall, Portugal's safety record is excellent, and most visitors have a trouble-free experience. The country has a strong police presence, and authorities take the safety of tourists seriously. With some basic precautions and an understanding of the local environment, visitors can minimize their risk of encountering problems and enjoy all that Portugal has to offer. From the stunning beaches and historic cities to the vibrant culture and friendly locals, Portugal is a wonderful destination for travelers of all ages and backgrounds.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas, such as train stations and tourist hotspots
- Beware of scams targeting tourists, such as the "gold ring" scam, where scammers try to sell fake gold jewelry
- Be wary of overly friendly strangers who approach you in tourist areas, as they may be trying to distract you while an accomplice steals your belongings
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Be cautious when using ATMs, especially at night, and avoid using machines in isolated areas
- Don't leave valuables unattended, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Portugal, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Portugal, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in Portugal are of high quality, with many hospitals and clinics catering to tourists
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, and many pharmacists speak English, making it easy to access medication and advice

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night, especially in urban areas
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the safe to store valuables
- Be cautious when using public transportation, especially at night
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Learn some basic Portuguese phrases, such as "thank you" (obrigado) and "help" (ajuda)

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Portugal is generally a safe country for solo and women travelers, with low rates of violent crime
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, such as city centers or tourist hotspots
- Be cautious when interacting with strangers, especially if they seem overly friendly or aggressive
