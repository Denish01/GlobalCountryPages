What Is Portugal?

Portugal is a sovereign country located in southwestern Europe, bordered by Spain to the east and north, and the Atlantic Ocean to the west and south. It is a part of the Iberian Peninsula and occupies an area of approximately 92,390 square kilometers. Portugal is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and stunning natural beauty, with a diverse landscape that ranges from rolling hills and mountains to beautiful beaches and coastal cliffs. The country has a mild climate, with warm summers and mild winters, making it a popular destination for tourists and expatriates alike.

Portugal has a long and complex history, with various periods of invasion, colonization, and independence. The country has a strong sense of national identity, with a unique culture that reflects its history, language, and traditions. The Portuguese language is the official language, and it is spoken by the vast majority of the population. Key History

  1. 1139 - Portugal becomes an independent kingdom, with Afonso Henriques as its first king.
  2. 1415 - Portugal begins its exploration and colonization of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, establishing a vast empire that lasts for centuries.
  3. 1755 - A devastating earthquake strikes Lisbon, killing tens of thousands of people and destroying much of the city.
  4. 1910 - The Portuguese monarchy is overthrown, and a republic is established.
  5. 1974 - A revolution leads to the overthrow of the authoritarian government, and Portugal becomes a democratic country.
  6. 1986 - Portugal joins the European Union, marking a significant step towards economic and political integration with Europe.
  7. 1999 - Portugal returns the territory of Macau to China, marking the end of its colonial era.
