[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Portugal has a well-developed transportation network, with a range of options available for getting around, including buses, trains, metro systems, and taxis. The quality of public transit is generally high, with frequent and reliable services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in Portugal get around using public transportation, with buses and trains being the most popular modes of transport. The country's compact size and relatively low population density make it easy to navigate, and the scenic countryside and coastal routes make driving a pleasure.

The public transportation system in Portugal is efficient and affordable, with a range of options available to suit different budgets and preferences. The country's major cities, including Lisbon and Porto, have comprehensive public transportation systems, including buses, trams, and metro lines. The metro systems in Lisbon and Porto are particularly efficient, with frequent services and affordable fares. Outside of the cities, buses and trains connect smaller towns and villages, providing a reliable and affordable way to get around.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider purchasing a rechargeable **Viva Viagem** card for convenient travel on public transportation in Lisbon and Porto.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Bolt for affordable and convenient transportation, especially at night or in areas with limited public transportation.
- Be aware of **toll roads** when driving, as they can add significant costs to your journey.
- Negotiate the fare with the taxi driver before you start your journey, especially if you're traveling to or from the airport.
- Take advantage of **discounts** and promotions offered by public transportation companies, such as reduced fares for students or seniors.
- Download the **Moovit** app for helpful route planning and real-time information on public transportation in Portugal.
