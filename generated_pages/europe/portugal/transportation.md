# Getting Around Portugal — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Portugal, you can utilize a well-developed transportation network that includes international airports, domestic flights, an extensive rail system, buses, and a range of local transportation options in cities.

Rail Network: extensive
Drives On: right
Road Quality: good
Ride-Hailing Apps: Uber, Bolt, local alternatives
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Portugal is easily accessible by air, with its main international airports being Lisbon Portela Airport (LIS) and Porto Francisco Sá Carneiro Airport (OPO), offering direct flights to numerous European and intercontinental destinations. Lisbon Airport is a major hub, serving as a gateway to Europe, Africa, and the Americas, while Porto Airport is a key entry point for those traveling from within Europe. For those preferring to enter the country by land, Portugal shares a border with Spain, and there are several overland entry points, including the Caia and Vilar Formoso border crossings, which are convenient for those traveling by car or bus.

In addition to air travel, Portugal can also be reached by sea, with several ports along its coastline, including the Port of Lisbon and the Port of Leixões near Porto, which receive cruise ships and ferries from various Mediterranean and European destinations. The country's geographical location makes it an ideal stopover for those traveling between Europe, Africa, and the Americas, and its transportation network is well-equipped to handle the influx of tourists and business travelers alike.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
For traveling within Portugal, there is a moderate domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights between the mainland and the islands of the Azores and Madeira. The main domestic airlines include TAP Air Portugal and Ryanair, which offer frequent flights between Lisbon, Porto, and other major cities. When booking domestic flights, it is advisable to compare prices and schedules in advance to secure the best deals, as prices can vary depending on the time of year and demand. Approximate costs for a one-way domestic flight can range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and airline.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Portugal has an extensive rail network, with ** Comboios de Portugal (CP)** being the primary operator, offering a range of services, including high-speed trains, intercity trains, and regional trains. The high-speed **Alfa Pendular** train connects Lisbon to Porto and other major cities, with frequent departures throughout the day. The booking process is relatively straightforward, with tickets available online or at train stations, and it is recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons. The rail network is well-maintained, and trains are generally punctual, making it a convenient and comfortable way to travel within the country.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
For those preferring to travel by bus, there are several intercity bus companies operating in Portugal, including **Rodoviária Nacional** and **FlixBus**, which offer affordable and efficient services between major cities. The buses are generally modern and well-maintained, with comfortable seating and onboard amenities. Prices vary depending on the route and operator, but approximate costs for a one-way bus ticket can range from $10 to $30. It is advisable to book tickets in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, to secure the best prices and availability.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Portugal, you will need a valid **driver's license**, which can be a European Union license or an international driving permit.
- The **road conditions** in Portugal are generally good, with well-maintained highways and scenic routes.
- **Fuel costs** are relatively high in Portugal, with prices averaging around $1.50 per liter for gasoline.
- When **renting a car**, it is advisable to book in advance and compare prices between different operators to secure the best deals.
- **Parking** in cities can be challenging, especially in historic areas, so it is recommended to use public parking lots or garages.
- **Toll roads** are common in Portugal, and it is advisable to have a **toll pass** or to pay with cash at toll booths.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **scams** in tourist areas, especially in crowded cities like Lisbon and Porto.
- **Validate your ticket** before boarding public transportation to avoid fines.
- Download the **CP (Comboios de Portugal) app** for easy booking and management of train tickets.
- Use a **reputable taxi company** or ride-hailing app to avoid overcharging.
- **Learn some basic Portuguese phrases**, such as "obrigado" (thank you) and "por favor" (please), to help navigate and interact with locals.
- **Respect local traffic rules** and drive defensively, especially on narrow roads and in rural areas.
- **Purchase a **Lisbon Card** or **Porto Card** for free public transportation and discounts on attractions in these cities.
- **Be prepared for **summer crowds** and **peak travel seasons**, and plan accordingly to avoid delays and long wait times.
