[SECTION] Overview: Portugal vs Spain [/SECTION]
Portugal and Spain are two neighboring countries in Western Europe that share many cultural and historical similarities, but also have distinct differences in terms of their economies and cost of living. Portugal has a lower GDP per capita than Spain, but also has a lower cost of living, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees. The economic context in both countries is similar, with a strong focus on tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing, but Portugal has been more successful in recent years in attracting foreign investment and promoting entrepreneurship.

The cost of living in Portugal is generally lower than in Spain, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being around 10-15% lower on average. This makes Portugal an attractive option for those looking to relocate to a European country with a warm climate and a high standard of living, but without the high prices of other Western European countries. However, it's worth noting that prices can vary significantly depending on the location, with cities like Lisbon and Porto in Portugal, and Madrid and Barcelona in Spain, being more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas.
