[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Denmark boasts an exceptional internet infrastructure, with high-speed connections available throughout the country. The average internet speed is 94 Mbps download, making it one of the fastest in the world. The urban-rural divide is minimal, with even rural areas enjoying reliable and fast internet connections. This is due to the Danish government's efforts to invest in digital infrastructure, ensuring that all citizens have access to high-quality internet. As a result, Denmark's internet is highly reliable, with minimal downtime and fast speeds, making it an attractive destination for remote workers and digital nomads.

The internet quality in Denmark is also characterized by a high level of security, with a strong focus on data protection and online safety. This is reflected in the country's strict data protection laws and regulations, which ensure that users' personal data is protected and secure. Additionally, Denmark's internet infrastructure is well-equipped to handle high demand, with a robust network of fiber-optic cables and mobile networks that provide comprehensive coverage throughout the country.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Denmark is a straightforward process, with several options available for tourists and visitors. The main carriers, TDC, Telenor, and Telia, offer a range of prepaid SIM cards and plans that can be purchased at their stores or online. To buy a SIM card, you will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, although some carriers may have additional requirements. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds.

The cost of a tourist SIM card is approximately 20 USD, and data plans start from around 10 USD per month for 1GB of data. It's also possible to purchase a SIM card with a larger data allowance, with prices starting from around 30 USD per month for 10GB of data. Some carriers also offer unlimited data plans, although these can be more expensive.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally excellent, with most hotels offering free and fast WiFi to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is widespread, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are abundant, with many offering high-speed WiFi and other amenities such as meeting rooms and printing facilities.
- Public WiFi is available in many cities and towns, with some municipalities offering free WiFi in public areas such as parks and squares.
- Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while dining out.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces such as **WeWork** and **Regus** offer high-speed WiFi and other amenities such as meeting rooms and printing facilities.
- Reliable cafe chains such as **Starbucks** and **Cafe Norden** offer free WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options such as **mobile hotspots** and **portable WiFi routers** are available for purchase or rental.
- Some cafes and coworking spaces also offer **VPN** services, which can be useful for remote workers who need to access secure networks.
- **Digital nomad communities** are also present in Denmark, with many online groups and meetups available for remote workers to connect with like-minded individuals.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is minimal in Denmark, with most websites and online services available without restriction.
- Some websites may be blocked due to copyright or licensing issues, but this is rare.
- **VPN** services can be useful for accessing blocked websites or secure networks.
- Roaming alternatives such as **eSIM** and **international SIM cards** are available for visitors who want to stay connected while traveling.
- It's recommended to purchase a local SIM card or use a portable WiFi router to avoid high roaming charges.
- **Public WiFi** networks can be insecure, so it's recommended to use a **VPN** or other security measures to protect your data when using public WiFi.
