# Top Things to Do in Denmark — Attractions & Experiences

Denmark offers a wide range of exciting activities and experiences, from exploring the vibrant city of Copenhagen to visiting the country's many historic castles and national parks, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all interests.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **hygge**-inspired cooking class to learn about traditional Danish cuisine and culture
- Visit the **Freetown Christiania**, a self-proclaimed autonomous neighborhood in Copenhagen known for its alternative culture and community
- Explore the **Clay Copenhagen** workshop, where you can learn to create traditional Danish ceramics
- Go on a **Viking tour** to discover Denmark's rich Viking history and heritage
- Attend a **Midsummer festival** to experience traditional Danish music, dance, and food
- Take a **beer tour** to sample some of Denmark's famous craft beers and learn about the brewing process
- Visit the **Designmuseum Danmark** to learn about Denmark's design heritage and see iconic Danish designs

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Møn Island**, known for its stunning white chalk cliffs and beautiful beaches
- Explore the **National Park Thy**, Denmark's first national park, featuring stunning coastal scenery and wildlife
- Go hiking in the **Silkeborg Lakes**, a beautiful area with numerous lakes, forests, and hiking trails
- Take a **kayak tour** through the canals of Copenhagen to see the city from a different perspective
- Visit the **Wadden Sea National Park**, a Unesco World Heritage Site and home to a diverse range of wildlife

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Gallery of Denmark**, featuring a comprehensive collection of Danish and international art
- Explore the **Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek**, a museum showcasing an impressive collection of art and cultural artifacts
- Attend a performance at the **Royal Danish Theatre**, one of the oldest and most respected theaters in Europe
- Take a **guided tour** of the **Assistens Cemetery**, the final resting place of famous Danes such as Hans Christian Andersen and Søren Kierkegaard
- Visit the **Danish Jewish Museum**, showcasing the history and culture of the Jewish community in Denmark

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **street food market** on **Paper Island**, featuring a variety of food stalls and vendors
- Explore the **Kunsthal Charlottenborg**, a contemporary art space featuring exhibitions and performances
- Take a **stroll through the King's Garden**, a beautiful and historic park in the heart of Copenhagen
- Visit the **Arken Museum of Modern Art**, located in a stunning building on the coast just outside Copenhagen
- Go to the **Vesterbro neighborhood**, known for its trendy bars, restaurants, and shops

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Helsingør**, located just outside Copenhagen, to visit the **Kronborg Castle** and the **Louisiana Museum of Modern Art** (distance: 45 km, travel time: 30 minutes)
- Visit the **Møn Island**, known for its stunning white chalk cliffs and beautiful beaches (distance: 130 km, travel time: 1.5 hours)
- Explore the **Odense**, the birthplace of Hans Christian Andersen, featuring a museum dedicated to the famous author (distance: 160 km, travel time: 2 hours)
- Take a day trip to **Aarhus**, Denmark's second-largest city, featuring a vibrant cultural scene and historic architecture (distance: 300 km, travel time: 3 hours)
- Visit the **Roskilde**, a historic town located just outside Copenhagen, featuring a stunning cathedral and a Viking ship museum (distance: 30 km, travel time: 20 minutes)
