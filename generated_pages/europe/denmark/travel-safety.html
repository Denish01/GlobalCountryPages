Overall Safety Assessment

Denmark is considered one of the safest countries to visit in the world. With low crime rates and a strong emphasis on social welfare, visitors can feel secure and comfortable while exploring the country. The Danish government takes the safety of its citizens and visitors seriously, and the country has a well-organized and efficient law enforcement system. However, as with any travel destination, it is still important to take common-sense precautions to ensure your safety, such as being aware of your surroundings and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Denmark is also a very welcoming and inclusive country, with a strong culture of respect for others. Visitors are unlikely to encounter any significant problems or hostility, and the country has a reputation for being very tolerant and open-minded. Overall, Denmark is an excellent choice for travelers who want to experience a safe and welcoming destination. Whether you are interested in exploring the vibrant city of Copenhagen, visiting the beautiful countryside, or enjoying the country's famous cuisine, Denmark is a great place to visit.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and bag snatching are the most common types of crime affecting tourists in Denmark, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
  • Bike theft is also a problem, so make sure to lock your bike securely and consider using a reputable bike rental company.
  • There have been some reports of scams targeting tourists, such as fake ticket sellers or street performers who demand money.
  • Be cautious of overly friendly strangers who may be trying to distract you while an accomplice steals your belongings.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when using ATMs or exchanging money, as there have been some reports of card skimming and other types of financial fraud.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable taxi companies or public transportation instead of hitchhiking.
  • Be careful when consuming alcohol, as Denmark has a strong drinking culture and it can be easy to overdo it.

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Denmark, but make sure you are up to date on all routine vaccinations.
  • Water Safety: Tap water is safe to drink in Denmark, and bottled water is widely available.
  • Medical Facilities: Denmark has a high-quality healthcare system, with well-equipped hospitals and medical facilities.
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available and well-stocked, with many open late or 24 hours.

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in areas with high foot traffic.
  • Use reputable taxi companies or public transportation instead of hitchhiking.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead.
  • Make sure to lock your bike securely and consider using a reputable bike rental company.
  • Be cautious of overly friendly strangers who may be trying to distract you while an accomplice steals your belongings.
  • Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuable items.
  • Be prepared for emergencies by knowing the location of the nearest hospital or police station.

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Denmark is generally a very safe country for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong culture of respect for others.
  • However, it is still important to take common-sense precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings and keeping an eye on your belongings.
  • Consider joining a group tour or using a reputable tour company to help you navigate the country and stay safe.
  • Be cautious when consuming alcohol, as it can be easy to overdo it and lose track of your surroundings.
