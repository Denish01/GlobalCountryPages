[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Tivoli Gardens** amusement park in Copenhagen is one of the oldest in the world, dating back to 1843, and features beautiful gardens and architecture.
- The **National Museum of Denmark** in Copenhagen showcases the country's rich history and culture, with exhibits on the Viking Age and the Middle Ages.
- The **Kronborg Castle**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its association with Shakespeare's **Hamlet** and features an impressive collection of art and artifacts.
- The **Danish Royal Ballet** is one of the oldest and most respected ballet companies in the world, with a repertoire that includes classical and contemporary works.
- The **Roskilde Festival**, a popular music festival held annually in Roskilde, features a diverse lineup of Danish and international artists.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Hans Christian Andersen** is one of Denmark's most famous authors, with his fairy tales translated into over 125 languages and inspiring countless adaptations and interpretations.
- **Arla Foods**, a Danish dairy company, is one of the largest in the world, with operations in over 100 countries and a commitment to sustainable agriculture.
- **Maersk**, a Danish shipping company, is one of the largest container shipping companies in the world, with a fleet of over 750 vessels and a presence in over 120 countries.
- **The Danish diaspora** has made significant contributions to various fields, including science, art, and politics, with notable figures such as **Viggo Mortensen**, an American-Danish actor, and **Lars Ulrich**, a Danish-American musician and drummer for **Metallica**.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- Denmark's **hospitality** is renowned, with visitors praising the warm and welcoming nature of the Danish people.
- The country's **cuisine** is a highlight, with popular dishes such as **smørrebrød** (open-faced sandwiches) and **flæskesteg** (roast pork) showcasing the country's love of local ingredients and traditional cooking methods.
- The **scenery** in Denmark is breathtaking, with visitors flocking to see the country's beautiful coastline, rolling hills, and picturesque villages.
- **Copenhagen**, the capital city, is a favorite among visitors, with its vibrant atmosphere, historic architecture, and world-class museums and galleries.
- The **bike-friendly infrastructure** in Denmark is a major draw, with visitors able to explore the country's scenic countryside and cities on two wheels.
- The **festival scene** in Denmark is thriving, with popular events such as the **Copenhagen Jazz Festival** and the **Roskilde Festival** showcasing the country's love of music and culture.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **island of Bornholm** is a lesser-known destination, but its stunning natural beauty, including white-sand beaches and rocky coastline, makes it a must-visit for nature lovers.
- The **city of Aarhus** is Denmark's second-largest city, but it is often overlooked in favor of Copenhagen, despite its vibrant cultural scene, historic architecture, and beautiful botanical gardens.
- The **Danish concept of hygge** (coziness) is a unique aspect of Danish culture, emphasizing the importance of warmth, comfort, and togetherness in everyday life.
- The **Mols Bjerge National Park** is one of Denmark's most beautiful national parks, featuring rolling hills, scenic coastline, and a diverse range of flora and fauna.
