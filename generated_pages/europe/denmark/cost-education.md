# Education Costs in Denmark

The cost of education in Denmark can range from free or subsidized for public schools to around $10,000 to $20,000 per year for private and international schools, with university tuition fees varying depending on the institution and student status.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Denmark's education system is known for its high quality, with a strong emphasis on **public education**. The language of instruction is Danish, but many schools also offer English and other foreign languages as part of their curriculum. Compulsory education in Denmark lasts for 10 years, from the age of 6 to 16, and is free for all students, including international students. Private schools, on the other hand, offer an alternative to public education, with some schools focusing on specific areas such as **international baccalaureate** programs or **special needs education**.

Denmark also has a number of **international schools** that cater to expat families and offer curricula such as the International Baccalaureate or the British GCSE. These schools often have a more diverse student body and faculty, and may offer additional support for students who are not fluent in Danish. Overall, Denmark's education system is highly regarded, with a strong focus on **academic achievement**, **social welfare**, and **personal development**.
