[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Romania has a well-developed transportation network, with various modes of transport available to get around the country. The most common modes of transportation are buses, trams, and taxis, with many cities also having metro systems. The quality of public transit in Romania is generally good, with frequent and reliable services. Most people in Romania use public transportation to get around, with buses and trams being the most popular options.

In larger cities like Bucharest, the public transportation system is more extensive, with a comprehensive network of buses, trams, and metro lines. The city also has a well-developed system of bike lanes and pedestrian paths, making it easy to get around without a car. In smaller towns and rural areas, public transportation may be less frequent, and taxis or ride-hailing services may be a more convenient option.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a monthly transit pass, which can be used on multiple modes of transport.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Bolt for convenient and affordable transportation.
- Always use licensed taxis and negotiate the fare before starting your journey.
- Be aware of traffic rules and regulations, especially when driving in larger cities.
- Consider renting a car for longer distances or for exploring rural areas, but be aware of toll roads and parking fees.
- Download transportation apps like Moovit or Citymapper to help navigate public transportation systems and plan your route.
