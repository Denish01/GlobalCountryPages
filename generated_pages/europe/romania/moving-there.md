# Moving to Romania — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Romania, you will need to research and understand the country's visa and residency options, learn about the culture and way of life, and plan carefully to ensure a smooth relocation process.

[SECTION] Why People Move to Romania [/SECTION]
Romania is a popular destination for expats due to its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and relatively low cost of living. Many people are drawn to the country's vibrant cities, such as **Bucharest**, which offers a unique blend of traditional and modern architecture, as well as a thriving arts and cultural scene. The country's **countryside** is also a major attraction, with its rolling hills, picturesque villages, and vast **Carpathian Mountains**. Expats from all over the world are relocating to Romania, with many coming from **Europe**, **North America**, and **Australia**. The quality of life in Romania is generally high, with good **healthcare**, **education**, and **infrastructure**.

The expat community in Romania is diverse and growing, with many people drawn to the country's **affordability** and **lifestyle**. The cost of living in Romania is relatively low compared to other European countries, making it an attractive option for **digital nomads**, **retirees**, and **families**. The country also offers a range of **outdoor activities**, such as hiking, skiing, and water sports, which makes it a great destination for **nature lovers**. Overall, Romania offers a unique and rewarding experience for expats, with its rich **history**, **culture**, and **natural beauty**.

Romania's **economy** is also growing, with many **job opportunities** in fields such as **IT**, **finance**, and **tourism**. The country is a member of the **European Union**, which makes it easy for **EU citizens** to live and work in Romania. Non-EU citizens can also relocate to Romania, but they will need to obtain a **visa** and **residency permit**. Overall, Romania is a great destination for expats who want to experience a new culture, learn a new language, and enjoy a high quality of life.

[SECTION] Relocation Checklist [/SECTION]
1. Research Romania's visa and residency options and choose the one that best suits your needs.
2. Gather all necessary documents, including a valid passport, birth certificate, and university degree.
3. Apply for a visa or residency permit at the Romanian embassy or consulate in your home country.
4. Find a place to live in Romania, either by renting an apartment or buying a house.
5. Open a bank account in Romania and transfer your money to the new account.
6. Register with the local authorities and obtain a residence permit.
7. Find a job or start a business in Romania, if you haven't already done so.
8. Learn some basic Romanian phrases, such as **"hello" (salut)**, **"thank you" (mulțumesc)**, and **"goodbye" (la revedere)**.
9. Join online expat communities and attend social events to meet new people.
10. Explore Romania's culture, history, and natural beauty by visiting museums, historical sites, and national parks.
11. Take care of your health and well-being by finding a good doctor and joining a gym or sports club.
12. Stay up-to-date with the latest news and developments in Romania by reading local newspapers and following expat blogs.

[SECTION] Expat Communities [/SECTION]
- **Bucharest** has a large and diverse expat community, with many social events and activities to choose from.
- **Cluj-Napoca** is a popular destination for expats, with a thriving arts and cultural scene.
- **Brasov** is a charming town in the heart of the Carpathian Mountains, with a growing expat community.
- The **Romania Expats** Facebook group is a great place to connect with other expats and ask for advice.
- The **Expats in Romania** forum is a useful resource for finding information and discussing topics related to expat life in Romania.

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- **Opening a bank account** in Romania is relatively easy, and can be done at a local bank branch with a valid passport and residence permit.
- **Money transfers** can be made using online services such as TransferWise or PayPal, or through a traditional bank transfer.
- **Currency considerations** should be taken into account, as the Romanian **lei (RON)** is the local currency.
- **Credit cards** are widely accepted in Romania, but it's still a good idea to have some cash on hand.
- **Taxes** should be considered, as Romania has a relatively low tax rate, but it's still important to understand your tax obligations as an expat.

[SECTION] Challenges & Honest Advice [/SECTION]
- **Culture shock** can be a challenge for expats in Romania, especially for those who are not used to the local customs and way of life.
- **Bureaucracy** can be frustrating, especially when dealing with government agencies or official paperwork.
- **Language barriers** can be a problem, especially for those who do not speak Romanian, but many locals speak English, especially in tourist areas.
- **Traffic and transportation** can be chaotic, especially in large cities like Bucharest.
- **Weather** can be unpredictable, with cold winters and hot summers, so it's a good idea to pack accordingly.
- **Homesickness** can be a challenge, especially for those who are far from family and friends, but joining expat communities and staying connected with loved ones can help.
