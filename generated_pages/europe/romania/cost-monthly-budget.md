# Monthly Budget Guide for Romania

To live in Romania, you would need approximately $400-700 per month for a budget lifestyle, $700-1,200 per month for a mid-range lifestyle, and $1,200-2,000 per month for a comfortable lifestyle.

[FACTBOX]
GDP per Capita (PPP): $49,559
Budget Living (monthly): $400-700
Mid-Range Living (monthly): $700-1,200
Comfortable Living (monthly): $1,200-2,000
Average Local Salary: $600-800 USD/month, approximate
Currency: RON
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Cost of Living Overview [/SECTION]
Romania offers a relatively affordable cost of living, with a GDP per capita of $49,559, which is lower compared to many Western European countries. This means that different budgets can go a long way in Romania, with budget lifestyles allowing for a basic but comfortable life, mid-range lifestyles offering a good balance between quality and affordability, and comfortable lifestyles providing a high standard of living. Compared to neighboring countries, Romania is generally more affordable than Hungary and Bulgaria, but slightly more expensive than Ukraine and Moldova.

The overall affordability of Romania makes it an attractive destination for digital nomads, retirees, and students, who can enjoy a good quality of life without breaking the bank. However, for families and those with high expectations, Romania may not be the cheapest option, and expenses can add up quickly. Overall, Romania offers a great balance between affordability and quality of life, making it an excellent choice for those looking for a affordable European destination.

[SECTION] Budget Tier Lifestyles [/SECTION]
A budget lifestyle in Romania would mean sharing an apartment outside of the city center, cooking most meals at home, and using public transportation. Daily life would be simple, with limited entertainment and socializing options. However, this lifestyle would allow for a basic but comfortable life, with some occasional treats and outings. For example, a budget of $400-700 per month would cover the costs of a shared apartment, basic food and groceries, and some public transportation, but would not leave much room for entertainment or travel.

A mid-range lifestyle in Romania would offer a good balance between quality and affordability. This would mean renting a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, eating out occasionally, and using a mix of public and private transportation. Daily life would be more comfortable, with more opportunities for entertainment and socializing. For example, a budget of $700-1,200 per month would cover the costs of a one-bedroom apartment, mid-range food and groceries, and some private transportation, as well as occasional trips and outings.

A comfortable lifestyle in Romania would mean enjoying a high standard of living, with a nice two-bedroom apartment, eating out frequently, and using private transportation. Daily life would be very comfortable, with many opportunities for entertainment, travel, and leisure activities. For example, a budget of $1,200-2,000 per month would cover the costs of a nice two-bedroom apartment, high-end food and groceries, and private transportation, as well as regular trips and outings.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Strategies [/SECTION]
- Cook at home instead of eating out, as food prices can be high in restaurants.
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or private cars.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets instead of high-end stores.
- Avoid tourist areas and opt for local bars and restaurants instead.
- Consider sharing an apartment with roommates to reduce rent costs.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost cultural events and activities.
- Learn some Romanian phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals.
- Use cash instead of credit cards to avoid foreign transaction fees.
