# Romania Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Romania before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Romania has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter without a visa, while others require a visa in advance or can obtain one upon arrival. Romania is a member of the European Union, and as such, it has a relatively liberal visa policy for EU citizens. Citizens of over 50 countries, including the US, Canada, and Australia, can enter Romania without a visa for a maximum stay of 90 days within a 180-day period.

Romania's visa policy is designed to facilitate travel and economic cooperation with other countries, while also ensuring the security and integrity of its borders. The country has a well-developed network of diplomatic missions and consular offices around the world, making it relatively easy for travelers to obtain the necessary visas and documentation. However, it's essential to note that visa requirements can change, and travelers should always verify the current requirements before planning their trip.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Romania without a visa for a maximum stay of 90 days within a 180-day period. They must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay. Tourists can typically stay in Romania for up to 90 days, but they can apply for an extension if they need to stay longer.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must have an invitation letter from a Romanian company, proof of business activities, and a valid passport. Business travelers may need to apply for a different type of visa, such as a work visa or a long-stay visa.
**Students:** Students who want to study in Romania must have a valid passport, proof of enrollment at a Romanian university, and a student visa. They must also have health insurance and sufficient funds for their stay. Student visas are usually issued for the duration of the study program.
**Workers:** Workers who want to work in Romania must have a valid passport, a work permit, and a long-stay visa. They must also have a job offer from a Romanian employer and meet the necessary qualifications and experience requirements. The work permit and visa application process can be complex and time-consuming.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
Romania has several main airports, including Henri Coanda International Airport in Bucharest and Cluj-Napoca International Airport. The country also has several land borders with its neighboring countries, including Ukraine, Moldova, Hungary, Serbia, and Bulgaria. When arriving in Romania, travelers must present their travel documents and go through immigration and customs procedures. They may be asked to provide additional information, such as their purpose of visit, length of stay, and accommodation details.

Travelers can expect a relatively smooth and efficient entry process, but it's essential to ensure that they have all the necessary documents and information ready. They should also be prepared to answer questions from immigration officers and provide additional documentation if required. Romania has a well-organized and modern border control system, making it relatively easy for travelers to enter the country.
