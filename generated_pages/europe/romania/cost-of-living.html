How Expensive Is Romania?

Romania is generally considered an affordable country to live in or visit, with a cost of living lower than many Western European countries. Compared to its neighbors, Romania is more expensive than Bulgaria and Moldova, but cheaper than Hungary and Serbia. The country offers a great balance of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. Overall, Romania is a great value for those looking to experience Europe without breaking the bank.

In terms of global averages, Romania's cost of living is below the average for European countries. However, prices in the capital city of Bucharest can be higher than in other parts of the country. The cost of accommodation, food, and transportation in Romania is generally lower than in Western Europe, making it an attractive option for those looking to live or travel on a budget.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs.
  • Eat at local restaurants or street food stalls instead of mid-range or fine dining restaurants.
  • Use public transportation instead of taxis to get around.
  • Buy groceries at local markets or supermarkets instead of eating out.
  • Avoid traveling during peak season (June to August) to save on accommodation and transportation costs.
  • Learn some basic Romanian phrases to negotiate prices and get a better deal from locals.

Overall, Romania is a great value destination for those looking to experience Europe on a budget. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culture, Romania has something to offer for every kind of traveler. By being mindful of costs and taking advantage of money-saving tips, visitors can have a great time in Romania without breaking the bank.
