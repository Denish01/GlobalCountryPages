[SECTION] Overview: Austria vs Italy [/SECTION]
Austria and Italy are two European countries with distinct cultural and economic profiles. Austria, with its strong economy and high standard of living, tends to be more expensive than Italy, which has a more mixed economy and a lower cost of living. The main difference in costs between the two countries lies in the prices of housing, food, and transportation. While Austria offers a high quality of life and excellent infrastructure, Italy provides a more affordable and relaxed lifestyle.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Austria has a strong and stable economy, with a high GDP per capita and low unemployment rate. Italy, on the other hand, has a more complex economy, with a lower GDP per capita and higher unemployment rate. However, Italy's economy has been growing in recent years, and the country offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that makes it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. Overall, Italy is generally cheaper than Austria, with a difference of around 10-20% in overall cost of living.
