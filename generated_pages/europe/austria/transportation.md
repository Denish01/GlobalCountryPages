# Getting Around Austria — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Austria, you can choose from a variety of transportation options, including a well-developed rail network, buses, domestic flights, and car rentals, making it easy to explore the country's cities and scenic countryside.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Austria is easily accessible by air, with two main international airports: Vienna International Airport (VIE) and Salzburg Airport (SZG). Vienna International Airport is a major hub with direct flights to many European cities, as well as some long-haul destinations. You can also enter Austria by land, with several overland entry points along its borders with neighboring countries, including Germany, the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary, Slovenia, Italy, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein. The country's strategic location in the heart of Europe makes it a convenient stopover or destination for travelers.

If you prefer to travel by train, you can reach Austria by rail from many European cities, including major hubs like Munich, Zurich, and Prague. The country's rail network is well-connected to the rest of Europe, with frequent trains to and from nearby cities. Additionally, you can also enter Austria by bus, with several companies operating routes from neighboring countries. Many tourists also choose to drive to Austria, taking advantage of the country's well-maintained roads and scenic routes.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Austria has a moderate domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights between major cities. The main domestic airlines are Austrian Airlines and People's Viennaline, which offer flights between Vienna, Salzburg, Innsbruck, and other smaller airports. You can book domestic flights online or through a travel agent, with approximate costs ranging from $50 to $200, depending on the route and time of year. It's worth noting that domestic flights can be a convenient option for traveling between cities, especially if you're short on time.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Austria has an extensive rail network, with frequent trains connecting major cities and towns. The country's rail network is operated by ÖBB (Austrian Federal Railways), which offers a range of services, including high-speed trains and scenic routes. You can book train tickets online or at train stations, with prices varying depending on the route and class of service. High-speed trains, such as the Railjet, operate on several routes, including Vienna to Salzburg and Innsbruck. The booking process is relatively straightforward, and you can choose from a range of ticket options, including single tickets, day passes, and multi-day passes.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Austria has a well-developed bus network, with several companies operating intercity routes. The main bus companies are FlixBus, Eurolines, and ÖBB Postbus, which offer frequent services between major cities and towns. You can book bus tickets online or at bus stations, with prices starting from around $10. The quality of the buses is generally good, with comfortable seats and onboard amenities like Wi-Fi and restrooms.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Austria, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- Road conditions in Austria are generally good, with well-maintained highways and scenic routes.
- Fuel costs in Austria are relatively high, with prices starting from around $1.50 per liter.
- Car rental companies are widely available, including major brands like Hertz, Avis, and Europcar, with prices starting from around $40 per day.
- When driving in Austria, be aware of potential hazards like narrow mountain roads and limited parking in city centers.
- It's also worth noting that some mountain roads may be closed during winter months due to snow and ice.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When using public transport, make sure to validate your ticket before boarding, as fines can be steep for unvalidated tickets.
- Consider purchasing a **Vorteilscard**, which offers discounts on public transport and attractions.
- Download the **ÖBB** app for easy booking and management of train tickets.
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
- When driving, always carry a **high-visibility vest** and a **first-aid kit** in your vehicle.
- Consider purchasing a **toll sticker** (Vignette) for driving on Austria's highways.
- When using ride-hailing apps, always check the estimated fare and arrival time before confirming your ride.
- Be mindful of **bike lanes** and pedestrian zones in city centers, and always follow local traffic rules and regulations.
