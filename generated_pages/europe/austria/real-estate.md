[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The Austrian property market is known for its stability and growth, driven by a strong economy, low unemployment, and a high standard of living. Currently, the market is experiencing a moderate growth phase, with prices increasing steadily over the past few years. Urban areas like Vienna and Salzburg tend to be more expensive than rural regions, with prices per square meter ranging from $4,000 to over $10,000. The trend is expected to continue, with a focus on sustainable and energy-efficient properties. 

The market can be divided into two main segments: urban and rural. Urban areas like Vienna, with its rich cultural heritage and vibrant lifestyle, tend to attract more buyers and renters, while rural regions offer a more relaxed pace of life and lower prices. The **housing market** in Austria is characterized by a mix of old and new buildings, with many historic properties being renovated and modernized to meet modern standards.

In recent years, there has been an increase in demand for **sustainable properties**, with features like solar panels, green roofs, and energy-efficient appliances becoming more popular. This trend is expected to continue, driven by government incentives and growing environmental awareness. Overall, the Austrian property market offers a range of opportunities for buyers and investors, from modern apartments in city centers to traditional chalets in the countryside.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners can buy property in Austria, but they may need to obtain a **permit** from the relevant authorities, depending on the type of property and their nationality.
- EU citizens do not need a permit, but non-EU citizens may need to apply for one, which can take several months to be processed.
- There are some restrictions on buying **agricultural land** or **forestry properties**, which require special permits and may be subject to certain conditions.
- Foreigners can also buy property through a **company structure**, which can provide more flexibility and tax benefits.
- In some cases, **leasehold** arrangements may be available, which can be a more affordable option for foreigners who want to use a property without buying it outright.

10. **Obtain a **habitation certificate** if the property is a new build or has been renovated.

[SECTION] Rental Market [/SECTION]
- **Tenant rights** are protected by law, and tenants have a strong position in the rental market.
- **Lease terms** are typically long-term, ranging from 3 to 5 years.
- **Deposits** are usually equivalent to 2-3 months' rent.
- **Furnished** properties are less common than unfurnished ones, but can be found in urban areas.
- **Rent control** measures are in place to protect tenants from excessive rent increases.
