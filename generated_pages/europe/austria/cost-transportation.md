# Transportation Costs in Austria

The cost of transportation in Austria can vary depending on the mode of transport, with a monthly transit pass costing around $73 USD, while a taxi start rate is approximately $3 USD.

[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Austria has a well-developed and efficient transportation system, with a wide range of options available for getting around. The main transportation modes in Austria include buses, trams, metro lines, and commuter trains, with most people relying on public transportation to get around. The quality of public transit in Austria is generally very high, with frequent and reliable services connecting major cities and towns. In Vienna, the capital city, a comprehensive network of buses, trams, and metro lines makes it easy to get around without a car.

Austria's public transportation system is also very affordable, with a range of ticket options available to suit different needs and budgets. Many people in Austria use a **Vorteilscard**, which is a discount card that offers reduced fares on public transportation. The card can be purchased for a one-time fee and is valid for one year. In addition to public transportation, Austria also has a well-developed network of bike paths and bike-sharing systems, making it easy to get around by bike.
