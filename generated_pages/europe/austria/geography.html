Location & Borders

Austria is a sovereign country located in Central Europe, bordered by eight countries: Germany, Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary, Slovenia, Italy, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein. It is situated in the eastern part of the European continent, with no direct access to the sea, making it a landlocked country. The country's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe has made it an important hub for trade, culture, and politics throughout history.

Austria's borders are defined by its neighboring countries, with the Danube River forming a natural border with Slovakia and Hungary. The country's terrain is characterized by a mix of mountains, forests, and plains, with the Alps covering a significant portion of its western and southern regions. The Austrian landscape is dotted with picturesque villages, towns, and cities, including the capital Vienna, which is a major cultural and economic center in Europe.

Terrain & Landscape

Austria's terrain is diverse and varied, with the Alps covering about 60% of the country's land area. The Austrian Alps are home to some of the highest peaks in Europe, including the Grossglockner, which is the country's highest point at 3,798 meters. The Alps are also home to numerous glaciers, valleys, and mountain lakes, making them a popular destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts. The eastern part of the country is characterized by a flat to rolling plains region, known as the Pannonian Basin, which is bounded by the Danube River to the north and the Alps to the south.

In addition to the Alps and the Pannonian Basin, Austria is also home to several major rivers, including the Danube, the Inn, and the Salzach. These rivers have played a significant role in shaping the country's landscape and have provided a source of water, transportation, and livelihood for its inhabitants throughout history. Austria's forests cover about 47% of the country's land area, making them an important part of its ecosystem and a popular destination for hiking and outdoor activities.

The country's diverse terrain and landscape have also given rise to a wide range of ecosystems, including alpine meadows, montane forests, and lowland plains. These ecosystems support a rich variety of flora and fauna, including many species that are found nowhere else in Europe. Austria's unique landscape and biodiversity have made it a popular destination for nature lovers, hikers, and scientists.

Best Time To Visit

Best months: May to September. Why: The weather is mild and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and sightseeing. Avoid: January and February, as they are the coldest months, with temperatures often below freezing and snowfall common in the mountains.

Natural Highlights

  • The Hohe Tauern National Park is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and one of the largest national parks in Europe, covering an area of over 1,800 square kilometers.
  • The Grossglockner High Alpine Road is a scenic road that passes through the Austrian Alps and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers.
  • The Wachau Valley is a picturesque valley along the Danube River, known for its stunning scenery, historic villages, and excellent wine production.
  • The Dachstein Mountains are a popular destination for skiing, hiking, and paragliding, with their rugged peaks and stunning scenery.
  • The Neusiedler See is a large lake in eastern Austria, known for its unique steppe ecosystem and diverse wildlife, including many species of birds and fish.
  • The Kalkalpen National Park is a protected area in the Austrian Alps, known for its stunning scenery, diverse wildlife, and unique limestone rocks and caves.
