[SECTION] Overview: Austria vs Hungary [/SECTION]
Austria and Hungary are two neighboring countries in Central Europe, with distinct economic profiles. Austria has a strong and stable economy, with a high standard of living and a GDP per capita of over $53,000. Hungary, on the other hand, has a more mixed economy, with a lower GDP per capita of around $34,000. As a result, the cost of living in Austria is generally higher than in Hungary, with prices for housing, food, and transportation tend to be more expensive. However, both countries offer a high quality of life, with rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

The cost of living difference between Austria and Hungary is significant, with Austria being around 20-30% more expensive in many categories. This is reflected in the prices of everyday items, such as housing, food, and transportation. However, Hungary offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and other living expenses. This makes Hungary an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a more affordable lifestyle without sacrificing too much in terms of quality of life.
