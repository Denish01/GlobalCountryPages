[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Moldova's internet quality is generally good, with an average download speed of 25 Mbps, making it suitable for streaming, browsing, and other online activities. However, there is a notable urban-rural divide, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in cities like Chisinau, compared to rural areas where speeds can be slower and more intermittent. The internet infrastructure in Moldova is relatively well-developed, with a good level of reliability, although occasional outages can occur, especially during severe weather conditions or maintenance work.

The internet in Moldova is widely available, with many cafes, restaurants, and hotels offering free WiFi, and mobile network coverage is good, with all major carriers offering 4G services. However, the quality of the internet can vary depending on the location and the provider, and some areas may experience slower speeds or more frequent outages. Overall, Moldova's internet is suitable for most users, including tourists, remote workers, and businesses, although some may need to take extra precautions to ensure a stable and secure connection.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Moldova, you can visit a store of one of the major carriers, such as Orange, Moldtelecom, or Moldcell, and purchase a tourist SIM card, which typically costs around 10 USD. You will need to provide a valid passport and fill out a registration form, and the SIM card will be activated immediately. The major carriers offer a range of data plans, including tourist-friendly options with affordable rates and generous data allowances. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from a official store or a reputable dealer to avoid any issues with the card's activation or functionality.
