Moldova is a landlocked country located in Eastern Europe, bordered by Romania to the west and Ukraine to the north, east, and south. It is situated in the region of Bessarabia, between the Carpathian Mountains and the Dniester River. Moldova is a strategic location, connecting Europe to the Black Sea and the Caucasus region. The country's borders are relatively straight, with a total length of approximately 1,885 kilometers.

The country's location has played a significant role in its history, with various empires and powers vying for control over the region. Today, Moldova is an independent nation, with a diverse culture and economy. The capital city, Chisinau, is located in the central part of the country and is the largest city in Moldova. The country's proximity to the Black Sea and the Danube River has made it an important trade route, with many goods passing through its borders.
