Location & Borders

Moldova is a landlocked country located in Eastern Europe, bordered by Romania to the west and Ukraine to the north, east, and south. It is situated in the region of Bessarabia, between the Carpathian Mountains and the Dniester River. Moldova is a strategic location, connecting Europe to the Black Sea and the Caucasus region. The country's borders are relatively straight, with a total length of approximately 1,885 kilometers.

The country's location has played a significant role in its history, with various empires and powers vying for control over the region. Today, Moldova is an independent nation, with a diverse culture and economy. The capital city, Chisinau, is located in the central part of the country and is the largest city in Moldova. The country's proximity to the Black Sea and the Danube River has made it an important trade route, with many goods passing through its borders.

Terrain & Landscape

Moldova's terrain is characterized by rolling hills, forests, and plains. The country's landscape is dominated by the Dniester River, which forms the border with Ukraine to the east. The Prut River forms the border with Romania to the west. The country's highest point, Balanesti Hill, is located in the central part of the country and has an elevation of 430 meters. The terrain is generally flat, with some hills and valleys, making it suitable for agriculture.

The country's landscape is also dominated by forests, which cover approximately 10% of the country's land area. The Codrii Plaiului forest is one of the largest forests in Moldova, covering an area of over 90,000 hectares. The forest is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including oak, beech, and pine trees, as well as wild boar, deer, and wolves. The country's terrain and landscape have made it an important location for trade and commerce, with many routes passing through its borders.

The country's terrain is also home to several lakes and reservoirs, including the Dniester Reservoir, which is one of the largest reservoirs in Eastern Europe. The reservoir is an important source of hydroelectric power and is also used for irrigation and fishing. The country's terrain and landscape have made it an attractive destination for tourists, with many opportunities for hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching.
