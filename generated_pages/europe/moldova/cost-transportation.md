[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Moldova has a relatively well-developed transportation system, with buses, minibuses, and trains connecting major cities and towns. The quality of public transit varies, with some cities having more frequent and reliable services than others. In Chisinau, the capital city, there is a comprehensive network of buses and trolleybuses, making it easy to get around without a car. Most people in Moldova rely on public transportation or walk or cycle for short distances, as the country's roads can be congested and parking spaces limited.

The main transportation modes in Moldova include buses, minibuses, and trains, with buses being the most popular and affordable option. The country's road network is relatively well-maintained, but traffic congestion can be a problem in urban areas. Many people in Moldova also use ride-hailing apps, such as Yandex.Taxi and Bolt, which are widely available and often cheaper than traditional taxis. For longer distances, trains and buses are the most common options, with several daily services connecting major cities and towns.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use public transportation or walk/cycle for short distances to save money and avoid traffic congestion.
- Download ride-hailing apps, such as Yandex.Taxi and Bolt, for convenient and affordable transportation.
- Negotiate taxi fares before starting your journey, as not all taxis have meters.
- Consider renting a car for longer distances or rural areas, where public transportation may be limited.
- Be aware of traffic rules and regulations, as well as potential **scams** or **overcharging** by taxi drivers or ride-hailing services.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps to ensure a safe and reliable journey.
