# Education Costs in Moldova

The cost of education in Moldova varies depending on the type of institution and the level of education, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Moldova's education system is divided into public and private institutions, with the public sector being the largest provider of education. The quality of public education is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on **Romanian language** and literature, as well as **mathematics** and **science**. However, the quality of education can vary depending on the region and the specific school, with schools in urban areas tend to be better equipped and staffed than those in rural areas. Private schools, on the other hand, tend to offer a more **international curriculum** and may have better facilities and more experienced teachers, but can be expensive.

The language of instruction in Moldova is **Romanian**, but some schools may also offer **Russian** or **English** as a second language. Compulsory education in Moldova lasts for 12 years, from age 6 to 18, and is divided into primary, secondary, and high school. International schools are also available in Moldova, particularly in the capital city of Chisinau, and offer a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **British A-levels**. These schools cater to the children of expats and diplomats, as well as to Moldovan students who wish to pursue an international education.
