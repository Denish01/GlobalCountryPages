# Moldova Politics & Government — System, Leaders & Stability

Moldova is governed as a **parliamentary representative democratic republic**, with a system that divides power among the executive, legislative, and judicial branches.

[SECTION] Political System [/SECTION]
Moldova's government is based on the principle of **separation of powers**, with the executive, legislative, and judicial branches each having distinct roles and responsibilities. The **executive branch** is headed by the **President**, who serves as the head of state, while the **Prime Minister** is the head of government. The **legislative branch**, known as the **Parliament**, is a unicameral body composed of 101 members elected by proportional representation. The **judicial branch** is independent and composed of the **Supreme Court of Justice** and lower courts. Moldova is a **unitary state**, with a centralized government and no federal divisions.

The government of Moldova is responsible for making and enforcing laws, collecting taxes, and providing public services such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure. The **separation of powers** is designed to prevent any one branch of government from becoming too powerful, and to ensure that each branch provides checks and balances on the others. This system is intended to promote **accountability**, **transparency**, and **stability** in the government.

Moldova's **political system** is also characterized by a **multi-party system**, with several major parties competing for power and representation in the Parliament. This system allows for a diversity of views and opinions to be represented, and provides a framework for **peaceful transfer of power** and **democratic governance**.

[SECTION] Elections [/SECTION]
- Moldova has a **proportional representation** electoral system, with voters casting ballots for party lists.
- **Parliamentary elections** are held every four years, with the most recent election taking place in 2021.
- The **presidential election** is held every four years, with the most recent election taking place in 2020.
- **Voter participation** is generally high, with over 50% of eligible voters casting ballots in recent elections.

[SECTION] Political Stability & Challenges [/SECTION]
- Moldova's **political climate** is characterized by ongoing debates over **European integration** and **relations with Russia**.
- **Corruption** remains a significant challenge, with widespread bribery and nepotism.
- The **economy** is heavily dependent on **agriculture** and **remittances**, making it vulnerable to external shocks.
- **Regional dynamics** are influenced by the **Transnistria conflict**, a frozen conflict with a breakaway region that has been ongoing since the early 1990s.
- Moldova's **freedom indices** are generally positive, with a **Freedom House** rating of "partly free" and a **Human Development Index** ranking of 90 out of 189 countries.
- The country faces **energy security** challenges, with a high dependence on **Russian natural gas** imports.

[SECTION] Foreign Relations [/SECTION]
- Moldova is a member of the **United Nations**, the **Council of Europe**, and the **World Trade Organization**.
- The country has **association agreements** with the **European Union** and the **European Free Trade Association**.
- Moldova participates in the **Eastern Partnership** program, which aims to promote economic and political cooperation with the EU.
- The country has **diplomatic relations** with over 100 countries, including the **United States**, **Russia**, and **China**.
- Moldova's **foreign policy** is focused on **European integration**, **regional security**, and **economic development**.
