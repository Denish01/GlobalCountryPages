# Transportation Costs in Sweden

The cost of transportation in Sweden varies depending on the mode of transport, with a monthly transit pass costing around $100-$150 USD, while a single ticket can cost between $3-$5 USD.

[FACTBOX]

Ride-Hailing Available: (Yes — Uber, Bolt)

[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Sweden has a well-developed transportation system, with a strong focus on public transportation, cycling, and walking. The country's capital, Stockholm, has a comprehensive network of buses, trams, and metro lines, making it easy to get around without a car. In fact, many Swedes prefer to use public transportation, cycle, or walk, as it is often faster and more convenient than driving. The quality of public transit in Sweden is generally high, with frequent and reliable services, although prices can be steep for visitors.

Most people in Sweden get around using public transportation, with many cities having their own systems. The **public transportation** network is well-integrated, allowing for easy travel between cities and towns. In addition to public transportation, many Swedes also use **ride-hailing apps** or **taxis** to get around, especially at night or in areas with limited public transportation. For longer distances, **trains** and **domestic flights** are popular options, with many routes connecting major cities.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider buying a **monthly transit pass** for unlimited travel on public transportation.
- Use **ride-hailing apps** or **taxis** at night or in areas with limited public transportation.
- Be aware of **peak hour** prices for public transportation, which can be higher than off-peak prices.
- Use **cashless payment** options, such as credit cards or mobile payments, for convenience and security.
- Download **transportation apps**, such as SL (Stockholm) or Västtrafik (Gothenburg), for route planning and ticket purchasing.
- Be mindful of **bike lanes** and **pedestrian zones** when driving or walking in Sweden, as they are heavily promoted and enforced.
