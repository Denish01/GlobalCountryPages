# Netherlands Infrastructure — Roads, Airports, Electricity & Water

The infrastructure in Netherlands is well-developed, with a strong focus on **sustainability** and **innovation**, providing a high level of access to basic services such as electricity, water, and transportation.

[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
The Netherlands has a highly developed infrastructure, with significant investments in recent years to improve the **efficiency** and **reliability** of its systems. The country's **urban-rural gap** is relatively small, with even rural areas having access to good quality roads, reliable electricity, and clean water. The government has also been investing in **green infrastructure**, such as wind farms and green roofs, to reduce the country's **carbon footprint**. Overall, the Netherlands' infrastructure is considered to be one of the best in the world, with a high level of **service quality** and **customer satisfaction**.

The Netherlands' infrastructure is also highly **integrated**, with a strong focus on **multimodal transport** and **smart cities**. The country's **transport hubs**, such as Amsterdam Schiphol Airport and the Port of Rotterdam, are among the busiest and most efficient in the world. The government has also been investing in **digital infrastructure**, such as **fiber-optic networks** and **5G mobile networks**, to support the country's growing **tech industry**.

The Netherlands' infrastructure is also highly **resilient**, with a strong focus on **disaster risk reduction** and **climate change adaptation**. The country's **flood protection systems**, such as the **Delta Works**, are among the most advanced in the world, and have protected the country from numerous **floods** and **storms** over the years.
