Overall Safety Assessment

The Netherlands is generally a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other European countries. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around cities and towns, and the country has a strong reputation for being welcoming and tolerant. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take some basic precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and bike theft can occur, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, visitors should be aware of the potential for scams, particularly in larger cities like Amsterdam.

Overall, the Netherlands is a great destination for travelers, with a strong focus on community and social cohesion. The country has a well-developed infrastructure and a high standard of living, which contributes to its safety and security. Visitors can feel confident exploring the country's many attractions, from the vibrant cities to the picturesque countryside. By taking some basic precautions and being aware of their surroundings, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to the Netherlands.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing in crowded areas, such as train stations and tourist hotspots
  • Bike theft is common, so make sure to lock your bike securely
  • Be aware of scams, such as people approaching you with fake petitions or charity collections
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or debit cards instead
  • Be cautious of overpriced or fake goods, especially in tourist areas
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas
  • Be cautious of ATMs and credit card skimming, and use secure machines

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to the Netherlands, but make sure you are up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
  • Water Safety: Tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is also widely available
  • Medical Facilities: The Netherlands has a high standard of medical care, with well-equipped hospitals and medical facilities
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available, and many are open late or 24 hours

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
  • Keep a close eye on your belongings, especially in tourist hotspots
  • Use a money belt or a secure bag to carry your valuables
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in dimly lit areas
  • Keep your hotel room door locked, and use the hotel safe if available
  • Be cautious of strangers approaching you, especially if they seem overly friendly or aggressive
  • Keep your bike locked securely, and use a good quality lock

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • The Netherlands is generally a very safe country for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong focus on equality
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas
  • Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, especially if you're traveling alone
  • Be cautious of strangers approaching you, especially if they seem overly friendly or aggressive
