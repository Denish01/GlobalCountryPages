[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
The Netherlands has a well-developed transportation network, with a strong focus on **cycling** and **public transportation**. The country's compact size and flat terrain make it ideal for cycling, with many cities having dedicated bike lanes and bike-sharing systems. Most people get around using public transportation, which includes buses, trams, and trains, with the **OV-chipkaart** being the primary payment method. The quality of public transit is generally high, with frequent and reliable services connecting major cities and towns.

The Netherlands is also known for its **canal network**, with many cities having water buses and ferries that provide an alternative mode of transportation. In addition, the country has a well-developed highway system, making it easy to get around by car. However, with the high cost of fuel and parking, as well as traffic congestion in major cities, many people opt for public transportation or cycling as a more convenient and affordable option.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
