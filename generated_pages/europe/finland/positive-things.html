Cultural Treasures

  • The Suomenlinna sea fortress, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a popular tourist destination and historic landmark.
  • The Kalevala, Finland's national epic, is a rich source of cultural heritage and inspiration for art and literature.
  • The Savonian dialect is a unique and colorful part of Finnish language and culture, with its own distinct grammar and vocabulary.
  • The Finnish sauna is a traditional and integral part of Finnish culture, with over 3 million saunas in the country.
  • The Helsinki Cathedral is a stunning example of neoclassical architecture and a iconic symbol of the city.

People & Global Impact

  • Linus Torvalds, the creator of the Linux operating system, is a famous Finnish computer scientist and programmer.
  • Tarja Halonen, Finland's first female president, is a respected global leader and advocate for human rights and gender equality.
  • The Finnish diaspora has made significant contributions to countries like the United States, Canada, and Australia, with many Finns settling and thriving abroad.

What Visitors Love

  • Finland's natural scenery, including its thousands of lakes, forests, and islands, is a major draw for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • The Finnish concept of "jokamiehenoikeudet" (everyman's rights) allows anyone to freely roam and forage in the countryside, making it easy to explore and enjoy nature.
  • Finnish cuisine, including dishes like karjalanpiirakka (Karelian pasties) and salmon soup, is a unique and delicious aspect of Finnish culture.
  • The Helsinki Market Square is a vibrant and bustling hub of activity, with fresh produce, handicrafts, and street food on offer.
  • Finnish hospitality, including the tradition of welcoming guests with coffee and treats, is renowned for its warmth and generosity.
  • The Midsummer festival, celebrated on the weekend closest to June 24th, is a magical and unforgettable experience, with bonfires, singing, and dancing.

Hidden Gems

  • The Koli National Park, located in eastern Finland, is a stunning and lesser-known destination for hiking, skiing, and nature photography.
  • The Finnish Archipelago, with its thousands of islands and islets, is a unique and fascinating region to explore by boat or kayak.
  • The Tampere region, known for its industrial heritage and cultural attractions, is a hidden gem for those interested in history, art, and design.
  • The Oulu region, with its stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities, is a great destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
