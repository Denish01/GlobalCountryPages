# Finland vs Sweden

When deciding between Finland and Sweden, consider your priorities, as both countries offer unique experiences, from stunning natural beauty to vibrant culture and history.

Finland and Sweden are often compared due to their geographical proximity, shared cultural heritage, and similar characteristics, making them popular destinations for travelers and expats alike. [SECTION] Key Differences [/SECTION]
- **Location**: Finland is located to the east of Sweden, bordering Russia, while Sweden is situated to the west, bordering Norway.
- **Language**: While both countries have English as a widely spoken language, Finnish is the primary language in Finland, whereas Swedish is the primary language in Sweden.
- **Currency**: Finland uses the Euro as its official currency, whereas Sweden uses the Swedish Krona.
- **Climate**: Finland has a subarctic climate, with colder winters and shorter summers, whereas Sweden has a temperate climate, with milder winters and longer summers.
- **Cost of Living**: Finland and Sweden both have a high cost of living, but the cost of living in Finland is slightly lower, especially when it comes to housing and transportation.
- **Cuisine**: Finnish cuisine is known for its hearty dishes, such as karjalanpiirakka and salmiakki, whereas Swedish cuisine is famous for its seafood, meatballs, and pastries.

[SECTION] Key Similarities [/SECTION]
- **Nature**: Both Finland and Sweden are known for their stunning natural beauty, with numerous lakes, forests, and mountains to explore.
- **Design**: Both countries have a strong focus on design, with Finland being famous for its minimalist and functional design, while Sweden is known for its sleek and modern design.
- **Education**: Both countries have a high-quality education system, with a strong emphasis on innovation, technology, and social welfare.
- **Social Welfare**: Both Finland and Sweden have a strong social welfare system, with a high level of social security, healthcare, and unemployment benefits.
