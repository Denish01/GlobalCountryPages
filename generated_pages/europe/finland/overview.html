What Is Finland?

Finland is a sovereign country located in Northern Europe, bordering the Baltic Sea, the Gulf of Finland, and the Arctic Circle. It shares borders with Sweden to the west, Norway to the north, and Russia to the east. With a population of 5.6 million people, Finland is a relatively small country, but it is known for its stunning natural beauty, with thousands of lakes, forests, and islands. The country is also famous for its vibrant culture, innovative design, and high standard of living.

Finland is a parliamentary republic, with a government that is divided into three branches: the legislative, executive, and judicial. The country has a strong economy, with a high-tech industry, timber and paper production, and a significant service sector. Finland is also a member of the European Union, the Eurozone, and the Schengen Area, which has facilitated travel and trade with other European countries.

Finland is known for its unique cultural heritage, which is reflected in its Finnish and Swedish languages, as well as its traditional music, dance, and handicrafts. The country is also home to a number of world-class museums, galleries, and festivals, which showcase its rich cultural history and contemporary art scene. From the stunning natural beauty of Lapland to the vibrant city life of Helsinki, Finland is a country that has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

Key History

  1. 1155 - Sweden establishes its rule over Finland, which lasts for nearly 700 years.
  2. 1809 - Finland becomes a Grand Duchy of Russia, with the Russian tsar as its head of state.
  3. 1917 - Finland declares its independence from Russia, following the Russian Revolution.
  4. 1939-1940 - Finland fights against the Soviet Union in the Winter War, which results in the loss of some of its territory.
  5. 1947 - Finland signs the Treaty of Paris, which establishes its post-war borders and relationship with the Soviet Union.
  6. 1995 - Finland joins the European Union, which has a significant impact on its economy and politics.
  7. 2002 - Finland adopts the euro as its official currency, replacing the Finnish markka.

What Makes Finland Unique

  • Finland is home to over 70,000 lakes, which cover about 30% of its surface area.
  • The country has a unique concept of "jokamiehenoikeus", or "everyman's right", which allows anyone to roam freely in the countryside, pick berries and mushrooms, and camp in the wilderness.
  • Finland is known for its high-quality education system, which is consistently ranked as one of the best in the world.
  • The country has a vibrant design scene, with famous brands like Marimekko and Iittala, and a strong tradition of innovation and technology.
  • Finland is home to the Northern Lights, or Aurora Borealis, which can be seen in the northern parts of the country during the winter months.

Quick Summary

Finland is a sovereign country in Northern Europe, known for its stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and high standard of living, with a unique blend of traditional and modern elements that make it a fascinating destination for travelers and a great place to live.
