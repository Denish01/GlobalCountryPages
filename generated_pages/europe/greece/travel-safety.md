[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Greece is generally a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other European countries. The majority of tourists visit Greece without encountering any problems, and the country has a strong reputation for hospitality. However, as with any popular tourist destination, petty crime and scams can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as being mindful of their surroundings, keeping valuables secure, and avoiding walking alone in isolated areas at night.

Greece has experienced some social and economic challenges in recent years, including austerity measures and refugee influxes, but these have not significantly impacted the overall safety of tourists. In fact, the Greek government has taken steps to increase security and improve tourist infrastructure, including the establishment of a dedicated tourist police force. With some basic precautions and awareness of potential risks, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Greece.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be wary of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, particularly in Athens and Thessaloniki
- Be cautious of **scams** involving **ATM skimming** and **credit card theft**, particularly in tourist areas
- Watch out for **overcharging** by taxi drivers and **unofficial tour guides**
- Be aware of **protests** and **demonstrations**, which can occur in Athens and other major cities
- Be careful when **driving** in Greece, as roads can be narrow and winding, and drivers can be aggressive
- Be mindful of **sun and heat exposure**, particularly in the summer months
- Be aware of **sea and water safety**, particularly when swimming or participating in water sports

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Greece, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are generally of high quality, particularly in major cities like Athens and Thessaloniki, but may be limited in rural areas
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available and well-stocked, particularly in tourist areas

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **copy of your passport** and **travel documents**
- Keep **valuables secure** and consider using a **money belt** or **hotel safe**
- Be **mindful of your surroundings**, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Avoid **walking alone** in isolated areas at night
- Use **reputable taxi services** and **agree on a price** before starting your journey
- Be **cautious when using ATMs** and **keep your credit card information secure**
- Stay **informed** about local conditions and **follow local advice**

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Greece is generally a **safe country** for solo and women travelers, but take normal precautions to protect yourself and your belongings
- Be **mindful of your surroundings**, particularly at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Consider **staying in well-lit and populated areas**, particularly in major cities like Athens and Thessaloniki
- Be **cautious when interacting with strangers**, particularly in tourist areas, and avoid accepting **unsolicited offers** or **rides** from unknown individuals
