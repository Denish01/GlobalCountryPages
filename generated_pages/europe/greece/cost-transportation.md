[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Greece has a well-developed transportation network, with a range of options available for getting around, including public transportation, taxis, ride-hailing services, and car rentals. The quality of public transit in Greece is generally good, with frequent and reliable bus and metro services in major cities like Athens and Thessaloniki. Most people in Greece get around using public transportation, with buses and metro lines connecting all parts of the city and suburbs. Additionally, many Greeks own cars, and driving is a popular mode of transportation, especially for longer distances.

The scenic landscape and picturesque villages of Greece make it a great destination for exploring by car or on foot. However, traffic in major cities can be heavy, especially during rush hour, and parking can be a challenge. To avoid traffic congestion, many locals use public transportation or walk or cycle whenever possible. With a little planning and research, visitors can easily navigate Greece's transportation network and enjoy all that the country has to offer.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a monthly transit pass or using ride-hailing apps.
- Use the **Beat** or **TaxiBeat** apps to book ride-hailing services, which are widely available in major cities.
- Be sure to negotiate the fare with the taxi driver before you start your journey, especially if you're traveling to the airport or a long distance.
- Always use the meter in a taxi, and make sure it's turned on at the start of your journey.
- Consider renting a car for longer distances or for exploring rural areas, but be aware that driving in Greece can be challenging, especially on narrow roads.
- Download the **Moovit** app for public transportation information and route planning, which covers many cities in Greece.
