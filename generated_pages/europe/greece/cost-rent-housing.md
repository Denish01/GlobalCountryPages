# Rent & Housing Prices in Greece

The cost of rent in Greece varies depending on the location, with average monthly rents for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center ranging from $400-$700 USD (approximately €350-€600 EUR), while outside the city center, prices range from $250-$450 USD (approximately €220-€390 EUR).

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
Greece's rental market is relatively stable, with a mix of short-term and long-term leases available. Typical lease terms range from 12 to 24 months, with a security deposit of one to two months' rent required. Tenant rights are protected by law, with rent increases capped at 10% per year. Compared to its regional neighbors, Greece's housing costs are relatively moderate, with Albania and North Macedonia offering more affordable options, while Bulgaria and Turkey have similar price ranges. However, Greece's unique cultural and historical attractions, as well as its **tourist infrastructure**, make it a popular destination for expats and tourists alike.

The Greek housing market has experienced a gradual recovery since the economic crisis, with **property prices** increasing steadily over the past few years. However, the market remains competitive, with a wide range of options available for renters and buyers. The **Golden Visa program**, which offers residency in exchange for property investment, has also attracted foreign investors to the Greek market. As a result, the housing market is expected to continue growing, with a focus on **sustainable development** and **energy efficiency**.

[SECTION] Renter Tips for Greece [/SECTION]
- Research neighborhoods thoroughly to find the best fit for your budget and lifestyle
- Work with a reputable real estate agent to find reliable landlords and avoid scams
- Be prepared to negotiate rent and amenities, especially for long-term leases
- Utilities are usually not included in the rent, so factor in additional costs for electricity, water, and internet
- Furnished apartments are common, but unfurnished options are also available, especially for long-term rentals
- Be aware of the **tourist season**, which can affect rental prices and availability, especially in popular areas like Mykonos and Santorini
