[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Greece is a sovereign country located in southeastern Europe, situated on the southern tip of the Balkan Peninsula. It borders Albania to the northwest, North Macedonia to the north, Bulgaria to the northeast, and Turkey to the east. The country is surrounded by several bodies of water, including the Aegean Sea to the east, the Ionian Sea to the west, and the Mediterranean Sea to the south. The strategic location of Greece has made it an important crossroads between Europe, Asia, and Africa, with a rich history and cultural heritage.

Greece's unique geography has played a significant role in shaping its history, culture, and economy. The country's mountainous terrain and numerous islands have made it difficult to traverse, but have also provided a natural defense against invaders. The borders of Greece have been shaped by its complex history, with the country having been occupied by various empires and civilizations over the centuries. Today, Greece is a member of the European Union and the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO), and its borders are an important part of its national identity.

The mountains are covered in dense forests, and are home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including bears, wolves, and eagles. The highest peak in Greece is **Mount Olympus**, which is located in the north of the country and is the mythical home of the gods in Greek mythology.

In addition to its mountains, Greece is also home to a number of rivers, including the **Achelous River**, which is the longest river in the country and flows into the Ionian Sea. The country's coastal plains are fertile and well-suited to agriculture, and are home to a number of important crops, including olives, grapes, and citrus fruits. Greece is also home to a number of islands, including **Crete**, **Rhodes**, and **Corfu**, which are popular tourist destinations and are known for their beautiful beaches, charming villages, and rich cultural heritage.

The Greek landscape is also characterized by its many **caves** and **gorges**, which are found throughout the country and are a popular destination for hikers and adventure seekers. The **Vikos Gorge**, which is located in the north of the country, is one of the deepest and most beautiful gorges in the world, and is a must-see destination for anyone interested in nature and the outdoors. Overall, the terrain and landscape of Greece are a key part of its national identity, and are an important part of what makes the country such a unique and fascinating place to visit.
