[SECTION] What Is Hungary? [/SECTION]
Hungary is a **sovereign** country located in Central Europe, bordered by seven countries: Austria, Slovakia, Ukraine, Romania, Serbia, Croatia, and Slovenia. With a population of 9.6 million people, Hungary is a relatively small country, but it has a rich history and culture. The country is known for its stunning **capital city**, Budapest, which is a popular tourist destination due to its beautiful architecture, thermal baths, and vibrant cultural scene. Hungary is also famous for its **cuisine**, which includes dishes such as goulash, paprikás csirke, and halászlé.

Hungary has a strategic geographic position, with the **Danube River** flowing through the country and the **Carpathian Mountains** located in the north. The country has a diverse landscape, with plains, hills, and mountains, making it an attractive destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Hungary is also home to several **UNESCO World Heritage Sites**, including the Budapest Parliament Building, the Buda Castle, and the Hollókő village.

Hungary is a member of the **European Union** and the **NATO** alliance, and it has a growing economy, with a strong focus on **industry**, **agriculture**, and **tourism**. The country has a high standard of living, with good healthcare and education systems, and it is considered one of the safest countries in Europe.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 895 - The **Magyars**, a nomadic people from Central Asia, arrive in the Carpathian Basin and establish the Hungarian state.
2. 1000 - Hungary becomes a **Christian kingdom**, with King Stephen I crowned as the first king of Hungary.
3. 1526 - The **Ottoman Empire** conquers Hungary, and the country is divided into three parts: the Ottoman Empire, the Habsburg Empire, and the Principality of Transylvania.
4. 1848 - The **Hungarian Revolution** breaks out, with the aim of establishing an independent Hungarian state.
5. 1989 - Hungary becomes a **democratic country**, with the fall of communism and the establishment of a parliamentary republic.
6. 2004 - Hungary joins the **European Union**, along with nine other countries.
7. 2011 - Hungary adopts a new **constitution**, which establishes the country as a parliamentary republic with a president as head of state.

[SECTION] What Makes Hungary Unique [/SECTION]
- Hungary is home to the largest **synagogue** in Europe, the Great Synagogue in Budapest.
- The country has a unique **language**, Hungarian, which is not related to any other language in Europe.
- Hungary is famous for its **thermal baths**, with over 1,000 natural springs and numerous spas.
- The country has a rich **folk culture**, with traditional music, dance, and crafts.
- Hungary is home to several **world-class museums**, including the Hungarian National Museum and the Museum of Fine Arts.
