# Transportation Costs in Hungary

The cost of transportation in Hungary can vary depending on the mode of transport, with a monthly transit pass costing around $23 USD and a taxi start rate of approximately $2.50 USD.

[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Hungary has a well-developed transportation system, with a variety of modes available to get around the country. The most common modes of transportation are **public transportation**, including buses, trams, and trains, which are efficient and affordable. Most people in Hungary use public transportation to get around, with many cities having a comprehensive network of buses and trams. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent departures and reliable services.

In addition to public transportation, **taxis** and **ride-hailing services** are also widely available, providing a convenient option for those who prefer a door-to-door service. For longer distances, **intercity buses** and **trains** are available, connecting major cities and towns across the country. Hungary is also a great country to explore by **car**, with a well-maintained road network and scenic routes. However, it's worth noting that **traffic** in larger cities like Budapest can be heavy, especially during rush hour.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a **monthly transit pass**, which can be used on all public transportation modes.
- Use **ride-hailing apps** like Bolt and Taxify for convenient door-to-door service.
- Always **negotiate the fare** before getting into a taxi, especially at night or for longer distances.
- Be aware of **traffic rules** and regulations, especially when driving in larger cities.
- Consider **cycling** as a mode of transportation, with many cities having bike-friendly infrastructure.
- Use **public transportation apps** like BKK Futár or Volánbusz to plan your route and track your journey.
