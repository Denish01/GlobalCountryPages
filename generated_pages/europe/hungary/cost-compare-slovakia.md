[SECTION] Overview: Hungary vs Slovakia [/SECTION]
Hungary and Slovakia are two neighboring countries in Central Europe, both offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. In terms of cost of living, Hungary is generally considered to be the more affordable option, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. The economic context of the two countries is similar, with both having a high-income economy and a strong service sector. However, Hungary's economy has been growing at a slower pace than Slovakia's in recent years, which may have contributed to the difference in cost of living.

The cost of living in Hungary is approximately 15-20% lower than in Slovakia, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees. The main areas where Hungary is cheaper are in accommodation, food, and transportation. For example, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center of Budapest can cost around $400-500 per month, while a similar apartment in Bratislava, the capital of Slovakia, can cost around $600-700 per month. Similarly, a meal at a mid-range restaurant in Hungary can cost around $10-15 per person, while in Slovakia it can cost around $15-20 per person.
