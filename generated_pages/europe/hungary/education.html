International Schools

  • International schools in Hungary offer a wide range of curricula, including International Baccalaureate (IB), British GCSE and A-level, and American high school diploma programs.
  • These schools are located in major cities, such as Budapest, Debrecen, and Szeged, and cater to the needs of expatriate families and Hungarian students who want to pursue an international education.
  • Tuition fees at international schools in Hungary vary widely, ranging from 2,000 to 10,000 USD per year, depending on the school and program.
  • Some international schools in Hungary also offer boarding facilities for students who come from other parts of the country or from abroad.
  • Examples of international schools in Hungary include the British International School, American International School, and International School of Budapest.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Hungary, foreign students need to obtain a student visa, which is issued by the Hungarian embassy or consulate in their home country.
  • Students who want to study in Hungary must also demonstrate a good command of the Hungarian language, although many programs are taught in English or other foreign languages.
  • Hungary offers a range of scholarships to foreign students, including the Stipendium Hungaricum program, which provides full scholarships to students from around the world.
  • Living costs in Hungary are relatively low compared to other European countries, with accommodation costs ranging from 200 to 500 USD per month.
  • Foreign students in Hungary can also work part-time to support themselves, with many universities offering part-time jobs on campus or in the local community.
  • Additionally, Hungary has a student health insurance system that provides medical coverage to foreign students.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • The education system in Hungary is known for its high quality and academic excellence, with many universities ranked among the top in the world.
  • However, the system also faces some challenges, including funding constraints and brain drain, as many Hungarian students choose to study abroad or work in other countries.
  • In recent years, the Hungarian government has implemented education reforms aimed at improving the quality of education and making it more relevant to the needs of the labor market.
  • One of the main challenges facing the education system in Hungary is the lack of digital skills among students and teachers, which can make it difficult for them to compete in the global economy.
  • To address these challenges, the Hungarian government has launched initiatives to promote digital education and provide students with the skills they need to succeed in the 21st century.
