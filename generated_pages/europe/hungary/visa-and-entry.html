Visa Policy Overview

Hungary has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others require a visa. As a member of the European Union and the Schengen Area, Hungary's visa policy is aligned with that of other EU countries. Citizens of over 60 countries can enter Hungary without a visa, including the United States, Canada, Australia, and the United Kingdom. However, the length of stay and requirements may vary depending on the nationality and purpose of the visit.

Hungary's visa policy aims to facilitate travel and tourism while maintaining national security and immigration control. The country has a well-developed visa system, with various types of visas available for different purposes, including tourism, business, study, and work. Visitors can stay in Hungary for up to 90 days within a 180-day period, depending on their nationality and the type of visa or visa waiver they hold.

Tourists: Tourists can stay in Hungary for up to 90 days within a 180-day period, depending on their nationality. They must have a valid passport, travel insurance, and proof of sufficient funds. Extensions are possible, but visitors must apply at the local immigration office.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may need to provide additional documents, such as an invitation letter from a Hungarian company, proof of business activities, and a valid passport. They may also need to register with the authorities if staying more than 30 days.
Students: Students must have a valid passport, proof of enrollment at a Hungarian university, and a student visa. The visa is usually issued for the duration of the study program, and students must register with the authorities within 30 days of arrival.
Workers: Workers must have a valid passport, a work permit, and a residence permit. The work permit is usually issued by the Hungarian labor authority, and the residence permit is issued by the local immigration office.

Hungary has several international airports, including Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport, which is the main entry point for most visitors. The country also has several land borders with neighboring countries, including Austria, Slovakia, Ukraine, Romania, Serbia, Croatia, and Slovenia. When arriving in Hungary, visitors must go through immigration control, where they will need to present their travel documents and answer questions about their stay. Visitors may also be required to register with the authorities if staying more than 30 days.
At the airport, visitors can expect to go through a relatively straightforward process, with separate lines for EU and non-EU citizens. At land borders, the process may be more complex, and visitors may need to provide additional documentation, such as proof of onward travel or insurance.
- Not registering with the authorities if staying more than 30 days
- Not having a valid passport or travel documents
- Not having sufficient funds or proof of onward travel
- Not having the correct type of visa or residence permit
- Not declaring goods or gifts at customs
- Not having health insurance that covers the entire stay
