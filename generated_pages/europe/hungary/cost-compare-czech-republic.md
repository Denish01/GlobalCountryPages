[SECTION] Overview: Hungary vs Czech Republic [/SECTION]
Hungary and the Czech Republic are two Central European countries with a rich history and culture, offering a unique blend of traditional and modern lifestyles. In terms of cost of living, Hungary is generally cheaper than the Czech Republic, with an overall cost of living that is around 15-20% lower. This is due to several factors, including lower housing costs, food prices, and transportation expenses. However, the Czech Republic has a more developed economy and a higher standard of living, which may appeal to some expats and tourists.

The economic context of both countries is also worth considering. Hungary has a growing economy, with a strong focus on tourism, manufacturing, and services. The Czech Republic, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy, with a strong presence of multinational companies and a highly developed IT sector. Despite these differences, both countries offer a relatively low cost of living compared to other European countries, making them attractive destinations for expats, students, and retirees.
