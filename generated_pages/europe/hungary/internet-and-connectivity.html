Internet Overview

Hungary has a well-developed internet infrastructure, with a high level of penetration and widespread availability of broadband connections. The average internet speed in Hungary is 24.51 Mbps, which is relatively fast compared to other European countries. The urban-rural divide is still present, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in cities like Budapest, Debrecen, and Szeged, while rural areas may experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. However, the Hungarian government has been investing heavily in expanding and upgrading the country's internet infrastructure, which has improved the overall quality and reliability of the internet.

The reliability of the internet in Hungary is generally good, with most providers offering stable and consistent connections. However, as with any country, outages and disruptions can occur, especially during severe weather events or maintenance periods. It's worth noting that Hungary has a well-developed fiber-optic network, which provides fast and reliable connections to many parts of the country. Additionally, the country has a number of internet exchange points, which help to improve the overall quality and speed of the internet.

Getting a SIM Card

Getting a SIM card in Hungary is relatively easy and straightforward, with a number of providers offering tourist-friendly plans and data packages. The main carriers in Hungary are Telenor, T-Mobile, and Vodafone, all of which offer a range of plans and packages to suit different needs and budgets. To get a SIM card, you will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and you can purchase SIM cards at most mobile phone stores, supermarkets, and newsstands. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds.

When choosing a SIM card, it's worth considering the data package and price, as well as any additional features or services that may be included. Some providers offer unlimited data packages, while others may have data caps or throttling. It's also worth checking the coverage and speeds in the areas you plan to visit, as some providers may have better coverage in certain areas.

WiFi Availability

  • Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels and hostels offering free and fast WiFi to their guests.
  • Cafe WiFi culture is widespread in Hungary, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers.
  • Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in Hungary, with many spaces offering fast and reliable WiFi, as well as other amenities like meeting rooms and printing facilities.
  • Public WiFi is available in many cities and towns, with many municipalities offering free WiFi in public areas like parks and squares.
  • Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi to customers, making it easy to stay connected while on the go.

For Remote Workers

  • Coworking spaces like Impact Hub and Kaptar offer fast and reliable WiFi, as well as other amenities like meeting rooms and printing facilities.
  • Reliable cafe chains like Costa Coffee and Starbucks offer free WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
  • Backup internet options like mobile hotspots and portable WiFi routers can be useful in case of an outage or disruption.
  • VPNs can be useful for remote workers who need to access sensitive or confidential information, as they provide an extra layer of security and encryption.
  • Many libraries and universities also offer free WiFi and a quiet working environment, making them a great option for remote workers.

Tips & Warnings

  • Internet censorship is relatively low in Hungary, but some websites and services may be blocked or restricted.
  • VPN use is recommended for sensitive or confidential activities, as they provide an extra layer of security and encryption.
  • Roaming alternatives like eSIM and mobile hotspots can be useful for travelers who need to stay connected while on the go.
  • It's worth checking the data package and price before purchasing a SIM card, as some providers may have data caps or throttling.
  • Public WiFi networks can be vulnerable to hacking and cyber attacks, so it's worth taking precautions like using a VPN and keeping software up to date.
  • It's also worth being aware of internet outages and disruptions, which can occur during severe weather events or maintenance periods.
