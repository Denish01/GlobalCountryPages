[SECTION] Overview: Ireland vs United Kingdom [/SECTION]
Ireland and the United Kingdom are two neighboring countries with distinct economic profiles. Ireland has a higher GDP per capita and a slightly higher inflation rate compared to the United Kingdom. The cost of living in Ireland is generally higher, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being more expensive. However, the difference in costs between the two countries is not drastic, and the United Kingdom offers a more affordable option for those looking to live in a European country with a high standard of living. The overall cost of living in Ireland is around 10-20% higher than in the United Kingdom, making it a more expensive option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Ireland has a strong economy, driven by its tech and pharmaceutical industries, which has led to a high demand for housing and a subsequent increase in prices. The United Kingdom, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy, with a strong services sector and a large manufacturing base. This diversity has helped to keep prices in check, making it a more affordable option for those looking to live in a European country. Overall, while Ireland is more expensive than the United Kingdom, the difference in costs is not significant enough to deter people from choosing to live in Ireland, especially considering its high standard of living and excellent quality of life.
