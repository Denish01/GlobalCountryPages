# Education Costs in Ireland

The cost of education in Ireland varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education being generally free or subsidized for locals, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Ireland's education system is known for its high quality, with a strong emphasis on **literacy** and **numeracy** skills. The system is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary levels, with 12 years of compulsory education. Public education is generally free or subsidized for locals, while private schools offer an alternative for those who can afford it. The language of instruction is primarily English, although Irish is also an official language and is taught in schools. International schools are available in larger cities, offering a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **British GCSE**.

The quality of education in Ireland is generally high, with public schools offering a well-rounded education and private schools often providing additional resources and facilities. However, the quality of education can vary depending on the school and location, with some areas having better-funded and more reputable schools than others. Expats and international students often opt for private or international schools, which can offer a more familiar curriculum and teaching style.

- The **best schools for expats** are often private or international schools, which offer a more familiar curriculum and teaching style.
