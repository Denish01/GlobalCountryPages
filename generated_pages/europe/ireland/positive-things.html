Cultural Treasures

  • The Cliffs of Moher are a UNESCO Global Geopark and one of Ireland's most iconic natural attractions, with over one million visitors per year.
  • The Book of Kells is an ornately illustrated manuscript from the ninth century that is now housed at Trinity College Dublin and is considered one of Ireland's greatest cultural treasures.
  • Irish traditional music is a unique and expressive art form that has been recognized by UNESCO as an intangible cultural heritage of humanity.
  • The Newgrange prehistoric monument is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most important archaeological sites in Ireland, dating back over 5,000 years.
  • The Wexford Opera Festival is a world-renowned festival that celebrates opera and classical music, with performances taking place in the historic town of Wexford.

People & Global Impact

  • Mary Robinson, the former President of Ireland, has been a leading figure in global human rights and has served as the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights.
  • The Irish diaspora has had a significant impact on the world, with over 70 million people around the world claiming Irish ancestry, including many notable figures such as Barack Obama and Joe Biden.
  • Bob Geldof, the lead singer of The Boomtown Rats, is a prominent Irish musician and activist who has worked to raise awareness and funds for humanitarian causes, including the Live Aid concert in 1985.
  • The values of hospitality and community are deeply ingrained in Irish culture, with a strong emphasis on welcoming strangers and supporting local communities.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Irish people is consistently praised by visitors, with many commenting on the warm and friendly welcome they receive.
  • Irish cuisine is a highlight for many visitors, with popular dishes such as Irish stew, soda bread, and Guinness beef stew.
  • The scenic landscapes of Ireland are a major draw, with visitors flocking to see the Cliffs of Moher, the Ring of Kerry, and the Giant's Causeway.
  • Traditional Irish pubs are a must-visit for many, with their lively atmosphere, live music, and wide range of beers and whiskeys.
  • The vibrant cities of Dublin, Cork, and Galway offer a wealth of cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and historical landmarks.
  • The outdoor activities available in Ireland, such as hiking, cycling, and surfing, are a major draw for adventure seekers.

Hidden Gems

  • The Aran Islands are a lesser-known destination off the west coast of Ireland, with a unique culture and stunning natural beauty.
  • The Glenstal Abbey in County Limerick is a beautiful and peaceful retreat that offers a glimpse into Ireland's rich monastic heritage.
  • The Copper Coast in County Waterford is a scenic and historic region that is home to many beautiful beaches, coves, and cliffs.
  • The Irish language is a hidden gem of Irish culture, with a unique grammar and syntax that is still spoken by over 70,000 people in Ireland.
