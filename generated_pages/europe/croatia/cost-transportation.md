[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Croatia has a well-developed transportation network, with a range of options available for getting around, including buses, trains, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent and reliable services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in Croatia use public transportation or walk or cycle, especially in urban areas, although car ownership is becoming increasingly popular.

The country's geography, with its mountainous regions and coastal areas, can make transportation challenging in some areas, but overall, the infrastructure is well-maintained and efficient. The capital city, Zagreb, has a comprehensive public transportation system, including buses and trams, while other major cities, such as Split and Dubrovnik, have reliable bus networks. The country's tourist industry is also well-developed, with many transportation options available for visitors, including shuttle services and private transfers.
