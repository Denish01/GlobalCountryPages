# Where to Stay in Croatia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Croatia, consider the country's diverse regions and choose an area that suits your travel style, such as the historic city of Dubrovnik, the vibrant capital of Zagreb, or the stunning Plitvice Lakes National Park.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Old Town Dubrovnik** (Dubrovnik)
Best For: Luxury / History Buffs
Vibe: This ancient walled city is filled with red-tiled roofs, cobblestone streets, and breathtaking views of the Adriatic Sea, offering a unique and romantic atmosphere. The area is perfect for those who want to immerse themselves in history and culture.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Unbeatable historic charm, stunning architecture, and proximity to major attractions. The area is also relatively safe and well-maintained.
Cons: Can be very crowded during peak season, and prices may be higher than in other areas.

**Upper Town Zagreb** (Zagreb)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is known for its beautiful parks, historic buildings, and vibrant cultural scene, offering a sophisticated and elegant atmosphere. The area is perfect for business travelers and those who want to experience the city's high-end lifestyle.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Excellent restaurants, museums, and theaters, as well as easy access to the city's main attractions. The area is also relatively safe and well-connected to public transportation.
Cons: May lack the charm and character of other neighborhoods, and prices can be steep.

**Hvar Town** (Hvar Island)
Best For: Family / Relaxation
Vibe: This picturesque island town is famous for its beautiful beaches, lush greenery, and vibrant nightlife, offering a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere. The area is perfect for families and those who want to unwind in a beautiful setting.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Stunning natural beauty, rich history, and a wide range of outdoor activities. The area is also relatively safe and family-friendly.
Cons: Can be quite crowded during peak season, and prices may be higher than in other areas.

**Split City Center** (Split)
Best For: Backpacker / Budget
Vibe: This vibrant and lively neighborhood is known for its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and lively atmosphere, offering a fun and energetic vibe. The area is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers who want to experience the city's nightlife and cultural scene.
Price Range: $30-100/night
Pros: Affordable prices, lively nightlife, and easy access to the city's main attractions. The area is also relatively safe and well-connected to public transportation.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, especially during peak season.

**Plitvice Lakes Area** (Plitvice Lakes National Park)
Best For: Nature Lover / Adventure Seeker
Vibe: This stunning national park is known for its breathtaking lakes, waterfalls, and lush vegetation, offering a peaceful and serene atmosphere. The area is perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers who want to explore the great outdoors.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Unbeatable natural beauty, wide range of outdoor activities, and proximity to the national park. The area is also relatively safe and well-maintained.
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and prices can be higher than in other areas.

**Zadar Old Town** (Zadar)
Best For: History Buff / Cultural Enthusiast
Vibe: This historic neighborhood is known for its beautiful architecture, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene, offering a unique and captivating atmosphere. The area is perfect for history buffs and cultural enthusiasts who want to experience the city's heritage.
Price Range: $50-150/night
Pros: Rich history, stunning architecture, and proximity to major attractions. The area is also relatively safe and well-maintained.
Cons: Can be crowded during peak season, and prices may be higher than in other areas.
