Getting There

Russia has several major international airports, with Sheremetyevo International Airport (SVO) being the busiest, serving as a hub for Aeroflot, the country's flag carrier. Direct flights to Russia are available from many major cities around the world, including European cities like London, Paris, and Berlin, as well as Asian cities like Beijing, Seoul, and Tokyo. Additionally, Russia shares land borders with several countries, including Norway, Finland, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Poland, Belarus, Ukraine, Georgia, Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, China, Mongolia, and North Korea, making it possible to enter the country by car, bus, or train.

For those traveling overland, there are several entry points along Russia's borders, including the border crossings with Norway and Finland in the northwest, as well as the borders with Ukraine and Belarus in the southwest. It's essential to research the specific requirements for entering Russia by land, including any necessary visas or documentation. Furthermore, some border crossings may have limited hours of operation or require special permits, so it's crucial to plan ahead and check with the relevant authorities before embarking on your journey.

Domestic Flights

Russia has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights between major cities. Aeroflot, S7 Airlines, and UTair are some of the major carriers, offering flights to destinations like Moscow, St. Petersburg, Yekaterinburg, and Vladivostok. Booking domestic flights in advance is recommended, as prices tend to increase closer to the departure date. Approximate costs for domestic flights can range from $50 to $200 or more, depending on the route and airline. It's also worth noting that some domestic flights may have limited schedules or availability, especially to more remote destinations.

Trains & Rail

Russia has an extensive rail network, with Russian Railways operating trains to most parts of the country. The rail network includes high-speed trains like the Sapsan, which connects Moscow to St. Petersburg, as well as overnight trains with sleeping compartments. Booking train tickets can be done online or at train stations, and it's recommended to book in advance, especially for popular routes. The quality of the rail network varies, with some routes offering modern and comfortable trains, while others may be more basic.

Buses & Coaches

Intercity bus companies like FlixBus and Lux Express operate in Russia, offering routes between major cities. The quality of the buses and coaches can vary, but many companies offer comfortable and modern vehicles. Costs for bus travel are generally lower than trains or flights, with approximate prices ranging from $10 to $50 or more, depending on the route and company. Bus travel can be a convenient and affordable option for shorter distances or for those on a budget.

Driving

  • To drive in Russia, you'll need a valid driver's license, and it's recommended to obtain a Russian driving permit if you plan to drive for an extended period.
  • Road conditions in Russia can be challenging, especially on rural roads, with potholes and uneven surfaces common.
  • Fuel costs in Russia are generally lower than in Western Europe, with gasoline and diesel available at most gas stations.
  • Car rental companies like Hertz, Avis, and Europcar operate in Russia, but it's essential to research and book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.
  • Hazards on Russian roads include aggressive drivers, poor road maintenance, and limited street lighting, especially at night.
  • It's also important to be aware of traffic laws and regulations, including the requirement to carry a first-aid kit and fire extinguisher in your vehicle.

Transport Tips

  • Research and book your transportation in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, to avoid high prices and limited availability.
  • Be prepared for language barriers, especially outside of major cities, and consider downloading translation apps or carrying a phrasebook.
  • Use official taxi services or ride-hailing apps to avoid scams and overcharging.
  • Always validate your ticket before boarding trains or buses, as fines can be steep for invalid tickets.
  • Consider purchasing a transport card or pass for convenient and affordable travel on public transportation.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots, and keep a close eye on your belongings to avoid pickpocketing or theft.
  • Download transportation apps like Yandex.Metro or Citymobil to help navigate Russia's public transportation systems.
  • Always follow local rules and regulations, including traffic laws and pedestrian crossings, to avoid fines or penalties.
