What Is Russia?

Russia, officially known as the Russian Federation, is a sovereign country located in both Europe and Asia. It is the largest country in the world by land area, covering more than 17 million square kilometers. Russia is situated in the northern part of the Eurasian continent, bordering several countries including Norway, Finland, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Poland, Belarus, Ukraine, Georgia, Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, China, Mongolia, and North Korea. The country has a diverse geography, featuring vast plains, mountains, forests, and coastlines along the Arctic and Pacific Oceans.

Russia is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its historic cities, such as Moscow and St. Petersburg, which are home to numerous museums, theaters, and architectural landmarks. The country has a long and complex history, with various empires and dynasties rising and falling over the centuries. Today, Russia is a major player in global politics and economy, with significant reserves of natural resources, including oil, gas, and minerals.

The Russian population is predominantly urban, with many people living in large cities such as Moscow, St. Petersburg, and Yekaterinburg. The official language is Russian, which is spoken by the vast majority of the population. Russia has a diverse economy, with a mix of state-owned and private enterprises, and a growing service sector. The country is also known for its scientific and technological achievements, including its space program and nuclear industry.

Key History

  1. 882 - The state of Kievan Rus' is established, marking the beginning of the Eastern Slavic civilization.
  2. 1328 - The Grand Duchy of Moscow is founded, which would eventually become the dominant power in the region.
  3. 1721 - The Russian Empire is established, with Peter the Great as its first emperor.
  4. 1917 - The Russian Revolution takes place, leading to the establishment of the Soviet Union.
  5. 1991 - The Soviet Union collapses, and the Russian Federation is established as an independent country.
  6. 2000 - Vladimir Putin becomes the President of Russia, marking the beginning of a new era in Russian politics.
  7. 2014 - Russia annexes Crimea, leading to international sanctions and tensions with the West.
