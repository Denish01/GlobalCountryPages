[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Russia has a well-developed transportation network, with a wide range of options available for getting around, including buses, trains, and taxis. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with major cities like Moscow and St. Petersburg having modern and efficient systems, while smaller cities and rural areas may have more limited options. Most people in Russia get around using public transportation, with buses and trains being the most popular modes of transport.

The country's vast size and diverse geography mean that transportation costs can vary significantly depending on the region and mode of transport. For example, a trip on the Trans-Siberian Railway can be a cost-effective way to see the country, while a taxi ride in a major city can be more expensive. Additionally, the cost of fuel and toll roads can add up for drivers, making it important to plan ahead and research the best options for your journey.
