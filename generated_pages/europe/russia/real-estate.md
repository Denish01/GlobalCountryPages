[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The Russian property market has experienced significant fluctuations in recent years, largely due to economic sanctions and geopolitical tensions. However, the market has shown signs of resilience and growth, particularly in major cities like Moscow and St. Petersburg. The current market conditions are characterized by a mix of **urbanization** and **infrastructure development**, driving demand for housing and commercial properties. The price trajectory has been steadily increasing, with a focus on **luxury properties** and **high-end developments**. Urban areas tend to be more expensive than rural areas, with the average price per square meter in Moscow being significantly higher than in other parts of the country.

The Russian government has implemented various initiatives to stimulate the property market, including **mortgage subsidies** and **tax incentives**. These measures have helped to boost demand and attract foreign investors. However, the market is still subject to various risks, including **economic uncertainty** and **regulatory changes**. Despite these challenges, Russia remains an attractive destination for property investors, with its vast natural resources, strategic location, and growing economy.

In terms of trends, there is a growing demand for **sustainable properties** and **eco-friendly developments**, particularly among younger buyers. This trend is driven by increasing awareness of environmental issues and a desire for **energy-efficient** and **green** living spaces. Additionally, there is a growing interest in **co-living** and **co-working** spaces, particularly in major cities.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners can buy property in Russia, but they must **register with the Federal Registration Service** and obtain a **Russian tax identification number**.
- Foreign companies can also buy property in Russia, but they must be **registered with the Russian authorities** and have a **Russian legal address**.
- There are **restrictions on foreign ownership** of certain types of property, such as **agricultural land** and **strategic assets**.
- Foreigners can **lease property** in Russia for up to 49 years, with the option to **extend the lease**.
- Foreigners must **comply with Russian tax laws** and **report their income** to the Russian tax authorities.

[SECTION] Buying Process [/SECTION]
1. **Research the market** and identify a suitable property.
2. **Appoint a real estate agent** to assist with the purchase.
3. **Conduct due diligence** on the property, including **title searches** and **property inspections**.
4. **Negotiate the purchase price** and **terms of the sale**.
5. **Sign a **purchase agreement** and pay a **deposit**.
6. **Obtain **financing**, if necessary.
7. **Register the property** with the **Federal Registration Service**.
8. **Obtain a **Russian tax identification number** and **register with the tax authorities**.
9. **Pay **stamp duty** and other **fees** associated with the purchase.
10. **Transfer the ownership** of the property and **receive the keys**.

[SECTION] Rental Market [/SECTION]
- **Tenant rights** are protected by Russian law, which includes **rent control** and **eviction protection**.
- **Typical lease terms** are 1-3 years, with the option to **renew** or **terminate** the lease.
- **Deposit norms** are typically 1-2 months' rent, which is **refundable** at the end of the lease.
- **Furnished vs unfurnished** properties are available, with **furnished properties** being more common in urban areas.
- **Rentals** are subject to **Russian tax laws**, which include **value-added tax** and **income tax**.
