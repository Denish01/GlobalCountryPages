Cultural Treasures

  • The Hermitage Museum in St. Petersburg is one of the largest and most famous museums in the world, with a collection of over 3 million items, including works by Rembrandt, Da Vinci, and Picasso.
  • The Bolshoi Theatre in Moscow is a world-renowned institution for ballet and opera, with a rich history dating back to 1776.
  • The Trans-Siberian Railway is a historic and culturally significant railway line that spans over 9,000 kilometers from Moscow to Vladivostok, passing through some of Russia's most stunning landscapes.
  • The Kremlin in Moscow is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the official residence of the President of Russia, with a rich history dating back to the 14th century.
  • The Lake Baikal region is home to a unique and diverse array of flora and fauna, with over 2,500 species of plants and animals found nowhere else in the world.

People & Global Impact

  • Leo Tolstoy was a Russian novelist and philosopher who is widely regarded as one of the greatest authors of all time, with works such as War and Peace and Anna Karenina still widely read and studied today.
  • The Russian diaspora has made significant contributions to science, art, and culture around the world, with notable examples including Sergei Rachmaninoff, a composer and pianist who was born in Russia but spent much of his life in the United States.
  • Mikhail Gorbachev was a Russian politician who played a key role in ending the Cold War and introducing reforms in the Soviet Union, earning him the Nobel Peace Prize in 1990.
  • The values of hospitality and community are highly valued in Russian culture, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome they receive from locals.

What Visitors Love

  • The traditional Russian cuisine is hearty and flavorful, with popular dishes such as borscht, beef stroganoff, and pelmeni.
  • The scenic landscapes of Russia are breathtaking, with vast expanses of wilderness, mountains, and coastline to explore.
  • The rich cultural heritage of Russia is evident in its many museums, theaters, and historical landmarks, with something to suit every interest and taste.
  • The friendly and welcoming locals are a major highlight of visiting Russia, with many visitors commenting on the warm hospitality they receive.
  • The vibrant cities of Moscow and St. Petersburg are must-visit destinations, with world-class nightlife, shopping, and entertainment options.
  • The unique and exotic destinations of Russia, such as Siberia and Kamchatka, offer a chance to experience the country's rugged wilderness and natural beauty.

Hidden Gems

  • The Kizhi Island in Lake Onega is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to a unique collection of wooden churches and buildings, showcasing traditional Russian architecture.
  • The Altai Mountains in southern Siberia are a hidden gem for outdoor enthusiasts, with stunning scenery, hiking trails, and opportunities for skiing and snowboarding.
  • The White Nights Festival in St. Petersburg is a unique and magical experience, with the city's streets and landmarks illuminated during the summer solstice.
  • The Russian banya is a traditional sauna experience that is not to be missed, with many Russians swearing by its health benefits and social advantages.
