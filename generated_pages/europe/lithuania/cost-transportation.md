[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Lithuania has a well-developed transportation system, with a range of options available for getting around, including buses, trains, and taxis. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent and reliable services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in Lithuania get around by car, with a high rate of car ownership, but public transport is also widely used, particularly in urban areas. The country's capital, Vilnius, has a comprehensive public transport system, including buses and trolleybuses, making it easy to get around without a car.

The transportation network in Lithuania is relatively affordable, with prices comparable to other European countries. However, prices can vary depending on the city and region, with Vilnius tend to be more expensive than other parts of the country. Additionally, the cost of fuel is relatively high, which can make driving a car more expensive than using public transport. Overall, Lithuania's transportation system is convenient and relatively affordable, making it easy to get around and explore the country.
