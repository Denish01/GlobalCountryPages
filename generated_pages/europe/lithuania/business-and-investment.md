# Doing Business in Lithuania — Investment, FDI & Opportunities

Doing business in Lithuania is relatively easy, with a favorable business environment and various investment opportunities, making it an attractive destination for foreign investors. 

[FACTBOX]
Ease of Doing Business: 11th (World Bank ranking context)
Corporate Tax Rate: 15%
FDI Inflow: $1.4 billion annual
Special Economic Zones: Yes, 7
Key Industries: **Information Technology**, **Manufacturing**, **Logistics**
Currency Stability: Stable
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
Lithuania has a business-friendly environment, with a government that actively promotes foreign investment and entrepreneurship. The country has made significant progress in recent years to simplify its regulatory framework and reduce bureaucracy. However, some areas, such as construction and public procurement, still require improvement. Corruption is relatively low compared to other European countries, and the government has implemented various measures to prevent and combat corruption. Overall, Lithuania offers a favorable climate for doing business, with a highly skilled workforce and a strategic location at the crossroads of Europe.

The government of Lithuania has a positive attitude toward foreign investment, and various institutions, such as the **Investment Development Agency**, provide support and assistance to investors. The country has also made significant investments in its infrastructure, including transportation networks and telecommunications. However, the bureaucracy level can still be a challenge, particularly for small and medium-sized enterprises. To address this issue, the government has introduced various e-government services and simplified procedures for registering and operating a business.

Lithuania's membership in the **European Union** and the **NATO** alliance provides a high level of economic and political stability, making it an attractive destination for foreign investors. The country has also signed various **free trade agreements**, including the **EU-Canada Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement**, which provides access to a large market and facilitates trade.

[SECTION] Foreign Direct Investment [/SECTION]
- FDI inflow has been steadily increasing in recent years, with a total of $1.4 billion in 2020.
- The main investing countries are **Sweden**, **Germany**, and **Netherlands**.
- The government offers various incentives, including **tax breaks**, **grants**, and **investment subsidies**, to attract foreign investors.
- Restricted sectors include **defense**, **energy**, and **transportation**, which require special permits and licenses.

[SECTION] Legal & Regulatory Framework [/SECTION]
- **Business laws** are based on EU directives and regulations, providing a high level of protection for investors.
- **Intellectual property protection** is strong, with laws and regulations in place to protect patents, trademarks, and copyrights.
- **Labor laws** are flexible, with a highly skilled and motivated workforce.
- **Dispute resolution** is efficient, with a well-developed court system and alternative dispute resolution mechanisms.
- **Tax laws** are favorable, with a corporate tax rate of 15% and various tax incentives for investors.

[SECTION] Challenges & Risks [/SECTION]
- **Bureaucracy** can still be a challenge, particularly for small and medium-sized enterprises.
- **Corruption** is relatively low, but still a risk in certain areas, such as construction and public procurement.
- **Market limitations** can be a challenge, particularly for companies that rely on the domestic market.
- **Currency risks** are low, due to the country's membership in the EU and the use of the euro as its currency.
- **Political risks** are low, due to the country's stable government and EU membership.

[SECTION] Free Zones & Incentives [/SECTION]
- **Special economic zones** offer various incentives, including tax breaks, grants, and investment subsidies.
- **Tax incentives** are available for companies that invest in certain sectors, such as IT and manufacturing.
- **Investment promotion agencies**, such as the **Investment Development Agency**, provide support and assistance to investors.
- **Research and development incentives** are available for companies that invest in R&D activities, including grants and tax credits.
