[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Hill of Crosses**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a unique monument featuring thousands of crosses and a symbol of Lithuanian faith and perseverance.
- The **Vilnius Old Town**, another UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the largest and most well-preserved medieval towns in Eastern Europe, with a rich history and cultural heritage.
- The **Lithuanian Song and Dance Festival**, a tradition dating back to 1924, showcases the country's rich musical and dance heritage, with thousands of participants and spectators attending the event.
- The **Kaunas Castle**, a 14th-century fortress, is one of the oldest and most impressive castles in Lithuania, featuring a museum, art gallery, and cultural events.
- The **Lithuanian National Museum of Art**, located in Vilnius, features a vast collection of Lithuanian and European art, including works by famous artists like **Mikalojus Konstantinas Čiurlionis**.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Vincas Kudirka**, a Lithuanian poet and writer, is credited with writing the country's national anthem, **Tautiška giesmė**.
- The **Lithuanian diaspora**, with communities in the United States, Canada, and Australia, has made significant contributions to the development of science, art, and culture in their adopted countries.
- **Arūnas Valinskas**, a Lithuanian entrepreneur, founded the company **UAB Ekspla**, which has become a leading manufacturer of high-powered lasers.
- **Birutė Galdikas**, a Lithuanian-Canadian anthropologist, is a renowned expert on orangutans and has dedicated her life to conservation efforts in Indonesia.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **warm hospitality** of the Lithuanian people, with visitors often commenting on the friendly and welcoming nature of the locals.
- The **delicious food**, featuring traditional dishes like **Cepelinai** (zeppelins) and **Kibinai** (meat pies), as well as a wide range of modern restaurants and cafes.
- The **stunning natural scenery**, with Lithuania's many national parks, forests, and lakes offering opportunities for hiking, camping, and outdoor activities.
- The **rich cultural heritage**, with numerous museums, historical sites, and cultural events showcasing the country's history and traditions.
- The **vibrant city life**, with cities like Vilnius and Kaunas offering a wide range of entertainment options, from bars and clubs to theaters and concert halls.
- The **affordability**, with Lithuania being one of the more affordable countries in Europe, making it an attractive destination for budget-conscious travelers.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Curonian Spit**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a unique and fragile ecosystem featuring a range of flora and fauna, as well as traditional fishing villages and scenic beaches.
- The **Lithuanian Lake District**, with its numerous lakes, forests, and hills, offers a peaceful and picturesque getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life.
- The **Kernavė Archaeological Site**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, features a range of ancient hill forts, burial sites, and other archaeological treasures.
- The **Lithuanian Folk Life Museum**, located in Rumšiškės, showcases traditional Lithuanian architecture, crafts, and way of life, with numerous exhibits and interactive displays.
