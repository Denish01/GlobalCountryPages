[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Lithuania has a highly developed internet infrastructure, with fast and reliable connections available throughout the country. The average internet speed in Lithuania is 74 Mbps download, which is one of the fastest in the European Union. The urban areas, such as the capital city Vilnius, have excellent internet coverage, with many cafes, restaurants, and public spaces offering free WiFi. However, there is a noticeable urban-rural divide, with rural areas having slower internet speeds and less reliable connections. Despite this, the overall internet quality in Lithuania is good, with most users experiencing minimal downtime and fast loading times.

The reliability of the internet in Lithuania is also noteworthy, with most internet service providers offering stable and secure connections. This makes it an attractive destination for remote workers and digital nomads who require a fast and reliable internet connection to stay productive. Additionally, the Lithuanian government has invested heavily in developing the country's digital infrastructure, which has led to the widespread adoption of **fiber-optic internet** and **5G mobile networks**. This has further improved the overall internet quality and availability in Lithuania, making it an excellent destination for anyone who requires a fast and reliable internet connection.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Lithuania is a straightforward process, with many mobile network operators offering tourist-friendly plans and easy registration. You can buy a SIM card at most mobile network operator stores, as well as at some newsstands and convenience stores. To register for a SIM card, you will need to provide your passport and proof of address, although some operators may not require the latter. The main carriers in Lithuania, such as Telia, Bitė, and Tele2, offer a range of plans and prices, with some operators offering **pay-as-you-go** options and others requiring a **monthly subscription**.

It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds. You can expect to pay around 10 USD for a tourist SIM card, which usually includes a certain amount of data and minutes. Some operators also offer **data-only plans**, which can be a good option for remote workers and digital nomads who require a lot of data.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels offering free WiFi to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is widespread, with many cafes and restaurants offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular, with many offering high-speed WiFi and other amenities.
- Public WiFi is available in many public spaces, such as parks and squares.
- Many shopping malls and airports also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while on the go.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces such as **Workland** and **Hub Vilnius** offer high-speed WiFi and other amenities.
- Reliable cafe chains such as **Coffee Inn** and **Vero Cafe** offer good WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options such as **mobile hotspots** and **portable WiFi routers** are available for purchase or rent.
- Some coworking spaces and cafes also offer **VPN services**, which can be useful for remote workers who require a secure and private connection.
- **Digital nomad communities** are also present in Lithuania, with many events and meetups organized for remote workers and digital nomads.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major issue in Lithuania, but some websites may be blocked due to copyright or other reasons.
- **VPN services** can be useful for accessing blocked websites and maintaining a secure connection.
- Roaming alternatives such as **eSIM cards** and **mobile hotspots** can be a good option for travelers who require a lot of data.
- It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable operator to avoid any issues with coverage or speeds.
- **Public WiFi networks** can be vulnerable to hacking and other security threats, so it is recommended to use a VPN or other security measures when using public WiFi.
- **Data limits** can be a issue, so it is recommended to monitor your data usage and purchase additional data if necessary.
