[SECTION] Overview: Lithuania vs Poland [/SECTION]
Lithuania and Poland are two neighboring countries in Eastern Europe that share some cultural and economic similarities. However, when it comes to the cost of living, Lithuania tends to be cheaper than Poland. The main reason for this is the difference in economic development between the two countries. Lithuania has a more developed economy, with a higher GDP per capita, which has led to higher prices in some areas. On the other hand, Poland has a larger population and a more diverse economy, which has kept prices relatively lower. Overall, Lithuania is around 10-20% cheaper than Poland, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a affordable place to live.

The cost of living in Lithuania and Poland can vary depending on the location, with cities like Vilnius and Warsaw tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. However, in general, Lithuania tends to be cheaper than Poland in most areas, including rent, food, transportation, and utilities. This makes Lithuania an attractive option for those looking for a affordable place to live without sacrificing too much in terms of quality of life. On the other hand, Poland has a more vibrant cultural scene and a wider range of amenities, which may make it a more attractive option for some people.
