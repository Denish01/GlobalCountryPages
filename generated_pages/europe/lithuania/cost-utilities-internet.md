# Utility & Internet Costs in Lithuania

The cost of utilities and internet in Lithuania can vary depending on the location, usage, and service provider, but on average, a monthly utility bill for an 85 sqm apartment can range from $150 to $300 USD.

[FACTBOX]
Monthly Utilities (85 sqm apt): $200 USD
Internet (60 Mbps): $25 USD/month
Mobile Data (10GB): $10 USD/month
Electricity Cost: $0.15 per kWh
Currency: EUR
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Lithuania's utility infrastructure is well-developed, with a reliable supply of electricity, water, and gas. The costs of utilities in Lithuania are generally stable, with minimal fluctuations throughout the year. However, there can be some seasonal variation in electricity and gas costs, with higher prices during the winter months due to increased demand for heating. The country's utility market is also subject to EU regulations, which helps to maintain a level of stability and transparency in pricing.

The quality of utility services in Lithuania is generally high, with a strong focus on customer service and reliability. The main utility providers in the country, such as ESO and Lietuvos dujos, offer a range of services and tariffs to suit different customer needs. However, it's worth noting that the costs of utilities can vary depending on the location, with urban areas tend to have higher prices than rural areas.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities in Lithuania, you will typically need to provide proof of address and identification, and sign a contract with the utility provider.
- Prepaid electricity meters are available, but postpaid plans are more common and often offer better rates.
- The best internet providers in Lithuania include Telia and Bité, which offer a range of plans and speeds to suit different needs.
- To save on utility bills, consider using energy-efficient appliances, turning off lights and electronics when not in use, and taking advantage of promotional offers from utility providers.
- It's also worth considering a phone and internet bundle, which can often be more cost-effective than separate plans.
