# Top Things to Do in Bosnia and Herzegovina — Attractions & Experiences

Bosnia and Herzegovina offers a diverse range of experiences, from historic cities and cultural landmarks to stunning natural beauty and outdoor adventures, making it a unique and exciting destination to explore.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **truffle hunting tour** in the forests of western Bosnia and learn about the art of finding these prized fungi
- Visit a **local winery** and taste some of the country's excellent wines, such as Žilavka and Blatina
- Attend a **traditional folklore performance** and experience the vibrant music and dance of Bosnia and Herzegovina
- Learn about the **history of coffee culture** in Sarajevo and sample some of the city's famous coffee
- Go on a **rafting trip** down the Neretva River and enjoy the stunning scenery and thrilling rapids
- Take a **cooking class** and learn how to make traditional Bosnian dishes, such as ćevapi and burek
- Explore the **street art scene** in Sarajevo and discover the vibrant murals and graffiti that cover the city's walls

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Sutjeska National Park**, home to the highest peak in the country and a stunning array of flora and fauna
- Hike to the top of **Maglić Mountain**, the highest peak in Bosnia and Herzegovina, and enjoy the breathtaking views
- Visit the **Kravica Waterfall** and enjoy a swim in the crystal-clear waters
- Take a **boat tour** of the **Buško Lake** and enjoy the stunning scenery and tranquil atmosphere
- Go **skiing** or **snowboarding** at one of the country's many resorts, such as Bjelašnica or Jahorina
- Explore the **Una National Park**, home to a stunning array of wildlife and natural beauty

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum of Bosnia and Herzegovina** in Sarajevo and learn about the country's rich history and culture
- Explore the **Gazi Husrev-bey's Mosque** in Sarajevo, one of the most beautiful and historic mosques in the country
- Attend a **traditional craft workshop** and learn about the art of Bosnian craftsmanship, such as woodworking or weaving
- Visit the **Sarajevo City Hall**, a stunning example of Austro-Hungarian architecture and a symbol of the city's rich history
- Take a **tour of the old town** of Mostar and learn about the city's history and cultural significance
- Visit the **Museum of the Assassination of Franz Ferdinand** in Sarajevo and learn about the events that led to the outbreak of World War I

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Lukomir Village**, a traditional Bosnian village that is home to many ancient **stećci medieval tombstones**
- Explore the **Ostrožac Castle**, a stunning medieval fortress that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside
- Take a **hike** through the **Bjelašnica Mountains** and enjoy the stunning scenery and tranquil atmosphere
- Visit the **Travnik Museum**, a small but fascinating museum that showcases the history and culture of the town
- Attend a **traditional festival**, such as the **Sarajevo Film Festival** or the **Mostar Summer Festival**

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip from Sarajevo to **Mostar** (130 km, 2 hours), and explore the historic old town and famous bridge
- Visit the **Kravica Waterfall** (40 km, 1 hour from Mostar), and enjoy a swim in the crystal-clear waters
- Take a day trip from Sarajevo to **Travnik** (90 km, 1.5 hours), and explore the historic town and its stunning fortress
- Visit the **Sutjeska National Park** (120 km, 2.5 hours from Sarajevo), and enjoy hiking and outdoor activities
- Take a day trip from Mostar to **Međugorje** (30 km, 45 minutes), and visit the famous Catholic pilgrimage site
