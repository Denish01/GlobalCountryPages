[SECTION] What Is Bosnia and Herzegovina? [/SECTION]
Bosnia and Herzegovina is a **sovereign** country located in **Southeastern Europe**, bordered by Croatia, Serbia, and Montenegro. The country has a rich and complex history, with a mix of **Bosnian**, **Croatian**, and **Serbian** cultures. Bosnia and Herzegovina is known for its stunning natural beauty, including the Dinaric Alps, the Neretva River, and the Adriatic coast. The country has a diverse geography, with mountains, forests, and rivers, making it an attractive destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Bosnia and Herzegovina is a federal parliamentary republic, consisting of two entities: the Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina and the Republika Srpska. The country has a president and a prime minister, with a complex system of government that reflects its history and cultural diversity. The capital city, **Sarajevo**, is a bustling metropolis with a rich cultural scene, known for its historic old town, museums, and traditional cuisine.

The country's unique blend of cultures, history, and natural beauty makes it a fascinating destination for travelers. From the **medieval** town of Mostar to the **Ottoman**-era architecture of Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina is a country that seamlessly blends **East** and **West**, **past** and **present**. With its stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and resilient people, Bosnia and Herzegovina is a country that is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors.

1. 1918 - Bosnia and Herzegovina becomes part of the Kingdom of Yugoslavia after World War I.
2. 1945 - The country becomes a socialist republic within Yugoslavia after World War II.
3. 1992 - Bosnia and Herzegovina declares independence from Yugoslavia, leading to a brutal civil war that lasts for three years.
4. 1995 - The Dayton Peace Accords are signed, bringing an end to the war and establishing the country's current system of government.
5. 2000 - Bosnia and Herzegovina begins to rebuild and recover from the war, with international assistance and investment.
6. 2014 - The country experiences severe flooding, which highlights the need for continued international support and cooperation.
7. 2016 - Bosnia and Herzegovina submits its application to join the European Union, marking a significant step towards European integration.
