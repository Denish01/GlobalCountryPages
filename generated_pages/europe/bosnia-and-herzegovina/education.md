[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Bosnia and Herzegovina is divided into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 15 and lasts for 9 years. Secondary education is divided into two tracks: general secondary education and vocational secondary education, both of which last for 4 years. Tertiary education is provided by universities and colleges, with a range of undergraduate and graduate programs available. The quality of education in Bosnia and Herzegovina is generally good, with a strong emphasis on **academic achievement** and a high literacy rate. However, the system is also criticized for being **bureaucratic** and **inefficient**, with a lack of resources and infrastructure in some areas.

The education system in Bosnia and Herzegovina is predominantly public, with a small but growing private sector. Public education is free and compulsory, and it is available to all citizens. Private education, on the other hand, is available at all levels, from primary to tertiary, and is often preferred by families who can afford it. Private schools and universities offer a range of programs and **specializations**, including **international curricula** and **language programs**. However, private education is not always **accessible** or **affordable** for all students, and there are concerns about **equity** and **fairness** in the system.

Overall, the education system in Bosnia and Herzegovina is **complex** and **multifaceted**, with a range of strengths and weaknesses. While there are challenges to be addressed, the system also offers many opportunities for students to succeed and thrive. With a strong emphasis on **academic achievement** and a growing private sector, Bosnia and Herzegovina is an attractive destination for students from around the world.

- To study in Bosnia and Herzegovina, international students need to obtain a **student visa**, which can be applied for through the **embassy** or **consulate** of Bosnia and Herzegovina in their home country.
- Students may need to demonstrate **proficiency** in one of the official languages, including **Bosnian**, **Croatian**, or **Serbian**.
- **Scholarships** are available for international students, including **government scholarships** and **university scholarships**.
- Living costs for students in Bosnia and Herzegovina are relatively **low**, with **accommodation** and **food** available at affordable prices.
- International students can also **work part-time** to support themselves, with **job opportunities** available in **retail**, **hospitality**, and **services**.
- Students should also consider **health insurance** and **travel insurance**, which can provide **medical** and **financial** protection.

- Recent reforms have aimed to **improve** the education system, including the introduction of **new curricula** and **teaching methods**.
