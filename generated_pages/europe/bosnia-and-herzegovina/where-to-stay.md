# Where to Stay in Bosnia and Herzegovina — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Bosnia and Herzegovina, the best places to stay are in the capital city of Sarajevo, the historic city of Mostar, and the charming town of Banja Luka, which offer a mix of cultural, historical, and natural attractions.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Baščaršija** (Sarajevo)
Best For: backpacker / family
Vibe: This historic neighborhood is known for its narrow cobblestone streets, Ottoman-era architecture, and lively atmosphere, with many shops, restaurants, and cafes to explore. The area is perfect for those looking to experience the local culture and history.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: rich history, cultural attractions, affordable prices
Cons: can be crowded and noisy

**Mostar Old Town** (Mostar)
Best For: luxury / family
Vibe: This picturesque area is famous for its reconstructed medieval architecture, including the iconic Stari Most bridge, and offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. The area is ideal for those looking for a mix of relaxation and cultural experiences.
Price Range: $50-100/night
Pros: stunning architecture, scenic views, rich history
Cons: can be touristy and expensive

**Banja Luka City Center** (Banja Luka)
Best For: business / luxury
Vibe: This modern area is known for its vibrant atmosphere, with many restaurants, cafes, and shops, as well as a range of cultural and historical attractions, including the Banja Luka Castle. The area is perfect for those looking for comfort and convenience.
Price Range: $40-80/night
Pros: modern amenities, cultural attractions, business facilities
Cons: limited historical significance

**Sarajevo Center** (Sarajevo)
Best For: luxury / business
Vibe: This area is the heart of the city, with many modern amenities, including shopping centers, restaurants, and hotels, as well as easy access to historical and cultural attractions. The area is ideal for those looking for comfort and convenience.
Price Range: $60-120/night
Pros: modern amenities, central location, business facilities
Cons: can be expensive and crowded

**Trebinje Old Town** (Trebinje)
Best For: family / luxury
Vibe: This charming area is known for its historic architecture, scenic views, and relaxed atmosphere, with many opportunities for outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking. The area is perfect for those looking for a peaceful and natural getaway.
Price Range: $30-60/night
Pros: historic architecture, scenic views, relaxed atmosphere
Cons: limited cultural attractions

**Ilidža** (Sarajevo)
Best For: family / budget
Vibe: This suburban area is known for its natural beauty, with many parks and green spaces, as well as easy access to the city center and historical attractions. The area is ideal for those looking for a peaceful and affordable stay.
Price Range: $20-40/night
Pros: natural beauty, affordable prices, family-friendly
Cons: limited cultural attractions

**Mejtaš** (Sarajevo)
Best For: backpacker / budget
Vibe: This area is a popular choice for budget travelers, with many affordable accommodation options, as well as easy access to the city center and historical attractions. The area is perfect for those looking for a budget-friendly and social stay.
Price Range: $15-30/night
Pros: affordable prices, social atmosphere, convenient location
Cons: limited amenities and services

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September)
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Airbnb, to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings
- Look for accommodations with free Wi-Fi and breakfast included
- Check the location and proximity to public transportation and attractions
- Read reviews and check the ratings of the accommodation before booking
- Be aware of any additional fees or charges, such as tourist tax or cleaning fees

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas at night
- Keep valuables secure and be mindful of pickpocketing or theft
- Use reputable taxi services or public transportation, and avoid hitchhiking
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or churches
- Stay informed about local events and demonstrations, and avoid areas of potential conflict
