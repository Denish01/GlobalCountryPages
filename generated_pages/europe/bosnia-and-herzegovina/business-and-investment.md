# Doing Business in Bosnia and Herzegovina — Investment, FDI & Opportunities

Doing business in Bosnia and Herzegovina can be challenging due to its complex bureaucratic system and corruption, but the country also offers various investment opportunities, particularly in the industries of manufacturing, energy, and tourism.

[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
The business environment in Bosnia and Herzegovina is characterized by a complex system of government, with multiple layers of authority, which can make it difficult for foreign investors to navigate. The country has a relatively high level of bureaucracy, and corruption is a significant issue. However, the government has been working to improve the business climate, and there are efforts to simplify procedures and reduce corruption. The country's strategic location in Southeastern Europe, with access to the Adriatic Sea, makes it an attractive location for trade and investment.

The government of Bosnia and Herzegovina has expressed a commitment to attracting foreign investment and has taken steps to create a more favorable business environment. The country has a number of free trade agreements, including with the European Union, which provides access to a large market. However, the country still faces significant challenges, including a complex and sometimes unpredictable regulatory environment.

Despite these challenges, Bosnia and Herzegovina has a number of advantages that make it an attractive location for investment. The country has a highly skilled and educated workforce, and labor costs are relatively low compared to other European countries. The country also has a rich natural resources, including coal, iron ore, and timber, which provides opportunities for investment in the extractive industries.

- Bureaucratic obstacles, which can make it difficult to start and operate a business

- Limited access to finance, which can make it difficult for businesses to obtain funding
- Currency risks, which can affect the value of investments
- Limited infrastructure, which can make it difficult to transport goods and people
