Location & Borders

Bosnia and Herzegovina is a country located in southeastern Europe, situated in the western part of the Balkan Peninsula. It is bordered by three countries: Croatia to the north, west, and south, Serbia to the east, and Montenegro to the southeast. The country has a short coastline along the Adriatic Sea, which is approximately 20 kilometers long. This strategic location has made Bosnia and Herzegovina an important crossroads between Eastern and Western Europe throughout history.

The country's borders are mountainous and rugged, with many peaks reaching elevations of over 2,000 meters. The Dinaric Alps, a mountain range that stretches along the eastern coast of the Adriatic Sea, covers much of western Bosnia and Herzegovina. The country's terrain and borders have played a significant role in shaping its history, culture, and economy. The borders with neighboring countries have been a source of both cooperation and conflict, with the country's unique cultural and geographical position making it an important player in regional politics.
