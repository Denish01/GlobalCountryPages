Education System Overview

The education system in Guernsey is based on the British model, with some modifications to suit the island's unique needs and culture. The system is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary education, with 11 years of compulsory education. Primary education starts at the age of 5 and lasts for 6 years, followed by 5 years of secondary education. Students can then choose to pursue tertiary education at the College of Further Education or the University of the Channel Islands. The quality of education in Guernsey is generally high, with a strong focus on academic achievement, extracurricular activities, and personal development. The public education system is free for all students, while private schools and colleges also offer high-quality education at a fee.

The education system in Guernsey is overseen by the Education Department, which is responsible for setting curriculum standards, assessing student performance, and providing resources and support to schools and teachers. The department works closely with schools, parents, and the community to ensure that all students have access to high-quality education and opportunities for personal and academic growth. Guernsey's education system is also known for its strong emphasis on vocational training and apprenticeships, providing students with practical skills and experience in a range of industries.

In addition to its academic strengths, the education system in Guernsey also places a strong emphasis on extracurricular activities, such as sports, music, and arts. Many schools and colleges offer a wide range of clubs and societies, allowing students to develop their interests and talents outside of the classroom. This well-rounded approach to education helps students develop into confident, capable, and well-rounded individuals, prepared to succeed in their chosen careers and make a positive contribution to society.

International Schools

  • The International School of Guernsey offers the International Baccalaureate (IB) curriculum, providing students with a globally recognized qualification.
  • The British School of Guernsey follows the British national curriculum, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and extracurricular activities.
  • The American School of Guernsey offers the American curriculum, with a focus on SAT and AP exams.
  • The French School of Guernsey provides a French-language education, following the French national curriculum.
  • Island Montessori offers a Montessori-based education, with a focus on child-centered learning and practical life skills.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Guernsey, international students must apply for a student visa, which requires proof of acceptance at a Guernsey educational institution, proof of funds, and health insurance.
  • English language proficiency is a requirement for most courses, with IELTS or TOEFL exams accepted as proof of language ability.
  • Scholarships are available for international students, including the Guernsey Scholarship and the Channel Islands Scholarship.
  • Living costs for students in Guernsey are relatively high, with accommodation and food costs averaging around $1,500 per month.
  • International students can work part-time to support themselves, with work permits available for up to 20 hours per week.
  • Health insurance is mandatory for all international students, with several options available through private providers.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • The education system in Guernsey is known for its high academic standards, with a strong focus on literacy and numeracy.
  • One of the challenges facing the education system is funding, with a need for increased investment in infrastructure and resources.
  • The system is also working to address issues of inclusivity and diversity, with a focus on special needs education and language support.
  • Teacher training and professional development are also areas of focus, with a need for ongoing support and resources for educators.
  • The education system in Guernsey is subject to regular inspections and evaluations, with a focus on quality assurance and accountability.
