# Healthcare Costs in Guernsey

The cost of healthcare in Guernsey varies depending on the type of service and whether you are a local or a foreigner, with public healthcare generally being more affordable than private healthcare.

[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
Guernsey has a mixed healthcare system, with both public and private providers offering a range of services. The public healthcare system is generally of high quality and is available to all residents, including foreigners who are registered with the local authorities. However, the quality of private healthcare can vary, and it is generally more expensive than public healthcare. Locals and foreigners who are registered with the local authorities can access public healthcare services, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and specialist consultations, at a lower cost than private services. However, foreigners who are not registered with the local authorities may need to pay out-of-pocket for healthcare services or have private health insurance.

The healthcare system in Guernsey is generally well-regarded, with modern facilities and equipment, and a high standard of care. However, there may be some differences in the quality of care between public and private providers, and between different locations on the island. It is recommended that foreigners have health insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses, as the cost of healthcare in Guernsey can be high. Additionally, some healthcare services, such as specialist consultations and hospital stays, may require a referral from a doctor or other healthcare provider.

[SECTION] Emergency Care [/SECTION]
In the event of a medical emergency, it's best to call the emergency number 999 or 112, which will connect you to the island's emergency services. Ambulances are available, and paramedics are trained to provide basic life support and transportation to the hospital. The best hospitals for foreigners are the Princess Elizabeth Hospital and the Guernsey Hospital, which offer a range of services, including emergency care, surgery, and specialist consultations. It's a good idea to have health insurance that covers emergency medical evacuation, in case you need to be transported to a hospital on the mainland or to another island.

The emergency services in Guernsey are well-regarded, and the island has a number of qualified paramedics and medical professionals. However, in the event of a serious medical emergency, it may be necessary to be transported to a hospital on the mainland or to another island, which can be expensive and may require specialized insurance coverage. It's a good idea to research the emergency services and healthcare options available in Guernsey before traveling to the island, and to make sure you have adequate health insurance coverage. Additionally, it's a good idea to carry a list of emergency contact numbers, including the emergency services number and the contact information for your health insurance provider.
