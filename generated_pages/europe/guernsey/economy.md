[SECTION] Economic Overview [/SECTION]
Guernsey's economy is classified as a high-income economy, with a high standard of living and a strong, stable financial system. The island's economy has undergone significant development over the years, transforming from an agriculture-based economy to a service-based economy. This transformation has been driven by the growth of the financial services sector, which has become a major driver of the island's economy. Guernsey's economy is also characterized by a high level of economic stability, with low inflation and a stable currency.

The island's economy has a high level of dependence on the financial services sector, which accounts for a significant proportion of the island's GDP. However, the government has been working to diversify the economy, with a focus on developing other sectors such as tourism, fishing, and e-commerce.
