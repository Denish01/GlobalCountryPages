# Guernsey Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The island of Guernsey offers a well-developed internet infrastructure with reliable and fast connections, making it an ideal destination for tourists and remote workers alike.

[FACTBOX]
Average Internet Speed: 50 Mbps download
Mobile Network Coverage: Excellent
Main Carriers: Sure, JT, Airtel
SIM Card Cost: 10 USD for tourist SIM
WiFi Availability: Widespread
5G Available: Yes
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Guernsey is of high quality, with fast and reliable connections available throughout the island. The average internet speed is around 50 Mbps download, which is comparable to other developed countries in Europe. The urban areas, such as **Saint Peter Port**, tend to have better internet coverage and faster speeds compared to rural areas, but the difference is not significant. The island's small size and relatively low population density contribute to its excellent internet infrastructure, with most areas having access to **fiber-optic** connections.

The reliability of the internet in Guernsey is also noteworthy, with minimal downtime and **outages**. This is due in part to the island's investment in its digital infrastructure, including the rollout of **5G** networks. As a result, visitors and residents can enjoy seamless and **high-speed** internet access, whether for personal or professional use. The excellent internet connectivity also makes Guernsey an attractive destination for **remote workers** and **digital nomads**.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Guernsey, visitors can purchase one from a local carrier, such as **Sure** or **JT**, at their retail stores or online. The process is relatively straightforward, requiring a valid passport and proof of address. The cost of a tourist SIM card is approximately 10 USD, which includes a set amount of data, minutes, and texts. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable carrier, as they offer better coverage and customer support. Data plans vary in price and data allowance, but most carriers offer affordable options for tourists and short-term visitors.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality in Guernsey is generally good, with most hotels offering free and **high-speed** internet access to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is also prevalent, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces, such as the **Guernsey Coworking Space**, offer reliable and **fast** internet access, as well as other amenities like meeting rooms and networking events.
- Public WiFi is available in some areas, such as the **Saint Peter Port** harbor, but it is not as widespread as in other European cities.
- Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi to their customers, making it easy to stay connected while dining or socializing.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces, like the **Guernsey Coworking Space**, offer a productive and **connected** environment for remote workers.
- Reliable cafe chains, such as **Costa Coffee**, offer free WiFi and a comfortable working space.
- Backup internet options, such as **mobile hotspots**, are available for purchase or rent, providing a reliable connection in case of an **outage**.
- VPNs, or **Virtual Private Networks**, are not strictly necessary in Guernsey, but they can provide an extra layer of **security** and **privacy** for remote workers.
- Many libraries and community centers also offer free WiFi and a quiet workspace, making them ideal for remote workers who need a **productive** environment.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a significant concern in Guernsey, but some websites may be **blocked** due to copyright or licensing restrictions.
- Using a VPN can help bypass these restrictions and provide **secure** access to **blocked** websites.
- Roaming alternatives, such as **eSIMs**, are available for visitors who want to avoid **roaming charges**.
- It is recommended to purchase a local SIM card or **eSIM** to avoid **high** roaming charges.
- Visitors should also be aware of **public WiFi security risks** and take necessary precautions to protect their devices and **personal data**.
- Finally, it is a good idea to check with your carrier before traveling to Guernsey to see if they offer any **international plans** or **add-ons** that can help reduce **roaming charges**.
