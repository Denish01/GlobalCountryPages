Cultural Treasures

  • The Hauteville House, the former home of Victor Hugo, is now a museum showcasing the author's life and work.
  • The Guernsey Museum and Art Gallery features a collection of local art and artifacts, including works by Peter Le Lievre and Denis Rillieux.
  • The St. Peter Port Harbour is a historic and picturesque area with a rich maritime heritage.
  • The Guernsey Folk and Costume Museum showcases the island's traditional dress and folk customs.
  • The Little Chapel, a small chapel decorated with seashells and pebbles, is a unique and fascinating example of Guernsey's cultural heritage.

What Visitors Love

  • The island's hospitality and welcoming atmosphere, with many visitors commenting on the friendly and helpful nature of the locals.
  • The seafood and local cuisine, which features fresh fish, shellfish, and other seafood delicacies.
  • The scenic coastline and beautiful beaches, which offer a range of outdoor activities, including walking, swimming, and water sports.
  • The historic towns and villages, such as St. Peter Port and St. Martin, which feature narrow streets, picturesque harbours, and historic buildings.
  • The island's unique culture and heritage, which is reflected in its traditional music, dance, and folk customs.
  • The range of outdoor activities, including cycling, horse riding, and kayaking, which take advantage of the island's stunning natural beauty.

Hidden Gems

  • The Fort Grey and the Shipwreck Museum, a unique museum showcasing a collection of shipwreck artifacts and offering stunning views of the surrounding coastline.
