[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Guernsey is a small island with a well-developed public transportation system, making it easy to get around without a car. The main transportation modes on the island are buses and taxis, with buses being the most popular and affordable option. The quality of public transit is good, with frequent bus services connecting major towns and villages. Most people get around Guernsey by bus, with many residents also using bicycles or walking due to the island's compact size and scenic routes.

The island's public transportation system is operated by **Island Coachways**, which offers a range of bus services, including regular routes and **Nightowl** services that operate later in the evening. The buses are modern and comfortable, with many routes offering scenic views of the island's coastline and countryside. In addition to buses, taxis are also widely available, with many taxi companies operating on the island, including **Island Taxis** and **Guernsey Taxis**.
