# Faroe Islands Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Faroe Islands is generally reliable with decent speeds, making it suitable for tourists and remote workers, with a range of options available for getting a SIM card or accessing WiFi.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The Faroe Islands have a relatively developed internet infrastructure, with a **fiber-optic network** covering most of the country. The average internet speed is around 20 Mbps download, which is sufficient for general browsing, streaming, and online work. However, the internet quality can vary depending on the location, with urban areas like Torshavn tend to have faster and more reliable connections than rural areas. The mobile network coverage is good, with the main carriers, Føroya Tele, Kall, and Vodafone, offering widespread coverage across the islands.

Despite the overall good internet quality, there can be some **outages and disruptions**, especially during bad weather or maintenance work. Additionally, the internet speed can be slower in rural areas, making it challenging for remote workers or those who require high-speed connections. However, the Faroese government has been investing in improving the internet infrastructure, including the rollout of **5G networks**, which is expected to enhance the overall internet experience in the country.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in the Faroe Islands is relatively easy, with several options available for tourists and visitors. The main carriers, Føroya Tele, Kall, and Vodafone, offer a range of **prepaid SIM cards** and **data plans** that can be purchased at their stores or online. To buy a SIM card, you will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and the cost is around 30 USD for a tourist SIM. The recommended carriers for tourists are Føroya Tele and Kall, which offer a range of data plans and **competitive pricing**.

It's also possible to purchase a SIM card at the airport or at some convenience stores, but the selection may be limited. It's a good idea to check the coverage and data plans before purchasing a SIM card, as some carriers may have better coverage in certain areas.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels offering free WiFi to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is widespread, with many cafes and restaurants offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are limited, but some popular options include the **Faroe Islands Coworking Space** and **Torshavn Coworking**.
- Public WiFi is available in some areas, including the **Torshavn City Center** and **Vágar Airport**.
- Many **tourist information centers** also offer free WiFi, making it easy to stay connected and plan your trip.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like **Faroe Islands Coworking Space** and **Torshavn Coworking** offer a productive environment and reliable internet.
- Reliable cafe chains like **Kaffihúsið** and **Cafe Natúr** offer good WiFi and a comfortable working space.
- Backup internet options like **mobile hotspots** and **portable WiFi routers** are available for rent or purchase.
- VPNs are recommended for secure internet browsing, especially when using public WiFi.
- Some **accommodation options** like **Airbnb** and **hostels** also offer coworking spaces or shared offices for remote workers.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major concern in the Faroe Islands, but some **blocked sites** may be restricted due to copyright or licensing issues.
- VPNs are recommended for secure internet browsing, especially when using public WiFi.
- Roaming alternatives like **eSIMs** and **international SIM cards** can be a cost-effective option for frequent travelers.
- **Data roaming** can be expensive, so it's a good idea to purchase a local SIM card or **data plan**.
- **Public WiFi** can be vulnerable to hacking, so it's essential to use a VPN and keep your devices and software up to date.
- **Weather conditions** like heavy rain or strong winds can cause **internet outages**, so it's a good idea to have a backup plan in place.
