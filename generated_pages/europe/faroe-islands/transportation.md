# Getting Around Faroe Islands — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in the Faroe Islands, you can use a combination of domestic flights, buses, and rental cars, as the territory has a limited but well-connected transportation network.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
The Faroe Islands are located about halfway between Iceland and Norway, and the main entry point is Vagar Airport, which receives flights from major European hubs like Copenhagen and Reykjavik. Direct flights to the Faroe Islands are limited, but you can connect through these cities to reach the territory. The airport is located on the island of Vagar, about 45 kilometers west of the capital city Torshavn. From the airport, you can take a bus or taxi to reach your destination. 

Overland entry points do not exist in the Faroe Islands, as it is an island territory surrounded by the North Atlantic Ocean. The only way to reach the islands is by air or sea, with regular ferry services connecting the Faroe Islands to Denmark and Iceland. However, these ferry services are more suited for cargo and locals, and tourists usually prefer to fly to the territory. 

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
The domestic air network in the Faroe Islands is limited, with only a few routes connecting the main islands. The national carrier, **Atlantic Airways**, operates flights between Vagar Airport and the smaller islands of Mykines, Suduroy, and Skuvoy. These flights are usually small propeller planes and can be affected by the weather. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during the peak tourist season. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $50 to $100 USD per person, depending on the route and time of booking. 

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
The Faroe Islands do not have a rail network, making it one of the few countries in Europe without a railway system. The rugged terrain and limited population have made it unnecessary to develop a rail network, and buses and ferries are the primary modes of public transportation. 

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
The bus network in the Faroe Islands is relatively well-developed, with **Strandfaraskip Landsins** being the main bus company. They operate routes connecting the capital city Torshavn to other towns and villages on the main islands. The buses are modern and comfortable, and the routes cover most of the territory. However, the frequency of buses can be limited, especially in rural areas, so it's essential to plan your itinerary in advance. Costs for bus travel are around $2 to $5 USD per person, depending on the route.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in the Faroe Islands, you need a valid **driver's license** from your home country, and it's recommended to have a 4x4 vehicle due to the rugged terrain.
- The road quality in the Faroe Islands is generally good, with most roads being paved and well-maintained.
- Fuel costs are around $1.50 to $2.50 USD per liter, depending on the location and type of fuel.
- Car rental companies like **Avis** and **Hertz** have offices at Vagar Airport, and it's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak season.
- Hazards on the road include narrow tunnels, steep cliffs, and unpredictable weather, so it's essential to drive carefully and follow local guidelines.
- Parking in Torshavn and other towns is usually free, but be aware of restricted areas and parking signs.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be prepared for **unpredictable weather**, and check the forecast before heading out.
- Download the **Atlantic Airways** app to book and manage your flights.
- Use **cash** for small purchases and **credit cards** for larger transactions.
- Learn some basic **Faroese phrases**, like "hej" (hello) and "takk" (thank you), to interact with the locals.
- Be aware of **limited internet connectivity** in rural areas, and plan your itinerary accordingly.
- Respect the **environment** and follow local guidelines for hiking and outdoor activities.
- Book your **accommodation** and **transportation** in advance, especially during peak season.
- Consider purchasing a **Faroe Islands tourist card**, which grants access to public transportation and discounts on attractions.
