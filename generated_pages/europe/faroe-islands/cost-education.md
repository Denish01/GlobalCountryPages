# Education Costs in Faroe Islands

The cost of education in the Faroe Islands is relatively low, with free or subsidized public education available to all residents, and tuition fees for private and international schools ranging from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in the Faroe Islands is of high quality, with a strong emphasis on **Faroese language and culture**. The public education system is free or subsidized for all residents, and is considered to be of high quality, with well-trained teachers and modern facilities. Private schools are also available, but are less common, and tend to cater to the children of expatriates or those seeking a specific type of education, such as **International Baccalaureate**. Compulsory education lasts for 10 years, from age 7 to 17, and is divided into primary and secondary education.

The language of instruction in public schools is **Faroese**, while private schools may offer instruction in **Danish** or **English**. International schools are available in the capital city of **Torshavn**, and offer a range of curricula, including **British** and **American**. The education system in the Faroe Islands is designed to provide students with a well-rounded education, and to prepare them for further study or entry into the workforce. The system is also designed to promote **Faroese culture and identity**, and to foster a sense of community and social responsibility.
