# Faroe Islands Visa & Entry Requirements

You do not need a visa to enter the Faroe Islands if you are a citizen of certain countries, including those in the EU, EEA, and some other nations, but it is essential to verify the current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of the Faroe Islands before traveling.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Faroe Islands before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
The Faroe Islands have a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of around 90 countries to enter without a visa, either for a short stay or for a longer period with specific requirements. The visa policy is closely aligned with that of Denmark, as the Faroe Islands are a territory of Denmark. Citizens of countries that do not have a visa-free arrangement with the Faroe Islands will need to apply for a visa at the Danish embassy or consulate in their home country.

The Faroe Islands offer a unique experience for travelers, with stunning landscapes and a distinct culture. The visa policy is designed to facilitate tourism and business travel while maintaining the security and integrity of the territory. The visa requirements and regulations are subject to change, so it is crucial to check the latest information before planning a trip to the Faroe Islands.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter the Faroe Islands without a visa if they are citizens of eligible countries. The typical stay for tourists is up to 90 days within a 180-day period. Extensions are possible, but they must be applied for at the local police station in Torshavn.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to provide additional documentation, such as an invitation letter from a Faroese company or a letter explaining the purpose of their visit. They may also need to apply for a work permit if they plan to engage in any work-related activities during their stay.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in the Faroe Islands will need to provide proof of enrollment at a Faroese educational institution and may need to apply for a residence permit. The duration of the stay will depend on the length of the study program.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in the Faroe Islands will need to have a job offer from a Faroese employer and will need to apply for a work permit. The employer must sponsor the worker and provide proof of the job offer.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airport in the Faroe Islands is Vágar Airport, which is located on the island of Vágar. The airport is served by several airlines, including Atlantic Airways and SAS. When arriving at the airport, travelers will need to go through immigration and customs. The procedures are relatively straightforward, and travelers will need to present their passport and any required documents to the immigration officer. The officer will then stamp the passport and allow the traveler to enter the Faroe Islands.

Travelers can also enter the Faroe Islands by sea, either on a ferry or on a private boat. The main port of entry is the port of Torshavn, which is located on the island of Streymoy. When arriving by sea, travelers will need to report to the port authorities and go through customs and immigration procedures.
