# Spain Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

Spain is a sovereign country located in southwestern Europe, bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the east and southeast, the Atlantic Ocean to the northwest, and the countries of France, Portugal, Andorra, Morocco, and Gibraltar.

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Spain is situated in the Iberian Peninsula, which it shares with Portugal, and is bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the east and southeast, the Atlantic Ocean to the northwest, and the Bay of Biscay to the north. The country's borders are formed by the Pyrenees Mountains to the northeast, which separate it from France and Andorra, while the southern border is marked by the Strait of Gibraltar, which separates Spain from Morocco.

The country's strategic location has played a significant role in its history, with the Mediterranean Sea and the Atlantic Ocean providing access to trade routes and facilitating the exchange of goods, ideas, and cultures with other regions. The border with Portugal is the longest, stretching for over 1,200 kilometers, while the border with France is marked by the Pyrenees Mountains, which form a natural barrier between the two countries. The borders with Andorra, Morocco, and Gibraltar are significantly shorter, with the latter being a British overseas territory located on the Iberian Peninsula.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Spain's terrain is characterized by a diverse range of landscapes, including **mountain ranges**, **rivers**, **deserts**, **forests**, and **coastlines**. The Pyrenees Mountains form the northern border, while the **Cantabrian Mountains** and the **Sierra de Guadarrama** are located in the north and central regions, respectively. The **Meseta Central**, a vast plateau, covers much of the central region, while the **Andalusian Plain** is located in the southern region.

The country's **river systems** include the **Tagus River**, the **Duero River**, and the **Ebro River**, which flow through the central and northern regions. The **Desert of Tabernas**, located in the southeastern region of Almería, is a unique and arid landscape, while the **Cantabrian Forest** and the **Pyrenean Forest** are examples of the country's diverse **forest ecosystems**. The **coastline** of Spain is approximately 3,079 kilometers long, with the **Mediterranean Coast** and the **Atlantic Coast** offering a range of beaches, cliffs, and coves.

The **islands** of Spain, including the **Balearic Islands** and the **Canary Islands**, are located off the coast and are known for their unique landscapes and biodiversity. The **Balearic Islands**, which include **Mallorca**, **Menorca**, **Ibiza**, and **Formentera**, are located in the Mediterranean Sea, while the **Canary Islands**, which include **Tenerife**, **Gran Canaria**, **Lanzarote**, and **Fuerteventura**, are located off the northwest coast of Africa.
