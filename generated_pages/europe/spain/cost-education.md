# Education Costs in Spain

The cost of education in Spain varies depending on the type of institution and the level of study, with public education being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $2,000 to $15,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Spain is known for its high quality, with a strong emphasis on public education. Public schools in Spain are generally free or subsidized, and the quality of education is considered to be high. However, private schools, including international schools, are also available and can offer a more personalized education, often with a focus on specific languages or curricula. Compulsory education in Spain lasts for 10 years, from the age of 6 to 16, and is divided into primary and secondary education. International schools are also available in major cities, offering a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate, British, and American systems.

The language of instruction in public schools is Spanish, although some schools may offer bilingual or multilingual programs. Private and international schools may offer instruction in other languages, such as English, French, or German. The education system in Spain is highly regarded, with a strong focus on academic achievement, as well as extracurricular activities and personal development. The country is home to some of the world's top-ranked universities, including the University of Barcelona and the Complutense University of Madrid.
