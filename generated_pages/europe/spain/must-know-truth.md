# The Truth About Spain - History & Real Facts

Most people get wrong the fact that Spain is a homogeneous country, when in reality it is a diverse nation with a complex history, varied geography, and distinct cultural identities. 

[SECTION] Historical Context [/SECTION]
Spain has a rich and complex history, with the **Iberian Peninsula** being inhabited by various civilizations, including the **Celts**, **Romans**, and **Moors**, before the formation of the modern Spanish state. The **Reconquista**, a period of Christian reconquest, lasted from the 8th to the 15th century, and ended with the **Catholic Monarchs** unifying the peninsula under a single rule. The **Spanish Empire** was established in the 16th century, with Spain becoming a major world power, and its territories spanning across the globe. The 20th century saw Spain undergo a **civil war**, followed by a **dictatorship** under **Francisco Franco**, before transitioning to a **constitutional monarchy** in 1975.

The **Spanish Constitution** of 1978 established Spain as a **parliamentary democracy**, with a **monarch** serving as the head of state. The country has since become a member of the **European Union** and the **NATO** alliance, and has played a significant role in international affairs. Spain's history has been marked by periods of **regionalism** and **nationalism**, with various regions, such as **Catalonia** and the **Basque Country**, seeking greater autonomy or independence. The country has also experienced **terrorist attacks**, particularly from the **ETA** separatist group, which was active from 1959 to 2018.

The **Spanish Golden Age**, which spanned from the 16th to the 17th century, saw a flourishing of **art**, **literature**, and **culture**, with notable figures such as **Miguel de Cervantes** and **Diego Velázquez**. This period also saw the establishment of the **Spanish Inquisition**, a tribunal that aimed to maintain **Catholic orthodoxy** and root out **heresy**. The **Inquisition** had a significant impact on Spanish society, leading to the **expulsion of Jews** and **Moriscos** from the country.

In recent years, Spain has faced challenges related to **economic crisis**, **unemployment**, and **immigration**, which have had a significant impact on the country's **social fabric**. Despite these challenges, Spain remains a **vibrant** and **dynamic** country, with a rich cultural heritage and a strong sense of **national identity**.
