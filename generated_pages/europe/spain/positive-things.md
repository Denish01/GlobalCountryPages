[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Alhambra** in Granada is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a masterpiece of Islamic architecture, with its intricate palaces and gardens.
- The **Tomatina** festival in Buñol is a unique and colorful tradition, where participants throw tomatoes at each other in a fun and playful celebration.
- **Flamenco** music and dance are an integral part of Spanish culture, with the passionate and expressive art form originating in the Andalusia region.
- The **Prado Museum** in Madrid is one of the world's greatest art museums, featuring an extensive collection of European art, including works by **Goya**, **Velázquez**, and **El Greco**.
- The **Semana Santa** processions in Spain are a significant cultural event, with elaborate floats and costumes showcasing the country's rich Catholic heritage.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Pablo Picasso** was a renowned Spanish artist, who is widely regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, with his pioneering work in **Cubism**.
- The Spanish **diaspora** has had a significant impact on the world, with many notable figures, such as **Jose Marti** and **Federico Garcia Lorca**, making important contributions to literature, politics, and culture.
- **Santiago Ramón y Cajal** was a Spanish neuroscientist, who was awarded the **Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine** in 1906 for his groundbreaking work on the structure of the nervous system.
- The values of **hospitality** and **community** are deeply ingrained in Spanish culture, with the concept of **siesta** and **tapas** reflecting the country's emphasis on social bonding and relaxation.
