[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Spain has a well-developed transportation system, with a variety of options available for getting around, including public transportation, taxis, ride-hailing services, and renting cars. The quality of public transit in Spain is generally good, with efficient and reliable bus and metro systems in major cities like Madrid and Barcelona. Most people in Spain get around using public transportation, with many cities having comprehensive networks of buses and metro lines. In addition, many Spaniards own cars, and the country has a well-maintained network of highways and roads.

The transportation system in Spain is also relatively affordable, with a range of options available to suit different budgets. From affordable public transportation to convenient ride-hailing services, there are many ways to get around Spain without breaking the bank. Additionally, the country's compact size and well-developed transportation network make it easy to explore different regions and cities, whether by car, bus, or train.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a monthly transit pass or using ride-hailing services during off-peak hours.
- Download apps like Citymapper or Google Maps to navigate public transportation and find the most efficient routes.
- Be aware of peak hour traffic and plan your journeys accordingly to avoid congestion.
- When taking a taxi, make sure to check the meter is running and agree on a fare before you start your journey.
- Consider renting a car for longer trips or exploring rural areas, but be aware of toll roads and parking fees.
- Always follow local traffic rules and regulations, and be mindful of pedestrian and cyclist safety.
