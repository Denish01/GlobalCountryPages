# Cost of Living in Balearic Islands - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in or visiting the Balearic Islands can vary greatly depending on the season and lifestyle, but a daily budget of $80-200 USD per person can cover basic expenses for a mid-range traveler.

Currency: EUR
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 5-10% in restaurants and bars is appreciated

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Balearic Islands? [/SECTION]
The Balearic Islands, located off the eastern coast of Spain, are known for their beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and rich culture. Compared to other European destinations, the Balearic Islands are considered to be on the more expensive side, especially during the peak summer months. The cost of living and visiting the islands can be higher than in other parts of Spain, but the unique experience and stunning landscapes make it a worthwhile destination for many travelers. The islands' popularity among tourists and the limited availability of certain resources, such as fresh water, contribute to the higher costs. However, with some planning and research, it is possible to find affordable options and enjoy the beauty of the Balearic Islands without breaking the bank.

The cost of living in the Balearic Islands can also vary depending on the specific island and location. For example, Ibiza is generally more expensive than Menorca, while Mallorca offers a mix of affordable and luxurious options. The islands' capital, Palma, is located on Mallorca and offers a range of accommodation, dining, and entertainment options. The cost of living in Palma can be higher than in other parts of the islands, but it is still possible to find affordable options, especially for those who are willing to live outside of the city center.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting the Balearic Islands during the shoulder season (April-May or September-October) to avoid peak prices and crowds.
- Look for accommodation options outside of the city center or in lesser-known areas to save on rent and living costs.
- Take advantage of the islands' beautiful beaches and natural scenery by spending time outdoors and enjoying free or low-cost activities.
- Try local cuisine and street food to save on dining costs and experience the authentic flavors of the Balearic Islands.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transport costs and enjoy the scenic views.
- Research and book accommodations, flights, and activities in advance to take advantage of early bird discounts and special offers.
