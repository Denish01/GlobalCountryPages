# Cost of Living in Basque Country - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Basque Country is relatively high, with daily expenses ranging from $60-120 USD per day for a backpacker to $200-350 USD per day for a mid-range traveler.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Expensive

Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $120-250 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $250-400 USD
Currency: EUR
Tipping Custom: expected (5-10%)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Basque Country? [/SECTION]
Basque Country, an autonomous region in northern Spain, has a unique cultural identity and a high standard of living, which contributes to its relatively high cost of living. Compared to other European regions, Basque Country is more expensive than some of its neighbors, such as Portugal or Eastern European countries, but less expensive than Western European countries like Switzerland or Norway. The region's strong economy, driven by industries like manufacturing and tourism, supports a high level of spending power, which is reflected in the prices of goods and services.

In general, Basque Country's prices are similar to those in other Western European countries, with some variations depending on the location and type of product or service. For example, prices in the capital city of Vitoria-Gasteiz tend to be higher than in smaller towns or rural areas. Overall, visitors to Basque Country can expect to pay a premium for accommodations, food, and transportation, especially during peak travel seasons.
