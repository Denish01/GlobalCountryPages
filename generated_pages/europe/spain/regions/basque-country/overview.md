[SECTION] What Is Basque Country? [/SECTION]
The Basque Country is a region located in the western Pyrenees, straddling the border between Spain and France. It is an autonomous community within Spain, with its own government and a distinct cultural identity. The region is known for its stunning natural beauty, with rugged mountains, scenic coastlines, and picturesque villages. The Basque people have a unique language and culture that is distinct from the rest of Spain and France, with a strong emphasis on tradition, folklore, and cuisine.

The Basque Country is situated in a strategic location, with the Pyrenees Mountains to the east and the Cantabrian Sea to the north. The region is bordered by the Spanish provinces of Navarre and Castile and León to the south, and the French region of Aquitaine to the north. The capital city of the Basque Country is Vitoria-Gasteiz, which is located in the province of Álava. The region is also home to the cities of Bilbao and San Sebastián, which are known for their vibrant cultural scenes and stunning architecture.

The Basque Country has a long and complex history, with a strong sense of national identity and a desire for self-governance. The region has been inhabited by the Basque people for thousands of years, with a unique culture and language that has been preserved despite centuries of external influence. Today, the Basque Country is a thriving region with a strong economy, a rich cultural heritage, and a unique sense of identity.
