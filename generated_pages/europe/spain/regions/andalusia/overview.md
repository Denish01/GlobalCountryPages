[SECTION] What Is Andalusia? [/SECTION]
Andalusia is a region located in the southern part of the Iberian Peninsula, covering an area of approximately 87,268 square kilometers. It is bordered by the autonomous communities of Extremadura to the west, Castile-La Mancha to the north, and Murcia to the east, as well as the Mediterranean Sea to the southeast and the Atlantic Ocean to the southwest. Andalusia is known for its diverse landscapes, including mountains, valleys, and coastlines, as well as its rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its architecture, art, and traditions. The region is home to several major cities, including Seville, Granada, and Málaga, each with its own unique character and history.

Andalusia has a long and complex history, with various cultures and civilizations having inhabited the region over the centuries. The region has been influenced by the Moors, the Romans, and the Visigoths, among others, and this is reflected in its architecture, language, and customs. Today, Andalusia is a thriving region, with a strong economy and a rich cultural scene. The region is known for its vibrant festivals, including the **Feria de Abril** in Seville and the **Festival de Música de Granada**, as well as its delicious cuisine, which includes dishes such as **gazpacho**, **jamón ibérico**, and **pescaíto frito**.

Andalusia is also home to several important natural parks and protected areas, including the **Doñana National Park**, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most important wetlands in Europe. The region's diverse landscapes and natural beauty make it a popular destination for tourists, with millions of visitors each year. From the snow-capped mountains of the **Sierra Nevada** to the sunny beaches of the **Costa del Sol**, Andalusia has something to offer for everyone.
