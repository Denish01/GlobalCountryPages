[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
The Valencian Community, an autonomous region in eastern Spain, is known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful beaches, and vibrant cities. With a strong focus on tourism, the region has implemented various measures to ensure visitor safety, including increased police presence in popular areas and multilingual support services. However, as with any travel destination, petty crimes such as pickpocketing and scams can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Visitors should remain vigilant and take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Overall, the Valencian Community is considered a safe region, with low crime rates compared to other major tourist destinations. The local authorities are proactive in maintaining public safety, and visitors can feel comfortable exploring the region's many attractions, from the stunning beaches of the Costa Blanca to the historic city centers of Valencia and Alicante. By taking common-sense precautions and staying informed, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in the Valencian Community.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake police officers or street performers
- Overcharging by taxi drivers or restaurants in tourist areas
- Petty theft in hotels or hostels, particularly in shared accommodation
- Credit card skimming or ATM scams, especially in isolated areas
- Distraction theft, where thieves create a diversion to steal valuables
- Online booking scams, where fake accommodation or tour operators target tourists
- Drink spiking or robbery in bars or clubs, particularly at night

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to the Valencian Community, but visitors should ensure they are up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in the Valencian Community, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it.
- **Medical Facilities:** The region has a well-developed healthcare system, with modern hospitals and medical facilities, particularly in larger cities.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, with many open late or 24 hours, and staff often speak multiple languages.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas or tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or hotel safe
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or debit cards instead
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel safe if available
- Be cautious when using ATMs or exchanging money, and avoid isolated areas
- Stay informed about local conditions and events, and follow local advice or instructions
- Learn some basic Spanish phrases, such as "¿Dónde está...?" (where is...) or "Ayuda" (help)

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be confident and assertive, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, particularly at night
- Keep your hotel room number and other personal details private
- Avoid accepting rides or drinks from strangers, and be cautious when meeting new people
- Stay connected with family or friends back home, and let them know your itinerary and plans
