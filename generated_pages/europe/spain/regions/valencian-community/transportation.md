# Getting Around Valencian Community — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Valencian Community, you can use a combination of transportation options including flights, trains, buses, and cars, with various modes of city transport available in major cities like Valencia and Alicante.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
The Valencian Community is easily accessible by air, with two main international airports: Valencia Airport (VLC) and Alicante Airport (ALC). Valencia Airport is a major hub with direct flights to many European cities, while Alicante Airport serves as a gateway to the southern part of the region. You can also enter the Valencian Community by car or bus from neighboring regions in Spain, such as Catalonia and Murcia. The region is well-connected to the rest of Spain by highway, making it a convenient destination to visit by car.

If you prefer to travel by train, you can take a high-speed train from major Spanish cities like Madrid and Barcelona to Valencia, which is a major rail hub in the region. The city of Valencia is also connected to other European cities by bus, with companies like FlixBus and Eurolines operating daily services. Additionally, you can take a ferry from the Balearic Islands to the port city of Valencia, which offers a scenic and relaxing way to arrive in the region.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
The domestic air network in Valencian Community is moderate, with several airlines operating flights between Valencia and other major cities in Spain. **Iberia** and **Vueling** are two of the main airlines that operate domestic flights in the region, with routes connecting Valencia to cities like Madrid, Barcelona, and the Balearic Islands. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through a travel agency, with prices starting from around $50 for a one-way ticket. It's a good idea to book in advance to get the best deals, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
The rail network in Valencian Community is extensive, with **Renfe** operating high-speed trains and regional trains throughout the region. The high-speed train, known as the **AVE**, connects Valencia to Madrid and other major cities in Spain, with journey times of around 3 hours to Madrid. You can book train tickets online through the Renfe website or at train stations, with prices starting from around $30 for a one-way ticket. The regional trains, known as **Cercanías**, connect Valencia to other cities in the region, such as Alicante and Castellón.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
The bus network in Valencian Community is well-developed, with several companies operating intercity buses throughout the region. **ALSA** is one of the main bus companies, operating routes connecting Valencia to other major cities in Spain, such as Madrid and Barcelona. You can book bus tickets online through the ALSA website or at bus stations, with prices starting from around $20 for a one-way ticket. The buses are comfortable and convenient, with onboard amenities like air conditioning and Wi-Fi.
