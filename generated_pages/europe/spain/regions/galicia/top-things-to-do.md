# Top Things to Do in Galicia — Attractions & Experiences

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **pilgrimage route** to Santiago de Compostela, following in the footsteps of centuries of travelers
- Attend a **traditional Galician festival**, such as the Festival of Saint James or the Festival of the Sea
- Sample **local seafood** at a traditional Galician restaurant, featuring dishes such as pulpo a feira and empanada gallega
- Visit a **traditional Galician village**, such as the Palloza de Teixois, to experience rural life and culture
- Take a **boat tour** of the Rías Baixas, exploring the scenic coastal inlets and marine life
- Learn about **Galician folklore** and traditional music, such as the gaita, at a local festival or workshop
- Explore the **historic center of Santiago de Compostela**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and discover its narrow streets and historic buildings

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Islas Cíes**, a national park with stunning beaches, wildlife, and scenic hiking trails
- Hike the **Camino de Santiago**, a network of pilgrimage routes that crisscross Galicia
- Visit the **Rías Baixas**, a scenic coastal area with beautiful beaches and marine life
- Discover the **Serra da Capelada**, a mountain range with stunning views and hiking trails
- Relax on the **beaches of the Costa da Morte**, a scenic coastline with stunning cliffs and beaches
- Explore the **Galician countryside**, with its rolling hills, traditional villages, and scenic landscapes

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Galician Museum of Contemporary Art**, showcasing modern and contemporary Galician art
- Explore the **Santiago de Compostela Cathedral**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and pilgrimage site
- Attend a **traditional Galician music performance**, featuring the gaita and other traditional instruments
- Learn about **Galician history and culture** at the Casa de la Troya, a historic house and museum
- Visit the **Pazo de Mariñán**, a 16th-century palace and gardens
- Discover the **traditional crafts of Galicia**, such as ceramics, textiles, and woodcarving, at a local workshop or market

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Village of Ribadavia**, a charming medieval town with historic buildings and scenic views
- Explore the **Souto da Retorta**, a scenic natural area with stunning views and hiking trails
- Discover the **Beach of Rodas**, a stunning beach with crystal-clear waters and scenic views
- Attend a **local festival**, such as the Festival of the Sea or the Festival of Saint James, to experience traditional Galician culture
- Visit the **Galician Wine Route**, a scenic route that takes you through the wine-producing regions of Galicia

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]

- Explore **Vigo**, a scenic coastal city with stunning beaches and marine life (distance: 90 km, travel time: 1.5 hours)

- Take a day trip to **The Costa da Morte**, a scenic coastline with stunning cliffs and beaches (distance: 100 km, travel time: 2 hours)
