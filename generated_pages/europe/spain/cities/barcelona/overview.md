[SECTION] What Is Barcelona? [/SECTION]
Barcelona is a city located in the northeastern part of Spain, facing the Mediterranean Sea. It is the second-most populous city in Spain and the capital of the autonomous community of Catalonia. The city is situated about 160 kilometers south of the Pyrenees Mountains and the border with France. Barcelona is known for its stunning architecture, beaches, and cultural events, making it a popular tourist destination. The city has a long history dating back to the Roman Empire and has been an important cultural and economic center in the region for centuries. As the capital of Catalonia, Barcelona is a hub for Catalan culture, language, and politics.

Barcelona is a major economic and cultural center in Spain and Europe, with a strong focus on innovation, entrepreneurship, and tourism. The city is home to many world-renowned landmarks, including the **Sagrada Familia**, **Park Güell**, and **La Rambla**. The city's beaches, such as **Barceloneta** and **Nova Icària**, are popular with tourists and locals alike. Barcelona is also known for its vibrant cultural scene, with numerous museums, theaters, and music venues. The city hosts many festivals and events throughout the year, including the **La Mercè** festival and the **Primavera Sound** music festival.

The city's geography is characterized by its coastal location and the surrounding mountains, which provide a unique and picturesque backdrop to the urban landscape. The **Serra de Collserola** mountain range is located just west of the city, and the **Mediterranean Sea** is to the east. The city's climate is mild and Mediterranean, with warm summers and mild winters. Barcelona is a popular destination for tourists and business travelers alike, with its international airport and port making it a major transportation hub in the region.

2. 1469 - The marriage of **Ferdinand II of Aragon** and **Isabella I of Castile** unites the crowns of Aragon and Castile, forming the Kingdom of Spain.
3. 1714 - The **War of the Spanish Succession** ends with the defeat of Catalonia and the imposition of Spanish rule.
4. 1888 - The **Universal Exposition** is held in Barcelona, marking a period of significant urban development and modernization.
5. 1936-1939 - The **Spanish Civil War** takes place, with Barcelona being a key battleground and the capital of the Republican government.
6. 1992 - Barcelona hosts the **Summer Olympics**, marking a major turning point in the city's modernization and international recognition.
7. 2017 - The **Catalan independence referendum** takes place, with a majority of voters supporting independence from Spain.
