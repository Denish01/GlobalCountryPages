[SECTION] What Is Malaga? [/SECTION]
Malaga is a city situated on the southern coast of Spain, in the autonomous community of Andalusia. It is the sixth-largest city in Spain and is known for its stunning beaches, mild climate, and rich cultural heritage. The city has a long and complex history, with various civilizations having ruled over it, including the Phoenicians, Romans, and Moors. Today, Malaga is a thriving tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year with its beautiful architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and stunning natural surroundings.

Malaga is strategically located on the Costa del Sol, a stretch of coastline that runs along the Mediterranean Sea. The city is surrounded by mountains and is close to several natural parks, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Malaga is also an important economic center, with a strong focus on tourism, technology, and innovation. The city is home to several major universities and research institutions, and is a hub for entrepreneurship and start-ups.

Malaga is also known for its vibrant cultural scene, with numerous museums, galleries, and festivals throughout the year. The city is home to the Picasso Museum, which showcases an extensive collection of the artist's work, as well as the Malaga Cathedral, a stunning example of Renaissance architecture. The city's historic center is a maze of narrow streets and alleys, filled with charming shops, restaurants, and bars.
