[SECTION] What Is Valencia? [/SECTION]
Valencia is a region in the eastern part of Spain, bordering the Mediterranean Sea to the east, and the autonomous communities of Catalonia to the north, Aragon to the west, and Murcia to the south. The region is divided into three provinces: Valencia, Alicante, and Castellón. Valencia is known for its beautiful beaches, such as the **Costa Blanca** and **Costa del Azahar**, as well as its vibrant city life, with a rich cultural heritage and a strong sense of identity. The region is home to a diverse landscape, ranging from the coastal plains to the mountainous interior, and is drained by several major rivers, including the **Júcar**, **Turia**, and **Segura**.

The city of Valencia is the capital and largest city of the region, and is known for its stunning architecture, including the **City of Arts and Sciences**, a complex of modern museums, parks, and aquariums. The city is also famous for its traditional cuisine, including dishes such as **paella**, **fideuà**, and **horchata**. The region is also home to several important festivals and traditions, including the **Falles** and **Tomatina**, which attract visitors from all over the world.

Valencia has a strong economy, with a mix of industries including tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing. The region is also home to several important universities and research institutions, including the **University of Valencia** and the **Polytechnic University of Valencia**. The region has a well-developed transportation network, with several major airports, including **Valencia Airport** and **Alicante Airport**, as well as a high-speed rail link to Madrid and other major cities in Spain.
