Valencia is generally a safe city to visit, with low crime rates compared to other major cities in Europe.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Valencia is a popular tourist destination, known for its beautiful beaches, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant nightlife. The city has a strong police presence, and visitors can feel safe walking around the city, even at night. However, as with any major city, there are some areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The city's low crime rate and efficient emergency services make it an attractive destination for tourists.

Valencia's safety is also reflected in its low rates of violent crime, with most crimes being petty theft or pickpocketing. The city's tourist areas are generally well-patrolled, and visitors can feel comfortable exploring the city's many attractions, such as the City of Arts and Sciences, the Central Market, and the Turia Gardens. However, visitors should still be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night, and take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching are common in crowded areas, such as train stations and tourist attractions.
- Scams targeting tourists, such as the "gold ring" scam, where a person tries to sell you a fake gold ring, are common in popular areas.
- Be wary of overly friendly strangers who approach you with a deal that seems too good to be true.
- Be careful when using ATMs, as card skimming and scams are possible.
- Be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night, and avoid walking in dimly lit or deserted areas.
- Keep an eye on your drinks and food when in bars and restaurants, as drink spiking and food tampering are possible.
- Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi, as it may not be secure.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Valencia, but it is recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Valencia, but bottled water is widely available.
- **Medical Facilities:** Valencia has a well-developed healthcare system, with many high-quality hospitals and medical facilities available.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are easily accessible and well-stocked, with many open 24 hours.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night.
- Keep valuables secure and out of sight, and consider using a money belt or a secure bag.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead.
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuables.
- Be cautious when using public transportation, and avoid traveling alone at night.
- Keep your phone charged and with you at all times, and consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot.
- Learn some basic Spanish phrases, such as "¿Dónde está...?" (where is...?) and "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (how much does it cost?), to help you navigate the city.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Valencia is generally a safe city for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong police presence.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas.
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, such as the City Center or Beaches, and avoid walking alone in neighborhoods with higher crime rates.
- Keep in touch with family and friends back home, and consider checking in regularly to let them know you are safe.
