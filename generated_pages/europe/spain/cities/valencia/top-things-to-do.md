# Top Things to Do in Valencia — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Valencia, visit the iconic **City of Arts and Sciences**, explore the historic **Barrio del Carmen**, and indulge in the local cuisine, including the famous **paella**.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **traditional Valencian cooking class** to learn the secrets of authentic paella and other local dishes
- Explore the **Albufera Natural Park**, a protected wetland area and home to a variety of bird species
- Visit a **local orange grove**, where you can learn about the production process and taste the fresh fruit
- Attend a **Fallas festival**, a unique and colorful celebration held in March
- Go on a **bike tour** along the Turia Gardens, exploring the city's scenic parks and historic landmarks
- Take a **sunset cruise** along the Valencia coast, offering breathtaking views of the city and the sea
- Learn about the **traditional Valencian crafts**, such as ceramics and textiles, at a local workshop

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Turia Gardens**, a 9 km long urban park perfect for walking and cycling
- **Albufera Natural Park**, a protected wetland area and home to a variety of bird species
- **Valencia Beach**, a popular urban beach with clear waters and scenic promenade
- **Montaña de la Serra Calderona**, a mountain range offering hiking trails and stunning views
- **Jardines del Real**, a historic garden featuring beautiful fountains and sculptures
- **Lloriguilla Beach**, a secluded beach located in a natural cove, perfect for swimming and snorkeling

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Valencia Cathedral**, a 13th-century Gothic cathedral with impressive bell tower
- **Museum of Fine Arts**, featuring an extensive collection of Spanish and European art
- **IVAM Modern Art Museum**, showcasing an impressive collection of modern and contemporary art
- **Silk Exchange**, a 15th-century Gothic building and UNESCO World Heritage Site
- **Palau de la Música**, a stunning example of modernist architecture, hosting various concerts and events
- **Central Market**, a vibrant market offering fresh produce, flowers, and local specialties

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **Barrio del Carmen**, a historic neighborhood with narrow streets, charming shops, and lively nightlife
- **El Miguelete**, a 14th-century bell tower offering stunning views of the city
- **Jardines del Real**, a historic garden featuring beautiful fountains and sculptures
- **Plaza Redonda**, a charming square with unique shops and cafes
- **La Lonja de la Seda**, a 15th-century Gothic building and UNESCO World Heritage Site

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
1. **Alicante**, located 220 km south of Valencia, featuring a beautiful beach and historic castle (approx. 2 hours by car or train)
2. **Peñíscola**, located 100 km north of Valencia, featuring a stunning medieval castle and beautiful beaches (approx. 1.5 hours by car or train)
3. **Xàtiva**, located 60 km south of Valencia, featuring a historic castle and stunning views of the surrounding countryside (approx. 1 hour by car or train)
4. **Sagunto**, located 30 km north of Valencia, featuring a historic Roman castle and stunning coastal views (approx. 30 minutes by car or train)
5. **Morella**, located 100 km north of Valencia, featuring a historic walled town and stunning mountain views (approx. 1.5 hours by car or train)
