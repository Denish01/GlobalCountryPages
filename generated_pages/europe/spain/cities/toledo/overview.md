[SECTION] What Is Toledo? [/SECTION]
Toledo is a **municipality** in the autonomous community of Castile-La Mancha, Spain, and it is not a country. It is situated in the central part of the Iberian Peninsula, about 70 kilometers southwest of Madrid. The city is nestled in a valley surrounded by the Tagus River and is known for its narrow cobblestone streets, historic landmarks, and stunning architecture. Toledo has a long and complex history, having been inhabited by various cultures, including the Romans, Visigoths, and Moors, each leaving their mark on the city.

Toledo's strategic location has made it an important cultural and economic center throughout history. The city's rich cultural heritage is reflected in its architecture, art, and traditions. Toledo is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world who come to explore its historic center, a **UNESCO World Heritage Site**. The city is also known for its traditional crafts, such as sword-making, damascene, and pottery.

Toledo's history dates back to the Roman era, and over the centuries, it has been influenced by various cultures, including the Moors, who introduced their own language, customs, and architectural styles. The city's unique blend of cultures is reflected in its cuisine, festivals, and traditions. Toledo is a city that seamlessly blends its rich history with modern life, making it a fascinating place to visit and explore.
