# Where to Stay in Toledo — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Toledo, consider the historic city center and surrounding neighborhoods, which offer a range of accommodations to suit different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a brief overview:
**Historic City Center** (Toledo)
Best For: luxury, history buffs, families
Vibe: The historic city center is a maze of narrow streets and historic landmarks, offering an immersive experience in Toledo's rich history and culture. The area is generally quiet and safe, with plenty of restaurants and shops nearby.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: proximity to major landmarks, historic charm, variety of restaurants and shops
Cons: narrow streets can be confusing to navigate, limited parking options

**Santa Teresa** (Toledo)
Best For: backpackers, budget travelers
Vibe: Santa Teresa is a lively neighborhood with a mix of budget-friendly accommodations, bars, and restaurants, making it a great option for young travelers. The area is within walking distance to the city center and has a vibrant atmosphere.
Price Range: $20-80/night
Pros: affordable prices, lively atmosphere, proximity to city center
Cons: can be noisy at night, limited amenities

**Bisagra** (Toledo)
Best For: business travelers, comfort seekers
Vibe: Bisagra is a modern neighborhood with a range of hotels and amenities, making it a great option for business travelers or those seeking comfort and convenience. The area is close to the city center and has easy access to public transportation.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: modern amenities, easy access to public transportation, variety of restaurants
Cons: less historic charm, can be busy with traffic

**San Juan de los Reyes** (Toledo)
Best For: families, mid-range travelers
Vibe: San Juan de los Reyes is a charming neighborhood with a mix of historic and modern buildings, offering a relaxed atmosphere and easy access to the city center. The area has plenty of parks and green spaces, making it a great option for families.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: family-friendly atmosphere, historic charm, proximity to city center
Cons: limited nightlife options, can be quiet at night

**Polígono** (Toledo)
Best For: budget travelers, long-stay visitors
Vibe: Polígono is a residential neighborhood with a range of budget-friendly accommodations, including apartments and guesthouses. The area is a bit further from the city center, but has easy access to public transportation.
Price Range: $30-80/night
Pros: affordable prices, easy access to public transportation, local amenities
Cons: limited historic charm, can be far from city center
