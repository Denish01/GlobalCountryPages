Toledo is situated in the autonomous community of Castile-La Mancha, approximately 70 kilometers southwest of Madrid, the capital city of Spain. The city is nestled in a valley, surrounded by the Tagus River, which has played a significant role in shaping the city's history and landscape. Toledo is landlocked, with no direct access to the sea, but its proximity to the Spanish capital and the country's extensive transportation network make it easily accessible by road, rail, or air.

Toledo's borders are shared with the surrounding provinces of Madrid and Castile-La Mancha, with the Sierra de Guadarrama mountain range located to the north and the Montes de Toledo range to the south. The city's strategic location has made it an important cultural and commercial center throughout history, with various civilizations, including the Romans, Visigoths, and Moors, leaving their mark on the city's architecture, art, and traditions.
