# Getting Around Toledo — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Toledo, a city in Spain, you can use a combination of public transportation options such as buses and trains, as well as taxis and ride-hailing services, to navigate the city and its surrounding areas.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Toledo is located in the central region of Spain, and the closest international airport is Adolfo Suárez Madrid–Barajas Airport (MAD), which is about 80 kilometers away from the city. From the airport, you can take a taxi, bus, or train to get to Toledo. The airport is a major hub for many airlines, including Iberia, Air Europa, and Ryanair, and offers direct flights to many destinations in Europe, America, and other parts of the world. You can also reach Toledo by car or bus from other cities in Spain, such as Madrid, which is connected to the city by a high-speed train line and several highways.

If you are coming from other parts of Europe, you can take a train or bus to Madrid and then transfer to a local train or bus to get to Toledo. The city is also accessible by car, and there are several parking options available in the city center. However, be aware that the historic center of Toledo is a pedestrian zone, and cars are not allowed to enter the area. You can park your car in one of the parking lots located outside the city center and then walk or take a taxi to your destination.
