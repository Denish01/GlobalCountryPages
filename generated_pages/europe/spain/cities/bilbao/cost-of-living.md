# Cost of Living in Bilbao - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Bilbao is generally considered to be moderate, with prices comparable to other major cities in Western Europe, but slightly lower than in larger Spanish cities like Madrid or Barcelona.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $60-100 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $100-180 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $180-300 USD
Currency: EUR
Tipping Custom: not expected, but rounding up the bill or leaving 5-10% is common
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Bilbao? [/SECTION]
Bilbao, the largest city in the Basque Country, is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning architecture, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant food scene. Compared to other European cities, Bilbao is relatively affordable, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation falling in the mid-range category. However, prices can vary depending on the time of year, with peak season (June to September) being the most expensive. Overall, Bilbao offers a great balance of quality and affordability, making it an attractive destination for travelers and expats alike.

In comparison to its neighbors, Bilbao is generally more expensive than smaller towns in the Basque Country, but less expensive than major cities like Madrid or Barcelona. The city's unique cultural identity and stunning natural surroundings make it a desirable place to visit or live, and its relatively moderate cost of living makes it an attractive option for those looking to experience the best of Spain without breaking the bank.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting during the off-season (October to May) to take advantage of lower prices for accommodation and tourist attractions.
- Take advantage of the city's affordable public transportation system, including buses and metro lines.
- Eat at local eateries and pintxos bars, which offer a wide range of delicious and affordable food options.
- Explore the city's many free museums and cultural attractions, such as the Guggenheim Museum's free outdoor exhibits.
- Shop at local markets, such as the Mercado de la Ribera, for fresh produce and unique souvenirs.
- Look for accommodation options outside of the city center, which can offer better value for money and a more authentic local experience.
