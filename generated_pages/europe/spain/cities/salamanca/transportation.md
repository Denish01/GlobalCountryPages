# Getting Around Salamanca — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Salamanca, you can use a combination of public transportation options, including buses, trains, and taxis, as well as walking and cycling, which are convenient and cost-effective ways to explore the city.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Salamanca is located in the western part of Spain, and the closest international airport is Adolfo Suárez Madrid–Barajas Airport (MAD), which is about 200 kilometers away. You can take a bus or train from the airport to Salamanca, or rent a car and drive. Another option is to fly into Valladolid Airport (VLL), which is about 100 kilometers away and offers connections to several European cities. From the airport, you can take a taxi or bus to Salamanca. Overland entry points include bus and train stations, which connect Salamanca to other major cities in Spain and Europe.

If you prefer to travel by land, you can take a bus or train from nearby cities such as Madrid, Valladolid, or Lisbon. The bus station in Salamanca is located in the city center, and it offers connections to many other cities in Spain and Portugal. The train station is also located in the city center and offers high-speed connections to Madrid and other major cities in Spain. You can book your tickets online or at the station, and it's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights in Spain are relatively affordable and convenient, with several airlines operating within the country. Iberia, Vueling, and Ryanair are some of the main airlines that operate domestic flights in Spain. You can fly from Madrid or other major cities to Salamanca, although there are no direct flights to Salamanca, you can fly to Valladolid or Madrid and then take a bus or train to Salamanca. Booking in advance is recommended, and you can expect to pay around 50-100 EUR for a one-way ticket. It's also worth noting that domestic flights may not be the most cost-effective option, especially if you're traveling with a group or have a lot of luggage.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Spain has an extensive rail network, with high-speed trains connecting major cities. The train station in Salamanca is located in the city center and offers connections to Madrid, Valladolid, and other major cities in Spain. You can take a high-speed train from Madrid to Salamanca, which takes around 2.5 hours. The train network is operated by Renfe, and you can book your tickets online or at the station. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons. The cost of a one-way ticket can range from 20-100 EUR, depending on the class and type of train.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Buses are a convenient and affordable way to get around Salamanca and its surroundings. Several bus companies operate in the city, including ALSA and FlixBus, which offer connections to other major cities in Spain and Europe. You can take a bus from the bus station in Salamanca to nearby cities such as Madrid, Valladolid, or Lisbon. The cost of a one-way ticket can range from 10-50 EUR, depending on the destination and class of service. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Salamanca, you need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The road quality in Salamanca is generally good, with well-maintained highways and city streets.
- Fuel costs can range from 1-2 EUR per liter, depending on the type of fuel and location.
- Car rental companies such as Europcar, Hertz, and Avis operate in Salamanca, and you can expect to pay around 40-100 EUR per day for a rental car.
- Hazards to watch out for include narrow streets in the city center and limited parking options.
- It's recommended to park your car outside the city center and use public transportation or walk to get around.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of pickpocketing and scams in tourist areas, especially in crowded buses and trains.
- Use a money belt or a secure bag to carry your valuables.
- Download the ALSA or Renfe app to book your bus or train tickets in advance.
- Use a taxi or ride-hailing service at night, especially if you're traveling alone.
- Be mindful of traffic rules and pedestrian zones in the city center.
- Consider purchasing a Salamanca tourist card, which includes public transportation and discounts to attractions.
- Learn some basic Spanish phrases, such as "¿Dónde está...?" (where is...) and "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (how much does it cost?), to help you navigate the city.
- Use cash or credit cards to pay for transportation, as not all providers accept mobile payments.
