# Cost of Living in Granada - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Granada can range from affordable to moderate, with daily budgets for backpackers starting at around $30-50 USD, mid-range travelers at $60-100 USD, and comfort seekers at $120-200 USD. 

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate

Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $60-100 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $120-200 USD
Currency: EUR
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 5-10% in restaurants and bars
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Granada? [/SECTION]
Granada, a city in southern Spain, is known for its rich cultural heritage and historic landmarks like the Alhambra. Compared to other major European cities, Granada has a relatively moderate cost of living. However, prices can vary depending on the time of year and the location within the city. The city's affordability makes it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. When compared to its neighbors, such as Madrid and Barcelona, Granada tends to be more affordable in terms of accommodation and food.

The cost of living in Granada is also influenced by its location in the autonomous community of Andalusia, which has a lower cost of living compared to other regions in Spain. This makes Granada an excellent choice for those looking to experience Spanish culture without breaking the bank. Overall, Granada offers a great balance between affordability and quality of life, making it an excellent destination for travelers and expats.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting during the off-season (October to May) to avoid high prices and tourist crowds.

- Eat at local tapas bars and restaurants, which offer a wide range of delicious and affordable food options.
- Explore the city's historic center on foot, which is free and offers a unique perspective on the city's culture and architecture.
- Look for accommodation options outside of the city center, which can be more affordable and offer a more authentic experience.
- Take a free walking tour of the city to get a sense of the layout and learn about its history and culture.
