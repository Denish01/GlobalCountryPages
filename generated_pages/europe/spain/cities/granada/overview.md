[SECTION] What Is Granada? [/SECTION]
Granada is a city and province located in the autonomous community of Andalusia, Spain. It is situated in the southeastern part of the Iberian Peninsula, at the foot of the Sierra Nevada mountains. The city is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and stunning architecture, including the famous Alhambra palace and fortress complex. Granada is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its unique blend of Moorish, Gothic, and Renaissance styles.

The city has a long and complex history, with various cultures and civilizations leaving their mark on the area. From the ancient Romans to the Moors, and later the Catholic Monarchs, each has contributed to the city's unique cultural and architectural landscape. Today, Granada is a thriving city, with a strong sense of identity and a deep connection to its history and heritage.

The province of Granada is also home to a diverse range of landscapes and ecosystems, including the Sierra Nevada National Park, which is home to the highest peak in continental Spain. The park is a popular destination for hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities, and is also home to a wide range of flora and fauna.
