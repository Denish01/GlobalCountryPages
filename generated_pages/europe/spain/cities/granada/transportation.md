# Getting Around Granada — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Granada, you can use a combination of public transportation, including buses and trains, as well as taxis and ride-hailing services, with the city's compact center also making it easy to explore on foot.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Granada is easily accessible by air, with the Federico García Lorca Granada-Jaén Airport, also known as Granada Airport, located about 15 kilometers west of the city center. The airport receives direct flights from several European cities, including London, Paris, and Amsterdam, as well as connections from major Spanish airports like Madrid and Barcelona. You can also reach Granada by bus or train from other cities in Spain, with the city's bus station and train station located near the city center. Overland entry points from neighboring countries are also available, with several bus companies operating routes from cities like Lisbon, Portugal, and Marseille, France.

The city's airport is well-connected to the city center, with several transportation options available, including taxis, buses, and ride-hailing services. The airport bus, known as the **Alsa Airport Bus**, runs regularly between the airport and the city center, with a journey time of about 30-40 minutes. You can also take a taxi or use a ride-hailing service, with the journey taking around 20-30 minutes depending on traffic. If you're traveling from other cities in Spain, you can take a bus or train, with several companies operating routes to Granada, including **Renfe** and **Alsa**.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Granada has a moderate domestic air network, with several flights per day to major Spanish cities like Madrid and Barcelona. You can fly with airlines like **Iberia** and **Vueling**, with prices starting from around $50-$100 for a one-way ticket. To book a domestic flight, you can use the airlines' websites or a travel agency, with online booking available up to a few hours before the flight. It's a good idea to book in advance to get the best prices, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Granada has a moderate rail network, with several trains per day to major Spanish cities like Madrid and Seville. The city's train station, **Granada Train Station**, is located near the city center, with several train companies operating routes, including **Renfe**. You can take a high-speed train, known as the **AVE**, to Madrid, with the journey taking around 2.5 hours. To book a train ticket, you can use the train company's website or a ticket machine at the station, with prices starting from around $30-$100 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Granada has an extensive network of buses and coaches, with several companies operating routes to other cities in Spain. You can take a bus from the city's bus station, **Granada Bus Station**, to destinations like Madrid, Seville, and Barcelona, with prices starting from around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket. The journey times vary depending on the route and traffic, but you can expect to take around 2-4 hours to reach nearby cities. Some of the major bus companies operating in Granada include **Alsa** and **FlixBus**.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Granada, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an **International Driving Permit**.
- The road quality in Granada is generally good, with well-maintained highways and city streets.
- Fuel costs in Granada are around $1.20-$1.50 per liter, depending on the type of fuel.
- You can rent a car from several companies, including **Europcar** and **Sixt**, with prices starting from around $40-$60 per day.
- Be aware of the **Zona de Bajo Emissions**, a low-emissions zone in the city center where only certain vehicles are allowed to enter.
- Parking in Granada can be challenging, especially in the city center, so be prepared to pay for parking or use a parking garage.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **scams** in tourist areas, especially in crowded buses and trains.
- Use a **reputable taxi company** or ride-hailing service to avoid overcharging.
- Download the **Granada Tourist App** to get information on public transportation and tourist attractions.
- Use a **cashless payment method**, such as a credit card or mobile payment, to avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
- Be mindful of **siesta time**, when many businesses and public transportation services may be closed or reduced.
- Consider purchasing a **Granada Tourist Card**, which includes free public transportation and discounts on attractions.
- Be prepared for **traffic congestion** in the city center, especially during peak hours.
