[SECTION] What Is San Sebastian? [/SECTION]
San Sebastian, also known as Donostia in Basque, is a city situated in the province of Gipuzkoa, in the autonomous community of the Basque Country, Spain. It is located on the coast of the Bay of Biscay, about 20 kilometers from the French border. The city is nestled in a beautiful bay, surrounded by mountains and beaches, making it a popular tourist destination. San Sebastian is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its historic center, which features a mix of medieval and modern architecture, and its vibrant cultural scene, which includes numerous festivals and events throughout the year.

San Sebastian is also famous for its gastronomy, with a wide range of traditional Basque dishes, including pintxos, which are similar to tapas, and fresh seafood. The city is home to several Michelin-starred restaurants, and its culinary scene is considered one of the best in Spain. In addition to its cultural and culinary attractions, San Sebastian is also a popular destination for outdoor activities, such as surfing, hiking, and cycling.

The city has a strong sense of identity and a unique cultural heritage, which is reflected in its language, customs, and traditions. The Basque language, also known as Euskara, is spoken by a significant portion of the population, and the city is home to several cultural institutions, including the San Sebastian City Hall, the Kursaal Congress Centre, and the Tabakalera International Centre for Contemporary Culture.
