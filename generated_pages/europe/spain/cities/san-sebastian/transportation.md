# Getting Around San Sebastian — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in San Sebastian, you can use a combination of public transportation options, including buses, trains, and taxis, as well as walk or bike through the city's pedestrian-friendly streets.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
San Sebastian is easily accessible by air, with two nearby airports: Bilbao Airport (BIO) and San Sebastian Airport (EAS). Bilbao Airport is the larger of the two and offers more international flights, including direct connections to major European cities like London, Paris, and Amsterdam. San Sebastian Airport, on the other hand, is smaller and primarily serves domestic flights, although it does offer some international connections to nearby cities. From both airports, you can take a taxi or bus to get to San Sebastian, with the journey taking around 30-60 minutes depending on traffic.

In addition to flying, you can also reach San Sebastian by bus or train from other cities in Spain and Europe. The city is well-connected to the rest of Spain by highway, and several bus companies operate daily services from major cities like Madrid and Barcelona. The train network is also extensive, with regular services to and from nearby cities like Bilbao and Pamplona. If you're coming from outside of Europe, you may need to take a connecting flight or train to reach San Sebastian.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
If you need to travel within Spain, there are several domestic airlines that operate flights from San Sebastian Airport, including Iberia, Vueling, and Ryanair. The main routes from San Sebastian include flights to Madrid, Barcelona, and the Canary Islands. Booking in advance is usually the best way to get a good deal, and you can expect to pay around $50-100 for a one-way ticket, depending on the time of year and how far in advance you book. It's also worth noting that some airlines may offer discounts or promotions for certain routes or travel periods, so it's worth checking the airlines' websites or a flight comparison website to find the best deals.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
San Sebastian has an extensive rail network, with regular services to and from nearby cities like Bilbao and Pamplona. The train station is located in the city center, making it easy to get to and from your destination. The trains are generally modern and comfortable, with food and drink services available on board. You can book tickets online in advance through the Renfe website, which is the main train operator in Spain. High-speed trains are also available on some routes, which can significantly reduce travel times. For example, the journey from San Sebastian to Madrid takes around 5 hours by high-speed train, compared to 7-8 hours by car or bus.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several intercity bus companies that operate services from San Sebastian to other cities in Spain and Europe. The main bus station is located in the city center, and from there you can catch buses to destinations like Madrid, Barcelona, and the French border. The bus companies that operate from San Sebastian include ALSA, FlixBus, and Eurolines, among others. The quality of the buses varies depending on the company and the route, but most are modern and comfortable, with air conditioning and onboard amenities like Wi-Fi and food and drink services. The cost of a ticket will depend on the route and the time of year, but you can expect to pay around $10-30 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in San Sebastian, you will need a valid driver's license from your home country, which must be held for at least one year.
- The road quality in and around San Sebastian is generally good, with well-maintained highways and urban roads.
- Fuel costs in Spain are relatively high, with gasoline and diesel prices averaging around $1.50-2.00 per liter.
- If you plan to rent a car, it's a good idea to book in advance to get the best rates, and be sure to check the rental agreement for any additional fees or charges.
- When driving in San Sebastian, be aware of the city's narrow streets and pedestrianized areas, and take care when parking, as fines can be steep for illegal parking.
- It's also a good idea to have a GPS or map with you, as the city's streets can be confusing, especially for visitors who don't speak Spanish.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of peak hour traffic in San Sebastian, which can cause significant delays, especially during rush hour.
- Consider purchasing a **Tarjeta de Transporte Publico**, which is a rechargeable public transport card that can be used on buses and trains.
- Download the **Moovit** app, which provides real-time information and route planning for public transport in San Sebastian.
- Be mindful of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, and keep a close eye on your belongings at all times.
- If you're taking a taxi, make sure to use a licensed operator and always check the meter is running before you start your journey.
- Consider visiting the **San Sebastian Tourist Information Office**, which can provide you with maps, guides, and advice on getting around the city.
- Don't forget to validate your ticket before boarding public transport, as fines can be steep for unvalidated tickets.
- Be respectful of local customs and rules, especially when driving or cycling, and always follow traffic laws and regulations.
