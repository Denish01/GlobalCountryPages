# Where to Stay in San Sebastian — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting San Sebastian, the best areas to stay are in the city center, near the beach, or in the historic neighborhoods, offering a range of options for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, San Sebastian offers a unique experience:
**Parte Vieja (Old Town)** (San Sebastian)
Best For: backpacker / nightlife
Vibe: Parte Vieja is the historic heart of San Sebastian, filled with narrow streets, bars, and restaurants, offering a lively and vibrant atmosphere. The area is perfect for those who want to experience the city's nightlife and cultural events.
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: central location, plenty of dining and entertainment options, rich history and culture. 
Cons: can be noisy, limited parking options.

**Gros** (San Sebastian)
Best For: luxury / family
Vibe: Gros is a trendy neighborhood located on the other side of the Urumea River, known for its upscale shops, restaurants, and bars. The area offers a more relaxed atmosphere, with easy access to the city center and the beach.
Price Range: $120-250/night
Pros: modern amenities, close to the beach, variety of dining options. 
Cons: can be pricey, limited budget options.

**Antiguo** (San Sebastian)
Best For: family / business
Vibe: Antiguo is a charming neighborhood located near the beach, offering a mix of traditional and modern architecture. The area is known for its peaceful atmosphere, making it ideal for families and business travelers.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: close to the beach, family-friendly, variety of accommodation options. 
Cons: can be quiet, limited nightlife options.

**Aiete** (San Sebastian)
Best For: luxury / business
Vibe: Aiete is an upscale neighborhood located in the hills of San Sebastian, offering stunning views of the city and the bay. The area is known for its modern architecture, luxurious amenities, and peaceful atmosphere.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: luxurious amenities, stunning views, peaceful atmosphere. 
Cons: can be pricey, limited budget options.

**Amara** (San Sebastian)
Best For: budget / backpacker
Vibe: Amara is a lively neighborhood located near the city center, known for its affordable accommodation options, restaurants, and bars. The area offers a mix of traditional and modern culture, making it ideal for budget-conscious travelers.
Price Range: $60-120/night
Pros: affordable prices, lively atmosphere, close to the city center. 
Cons: can be noisy, limited luxury options.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- San Sebastian is a relatively safe city, but take normal precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night.
- Use licensed taxis or public transportation to get around the city.
- Stay near the city center or in well-lit areas for easy access to amenities and services.
