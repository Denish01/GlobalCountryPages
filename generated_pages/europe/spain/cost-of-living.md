# Cost of Living in Spain - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Spain can vary greatly depending on the region, city, and lifestyle, but on average, it is considered to be moderate, with daily expenses ranging from $60-150 per person.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $60-100 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $100-150 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $150-250 USD
Currency: EUR
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 5-10% in restaurants and bars is common
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Spain? [/SECTION]
Spain is a popular tourist destination and a great place to live, with a relatively low cost of living compared to other Western European countries. The cost of living in Spain is lower than in countries like France, Germany, and the UK, but higher than in Eastern European countries. Compared to its neighbors, Spain is more expensive than Portugal, but less expensive than France. The cost of living in Spain can vary greatly depending on the region, with cities like Madrid and Barcelona being more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas.

Overall, Spain offers a great balance of quality of life, culture, and affordability, making it an attractive destination for tourists, expats, and digital nomads. The country has a well-developed infrastructure, a rich cultural heritage, and a vibrant social scene, with many restaurants, bars, and festivals to enjoy. However, the cost of living in Spain can be higher than in some other countries, especially when it comes to housing and transportation.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider living outside of city centers, where rent and living expenses are lower.
- Eat at local restaurants and tapas bars, which are often cheaper and more authentic than touristy places.
- Use public transportation, which is efficient and affordable in Spain.
- Take advantage of free walking tours and museum visits, which are often available in cities like Madrid and Barcelona.
- Shop at local markets and supermarkets, which offer fresh produce and products at lower prices than touristy shops.
- Learn some basic Spanish phrases, which can help you navigate and negotiate prices more effectively.
