# Where to Stay in Serbia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Serbia, the best places to stay are in the capital city of Belgrade and other major cities like Novi Sad and Niš, which offer a range of accommodations to suit different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here are the details:
**Vračar** (Belgrade)
Best For: luxury travelers
Vibe: Upscale and sophisticated, with many high-end restaurants and shops, Vračar is a great area to experience the best of Belgrade's nightlife and culture.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: close to major attractions, many dining options, safe and clean
Cons: can be noisy at night, parking can be difficult

**Savamala** (Belgrade)
Best For: backpackers and young travelers
Vibe: Trendy and artistic, Savamala is a former industrial area turned hip neighborhood with many bars, clubs, and street art.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: affordable, lively atmosphere, many restaurants and bars
Cons: can be loud and crowded, limited amenities

**Downtown** (Novi Sad)
Best For: families and business travelers
Vibe: Historic and charming, Novi Sad's downtown area is filled with beautiful architecture, museums, and cultural events.
Price Range: $40-120/night
Pros: central location, many amenities, family-friendly
Cons: can be crowded during peak season, limited parking

**Niš City Center** (Niš)
Best For: history buffs and couples
Vibe: Historic and romantic, Niš's city center is filled with ancient ruins, museums, and cultural landmarks.
Price Range: $30-80/night
Pros: rich history, many landmarks, affordable
Cons: limited nightlife, can be quiet

**Zemun** (Belgrade)
Best For: families and those who want a quieter stay
Vibe: Quaint and suburban, Zemun is a charming neighborhood with a riverside promenade and many parks.
Price Range: $30-70/night
Pros: peaceful atmosphere, many parks, affordable
Cons: limited nightlife, far from city center

**Kalemegdan** (Belgrade)
Best For: luxury travelers and history buffs
Vibe: Historic and upscale, Kalemegdan is a fortified neighborhood with many museums, restaurants, and stunning views of the Danube and Sava rivers.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: stunning views, historic landmarks, many amenities
Cons: can be crowded, limited parking

**Dorćol** (Belgrade)
Best For: young travelers and backpackers
Vibe: Trendy and lively, Dorćol is a neighborhood with many bars, clubs, and restaurants, and a vibrant nightlife.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: affordable, lively atmosphere, many restaurants and bars
Cons: can be loud and crowded, limited amenities
