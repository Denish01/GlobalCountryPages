[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Serbia is located in the southeastern part of Europe, in the Balkan Peninsula. It is a landlocked country, situated in the central part of the peninsula, and it borders several countries, including Hungary to the north, Romania and Bulgaria to the east, North Macedonia and Kosovo to the south, and Montenegro, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Croatia to the west. The country's unique location at the crossroads of Europe has made it an important cultural and trade hub throughout history. The Danube and Sava rivers, which flow through the country, have also played a significant role in shaping Serbia's geography and economy.

The country's borders are a result of its complex history, with various empires and states vying for control over the region. Today, Serbia is a sovereign state, with a diverse landscape and a rich cultural heritage. Its location in the heart of the Balkans makes it an attractive destination for tourists and travelers, with its vibrant cities, stunning natural beauty, and historic landmarks. From the rolling hills and fertile plains of the north to the rugged mountains and scenic valleys of the south, Serbia's diverse landscape offers something for everyone.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Serbia's terrain is characterized by a mix of mountains, hills, and plains. The country can be divided into three main regions: the **Pannonian Plain** in the north, the **Šumadija** region in the central part of the country, and the **Dinaric Alps** in the south. The Pannonian Plain is a fertile and densely populated region, with the Danube and Sava rivers flowing through it. The Šumadija region is a hilly area, with a mix of forests, pastures, and agricultural land. The Dinaric Alps, which stretch across the western part of the country, are a rugged and scenic mountain range, with peaks reaching over 2,000 meters.

The country's landscape is also dotted with numerous rivers, lakes, and wetlands, which provide a habitat for a wide range of flora and fauna. The **Danube River**, which forms part of Serbia's northern border, is one of the longest and most important rivers in Europe, and it plays a significant role in the country's economy and ecosystem. The **Kopaonik** mountain range, which is located in the south of the country, is a popular ski resort and a haven for nature lovers, with its stunning scenery and diverse wildlife.

The country's diverse landscape has also created a range of different microclimates, with the climate varying from the north to the south. The northern part of the country has a more **temperate climate**, with cold winters and warm summers, while the southern part of the country has a more **Mediterranean climate**, with mild winters and hot summers. This diversity of climate and landscape has made Serbia a popular destination for tourists and travelers, with its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant cities.
