[SECTION] Overview: Serbia vs Kosovo [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Serbia and Kosovo varies significantly, with Serbia being the more affordable option. Serbia has a higher GDP per capita and a more developed economy, which contributes to its lower cost of living. In contrast, Kosovo has a smaller economy and is still recovering from the aftermath of the Yugoslav Wars, resulting in higher prices for certain goods and services. Overall, Serbia offers better value for money, with prices around 15-20% lower than in Kosovo.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Serbia has a more diverse economy, with a strong service sector, industry, and agriculture, which helps to keep prices low. Kosovo, on the other hand, has a smaller and more limited economy, with a greater reliance on imports, which drives up prices. Additionally, Serbia has a more developed infrastructure, including transportation and telecommunications, which makes it easier and cheaper to get around and access essential services.
