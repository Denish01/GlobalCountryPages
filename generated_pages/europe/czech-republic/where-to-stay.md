# Where to Stay in Czech Republic — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in the Czech Republic, consider the historic city of Prague as a top destination, with its various neighborhoods offering a range of accommodations for different types of travelers, from budget-friendly options to luxury hotels. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, the following characteristics are considered:

**Old Town** (Prague)
Best For: Luxury, Family
Vibe: Old Town is the heart of Prague's historic center, offering a unique and authentic experience with its cobblestone streets, medieval architecture, and lively atmosphere. The area is perfect for those who want to be close to major attractions like the Charles Bridge and Old Town Square.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Central location, historic architecture, plenty of restaurants and shops
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited parking options

**New Town** (Prague)
Best For: Backpacker, Business
Vibe: New Town is a bustling area with a mix of modern and historic buildings, offering a wide range of accommodations, restaurants, and entertainment options. The area is great for those who want to be close to the city center but also have easy access to public transportation.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Affordable prices, convenient location, plenty of amenities
Cons: Less historic character compared to Old Town, can be busy and noisy

**Smíchov** (Prague)
Best For: Family, Business
Vibe: Smíchov is a charming neighborhood located on the west bank of the Vltava River, offering a more laid-back atmosphere and stunning views of the city. The area is perfect for families and business travelers who want to be close to the city center but also have easy access to nature and outdoor activities.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Scenic views, convenient location, plenty of parks and green spaces
Cons: Limited nightlife options, can be quiet and residential

**Karlín** (Prague)
Best For: Luxury, Business
Vibe: Karlín is a trendy and upscale neighborhood located just east of the city center, offering a unique blend of historic and modern architecture. The area is great for luxury travelers and business professionals who want to be close to the city's financial and commercial districts.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Upscale amenities, convenient location, plenty of restaurants and bars
Cons: Can be expensive, limited budget-friendly options

**Brno City Center** (Brno)
Best For: Backpacker, Family
Vibe: Brno is the second-largest city in the Czech Republic, offering a more laid-back and affordable alternative to Prague. The city center is filled with historic buildings, cultural attractions, and a lively atmosphere, making it perfect for backpackers and families.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Affordable prices, rich history and culture, convenient location
Cons: Limited luxury options, can be quiet and residential

**Český Krumlov** (Český Krumlov)
Best For: Luxury, Family
Vibe: Český Krumlov is a picturesque town located in southern Bohemia, famous for its medieval castle and charming old town. The area is perfect for luxury travelers and families who want to experience the authentic beauty of the Czech countryside.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Unique and historic architecture, scenic views, plenty of outdoor activities
Cons: Limited amenities and services, can be quiet and residential
