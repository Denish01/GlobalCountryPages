[SECTION] Overview: Czech Republic vs Germany [/SECTION]
The Czech Republic and Germany are two neighboring countries in Central Europe with distinct economies and cost of living. The Czech Republic has a lower cost of living compared to Germany, making it an attractive destination for expats, students, and retirees. The country's GDP per capita is significantly lower than Germany's, which contributes to the overall lower cost of living. However, both countries have a high standard of living, with access to quality education, healthcare, and infrastructure.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in the cost of living. Germany is a major economic power in Europe, with a strong industry and high demand for labor, which drives up prices. In contrast, the Czech Republic has a smaller economy, but it is still a popular destination for foreign investment and tourism. The country's lower cost of living makes it an attractive option for those looking to relocate or start a business. Overall, the Czech Republic is around 20-30% cheaper than Germany, making it a more affordable option for those looking to live in Central Europe.
