[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
The Czech Republic has a well-developed public transportation system, with buses, trams, and metro lines connecting major cities and towns. The quality of public transit is generally high, with frequent and reliable services. Most people get around using public transportation, with many cities having a comprehensive network of buses and trams. In larger cities like Prague, the metro system is also a popular mode of transport, with three lines covering much of the city.

In addition to public transportation, many people in the Czech Republic also use bicycles, with many cities having dedicated bike lanes and paths. Walking is also a popular mode of transport, with many cities having pedestrianized zones and historic centers. The country's compact size and relatively flat terrain make it easy to get around without a car, and many tourists and locals alike take advantage of the affordable and efficient public transportation system.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider purchasing a **public transportation card** for frequent travel, as it can save you money in the long run.
- Use **ride-hailing apps** like Bolt or Uber for convenient and affordable transportation, especially at night or in areas with limited public transportation.
- Be sure to **negotiate the fare** before getting into a taxi, especially if you're traveling to or from the airport.
- Take advantage of **free walking tours** in major cities to get a feel for the area and learn about its history and culture.
- Consider **renting a car** for longer trips or to explore the countryside, but be aware of **toll roads** and **parking fees**.
- Always **validate your ticket** before boarding public transportation to avoid fines and penalties.
