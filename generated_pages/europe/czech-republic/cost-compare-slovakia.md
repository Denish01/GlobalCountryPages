[SECTION] Overview: Czech Republic vs Slovakia [/SECTION]
The Czech Republic and Slovakia are two neighboring countries in Central Europe that share a common history and cultural heritage. However, they have distinct economic profiles, with the Czech Republic being a more developed and prosperous country. The Czech Republic has a higher GDP per capita and a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on services, manufacturing, and tourism. In contrast, Slovakia has a smaller economy, with a stronger focus on manufacturing and industry. As a result, the cost of living in the Czech Republic is generally higher than in Slovakia.

The overall cost of living in the Czech Republic is around 15-20% higher than in Slovakia, with the biggest differences in housing, food, and transportation costs. However, both countries offer a high standard of living and a rich cultural experience, making them attractive destinations for expats, students, and tourists. The Czech Republic is particularly popular for its vibrant cities, historic landmarks, and world-class beer, while Slovakia is known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, and traditional folk culture.
