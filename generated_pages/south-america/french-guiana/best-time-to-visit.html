The best time to visit French Guiana is during the dry season, from August to November, and from February to June, when the weather is more pleasant and there are fewer crowds.

Best months to visit French Guiana: August to November and February to June. Peak season: July to August and December. Budget season: April to May and September to November. Avoid: January and March due to heavy rainfall and February 15 to 21 for Carnival celebrations.

Seasons Overview

French Guiana has a tropical climate with two main seasons: the dry season and the wet season. The dry season, which runs from July to November and from February to June, is characterized by low humidity and moderate temperatures, making it the best time to visit the territory. The wet season, which runs from December to January and from March to May, is marked by heavy rainfall and high humidity, making it less ideal for travel. The climate varies across different regions of French Guiana, with the coastal areas experiencing a more moderate climate than the interior. The dry season is the best time to explore the territory's rainforests, beaches, and cultural attractions, while the wet season is better suited for nature lovers who want to see the lush vegetation and abundant wildlife.

The seasons in French Guiana have a significant impact on travel, with the dry season being the best time to visit the territory's many outdoor attractions, such as the Guiana Space Centre, the Kourou River, and the Tresor Nature Reserve. The wet season, on the other hand, can make it difficult to access some of these areas due to flooding and landslides. However, the wet season is also a great time to see the territory's waterfalls and rivers in full flow, making it a good time for kayaking and rafting.

The climate in French Guiana is also affected by the trade winds, which bring a cooling breeze to the coastal areas during the dry season. The trade winds also play a significant role in shaping the territory's climate, with the winds bringing warm air from the equator during the wet season. Overall, the best time to visit French Guiana depends on your preferences and what you want to do during your trip.

Regional Variations

  • The coastal areas, such as Cayenne and Kourou, have a more moderate climate than the interior, with a cooler temperature and lower humidity.
  • The interior, such as the Tresor Nature Reserve, has a more extreme climate, with high temperatures and high humidity during the wet season.
  • The Maroni River area has a unique microclimate, with a cooler temperature and lower humidity than the rest of the territory.
  • The Guiana Space Centre area has a dry and hot climate, with very little rainfall during the dry season.
  • The Amapari area has a more humid climate than the rest of the territory, with a higher rainfall during the wet season.

Practical Tips

  • Book your flights and accommodations well in advance, especially during peak season, to avoid high prices and availability issues.
  • Consider visiting during the shoulder season, from April to May and September to November, for lower prices and smaller crowds.
  • Pack light and breathable clothing, as well as waterproof gear, to prepare for the hot and humid climate.
  • Bring insect repellent and sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun and insects.
  • Avoid traveling during the wet season, from December to January and from March to May, if you're not comfortable with heavy rainfall and high humidity.
  • Research and book your tours and activities in advance, especially during peak season, to avoid availability issues.
