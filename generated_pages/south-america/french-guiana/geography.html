Location & Borders

French Guiana is situated on the northern coast of South America, where the continent meets the Atlantic Ocean. The territory is bounded by Brazil to the south and east, with a border that stretches for over 673 kilometers, and Suriname to the west, with a border of approximately 510 kilometers. To the north, French Guiana is bordered by the Atlantic Ocean, with a coastline that offers a mix of sandy beaches, rocky shores, and mangrove swamps. The territory's unique location, where the equatorial forest meets the ocean, creates a diverse range of ecosystems and landscapes.

The location of French Guiana has played a significant role in shaping its history, culture, and economy. The territory's proximity to the equator and its tropical climate have made it an attractive location for colonial powers, with France establishing its first settlement in the area in the 17th century. Today, French Guiana remains an overseas department of France, with a distinct cultural identity that reflects its European, African, and indigenous influences. The territory's borders with Brazil and Suriname have also had an impact on its economy, with trade and commerce playing an important role in the region.
