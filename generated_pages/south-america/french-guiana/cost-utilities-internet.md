[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
French Guiana has a well-developed utility infrastructure, with reliable access to **electricity**, **water**, and **gas**. However, the costs of these utilities can fluctuate depending on the season, with higher costs during the dry season (August to November) due to increased energy demand. The territory's electricity is primarily generated from **hydropower** and **fossil fuels**, which can lead to variations in pricing. Additionally, the cost of utilities in French Guiana is generally higher compared to other countries in South America, due to its remote location and reliance on imported fuels.

The quality of utility services in French Guiana is generally good, with a **reliability rate** of over 90%. However, there may be occasional outages and disruptions, particularly during periods of heavy rainfall or high winds. The government and utility providers are working to improve the infrastructure and reduce the frequency of outages. It is also worth noting that the cost of utilities can vary depending on the location, with **urban areas** tend to have higher costs than **rural areas**.
