# French Guiana Visa & Entry Requirements

To enter French Guiana, citizens of most countries will need a visa, although the specific requirements and regulations vary depending on nationality and purpose of visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of French Guiana before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
French Guiana has a moderate visa policy, with citizens of some countries eligible for visa-free access, while others require a visa in advance or can obtain one upon arrival. As an overseas department of France, French Guiana's visa policy is closely tied to that of France, and many of the same rules and regulations apply. Citizens of European Union countries, as well as some other European nations, do not require a visa to enter French Guiana, while citizens of many other countries, including the United States, Canada, and Australia, may need to obtain a visa in advance.

The visa policy of French Guiana is designed to balance the need to facilitate tourism and business travel with the need to maintain national security and control over immigration. As a result, the requirements for entry can be complex and vary depending on the specific circumstances of the visitor. It is essential for travelers to carefully review the requirements and regulations before planning a trip to French Guiana.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists visiting French Guiana typically need to provide proof of onward travel, as well as sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. The maximum stay for tourists is usually 90 days, although this can be extended in some circumstances. To apply for a tourist visa, visitors will typically need to provide a range of documents, including a valid passport, proof of travel arrangements, and proof of sufficient funds.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to provide additional documentation, such as an invitation letter from a French Guianese company or a letter explaining the purpose of their visit. They may also need to apply for a different type of visa, such as a business visa or a work permit.
**Students:** Students planning to study in French Guiana will need to provide proof of enrollment in a recognized educational institution, as well as proof of sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. They may also need to apply for a student visa, which can be valid for up to one year.
**Workers:** Workers planning to take up employment in French Guiana will need to provide a range of documents, including a work contract, proof of qualifications, and proof of sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. They will also need to apply for a work permit, which can be a complex and time-consuming process.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airport in French Guiana is Cayenne-Rochambeau Airport, which receives flights from Paris and other destinations in France, as well as from neighboring countries in South America. Travelers can also enter French Guiana by land or sea, although these routes are less common. Upon arrival, travelers will need to present their passport and visa (if required) to immigration officials, who will check the documents and ask questions about the purpose of the visit. It is essential to ensure that all documents are in order and that the traveler has all the necessary information and supporting documents.

The entry procedures in French Guiana are generally straightforward, although travelers should be prepared to wait in line and to answer questions from immigration officials. It is also important to ensure that the passport is valid for at least six months beyond the planned departure date from French Guiana.
