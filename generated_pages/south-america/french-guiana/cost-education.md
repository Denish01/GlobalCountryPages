The cost of education in French Guiana varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools can range from $2,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in French Guiana is based on the French model, with a strong emphasis on public education. Public schools in French Guiana are generally considered to be of good quality, with a high level of instruction and a wide range of subjects offered. However, private schools are also available, offering a more personalized education and often smaller class sizes. The language of instruction is French, and students are required to attend school from the age of 6 to 16, with 11 years of compulsory education. International schools are also available in French Guiana, offering a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the French baccalaureate.

The education system in French Guiana is overseen by the French Ministry of Education, and the curriculum is similar to that in France. Students in French Guiana can attend public or private schools, and there are also several international schools that cater to the expat community. The quality of education in French Guiana is generally considered to be high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and a wide range of extracurricular activities available. However, there are some challenges facing the education system in French Guiana, including a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of resources in some schools.
