# French Guiana Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

French Guiana is generally considered a safe destination for tourists, with low to moderate crime rates and a strong French influence on the territory's culture and infrastructure.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
French Guiana is a relatively safe territory, with most crimes being petty in nature, such as pickpocketing and theft. The territory has a strong French influence, which contributes to its stability and security. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night, and take steps to protect themselves and their belongings.

The French Guianese authorities take the safety of tourists seriously, and there is a dedicated tourist police force to assist visitors. Additionally, the territory has a well-developed infrastructure, with good roads, reliable public transportation, and modern amenities. Despite these positives, French Guiana is not without its risks, and visitors should be aware of the potential for natural disasters, such as hurricanes and floods, as well as health risks, such as malaria and dengue fever.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Petty theft and pickpocketing are common in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake tour operators and overpriced services, are relatively rare but can occur.
- Credit card skimming and ATM fraud have been reported in some areas.
- Visitors should be cautious when using public transportation, especially at night.
- The risk of malaria and dengue fever is present in some areas, especially during the rainy season.
- Visitors should be aware of the risk of natural disasters, such as hurricanes and floods, and take necessary precautions.
- Border areas, such as Saint-Laurent-du-Maroni and Albina, have a higher risk of crime and smuggling.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever vaccination is required for travelers coming from or passing through certain countries in South America and Africa.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is recommended for visitors who are unsure.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are modern and well-equipped, especially in Cayenne and Kourou, but can be limited in remote areas.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are readily available in major towns and cities, but may be limited in remote areas.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night.
- Keep valuables secure and use a hotel safe or a secure bag.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead.
- Stay informed about local conditions and events that may affect your safety.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting remote or indigenous areas.
- Learn some basic French phrases, such as "bonjour" and "merci," to show respect for the local culture.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- French Guiana is generally a safe destination for solo and women travelers, but it's still important to take necessary precautions.
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night, and use reputable taxi services instead.
- Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting remote or indigenous areas.
- Stay informed about local conditions and events that may affect your safety, and stay in touch with family and friends back home.
