[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The property market in French Guiana is relatively small and stable, with a mix of **residential** and **commercial** properties available for purchase or rent. The market is influenced by the territory's economy, which is largely driven by the **space industry** and **mining**. The current market conditions are characterized by a moderate demand for properties, particularly in the capital city of Cayenne, where prices have been steadily increasing over the past few years. The price trajectory is expected to continue upward, driven by the growing demand for housing and the limited supply of available properties. Urban areas, such as Cayenne and Kourou, tend to have higher property prices compared to rural areas, where prices are generally lower.

The property market in French Guiana is also influenced by the territory's **geographic location**, with its proximity to the Amazon rainforest and the Atlantic Ocean. The market is subject to **seasonal fluctuations**, with a peak demand for properties during the dry season, which runs from July to November. The **French government** has implemented various initiatives to support the development of the territory, including investments in infrastructure and housing, which are expected to have a positive impact on the property market.

Overall, the property market in French Guiana offers a range of opportunities for investors and buyers, particularly those looking for a unique and **exotic location**. However, it is essential to approach the market with caution and to carefully consider the local regulations and requirements before making a purchase.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners are allowed to buy property in French Guiana, but they must obtain a **residence permit** before making a purchase.
- Foreign companies can also buy property in French Guiana, but they must be registered with the **French authorities** and obtain a **tax identification number**.
- Foreigners who are not residents of French Guiana may be subject to **capital gains tax** when selling a property.
- Foreigners who buy property in French Guiana must also comply with the **local building regulations** and obtain any necessary **permits** before making any modifications to the property.
- Foreigners who are not fluent in **French** may need to appoint a **power of attorney** to represent them in the purchase process.
