[SECTION] Political System [/SECTION]
The government of French Guiana is based on the principles of the French Republic, with a **unitary** system where the central government in Paris maintains significant control over the territory. The French Constitution applies to French Guiana, and the territory is represented in the French National Assembly and Senate. The **separation of powers** is established between the executive, legislative, and judicial branches, with the Prefect of French Guiana serving as the representative of the French government in the territory. The French Guiana Assembly, composed of 51 members elected by universal suffrage, has the power to deliberate on local matters and make decisions on the budget and economic development of the territory.

The political system in French Guiana is characterized by a strong emphasis on **decentralization**, with the territory having a significant degree of autonomy in matters such as education, healthcare, and economic development. However, the French government maintains control over key areas such as defense, foreign policy, and justice. The **European Union** also plays a significant role in the governance of French Guiana, as the territory is an overseas department of France and is therefore subject to EU law.

The government of French Guiana is also influenced by the **local culture** and **history** of the territory, with a strong emphasis on preserving the unique cultural heritage of the region. The territory has a diverse population, with a mix of European, African, and indigenous peoples, and the government has implemented policies aimed at promoting **social cohesion** and **economic development**.
