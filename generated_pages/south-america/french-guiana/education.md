[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in French Guiana is structured into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education, also known as **école primaire**, is compulsory for children between the ages of 6 and 11 and provides a foundation in subjects such as French, mathematics, and science. Secondary education, or **collège**, is compulsory for students between the ages of 11 and 15 and offers a range of subjects, including languages, mathematics, science, and social sciences. Tertiary education, or **université**, is available to students who have completed their secondary education and offers a range of undergraduate and graduate programs. The quality of education in French Guiana is generally high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and intellectual development. Public education is free and available to all citizens, while private education is also available for those who can afford it.

The education system in French Guiana is overseen by the **French Ministry of National Education**, which sets the curriculum and standards for all schools in the territory. The system is also influenced by the **European Union's education policies**, which aim to promote mobility and cooperation among European universities. In terms of quality, the education system in French Guiana is considered to be among the best in the region, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and intellectual development. However, there are also challenges, such as limited resources and a shortage of qualified teachers in certain subjects.

Overall, the education system in French Guiana provides students with a solid foundation in a range of subjects and prepares them for further study and careers in a variety of fields. The system is also constantly evolving, with reforms aimed at improving the quality of education and making it more accessible to all citizens. For example, the **Introduction of the Baccalaureate**, a diploma that students can earn at the end of their secondary education, has been introduced to provide students with a more comprehensive education and better preparation for tertiary education.
