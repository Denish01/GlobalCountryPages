# French Guiana Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

French Guiana's culture is a unique blend of French, African, Asian, and indigenous influences, shaped by its history as a French territory in South America. 

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
French Guiana's cultural identity is defined by its strategic location, where the Amazon rainforest meets the Atlantic Ocean, and its history as a French territory. The territory's ethnic makeup is diverse, with a mix of **Creole**, **European**, **African**, **Asian**, and **indigenous** communities. The official language is **French**, but many residents also speak **Creole**, **English**, and other languages. The cultural influences of French Guiana's neighbors, **Brazil** and **Suriname**, are also evident in its music, food, and traditions.

The territory's history as a penal colony has also left a lasting impact on its culture, with many residents descended from **prisoners** and **colonizers**. Today, French Guiana is a **multicultural** society, where people from different backgrounds live and work together. The territory's cultural identity is also shaped by its **indigenous** communities, including the **Wayampi** and **Palikur** peoples, who have lived in the region for thousands of years.

French Guiana's cultural identity is a rich and complex blend of different influences, with a strong sense of **community** and **resilience**. The territory's unique cultural heritage is reflected in its music, art, and traditions, which are an important part of its identity. From the vibrant **Carnival** celebrations to the traditional **indigenous** festivals, French Guiana's cultural scene is a fascinating and exciting place to explore.

[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Greet people with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek, as a sign of respect
- Use formal titles like **Monsieur** or **Madame** when addressing older people or those in positions of authority
- Try local foods and drinks, and show appreciation for the territory's unique cuisine
- Learn a few basic phrases in **French** or **Creole**, to show respect for the local culture
- Respect the territory's **indigenous** communities and their traditions
- Remove your shoes when entering a mosque or a temple, as a sign of respect

**Don't:**
- Don't touch or point at people, as this is considered rude in French Guiana
- Don't take pictures of people without their permission, especially in **indigenous** communities
- Don't litter or pollute the environment, as this is a serious issue in the territory
- Don't discuss politics or sensitive topics, especially with people you don't know well
- Don't refuse food or drink when offered, as this is considered impolite
- Don't ignore local customs and traditions, especially during festivals and celebrations
