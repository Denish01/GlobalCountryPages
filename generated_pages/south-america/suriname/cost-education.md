# Education Costs in Suriname

The cost of education in Suriname varies depending on the type of institution and the level of education, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge fees ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Suriname is overseen by the Ministry of Education, Science, and Culture, and it is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Public schools in Suriname are generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on the **Dutch language**, which is the official language of instruction. However, private schools and international institutions also offer high-quality education, often with a more diverse curriculum and **English language** instruction. Compulsory education in Suriname lasts for 10 years, from the age of 6 to 16, and it is free or subsidized for all citizens.

The quality of education in Suriname is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on **science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM)** subjects, as well as **language and literature**. International schools are also available in Suriname, offering a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate (IB)** and **Cambridge International Examinations (CIE)**. These schools cater to the needs of expatriate families and offer a high-quality education with a global perspective.
