[SECTION] What Is Suriname? [/SECTION]
Suriname is a **sovereign** country located on the northern coast of **South America**, bordered by Guyana to the west, Brazil to the south, and French Guiana to the east. With a population of approximately 0.62 million people, Suriname is one of the smallest countries in South America. The country has a unique cultural heritage, shaped by its history as a Dutch colony, and is known for its diverse **tropical rainforest** and **wildlife**. The capital city, Paramaribo, is the country's largest city and is home to a mix of colonial architecture, vibrant markets, and a blend of cultures.

Suriname is situated on the **Atlantic coast**, with a coastline of approximately 386 kilometers. The country's terrain is characterized by a narrow coastal plain, with **swampy** and **muddy** areas, and a dense **rainforest** interior. The climate is **tropical**, with high temperatures and humidity levels throughout the year. Suriname is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including **exotic birds**, **monkeys**, and **jaguars**.

The country's economy is primarily driven by **mining**, **agriculture**, and **forestry**, with **bauxite**, **gold**, and **timber** being major exports. Suriname is also a member of the **Caribbean Community (CARICOM)** and the **Union of South American Nations (UNASUR)**, and has strong trade and cultural ties with its neighboring countries.
