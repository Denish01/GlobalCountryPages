# Suriname Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Suriname is relatively reliable, with a growing number of options for getting online, including SIM cards and WiFi hotspots, although the quality and speed can vary depending on the location and provider.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Suriname has improved significantly over the past few years, with **fiber-optic connections** and **4G networks** now available in many parts of the country. However, there is still a significant urban-rural divide, with faster and more reliable internet available in the capital city of **Paramaribo** and other urban areas, compared to the more remote and rural regions. The reliability of the internet can also vary, with outages and slow speeds not uncommon, particularly during periods of heavy rainfall or other extreme weather conditions.

In general, the internet quality in Suriname is **good enough for basic tasks** such as browsing, email, and social media, but may not be sufficient for more demanding activities such as **online gaming** or **video streaming**. The average internet speed in Suriname is around 10 Mbps, which is relatively slow compared to other countries in the region. However, the government and internet service providers are working to improve the internet infrastructure and expand access to more areas of the country.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Suriname is relatively easy, with several mobile providers offering **tourist-friendly plans** and **prepaid SIM cards**. The main carriers, including **Telesur**, **Digicel**, and **Uniqa**, have stores and authorized dealers located throughout the country, particularly in Paramaribo and other urban areas. To purchase a SIM card, you will typically need to provide a **passport** and other identification documents, and pay a fee of around 20 USD for a tourist SIM card. The SIM card can usually be activated immediately, and you can choose from a range of data plans and packages to suit your needs.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi is generally available in most **mid-range and luxury hotels**, although the quality and speed can vary.
- Cafe WiFi culture is not as prevalent in Suriname as in other countries, but many **restaurants and cafes** in Paramaribo and other urban areas do offer free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming more popular in Suriname, particularly in Paramaribo, and often offer **fast and reliable WiFi** to members and visitors.
- Public WiFi is not widely available in Suriname, although some **public parks and plazas** in Paramaribo do offer free WiFi hotspots.
- Many **tourist information centers** and **travel agencies** also offer free WiFi to visitors.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces such as **The Hub** and **Paramaribo Coworking** offer fast and reliable WiFi, as well as other amenities such as meeting rooms and printing services.
- Reliable cafe chains such as **The Coffee Company** and **Cafe de Paris** offer free WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options such as **mobile hotspots** and **portable WiFi routers** can be useful in case of internet outages or slow speeds.
- VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) can be useful for remote workers who need to access **secure and encrypted internet connections**.
- Many **coliving spaces** and **hostels** in Paramaribo and other urban areas also offer coworking facilities and fast WiFi to guests.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a significant issue in Suriname, although some **websites and online services** may be blocked by the government or internet service providers.
- Some **online banking and financial services** may not be available in Suriname, or may require additional security measures such as **two-factor authentication**.
- VPNs can be useful for accessing **geo-restricted content** and **secure online services**, but may not be necessary for general internet use.
- Roaming alternatives such as **eSIMs** and **portable WiFi routers** can be useful for travelers who need to stay connected while on the move.
- It's a good idea to **check with your internet service provider** before traveling to Suriname to see if they offer any **roaming packages or discounts**.
- **Public computers and internet cafes** can be a convenient option for accessing the internet, but may not be as secure as using your own device and internet connection.
