# Cost of Living in Suriname - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Suriname is generally considered to be moderate, with daily expenses ranging from $30-100 USD per day for a backpacker, $50-150 USD per day for mid-range travelers, and $100-250 USD per day for those seeking a more comfortable experience.

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Suriname? [/SECTION]
Suriname is a relatively affordable country to visit or live in, especially when compared to its neighbors in South America. The cost of living in Suriname is lower than in countries like Brazil and French Guiana, but higher than in Guyana. The country's economy is primarily driven by its natural resources, including oil, gold, and timber, which contributes to its moderate cost of living. Overall, Suriname offers a unique blend of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike.

In terms of global averages, Suriname's cost of living is slightly higher than the global average, but still relatively affordable. The country's capital, Paramaribo, is the most expensive city in Suriname, with higher prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. However, the cost of living in other parts of the country is significantly lower, making it an attractive option for those looking to escape the city.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls to experience authentic Surinamese cuisine at a lower cost.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transportation expenses.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets for groceries and other essentials.
- Avoid exchanging money at airports or hotels, as the rates are often unfavorable.
- Learn some basic Dutch phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals, which can help you save money and have a more authentic experience.
