Healthcare Overview

The healthcare system in Pernambuco is a mix of public and private providers, with the public system being the primary source of healthcare for the majority of the population. The public system is funded by the government and provides free or low-cost healthcare to all citizens, including foreigners with a valid visa. However, the public system often faces challenges such as long wait times, limited resources, and a shortage of specialized doctors. The private system, on the other hand, offers higher-quality care and shorter wait times, but is generally more expensive and may not be accessible to all. In urban areas, such as the capital city of Recife, there are many high-quality hospitals and clinics, both public and private, that offer a wide range of specialized services. In rural areas, however, access to healthcare can be limited, and patients may have to travel long distances to receive specialized care.

The quality of care in Pernambuco varies depending on the provider and the location. In general, the public system provides good basic care, but may not have the resources or specialized equipment to provide more complex treatments. The private system, on the other hand, offers high-quality care, with many hospitals and clinics having international accreditation and modern equipment. Despite these challenges, Pernambuco has made significant progress in improving its healthcare system in recent years, with investments in new hospitals, clinics, and healthcare technology.

In terms of access to healthcare, Pernambuco has a relatively high density of healthcare providers, with many hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies located throughout the region. However, there are still significant disparities in access to healthcare, particularly in rural areas, where there may be limited access to specialized care and healthcare services.

Health Insurance

  • Private health insurance is recommended for foreigners and Brazilians who want to access high-quality private healthcare services.
  • Local insurance options are available, such as Amil, SulAmerica, and Porto Seguro, which offer a range of plans and coverage options.
  • Coverage for expats is available through some private insurance companies, which may offer international coverage and emergency evacuation services.
  • Costs of health insurance vary depending on the provider, plan, and level of coverage, but can range from R$500 to R$5,000 per month.
  • Public health insurance is available through the Brazilian public healthcare system, which provides free or low-cost healthcare to all citizens, including foreigners with a valid visa.
  • Supplemental insurance is available to supplement the public healthcare system, which can provide additional coverage and benefits.

Pharmacies & Medications

  • Availability of medications is generally good in Pernambuco, with many pharmacies and drugstores located throughout the region.
  • Prescription requirements vary depending on the medication, but many medications are available over-the-counter or with a prescription from a doctor.
  • Common brands of medications are available, including international brands and generic versions.
  • Costs of medications vary depending on the type and brand, but are generally lower than in many other countries.
  • Pharmacy access is widespread, with many pharmacies open 24 hours a day and located in convenient locations.

Vaccinations & Health Risks

  • Required vaccinations for travel to Pernambuco include yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid fever.
  • Recommended vaccinations include hepatitis B, rabies, and influenza.
  • Endemic diseases in Pernambuco include dengue fever, Zika virus, and chikungunya.
  • Water safety is a concern in some areas, and visitors should take precautions to avoid drinking untreated water.
  • Food safety is generally good, but visitors should take precautions to avoid eating undercooked or raw foods.
  • Vector-borne diseases such as malaria and leishmaniasis are present in some areas, and visitors should take precautions to avoid insect bites.
