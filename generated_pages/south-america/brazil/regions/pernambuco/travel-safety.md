# Pernambuco Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Pernambuco is generally a safe region to visit, with low to moderate crime rates compared to other parts of Brazil, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Pernambuco is a region in northeastern Brazil known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful beaches, and vibrant cities. While it is generally a safe place to visit, there are some areas that are considered safer than others. The region has experienced a decrease in crime rates in recent years, but petty crimes such as pickpocketing and street robbery can still occur, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as being aware of their surroundings, keeping valuables secure, and avoiding walking alone in isolated areas at night.

Overall, Pernambuco is a great destination for travelers who want to experience the unique culture and natural beauty of northeastern Brazil. With some basic precautions and knowledge of the local conditions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip. It's worth noting that the region has a strong police presence, and tourist areas are generally well-patrolled. Additionally, many hotels, restaurants, and tourist attractions have good security measures in place, which can help to minimize the risk of crime.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be aware of pickpocketing and street robbery in crowded areas, especially in Recife and other large cities
- Be cautious of scams targeting tourists, such as fake taxi drivers or tour operators
- Be careful when using ATMs, as card skimming and robbery can occur
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night, especially in isolated areas
- Be cautious of locals who approach you with unsolicited offers or services
- Be careful when eating at street food stalls or markets, as food poisoning can occur
- Be aware of the risk of **dengue fever** and **zika virus**, and take precautions to prevent mosquito bites

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, typhoid, and yellow fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to Pernambuco
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are generally good in larger cities, but can be limited in rural areas
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, especially in larger cities, but may have limited supplies of certain medications

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a copy of your passport and important documents
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or hotel safe
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid walking alone at night, especially in isolated areas
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Keep your hotel room door locked and consider using a chain lock
- Be cautious of locals who approach you with unsolicited offers or services
- Stay informed about local conditions and any potential safety risks

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be extra cautious when walking alone at night, and consider using a reputable taxi service or ride-sharing app
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
- Be cautious of locals who approach you with unsolicited offers or services, and trust your instincts if you feel uncomfortable
