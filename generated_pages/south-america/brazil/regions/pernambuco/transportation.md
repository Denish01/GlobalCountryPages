# Getting Around Pernambuco — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Pernambuco, you can use a combination of domestic flights, buses, taxis, and ride-hailing apps, as well as drive on the region's roads, which require a valid driver's license and adherence to local traffic laws.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Pernambuco is a region in northeastern Brazil, and the main entry point is the Recife/Guararapes-Gilberto Freyre International Airport (REC), which receives direct flights from major Brazilian cities like São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Brasília, as well as some international flights from Europe and the Americas. The airport is located about 14 kilometers from the city center of Recife, the capital of Pernambuco, and offers various transportation options, including taxis, buses, and ride-hailing apps. Overland entry points include the BR-101 and BR-232 highways, which connect Pernambuco to other states in Brazil.

The Recife airport is a major hub for domestic flights, and it's also possible to enter Pernambuco by bus or car from neighboring states like Paraíba, Ceará, and Alagoas. The region's strategic location makes it an important transportation hub in northeastern Brazil, with many bus companies operating routes to and from Recife and other major cities in Pernambuco. Additionally, the Port of Recife is an important entry point for cargo ships and cruise liners, offering another option for travelers to arrive in the region.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights are a convenient way to get around Pernambuco and connect to other parts of Brazil. The region is served by several domestic airlines, including **Gol**, **Azul**, and **LATAM**, which operate flights from Recife to other major cities in Brazil. The main routes include flights from Recife to São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, Brasília, and other state capitals in northeastern Brazil. Booking tips include checking for discounts and promotions on airline websites, as well as using travel agencies or online booking platforms like **Expedia** or **Booking.com**. Approximate costs for domestic flights in Brazil can range from $50 to $200 USD, depending on the route and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
The rail network in Pernambuco is limited, with no high-speed trains or extensive rail connections to other parts of Brazil. However, there are some freight trains and commuter trains that operate in the region, mainly for cargo transport and local passenger services. The **Companhia Brasileira de Trens Urbanos (CBTU)** operates some train lines in Recife and other cities in Pernambuco, but these are not a primary mode of transportation for tourists or travelers. For those interested in using trains, it's best to check with local transportation authorities or train operators for schedules and fares.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Buses and coaches are a popular way to get around Pernambuco and connect to other parts of Brazil. Several intercity bus companies operate in the region, including **Expresso Guanabara**, **São Geraldo**, and **Rota do Sol**, which offer routes to and from Recife and other major cities in Pernambuco. The quality of bus services can vary, but many companies offer comfortable and air-conditioned buses with onboard amenities like food and beverage services. Costs for bus travel can range from $10 to $50 USD, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Pernambuco, you need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driving permit.
- Road conditions in Pernambuco can be moderate, with some highways and roads in good condition, but others may be poorly maintained or congested.
- Fuel costs in Brazil can be relatively high, with prices ranging from $1.50 to $3.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies like **Hertz**, **Avis**, and **Localiza** operate in Recife and other major cities in Pernambuco, offering a range of vehicles for rent.
- Hazards on the road include aggressive drivers, pedestrians, and animals, especially in rural areas.
- It's recommended to drive during the day and avoid driving at night, especially on rural roads or in areas with poor lighting.
