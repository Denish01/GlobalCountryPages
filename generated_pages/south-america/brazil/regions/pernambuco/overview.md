[SECTION] What Is Pernambuco? [/SECTION]
Pernambuco is a region in the northeastern part of Brazil, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the east, Paraíba to the north, Ceará to the northwest, Alagoas to the south, and Bahia to the southwest. The state has a total area of approximately 98,311.616 square kilometers and a population of around 9.6 million people. Pernambuco is known for its vibrant culture, which is a unique blend of indigenous, African, and European influences. The state is home to numerous historical sites, beautiful beaches, and lush forests, making it a popular tourist destination.

The capital city of Pernambuco is Recife, which is known for its rich cultural heritage and historic architecture. The city is situated on the coast and is known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and delicious cuisine. Pernambuco is also home to the historic city of Olinda, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and features stunning colonial architecture and breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Pernambuco has a diverse economy, with major industries including agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism. The state is known for its production of sugarcane, corn, and soybeans, and is also home to several major manufacturing centers. The tourism industry is also a significant contributor to the state's economy, with visitors drawn to the region's beautiful beaches, historic sites, and vibrant culture.
