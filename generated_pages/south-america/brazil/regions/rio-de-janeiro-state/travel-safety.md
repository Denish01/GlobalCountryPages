[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Rio de Janeiro State is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning beaches, vibrant culture, and breathtaking landscapes. However, like many major cities, it also has its share of safety concerns. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing, theft, and street robbery are common, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, violent crimes like armed robbery and assault can occur, particularly at night or in areas with high crime rates. It's essential to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, especially in unfamiliar areas.

Visitors should be mindful of their belongings, avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use reputable transportation services. It's also crucial to stay informed about local conditions, demonstrations, and protests, which can sometimes turn violent. By taking necessary precautions and staying vigilant, tourists can minimize their risk of encountering safety issues and have a enjoyable and memorable experience in Rio de Janeiro State.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **pickpocketing** and **street robbery** in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Watch out for **scams** involving ATMs, credit card machines, and fake police officers
- Avoid carrying large amounts of **cash** and use reputable **transportation services**
- Be mindful of **demonstrations** and **protests**, which can sometimes turn violent
- Don't leave **valuables** unattended, especially in crowded areas or on public transportation
- Be aware of **fake** or **unlicensed** tour operators and guides
- Watch out for **drinks** being **spiked** or **stolen** in bars and clubs

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, typhoid, and yellow fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to Rio de Janeiro State
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are generally good in major cities, but can be limited in rural areas; travel insurance is recommended
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but some medications may require a prescription

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Stay informed about local conditions and **demonstrations** through government advisories and local news
- Avoid walking alone in **unfamiliar areas** at night and use reputable **transportation services**
- Keep **valuables** secure and consider using a **money belt** or **hotel safe**
- Be mindful of **ATM scams** and use machines located in secure areas
- Avoid displaying **signs of wealth**, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Stay **hydrated** and take regular breaks in **hot weather**
- Learn some basic **Portuguese phrases**, such as "help" (socorro) and "where is..." (onde fica...)

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your **surroundings** and trust your instincts, especially at night
- Avoid walking alone in **unfamiliar areas** and use reputable **transportation services**
- Consider joining a **group tour** or **hiring a guide** for added safety and security
- Be mindful of **catcalling** and **harassment**, and avoid engaging with **aggressive** individuals
