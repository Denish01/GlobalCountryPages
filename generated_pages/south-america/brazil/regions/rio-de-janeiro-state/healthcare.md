[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
The healthcare system in Rio de Janeiro State is a mixed model, with both public and private providers offering a range of services. The public healthcare system, known as the **Sistema Único de Saúde (SUS)**, provides free or low-cost healthcare to all citizens, including foreigners with a residence visa. However, the quality of care and availability of services can vary significantly depending on the location, with urban areas generally having better-equipped facilities and more specialized care. In contrast, private healthcare providers offer high-quality services, but at a higher cost, and are often preferred by those who can afford them. The quality of care in Rio de Janeiro State is generally good, with many well-trained healthcare professionals and modern facilities, but there can be significant disparities between public and private providers.

The public healthcare system in Rio de Janeiro State is funded by the government and provides a range of services, including primary care, hospital care, and specialized treatments. However, the system can be overwhelmed, and wait times for non-emergency procedures can be long. In contrast, private healthcare providers offer a range of services, including specialized treatments and elective procedures, but at a higher cost. Many private healthcare providers in Rio de Janeiro State have international accreditation and offer high-quality care, making them a popular choice for foreigners and expats.

Access to healthcare in Rio de Janeiro State can vary significantly depending on the location. Urban areas, such as Rio de Janeiro City, have a high concentration of healthcare providers, including public and private hospitals, clinics, and specialized treatment centers. In contrast, rural areas may have limited access to healthcare services, with fewer providers and less specialized care. However, the government has implemented initiatives to improve access to healthcare in rural areas, including the deployment of mobile health units and the expansion of telemedicine services.
