[SECTION] What Is Rio de Janeiro State? [/SECTION]
Rio de Janeiro State is a federal state in Brazil, located in the southeastern region of the country. It is bordered by the states of Minas Gerais to the north and northwest, Espírito Santo to the northeast, and São Paulo to the southwest. The state has a diverse geography, with mountains, forests, and a long coastline along the Atlantic Ocean. The capital and largest city is Rio de Janeiro, which is known for its iconic landmarks such as **Christ the Redeemer** and **Sugarloaf Mountain**.

The state has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of indigenous, African, and European influences. The city of Rio de Janeiro is famous for its **Carnival** celebrations, which attract millions of tourists every year. The state is also home to many beautiful beaches, such as **Copacabana** and **Ipanema**, which are popular destinations for surfers and sunbathers.

The economy of Rio de Janeiro State is diverse, with major industries such as oil and gas, manufacturing, and tourism. The state is also home to many universities and research institutions, making it a hub for education and innovation.
