[SECTION] How Rio de Janeiro State Is Divided [/SECTION]
Rio de Janeiro State is divided into 92 municipalities, each with its own local government and administrative structure. The state is further divided into several mesoregions, which are groups of municipalities that share similar economic, social, and cultural characteristics. These mesoregions are then divided into microregions, which are smaller groups of municipalities that are often centered around a larger city or urban area.

The administrative structure of Rio de Janeiro State is designed to provide a framework for governance and decision-making at the local level. The state government is responsible for providing services and infrastructure to the entire state, while the municipalities are responsible for providing local services and governing their own affairs. This system allows for a degree of autonomy and flexibility at the local level, while also providing a framework for coordination and cooperation at the state level.
