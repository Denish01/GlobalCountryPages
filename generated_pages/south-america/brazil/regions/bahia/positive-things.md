[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Historic Center of Salvador**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a testament to the region's rich cultural heritage, with its colorful colonial architecture and historic landmarks.
- The **Candomblé** tradition, an Afro-Brazilian religious practice, is an important part of Bahia's cultural identity, with many temples and communities throughout the region.
- The **Festival of Iemanjá**, a celebration of the Afro-Brazilian goddess of the sea, takes place every year in Salvador, featuring music, dance, and offerings to the goddess.
- The **Museu de Arte da Bahia** in Salvador is a prominent art museum, showcasing a collection of Bahian and Brazilian art, including works by renowned artists such as **Pierre Verger**.
- The **Capoeira** tradition, a unique martial art that combines elements of dance, acrobatics, and music, is an important part of Bahian culture, with many schools and communities throughout the region.

- The **cultural events** and festivals, such as the **Carnival** and the **Festival of Iemanjá**, are a major draw for visitors, with many opportunities to experience the region's vibrant music, dance, and art.
- The **historic city of Salvador**, with its colorful colonial architecture and historic landmarks, is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history and culture.
- The **natural scenery** of Bahia, including the **Chapada Diamantina National Park**, offers many opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, trekking, and wildlife watching.
