Getting There

Bahia is a region in the northeastern part of Brazil, and the main entry point is Salvador International Airport (SSA), which receives direct flights from major cities in Brazil and some international flights from South America and Europe. You can also enter Bahia by bus or car from neighboring states, such as Sergipe, Alagoas, and Pernambuco. Trains & Rail

The rail network in Bahia is limited, with only a few lines connecting the cities of Salvador, Feira de Santana, and Alagoinhas. The trains are operated by the Brazilian railway company, Vale, and are mainly used for freight transport. There are no high-speed trains in Bahia, and the existing lines are not well-maintained, so train travel is not a convenient option for tourists. However, there are plans to expand and modernize the rail network in the future, which could improve transportation options in the region.

Driving

  • To drive in Bahia, you will need a valid driver's license from your home country, which is recognized in Brazil for up to 180 days.
  • The road quality in Bahia is moderate, with some highways and main roads in good condition, but others, especially in rural areas, can be poor and unpaved.
  • Fuel costs in Brazil are relatively high, with prices ranging from $1.50 to $2.50 USD per liter, depending on the type of fuel and location.
  • Car rental companies, such as Hertz and Avis, are available at airports and in major cities, and prices start from around $40 USD per day.
  • When driving in Bahia, be aware of the risk of robbery and theft, especially in urban areas, and take necessary precautions, such as locking your car and keeping valuables out of sight.
  • It is also important to be aware of the local traffic laws and customs, such as the requirement to wear a seatbelt and the use of horns and headlights.

Transport Tips

  • When using taxis or ride-hailing apps, make sure to use a reputable company and always check the driver's ID and license plate before getting in.
  • Be aware of scams, such as overcharging or taking a longer route, and always agree on the fare before starting your journey.
  • When driving, be careful of pedestrians and motorcycles, which can be unpredictable and may not follow traffic rules.
  • Download the Waze or Google Maps app to navigate the roads and avoid traffic congestion.
  • Consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot to stay connected and access maps and information on the go.
  • Always carry a copy of your passport and driver's license, and keep the contact information of your embassy and insurance provider handy.
  • Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas and public transportation, and take necessary precautions to prevent theft and loss.
