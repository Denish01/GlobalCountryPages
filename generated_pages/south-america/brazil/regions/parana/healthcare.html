Health Insurance

  • Private health insurance is recommended for anyone living or traveling in Parana, as it can provide access to better facilities and more specialized care.
  • Many local insurance companies, such as Amil and SulAmerica, offer health insurance plans that cover a range of services, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and prescriptions.
  • Expats and foreigners can also purchase international health insurance plans that cover them in Parana and other countries.
  • The cost of health insurance in Parana can vary significantly depending on the type of plan and provider, but overall, it is considered to be relatively affordable.
  • Some employers in Parana may offer health insurance as a benefit to their employees, so it's worth checking with your employer to see if this is an option.
  • It's also worth noting that some health insurance plans may have deductibles or co-pays, so it's essential to carefully review the terms of your plan before purchasing.

Pharmacies & Medications

  • Pharmacies are widespread in Parana, with many locations in urban and rural areas.

Vaccinations & Health Risks

  • The Brazilian Ministry of Health recommends that all travelers to Parana be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella.
  • Additional vaccinations, such as hepatitis A and typhoid, may be recommended depending on the type of travel and activities planned.
  • Parana is considered to be a low-risk area for malaria, but dengue fever and Zika virus are present in some areas.
  • It's essential to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as using insect repellent and wearing protective clothing.
  • The water quality in Parana is generally considered to be good, but it's still essential to take precautions, such as drinking bottled or filtered water.
  • The food safety in Parana is generally considered to be good, but it's still essential to take precautions, such as eating at reputable restaurants and avoiding undercooked meat and seafood.
