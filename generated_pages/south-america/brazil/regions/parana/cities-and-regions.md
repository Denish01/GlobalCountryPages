[SECTION] How Parana Is Divided [/SECTION]
Parana is a state in the southern region of Brazil, bordered by the states of Sao Paulo to the northeast, Mato Grosso do Sul to the northwest, Santa Catarina to the south, and the countries of Argentina and Paraguay to the west. The state is divided into 399 municipalities, each with its own local government and administrative structure. The capital city, Curitiba, is the largest city in the state and serves as the economic and cultural hub of Parana.

The administrative structure of Parana is further divided into 10 mesoregions, which are groups of municipalities that share similar economic, social, and cultural characteristics. These mesoregions are then divided into 29 microregions, which are smaller groups of municipalities that work together to provide public services and promote local development. This system of division allows for more effective governance and administration of the state's resources and services.

- The western regions are known for their natural beauty, with a strong focus on eco-tourism and outdoor activities.

[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
Parana has a well-developed transportation system, with several domestic airports, including the Afonso Pena International Airport in Curitiba, which offers flights to major cities in Brazil and internationally. The state is also connected by a network of highways, including the BR-116 and BR-277, which link Parana to other states in Brazil. Bus services are also widely available, with several companies operating routes between cities and towns. Driving conditions in Parana can be challenging, especially in rural areas, where roads may be poorly maintained and traffic can be heavy. The public transportation system in Parana's cities is also well-developed, with a range of options available, including buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services. Overall, getting around Parana is relatively easy, with a range of options available to suit different budgets and preferences.
