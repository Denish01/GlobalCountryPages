# Getting Around Sao Paulo State — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Sao Paulo State, you can use a variety of transportation options, including airports, domestic flights, rail networks, buses, city transport, and driving, with each mode offering a unique set of benefits and challenges.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Sao Paulo State has several international airports, with Guarulhos International Airport (GRU) being the primary gateway to the state, offering direct flights to major hubs in South America, North America, Europe, and Asia. Viracopos International Airport (VCP) and Congonhas Airport (CGH) also receive international flights, although to a lesser extent. If you're traveling overland, you can enter Sao Paulo State from neighboring states such as Minas Gerais, Paraná, and Rio de Janeiro via well-maintained highways.

For those traveling from other parts of Brazil or neighboring countries, there are several overland entry points, including bus stations and border crossings. The main bus station in Sao Paulo City, Terminal Rodoviário Tietê, offers connections to major cities in Brazil and neighboring countries. Additionally, the state has several border crossings with neighboring countries, including Uruguay and Paraguay, although these are less frequently used by tourists.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Sao Paulo State has an extensive domestic air network, with multiple daily flights connecting the state's main cities, including Sao Paulo City, Campinas, and Ribeirão Preto. Domestic airlines such as Gol, Azul, and LATAM operate flights to various destinations within the state and to other parts of Brazil. When booking domestic flights, it's recommended to compare prices across different airlines and book in advance to secure the best deals. Approximate costs for domestic flights within Sao Paulo State can range from $50 to $200 USD, depending on the route and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Sao Paulo State has a limited rail network, with most trains operating on commuter lines in the Greater Sao Paulo area. The state's rail network is primarily used for freight transport, with passenger services limited to a few routes. However, there are plans to expand the rail network in the coming years, including the construction of a high-speed rail line connecting Sao Paulo City to Rio de Janeiro. For now, trains are not a convenient option for getting around the state, and buses or cars are generally preferred.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Sao Paulo State has an extensive network of intercity buses, with companies such as Cometa, Util, and Expresso Brasília operating routes to major cities within the state and to other parts of Brazil. Bus travel is a convenient and affordable option, with prices starting from around $10 USD for shorter routes. The quality of bus services varies, but most companies offer comfortable and well-maintained vehicles, with onboard amenities such as air conditioning, Wi-Fi, and snacks.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Sao Paulo State, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an International Driving Permit (IDP).
- Road conditions in the state are generally good, with well-maintained highways and urban roads.
- Fuel costs in Brazil are relatively high, with prices ranging from $1.50 to $2.50 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies such as Hertz, Avis, and Localiza operate in Sao Paulo State, with a wide range of vehicles available.
- When driving in the state, be aware of hazards such as heavy traffic, aggressive drivers, and poor road conditions in rural areas.
- It's also important to be mindful of parking rules and regulations, as fines can be steep for illegal parking.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of scams targeting tourists, such as overpriced taxi fares or fake ticket vendors.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps to avoid scams.
- Download transport apps such as Moovit or Waze to navigate the state's public transport system and avoid traffic congestion.
- Always carry a valid form of ID and your driver's license when driving or using public transport.
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas such as bus stations and airports.
- Use cash or credit cards to pay for transport, as not all vendors accept foreign currency or alternative payment methods.
- Learn some basic Portuguese phrases to help you navigate the transport system and communicate with locals.
- Consider purchasing a **Bilhete Único** card, which offers discounted fares and convenient travel on public transport in Sao Paulo City.
