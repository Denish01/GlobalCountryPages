# Getting Around Minas Gerais — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Minas Gerais, you can use a variety of transportation options, including domestic flights, buses, taxis, and ride-hailing apps, as well as drive on your own or take trains, with the region offering a range of choices to suit different budgets and preferences.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Minas Gerais is easily accessible by air, with two main international airports, Tancredo Neves International Airport (CNF) and Zona da Mata Regional Airport (IZA), offering connections to major cities in Brazil and internationally. The state's capital, Belo Horizonte, is a major hub with direct flights to many destinations, including São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Brasília. You can also enter Minas Gerais by bus or car, with several overland entry points from neighboring states, including São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Bahia.

The state's strategic location in southeastern Brazil makes it a convenient stopover for travelers exploring the region. Many tour operators and travel agencies offer packages that include transportation to and from Minas Gerais, as well as accommodation and sightseeing options. Whether you're arriving by air, land, or sea, you'll find it easy to get to Minas Gerais and start exploring its many attractions.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Minas Gerais has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to and from the state's airports. **Gol Transportes Aéreos**, **Azul Brazilian Airlines**, and **LATAM** are some of the main carriers, offering connections to major cities in Brazil, including São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Brasília. You can book flights online or through a travel agency, with prices varying depending on the route, time of year, and demand. Approximate costs for domestic flights in Brazil range from $50 to $200 USD per person, depending on the distance and airline.

When booking domestic flights, it's a good idea to compare prices and schedules across different airlines to find the best option for your needs. You can also consider booking in advance to secure the best rates, as prices tend to increase closer to the departure date. Additionally, be sure to check the airline's baggage policy and any additional fees that may apply.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Minas Gerais has a limited rail network, with most trains operating on freight lines rather than passenger routes. However, there are some tourist trains and scenic rail lines that offer a unique way to see the state's countryside. The **Trem do Coração** (Heart Train) is a popular option, running from Belo Horizonte to the historic town of São João del-Rei. The train journey takes around 4 hours, passing through scenic valleys and picturesque villages. You can book tickets online or at the train station, with prices starting from around $20 USD per person.

While the rail network in Minas Gerais is limited, it's still a great way to see the state's natural beauty and experience its rich history and culture. Be sure to check the train schedule and book in advance, as seats can fill up quickly, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Minas Gerais has an extensive network of buses and coaches, connecting major cities and towns across the state. **Cometa**, **Util**, and **Pássaro Marron** are some of the main bus companies, offering comfortable and affordable transportation options. You can book tickets online or at the bus station, with prices starting from around $10 USD per person for shorter journeys. The bus network is a great way to see the state's countryside and experience its local culture, with many routes passing through scenic valleys and picturesque villages.

When traveling by bus, be sure to check the schedule and book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons. You can also consider booking a **leito** (sleeper bus) for longer journeys, which offers more comfort and amenities. Additionally, be sure to check the bus company's reputation and safety record before booking, as some companies may have better safety standards than others.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Minas Gerais, you'll need a valid **driver's license** from your home country or an international driving permit.
- **Road conditions** can vary, with some highways and main roads in good condition, while others may be poorly maintained.
- **Fuel costs** are relatively high in Brazil, with prices starting from around $1.50 USD per liter.
- When **renting a car**, be sure to choose a reputable company and check the vehicle's condition before driving.
- **Hazards** to watch out for include potholes, speed bumps, and aggressive drivers, especially in urban areas.
- It's also a good idea to **learn some basic Portuguese** phrases, such as directions and traffic signs, to help you navigate the roads.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **scams** targeting tourists, such as overcharging for taxi fares or fake ticket sellers.
- **Payment methods** can vary, with some businesses accepting credit cards, while others may only take cash.
- **Download** the **99** or **Uber** app to book ride-hailing services, which can be more convenient than hailing a taxi on the street.
- **Traffic** in Belo Horizonte and other major cities can be heavy, especially during rush hour, so plan your journeys accordingly.
- **Respect local customs** and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas or small towns.
- **Learn about** the **local bus network** and how to use it, as it can be a convenient and affordable way to get around.
- **Keep valuables safe** and secure, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots, to avoid pickpocketing or theft.
