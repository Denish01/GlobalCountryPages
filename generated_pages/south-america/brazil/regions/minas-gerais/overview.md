[SECTION] What Is Minas Gerais? [/SECTION]
Minas Gerais is one of the 26 states of Brazil and is situated in the southeastern region of the country. It is the second most populous state in Brazil, with a population of over 20 million people, and covers an area of approximately 586,514 square kilometers. The state is known for its diverse geography, which includes mountains, valleys, and savannas, as well as its rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its historic cities, colonial architecture, and vibrant arts scene. Minas Gerais is also an important economic center, with a strong mining industry, agricultural sector, and thriving services sector.

The state is bordered by several other Brazilian states, including São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Bahia, and is connected to the rest of the country by a network of roads, railways, and airports. The capital city, Belo Horizonte, is a major urban center and is known for its modern architecture, cultural attractions, and vibrant nightlife. Minas Gerais is also home to several other important cities, including Ouro Preto, Diamantina, and Tiradentes, which are famous for their historic architecture, cultural landmarks, and natural beauty.

Minas Gerais has a rich history and culture, with a unique blend of indigenous, African, and European influences. The state is home to several important historical landmarks, including the historic city of Ouro Preto, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the Inconfidência Museum, which showcases the state's history and cultural heritage. The state is also known for its traditional cuisine, which includes dishes such as feijoada, pão de queijo, and tutu à mineira, as well as its vibrant arts scene, which includes music, dance, and theater.
