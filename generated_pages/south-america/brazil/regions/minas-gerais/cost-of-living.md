# Cost of Living in Minas Gerais - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Minas Gerais, a region in Brazil, can vary depending on lifestyle and location, but overall it is considered to be affordable, with daily budgets ranging from $30-60 USD for backpackers to $100-200 USD for mid-range travelers.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $30-60 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $100-200 USD
Currency: BRL
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 10% in restaurants and bars is common
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Minas Gerais? [/SECTION]
Minas Gerais is a large and diverse region in Brazil, with varying costs of living depending on the location. Compared to other regions in Brazil, Minas Gerais is considered to be relatively affordable, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. However, prices can be higher in larger cities like Belo Horizonte, the capital, and lower in smaller towns and rural areas. Overall, Minas Gerais offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike.

In comparison to global averages, Minas Gerais is generally cheaper than many Western countries, but more expensive than some other destinations in South America. For example, the cost of accommodation and food in Minas Gerais is generally lower than in countries like the United States or Europe, but higher than in countries like Peru or Bolivia. However, the region's unique cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and rich history make it a unique and rewarding destination that is worth visiting.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in smaller towns or outside of city centers to save on accommodation costs
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls to save on food costs
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transport costs
- Avoid traveling during peak season (December to March) to save on accommodation and tour prices
- Learn some basic Portuguese phrases to negotiate prices and get a better deal
- Consider buying groceries and cooking your own meals instead of eating out to save on food costs
