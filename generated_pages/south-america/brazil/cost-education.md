# Education Costs in Brazil

The cost of education in Brazil varies widely depending on the type of institution, location, and level of study, with public schools being generally free or low-cost, while private and international schools can range from a few thousand to over $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Brazil's education system is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being the largest and most accessible. The quality of public education can vary greatly depending on the region and school, with some schools facing issues such as overcrowding and lack of resources. On the other hand, private schools in Brazil are generally considered to be of higher quality, with better facilities, smaller class sizes, and more experienced teachers. The language of instruction in Brazil is **Portuguese**, and compulsory education lasts for 9 years, from age 6 to 14.

The education system in Brazil is also home to a number of international schools, which cater to the children of expatriates and wealthy locals. These schools typically follow a foreign curriculum, such as the **International Baccalaureate** or the **British Curriculum**, and may have instruction in languages other than Portuguese. International schools in Brazil can be quite expensive, but they offer a high-quality education and a unique cultural experience for students.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Tips [/SECTION]
- The **British School of Rio de Janeiro** and the **International School of São Paulo** are two of the best schools for expats in Brazil, offering a high-quality education and a unique cultural experience.
- Scholarship availability in Brazil is limited, but some universities and organizations offer scholarships to international students, such as the **Brazilian Government Scholarship**.
- Online learning options are becoming increasingly popular in Brazil, with many universities and schools offering online courses and degree programs.
- Education quality in Brazil can vary greatly depending on the region and school, but overall it is considered to be of a lower quality compared to other countries in the region, such as **Chile** and **Argentina**.
- Expats in Brazil should research schools thoroughly and consider factors such as location, curriculum, and extracurricular activities when choosing a school for their children.
- Learning **Portuguese** is highly recommended for anyone planning to study or live in Brazil, as it will greatly enhance their experience and opportunities.
