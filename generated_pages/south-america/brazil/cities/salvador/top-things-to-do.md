# Top Things to Do in Salvador — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Salvador, a city in the northeastern part of Brazil, visit the **Pelourinho**, a historic neighborhood with colorful colonial architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and stunning views of the Bay of All Saints.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **capoeira** class, a unique Afro-Brazilian martial art that combines dance, acrobatics, and music.
- Attend a **samba** performance, a lively and energetic musical genre that is an essential part of Brazilian culture.
- Visit a **candomblé** temple, a Afro-Brazilian religious center that showcases the city's rich cultural heritage.
- Go on a **street food tour**, sampling local delicacies such as **acarajé**, **abará**, and **moqueca**.
- Take a **sunset sail** around the Bay of All Saints, offering breathtaking views of the city and the sea.
- Participate in a **carnival** celebration, a vibrant and colorful event that takes place every year in February or March.

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Parque da Cidade**, a large urban park with walking trails, gardens, and a lake.
- Explore the **Mata Atlântica**, a lush and biodiverse forest that surrounds the city.
- Relax on the **Praia do Porto da Barra**, a beautiful beach with calm waters and stunning views.
- Go on a **hiking** trip to the **Serra do Mar**, a mountain range with scenic trails and breathtaking views.
- Take a **boat tour** of the **Bay of All Saints**, offering stunning views of the city and the surrounding islands.

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Museu de Arte da Bahia**, a museum that features a collection of Bahian art from the 18th to the 20th centuries.
- Attend a **performance** at the **Teatro Castro Alves**, a historic theater that hosts a variety of cultural events.
- Explore the **Instituto Geográfico e Histórico da Bahia**, a museum that showcases the history and geography of the state.
- Visit the **Igreja de São Francisco**, one of the most beautiful and historic churches in Brazil.
- Take a **cooking class**, learning how to prepare traditional Bahian dishes such as **moqueca** and **feijoada**.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Liberdade**, a charming neighborhood with a rich cultural heritage and stunning views of the city.
- Explore the **Mercado de São José**, a bustling marketplace that offers a wide variety of local products and handicrafts.
- Attend a **concert** at the **Salvador Music Festival**, a event that showcases the city's vibrant music scene.
- Visit the **Museu do Recôncavo**, a museum that features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the Recôncavo region.
- Go on a **bike tour** of the city, exploring the historic neighborhoods and scenic coastal routes.

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to the **Island of Itaparica**, located in the Bay of All Saints, with a distance of 30 km and a travel time of 1 hour, highlighting the island's beautiful beaches and historic lighthouse.
- Visit the **town of Cachoeira**, located 100 km from Salvador, with a travel time of 2 hours, highlighting the town's historic architecture, beautiful waterfalls, and vibrant cultural scene.
- Go on a **hiking trip** to the **Serra do Mar**, a mountain range located 50 km from Salvador, with a travel time of 1.5 hours, highlighting the scenic trails and breathtaking views.
- Take a **boat tour** of the **Bay of All Saints**, with a distance of 20 km and a travel time of 1 hour, highlighting the bay's stunning views, beautiful beaches, and historic islands.
- Visit the **town of Lençóis**, located 400 km from Salvador, with a travel time of 6 hours, highlighting the town's stunning natural beauty, including waterfalls, caves, and scenic hiking trails.
