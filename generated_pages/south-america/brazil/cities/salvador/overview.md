[SECTION] What Is Salvador? [/SECTION]
Salvador is a major city located on the northeast coast of Brazil, in the state of Bahia. It is situated on a peninsula that separates the Bay of All Saints from the Atlantic Ocean. The city is known for its historic center, which features a mix of colonial and Art Deco architecture, and its vibrant cultural scene, which reflects the city's African, Portuguese, and indigenous influences. Salvador is also an important economic and educational center in the region, with a major port and several universities.

Salvador is a significant tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and cultural events. The city is home to many museums, theaters, and music venues, and it hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the famous Carnaval celebrations. Salvador is also a major hub for Afro-Brazilian culture, with many cultural institutions and events dedicated to preserving and promoting the city's African heritage.

The city's geographic position makes it an important center for trade and commerce in the region. Salvador is connected to the rest of Brazil by a major highway and a international airport, and its port is one of the busiest in the country. The city's economy is diverse, with major industries including manufacturing, tourism, and services.
