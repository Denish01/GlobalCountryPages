# Curitiba Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Curitiba, be sure to try the city's unique blend of Italian, German, and indigenous influences, which have shaped the local cuisine, with must-try dishes including **Feijoada**, **Churrasco**, and **Açaí**.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: rice, beans, cassava, and pork
National Dish: Feijoada, a hearty stew made with black beans and pork
Meal Times: breakfast from 7-10 am, lunch from 11 am-2 pm, dinner from 6-9 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 10% of the total bill is customary
Dietary Notes: many restaurants offer vegetarian and gluten-free options, but halal and kosher options may be limited
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Curitiba's cuisine is a reflection of the city's cultural diversity, with significant influences from Italian, German, and indigenous communities. The city's proximity to the Atlantic coast has also shaped its cuisine, with fresh seafood featuring prominently in many dishes. The local cuisine is known for its hearty and comforting dishes, often made with locally-sourced ingredients such as cassava, beans, and pork. Regional variations within the state of Paraná, where Curitiba is located, have also contributed to the city's unique culinary identity.

The Italian influence on Curitiba's cuisine is particularly notable, with many restaurants serving traditional Italian dishes such as pasta and pizza. However, these dishes have often been adapted to incorporate local ingredients and flavors, resulting in a unique fusion of Italian and Brazilian cuisine. The city's German influence is also evident, with many restaurants serving traditional German dishes such as sausages and schnitzel.

Overall, Curitiba's cuisine is a vibrant and eclectic mix of different cultures and influences, reflecting the city's history and cultural diversity. From traditional Brazilian dishes to international cuisine, there is something for every palate in Curitiba.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Tipping**: it is customary to tip 10% of the total bill at restaurants and bars
- **Reservations**: it is recommended to make reservations at popular restaurants, especially on weekends and holidays
- **Dress Code**: the dress code is generally casual, but some restaurants may require more formal attire
- **Eating Etiquette**: it is customary to wait for the host to indicate where to sit, and to keep your hands visible on the table
- **Payment**: credit cards are widely accepted, but it is recommended to have some cash on hand for smaller purchases
- **Meal Times**: mealtimes are generally later than in other countries, with lunch often served between 11 am and 2 pm, and dinner between 6 pm and 9 pm
