# Where to Stay in Recife — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Recife, the best areas to stay are in neighborhoods that offer a mix of safety, accessibility, and local culture, such as Boa Viagem, Olinda, and Recife Antigo. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Recife, here's what you can expect:
**Boa Viagem** (Recife)
Best For: Luxury/Family
Vibe: Boa Viagem is a popular neighborhood with a beautiful beach and a vibrant nightlife, offering a mix of relaxation and entertainment options. The area is known for its upscale atmosphere and wide range of amenities.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Close proximity to the beach, plenty of restaurants and shops, and a variety of accommodation options. 
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, especially at night.

**Olinda** (Recife)
Best For: Backpacker/Culture
Vibe: Olinda is a historic neighborhood with a rich cultural heritage, featuring colorful colonial architecture, lively markets, and a thriving arts scene. The area is known for its bohemian vibe and stunning views of the city.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Unique cultural experiences, stunning views, and a lively atmosphere. 
Cons: Steep hills and narrow streets can be challenging to navigate.

**Recife Antigo** (Recife)
Best For: Business/Luxury
Vibe: Recife Antigo is the historic center of Recife, featuring a mix of modern and colonial architecture, upscale restaurants, and cultural attractions. The area is known for its sophisticated atmosphere and proximity to business districts.
Price Range: $40-250/night
Pros: Convenient location, rich history, and high-end amenities. 
Cons: Traffic and parking can be a challenge.

**Piedade** (Recife)
Best For: Family/Mid-range
Vibe: Piedade is a residential neighborhood with a relaxed atmosphere, featuring parks, restaurants, and shops. The area is known for its friendly locals and affordable accommodation options.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: Quiet and safe, with easy access to public transportation and local amenities. 
Cons: Limited nightlife and entertainment options.

**Santo Antônio** (Recife)
Best For: Budget/Backpacker
Vibe: Santo Antônio is a lively neighborhood with a mix of cultural attractions, street food, and nightlife. The area is known for its vibrant atmosphere and affordable accommodation options.
Price Range: $15-80/night
Pros: Affordable prices, lively atmosphere, and close proximity to cultural attractions. 
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, with limited high-end amenities.
