# Cost of Living in Recife - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Recife, Brazil is generally affordable, with a wide range of options for accommodation, food, and transportation, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $30-50 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $50-100 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $100-200 USD
Currency: BRL
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 10% in restaurants and bars is appreciated
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Recife? [/SECTION]
Recife is a city in northeastern Brazil that offers a unique blend of urban and natural attractions, with a cost of living that is lower compared to other major cities in Brazil, such as Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo. The city's affordability makes it an attractive option for tourists and expats, with a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Compared to global averages, Recife is generally considered to be an affordable city, with prices for food, transportation, and accommodation being relatively low.

In comparison to its neighbors, Recife has a similar cost of living to other cities in northeastern Brazil, such as Salvador and Fortaleza. However, prices for certain items, such as accommodation and food, can be higher in Recife due to its popularity as a tourist destination. Overall, Recife offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an excellent choice for those looking to experience the best of Brazil without breaking the bank.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse outside of the city center to save on accommodation costs
- Eat at local restaurants and street food stalls to experience the authentic flavors of Recife at a lower cost
- Use public transportation, such as buses and metro, to get around the city instead of taxis
- Take advantage of free attractions and activities, such as visiting the beaches and exploring the historic center
- Learn some basic Portuguese phrases to help you navigate and negotiate prices
- Avoid traveling during peak season (December to March) to save on accommodation and flight costs
