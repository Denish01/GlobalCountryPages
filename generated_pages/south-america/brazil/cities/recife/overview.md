[SECTION] What Is Recife? [/SECTION]
Recife is a major city located in the northeastern part of Brazil, in the state of Pernambuco. It is situated near the Atlantic Ocean, where the Beberibe and Capibaribe rivers meet, and is known for its unique cultural identity, which reflects a mix of indigenous, African, and European influences. The city's historic center is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring a rich collection of **Baroque** architecture, including numerous churches, monasteries, and other historic buildings. Recife is also an important economic and cultural hub in the region, with a strong focus on **technology**, **education**, and **arts**.

Geographically, Recife is part of the larger metropolitan area of Recife, which includes several surrounding cities and has a population of over 3.9 million people. The city's climate is tropical, with high temperatures and high humidity levels throughout the year. Recife is also known for its beautiful beaches, such as **Boa Viagem** and **Pina**, which are popular tourist destinations.

Recife has a long history dating back to the 16th century, when it was founded by the Portuguese. Over time, the city has developed a unique cultural identity, which reflects its history, geography, and cultural influences. Today, Recife is a thriving city with a rich cultural scene, a strong economy, and a high quality of life.
