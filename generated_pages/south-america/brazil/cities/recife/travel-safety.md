[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Recife, the capital of Pernambuco state in northeastern Brazil, is a city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. However, it also faces significant safety challenges, particularly when it comes to petty crime and violent crime. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night, and take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. While the city has taken steps to improve security, including the presence of tourist police, it is still important for visitors to exercise caution and be mindful of their safety.

The overall safety situation in Recife can vary depending on the area and the time of day. Some neighborhoods, such as Olinda and Boa Viagem, are considered safer and more tourist-friendly, while others, such as the city center, can be more prone to crime. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of natural disasters, such as flooding and landslides, which can occur during the rainy season. Despite these challenges, Recife remains a popular destination for tourists, and with the right information and precautions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching are common in crowded areas and public transportation
- Scams targeting tourists, such as overcharging for services or selling fake goods
- Armed robbery and mugging, particularly at night or in isolated areas
- Traffic accidents and congestion, especially during rush hour
- Flooding and landslides during the rainy season
- Petty theft and burglary in hotels and hostels
- Corruption and bribery, particularly among police and government officials
- **Express kidnappings**, where victims are held for a short period and forced to withdraw money from ATMs

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever, hepatitis A, and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travelers to Recife
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Recife has a number of good-quality hospitals and medical facilities, but some may require payment upfront or have limited English-speaking staff
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but some medications may be difficult to find or require a prescription

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or a money belt
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night
- Avoid using public transportation at night or in isolated areas
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and consider using a chain lock
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Learn some basic Portuguese phrases, such as "por favor" (please) and "obrigado" (thank you)

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious when walking alone at night, and consider using a taxi or ride-sharing service instead
- Avoid wearing revealing clothing or displaying signs of wealth, as this can attract unwanted attention
- Keep your hotel room number and personal belongings secure, and consider using a hotel with good security
- Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts, especially in crowded areas or when interacting with strangers
- Consider joining a group tour or using a reputable guide service to explore the city and its surroundings
