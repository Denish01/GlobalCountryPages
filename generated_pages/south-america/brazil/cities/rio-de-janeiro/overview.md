[SECTION] What Is Rio de Janeiro? [/SECTION]
Rio de Janeiro is a city located in the state of Rio de Janeiro, in the southeastern region of Brazil. It is situated on the Atlantic coast, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the south, the state of São Paulo to the southwest, and the states of Minas Gerais and Espírito Santo to the north and northeast. Rio de Janeiro is known for its stunning natural beauty, with mountains, forests, and beaches that make it a popular tourist destination. The city is also famous for its vibrant cultural scene, with a rich history of music, dance, and art.

Rio de Janeiro is a major economic and cultural center in Brazil, with a diverse economy that includes industries such as finance, commerce, and tourism. The city is home to many universities, research institutions, and cultural organizations, making it a hub for education, science, and innovation. Rio de Janeiro is also known for its iconic landmarks, such as the Christ the Redeemer statue, which is one of the Seven Wonders of the World, and Sugarloaf Mountain, which offers breathtaking views of the city.

The city's sovereignty status is that of a municipality, with a mayor and a city council that govern the city's affairs. Rio de Janeiro is part of the federal republic of Brazil, with a president and a national congress that govern the country as a whole. The city's geographic position makes it an important hub for trade and commerce, with a major port and an international airport that connect it to the rest of the world.

1. 1565 - The city of Rio de Janeiro was founded by the Portuguese, who established a settlement on the site of the present-day city.
2. 1763 - The city became the capital of the Portuguese colony of Brazil, and it remained the capital until 1960, when the capital was moved to Brasília.
3. 1808 - The Portuguese royal family fled to Rio de Janeiro to escape the Napoleonic Wars, and the city became the de facto capital of the Portuguese Empire.
4. 1889 - The city became the capital of the newly independent Republic of Brazil, and it remained the capital until 1960.
5. 1950s-1960s - The city underwent rapid urbanization and modernization, with the construction of new roads, buildings, and infrastructure.
6. 1992 - The city hosted the Earth Summit, a major international conference on environmental issues, which helped to raise the city's profile as a global center for sustainability and environmentalism.
7. 2014 - The city hosted the FIFA World Cup, which brought millions of visitors to the city and helped to boost its tourism industry.
