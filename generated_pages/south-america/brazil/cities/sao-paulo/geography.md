The city of Sao Paulo, which is the capital of the state, is located in the southeastern part of the state, about 70 kilometers from the coast. The state's strategic location makes it a major hub for trade and commerce in Brazil.

The state of Sao Paulo is surrounded by several important geographical features, including the Serra do Mar mountain range, which runs along the coast, and the Paraná River, which forms the border with the state of Paraná. The state's terrain is also characterized by several valleys and plains, including the Paraíba Valley and the São Paulo Plateau. The climate and geography of Sao Paulo make it an ideal location for agriculture, with the state being one of the largest producers of coffee, sugar cane, and oranges in the world.
