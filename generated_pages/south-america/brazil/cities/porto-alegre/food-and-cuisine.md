# Porto Alegre Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Porto Alegre, be sure to try the city's unique blend of Brazilian, Italian, and German-inspired dishes, including Churrasco, Feijoada, and Açaí, which showcase the city's rich cultural heritage and culinary diversity.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: rice, beans, farofa, and churrasco
National Dish: Feijoada, a hearty stew made with black beans and various meats
Meal Times: breakfast from 7-10 am, lunch from 12-2 pm, dinner from 7-10 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 10% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: Porto Alegre offers a variety of vegetarian and gluten-free options, but it's best to ask ahead of time to ensure availability
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Porto Alegre's cuisine is a unique blend of Brazilian, Italian, and German influences, reflecting the city's cultural heritage. The city's location in the southern region of Brazil, near the border with Uruguay and Argentina, has also contributed to the development of its distinct culinary style. Churrasco, a popular Brazilian barbecue style, is a staple of Porto Alegre's cuisine, with many restaurants offering a variety of grilled meats. The city is also known for its traditional Brazilian dishes, such as Feijoada and Moqueca, which are often served with rice, beans, and farofa.

The Italian and German influences on Porto Alegre's cuisine are also evident in the city's many restaurants and cafes, which offer a range of pasta dishes, pizzas, and traditional German sausages. The city's cultural diversity has also led to the creation of unique fusion dishes, such as Churrasco-style pizza and Brazilian-style sushi. Visitors to Porto Alegre can experience the city's vibrant food culture by visiting its many markets, food courts, and restaurants, which offer a wide range of traditional and modern dishes.

Porto Alegre's food culture is also deeply rooted in its community and family traditions. Many restaurants and cafes are family-owned and operated, and offer traditional dishes that have been passed down through generations. The city's many festivals and celebrations, such as the Festa do Divino Espírito Santo, also feature traditional foods and drinks, which are an important part of the city's cultural heritage.
