# Getting Around Porto Alegre — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Porto Alegre, you can use a combination of public transportation options, including buses and taxis, as well as ride-hailing apps, while driving is also a viable option with a valid license.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Porto Alegre is a major city in southern Brazil, and its Salgado Filho International Airport (POA) is a key entry point for international and domestic flights. The airport is located about 6 kilometers from the city center and offers connections to major Brazilian cities, as well as some international destinations in South America. You can fly directly to Porto Alegre from hubs like São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Buenos Aires, among others. Overland entry points are also available, with several bus companies operating routes from neighboring cities and countries, including Uruguay and Argentina.

For those traveling from other parts of Brazil, there are also several overland options, including bus and car rentals, which can be a more affordable and scenic way to reach Porto Alegre. The city is connected to the rest of the country by a network of highways, including the BR-116 and BR-290, which link Porto Alegre to major cities like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro. Additionally, the city has a number of bus terminals, including the Terminal Rodoviário de Porto Alegre, which offers connections to cities throughout Brazil and beyond.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights are a convenient way to get to Porto Alegre from other parts of Brazil, with several airlines operating routes to the city. Some of the main domestic airlines that fly to Porto Alegre include **Gol Transportes Aéreos**, **Azul Brazilian Airlines**, and **LATAM Brasil**. These airlines offer flights from major hubs like São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Brasília, as well as from smaller cities in the south of the country. Booking in advance is recommended, as prices can vary depending on the time of year and demand. Approximate costs for domestic flights to Porto Alegre can range from $100 to $500 USD or more, depending on the route and airline.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Porto Alegre has a limited rail network, with no high-speed options available. However, there are some commuter trains that connect the city to nearby suburbs and towns, operated by the **Trensurb** company. These trains are a convenient option for getting around the city and its surroundings, with frequent departures and affordable prices. However, for longer distances, buses and flights are generally the better option.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity buses are a popular way to get to Porto Alegre from other parts of Brazil, with several companies operating routes to the city. Some of the main bus companies that operate in Porto Alegre include **Ormeño**, **Util**, and **Unesul**, which offer connections to cities throughout the country. The quality of the buses can vary, but many companies offer comfortable and modern vehicles with amenities like air conditioning and reclining seats. Prices can range from $20 to $100 USD or more, depending on the route and company.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Porto Alegre, you will need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driving permit.
- Road conditions in Porto Alegre can be moderate, with some roads in good condition and others with potholes and traffic congestion.
- Fuel costs in Brazil can be relatively high, with prices ranging from $1.50 to $3.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies like **Hertz**, **Avis**, and **Localiza** are available at the airport and in the city center, with prices starting from around $40 USD per day.
- When driving in Porto Alegre, be aware of pedestrians and cyclists, as well as traffic congestion during peak hours.
- Parking in the city center can be challenging, with limited spaces available and high fees for parking garages.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **traffic congestion** during peak hours, especially in the city center.
- Use a **reputable taxi company** or ride-hailing app to avoid scams.
- **Download the 99 app** for convenient ride-hailing and affordable prices.
- **Avoid carrying large amounts of cash** and use credit or debit cards instead.
- **Be mindful of pickpocketing** in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
- **Learn some basic Portuguese phrases**, such as "obrigado" (thank you) and "desculpe" (excuse me), to help you navigate the city.
- **Check the weather forecast** before traveling, as Porto Alegre can experience heavy rainfall during the summer months.
- **Consider purchasing a **city transportation card**, which can be used on buses and the metro and can help you save money and time.
