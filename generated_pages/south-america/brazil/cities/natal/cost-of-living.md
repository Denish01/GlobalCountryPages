# Cost of Living in Natal - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Natal is generally considered to be moderate, with a daily budget of $30-60 USD for backpackers, $60-120 USD for mid-range travelers, and $120-250 USD for comfort seekers.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $30-60 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $120-250 USD
Currency: BRL
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 10% in restaurants and bars is common
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Natal? [/SECTION]
Natal, a city in northeastern Brazil, offers a unique blend of urban and natural attractions, with a cost of living that is relatively moderate compared to other major cities in Brazil. The city's prices are influenced by its tourist industry, with a peak season during the summer months and holidays. Compared to global averages, Natal is considered to be moderately priced, with a cost of living index that is lower than many major cities in South America. However, prices can vary depending on the neighborhood, with areas like Ponta Negra and Via Costeira tend to be more expensive than others.

In terms of neighbors, Natal is situated near other major cities in northeastern Brazil, such as Fortaleza and Recife, which have similar cost of living indices. However, Natal's unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and urban amenities makes it a popular destination for tourists and expats alike. The city's economy is driven by tourism, commerce, and industry, which contributes to its moderate cost of living. Overall, Natal offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for travelers and expats.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel outside of the city center to save on accommodation costs.

- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transport costs.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost attractions, such as visiting the city's beaches and parks.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets for groceries and other essentials to save on expenses.
