🇧🇷 Where to Stay in Brasilia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Brasilia, consider the North Hotel Sector, Asa Sul, or Asa Norte neighborhoods for a mix of convenience, amenities, and affordability.

Best Areas to Stay

For each of the top areas in Brasilia, the following neighborhoods stand out:

Asa Sul (Brasilia)

Best For: Luxury / Business

Vibe: Asa Sul is known for its upscale atmosphere, with high-end shopping centers and restaurants, making it perfect for those seeking a sophisticated stay. The area is also close to the city's main attractions, such as the National Congress and the Cathedral of Brasilia.

Price Range: $100-300/night

Pros: Excellent infrastructure, proximity to major attractions, and a wide range of dining options. The area is also well-connected to the rest of the city via public transportation.

Cons: It can be quite crowded, especially during peak hours, and parking can be a challenge.

Asa Norte (Brasilia)

Best For: Family / Backpacker

Vibe: Asa Norte offers a more laid-back atmosphere, with plenty of parks, museums, and cultural events, making it an ideal choice for families and backpackers. The area is also home to the city's main bus terminal, providing easy access to other parts of the city.

Price Range: $50-150/night

Pros: Affordable accommodation options, plenty of green spaces, and a vibrant cultural scene. The area is also close to the city's main university, making it a hub for young people and students.

Cons: Some areas can be quite noisy, especially at night, and the streets can be crowded.

North Hotel Sector (Brasilia)

Best For: Business / Luxury

Vibe: The North Hotel Sector is a modern area with a focus on business and hospitality, featuring many high-end hotels and conference centers. The area is well-connected to the city's main attractions and is close to the international airport.

Price Range: $150-400/night

Pros: State-of-the-art infrastructure, excellent service, and a wide range of amenities. The area is also very safe, with 24/7 security and surveillance.

Cons: It can be quite expensive, and the area can feel somewhat isolated from the rest of the city.

Lago Sul (Brasilia)

Best For: Family / Relaxation

Vibe: Lago Sul is a charming neighborhood located on the shores of Lake Paranoa, offering a peaceful and relaxed atmosphere. The area is perfect for those seeking a tranquil stay, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking and water sports.

Price Range: $80-250/night

Pros: Stunning views of the lake, plenty of green spaces, and a range of outdoor activities. The area is also close to the city's main attractions, making it an ideal choice for families.

Cons: The area can be quite far from the city center, and public transportation options are limited.

Plano Piloto (Brasilia)

Best For: Backpacker / Budget

Vibe: Plano Piloto is the city's central neighborhood, featuring a mix of historic buildings, cultural attractions, and budget-friendly accommodation options. The area is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers, with plenty of affordable dining options and a vibrant nightlife.

Price Range: $30-100/night

Pros: Affordable accommodation options, plenty of cultural attractions, and a vibrant nightlife. The area is also close to the city's main bus terminal, making it easy to get around.

Cons: The area can be quite crowded, and some areas can be noisy at night.

Booking Tips

  • Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to March), to ensure availability and the best rates.
  • Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals.
  • Consider negotiating the price, especially for long-term stays or during the off-season.
  • Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during holidays and special events.
  • Read reviews and check the hotel's location before booking to ensure it meets your needs.
  • Look for package deals or special offers, which can include extras such as breakfast, parking, or tours.
  • Use travel agencies or local tour operators to book accommodation and tours, as they often have insider knowledge and can offer personalized recommendations.

Safety & Location Tips

  • Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas, especially at night.
  • Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas, such as bus terminals or tourist attractions.
  • Keep valuables safe and secure, and use hotel safes or lockers when available.
  • Choose accommodation in safe and well-lit areas, with 24/7 security and surveillance.
  • Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation, such as Asa Sul or Asa Norte.
  • Be mindful of traffic and pedestrian rules, as Brasilia's roads can be busy and chaotic.
