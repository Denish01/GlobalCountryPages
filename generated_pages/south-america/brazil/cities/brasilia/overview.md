[SECTION] What Is Brasilia? [/SECTION]
Brasilia is a **federal capital** located in the Federal District of Brazil, a country in South America. It is situated in the central part of the country, approximately 1,000 kilometers northwest of Rio de Janeiro. Brasilia is known for its modernist architecture, designed by Oscar Niemeyer, and its urban planning, designed by Lúcio Costa. The city was built in the 1950s to replace Rio de Janeiro as the capital of Brazil, with the goal of promoting the development of the country's interior region.

Brasilia is a **planned city**, designed to be a symbol of Brazilian modernity and progress. The city's unique urban planning and architecture have made it a popular tourist destination, with attractions such as the **National Congress**, the **Cathedral of Brasilia**, and the **Palace of the Planalto**. Brasilia is also an important cultural and educational center, with several universities and research institutions.

The city's location in the central part of Brazil makes it an important hub for transportation and communication. Brasilia is connected to other major cities in Brazil by highways, railroads, and airways, and it is also an important center for international trade and diplomacy. The city's unique blend of modern architecture and natural beauty, with several parks and lakes, makes it a popular destination for both tourists and business travelers.
