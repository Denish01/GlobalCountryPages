[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Manaus, the capital of the Amazonas state in Brazil, is a bustling metropolis surrounded by the lush Amazon rainforest. While the city offers a unique blend of urban and natural attractions, its safety landscape is complex. Petty crimes, such as pickpocketing and street theft, are common in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, there have been instances of violent crime, although these are generally not targeted at tourists. It is essential for visitors to be aware of their surroundings, especially in unfamiliar areas, and to take necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

The city's infrastructure and services, including the tourist police, are generally well-equipped to handle tourist-related issues. However, as with any travel destination, it is crucial to stay informed about local conditions and to exercise caution when exploring the city. This includes avoiding traveling alone at night, using reputable taxi services, and keeping valuables secure. By being mindful of these factors, visitors can minimize their risk and have a safe and enjoyable experience in Manaus.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of taxi scams, where drivers may overcharge or take longer routes to increase fares
- Pickpocketing and street theft are common in crowded areas, keep valuables secure
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Be wary of friendly locals who approach you with unsolicited advice or offers, they may be attempting to scam you
- Credit card skimming and ATM scams have been reported, use reputable machines and keep an eye on your accounts
- Boat tours and excursions can be hazardous if not operated by reputable companies, research and choose operators carefully
- Panhandling and begging are common, be firm but polite when declining requests

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Yellow fever vaccination is recommended for travel to Manaus, as well as other standard vaccinations such as hepatitis A and typhoid
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, bottled or filtered water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Manaus has good quality medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, but medical care can be expensive, and travel insurance is recommended
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are readily available and well-stocked, but some medications may require a prescription

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Stay informed about local conditions and events that may affect safety
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Keep valuables secure and be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit and populated areas
- Learn some basic Portuguese phrases, such as "where is..." and "how much...", to help you navigate
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe for valuables
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting indigenous communities

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious when interacting with strangers, and avoid accepting rides or invitations from unknown individuals
- Dress modestly and avoid displaying signs of wealth, to minimize unwanted attention
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home, and let them know your itinerary and whereabouts
- Consider joining a guided tour or group excursion, to meet other travelers and stay safe in numbers
