# Getting Around Manaus — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Manaus, you can use a variety of transportation options including buses, taxis, and ride-hailing apps, as well as domestic flights and intercity buses to explore the surrounding region.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Manaus is a major city in the Amazonas state of Brazil, and it has a well-connected international airport, Manaus International Airport (MAO), which receives direct flights from major hubs in Brazil, such as Sao Paulo and Rio de Janeiro, as well as international flights from neighboring countries. The airport is located about 14 kilometers from the city center, and you can get there by taxi, bus, or ride-hailing app. If you're coming from other parts of Brazil, you can also take a domestic flight to Manaus, which is often the fastest and most convenient option.

If you prefer to travel overland, you can take a bus or drive to Manaus from other cities in Brazil, such as Belo Horizonte or Brasilia. The city is connected to the rest of the country by a network of highways, including the BR-174 and BR-319, which pass through the Amazon rainforest. However, be aware that road conditions can be challenging, especially during the rainy season, and the journey can take several days. Additionally, there are no direct overland entry points from neighboring countries, so you'll need to go through a major Brazilian city first.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Manaus has an extensive domestic air network, with flights to major cities in Brazil, such as Sao Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Brasilia. The main domestic airlines that operate in Manaus are Azul, Gol, and LATAM, which offer multiple daily flights to these destinations. You can book your flights online or through a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the time of year and how far in advance you book. A one-way ticket from Manaus to Sao Paulo, for example, can cost between $100 and $300.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Manaus does not have a rail network, so you won't be able to take a train to get around the city or to other parts of Brazil. The city's remote location in the Amazon rainforest makes it difficult to build and maintain a rail network, and most transportation is done by air or road.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity buses are a popular way to travel to and from Manaus, especially for shorter distances. Companies such as Expresso Brasilia and Util offer daily bus services to nearby cities, such as Belo Horizonte and Brasilia. The buses are generally comfortable and well-maintained, but the journey can be long and tiring, especially on rough roads. Prices vary depending on the destination and the type of bus, but you can expect to pay between $20 and $50 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Manaus, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- Road conditions in Manaus can be challenging, especially during the rainy season, so make sure to check the weather forecast before you leave.
- Fuel costs are relatively high in Brazil, so be prepared to pay around $1.50 per liter of gasoline.
- Car rental companies such as Hertz and Avis are available in Manaus, but be aware that prices can be steep, especially for 4x4 vehicles.
- Hazards such as potholes, pedestrians, and wildlife can be a problem on the roads, so drive carefully and defensively.
- Parking in the city center can be difficult, so consider using a parking garage or lot.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of scams and overcharging by taxi drivers, and always use a licensed taxi or ride-hailing app.
- Make sure to have some local currency, the **Brazilian Real**, for transportation costs, as not all drivers or companies accept credit cards.
- Download the **Uber** or **99** app to use ride-hailing services in Manaus.
- Consider purchasing a **rechargeable bus card** for convenient travel on the city bus system.
- Be prepared for **delays and cancellations** due to weather or traffic conditions.
- Always wear a **seatbelt** and follow local traffic laws to stay safe on the roads.
- Consider hiring a **private driver** or guide for the day to get a more personalized and safe transportation experience.
- Be aware of **traffic congestion** in the city center, especially during rush hour, and plan your route accordingly.
