[SECTION] Overview: Brazil vs Peru [/SECTION]
Brazil and Peru are two of the largest and most populous countries in South America, with distinct economies and cultures. Brazil is the largest economy in the region, with a strong service sector and a large industrial base, while Peru has a more diverse economy with a strong mining sector and a growing tourism industry. In terms of cost of living, Peru is generally cheaper than Brazil, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. The cost of living in Brazil is around 20-30% higher than in Peru, making Peru a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees on a budget.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in their cost of living. Brazil has a more developed economy and a larger middle class, which drives up prices for goods and services. Peru, on the other hand, has a more unequal economy, with a smaller middle class and a larger informal sector, which keeps prices lower. Additionally, the exchange rate between the Brazilian real and the Peruvian sol can also affect the cost of living, with the sol being generally weaker than the real. Overall, Peru offers a more affordable lifestyle, with a lower cost of living and a more relaxed pace of life.
