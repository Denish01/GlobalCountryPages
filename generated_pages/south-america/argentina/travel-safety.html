Overall Safety Assessment

Argentina is a vast and diverse country, and like many destinations, it has its share of safety concerns. While most visitors have a trouble-free experience, petty crime and scams are common in tourist areas, particularly in large cities like Buenos Aires. Visitors should be mindful of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, where pickpocketing and bag snatching are prevalent. Additionally, demonstrations and protests can occur, and while they are generally peaceful, they can occasionally turn violent.

However, with some basic precautions and awareness, visitors can minimize their risk and have a safe and enjoyable trip. Argentina has a rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant atmosphere, making it a popular destination for travelers. The country has a well-developed tourist infrastructure, and many areas are considered safe for visitors. By taking necessary precautions and staying informed, travelers can have a positive experience in Argentina.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be aware of pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Be cautious of scams targeting tourists, such as the "mustard scam" or "gold ring scam"
  • Be mindful of demonstrations and protests, which can occasionally turn violent
  • Be aware of taxi scams, where drivers may overcharge or take passengers on a longer route
  • Be cautious when using ATMs, as card skimming and theft can occur
  • Be aware of drink spiking and theft in bars and clubs
  • Be cautious when exchanging money, as some exchange offices may offer poor rates or engage in scams

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended for most travelers
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are generally good in major cities, but can be limited in rural areas. Travelers may need to pay upfront for medical services, and having travel insurance is recommended
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available in major cities, but may be limited in rural areas

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
  • Be cautious when using ATMs and avoid using them at night or in isolated areas
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases, such as "쮻칩nde est치...?" (where is...) and "쮺u치nto cuesta?" (how much does it cost)
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting churches or attending cultural events
  • Consider hiring a reputable taxi service or using a ride-hailing app instead of hailing a taxi on the street

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
  • Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, such as Recoleta or Palermo in Buenos Aires
  • Be cautious when interacting with strangers, especially if they are overly friendly or persistent
  • Consider joining a group tour or finding a travel buddy to explore the country with
