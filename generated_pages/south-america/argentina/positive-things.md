[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Iguazú Falls**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in the world, located on the border of Argentina and Brazil.
- The **Tango** is a traditional Argentine dance that originated in the late 19th century and is characterized by its passionate and sensual movements.
- The **Festival de Cine de Mar del Plata** is one of the most important film festivals in Latin America, showcasing the best of Argentine and international cinema.
- The **Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes** in Buenos Aires is one of the most important art museums in Latin America, featuring a collection of over 12,000 works of art.
- The **Carnaval de Gualeguaychú** is a vibrant and colorful carnival that takes place every year in the city of Gualeguaychú, featuring parades, music, and dance.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Che Guevara**, the famous Argentine revolutionary, played a significant role in the Cuban Revolution and became an international symbol of socialism and revolution.
- **Jorge Luis Borges**, one of the most important Argentine writers of the 20th century, was a major influence on world literature, with his works translated into over 30 languages.
- **Diego Maradona**, the legendary Argentine football player, is widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time, leading Argentina to victory in the 1986 World Cup.
- **Marta Minujín**, a renowned Argentine artist, has created numerous iconic works, including the **Tower of Babel**, a giant sculpture made of books.

- The **delicious Argentine cuisine**, which features popular dishes like **asado**, **empanadas**, and **dulce de leche**, is a major draw for foodies.
- The **stunning natural scenery** of Argentina, from the **Andes mountains** to the **Patagonia** region, offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures.
- The **vibrant city life** of Buenos Aires, with its **European-style architecture**, **world-class museums**, and **lively nightlife**, is a major attraction for urban enthusiasts.

- The **world-class wine regions** of Argentina, such as **Mendoza**, offer wine tastings and tours that showcase the country's renowned **Malbec** wines.
