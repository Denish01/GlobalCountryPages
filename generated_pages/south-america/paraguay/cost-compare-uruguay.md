[SECTION] Overview: Paraguay vs Uruguay [/SECTION]
Paraguay and Uruguay are two neighboring countries in South America with distinct economic profiles. Paraguay has a relatively low cost of living, driven by its large agricultural sector and limited industrial base. In contrast, Uruguay has a more diversified economy with a strong service sector, which contributes to its higher cost of living. The overall cost of living in Paraguay is around 30-40% lower than in Uruguay, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Paraguay has a relatively low GDP per capita and a large informal economy, which keeps prices low. Uruguay, on the other hand, has a higher GDP per capita and a more developed economy, which drives up prices. Additionally, Uruguay has a higher inflation rate than Paraguay, which can erode the purchasing power of consumers over time. Overall, Paraguay's lower cost of living and more stable economy make it a more attractive option for those looking for an affordable lifestyle.
