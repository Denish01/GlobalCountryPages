# Top Things to Do in Paraguay — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Paraguay, visit its historic capital Asuncion, explore the stunning Iguazu Falls, and delve into the unique blend of Spanish and Guarani cultures that define this South American country.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Guarani language class** to learn about the indigenous culture and communicate with locals
- Attend a traditional **Asado** barbecue, where you can savor local meats and sides
- Visit a **yerba mate** plantation to understand the production of this popular South American beverage
- Explore the **Mercado de Abasto** in Asuncion, a bustling marketplace filled with fresh produce and handicrafts
- Join a **harpsichord concert** at the Palacio de López, featuring Paraguayan music and dance
- Learn about **Ñanduti** lace-making, a traditional craft that is unique to Paraguay
- Participate in a **Fiesta de San Juan** celebration, a vibrant festival with music, dance, and food

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Chaco National Park**, home to a wide range of wildlife, including jaguars, pumas, and toucans
- Hike to the **Salto del Monday**, a stunning waterfall in the Mbaracayu Forest Reserve
- Visit the **Ibera Wetlands**, a vast and diverse ecosystem with opportunities for birdwatching and boat tours
- Relax on the beaches of **Lake Ypacaray**, a popular spot for swimming and water sports
- Take a guided tour of the **Defensores del Chaco National Park**, which features unique rock formations and scenic views

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes**, which showcases Paraguayan art from the colonial period to the present
- Explore the **Casa de la Independencia**, a museum dedicated to the country's struggle for independence
- Attend a performance at the **Teatro Municipal**, a historic theater that hosts concerts, plays, and dance recitals
- Learn about the history of the **Jesuit Missions** and their impact on Paraguayan culture
- Visit the **Museo de la Historia del Arte**, which features a collection of Paraguayan and international art
- Take a **cooking class** to learn about traditional Paraguayan cuisine and ingredients

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **town of San Bernardino**, a charming lakeside resort with a rich history and cultural significance
- Explore the **Mbaracayu Forest Reserve**, a protected area with unique flora and fauna
- Discover the **Cueva de los Cristales**, a cave filled with stunning crystal formations
- Attend a **Festival de Música Barroca**, a celebration of Baroque music and culture
- Visit the **Isla de Francia**, a scenic island in the Paraguay River with historic significance and natural beauty

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Ciudad del Este** (200 km, 3 hours), a bustling city with a vibrant market and stunning views of the Iguazu Falls
- Visit the **Jesuit Missions** (150 km, 2.5 hours), a collection of historic sites that demonstrate the influence of Jesuit settlers
- Explore the **Chaco National Park** (400 km, 6 hours), a protected area with diverse wildlife and ecosystems
- Go to **San Bernardino** (50 km, 1 hour), a charming lakeside resort with a rich history and cultural significance
- Visit the **Ibera Wetlands** (300 km, 5 hours), a vast and diverse ecosystem with opportunities for birdwatching and boat tours
