[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Falkland Islands is based on the British model, with **English** being the primary language of instruction. The system is generally considered to be of high quality, with a strong emphasis on core subjects such as mathematics, science, and language. Public education is free and compulsory for all children between the ages of 5 and 16, and is subsidized by the government. Private education is also available, although options are limited due to the small size of the islands. There are no international schools in Falkland Islands, although some students may choose to attend schools in the UK or other countries.

The education system in Falkland Islands is designed to provide students with a well-rounded education, with a focus on academic achievement as well as personal development. The curriculum is based on the UK National Curriculum, with some modifications to reflect the unique cultural and geographical context of the islands. Students typically attend primary school from ages 5 to 11, and then attend secondary school from ages 11 to 16. After completing their secondary education, students may choose to pursue further education or vocational training, either in Falkland Islands or abroad.
