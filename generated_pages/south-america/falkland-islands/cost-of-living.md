# Cost of Living in Falkland Islands - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Falkland Islands is generally expensive due to its remote location and limited access to resources, making it a challenging destination for budget travelers. 

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Expensive
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $100-150 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $200-300 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $350-500 USD
Currency: FKP
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Falkland Islands? [/SECTION]
Falkland Islands is considered one of the more expensive destinations in South America, mainly due to its isolated location and reliance on imports from the United Kingdom and other countries. The cost of living is significantly higher than in neighboring Argentina, and even higher than in many parts of the United Kingdom. This is reflected in the prices of accommodation, food, and transportation, making it a challenging destination for budget travelers. The islands' economy is primarily driven by fishing and tourism, which contributes to the high cost of living.

The remote location of Falkland Islands also means that many everyday items are imported, which increases their cost. Furthermore, the limited availability of certain goods and services can drive up prices, making it essential for visitors to plan carefully and budget accordingly. Despite the high costs, Falkland Islands offers a unique and rewarding experience for those who are willing to invest in exploring this remote and beautiful archipelago.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Plan ahead and book accommodation and tours in advance to secure the best prices.
- Consider visiting during the shoulder season (April to May or September to November) when prices are lower.
- Take advantage of the islands' natural beauty and enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and wildlife watching, which are often free or low-cost.
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets for groceries and supplies instead of relying on tourist-oriented shops.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transportation costs.
- Cook your own meals instead of eating out at restaurants to save money on food expenses.
