# Where to Stay in Colombia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Colombia, consider the vibrant cities of Bogota, Medellin, and Cartagena, each offering a unique blend of culture, history, and entertainment that cater to different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas/neighborhoods in Colombia, there's a distinct character and set of advantages that make them ideal for specific types of travelers.

**La Candelaria** (Bogota)
Best For: backpacker / culture enthusiast
Vibe: This historic neighborhood in Bogota is known for its picturesque streets, colonial architecture, and lively cultural scene, making it perfect for those who want to immerse themselves in the city's rich history and nightlife. The area is filled with museums, restaurants, and bars, offering something for every interest.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: central location, access to major attractions, vibrant nightlife
Cons: can be noisy, limited parking options

**El Poblado** (Medellin)
Best For: luxury / business
Vibe: As one of the most upscale neighborhoods in Medellin, El Poblado offers a sophisticated and modern atmosphere, with high-end shopping centers, restaurants, and hotels that cater to the needs of business travelers and those seeking luxury. The area is also known for its stunning views of the city and its proximity to major business centers.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: modern amenities, safe, excellent dining options
Cons: can be expensive, traffic congestion

**Getsemani** (Cartagena)
Best For: family / budget
Vibe: This charming neighborhood in Cartagena is a hidden gem for families and budget travelers, offering a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere, with affordable accommodations, local restaurants, and a vibrant cultural scene. The area is filled with colorful streets, historic landmarks, and a lively nightlife.
Price Range: $15-30/night
Pros: affordable, family-friendly, close to the beach
Cons: can be crowded, limited parking

**Parque Lleras** (Medellin)
Best For: nightlife / social
Vibe: As the heart of Medellin's nightlife, Parque Lleras is a bustling neighborhood filled with bars, clubs, and restaurants that cater to the city's vibrant social scene. The area is perfect for those who want to experience the city's energetic nightlife and meet new people.
Price Range: $30-60/night
Pros: lively atmosphere, many dining options, central location
Cons: can be noisy, crowded

**San Diego** (Cartagena)
Best For: history / romance
Vibe: This historic neighborhood in Cartagena is a romantic's paradise, with its picturesque streets, colonial architecture, and stunning views of the city walls. The area is filled with boutique hotels, restaurants, and shops that cater to couples and history enthusiasts.
Price Range: $50-100/night
Pros: historic charm, romantic atmosphere, excellent dining
Cons: can be touristy, expensive

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak travel seasons (December to March and June to August).
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider negotiating prices directly with hotel owners or guesthouse managers for longer stays.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during holidays and festivals.
- Read reviews and check the location of your accommodation to ensure it meets your needs.
- Use travel rewards credit cards or loyalty programs to earn points or discounts on bookings.
- Be cautious of hidden fees or charges, and always read the fine print before booking.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid staying in areas with high crime rates, such as the border regions with Venezuela or certain neighborhoods in major cities.
- Choose accommodations with good security measures, such as 24-hour reception, cameras, and safes.
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
- Stay in areas with easy access to public transportation, such as bus stations or metro lines.
- Consider staying in well-known neighborhoods or areas with a strong tourist presence, such as La Candelaria in Bogota or El Poblado in Medellin.
- Research your accommodation's location and proximity to major attractions, restaurants, and shops to ensure it meets your needs.
