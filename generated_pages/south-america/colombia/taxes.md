# Colombia Tax Guide — Income, Corporate & VAT Rates

Tax rates in Colombia range from 0% to 33% for personal income, with a corporate tax rate of 25%, and the country has a territorial taxation system, where residents are taxed on their Colombian-sourced income.

[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
Colombia has a territorial taxation system, where residents are taxed on their Colombian-sourced income, and non-residents are taxed only on their income derived from Colombian sources. The **residency rules** for tax purposes in Colombia are based on the individual's physical presence in the country, with individuals being considered residents if they have a permanent home in Colombia, or if they spend more than 183 days in the country within a calendar year.

The Colombian tax system is administered by the **Dirección de Impuestos y Aduanas Nacionales (DIAN)**, which is responsible for collecting taxes, customs, and other government revenues. The tax system is designed to promote economic growth, reduce poverty, and increase government revenues, with a focus on **tax fairness** and **compliance**. Colombia has a network of tax treaties with other countries, which helps to reduce double taxation and promote international trade and investment.

The personal income tax in Colombia is **progressive**, with higher income brackets subject to higher tax rates. Taxpayers are allowed to claim **deductions** and **allowances** for expenses such as mortgage interest, charitable donations, and education expenses. The tax filing deadline is typically in April of each year, and taxpayers can file their tax returns online or through a tax professional. It is recommended that taxpayers **consult a tax professional** to ensure compliance with all tax laws and regulations.

[SECTION] Corporate & Business Tax [/SECTION]
- The **corporate tax rate** in Colombia is 25%, which applies to all business profits, including income from sales, services, and investments.
- Small businesses and start-ups may be eligible for **tax incentives**, such as reduced tax rates or exemptions, to promote entrepreneurship and job creation.
- Colombia has a network of **free zones**, which offer tax exemptions and other incentives to businesses that operate within these zones.
- Businesses must **register** with the DIAN and obtain a tax ID number to operate in Colombia.
- Businesses must also comply with **value-added tax (VAT)** regulations, which apply to most goods and services.

[SECTION] VAT / Sales Tax [/SECTION]
- The **standard VAT rate** in Colombia is 19%, which applies to most goods and services.
- There are **reduced VAT rates** of 5% and 0% for certain goods and services, such as basic food items, healthcare services, and education.
- Some goods and services are **exempt** from VAT, such as financial services, insurance, and real estate transactions.
- Colombia has a **tourist refund scheme**, which allows foreign tourists to claim a refund of VAT paid on certain goods and services.

[SECTION] For Expats & Foreign Workers [/SECTION]
- **Tax residency rules** apply to expats and foreign workers, who are considered residents if they spend more than 183 days in Colombia within a calendar year.
- Colombia has a network of **double taxation treaties** with other countries, which helps to reduce double taxation and promote international trade and investment.
- Expats and foreign workers may be subject to **social security** contributions, which are used to fund pension, healthcare, and other social benefits.
- Expats and foreign workers must **comply with tax laws** and regulations, including filing tax returns and paying taxes on their Colombian-sourced income.
- Expats and foreign workers may be eligible for **tax credits** for taxes paid in their home country, which can help reduce their tax liability in Colombia.
- Expats and foreign workers should **consult a tax professional** to ensure compliance with all tax laws and regulations.

[SECTION] Crypto & Investment Income [/SECTION]
- **Investment income**, such as dividends, interest, and capital gains, is subject to taxation in Colombia.
- **Cryptocurrency** transactions are subject to taxation, with gains from cryptocurrency sales subject to capital gains tax.
- **Dividends** paid to shareholders are subject to a **withholding tax** of 5%, which can be reduced or eliminated under certain circumstances.
- **Capital gains** from the sale of securities, such as stocks and bonds, are subject to a **capital gains tax** of 10%, which can be reduced or eliminated under certain circumstances.
