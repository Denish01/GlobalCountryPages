# Getting Around Colombia — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Colombia, you can use a combination of domestic flights, buses, taxis, and ride-hailing apps, as well as explore the country's improving road network and limited rail options.

[FACTBOX]
Main International Airport(s): El Dorado International Airport (BOG), José María Córdova International Airport (MDE)
Domestic Air Network: extensive
Rail Network: limited
Drives On: right
Road Quality: moderate
Ride-Hailing Apps: Uber, Cabify, local alternatives
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Colombia has several international airports, with the main ones being El Dorado International Airport in Bogotá and José María Córdova International Airport in Medellín. These airports receive direct flights from major hubs in the Americas, Europe, and Asia, making it relatively easy to get to Colombia from anywhere in the world. Some of the key direct flight hubs include Miami, Madrid, and Panama City. If you prefer to enter Colombia overland, you can do so from neighboring countries such as Venezuela, Brazil, Peru, Ecuador, and Panama, with the most popular entry points being at the border towns of Cúcuta, Ipiales, and Turbo.

The main overland entry points are well-connected to the rest of the country by bus and taxi services, making it relatively easy to get to your final destination. However, be sure to research the current border crossing requirements and any necessary visas or documentation before attempting to enter Colombia by land. Additionally, some border regions have experienced safety concerns in the past, so it's essential to stay informed and plan your route accordingly.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Colombia has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to major cities and tourist destinations. Some of the main domestic airlines include Avianca, Copa Airlines, and LATAM, which offer frequent flights to cities such as Medellín, Cali, and Cartagena. You can book domestic flights online or through a travel agency, and it's recommended to do so in advance to secure the best prices. Approximate costs for domestic flights can range from $50 to $200 USD, depending on the route and time of year. Some popular routes include Bogotá to Medellín, Bogotá to Cartagena, and Medellín to Cali.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Colombia's rail network is limited, with only a few tourist trains operating in certain regions. The most notable one is the **Turistren**, which runs from Bogotá to Zipaquirá and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside. There are no high-speed rail options in Colombia, and the existing rail network is primarily used for freight transport. If you're interested in taking a tourist train, you can book tickets online or through a travel agency, but be aware that options are limited and may not be available year-round.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Colombia has an extensive network of intercity buses and coaches, which are a popular and affordable way to get around the country. Some of the main bus companies include Expreso Brasilia, Copetran, and Flota Occidental, which offer services to major cities and tourist destinations. Bus quality can vary depending on the company and route, but many buses are modern and comfortable, with amenities such as air conditioning and onboard Wi-Fi. Costs can range from $10 to $50 USD, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Colombia, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country, as well as a **permiso de conducción** (driving permit) if you plan to stay for an extended period.
- Road conditions in Colombia can be challenging, especially in rural areas, with **potholes** and **landslides** being common hazards.
- Fuel costs in Colombia are relatively low, with gasoline and diesel prices averaging around $0.50-1.00 USD per liter.
- If you plan to rent a car, be sure to research reputable companies and **read reviews** beforehand to ensure you're getting a reliable vehicle.
- Be aware of **traffic laws** and regulations, such as speed limits and seatbelt requirements, to avoid fines or penalties.
- Consider hiring a **driver or guide** if you're not comfortable driving in Colombia, especially in large cities or rural areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **scams** and **overcharging** by taxi drivers or street vendors, and always agree on a price beforehand.
- Use **reputable transportation companies** and research **reviews** to ensure you're getting a safe and reliable service.
- Download **transportation apps** such as Uber or Cabify to book rides and track your journey.
- Keep **valuables secure** and be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas or public transportation.
- Consider purchasing **travel insurance** to cover any unexpected medical or transportation-related expenses.
- Stay **informed** about local events, protests, or demonstrations, which can affect transportation and safety.
- Learn some basic **Spanish phrases** to help you navigate and communicate with locals during your journey.
- Be prepared for **delays** or **cancellations** due to weather or other factors, and stay flexible with your travel plans.
