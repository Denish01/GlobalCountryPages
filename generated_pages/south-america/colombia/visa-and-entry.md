# Colombia Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Colombia before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Colombia has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter without a visa or with a visa on arrival. The country has been working to promote tourism and business, and as a result, it has relaxed its visa requirements for many nationalities. Currently, citizens of over 90 countries can enter Colombia without a visa, including many countries in the Americas, Europe, and Asia. However, it's essential to note that even if you don't need a visa, you may still need to meet specific requirements, such as having a valid passport or proof of onward travel.

Colombia's visa policy is designed to promote tourism, business, and cultural exchange, while also ensuring the security and integrity of the country. The government has implemented various measures to facilitate the entry of foreign visitors, including the introduction of an electronic visa system and the expansion of visa-free access to citizens of certain countries. Overall, Colombia's visa policy is relatively liberal, making it easy for many people to visit and explore this beautiful and vibrant country.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can stay in Colombia for up to 90 days without a visa, and can extend their stay for an additional 90 days. To enter the country, tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to obtain a visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They must provide an invitation letter from a Colombian company, proof of business activities, and other supporting documents. **Students:** Students must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a Colombian university, a valid passport, and sufficient funds. The student visa is usually valid for the duration of the study program. **Workers:** Workers must obtain a work visa, which requires a job offer from a Colombian employer, a valid passport, and other supporting documents. The work visa is usually valid for the duration of the employment contract.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
Colombia has several main airports, including El Dorado International Airport in Bogota, Jose Maria Cordova International Airport in Medellin, and Alfonso Bonilla Aragon International Airport in Cali. When arriving at an airport, visitors must go through immigration and customs, where they will need to present their travel documents and answer questions from immigration officials. The process is usually straightforward, but it's essential to have all required documents ready and to be prepared to answer questions about the purpose of your visit and your plans while in Colombia. Land borders are also an option, with Colombia sharing borders with Venezuela, Brazil, Peru, Ecuador, and Panama. When crossing the border by land, visitors must stop at the immigration office and present their documents before proceeding.

- Not completing the visa application form correctly (if required)
- Not bringing recent passport-sized photos
- Not having a hotel reservation or confirmation of accommodation
- Not declaring goods or items subject to customs duties
- Not having health insurance or proof of medical coverage (recommended)
