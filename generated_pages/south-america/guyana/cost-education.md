# Education Costs in Guyana

The cost of education in Guyana varies depending on the type of institution and level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Guyana is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being generally free or subsidized. The language of instruction is **English**, and compulsory education lasts for 12 years, from age 5 to 17. The public education system is managed by the Ministry of Education, and while it is generally considered to be of good quality, some schools may lack resources and infrastructure. Private schools, on the other hand, are often considered to be of higher quality, but can be expensive and exclusive.

The academic year in Guyana typically runs from September to July, with a break in December and another in April. International schools are also available in Guyana, particularly in the capital city of Georgetown, and offer a range of curricula, including **Cambridge International Examinations** and **International Baccalaureate**. These schools cater mainly to expatriate children and offer a high-quality education, but can be very expensive.
