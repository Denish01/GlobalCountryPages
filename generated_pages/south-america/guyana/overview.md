[SECTION] What Is Guyana? [/SECTION]
Guyana is a **sovereign** country located on the northern coast of **South America**, bordered by **Venezuela** to the west, **Brazil** to the south, and **Suriname** to the east. With a population of approximately 0.81 million people, Guyana is the only English-speaking country in South America. The country is known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse wildlife, and vast natural resources, including gold, diamonds, and timber. Guyana is also home to a unique blend of indigenous, African, European, and Asian influences, making it a fascinating destination for travelers and cultural enthusiasts.

Geographically, Guyana is characterized by its dense rainforests, vast savannas, and rugged mountains. The country's terrain is divided into five main regions: the **Coastal Plain**, the **Hilly Sand and Clay Region**, the **Pacaraima Mountains**, the **Rupununi Savannah**, and the **Essequibo River Basin**. Guyana's diverse geography supports a wide range of flora and fauna, including the iconic **giant water lily**, the **jaguar**, and the **harpy eagle**.

Guyana's strategic location and natural resources have made it an important player in regional and international trade. The country is a member of the **Caribbean Community (CARICOM)** and the **Union of South American Nations (UNASUR)**, and has strong economic ties with neighboring countries. Guyana's economy is primarily driven by the **agriculture**, **mining**, and **forestry** sectors, with a growing focus on **tourism** and **renewable energy**.
