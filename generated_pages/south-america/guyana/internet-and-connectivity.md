# Guyana Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Guyana is generally reliable in urban areas, but speeds and coverage can be limited in rural regions, making it essential to plan ahead and research the best options for staying connected.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Guyana has improved significantly over the years, with **average internet speeds** reaching up to 10 Mbps download. However, there is a noticeable **urban vs rural divide**, with faster and more reliable connections available in cities like Georgetown, while rural areas often experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. The **reliability of internet connections** can also vary depending on the provider and location, with some areas experiencing more frequent drops in service than others. Overall, while the internet in Guyana is not the fastest or most reliable, it is generally sufficient for basic tasks like browsing, emailing, and social media.

In terms of **infrastructure**, Guyana's internet is largely based on **fiber-optic cables** and **mobile networks**, with many providers offering **3G and 4G** services. However, the lack of **5G coverage** and limited **rural connectivity** can make it difficult for those in remote areas to stay connected. Despite these challenges, the Guyanese government has been working to improve the country's **digital infrastructure**, with plans to expand **broadband access** and increase **internet speeds** in the coming years.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Guyana, visitors can purchase one from a local provider like **Digicel** or **GTT** at the airport or in major towns. To buy a SIM card, you will typically need to provide a **passport** and **proof of address**, although some providers may have additional requirements. **Tourist SIM cards** are available for around 10 USD and often come with a set amount of **data and minutes**. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable provider and to research the different plans and options available to find the one that best suits your needs.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi is generally **reliable and fast**, with many establishments offering **free WiFi** to guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is not as prevalent in Guyana as it is in other countries, but many **restaurants and bars** do offer **free WiFi** to customers.
- **Coworking spaces** are limited in Guyana, but there are a few options available in Georgetown and other major towns.
- Public WiFi is not widely available in Guyana, but some **malls and shopping centers** do offer **free WiFi** to visitors.
- **Internet cafes** are still a common sight in Guyana, especially in rural areas where **home internet** is not always available.
