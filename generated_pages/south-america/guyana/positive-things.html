Notable Achievements

ScienceBiodiversity ConservationGuyana has made significant strides in conserving its unique biodiversity, with over 80% of its land area remaining intact as forests, making it a global leader in environmental conservation.
SportsCricket TeamThe Guyana national cricket team has won several regional tournaments, including the Caribbean Premier League, and has produced notable players such as Shivnarine Chanderpaul and Ramnaresh Sarwan.
ArtsAmerindian ArtGuyana's indigenous communities have a rich artistic heritage, with intricate wood carvings, vibrant textiles, and traditional music, such as the Pattamunai flute, being recognized and celebrated globally.

Global ContributionsUN PeacekeepingGuyana has contributed troops to several United Nations peacekeeping missions, including in Liberia and the Democratic Republic of Congo, demonstrating its commitment to global peace and security.

Cultural Treasures

  • The Kaieteur Falls is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most spectacular waterfalls in the world, with a total height of 741 feet.
  • The St. George's Cathedral in Georgetown is a stunning example of Gothic Revival architecture and one of the tallest wooden buildings in the world.
  • Guyana's Amerindian communities have a rich cultural heritage, with nine distinct indigenous groups, each with their own language, traditions, and customs.
  • The Guyana National Museum showcases the country's history, art, and culture, with a collection of artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays.
  • The Mashramani festival is a vibrant celebration of Guyanese culture, music, and dance, held annually to commemorate the country's independence.

People & Global Impact

  • Forbes Burnham, Guyana's first prime minister, played a key role in the country's struggle for independence and was a prominent figure in regional politics.
  • The Guyanese diaspora has made significant contributions to the cultural, economic, and social development of countries such as the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom.
  • Rudolph Dunbar, a Guyanese-born musician and composer, was a pioneer of classical music in the Caribbean and a renowned conductor.
  • Guyana's indigenous leaders, such as Janette Bulkan, have been recognized internationally for their advocacy on environmental and human rights issues.

What Visitors Love

  • Guyana's hospitality is renowned, with visitors often commenting on the warm and welcoming nature of the Guyanese people.
  • The country's cuisine is a unique blend of Caribbean, African, and Asian flavors, with popular dishes such as cook-up rice and pepper pot soup.
  • The Kaieteur Falls is a must-visit attraction, with its breathtaking natural beauty and stunning scenery.
  • Guyana's rainforests offer a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting.
  • The Essequibo River is a popular spot for boat tours and fishing, with its tranquil waters and picturesque scenery.
  • Guyana's cultural events, such as the Mashramani festival, are a great way to experience the country's vibrant music, dance, and art.

Hidden Gems

  • The Iwokrama River Lodge is a secluded eco-lodge located in the heart of Guyana's rainforest, offering stunning views and a range of outdoor activities.
  • The Burro-Burro River is a tranquil and picturesque waterway that is home to a range of wildlife, including giant otters and harpy eagles.
  • The Guyana Marine Turtle Conservation Society is a non-profit organization that works to protect the country's marine turtle population and their habitats.
  • The Amerindian community of Annai is a small village located in the north of Guyana, known for its traditional crafts and warm hospitality.
