# Where to Stay in South Georgia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in South Georgia, consider the research stations and settlements as the main areas for accommodation, with limited options available due to the remote and protected nature of the territory.

**King Edward Point** (King Edward Point)
Best For: Luxury/Research
Vibe: This area offers a unique and exclusive experience, with access to research facilities and stunning natural scenery, but it is also quite isolated and limited in terms of amenities. The vibe is quiet and focused on scientific research and exploration.

Pros: Exclusive access to research facilities, stunning natural scenery, unique experience

**Grytviken** (Grytviken)
Best For: Family/History
Vibe: This former whaling station turned settlement offers a glimpse into the island's past, with a museum and historic buildings, and a sense of community among the residents. The vibe is nostalgic and educational, with a focus on the island's history and heritage.
Price Range: $300-700/night
Pros: Historic significance, sense of community, access to museum
Cons: Limited amenities, weather can be harsh

**Stromness** (Stromness)
Best For: Backpacker/Nature
Vibe: This abandoned whaling station is now a popular spot for hiking and wildlife watching, with stunning scenery and a sense of adventure. The vibe is rugged and natural, with a focus on exploration and discovery.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: Stunning scenery, hiking and wildlife watching opportunities, sense of adventure
Cons: Abandoned and rugged, limited amenities

**Husvik** (Husvik)
Best For: Luxury/Adventure
Vibe: This former whaling station turned luxury lodge offers high-end amenities and exclusive access to outdoor activities, with a focus on comfort and relaxation. The vibe is luxurious and indulgent, with a sense of exclusivity and privilege.
Price Range: $800-1500/night
Pros: Luxury amenities, exclusive access to outdoor activities, stunning scenery
Cons: Expensive, limited availability

**Godthul** (Godthul)
Best For: Family/Nature

Price Range: $400-900/night
Pros: Stunning views, wildlife watching opportunities, sense of peace
Cons: Limited amenities, weather can be harsh
