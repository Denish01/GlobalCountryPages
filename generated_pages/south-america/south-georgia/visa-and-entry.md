[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of South Georgia before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
South Georgia has a strict visa policy, with only a limited number of nationalities eligible for visa-free access. The territory is sensitive to the impact of tourism on its fragile environment, and as such, visitors must adhere to strict guidelines and regulations. Currently, only British, Falkland Islands, and South Georgia citizens do not require a permit to enter the territory, while all other nationalities must apply for a permit in advance.

The Government of South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands issues permits for tourism, scientific research, and other purposes, and these permits are typically granted for a specific period, usually up to 30 days. The permit application process involves providing detailed information about the purpose of the visit, itinerary, and accommodation arrangements.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** To visit South Georgia as a tourist, you will need to apply for a permit through a licensed tour operator. The typical stay for tourists is up to 30 days, and extensions are not usually possible. Tourists must provide detailed information about their itinerary, including accommodation arrangements and planned activities.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a permit and an invitation letter from a local organization or company. The requirements for business travelers are similar to those for tourists, but may involve additional documentation, such as proof of business activities.
**Students:** Students conducting research in South Georgia must apply for a permit and provide proof of enrollment at a recognized institution. The duration of the permit will depend on the length of the research project, but is typically up to 6 months.
**Workers:** Workers, including scientists and support staff, must apply for a permit and provide proof of sponsorship from a local organization or company. The permit application process involves providing detailed information about the work to be undertaken, and the worker's qualifications and experience.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry point to South Georgia is the port of King Edward Point, which is located on the north coast of the island. Visitors must arrive by sea, as there are no commercial airports or land borders. Upon arrival, visitors will need to clear customs and immigration, and may be required to provide additional documentation, such as proof of permit and health insurance. The immigration process typically involves a briefing on the rules and regulations of the territory, and visitors may be required to sign a declaration stating that they will comply with these regulations.

Visitors should be prepared to provide detailed information about their itinerary, including planned activities and destinations, and may be required to undergo a baggage inspection. The immigration process can be time-consuming, and visitors should allow at least 2 hours to clear customs and immigration.
