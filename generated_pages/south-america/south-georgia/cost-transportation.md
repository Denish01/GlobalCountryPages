South Georgia is a remote island territory with limited transportation options, making it challenging to get around without prior planning. The most common mode of transportation is by boat, with several cruise lines and research vessels visiting the island throughout the year. For those living on the island, walking or using all-terrain vehicles (ATVs) are the primary means of getting around, given the lack of paved roads and public transportation systems. The quality of public transit is limited, and most people rely on private arrangements or government-provided transportation services.

The island's rugged terrain and harsh climate make it difficult to maintain a comprehensive public transportation system. However, the government and research stations on the island provide limited transportation services for residents and visitors. These services typically include boat transfers between the island's settlements and research stations, as well as limited land transportation using ATVs or 4x4 vehicles. Despite the challenges, the scenic beauty of the island and its unique wildlife make it an attractive destination for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts.
