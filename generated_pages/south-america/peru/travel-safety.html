Overall Safety Assessment

Peru is generally a safe country to visit, with low rates of violent crime. However, petty crime and scams can be a problem, particularly in tourist areas and major cities like Lima and Cusco. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as using reputable taxi services, avoiding traveling alone at night, and keeping valuables secure. Peru is also prone to natural disasters like earthquakes and floods, which can affect travel plans and safety.

Peru has a strong tourist police presence, which can provide assistance and support to visitors. Additionally, many popular tourist areas have implemented security measures, such as cameras and security patrols, to help prevent crime. Overall, with some basic precautions and awareness, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Peru. It's essential to stay informed about local conditions and take necessary precautions to minimize risks. By being aware of potential hazards and taking steps to mitigate them, visitors can have a positive and safe experience in Peru.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Avoid using unlicensed taxis or unofficial tour operators, as they may be scams or pose a safety risk
  • Be aware of altitude sickness when traveling to high-altitude areas like Cusco and Machu Picchu
  • Watch out for scams like the "help me" scam, where locals may approach tourists and ask for assistance with a fake problem
  • Be mindful of traffic accidents, as road conditions and driving habits can be hazardous
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use reputable ATMs and credit card machines
  • Be cautious of food and waterborne illnesses, and take necessary precautions to stay healthy

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A, typhoid, and yellow fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to Peru
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are generally good in major cities, but may be limited in rural areas; health insurance is recommended
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available, but prescription medications may be limited; it's a good idea to bring a supply of essential medications

Practical Safety Tips

  • Research and book reputable tour operators and accommodations to minimize risks
  • Stay informed about local conditions and weather forecasts to plan accordingly
  • Be mindful of cultural norms and local customs, and respect them to avoid unintended offense
  • Keep valuables secure and use hotel safes or lockers when available
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Stay hydrated and take necessary precautions to avoid altitude sickness
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases to help communicate with locals and navigate situations

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be aware of catcalling and harassment, and take steps to avoid or ignore it
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs to avoid unintended attention
  • Stay in well-lit and populated areas, and avoid walking alone at night
  • Consider joining group tours or female-only travel groups for added safety and support
