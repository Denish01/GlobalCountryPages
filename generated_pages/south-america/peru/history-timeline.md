# Peru History — Key Events & Timeline

The history of Peru is a rich and complex narrative that spans over 20,000 years, from the earliest known human presence in the region to the modern era, with significant events and periods that have shaped the country into what it is today.

[SECTION] Historical Overview [/SECTION]
The history of Peru can be broadly divided into several periods, including the pre-colonial era, the colonial period, the struggle for independence, and the modern era. The pre-colonial era saw the rise and fall of various civilizations, including the **Norte Chico Civilization**, which is considered one of the oldest civilizations in the Americas, dating back to around 3200 BCE. The **Inca Empire**, which emerged in the 13th century, was the most powerful and influential civilization in the region, stretching from modern-day Colombia to Chile. The arrival of the Spanish conquistadors in the 16th century marked the beginning of the colonial period, during which the indigenous population was subjected to exploitation, violence, and the imposition of European culture. Peru declared its independence from Spain in 1821, after a long and bloody struggle, and has since then experienced periods of political instability, economic growth, and social change.

The modern era has seen significant developments in Peru, including the discovery of oil and gas reserves, the growth of the tourism industry, and the implementation of economic reforms. However, the country has also faced challenges such as poverty, inequality, and corruption, which have hindered its progress and development. Despite these challenges, Peru has made significant strides in recent years, with a growing economy, improved infrastructure, and a more stable political environment. The country has also become a major player in regional and international affairs, participating in organizations such as the **Union of South American Nations** and the **Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation** forum.

The history of Peru is also marked by significant cultural and social developments, including the preservation of indigenous traditions and the promotion of cultural heritage. The country is home to a rich cultural landscape, with a diverse range of languages, customs, and beliefs. The **Spanish language** is the official language, but many indigenous languages are also spoken, including **Quechua** and **Aymara**. The country's cultural heritage is reflected in its music, art, literature, and cuisine, which are all deeply rooted in its history and traditions.

Peru's history has also been shaped by its geography, with the **Andes Mountains** running along its eastern edge and the **Amazon rainforest** covering a significant portion of its territory. The country's diverse geography has supported a wide range of ecosystems and has played a crucial role in the development of its cultures and civilizations. From the **deserts** of the coastal region to the **cloud forests** of the Andes, Peru's natural environment is incredibly diverse and has been an important factor in the country's history and development.

1. **15,000 BCE** - The first human presence in Peru is recorded, with evidence of hunter-gatherer communities in the region. The earliest known inhabitants of Peru were likely migrants from Asia who crossed the **Bering Strait** and made their way down the western coast of South America.
2. **3200 BCE** - The **Norte Chico Civilization** emerges in the coastal region of Peru, marking the beginning of urbanization and the development of complex societies. This civilization is considered one of the oldest in the Americas and is known for its sophisticated irrigation systems and architectural achievements.
3. **1200 CE** - The **Inca Empire** is founded by **Manco Cápac**, who establishes the city of **Cusco** as the capital. The Inca Empire would go on to become the largest and most powerful empire in pre-Columbian America, stretching from modern-day Colombia to Chile.
4. **1531 CE** - The Spanish conquistador **Francisco Pizarro** arrives in Peru, marking the beginning of the colonial period. Pizarro and his men would eventually defeat the Inca Empire and establish Spanish rule over the region.
5. **1542 CE** - The **Viceroyalty of Peru** is established, with **Lima** as its capital. The Viceroyalty of Peru was a Spanish colony that covered a vast territory, including modern-day Peru, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Bolivia, and parts of Argentina and Brazil.
6. **1780 CE** - The **Inca Rebellion** breaks out, led by **Túpac Amaru II**, who seeks to overthrow Spanish rule. The rebellion was ultimately unsuccessful, but it marked a significant turning point in the struggle for independence.
7. **1821 CE** - Peru declares its independence from Spain, with **José de San Martín** as its first leader. San Martín was a key figure in the Latin American wars of independence and played a crucial role in the liberation of Peru and other countries in the region.
8. **1822 CE** - The **Peruvian Constitution** is adopted, establishing the country as a republic. The constitution has been amended several times since then, but it remains the foundation of Peru's government and political system.
9. **1845 CE** - The **War of the Pacific** breaks out, pitting Peru against Chile and resulting in the loss of significant territory. The war was fought over control of the **Atacama Desert**, which is rich in mineral resources.
10. **1879 CE** - The **War of the Pacific** ends, with Peru ceding control of the **Atacama Desert** to Chile. The war had a significant impact on Peru's economy and politics, leading to a period of instability and stagnation.
11. **1912 CE** - The **Peruvian Communist Party** is founded, marking the beginning of a long period of left-wing activism in the country. The party has played a significant role in Peruvian politics, with many of its members holding important positions in government and society.
12. **1945 CE** - The **United Nations** is founded, with Peru as one of its founding members. Peru has been an active participant in the United Nations, playing a key role in regional and international affairs.
13. **1956 CE** - The **Peruvian economy** begins to experience rapid growth, driven by investment in mining and agriculture. The economy has continued to grow and diversify since then, with a focus on services, manufacturing, and tourism.
14. **1968 CE** - A **military coup** overthrows the government, leading to a period of authoritarian rule. The coup was led by **Juan Velasco Alvarado**, who would go on to rule Peru for several years.
15. **1980 CE** - Peru returns to democratic rule, with **Fernando Belaúnde** as its first democratically elected president. Belaúnde's government marked a significant turning point in Peruvian history, as the country began to transition towards democracy and economic liberalization.
