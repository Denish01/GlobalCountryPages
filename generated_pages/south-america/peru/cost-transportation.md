[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Peru has a well-developed transportation system, with various modes of transport available, including buses, taxis, ride-hailing services, and trains. The quality of public transit is generally good, with many cities having a comprehensive network of buses and taxis. Most people in Peru get around using public transportation, with buses being the most popular mode of transport. The bus network is extensive, covering most cities and towns, and is relatively affordable, with fares starting from $0.50 USD.

In larger cities like Lima, the public transportation system is more developed, with a metro system and a network of buses and taxis. The metro system is efficient and reliable, with fares starting from $0.50 USD. Taxis are also widely available, but it's recommended to use licensed taxis or ride-hailing services to ensure safety. The cost of transportation can vary depending on the city, with Lima being one of the more expensive cities in terms of transportation costs.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using public buses or minivans, which are relatively cheap and widely available.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Cabify, which are convenient and often cheaper than taxis.
- Always negotiate the fare before getting into a taxi, and make sure to use licensed taxis.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded public transportation.
- Consider purchasing a reusable **Billetaje** card, which can be used to pay for public transportation fares and can be refilled at various locations.
- Download the **Moovit** app, which provides information on public transportation routes and schedules, and can help you navigate the city more easily.
