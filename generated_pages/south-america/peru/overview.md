[SECTION] What Is Peru? [/SECTION]
Peru is a **sovereign** country located in western South America, bordered by Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, Bolivia, and Chile. It has a diverse geography, with a long coastline along the Pacific Ocean, as well as a vast array of landscapes, including the Andes Mountains, the Amazon rainforest, and the Atacama Desert. Peru is known for its rich cultural heritage, including the Inca Empire, which was one of the most powerful and advanced civilizations in the Americas before the arrival of the Spanish conquistadors. Today, Peru is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world with its vibrant cities, ancient ruins, and stunning natural beauty.

Peru's geographic position has played a significant role in shaping its history and culture. The country's strategic location has made it an important center for trade and commerce, with the city of Lima serving as a major hub for the Spanish Empire in the 16th century. The country's diverse landscapes have also supported a wide range of ecosystems, including the Amazon rainforest, which is home to an incredible array of plant and animal species. Peru's unique cultural heritage is reflected in its music, art, and cuisine, which have been influenced by the country's indigenous, Spanish, African, and Asian roots.

Peru's sovereignty status as a **republic** has allowed it to maintain its independence and self-governance since the country declared its independence from Spain in 1821. Today, Peru is a stable democracy, with a growing economy and a strong commitment to preserving its cultural heritage and natural resources. The country's government has implemented a range of initiatives to promote sustainable tourism, protect the environment, and support the development of local communities.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1533 - The Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro arrives in Peru and captures the Inca emperor Atahualpa, marking the beginning of Spanish rule in the country.
2. 1821 - Peru declares its independence from Spain, with José de San Martín serving as the country's first leader.
3. 1866 - Peru becomes a republic, with a new constitution and a president serving as head of state.
4. 1968 - A military coup led by General Juan Velasco Alvarado overthrows the government, leading to a period of military rule that lasts until 1980.
5. 1990 - Peru returns to democratic rule, with the election of President Alberto Fujimori, who serves until 2000.
6. 2001 - The Peruvian government establishes the **Truth and Reconciliation Commission** to investigate human rights abuses committed during the country's internal conflict, which lasted from 1980 to 2000.
7. 2011 - Peru signs a **free trade agreement** with the United States, marking an important step in the country's economic development.

[SECTION] What Makes Peru Unique [/SECTION]
- Peru is home to the **Inca Trail**, a famous hiking trail that leads to the ancient Inca city of Machu Picchu, one of the **Seven Wonders of the World**.
- The country has a rich cultural heritage, with over 60% of the population identifying as **indigenous**, and a wide range of traditional festivals and celebrations throughout the year.
- Peru is home to a diverse range of **wildlife**, including the Andean condor, the jaguar, and the **vicuña**, a wild South American camelid that is considered a national symbol.
- The country has a vibrant **gastronomy**, with popular dishes such as **ceviche**, **lomo saltado**, and **aji de gallina**, which reflect the country's indigenous, Spanish, and African roots.
- Peru is a major producer of **minerals**, including copper, gold, and silver, with the country's mining industry playing an important role in the national economy.
