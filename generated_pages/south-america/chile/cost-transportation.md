[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Chile has a well-developed transportation system, with a variety of options available for getting around, including public buses, metro lines, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent and reliable services in major cities like Santiago. Most people in Chile get around using public transportation, with buses and metro lines being the most popular options. However, owning a car is also common, especially in smaller cities and rural areas.

In terms of transportation costs, Chile is generally more expensive than other countries in South America. However, the cost of transportation can vary depending on the city and the mode of transport. For example, a monthly transit pass in Santiago can cost around $30-$40 USD, while a single fare on a city bus can cost around $1-$2 USD. Taxis are also widely available, with a start rate of around $2-$3 USD.
