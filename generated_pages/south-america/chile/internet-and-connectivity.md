[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Chile has a relatively high level of internet penetration, with over 80% of the population having access to the internet. The internet quality in urban areas such as **Santiago** is generally good, with fast speeds and reliable connections. However, there is a significant urban-rural divide, with rural areas often experiencing slower speeds and less reliable connections. Despite this, the overall internet infrastructure in Chile is well-developed, with a range of internet service providers offering a variety of plans and packages to suit different needs and budgets.

The reliability of the internet in Chile is generally good, with minimal downtime and a strong network infrastructure. This makes it an attractive destination for **digital nomads** and remote workers, who can stay connected and productive while enjoying the country's unique culture and natural beauty. However, as with any country, there may be occasional outages or disruptions, particularly in rural areas. Overall, Chile's internet infrastructure is well-suited to support the needs of tourists, businesses, and residents alike.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Chile is relatively straightforward, with a range of options available for tourists and travelers. The main carriers, **Entel**, **Movistar**, and **Claro**, all offer SIM cards and prepaid plans that can be purchased at airports, stores, and other locations throughout the country. To buy a SIM card, you will typically need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, although some carriers may have additional requirements. It's a good idea to research the different options and plans available before making a purchase, as prices and data allowances can vary significantly.
