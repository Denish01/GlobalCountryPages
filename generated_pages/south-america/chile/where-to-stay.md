# Where to Stay in Chile — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Chile, consider the country's diverse geography and culture, and choose from a range of neighborhoods that cater to different types of travelers, from backpackers to luxury seekers, with popular areas including Santiago's **Lastarria** and **Bellavista**, as well as **Valparaíso** and **Pucon**.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**Lastarria** (Santiago)
Best For: luxury / couples
Vibe: This trendy neighborhood is known for its upscale boutiques, art galleries, and fine dining restaurants, offering a sophisticated and elegant atmosphere. Lastarria is also home to several historic landmarks and cultural attractions, making it a great base for exploring the city.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: close proximity to downtown Santiago, vibrant nightlife, high-end amenities
Cons: can be quite expensive, limited budget options

**Bellavista** (Santiago)
Best For: backpacker / nightlife
Vibe: This lively neighborhood is famous for its bohemian vibe, street art, and lively bars and clubs, making it a great spot for young travelers and partygoers. Bellavista is also home to the famous **Patio Bellavista**, a popular spot for dining and entertainment.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: affordable accommodations, vibrant nightlife, close to city center
Cons: can be noisy, limited parking options

**Valparaíso** (Valparaíso)
Best For: family / cultural
Vibe: This historic port city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its colorful hills, stunning views, and rich cultural heritage. Valparaíso is a great base for exploring the Pacific coast and experiencing Chile's unique culture.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: unique cultural experiences, stunning views, family-friendly atmosphere
Cons: steep hills can be challenging, limited high-end amenities

**Pucon** (Pucon)
Best For: adventure / nature
Vibe: This picturesque town is situated in the heart of the **Lake District**, surrounded by stunning natural beauty, including lakes, volcanoes, and national parks. Pucon is a great base for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, skiing, and water sports.
Price Range: $40-150/night
Pros: stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, relaxed atmosphere
Cons: limited cultural attractions, can be crowded during peak season

**Vitacura** (Santiago)
Best For: business / luxury
Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is known for its modern amenities, high-end shopping centers, and vibrant business district, making it a great spot for business travelers and luxury seekers. Vitacura is also home to several top-rated restaurants and bars.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: modern amenities, convenient location, high-end services
Cons: can be quite expensive, limited budget options

**Recoleta** (Santiago)
Best For: family / mid-range
Vibe: This charming neighborhood is known for its historic architecture, lively markets, and family-friendly atmosphere, making it a great spot for families and mid-range travelers. Recoleta is also home to several top-rated restaurants and cafes.
Price Range: $30-100/night
Pros: affordable accommodations, family-friendly atmosphere, historic charm
Cons: limited high-end amenities, can be noisy
