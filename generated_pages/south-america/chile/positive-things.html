People & Global Impact

  • Salvador Allende, the former President of Chile, was a key figure in the country's socialist movement and a symbol of resistance against authoritarianism.
  • Isabel Allende, the renowned author, is a member of the Chilean diaspora and has written extensively about the country's history and culture.
  • The Chilean-American community has made significant contributions to the United States, with notable figures like Ricardo Lagos, the former President of Chile, who has taught at several US universities.
  • Michelle Bachelet, the former President of Chile, has been a leading figure in international women's rights and has served as the Executive Director of UN Women.

What Visitors Love

  • The warm hospitality of the Chilean people, who are known for their welcoming nature and friendly attitude towards visitors.
  • The delicious cuisine, which features a mix of indigenous, Spanish, and Italian influences, with popular dishes like pastel de choclo and chupe de mariscos.
  • The stunning natural scenery, with a unique combination of Andean mountains, Pacific coastline, and Atacama Desert landscapes.
  • The world-class wine, with several regions like Maipo Valley and Colchagua Valley producing high-quality wines.
  • The vibrant cities, like Santiago and Valparaiso, which offer a mix of historic architecture, cultural attractions, and modern entertainment options.
  • The outdoor activities, like skiing, hiking, and surfing, which are popular in Chile's diverse landscapes.
