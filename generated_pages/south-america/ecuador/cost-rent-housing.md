# Rent & Housing Prices in Ecuador

The cost of rent in Ecuador can vary greatly depending on the location, with average monthly rents for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center ranging from $200 to $500 USD.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
Ecuador's rental market is relatively **affordable** compared to other countries in South America, with a wide range of options available for renters. The typical lease term in Ecuador is one year, with a deposit requirement of one to two months' rent. Tenant rights are protected by law, and renters can expect to have access to basic amenities such as water, electricity, and internet. Compared to its regional neighbors, Colombia and Peru, Ecuador's housing costs are relatively low, making it an attractive option for expats and tourists.

The rental market in Ecuador is also influenced by the country's **tourist season**, with prices tend to be higher during peak season (June to September and December to January). Additionally, the cost of living in Ecuador is relatively low, with a **low inflation rate** of 1.6% in 2024, making it an attractive option for those looking to retire or live abroad. However, it's worth noting that the **out-of-pocket health spend** is relatively high, at 30.9% of total health expenditure, so renters should consider this when planning their expenses.
