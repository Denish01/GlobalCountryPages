[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Ecuador has a well-developed transportation system, with a variety of options available for getting around, including buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent and reliable services in major cities like Quito and Guayaquil. Most people in Ecuador get around using buses and taxis, which are affordable and widely available. However, traffic congestion can be a problem in larger cities, especially during rush hour.

In addition to public transportation, many Ecuadorians also use **private vehicles**, which are becoming increasingly popular as the economy grows. However, this has also contributed to traffic congestion and air pollution in urban areas. The government is working to improve the transportation infrastructure, including the development of new bus rapid transit systems and the expansion of bike lanes and pedestrian walkways.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using public buses or **carpooling** with friends or colleagues.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Cabify to get around, as they are often cheaper than taxis.
- Always **negotiate the fare** before getting into a taxi, especially if you're not using a metered taxi.
- Be aware of **traffic congestion** and plan your route accordingly to avoid getting stuck in traffic.
- Consider **renting a car** for long-distance trips or if you plan to explore rural areas.
- Use **transportation apps** like Moovit or Transit to plan your route and get real-time updates on public transportation.
