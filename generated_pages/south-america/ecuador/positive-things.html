ScienceGalapagos ConservationEcuador has made significant efforts to protect the unique ecosystem of the Galapagos Islands, a UNESCO World Heritage site, through conservation programs and sustainable tourism practices.

Cultural Treasures

  • The City of Quito is a UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its well-preserved colonial architecture and historic center.
  • The Galapagos Islands are home to an incredible array of unique and endemic species, and have been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Toquilla straw hat weaving is a traditional craft that has been recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.
  • The Fiestas de Quito are a celebration of the city's founding, featuring traditional music, dance, and food.
  • The Museo Nacional del Ecuador is a museum that showcases the country's history, art, and culture, with a collection of over 20,000 artifacts.

People & Global Impact

  • Nobel Prize winner Maria Fernanda Espinosa, an Ecuadorian diplomat and politician, was the first woman to serve as President of the United Nations General Assembly.
  • The Ecuadorian diaspora has made significant contributions to the economies and cultures of countries such as the United States, Spain, and Italy.
  • Environmental activist Nina Gualinga has worked to protect the rights of indigenous communities and the environment, particularly in the Amazon region.
  • The values of hospitality and community are highly valued in Ecuadorian culture, with many families and communities coming together to celebrate traditional holidays and events.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Ecuadorian people is often cited as one of the country's greatest strengths, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome they receive.
  • The traditional cuisine of Ecuador is a fusion of indigenous, Spanish, and African influences, with popular dishes such as locro de papas and seco de pollo.
  • The scenic beauty of Ecuador is unparalleled, with the Andes mountains, the Amazon rainforest, and the Galapagos Islands all located within the country.
  • The affordability of Ecuador makes it an attractive destination for travelers, with a low cost of living and a wide range of accommodation options.
  • The rich cultural heritage of Ecuador is evident in its many museums, historical sites, and cultural festivals, which showcase the country's history, art, and traditions.
  • The outdoor activities available in Ecuador, such as hiking, surfing, and wildlife watching, make it a paradise for adventure seekers.
