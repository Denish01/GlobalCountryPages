[SECTION] Overview: Bolivia vs Peru [/SECTION]
Bolivia and Peru are two neighboring countries in South America that offer distinct cultural and economic experiences. Bolivia is a relatively low-cost country with a rich cultural heritage, while Peru is a more developed economy with a higher cost of living. The main difference in cost between the two countries lies in the prices of accommodations, food, and transportation. Bolivia's lower cost of living makes it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle. On the other hand, Peru's stronger economy and more developed infrastructure make it a popular destination for tourists and business travelers.

In terms of economic context, Bolivia has a smaller economy than Peru, with a GDP per capita of around $7,500 compared to Peru's $13,400. However, Bolivia's economy has been growing steadily in recent years, driven by its natural resources and tourism industries. Peru, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy with a strong focus on services, industry, and agriculture. Despite these differences, both countries offer a unique and rewarding experience for visitors and residents alike. Overall, Bolivia's lower cost of living and rich cultural heritage make it a great option for those looking for an affordable and immersive experience in South America.
