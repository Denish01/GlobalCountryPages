[SECTION] What Is Bolivia? [/SECTION]
Bolivia is a **sovereign** country located in the western part of South America. It is bordered by Brazil, Paraguay, Argentina, Chile, and Peru, and has a diverse geography that includes mountains, deserts, and tropical rainforests. Bolivia is known for its rich cultural heritage, with a mix of indigenous, Spanish, and African influences. The country has a total area of approximately 1,098,581 square kilometers and a population of around 12.1 million people.

Bolivia is a **plurinational** state, meaning that it recognizes the existence of multiple nations within its territory. The country has 36 official languages, including Spanish, Quechua, and Aymara, which are the most widely spoken. The capital city, Sucre, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture. Bolivia is also home to the world's largest salt flat, the Salar de Uyuni, which is a popular tourist destination.

The country has a diverse economy, with a mix of agriculture, mining, and manufacturing. Bolivia is rich in natural resources, including natural gas, oil, and minerals such as tin, silver, and gold. The country is also home to a number of important rivers, including the Amazon River and the Rio Grande.
