# Venezuela Visa & Entry Requirements

To enter Venezuela, most foreign nationals require a visa, which can be obtained through various types and requirements depending on their nationality and purpose of visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Venezuela before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Venezuela has a moderate visa policy, with around 70 countries eligible for visa-free entry, while others require a visa on arrival or in advance. The country's visa stance can be complex, with various requirements and restrictions in place. Citizens of neighboring countries, such as Colombia and Brazil, can enter visa-free for a limited period, while nationals from further afield may need to apply for a visa at a Venezuelan embassy or consulate.

The visa policy is subject to change, and it's essential to check the latest requirements before traveling to Venezuela. The country has been experiencing economic and social challenges, which may impact the visa application process and travel requirements. Visitors should be prepared for potential delays and changes in the visa process.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can stay in Venezuela for up to 90 days, and may need to provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds. Extensions are possible, but must be applied for at the immigration office in Caracas. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers require a visa and an invitation letter from a Venezuelan company, as well as proof of their business activities. They may also need to register with the Venezuelan tax authority. **Students:** Students must provide proof of enrollment at a Venezuelan university and may need to apply for a student visa. The duration of the visa will depend on the length of their studies. **Workers:** Workers require a visa and a sponsorship from a Venezuelan employer, as well as proof of their qualifications and experience. The process can be complex and may take several months.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Venezuela are Simón Bolívar International Airport in Caracas and the border crossings with Colombia and Brazil. Upon arrival, visitors will need to go through immigration and customs, where they will be required to present their passport, visa (if required), and other documents. The process can be slow, and visitors should be prepared to wait in line. It's also essential to ensure that all documents are in order and that visitors have the required vaccinations and health insurance.

The immigration process can be complex, and visitors may be asked to provide additional information or documentation. It's recommended to have all documents translated into Spanish and to have a copy of the visa application and supporting documents. Visitors should also be aware of the local customs and regulations, and to respect the country's laws and traditions.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not verifying the current visa requirements before traveling
- Not having all necessary documents, including proof of onward travel and sufficient funds
- Not registering with the Venezuelan authorities, if required
- Not having the required vaccinations and health insurance
- Not respecting local customs and regulations
- Not having a copy of the visa application and supporting documents, in case of loss or theft

It's essential to be well-prepared and informed before traveling to Venezuela, to avoid any potential issues or delays. Visitors should research the current requirements and regulations, and plan accordingly. With the right preparation and knowledge, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Venezuela.
