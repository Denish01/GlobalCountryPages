[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Canaima National Park**, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a unique and breathtaking natural wonder, featuring **Angel Falls**, the highest uninterrupted waterfall in the world.
- The **Orinoco Delta**, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, is a diverse and fascinating region, home to a wide variety of **wildlife** and **indigenous communities**.
- The **Venezuelan Carnival**, a vibrant and colorful celebration, is an important part of the country's cultural heritage, featuring **traditional music** and **dancing**.
- The **Museo de Bellas Artes** in Caracas, a renowned art museum, showcases an impressive collection of **Venezuelan art**, including works by **Armando Reverón** and **Héctor Poleo**.
- The **Panteón Nacional**, a historic monument, is the final resting place of **Simón Bolívar**, the country's national hero and a key figure in **Latin American history**.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Simón Bolívar**, the country's national hero, played a crucial role in the **Latin American independence movement**, leading the fight against Spanish colonial rule and shaping the region's history.
- **Andrés Bello**, a Venezuelan poet and philosopher, made significant contributions to **Latin American literature** and **education**, and is considered one of the most important figures in the country's intellectual history.
- The **Venezuelan diaspora** has had a profound impact on the global community, with many Venezuelans making significant contributions to **science**, **art**, and **politics** in their adopted countries.
- **Jacobo Borges**, a renowned Venezuelan artist, has gained international recognition for his **sculptures** and **installations**, which explore themes of **identity** and **social justice**.
