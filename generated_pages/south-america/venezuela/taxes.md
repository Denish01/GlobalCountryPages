# Venezuela Tax Guide — Income, Corporate & VAT Rates

The tax rates in Venezuela range from 6% to 34% for individual income tax, with a corporate tax rate of 34% and a value-added tax (VAT) rate of 16%. 

[CALLOUT] Tax laws change frequently. Always consult a qualified tax professional for advice specific to your situation. This is a general guide only. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
Venezuela has a **territorial tax system**, where individuals and companies are taxed on their Venezuelan-source income, regardless of their tax residency status. However, **worldwide taxation** applies to Venezuelan tax residents, who are taxed on their global income, with credits available for taxes paid abroad. To be considered a tax resident in Venezuela, an individual must have a permanent home in the country, or spend more than 183 days in the country within a calendar year. Venezuelan tax residents are required to file a tax return and report their worldwide income.

The Venezuelan tax system is complex, with various taxes and fees applicable to individuals and companies. The tax authorities, known as the **Servicio Nacional Integrado de Administración Aduanera y Tributaria (SENIAT)**, are responsible for administering and collecting taxes in Venezuela. Taxpayers can access various tax services and information through the SENIAT website, including tax return filing, payment of taxes, and tax clearance certificates.

Venezuelan tax residents are required to file a tax return and report their income from all sources, including employment, self-employment, and investments. Taxpayers are eligible for various **deductions and allowances**, including a personal exemption, deductions for mortgage interest and charitable donations, and allowances for dependents. Tax returns must be filed by April 30th of each year, and tax payments can be made online or at authorized banks.

[SECTION] Corporate & Business Tax [/SECTION]
- The **corporate tax rate** in Venezuela is 34%, applicable to companies' taxable income.
- Small businesses with annual turnover of less than VES 50 million may be eligible for **small business incentives**, including reduced tax rates and simplified tax compliance.
- Venezuela has several **free zones**, including the Puerto Cabello Free Zone and the Sucre Free Zone, which offer tax exemptions and other incentives to companies operating within these zones.
- Companies must **register with the SENIAT** and obtain a tax identification number (RIF) to operate in Venezuela.
- Companies are required to file **annual tax returns** and pay taxes on their taxable income.

[SECTION] VAT / Sales Tax [/SECTION]
- The **standard VAT rate** in Venezuela is 16%, applicable to most goods and services.
- **Reduced VAT rates** of 8% and 5% apply to certain goods and services, including basic food items and pharmaceuticals.
- **Exemptions** from VAT are available for certain goods and services, including exports, financial services, and educational services.
- Venezuela has a **tourist refund scheme**, which allows foreign tourists to claim a refund of VAT paid on certain goods and services.

[SECTION] For Expats & Foreign Workers [/SECTION]
- **Tax residency rules** apply to expats and foreign workers who spend more than 183 days in Venezuela within a calendar year.
- Venezuela has **double taxation treaties** with 23 countries, which can help reduce or eliminate double taxation on income earned by expats and foreign workers.
- Expats and foreign workers may be required to **register with the SENIAT** and obtain a tax identification number (RIF) to work in Venezuela.
- Expats and foreign workers may be eligible for **social security benefits**, including pension and healthcare benefits, if they have made social security contributions in Venezuela.
- **Remittance rules** apply to expats and foreign workers who remit income from Venezuela to their home country.
- Expats and foreign workers may be required to **file a tax return** in their home country, as well as in Venezuela, if they are considered tax residents in both countries.

[SECTION] Crypto & Investment Income [/SECTION]
- **Investment income**, including dividends, interest, and capital gains, is subject to income tax in Venezuela.
- **Cryptocurrency** is considered a taxable asset in Venezuela, and gains from the sale of cryptocurrency are subject to capital gains tax.
- **Dividends** received by Venezuelan tax residents are subject to a **withholding tax** of 34%, which can be credited against the taxpayer's income tax liability.
- **Foreign investment income** earned by Venezuelan tax residents may be subject to **foreign tax credits**, which can help reduce or eliminate double taxation on foreign-sourced income.
