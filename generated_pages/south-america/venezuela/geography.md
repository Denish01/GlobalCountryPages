# Venezuela Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

Venezuela is a sovereign country located in the northern part of South America, bordered by Colombia to the west, Brazil to the south, Guyana to the east, and the Caribbean Sea to the north.

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Venezuela is situated in the northern part of South America, with the Caribbean Sea to the north, the Atlantic Ocean to the northeast, and the Pacific Ocean not bordering the country but having a coastline along the Caribbean Sea. The country shares borders with Colombia to the west, Brazil to the south, and Guyana to the east, with a total border length of approximately 4,993 kilometers. The country's unique geography is characterized by a mix of coastal plains, mountains, and highlands, with the Andes Mountains running along the northwest border with Colombia.

The country's location in the tropics, near the equator, and its geography, with a mix of coastlines, mountains, and valleys, create a diverse range of ecosystems and climates. The coastline along the Caribbean Sea provides a long stretch of beaches, bays, and islands, while the interior of the country is dominated by the **Orinoco River** basin, which covers nearly 70% of the country. The Orinoco River is the longest river in Venezuela and one of the longest in South America, stretching over 2,140 kilometers from its source in the Andes to its mouth in the Atlantic Ocean.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Venezuela's terrain is characterized by a mix of mountains, valleys, and coastal plains. The **Andes Mountains** run along the northwest border with Colombia, with the highest point being Pico Bolivar, which reaches an elevation of 4,978 meters. The **Cordillera de la Costa** mountain range runs along the Caribbean coast, with peaks reaching elevations of over 2,000 meters. The interior of the country is dominated by the Orinoco River basin, which is a vast, fertile valley surrounded by mountains and hills.

The country's coastline along the Caribbean Sea is characterized by a mix of beaches, bays, and islands, with the **Margarita Island** being a popular tourist destination. The **Orinoco Delta** is a unique and diverse ecosystem, with a mix of freshwater and saltwater rivers, canals, and wetlands. The delta is home to a wide range of wildlife, including **caimans**, **dolphins**, and over 300 species of birds. The country's geography also includes several deserts, including the **Coro Desert**, which is a small desert located in the northwest part of the country.

The country's landscape is also characterized by several **table-top mountains**, known as **tepuis**, which are unique, flat-topped mountains that are found only in this part of South America. The most famous tepui is **Angel Falls**, which is the highest uninterrupted waterfall in the world, with a height of 979 meters. The country's diverse geography and climate support a wide range of ecosystems, including tropical rainforests, cloud forests, and grasslands.
