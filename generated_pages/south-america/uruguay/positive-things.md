[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Colonia del Sacramento** historic town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination, known for its well-preserved colonial architecture.
- The **Tango** music and dance style is an important part of Uruguayan culture, with many tango clubs and festivals throughout the country.
- The **Candombe** music and dance style is a unique and important part of Uruguayan culture, with several Candombe clubs and festivals in Montevideo.
- The **Gaucho** tradition is an important part of Uruguayan culture, with many gauchos (cowboys) still living and working on ranches throughout the country.
- The **Feria de Tristán Narvaja** is a popular market in Montevideo, where visitors can find a wide range of traditional Uruguayan goods, including food, crafts, and antiques.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **José Enrique Rodó** was a Uruguayan writer and philosopher who had a significant impact on Latin American literature and thought.
- **Pedro Figari** was a Uruguayan painter and writer who is considered one of the most important Uruguayan artists of the 20th century.
- The **Uruguayan diaspora** has made significant contributions to the culture and economy of several countries, including the United States, Argentina, and Spain.
- **Tabaré Vázquez** was a Uruguayan politician who served as President of Uruguay from 2005 to 2010 and again from 2015 to 2020, and was known for his progressive social policies.
