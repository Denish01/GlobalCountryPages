[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Uruguay is generally of high quality, with fast download speeds and reliable connections. In urban areas such as Montevideo, the capital city, you can expect to find excellent internet coverage, with many cafes, restaurants, and public spaces offering free WiFi. However, in rural areas, the internet quality can be more variable, with slower speeds and less reliable connections. Despite this, the overall quality of the internet in Uruguay is good, making it easy to stay connected and get work done.

In terms of reliability, the internet in Uruguay is generally stable, with few outages or disruptions. This makes it a good option for remote workers and digital nomads, who need to be able to stay connected and productive. Additionally, the widespread availability of WiFi and mobile networks means that you can easily get online, whether you're in a urban or rural area. However, it's worth noting that the internet quality can be affected by weather conditions, such as heavy rain or thunderstorms, which can cause outages and disruptions.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Uruguay, you can visit one of the main carriers, such as Movistar, Claro, or Antel, and purchase a tourist SIM card. You will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and the cost will be around 20 USD. The SIM card will come with a set amount of data, and you can top up your account as needed. It's also possible to purchase a prepaid SIM card from a convenience store or kiosk, but the options may be more limited.

It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds. Additionally, many carriers offer tourist-friendly plans, which include a set amount of data and minutes, and can be a good option for short-term visitors. It's also worth noting that some carriers offer eSIM options, which can be a convenient alternative to traditional SIM cards.
