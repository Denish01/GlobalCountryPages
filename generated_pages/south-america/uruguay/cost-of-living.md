# Cost of Living in Uruguay - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Uruguay is generally considered to be moderate, with daily budgets ranging from $30-60 USD for backpackers to $100-200 USD for mid-range travelers.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $30-60 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $100-200 USD
Currency: UYU
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 10% in restaurants and bars is common
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Uruguay? [/SECTION]
Uruguay is often considered one of the more expensive countries in South America, particularly when compared to its neighbors Argentina and Brazil. However, it still offers a relatively affordable cost of living compared to many countries in North America and Europe. The country's moderate cost of living is due in part to its stable economy and high standard of living. Overall, Uruguay is a great destination for travelers who want to experience a unique blend of European and South American culture without breaking the bank.

Uruguay's cost of living is also influenced by its location, with the capital city Montevideo being one of the most expensive cities in the country. However, outside of Montevideo, prices tend to be lower, making it possible to find affordable accommodation and food options. Additionally, Uruguay has a relatively low cost of living compared to other countries in the region, making it an attractive destination for expats and digital nomads.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls to try traditional Uruguayan cuisine at a lower cost.
- Use public transportation instead of taxis to get around the city.
- Buy groceries at local markets or supermarkets instead of eating out for every meal.
- Take advantage of free walking tours and cultural events to explore the city without spending money.
- Learn some basic Spanish phrases to navigate the country and interact with locals, which can also help you find better deals and discounts.
