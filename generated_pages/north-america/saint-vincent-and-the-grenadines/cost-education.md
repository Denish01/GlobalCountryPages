# Education Costs in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines

The cost of education in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge tuition fees ranging from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being generally considered to be of good quality. The language of instruction is **English**, and compulsory education lasts for 12 years, from primary to secondary school. Public schools are free or subsidized, making education accessible to most of the population. On the other hand, private schools and international institutions offer a more exclusive education experience, often with smaller class sizes and more resources, but at a higher cost.

The public education system in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines is overseen by the Ministry of Education, and it provides a comprehensive curriculum that includes subjects such as mathematics, science, language arts, and social studies. International schools are also available, offering curricula such as the **International Baccalaureate** or **Cambridge International Examinations**, catering to expatriate families and those seeking a more global education experience. These schools often have higher tuition fees, but provide a more diverse and international learning environment.
