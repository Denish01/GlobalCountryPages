Cultural Treasures

  • The Petroglyphs at the Layou River are a testament to the island's rich indigenous history and cultural heritage.
  • The Kingstown Market is a vibrant hub of activity, showcasing the country's unique blend of African, European, and Caribbean traditions.
  • The Saint Vincent and the Grenadines Carnival is a colorful and energetic celebration of music, dance, and culture.
  • The Owia Salt Pond is a unique natural wonder, featuring a saltwater pond surrounded by stunning rock formations and scenic views.
  • The Fort Charlotte is a historic landmark that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and insight into the country's colonial past.

People & Global Impact

  • Sir James Mitchell, a former Prime Minister of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, played a key role in shaping the country's development and foreign policy.
  • The Vincentian diaspora has made significant contributions to the global community, with notable figures such as Jezelle Gray, a British-Vincentian entrepreneur and business leader.
