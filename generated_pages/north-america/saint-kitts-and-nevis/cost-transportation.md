[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
The main transportation modes in Saint Kitts and Nevis include buses, taxis, and rental cars. The quality of public transit is generally good, with an extensive network of buses covering most areas of the islands. However, the frequency and reliability of bus services can vary depending on the route and time of day. Most people get around the islands using buses or taxis, with many locals also owning their own vehicles.

The roads in Saint Kitts and Nevis are generally in good condition, with the main roads being well-maintained and paved. However, some of the secondary roads can be narrow and winding, making them more challenging to navigate. Despite this, the scenic views and picturesque landscapes make driving around the islands a pleasant experience. Additionally, the government has invested in improving the public transportation system, with new buses and infrastructure being introduced to enhance the overall travel experience.
