[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Saint Kitts and Nevis is structured into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 5 and 12, and is provided free of charge by the government. Secondary education is also compulsory, and students typically attend school between the ages of 12 and 17. The quality of education in Saint Kitts and Nevis is generally high, with a strong focus on **academic achievement** and **vocational training**. The system is a mix of public and private schools, with the majority of students attending public schools. The private schools, on the other hand, are often preferred by expatriate families and offer a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **British GCSE**.

The government of Saint Kitts and Nevis has made significant investments in education in recent years, with a focus on improving the quality of teaching and learning, as well as increasing access to education for all citizens. This has led to a significant improvement in **student outcomes**, with more students achieving good grades and going on to pursue higher education. The education system is overseen by the Ministry of Education, which is responsible for setting policy, managing schools, and providing support to teachers and students.

In terms of the structure of the education system, students typically progress from primary school to secondary school, and then on to tertiary education if they choose to do so. Tertiary education is provided by a range of institutions, including the **University of the West Indies** and the **Ross University School of Veterinary Medicine**. These institutions offer a range of programs, including **medicine**, **law**, and **business**, and are well-regarded internationally.

- To study in Saint Kitts and Nevis, international students must obtain a **student visa**, which can be applied for through the Immigration Department.

- A range of **scholarships** are available to international students, including the Saint Kitts and Nevis Government Scholarship and the University of the West Indies Scholarship.
