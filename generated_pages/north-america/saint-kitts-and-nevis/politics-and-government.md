# Saint Kitts and Nevis Politics & Government — System, Leaders & Stability

Saint Kitts and Nevis is governed as a federal parliamentary constitutional monarchy, with a unique blend of British and Caribbean influences shaping its political system.

[FACTBOX]
Government Type: Federal Parliamentary Constitutional Monarchy
Head of State: King Charles III, represented by the Governor-General
Head of Government: Prime Minister, currently Terrance Drew
Legislature: National Assembly, consisting of 14 elected members and 3 appointed senators
Legal System: Common law, based on English common law
Political Stability Index: Ranked 33rd out of 178 countries in the 2020 Global Peace Index
Corruption Index: Ranked 24th out of 180 countries in the 2021 Corruption Perceptions Index by Transparency International
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Political System [/SECTION]
The government of Saint Kitts and Nevis is characterized by a **separation of powers** between the executive, legislative, and judicial branches. The **federal** system is unitary in nature, with a strong central government and minimal autonomy for the two islands. The **parliamentary** system is based on the British model, with a prime minister as the head of government and a governor-general representing the monarch. The National Assembly is the supreme legislative body, responsible for passing laws and overseeing the government.

The political system of Saint Kitts and Nevis is designed to promote **stability** and **accountability**, with a strong emphasis on democratic principles and the rule of law. The country has a long tradition of peaceful transfers of power and a robust system of checks and balances. However, like many small island nations, Saint Kitts and Nevis faces challenges related to **sustainability** and **resilience**, including climate change, economic vulnerability, and regional security concerns.

The government of Saint Kitts and Nevis is committed to **good governance** and **transparency**, with a range of initiatives aimed at promoting accountability, combating corruption, and enhancing citizen participation. These efforts include the establishment of an **Integrity Commission**, a **Freedom of Information Act**, and a **National Anti-Corruption Strategy**. Despite these efforts, the country still faces challenges related to **bureaucratic inefficiency** and **political polarization**.

[SECTION] Elections [/SECTION]
- The electoral system in Saint Kitts and Nevis is based on **universal adult suffrage**, with all citizens aged 18 and above eligible to vote.
- Elections are held every **five years**, with the most recent election taking place in 2022.
- The **last election** saw the Saint Kitts and Nevis Labour Party winning a majority of seats, with Terrance Drew becoming the new prime minister.
- The **next election** is scheduled for 2027, with voter participation expected to be high due to the country's strong democratic traditions.
