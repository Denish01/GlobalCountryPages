[SECTION] What Is Saint Kitts and Nevis? [/SECTION]
Saint Kitts and Nevis is a **sovereign** island nation located in the eastern Caribbean Sea, consisting of two main islands: Saint Kitts and Nevis. The country is situated approximately 1,300 miles southeast of Miami, Florida, and is part of the **Leeward Islands** chain. With a total area of approximately 104 square miles, Saint Kitts and Nevis is one of the smallest countries in the world, both in terms of population and land area. The country is known for its stunning natural beauty, including its beautiful beaches, lush rainforests, and majestic volcanic peaks.

The country has a rich history, having been colonized by European powers, including the British and the French, and has a unique cultural heritage that reflects its African, European, and indigenous influences. The capital city, **Basseterre**, is located on the island of Saint Kitts and is the commercial and administrative center of the country. The official language is **English**, and the country uses the **East Caribbean Dollar** as its official currency. Saint Kitts and Nevis is a member of the **Commonwealth of Nations** and the **United Nations**, and has a **federal parliamentary constitutional monarchy** system of government.

The country's economy is primarily driven by **tourism**, with visitors drawn to its beautiful beaches, historic sites, and vibrant culture. The country is also known for its **sugar production**, although the industry has declined in recent years. Saint Kitts and Nevis has a relatively high standard of living, with a strong emphasis on education and healthcare. The country has a **tropical climate**, with average temperatures ranging from 70 to 85 degrees Fahrenheit throughout the year.
