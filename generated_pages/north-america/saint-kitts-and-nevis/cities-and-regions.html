How Saint Kitts and Nevis Is Divided

Saint Kitts and Nevis is a sovereign nation located in the Caribbean, consisting of two main islands. The country is divided into 14 parishes, with Saint Kitts having 9 parishes and Nevis having 5 parishes. The parishes serve as the primary administrative divisions, with each parish having its own local government and responsibilities. The capital city, Basseterre, is located on the island of Saint Kitts and serves as the center of government, commerce, and culture.

The division of the country into parishes allows for a more localized approach to governance and development, with each parish having its own unique character and needs. The parishes are responsible for providing various services such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure, which are funded by the central government. The system of parishes has been in place since the colonial era and has been maintained to this day, with some adjustments made over the years to reflect changing needs and circumstances.

Regional Differences

  • The island of Saint Kitts is more mountainous and scenic, with a focus on outdoor activities such as hiking and birdwatching.
  • The island of Nevis is more volcanic and scenic, with a focus on historic sites, beaches, and vibrant culture.
  • The parishes on Saint Kitts tend to be more urban and densely populated, while those on Nevis are more rural and laid-back.
  • The northern parishes of Saint Kitts are known for their scenic countryside and historic sites, while the southern parishes are more urban and commercial.

Getting Around

Getting around Saint Kitts and Nevis is relatively easy, with a range of transportation options available. The country has a well-developed road network, with buses and taxis providing regular services between towns and villages. There are also several car rental companies, allowing visitors to explore the islands at their own pace. For those looking to travel between the two islands, there are regular ferry services, as well as domestic flights from the Robert L. Bradshaw International Airport on Saint Kitts to the Vance W. Amory International Airport on Nevis.

The road conditions on Saint Kitts and Nevis are generally good, with most roads being paved and well-maintained. However, some of the rural roads can be narrow and winding, and may require a 4x4 vehicle to navigate. The public transportation system is reliable and affordable, with buses and taxis providing regular services throughout the day. Visitors can also hire a car or scooter to explore the islands, which provides more flexibility and freedom to create their own itinerary. Overall, getting around Saint Kitts and Nevis is relatively easy and convenient, with a range of options available to suit different needs and preferences.
