Visa Policy Overview

Saint Kitts and Nevis has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others require a visa on arrival or in advance. The country offers visa-free access to citizens of over 80 countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, Canada, and most European Union nations. This makes it a popular destination for tourists and business travelers alike.

The visa policy of Saint Kitts and Nevis is designed to promote tourism and economic development, while also ensuring the security and integrity of the country's borders. The government has implemented various measures to facilitate the entry of legitimate travelers, including the introduction of an electronic visa system and a visa-on-arrival program for certain nationalities.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Citizens of eligible countries can stay in Saint Kitts and Nevis for up to 90 days without a visa. Tourists must provide proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and health insurance. Extensions of stay are possible, but must be applied for at the Ministry of National Security.

Business Travelers: Business travelers may require a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must provide a letter of invitation from a local company, proof of business activities, and other relevant documents. Business travelers may be granted a stay of up to 30 days, with the possibility of extension.

Students: Students must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment at a recognized educational institution, sufficient funds, and health insurance. The duration of stay is typically limited to the length of the academic program.

Workers: Workers must obtain a work permit, which requires sponsorship by a local employer. The application process involves submitting various documents, including a job offer, proof of qualifications, and a medical certificate.

Documents Checklist

  1. Valid passport with at least six months' validity
  2. Completed visa application form (if required)
  3. Recent passport-sized photographs
  4. Proof of onward travel (e.g. return ticket or travel itinerary)
  5. Proof of sufficient funds (e.g. bank statement or cash)
  6. Health insurance certificate
  7. Hotel reservation or confirmation of accommodation
  8. Letter of invitation (for business travelers or students)
  9. Proof of enrollment (for students)
  10. Medical certificate (for workers)

Entry Points & Procedures

The main entry points to Saint Kitts and Nevis are the Robert L. Bradshaw International Airport (SKB) and the Vance W. Amory International Airport (NEV). Upon arrival, travelers must present their travel documents and complete an immigration form. The immigration process typically involves a brief interview with an immigration officer, who will ask questions about the purpose of the visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements. Travelers may also be required to provide biometric data, such as fingerprints.

Travelers arriving by sea must clear customs and immigration at the designated ports of entry, including the Port Zante Cruise Terminal in Basseterre or the Charlestown Port in Nevis. The procedures are similar to those at the airports, with the addition of customs declaration forms for goods being imported.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
  • Failing to provide proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
  • Not completing the visa application form correctly (if required)
  • Not having the necessary documents for business or student travel
  • Not declaring goods or currency being imported
  • Not having health insurance that covers the duration of stay
