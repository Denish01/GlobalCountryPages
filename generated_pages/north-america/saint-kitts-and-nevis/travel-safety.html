Overall Safety Assessment

Saint Kitts and Nevis is considered a relatively safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates compared to other Caribbean islands. The country has a strong focus on tourism, and as such, the government has implemented various measures to ensure visitor safety. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to minimize the risk of encountering problems. Petty crimes, such as pickpocketing and theft, can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, but violent crimes are rare. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of natural disasters, such as hurricanes, which can affect the island during the Atlantic hurricane season.

Overall, Saint Kitts and Nevis is a great destination for travelers who want to experience the beauty and charm of the Caribbean without the high crime rates found in some other islands. With its stunning beaches, lush rainforests, and vibrant culture, Saint Kitts and Nevis has something to offer for every type of traveler. By taking basic precautions and being aware of potential risks, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to this beautiful island nation.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas, such as markets and beaches
  • Be aware of scams targeting tourists, such as fake lotteries and investment schemes
  • Traffic accidents can occur, especially when driving on rural roads
  • Hurricanes can affect the island during the Atlantic hurricane season, which runs from June to November
  • Sunburn and heat exhaustion can be a risk, especially for visitors who are not used to the tropical sun
  • Water sports accidents can occur, especially when participating in activities such as snorkeling or diving
  • Petty theft can occur in hotels and resorts, so it's essential to secure valuables and use the hotel safe

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Saint Kitts and Nevis, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
  • Water Safety: Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is still recommended
  • Medical Facilities: The Joseph N. France General Hospital in Basseterre and the Alexandra Hospital in Charlestown provide good medical care, but serious cases may require evacuation to another island or the US
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are available in major towns, but the selection of medications may be limited

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always lock doors and windows, especially when leaving your hotel room or rental property
  • Use a hotel safe or a secure location to store valuables
  • Be cautious when using ATMs and credit card machines, and always cover the keypad when entering your PIN
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night
  • Keep your hotel room door locked, even when you're in the room
  • Use reputable taxi services or rental cars, and always wear a seatbelt

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Saint Kitts and Nevis is generally a safe destination for solo and women travelers, but it's still essential to take basic precautions
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night
  • Use reputable taxi services or rental cars, and always sit in the back seat
  • Keep your hotel room number and other personal information private
  • Be cautious when interacting with strangers, and avoid accepting rides or invitations from people you don't know
