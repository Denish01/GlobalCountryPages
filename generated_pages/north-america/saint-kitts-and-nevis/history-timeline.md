The British ultimately established the first permanent settlement on the island in 1624, and it became a key location for the production of **sugar**, a crop that would dominate the island's economy for centuries. The neighboring island of Nevis was also colonized by the British, and the two islands were administered together as a single entity.

The colonial period was marked by significant conflict, including wars with the French and **slave uprisings**. The British abolished slavery in 1834, but the island's economy continued to be driven by the production of sugar, with former slaves and their descendants providing the labor. As the sugar industry declined in the 20th century, the island's economy began to diversify, with a growing focus on **tourism** and other sectors. Saint Kitts and Nevis gained independence from Britain in 1983, with **Kennedy Simmonds** as the country's first prime minister.
