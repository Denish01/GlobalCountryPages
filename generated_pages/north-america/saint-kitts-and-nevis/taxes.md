[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
The tax system in Saint Kitts and Nevis is based on a **territorial taxation** system, where individuals and companies are taxed only on their income derived from sources within the country. This means that foreign-sourced income is not subject to taxation in Saint Kitts and Nevis, unless it is remitted to the country. To be considered a tax resident in Saint Kitts and Nevis, an individual must be physically present in the country for at least 183 days in a calendar year. Individuals who are tax residents are subject to taxation on their worldwide income, but only to the extent that it is derived from sources within Saint Kitts and Nevis.

The tax system in Saint Kitts and Nevis is designed to encourage foreign investment and economic growth. The country has a relatively low **tax burden**, with a range of incentives and exemptions available to individuals and companies. The tax authority in Saint Kitts and Nevis is the Inland Revenue Department, which is responsible for administering and enforcing the country's tax laws.
