# Education Costs in Turks and Caicos

The cost of education in Turks and Caicos varies significantly depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Turks and Caicos is based on the British model, with English as the primary language of instruction. Public schools are free or subsidized for local students, but the quality can vary, with some schools facing challenges related to resources and infrastructure. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a higher quality of education, but at a significantly higher cost. Compulsory education in Turks and Caicos lasts for 12 years, from primary to secondary school. International schools are also available, offering a range of curricula, including British, American, and International Baccalaureate.

Public schools in Turks and Caicos are managed by the Ministry of Education, and they follow a curriculum that is similar to the British National Curriculum. The quality of education in public schools can vary, with some schools performing better than others. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a more personalized and rigorous education, with smaller class sizes and better resources. International schools in Turks and Caicos offer a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate, which is popular among expat families.
