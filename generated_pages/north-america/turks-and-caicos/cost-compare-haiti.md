[SECTION] Overview: Turks and Caicos vs Haiti [/SECTION]
Turks and Caicos and Haiti are two neighboring countries in the Caribbean with vastly different economic contexts. Turks and Caicos is a British Overseas Territory with a high-income economy, driven by tourism and financial services, while Haiti is one of the poorest countries in the Western Hemisphere, with a largely informal economy. The cost of living in Turks and Caicos is significantly higher than in Haiti, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being substantially greater. On the other hand, Haiti's economy is characterized by high inflation and a lack of infrastructure, which can make everyday life more challenging.

The cost of living difference between the two countries is substantial, with Haiti being around 50-60% cheaper than Turks and Caicos. This is reflected in the prices of everyday items, such as housing, food, and transportation. However, it's worth noting that the quality of life and standard of living in Turks and Caicos are generally higher than in Haiti, with better infrastructure, healthcare, and education systems. Expats and tourists often prefer Turks and Caicos for its beautiful beaches, clear waters, and modern amenities, while Haiti is often chosen for its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and lower costs.
