[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Turks and Caicos has a relatively well-developed transportation system, with a range of options available for getting around the islands. The most common modes of transportation are taxis, rental cars, and buses, with many visitors also opting to use bikes or walk. The quality of public transit is generally good, with regular bus services connecting the main towns and villages. However, the lack of a comprehensive public transportation system means that many people rely on private vehicles or taxis to get around.

The majority of people in Turks and Caicos get around by car, with many residents owning their own vehicles. However, for visitors, there are plenty of alternative options available, including taxi services, bike rentals, and guided tours. The islands are relatively small, making it easy to get around without a car, and many of the main attractions are within walking distance of each other. Additionally, the **Turks and Caicos Tourist Board** provides a range of transportation options, including shuttle services and guided tours, to help visitors get around the islands.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using public buses or shared taxis instead of private taxis or rental cars.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Lyft to get around, especially in main towns.
- Always negotiate the fare with taxi drivers before you start your journey.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
- Consider renting a car for a day or two to explore the islands, but be aware that driving can be challenging, especially on the left-hand side of the road.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps to ensure your safety, especially at night.
