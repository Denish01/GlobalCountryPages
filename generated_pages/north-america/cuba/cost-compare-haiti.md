[SECTION] Overview: Cuba vs Haiti [/SECTION]
Cuba and Haiti are two neighboring countries in the Caribbean with distinct economic profiles. Cuba has a socialist economy with a strong focus on social welfare, while Haiti has a developing economy with a large informal sector. The cost of living in Cuba is generally lower than in Haiti, due to the former's subsidized economy and price controls. However, Haiti has a more flexible economy with a wider range of consumer goods available. Overall, Cuba is a more affordable destination for expats and travelers, with a lower cost of living and a more comprehensive social safety net.

The economic context of the two countries is also worth considering. Cuba has a highly developed education and healthcare system, with free access to these services for all citizens. Haiti, on the other hand, struggles with poverty, corruption, and a lack of infrastructure. Despite these challenges, Haiti has a more vibrant culture and a wider range of outdoor activities, making it an attractive destination for adventure-seekers. In terms of cost, Cuba is generally 20-30% cheaper than Haiti, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation.

[SECTION] Bottom Line [/SECTION]
Cuba and Haiti offer different value propositions for different lifestyles. For expats and retirees, Cuba's low cost of living and comprehensive social safety net make it an attractive destination. Overall, Cuba is the better value for those looking for a low-cost, high-quality lifestyle, while Haiti is the better choice for those seeking adventure and cultural immersion.
