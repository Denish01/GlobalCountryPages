[SECTION] Overview: Cuba vs United States [/SECTION]
Cuba and the United States have distinct economic contexts that significantly impact the cost of living in each country. Cuba, a socialist state, has a planned economy with a strong emphasis on social welfare and government control, which contributes to its lower cost of living. In contrast, the United States has a mixed economy with a strong private sector, leading to higher prices for goods and services. Overall, Cuba is generally cheaper than the United States, with a cost of living that is approximately 50-60% lower. This is due to lower prices for housing, food, and transportation, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of each country also plays a significant role in shaping the cost of living. Cuba's economy has been heavily influenced by its socialist system, with a strong emphasis on government control and social welfare. This has led to a more equal distribution of wealth, with lower prices for essential goods and services. In contrast, the United States has a more unequal distribution of wealth, with higher prices for luxury goods and services. As a result, Cuba is a more affordable option for those on a budget, while the United States offers more opportunities for luxury and convenience.

[SECTION] Bottom Line [/SECTION]
