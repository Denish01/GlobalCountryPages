[SECTION] What Is Cuba? [/SECTION]
Cuba is a **sovereign** island nation located in the northern part of the Caribbean Sea, with a geographic position that has played a significant role in its history and culture. The country is situated approximately 90 miles south of the United States and is bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the north, the Caribbean Sea to the south, the Gulf of Mexico to the west, and the Straits of Florida to the northwest. Cuba is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and complex history, which has been shaped by its indigenous people, Spanish colonization, African influences, and socialist revolution.

Cuba is the largest island in the Caribbean and has a diverse geography, with mountains, forests, and coastal plains. The country's capital, **Havana**, is a vibrant city known for its historic architecture, lively music scene, and friendly people. Cuba is also famous for its beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and vibrant arts scene, which reflects the country's African, Spanish, and indigenous influences. The country's unique cultural identity has been shaped by its history, geography, and the blending of different cultures, making it a fascinating destination for visitors and a source of national pride for its people.

Cuba's sovereignty status as a socialist republic has had a significant impact on its economy, politics, and culture. The country has a planned economy, with a strong emphasis on social welfare, education, and healthcare. Cuba's socialist system has provided its citizens with access to free education and healthcare, but it has also limited economic freedom and individual liberties. The country's complex history and cultural identity have made it a subject of fascination and debate, with many people around the world interested in learning more about this unique and fascinating nation.
