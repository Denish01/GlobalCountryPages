[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Aruba is a small island with a well-developed transportation system, making it easy to get around without a car. The main transportation modes include buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit is good, with frequent bus services connecting major towns and cities. Most people get around by using the public bus system or taxis, while some prefer to rent cars or use ride-hailing services. The island's small size and relatively flat terrain make it ideal for cycling or walking, and many tourists choose to explore the island on foot or by bike.

The public transportation system in Aruba is efficient and affordable, with buses running frequently throughout the day. The bus network covers most areas of the island, including popular tourist destinations. Taxis are also widely available, and many drivers speak English, making it easy for tourists to get around. Ride-hailing services are also available, with Uber and Easy Taxi operating on the island. For those who prefer to drive, car rental services are available, and the island's roads are generally in good condition.
