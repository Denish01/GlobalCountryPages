[SECTION] Overview: Aruba vs Bonaire [/SECTION]
Aruba and Bonaire are two islands in the southern Caribbean, known for their beautiful beaches and rich cultural heritage. While both islands have a high standard of living, Aruba tends to be more expensive due to its larger size, more developed economy, and greater tourist demand. Bonaire, on the other hand, is a smaller island with a more laid-back atmosphere and lower prices. The difference in cost of living between the two islands can be significant, with Aruba being around 10-20% more expensive than Bonaire. This is reflected in the prices of housing, food, transportation, and other living expenses.

The economic context of the two islands also plays a role in their cost of living. Aruba has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on tourism, finance, and industry, which drives up demand and prices for goods and services. Bonaire, on the other hand, has a smaller economy that is more focused on tourism and fishing, which results in lower prices and a more relaxed atmosphere. Overall, Bonaire is the cheaper option for those looking to live or visit the Caribbean, while Aruba offers a more upscale and developed experience.
