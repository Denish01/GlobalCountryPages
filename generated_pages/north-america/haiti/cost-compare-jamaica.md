[SECTION] Overview: Haiti vs Jamaica [/SECTION]
Haiti and Jamaica are two Caribbean countries with distinct economic profiles. Haiti is one of the poorest countries in the Western Hemisphere, with a largely informal economy and a significant reliance on foreign aid. Jamaica, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy with a strong tourism sector and a growing services industry. In terms of cost of living, Haiti is generally cheaper than Jamaica, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. However, Jamaica offers a more developed infrastructure and a higher standard of living, which may justify the higher costs for some expats and visitors.

The cost of living difference between Haiti and Jamaica is significant, with Haiti being approximately 20-30% cheaper overall. This is largely due to the lower cost of accommodation and food in Haiti, as well as the country's lower overall price level. However, Jamaica offers a more developed economy and a higher standard of living, which may make it a more attractive option for some visitors and expats. In terms of specific costs, Haiti is generally cheaper for accommodation, food, and transportation, while Jamaica is more expensive for these categories. However, Jamaica may offer better value for money in terms of quality of life and access to amenities.
