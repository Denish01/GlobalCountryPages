Foreign Ownership Rules

  • Foreigners can buy property in Haiti, but they must obtain a permit from the Ministry of Justice and Public Security.
  • Foreigners can also lease property for a period of up to 99 years, which can be renewed.
  • To buy property, foreigners must register with the Haitian government and obtain a tax identification number.
  • Foreigners can also set up a Haitian company to buy property, which can provide additional tax benefits.
  • Foreigners must also comply with environmental regulations and zoning laws when buying or developing property.

Rental Market

  • Tenant rights are protected under Haitian law, which requires landlords to provide a written lease agreement.
  • Typical lease terms are 6-12 months, with a security deposit equivalent to 1-2 months' rent.
  • Furnished properties are more common in urban areas, while unfurnished properties are more common in rural areas.
  • Rent increases are subject to government regulations, which limit annual increases to 10%.
  • Eviction procedures are complex and can take several months to complete.
