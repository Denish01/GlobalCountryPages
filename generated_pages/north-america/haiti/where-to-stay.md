# Where to Stay in Haiti — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Haiti, the best areas to stay are typically found in the capital city of Port-au-Prince and surrounding areas, offering a range of options for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**Pétion-Ville** (Port-au-Prince)
Best For: Luxury, Business
Vibe: This upscale neighborhood offers a more modern and secure environment, with plenty of restaurants, shops, and entertainment options, and is known for its vibrant nightlife and cultural events.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to many businesses and organizations, a wide range of dining options, and modern amenities, as well as a lively atmosphere with many cultural events and festivals.
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and traffic in the area can be heavy at times.

**Bourdon** (Port-au-Prince)
Best For: Family, Mid-range
Vibe: This charming neighborhood is known for its picturesque views, historic architecture, and family-friendly atmosphere, with many parks and green spaces, as well as a variety of restaurants and shops.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Quiet and safe environment, stunning views of the city, and a range of amenities, including parks, restaurants, and shops, as well as a strong sense of community.
Cons: May be a bit far from the city center, and some areas can be quite hilly.

**Jacmel** (Jacmel)
Best For: Backpacker, Artist
Vibe: This coastal town is a haven for artists and creatives, with a laid-back atmosphere, stunning beaches, and a thriving cultural scene, featuring many galleries, studios, and festivals.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable prices, a vibrant arts scene, and stunning natural beauty, as well as a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere, perfect for those looking to unwind and express themselves.
Cons: Can be quite crowded during peak season, and some areas may lack modern amenities.

**Cap-Haïten** (Cap-Haïten)
Best For: History Buff, Cultural Enthusiast
Vibe: This historic city is a treasure trove of Haitian culture and history, with many museums, landmarks, and cultural events, as well as a rich and vibrant atmosphere.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: Rich cultural heritage, historic architecture, and a range of cultural events and festivals, as well as a strong sense of community and a welcoming atmosphere.
Cons: May be a bit off the beaten path, and some areas can be quite crowded.

**Kenscoff** (Kenscoff)
Best For: Nature Lover, Adventure Seeker
Vibe: This mountain town is a nature lover's paradise, with stunning views, hiking trails, and a range of outdoor activities, as well as a cool and refreshing climate.
Price Range: $40-200/night
Pros: Breathtaking natural beauty, a range of outdoor activities, and a cooler climate, as well as a peaceful and serene atmosphere, perfect for those looking to escape the city.
Cons: Can be quite remote, and some areas may lack modern amenities.
