[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Montserrat is based on the British model, with English as the primary language of instruction. Public schools in Montserrat are generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong focus on core subjects such as mathematics, science, and English. Compulsory education in Montserrat lasts for 12 years, from the age of 5 to 17, and is divided into primary and secondary education. While there are no international schools on the island, some private schools offer international curricula such as the Cambridge International Examinations.

The quality of education in Montserrat is generally considered to be high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and personal development. Public schools are free or subsidized, making education accessible to all citizens, while private schools cater to those who can afford to pay higher fees. The language of instruction is English, and the curriculum is designed to prepare students for further education and career opportunities. Despite the lack of international schools, Montserrat's education system is well-regarded in the region, and many students go on to pursue higher education at universities in the UK, US, or other Caribbean islands.
