# Where to Stay in Montserrat — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Montserrat, the best areas to stay are in the north, particularly in the neighborhoods of Brades, Salem, and Olveston, which offer a range of accommodations and easy access to the island's main attractions.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, the following are highly recommended:
**Brades** (Brades)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: Brades has a vibrant and modern vibe, with many amenities and services available, including restaurants, shops, and a hospital. The area is also home to the island's government offices and is a hub for business and commerce.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Modern amenities, central location, easy access to government offices and services. 
Cons: Can be busy and noisy, limited nightlife options.

**Salem** (Salem)
Best For: Family / Backpacker
Vibe: Salem has a charming and laid-back atmosphere, with many historic buildings and a beautiful beach. The area is popular with families and budget travelers, and offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and snorkeling.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Affordable, beautiful beach, historic charm. 
Cons: Limited amenities, can be quiet and secluded.

**Olveston** (Olveston)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Olveston has a luxurious and secluded vibe, with many high-end villas and resorts. The area is popular with families and couples, and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and ocean.
Price Range: $300-$600/night
Pros: Luxurious amenities, stunning views, secluded and private. 
Cons: Can be expensive, limited access to public transportation.

**Woodlands** (Woodlands)
Best For: Backpacker / Adventure
Vibe: Woodlands has a rugged and natural vibe, with many hiking trails and outdoor activities available. The area is popular with backpackers and adventure seekers, and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
Price Range: $80-$200/night
Pros: Affordable, beautiful scenery, many outdoor activities. 
Cons: Limited amenities, can be remote and inaccessible.

**St. Peter's** (St. Peter's)
Best For: Family / Luxury
Vibe: St. Peter's has a charming and historic vibe, with many old buildings and a beautiful church. The area is popular with families and couples, and offers a range of amenities, including restaurants and shops.
Price Range: $150-$400/night
Pros: Historic charm, beautiful church, many amenities. 
Cons: Can be busy and noisy, limited nightlife options.

**Cavalla Hill** (Cavalla Hill)
Best For: Luxury / Romance
Vibe: Cavalla Hill has a luxurious and romantic vibe, with many high-end villas and stunning views of the surrounding ocean. The area is popular with couples and honeymooners, and offers a range of amenities, including private pools and outdoor kitchens.
Price Range: $400-$800/night
Pros: Luxurious amenities, stunning views, romantic and private. 
Cons: Can be expensive, limited access to public transportation.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to April), to ensure availability and the best rates.
- Use online booking platforms, such as Expedia or Booking.com, to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider negotiating the rate, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings.
- Look for packages and promotions that include amenities, such as breakfast or car rentals.
- Be aware of any additional fees or taxes, such as the island's 10% accommodation tax.
- Check the cancellation policy and any restrictions on changes or refunds.
- Read reviews and check the property's ratings before booking to ensure it meets your needs and expectations.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of the island's volcanic activity and follow any instructions or warnings from local authorities.
- Choose accommodations in safe and accessible areas, such as Brades or Salem.
- Consider the proximity to public transportation, amenities, and services when selecting a location.
- Be mindful of the island's narrow roads and limited parking options.
- Keep valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night.
- Follow local customs and regulations, such as respecting private property and protected areas.
