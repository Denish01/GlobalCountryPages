[SECTION] What Is Montserrat? [/SECTION]
Montserrat is a **British Overseas Territory** located in the Caribbean Sea, part of the Leeward Islands in the Lesser Antilles. It is situated approximately 43 kilometers southwest of Antigua and Barbuda, and 64 kilometers northwest of Guadeloupe. The island has a total area of approximately 102 square kilometers and is known for its stunning natural beauty, with lush green mountains, beautiful beaches, and a diverse range of flora and fauna. Montserrat is also home to the **Soufrière Hills volcano**, which has been actively erupting since 1995, causing significant damage to the island and its infrastructure.

The island's sovereignty status is that of a British Overseas Territory, with the **United Kingdom** responsible for its defense and foreign affairs. The local government is headed by a **Governor** appointed by the British monarch, and a **Legislative Council** composed of elected members. Montserrat has a relatively small population, with a strong sense of community and a rich cultural heritage. The island's economy is primarily based on **tourism** and **agriculture**, with a growing focus on **sustainable development** and **renewable energy**.

Montserrat's geographic position in the Caribbean makes it vulnerable to natural disasters such as **hurricanes** and **volcanic eruptions**. However, the island's stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality make it a unique and attractive destination for visitors. Despite the challenges posed by the volcanic eruption, Montserrat remains a popular destination for **eco-tourists**, **adventure seekers**, and those looking to experience the island's unique culture and history.
