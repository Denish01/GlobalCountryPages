[SECTION] Overview: Montserrat vs Dominica [/SECTION]
Montserrat and Dominica are two island nations in the Caribbean, with distinct economic profiles. Montserrat, a British overseas territory, has a relatively high GDP per capita and a stable economy, driven by tourism and financial services. In contrast, Dominica has a more diverse economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism. The cost of living in Montserrat is higher due to its smaller size, limited resources, and dependence on imports. Dominica, on the other hand, has a more affordable cost of living, making it an attractive destination for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of both countries plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Montserrat's economy is more service-oriented, with a high demand for skilled labor, which drives up the cost of living. Dominica, with its more diverse economy, has a lower cost of living, but also lower average salaries. Overall, Dominica is generally cheaper than Montserrat, with a difference of around 20-30% in overall cost of living. This makes Dominica an attractive option for those looking for an affordable Caribbean lifestyle.
