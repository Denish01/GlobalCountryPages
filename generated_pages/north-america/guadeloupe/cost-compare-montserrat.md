[SECTION] Overview: Guadeloupe vs Montserrat [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe and Montserrat are two island territories in the Caribbean, with distinct economic contexts. Guadeloupe is an overseas department of France, which means it has a more developed economy and infrastructure, but also a higher cost of living compared to some other Caribbean islands. Montserrat, on the other hand, is a British Overseas Territory with a smaller population and a more limited economy. Despite these differences, Guadeloupe tends to be cheaper than Montserrat, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. The cost of living in Guadeloupe is approximately 15-20% lower than in Montserrat, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of the two territories also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Guadeloupe has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing, which contributes to its lower cost of living. Montserrat, on the other hand, has a more limited economy, with a strong reliance on tourism and foreign aid. This difference in economic context is reflected in the prices of goods and services, with Guadeloupe generally offering better value for money. Overall, Guadeloupe is the more affordable option, but Montserrat offers a unique blend of natural beauty and British culture that may appeal to some visitors and residents.
