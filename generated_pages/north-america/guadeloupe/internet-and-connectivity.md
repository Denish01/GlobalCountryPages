[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet quality in Guadeloupe is generally good, with average speeds of 20 Mbps download, making it suitable for streaming, browsing, and online work. However, there is a noticeable urban-rural divide, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in urban areas such as Basse-Terre and Pointe-a-Pitre, while rural areas may experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Despite this, the internet infrastructure in Guadeloupe is relatively well-developed, with a good mobile network coverage and widespread WiFi availability, making it easy to stay connected throughout the island.

The reliability of the internet in Guadeloupe is also relatively good, with few reports of major outages or disruptions. However, as with any island nation, the internet infrastructure can be vulnerable to natural disasters such as hurricanes, which can cause power outages and damage to infrastructure. Overall, the internet in Guadeloupe is suitable for tourists and digital nomads, with a good balance of speed, reliability, and affordability.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Guadeloupe, you can visit one of the main carriers' stores, such as Orange, SFR, or Digicel, with your passport and a valid form of identification. The cost of a tourist SIM card is approximately 20 USD, and it usually comes with a set amount of data, minutes, and texts. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds. You can also purchase a SIM card at the airport or at various retail stores throughout the island.

The documents needed to purchase a SIM card in Guadeloupe include a valid passport and a proof of address, such as a hotel reservation or a utility bill. Some carriers may also require a copy of your identification, such as a driver's license or a national ID card. It's recommended to check with the carrier before purchasing a SIM card to confirm their requirements.
