# Getting Around Guadeloupe — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Guadeloupe, you can use a variety of transportation options, including flights, buses, taxis, and rental cars, which cater to different budgets and preferences.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe is easily accessible by air, with the Pôle Caraïbes Airport (PTP) being the main international airport. The airport is located in the commune of Abymes, about 5 kilometers northwest of Pointe-à-Pitre, the largest city in Guadeloupe. Direct flights are available from major hubs such as Paris, Miami, and Fort-de-France, making it convenient for travelers to reach the island. Additionally, Guadeloupe can be reached by sea, with several cruise lines and ferry services operating from neighboring islands.

For those traveling from nearby islands, there are also overland entry points, including the port of Pointe-à-Pitre, which receives ferries from Dominica, Antigua and Barbuda, and Montserrat. The port is well-equipped to handle both passenger and cargo traffic, making it a convenient entry point for travelers. Furthermore, the island's strategic location in the Caribbean makes it an ideal stopover for travelers exploring the region.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe has a limited domestic air network, with Air Caraibes and Air Antilles Express being the main airlines operating within the island. The main routes include flights between Pôle Caraïbes Airport (PTP) and the airport in Marie-Galante (GBJ), as well as Les Saintes (LSS). Booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as flights can be limited. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $100 to $200 USD, depending on the route and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe does not have a rail network, making other modes of transportation, such as buses and rental cars, more convenient for getting around the island. However, there are plans to develop a tramway system in the future, which would connect the main cities and towns.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe has an extensive network of buses and coaches, operated by Karu'Lis and other private companies. The buses connect the main cities and towns, including Basse-Terre, Pointe-à-Pitre, and Sainte-Rose. The quality of the buses is generally good, with air-conditioned vehicles and regular schedules. Costs are relatively affordable, ranging from $2 to $5 USD per ride, depending on the route.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- A valid driver's license is required to rent a car in Guadeloupe, and some rental companies may require an international driving permit.
- Road conditions are generally good, with well-maintained highways and scenic coastal roads.
- Fuel costs are relatively high, with prices ranging from $1.50 to $2.50 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies, such as Europcar and Hertz, are available at the airport and in major towns.
- Drivers should be cautious of narrow roads and pedestrian traffic, especially in urban areas.
- Parking can be challenging in crowded areas, and drivers should be mindful of parking restrictions and fees.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **traffic congestion** in urban areas, especially during rush hour.
- Use **reputable taxi companies** or ride-hailing apps to avoid scams.
- **Bargain** with taxi drivers or vendors, as prices may be negotiable.
- Download the **Goub'Car** app for convenient ride-hailing services.
- Be mindful of **road signs** and **speed limits**, as traffic laws are strictly enforced.
- Consider purchasing a **local SIM card** or portable Wi-Fi hotspot for navigation and communication.
- Always **wear a seatbelt** and follow basic road safety rules to avoid accidents.
