[SECTION] Overview: Guadeloupe vs Martinique [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe and Martinique are two French overseas territories in the Caribbean, known for their stunning beaches, lush rainforests, and vibrant culture. While both islands share many similarities, they also have distinct differences in terms of cost of living. Guadeloupe has a slightly lower cost of living compared to Martinique, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being generally 10-20% lower. This is due to various factors, including the size of the islands, economic conditions, and government policies.

The economic context of both islands is also worth considering. Guadeloupe has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism. Martinique, on the other hand, has a more service-based economy, with a strong emphasis on tourism and finance. This difference in economic structure can affect the cost of living, with Guadeloupe tend to be more affordable for everyday expenses. Overall, Guadeloupe is a more budget-friendly option for those looking to live or visit the Caribbean, while Martinique is more geared towards luxury tourism and high-end lifestyles.
