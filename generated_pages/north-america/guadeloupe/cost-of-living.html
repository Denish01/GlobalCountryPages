How Expensive Is Guadeloupe?

Guadeloupe is an overseas department of France, and as such, its economy and cost of living are closely tied to those of Western Europe. The cost of living in Guadeloupe is generally higher than in many other Caribbean islands, due to its strong economy and high standard of living. However, prices in Guadeloupe are still generally lower than those in major European cities like Paris or London. Compared to its neighbors, Guadeloupe is more expensive than Dominica and Montserrat, but similar in price to Antigua and Barbuda.

The cost of living in Guadeloupe can vary depending on the location, with the capital city of Basse-Terre and the popular tourist areas tend to be more expensive than the rest of the island. However, overall, Guadeloupe offers a unique blend of French and Caribbean culture, beautiful beaches, and a high standard of living, making it a popular destination for tourists and expats alike.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Consider staying in an apartment or house outside of the city center to save on rent.
  • Eat at local eateries or food stalls to save on food costs.
  • Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transport costs.
  • Avoid eating at touristy restaurants or hotels, as prices tend to be higher.
  • Shop at local markets or grocery stores for fresh produce and other essentials.
  • Look for package deals or discounts on activities and tours to save on entertainment costs.

Overall, Guadeloupe offers a unique and enjoyable experience for visitors and expats, with a mix of French and Caribbean culture, beautiful beaches, and a high standard of living. With some planning and research, it is possible to have a comfortable and enjoyable stay in Guadeloupe without breaking the bank.
