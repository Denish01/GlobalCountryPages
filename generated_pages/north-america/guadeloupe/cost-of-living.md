# Cost of Living in Guadeloupe - Prices & Budget Guide

To live in or visit Guadeloupe, you can expect to pay moderate to expensive prices, with daily budgets ranging from $80-150 USD for backpackers to $250-400 USD for comfort seekers.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $80-120 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $150-250 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $250-400 USD
Currency: EUR
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 5-10% is appreciated
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Guadeloupe? [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe is an overseas department of France, and as such, it has a higher cost of living compared to other Caribbean islands. The prices in Guadeloupe are similar to those in France, making it a moderately expensive destination. Compared to its neighbors, such as Dominica and Antigua and Barbuda, Guadeloupe is more expensive, but it offers a unique blend of French and Caribbean culture, beautiful beaches, and a wide range of outdoor activities. The cost of living in Guadeloupe is also influenced by its remote location, which can make transportation and shipping of goods more expensive.

The cost of living in Guadeloupe can vary depending on the location, with the capital city of Basse-Terre and the tourist areas tend to be more expensive than the rest of the island. However, with some planning and research, it is possible to find affordable accommodations, food, and activities, making Guadeloupe a great destination for travelers and expats alike.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel, or renting an apartment outside of the city center to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls, which offer delicious and affordable meals.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transportation costs.
- Buy groceries at local markets or supermarkets instead of relying on tourist-oriented shops.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost activities, such as visiting the beach, hiking, or exploring the island's natural beauty.
- Learn some basic French phrases, which can help you navigate the island and interact with locals, potentially leading to more affordable options and a more authentic experience.
