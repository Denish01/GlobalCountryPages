[SECTION] Overview: Guadeloupe vs Reunion [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Guadeloupe and Reunion, two French overseas territories, differs significantly due to their unique economic contexts. Guadeloupe, located in the Caribbean, has a smaller economy and lower prices compared to Reunion, an island in the Indian Ocean with a more developed economy. The main factors contributing to the cost difference are the prices of housing, food, and transportation. On average, Guadeloupe is around 15-20% cheaper than Reunion, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable Caribbean lifestyle.

The economic context of both territories also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Guadeloupe's economy is primarily driven by tourism, agriculture, and services, while Reunion's economy is more diversified, with a strong focus on industry, tourism, and services. The difference in economic structure contributes to the variation in prices, with Reunion tend to have higher prices due to its more developed economy and higher standard of living. However, Guadeloupe's lower prices and stunning natural beauty make it an excellent choice for those seeking a more affordable and relaxed Caribbean experience.
