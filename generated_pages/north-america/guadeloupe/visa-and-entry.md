# Guadeloupe Visa & Entry Requirements

To determine if you need a visa for Guadeloupe, it is essential to check the specific requirements based on your nationality, as Guadeloupe, being an overseas department of France, follows the French visa policy.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Guadeloupe before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others may need to obtain a visa before arrival or on arrival. As a territory of France, Guadeloupe's visa policy is aligned with that of France, which allows citizens of many countries to visit without a visa for a specified period. Guadeloupe offers visa-free access to citizens of around 90 countries, including those from the European Union, the United States, Canada, and Australia, for stays of up to 90 days.

The visa policy of Guadeloupe is designed to facilitate travel for tourists, business travelers, and other visitors, while ensuring the security and integrity of the territory. Visitors who require a visa must apply in advance, and the application process typically involves submitting various documents, including a valid passport, proof of purpose, and proof of sufficient funds.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Guadeloupe visa-free for up to 90 days, provided they have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, and extensions are possible in certain circumstances. Tourists must also have travel insurance that covers them for the duration of their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to obtain a **business visa**, which requires an invitation letter from a Guadeloupe-based company, proof of business activities, and other supporting documents. The requirements for business travelers are more stringent than those for tourists, and the application process may take longer.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in Guadeloupe must obtain a **student visa**, which requires proof of enrollment in a Guadeloupe-based educational institution, sufficient funds, and health insurance. The student visa is typically valid for the duration of the study program, and students may need to renew their visa annually.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Guadeloupe must obtain a **work visa**, which requires sponsorship from a Guadeloupe-based employer, proof of qualifications, and other supporting documents. The work visa application process can be lengthy, and workers may need to undergo a medical examination and obtain a residence permit.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airport in Guadeloupe is Pôle Caraïbes Airport, which receives international flights from Europe, North America, and other Caribbean islands. Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and answer questions about their stay. Visitors may also be asked to provide proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and travel insurance. The immigration process is typically straightforward, but visitors should be prepared to wait in line, especially during peak travel seasons.

Guadeloupe also has several seaports, including the Port of Pointe-à-Pitre, which receives cruise ships and private boats. Visitors arriving by sea must also comply with immigration procedures and may need to present additional documents, such as a crew list or a charter agreement.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not having proof of onward travel or return ticket
- Not having sufficient funds or travel insurance
- Not applying for a visa in advance (if required)
- Not bringing required documents, such as an invitation letter or proof of enrollment
- Not declaring goods or currency upon arrival, which may result in fines or penalties.
