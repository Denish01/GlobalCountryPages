[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe's healthcare system is considered to be of high quality, with both public and private healthcare services available. The public healthcare system is generally more affordable for locals and foreigners with insurance, while private healthcare services are more expensive but often offer more specialized care and shorter wait times. The system works similarly for locals and foreigners, with the main difference being that foreigners may need to pay out-of-pocket for services if they do not have insurance. It is recommended that visitors and expats have insurance to cover any medical expenses, as the cost of healthcare can be high.

The public healthcare system in Guadeloupe is funded by the French government and is free or low-cost for locals and foreigners with insurance. The system includes a network of public hospitals, clinics, and healthcare centers that provide a range of medical services, including emergency care, specialized care, and preventive care. Private healthcare services are also available, including private hospitals and clinics, and are often preferred by foreigners and those who can afford to pay out-of-pocket for services.

[SECTION] Pharmacy & Medication Costs [/SECTION]
- Prescription medication can cost between $10-$50 per month, depending on the type and dosage.
- Over-the-counter medication is widely available at pharmacies and can cost between $5-$20 per item.
- Pharmacies are readily available in urban areas, but may be scarce in rural areas.
- A prescription from a doctor is required for most medication, but some medication can be purchased over-the-counter.
- The cost of medication can vary depending on the pharmacy and location.
- Some pharmacies may offer discounts for bulk purchases or loyalty programs.
