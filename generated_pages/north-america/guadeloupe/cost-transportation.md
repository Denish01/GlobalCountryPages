[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Guadeloupe offers a range of transportation options, including public buses, taxis, and rental cars. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent bus services connecting major towns and cities. Most people get around Guadeloupe by car, either by renting a vehicle or using taxis. The island's scenic roads and stunning scenery make driving a popular option, but public transportation is also a convenient and affordable way to explore the island.

The public bus network in Guadeloupe is operated by Karu'Lis, which provides frequent services to most parts of the island. Buses are a great way to get around, especially for shorter journeys, and are relatively inexpensive. Taxis are also widely available, but can be more expensive than buses. Ride-hailing apps such as Uber and Bolt are also available in Guadeloupe, providing an alternative to traditional taxis.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a monthly transit pass or using public buses for shorter journeys.
- Use ride-hailing apps such as Uber or Bolt for door-to-door services, especially at night or in rural areas.
- Always negotiate taxi fares before starting your journey, especially for longer distances.
- Be aware of limited parking spaces in city centers and consider using park-and-ride facilities.
- Drive carefully, especially on rural roads, and be mindful of pedestrians and cyclists.
- Consider renting a car for longer periods to explore the island at your own pace, but be aware of the costs of fuel and parking.
