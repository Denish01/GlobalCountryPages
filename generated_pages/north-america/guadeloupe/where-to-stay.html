To determine the best place to stay in Guadeloupe, consider the island's two main areas, Basse-Terre and Grande-Terre, each offering a unique experience, with top recommendations including the cities of Deshaies, Sainte-Rose, and Gosier.

Best Areas to Stay

For each of the top areas, Guadeloupe offers a diverse range of options to cater to different types of travelers.

Deshaies (Basse-Terre)

Best For: Luxury/Family

Vibe: Deshaies is a picturesque fishing village with a relaxed atmosphere, surrounded by beautiful beaches and lush greenery, making it perfect for those seeking a tranquil retreat. The village offers a range of activities, including snorkeling, diving, and boat tours.

Price Range: $200-$500/night

Pros: Beautiful beaches, excellent seafood restaurants, and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and water sports.

Cons: Limited nightlife options, and the area can be quite crowded during peak season.

Sainte-Rose (Basse-Terre)

Best For: Backpacker/Family

Vibe: Sainte-Rose is a charming town with a rich history, offering a mix of cultural attractions, beautiful beaches, and outdoor activities, making it an ideal destination for families and backpackers alike. The town has a lively atmosphere, with a range of restaurants, bars, and shops.

Price Range: $100-$300/night

Pros: Rich history, beautiful beaches, and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and water sports, as well as a lively atmosphere with many restaurants and bars.

Cons: Limited luxury accommodation options, and the area can be quite crowded during peak season.

Gosier (Grande-Terre)

Best For: Business/Luxury

Vibe: Gosier is a bustling town with a modern vibe, offering a range of amenities, including shopping centers, restaurants, and bars, making it perfect for business travelers and those seeking a more urban experience. The town has a lively atmosphere, with many events and activities throughout the year.

Price Range: $250-$600/night

Pros: Modern amenities, excellent restaurants, and a range of entertainment options, including a casino and a water park.

Cons: Limited historic charm, and the area can be quite crowded and noisy.

Terre-de-Bas (Basse-Terre)

Best For: Backpacker/Couple

Vibe: Terre-de-Bas is a peaceful and picturesque island with a relaxed atmosphere, surrounded by beautiful beaches and lush greenery, making it perfect for couples and backpackers seeking a tranquil retreat. The island offers a range of outdoor activities, including snorkeling, diving, and hiking.

Price Range: $150-$350/night

Pros: Beautiful beaches, peaceful atmosphere, and a range of outdoor activities, including water sports and hiking.

Cons: Limited amenities, and the island can be quite isolated.

Booking Tips

  • Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (December to April), to ensure availability and the best rates.
  • Consider booking through online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb for a wide range of options and competitive prices.
  • Look for package deals that include flights, accommodations, and car rentals for a convenient and cost-effective option.
  • Negotiate prices directly with hoteliers or property owners for long-term stays or off-season bookings.
  • Be aware of seasonal pricing, with rates tend to be higher during peak season and lower during the off-season.
  • Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before booking to ensure a smooth and stress-free experience.
  • Consider booking a room with a view, especially in areas like Deshaies or Sainte-Rose, to make the most of the island's beautiful scenery.

Safety & Location Tips

  • Avoid areas with high crime rates, such as some neighborhoods in Pointe-a-Pitre, and instead opt for safer areas like Basse-Terre or Gosier.
  • Be mindful of natural hazards like hurricanes and tropical storms, which can occur between June and November, and take necessary precautions to stay safe.
  • Choose accommodations with good proximity to public transportation, such as buses or ferries, to easily explore the island.
  • Consider renting a car to explore the island, but be aware of narrow roads and limited parking options.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events.
  • Take necessary precautions to protect yourself from the sun, heat, and mosquitoes, and stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
