[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Guadeloupe National Park** is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, featuring stunning natural beauty and diverse wildlife.
- The **Saint-Jean-Baptiste Cathedral** in Basse-Terre is a historic landmark and a testament to the island's rich cultural heritage.
- The **Memorial ACTe** in Pointe-à-Pitre is a museum and cultural center dedicated to the history and legacy of slavery and the slave trade.
- The **Guadeloupe Carnival** is a vibrant and colorful celebration of music, dance, and culture, which takes place every year in February and March.
- The **Fête des Cuisinières** is a traditional festival celebrating the island's culinary heritage and the importance of women in Guadeloupean society.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Aimé Césaire**, a renowned poet and politician, was born in Basse-Terre and played a key role in the development of the **Négritude** movement, which promoted black cultural identity and pride.
- The **Guadeloupean diaspora** has made significant contributions to the cultural and economic development of France and other countries, with many Guadeloupeans holding prominent positions in fields such as politics, arts, and sports.
- **Maryse Condé**, a celebrated author and scholar, was born in Pointe-à-Pitre and has written extensively on the history and culture of Guadeloupe and the Caribbean.
- The **values of community and solidarity** are deeply ingrained in Guadeloupean culture, with many residents actively involved in volunteer work and community development initiatives.
