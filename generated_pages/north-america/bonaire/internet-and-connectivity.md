[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Bonaire is relatively well-developed, considering its small size and island location. The average internet speed is around 10 Mbps, which is sufficient for basic tasks like browsing, email, and social media. However, for more demanding activities like streaming and online gaming, the speeds might not be fast enough. The urban areas, particularly the capital Kralendijk, tend to have better internet coverage and faster speeds compared to the rural areas. Despite this, the overall reliability of the internet in Bonaire is good, with minimal downtime and outages.

The internet infrastructure in Bonaire is mostly based on cable and fiber-optic connections, with some areas also having access to satellite internet. This has helped to improve the overall quality and availability of the internet, especially in the more populated areas. However, the lack of 5G coverage and limited mobile network coverage in some rural areas can be a drawback for those who need high-speed internet on the go. Overall, the internet in Bonaire is suitable for most tourists and remote workers, but it may not be ideal for those with very high internet demands.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Bonaire, tourists can visit one of the main carriers' stores, such as Digicel or TELBO, and purchase a prepaid SIM card. The cost of a tourist SIM card is around 20 USD, and it usually comes with some credit and data. To buy a SIM card, tourists will need to provide a valid passport and fill out a registration form. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they tend to have better coverage and more reliable services. Data plans vary in price and data allowance, but most carriers offer affordable options for tourists.

- Roaming alternatives like **eSIM** and **international roaming plans** are available, but they can be expensive and have limited data allowances.
