Education System Overview

The education system in Bonaire is based on the Dutch system, with Papiamento and Dutch being the primary languages of instruction. Public education is generally of good quality, but private schools tend to have better facilities and more experienced teachers. Compulsory education in Bonaire lasts for 12 years, starting from the age of 4 and ending at the age of 16. International schools are also available, offering English and Spanish as languages of instruction, and catering to the needs of expat families.

The public education system in Bonaire is free or subsidized, making it accessible to all residents. However, private schools offer more flexibility in terms of curriculum and extracurricular activities, making them a popular choice among expat families. The island's small size and close-knit community also make it an ideal location for international schools, which offer a more global perspective and diverse student body. The International Baccalaureate (IB) program is also available in some schools, providing students with a comprehensive and internationally recognized education.
