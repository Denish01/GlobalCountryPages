# Moving to Bonaire — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Bonaire, you will need to research and understand the island's visa and residency options, plan carefully, and be prepared for a unique blend of Caribbean and Dutch cultures in a small island setting. 

[SECTION] Why People Move to Bonaire [/SECTION]
Bonaire is a small island territory located in the southern Caribbean, known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs, making it a popular destination for **scuba divers** and **snorkelers**. The island's warm and sunny climate, combined with its laid-back atmosphere, also attracts **retirees** and **digital nomads** looking to escape the stress of city life. Many expats are drawn to Bonaire's unique cultural blend, which reflects its history as a Dutch colony, as well as its stunning natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities.

The expat community in Bonaire is relatively small, but diverse, with people from various countries and backgrounds, including the Netherlands, the United States, and other European nations. The island's **quality of life** is generally high, with a low crime rate, good healthcare, and a range of amenities and services, including shops, restaurants, and entertainment options. However, the island's remote location and limited job opportunities can also present challenges for some expats.

Overall, Bonaire offers a unique and attractive lifestyle for those who value **outdoor activities**, **cultural diversity**, and a relaxed, island atmosphere. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and friendly community, Bonaire is an excellent choice for expats looking to experience the best of the Caribbean.
