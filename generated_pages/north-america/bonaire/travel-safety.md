[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Bonaire is considered one of the safest islands in the Caribbean, with a low crime rate and a strong emphasis on preserving the island's natural beauty and promoting tourism. The island has a small and close-knit community, which contributes to its safe and welcoming atmosphere. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take normal precautions to ensure your safety, such as being aware of your surroundings and keeping valuables secure. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around the island, even at night, and the locals are generally friendly and helpful.

The island's economy is heavily reliant on tourism, and as a result, the government and local authorities take the safety and security of visitors very seriously. The island has a well-organized and effective emergency services system, including a tourist police unit that is dedicated to assisting visitors. Additionally, the island's small size and low population density make it easy to get around and find help if needed. Overall, Bonaire is an excellent choice for travelers looking for a safe and relaxing vacation experience.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and theft can occur in crowded areas, so visitors should keep a close eye on their belongings.
- Scams can occur when renting cars or equipment, so be sure to research and choose reputable companies.
- Be cautious when engaging in water sports, as strong currents and rough seas can be hazardous.
- Watch out for wildlife, such as sea urchins and jellyfish, when swimming or snorkeling.
- Be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
- Credit card skimming can occur, so be sure to monitor your account activity and report any suspicious transactions.
- Visitors should be cautious when using ATMs, and avoid using them in isolated areas.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Bonaire, but it's recommended that visitors be up to date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it.
- **Medical Facilities:** The island has a well-equipped hospital and several medical clinics, but serious medical cases may require evacuation to a larger medical facility.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are readily available, and many medications can be obtained without a prescription.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be sure to research and choose reputable tour operators and rental companies.
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a safe or locker.
- Be aware of your surroundings and watch out for potential hazards, such as wildlife and steep terrain.
- Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen, as the sun can be intense.
- Learn some basic Papiamento phrases, such as "bon bini" (hello) and "danki" (thank you), to show respect for the local culture.
- Be respectful of the island's natural environment and follow any rules or regulations for protecting the marine ecosystem.
- Consider purchasing travel insurance that covers medical evacuation and other emergencies.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Bonaire is generally a very safe destination for solo and women travelers, with a low crime rate and a strong sense of community.
- Women should still take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secure.
- Solo travelers may want to consider joining a guided tour or group activity to meet other travelers and stay connected.
- Be sure to research and choose reputable accommodations and tour operators, and consider staying in well-lit and populated areas.
