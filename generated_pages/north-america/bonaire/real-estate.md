[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]

The urban areas, such as Kralendijk, tend to have higher property prices compared to the rural areas, where **land** and **agricultural properties** are more common. The overall price trajectory is upward, with a steady increase in property values over the past few years.

The urban-rural divide is also evident in the types of properties available, with **luxury homes** and **condominiums** more common in the urban areas, while **farming land** and **rural homes** are more prevalent in the countryside.

The Bonaire government has implemented various initiatives to promote **sustainable development** and **environmental conservation**, which have contributed to the island's growing appeal as a **eco-tourism** destination. This has led to an increase in demand for **eco-friendly properties** and **sustainable developments**, which are expected to drive growth in the property market in the coming years.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners can buy property in Bonaire, but they must **register** with the **Bonaire Chamber of Commerce** and obtain a **tax identification number**.
- There are no **restrictions** on foreign ownership of property, but **non-resident buyers** may be required to obtain a **permit** from the **Bonaire government**.
- **Leasehold** agreements are also available, which can provide a **workaround** for foreigners who are not eligible for a **mortgage**.
- Foreign buyers must also comply with **local building regulations** and **environmental laws**.
- It is recommended that foreign buyers **consult with a local attorney** to ensure compliance with all relevant laws and regulations.
