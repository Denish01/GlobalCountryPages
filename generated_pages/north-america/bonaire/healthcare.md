[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
The healthcare system in Bonaire is a mixed system, with both public and private facilities available. The public healthcare system is funded by the government and provides a range of services, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and prescriptions. Private healthcare facilities, on the other hand, offer more specialized care and often have shorter wait times. The quality of care in Bonaire is generally good, with many healthcare professionals having received training in the Netherlands or other European countries. However, there can be limitations in terms of specialized care, and in some cases, patients may need to be transferred to a hospital in Curaçao or the Netherlands for more complex treatments.

In terms of access to healthcare, there are no significant differences between urban and rural areas, as the island is relatively small and most facilities are located in or near the capital, Kralendijk. However, there may be some variations in the types of services available, with more specialized care often only available in the larger hospitals. Overall, the healthcare system in Bonaire is well-organized and provides good quality care, although there may be some limitations in terms of specialized services.

The healthcare system in Bonaire is also influenced by its relationship with the Netherlands, with many healthcare professionals having received training in the Netherlands and with the island receiving financial support from the Dutch government. This relationship also means that many Dutch healthcare standards and protocols are followed in Bonaire, which can provide an added layer of quality control and assurance.

[SECTION] Health Insurance [/SECTION]
- **Health insurance** is recommended for all visitors and residents, as it can help cover the costs of medical care and provide access to specialized services.
- There are several local insurance options available, including **AZV** (Algemene Ziektekosten Verzekering), which is a mandatory health insurance program for all residents.
- Expats and visitors can also purchase private health insurance, which can provide more comprehensive coverage and access to a wider range of services.
- The cost of health insurance in Bonaire can vary depending on the type of coverage and the provider, but on average, it can range from $500 to $2,000 per year.
- Some employers may also offer health insurance as part of their benefits package, so it's worth checking with your employer to see what options are available.
