# Where to Stay in Bonaire — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Bonaire, the best areas to stay are in and around the capital city of Kralendijk, which offers a range of accommodations and easy access to the island's top attractions.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**Kralendijk** (Kralendijk)
Best For: Luxury, family, business
Vibe: Kralendijk is the bustling capital city of Bonaire, with a charming waterfront area and a variety of shops and restaurants. The city has a lively atmosphere, but it's still small enough to feel relaxed and welcoming.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to main attractions, wide range of dining options, excellent shopping
Cons: Can be noisy, especially in the evenings

**Belnem** (Kralendijk)
Best For: Family, luxury
Vibe: Belnem is a quiet and upscale neighborhood just south of Kralendijk, with beautiful ocean views and a peaceful atmosphere. It's ideal for families and those looking for a relaxing vacation.
Price Range: $200-400/night
Pros: Quiet and secluded, stunning ocean views, close to Kralendijk
Cons: Limited dining options

**Sabana** (Kralendijk)
Best For: Backpacker, budget
Vibe: Sabana is a small and laid-back neighborhood just outside of Kralendijk, with a mix of local homes and budget-friendly accommodations. It's a great option for travelers on a tight budget.
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: Affordable, close to Kralendijk, local atmosphere
Cons: Limited amenities

**Sorobon** (Lac Bay)
Best For: Luxury, relaxation
Vibe: Sorobon is a picturesque beach town on the southeastern coast of Bonaire, known for its stunning white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters. It's a perfect spot for those looking to unwind and enjoy water sports.
Price Range: $250-450/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, tranquil atmosphere, excellent snorkeling and diving
Cons: Remote location, limited dining options

**Hato** (Washington)
Best For: Nature lovers, adventure seekers
Vibe: Hato is a rural area on the northern coast of Bonaire, surrounded by lush nature reserves and scenic landscapes. It's ideal for nature lovers and adventure seekers who want to explore the island's great outdoors.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Stunning natural beauty, opportunities for hiking and birdwatching, peaceful atmosphere
Cons: Remote location, limited amenities

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (December to April)
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices for long-term stays or off-season bookings
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with higher rates during holidays and special events
- Read reviews and check the property's location before making a booking
- Look for packages that include activities or amenities, such as diving or snorkeling trips
- Check the cancellation policies and fees before booking

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night, especially in rural neighborhoods
- Keep valuables secure and be mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas
- Choose accommodations with good lighting and security features, such as gated communities or 24-hour reception
- Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation or rental cars
- Be aware of the island's geography and weather conditions, with potential for strong winds and rough seas
- Stay informed about local events and festivals, which can affect traffic and accommodation availability
