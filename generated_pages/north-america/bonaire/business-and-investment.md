[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
Bonaire's business environment is characterized by a stable economy, a favorable investment climate, and a relatively low level of bureaucracy. The government of Bonaire, in collaboration with the Netherlands, has implemented various policies to attract foreign investment and promote economic growth. The island's strategic location in the southern Caribbean, its stable currency, and its highly developed infrastructure make it an attractive destination for businesses looking to expand into the region. However, the small size of the island and its remote location can also pose some challenges, such as limited access to certain markets and a limited pool of skilled labor.

The government of Bonaire has a positive attitude towards foreign investment, and it offers various incentives to attract businesses to the island. The island's **Free Zone** regime, for example, provides companies with a range of benefits, including exemption from certain taxes, simplified customs procedures, and access to a highly skilled and multilingual workforce. Additionally, the government has established a number of **investment promotion agencies** to provide support and guidance to foreign investors.

Despite the many advantages of doing business in Bonaire, the island's bureaucracy can still be a challenge for some businesses. The process of obtaining permits and licenses, for example, can be time-consuming and may require the assistance of a local lawyer or consultant. Furthermore, the island's **corruption** context is generally considered to be low, but it is still essential for businesses to maintain high standards of integrity and transparency in all their dealings.
