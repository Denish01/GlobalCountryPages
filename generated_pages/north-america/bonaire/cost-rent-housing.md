The cost of rent in Bonaire can range from $800 to $2,000 per month for a one-bedroom apartment, depending on the location and amenities, while housing prices can vary from $2,000 to $5,000 per square meter in the city center.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
The rental market in Bonaire is relatively small, with a limited number of properties available, especially in the city center of Kralendijk. Typical lease terms are for one to two years, with a deposit requirement of one to two months' rent. Tenant rights are protected by law, with the **Bonaire Rental Law** regulating the relationship between landlords and tenants. Compared to its regional neighbors, such as Curacao and Aruba, Bonaire's housing costs are relatively moderate, with a lower cost of living overall.

The housing market in Bonaire is also influenced by the island's **tourism industry**, with many properties being rented out as vacation homes or apartments. This can drive up prices, especially in popular areas like the city center and coastal regions. However, for those looking to rent or buy a property, there are still affordable options available, especially in the suburbs or rural areas. It's worth noting that Bonaire's housing market is also affected by its **relationship with the Netherlands**, with many Dutch citizens and companies investing in the island's real estate.
