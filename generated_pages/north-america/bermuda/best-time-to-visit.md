# Best Time to Visit Bermuda — Month-by-Month Guide

The best time to visit Bermuda is from April to October, with the peak season being June to August, and the budget season being November to March, while avoiding the hurricane-prone months of September and October due to potential storm risks.

[CALLOUT] Best months to visit Bermuda: April to October. Peak season: June to August. Budget season: November to March. Avoid: September and October, due to the possibility of hurricanes. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Seasons Overview [/SECTION]
Bermuda is a **subtropical** island territory located in the North Atlantic Ocean, with a mild climate that is influenced by the **Gulf Stream**. The island experiences a distinct **dry season** from April to October, with low humidity and plenty of sunshine, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities such as **beachgoing**, **snorkeling**, and **golfing**. In contrast, the **wet season**, which runs from November to March, is characterized by higher humidity and more frequent rainfall, although the temperatures remain mild. The seasons have a significant impact on travel, with the peak season being the busiest and most expensive time to visit, while the budget season offers lower prices and smaller crowds.

The climate in Bermuda is generally warm and sunny, with average temperatures ranging from 68°F (20°C) in the winter to 85°F (30°C) in the summer. The island is vulnerable to **hurricanes**, which can occur between June and November, with the peak hurricane season being September and October. Visitors should be aware of the hurricane risk and plan their trip accordingly, considering travel insurance and staying informed about weather conditions.

Bermuda's climate and seasons also affect the types of activities and events that take place on the island. For example, the summer months are ideal for **water sports**, such as **surfing** and **kayaking**, while the winter months are better suited for **hiking** and **biking**. The island's festivals and events calendar is also influenced by the seasons, with many events taking place during the peak season.

[SECTION] Regional Variations [/SECTION]
- The **east coast** of Bermuda is generally cooler and windier than the west coast, making it better suited for **water sports** and **outdoor activities**.
- The **west coast** is warmer and sunnier, making it ideal for **beachgoing** and **sunbathing**.
- The **central parishes** are home to many of Bermuda's **festival and events**, making them a great place to experience the island's culture and entertainment.
- The **northern parishes** are more rural and less developed, offering a glimpse into Bermuda's **natural beauty** and **wildlife**.
- The **southern parishes** are home to many of Bermuda's **historic sites** and **museums**, making them a great place to learn about the island's history and heritage.

[SECTION] Practical Tips [/SECTION]
- Book your accommodations and flights well in advance, especially during the peak season, to avoid high prices and availability issues.
- Consider visiting during the shoulder season, which offers lower prices and smaller crowds, but still plenty of sunshine and warm weather.
- Pack **sunscreen**, **sunglasses**, and **light clothing** for the summer months, and **warm clothing** for the winter months.
- Be aware of the **hurricane season** and plan your trip accordingly, considering travel insurance and staying informed about weather conditions.
- Avoid traveling during the **holiday seasons**, such as Christmas and New Year's, when prices are higher and crowds are larger.
- Take advantage of the **public transportation** system, which includes buses and ferries, to get around the island and experience its **natural beauty** and **culture**.
