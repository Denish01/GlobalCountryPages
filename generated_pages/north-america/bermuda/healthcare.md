# Bermuda Healthcare — Hospitals, Insurance & Medical Guide

Bermuda has a high-quality, mixed healthcare system with a combination of public and private facilities, providing excellent medical care to its residents and visitors, with a strong emphasis on preventative care and access to specialized treatments.

[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
Bermuda's healthcare system is designed to provide comprehensive medical care to its population, with a mix of public and private providers. The **Bermuda Hospital Board** is responsible for overseeing the public healthcare system, which includes the King Edward VII Memorial Hospital, a 280-bed facility that provides a wide range of medical services. Private healthcare providers, including clinics and hospitals, also play a significant role in the healthcare system, offering specialized services and high-quality care. The quality of care in Bermuda is generally high, with access to advanced medical technology and a well-trained healthcare workforce. Urban areas, such as Hamilton, have better access to healthcare services than rural areas, but the island's small size ensures that medical facilities are never far away.

The Bermudian government has implemented various initiatives to improve healthcare outcomes, including **health promotion programs** and **disease prevention initiatives**. These programs aim to reduce the burden of chronic diseases, such as diabetes and heart disease, and promote healthy lifestyles. Additionally, the government has invested in **healthcare infrastructure**, including the development of new medical facilities and the upgrade of existing ones.

Overall, Bermuda's healthcare system is well-regarded, with a strong focus on providing high-quality, patient-centered care. Visitors and residents alike can expect to receive excellent medical attention, with access to a wide range of specialized services and treatments.

[SECTION] Health Insurance [/SECTION]
- **Private health insurance** is recommended for visitors and residents, as it provides access to a wider range of medical services and facilities.
- **Local insurance options** are available, including the **Bermuda Health Insurance Plan**, which provides coverage for hospital stays, doctor visits, and other medical expenses.
- **Expats** should consider purchasing private health insurance that covers them globally, including in Bermuda.
- **Costs** can vary depending on the type of insurance and the level of coverage, but expect to pay around $500-$1,000 per month for a comprehensive plan.
- **Government-sponsored insurance** is available for certain groups, including seniors and low-income individuals.
- **Supplemental insurance** options, such as **dental and vision insurance**, are also available to complement existing health insurance plans.

[SECTION] Pharmacies & Medications [/SECTION]
- **Prescription medications** are widely available at pharmacies across the island, including **Phoenix Store** and **People's Pharmacy**.
- **Common brands** of medications are available, including **over-the-counter medications** and **prescription drugs**.
- **Prescription requirements** vary depending on the type of medication, but a valid prescription from a licensed physician is usually required.
- **Costs** can vary depending on the type of medication and the pharmacy, but expect to pay around $10-$50 for a prescription medication.
- **Availability of medications** is generally good, but some specialized medications may need to be imported from the United States or other countries.

[SECTION] Vaccinations & Health Risks [/SECTION]
- **Recommended vaccinations** for travel to Bermuda include **hepatitis A** and **typhoid**, as well as **influenza** and **pneumococcal** vaccinations for certain groups.
- **Required vaccinations** include **measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR)** and **tetanus, diphtheria, and pertussis (Tdap)**.
- **Endemic diseases** in Bermuda include **dengue fever** and **chikungunya**, which are transmitted by mosquitoes.
- **Water safety** is generally good, but visitors should take precautions to avoid **waterborne illnesses**.
- **Food safety** is also generally good, but visitors should take precautions to avoid **foodborne illnesses**.
- **Other health risks** include **sunburn** and **heat exhaustion**, as well as **injuries from water sports** and other activities.

[SECTION] Medical Tourism [/SECTION]
- **Bermuda** is a popular destination for **medical tourism**, with many visitors traveling to the island for **specialized medical procedures**.
- **Popular procedures** include **cosmetic surgery**, **orthopedic surgery**, and **dental procedures**.
- **Accredited hospitals** include the **King Edward VII Memorial Hospital** and the **Bermuda Medical Center**.
- **Cost savings** can be significant, with prices for medical procedures often lower than in the United States or other developed countries.

[SECTION] Emergency Procedures [/SECTION]
- **In an emergency**, call **911** or visit the **King Edward VII Memorial Hospital** emergency department.
- **Ambulance reliability** is generally good, with **emergency medical services (EMS)** available 24/7.
- **Nearest hospitals** include the **King Edward VII Memorial Hospital** and the **Bermuda Medical Center**.
- **Insurance claims** should be submitted to the insurance provider as soon as possible after receiving medical treatment.
- **Medical records** should be kept up to date and easily accessible in case of an emergency.
