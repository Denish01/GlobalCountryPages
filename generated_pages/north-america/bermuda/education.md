The education system in Bermuda is based on the British model, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and extracurricular activities. The system is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 5 and 11, and is provided by a range of public and private schools. Secondary education is also compulsory, and is provided by a range of public and private schools, including **comprehensive schools** and **grammar schools**. Tertiary education is provided by the **Bermuda College**, which offers a range of academic and vocational programs.

The quality of education in Bermuda is generally high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and extracurricular activities. The system is well-funded, with a high level of investment in schools and education infrastructure. However, there are some challenges facing the system, including a shortage of qualified teachers in certain subjects, and a need for more **vocational training** programs. Public schools in Bermuda are generally well-regarded, but private schools are also popular, particularly among expatriate families.

Overall, the education system in Bermuda provides students with a strong foundation in academics, as well as a range of extracurricular activities and sports. The system is well-structured and well-funded, and provides a range of options for students, including **special needs education** and **gifted and talented programs**.
