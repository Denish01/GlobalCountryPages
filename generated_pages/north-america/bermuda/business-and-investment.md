[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
Bermuda's business environment is characterized by a stable and secure setting, with a highly developed infrastructure and a strong financial system. The government has a positive attitude toward foreign investment and has implemented various policies to attract investors, including the creation of a special economic zone. However, the bureaucracy level can be moderate, with some procedures requiring multiple steps and approvals. Corruption is relatively low in Bermuda, with the country ranking 23rd in the Corruption Perceptions Index. The government has also established various agencies to promote business and investment, including the Bermuda Business Development Agency and the Bermuda Monetary Authority.

The business environment in Bermuda is also influenced by its relationship with the United Kingdom, as a British Overseas Territory. This relationship provides a level of stability and security, as well as access to the UK's market and financial system. However, it also means that Bermuda is subject to certain UK regulations and laws, which can impact the business environment. Overall, Bermuda's business environment is favorable, with a strong focus on financial services, tourism, and international trade.

Bermuda's economy is highly dependent on international trade, with a strong focus on **financial services**, **tourism**, and **insurance**. The country has a highly developed infrastructure, including a modern airport and a well-equipped port. The government has also invested heavily in the development of the country's telecommunications system, making it an attractive location for businesses that require reliable and high-speed internet connectivity.
