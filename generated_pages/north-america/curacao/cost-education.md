# Education Costs in Curacao

The cost of education in Curacao varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools charge tuition fees ranging from $2,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Curacao is based on the Dutch system, with **Papiamento** and **Dutch** being the primary languages of instruction. Public education is generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on **mathematics**, **science**, and **language arts**. Compulsory education lasts for 12 years, from the age of 4 to 16, and is divided into primary and secondary education. Private education is also available, with several international schools offering **English**, **Spanish**, and **Dutch** as languages of instruction.

The quality of education in Curacao is generally considered to be high, with a strong focus on **academic achievement** and **personal development**. International schools in Curacao offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Examinations**. These schools cater to expat families and offer a high-quality education that is recognized globally. However, the cost of attending an international school in Curacao can be quite high, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $10,000 per year.
