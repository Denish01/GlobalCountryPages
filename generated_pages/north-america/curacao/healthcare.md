[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
The healthcare system in Curacao is a mixed model, combining elements of public and private healthcare. The public healthcare system is funded by the government and provides access to medical services for all residents, including foreigners. The private sector offers a range of healthcare services, from basic medical care to specialized treatments, and is often preferred by those who can afford it. The quality of care in Curacao is generally good, with well-trained medical professionals and modern medical facilities. However, there may be some differences in the quality of care between urban and rural areas, with Willemstad, the capital, offering the most comprehensive range of medical services.

In terms of access to healthcare, Curacao has a relatively high standard of living and a well-developed infrastructure, which facilitates access to medical services. However, there may be some challenges in accessing specialized care, particularly in rural areas. The government has implemented various initiatives to improve access to healthcare, including the establishment of community health centers and the introduction of health insurance schemes.

The public healthcare system in Curacao is generally considered to be of a high standard, with a range of medical services available, including emergency care, surgical services, and specialized treatments. However, there may be some waiting times for non-emergency procedures, and the quality of care can vary depending on the facility and the medical professionals involved.

[SECTION] Health Insurance [/SECTION]
- **Health insurance** is recommended for all residents and visitors to Curacao, as medical costs can be high.
- There are several local insurance options available, including the **SVB** (Social Insurance Bank) and **AZV** (General Insurance for Curacao).
- **Expats** and foreigners may also be able to access health insurance through their employers or private insurance companies.
- The cost of health insurance in Curacao can vary depending on the provider and the level of coverage, but it is generally considered to be relatively affordable.
- Some health insurance plans may also cover **dental and vision care**, as well as **prescription medications**.

[SECTION] Pharmacies & Medications [/SECTION]
- **Pharmacies** are widespread in Curacao, with many locations throughout Willemstad and other major towns.
- A **prescription** is generally required to purchase medications, although some over-the-counter medications may be available without a prescription.
- **Common brand names** are widely available, including medications from the US and Europe.
- The cost of **prescription medications** can vary depending on the type and brand, but it is generally considered to be relatively affordable.
- Some pharmacies may also offer **delivery services**, making it easy to get the medications you need.

[SECTION] Vaccinations & Health Risks [/SECTION]
- **Hepatitis A** and **typhoid** vaccinations are recommended for all travelers to Curacao.
- **Rabies** vaccination is also recommended for those who will be spending time outdoors or working with animals.
- **Dengue fever** and **chikungunya** are present in Curacao, and **insect repellent** and other precautions are recommended to prevent transmission.
- **Water safety** is generally good, with modern water treatment facilities and safe drinking water available throughout the island.
- **Food safety** is also generally good, with a range of restaurants and food establishments available, although it is always a good idea to take precautions when eating outside of major restaurants and hotels.

[SECTION] Medical Tourism [/SECTION]
- **Medical tourism** is a growing industry in Curacao, with several hospitals and clinics offering specialized treatments and procedures.
- **Orthopedic and trauma care** are popular specialties, with several hospitals and clinics offering high-quality care.
- **Dental care** is also a popular option, with several clinics and hospitals offering a range of dental procedures.
- The cost of medical procedures in Curacao can be significantly lower than in other countries, making it an attractive option for those looking for affordable medical care.

[SECTION] Emergency Procedures [/SECTION]
- In the event of an **emergency**, call the emergency number, **912**, for assistance.
- **Ambulance services** are available, although they may not always be reliable, and it is a good idea to have a **back-up plan** in place.
- The **nearest hospital** will depend on your location, but St. Elisabeth Hospital and SEHOS Orthopedic and Trauma Hospital are both well-equipped to handle emergency situations.
- If you have **health insurance**, be sure to contact your provider as soon as possible to report the incident and initiate the claims process.
