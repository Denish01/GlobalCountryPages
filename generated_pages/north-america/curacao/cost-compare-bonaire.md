[SECTION] Overview: Curacao vs Bonaire [/SECTION]
Curacao and Bonaire are two islands in the southern Caribbean, part of the Dutch Caribbean territories. However, Curacao has a slightly larger population and a more developed economy, which contributes to its lower cost of living. The cost of living in Curacao is around 10-15% lower than in Bonaire, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable Caribbean lifestyle.

The economic context of both islands is characterized by a strong service sector, with tourism being the main driver of the economy. However, Curacao has a more diversified economy, with a larger financial sector and a stronger presence of international companies. This diversification contributes to a more stable economy and a lower cost of living. Additionally, Curacao has a more developed infrastructure, with a larger airport and a more extensive public transportation system, which makes it easier to get around the island and access basic services.
