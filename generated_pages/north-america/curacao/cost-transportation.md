[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Curacao is a small island with a well-developed transportation system, making it easy to get around without a car. The main transportation modes on the island are buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent bus services connecting major towns and cities. Most people get around Curacao using buses or taxis, with many locals also owning cars due to the relatively low cost of fuel and parking.

The island's compact size and good road network make it easy to navigate, with many popular tourist destinations within a short drive or bus ride from the capital, Willemstad. The **Papiamento** and Dutch-speaking population is generally friendly and helpful, making it easy for visitors to get around and find their way. With a GDP per capita PPP of $32,693, Curacao has a relatively high standard of living, which is reflected in its modern and efficient transportation system.

- Consider renting a car for a day or two to explore the island, but be aware that parking spaces can be limited in Willemstad.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing services to ensure your safety, especially at night or when traveling alone.
