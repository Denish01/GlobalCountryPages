Education System Overview

The education system in Curacao is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary levels, with a strong focus on primary education as the foundation for future academic success. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 12, and is provided by both public and private schools. Secondary education is also compulsory, and typically lasts for six years, with students having the option to pursue pre-university or vocational tracks. The quality of education in Curacao is generally considered to be high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and personal development. Public schools are free and open to all, while private schools offer a more specialized and often bilingual education.

The education system in Curacao is overseen by the Ministry of Education, Science, Culture and Sports, which is responsible for setting educational policies and standards. The ministry works closely with schools and teachers to ensure that students receive a high-quality education that prepares them for success in an increasingly globalized and competitive world. In recent years, there has been a growing trend towards internationalization and innovation in education, with many schools and universities offering exchange programs and online courses to students.

Overall, the education system in Curacao provides students with a solid foundation for future academic and professional success, and offers a unique and enriching educational experience that combines the best of Dutch and local traditions.

International Schools

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Curacao, foreign students must obtain a student visa, which can be applied for through the Royal Netherlands Embassy or Consulate in their home country.
  • Foreign students must also demonstrate proficiency in the Dutch language, or in English if they plan to study at an international school.
  • Scholarships are available to foreign students, including the Netherlands Fellowship Program and the Erasmus Mundus Scholarship.
  • The cost of living in Curacao is relatively high, with rent, food, and transportation costs similar to those in the Netherlands.
  • Foreign students can also take advantage of internship and work-study opportunities, which can help them gain practical experience and build their professional network.
  • Additionally, foreign students can participate in cultural exchange programs, which can help them immerse themselves in the local culture and develop their language skills.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • One of the major strengths of the education system in Curacao is its bilingual nature, with students having the opportunity to learn in both Papiamento and Dutch.
  • However, the education system also faces challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of resources in some schools.
  • In recent years, there has been a growing trend towards innovation and technology in education, with many schools and universities investing in digital infrastructure and online learning platforms.
  • The education system in Curacao also places a strong emphasis on vocational training and practical skills, with many students pursuing technical and vocational tracks.
  • Additionally, the education system in Curacao is accredited by the Netherlands and recognized internationally, which can provide students with a competitive advantage in the global job market.
