When visiting Curacao, the best areas to stay are in and around Willemstad, the capital city, where you can find a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences.

Best Areas to Stay

For each of the top areas, consider the following:

Punda (Willemstad)

Best For: Luxury, business, and families

Vibe: Punda is a historic and vibrant neighborhood with a mix of colonial architecture, upscale shops, and restaurants, offering a lively and sophisticated atmosphere. The area is also known for its picturesque harbor and waterfront promenade.

Price Range: $150-300/night

Pros: Convenient location, easy access to amenities, and a wide range of dining options. Additionally, Punda is within walking distance to many of Willemstad's main attractions, including the Queen Emma Bridge and the Fort Amsterdam.

Cons: Can be noisy, especially at night, and parking can be a challenge.

Otrobanda (Willemstad)

Best For: Backpackers, budget travelers, and those looking for a local experience

Vibe: Otrobanda is a charming and laid-back neighborhood with a mix of colonial and modern architecture, offering a more relaxed and authentic atmosphere. The area is also home to many local shops, restaurants, and markets.

Price Range: $80-150/night

Pros: Affordable prices, local culture, and a short walk to the city center. Otrobanda is also known for its street art and vibrant nightlife.

Cons: Some areas can be run-down, and amenities may be limited.

Booking Tips

  • Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to April), to ensure availability and the best rates.
  • Compare prices across multiple booking platforms, including Expedia, Booking.com, and Airbnb.
  • Consider negotiating the price, especially for longer stays or during the off-season.
  • Look for packages and special deals, including all-inclusive options and discounts for booking multiple nights.
  • Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before making a booking.
  • Be aware of any additional fees, such as taxes, cleaning fees, or resort fees.
  • Use travel rewards credit cards or loyalty programs to earn points or discounts.

Safety & Location Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas.
  • Keep valuables secure, and consider using a hotel safe or a secure locker.
  • Choose accommodations in safe and reputable areas, such as Punda or Otrobanda.
  • Research the location and proximity to amenities, including beaches, restaurants, and public transportation.
  • Consider renting a car or using taxis to get around, especially if you plan to explore the island.
  • Be mindful of the weather, especially during hurricane season (June to November), and stay informed about local conditions.
