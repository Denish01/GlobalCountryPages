[SECTION] Overview: Anguilla vs Sint Maarten [/SECTION]
Anguilla and Sint Maarten are two neighboring islands in the Caribbean, with distinct economic contexts. Anguilla is a British overseas territory with a high standard of living, while Sint Maarten is a constituent country of the Kingdom of the Netherlands with a more diverse economy. The cost of living in Anguilla is generally higher, driven by its reliance on imports and a smaller population. In contrast, Sint Maarten has a larger population and a more developed economy, which contributes to its lower cost of living.

The difference in cost of living between the two islands can be attributed to various factors, including the size of the economy, the cost of imports, and the level of economic development. Anguilla's smaller economy and limited resources make it more expensive to live there, while Sint Maarten's larger economy and more developed infrastructure make it more affordable. Overall, Sint Maarten is the more affordable option, with a cost of living that is around 15-20% lower than Anguilla.
