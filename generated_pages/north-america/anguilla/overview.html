What Is Anguilla?

Anguilla is a British overseas territory located in the eastern Caribbean Sea, situated in the northern part of the Leeward Islands. It is a small island with a total area of approximately 35 square miles, making it one of the smallest territories in the world. Anguilla is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs, making it a popular destination for tourists and snorkeling enthusiasts. The island's unique blend of British and African influences is reflected in its culture, architecture, and cuisine.

Geographically, Anguilla is positioned about 150 miles east of Puerto Rico and 200 miles north of Saint Kitts and Nevis. The island is separated from the neighboring island of Saint Martin by the Anguilla Channel. Anguilla's sovereignty status as a British overseas territory means that it is under the jurisdiction of the United Kingdom, with the British monarch serving as the head of state. The island has a high degree of autonomy, with its own local government and laws.

Despite its small size, Anguilla has a distinct identity and a strong sense of community. The island's economy is primarily driven by tourism, with a focus on high-end resorts and eco-tourism. Anguilla is also known for its vibrant arts scene, with a number of local artists and musicians contributing to the island's unique cultural heritage.

Key History

  1. 1650 - Anguilla is first colonized by English settlers from Saint Kitts.
  2. 1666 - The island is invaded by French forces, but is later recaptured by the British.
  3. 1825 - Anguilla becomes part of a British colony that includes Saint Kitts and Nevis.
  4. 1967 - Anguilla declares its independence from Saint Kitts and Nevis, but this is not recognized by the British government.
  5. 1971 - Anguilla becomes a separate British overseas territory, with its own local government and laws.
  6. 1980 - Anguilla adopts a new constitution, which provides for greater autonomy and self-governance.
  7. 2017 - Anguilla is hit by Hurricane Irma, which causes significant damage to the island's infrastructure and economy.

What Makes Anguilla Unique

  • Anguilla is home to a number of unique and endangered species, including the Anguillian bank anole and the green turtle.
  • The island has a vibrant arts scene, with a number of local artists and musicians contributing to the island's cultural heritage.
  • Anguilla is known for its pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters, making it a popular destination for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts.
  • The island has a unique blend of British and African influences, reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and cultural traditions.
  • Anguilla is a popular destination for offshore banking and financial services, due to its stable and secure financial system.

Quick Summary

Anguilla is a small but unique British overseas territory in the eastern Caribbean, known for its pristine beaches, vibrant arts scene, and distinct cultural heritage, with a strong sense of community and a growing economy driven by tourism and financial services.
