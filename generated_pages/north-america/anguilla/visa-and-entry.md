# Anguilla Visa & Entry Requirements

You will likely need a visa or meet specific requirements to enter Anguilla, depending on your nationality and the purpose of your visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Anguilla before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Anguilla has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others require a visa on arrival or in advance. As a British overseas territory, Anguilla's visa policy is closely aligned with that of the United Kingdom. Citizens of over 50 countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, and Canada, can enter Anguilla visa-free for tourism or business purposes. However, the duration of stay and requirements for entry vary depending on nationality and purpose of visit.

Anguilla's visa policy is designed to balance the need to control immigration with the need to facilitate tourism and economic development. The territory has a relatively small population and a limited workforce, so it is selective about the types of visitors it allows. Visitors who require a visa must meet specific requirements, such as having a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Anguilla visa-free for up to 90 days, provided they have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. Extensions are possible, but visitors must apply to the immigration department before their initial stay expires. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They must provide an invitation letter from a local company, proof of business activities, and meet specific requirements. **Students:** Students must have a valid visa and provide proof of enrollment in a local educational institution. The duration of stay is typically limited to the duration of the course. **Workers:** Workers require a work permit, which must be sponsored by a local employer. The process can be complex and time-consuming, and applicants must meet specific requirements, such as having a valid passport and relevant work experience.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry point to Anguilla is the Clayton J. Lloyd International Airport, which receives flights from major carriers. Visitors can also arrive by boat at the Road Bay harbor. Upon arrival, visitors must present their travel documents and complete an immigration form. The immigration process is typically straightforward, but visitors should be prepared to answer questions about their purpose of visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements. Visitors may also be required to provide biometric data, such as fingerprints, as part of the immigration process.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not having proof of onward travel, such as a return ticket or a reservation for onward travel
- Not having sufficient funds to support yourself during your stay
- Not completing the immigration form correctly or failing to provide required documents
- Not meeting the specific requirements for your type of visa or stay
- Not registering with the local immigration department if you are staying for an extended period

By understanding the visa requirements and entry procedures for Anguilla, visitors can ensure a smooth and hassle-free trip to this beautiful Caribbean territory.
