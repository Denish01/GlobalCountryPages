# Moving to Anguilla — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Anguilla, you will need to research and understand the island's visa and residency options, relocate your belongings, and prepare for the unique challenges and opportunities that this small Caribbean territory presents. 

[SECTION] Why People Move to Anguilla [/SECTION]
Anguilla is a popular destination for expats and tourists alike, drawn by its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and laid-back atmosphere. The island's small population and relatively low cost of living make it an attractive option for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Many expats are also drawn to Anguilla's **tax-neutral** status, which makes it an attractive option for **digital nomads** and **remote workers**. The island has a growing expat community, with many residents from the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom.

The quality of life in Anguilla is generally high, with a strong focus on **community** and **outdoor activities**. The island has a range of amenities, including restaurants, bars, and shops, as well as a number of **beaches** and **parks**. However, the island's small size and limited resources can also present challenges, particularly when it comes to ** healthcare** and **education**. Despite these challenges, many expats find that the unique lifestyle and stunning natural beauty of Anguilla make it an ideal place to call home.

In terms of demographics, the expat community in Anguilla is relatively small and diverse, with a mix of **retirees**, **families**, and **young professionals**. The island has a strong sense of community, with many expats actively involved in local **charities** and **volunteer organizations**. Overall, Anguilla offers a unique and rewarding lifestyle for those who are looking for a relaxed and **tropical** environment.

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- **Opening a bank account** in Anguilla can be a straightforward process, with many banks offering a range of services to expats.
- **Money transfers** can be made through a number of services, including **Western Union** and **MoneyGram**.
- The **Eastern Caribbean Dollar (XCD)** is the local currency, although **US dollars** are widely accepted.
- **Credit cards** are widely accepted in Anguilla, with many businesses accepting **Visa** and **Mastercard**.
- **Taxes** in Anguilla are relatively low, with no **income tax** or **capital gains tax**.

- **Bureaucracy** can be slow and frustrating, particularly when it comes to dealing with government agencies.
- **Language barriers** are not typically a problem in Anguilla, as **English** is the official language.
- **Hurricanes** can be a threat to the island, and expats should be prepared for **emergency situations**.
- **Limited job opportunities** can make it difficult for some expats to find work, particularly in certain industries.
- **High cost of living** can be a challenge for some expats, particularly when it comes to **housing** and **food**.
