The cost of education in Anguilla varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $2,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Anguilla is based on the British model, with English as the primary language of instruction. Public education is free and compulsory for children between the ages of 5 and 17, and is generally considered to be of good quality. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a more exclusive and often higher-quality education, but at a significantly higher cost. There are also several international schools in Anguilla that cater to the children of expatriates and offer a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the Cambridge International Examinations.

The education system in Anguilla is divided into three main stages: primary education, which lasts for six years, secondary education, which lasts for five years, and post-secondary education, which includes vocational training and university education. The academic year in Anguilla typically runs from September to June, with a break in December and another in April. The language of instruction is English, and the curriculum is designed to prepare students for the Caribbean Examinations Council (CXC) exams, which are taken at the end of secondary school.
