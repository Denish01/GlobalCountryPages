[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet quality in Anguilla is good, with an average internet speed of 10 Mbps download, which is suitable for general browsing, email, and social media. However, the urban-rural divide is noticeable, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in the capital, The Valley, and other urban areas. In rural areas, the internet speed can be slower, and the connection may be less reliable. Despite this, the overall internet infrastructure in Anguilla is relatively well-developed, with a good mobile network coverage and widespread WiFi availability.

The reliability of the internet in Anguilla is also good, with minimal downtime and outages. This makes it a suitable destination for remote workers and digital nomads who require a reliable internet connection to work. However, it's always a good idea to have a backup plan, such as a portable WiFi hotspot or a prepaid SIM card from a different carrier, in case of an unexpected outage.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Anguilla, tourists can visit one of the main carriers, such as Digicel, Flow, or AP&T, and purchase a prepaid SIM card. The cost of a tourist SIM card is approximately 20 USD, and it usually comes with a set amount of data, voice minutes, and text messages. To purchase a SIM card, tourists will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, such as a hotel reservation or a rental agreement. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable carrier, as they offer better coverage and more reliable connections.

The main carriers in Anguilla offer a range of prepaid plans, including tourist-friendly plans that include data, voice minutes, and text messages. These plans can be purchased at the carrier's store or at authorized resellers, such as convenience stores or pharmacies.
