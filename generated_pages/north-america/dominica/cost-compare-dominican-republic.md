[SECTION] Overview: Dominica vs Dominican Republic [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Dominica and the Dominican Republic differs significantly, despite both being located in the Caribbean. Dominica, a smaller island nation with a population of around 67,000, has a more limited economy and a higher cost of living. In contrast, the Dominican Republic, with a population of over 10 million, has a more developed economy and a lower cost of living. The main reason for the difference in cost of living is the size and diversity of the economies, with the Dominican Republic having a more diverse range of industries and a larger market.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Dominica's economy is heavily reliant on tourism and agriculture, which can lead to fluctuations in prices and availability of goods. The Dominican Republic, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on manufacturing, tourism, and remittances from abroad. This diversification helps to keep prices stable and affordable. Overall, the Dominican Republic is generally cheaper than Dominica, with a cost of living that is around 20-30% lower.
