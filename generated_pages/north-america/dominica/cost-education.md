# Education Costs in Dominica

The cost of education in Dominica varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Dominica is modeled after the British system, with English as the primary language of instruction. The public education system is generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on core subjects such as math, science, and language arts. However, private schools and international schools may offer a more comprehensive and diverse range of subjects, as well as smaller class sizes and more individualized attention. Compulsory education in Dominica lasts for 12 years, from primary school to secondary school, and students typically attend school from the age of 5 to 17.

The quality of education in Dominica is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on core subjects and a range of extracurricular activities. However, some schools may have limited resources and facilities, particularly in rural areas. International schools are available in Dominica, offering a range of curricula such as the International Baccalaureate and the Cambridge International Examinations. These schools are popular with expatriate families and offer a high-quality education with a global perspective.
