[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Dominica has improved significantly over the years, with **mobile network coverage** spanning across the island, although there may be some occasional outages and slower speeds in rural areas. The **urban vs rural divide** is noticeable, with faster internet speeds and more reliable connections available in urban centers like Roseau, while rural areas may experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Overall, the internet in Dominica is relatively **reliable**, with most users experiencing few issues with their connections.

However, it's worth noting that **natural disasters** such as hurricanes can impact internet connectivity, and users may experience outages or slower speeds during these events. Additionally, **internet speeds** can be slower compared to other countries, with average download speeds of around 10 Mbps. Despite these limitations, the internet in Dominica is generally suitable for most users, including tourists and remote workers who require a reliable connection to stay productive.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a **SIM card** in Dominica, tourists can visit the offices of the main carriers, such as Digicel, Flow, or Orange, and purchase a prepaid SIM card. The process is relatively straightforward, and users will need to provide a valid passport and fill out a registration form. **Recommended carriers** include Digicel and Flow, which offer a range of **data plans** and competitive pricing. It's also possible to purchase SIM cards at some retail stores and pharmacies, although the selection may be limited.
