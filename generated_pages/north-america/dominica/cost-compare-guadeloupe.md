[SECTION] Overview: Dominica vs Guadeloupe [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Dominica and Guadeloupe varies significantly, with Dominica offering a more affordable lifestyle. The economic context of both countries plays a significant role in determining the cost of living, with Dominica's economy driven by agriculture, tourism, and manufacturing, while Guadeloupe's economy is more diversified, with a strong focus on services and industry. As a result, Guadeloupe tends to have higher prices for goods and services, particularly in the areas of housing, food, and transportation.

In general, Dominica is a more budget-friendly option, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. However, Guadeloupe offers a more developed infrastructure, with better amenities and services, which may be attractive to some expats and tourists. The cost difference between the two countries can be significant, with Dominica offering savings of up to 30% on certain expenses. For example, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center of Roseau, Dominica, can cost around $400-500 per month, while a similar apartment in Pointe-a-Pitre, Guadeloupe, can cost upwards of $700-800 per month.
