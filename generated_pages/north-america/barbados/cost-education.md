# Education Costs in Barbados

The cost of education in Barbados varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $2,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Barbados is highly regarded, with a strong emphasis on primary and secondary education. Public schools in Barbados are generally of high quality, with a focus on core subjects such as English, mathematics, and science. The language of instruction is **English**, and education is compulsory for 11 years, from age 5 to 16. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a more exclusive and often specialized education, with some schools focusing on specific areas such as **arts** or **sports**. International schools are also available, catering to the expat community and offering a range of curricula, including **IB** and **Cambridge**.

The quality of education in Barbados is generally high, with a strong focus on academic achievement and **extracurricular activities**. Public schools are well-funded and well-equipped, with modern facilities and qualified teachers. Private schools, while more expensive, offer a more personalized education and often have smaller class sizes. International schools, meanwhile, provide a global perspective and a range of curricula, making them a popular choice for expat families. Overall, the education system in Barbados is well-regarded and provides students with a solid foundation for future success.
