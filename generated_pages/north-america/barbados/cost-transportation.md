[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Barbados has a relatively well-developed public transportation system, with buses and minibuses being the most popular mode of transportation for both locals and tourists. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent bus services connecting major towns and cities. Most people get around the island using public transportation or taxis, with some opting for car rentals or ride-hailing services. The island's small size and relatively flat terrain make it easy to navigate, with many popular destinations within walking distance or a short drive.

The main transportation modes in Barbados include public buses, minibuses, and taxis, with private car rentals and ride-hailing services also available. The public transportation system is managed by the Barbados Transport Board, which operates an extensive network of bus routes covering most areas of the island. Taxis are readily available, with many operating on a fixed-rate basis, while ride-hailing services like Uber have become increasingly popular in recent years.
