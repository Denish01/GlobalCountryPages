# Rent & Housing Prices in Barbados

Rent in Barbados can range from $800 to $2,000 per month for a one-bedroom apartment, depending on the location and amenities, with housing prices varying from $2,500 to $6,000 per square meter in the city center.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
The rental market in Barbados is relatively stable, with a mix of short-term and long-term lease options available. Typical lease terms range from 6 to 12 months, with a security deposit of one to two months' rent required. Tenant rights are protected by the Rent Restriction Act, which regulates rent increases and eviction procedures. Compared to its regional neighbors, such as Saint Lucia and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Barbados's housing costs are relatively high, reflecting its strong economy and high standard of living. However, the island's small size and limited housing stock can lead to a competitive rental market, especially in popular areas like the city center.

The housing market in Barbados is also influenced by the tourism industry, with many properties being used as vacation rentals or holiday homes. This can drive up prices and reduce the availability of long-term rentals, especially in coastal areas. However, the government has implemented measures to encourage the development of affordable housing and to regulate the short-term rental market. Overall, Barbados's housing market offers a range of options for renters and buyers, from modern apartments to traditional homes and villas, but prices can be steep, especially in the city center.
