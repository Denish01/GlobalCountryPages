[SECTION] Overview: Barbados vs Jamaica [/SECTION]
Barbados and Jamaica are two popular islands in the Caribbean, known for their beautiful beaches, rich culture, and vibrant atmosphere. However, when it comes to cost of living, there are significant differences between the two countries. Barbados has a higher GDP per capita and a more developed economy, which translates to higher prices for goods and services. Jamaica, on the other hand, has a lower GDP per capita and a more affordable cost of living. The overall cost of living in Barbados is around 20-30% higher than in Jamaica, making Jamaica a more attractive option for budget-conscious travelers and expats.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Barbados has a more diverse economy, with a strong focus on tourism, finance, and international business, which drives up prices for goods and services. Jamaica, on the other hand, has a more agriculture-based economy, which keeps prices lower. Additionally, Jamaica has a larger population and a more competitive market, which also helps to keep prices in check. Overall, Jamaica is generally cheaper than Barbados, with a difference of around 20-30% in overall cost of living.
