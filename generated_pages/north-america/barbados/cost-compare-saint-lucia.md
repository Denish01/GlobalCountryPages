[SECTION] Overview: Barbados vs Saint Lucia [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Barbados and Saint Lucia can vary significantly, with Barbados being the more expensive of the two. This is due in part to Barbados' stronger economy and higher standard of living, which drives up prices for goods and services. The GDP per capita in Barbados is $18,794, compared to $13,578 in Saint Lucia, indicating a higher level of economic activity and prosperity in Barbados. As a result, expats and tourists can expect to pay more for accommodation, food, and transportation in Barbados.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in their cost of living. Barbados has a more developed tourism industry, which drives up prices for accommodations and services. Saint Lucia, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy with a strong focus on agriculture and manufacturing, which helps to keep prices lower. Overall, Saint Lucia is the more affordable option for those looking to live or vacation in the Caribbean, with prices that are around 15% lower than in Barbados.
