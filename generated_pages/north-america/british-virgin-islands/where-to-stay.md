# Where to Stay in British Virgin Islands — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

The British Virgin Islands offer a range of accommodations to suit different types of travelers, with the best areas to stay including Road Town, Tortola, Virgin Gorda, Jost Van Dyke, Anegada, Peter Island, and Salt Island. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, the character and advantages vary:
**Road Town** (Tortola)
Best For: business / luxury
Vibe: Road Town is the capital and commercial center of the British Virgin Islands, offering a mix of modern amenities and historic charm, with a vibrant atmosphere and easy access to shopping and dining. The town has a bustling harbor and a variety of cultural attractions.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: convenient location, wide range of amenities, easy access to transportation
Cons: can be crowded and noisy

**Tortola** (Tortola)
Best For: family / backpacker
Vibe: Tortola is the largest island in the British Virgin Islands, offering a relaxed and natural atmosphere, with beautiful beaches and outdoor activities. The island has a strong sense of community and a variety of cultural events.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, affordable options
Cons: limited nightlife

**Virgin Gorda** (Virgin Gorda)
Best For: luxury / family
Vibe: Virgin Gorda is a tranquil and secluded island, known for its stunning natural beauty, with pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters. The island has a relaxed and sophisticated atmosphere, with a range of high-end amenities.
Price Range: $300-$600/night
Pros: beautiful beaches, secluded atmosphere, high-end amenities
Cons: limited dining options

**Jost Van Dyke** (Jost Van Dyke)
Best For: backpacker / luxury
Vibe: Jost Van Dyke is a small and vibrant island, known for its beautiful beaches and lively atmosphere, with a range of bars and restaurants. The island has a strong sense of community and a variety of cultural events.
Price Range: $150-$400/night
Pros: beautiful beaches, lively atmosphere, affordable options
Cons: limited amenities

**Anegada** (Anegada)
Best For: family / nature lover
Vibe: Anegada is a peaceful and secluded island, known for its stunning natural beauty, with pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters. The island has a relaxed and natural atmosphere, with a range of outdoor activities.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, secluded atmosphere
Cons: limited amenities

**Peter Island** (Peter Island)
Best For: luxury / honeymooner
Vibe: Peter Island is a private and secluded island, known for its stunning natural beauty, with pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters. The island has a relaxed and sophisticated atmosphere, with a range of high-end amenities.
Price Range: $500-$1,000/night
Pros: beautiful beaches, secluded atmosphere, high-end amenities
Cons: limited dining options

**Salt Island** (Salt Island)
Best For: diver / nature lover
Vibe: Salt Island is a small and secluded island, known for its stunning natural beauty, with pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters. The island has a relaxed and natural atmosphere, with a range of outdoor activities.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, secluded atmosphere
Cons: limited amenities
