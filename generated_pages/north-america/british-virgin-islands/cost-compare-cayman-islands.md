[SECTION] Overview: British Virgin Islands vs Cayman Islands [/SECTION]
The British Virgin Islands and the Cayman Islands are both popular destinations in the Caribbean, known for their beautiful beaches and high standard of living. However, when it comes to cost of living, there are some significant differences between the two islands. The British Virgin Islands has a slightly lower GDP per capita and a higher inflation rate compared to the Cayman Islands, which can affect the overall cost of living. In general, the Cayman Islands is considered to be around 10-20% cheaper than the British Virgin Islands, making it a more attractive option for expats and tourists looking for a more affordable Caribbean experience.

The economic context of the two islands also plays a role in their cost of living. The British Virgin Islands has a strong financial sector, with a focus on offshore banking and tourism, while the Cayman Islands has a more diversified economy with a strong focus on finance, tourism, and healthcare. This diversification can help to keep costs lower in the Cayman Islands, as there is more competition and a wider range of services available. Overall, while both islands offer a high standard of living, the Cayman Islands is generally the more affordable option.
