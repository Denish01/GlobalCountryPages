[SECTION] Overview: British Virgin Islands vs Anguilla [/SECTION]
The British Virgin Islands and Anguilla are two Caribbean territories with distinct economic profiles. The British Virgin Islands has a more developed financial sector, with a strong focus on offshore banking and tourism, which contributes to its higher cost of living. In contrast, Anguilla has a smaller economy, with a greater emphasis on tourism and fishing. As a result, Anguilla tends to be more affordable, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. Overall, Anguilla is around 15-20% cheaper than the British Virgin Islands, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a more affordable Caribbean lifestyle.

The economic context of the two territories also plays a role in their cost of living. The British Virgin Islands has a more diversified economy, with a strong service sector and a high standard of living. This has driven up prices for housing, food, and other essentials, making it one of the more expensive territories in the Caribbean. Anguilla, on the other hand, has a more limited economy, with a greater reliance on tourism and external aid. This has kept prices lower, making it a more affordable option for those looking to live in the Caribbean. However, it's worth noting that Anguilla's smaller economy can also limit job opportunities and career advancement.
