# Education Costs in British Virgin Islands

The cost of education in British Virgin Islands varies depending on the type of institution and the level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in British Virgin Islands is based on the British model, with English as the primary language of instruction. The system consists of primary, secondary, and tertiary levels, with 12 years of compulsory education. Public schools are generally free or subsidized, but the quality can vary, with some schools facing challenges in terms of resources and infrastructure. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a higher quality of education, but at a significant cost. International schools are also available, offering a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the British GCSE.

The quality of education in British Virgin Islands is generally good, with a literacy rate of around 95%. However, there are some challenges, particularly in terms of access to education for disadvantaged groups. The government has implemented various initiatives to improve the education system, including the introduction of new curriculum and the provision of training for teachers. Despite these efforts, there is still a need for improvement, particularly in terms of resources and infrastructure. For expats, international schools are a popular option, offering a range of curricula and a high quality of education.
