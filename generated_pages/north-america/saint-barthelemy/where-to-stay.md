# Where to Stay in Saint Barthelemy — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Saint Barthelemy, consider the areas of Gustavia, Saint-Jean, and Colombier, which offer a range of accommodation options for different types of travelers, from luxury to budget-friendly.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Saint Barthelemy, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Gustavia** (Gustavia)
Best For: Luxury travelers
Vibe: Gustavia is the capital and commercial center of Saint Barthelemy, offering a vibrant and upscale atmosphere with a mix of French colonial architecture and modern amenities. The area is known for its high-end boutiques, restaurants, and yachting facilities.
Price Range: $500-$1,500/night
Pros: Easy access to shopping and dining, beautiful harbor views, and a wide range of amenities.
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and parking can be challenging.

**Saint-Jean** (Saint-Jean)
Best For: Families and couples
Vibe: Saint-Jean is a popular area for families and couples, with a more relaxed atmosphere and a beautiful beach. The area offers a range of accommodation options, from hotels to vacation rentals.
Price Range: $300-$800/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, plenty of restaurants and shops, and a range of accommodation options.
Cons: Can be quite crowded during peak season.

**Colombier** (Colombier)
Best For: Nature lovers and those seeking seclusion
Vibe: Colombier is a peaceful and secluded area, surrounded by lush green hills and offering stunning views of the ocean. The area is ideal for nature lovers and those seeking a more relaxed atmosphere.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Peaceful and secluded, stunning views, and plenty of opportunities for hiking and nature walks.
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and can be quite isolated.

**Pointe Milou** (Pointe Milou)
Best For: Luxury travelers and couples
Vibe: Pointe Milou is an upscale area, known for its stunning views of the ocean and its luxurious amenities. The area offers a range of high-end hotels and vacation rentals.
Price Range: $400-$1,200/night
Pros: Stunning views, luxurious amenities, and a range of high-end accommodation options.
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and limited access to shopping and dining.

**Lorient** (Lorient)
Best For: Budget-conscious travelers and surfers
Vibe: Lorient is a more laid-back area, popular with surfers and budget-conscious travelers. The area offers a range of affordable accommodation options, including guesthouses and vacation rentals.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, plenty of surf spots, and a relaxed atmosphere.
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and can be quite crowded during peak season.

**Flamands** (Flamands)
Best For: Families and couples
Vibe: Flamands is a popular area for families and couples, with a beautiful beach and a range of accommodation options. The area offers a more relaxed atmosphere and plenty of opportunities for water sports.
Price Range: $250-$600/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, plenty of restaurants and shops, and a range of accommodation options.
Cons: Can be quite crowded during peak season, and limited access to shopping and dining.
