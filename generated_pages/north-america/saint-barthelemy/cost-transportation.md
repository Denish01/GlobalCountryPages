[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
The primary mode of transportation in Saint Barthelemy is by car, with many residents and visitors opting to rent vehicles to get around the island. The quality of public transit is limited, with no comprehensive bus system in place. However, there are some **minibus** services and **taxi** options available, which can be a convenient way to travel short distances. Most people get around the island by driving, and the **road network** is generally well-maintained, making it easy to navigate.

The island's small size and limited public transportation options mean that many visitors choose to walk or bike around the capital, **Gustavia**, and other towns. This can be a great way to explore the local area and take in the sights and sounds of the island. However, for longer journeys, a car or taxi is often necessary. The **French** influence on the island is evident in the road signs and driving customs, which are similar to those in France.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transport, consider renting a car for a week or more, as daily rates can be high.
- Use **cash** to pay for taxis and minibuses, as not all drivers accept credit cards.
- Be aware of **road conditions**, especially during the rainy season, and drive carefully.
- Negotiate **taxi fares** before you start your journey, especially for longer trips.
- Consider **biking** or **walking** around Gustavia and other towns to explore the local area and save money.
- Use **online maps** or **GPS** to navigate the island's road network, as signage can be limited in some areas.
