[SECTION] Overview: Saint Barthelemy vs Anguilla [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Saint Barthelemy and Anguilla is influenced by their unique economic contexts. Saint Barthelemy, as a French territory, has a high standard of living and a strong economy, which contributes to its higher cost of living. In contrast, Anguilla, a British overseas territory, has a more modest economy and a lower cost of living. The main difference in costs between the two islands lies in the prices of housing, food, and transportation. Saint Barthelemy's luxury tourism industry drives up prices, making it a more expensive destination for visitors and residents alike.

The economic context of both islands also plays a significant role in shaping their cost of living. Saint Barthelemy's economy is driven by tourism, finance, and trade, which generates a high demand for goods and services, resulting in higher prices. Anguilla, on the other hand, has a smaller economy with a greater emphasis on tourism and fishing, which keeps prices relatively lower. Overall, Anguilla is generally cheaper than Saint Barthelemy, with a cost of living difference of around 15-20%. This makes Anguilla a more attractive option for budget-conscious travelers and expats.
