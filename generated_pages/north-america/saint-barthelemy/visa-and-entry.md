# Saint Barthelemy Visa & Entry Requirements

You do not need a visa to enter Saint Barthelemy if you are a citizen of certain countries, including those within the European Union, but it is essential to verify the current requirements with the official embassy or consulate before traveling.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Saint Barthelemy before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Saint Barthelemy has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of around 100 countries to enter without a visa, while others may need to obtain a visa on arrival or before their trip. As an overseas collectivity of France, Saint Barthelemy's visa policy is closely tied to that of France, and many countries that have a visa-free agreement with France also have the same privileges in Saint Barthelemy. The territory's visa policy aims to balance the need to attract tourists and business travelers with the need to maintain national security and control over immigration.

The visa-free policy applies to citizens of countries such as the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, and Australia, among others, who can stay in Saint Barthelemy for up to 90 days without a visa. However, it is crucial to note that even if a visa is not required, travelers must still meet certain requirements, such as holding a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can typically stay in Saint Barthelemy for up to 90 days without a visa, provided they meet the necessary requirements, such as holding a valid passport and proof of onward travel. Extensions of stay are possible but must be requested at the local authorities before the initial stay period expires.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to provide additional documentation, such as an invitation letter from a local company or a certificate of registration, to enter Saint Barthelemy. They may also need to apply for a specific type of visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in Saint Barthelemy must provide proof of enrollment at a local educational institution and may need to apply for a student visa. The duration of stay will depend on the length of the study program.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Saint Barthelemy must have a job offer from a local employer and may need to apply for a work visa. The employer must sponsor the worker's visa application and provide documentation to support the application.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry point to Saint Barthelemy is the Gustaf III Airport, which receives flights from several international destinations. Travelers can also arrive by boat at the Gustavia harbor. Upon arrival, travelers will need to go through immigration and customs, where they will be required to present their travel documents and answer questions about the purpose of their stay. It is essential to ensure that all documents are in order and that travelers have all the necessary information to facilitate a smooth entry process.

Travelers should also be aware of the customs regulations in Saint Barthelemy, which include restrictions on the importation of certain goods, such as firearms and narcotics. It is recommended to check with the local authorities or the French embassy or consulate for the most up-to-date information on customs regulations.
