[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Saint Barthelemy is characterized by a relatively high level of reliability and speed, with most areas of the island having access to **broadband internet**. However, there can be a noticeable difference in internet quality between urban and rural areas, with the capital Gustavia tend to have faster and more reliable connections. The island's small size and relatively low population density contribute to a generally good internet infrastructure, with most internet service providers offering a range of plans to suit different needs and budgets.

The reliability of the internet in Saint Barthelemy is also a major factor, with most providers offering **uninterrupted service** and minimal downtime. This makes it an attractive destination for **remote workers** and digital nomads who require a stable and fast internet connection to stay productive. Additionally, the widespread availability of **WiFi** in public areas, cafes, and hotels makes it easy to stay connected and access the internet on the go.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Saint Barthelemy is a relatively straightforward process, with several options available to visitors. The main carriers, including **Saint-Barth Telecom**, **Digicel**, and **Orange**, offer a range of plans and packages, including **tourist SIMs** that can be purchased at the airport or at various retail outlets around the island. To purchase a SIM card, visitors will typically need to provide a **passport** and may need to register their SIM card with the provider.

It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds. **Saint-Barth Telecom** is the largest provider and offers a range of plans, including a **tourist plan** that includes 1GB of data and unlimited calls and texts for 20 USD. **Digicel** and **Orange** also offer similar plans, with prices starting from around 15 USD.
