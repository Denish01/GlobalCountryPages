What Is Saint Barthelemy?

Saint Barthelemy, also known as Saint Barts, is an overseas collectivity of France located in the northeastern Caribbean Sea. The island is situated approximately 35 kilometers southeast of Saint Martin and 230 kilometers northwest of Anguilla. With a total area of about 25 square kilometers, Saint Barthelemy is a small but picturesque island known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant culture. The island's sovereignty status is that of an overseas collectivity, which means it is a part of France but has a significant degree of autonomy.

Geographically, Saint Barthelemy is a volcanic island with a rugged terrain, surrounded by coral reefs and white sandy beaches. The island's landscape is characterized by rolling hills, scenic valleys, and a dramatic coastline. The capital and largest city, Gustavia, is a charming port town with a rich history and a blend of French and Caribbean architectural styles. Saint Barthelemy is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world with its unique blend of French elegance and Caribbean charm.

The island's economy is primarily driven by tourism, with a focus on high-end resorts, luxury villas, and fine dining restaurants. The official language is French, although many residents also speak English and other languages. The local culture is a unique blend of French, African, and Caribbean influences, reflected in the island's music, dance, and art. Saint Barthelemy is also known for its vibrant festivals and events, including the Saint Barts Carnival and the Saint Barts Music Festival.

Key History

  1. 1648 - Saint Barthelemy was first colonized by France, and the island became a French possession.
  2. 1784 - The island was sold to Sweden, and it became a Swedish colony for nearly a century.
  3. 1878 - Saint Barthelemy was returned to France, and it became an overseas territory.
  4. 1946 - The island became an overseas department of France, with the same rights and privileges as departments in metropolitan France.
  5. 2007 - Saint Barthelemy became an overseas collectivity of France, with a significant degree of autonomy and self-governance.
  6. 2010 - The island was affected by Hurricane Earl, which caused significant damage and disruption to the local economy.
  7. 2017 - Saint Barthelemy was hit by Hurricane Irma, which caused widespread destruction and damage to the island's infrastructure.
