The cost of education in Saint Barthelemy varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Saint Barthelemy is based on the French model, with **compulsory education** lasting from age 5 to 18. The language of instruction is **French**, and the curriculum is similar to that in France. Public schools in Saint Barthelemy are generally considered to be of good quality, with well-trained teachers and adequate resources. However, some parents may prefer to send their children to private schools, which can offer smaller class sizes and more individualized attention. There are also several international schools on the island, which offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and the **British A-level**.

The quality of education in Saint Barthelemy is considered to be high, with a strong emphasis on **academic achievement** and **cultural development**. The island's small size and close-knit community also make it an ideal place for students to develop their social and emotional skills. However, the education system can be quite **competitive**, and students may face significant pressure to perform well in exams and assessments. Despite these challenges, many students from Saint Barthelemy go on to attend top universities in France and around the world.
