[SECTION] Overview: Grenada vs Trinidad and Tobago [/SECTION]
Grenada and Trinidad and Tobago are two neighboring island nations in the Caribbean, with distinct economic contexts. Grenada has a smaller economy, with a GDP per capita of around $14,620, while Trinidad and Tobago has a more developed economy, with a GDP per capita of around $31,930. Despite this, Grenada's cost of living is generally lower, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees. The overall cost of living difference between the two countries is around 15-20%, with Grenada being the more affordable option.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Trinidad and Tobago has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on oil and gas production, while Grenada's economy is more reliant on tourism and agriculture. This diversity in Trinidad and Tobago's economy contributes to its higher cost of living, while Grenada's smaller economy and lower labor costs keep prices lower. Additionally, Grenada's government has implemented policies to attract foreign investment and stimulate economic growth, which has helped to keep costs low.
