Location & Borders

Grenada is located in the southernmost part of the Caribbean Sea, northeast of Venezuela. It is situated in the Lesser Antilles island chain, which includes other islands such as Trinidad and Tobago and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. The country is an island nation, with a total coastline of 121 kilometers, and is bordered by the Caribbean Sea to the west and the Atlantic Ocean to the east. The neighboring countries of Trinidad and Tobago and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines are located to the south and north, respectively.

Grenada is a small island nation, but its strategic location in the Caribbean makes it an important hub for trade and tourism. The country's proximity to the Equator gives it a tropical climate, with warm temperatures and high levels of humidity throughout the year. The island is also vulnerable to hurricanes, which can occur between June and November. Despite its small size, Grenada has a diverse geography, with mountains, forests, and coastlines that make it a popular destination for tourists.

Terrain & Landscape

Grenada's terrain is characterized by mountains, hills, and coastal plains. The highest point on the island is Mount Saint Catherine, which rises to an elevation of 840 meters. The mountainous interior of the island is covered in tropical rainforests, which are home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. The coastal plains are generally flat and narrow, with beaches and coves that are popular with tourists. The island's terrain is also dotted with volcanic features, including volcanic lakes and hot springs.

The island's landscape is also shaped by its rivers, which flow from the mountains to the sea. The Grand Etang River is one of the longest rivers on the island, and flows through a scenic gorge before emptying into the sea. The island's waterfalls are also a popular attraction, with the Annandale Falls being one of the most well-known. Grenada's terrain and landscape make it a popular destination for hikers, birdwatchers, and nature lovers.

Grenada's coastline is also an important feature of its terrain and landscape. The island has a total of 121 kilometers of coastline, with beaches, coves, and bays that are popular with tourists. The Grand Anse Beach is one of the most popular beaches on the island, and is known for its white sand and clear waters. The island's coastline is also home to a variety of marine life, including coral reefs and sea turtles.
