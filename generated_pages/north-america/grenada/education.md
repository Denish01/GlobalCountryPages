[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Grenada is based on the British model, with students attending primary school from age 5 to 11, followed by secondary school from age 11 to 18. The school system is divided into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children and focuses on developing basic skills in reading, writing, and mathematics. Secondary education is also compulsory and provides students with a broader range of academic subjects, including **science**, **technology**, **engineering**, and **mathematics (STEM)**. Tertiary education is available at institutions such as the **T.A. Marryshow Community College** and the **St. George's University**, which offer a range of undergraduate and graduate programs.

The quality of education in Grenada is generally high, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and **critical thinking**. Public schools are free and accessible to all citizens, while private schools offer an alternative for those who can afford the fees. The public education system is well-funded and well-organized, with a strong focus on teacher training and **curriculum development**.

The education system in Grenada is also supported by a range of **extracurricular activities**, including sports, music, and arts programs. These programs provide students with opportunities to develop their talents and interests outside of the classroom and help to promote a well-rounded education.
