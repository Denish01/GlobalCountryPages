The tax rates in Grenada range from 10% to 30% for individuals and 20% for corporations, with various incentives and exemptions available for specific industries and individuals.

[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
Grenada has a **territorial taxation** system, which means that only income earned within the country is subject to tax, with some exceptions for international business companies and other special entities. Residency rules for tax purposes in Grenada are based on the number of days spent in the country, with individuals considered resident if they spend more than 183 days in a calendar year, or have a permanent home available for their use at any time during the year.

The tax system in Grenada is designed to encourage foreign investment and economic growth, with various incentives and exemptions available for specific industries, such as tourism and manufacturing. The country has a relatively low **tax burden**, with a total tax revenue of around 25% of GDP. The tax authority in Grenada is the Inland Revenue Division, which is responsible for collecting and administering taxes, as well as providing guidance and support to taxpayers.

[SECTION] Corporate & Business Tax [/SECTION]
- The **corporate tax rate** in Grenada is 20%, with a reduced rate of 10% for companies engaged in certain activities, such as tourism and manufacturing.
- Small businesses and startups may be eligible for **incentives and exemptions**, such as a reduced tax rate or a tax holiday, depending on their specific circumstances.
- Grenada has several **free zones**, including the Grenada Free Zone and the Grenada Industrial Estate, which offer a range of benefits, including tax exemptions and streamlined customs procedures.
- Companies must **register with the Inland Revenue Division** and obtain a tax identification number in order to operate in Grenada.
- Companies are required to file annual tax returns and pay any taxes due by the end of the tax year.

[SECTION] VAT / Sales Tax [/SECTION]
- The **standard VAT rate** in Grenada is 15%, with a reduced rate of 10% for certain goods and services, such as food and hotel accommodation.
- **Exemptions** are available for certain goods and services, such as healthcare and education, as well as for charitable organizations and non-profit entities.
- A **tourist refund scheme** is available for international visitors, allowing them to claim a refund of VAT on certain purchases made during their stay.
- VAT returns must be filed and any taxes due must be paid by the 21st of the month following the end of the tax period.
