# Where to Stay in Grenada — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To find the best place to stay in Grenada, consider the island's top areas, including Grand Anse, Morne Rouge, and St. George's, each offering a unique blend of amenities, activities, and accommodation options.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Grenada, there's a distinct character and set of advantages that cater to different types of travelers. 
**Grand Anse** (St. George's)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Grand Anse is known for its stunning beach and vibrant atmosphere, with plenty of restaurants, bars, and water sports. The area is perfect for those seeking a relaxing beach vacation with easy access to amenities.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Beautiful beach, plenty of dining options, water sports and activities
Cons: Can be crowded, expensive

**Morne Rouge** (St. George's)
Best For: Backpacker / Budget
Vibe: Morne Rouge is a charming area with a mix of local culture and natural beauty, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills and sea. The area is ideal for budget-conscious travelers seeking an authentic experience.
Price Range: $80-$200/night
Pros: Affordable, local culture, natural beauty
Cons: Limited amenities

**St. George's** (St. George's)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: St. George's is the capital city of Grenada, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and modern amenities. The area is perfect for business travelers and those seeking a luxurious experience.
Price Range: $250-$600/night
Pros: Historic charm, modern amenities, business facilities
Cons: Can be noisy, expensive

**Sauteurs** (Sauteurs)
Best For: Family / Nature
Vibe: Sauteurs is a picturesque town on the north coast of Grenada, known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities. The area is ideal for families and nature lovers seeking a peaceful retreat.
Price Range: $150-$350/night
Pros: Natural beauty, outdoor activities, peaceful
Cons: Limited amenities, remote

**Gouyave** (Gouyave)
Best For: Budget / Local
Vibe: Gouyave is a charming fishing village on the west coast of Grenada, offering a unique glimpse into local culture and traditions. The area is perfect for budget-conscious travelers seeking an authentic experience.
Price Range: $60-$180/night
Pros: Affordable, local culture, fishing village
Cons: Limited amenities, basic facilities

**Grand Etang** (St. George's)
Best For: Nature / Adventure
Vibe: Grand Etang is a national park and forest reserve, offering stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities. The area is ideal for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
Price Range: $100-$250/night
Pros: Natural beauty, outdoor activities, unique experience
Cons: Limited amenities, remote

- Consider negotiating prices for long-term stays or off-season bookings
- Check for package deals that include flights, accommodation, and activities
- Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before booking
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during peak season
- Use travel insurance to protect against unexpected cancellations or changes

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night
- Use licensed taxis or shuttle services for transportation
- Keep valuables secure and be mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas
