# Rent & Housing Prices in Grenada

The cost of rent in Grenada can range from $400 to $1,200 USD per month for a one-bedroom apartment, depending on the location and quality of the accommodation.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
Grenada's housing market is characterized by a mix of modern and traditional properties, with a range of options available for rent and purchase. The rental market is relatively stable, with typical lease terms ranging from 6 to 12 months. Deposit requirements are usually one to two months' rent, and tenant rights are protected by law. Compared to its regional neighbors, such as Trinidad and Tobago and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Grenada's housing costs are relatively moderate. However, the cost of living in Grenada is still relatively high, particularly in the capital city of St. George's.

The housing market in Grenada is also influenced by the island's tourism industry, with many properties being used as vacation rentals or guesthouses. This can drive up prices, particularly in popular areas such as Grand Anse and Morne Rouge. However, there are still affordable options available, particularly in the suburbs and rural areas. Overall, Grenada's housing market offers a range of options for renters and buyers, from modern apartments to traditional houses and villas.

[SECTION] Renter Tips for Grenada [/SECTION]
- Research thoroughly: look for properties online, and visit the island to get a feel for the different areas and neighborhoods
- Work with a reputable agent: a good agent can help you find the right property and negotiate a fair price
