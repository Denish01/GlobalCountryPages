[SECTION] What Is Grenada? [/SECTION]
Grenada is a **sovereign** island nation located in the southeastern Caribbean Sea, at the southernmost end of the Grenadines island chain. It is situated approximately 100 miles north of Venezuela and is part of the **Lesser Antilles** archipelago. Grenada is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant economy, which is driven primarily by **tourism** and **agriculture**.

The country's geographic position makes it susceptible to **hurricanes** and **tropical storms**, which can have a significant impact on its economy and infrastructure. Despite these challenges, Grenada has made significant progress in recent years, with a growing economy and a high standard of living. The capital city, St. George's, is a popular tourist destination, with its colorful buildings, bustling markets, and historic **Fort George**.

Grenada is often referred to as the **"Spice Island"** due to its reputation as a major producer of **nutmeg**, **cinnamon**, and other spices. The country's lush interior is home to a variety of exotic plants and animals, including the **Grenada dove**, a critically endangered species found only on the island. With its unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality, Grenada is a popular destination for tourists and travelers from around the world.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1498 - Christopher Columbus sights Grenada on his third voyage to the New World.
2. 1650 - The French establish the first European settlement on the island.
3. 1762 - The British capture Grenada from the French and establish a colonial government.
4. 1974 - Grenada gains independence from the United Kingdom, with Eric Gairy as its first prime minister.
5. 1979 - A Marxist revolution led by Maurice Bishop overthrows the Gairy government.
6. 1983 - The United States and other Caribbean nations intervene in Grenada, toppling the Marxist government and establishing a new democratic government.
7. 2004 - Hurricane Ivan devastates the island, causing widespread destruction and loss of life.

[SECTION] What Makes Grenada Unique [/SECTION]
- Grenada is home to the world's first **underwater sculpture park**, located off the coast of St. George's.
- The island is a major producer of **organic cocoa**, with several farms and chocolate makers operating in the country.
- Grenada has a unique **cultural heritage**, with a blend of African, European, and indigenous influences.
- The country is home to several **hot springs** and **waterfalls**, including the popular **Concord Falls**.
- Grenada has a vibrant **music scene**, with a mix of **calypso**, **soca**, and **reggae** influences.
