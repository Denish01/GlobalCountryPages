Food Culture Overview

Martinique's cuisine is a reflection of its history and cultural diversity, with influences from French, African, and Caribbean traditions. The island's unique blend of flavors and ingredients has resulted in a distinct Creole cuisine, which is characterized by the use of spices, herbs, and fresh seafood. The cuisine is also shaped by the island's geography, with a focus on local ingredients such as christophine (a type of squash), dasheen (a type of taro), and yams. Regional variations can be seen in the different parts of the island, with the north being known for its boudin crÃ©ole (a type of blood sausage) and the south being famous for its fruits de mer (seafood).
