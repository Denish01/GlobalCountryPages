[SECTION] How Martinique Is Divided [/SECTION]
Martinique is an overseas department of France, and as such, it is divided into four arrondissements, which are further subdivided into 34 communes. The arrondissements are responsible for providing various public services, such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure, to the local population. The administrative structure of Martinique is similar to that of other French overseas departments, with a prefect appointed by the French government to oversee the administration of the island.

The four arrondissements of Martinique are distinct in terms of their geography, economy, and cultural identity. The arrondissement of Fort-de-France, for example, is the most populous and serves as the economic and cultural hub of the island. The arrondissement of Le Marin, on the other hand, is known for its beautiful beaches and vibrant tourist industry. The arrondissements of Saint-Pierre and Trinité are more rural and are known for their natural beauty and historic landmarks.

[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
Martinique has a well-developed transportation network, with a range of options available for getting around the island. The island's international airport, located in Le Lamentin, receives flights from major carriers and offers connections to other Caribbean islands and European cities. There are also several domestic airports and heliports, which provide access to more remote areas of the island. The island's road network is well-maintained, and rental cars are widely available. Buses and taxis are also available, and provide a convenient and affordable way to get around.
