Historical Overview

In 1502, Christopher Columbus sighted the island on his fourth voyage to the New World, marking the beginning of European involvement in Martinique. The French established the first permanent settlement on the island in 1635, and over time, Martinique became a French colony, with the French imposing their language, culture, and customs on the island. The island's economy was driven by sugar production, with enslaved Africans being brought to the island to work on plantations. The French Revolution had a significant impact on Martinique, with the island being a major hub for the slave trade and the subsequent abolition of slavery in 1848.
