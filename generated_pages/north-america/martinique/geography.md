The island is bordered to the north by the Atlantic Ocean and to the south by the Caribbean Sea, with the neighboring islands of Dominica to the north and Saint Lucia to the south. The island's strategic location in the Caribbean makes it an important stopover for ships traveling between Europe and the Americas.

The island's borders are entirely maritime, with no land borders with other countries. The closest countries to Martinique are Dominica and Saint Lucia, which are both located to the north and south of the island, respectively. The island is also close to other Caribbean islands, including Guadeloupe and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. The waters surrounding Martinique are home to a diverse range of marine life, including coral reefs, sea turtles, and colorful fish.
