# Martinique Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Martinique is generally a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates compared to other Caribbean islands, but visitors should still take normal precautions to ensure their safety.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Martinique is a French overseas department, and as such, it benefits from a strong and effective law enforcement system. The island has a reputation for being a safe and welcoming destination, with low levels of violent crime. However, as with any popular tourist destination, there are still some petty crimes and scams that visitors should be aware of. The most common crimes against tourists are pickpocketing, purse snatching, and theft from rental cars. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their property, such as keeping valuables secure and being mindful of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

The island's natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities also come with some risks, such as strong currents and rip tides at some beaches, and steep and winding roads. Visitors should exercise caution when engaging in outdoor activities and follow local advice and guidelines. Overall, Martinique is a safe and enjoyable destination for tourists, but it's always important to be aware of one's surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and purse snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Theft from rental cars, especially at beaches and parking areas
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake parking attendants or street vendors
- Strong currents and rip tides at some beaches, such as Anse-à-l'Ane
- Steep and winding roads, especially in rural areas
- Limited access to medical facilities in rural areas
- Petty crime and theft in some rural areas, such as Le Carbet and Le Prêcheur
- Overcharging by some taxi drivers and street vendors

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Martinique, but visitors should ensure they are up to date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended for visitors with sensitive stomachs
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are generally good, with several hospitals and clinics in Fort-de-France and other major towns, but visitors may need to pay upfront for medical treatment
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, especially in Fort-de-France and other major towns, and many pharmacies are open late or 24 hours

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or a money belt
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Be cautious when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking or swimming, and follow local advice and guidelines
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe
- Be aware of your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Follow local advice and guidelines for outdoor activities, such as hiking or swimming

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Martinique is generally a safe destination for solo and women travelers, but visitors should still take normal precautions to ensure their safety
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas, and consider using a taxi or shuttle service instead
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Consider staying in a hotel or resort with good security and a 24-hour front desk, and make sure to research the area before booking a room.
