# Martinique Visa & Entry Requirements

As a general rule, citizens of most countries do not need a visa to enter Martinique for tourism or business stays up to 90 days, but it's essential to verify the specific requirements for your nationality before traveling.

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Martinique, being an overseas department of France, has a relatively liberal visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter without a visa for short-term stays. The territory's visa policy is aligned with that of France, and as such, it follows the Schengen visa rules. Citizens of over 100 countries, including the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom, can enter Martinique visa-free for up to 90 days. However, it's crucial to note that while a visa may not be required, travelers must still meet specific entry requirements, such as holding a valid passport and proof of onward travel.

The visa policy in Martinique is designed to facilitate travel and tourism, while also ensuring the security and integrity of the territory. The authorities strive to strike a balance between welcoming visitors and maintaining the necessary controls to prevent unauthorized stays or activities. As a result, the visa requirements and regulations can change, so it's essential to stay informed and verify the current requirements before planning a trip to Martinique.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Martinique without a visa for up to 90 days, provided they have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds for their stay. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, but this can be extended by applying for a temporary residence permit. Tourists are not allowed to engage in any form of employment or business activities during their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers can enter Martinique without a visa for up to 90 days, provided they have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and an invitation letter from a local company or organization. Business travelers may need to provide additional documentation, such as a letter from their employer or a copy of their business license.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in Martinique must apply for a student visa at a French embassy or consulate. They must provide proof of enrollment in a local educational institution, as well as sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. The duration of the student visa will depend on the length of the study program.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Martinique must apply for a work visa at a French embassy or consulate. They must have a job offer from a local employer and meet specific requirements, such as having a certain level of qualifications or experience. The work visa will be issued for a specific period, and the worker must leave the territory at the end of their contract.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airport in Martinique is the Martinique Aimé Césaire International Airport, which receives flights from major airlines. The airport is located about 8 kilometers from the capital city, Fort-de-France. Upon arrival, travelers will need to go through immigration and customs, where they will be required to present their travel documents and answer questions about their stay. The immigration process is typically straightforward, but travelers should be prepared to provide information about their itinerary, accommodation, and activities during their stay. Land borders are not a common entry point, as Martinique is an island territory.

The entry procedures in Martinique are designed to ensure the security and integrity of the territory, while also facilitating the arrival of legitimate travelers. The authorities may conduct random checks or interviews to verify the information provided by travelers, so it's essential to be honest and transparent when answering questions. Travelers who are unsure about the entry requirements or procedures can contact the official embassy or consulate of Martinique for guidance and support.
