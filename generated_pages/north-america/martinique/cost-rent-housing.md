# Rent & Housing Prices in Martinique

The cost of rent in Martinique can range from $400 to $1,200 USD per month for a one-bedroom apartment, depending on the location and amenities, with housing prices varying from $2,000 to $6,000 USD per square meter.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
The rental market in Martinique is relatively stable, with a mix of modern and traditional apartments available for rent. Typical lease terms are 3-6 months for short-term rentals and 1-2 years for long-term rentals. Deposit requirements usually range from one to three months' rent, and tenant rights are protected by French law, which applies in Martinique as an overseas department of France. Compared to its regional neighbors, such as Dominica and Saint Lucia, Martinique's housing costs are relatively high, but the island's unique blend of French and Caribbean culture, as well as its stunning natural beauty, make it an attractive destination for expats and tourists.

The housing market in Martinique is also influenced by the island's tourism industry, with many apartments and houses being rented out as vacation rentals. This can drive up prices, especially in popular areas such as Fort-de-France and the coastal towns. However, for those looking for a more affordable option, there are also apartments and houses available in the suburbs and rural areas. It's worth noting that the cost of living in Martinique is generally higher than in other Caribbean islands, due to its connection to France and the resulting importation of goods and services.
