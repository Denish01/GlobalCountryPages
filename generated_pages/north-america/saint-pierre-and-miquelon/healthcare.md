The quality of care is generally high, with well-trained medical professionals and modern equipment. However, the remote location of the territory can make it difficult to access specialized care, and patients may need to be evacuated to France or Canada for certain treatments.

The public healthcare system is the primary provider of healthcare services in Saint Pierre and Miquelon, with a range of services available, including doctor's visits, hospital stays, and pharmaceuticals. Private healthcare services are also available, although they are not as widespread as public services. In urban areas, such as Saint-Pierre, access to healthcare services is generally good, with a range of medical facilities and professionals available. However, in rural areas, access to healthcare services can be more limited, with fewer medical facilities and professionals available.

Despite the challenges posed by its remote location, the healthcare system in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is well-organized and effective, with a strong emphasis on preventative care and health promotion. The territory has a high **life expectancy** and low **infant mortality rate**, indicating a high level of health and wellbeing among the population.
