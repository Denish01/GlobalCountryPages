[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Saint Pierre and Miquelon is a small archipelago with a limited range of transportation options. The main modes of transportation are walking, cycling, and driving, with some public transportation available. The quality of public transit is relatively good, with regular bus services connecting the main towns and villages. However, the lack of a large population and limited infrastructure means that options can be limited, especially outside of the capital, Saint-Pierre.

Most people get around Saint Pierre and Miquelon by car, with many residents owning their own vehicles. The island's small size and limited traffic make it easy to navigate, and parking is generally not a problem. However, the lack of public transportation options can make it difficult for visitors to get around without a car. There are some taxi services available, but these can be expensive, especially for longer journeys.
