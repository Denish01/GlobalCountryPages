Overview: Saint Pierre and Miquelon vs Faroe Islands

The cost of living in Saint Pierre and Miquelon and the Faroe Islands can be quite different, despite both being remote island territories. Saint Pierre and Miquelon, an overseas collectivity of France, has a high standard of living and a strong economy, which contributes to its higher cost of living. The Faroe Islands, a self-governing region within the Kingdom of Denmark, also has a high standard of living, but its cost of living is slightly lower due to its smaller economy and lower prices for certain goods and services. Overall, the Faroe Islands are around 15-20% cheaper than Saint Pierre and Miquelon, making it a more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of both territories also plays a significant role in their cost of living. Saint Pierre and Miquelon has a strong fishing industry and a growing tourism sector, which drives up prices for accommodations and food. The Faroe Islands, on the other hand, have a more diversified economy with a strong focus on fishing, tourism, and renewable energy. This diversification helps to keep prices stable and lower compared to Saint Pierre and Miquelon. Additionally, the Faroe Islands have a lower tax burden, which also contributes to its lower cost of living.

Where Each Country Wins

  • Saint Pierre and Miquelon is cheaper for:
  • Certain types of seafood, which are abundant and fresh in the territory
  • Some cultural events and festivals, which are subsidized by the French government
  • High-end accommodations, which can be found at a lower price point than in other European destinations
  • Certain types of outdoor activities, such as hiking and fishing, which are readily available and free
  • Faroe Islands is cheaper for:
  • Rent and housing, with lower prices for apartments and houses
  • Groceries, with lower prices for basic items like meat, dairy, and produce
  • Transportation, with lower prices for fuel and public transportation
  • Certain types of clothing and gear, such as outdoor apparel and equipment

Bottom Line

The choice between Saint Pierre and Miquelon and the Faroe Islands ultimately depends on your lifestyle and priorities. If you value a high standard of living, a strong economy, and a unique cultural experience, Saint Pierre and Miquelon may be the better choice. However, if you are looking for a more affordable option with a lower cost of living, the Faroe Islands are definitely worth considering. Expats, students, and retirees may find the Faroe Islands to be a more affordable option, while families and young professionals may prefer the amenities and services available in Saint Pierre and Miquelon. Ultimately, both territories offer a unique and rewarding experience, and the right choice will depend on your individual circumstances and priorities.
