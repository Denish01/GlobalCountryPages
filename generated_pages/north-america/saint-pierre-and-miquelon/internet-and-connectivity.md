# Saint Pierre and Miquelon Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is generally reliable, with decent speeds and coverage, especially in the capital city of Saint-Pierre, making it relatively easy for tourists and residents to stay connected.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is provided by a few local carriers, with SPM Telecom and Orange Saint-Pierre et Miquelon being the most popular ones. The internet quality is generally good, with average speeds of around 10 Mbps download, which is sufficient for basic browsing, emailing, and social media. However, the speeds can be slower in rural areas, and the coverage can be limited in some parts of the islands. The reliability of the internet connection is generally good, with minimal downtime and outages.

The urban-rural divide is noticeable when it comes to internet connectivity in Saint Pierre and Miquelon. The capital city of Saint-Pierre has the best coverage and speeds, while the rural areas and smaller villages may have slower speeds and more limited coverage. This can be a challenge for residents and tourists who need to stay connected in these areas. However, the local carriers are continuously working to improve the infrastructure and expand the coverage to more areas.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Saint Pierre and Miquelon, tourists can visit the local carrier stores, such as SPM Telecom or Orange Saint-Pierre et Miquelon, and provide a valid passport and proof of address. The SIM card cost is around 20 USD, and it usually comes with a prepaid plan that includes a certain amount of data, minutes, and texts. The recommended carriers for tourists are SPM Telecom and Orange Saint-Pierre et Miquelon, as they offer the best coverage and speeds.

The data plans offered by the local carriers are generally affordable, with prices starting from around 10 USD per month for a basic plan with 1 GB of data. The plans can be purchased at the carrier stores, and they usually require a prepaid payment. It's recommended to check the carrier's website or visit their store to get the most up-to-date information on the available plans and prices.
