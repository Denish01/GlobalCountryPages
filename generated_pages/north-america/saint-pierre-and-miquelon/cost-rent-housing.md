[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
The housing market in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is relatively small and expensive, with a limited supply of apartments and houses available for rent or purchase. The rental market is dominated by short-term leases, typically ranging from 6 to 12 months, with a security deposit equivalent to one to two months' rent. Tenant rights are protected by French law, which applies in the territory. Compared to regional neighbors like Canada, Saint Pierre and Miquelon's housing costs are relatively high, due to the territory's remote location and limited economy. However, the cost of living in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is still lower than in major Canadian cities like Montreal or Toronto.

The housing market in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is also influenced by the territory's small size and limited population. The demand for housing is relatively low, which can make it easier for renters to find accommodation. However, the supply of affordable housing is limited, and prices can be high, especially in the city center. The territory's government has implemented measures to control rent prices and provide assistance to low-income families, but the housing market remains relatively expensive.
