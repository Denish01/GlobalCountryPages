# Education Costs in Saint Pierre and Miquelon

The cost of education in Saint Pierre and Miquelon varies, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge tuition fees ranging from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is based on the French model, with French being the primary language of instruction. Public schools are generally considered to be of high quality, with a strong emphasis on core subjects such as mathematics, science, and language. Compulsory education lasts for 12 years, from the age of 6 to 18, and is divided into primary, secondary, and post-secondary levels. Private schools and international institutions are also available, although they are less common due to the territory's small size and limited population.

The quality of education in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is generally considered to be high, with a strong focus on academic achievement and preparation for higher education. However, the limited number of schools and institutions can make it difficult for students to access certain programs or courses. International schools are available, but they are often private and can be expensive. The language of instruction is French, which can make it challenging for non-French speaking students to integrate into the education system.
