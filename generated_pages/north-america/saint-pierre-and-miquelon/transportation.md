# Getting Around Saint Pierre and Miquelon — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Saint Pierre and Miquelon, you can use a combination of domestic flights, buses, taxis, and rental cars, as the territory has a limited but functional transportation network.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Saint Pierre and Miquelon can be reached by air or sea, with the Saint-Pierre Airport (FSP) being the main entry point. The airport receives direct flights from Canada, particularly from St. John's, Newfoundland, and Halifax, Nova Scotia, as well as from other French territories. You can fly to Saint Pierre and Miquelon with **Air Saint-Pierre**, which operates regular flights from these destinations. There are no overland entry points, as the territory is an archipelago located off the coast of Canada.

If you prefer to travel by sea, you can take a ferry from Fortune, Newfoundland, to Saint-Pierre, which is operated by **SPM Ferries**. This option is more time-consuming but offers a unique perspective on the archipelago's coastline. Keep in mind that ferry schedules may be limited, especially during the winter months, so it's essential to plan ahead and book your tickets in advance.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Saint Pierre and Miquelon has a limited domestic air network, with **Air Saint-Pierre** being the main carrier. The airline operates flights between Saint-Pierre and Miquelon, as well as to other smaller islands in the archipelago. You can book your flights online or through a local travel agency, and it's recommended to do so in advance, especially during peak travel seasons. The cost of a domestic flight can range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
There is no rail network in Saint Pierre and Miquelon, so you won't be able to travel by train. The territory's geography, with its rugged coastline and limited land area, makes it difficult to establish a rail network. However, the lack of trains is compensated by a decent road network and a reliable bus system.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
The bus system in Saint Pierre and Miquelon is operated by **Société de Transport de Saint-Pierre et Miquelon**, which offers regular services between the main towns and villages. The buses are comfortable and relatively affordable, with fares ranging from $2 to $5, depending on the distance. You can pay the driver directly, and it's a good idea to have some cash with you, as not all buses accept credit or debit cards.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Saint Pierre and Miquelon, you'll need a valid **driver's license** from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The road quality is moderate, with some paved roads and others that are gravel or dirt.
- Fuel costs are relatively high, with prices ranging from $1.50 to $2.50 per liter.
- Car rental companies, such as **Avis** and **Europcar**, are available at the airport and in Saint-Pierre, with prices starting from $40 per day.
- When driving, be aware of the **narrow roads** and **limited parking** options, especially in the town centers.
- Be prepared for **foggy conditions**, especially during the winter months, and drive slowly and cautiously.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When taking a taxi, make sure to **agree on the fare** before you start your journey, as meters may not always be available.
- **Cash is king** in Saint Pierre and Miquelon, so it's a good idea to have some euros or Canadian dollars with you, especially when using public transport or taxis.
- **Download the Air Saint-Pierre app** to book your flights and check schedules, as well as to get updates on any travel disruptions.
- Be aware of **scams targeting tourists**, such as overpriced taxi fares or fake tour operators, and always research and book with reputable companies.
- **Learn some basic French phrases**, as not everyone speaks English, and it will help you to navigate the territory more easily.
- **Respect local customs** and traditions, especially when visiting churches or attending cultural events.
- **Be prepared for variable weather**, with layers and waterproof clothing, as the climate in Saint Pierre and Miquelon can be unpredictable.
