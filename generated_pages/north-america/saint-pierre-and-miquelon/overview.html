What Is Saint Pierre and Miquelon?

Saint Pierre and Miquelon is a French territory located off the coast of Canada in the North Atlantic Ocean. It is an overseas collectivity of France, which means it is a part of France but has a degree of autonomy. The territory consists of two main islands, Saint Pierre and Miquelon, as well as several smaller islands. Saint Pierre and Miquelon is known for its stunning natural beauty, including rugged coastlines, scenic harbors, and picturesque fishing villages.

The territory has a strategic geographic position, situated near the Grand Banks of Newfoundland, which has made it an important location for the fishing industry. The islands have a rich history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the pre-Columbian era. Today, Saint Pierre and Miquelon is a popular destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts, who come to enjoy the territory's unique culture, stunning landscapes, and exciting activities such as hiking, fishing, and whale watching.

The territory's economy is primarily based on fishing, with the majority of the population employed in the industry. However, in recent years, the government has been working to diversify the economy and promote tourism, which has become an increasingly important sector. Despite its small size, Saint Pierre and Miquelon has a distinct culture and identity, shaped by its history, geography, and French heritage.
