[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
Sint Maarten has a mixed healthcare system, with both public and private providers offering a range of medical services. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of lower quality than the private system, with longer wait times and fewer specialized services available. Locals and foreigners can access both public and private healthcare services, but foreigners may be required to pay out-of-pocket for treatment or have international health insurance to cover their medical expenses. The public healthcare system is funded through a combination of government subsidies and patient fees, while the private system is largely self-funded through patient payments and private insurance.

The quality of healthcare in Sint Maarten can vary significantly depending on the provider and the specific services being sought. Public hospitals and clinics may have more limited resources and equipment, while private hospitals and clinics may offer more specialized and high-tech services. In general, foreigners and those with private insurance may prefer to seek medical care at private facilities, which are often staffed by English-speaking doctors and offer more comprehensive services. However, even at private facilities, the cost of medical care can be high, and patients may be required to pay upfront for treatment or provide proof of insurance coverage.

[SECTION] Pharmacy & Medication Costs [/SECTION]
- Prescription medications may require a doctor's prescription and can cost $10-$50 per month
- Over-the-counter medications are widely available at pharmacies and may cost $5-$20 per month
- Pharmacies are generally well-stocked and offer a wide range of medications, including **brand-name** and **generic** options
- Patients may be required to pay upfront for medications, but some insurance plans may offer reimbursement or discounts
- **Vaccinations** and other preventive medications may be available at public health clinics or private pharmacies

[SECTION] Emergency Care [/SECTION]
In the event of a medical emergency, patients should call 911 or visit the emergency room at the **St. Maarten Medical Center**, which is the main public hospital on the island. Private hospitals and clinics may also offer emergency services, but may require upfront payment or proof of insurance. Ambulance services are available, but may be limited in their coverage and equipment. Foreigners and those with private insurance may prefer to seek medical care at private facilities, which may offer more comprehensive and personalized services. It is recommended that patients have a plan in place for emergency medical care, including **travel insurance** or **international health insurance**, to cover the cost of treatment and any necessary evacuations.
