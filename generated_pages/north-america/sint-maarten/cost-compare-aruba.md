[SECTION] Overview: Sint Maarten vs Aruba [/SECTION]
Sint Maarten and Aruba are two popular island destinations in the Caribbean, known for their beautiful beaches, rich culture, and high standard of living. However, when it comes to the cost of living, there are some significant differences between the two. Sint Maarten, with its strong tourism industry and limited land area, tends to have higher prices for housing, food, and other living expenses. On the other hand, Aruba, with its more diverse economy and larger land area, offers a more affordable lifestyle. Overall, Aruba is around 15-20% cheaper than Sint Maarten, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees on a budget.

The economic context of the two islands also plays a role in their cost of living. Sint Maarten has a strong focus on tourism, which drives up prices for accommodations, food, and activities. Aruba, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on manufacturing, finance, and technology. This diversification helps to keep prices more stable and affordable. Additionally, Aruba has a larger land area, which means that there is more space for development and a greater variety of housing options, leading to lower prices.
