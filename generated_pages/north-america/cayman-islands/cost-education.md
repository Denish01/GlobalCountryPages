# Education Costs in Cayman Islands

The cost of education in the Cayman Islands varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The Cayman Islands' education system is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being free or subsidized for Caymanian students. The language of instruction is **English**, and compulsory education lasts for 11 years, from primary to secondary level. The public education system is generally considered to be of high quality, with a strong emphasis on **academic achievement** and **extracurricular activities**. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a more personalized and specialized education, often with smaller class sizes and a wider range of **curricular options**. International schools are also available, offering **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge** curricula, among others.

The Cayman Islands' education system is overseen by the **Ministry of Education**, which is responsible for setting **curriculum standards** and **education policies**. The ministry also provides support for **special needs students** and **English language learners**. In addition to traditional schools, the Cayman Islands also offer **vocational training** and **apprenticeship programs** for students who wish to pursue a more practical education. These programs are designed to provide students with the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in the **workforce**.
