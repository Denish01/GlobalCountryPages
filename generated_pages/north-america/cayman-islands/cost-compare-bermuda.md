[SECTION] Overview: Cayman Islands vs Bermuda [/SECTION]
The Cayman Islands and Bermuda are both popular Caribbean destinations known for their beautiful beaches, financial hubs, and high standard of living. However, when it comes to the cost of living, there are significant differences between the two territories. The Cayman Islands has a higher cost of living, mainly due to its limited land area and heavy reliance on imports. Bermuda, on the other hand, has a more diverse economy and a stronger focus on tourism, which helps to keep costs lower. Overall, Bermuda is around 15-20% cheaper than the Cayman Islands, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a more affordable Caribbean lifestyle.

The economic context of the two territories also plays a significant role in their cost of living. The Cayman Islands has a large financial sector, which drives up demand for housing, food, and other services, resulting in higher prices. Bermuda, while also having a strong financial sector, has a more balanced economy with a stronger focus on tourism, which helps to distribute wealth and keep costs lower. Additionally, Bermuda has a more developed public transportation system, which reduces the need for private vehicles and helps to lower transportation costs. Overall, the combination of a more diverse economy and a stronger focus on tourism makes Bermuda a more affordable option for those looking to live in the Caribbean.
