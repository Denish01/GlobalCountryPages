[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
The rental market in Nicaragua is relatively **affordable**, with a wide range of options available for expats and locals alike. Typical lease terms are for one year, with a security deposit of one to two months' rent required. Tenant rights are generally well-protected, with laws in place to prevent **unfair eviction** and ensure **rental stability**. Compared to its regional neighbors, Nicaragua's housing costs are generally lower than those in Costa Rica, but higher than those in Honduras.

However, the country's **GDP per capita** of $8,709 and **health expenditure per capita** of $217 indicate a relatively stable economy and access to basic services. The **education spend** of 2.9% of GDP also suggests a commitment to human development. Overall, Nicaragua's housing market offers a range of options for those looking to rent or buy, from **modern apartments** in the city center to **traditional houses** in the suburbs.
