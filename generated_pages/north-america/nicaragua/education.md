The education system in Nicaragua is structured into three main levels: **primary**, **secondary**, and **tertiary education**. Primary education, which is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 12, is typically provided by public schools, although private institutions are also available. Secondary education, which is optional but highly encouraged, is typically provided by public and private institutions, and can last from 4 to 6 years. Tertiary education, which includes **universities** and **technical institutes**, is available to students who have completed secondary education, and can last from 4 to 6 years. The quality of education in Nicaragua is generally considered to be good, although there are some challenges, particularly in rural areas where resources may be limited.

The education system in Nicaragua is also divided into public and private institutions.
