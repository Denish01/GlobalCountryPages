Location & Borders

Nicaragua is a country located in Central America, situated in the southern part of the Isthmus of Central America. It is bordered by two countries: Honduras to the north and Costa Rica to the south. To the east, Nicaragua is bounded by the Caribbean Sea, while to the west, it is bordered by the Pacific Ocean. This unique geography makes Nicaragua a significant country in terms of biodiversity and natural resources. The country's strategic location also makes it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region.

Nicaragua's borders with its neighboring countries are well-defined, with the border with Honduras stretching for approximately 922 kilometers and the border with Costa Rica stretching for about 309 kilometers. The country's coastline along the Pacific Ocean is approximately 910 kilometers long, while its coastline along the Caribbean Sea is around 450 kilometers long. The Coco River forms part of the border with Honduras, while the San Juan River forms part of the border with Costa Rica. Nicaragua's location and borders have played a significant role in shaping the country's history, culture, and economy.

The country's rivers also play a significant role in shaping the landscape, with the San Juan River being the longest river in Nicaragua. Other significant rivers in Nicaragua include the Coco River, the Escondido River, and the Tuma River. These rivers support a wide range of wildlife, including fish, birds, and mammals, and are also an important source of hydroelectric power.
