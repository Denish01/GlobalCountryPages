# Utility & Internet Costs in Trinidad and Tobago

The average cost of utilities and internet in Trinidad and Tobago for an 85 sqm apartment is around $250-300 USD per month, with internet plans starting at $50-70 USD per month for a 60 Mbps connection.

[FACTBOX]
Monthly Utilities (85 sqm apt): ($250-300 USD)
Internet (60 Mbps): ($50-70 USD/month)
Mobile Data (10GB): ($20-30 USD/month)
Electricity Cost: ($0.25-0.35 per kWh)
Currency: TTD
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Trinidad and Tobago has a well-developed utility infrastructure, with electricity, water, and gas services available to most households. The cost of utilities in the country is generally stable, with minimal fluctuations in prices. However, there may be some seasonal variation in electricity costs, with higher rates applying during peak periods (June to September). The **Trinidad and Tobago Electricity Commission (T&TEC)** is the primary electricity provider, while the **Water and Sewerage Authority (WASA)** manages water services.

The cost of utilities in Trinidad and Tobago is influenced by various factors, including the size of the household, usage patterns, and the type of appliances used. Generally, smaller households tend to have lower utility bills, while larger households with multiple occupants and high-energy appliances may incur higher costs. It is essential to note that the **inflation rate** in Trinidad and Tobago is relatively low, at 0.5% (2024), which means that utility prices are unlikely to increase significantly in the short term.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities in Trinidad and Tobago, it is essential to contact the relevant providers (T&TEC, WASA, etc.) and provide identification and proof of address.
- Consider using **prepaid electricity** plans to avoid unexpected bills and consider **postpaid plans** for more flexible payment options.
- Choose the best internet provider based on your location and usage needs, with **Flow** and **Digicel** being popular options.
- To save on utility bills, use energy-efficient appliances, turn off lights and electronics when not in use, and consider investing in solar panels or other renewable energy sources.
- Regularly review and adjust your utility plans to ensure you are getting the best value for your money.
