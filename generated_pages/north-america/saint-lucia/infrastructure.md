[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Saint Lucia has made significant investments in its infrastructure in recent years, with a focus on improving the quality of its roads, expanding its airport and port facilities, and increasing access to electricity and water. The country's infrastructure development level is relatively high compared to other Caribbean nations, with a strong emphasis on supporting the tourism industry. However, there is still a notable gap between urban and rural areas, with rural communities often lacking access to basic services such as reliable electricity and clean water. The government has implemented various initiatives to address these disparities, including the development of rural electrification programs and improvements to the road network.

The country's infrastructure has been shaped by its history, geography, and economy, with a focus on supporting the tourism industry and promoting economic growth. Saint Lucia's strategic location in the Caribbean makes it an attractive destination for tourists and investors alike, and the government has invested heavily in developing its infrastructure to support this growth. Despite these efforts, the country still faces challenges related to its infrastructure, including the need for ongoing maintenance and upgrades to its roads, bridges, and other critical systems.

In recent years, Saint Lucia has seen significant investment in its infrastructure, including the development of new hotels, resorts, and other tourism-related facilities. The government has also invested in improving the country's energy sector, including the development of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. These efforts have helped to improve the overall quality of life for citizens and visitors alike, and have contributed to the country's growing reputation as a popular tourist destination.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]

- However, the country still experiences occasional **power outages**, particularly during periods of high demand or when there are disruptions to the grid.
- The government has implemented initiatives to improve the **reliability** of the energy system, including the development of new power plants and grid upgrades.
- Saint Lucia has set a goal of increasing its use of **renewable energy** to 35% of total energy production by 2025, as part of its efforts to reduce its reliance on fossil fuels and mitigate the impacts of climate change.
