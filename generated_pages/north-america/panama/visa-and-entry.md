[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Panama has a relatively liberal visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter without a visa or with a visa on arrival. The country's strategic location and growing economy have made it an attractive destination for tourists, business travelers, and expats. Citizens of over 100 countries can enter Panama visa-free, making it a popular destination for international travelers. The Panamanian government has also implemented various initiatives to facilitate the entry of foreign visitors, including the introduction of an electronic visa system and a special visa program for remote workers.

The visa policy in Panama is designed to promote tourism, trade, and investment, while also ensuring the security and integrity of the country's borders. The government has established a range of visa categories, including tourist, business, student, and work visas, each with its own set of requirements and regulations. Visitors can stay in Panama for up to 180 days, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Panama without a visa and stay for up to 180 days. They must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay. Tourists can extend their stay for an additional 30 days, but must apply for an extension at a local immigration office.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need a visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They must have an invitation letter from a Panamanian company, proof of business activities, and a valid passport. Business travelers can stay for up to 30 days, but may need to apply for a special visa or permit for longer stays.
**Students:** Students must have a valid visa, which can be obtained through a Panamanian embassy or consulate. They must have proof of enrollment at a recognized educational institution, a valid passport, and sufficient funds to support their stay. Student visas are typically valid for one year, but can be renewed.
**Workers:** Workers must have a valid work visa, which can be obtained through a Panamanian embassy or consulate. They must have a job offer from a Panamanian employer, a valid passport, and sufficient funds to support their stay. Work visas are typically valid for one year, but can be renewed.
