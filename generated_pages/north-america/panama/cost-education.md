# Education Costs in Panama

The cost of education in Panama varies widely, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from a few thousand to over $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Panama's education system is divided into public and private institutions, with varying levels of quality. Public schools are generally free or subsidized, but the quality of education can be lower compared to private schools. The language of instruction is Spanish, but some private schools may offer English or bilingual programs. Compulsory education in Panama lasts for 12 years, from primary to secondary school. International schools are available in major cities like Panama City, offering curricula such as the International Baccalaureate or American high school diplomas.

The quality of education in Panama has been improving in recent years, with the government investing in programs to enhance teacher training and infrastructure. However, there is still a significant gap between public and private schools, with private schools generally offering better facilities and more qualified teachers. Expats and foreigners may prefer to enroll their children in private or international schools, which can provide a more familiar curriculum and better preparation for higher education abroad.
