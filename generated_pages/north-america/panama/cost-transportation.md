[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Panama has a well-developed transportation system, with various modes of transportation available, including buses, taxis, ride-hailing services, and a metro system in Panama City. The quality of public transit is generally good, with a comprehensive network of buses and a modern metro system. Most people in Panama get around using a combination of these modes, with buses being the most affordable option. The country's geography, with a long and narrow shape, makes transportation along the coast and between cities relatively easy.

The transportation system in Panama is also relatively affordable, with the cost of a monthly transit pass being around $30-$40 USD. Taxis are also widely available, with a start rate of approximately $2-$3 USD. Ride-hailing services, such as Uber and Cabify, are also popular in Panama, providing an alternative to traditional taxis. The fuel price in Panama is around $0.80-$1.00 USD per liter, making it relatively affordable to drive in the country.
