[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Getting around Puerto Rico can be relatively easy, with a range of transportation options available, including public buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The public transportation system in Puerto Rico is well-developed, with an extensive network of buses and a metro system in the capital city of San Juan. However, the quality of public transit can vary depending on the location, with some areas having more frequent and reliable services than others. Most people in Puerto Rico get around by car, but using public transportation or ride-hailing services can be a more affordable and convenient option, especially for tourists.

The main transportation modes in Puerto Rico include driving, public buses, and taxis, with many people also using ride-hailing services. The quality of the roads in Puerto Rico is generally good, but traffic can be heavy in urban areas, especially during rush hour. The public transportation system is relatively affordable, with a single fare on the city bus costing around $1.50 USD, and a monthly pass costing around $70 USD. However, the frequency and coverage of public transportation can vary depending on the location, with some areas having more limited services.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Using public transportation or ride-hailing services can be a more affordable and convenient option than renting a car, especially for tourists.
- Be sure to agree on the fare with the taxi driver before starting the journey, as some drivers may not use meters.
- Consider purchasing a monthly transit pass for unlimited travel on public buses and the metro.
- Use **Uber** or **Lyft** for ride-hailing services, as they are widely available and often cheaper than taxis.
- Be aware of traffic and road conditions, especially during rush hour or in areas with heavy construction.
- Consider renting a car for longer trips or for exploring rural areas, but be aware of the costs of fuel, parking, and tolls.
