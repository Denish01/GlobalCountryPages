Utility Costs Overview

Puerto Rico has a well-developed utility infrastructure, with most areas having access to electricity, water, and gas. However, the cost of utilities can fluctuate depending on the season, with higher costs during the summer months due to increased air conditioning usage. The Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority (PREPA) is the main provider of electricity, and the cost of electricity is around $0.25 per kWh. The cost of water is relatively low, with an average monthly cost of $20-$30 for a medium-sized apartment.

The quality of utility services in Puerto Rico is generally good, with most areas having access to reliable electricity and water. However, there can be occasional power outages and water shortages, especially during heavy rainfall or hurricanes. The cost of utilities is also subject to change, with price increases or decreases depending on various factors such as fuel prices and government regulations. It's essential to check with service providers for the latest rates and promotions to get the best deals.

Utility Tips

  • To set up utilities, contact the service providers at least a week in advance to schedule an appointment.
  • Consider prepaid electricity plans to avoid surprise bills and track your usage.
  • Choose internet providers with reliable speeds and customer service, such as Claro or Liberty.
  • Save on bills by using energy-efficient appliances, turning off lights and electronics when not in use, and taking advantage of promotions and discounts.
  • Check with your landlord or property manager to see if any utilities are included in the rent, such as water or garbage collection.
