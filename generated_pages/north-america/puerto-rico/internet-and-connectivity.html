Internet Overview

The internet in Puerto Rico is generally of good quality, with average download speeds of 18 Mbps, which is relatively fast compared to other Caribbean islands. However, there is a significant urban-rural divide, with faster and more reliable connections available in cities like San Juan, and slower speeds in rural areas. The reliability of the internet can also be affected by the weather, with hurricanes and tropical storms occasionally causing outages and disruptions.

In terms of internet infrastructure, Puerto Rico has a well-developed network of undersea cables and fiber-optic connections, which provide fast and reliable links to the US mainland and other parts of the world. This has made it an attractive location for data centers and other internet-based businesses. However, the island's geography, with its mountainous terrain and rugged coastline, can make it difficult to provide internet access to some remote areas.

Getting a SIM Card

To get a SIM card in Puerto Rico, you can visit a store or kiosk of one of the main carriers, such as Claro, T-Mobile, or AT&T. You will typically need to provide a valid form of identification, such as a passport, and may need to pay a deposit or activation fee. The cost of a tourist SIM card is around 20 USD, and you can choose from a range of data plans and packages. It's a good idea to check the coverage and pricing of each carrier before making a decision, as they can vary depending on your specific needs and travel plans.

The main carriers in Puerto Rico offer a range of tourist-friendly plans, including prepaid SIM cards and data packages. These plans can be a convenient and cost-effective way to stay online during your trip, and can be easily topped up or renewed as needed.

WiFi Availability

  • Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with many hotels and resorts offering fast and reliable connections
  • Cafe WiFi culture is well-established in Puerto Rico, with many coffee shops and cafes offering free WiFi to customers
  • Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in San Juan and other cities, offering shared office space and high-speed internet
  • Public WiFi is available in many areas, including airports, shopping malls, and public parks
  • Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi to customers, making it easy to stay online during meals and social events

For Remote Workers

  • Coworking spaces like Piloto 151 and WeWork offer shared office space and high-speed internet in San Juan
  • Reliable cafe chains like Starbucks and The Coffee Bean & Tea Leaf offer free WiFi and a comfortable workspace
  • Backup internet options like portable WiFi hotspots and mobile networks can be useful in case of outages or disruptions
  • VPNs can be useful for remote workers who need to access secure or sensitive information online
  • Many hotels and resorts also offer coworking spaces and business facilities, making them a good option for remote workers who need to stay productive during their trip

Tips & Warnings

  • Internet censorship is not a significant issue in Puerto Rico, but some websites and online services may be blocked due to US laws and regulations
  • VPN use is recommended for remote workers and online security, as it can help to protect against hacking and data breaches
  • Roaming alternatives like portable WiFi hotspots and local SIM cards can be a cost-effective way to stay online during your trip
  • eSIM options are also available from some carriers, offering a convenient and flexible way to stay connected
  • It's a good idea to check the coverage and pricing of each carrier before making a decision, as they can vary depending on your specific needs and travel plans
  • Be aware of data limits and overage charges when using mobile networks or prepaid SIM cards, as they can add up quickly if you're not careful.
