# Education Costs in Puerto Rico

The cost of education in Puerto Rico varies widely, with public schools being free or subsidized, while private schools and universities can range from a few thousand to over $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Puerto Rico is overseen by the **Department of Education**, which provides free or subsidized education to all residents. Public schools in Puerto Rico are generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on **bilingual education** in both Spanish and English. The **compulsory education** period in Puerto Rico lasts for 12 years, from primary school to high school. Private schools are also available, and many of these schools offer a higher level of **academic rigor** and **extracurricular activities**. International schools are also present in Puerto Rico, offering a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **US-based curricula**.

In terms of **language of instruction**, both Spanish and English are used in public schools, while private schools may use one or both languages. The quality of education in Puerto Rico is generally considered to be high, with many students going on to attend **universities** in the US mainland or other countries. However, there are some challenges facing the education system, including a **high dropout rate** and a need for more **resources and funding**. Despite these challenges, Puerto Rico has a highly educated population, with a **literacy rate** of around 95%.
