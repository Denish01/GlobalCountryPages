[SECTION] Overview: Puerto Rico vs US Virgin Islands [/SECTION]
Puerto Rico and the US Virgin Islands are two popular destinations in the Caribbean, known for their beautiful beaches, rich culture, and vibrant cities. However, when it comes to the cost of living, there are some significant differences between the two territories. Puerto Rico has a more developed economy and a larger population, which can lead to lower prices for certain goods and services. On the other hand, the US Virgin Islands are a smaller and more remote territory, which can result in higher prices due to transportation costs and limited competition.

The economic context of the two territories also plays a role in their cost of living. Puerto Rico has a more diverse economy, with a strong service sector and a growing tourism industry, which can lead to more job opportunities and higher wages. The US Virgin Islands, on the other hand, have a more limited economy, with a strong focus on tourism and a smaller workforce. This can result in higher prices for goods and services, as well as lower wages for workers. Overall, Puerto Rico is generally cheaper than the US Virgin Islands, with an overall cost of living that is around 20-30% lower.

[SECTION] Bottom Line [/SECTION]
Puerto Rico and the US Virgin Islands are both popular destinations in the Caribbean, but they offer different value propositions for different types of travelers.
