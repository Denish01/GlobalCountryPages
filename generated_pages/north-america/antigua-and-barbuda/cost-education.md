# Education Costs in Antigua and Barbuda

The cost of education in Antigua and Barbuda varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Antigua and Barbuda is based on the British model, with English being the primary language of instruction. The system is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary levels, with 12 years of compulsory education. Public schools are generally free or subsidized, but the quality can vary, with some schools facing issues with infrastructure and resources. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a higher quality of education, but at a higher cost. International schools are also available, offering curricula such as the International Baccalaureate (IB) and the Cambridge International Examinations (CIE).

The quality of education in Antigua and Barbuda is generally considered to be good, with a literacy rate of around 95%. However, there are some challenges facing the education system, including a lack of resources and infrastructure in some public schools. Despite these challenges, the country has made significant progress in recent years, with an increase in the number of students pursuing higher education. The government has also implemented initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curricula and the provision of training for teachers.
