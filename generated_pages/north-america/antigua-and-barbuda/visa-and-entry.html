Visa Policy Overview

Antigua and Barbuda has a relatively liberal visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival. The country is a popular tourist destination, and its visa policy is designed to facilitate travel while ensuring national security and public safety. Citizens of over 100 countries, including the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, and many European nations, can enter Antigua and Barbuda without a visa or with a visa on arrival.

The visa policy of Antigua and Barbuda is designed to promote tourism, business, and cultural exchange while maintaining the country's sovereignty and security. The government has implemented various measures to streamline the visa application process and make it more efficient for travelers. Visitors can stay in the country for up to six months, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit.

Tourists: Tourists can stay in Antigua and Barbuda for up to six months without a visa, depending on their nationality. They must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay. Extensions are possible, but tourists must apply at the immigration department before their initial stay expires.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may need a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must provide an invitation letter from a local company, proof of business activities, and other required documents. Business travelers can stay in the country for up to 30 days, but extensions are possible with the approval of the immigration department.
Students: Students must obtain a student visa before entering Antigua and Barbuda. They must provide proof of enrollment at a local educational institution, a valid passport, and sufficient funds to support their stay. Student visas are typically valid for one year, but can be extended with the approval of the immigration department.
Workers: Workers must obtain a work permit before entering Antigua and Barbuda. They must have a job offer from a local employer, a valid passport, and sufficient qualifications and experience for the job. Work permits are typically valid for one year, but can be extended with the approval of the immigration department.

The main entry points to Antigua and Barbuda are the V.C. Bird International Airport and the St. John's Harbour. Upon arrival, visitors must present their travel documents to the immigration officer and complete the necessary procedures. The immigration officer will stamp the visitor's passport and provide a stay permit, which must be presented upon departure. Visitors must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country and comply with customs regulations.
The V.C. Bird International Airport is a modern facility with all the necessary amenities, including restaurants, shops, and currency exchange services. The airport is served by several international airlines, including American Airlines, British Airways, and Caribbean Airlines. The St. John's Harbour is a busy port that receives cruise ships and private yachts. Visitors arriving by sea must present their travel documents to the immigration officer and complete the necessary procedures before disembarking.
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not providing proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
- Not completing the visa application form correctly (if required)
- Not declaring goods upon arrival or complying with customs regulations
- Not presenting the stay permit upon departure
- Not respecting local laws and regulations, including those related to employment, education, and healthcare
