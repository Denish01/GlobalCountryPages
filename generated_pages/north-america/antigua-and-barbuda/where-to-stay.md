# Where to Stay in Antigua and Barbuda — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When planning a trip to Antigua and Barbuda, the best areas to stay include St. John's, English Harbour, Falmouth, Dickenson Bay, Jolly Harbour, and Valley Church, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**St. John's** (St. John's)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: St. John's is the capital city, offering a mix of historical sites, shopping, and nightlife, with a lively atmosphere. The city is surrounded by beautiful beaches and offers stunning views of the harbor.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Easy access to historical sites, shopping, and nightlife, with a wide range of accommodations available, from budget-friendly options to luxury resorts.
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, especially during peak season.

**English Harbour** (English Harbour)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: English Harbour is a picturesque and historic area, with a relaxed and sophisticated atmosphere, surrounded by beautiful beaches and yachting facilities. The area is known for its stunning views of the harbor and its rich history.
Price Range: $300-$600/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, historic sites, and yachting facilities, with a range of luxury accommodations and amenities.
Cons: Can be expensive, and limited options for budget travelers.

**Falmouth** (Falmouth)
Best For: Backpacker / Family
Vibe: Falmouth is a charming and laid-back area, with a mix of historical sites, beaches, and outdoor activities, offering a relaxed and natural atmosphere. The area is known for its beautiful beaches and its proximity to the English Harbour.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Affordable accommodations, beautiful beaches, and outdoor activities, with a range of options for budget travelers.
Cons: Limited nightlife and shopping options.

**Dickenson Bay** (St. John's)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Dickenson Bay is a popular and upscale area, with a lively and sophisticated atmosphere, offering beautiful beaches, restaurants, and water sports. The area is known for its stunning views of the bay and its range of luxury accommodations.
Price Range: $250-$500/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, restaurants, and water sports, with a range of luxury accommodations and amenities.
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, especially during peak season.

**Jolly Harbour** (Bolans)
Best For: Family / Business
Vibe: Jolly Harbour is a lively and modern area, with a mix of shopping, dining, and entertainment options, offering a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere. The area is known for its beautiful beaches and its range of amenities, including a marina and a golf course.
Price Range: $200-$400/night
Pros: Modern amenities, beautiful beaches, and a range of activities, with a range of accommodations available.
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, especially during peak season.

**Valley Church** (Jolly Harbour)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Valley Church is a secluded and upscale area, with a peaceful and natural atmosphere, offering beautiful beaches and stunning views. The area is known for its luxury accommodations and its range of amenities, including a private beach and a spa.
Price Range: $300-$600/night
Pros: Secluded and peaceful, beautiful beaches, and luxury accommodations, with a range of amenities available.
Cons: Can be expensive, and limited options for budget travelers.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to April), to ensure availability and the best rates.
- Consider booking a package deal, including flights and accommodations, for a discounted rate.
- Look for special offers and discounts, especially during the off-season.
- Use online booking platforms, such as Expedia or Booking.com, for a wide range of options and competitive rates.
- Negotiate with hotel staff or property owners for a better rate, especially for long-term stays.
- Be aware of any additional fees or taxes, such as the government-imposed "room tax".
- Consider booking a room with a kitchen or kitchenette, to save on food costs.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night, especially in St. John's.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas, and keep an eye on your belongings.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always agree on a fare before starting your journey.
- Consider staying in a hotel or resort with 24-hour security, for added peace of mind.
- Look for accommodations with a convenient location, close to beaches, restaurants, and other amenities.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting historical sites or attending cultural events.
