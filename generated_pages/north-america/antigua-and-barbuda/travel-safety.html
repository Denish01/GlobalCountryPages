Overall Safety Assessment

Antigua and Barbuda is generally a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates compared to other Caribbean islands. The country has a strong reputation for being a secure and welcoming place for visitors. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and theft can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, the country is susceptible to natural disasters like hurricanes, which can impact travel plans and safety.

Overall, Antigua and Barbuda offers a pleasant and secure environment for tourists, with a low risk of violent crime and a strong police presence. The local authorities take the safety of visitors seriously, and there are various initiatives in place to prevent and respond to crimes. Nevertheless, it's crucial to remain aware of your surroundings, keep valuables secure, and follow local advice to minimize the risk of encountering any problems. By taking these precautions and staying informed, you can have a wonderful and safe experience in Antigua and Barbuda.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas, particularly in St. John's and English Harbour
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable taxi services
  • Be aware of scams targeting tourists, such as fake lottery winnings or investment opportunities
  • Keep valuables secure and use hotel safes or lockboxes
  • Be mindful of your surroundings when using ATMs or exchanging money
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night
  • Be cautious of unlicensed water sports operators and ensure that you use reputable companies
  • Be aware of the risk of credit card skimming and monitor your accounts regularly

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Antigua and Barbuda, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
  • Water Safety: Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is recommended for sensitive stomachs
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are available, but can be limited, especially on the island of Barbuda. It's recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical evacuations
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are available in St. John's and other major towns, but can be limited in rural areas

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always lock your hotel room door and use the safe or lockbox
  • Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas
  • Use reputable taxi services and agree on the fare before you start your journey
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
  • Keep your hotel room number and other personal information private
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night
  • Follow local advice and instructions from authorities, especially during hurricane season
  • Keep your valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
