Location & Borders

Antigua and Barbuda is a sovereign island nation located in the eastern Caribbean Sea, where the Caribbean Sea meets the Atlantic Ocean. The country is situated about 40 kilometers south of Saint Kitts and Nevis, and approximately 600 kilometers southeast of Puerto Rico. The nation consists of two major islands, Antigua and Barbuda, as well as several smaller islands. The country is surrounded by the Caribbean Sea to the south and west, and the Atlantic Ocean to the north and east.

The borders of Antigua and Barbuda are entirely maritime, with no land borders with any other country. The country's closest neighbors are Saint Kitts and Nevis to the northwest, and Montserrat to the southwest. The island nation is a popular tourist destination due to its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant culture. The capital city, St. John's, is located on the northwest coast of Antigua and is the country's largest city and commercial center.

Terrain & Landscape

The terrain of Antigua and Barbuda is generally low-lying and flat, with some hills and valleys. The highest point on the islands is Mount Obama, which is located on Antigua and has an elevation of 402 meters. The islands are volcanic in origin, and the landscape is characterized by rolling hills, scenic valleys, and rugged coastlines. The interior of the islands is covered with lush tropical forests, while the coastlines are lined with beautiful white-sand beaches and coral reefs.

The island of Barbuda is smaller and more rugged than Antigua, with a landscape dominated by limestone cliffs, rocky shores, and salt ponds. The island is home to a large frigate bird sanctuary, and its beaches are known for their pink sand. The coastal areas of both islands are generally flat and low-lying, with numerous bays, coves, and inlets. The islands have a number of scenic beaches, including Dickenson Bay, Runaway Bay, and Valley Church Beach, which are popular with tourists.

The landscape of Antigua and Barbuda has been shaped by its geological history, with volcanic activity and erosion creating the islands' unique terrain. The islands are also home to a number of caves, including the famous Hell's Gate cave on Antigua, which features stunning rock formations and underground pools. The country's natural beauty and diverse landscape make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with activities such as hiking, snorkeling, and sailing available.
