[SECTION] How Antigua and Barbuda Is Divided [/SECTION]
Antigua and Barbuda is a sovereign nation divided into 6 parishes and 2 dependencies. The parishes are Saint George, Saint John, Saint Mary, Saint Paul, Saint Peter, and Saint Philip, while the dependencies are the islands of Barbuda and Redonda. This administrative structure allows for local governance and decision-making, with the capital city of St. John's serving as the central hub for national administration. The parishes are further divided into smaller communities and villages, each with their own unique character and charm.

The division of Antigua and Barbuda into parishes and dependencies reflects the country's history and geography. The parishes were established by the British colonial administration, while the dependencies of Barbuda and Redonda are smaller islands that are part of the nation but have distinct cultural and ecological identities. This administrative structure has been maintained since independence in 1981 and continues to shape the country's governance and development.
