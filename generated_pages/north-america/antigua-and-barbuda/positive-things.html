Cultural Treasures

  • The Nelson's Dockyard National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination, showcasing the country's rich maritime history.
  • The Museum of Antigua and Barbuda features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the country's history and culture.
  • The Antigua Carnival is a vibrant celebration of music, dance, and costumes, and is considered one of the best in the Caribbean.
  • The Barbuda Codrington Village is a historic village that showcases the country's colonial past and features several historic buildings and landmarks.
  • The Antigua and Barbuda Folklore and Cultural Centre preserves and promotes the country's folklore and cultural heritage through music, dance, and theater performances.

People & Global Impact

  • Sir Vivian Richards is a world-renowned cricketer and one of the greatest batsmen of all time, who has made significant contributions to the sport.
  • The Antiguan and Barbudan diaspora has made significant contributions to the development of the country, with many individuals returning to invest in the economy and support community development projects.
  • Dame Louise Lake-Tack is a prominent figure in Antiguan and Barbudan society, who has made significant contributions to the country's development and has served as the Governor-General of the country.
  • The country's values of hospitality and community are highly valued, with many visitors commenting on the warm welcome they receive from the locals.

What Visitors Love

  • The country's beautiful beaches, including Dickenson Bay and Valley Church Beach, are a major draw for tourists, with their crystal-clear waters and powdery white sand.
  • The local cuisine, which features a mix of Caribbean, African, and European flavors, is a highlight of many visitors' trips, with popular dishes including pepper pot soup and fungi.
  • The historic sites, including Nelson's Dockyard and Fort James, provide a glimpse into the country's rich history and cultural heritage.
  • The vibrant nightlife, with many bars, clubs, and restaurants to choose from, is a major attraction for young visitors.
  • The outdoor activities, including hiking, sailing, and snorkeling, provide plenty of opportunities for adventure and exploration.
  • The friendly locals, who are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature, make visitors feel at home.

Hidden Gems

  • The Frigate Bird Sanctuary on Barbuda is a unique and fascinating place to visit, with a large colony of frigate birds that can be seen nesting and flying overhead.
  • The Indian Town National Park is a scenic park that features several hiking trails, scenic views, and historic sites, including the Indian Town Point.
  • The Antigua and Barbuda Humane Society is a local organization that works to protect and care for animals, and is a great place to visit for animal lovers.
  • The Morris Bay area is a picturesque and tranquil spot, with a beautiful beach and several restaurants and bars to choose from.
