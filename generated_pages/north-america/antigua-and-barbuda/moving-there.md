# Moving to Antigua and Barbuda — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Antigua and Barbuda, you will need to research and understand the various visa and residency options, relocation procedures, and cultural adjustments required to make a successful transition to this Caribbean island nation.

[SECTION] Why People Move to Antigua and Barbuda [/SECTION]
Antigua and Barbuda is a popular destination for expats and tourists alike, drawn by its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant culture. The country offers a high quality of life, with a mix of modern amenities and traditional Caribbean charm. Many expats are attracted to the island's laid-back atmosphere, making it an ideal location for those seeking a relaxing and peaceful lifestyle. The majority of expats in Antigua and Barbuda are retirees or individuals working in the tourism and hospitality industries, with a smaller number of entrepreneurs and digital nomads also calling the island home. The expat community is relatively small, with a population of around 10,000, but it is active and welcoming, with many social groups and clubs available to join.

The quality of life in Antigua and Barbuda is generally high, with good healthcare facilities, a range of educational options, and a low crime rate. The country has a well-developed infrastructure, with modern amenities such as shopping centers, restaurants, and entertainment venues. However, the cost of living in Antigua and Barbuda can be relatively high, particularly when it comes to housing and food. Despite this, many expats find that the benefits of living in Antigua and Barbuda, including its beautiful natural environment and friendly people, make it an attractive and rewarding place to call home.

In terms of demographics, the expat community in Antigua and Barbuda is relatively diverse, with people from a range of countries and backgrounds. The majority of expats are from the United Kingdom, United States, and Canada, but there are also significant numbers of expats from other European countries, as well as from the Caribbean and Central and South America.

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- Opening a bank account in Antigua and Barbuda can be a straightforward process, with several international banks having branches on the island.
- Money transfers can be made through banks or online services, such as Western Union or PayPal.
- The local currency is the Eastern Caribbean dollar (XCD), although US dollars are widely accepted.
- Credit cards, such as Visa and Mastercard, are widely accepted in Antigua and Barbuda.
- It is recommended to have a mix of cash, credit cards, and debit cards to access your funds in Antigua and Barbuda.
