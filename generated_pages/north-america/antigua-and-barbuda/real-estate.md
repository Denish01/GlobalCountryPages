[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The property market in Antigua and Barbuda is characterized by a mix of luxury developments, residential homes, and commercial properties, with a strong focus on tourism and hospitality. The market has experienced steady growth in recent years, driven by government incentives, infrastructure development, and a growing demand for **second homes** and **vacation rentals**. The price trajectory has been upward, with a notable increase in high-end properties and luxury developments. Urban areas, such as St. John's and English Harbour, tend to be more expensive than rural areas, which offer a more affordable option for buyers.

The market is also influenced by the country's **Citizenship by Investment Program**, which offers foreign buyers the opportunity to obtain citizenship in exchange for a significant investment in property or other qualifying projects. This program has attracted a number of high-net-worth individuals and families, contributing to the growth of the luxury property market. Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, the property market in Antigua and Barbuda has shown resilience, with many buyers taking advantage of the country's **relaxed travel restrictions** and **favourable tax environment**.

In terms of trends, there is a growing demand for **sustainable** and **eco-friendly** properties, as well as a increasing interest in **island living** and **wellness retreats**. Buyers are also looking for properties with **modern amenities** and **high-quality finishes**, reflecting the country's growing reputation as a luxury tourist destination.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreign buyers can purchase property in Antigua and Barbuda, but may need to obtain an **Alien Landholding License** from the government.
- There are no restrictions on the type of property that can be purchased, but buyers may need to meet certain **investment thresholds** to qualify for residency or citizenship.
- Foreign buyers can also consider **leasehold** options, which can provide a more affordable entry point into the market.
- Buyers may need to establish a **local company** or **trust** to hold the property, depending on their individual circumstances.
- It is recommended that foreign buyers work with a **reputable lawyer** or **real estate agent** to navigate the buying process and ensure compliance with all relevant regulations.

[SECTION] Buying Process [/SECTION]
1. Research the market and identify a suitable property.
2. Work with a real estate agent to view properties and negotiate the purchase price.
3. Conduct due diligence on the property, including **title searches** and **property inspections**.
4. Obtain an **Alien Landholding License** if required.
5. Establish a local company or trust to hold the property if necessary.
6. Open a local bank account to pay for the property and handle future transactions.
7. Transfer the funds to complete the purchase.
8. Register the property with the relevant authorities.
9. Obtain any necessary **permits** and **licenses** to occupy or rent the property.
10. Consider working with a **property management company** to handle ongoing maintenance and rental activities.

[SECTION] Rental Market [/SECTION]
- The rental market in Antigua and Barbuda is driven by tourism, with a high demand for **short-term rentals** and **vacation properties**.
- **Tenant rights** are protected under local law, and renters should be aware of their obligations and responsibilities.
- Typical **lease terms** range from 6-12 months, with some properties available for longer-term rentals.
- **Deposit norms** vary, but typically range from 1-2 months' rent.
- Properties are often **furnished** and **equipped** with modern amenities, reflecting the country's focus on luxury tourism.
