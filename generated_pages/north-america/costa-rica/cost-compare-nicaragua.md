[SECTION] Overview: Costa Rica vs Nicaragua [/SECTION]
Costa Rica and Nicaragua are two neighboring countries in Central America, with distinct economic profiles and cost of living standards. Costa Rica is known for its high standard of living, stable economy, and well-developed tourism industry, which contributes to its relatively higher cost of living. In contrast, Nicaragua is one of the poorest countries in Central America, with a lower cost of living and a more challenging economic environment. Overall, Nicaragua offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining their cost of living. Costa Rica has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing, which drives up prices for goods and services. Nicaragua, on the other hand, has a more limited economy, with a greater reliance on agriculture and remittances from abroad. As a result, Nicaragua's cost of living is generally lower, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle. The cost difference between the two countries can range from 30-40%, with Nicaragua being the more affordable option.
