Business Environment

Costa Rica has a long tradition of democratic stability and a strong institutional framework, which provides a solid foundation for doing business. The government has implemented various policies to attract foreign investment, including the creation of free trade zones and investment promotion agencies. However, the country still faces challenges related to bureaucracy and corruption, which can increase the time and cost of doing business. The government has taken steps to address these issues, including the implementation of electronic procedures and transparency measures.

The overall business climate in Costa Rica is favorable, with a highly educated workforce and a strategic location that provides easy access to the US and other Central American markets. The country has also made significant investments in infrastructure, including the expansion of its ports and airports. However, the business environment can be competitive, and companies must be prepared to adapt to the local market and regulatory requirements.

Costa Rica has a favorable attitude towards foreign investment, with a range of incentives and benefits available to foreign investors. The government has established a one-stop shop for foreign investors, which provides assistance with the registration and licensing process. Additionally, the country has a network of trade agreements, including the US-Central America Free Trade Agreement, which provides preferential access to the US market.

Foreign Direct Investment

  • Costa Rica has a long history of attracting foreign direct investment, with a focus on the US and other Central American countries.
  • The main investing countries in Costa Rica are the US, China, and Spain.
  • The government offers a range of incentives to foreign investors, including tax breaks and subsidies.
  • The restricted sectors for foreign investment include fishing and mining, which are subject to special regulations and permits.
  • Costa Rica has a stable and predictable investment climate, with a strong rule of law and a highly developed financial system.

Legal & Regulatory Framework

  • The business laws in Costa Rica are based on the Civil Code and the Commercial Code, which provide a framework for commercial activities.
  • Intellectual property protection is provided by the Intellectual Property Law, which includes provisions for patents, trademarks, and copyrights.
  • The labor laws in Costa Rica are comprehensive and provide strong protections for workers, including a minimum wage and benefits.
  • The dispute resolution process in Costa Rica is based on the Civil Procedure Code, which provides for mediation, arbitration, and litigation.
  • The tax laws in Costa Rica are based on the Tax Code, which includes provisions for corporate and individual taxation.
  • The environmental regulations in Costa Rica are strict and provide protections for the country's natural resources and biodiversity.

Challenges & Risks

  • One of the main challenges facing businesses in Costa Rica is the bureaucracy, which can be slow and inefficient.
  • Corruption is also a risk in Costa Rica, particularly in the public sector.
  • The infrastructure in Costa Rica can be underdeveloped, particularly in rural areas.
  • The currency risk is relatively low in Costa Rica, as the colon is pegged to the US dollar.
  • The political risk is also relatively low, as Costa Rica has a stable and democratic government.
  • The market limitations in Costa Rica can be a challenge, particularly for companies that are looking to export to other countries.

Free Zones & Incentives

  • Costa Rica has a range of free zones and incentives available to foreign investors, including the Free Zone Regime and the Tourism Incentives Law.
  • The free zones in Costa Rica provide a range of benefits, including tax exemptions and streamlined procedures.
  • The investment promotion agencies in Costa Rica, such as the Costa Rican Investment Promotion Agency (CINDE), provide assistance and support to foreign investors.
  • The tax incentives in Costa Rica include corporate tax exemptions and value-added tax exemptions, which can be available to companies that invest in certain sectors or regions.
