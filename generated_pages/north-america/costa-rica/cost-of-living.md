[SECTION] How Expensive Is Costa Rica? [/SECTION]
Costa Rica is considered an affordable country to visit or live in, with a cost of living that is relatively lower compared to other countries in North America. However, it is more expensive than its neighboring countries, Nicaragua and Panama. The cost of living in Costa Rica is influenced by its growing economy, tourism industry, and high standard of living. Overall, Costa Rica offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists, expats, and digital nomads.

In comparison to global averages, Costa Rica's cost of living is slightly higher than the global average, but still relatively affordable. The country's unique blend of urban and rural areas, as well as its eco-tourism industry, contribute to its distinct cost of living. With a little planning and research, visitors and residents can enjoy a comfortable lifestyle in Costa Rica without breaking the bank.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider living outside of city centers, such as San Jose, to reduce rent costs
- Eat at local eateries and street food stalls to save on food expenses
- Use public transportation, such as buses and shuttles, instead of taxis or rental cars
- Take advantage of free or low-cost activities, such as visiting beaches and national parks
- Learn some basic Spanish to negotiate prices and interact with locals
- Shop at local markets and stores instead of tourist-oriented shops and malls

By following these tips and being mindful of expenses, visitors and residents can enjoy a comfortable and affordable lifestyle in Costa Rica. With its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and friendly locals, Costa Rica is an excellent choice for anyone looking to experience the best of Central America.
