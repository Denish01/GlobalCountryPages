[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Costa Rica has a well-developed transportation network, with a variety of options available for getting around, including public buses, taxis, ride-hailing services, and rental cars. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent bus services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in Costa Rica get around by bus, with the public bus system being an affordable and convenient option. However, in larger cities like San Jose, traffic can be a problem, and ride-hailing services or taxis may be a better option.

The country's geography, with its mountainous terrain and rugged coastline, can make transportation challenging in some areas. However, the government has invested heavily in improving the road network, and many roads are now paved and well-maintained. Additionally, there are many alternative transportation options available, such as shuttle services and bike rentals, which can be a great way to get around, especially in more rural areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using public buses or shuttle services, which are often cheaper than taxis or ride-hailing services.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Cabify to get around, especially in larger cities.
- Always negotiate the fare with taxi drivers before you start your journey, and make sure the meter is turned on.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid traveling alone in isolated areas.
- Consider renting a car for longer trips or to explore more rural areas, but be aware of the cost of fuel and parking.
- Use apps like Waze or Google Maps to navigate the roads and avoid traffic congestion.
