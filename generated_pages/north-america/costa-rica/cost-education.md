# Education Costs in Costa Rica

The cost of education in Costa Rica varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from a few thousand to over $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Costa Rica is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the largest and most accessible. Public schools in Costa Rica are generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on **bilingual education** and **environmental awareness**. The language of instruction is Spanish, but many schools also offer English classes, making it an attractive option for expat families. Compulsory education in Costa Rica lasts for 11 years, from primary school to secondary school, and is free for all citizens. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a more personalized education and often have smaller class sizes, but can be quite expensive.

In addition to public and private schools, Costa Rica is also home to a number of international schools that cater to expat families and offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **US-style curriculum**. These schools are often located in urban areas, such as San Jose, and can be a good option for families who want their children to receive a high-quality education in a familiar curriculum. However, it's worth noting that international schools can be very expensive, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to over $15,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Tips [/SECTION]
- The **University of Costa Rica** is considered one of the best universities in the country, with a range of high-quality programs.
- **Scholarships** are available for both local and international students, but can be competitive.
- **Online learning options** are available for some programs, but may not be as comprehensive as traditional classroom-based education.
- Education quality in Costa Rica is generally considered to be high compared to other countries in the region.
- **Bilingual education** is highly valued in Costa Rica, and many schools offer English classes or bilingual programs.
- **Expat families** may want to consider enrolling their children in international schools, which can offer a more familiar curriculum and teaching style.
