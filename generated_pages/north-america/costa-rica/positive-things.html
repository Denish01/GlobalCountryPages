Cultural Treasures

  • The Tortuguero Canals, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a network of canals and rivers that are home to a diverse array of wildlife, including sea turtles, monkeys, and sloths.
  • The Pre-Columbian Gold Museum in San Jose is one of the most important collections of pre-Columbian art in the Americas, with over 1,600 pieces on display.
  • The Fiestas de San Jose are a traditional celebration held in San Jose, featuring music, dance, and traditional food.
  • The Boruca Mask Festival is a unique and colorful celebration, where locals wear traditional masks and costumes to celebrate the region's indigenous heritage.
  • The National Theater of Costa Rica is a stunning example of neoclassical architecture, hosting a variety of performances, including concerts, plays, and ballets.

People & Global Impact

  • Oscar Arias, a former President of Costa Rica, was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1987 for his efforts to promote peace and democracy in Central America.
  • Franklin Chang Diaz, a Costa Rican-American astronaut, was the first Latin American to travel to space, and has made significant contributions to the field of astronautics.
  • The Costa Rican diaspora has made significant contributions to the global community, with many notable figures in fields such as science, arts, and politics, including the former President of the World Bank, Jose Figueres.
  • The Tico culture values community, family, and social cohesion, with a strong emphasis on respect for the environment and a commitment to peace and democracy.

What Visitors Love

  • The warm hospitality of the Costa Rican people, who are known for their friendly and welcoming nature.
  • The delicious cuisine, which features a mix of Spanish, African, and indigenous influences, with popular dishes like gallo pinto, casados, and empanadas.
  • The stunning natural beauty, with a diverse range of landscapes, including beaches, rainforests, and mountains.
  • The exciting outdoor activities, such as surfing, ziplining, and hiking, which offer a range of adventures for all ages and skill levels.
  • The rich cultural heritage, with a mix of indigenous, Spanish, and African influences, reflected in the country's music, dance, and art.
  • The relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, with a strong emphasis on well-being and sustainability, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a tranquil and rejuvenating experience.

Hidden Gems

  • The Monteverde Cloud Forest, a unique and fascinating ecosystem, with a stunning array of flora and fauna, including the resplendent quetzal, a iconic bird species.
  • The Corcovado National Park, a remote and pristine wilderness area, with a diverse range of wildlife, including monkeys, sloths, and jaguars.
  • The Tortuga Island, a peaceful and secluded island, with stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a range of outdoor activities, including snorkeling and kayaking.
  • The San Gerardo de Dota, a charming and picturesque town, nestled in the mountains, with a strong focus on sustainable tourism and community development.
