[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
Costa Rica has a long tradition of **democratic stability** and a strong **institutional framework**, which provides a solid foundation for doing business. The government has implemented various policies to attract foreign investment, including the creation of **free trade zones** and **investment promotion agencies**. However, the country still faces challenges related to **bureaucracy** and **corruption**, which can increase the time and cost of doing business. The government has taken steps to address these issues, including the implementation of **electronic procedures** and **transparency measures**.

The overall business climate in Costa Rica is favorable, with a **highly educated workforce** and a **strategic location** that provides easy access to the US and other Central American markets. The country has also made significant investments in **infrastructure**, including the expansion of its ports and airports. However, the business environment can be **competitive**, and companies must be prepared to adapt to the local market and regulatory requirements.

Costa Rica has a **favorable attitude towards foreign investment**, with a range of incentives and benefits available to foreign investors. The government has established a **one-stop shop** for foreign investors, which provides assistance with the registration and licensing process. Additionally, the country has a **network of trade agreements**, including the US-Central America Free Trade Agreement, which provides preferential access to the US market.
