In 1838, Costa Rica became an independent republic, with **Juan Mora Fernández** serving as its first head of state. The country's early years of independence were marked by political instability, with various factions vying for power. Costa Rica has also been influenced by its cultural heritage, with a mix of indigenous, Spanish, and African traditions.
