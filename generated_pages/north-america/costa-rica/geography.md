[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Costa Rica is a **sovereign** country located in **Central America**, connecting **North America** to **South America**. It is situated between two large bodies of water, the **Pacific Ocean** to the west and the **Caribbean Sea** to the east. The country shares borders with **Nicaragua** to the north and **Panama** to the southeast. This unique geography creates a variety of ecosystems and microclimates, making Costa Rica a popular destination for tourists and scientists alike.

The country's strategic location allows for easy access to both the Pacific and Caribbean coasts, making it an important hub for trade and commerce. The **Inter-American Highway**, which spans from the United States to Panama, passes through Costa Rica, connecting the country to its neighbors and the rest of the continent. The country's borders are well-defined, with a total length of approximately 639 kilometers with Nicaragua and 330 kilometers with Panama.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Costa Rica's terrain is characterized by a mix of **mountain ranges**, **volcanic regions**, and **coastal plains**. The **Central Mountain Range**, which includes the country's highest peak, **Cerro Chirripó**, stretches from the north to the southeast. This range is home to several **active volcanoes**, including **Arenal** and **Poás**, which are popular tourist destinations. The **Coastal Mountain Range** runs along the Pacific coast, creating a scenic landscape of rugged hills and valleys.

The country's landscape is also dotted with **rivers**, **lakes**, and **wetlands**, which provide habitats for a wide variety of **wildlife**. The **Rio Grande de Tarcoles**, which flows into the Pacific Ocean, is one of the country's most important rivers. Costa Rica's **coastal regions** are known for their beautiful **beaches**, **coral reefs**, and **mangrove forests**, which are home to a diverse array of **marine life**. The country's unique geography and geology have created a rich and varied landscape, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

The country's varied terrain and climate have also created a range of **ecosystems**, including **tropical rainforests**, **cloud forests**, and **dry forests**. These ecosystems are home to a vast array of **plant and animal species**, many of which are found nowhere else in the world. The **Osa Peninsula**, located on the southern coast, is one of the most **biodiverse** regions on the planet, with a wide variety of **wildlife** and **vegetation**.
