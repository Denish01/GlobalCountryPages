[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Costa Rica is generally a safe country to visit, with low levels of violent crime and a strong focus on tourism. The country has a well-developed infrastructure and a high standard of living, which contributes to its safety. However, as with any country, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and tourists should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, can be a problem in tourist areas, and visitors should be mindful of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night.

Overall, Costa Rica is a great destination for travelers, with a wide range of activities and experiences to enjoy, from surfing and hiking to wildlife watching and relaxation. With some basic precautions and awareness of the local environment, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip. It's worth noting that Costa Rica has a dedicated tourist police force, which provides an additional layer of security and support for visitors. The country also has a well-developed system for reporting and responding to crimes, which helps to ensure that visitors receive the help they need in case of an emergency.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas, such as buses and markets
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use **credit cards** or **traveler's checks** instead
- Be cautious of **scams** involving **ATM** machines, such as card skimming or phishing
- Watch out for **overpriced** tours or activities, and always **research** and **compare** prices before booking
- Be aware of **traffic** and **road conditions**, especially when driving or crossing the street
- Avoid **drinking tap water**, and opt for **bottled water** instead
- Be cautious of **solicitations** or **approaches** from strangers, especially in tourist areas

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended, but not required
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** High-quality medical facilities are available in San Jose and other major cities, but may be limited in rural areas
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but may not always have the same medications as those found in other countries

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always **lock** your hotel room door and **use** the safe
- Be **aware** of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night
- Keep **valuable** items secure and **out of sight**
- Use **reputable** taxi services or **ride-sharing** apps
- Avoid **traveling** alone at night, especially in rural areas
- Keep **important phone numbers** handy, such as the emergency number and tourist police
- Be **respectful** of local customs and **environment**, and take steps to **reduce** your impact on the environment

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- **Research** and **plan** ahead, especially when traveling to rural areas
- Be **aware** of your surroundings and **trust** your instincts
- Avoid **traveling** alone at night, and opt for **group tours** or **accommodations** with other travelers
- Be **respectful** of local customs and **dress** modestly, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events
