Location & Borders

Costa Rica is a sovereign country located in Central America, connecting North America to South America. It is situated between two large bodies of water, the Pacific Ocean to the west and the Caribbean Sea to the east. The country shares borders with Nicaragua to the north and Panama to the southeast. This unique geography creates a variety of ecosystems and microclimates, making Costa Rica a popular destination for tourists and scientists alike.

The country's strategic location allows for easy access to both the Pacific and Caribbean coasts, making it an important hub for trade and commerce. The Inter-American Highway, which spans from the United States to Panama, passes through Costa Rica, connecting the country to its neighbors and the rest of the continent. The country's borders are well-defined, with a total length of approximately 639 kilometers with Nicaragua and 330 kilometers with Panama.
