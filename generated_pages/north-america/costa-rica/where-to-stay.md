# Where to Stay in Costa Rica — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To determine where to stay in Costa Rica, consider the top areas of San Jose, Tamarindo, Puerto Viejo, Manuel Antonio, Arenal, and Monteverde, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top 6 areas/neighborhoods:

**San Jose** (San Jose)
Best For: Business / Culture
Vibe: San Jose is a bustling city with a rich cultural scene, offering a mix of historic and modern attractions, from museums to nightlife. The city has a lively atmosphere, with many restaurants, bars, and shops to explore.
Price Range: $80-250/night
Pros: Convenient location, rich cultural scene, many amenities
Cons: Traffic and noise can be overwhelming

**Tamarindo** (Tamarindo)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Tamarindo is a coastal town with a laid-back surfer vibe, offering beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, and a variety of restaurants and shops. The town has a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for families and those looking for a beach vacation.
Price Range: $150-400/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, variety of amenities
Cons: Can be crowded and expensive during peak season

**Puerto Viejo** (Puerto Viejo)
Best For: Backpacker / Surfer
Vibe: Puerto Viejo is a laid-back coastal town with a strong surfer culture, offering a relaxed atmosphere, beautiful beaches, and a variety of outdoor activities. The town has a bohemian vibe, with many budget-friendly options for accommodation and dining.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: Affordable, beautiful beaches, outdoor activities
Cons: Limited amenities and services

**Manuel Antonio** (Manuel Antonio)
Best For: Nature / Adventure
Vibe: Manuel Antonio is a coastal town surrounded by a national park, offering a mix of outdoor activities, beautiful beaches, and wildlife viewing opportunities. The town has a natural and serene atmosphere, perfect for those looking to connect with nature.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Beautiful national park, outdoor activities, wildlife viewing
Cons: Can be crowded and expensive during peak season

**Arenal** (Arenal)
Best For: Adventure / Nature
Vibe: Arenal is a town located near a volcanic lake, offering a mix of outdoor activities, hot springs, and stunning natural scenery. The town has a peaceful atmosphere, perfect for those looking to relax and connect with nature.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Beautiful scenery, outdoor activities, hot springs
Cons: Limited amenities and services

**Monteverde** (Monteverde)
Best For: Nature / Adventure
Vibe: Monteverde is a cloud forest town with a unique and breathtaking natural scenery, offering a mix of outdoor activities, wildlife viewing, and eco-lodges. The town has a serene and natural atmosphere, perfect for those looking to connect with nature.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Unique natural scenery, outdoor activities, eco-lodges
Cons: Can be cool and rainy, limited amenities and services

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to April)
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, and Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices directly with hotel owners or managers, especially for long-term stays
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during peak season and decreasing during the off-season
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs
- Look for package deals that include activities and transportation to save money
- Use travel agencies or tour operators to book accommodations and activities, especially for customized itineraries

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas with high crime rates, such as some neighborhoods in San Jose
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and take necessary precautions to stay safe
- Choose accommodations with good security measures, such as 24-hour reception and secure parking
- Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation, such as buses and taxis
- Research the location before booking to ensure it is close to amenities and activities
- Be mindful of natural hazards, such as earthquakes and volcanic eruptions, and stay informed about local conditions
- Stay healthy by taking precautions against diseases, such as Zika and dengue fever, and staying hydrated in the tropical climate
