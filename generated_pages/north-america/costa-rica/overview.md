[SECTION] What Is Costa Rica? [/SECTION]
Costa Rica is a **sovereign** country located in **Central America**, bordering the Caribbean Sea to the east and the Pacific Ocean to the west. It is situated between **Nicaragua** to the north and **Panama** to the south, with a total area of approximately 51,100 square kilometers. Costa Rica is known for its rich biodiversity, stunning natural beauty, and eco-tourism opportunities, earning it the nickname "**Switzerland of the Americas**".

Costa Rica is a popular destination for tourists and nature lovers, with its tropical climate, beautiful beaches, and lush rainforests. The country has a strong focus on conservation and sustainability, with over 25% of its land protected as national parks and wildlife refuges. Costa Rica is also home to a growing economy, with a mix of agriculture, manufacturing, and services, and a high standard of living compared to other countries in the region.

The country has a unique cultural identity, shaped by its Spanish colonial history, African influences, and indigenous heritage. Costa Ricans, also known as "**Ticos**", are known for their warm hospitality, love of nature, and strong sense of community. With its modern infrastructure, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty, Costa Rica is an attractive destination for visitors, investors, and expats alike.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1502 - Christopher Columbus arrives in Costa Rica on his fourth voyage to the New World, marking the beginning of Spanish colonization.
2. 1821 - Costa Rica gains independence from Spain, along with other Central American countries, and becomes part of the Federal Republic of Central America.
3. 1838 - Costa Rica becomes an independent republic, with José María Castro Madriz as its first president.
4. 1948 - A civil war breaks out in Costa Rica, leading to the abolition of the army and the establishment of a new constitution.
5. 1983 - Costa Rica signs a peace treaty with Nicaragua, ending a long-standing border dispute.
6. 1994 - Costa Rica joins the **Central American Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA)**, promoting trade and economic integration with other regional countries.
7. 2010 - Costa Rica celebrates its bicentennial anniversary, marking 200 years of independence from Spain.

[SECTION] What Makes Costa Rica Unique [/SECTION]
- Costa Rica is home to over 5% of the world's **biodiversity**, despite covering only 0.03% of the Earth's surface.
- The country has a high level of **human development**, with a strong focus on education, healthcare, and social welfare.
- Costa Rica is a global leader in **renewable energy**, with over 90% of its electricity generated from hydro, wind, and geothermal sources.
- The country has a unique **cultural heritage**, with a mix of Spanish, African, and indigenous influences, reflected in its music, dance, and art.
- Costa Rica is known for its **ecotourism** opportunities, with a wide range of activities, including hiking, birdwatching, surfing, and wildlife watching.

Costa Rica is a sovereign country in Central America, known for its stunning natural beauty, rich biodiversity, and strong focus on conservation and sustainability, making it a popular destination for eco-tourists and nature lovers.
