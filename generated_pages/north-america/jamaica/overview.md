[SECTION] What Is Jamaica? [/SECTION]
Jamaica is a **sovereign** island nation located in the Caribbean Sea, situated to the south of Cuba and to the west of Haiti. With a population of approximately 3.0 million people, Jamaica is a significant player in the region, known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant music scene. The country's geographic position in the Caribbean makes it prone to natural disasters such as hurricanes, but its strategic location also facilitates trade and tourism with other countries in the region.

Jamaica is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world with its beautiful beaches, lush mountains, and vibrant culture. The country is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including many species of birds, reptiles, and amphibians that are found nowhere else in the world. Jamaica's capital city, Kingston, is a bustling metropolis with a rich history and culture, and is home to many of the country's major institutions, including the government, universities, and museums.

Jamaica is also known for its significant contributions to music, particularly **reggae** and **ska**, which have had a major impact on the global music scene. The country has produced many world-renowned musicians, including Bob Marley, Peter Tosh, and Toots and the Maytals, who have helped to promote Jamaican music and culture around the world. Jamaica's unique cultural heritage, which reflects the country's history as a British colony and its African, Spanish, and indigenous influences, is an important part of its identity and a major draw for tourists and cultural enthusiasts.

4. 1944 - Jamaica is granted internal self-government, with Alexander Bustamante as the country's first prime minister.
5. 1962 - Jamaica gains independence from Britain, with Bustamante as the country's first prime minister.
