[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Jamaica has a relatively well-developed transportation system, with various modes of transportation available, including buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent bus services connecting major towns and cities. However, the roads can be congested, especially in urban areas, and traffic jams are common. Most people in Jamaica get around using public transportation, with buses and route taxis being the most popular modes of transport.

The country's geography, with its mountainous terrain and coastal roads, can make traveling by car or bus challenging, especially during the rainy season. Despite this, the scenic routes and stunning natural beauty of Jamaica make it a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. The transportation system in Jamaica is also relatively affordable, with prices comparable to other Caribbean islands. However, prices can vary depending on the location, with urban areas tend to be more expensive than rural areas.
