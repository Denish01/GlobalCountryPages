[SECTION] What Is Bahamas? [/SECTION]
The Bahamas is a **sovereign** island nation located in the Atlantic Ocean, southeast of the United States. It is an archipelago of over 3,000 islands, cays, and islets, with only about 30 of them inhabited. The Bahamas is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant culture. As a popular tourist destination, the country offers a unique blend of relaxation, adventure, and entertainment.

Geographically, the Bahamas is situated in the northern part of the Caribbean, with the United States to the north and Cuba to the southwest. The country's strategic location has made it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region. The Bahamas is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, with many species found nowhere else in the world. The country's coral reefs, in particular, are a major attraction for scuba divers and snorkelers.

The Bahamas has a long history of human settlement, with the first inhabitants arriving over 1,000 years ago. Today, the country is a popular destination for tourists, with millions of visitors arriving each year to enjoy its beautiful beaches, vibrant culture, and wide range of activities. From swimming with dolphins to exploring the country's rich history, there's something for everyone in the Bahamas.
