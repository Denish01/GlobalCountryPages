# Education Costs in Bahamas

The cost of education in the Bahamas varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $2,000 to $10,000 or more per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in the Bahamas is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being the largest. Public schools are generally free or subsidized, but the quality of education can vary depending on the location and resources available. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a higher quality of education, but at a significantly higher cost. The language of instruction in the Bahamas is **English**, and compulsory education lasts for 12 years, from age 5 to 17. International schools are also available, offering a range of curricula, including **IB**, **AP**, and **British** programs.

The quality of education in the Bahamas is generally considered to be good, with a strong focus on **literacy** and **numeracy**. However, there are some challenges, particularly in the public system, where resources can be limited and teacher training may be inadequate. Despite these challenges, the Bahamas has made significant progress in improving its education system, with a **literacy rate** of over 95%. For expats, private and international schools are often the preferred option, offering a high-quality education and a range of extracurricular activities.
