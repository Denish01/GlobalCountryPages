[SECTION] Overview: Bahamas vs Turks and Caicos [/SECTION]
The Bahamas and Turks and Caicos are two popular destinations in the Caribbean, known for their beautiful beaches, clear waters, and rich cultural heritage. However, when it comes to cost of living, there are some significant differences between the two countries. The Bahamas has a more developed economy, with a stronger focus on tourism and financial services, which has led to a higher GDP per capita. On the other hand, Turks and Caicos has a smaller economy, with a greater reliance on tourism and fishing. As a result, the cost of living in the Bahamas is generally lower than in Turks and Caicos, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in determining the cost of living. The Bahamas has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on manufacturing, agriculture, and financial services, which has helped to keep costs down. In contrast, Turks and Caicos has a more limited economy, with a greater reliance on imports, which has driven up costs. Overall, the Bahamas offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. However, Turks and Caicos has its own unique charm and attractions, and may be worth considering for those who are willing to pay a premium for a more exclusive and secluded experience.
