[SECTION] Overview: Bahamas vs Bermuda [/SECTION]
The Bahamas and Bermuda are two popular island destinations in the North Atlantic, known for their beautiful beaches, clear turquoise waters, and vibrant cultures. However, when it comes to cost of living, these two countries have distinct differences. The Bahamas has a more laid-back and affordable atmosphere, with a lower cost of living compared to Bermuda. The country's economy is driven by tourism, finance, and fishing, which contributes to its relatively lower prices. On the other hand, Bermuda is a more upscale and expensive destination, with a strong financial sector and a high standard of living. The country's economy is driven by finance, insurance, and tourism, which results in higher prices for goods and services.

The cost of living in the Bahamas is generally lower than in Bermuda, with significant differences in housing, food, and transportation costs. For example, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center of Nassau, the capital of the Bahamas, can cost around $1,200 per month, while a similar apartment in Hamilton, the capital of Bermuda, can cost around $2,000 per month. Additionally, the cost of food, transportation, and utilities is also lower in the Bahamas, making it a more attractive destination for those looking for an affordable island lifestyle.
