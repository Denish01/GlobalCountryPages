[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in the US Virgin Islands is modeled after the American education system, with **primary education** starting at age 5 and continuing until age 11, followed by **middle school** from ages 11 to 14, and **high school** from ages 14 to 18. The system is overseen by the US Virgin Islands Department of Education, which is responsible for ensuring that all public schools meet the required standards. The quality of education in the US Virgin Islands is generally good, with a strong focus on **core subjects** such as mathematics, science, and English. Public schools are free and open to all residents, while private schools offer an alternative for those who can afford the tuition fees.

The public education system in the US Virgin Islands is **free and compulsory** for all children between the ages of 5 and 18, and it is divided into three main levels: elementary, middle, and high school. The **elementary level** focuses on basic subjects such as reading, writing, and mathematics, while the **middle school level** introduces students to a wider range of subjects, including science, social studies, and foreign languages. The **high school level** prepares students for college or the workforce, with a range of **academic and vocational programs** available.

In addition to public schools, the US Virgin Islands is also home to a number of **private schools**, which offer an alternative education option for those who can afford the tuition fees. These schools often have smaller class sizes and more resources than public schools, and they may offer a more specialized education in areas such as **arts or technology**.
