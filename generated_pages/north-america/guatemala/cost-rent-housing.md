# Rent & Housing Prices in Guatemala

The cost of rent in Guatemala varies widely depending on location, with average monthly rents for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center ranging from $200-$500 USD, equivalent to 1,500-3,750 GTQ.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
Guatemala's rental market is relatively affordable compared to other countries in the region, with a wide range of options available for expats and locals alike. Typical lease terms are for one year, with a deposit requirement of one to two months' rent. Tenant rights are generally protected by law, with provisions for notice periods and dispute resolution. However, the quality of housing can vary significantly depending on location and age of the property. Compared to its regional neighbors, Guatemala's housing costs are generally lower than those in Mexico and Belize, but higher than those in Honduras and El Salvador.

The housing market in Guatemala is also influenced by the country's economic conditions, with a GDP per capita of $14,369 and an inflation rate of 2.9%. The government's expenditure on education and healthcare also has an impact on the housing market, with 3.1% of GDP spent on education and $392 per capita spent on healthcare. Out-of-pocket health expenditure is relatively high, at 57.2% of total health expenditure, which may affect the affordability of housing for some individuals. Overall, Guatemala's housing market offers a range of options for those looking to rent or buy, from modern apartments in the city center to traditional houses in the suburbs.

[SECTION] Renter Tips for Guatemala [/SECTION]
- Research neighborhoods thoroughly before signing a lease, considering factors such as safety, amenities, and commute time
- Work with a reputable real estate agent to find a property that meets your needs and budget
- Be prepared to negotiate the price, as landlords may be willing to lower the rent for a long-term tenant
- Be aware of scams, such as fake listings or phishing attempts, and never send money without seeing the property in person
- Utilities such as water and electricity are usually not included in the rent, and may be paid separately
- Furnished apartments are common, but unfurnished options are also available, and may be more affordable for long-term renters
