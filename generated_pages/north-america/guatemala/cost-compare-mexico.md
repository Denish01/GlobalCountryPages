[SECTION] Overview: Guatemala vs Mexico [/SECTION]
Guatemala and Mexico are two neighboring countries in North America that offer distinct cultural and economic experiences. While Mexico is a more developed economy with a higher GDP per capita, Guatemala offers a lower cost of living, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees. The economic context of both countries is shaped by their history, geography, and trade relationships, with Guatemala being a more agricultural-based economy and Mexico having a more diversified economy. Overall, Guatemala is generally cheaper than Mexico, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being lower.

The cost of living difference between Guatemala and Mexico is significant, with Guatemala being around 20-30% cheaper. This is reflected in the prices of everyday items such as rent, groceries, and transportation. However, it's worth noting that Mexico has a more developed economy and a higher standard of living, which may be reflected in the quality of services and infrastructure. For example, Mexico has a more extensive public transportation system and better healthcare services. On the other hand, Guatemala offers a more laid-back and traditional way of life, with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty.
