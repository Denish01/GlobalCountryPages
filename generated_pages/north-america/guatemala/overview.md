[SECTION] What Is Guatemala? [/SECTION]
Guatemala is a **sovereign** country located in **North America**, bordered by Mexico to the north and west, Belize to the northeast, Honduras to the east, and El Salvador to the southeast. It is the most populous country in Central America, with a diverse geography that includes **coastlines** on the Pacific Ocean and the Caribbean Sea, as well as a **highland** region with numerous **volcanoes**. Guatemala is known for its rich **cultural heritage**, which is a blend of **indigenous**, **Spanish**, and **African** influences.

Guatemala has a long and complex history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the **Maya** civilization, which flourished in the region from 2000 BCE to 1500 CE. The country was colonized by Spain in the 16th century and gained independence in 1821. Today, Guatemala is a **democratic** country with a growing economy and a strong focus on **tourism**, **agriculture**, and **manufacturing**.

The country's **geographic position** makes it a popular destination for travelers, with its **tropical climate**, **beautiful beaches**, and **lively culture**. Visitors can explore the **ancient ruins** of the Maya civilization, hike through the **cloud forests**, or relax on the **coastal towns** of the Pacific Ocean. Guatemala is also known for its vibrant **markets**, where locals sell **traditional handicrafts**, **textiles**, and **local cuisine**.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1524 - The Spanish conquistador Pedro de Alvarado arrives in Guatemala and begins the colonization of the region.
2. 1821 - Guatemala gains independence from Spain and becomes part of the Federal Republic of Central America.
3. 1839 - The Federal Republic of Central America is dissolved, and Guatemala becomes an independent country.
4. 1944 - A revolution led by Juan José Arévalo and Jacobo Árbenz overthrows the government and establishes a **democratic** regime.
5. 1954 - A **military coup** led by Carlos Castillo Armas overthrows the government of Jacobo Árbenz, leading to a period of **political instability**.
6. 1996 - The government of Guatemala and the **Guatemalan National Revolutionary Unity** sign a **peace agreement**, ending a 36-year **civil war**.
7. 2015 - The **International Commission against Impunity in Guatemala** is established to investigate and prosecute **corruption** and **organized crime**.

[SECTION] What Makes Guatemala Unique [/SECTION]
- Guatemala is home to **33 volcanoes**, with several of them still active, including the famous **Pacaya Volcano**.
- The country has a rich **cultural heritage**, with over 20 **indigenous languages** spoken and a strong tradition of **traditional music** and **dance**.
- Guatemala is known for its **vibrant textiles**, with **colorful fabrics** and **traditional clothing** being an important part of the country's cultural identity.
- The country is home to several **UNESCO World Heritage Sites**, including the **Tikal National Park** and the **Antigua Guatemala** colonial city.
- Guatemala has a **unique cuisine**, with popular dishes such as **jocón**, **pepián**, and **rellenitos**.
