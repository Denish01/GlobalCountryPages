# Guatemala vs Honduras

When deciding between Guatemala and Honduras, the choice ultimately depends on your individual preferences and priorities, as both countries offer unique experiences and attractions. 

[SECTION] Why People Compare Them [/SECTION]
Guatemala and Honduras are often compared due to their geographical proximity and cultural similarities, as both countries are located in Central America and share a common border. They also share a rich Mayan heritage and a mix of colonial and indigenous influences, making them popular destinations for travelers interested in history, culture, and natural beauty. Additionally, both countries offer a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, surfing, and diving, which appeals to adventure-seekers.

[SECTION] Key Differences [/SECTION]
- **Population**: Guatemala has a significantly larger population than Honduras, with over 18 million people compared to Honduras' 10 million.
- **Size**: Honduras is slightly larger than Guatemala in terms of land area, with 112,492 km² compared to Guatemala's 108,889 km².
- **Currency**: Guatemala uses the Quetzal (GTQ) as its official currency, while Honduras uses the Lempira (HNL).
- **Climate**: Guatemala has a more varied climate due to its mountainous terrain, while Honduras has a tropical climate with a Caribbean and Pacific coast.
- **Cost Level**: Honduras is generally considered to be more affordable than Guatemala, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and activities.
- **Safety Level**: Both countries have similar safety concerns, but Guatemala is considered to be slightly safer for tourists.

[SECTION] Key Similarities [/SECTION]
- **Language**: Both countries have Spanish as their official language, making it easy for travelers to communicate and navigate.
- **Culture**: Guatemala and Honduras share a rich Mayan heritage and a mix of colonial and indigenous influences, with many cultural festivals and traditions.
- **Outdoor Activities**: Both countries offer a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, surfing, and diving, due to their natural beauty and diverse landscapes.
- **History**: Both countries have a complex and fascinating history, with many historical sites and landmarks to explore, including Mayan ruins and colonial cities.
