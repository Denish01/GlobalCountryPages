# Transportation Costs in Guatemala

The cost of transportation in Guatemala varies depending on the mode of transport, with a monthly transit pass costing around $25-$30 USD, while a taxi start rate is approximately $2-$3 USD.

[FACTBOX]
Monthly Transit Pass: ($25-$30 USD)

Fuel (per liter): ($0.80-$1.00 USD)

Currency: GTQ (1 USD = approximately 7.5 GTQ)

[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Guatemala has a well-developed public transportation system, with **buses** and **minibuses** being the primary modes of transport for locals and tourists alike. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with Guatemala City having a more comprehensive network of buses and minibuses. Most people in Guatemala get around using public transportation, which is relatively affordable and widely available. However, the quality of roads and traffic conditions can be a challenge, especially during peak hours.

The country's geography, with its mountains and valleys, can make traveling by road challenging, but the scenic routes offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Guatemala City, being the capital, has a more developed transportation system, with a greater frequency of buses and minibuses, while smaller towns and rural areas may have limited transportation options. Despite these challenges, the Guatemalan transportation system is relatively affordable and offers a range of options for travelers.
