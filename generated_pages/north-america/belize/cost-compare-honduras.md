[SECTION] Overview: Belize vs Honduras [/SECTION]
Belize and Honduras are two neighboring countries in Central America, each offering a unique cultural and economic experience. Belize has a more developed economy, with a strong focus on tourism and a relatively high standard of living. Honduras, on the other hand, is a larger country with a more diverse economy, but also faces more significant economic challenges. In terms of cost of living, Belize is generally more expensive than Honduras, with higher prices for housing, food, and transportation. However, the cost difference between the two countries can vary significantly depending on the specific location and lifestyle.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Belize has a more stable economy, with a strong focus on tourism and a relatively high GDP per capita. Honduras, on the other hand, has a more diverse economy, with a larger industrial sector and a stronger focus on exports. However, Honduras also faces more significant economic challenges, including higher inflation and poverty rates. Overall, the cost of living in Belize is around 20-30% higher than in Honduras, making Honduras a more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees.
