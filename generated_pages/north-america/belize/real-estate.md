[SECTION] Market Overview [/SECTION]
The property market in Belize is growing, driven by its **tourism industry** and **retirement** opportunities. The country's strategic location in Central America, with a **Caribbean coastline** and a **English-speaking** population, makes it an attractive destination for foreigners. Currently, the market is experiencing a moderate price increase, with a mix of **urban** and **rural** development. The urban areas, such as Belize City and Belmopan, offer a range of amenities and services, while the rural areas, like the Cayo District, provide a more relaxed and natural environment.

The Belizean property market is also influenced by its **seasonal** nature, with prices and demand varying depending on the time of year. The high season, which typically runs from December to April, sees an increase in tourism and, consequently, a rise in property prices. In contrast, the low season, from May to November, is characterized by lower prices and fewer tourists. Despite these fluctuations, the overall trend is positive, with **foreign investment** and **infrastructure development** driving growth.

The **government** has implemented various initiatives to promote the property market, including incentives for **foreign buyers** and **developers**. These initiatives, combined with the country's natural beauty and **relaxed lifestyle**, make Belize an appealing destination for property investors. However, it is essential to conduct thorough research and due diligence when purchasing property in Belize, as the market can be **volatile**, and **local regulations** may apply.

[SECTION] Foreign Ownership Rules [/SECTION]
- Foreigners can buy property in Belize, but they must obtain a **alien landholding license** from the government.
- The license is typically granted for a **fee**, which is a percentage of the property's value.
- Foreigners can also buy property through a **Belizean company**, which can provide **tax benefits** and **anonymity**.
- However, the company must be registered in Belize, and the foreign owner must comply with **local regulations**.
- Foreigners are also required to obtain a **permit** from the **Central Bank of Belize** if they plan to purchase property using **foreign currency**.

[SECTION] Buying Process [/SECTION]
1. Research and select a property, considering factors such as **location**, **price**, and **condition**.
2. Appoint a **real estate agent** to assist with the purchase process.
3. Conduct a **title search** to ensure the property has a **clear title**.
4. Negotiate the **purchase price** and **terms** with the seller.
5. Sign a **sales agreement**, which should include the **price**, **terms**, and **conditions**.
6. Obtain **financing**, if necessary, from a **Belizean bank** or **international lender**.
7. Apply for an **alien landholding license**, if required.
8. Register the property with the **Belizean authorities**, including the **Land Registry** and **Tax Department**.
9. Obtain a **permit** from the **Central Bank of Belize**, if using **foreign currency**.
10. Complete the **transfer of ownership**, which should be facilitated by a **lawyer**.

[SECTION] Rental Market [/SECTION]
- The rental market in Belize is **competitive**, with a range of options available, from **apartments** to **houses**.
- **Tenant rights** are protected by law, and renters can expect a **minimum lease term** of one year.
- **Typical lease terms** range from six months to two years, with **rental yields** averaging 5-7%.
- **Deposit norms** typically require a **security deposit** equivalent to one to two months' rent.
- **Furnished** properties are common, especially in **tourist areas**, but **unfurnished** properties are also available.
