[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Belize Barrier Reef**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the largest barrier reef in the Western Hemisphere and home to a diverse range of marine life.
- The **Garifuna Settlement Day** is a national holiday that celebrates the arrival of the Garifuna people in Belize and features traditional music, dance, and food.
- The **Actun Tunichil Muknal** cave is a ancient Mayan archaeological site that features stunning limestone formations and artifacts.
- The **Belize Museum** is a repository of the country's history and culture, with exhibits on the Mayan civilization, the colonial era, and the modern nation.
- The **St. George's Caye** is a historic island that was the site of the Battle of St. George's Caye, a pivotal battle in the country's history.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Baron Bliss**, a British nobleman, left a significant portion of his estate to the country of Belize, which was used to establish the Baron Bliss Trust, a charitable organization that supports education, healthcare, and cultural development.
- **George Price**, the country's first prime minister, played a key role in Belize's independence movement and was a leading figure in regional politics.
- The **Belizean diaspora** has made significant contributions to the development of the country, with many Belizeans living abroad sending remittances back to their families and investing in local businesses.
- The **Garifuna community** has a rich cultural heritage and has made significant contributions to the country's music, art, and literature.
