# Education Costs in Belize

The cost of education in Belize varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Belize is divided into public and private institutions, with the former being generally free or subsidized, while the latter can be quite expensive. The language of instruction in Belize is **English**, and compulsory education lasts for 12 years, from primary to secondary school. The quality of public education in Belize is generally considered to be lower than that of private schools, with limited resources and outdated curricula. However, there are some excellent private schools in Belize, particularly in the larger cities like Belize City and Belmopan, which offer a high-quality education and a range of extracurricular activities.

International schools are also available in Belize, particularly in the tourist areas like Ambergris Caye and Placencia, which cater to expat children and offer a range of curricula, including **IB** and **AP** programs. These schools are generally very expensive, but offer a high-quality education and a range of extracurricular activities. The academic year in Belize typically runs from September to June, with a long break in July and August. Overall, the education system in Belize is well-structured, but there are significant disparities between public and private schools, and between urban and rural areas.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Tips [/SECTION]
- The best schools for expats in Belize are generally the international schools, which offer a high-quality education and a range of extracurricular activities.
- Scholarships are available for local students, particularly for those attending public universities, but these are limited and competitive.
- Online learning options are becoming increasingly popular in Belize, particularly for those living in rural areas, with a range of **online courses** and **degree programs** available.
- The education quality in Belize is generally considered to be lower than that of other countries in the region, particularly when it comes to public schools.
- The **University of Belize** is the top-ranked university in the country, offering a range of programs, including **business**, **education**, and **health sciences**.
- Expats and international students should research thoroughly and plan carefully before choosing a school or university in Belize, as the quality and availability of education can vary significantly depending on the institution and location.
