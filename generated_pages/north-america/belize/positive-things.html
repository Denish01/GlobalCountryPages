Cultural Treasures

  • The Belize Barrier Reef, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the largest barrier reef in the Western Hemisphere and home to a diverse range of marine life.
  • The Garifuna Settlement Day is a national holiday that celebrates the arrival of the Garifuna people in Belize and features traditional music, dance, and food.
  • The Actun Tunichil Muknal cave is a ancient Mayan archaeological site that features stunning limestone formations and artifacts.
  • The Belize Museum is a repository of the country's history and culture, with exhibits on the Mayan civilization, the colonial era, and the modern nation.
  • The St. George's Caye is a historic island that was the site of the Battle of St. George's Caye, a pivotal battle in the country's history.

People & Global Impact

  • Baron Bliss, a British nobleman, left a significant portion of his estate to the country of Belize, which was used to establish the Baron Bliss Trust, a charitable organization that supports education, healthcare, and cultural development.
  • George Price, the country's first prime minister, played a key role in Belize's independence movement and was a leading figure in regional politics.
  • The Belizean diaspora has made significant contributions to the development of the country, with many Belizeans living abroad sending remittances back to their families and investing in local businesses.
  • The Garifuna community has a rich cultural heritage and has made significant contributions to the country's music, art, and literature.

What Visitors Love

  • The beautiful beaches and clear waters of the Caribbean Sea, which offer a range of activities, including snorkeling, diving, and fishing.
  • The warm hospitality of the Belizean people, who are known for their friendly and welcoming nature.
  • The delicious cuisine, which features a unique blend of Caribbean, Latin American, and African influences, and includes popular dishes such as stewed chicken, rice and beans, and lobster.
  • The rich cultural heritage, which includes a mix of Mayan, Garifuna, and colonial influences, and is reflected in the country's music, art, and traditions.
  • The adventure activities, such as zip lining, cave tubing, and jungle treks, which offer a thrilling way to experience the country's natural beauty.
  • The relaxed atmosphere, which makes Belize an ideal destination for those looking to unwind and escape the stresses of everyday life.

Hidden Gems

  • The Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary, a protected area that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including jaguars, ocelots, and toucans.
  • The Placencia Peninsula, a charming coastal community that is known for its beautiful beaches, excellent fishing, and vibrant arts scene.
  • The Lamanai, a ancient Mayan archaeological site that features stunning ruins and artifacts, and offers a glimpse into the country's rich cultural heritage.
  • The Glover's Reef Atoll, a coral atoll that is home to a diverse range of marine life, including sea turtles, rays, and colorful fish.
