[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Mexico has a well-developed transportation system, with various modes of transport available, including public buses, metro systems, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit in Mexico is generally good, with frequent and reliable services, especially in major cities like Mexico City and Guadalajara. Most people in Mexico get around using a combination of public transportation and taxis, with many also opting for ride-hailing services due to their convenience and affordability.

The transportation infrastructure in Mexico is constantly improving, with new roads, highways, and public transportation systems being built to cater to the growing population and increasing demand for transportation. However, traffic congestion is a significant issue in major cities, and travelers should be prepared for delays and long commute times. Additionally, the cost of transportation in Mexico can vary significantly depending on the location, with cities like Mexico City and Cancun tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use public transportation or ride-hailing services to save money on transportation costs.
- Always use a metered taxi or ride-hailing app to avoid scams and overcharging.
- Download transportation apps like Didi, Uber, or Cabify to book ride-hailing services.
- Be cautious of traffic congestion and plan your journey accordingly.
- Consider purchasing a **Reforma** or **IATA** card for discounted public transportation fares.
- Negotiate taxi fares before you start your journey, especially for longer trips or airport transfers.
