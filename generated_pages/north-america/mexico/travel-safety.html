Overall Safety Assessment

Mexico is a vast and diverse country with varying safety conditions. While some areas are considered very safe, others pose significant risks to tourists. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing, bag snatching, and scams, is common in tourist areas, particularly in large cities like Mexico City and Cancun. Additionally, there have been reports of violent crime, including armed robbery and kidnapping, in certain regions. However, it's essential to note that most crimes against tourists are opportunistic and can be prevented with proper precautions and awareness.

It's crucial to stay informed about local conditions, respect local customs, and take necessary precautions to stay safe. The Mexican government has implemented various measures to improve security, including the deployment of tourist police in major tourist areas. Many popular destinations, such as Playa del Carmen and Tulum, are considered relatively safe, with low crime rates and a strong police presence. Nevertheless, it's essential to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night or in crowded areas.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Scams targeting tourists, such as fake timeshare presentations and pyramid schemes
  • Petty theft, including theft of valuables from hotel rooms and rental cars
  • Armed robbery and kidnapping in certain regions, particularly at night or in isolated areas
  • Credit card skimming and ATM scams
  • Overcharging by taxi drivers and vendors
  • Petty crime and scams at airports and bus stations

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A and typhoid fever are recommended, and rabies vaccination is required for certain activities, such as hiking or biking
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Quality medical facilities are available in major cities, but may be limited in rural areas, and insurance is often required
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacy access is generally easy, but some medications may require a prescription

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night
  • Keep valuables secure and use hotel safes or lockers
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Avoid walking alone at night and stick to well-lit areas
