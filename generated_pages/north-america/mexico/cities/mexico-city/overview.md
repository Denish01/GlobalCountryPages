[SECTION] What Is Mexico City? [/SECTION]
Mexico City is a **federal entity** that serves as the capital of Mexico, a country in North America. The city is situated in the Valley of Mexico, a large valley in the high plateaus of south-central Mexico. Mexico City is known for its rich cultural heritage, with a blend of modern and ancient attractions, including the historic center, which is a **UNESCO World Heritage Site**. The city is home to numerous museums, galleries, and historic landmarks, such as the **Palacio de Bellas Artes** and the **Catedral Metropolitana**.

Mexico City is the largest city in Mexico and one of the largest cities in the world, with a population of over 21 million people. The city is a major economic, cultural, and political center, with a diverse range of industries, including finance, manufacturing, and tourism. Mexico City is also known for its vibrant cultural scene, with a wide range of festivals, events, and traditions, such as the **Día de los Muertos** (Day of the Dead) and the **Fiestas de la Vendimia** (Grape Harvest Festival).

The city's geographic position has played a significant role in its history and development, with the Valley of Mexico being a major hub for trade and commerce in pre-Columbian times. Today, Mexico City is a major transportation hub, with a large international airport and a comprehensive network of roads, highways, and public transportation systems.
