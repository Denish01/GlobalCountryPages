# Top Things to Do in Mexico City — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Mexico City, visit the ancient Aztec temple of **Templo Mayor**, explore the vibrant **La Condesa** neighborhood, and sample the city's renowned street food at **Taquería Los Güeros**.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **lucha libre** wrestling match at the **Arena México**, a unique and exciting experience that showcases Mexico's popular sport.
- Explore the **Mercado de San Juan**, a bustling marketplace with a wide variety of traditional foods, handicrafts, and souvenirs.
- Visit the **Floating Gardens of Xochimilco** at night, when the canals are illuminated and the atmosphere is magical.
- Attend a **mariachi performance** at the **Plaza Garibaldi**, where you can enjoy traditional Mexican music and dance.
- Learn about **traditional Mexican cuisine** with a cooking class at the **La Casa de Toño**, where you can discover the secrets of local dishes like tacos and mole.
- Visit the **Dolores Olmedo Museum**, which showcases an impressive collection of Mexican art, including works by Frida Kahlo and Diego Rivera.
- Take a **hot air balloon ride** over the **Teotihuacán** pyramids, a breathtaking experience that offers a unique perspective on these ancient ruins.

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Desierto de los Leones National Park**, a beautiful protected area with hiking trails, scenic views, and a variety of wildlife.
- Visit the **Chapultepec Lake**, a popular spot for boating, fishing, and relaxation.
- Hike to the **Cerro del Ajusco**, the highest point in Mexico City, which offers stunning views of the city and surrounding landscape.
- Take a **bike tour** through the **Bosque de Chapultepec**, a large and scenic forest with plenty of trails and attractions.
- Visit the **Jardín Botánico**, a beautiful botanical garden with a wide variety of plants and flowers.

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Museo Nacional de Antropología**, a world-class museum that showcases Mexico's rich cultural heritage.
- Explore the **Palacio de Bellas Artes**, a stunning art nouveau building with an impressive art collection.
- Attend a **performance** at the **Teatro de la Ciudad**, a historic theater that hosts a variety of cultural events.
- Visit the **Templo de San Francisco**, a beautiful colonial church with a rich history and stunning architecture.
- Take a **tour** of the **Catedral Metropolitana**, a historic cathedral that dominates the **Zócalo** and offers stunning views of the city.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Museo de la Ciudad de México**, a small but fascinating museum that showcases the history and culture of the city.
- Explore the **Barrio Chino**, a charming neighborhood with a unique blend of Chinese and Mexican cultures.
- Take a **stroll** through the **Parque México**, a beautiful urban park with plenty of green space and scenic views.
- Visit the **Ex-Terpac Calli**, a former prison that has been converted into a museum and cultural center.
- Attend a **concert** at the **Sala Nezahualcóyotl**, a historic concert hall that hosts a variety of musical events.

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a **day trip** to the **Teotihuacán** pyramids, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located about 40 kilometers northeast of Mexico City (1 hour by car).
- Visit the **Cuernavaca**, a charming city located about 80 kilometers south of Mexico City (1.5 hours by car), known for its beautiful colonial architecture and pleasant climate.
- Explore the **Taxco**, a historic town located about 180 kilometers southwest of Mexico City (3 hours by car), famous for its silver mining and stunning natural scenery.
- Take a **day trip** to the **Puebla**, a city located about 130 kilometers east of Mexico City (2 hours by car), known for its rich cultural heritage and delicious cuisine.
- Visit the **Toluca**, a city located about 60 kilometers west of Mexico City (1 hour by car), famous for its beautiful colonial architecture and stunning natural scenery.
