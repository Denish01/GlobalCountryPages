# Where to Stay in Mexico City — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To find the best place to stay in Mexico City, consider the top neighborhoods of Condesa, Roma, Polanco, Juárez, Coyoacán, and Xochimilco, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, the following information is provided:
**Condesa** (Mexico City)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families
Vibe: Condesa is known for its upscale atmosphere, beautiful parks, and trendy restaurants, making it a great spot for those looking for a high-end experience in a safe and family-friendly environment. The neighborhood is surrounded by trees and has a tranquil ambiance, perfect for relaxation.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Beautiful parks, trendy restaurants, and a safe environment. Condesa is also close to many popular attractions, making it a convenient spot for tourists. Additionally, the neighborhood has a wide range of amenities, including high-end shopping centers and spas.
Cons: It can be quite expensive, and the area is often crowded with tourists.

**Roma** (Mexico City)
Best For: Backpackers and young travelers
Vibe: Roma is a vibrant and eclectic neighborhood, known for its street art, independent shops, and lively nightlife, making it an ideal spot for young travelers looking for a unique and exciting experience. The neighborhood has a mix of old and new buildings, giving it a distinctive character.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable prices, lively nightlife, and a wide range of restaurants and bars. Roma is also a great spot for shopping, with many independent boutiques and markets. Additionally, the neighborhood is close to many popular attractions, making it a convenient spot for tourists.
Cons: The area can be noisy, and some parts of the neighborhood are still developing.

**Polanco** (Mexico City)
Best For: Business travelers and luxury seekers
Vibe: Polanco is a upscale neighborhood, known for its high-end shopping centers, restaurants, and hotels, making it a great spot for business travelers and those looking for a luxurious experience. The neighborhood is safe and clean, with a mix of modern and traditional architecture.
Price Range: $150-500/night
Pros: High-end amenities, safe environment, and proximity to many businesses and attractions. Polanco is also a great spot for shopping, with many high-end malls and boutiques. Additionally, the neighborhood has a wide range of restaurants, offering a variety of international cuisine.
Cons: It can be quite expensive, and the area is often crowded with business travelers.

**Juárez** (Mexico City)
Best For: Nightlife enthusiasts and budget travelers
Vibe: Juárez is a lively and vibrant neighborhood, known for its nightlife, restaurants, and bars, making it an ideal spot for those looking for a fun and affordable experience. The neighborhood has a mix of old and new buildings, giving it a distinctive character.
Price Range: $15-50/night
Pros: Affordable prices, lively nightlife, and a wide range of restaurants and bars. Juárez is also a great spot for shopping, with many markets and independent boutiques. Additionally, the neighborhood is close to many popular attractions, making it a convenient spot for tourists.
Cons: The area can be noisy, and some parts of the neighborhood are still developing.

**Coyoacán** (Mexico City)
Best For: Families and those looking for a historic experience
Vibe: Coyoacán is a historic and charming neighborhood, known for its colonial architecture, picturesque plazas, and family-friendly atmosphere, making it a great spot for families and those looking for a unique and historic experience. The neighborhood is surrounded by trees and has a tranquil ambiance, perfect for relaxation.

Additionally, the neighborhood has a wide range of restaurants, offering a variety of traditional Mexican cuisine.

**Xochimilco** (Mexico City)

Vibe: Xochimilco is a unique and scenic neighborhood, known for its canals, floating gardens, and traditional Mexican cuisine, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers and those looking for a unique experience. The neighborhood has a mix of old and new buildings, giving it a distinctive character.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas like Tepito and Doctores, known for high crime rates and safety concerns
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
- Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Stay informed about local events and demonstrations, which can affect traffic and safety
- Consider staying in a neighborhood with a strong police presence, like Condesa or Polanco, for added safety and security
