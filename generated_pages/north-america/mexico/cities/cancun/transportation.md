# Getting Around Cancun — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Cancun, you have a variety of transportation options, including taxis, buses, and rental cars, as well as a well-developed airport and a growing public transportation system.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Cancun International Airport is the primary gateway to the city, with direct flights from many major cities in the United States, Canada, and Europe. The airport is located about 15 kilometers southwest of the city center and is well-connected to the rest of the city by taxi, bus, and shuttle services. Some of the major airlines that operate direct flights to Cancun include American Airlines, Delta Air Lines, and United Airlines from the United States, as well as Air Canada and WestJet from Canada. Additionally, there are several overland entry points from the neighboring cities and towns, including Playa del Carmen and Tulum, which can be reached by bus or car.

For those traveling from other parts of Mexico, there are also several bus companies that operate daily services to Cancun, including ADO and Mayab, which offer a convenient and affordable way to get to the city. The bus terminal is located in the city center, making it easy to get to your final destination. Furthermore, Cancun is also accessible by car, with several major highways connecting the city to the rest of Mexico, including the MEX 180 highway, which runs along the Caribbean coast.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
For domestic travel within Mexico, there are several airlines that operate flights from Cancun to other major cities, including Mexico City, Guadalajara, and Monterrey. Some of the main domestic airlines that operate from Cancun include Aeromexico, Interjet, and VivaAerobus, which offer a range of flights to destinations across the country. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as prices can be high. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Cancun can range from $100 to $300 USD, depending on the destination and time of year. It's also worth noting that some airlines may offer discounts or promotions, especially for students or seniors, so it's worth checking the airline's website for any available deals.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Unfortunately, there is no rail network in Cancun, which means that trains are not a viable option for getting around the city. However, there are plans to develop a light rail system in the future, which would provide a convenient and efficient way to get around the city. In the meantime, visitors will need to rely on other forms of transportation, such as buses, taxis, or rental cars.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several bus companies that operate in Cancun, including ADO and Mayab, which offer a range of services to destinations across the city and beyond. The buses are generally modern and well-maintained, with air conditioning and comfortable seating. Fares are relatively affordable, with prices starting from around $1 USD for a short journey. Some of the main bus routes in Cancun include the R-1 and R-2 routes, which run along the hotel zone and connect to the city center. It's also worth noting that some buses may offer additional amenities, such as Wi-Fi or onboard entertainment, so it's worth checking with the bus company for more information.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]

- The road quality in Cancun is generally good, with well-maintained highways and roads.
- Fuel costs in Mexico can be relatively high, with prices starting from around $2.50 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies such as Hertz, Avis, and Europcar are available at the airport and in the city center.
- When driving in Cancun, be aware of potential hazards such as potholes, pedestrians, and aggressive drivers.
- It's also a good idea to purchase car insurance, as it is mandatory in Mexico and can help protect you in case of an accident.
