# Getting Around Puebla — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Puebla, you can use a variety of transportation options including buses, taxis, ride-hailing apps, and walking, with the city's historic center being relatively compact and easy to navigate on foot.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
The main airport serving Puebla is the Hermanos Serdán International Airport, also known as Puebla International Airport, which is located about 23 kilometers west of the city center. The airport receives direct flights from several major cities in Mexico, as well as from the United States. Some of the direct flight hubs include Mexico City, Guadalajara, and Monterrey, with airlines such as Aeromexico, VivaAerobus, and Volaris operating flights to and from Puebla. Overland entry points to Puebla include several major highways, including the Mexico City-Puebla highway and the Puebla-Veracruz highway, which connect the city to other major cities in Mexico.

For travelers coming from other parts of North America, there are several bus companies that operate routes to Puebla, including Greyhound and Estrella de Oro. Additionally, it is possible to take a taxi or rent a car from the airport or from nearby cities. The drive from Mexico City to Puebla takes around 2-3 hours, depending on traffic, and the drive from Veracruz takes around 4-5 hours. It is also possible to take a bus or shuttle from the airport to the city center, which takes around 30-40 minutes and costs around 100-150 pesos.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Puebla, you will need a valid driver's license from your home country, as well as car insurance that covers you in Mexico.
- The road quality in Puebla is generally moderate, with some roads being in good condition and others being in poor condition.
- The cost of fuel in Puebla is generally lower than in the United States, with the price of gasoline ranging from 15 to 20 pesos per liter.
- To rent a car in Puebla, you will need to be at least 21 years old and have a valid credit card.
- It is recommended to exercise caution when driving in Puebla, especially at night, as there can be a lot of traffic and pedestrians on the roads.
- It is also recommended to avoid driving in areas that are known to be high-crime, such as the outskirts of the city.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas and on public transportation.
- Use authorized taxi services or ride-hailing apps to avoid scams and ensure your safety.
- Always agree on the price before getting into a taxi, and make sure the driver uses the meter.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or debit cards instead.
- Download ride-hailing apps such as Uber or Didi to get around the city.
- Learn some basic Spanish phrases, such as "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (how much does it cost?) and "¿Dónde está...?" (where is...?), to help you navigate the city.
