[SECTION] What Is Puebla? [/SECTION]
Puebla is a state in Mexico, situated in the eastern central part of the country. It is bordered by the states of Veracruz to the east, Hidalgo to the north, Tlaxcala to the northwest, Mexico State to the west, and Morelos and Guerrero to the southwest. Puebla is known for its historic city center, which is a **UNESCO World Heritage Site**, featuring a mix of colonial and indigenous architectural styles. The state is also famous for its traditional cuisine, which includes dishes such as **mole poblano**, **chalupas**, and **tacos arabes**.

Puebla's geographic position makes it a strategic location for trade and commerce. The state is situated near the **Volcanic Axis**, a mountain range that stretches across central Mexico, and is home to several active volcanoes, including **Popocatépetl** and **La Malinche**. The state's terrain is varied, with mountains, valleys, and plains, making it suitable for a range of agricultural activities, including the production of **sugar cane**, **coffee**, and **flowers**.

Puebla's cultural heritage is also an important aspect of its identity. The state is home to a number of indigenous groups, including the **Nahuas**, **Tlaxcalans**, and **Mixtecs**, each with their own distinct traditions and customs. The state's capital, Puebla de Zaragoza, is a major cultural center, with a number of museums, galleries, and festivals throughout the year.
