# Top Things to Do in Merida — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Merida, visit the **Uxmal Mayan Ruins**, explore the **Centro Historico**, and discover the city's rich cultural heritage by attending a traditional **Yucatecan music** performance.

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| Merida Cathedral | Centro Historico, Merida | Historical landmark | Stunning example of colonial architecture and a symbol of the city |
| Museo Fernando Garcia Ponce-Macay | Calle 60, Merida | Art museum | Features an impressive collection of Mexican art from the 20th and 21st centuries |

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Attend a traditional **Yucatecan music** performance at the **Teatro Peon Contreras**
- Take a **cooking class** to learn about the local cuisine and ingredients
- Explore the **Mercado de San Ildefonso** to try local foods and buy handicrafts
- Visit a **hacienda** to learn about the history of the region's sisal plantations
- Go on a **birdwatching tour** to spot some of the region's unique species
- Take a **Spanish language class** to improve your language skills and learn about the local culture

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Cenote Xkeken** for a swim in a natural pool
- Explore the **Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Go **hiking** in the **Reserva de la Biosfera Ria Lagartos**
- Relax on the beach at **Progreso**, a charming coastal town
- Take a **boat tour** to see **dolphins** and other marine life

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Museo de la Ciudad de Merida** to learn about the city's history and culture
- Attend a performance at the **Teatro Peon Contreras**, a stunning example of colonial architecture
- Explore the **Centro Historico**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Visit the **Iglesia de San Idelfonso**, a beautiful colonial church
- Take a **tour of the city's historic neighborhoods**, such as **Santiago** and **La Ermita**

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Parque de Santa Ana**, a charming park with a beautiful chapel
- Explore the **Barrio de Santiago**, a historic neighborhood with stunning architecture
- Go to the **Museo de Arte Popular**, a small museum featuring local handicrafts
- Take a **stroll along the Paseo de Montejo**, a beautiful avenue lined with historic mansions

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
1. **Uxmal and Kabah**: Visit the stunning Mayan ruins of Uxmal and Kabah, located about 60 miles south of Merida (1.5 hours by car)
2. **Izamal**: Explore the charming colonial town of Izamal, known as the "Yellow Town" due to its yellow-painted buildings (45 miles east of Merida, 1 hour by car)
3. **Celestun**: Visit the beautiful beach town of Celestun, known for its stunning sunsets and wildlife reserve (60 miles west of Merida, 1.5 hours by car)
4. **Valladolid**: Explore the charming colonial town of Valladolid, known for its stunning architecture and traditional cuisine (100 miles east of Merida, 2 hours by car)
