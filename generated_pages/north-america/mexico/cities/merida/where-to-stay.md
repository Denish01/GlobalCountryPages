# Where to Stay in Merida — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Merida, consider the city's vibrant cultural scene, rich history, and convenient location, with top neighborhoods including Centro, Paseo de Montejo, and Garcia Gineres offering a range of accommodations for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, the following characteristics are notable:
**Centro** (Merida) 
Best For: backpacker / family / business
Vibe: The historic center of Merida is a bustling area filled with colonial architecture, lively markets, and a wide range of restaurants and shops, making it an ideal location for those who want to experience the city's vibrant culture. The area is also relatively safe and has a strong police presence.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: proximity to major landmarks, variety of dining options, lively atmosphere
Cons: can be noisy, limited parking options

**Paseo de Montejo** (Merida) 
Best For: luxury / business
Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is known for its beautiful tree-lined avenue, stunning colonial mansions, and high-end boutiques, making it a great option for those looking for a more refined and sophisticated experience. The area is also home to several top-rated restaurants and hotels.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: elegant atmosphere, proximity to top attractions, high-end amenities
Cons: can be expensive, limited budget options

**Garcia Gineres** (Merida) 
Best For: family / luxury
Vibe: This charming neighborhood is a popular choice for families and those looking for a more laid-back atmosphere, with its quiet streets, beautiful parks, and convenient access to top attractions. The area is also known for its vibrant expat community and a range of amenities.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: family-friendly atmosphere, convenient location, range of amenities
Cons: can be quiet, limited nightlife options

**Francisco de Montejo** (Merida) 
Best For: business / luxury
Vibe: This modern neighborhood is a hub for business and commerce, with its sleek skyscrapers, upscale hotels, and range of amenities, making it an ideal location for those traveling for work or looking for a more contemporary experience. The area is also home to several top-rated restaurants and bars.
Price Range: $40-250/night
Pros: modern amenities, convenient location, range of dining options
Cons: can be expensive, limited budget options

**Santa Ana** (Merida) 
Best For: backpacker / family
Vibe: This lively neighborhood is a popular choice for backpackers and families, with its vibrant atmosphere, range of budget-friendly accommodations, and convenient access to top attractions. The area is also known for its beautiful parks and lively markets.
Price Range: $15-80/night
Pros: affordable options, lively atmosphere, convenient location
Cons: can be noisy, limited high-end amenities
