[SECTION] What Is Guadalajara? [/SECTION]
Guadalajara is a city located in the state of Jalisco, Mexico, and is the second-largest city in the country. It is situated in the central part of the state, about 540 kilometers northwest of Mexico City. Guadalajara is known for its rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its historic architecture, vibrant arts scene, and traditional cuisine. The city is also an important economic and industrial center, with a strong focus on technology, manufacturing, and tourism.

Geographically, Guadalajara is situated in the Valley of Atemajac, a fertile region surrounded by mountains and volcanoes. The city has a mild climate, with warm summers and mild winters, making it an attractive destination for tourists and business travelers. Guadalajara is also a major transportation hub, with a large international airport and a network of highways and railroads connecting it to other parts of Mexico and the United States.

Guadalajara is a city with a strong sense of identity and culture, which is reflected in its historic center, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city is home to many historic buildings, including the stunning Guadalajara Cathedral, the Government Palace, and the Hospicio Cabañas, a former hospital and orphanage that is now a museum and cultural center. Guadalajara is also known for its vibrant arts scene, with numerous museums, galleries, and festivals throughout the year.
