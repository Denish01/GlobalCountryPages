[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Tijuana is situated in the northwestern part of Mexico, directly across the border from San Diego, California, in the United States. The city is part of the Tijuana Municipality, which also includes the surrounding areas and smaller towns. The city's proximity to the US-Mexico border makes it a significant cultural and economic hub, with many residents commuting across the border for work or tourism. The city is also close to the Pacific Ocean, with a coastline that stretches for approximately 28 kilometers.

The city's location in a **semi-arid** region means that it has a unique combination of desert and coastal influences. To the east, Tijuana is bordered by the municipality of Tecate, while to the south, it is bordered by the municipality of Playas de Rosarito. The city's northern border is formed by the **US-Mexico border**, which separates it from the city of San Diego. The Pacific Ocean lies to the west, providing a scenic coastline and a range of outdoor recreational activities.

Tijuana's terrain is characterized by a mix of coastal plains, hills, and mountains. The city is situated in a valley, surrounded by the **Sierra de San Pedro Mártir** mountains to the east and the **Pacific Coast Range** to the west. The landscape is dominated by **chaparral** vegetation, which is typical of the region's semi-arid climate. The city's coastal area is relatively flat, with sandy beaches and scenic cliffs along the Pacific Ocean. Inland, the terrain becomes more rugged, with hills and mountains rising up to form a scenic backdrop to the city.

The **Tijuana River** flows through the city, providing a source of water and a range of recreational activities. The river is an important part of the city's ecosystem, supporting a range of plant and animal life. However, the river has also been affected by urbanization and pollution, which have impacted its water quality and biodiversity. The city's terrain has also been shaped by human activity, with urban development and agriculture transforming the landscape over time. The city's coastal area has been developed for tourism, with a range of hotels, restaurants, and recreational facilities.
