[SECTION] What Is Tijuana? [/SECTION]
Tijuana is a city in the Mexican state of Baja California, situated on the US-Mexico border, directly adjacent to the city of San Diego, California. The city has a long and complex history, dating back to the 18th century, and has evolved into a thriving cultural and economic center. Tijuana is known for its vibrant arts scene, delicious cuisine, and lively nightlife, making it a popular tourist destination. The city's strategic location on the border has also made it an important hub for trade and commerce between the United States and Mexico.

Geographically, Tijuana is situated in a coastal valley, surrounded by mountains and the Pacific Ocean. The city's climate is mild, with warm summers and cool winters, making it an attractive location for tourists and residents alike. Tijuana is also home to a diverse range of neighborhoods, each with its own unique character and charm, from the historic center to the modern suburbs.

Tijuana's sovereignty status is that of a municipality within the state of Baja California, Mexico. As such, it is subject to the laws and regulations of the Mexican federal government, while also having a degree of autonomy in local matters. The city's government is headed by a mayor, who is elected by the citizens of Tijuana.
