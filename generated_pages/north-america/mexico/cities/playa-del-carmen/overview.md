[SECTION] What Is Playa del Carmen? [/SECTION]
Playa del Carmen is a city located in the state of Quintana Roo, Mexico, and is part of the Riviera Maya, a 120-kilometer stretch of coastline known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush tropical forests. The city is situated about 35 miles south of Cancun, the largest city in the state, and is easily accessible by car, bus, or taxi. Playa del Carmen is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year with its vibrant nightlife, wide range of accommodations, and numerous activities, including snorkeling, scuba diving, and golfing.

Playa del Carmen is also known for its rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Mayan and Mexican influences. The city is home to several ancient Mayan ruins, including the nearby site of Tulum, which is perched on a cliff overlooking the Caribbean Sea. Visitors can also explore the city's many shops, restaurants, and markets, which offer a wide range of local handicrafts, souvenirs, and traditional Mexican cuisine.

The city's economy is largely driven by tourism, with many hotels, resorts, and vacation rentals available to suit all budgets and preferences. Playa del Carmen is also a popular destination for weddings, honeymoons, and other special occasions, with its beautiful beaches, luxurious spas, and romantic atmosphere making it an ideal location for couples and families alike.
