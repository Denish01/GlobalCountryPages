[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Playa del Carmen, a popular tourist destination on the Caribbean coast of Mexico, is generally a safe city for visitors. The city has a strong police presence, and most areas are well-patrolled, especially in the tourist zones. However, as with any tourist destination, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and scams are common, but violent crimes are relatively rare. The city's reputation as a safe destination is well-deserved, but it's still important for visitors to be mindful of their belongings and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas at night.

The Mexican government has taken steps to improve safety in Playa del Carmen, including increasing the number of police officers and implementing security cameras in tourist areas. Additionally, many resorts and hotels have their own security personnel, which helps to create a safe environment for guests. Despite these efforts, it's still important for visitors to be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. This includes being mindful of their belongings, avoiding walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas at night, and being cautious when using ATMs or exchanging money.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Pickpocketing**: Common in crowded areas such as 5th Avenue and public transportation, be mindful of your belongings and keep valuables secure.
- **Scams**: Common scams include timeshare presentations, fake charity solicitations, and overpriced tourist services, be cautious when approached by strangers and research any services or charities before donating.
- **ATM skimming**: Be cautious when using ATMs, especially in isolated areas, and cover the keypad when entering your PIN.
- **Taxi scams**: Only use licensed taxis, and agree on the fare before you start your journey.
- **Water sports accidents**: Be cautious when participating in water sports such as snorkeling or jet-skiing, and make sure to use reputable and licensed operators.
- **Credit card skimming**: Be cautious when using credit cards, especially in isolated areas, and keep an eye on your statements for any suspicious activity.
- **Drunk and disorderly behavior**: Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your drinks, especially in crowded areas such as bars and clubs.
- **Sun and heat exposure**: Be mindful of the sun and heat, and take necessary precautions such as wearing sunscreen, a hat, and staying hydrated.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Playa del Carmen, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is not safe to drink, bottled or filtered water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities**: Medical facilities in Playa del Carmen are generally of good quality, with many hospitals and clinics catering to tourists, but medical insurance is recommended.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacies are readily available, and many are open 24 hours, but it's still a good idea to bring any prescription medications with you.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas such as 5th Avenue and public transportation.
- Use reputable and licensed taxi services, and agree on the fare before you start your journey.
- Keep valuables secure, and consider using a hotel safe or a secure bag.
- Be cautious when using ATMs, especially in isolated areas, and cover the keypad when entering your PIN.
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas at night.
- Stay hydrated, and take necessary precautions such as wearing sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun and heat.
- Research any services or charities before donating, and be cautious when approached by strangers.
- Keep an eye on your drinks, especially in crowded areas such as bars and clubs.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas.
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, such as 5th Avenue or the Hotel Zone.
- Keep your hotel room door locked, and consider using a hotel safe or a secure bag.
- Be cautious when using public transportation, and consider using reputable and licensed taxi services instead.
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home, and let them know your itinerary and where you can be reached.
