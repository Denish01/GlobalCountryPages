# Top Things to Do in Oaxaca — Attractions & Experiences

Oaxaca, a culturally rich and historically significant city in Mexico, offers a wide range of activities and experiences that cater to different interests and preferences, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **traditional cooking class** and learn how to prepare local dishes such as mole and tlayudas
- Visit a **mezcal distillery** and learn about the production process of this traditional spirit
- Attend a **Guelaguetza** celebration, a traditional Oaxacan festival that showcases music, dance, and food
- Explore the **Tule Canyon**, a scenic canyon with stunning views and opportunities for hiking and swimming
- Visit a **traditional Zapotec village** and learn about the customs and traditions of the indigenous community
- Take a **natural dye workshop** and learn how to create natural dyes from plants and other materials

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Benito Juárez National Park**, a protected area with hiking trails and stunning views of the surrounding landscape
- Explore the **Sierra Norte mountains**, a scenic range with opportunities for hiking, camping, and wildlife spotting
- Relax on the **Zipolite Beach**, a secluded beach with crystal-clear waters and stunning sunsets
- Take a **hiking tour** of the **Copper Canyon**, a scenic canyon with stunning views and opportunities for wildlife spotting
- Visit the **Lagunas de Chacahua National Park**, a protected area with opportunities for birdwatching and boat tours

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Museo de Arte Contemporáneo de Oaxaca**, a museum that showcases contemporary art from Oaxacan artists
- Explore the **Santo Domingo de Guzmán Church**, a historic church that showcases the city's rich cultural heritage
- Attend a **traditional music performance**, such as a mariachi concert or a traditional Zapotec dance
- Take a **textile workshop** and learn about the traditional weaving and embroidery techniques of the indigenous community
- Visit the **Tlacolula de Matamoros Market**, a vibrant marketplace where visitors can sample local cuisine and buy traditional handicrafts

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **San Bartolo Coyotepec**, a small town known for its traditional pottery and ceramics
- Explore the **Mitla Archaeological Site**, a lesser-known archaeological site with stunning architecture and history
- Take a **bike tour** of the **Oaxaca Valley**, a scenic valley with stunning views and opportunities for wildlife spotting
- Relax in the **Hierve el Agua hot springs**, a natural hot spring with stunning views of the surrounding landscape
- Visit the **Teotitlán del Valle Museum**, a small museum that showcases the history and culture of the town

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Puerto Escondido**, a coastal town with stunning beaches and opportunities for surfing and snorkeling (distance: 160 km, travel time: 3 hours)
- Visit the **Monte Albán archaeological site**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with stunning architecture and history (distance: 10 km, travel time: 30 minutes)
- Explore the **Tule Tree**, a massive and ancient tree that is considered one of the oldest and largest in the world (distance: 10 km, travel time: 30 minutes)
- Take a day trip to **Mitla**, a small town known for its traditional weaving and natural dyes (distance: 40 km, travel time: 1 hour)
- Visit the **Lagunas de Chacahua National Park**, a protected area with opportunities for birdwatching and boat tours (distance: 120 km, travel time: 2.5 hours)
