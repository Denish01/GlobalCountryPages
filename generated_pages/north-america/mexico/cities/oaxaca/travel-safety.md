# Oaxaca Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Oaxaca is generally a safe destination to visit, with low to moderate crime rates compared to other major cities in Mexico, but as with any travel, it's essential to take necessary precautions to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Oaxaca is a vibrant and culturally rich city, known for its delicious cuisine, beautiful architecture, and warm hospitality. While it's generally a safe destination, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, purse snatching, and scams can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Visitors should be mindful of their surroundings, especially at night, and take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Additionally, Oaxaca is prone to natural disasters such as earthquakes and hurricanes, and visitors should be prepared for emergency situations.

Oaxaca has a strong police presence, including a dedicated tourist police force, which helps to maintain a safe and secure environment for visitors. However, as with any city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should exercise caution when exploring the city, especially at night. It's also essential to stay informed about local conditions, demonstrations, and protests, which can sometimes turn violent. By taking necessary precautions and staying informed, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Oaxaca.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of pickpocketing and purse snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable ATMs and banks
- Be wary of scams, such as the "gold ring scam" or the "help me buy a bus ticket" scam
- Avoid traveling alone or at night in rural areas or areas with high crime rates
- Be mindful of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Be cautious of unlicensed taxis or transportation services

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to Oaxaca
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water
- **Medical Facilities:** Oaxaca has a range of medical facilities, including public and private hospitals, with varying levels of quality and availability
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, and many medications can be purchased over-the-counter

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Stay informed about local conditions and demonstrations
- Avoid traveling alone or at night in rural areas or areas with high crime rates
- Keep your valuables secure and be mindful of your surroundings
- Use reputable transportation services, such as licensed taxis or ride-sharing apps
- Avoid displaying signs of wealth, such as expensive jewelry or watches
- Learn some basic Spanish phrases, such as "¿Dónde está...?" (where is...) and "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (how much does it cost?)
- Carry a copy of your passport and important documents, and leave a copy with a trusted friend or family member

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid traveling alone or at night in rural areas or areas with high crime rates
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, such as the Historic Center or Zocalo
- Be cautious of unwanted attention or harassment, and don't hesitate to seek help if necessary
- Consider joining a guided tour or group to explore the city and surrounding areas
