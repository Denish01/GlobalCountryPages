# Cost of Living in Monterrey - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Monterrey is generally moderate, with a daily budget ranging from $30-60 USD for backpackers to $100-200 USD for comfort seekers.

[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Monterrey? [/SECTION]
Monterrey, a major city in Mexico, offers a unique blend of industrial and cultural attractions, making it an interesting destination for tourists and expats alike. Compared to other major cities in North America, Monterrey has a relatively moderate cost of living, making it an attractive option for those looking to experience Mexican culture without breaking the bank. However, prices can vary depending on the neighborhood, with areas like San Pedro Garza García and Monterrey's city center tend to be more expensive than others. In terms of global averages, Monterrey's cost of living is lower than many cities in the United States and Canada, but higher than some other Mexican cities.

In relation to its neighbors, Monterrey's cost of living is comparable to other major Mexican cities like Guadalajara and Puebla, but lower than Mexico City. The city's economy is driven by industries such as steel, cement, and glass, which contributes to its moderate cost of living. Additionally, Monterrey's proximity to the US-Mexico border also influences its economy and cost of living, with many international companies having a presence in the city.
