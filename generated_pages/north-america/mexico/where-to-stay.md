# Where to Stay in Mexico — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Mexico, consider the country's diverse regions and neighborhoods, which offer a range of options to suit different types of travelers, from vibrant city centers to relaxing coastal towns.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, we've highlighted the best neighborhoods to stay in Mexico:
**Puerto Vallarta** (Puerto Vallarta)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Puerto Vallarta is a coastal city with a vibrant old town, stunning beaches, and a thriving nightlife, offering a mix of relaxation and entertainment options. The city's scenic Malecon waterfront promenade is perfect for evening strolls.
Price Range: $80-250/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, water sports, and outdoor activities, excellent dining options
Cons: Can be crowded during peak season, traffic in the city center

**Polanco** (Mexico City)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: Polanco is an upscale neighborhood in Mexico City, known for its high-end shopping malls, restaurants, and cultural attractions, offering a sophisticated and convenient stay. The neighborhood is also close to the city's main business districts.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Excellent shopping and dining options, convenient location, safe and secure area, easy access to public transportation
Cons: Traffic in the area can be heavy, limited budget options

**Playa del Carmen** (Playa del Carmen)
Best For: Backpacker / Family
Vibe: Playa del Carmen is a lively coastal town on the Riviera Maya, known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and outdoor activities, offering a fun and relaxed atmosphere. The town's famous Fifth Avenue is lined with shops, restaurants, and bars.
Price Range: $40-150/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, outdoor activities, and water sports, affordable accommodation options
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited cultural attractions

**Condesa** (Mexico City)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Condesa is a trendy neighborhood in Mexico City, known for its beautiful parks, art deco architecture, and vibrant cultural scene, offering a unique and sophisticated stay. The neighborhood is also close to the city's main attractions.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Beautiful parks, cultural attractions, convenient location, safe and secure area, excellent dining options
Cons: Limited budget options, traffic in the area can be heavy

**Tulum** (Tulum)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Tulum is a coastal town on the Riviera Maya, known for its stunning beaches, ancient Mayan ruins, and eco-tourism activities, offering a relaxing and culturally rich experience. The town's scenic coastal road is perfect for biking and hiking.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, ancient Mayan ruins, eco-tourism activities, and outdoor adventures, excellent dining options
Cons: Can be expensive, limited budget options, crowded during peak season

**San Miguel de Allende** (San Miguel de Allende)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: San Miguel de Allende is a colonial town in central Mexico, known for its historic architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and natural hot springs, offering a unique and relaxing experience. The town's scenic cobblestone streets are perfect for walking and exploring.
Price Range: $60-200/night
Pros: Historic architecture, cultural attractions, natural hot springs, and outdoor activities, excellent dining options
Cons: Limited budget options, traffic in the area can be heavy

**Coyoacan** (Mexico City)
Best For: Backpacker / Family
Vibe: Coyoacan is a historic neighborhood in Mexico City, known for its colonial architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and lively markets, offering a fun and authentic experience. The neighborhood is also close to the city's main attractions.
Price Range: $30-100/night
Pros: Historic architecture, cultural attractions, lively markets, and affordable accommodation options
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited high-end options

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to April)
- Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings
- Look for packages that include amenities like breakfast or tours
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during holidays and summer months
- Read reviews and check the hotel's or hostel's location and amenities before booking
- Use travel apps like Hotel Tonight or Last Minute Travel to find last-minute deals

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Research the area before booking, and avoid neighborhoods with high crime rates
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID
- Keep valuables secure, and use hotel safes or lockers when available
- Be aware of your surroundings, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash
- Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation, like metro stations or bus stops
