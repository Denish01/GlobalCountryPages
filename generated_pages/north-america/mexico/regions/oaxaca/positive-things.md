[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Templo de Santo Domingo de Guzmán** in Oaxaca City is a stunning example of **Baroque architecture** and a testament to the region's rich cultural heritage.
- The **Día de los Muertos** celebrations in Oaxaca are a unique and fascinating blend of **indigenous and Catholic traditions**.
- The **Guelaguetza** is a vibrant and colorful celebration of **Oaxacan music, dance, and culture** that takes place every year in Oaxaca City.
- The **Tule Tree**, a massive and ancient **ahuehuete tree**, is a natural wonder and a sacred site for the indigenous people of the region.
- The **Museo de las Culturas de Oaxaca** is a world-class museum that showcases the region's rich cultural diversity and history.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Benito Juárez**, one of Mexico's most beloved presidents, was born in Oaxaca and played a significant role in the country's history and development.
- **Rufino Tamayo**, a renowned Oaxacan artist, made significant contributions to **Mexican modern art** and helped to promote the region's cultural heritage globally.
- The **Oaxacan diaspora** has had a profound impact on the cultural and economic development of many countries, particularly in the United States, where Oaxacan immigrants have established thriving communities and businesses.
- The **values of community and cooperation** that are deeply ingrained in Oaxacan culture have inspired social and economic development projects around the world.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **hospitality of the Oaxacan people** is legendary, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome they receive from locals.
- The **cuisine of Oaxaca** is a major highlight, with popular dishes like **mole negro**, **tlayudas**, and **tacos de chapulines** being a must-try for any visitor.
- The **natural scenery of Oaxaca** is breathtaking, with the **Sierra Norte** mountains, the **Lagunas de Chacahua** national park, and the **Pacific coast** offering a wide range of outdoor activities and stunning views.
- The **rich cultural heritage of Oaxaca** is a major draw, with visitors able to explore ancient ruins, museums, and traditional villages.
- The **vibrant markets and festivals** of Oaxaca, such as the **Mercado de Abastos** and the **Guelaguetza**, are a feast for the senses and a great way to experience the region's culture and traditions.
- The **mezcal distilleries and tastings** are a popular activity among visitors, with many opportunities to learn about the production process and sample some of the region's finest mezcals.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Pueblo Magico de Teotitlán del Valle** is a charming and traditional village that is home to many skilled artisans and offers a glimpse into Oaxacan rural life.
- The **Hierve el Agua** natural spring is a stunning and secluded spot that is perfect for hiking and swimming.
- The **Lagunas de Chacahua** national park is a protected area that is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including crocodiles, birds, and turtles.
- The **Cueva del Molino** is a fascinating cave system that features stunning rock formations and ancient petroglyphs.
