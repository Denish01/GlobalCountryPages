[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **pickpocketing** and **bag snatching** in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations.
- Be aware of **scams** targeting tourists, including fake police officers and **ATM scams**.
- Take precautions to avoid **food poisoning**, by eating at reputable restaurants and avoiding undercooked meat and raw vegetables.
- Be mindful of **traffic accidents**, which can occur on Oaxaca's roads, particularly at night.
- Be cautious of **protests and demonstrations**, which can occasionally turn violent.
- Be aware of **petty theft**, including **hotel room break-ins** and **theft from rental cars**.
- Take precautions to avoid **mosquito-borne illnesses**, such as **dengue fever** and **zika virus**.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for travel to Oaxaca.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not considered safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities:** Oaxaca City has a range of modern medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, but medical care can be limited in rural areas.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available in Oaxaca, and many medications can be purchased over-the-counter.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always use licensed taxis or reputable transportation services, and agree on the fare before you start your journey.
- Be mindful of your belongings, particularly in crowded areas, and keep valuables secure.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit and populated areas.
- Keep your hotel room door locked, and use the hotel safe to store valuables.
- Be cautious of **ATM scams**, and use machines located in secure areas, such as banks or hotels.
- Learn some basic Spanish phrases, and be respectful of local customs and traditions.
- Stay informed about local conditions, through your government's travel advisory and local news sources.
