[SECTION] What Is Jalisco? [/SECTION]
Jalisco is a region in western Mexico, bordered by the states of Nayarit to the northwest, Zacatecas to the north, Aguascalientes to the northeast, San Luis Potosí to the east, Michoacán to the southeast, and Colima to the south. The state has a diverse geography, with mountains, valleys, and coastal regions, and is home to several major cities, including Guadalajara, the second-largest city in Mexico. Jalisco is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its traditional music, dance, and cuisine, as well as its historic architecture and vibrant arts scene.

Jalisco is also a major economic hub, with a strong focus on industry, agriculture, and tourism. The state is home to several major universities and research institutions, and is a center for innovation and technology in Mexico. The capital city, Guadalajara, is a popular tourist destination, known for its historic center, cultural attractions, and vibrant nightlife.

The state of Jalisco has a long and complex history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to pre-Columbian times. The region was an important center for the Aztecs and other indigenous cultures, and was later colonized by the Spanish. Today, Jalisco is a thriving and diverse region, with a strong sense of identity and a rich cultural heritage.
