# Cost of Living in Jalisco - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Jalisco, a region in Mexico, is generally considered to be affordable, with a daily budget of $30-60 USD for backpackers, $60-120 USD for mid-range travelers, and $150-300 USD for those seeking comfort.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $30-60 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $150-300 USD
Currency: MXN
Tipping Custom: expected (10-15% in restaurants and bars)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Jalisco? [/SECTION]
Jalisco is a region in western Mexico that offers a unique blend of urban and rural experiences. The cost of living in Jalisco is generally lower compared to other major cities in Mexico, such as Mexico City or Cancun. However, prices can vary depending on the location, with cities like Guadalajara and Puerto Vallarta tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. Compared to global averages, Jalisco is considered to be an affordable destination, with a cost of living that is lower than in many countries in North America and Europe.

In terms of neighbors, Jalisco is bordered by the states of Nayarit, Zacatecas, Aguascalientes, and Colima, among others. The cost of living in these neighboring states can be similar to Jalisco, although prices can vary depending on the specific location and local economy. Overall, Jalisco offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists, expats, and digital nomads.
