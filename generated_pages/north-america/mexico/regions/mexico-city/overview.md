# Mexico City - Overview & Essential Facts

Mexico City is the capital and largest city of Mexico, a country located in the southern portion of North America, known for its rich cultural heritage, historic landmarks, and vibrant urban life.

[SECTION] What Is Mexico City? [/SECTION]
Mexico City is a **megacity**, located in the Valley of Mexico, a large valley in the high plateaus of south-central Mexico. It is situated at an altitude of 2,240 meters above sea level and is surrounded by mountains and volcanoes. Mexico City is known for its **cultural significance**, with a rich history dating back to the time of the Aztecs, who founded the city in 1325. The city is home to many historic landmarks, including the **Zocalo**, the main square of the city, and the **Cathedral of the Assumption**, a stunning example of colonial architecture.

Mexico City is also a major economic and financial center, with a strong **service sector** and a growing **technology industry**. The city is home to many universities and research institutions, including the **National Autonomous University of Mexico**, one of the largest and most prestigious universities in the country. Mexico City is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year with its vibrant **cultural scene**, delicious **cuisine**, and friendly **people**.

The city's **geographic position** makes it an important hub for trade and commerce, with many major highways and railroads connecting it to other parts of the country and to the United States. Mexico City is also a major **air travel hub**, with two international airports, including the **Benito Juarez International Airport**, which is one of the busiest airports in Latin America.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1325 - The Aztecs found the city of Tenochtitlan, which would later become Mexico City.
2. 1521 - The Spanish conquistador Hernan Cortes conquers the city and establishes it as the capital of the Viceroyalty of New Spain.
3. 1821 - Mexico gains independence from Spain, and Mexico City becomes the capital of the new nation.
4. 1864 - The French occupy the city and establish the Second Mexican Empire, with Maximilian I as emperor.
5. 1910 - The Mexican Revolution begins, and Mexico City becomes a major battleground in the fight for independence and democracy.
6. 1968 - The city hosts the Summer Olympics, which helps to establish Mexico City as a major international city.
7. 1985 - A devastating earthquake strikes the city, killing thousands of people and causing widespread damage.

[SECTION] What Makes Mexico City Unique [/SECTION]
- Mexico City is home to the **ancient Aztec temple of Templo Mayor**, which is one of the most important archaeological sites in the country.
- The city has a unique **cuisine**, which is a blend of indigenous, Spanish, and African influences, and is known for its delicious dishes such as **tacos al pastor** and **mole poblano**.
- Mexico City is a major **cultural center**, with many museums, galleries, and performance venues, including the **Palacio de Bellas Artes** and the **Teatro de la Ciudad**.
- The city has a vibrant **nightlife scene**, with many bars, clubs, and lounges, especially in the **Reforma** and **Condesa** neighborhoods.
- Mexico City is surrounded by **natural beauty**, with many parks and gardens, including the **Chapultepec Park**, which is one of the largest urban parks in the world.

[SECTION] Quick Summary [/SECTION]
Mexico City is a vibrant and culturally rich city that is known for its historic landmarks, delicious cuisine, and friendly people, making it a popular destination for tourists and business travelers alike.
