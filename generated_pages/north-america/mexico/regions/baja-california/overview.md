[SECTION] What Is Baja California? [/SECTION]
Baja California is a **peninsula** located in the northwestern part of Mexico, bordered by the Pacific Ocean to the west, the Gulf of California to the east, and the U.S.-Mexico border to the north. The region is part of the **Baja California Peninsula**, which also includes the state of Baja California Sur. Baja California is known for its unique cultural heritage, which is a blend of indigenous, Spanish, and American influences. The region is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including **desert landscapes**, **beaches**, and **mountain ranges**.

Geographically, Baja California is a narrow peninsula, stretching over 1,200 kilometers from the U.S.-Mexico border to the southern tip of the peninsula. The region is characterized by a diverse range of landscapes, including **coastal plains**, **deserts**, and **mountain ranges**. The **Sierra de Baja California** mountain range runs along the eastern edge of the peninsula, while the **Pacific Coast** is home to a series of **beaches** and **bays**.

Baja California is also an important economic region, with a strong focus on **agriculture**, **mining**, and **tourism**. The region is home to a number of major cities, including **Mexicali**, **Tijuana**, and **Ensenada**, which are known for their vibrant cultural scenes and economic opportunities.
