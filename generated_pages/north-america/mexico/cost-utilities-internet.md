# Utility & Internet Costs in Mexico

The cost of utilities and internet in Mexico can vary depending on the location, usage, and provider, but on average, a medium-sized apartment (85 sqm) can expect to pay around $100-150 USD per month for utilities and $20-50 USD per month for internet.

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Mexico's utility infrastructure is generally well-developed, with most urban areas having access to reliable electricity, water, and gas services. However, costs can fluctuate depending on the season, with higher electricity bills during the summer months due to increased air conditioning usage. The Mexican government has implemented measures to stabilize utility prices, but inflation can still impact the cost of utilities. Overall, the quality of utility services in Mexico is good, but it's essential to be mindful of usage to keep costs under control.

The cost of utilities in Mexico can also vary depending on the region, with cities like Mexico City and Guadalajara tend to have higher prices than smaller towns and rural areas. Additionally, some utilities, such as electricity and gas, may have different rates for residential and commercial users. It's crucial to research and understand the local utility rates and regulations to avoid unexpected costs.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities, it's recommended to contact the local provider (e.g., CFE for electricity) and provide proof of address and identification.
- Consider using prepaid electricity plans to avoid unexpected bills and **save on costs**.
- For internet services, **Telmex** and **Axtel** are popular providers, but it's essential to research and compare plans to find the best option.
- To save on utility bills, **reduce energy consumption** by using energy-efficient appliances and turning off lights and electronics when not in use.
- Be aware of **seasonal variations** in utility prices and plan accordingly to avoid higher bills during peak months.
