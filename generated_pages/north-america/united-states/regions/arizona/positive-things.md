[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Taliesin West** historic site, located in Scottsdale, is a **National Historic Landmark** and was the winter home and studio of famous architect **Frank Lloyd Wright**.
- The **Heard Museum** in Phoenix is dedicated to the preservation and celebration of **Native American** art and culture, with a collection of over 40,000 objects and artifacts.
- The **Arizona Renaissance Festival** is an annual event that showcases medieval-themed entertainment, food, and crafts, attracting over 200,000 visitors each year.
- The **Tucson Symphony Orchestra** is a professional orchestra that performs a wide range of classical music, from **Mozart** to **Beethoven**, and has a strong commitment to music education and community outreach.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Sandra Day O'Connor**, the first female **Supreme Court Justice**, was born in Arizona and attended **Stanford University**, where she earned her undergraduate and law degrees.
- **Cesar Chavez**, the famous labor leader and civil rights activist, was born in Arizona and worked to improve the lives of **Mexican-American** farmworkers, co-founding the **United Farm Workers** union.
- **John McCain**, the late **U.S. Senator** and **Presidential candidate**, was a prominent figure in Arizona politics and had a significant impact on national policy, serving as a **Naval Aviator** during the **Vietnam War**.
- The **Native American** community in Arizona has made significant contributions to the state's cultural and economic development, with tribes like the **Navajo Nation** and **Tohono O'odham Nation** operating successful businesses and preserving their traditional way of life.
