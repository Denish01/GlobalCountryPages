[SECTION] What Is Arizona? [/SECTION]
Arizona is a state located in the southwestern United States, bordered by the states of California, Nevada, Utah, New Mexico, and the Mexican state of Sonora. It is the sixth largest state in the country, covering an area of approximately 113,990 square miles. Arizona is known for its diverse geography, which includes deserts, mountains, and plateaus, and its rich cultural heritage, which reflects the influence of Native American, Spanish, and Mexican traditions.

Arizona is not an independent country, but rather a part of the United States, with its own state government and a strong economy based on industries such as tourism, agriculture, and mining. The state capital, Phoenix, is the most populous city in the state and a major cultural and economic center. Arizona is also home to many important national parks and monuments, including the Grand Canyon, one of the most famous natural wonders in the United States.

The state's unique geography and climate have made it an attractive destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts, who come to enjoy activities such as hiking, camping, and skiing. Arizona is also known for its vibrant cities, including Tucson, Flagstaff, and Sedona, which offer a range of cultural and recreational activities, including museums, festivals, and performances.
