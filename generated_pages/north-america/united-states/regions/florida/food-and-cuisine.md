# Florida Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Florida, be sure to try the state's unique fusion of **Seafood**, **Caribbean**, and **Southern** cuisine, which showcases a diverse range of dishes, from fresh **Stone Crab Claws** to **Cuban Sandwiches**.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: Fresh seafood, citrus fruits, key limes, conch meat
National Dish: **Florida Stone Crab Claws**, a seasonal delicacy harvested from October to May
Meal Times: Typical breakfast times are 7-10 am, lunch from 11 am-2 pm, and dinner from 5-9 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 15-20% of the total bill is customary
Dietary Notes: Florida offers a wide range of halal, kosher, and vegetarian-friendly options, particularly in larger cities like Miami and Tampa
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Florida's cuisine is a unique blend of different cultures, including **Caribbean**, **Latin American**, and **Southern** influences. The state's geographic location and history have contributed to this diverse culinary landscape, with **Key West** being a hub for **Conch Republic** cuisine and **Miami** showcasing a strong **Cuban** influence. The availability of fresh **seafood**, particularly **stone crab claws**, **grouper**, and **lobster**, has also played a significant role in shaping the state's cuisine. From **beachside barbecue** to **fine dining** establishments, Florida offers a wide range of culinary experiences.

The **Southern** influence is evident in the state's love for **comfort food**, including **fried chicken**, **biscuits and gravy**, and **sweet potato pie**. Meanwhile, the **Caribbean** and **Latin American** influences can be seen in the use of **citrus**, **coconut**, and **spicy** flavors in many dishes. Florida's cuisine is also characterized by its emphasis on **fresh**, **local**, and **seasonal** ingredients, which reflects the state's strong agricultural industry.

Florida's food culture is not just about the cuisine itself, but also about the **dining experience**. From **casual beachside eateries** to **upscale restaurants**, the state offers a wide range of settings in which to enjoy its unique cuisine. Whether you're looking for a **romantic dinner** or a **family-friendly meal**, Florida has something to offer. With its diverse culinary landscape and emphasis on fresh, local ingredients, Florida is a food lover's paradise.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Tipping**: 15-20% of the total bill is customary in restaurants and bars
- **Reservations**: Recommended for upscale restaurants, especially during peak season
- **Dress Code**: Casual attire is acceptable in most restaurants, but upscale establishments may require dressy casual or formal wear
- **Eating Etiquette**: Table manners are generally informal, but it's customary to keep your hands visible and not to eat with your hands
- **Payment**: Credit cards are widely accepted, but cash is also accepted in most establishments

[SECTION] Where to Eat [/SECTION]
- **Beachside Restaurants**: Offer stunning views of the ocean and a casual, laid-back atmosphere
- **Food Trucks**: Provide a wide range of cuisine options, from seafood to BBQ, often at affordable prices
- **Markets**: Such as the **Miami Flea** or **Tampa Market**, offer a variety of food vendors and local produce
- **Fine Dining Establishments**: Such as **Joe's Stone Crab** or **The Forge**, offer upscale cuisine and exceptional service
- **Cafes**: Such as **Cafe Versailles** or **The Salty Donut**, offer a cozy atmosphere and a wide range of coffee and pastry options

[SECTION] Food Markets & Food Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Miami Flea**: A monthly market featuring local vendors, live music, and a wide range of cuisine options, located in the **Wynwood** neighborhood
- **Tampa Market**: A weekly market featuring local produce, artisanal goods, and a variety of food vendors, located in the **Downtown** area
- **Key West Lobsterfest**: An annual festival celebrating the **lobster** season, featuring live music, cooking demonstrations, and a wide range of seafood options, located in the **Key West** area
- **Everglades City Seafood Festival**: An annual festival featuring fresh seafood, live music, and a wide range of vendors, located in the **Everglades City** area
- **Florida Keys Food and Wine Festival**: A annual festival featuring local cuisine, wine, and live music, located in the **Florida Keys** area
