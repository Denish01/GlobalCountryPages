Getting There

Ohio has several major airports that provide easy access to the state, including John Glenn Columbus International Airport, Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, and Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky International Airport. These airports offer direct flights to many domestic and international destinations, making it convenient to get to Ohio from anywhere in the world. Additionally, Ohio's strategic location in the Midwest makes it a major hub for road and rail travel, with several major highways and interstates passing through the state, including I-70, I-71, and I-80.

For those traveling overland, Ohio has several major entry points, including the Pennsylvania border to the east, the West Virginia border to the southeast, the Kentucky border to the south, the Indiana border to the west, and the Michigan border to the north. These entry points provide easy access to Ohio's major cities and attractions, and are well-connected to the state's highway system. Whether you're driving, flying, or taking the train, getting to Ohio is relatively easy, and the state's well-developed transportation system makes it simple to get around once you arrive.

Domestic Flights

Ohio has an extensive domestic air network, with several major airlines operating flights to and from the state's major airports. Some of the major domestic airlines that operate in Ohio include American Airlines, Delta Air Lines, United Airlines, and Southwest Airlines. These airlines offer flights to many destinations within the United States, including major hubs like New York City, Los Angeles, and Chicago. When booking domestic flights in Ohio, it's a good idea to compare prices across multiple airlines to find the best deal, and to book in advance to ensure availability. Approximate costs for domestic flights in Ohio can range from $100 to $500 or more, depending on the route and time of year.

Trains & Rail

Ohio has a moderate rail network, with several major routes passing through the state. The state is served by Amtrak, the national rail service, which operates several routes through Ohio, including the Lake Shore Limited, the Cardinal/Hoosier State, and the Capitol Limited. These routes provide access to many of Ohio's major cities, including Columbus, Cleveland, and Cincinnati, as well as connections to other major cities in the Midwest and East Coast. While Ohio's rail network is not as extensive as some other states, it provides a convenient and relatively affordable way to travel, with approximate costs ranging from $50 to $200 or more, depending on the route and class of service.

Buses & Coaches

Ohio has a well-developed bus and coach network, with several major companies operating routes throughout the state. Some of the major bus companies that operate in Ohio include Greyhound, Megabus, and Barons Bus Lines. These companies offer routes to many destinations within Ohio, as well as connections to other major cities in the Midwest and East Coast. The quality of bus travel in Ohio is generally good, with comfortable and well-maintained vehicles, and approximate costs ranging from $20 to $100 or more, depending on the route and time of year.

Driving

  • To drive in Ohio, you will need a valid driver's license from the United States or a valid international driver's license.
  • Road conditions in Ohio are generally good, with well-maintained highways and interstates, but be aware of potential construction delays and winter weather conditions.
  • Fuel costs in Ohio are generally lower than in other parts of the country, with approximate costs ranging from $2 to $3 per gallon.
  • Car rental companies are widely available in Ohio, including major brands like Enterprise, Hertz, and Avis, and can be booked in advance online or at airport locations.
  • When driving in Ohio, be aware of potential hazards like deer and other wildlife, especially at night, and be sure to follow all traffic laws and regulations.
  • It's also a good idea to have a GPS or map, as some areas of the state can be rural and have limited cell phone reception.

Transport Tips

  • Be aware of potential scams, like fake taxi drivers or ride-hailing services, and always use licensed and reputable companies.
  • Payment methods in Ohio are generally credit or debit card, but some smaller businesses may only accept cash, so it's a good idea to have some local currency on hand.
  • Download transportation apps like Uber or Lyft to make it easy to get around, and consider using public transportation or walking or biking when possible.
  • Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas or tourist destinations, and keep an eye on your luggage and personal items at all times.
  • Consider purchasing a Ohio State Park Pass for access to many of the state's beautiful parks and outdoor recreation areas.
  • Don't drink and drive, and always follow all traffic laws and regulations to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.
  • Be prepared for winter weather conditions, like snow and ice, and use caution when driving or walking in these conditions.
  • Take advantage of Ohio's many scenic byways and rural roads to explore the state's charming countryside and small towns.
