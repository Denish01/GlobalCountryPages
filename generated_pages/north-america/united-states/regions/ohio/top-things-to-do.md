# Top Things to Do in Ohio — Attractions & Experiences

Ohio offers a diverse range of activities and attractions that cater to different interests, from visiting the iconic **Rock and Roll Hall of Fame** in Cleveland to exploring the scenic **Hocking Hills** in southeastern Ohio.

[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| Cedar Point | Sandusky | Amusement Park | Experience thrilling roller coasters and water rides |

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **hot air balloon ride** over the Ohio countryside for a breathtaking view
- Visit the **Amish Country** in Holmes County to experience the traditional way of life of the Amish community
- Go on a **wine tasting tour** in the Lake Erie Islands or the Ohio River Valley
- Attend a **Cleveland Indians baseball game** at Progressive Field
- Explore the **Street Art scene** in Columbus's Short North neighborhood
- Take a **stargazing tour** at the **John Glenn Astronomy Park** in Logan
- Visit the **Ohio Light Opera** in Wooster for a unique musical performance

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Hike the **Buckeye Trail**, a 1,444-mile trail that spans the state
- Visit the **Cuyahoga Valley National Park** for scenic hiking and biking trails
- Explore the **Lake Erie Islands**, including **Put-in-Bay** and **Kelleys Island**
- Go **kayaking** or **canoeing** on the **Ohio River** or **Lake Erie**
- Visit the **Shawnee State Forest** for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Cleveland Museum of Art** to see a collection of over 45,000 works of art
- Explore the **National Underground Railroad Freedom Center** in Cincinnati
- Attend a performance at the **Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra**
- Visit the **Ohio History Center** in Columbus to learn about the state's history
- Take a **glassblowing class** at the **Glass Axis** in Columbus
- Visit the **Taft Museum of Art** in Cincinnati to see a collection of American and European art

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Gorman Nature Center** in Mansfield for hiking and wildlife watching
- Explore the **German Village** neighborhood in Columbus for historic architecture and cultural heritage
- Take a **tour of the Ohio Statehouse** in Columbus
- Visit the **Toledo Museum of Art** to see a collection of over 30,000 works of art
- Go **antiquing** in the **Cambridge** area, known for its historic shops and dealers

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip from Columbus to **Hocking Hills** (120 miles, 2 hours) for hiking and scenic views
- Visit **Cleveland** (140 miles, 2.5 hours) from Columbus for the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and other attractions
- Take a day trip from Cincinnati to **Lexington, Kentucky** (90 miles, 1.5 hours) for horse racing and historic sites
- Visit **Put-in-Bay** (120 miles, 2.5 hours) from Cleveland for island hopping and beach activities
- Explore **Amish Country** (60 miles, 1 hour) from Columbus for a unique cultural experience

[SECTION] Planning Tips [/SECTION]
