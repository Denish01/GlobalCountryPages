[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
Illinois has a well-developed transportation system, with domestic flights, trains, buses, and highways connecting the state's major cities and regions. The state is served by two major airports, O'Hare International Airport and Midway International Airport, both located in the Chicago area. Amtrak trains connect Chicago to other major cities in the state, as well as to other parts of the country. Greyhound buses and other regional bus services provide connections to smaller cities and towns, while the state's highways and interstates make driving a convenient option for exploring the state. Overall, Illinois is easily accessible by air, land, or rail, making it a great destination for visitors from all over the world. 

[CALLOUT] Visitors to Illinois should be aware that traffic in the Chicago area can be heavy, especially during rush hour, and that parking in the city can be limited and expensive.
