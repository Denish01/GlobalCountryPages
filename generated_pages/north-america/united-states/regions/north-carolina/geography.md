[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
North Carolina is situated in the southeastern United States, lying between the **Atlantic Coastal Plain** and the **Appalachian Mountains**. The state is bordered by Virginia to the north, Tennessee to the west, Georgia and South Carolina to the south, and the Atlantic Ocean to the east. The state's unique geography has played a significant role in shaping its history, economy, and culture. The **Blue Ridge Mountains** and the **Great Smoky Mountains** form part of the state's western border, while the **Outer Banks** and the **Cape Fear River** are notable geographical features along the coast.

The state's location has made it an important hub for trade and commerce, with several major ports and transportation routes passing through it. The **Neuse River**, **Cape Fear River**, and **Roanoke River** are some of the major rivers that flow through the state, providing waterways for transportation and supporting a diverse range of aquatic life. The state's coastline is approximately 301 miles long, with a mix of sandy beaches, barrier islands, and scenic coastal towns.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
North Carolina's terrain is diverse, ranging from the **coastal plains** in the east to the **mountains** in the west. The state can be divided into three main geographic regions: the **Coastal Plain**, the **Piedmont**, and the **Mountain Region**. The Coastal Plain is a low-lying area that stretches from the Atlantic Ocean to the **Fall Line**, a geographic boundary that marks the transition from the coastal plain to the piedmont region. The Piedmont region is a plateau that covers much of the state's central area, with rolling hills, valleys, and scenic countryside.

The Mountain Region is home to some of the highest peaks in the eastern United States, including **Mount Mitchell**, the highest point in the state. The **Blue Ridge Parkway**, a 469-mile scenic road, runs through the mountains, offering stunning views and access to hiking trails, waterfalls, and other natural attractions. The state's mountains are also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including **deciduous forests**, **evergreen forests**, and a variety of **wildlife** such as black bears, deer, and turkey.

The state's landscape has been shaped by millions of years of geological activity, including **erosion**, **weathering**, and **tectonic activity**. The state's unique geology has created a wide range of **landforms**, including **caves**, **waterfalls**, and **rock formations**. The **Jordan Lake State Recreation Area** and the **Lake Norman** are popular destinations for outdoor recreation, with opportunities for boating, fishing, and hiking.
