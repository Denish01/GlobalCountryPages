[SECTION] What Is North Carolina? [/SECTION]
North Carolina is a **state** located in the southeastern United States, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the east, Virginia to the north, Tennessee to the west, and South Carolina and Georgia to the south. It is part of the **United States of America** and is divided into 100 counties. The state has a diverse geography, ranging from the **Blue Ridge Mountains** in the west to the **Outer Banks** along the coast. North Carolina is known for its rich history, including its role in the **American Revolution** and the **Civil War**, as well as its vibrant culture, which is influenced by its **African American**, **European**, and **Native American** heritage.

The state has a strong economy, with major industries including **technology**, **finance**, **manufacturing**, and **agriculture**. The **Research Triangle Park**, located in the Raleigh-Durham area, is one of the largest research parks in the world and is home to many major companies, including **IBM** and **Cisco Systems**. North Carolina is also known for its **education system**, which includes several top-ranked universities, including **Duke University** and **University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill**.

North Carolina has a diverse climate, ranging from **humid subtropical** in the east to **humid continental** in the west. The state experiences **hot summers** and **mild winters**, with **hurricanes** and **tornadoes** occasionally affecting the area. The state's natural beauty, including its **mountains**, **coastlines**, and **forests**, makes it a popular destination for **tourism** and **outdoor recreation**.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1585 - English explorer **Sir Walter Raleigh** establishes the first English settlement in North America, **Roanoke Colony**, on an island in present-day Dare County.
2. 1712 - The **Tuscarora War** breaks out, a conflict between the **Tuscarora Native American tribe** and European colonists.
3. 1776 - North Carolina declares its independence from **Great Britain** and becomes one of the original 13 states of the United States.
4. 1861 - North Carolina secedes from the Union and joins the **Confederate States of America**, leading to its involvement in the **American Civil War**.
5. 1950s - The **Civil Rights Movement** gains momentum in North Carolina, with events such as the **Woolworth's lunch counter sit-in** in Greensboro.
6. 1980s - The **Research Triangle Park** is established, leading to rapid economic growth and development in the state.
7. 2010s - North Carolina experiences significant population growth, with the state's population increasing by over 20% between 2010 and 2020.

[SECTION] What Makes North Carolina Unique [/SECTION]
- North Carolina is home to the **highest peak east of the Mississippi River**, **Mount Mitchell**, which stands at 6,684 feet.
- The state has a **unique barbecue style**, with different regions specializing in **vinegar-based**, **tomato-based**, or **mustard-based** sauces.
- North Carolina is the **leading producer of sweet potatoes** in the United States, accounting for over 50% of the country's production.
- The state has a **thriving arts scene**, with numerous **museums**, **galleries**, and **festivals** throughout the year.
- North Carolina is home to the **Wright Brothers National Memorial**, which commemorates the site where **Orville and Wilbur Wright** successfully flew the first powered aircraft in 1903.
