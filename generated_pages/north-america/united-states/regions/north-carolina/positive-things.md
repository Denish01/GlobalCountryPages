[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Biltmore Estate**, located in Asheville, North Carolina, is the largest privately owned home in the United States and a premier example of **Gilded Age** architecture.
- The **North Carolina Museum of Art**, located in Raleigh, features a collection of over 5,000 works of art, including pieces by **Monet**, **Renoir**, and **Picasso**.
- The **Barbecue** tradition in North Carolina is a beloved cultural institution, with different regions of the state specializing in distinct styles of barbecue, such as **Lexington-style** and **Eastern-style**.
- The **Wilmington Riverwalk**, located in southeastern North Carolina, is a scenic pedestrian path along the Cape Fear River, featuring public art installations, shops, and restaurants.
- The **Asheville River Arts District**, located in western North Carolina, is a former industrial area turned arts district, featuring over 200 studios, galleries, and restaurants.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Madam C.J. Walker**, a native of Delta, North Carolina, was one of the wealthiest self-made women of her time, building a business empire in the early 20th century through her line of hair care and beauty products.
- **James Taylor**, a singer-songwriter from Chapel Hill, North Carolina, is a legendary musician known for his soothing voice and hit songs like **"Fire and Rain"** and **"Sweet Baby James"**.
- The **Lumbee** tribe, an indigenous people native to North Carolina, has made significant contributions to the state's cultural heritage, including their traditional music, art, and crafts.
- **John Edwards**, a former United States Senator from North Carolina, has been a prominent figure in national politics, advocating for issues such as healthcare reform and economic development.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **Blue Ridge Parkway**, a 469-mile scenic highway that runs through the western part of the state, is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering stunning views of the mountains and surrounding landscape.
- The **Outer Banks**, a chain of barrier islands along the North Carolina coast, is a beloved spot for beachgoers, with its natural beauty, historic lighthouses, and water sports.
- The **Asheville food scene**, which features a diverse range of restaurants, cafes, and breweries, is a major draw for visitors, with its emphasis on local ingredients, artisanal products, and creative cuisine.
- The **Great Smoky Mountains National Park**, which offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and fishing, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
- The **North Carolina wine country**, which includes over 200 wineries and vineyards, is a growing destination for wine enthusiasts, with its scenic landscapes, wine tastings, and vineyard tours.
- The **Wrightsville Beach**, a popular beach town located near Wilmington, is known for its water sports, fishing, and sunbathing, making it an ideal spot for families and outdoor enthusiasts.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]

- The **Lake Norman**, a large man-made lake located in the Piedmont region, offers a range of recreational activities, including boating, fishing, and water sports, making it a hidden gem for outdoor enthusiasts.
- The **Uwharrie National Forest**, located in the central part of the state, is a scenic forest area featuring hiking trails, camping sites, and scenic drives, providing a peaceful retreat for nature lovers.
