[SECTION] How Pennsylvania Is Divided [/SECTION]
Pennsylvania is divided into 67 counties, which are further divided into townships, boroughs, and cities. The state has a total of 2,561 municipalities, each with its own local government and administrative structure. The counties are grouped into several regions, including the Southeastern, South Central, Northeastern, and Northwestern regions, each with its own unique character and attractions. The state government is headquartered in Harrisburg, which is located in the central part of the state and serves as the capital.

The administrative structure of Pennsylvania is designed to provide local control and decision-making, while also ensuring that the state as a whole is well-coordinated and managed. The state government is responsible for providing a range of services, including education, healthcare, and transportation, while the local governments are responsible for providing more localized services, such as law enforcement, zoning, and waste management. This division of labor allows Pennsylvania to provide a high level of service to its citizens, while also ensuring that local communities have a strong voice in decision-making.

Pennsylvania has a well-developed transportation network, with a range of options for getting around the state.
