# Cost of Living in Pennsylvania - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Pennsylvania can vary depending on the location, lifestyle, and personal preferences, but overall it is considered to be moderately priced, with a daily budget ranging from $60-150 USD for a backpacker to $200-350 USD for a mid-range traveler.

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Pennsylvania? [/SECTION]
Pennsylvania is a state located in the northeastern United States, and its cost of living is relatively moderate compared to other states in the country. The cost of living in Pennsylvania is lower than in states like New York or California, but higher than in states like Ohio or Tennessee. The state's economy is diverse, with major industries in healthcare, education, and manufacturing, which contributes to its moderate cost of living. Overall, Pennsylvania is an affordable place to live, with a cost of living index score of 94.7, which is slightly lower than the national average.

In comparison to its neighboring states, Pennsylvania's cost of living is similar to that of New Jersey and Maryland, but lower than that of New York. The state's cost of living is also influenced by its urban and rural areas, with cities like Philadelphia and Pittsburgh tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. However, the state's overall cost of living is still relatively affordable, making it an attractive place to live, work, and visit.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting during the shoulder season (April-May or September-October) to avoid peak tourist prices.
- Take advantage of the state's many free attractions, such as the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall in Philadelphia.
- Use public transportation, such as buses and subways, to get around cities instead of renting a car or taking taxis.
- Eat at local eateries and food trucks instead of mid-range or fine dining restaurants.
- Look for discounts and promotions on accommodations, such as booking a hotel room outside of the city center or staying in a hostel.
- Take a hike or go for a walk in one of Pennsylvania's many beautiful state parks instead of paying for expensive outdoor activities.
