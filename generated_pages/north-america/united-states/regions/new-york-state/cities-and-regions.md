[SECTION] Regional Differences [/SECTION]
- The regions of New York State differ significantly in terms of culture, with the New York City metropolitan area being a global center for finance, media, and entertainment, while the rural areas of upstate New York have a more traditional, small-town way of life.
- The economy of the state also varies by region, with the New York City area being a hub for finance and technology, while the upstate regions have a more diversified economy based on industry, agriculture, and tourism.
- The climate of the state also differs by region, with the southeastern part of the state having a more moderate climate, while the northern and western parts of the state are colder and more prone to snow.
- The language spoken in the state is predominantly English, but there are also many other languages spoken, particularly in the New York City area, which is one of the most linguistically diverse cities in the world.

The state is also served by a range of private bus companies, including Greyhound and Trailways, which provide intercity bus service throughout the state. Overall, getting around New York State is relatively easy, with a range of options available to suit all budgets and preferences.
