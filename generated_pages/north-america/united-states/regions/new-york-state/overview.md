[SECTION] What Is New York State? [/SECTION]
New York State is a **federated state** located in the northeastern part of the United States, bordered by Vermont, Massachusetts, Connecticut, New Jersey, and Pennsylvania. It is situated in the **Northeastern United States**, a region that is home to some of the country's most populous cities and diverse landscapes. New York State is known for its vibrant cities, scenic natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage, making it a popular destination for tourists and a hub for business and industry.

The state's geography is characterized by its diverse landscapes, including the **Adirondack Mountains**, the **Hudson Valley**, and the **Niagara Falls**. The state's climate is also varied, with cold winters and warm summers, making it an ideal location for outdoor activities such as skiing, hiking, and fishing. New York State is also home to some of the world's most famous cities, including **New York City**, the state's largest city and a global center for finance, entertainment, and culture.

New York State has a long and complex history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the **Paleoindian period**. The state was originally inhabited by various Native American tribes, including the **Iroquois** and the **Algonquin**. In the 17th century, the state was colonized by the Dutch and the British, who established settlements and trading posts along the **Hudson River**. Today, New York State is a thriving and diverse region, with a strong economy, a rich cultural heritage, and a vibrant arts and entertainment scene.
