[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Mesa Verde National Park** is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its well-preserved Ancestral Puebloan cliff dwellings and mesa-top sites.
- The **Denver March Powwow** is a vibrant celebration of Native American culture, featuring traditional music, dance, and art.
- The **Crested Butte Wildflower Festival** showcases the stunning natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains, with guided hikes and workshops focused on wildflower identification and conservation.
- The **Telluride Bluegrass Festival** is a popular music event that has been running for over 40 years, featuring a mix of bluegrass, folk, and rock music.
- The **History Colorado Center** in Denver offers a unique glimpse into the state's rich history, with interactive exhibits and artifacts showcasing the lives of early Coloradans.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **John Denver**, the famous musician, was a long-time resident of Colorado and a passionate advocate for environmental conservation and social justice.
- **Madam C.J. Walker**, one of the first self-made female millionaires in America, got her start in business in Denver, where she developed a line of hair care products for African American women.
- **Jack Dempsey**, the legendary boxer, was born in Manassa, Colorado, and went on to become one of the most iconic figures in the history of the sport.
- The **Hutterite community** in Colorado has made significant contributions to the state's agricultural industry, with many Hutterite colonies operating successful farms and ranches throughout the state.
