# Cost of Living in Colorado - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Colorado can vary depending on the location, lifestyle, and personal preferences, but overall, it tends to be more expensive than the national average, with a daily budget ranging from $60-150 USD for backpackers, $100-250 USD for mid-range travelers, and $200-400 USD for comfort seekers.

Daily Budget (Comfort): $200-400 USD
Currency: USD
Tipping Custom: expected (15-20% in restaurants and bars)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Colorado? [/SECTION]
Colorado is considered one of the more expensive states in the United States, with a high cost of living index score of 126.7, compared to the national average of 100. The state's stunning natural beauty, outdoor recreational opportunities, and a strong economy have driven up housing costs, transportation, and other living expenses. Compared to its neighboring states, Colorado is more expensive than Utah and Arizona but comparable to Wyoming and New Mexico. The cost of living in Colorado's major cities, such as Denver and Boulder, is particularly high, with prices for housing, food, and transportation exceeding the national average.

The state's popularity among tourists and outdoor enthusiasts has also contributed to the high cost of living, with prices for accommodations, food, and activities increasing during peak season. However, with some planning and research, it is possible to find affordable options and enjoy all that Colorado has to offer. Visitors can explore the state's many free or low-cost attractions, such as national parks, forests, and scenic byways, and take advantage of discounts and package deals for accommodations and activities.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting during the off-season (April-May or September-October) to avoid peak prices for accommodations and activities.
- Take advantage of Colorado's many free or low-cost outdoor recreational opportunities, such as hiking, biking, or skiing.
- Look for package deals or discounts for accommodations, activities, and transportation.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on fuel and parking costs.
- Cook your own meals instead of eating out, and take advantage of happy hour specials or early bird discounts at restaurants.
- Research and compare prices for accommodations, activities, and transportation to find the best deals and save money.
