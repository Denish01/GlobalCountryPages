Georgia is situated in the southeastern United States, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the east and surrounded by several neighboring states. The state is bounded by Florida to the south, Tennessee and North Carolina to the north, and South Carolina and Alabama to the west. The Atlantic coastline of Georgia stretches for approximately 160 kilometers, providing a unique blend of coastal and inland landscapes. The state's strategic location allows for easy access to major ports and transportation routes, making it an important hub for trade and commerce.

The state's borders are defined by its geographic features, including the Blue Ridge Mountains to the north and the Okefenokee Swamp to the south. The state's diverse geography and climate support a wide range of flora and fauna, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.
