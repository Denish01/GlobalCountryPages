# Top Things to Do in Michigan — Attractions & Experiences

Michigan offers a diverse range of activities and attractions for visitors, from its stunning natural beauty to its vibrant cities and rich cultural heritage.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **pasties**-making class to learn about the traditional Upper Peninsula dish
- Go on a **wine tour** in the Leelanau Peninsula, known for its Riesling and other cold-climate wines
- Attend a **Detroit Tigers** baseball game at Comerica Park
- Explore the **Street Art** scene in Detroit's Eastern Market, featuring vibrant murals by local and international artists
- Visit the **Motown Museum**, the birthplace of the iconic record label and music genre
- Take a **dog sled tour** through the snowy landscapes of the Upper Peninsula
- Go on a **lighthouse tour** to see the historic beacons that line Michigan's coastline

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Hike the **Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park**, with its scenic trails and stunning views of Lake of the Clouds
- Visit the **Tahquamenon Falls State Park**, home to the picturesque Tahquamenon River and its waterfalls
- Explore the **Seney National Wildlife Refuge**, a protected area for migratory birds and other wildlife
- Go **kayaking** or **canoeing** on the tranquil waters of **Lake Huron** or **Lake Michigan**
- Take a **ski lift** ride at **Boyne Mountain Resort**, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History**, the largest institution of its kind in the world
- Explore the **Detroit Symphony Orchestra**, one of the most renowned orchestras in the United States
- Attend a **Festival of the Arts** in Grand Rapids, featuring live music, food, and artwork by local artists
- Take a **brewery tour** in Ann Arbor, home to a thriving craft beer scene
- Visit the **Michigan State Capitol**, a historic building with guided tours available

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Paulding Light**, a mysterious and unexplained phenomenon in the Upper Peninsula
- Explore the **Gerald R. Ford Birthsite and Gardens**, a peaceful oasis in the heart of Grand Rapids
- Go to the **Marquette Maritime Museum**, showcasing the city's rich maritime history
- Take a **ghost tour** of the historic town of **Marshall**, known for its paranormal activity
- Visit the **Keweenaw National Historical Park**, a unique park that preserves the region's copper mining heritage

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip from Detroit to **Ann Arbor** (45 miles, 1 hour), visiting the University of Michigan and the **Kerrytown Market**
- Drive from Grand Rapids to **Saugatuck** (50 miles, 1 hour), exploring the charming town's art galleries and beaches
- Visit **Mackinac Island** from **Mackinaw City** (10 miles, 1 hour, including ferry ride), experiencing the island's Victorian-era charm
- Go from Traverse City to **Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore** (30 miles, 45 minutes), hiking the dunes and enjoying the scenic views
- Take a day trip from Lansing to **East Lansing** (5 miles, 15 minutes), visiting the Michigan State University campus and the **Eli and Edythe Broad Art Museum**

[SECTION] Planning Tips [/SECTION]
