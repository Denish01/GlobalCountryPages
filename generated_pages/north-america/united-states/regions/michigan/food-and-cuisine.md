# Michigan Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Michigan, be sure to try the state's iconic dishes, such as **Pasties**, **Smoked Whitefish**, and **Cherry Pie**, which showcase the region's unique cultural influences and local ingredients.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: cherries, blueberries, beef, freshwater fish
National Dish: Pasties, a traditional Upper Peninsula dish filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables
Meal Times: 7-10 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 5-8 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 15-20% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: vegetarian-friendly options available, especially in larger cities like Ann Arbor and Detroit
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Michigan's cuisine is defined by its location in the Great Lakes region, with a strong emphasis on **freshwater fish**, **cherries**, and other locally sourced ingredients. The state's cultural influences, including its Native American, European, and Canadian heritage, have shaped its culinary landscape. The Upper Peninsula, in particular, is known for its traditional **Finnish** and **Cornish** dishes, such as Pasties and **Karjalanpiirakka** (Karelian pasties). In contrast, the Lower Peninsula has a more diverse culinary scene, with influences from **German**, **Italian**, and **Mexican** cuisines.

Michigan's food culture is also characterized by its **farm-to-table** movement, with many restaurants and cafes showcasing local, organic produce and meats. The state's **wineries** and **breweries** are also worth exploring, with many offering wine and beer tastings, as well as tours of their facilities. From the **Detroit-style pizza** to the **Traverse City cherry** festivals, Michigan's culinary scene is a reflection of its rich cultural heritage and natural resources.

The regional variations in Michigan's cuisine are also worth noting, with different parts of the state specializing in different types of cuisine. For example, the **Leelanau Peninsula** is known for its **wineries** and **cherry orchards**, while the **Upper Peninsula** is famous for its **smoked fish** and **game meats**. Understanding these regional variations can help visitors appreciate the diversity and complexity of Michigan's culinary landscape.

[SECTION] Street Food & Snacks [/SECTION]
- **Better Made Chips**: a Detroit-based potato chip company's crunchy, flavorful chips, $2-3 per bag
- **Kettle Corn**: a sweet and salty snack made with popcorn and often flavored with cherry or blueberry, $3-5 per bag
- **Smoked Fish**: smoked whitefish or trout, often served with crackers or bread, $5-10 per serving
- **Pasties**: a traditional Upper Peninsula dish filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables, $5-10 per serving
- **Cherry Pie**: a classic Michigan dessert made with fresh cherries and often served with vanilla ice cream, $5-10 per slice
- **Detroit-style Coney Dogs**: a classic Detroit street food made with hot dogs, chili, and onions, $3-5 per dog
- **Fried Pickles**: a crispy and tangy snack made with dill pickles and often served with ranch dressing, $4-6 per serving
- **Sanders Hot Fudge**: a Detroit-based chocolate company's famous hot fudge, $3-5 per serving

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Tipping**: 15-20% of the total bill is customary in restaurants and bars
- **Reservations**: recommended for popular restaurants, especially on weekends and during peak travel seasons
- **Dress Code**: casual to formal, depending on the restaurant and location
- **Eating Etiquette**: table manners are generally informal, but it's considered polite to wait for the host to start eating before beginning your meal
- **Food Sharing**: not common in Michigan, but it's becoming more popular in some restaurants and social settings
- **Dining Hours**: typical meal times are 7-10 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, and 5-8 pm for dinner

[SECTION] Where to Eat [/SECTION]
- **Restaurants**: a wide range of options, from fine dining to casual eateries, throughout Michigan
- **Food Courts**: found in many shopping malls and markets, offering a variety of cuisines and snacks
- **Markets**: such as the **Eastern Market** in Detroit and the **Traverse City Farmers Market**, offering fresh produce, meats, and specialty foods
- **Food Trucks**: a growing trend in Michigan, especially in larger cities like Detroit and Ann Arbor
- **Breweries**: many Michigan breweries, such as **Bell's Brewery** and **Founders Brewing Co.**, offer food and drinks on site
