[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History** is one of the largest institutions dedicated to African American history and culture in the US.
- The **Detroit Institute of Arts** is a premier art museum, featuring a collection of over 65,000 works of art, including pieces by famous artists such as Van Gogh and Monet.
- The **National Cherry Festival** is an annual celebration of Michigan's cherry harvest, with parades, music, and cherry-themed activities.
- The **Michigan Opera Theatre** is a professional opera company, producing a range of operas and musicals throughout the year.
- The **Ann Arbor Art Fair** is a popular event, showcasing the work of local and national artists, with a focus on contemporary art and crafts.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Henry Ford**, the founder of the Ford Motor Company, was a pioneering figure in the automotive industry, with a significant impact on modern transportation and manufacturing.
- **Stevie Wonder**, the famous musician, was born in Saginaw, Michigan, and has had a profound influence on the music industry, with a career spanning over five decades.
- **Magic Johnson**, the basketball legend, was born in Lansing, Michigan, and has made significant contributions to the sport, as well as to philanthropy and business.
- **Terry Crews**, the actor and former NFL player, was born in Flint, Michigan, and has become a prominent figure in the entertainment industry, with a range of film and TV credits.
