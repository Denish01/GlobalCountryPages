[SECTION] How Expensive Is Michigan? [/SECTION]
Michigan is a state located in the Great Lakes region of the United States, and its cost of living is relatively moderate compared to other states in the country. The cost of living in Michigan is lower than in many coastal states, such as California and New York, but higher than in some Southern states, such as Mississippi and Arkansas. Overall, Michigan's cost of living is close to the national average, making it an attractive destination for people looking for a balance between affordability and quality of life. Michigan's neighbors, such as Illinois, Indiana, and Ohio, have similar cost of living indices, making the region a relatively affordable place to live.

Michigan's economy is diverse, with major industries in manufacturing, healthcare, and education, which contributes to its moderate cost of living. The state's largest city, Detroit, has a higher cost of living than other parts of the state, but it is still relatively affordable compared to other major cities in the United States. The cost of living in Michigan can vary depending on the location, with cities like Ann Arbor and Grand Rapids tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas.
