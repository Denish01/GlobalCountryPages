# Getting Around Texas — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Texas, you can use a variety of transportation options including flying, trains, buses, and driving, with major cities like Houston and Dallas having well-developed public transportation systems.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Texas has several major international airports, with George Bush Intercontinental Airport (IAH) in Houston and Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) being the largest and busiest. These airports offer direct flights to numerous domestic and international destinations, making it easy to get to Texas from almost anywhere in the world. Additionally, there are several smaller international airports in cities like San Antonio, Austin, and El Paso, which offer flights to Mexico, Canada, and other destinations in the United States.

If you're coming from a neighboring state or Mexico, you can also enter Texas by car or bus, with several major highways and interstates connecting the state to its neighbors. The main overland entry points include Interstate 10 from Louisiana, Interstate 35 from Oklahoma, and US Highway 54 from New Mexico. There are also several bus companies that operate from Mexico and other states, offering a convenient and affordable way to get to Texas.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Texas has an extensive domestic air network, with several major airlines operating flights within the state. American Airlines, United Airlines, and Southwest Airlines are some of the main carriers, offering frequent flights between major cities like Houston, Dallas, San Antonio, and Austin. You can book flights online or through the airlines' mobile apps, and prices vary depending on the route and time of year. On average, a one-way domestic flight can cost between $100 and $300, depending on the distance and demand.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Texas has a limited rail network, with Amtrak being the main operator of passenger trains in the state. The Texas Eagle route runs from Chicago to San Antonio, with stops in Dallas and Austin, while the Sunset Limited route runs from New Orleans to Los Angeles, with stops in Houston and El Paso. The trains are comfortable and offer a scenic way to see the state, but the network is limited and not as extensive as in other parts of the country. You can book tickets online or at train stations, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several intercity bus companies that operate in Texas, including Greyhound, Megabus, and FlixBus. These companies offer affordable and convenient travel between major cities, with prices starting from around $20 for a one-way ticket. The buses are comfortable and offer amenities like Wi-Fi and power outlets, but the journey times can be long and the routes may not be as frequent as flights or trains.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Texas, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home state or country, and car insurance is mandatory.
- The road conditions in Texas are generally good, with well-maintained highways and interstates.
- Fuel costs can be high, especially for long road trips, so it's a good idea to budget accordingly.
- Car rental companies like Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise have locations at major airports and cities, and prices vary depending on the type of vehicle and rental period.
- When driving in Texas, be aware of the large size of the state and the potential for long driving times, as well as the risk of traffic jams and road closures due to weather or construction.
- It's also a good idea to be aware of the local driving laws and regulations, such as the use of seatbelts and the restriction on using mobile phones while driving.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of the **traffic laws** in Texas, including the use of seatbelts and the restriction on using mobile phones while driving.
- Use **ride-hailing apps** like Uber and Lyft to get around cities, as they are convenient and affordable.
- Consider purchasing a **public transportation pass** for unlimited travel on buses and trains in major cities.
- Be prepared for **long driving times** and potential **traffic jams** when driving in Texas.
- Use **gas stations** and **rest stops** along highways to refuel and take breaks.
- Download **traffic and navigation apps** like Waze or Google Maps to help you navigate the roads and avoid traffic.
- Be aware of **scams** and **petty crime** in tourist areas, and take necessary precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.
- Consider purchasing **travel insurance** to cover unexpected medical or travel-related expenses.
