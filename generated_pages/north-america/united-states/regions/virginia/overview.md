[SECTION] What Is Virginia? [/SECTION]
Virginia is a region in the United States, bordered by the states of Maryland, West Virginia, Kentucky, Tennessee, and North Carolina. It is situated in the eastern part of the country, with the Atlantic Ocean to the east and the Appalachian Mountains to the west. The state is known for its historical significance, having been the birthplace of many prominent American figures, including eight U.S. presidents. Virginia is also famous for its natural beauty, with the Blue Ridge Mountains, the Shenandoah Valley, and the Chesapeake Bay being some of its most notable features.

Virginia's geography is diverse, with coastal plains, mountains, and forests, making it an attractive destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The state's economy is driven by various industries, including technology, agriculture, and tourism. Virginia is home to many prestigious universities and research institutions, including the University of Virginia and Virginia Tech. The state's culture is a unique blend of traditional and modern elements, with a strong emphasis on history, music, and arts.

The name "Virginia" is derived from Elizabeth I, the "Virgin Queen" of England, who was the reigning monarch when the English established their first permanent settlement in the New World. The state's nickname, "The Old Dominion," reflects its long history and its role as one of the original 13 colonies. Today, Virginia is a thriving state with a strong economy, a rich cultural heritage, and a diverse population.
