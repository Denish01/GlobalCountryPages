# Detroit Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Detroit, be sure to try the city's unique blend of American, European, and Middle Eastern cuisines, which is reflected in its signature dishes such as square pizza, Coney dogs, and pierogies.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: square pizza crust, ground beef, sausage, Middle Eastern spices
National Dish: Coney dog, a hot dog topped with chili, onions, and mustard
Meal Times: typical breakfast times are 7-10 am, lunch is 11 am-2 pm, and dinner is 5-9 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 15-20% of the total bill is customary
Dietary Notes: Detroit has a variety of halal, kosher, and vegetarian-friendly options, particularly in the Middle Eastern and Eastern European communities
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Detroit's cuisine is shaped by its history of immigration, with influences from Eastern Europe, the Middle East, and America. The city is famous for its **Coney Islands**, restaurants that serve classic American diner fare with a Greek twist, and its **square pizza**, a style of pizza that originated in Detroit. The city's food culture is also defined by its **neighborhoods**, each with its own unique culinary identity, such as the **Eastern Market**, which offers a variety of fresh produce, meats, and specialty foods.

Detroit's cuisine has been influenced by its **industrial heritage**, with many workers from different ethnic backgrounds coming to the city to work in the auto factories. This diversity is reflected in the city's **food traditions**, which include dishes such as **pierogies**, **kibbeh**, and **gyro sandwiches**. The city's food culture is also characterized by its **casual, no-frills dining style**, with many restaurants and eateries serving hearty, comforting food at affordable prices.

From **classic diners** to **high-end restaurants**, Detroit's food scene has something for everyone. The city is also home to a thriving **food truck scene**, with many vendors serving everything from traditional American fare to international cuisine. Whether you're in the mood for a **Coney dog**, a **square pizza**, or a **Middle Eastern feast**, Detroit has a wide range of culinary options to choose from.

- **Eating etiquette**: it is customary to eat with your hands at casual restaurants, but to use utensils at more formal establishments
