# Minneapolis Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Minneapolis, you should eat dishes like Juicy Lucy, Tater Tot Hotdish, and Walleye, which showcase the city's unique blend of Scandinavian, German, and Midwestern cuisines.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: beef, potatoes, corn, and cheese
National Dish: Tater Tot Hotdish, a casserole made with tater tots, ground beef, and canned condensed cream of mushroom soup
Meal Times: typical breakfast times are 7-10 am, lunch times are 11 am-2 pm, and dinner times are 5-9 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 15-20% of the total bill is customary
Dietary Notes: Minneapolis has a variety of halal, kosher, and vegetarian-friendly options, particularly in the Uptown and Dinkytown neighborhoods
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Minneapolis's cuisine is defined by its **Scandinavian** and **German** heritage, with a strong emphasis on **meat and potatoes**. The city's proximity to the Mississippi River and the surrounding farmland also means that **freshwater fish** and **locally grown produce** are staples of the local cuisine. Regional variations can be seen in the different neighborhoods, such as the **Norwegian**-influenced cuisine in the **Norse** neighborhood and the **Somali**-influenced cuisine in the **Cedar-Riverside** neighborhood.

The city's food culture is also influenced by its **Midwestern** roots, with a strong tradition of **comfort food** and **home cooking**. Many local restaurants serve classic dishes like **meatloaf** and **mashed potatoes**, as well as more modern twists on traditional cuisine. The city's **food truck scene** is also thriving, with many vendors serving everything from **gourmet grilled cheese** to **Korean BBQ**.

Minneapolis is also known for its **craft beer scene**, with many local breweries offering tours and tastings. The city's **coffee culture** is also thriving, with many independent coffee shops serving specialty coffee drinks and pastries. Overall, Minneapolis's cuisine is a unique blend of traditional and modern influences, with a strong emphasis on local ingredients and community-oriented dining.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Tipping**: 15-20% of the total bill is customary at restaurants and bars
- **Reservations**: reservations are recommended for popular restaurants, especially on weekends
- **Dress Code**: dress codes vary by restaurant, but casual attire is acceptable at most establishments
- **Eating Etiquette**: it is customary to wait for the host to indicate where to sit, and to keep your hands visible on the table
- **Food Sharing**: food sharing is common in Minneapolis, especially at family-style restaurants
- **Credit Cards**: most restaurants and cafes accept major credit cards, but it's always a good idea to have cash on hand
