# Getting Around Minneapolis — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Minneapolis, you can use a combination of public transportation options, including buses and light rail, as well as ride-hailing services and driving, with various transportation modes available to suit different needs and preferences.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Minneapolis is easily accessible by air, with the Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP) serving as a major hub for Delta Air Lines and offering direct flights to numerous domestic and international destinations. The airport is located about 10 miles southeast of downtown Minneapolis and is connected to the city center by various transportation options, including light rail and bus services. For those traveling overland, Minneapolis is situated at the intersection of several major highways, including Interstate 35W and Interstate 94, making it easily accessible by car or bus from neighboring cities and states.

For international travelers, MSP Airport offers direct flights to several major cities in Europe, Asia, and Latin America, including Amsterdam, London, Tokyo, and Mexico City. Additionally, the airport is a major hub for domestic flights, with numerous daily flights to cities across the United States, including New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Dallas. Visitors can also reach Minneapolis by bus or train, with several companies, including Greyhound and Amtrak, offering services to the city from nearby cities and states.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Minneapolis has an extensive domestic air network, with several major airlines, including Delta, American Airlines, and United, offering multiple daily flights to cities across the United States. The main routes include flights to major hubs like Atlanta, Boston, and Seattle, as well as to smaller regional airports. When booking domestic flights, it's a good idea to compare prices across different airlines and book in advance to secure the best deals. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Minneapolis can range from $200 to $500 or more, depending on the destination and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Minneapolis has a moderate rail network, with Amtrak offering several daily trains to nearby cities, including Chicago, Milwaukee, and Duluth. The city is also served by the Northstar Commuter Rail, which connects Minneapolis to the northwest suburbs. While there are no high-speed rail options available, the existing rail network provides a convenient and relatively affordable way to travel to nearby cities. Booking train tickets can be done online or at the train station, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several intercity bus companies, including Greyhound, Megabus, and Jefferson Lines, operate in Minneapolis, offering affordable and convenient travel options to nearby cities and states. The buses typically depart from the Minneapolis Bus Station, which is located in downtown Minneapolis, and offer routes to cities like Chicago, Milwaukee, and Des Moines. The quality of the buses and services can vary, but most companies offer comfortable and clean vehicles with onboard amenities like Wi-Fi and restrooms. Prices for bus tickets can range from $20 to $50 or more, depending on the route and time of booking.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- A valid driver's license is required to rent a car in Minneapolis, and visitors from outside the United States may need to obtain an International Driving Permit.
- The road conditions in Minneapolis are generally good, with well-maintained highways and streets, but winter weather can make driving challenging.
- Fuel costs can vary, but gas prices are generally lower than in other major US cities.
- Car rental companies like Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise have locations at the airport and in downtown Minneapolis, and it's a good idea to book in advance to secure the best rates.
- Hazards to watch out for include winter weather, construction zones, and aggressive drivers.
- Parking in Minneapolis can be limited, especially in downtown areas, and visitors should be prepared to pay for parking or use public transportation.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of traffic congestion during rush hour, especially on highways and major streets.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to avoid parking challenges and reduce traffic congestion.
- Download the Metro Transit app to plan routes and track buses and trains in real-time.
- Be prepared for winter weather, including snow and ice, and use caution when driving or walking.
- Use reputable taxi or ride-hailing services, and always check the driver's ID before getting in the vehicle.
- Avoid traveling during peak hours or construction zones to minimize delays.
- Keep valuables secure and be mindful of surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night.
- Consider purchasing a Visitor Pass for unlimited public transportation rides during your stay.
