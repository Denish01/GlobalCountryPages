# Minneapolis Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

Minneapolis is a city with a unique cultural identity shaped by its Scandinavian and European heritage, its location on the Mississippi River, and its thriving arts and music scene. 

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Minneapolis's culture is defined by its strong sense of community and a blend of traditional and modern influences. The city has a significant Scandinavian population, with many residents claiming Norwegian, Swedish, or Danish ancestry, which is reflected in its cuisine, festivals, and cultural events. The city is also home to a diverse range of ethnic groups, including African American, Asian, and Latin American communities, which have contributed to its vibrant cultural landscape. Minneapolis has a thriving arts scene, with numerous museums, galleries, and performance venues, and is known for its support of local artists and musicians.

The city's cultural identity is also influenced by its location on the Mississippi River, which has played a significant role in its history and development. The river has been a source of inspiration for many artists and musicians, and has helped to shape the city's unique character. Minneapolis is also home to a number of cultural institutions, including the Walker Art Center, the Minneapolis Institute of Art, and the Guthrie Theater, which showcase the city's rich cultural heritage.

Minneapolis has a strong sense of community, with many residents actively engaged in volunteer work, community organizations, and local initiatives. The city is also known for its **progressive values**, with a strong emphasis on social justice, environmental sustainability, and community development. This is reflected in its many community gardens, farmers' markets, and cooperative businesses, which have helped to create a sense of shared ownership and responsibility among residents.

[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Be friendly and welcoming to locals and visitors alike
- Respect the city's strong sense of community and volunteer spirit
- Try the local cuisine, including Juicy Lucys and tater tot hotdish
- Explore the city's many parks and outdoor spaces, including the Mississippi River waterfront
- Attend a Minnesota Twins or Vikings game to experience the city's sports culture
- Visit the city's many museums and cultural institutions, including the Walker Art Center and the Minneapolis Institute of Art

**Don't:**
- Don't be afraid to ask for directions or advice from locals, who are known for their friendly and helpful nature
- Don't assume that everyone in Minneapolis is a fan of the Minnesota Vikings or Twins - while sports are popular, not everyone is a fan
- Don't litter or dispose of trash improperly, as the city takes pride in its cleanliness and environmental sustainability
- Don't neglect to try the city's many craft beers, which are a staple of the local drinking culture
- Don't be surprised if you're invited to a **potluck dinner**, which is a common social gathering in Minneapolis
- Don't forget to bundle up during the winter months, as the city can be very cold and snowy

[SECTION] Arts & Music [/SECTION]
- The city is home to a thriving **indie rock** scene, with bands like The Replacements and Hüsker Dü emerging from the city's vibrant music scene
- Minneapolis is also known for its **jazz and blues** heritage, with legendary musicians like Prince and Bob Dylan getting their start in the city
- The city has a strong **theater scene**, with the Guthrie Theater and the Jungle Theater showcasing a range of productions, from classical plays to contemporary works
- The **Walker Art Center** is a leading contemporary art museum, with a collection of modern and contemporary art
- The city is also home to a number of **street artists**, who have created vibrant murals and public installations throughout the city
- The **Minneapolis Institute of Art** is a world-class museum with a collection of over 89,000 works of art, spanning 5,000 years of human history
