# Getting Around Seattle — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Seattle, you can use a variety of transportation options including public transit, ride-hailing services, taxis, and car rentals, as well as walk or bike in this pedestrian-friendly city.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, also known as Sea-Tac Airport, is the primary airport serving the city and is a major hub for Alaska Airlines and Delta Air Lines. The airport is located about 15 miles south of downtown Seattle and offers direct flights to numerous domestic and international destinations. If you're coming from within the United States, you can also consider flying into Seattle from major hubs like Los Angeles, Chicago, or New York. For those traveling overland, Seattle is accessible by car via Interstate 5, which connects the city to other major West Coast cities like Portland and Vancouver.

If you're entering Seattle from Canada, you can cross the border by car or bus via Interstate 5 or US Highway 99. There are also several bus companies that offer daily services from Vancouver and other Canadian cities to Seattle. Additionally, you can take the train from Vancouver to Seattle on the Amtrak Cascades route, which offers stunning views of the Pacific Northwest coastline. Alternatively, you can also enter Seattle by sea, with several cruise lines offering itineraries that include the city as a port of call.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Seattle is a major hub for domestic flights, with several airlines offering frequent services to destinations across the United States. Alaska Airlines, Delta Air Lines, and American Airlines are among the major carriers that operate out of Seattle-Tacoma International Airport. You can fly to major cities like Los Angeles, San Francisco, Chicago, and New York, as well as to smaller regional airports. When booking domestic flights, it's a good idea to compare prices across different airlines and book in advance to get the best deals. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Seattle can range from $200 to $500 or more, depending on the destination and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Seattle has a moderate rail network, with Amtrak offering several routes that connect the city to other major destinations in the Pacific Northwest and beyond. The Amtrak Cascades route runs from Vancouver to Eugene, Oregon, with multiple stops in between, including Seattle. You can also take the Amtrak Coast Starlight route, which runs from Seattle to Los Angeles, with stops in Portland and other California cities. While there are no high-speed rail options in Seattle, the scenic routes offer a comfortable and convenient way to see the region. You can book train tickets online or at the station, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several intercity bus companies operate in Seattle, offering affordable and convenient services to destinations across the Pacific Northwest and beyond. Companies like Greyhound, BoltBus, and FlixBus offer daily services to cities like Portland, Vancouver, and Los Angeles, with prices starting from around $20. The buses are generally comfortable and well-maintained, with onboard amenities like Wi-Fi and power outlets. You can book tickets online or at the station, and it's a good idea to compare prices and schedules across different companies to find the best option for your needs.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Seattle, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or state, and car insurance is mandatory.
- Road conditions in Seattle are generally good, with well-maintained highways and city streets, but be aware of traffic congestion during rush hour.
- Fuel costs in Seattle are relatively high, with gas prices averaging around $3.50 per gallon.
- Car rental companies like Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise have locations at the airport and in the city center, and prices start from around $40 per day.
- When driving in Seattle, be aware of the city's many one-way streets and traffic circles, and watch out for pedestrians and cyclists.
- Parking in Seattle can be challenging, especially in the city center, so consider using public parking garages or street parking with meters.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of traffic congestion during rush hour, especially on highways and major streets.
- Consider using public transportation or ride-hailing services instead of driving, especially during peak hours.
- Download the Transit app to plan your route and track public transportation in real-time.
- Use a credit card or cash to pay for transportation, as not all services accept mobile payments.
- Be careful when using ride-hailing services, and always check the driver's ID and license plate before getting in.
- Consider purchasing a Seattle CityPASS, which includes public transportation and admission to several attractions.
- Be mindful of bike lanes and pedestrian zones, and always follow local traffic laws and regulations.
- Take advantage of Seattle's walkable neighborhoods and scenic waterfront to explore the city on foot.
