# Top Things to Do in Dallas — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Dallas, some of the top things to do include exploring the Sixth Floor Museum at Dealey Plaza, strolling through the Dallas Arboretum and Botanical Garden, and experiencing the city's vibrant arts and culture scene.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Bishop Arts District** food tour to sample the city's best cuisine and drinks
- Explore the **Deep Ellum** neighborhood, known for its street art, live music venues, and eclectic shops
- Attend a performance at the **Dallas Symphony Orchestra** or the **AT&T Performing Arts Center**
- Visit the **Trinity Groves** area, a former industrial site turned vibrant entertainment district
- Go on a **Dallas Food Tour** to discover the city's diverse culinary scene
- Take a stroll through the **Klyde Warren Park**, a 17-acre urban green space with free outdoor activities and events

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit the **White Rock Lake**, a 9.33-mile lake with hiking and biking trails, kayaking, and paddle-boarding
- Explore the **Cedar Ridge Preserve**, a 600-acre nature preserve with hiking trails and scenic views
- Go hiking at the **Trinity River Audubon Center**, a 120-acre nature preserve with trails and bird-watching opportunities
- Attend a concert or festival at the **Klyde Warren Park**, a 17-acre urban green space
- Visit the **Dallas Arboretum and Botanical Garden**, a 66-acre garden with seasonal flower displays and beautiful views of White Rock Lake

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Dallas Museum of Art**, a museum with a diverse collection of art from around the world
- Explore the **Perot Museum of Nature and Science**, a museum with interactive exhibits on science, nature, and technology
- Attend a performance at the **Dallas Symphony Orchestra** or the **AT&T Performing Arts Center**
- Take a tour of the **Bishop Arts District**, a vibrant neighborhood with eclectic shops and restaurants
- Visit the **Nasher Sculpture Center**, a museum with an extensive collection of modern and contemporary sculpture

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **The Wild Detectives**, a bookstore and bar with a unique atmosphere and literary events
- Explore the **Oak Cliff** neighborhood, a charming area with boutique shops and restaurants
- Take a tour of the **Southfork Ranch**, a famous filming location for the TV show "Dallas"
- Go to the **AllGood Cafe**, a restaurant with a unique atmosphere and live music performances

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
1. **Fort Worth**: 30 miles, 45-minute drive, highlights include the Fort Worth Stockyards National Historic District and the National Cowgirl Museum and Hall of Fame
2. **Glen Rose**: 75 miles, 1-hour 15-minute drive, highlights include the Fossil Rim Wildlife Center and the Dinosaur Valley State Park
3. **Waco**: 95 miles, 1-hour 30-minute drive, highlights include the Magnolia Market at the Silos and the Dr Pepper Museum
4. **Denton**: 40 miles, 45-minute drive, highlights include the University of North Texas and the Bayless-Selby House Museum
5. **Grapevine**: 20 miles, 30-minute drive, highlights include the Grapevine Vintage Railroad and the Great Wolf Lodge
