# Cost of Living in Dallas - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Dallas is generally moderate, with a daily budget ranging from $60-120 USD for backpackers to $250-400 USD for comfort seekers.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $120-250 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $250-400 USD
Currency: USD
Tipping Custom: expected (15-20% in restaurants and bars)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Dallas? [/SECTION]
Dallas is a major city in the United States, and its cost of living is relatively moderate compared to other large cities in the country. The city's cost of living index is around 90, which is slightly lower than the national average. However, prices can vary depending on the neighborhood, with areas like Uptown and Downtown tend to be more expensive than others. Compared to its neighboring cities, such as Houston and San Antonio, Dallas has a similar cost of living, but it is more expensive than some other cities in the southern United States.

In terms of global averages, Dallas is more expensive than many cities in Asia and Latin America, but it is relatively affordable compared to cities in Western Europe and other major cities in the United States. The city's economy is diverse, with major industries in technology, healthcare, and finance, which contributes to its moderate cost of living. Overall, Dallas is a great option for people who want to experience the amenities of a big city without the extremely high prices of cities like New York or San Francisco.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider living outside the city center to save on rent and utilities.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on fuel and parking costs.
- Take advantage of happy hour deals and discounts at local restaurants and bars.
- Visit the Dallas Museum of Art, which offers free admission every day.
- Shop at local markets and grocery stores instead of relying on convenience stores and restaurants.
- Look for apartments and houses that include utilities in the rent to avoid extra costs.
