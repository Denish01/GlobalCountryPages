# Getting Around San Francisco — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around San Francisco, you can use a variety of transportation options, including the city's famous cable cars, buses, and trains, as well as ride-hailing services and bike-sharing programs.

Domestic Air Network: extensive

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
San Francisco is easily accessible by air, with three major international airports serving the city: San Francisco International Airport (SFO), Oakland International Airport (OAK), and Norman Y. Mineta San Jose International Airport (SJC). SFO is the largest and most convenient option, with direct flights to destinations around the world, including Europe, Asia, and Latin America. You can also reach San Francisco by car or bus, with several major highways and intercity bus routes connecting the city to other parts of the United States.

If you're coming from within the United States, you can also take a train or bus to San Francisco. The city is a major hub for **Amtrak**, the national rail network, and several bus companies, including **Greyhound** and **Megabus**, offer daily services to and from San Francisco. Additionally, you can take a ferry from nearby cities, such as Oakland or Sausalito, to get to San Francisco.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
San Francisco is a major hub for domestic flights, with several airlines offering services to and from the city. **United Airlines** and **American Airlines** are two of the largest carriers, with multiple daily flights to destinations across the United States. You can also fly with **Delta Air Lines**, **Southwest Airlines**, and **Alaska Airlines**, among others. To book a domestic flight, you can use the airlines' websites or a travel booking website like **Expedia** or **Kayak**. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the route and time of year, but you can expect to pay between $100 and $500 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
San Francisco has a moderate rail network, with several **Amtrak** routes connecting the city to other parts of the United States. The **Coast Starlight** route runs along the West Coast, from Seattle to Los Angeles, while the **California Zephyr** route connects San Francisco to Chicago. You can also take a **Caltrain** commuter train to nearby cities, such as San Jose or Palo Alto. To book a train ticket, you can use the **Amtrak** website or visit a ticket office in person. High-speed rail options are limited in the United States, but you can take a **bullet train** from San Francisco to Los Angeles or Seattle on the **Amtrak** network.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several intercity bus companies operate in San Francisco, offering affordable and convenient services to destinations across the United States. **Greyhound** is one of the largest carriers, with multiple daily buses to cities like Los Angeles, New York, and Chicago. You can also take a **Megabus** or **FlixBus** to nearby cities, such as Oakland or San Jose. The quality of bus services varies, but most companies offer comfortable seats and onboard amenities like Wi-Fi and power outlets. Costs vary depending on the route and time of year, but you can expect to pay between $20 and $100 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in San Francisco, you'll need a valid **driver's license** from your home country or state.
- Road conditions in San Francisco are generally good, but be aware of **traffic congestion** and **steep hills**.
- Fuel costs are relatively high in California, with **gasoline** prices averaging around $3.50 per gallon.
- To rent a car, you'll need to be at least 21 years old and have a valid credit card.
- Be aware of **parking restrictions** and **traffic laws**, such as **right-turn-only** lanes and **bike lanes**.
- If you plan to drive in San Francisco, consider renting a car with **GPS** and **automatic transmission**, as the city's hills and traffic can be challenging to navigate.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To avoid **traffic congestion**, consider using public transportation or ride-hailing services during peak hours.
- Be aware of **scams** and **pickpocketing** in tourist areas, and keep a close eye on your belongings.
- You can pay for public transportation using **cash** or **credit cards**, but some services may not accept **mobile payments**.
- Download the **Muni** or **BART** apps to plan your route and track your journey.
- Consider purchasing a **Clipper card**, which can be used to pay for public transportation throughout the Bay Area.
- Be mindful of **bike lanes** and **pedestrian zones**, and always follow **traffic laws** and **signs**.
- If you're using a ride-hailing service, make sure to **check the driver's ID** and **license plate** before getting in the car.
