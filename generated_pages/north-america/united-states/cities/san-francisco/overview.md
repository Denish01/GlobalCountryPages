[SECTION] What Is San Francisco? [/SECTION]
San Francisco is a city located in the state of California, on the west coast of the United States. It is situated on the northern tip of the San Francisco Peninsula, surrounded by the Pacific Ocean to the west, the San Francisco Bay to the east, and the Golden Gate Strait to the north. The city is known for its iconic landmarks, such as the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and steep hills, as well as its vibrant cultural scene, diverse neighborhoods, and thriving technology industry. San Francisco is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year with its unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and modern attractions.

The city's geographic position has played a significant role in its development, with the San Francisco Bay providing a natural harbor and the Pacific Ocean offering access to international trade routes. San Francisco's proximity to Silicon Valley, a major technology hub, has also contributed to its growth as a center for innovation and entrepreneurship. The city's diverse neighborhoods, such as Fisherman's Wharf, Chinatown, and Haight-Ashbury, reflect its rich cultural heritage and offer a unique experience for visitors.

San Francisco is also known for its **progressive politics**, **cultural events**, and **educational institutions**, making it a hub for arts, activism, and learning. The city is home to several prestigious universities, including the University of California, San Francisco, and San Francisco State University, as well as a number of cultural institutions, such as the de Young Museum and the San Francisco Symphony.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1776 - The Spanish establish the Presidio of San Francisco, a military outpost that marks the beginning of European settlement in the area.
2. 1848 - The California Gold Rush brings a massive influx of settlers to San Francisco, transforming it into a major commercial center.
3. 1906 - A devastating earthquake and fire destroy much of the city, leading to a major rebuilding effort and the development of new architectural styles.
4. 1967 - The Summer of Love, a countercultural movement, takes place in San Francisco's Haight-Ashbury neighborhood, marking a pivotal moment in the city's cultural history.
5. 1978 - The assassination of Mayor George Moscone and Supervisor Harvey Milk sparks a national debate about gay rights and social justice.
6. 1989 - The Loma Prieta earthquake strikes, causing significant damage and prompting major improvements to the city's infrastructure.
7. 2000s - San Francisco experiences a technology boom, with companies like Twitter, Airbnb, and Uber emerging as major players in the industry.
