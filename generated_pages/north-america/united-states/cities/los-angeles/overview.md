[SECTION] What Is Los Angeles? [/SECTION]
Los Angeles, often abbreviated as LA, is the largest city in the state of California and the second-most populous city in the United States. It is situated in Southern California, bordered by the Pacific Ocean to the west, the San Gabriel Mountains to the east, and the Santa Monica Mountains to the south. Los Angeles is a global center for the entertainment industry, including film, television, and music, with Hollywood being a major hub. The city is also known for its diverse culture, beautiful beaches, and pleasant climate, making it a popular tourist destination.

Los Angeles is a significant economic center, with a diverse range of industries, including technology, healthcare, and international trade. The city is home to the Port of Los Angeles, one of the busiest ports in the world, and Los Angeles International Airport, a major aviation hub. The city's cultural scene is also thriving, with numerous museums, galleries, and performance venues, including the Los Angeles County Museum of Art and the Walt Disney Concert Hall.

The city's geography is characterized by a mix of urban and natural landscapes, with mountains, forests, and beaches providing ample opportunities for outdoor recreation. The climate is Mediterranean, with mild winters and hot summers, making it an attractive destination for people from all over the world. Los Angeles is also a hub for education and research, with several top-ranked universities, including the University of California, Los Angeles and the University of Southern California.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1781 - The city of Los Angeles was founded by Spanish governor Felipe de Neve, with the name "El Pueblo de Nuestra Señora la Reina de los Ángeles".
2. 1821 - Mexico gained independence from Spain, and Los Angeles became part of the Mexican Republic.
3. 1848 - The Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo was signed, ceding California, including Los Angeles, to the United States.
4. 1913 - The Los Angeles Aqueduct was completed, providing a reliable water supply to the city and enabling rapid growth.
5. 1920s - The film industry began to develop in Los Angeles, with the establishment of studios such as Paramount Pictures and Warner Bros.
6. 1965 - The Watts riots took place, highlighting issues of racial tension and social inequality in the city.
7. 1984 - Los Angeles hosted the Summer Olympics, showcasing the city's ability to host major international events.
