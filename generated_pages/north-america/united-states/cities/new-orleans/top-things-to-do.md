# Top Things to Do in New Orleans — Attractions & Experiences

New Orleans is a city that offers a unique blend of history, music, and culture, with top attractions and experiences that include exploring the French Quarter, visiting the National WWII Museum, and taking a steamboat ride along the Mississippi River.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Voodoo Tour** to learn about the city's mystical history and visit the New Orleans Historic Voodoo Museum
- Explore the **French Market**, a historic outdoor market that offers a variety of food, drinks, and souvenirs
- Attend a **Jazz Funeral**, a unique New Orleans tradition that celebrates the life of the deceased with music and dancing
- Visit the **New Orleans Jazz National Historical Park** to learn about the history and preservation of jazz music
- Take a **Cocktail Tour** to sample the city's famous drinks, such as the Sazerac and the Hurricane
- Go on a **Ghost Tour** to explore the city's haunted history and visit famous sites like the LaLaurie House

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit **City Park**, a 1,300-acre park that features several lakes, walking trails, and the Besthoff Sculpture Garden
- Explore the **Jean Lafitte National Historical Park and Preserve**, a unique blend of natural and cultural resources
- Take a **Swamp Tour** to see alligators, birds, and other wildlife in their natural habitat
- Walk along the **Mississippi River Levee**, which offers stunning views of the city and the river
- Visit the **Audubon Nature Institute**, which includes a zoo, aquarium, and butterfly garden

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **New Orleans Museum of Art**, which features a diverse collection of art from around the world
- Explore the **Ogden Museum of Southern Art**, which showcases the art and culture of the American South
- Attend a performance at the **Mahalia Jackson Theater for the Performing Arts**, which hosts a variety of music, dance, and theater productions
- Visit the **Backstreet Cultural Museum**, which celebrates the city's unique cultural heritage
- Take a **Cooking Class** to learn about the city's famous Creole and Cajun cuisine

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **New Orleans Pharmacy Museum**, which showcases the city's pharmaceutical history
- Explore the **Longue Vue House and Gardens**, a stunning estate with beautiful gardens and views of the city
- Take a **Street Art Tour** to see the city's vibrant murals and graffiti
- Visit the **New Orleans Historic Voodoo Museum**, which features a unique collection of voodoo artifacts and exhibits
- Go to the **Preservation Hall**, an intimate venue that hosts traditional jazz performances

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Baton Rouge**, the capital of Louisiana, which is approximately 80 miles from New Orleans (1.5 hours by car)
- Visit **Houmas House Plantation and Gardens**, a historic plantation with stunning gardens and a museum (approximately 60 miles from New Orleans, 1 hour by car)
- Explore the **Oak Alley Plantation**, a stunning antebellum mansion with beautiful gardens and a historic slave quarters (approximately 60 miles from New Orleans, 1 hour by car)
- Go to **Lafayette**, a city known for its Cajun culture and cuisine, which is approximately 130 miles from New Orleans (2.5 hours by car)
- Take a day trip to **Gulf Shores**, a beautiful beach town on the Gulf of Mexico, which is approximately 210 miles from New Orleans (3.5 hours by car)

[SECTION] Planning Tips [/SECTION]
