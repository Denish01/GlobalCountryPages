# Top Things to Do in Washington D.C. — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Washington D.C., the top things to do include exploring iconic landmarks, experiencing unique cultural events, and discovering the city's rich history, which can be found in its numerous museums, historical sites, and hidden gems.

[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| National Mall | Downtown Washington D.C. | Historic Landmark | Iconic stretch of parkland with monuments and museums |
| United States Capitol | Capitol Hill | Government Building | Guided tours of the historic building and its surroundings |
| Lincoln Memorial | West End | Historic Landmark | Impressive tribute to President Abraham Lincoln with stunning views |
| National Air and Space Museum | National Mall | Museum | Explore historic aircraft and spacecraft, including the Wright Brothers' Flyer |
| National Museum of Natural History | National Mall | Museum | Discover over 145 million natural history specimens and artifacts |

| National Archives | Pennsylvania Avenue NW | Government Building | Home to historic documents such as the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution |

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Attend a performance at the **Kennedy Center**, the national cultural center of the United States
- Take a stroll through the beautiful **Tidal Basin** during the National Cherry Blossom Festival
- Visit the **Newseum**, an interactive museum showcasing the history of the news industry
- Go on a guided tour of the **White House**, the official residence and workplace of the President
- Explore the vibrant **Georgetown** neighborhood, known for its historic architecture, upscale shops, and lively nightlife
- Take a **Potomac River** cruise to see the city's landmarks from a different perspective

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Rock Creek Park**, a large urban park with hiking trails, a nature center, and a planetarium
- Visit the **United States Botanic Garden**, a beautiful garden featuring a stunning glass conservatory
- Go hiking at **Great Falls Park**, which offers scenic views of the Potomac River
- Take a walk through the **Kenilworth Aquatic Gardens**, a unique park featuring water plants and aquatic life
- Visit the **National Zoo**, home to over 1,800 animals from around the world

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum of African American History and Culture**, which explores the rich history and culture of African Americans
- Explore the **Smithsonian American Art Museum**, which features a diverse collection of American art from the colonial period to the present
- Attend a performance at the **National Theatre**, a historic theater that hosts a variety of productions
- Visit the **Hillwood Estate, Museum & Gardens**, the former home of Marjorie Merriweather Post, which features an impressive collection of Russian and French art
- Take a tour of the **National Cathedral**, a beautiful Episcopal cathedral that offers stunning views of the city

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Dumbarton Oaks**, a historic estate that features beautiful gardens and a museum of Byzantine and Pre-Columbian art
- Explore the **Georgetown Waterfront**, a charming area with shops, restaurants, and stunning views of the Potomac River
- Take a stroll through the **Kenilworth neighborhood**, which features beautiful historic homes and a vibrant arts scene
- Visit the **National Building Museum**, a unique museum that explores the history of architecture and design

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Mount Vernon**, the former home of George Washington, which is located about 13 miles south of Washington D.C. (30-minute drive)
- Visit the **Antietam National Battlefield**, which is located about 70 miles northwest of Washington D.C. (1.5-hour drive)
- Explore the **Shenandoah National Park**, which is located about 75 miles west of Washington D.C. (2-hour drive)
- Take a day trip to **Baltimore**, a city located about 40 miles northeast of Washington D.C. (1-hour drive), which features a rich history, cultural attractions, and a vibrant waterfront
- Visit the **Gettysburg National Military Park**, which is located about 80 miles northwest of Washington D.C. (2-hour drive)
