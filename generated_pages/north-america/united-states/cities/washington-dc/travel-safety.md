Washington D.C. is generally a safe city to visit, with low to moderate crime rates compared to other major cities in the United States.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Washington D.C. is a major tourist destination, and as such, it has a significant police presence, particularly in areas with high foot traffic. The city has a range of safety measures in place, including security cameras and emergency response systems. However, as with any city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The city's crime rate has been trending downward in recent years, but petty crimes such as pickpocketing and theft can still occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

Overall, Washington D.C. is a safe city for visitors, with a strong police presence and a range of safety measures in place. However, it's still important for visitors to be aware of their surroundings and take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. This includes being mindful of their belongings, especially in crowded areas, and avoiding walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night. By taking these precautions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Washington D.C.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake ticket sellers or street performers
- Panhandling and aggressive begging in some areas
- Traffic congestion and pedestrian safety concerns
- Protest and demonstration activity, which can sometimes turn violent
- Identity theft and credit card scams at ATMs and restaurants
- Overpriced or low-quality goods and services targeted at tourists

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Washington D.C., but visitors should ensure they are up to date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Washington D.C.
- **Medical Facilities:** Washington D.C. has a range of high-quality medical facilities, including hospitals and urgent care centers. Many medical facilities accept international health insurance, but visitors should check with their provider before traveling.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available in Washington D.C., and many are open 24 hours.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe
- Be aware of your surroundings and avoid distractions such as using your phone in public
- Follow local advice and instructions from police and other authorities
- Stay informed about local conditions and events that may affect your safety

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, such as Georgetown or Dupont Circle
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Keep in touch with friends and family back home and let them know your itinerary and plans
- Consider joining a group tour or using a reputable tour guide to explore the city
