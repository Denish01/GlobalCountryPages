# Where to Stay in Houston — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Houston, the best areas to stay include Downtown, Midtown, Montrose, the Galleria, the Medical Center, EaDo, and the Heights, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's what you can expect:
**Downtown** (Houston)
Best For: Business travelers and those looking for a lively atmosphere
Vibe: Downtown Houston is the city's central business district, offering a vibrant and bustling environment with plenty of restaurants, bars, and entertainment options. It's a great place to experience the city's energy.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to major business hubs, a wide range of dining and entertainment options, and easy access to public transportation. 
Cons: Can be quite crowded and noisy, especially during rush hour.

**Midtown** (Houston)
Best For: Young professionals and couples
Vibe: Midtown is a trendy neighborhood with a mix of old and new, offering a unique blend of art, culture, and entertainment. It's a great place to experience the city's vibrant cultural scene.
Price Range: $120-250/night
Pros: Close proximity to Downtown, a variety of restaurants and bars, and a thriving arts scene. 
Cons: Parking can be a challenge, and some areas can be quite crowded.

**Montrose** (Houston)
Best For: Luxury travelers and those looking for a unique experience
Vibe: Montrose is an upscale neighborhood with a rich history, offering a mix of boutique shops, art galleries, and fine dining restaurants. It's a great place to experience the city's luxury side.
Price Range: $200-400/night
Pros: Upscale amenities, unique shopping and dining experiences, and a rich cultural heritage. 
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and some areas can be quite crowded.

**The Galleria** (Houston)
Best For: Shopaholics and families
Vibe: The Galleria is a large shopping district with a wide range of stores, restaurants, and entertainment options. It's a great place to shop and have fun.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to one of the largest shopping malls in the country, a variety of dining options, and plenty of entertainment choices. 
Cons: Can be quite crowded and noisy, especially on weekends.

**The Medical Center** (Houston)
Best For: Medical tourists and business travelers
Vibe: The Medical Center is a large medical complex with a range of hospitals, research centers, and medical facilities. It's a great place to access top-notch medical care.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: Close proximity to world-class medical facilities, a range of accommodation options, and easy access to public transportation. 
Cons: Can be quite crowded and noisy, especially during rush hour.

**EaDo** (Houston)
Best For: Backpackers and budget travelers
Vibe: EaDo is a vibrant and eclectic neighborhood with a mix of art, culture, and entertainment. It's a great place to experience the city's alternative scene.
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, a thriving arts scene, and plenty of dining and entertainment choices. 
Cons: Can be quite noisy, especially at night, and some areas can be quite crowded.

**The Heights** (Houston)
Best For: Families and those looking for a more laid-back atmosphere
Vibe: The Heights is a charming neighborhood with a small-town feel, offering a mix of boutique shops, restaurants, and parks. It's a great place to relax and unwind.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: Close proximity to Downtown, a range of accommodation options, and plenty of outdoor activities. 
Cons: Can be quite crowded, especially on weekends, and some areas can be quite noisy.
