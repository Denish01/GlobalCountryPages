# Top Things to Do in Houston — Attractions & Experiences

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Brewery Tour** of Houston's thriving craft beer scene, which features a variety of local breweries such as 8th Wonder Brewery and Saint Arnold Brewing Company.
- Explore the **Chinatown** neighborhood, which offers a unique blend of Asian cultures and cuisines.
- Visit the **Houston Arboretum and Nature Center**, a 155-acre nature preserve with walking trails and a variety of plant and animal species.
- Attend a performance by the **Houston Symphony**, which is one of the largest and most respected orchestras in the United States.
- Go on a **Food Tour** of Houston's diverse culinary scene, which features a wide range of international cuisines.
- Take a **Helicopter Tour** of the city, which offers a unique perspective on Houston's skyline and attractions.
- Visit the **Orange Show**, a folk art monument dedicated to the orange, which is a unique and quirky attraction.

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Armand Bayou Nature Center**, a 2,500-acre nature preserve with hiking trails and a variety of plant and animal species.
- **Memorial Park**, a 1,500-acre park with hiking and biking trails, a golf course, and a variety of recreational activities.
- **Buffalo Bayou Park**, a 160-acre park with hiking and biking trails, a dog park, and stunning views of the Houston skyline.
- **Galveston Island**, a popular beach destination located just outside of Houston, with a variety of recreational activities such as surfing and fishing.
- **Lake Houston**, a 4,787-acre lake with boating, fishing, and hiking opportunities.

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **The Menil Collection**, a museum featuring a diverse collection of art and cultural artifacts from around the world.
- **The Museum of Fine Arts, Houston**, with a collection of over 65,000 works of art, including European, American, and Asian art.
- **The Houston Museum of African American Culture**, which showcases the art, history, and culture of African Americans.
- **The Asia Society Texas Center**, which features a variety of cultural exhibitions and performances.
- **The Alley Theatre**, a renowned theater company that produces a wide range of plays and musicals.
- **The Houston Grand Opera**, which is one of the largest and most respected opera companies in the United States.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **The Rothko Chapel**, a peaceful and contemplative space featuring the works of artist Mark Rothko.
- **The Lawndale Art Center**, a contemporary art center featuring a variety of exhibitions and performances.
- **The Project Row Houses**, a community-based arts and cultural organization that showcases the art and culture of African Americans.
- **The 19th Street in the Heights**, a charming neighborhood with a variety of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.
- **The Market Square Park**, a vibrant public park in downtown Houston with a variety of shops, restaurants, and cultural events.
