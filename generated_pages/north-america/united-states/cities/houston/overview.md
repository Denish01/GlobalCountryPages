[SECTION] What Is Houston? [/SECTION]
Houston is the most populous city in the state of Texas and the fourth-most populous city in the United States. It is located in the southeastern part of the state, near the Gulf of Mexico, and is situated in Harris County, which is one of the largest counties in the United States. Houston is known for its diverse culture, with a thriving arts scene, a variety of international cuisines, and a strong sense of community. The city is also home to many major industries, including energy, healthcare, and aerospace, and is a hub for international trade and commerce.

Houston's geographic position has played a significant role in its development and growth. The city is located near the Houston Ship Channel, which connects the city to the Gulf of Mexico and provides access to international trade routes. The city's proximity to the Gulf Coast also makes it vulnerable to hurricanes and other natural disasters, which has led to the development of a sophisticated storm protection system.

Houston is also known for its vibrant cultural scene, with a variety of museums, theaters, and music venues. The city is home to the Houston Museum District, which includes 19 museums, including the Museum of Fine Arts, Houston, and the Children's Museum of Houston. The city also hosts a number of festivals and events throughout the year, including the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, which is one of the largest rodeos in the world.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1836 - The city of Houston is founded by Augustus Chapman Allen and John Kirby Allen, who named it after Sam Houston, a key figure in the Texas Revolution.
2. 1901 - The discovery of oil at Spindletop, near Beaumont, Texas, leads to a surge in oil production and transforms Houston into a major energy hub.
3. 1962 - The Houston Space Center is established, and the city becomes a major hub for the US space program.
4. 1980s - Houston experiences a period of rapid growth and development, driven by the energy industry and international trade.
5. 2001 - The city is affected by Tropical Storm Allison, which causes widespread flooding and damage.
6. 2017 - Hurricane Harvey hits Houston, causing catastrophic flooding and damage to the city.
7. 2020 - The city continues to grow and develop, with a strong focus on innovation, technology, and sustainability.
