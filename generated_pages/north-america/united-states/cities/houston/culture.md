# Houston Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

Houston's culture is a vibrant and diverse blend of international influences, Southern hospitality, and a thriving arts and food scene, making it an exciting destination for visitors from around the world.

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Houston's cultural identity is defined by its diverse population, with over 145 languages spoken in the city, making it one of the most linguistically diverse cities in the world. The city's cultural landscape is shaped by its strategic location near the Gulf of Mexico, its history as a major oil and gas hub, and its strong connections to the Southern United States, Latin America, and Asia. As a result, Houston's cultural identity is a unique blend of **Southern charm**, **Latin American flair**, and **international sophistication**. The city is home to a thriving **African American community**, as well as significant **Asian American** and **Hispanic populations**, each contributing their own distinct cultural traditions and practices.

The city's cultural diversity is reflected in its many **ethnic neighborhoods**, such as the **Chinatown** and **Koreatown** districts, which offer a wealth of cultural attractions, restaurants, and shops. Houston is also home to a number of **cultural institutions**, including the **Houston Museum of African American Culture**, the **Asia Society Texas Center**, and the **Latin American Association**, which promote cross-cultural understanding and exchange. Overall, Houston's cultural identity is characterized by its **warm hospitality**, **vibrant diversity**, and **thriving arts and cultural scene**.

Houston's cultural landscape is also shaped by its strong **economy** and **industry**, which have attracted people from all over the world to live and work in the city. The city is home to many **major corporations**, including energy companies, medical centers, and aerospace firms, which have created a **thriving business community**. This has led to the development of a **diverse and cosmopolitan** cultural scene, with a wide range of cultural attractions, restaurants, and entertainment options.

[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Be prepared for **traffic and congestion**, and plan your itinerary accordingly
- Try the local **cuisine**, including Tex-Mex, barbecue, and seafood
- Visit the city's **museums and cultural attractions**, such as the Houston Museum of Fine Arts and the Space Center Houston
- Explore the city's **ethnic neighborhoods**, such as Chinatown and Koreatown
- Attend a **sports event**, such as a Houston Texans or Astros game
- Be respectful of the city's **diverse cultural traditions** and customs

**Don't:**
- Assume that everyone speaks **English**, and be prepared to encounter other languages
- Forget to **tip** at restaurants and bars, as this is expected in Houston
- Ignore the city's **heat and humidity**, and take precautions to stay cool and hydrated
- Disrespect the city's **cultural institutions** and traditions
- Litter or **pollute** the city's environment
- Fail to **follow traffic rules** and regulations, including wearing a seatbelt and using a designated driver

- The **Museum of Fine Arts, Houston** is one of the largest and most visited art museums in the country
- The **Houston Theater District** is a vibrant area that features a range of performance venues, including the Alley Theatre and the Hobby Center
- The city is also home to a **thriving music scene**, with a range of genres and styles, including **Texas blues**, **zycdeco**, and **hip-hop**
