[SECTION] How Expensive Is Las Vegas? [/SECTION]
Las Vegas is a city in the state of Nevada, United States, known for its vibrant entertainment scene, casinos, and nightlife. Compared to other major cities in the United States, Las Vegas is relatively affordable, with a cost of living index score of 94.3, which is slightly lower than the national average. However, prices can vary greatly depending on the location, with the Strip and surrounding areas tend to be more expensive than other parts of the city. Overall, Las Vegas offers a unique blend of affordability and luxury, making it an attractive destination for tourists and residents alike.

In comparison to other neighboring cities, Las Vegas is more expensive than cities like Phoenix, Arizona, but more affordable than cities like Los Angeles, California. The city's economy is driven by tourism, entertainment, and hospitality, which can lead to higher prices for certain goods and services, especially during peak travel seasons. However, with some planning and research, visitors and residents can find affordable options for accommodation, food, and entertainment, making Las Vegas a great value destination.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel outside of the Strip to save on accommodation costs.
- Take advantage of happy hour deals and discounts at restaurants and bars to save on food and drink.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transport costs.
- Look for package deals and discounts on shows and attractions to save on entertainment costs.
- Avoid visiting during peak travel seasons (December to April) to save on accommodation and tourist attraction prices.
- Take a stroll along the LINQ Promenade or visit the Fountains of Bellagio for free entertainment and people-watching opportunities.
