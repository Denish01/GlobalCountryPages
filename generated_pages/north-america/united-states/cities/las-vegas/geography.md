The city is surrounded by mountains, including the Spring Mountains to the west and the McCullough Mountains to the south. Las Vegas is landlocked, with no direct access to the ocean, but it is connected to the Colorado River via the Lake Mead National Recreation Area. The city's location in the desert has played a significant role in shaping its climate, economy, and culture.
