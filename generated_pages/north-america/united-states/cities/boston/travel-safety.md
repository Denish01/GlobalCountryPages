[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Boston is considered one of the safest major cities in the United States, with a strong police presence and low crime rates. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around the city, exploring its many neighborhoods and attractions, without undue concern for their safety. However, as with any city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. This includes being aware of their surroundings, keeping valuables secure, and avoiding walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night.

Overall, Boston is a great destination for tourists, with a wide range of cultural, historical, and entertainment options to explore. With its compact size and walkable neighborhoods, visitors can easily get around the city without needing a car, and the city's public transportation system is efficient and reliable. While there are some areas that are considered safer than others, the city as a whole is considered very safe, and visitors can feel confident exploring all that Boston has to offer.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas like Faneuil Hall and the subway
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake ticket sellers or street performers asking for donations
- Overcharging by taxi drivers or at restaurants and bars
- Bike theft, especially in areas with high foot traffic
- Aggressive panhandling in some areas of the city
- Traffic accidents, especially for pedestrians and cyclists
- Identity theft, especially when using ATMs or credit cards at tourist areas

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Boston, but visitors should be up to date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Boston
- **Medical Facilities:** Boston is home to many world-class hospitals and medical facilities, including Massachusetts General Hospital and Boston Medical Center, and visitors can expect high-quality medical care
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available throughout the city, including chain stores like CVS and Walgreens

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-sharing services
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe for valuables
- Be cautious when using ATMs or credit cards at tourist areas
- Follow local advice and instructions from police or other authorities

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
- Avoid walking alone in areas that are considered unsafe, such as Roxbury or Mattapan
- Keep in touch with family and friends back home and let them know your itinerary
- Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts, especially if you feel uncomfortable or threatened in any way
