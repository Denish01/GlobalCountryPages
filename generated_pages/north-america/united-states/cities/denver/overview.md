[SECTION] What Is Denver? [/SECTION]
Denver is the capital and most populous city of the state of Colorado, located in the western United States. It is situated in the Rocky Mountain region, approximately 1 mile above sea level, and is known for its stunning natural beauty, with the Rocky Mountains to the west and the Great Plains to the east. The city has a rich cultural scene, with a variety of museums, galleries, and performance venues, and is home to many major companies and industries, including energy, healthcare, and technology.

Denver is a **major urban center**, with a diverse population of over 700,000 people, and a wider metropolitan area of over 3 million. The city has a strong economy, with a mix of industries, including tourism, manufacturing, and services. Denver is also known for its **outdoor recreation opportunities**, with numerous parks, trails, and open spaces, and is close to several major ski resorts and other outdoor destinations.

The city has a rich history, dating back to the mid-19th century, when it was founded as a mining town during the **Colorado Gold Rush**. Today, Denver is a thriving city, with a vibrant cultural scene, a strong economy, and a high quality of life. The city is also home to many major educational institutions, including the University of Denver and the University of Colorado Denver.
