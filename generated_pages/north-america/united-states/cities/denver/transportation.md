# Getting Around Denver — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Denver, you can use a variety of transportation options, including the city's international airport, domestic flights, rail network, buses, and city transport systems, as well as driving or using ride-hailing services.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Denver International Airport (DEN) is the primary airport serving the city, with direct flights to over 200 destinations worldwide, including major hubs in North America, Europe, and Asia. The airport is located about 25 miles northeast of downtown Denver and is easily accessible by car, bus, or ride-hailing services. If you're traveling from within the United States, you can also consider flying into one of the nearby regional airports, such as Colorado Springs Airport (COS) or Fort Collins-Loveland Municipal Airport (FNL), although options may be limited.

For those traveling overland, Denver is situated at the intersection of several major highways, including Interstate 25 (I-25) and Interstate 70 (I-70), making it easily accessible by car or bus from neighboring states. Additionally, you can take the bus from nearby cities, such as Boulder or Colorado Springs, or from other major cities in the western United States, such as Los Angeles or Chicago. Greyhound and other bus companies operate daily services to Denver, with multiple departures throughout the day.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Denver has an extensive domestic air network, with multiple daily flights to major cities across the United States. Domestic airlines, such as United, American Airlines, and Delta, operate flights from Denver International Airport to destinations such as New York City, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Miami. You can book flights online through the airlines' websites or through travel booking websites, such as Expedia or Kayak. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Denver vary depending on the destination and time of year, but you can expect to pay around $200-$500 for a one-way ticket to a major city.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Denver has a moderate rail network, with Amtrak operating daily services to nearby cities, such as Boulder and Colorado Springs, as well as to major cities in the western United States, such as Salt Lake City and Los Angeles. The Amtrak California Zephyr route passes through Denver, offering stunning views of the Rocky Mountains. You can book train tickets online through the Amtrak website or at the train station. While there are no high-speed rail options in Denver, the city is planning to expand its rail network in the coming years, with new lines and stations under construction.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several intercity bus companies operate in Denver, including Greyhound, BoltBus, and Megabus, offering affordable and convenient travel options to nearby cities and major destinations in the western United States. Bus services vary in quality and cost, but you can expect to pay around $20-$50 for a one-way ticket to a nearby city. Some bus companies, such as Greyhound, offer more amenities, such as Wi-Fi and power outlets, while others, such as BoltBus, offer cheaper fares but with fewer amenities.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Denver, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or state, as well as car insurance that meets the state's minimum requirements.
- Road conditions in Denver are generally good, with well-maintained highways and streets, but be aware of potential snow and ice during winter months.
- Fuel costs in Denver are relatively low, with gas prices averaging around $2.50 per gallon.
- If you plan to rent a car, consider booking in advance to ensure availability, especially during peak travel seasons.
- Be aware of potential hazards, such as aggressive drivers and construction zones, and take necessary precautions to stay safe on the road.
- Parking in Denver can be limited, especially in the downtown area, so be prepared to pay for parking or use public transportation.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of potential scams, such as unlicensed taxi drivers or fake ride-hailing services, and always use reputable companies.
- Payment methods in Denver include cash, credit cards, and mobile payments, such as Apple Pay or Google Pay.
- Download the **RTD** (Regional Transportation District) app to plan your route and track bus and train schedules.
- Consider purchasing a **Denver CityPASS**, which offers discounted admission to multiple attractions and public transportation.
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots, and keep an eye on your luggage at all times.
- If you're driving, be aware of potential road closures or construction delays, and plan your route accordingly.
- Take advantage of Denver's **bike-sharing** system, which offers a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.
