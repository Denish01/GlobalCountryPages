# Where to Stay in Denver — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When it comes to deciding where to stay in Denver, the city offers a diverse range of neighborhoods that cater to different types of travelers, from luxury seekers to budget-conscious backpackers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, Denver has something unique to offer:
**LoDo (Lower Downtown)** (Denver)
Best For: Luxury travelers and history buffs
Vibe: LoDo is known for its historic charm, upscale restaurants, and lively nightlife, making it an ideal spot for those looking for a sophisticated urban experience. The area is filled with beautifully restored 19th-century buildings, now home to trendy bars, boutiques, and art galleries.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Easy access to public transportation, a wide range of dining options, and close proximity to major attractions like Union Station and Coors Field. 
Cons: Can be quite pricey, and the area can get crowded, especially on weekends.

**Highland** (Denver)
Best For: Families and outdoor enthusiasts
Vibe: Highland is a charming neighborhood with a small-town feel, boasting beautiful parks, trendy restaurants, and a strong sense of community. The area is perfect for families and those who enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and biking.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Plenty of green spaces, a variety of family-friendly restaurants, and a short drive to downtown Denver. 
Cons: Limited public transportation options, and parking can be a challenge.

**Capitol Hill** (Denver)
Best For: Young professionals and nightlife enthusiasts
Vibe: Capitol Hill is a vibrant and eclectic neighborhood, known for its lively nightlife, diverse community, and stunning views of the city skyline. The area is filled with bars, clubs, and restaurants, making it an ideal spot for young professionals and partygoers.
Price Range: $100-$250/night
Pros: Close proximity to downtown Denver, a wide range of nightlife options, and a diverse community. 
Cons: Can be quite loud, especially on weekends, and parking can be a challenge.

**Cherry Creek** (Denver)
Best For: Upscale shoppers and foodies
Vibe: Cherry Creek is an affluent neighborhood, famous for its high-end shopping centers, gourmet restaurants, and beautiful parks. The area is perfect for those who enjoy luxury shopping and fine dining.
Price Range: $200-$400/night
Pros: Easy access to upscale shopping and dining, beautiful parks, and a short drive to downtown Denver. 
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and the area can feel a bit sterile.

**RiNo (River North Arts District)** (Denver)
Best For: Art lovers and hipsters
Vibe: RiNo is a trendy and artsy neighborhood, known for its vibrant street art, boutique galleries, and hip restaurants. The area is perfect for art lovers and those who enjoy a more alternative scene.
Price Range: $100-$250/night
Pros: A wide range of art galleries, trendy restaurants, and a short drive to downtown Denver. 
Cons: Can be quite loud, especially on weekends, and parking can be a challenge.

**Washington Park** (Denver)
Best For: Outdoor enthusiasts and families
Vibe: Washington Park is a beautiful neighborhood, surrounding a large park of the same name. The area is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, families, and those who enjoy a more relaxed atmosphere.
Price Range: $100-$250/night
Pros: Plenty of green spaces, a variety of family-friendly restaurants, and a short drive to downtown Denver. 
Cons: Limited public transportation options, and parking can be a challenge.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations well in advance, especially during peak travel seasons (June to August and December).
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find the best deals.
- Consider staying in a neighborhood that suits your interests and travel style.
- Look for hotels or guesthouses that offer free amenities, such as breakfast or parking.
- Be sure to read reviews and check the cancellation policies before making a booking.
- Negotiate the price if you're booking directly with a hotel or guesthouse, especially for longer stays.
- Keep an eye on seasonal pricing, as rates can fluctuate depending on the time of year.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas with high crime rates, such as certain parts of the Five Points neighborhood.
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas like Union Station or the 16th Street Mall.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit streets.
- Keep an eye on local news and events, as demonstrations or festivals can affect traffic and safety.
- Consider staying in a neighborhood with easy access to public transportation, such as LoDo or Capitol Hill.
