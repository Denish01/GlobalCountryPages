[SECTION] What Is Atlanta? [/SECTION]
Atlanta is a city located in the southeastern United States, in the state of Georgia. It is the capital and most populous city of the state, with a population of over 498,000 people, and a metropolitan area of over 6 million people. Atlanta is situated in the northwestern part of the state, near the Chattahoochee River, and is known for its humid subtropical climate, with mild winters and hot, humid summers. The city has a rich history, dating back to the Civil War era, and has played a significant role in the country's history, including the Civil Rights Movement.

Atlanta is a major economic center, with a diverse range of industries, including film and television production, logistics, and technology. The city is home to several major corporations, including Coca-Cola, UPS, and Delta Air Lines, and has a thriving startup scene. Atlanta is also known for its cultural attractions, including the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historical Park, the Atlanta History Center, and the High Museum of Art.

The city has a vibrant music scene, with a variety of genres, including hip-hop, R&B, and rock, and is home to several major music festivals, including the Atlanta Jazz Festival and the Music Midtown festival. Atlanta is also a major sports center, with several professional teams, including the Atlanta Braves (baseball), the Atlanta Falcons (football), and the Atlanta Hawks (basketball).
