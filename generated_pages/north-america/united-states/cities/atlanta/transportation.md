# Getting Around Atlanta — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Atlanta, you can use a variety of transportation options, including the city's international airport, domestic flights, rail network, buses, and city transport systems, as well as driving or using ride-hailing services.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Atlanta is a major transportation hub in the southeastern United States, with Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL) being one of the busiest airports in the world. The airport is a direct flight hub for many domestic and international airlines, including Delta Air Lines, which has its largest hub at ATL. You can fly into Atlanta from almost any major city in the United States, as well as from many international destinations. If you prefer to enter the city by land, you can drive into Atlanta from neighboring states, including Georgia, Alabama, Tennessee, and the Carolinas.

The city is also accessible by bus, with several major bus companies, including Greyhound and Megabus, offering routes to Atlanta from cities across the country. Additionally, you can take a train into Atlanta, with Amtrak offering routes from several major cities, including New York City, Washington, D.C., and Miami. The city's main train station, Peachtree Station, is located in the heart of downtown Atlanta, making it easy to get to your destination once you arrive.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Atlanta is a major hub for domestic flights, with many airlines offering routes to and from the city. Delta Air Lines is the largest carrier at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, but you can also fly with other major airlines, including American Airlines, United Airlines, and Southwest Airlines. Domestic flights are frequent and convenient, with many routes offering multiple flights per day. To book a domestic flight, you can use the airline's website or a travel booking website, such as Expedia or Kayak. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the route and time of year, but you can expect to pay between $100 and $500 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Atlanta has a moderate rail network, with several routes connecting the city to other major cities in the southeastern United States. Amtrak offers several routes to and from Atlanta, including the Crescent route, which runs from New York City to New Orleans, and the Silver Star route, which runs from New York City to Miami. The rail network is not as extensive as in some other parts of the country, but it is a convenient and comfortable way to travel, especially for longer routes. To book a train ticket, you can use Amtrak's website or visit a train station in person. High-speed rail options are limited in the United States, but there are plans to develop a high-speed rail line connecting Atlanta to other major cities in the southeast.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several major bus companies offer routes to and from Atlanta, including Greyhound, Megabus, and BoltBus. These buses are a convenient and affordable way to travel, especially for shorter routes. The quality of the buses varies depending on the company, but many offer comfortable seating, Wi-Fi, and other amenities. Routes and costs vary depending on the company and the route, but you can expect to pay between $20 and $100 for a one-way ticket. Some popular routes include Atlanta to New York City, Atlanta to Miami, and Atlanta to Nashville.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Atlanta, you will need a valid driver's license from your home state or country.
- Road conditions in Atlanta are generally good, with many major highways and interstates connecting the city to other parts of the country.
- Fuel costs vary depending on the time of year and the location, but you can expect to pay around $2.50 per gallon for gasoline.
- Car rental companies are widely available at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport and throughout the city, with prices starting at around $40 per day.
- When driving in Atlanta, be aware of the city's notorious traffic, especially during rush hour, and be sure to follow all traffic laws and regulations.
- Parking in Atlanta can be challenging, especially in the downtown area, so be sure to plan ahead and research parking options before you arrive.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of traffic in Atlanta, especially during rush hour, and plan your route accordingly.
- Use a mapping app, such as Google Maps or Waze, to navigate the city and avoid traffic.
- Consider using public transportation, such as the MARTA rail system, to get around the city.
- Be sure to follow all traffic laws and regulations, including wearing a seatbelt and not using your phone while driving.
- Use a reputable taxi or ride-hailing service, and always check the driver's ID before getting in the car.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night, and keep an eye on your belongings.
- Consider purchasing a **MARTA Visitor Pass**, which offers unlimited public transportation for a set period of time.
- Download the **MARTA On The Go** app to plan your route and track your bus or train in real-time.
