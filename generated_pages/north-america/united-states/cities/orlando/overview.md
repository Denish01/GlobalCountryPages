[SECTION] What Is Orlando? [/SECTION]
Orlando is a city located in the central part of the state of Florida, in the southeastern United States. It is situated in Orange County, which is part of the Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford Metropolitan Statistical Area. The city is known for its numerous theme parks, including **Walt Disney World**, **Universal Orlando**, and **SeaWorld**, which attract millions of visitors each year. Orlando is also a major center for business, technology, and education, with a diverse economy and a growing population.

Orlando's geographic position makes it an ideal location for tourism and trade. The city is located near several major highways and is served by the Orlando International Airport, which provides connections to destinations around the world. The city's climate is subtropical, with mild winters and hot, humid summers, making it a popular destination for people from cooler climates.

The city of Orlando has a rich history dating back to the 19th century, when it was a small agricultural community. Over time, the city grew and developed, with the establishment of theme parks and other tourist attractions in the mid-20th century. Today, Orlando is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world, with a wide range of activities and attractions to suit all interests.
