# Cost of Living in Orlando - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Orlando can range from moderate to expensive, with daily budgets varying greatly depending on individual lifestyle choices and preferences.

Currency: USD
Tipping Custom: expected (15-20% in restaurants and bars)

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Orlando? [/SECTION]
Orlando is a major city in the United States, known for its theme parks and tourist attractions, which can drive up the cost of living. Compared to other cities in the United States, Orlando has a moderate cost of living, with some expenses, such as housing and food, being relatively affordable. However, the cost of visiting Orlando can be expensive, especially when considering the cost of theme park tickets and other tourist activities. Overall, Orlando is more expensive than some neighboring cities in Florida, but less expensive than major cities like New York or Los Angeles.

The cost of living in Orlando can vary depending on the time of year and the location within the city. The city center and areas surrounding the theme parks tend to be more expensive, while outer neighborhoods and suburbs can be more affordable. Additionally, the cost of living in Orlando can be affected by the city's tourist season, with prices for accommodations and tourist activities tend to be higher during peak season (December to April).

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting Orlando during the off-season (May to November) to save on accommodations and tourist activities.
- Look for affordable accommodations outside of the city center or in nearby neighborhoods.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transport costs.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost activities in Orlando, such as visiting the Lake Eola Park or the Orlando Science Center.
- Plan your meals in advance and consider cooking your own food instead of eating out for every meal.
- Look for discounts and promotions on theme park tickets and other tourist activities to save on entertainment costs.
