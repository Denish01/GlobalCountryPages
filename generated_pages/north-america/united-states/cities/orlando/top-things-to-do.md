# Top Things to Do in Orlando — Attractions & Experiences

Orlando is a city that offers a wide range of exciting activities and attractions for visitors of all ages, from theme parks and water parks to outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and unique events. 

[SECTION] Top Attractions [/SECTION]
[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Walt Disney World | Lake Buena Vista | Theme Park | The largest and most visited theme park in the world, featuring four separate parks: Magic Kingdom, Epcot, Hollywood Studios, and Animal Kingdom |
| Universal Orlando Resort | Universal City | Theme Park | A popular destination for movie and TV fans, with two theme parks, a water park, and several on-site hotels |
| SeaWorld Orlando | SeaWorld Drive | Marine Park | A marine-life theme park with roller coasters, shows, and animal exhibits, including dolphins, sea lions, and penguins |
| Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex | Merritt Island | Museum | The official visitor center for the Kennedy Space Center, with interactive exhibits, a Saturn V rocket, and a chance to meet astronauts |
| Gatorland | Orange Blossom Trail | Wildlife Park | A 110-acre theme park and wildlife preserve with thousands of alligators and crocodiles, live shows, and a zip line |
| Orlando Science Center | East Princeton Street | Science Museum | A family-friendly museum with interactive exhibits on science and technology, including a planetarium and a giant screen theater |
| LEGOLAND Florida | Winter Haven | Theme Park | A theme park designed for kids aged 2-12, with rides, shows, and attractions made from millions of LEGO bricks |
| Madame Tussauds Orlando | International Drive | Wax Museum | A wax museum featuring life-like replicas of famous celebrities, historical figures, and fictional characters |
| Orlando Eye | International Drive | Observation Wheel | A 400-foot tall Ferris wheel with enclosed capsules and stunning views of the city |
| Wet 'n Wild Orlando | International Drive | Water Park | A water park with slides, a wave pool, and a lazy river, perfect for hot summer days |
[/TABLE]

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take an **Airboat Tour** of the Florida wetlands, where you can spot alligators, birds, and other wildlife in their natural habitat
- Visit the **Albin Polasek Museum & Sculpture Gardens**, a unique museum showcasing over 200 works by Czech-American sculptor Albin Polasek
- Go on a **Ghost Tour** of downtown Orlando, exploring the city's haunted history and paranormal activity
- Take a **Hot Air Balloon Ride** over the city, offering breathtaking views of the theme parks and surrounding landscape
- Attend a performance at the **Dr. Phillips Center for the Performing Arts**, a state-of-the-art venue hosting concerts, plays, and musicals
- Visit the **Leu Gardens**, a 50-acre botanical garden featuring a variety of plant species, walking paths, and a butterfly garden

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Wekiwa Springs State Park**: a natural spring and park with hiking trails, kayaking, and wildlife viewing opportunities
- **Lake Eola**: a downtown lake with walking paths, a fountain show, and swan-shaped paddle boat rentals
- **Bill Frederick Park at Turkey Lake**: a 45-acre park with a lake, walking trails, and picnic areas
- **Winter Park Chain of Lakes**: a series of six lakes connected by canals, perfect for boating, fishing, or taking a scenic stroll
- **Kissimmee Chain of Lakes**: a series of lakes and waterways popular for fishing, boating, and birdwatching

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Orlando Museum of Art**: a museum featuring a collection of American and African art, as well as rotating exhibitions
- **Cornell Fine Arts Museum**: a museum located on the campus of Rollins College, with a collection of over 5,000 works of art
- **Enzian Theater**: an independent movie theater showing art-house films and hosting film festivals
- **Orlando Shakespeare Theater**: a professional theater company producing classic and contemporary plays
- **Harry P. Leu Gardens**: a 50-acre botanical garden featuring a variety of plant species, walking paths, and a butterfly garden

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **The Coop**: a fried chicken restaurant in the East End Market, serving creative and delicious chicken dishes
- **The Polite Pig**: a modern barbecue restaurant at Disney Springs, offering a variety of meats and sides
- **Lake Lily**: a small lake in Maitland with walking paths, a park, and a beautiful fountain show
- **Thornton Park**: a charming neighborhood with boutique shops, restaurants, and a vibrant atmosphere
- **Winter Park**: a suburb of Orlando with a beautiful downtown area, featuring shops, restaurants, and a scenic park

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- **Kennedy Space Center** (60 miles, 1 hour): a day trip to the Kennedy Space Center, where you can tour the facilities and learn about the history of space exploration
- **St. Augustine** (100 miles, 2 hours): a day trip to the nation's oldest city, featuring historic landmarks, museums, and a beautiful beach
- **Crystal River** (80 miles, 1.5 hours): a day trip to Crystal River, where you can swim with manatees, go kayaking, or take an **Airboat Tour**
- **Cocoa Beach** (60 miles, 1 hour): a day trip to Cocoa Beach, featuring a beautiful beach, a pier, and a variety of water sports
- **Gainesville** (120 miles, 2.5 hours): a day trip to Gainesville, home to the University of Florida and featuring a variety of museums, parks, and cultural attractions
