# Orlando Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Orlando, you should try a variety of dishes that showcase the city's diverse culinary scene, including Southern comfort food, fresh seafood, and international cuisine.

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Orlando's cuisine is defined by its Southern roots, with a strong emphasis on comfort food, barbecue, and fresh seafood. The city's culinary scene is also influenced by its international population, with a wide range of ethnic restaurants serving everything from Mexican to Vietnamese cuisine. Orlando's location in central Florida means that it has access to a wide range of fresh produce, including citrus fruits, strawberries, and blueberries. The city's theme parks and resorts also play a significant role in shaping its culinary scene, with many restaurants and eateries catering to tourists and visitors.

Orlando's food culture is also characterized by its casual, laid-back atmosphere, with many restaurants and eateries offering outdoor seating and a relaxed, family-friendly environment. The city's food trucks and street food scene are also worth exploring, with many vendors offering unique and delicious dishes at affordable prices. From traditional Southern dishes like fried chicken and biscuits and gravy to international cuisine like sushi and tacos, Orlando's culinary scene has something to offer for every taste and preference.

In terms of regional variations, Orlando's cuisine is similar to other cities in the Southern United States, with a strong emphasis on comfort food and traditional Southern dishes. However, the city's international population and tourist industry have also introduced a wide range of international flavors and cuisines, making it a unique and exciting place to explore for foodies.
