[SECTION] What Is Portland? [/SECTION]
Portland is a city located in the state of Oregon, in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. It is situated near the confluence of the Willamette and Columbia rivers, approximately 70 miles from the Pacific Ocean. The city is known for its unique blend of urban and natural environments, with numerous parks, gardens, and waterways. Portland is also recognized for its vibrant cultural scene, which includes a variety of art galleries, museums, and performance venues.

Portland is not an independent country, but rather a city within the United States. As such, it does not have its own sovereignty or government, but is instead governed by a mayor-council system. The city is home to a diverse population of over 650,000 people, with a wide range of industries, including technology, healthcare, and manufacturing. Portland is also a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world with its food trucks, microbreweries, and outdoor recreational opportunities.

The city's geographic position makes it an ideal location for trade and commerce, with access to the Pacific Ocean and major transportation routes. Portland is also surrounded by a variety of natural attractions, including the Columbia River Gorge, Mount Hood, and the Oregon Coast. These natural features provide a range of outdoor recreational activities, including hiking, skiing, and water sports.
