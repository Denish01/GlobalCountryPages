# Portland Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Portland, be sure to try the city's renowned **food cart scene**, as well as its many restaurants serving a wide range of cuisines, including seafood, farm-to-table, and craft beers.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: seafood, particularly **Dungeness crab** and **salmon**, as well as **hazelnuts** and **berries**
National Dish: **Voodoo Doughnut**, a unique and quirky pastry that reflects the city's eclectic spirit
Meal Times: typical breakfast times are between 7-10 am, lunch from 11 am-2 pm, and dinner from 5-9 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 15-20% of the total bill is customary
Dietary Notes: Portland is known for being **vegetarian-friendly** and **gluten-free**, with many restaurants offering options for these diets
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Portland's cuisine is defined by its proximity to the ocean and the surrounding farmland, with a focus on **fresh seafood** and **locally sourced produce**. The city's food scene is also heavily influenced by its **food cart culture**, with over 600 carts serving everything from traditional **Vietnamese banh mi** to **Egyptian koshari**. Additionally, Portland is known for its **craft beer scene**, with many breweries offering **beer pairings** with their menus. The city's cuisine is also shaped by its **seasonal ingredients**, with many restaurants changing their menus to reflect the freshest ingredients available.

Portland's food scene is also characterized by its **eclectic mix of cuisines**, with a wide range of international options available, including **Mexican**, **Italian**, and **Thai**. The city's **farm-to-table** movement is also prominent, with many restaurants emphasizing the importance of **sustainable agriculture** and **local sourcing**. This focus on local ingredients and seasonal menus has helped to establish Portland as a **foodie destination**, with many visitors coming to the city specifically to experience its unique culinary scene.

The city's **neighborhoods** also play a role in shaping its food culture, with different areas offering distinct dining experiences. For example, the **Pearl District** is known for its **upscale restaurants**, while the **Alberta Arts District** is home to a **diverse range of international cuisines**. This diversity of dining options helps to make Portland a **vibrant and exciting** food city, with something to offer every type of eater.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Tipping** is customary, with 15-20% of the total bill expected
- **Reservations** are recommended for popular restaurants, especially on weekends
- **Dress code** is generally **casual**, but some upscale restaurants may require **dressy attire**
- **Eating etiquette** is generally **relaxed**, with an emphasis on **enjoying good food and company**
- **Food allergies** and **dietary restrictions** are generally **accommodated**, with many restaurants offering **gluten-free** and **vegetarian** options
- **Credit cards** are widely accepted, but **cash** is also accepted at many establishments
