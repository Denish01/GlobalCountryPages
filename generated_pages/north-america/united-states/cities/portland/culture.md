# Portland Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

Portland, a city in the United States, is known for its unique blend of eccentricity, environmentalism, and a thriving arts and culture scene, making it a fascinating destination to visit and explore. 

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Portland's culture is defined by its **progressive values**, **diverse community**, and a strong sense of **individuality**. The city has a long history of attracting people from all over the world, resulting in a unique ethnic and linguistic makeup. While English is the primary language spoken, Portland is also home to a significant number of **Spanish**, **Chinese**, and **Vietnamese** speakers. The city's cultural identity is also influenced by its **indigenous peoples**, including the Multnomah and Clackamas tribes, who have lived in the region for thousands of years. Portland's cultural scene is characterized by a thriving **arts community**, a **vibrant music scene**, and a strong emphasis on **sustainability** and **environmentalism**.

Portland's cultural identity is also shaped by its **food culture**, which is known for its emphasis on **local**, **organic**, and **sustainable** ingredients. The city is home to a wide range of **farmers' markets**, **food trucks**, and **restaurants**, offering a diverse range of cuisines from around the world. Portland's cultural scene is also influenced by its **neighborhoods**, each with its own unique character and charm. From the **trendy boutiques** and **art galleries** of the Pearl District to the **vibrant nightlife** of Old Town, Portland's neighborhoods offer a wide range of cultural experiences and attractions.

The city's cultural identity is also reflected in its **community events** and **festivals**, which celebrate the city's diversity and individuality. From the **Portland Pride Parade** to the **Portland Jazz Festival**, the city hosts a wide range of events and festivals throughout the year, showcasing its vibrant cultural scene. Overall, Portland's cultural identity is a unique blend of **progressive values**, **diverse community**, and a strong sense of **individuality**, making it a fascinating destination to visit and explore.

[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Be **respectful** of the city's **environmental values** and try to reduce your carbon footprint during your visit
- **Tip** your servers and bartenders, as they rely on tips to make a living wage
- **Explore** the city's many **neighborhoods** and **local businesses**
- **Attend** one of the city's many **festivals** or **events** to experience the local culture
- **Try** some of the city's **unique foods**, such as Voodoo Doughnut or Pok Pok's fish sauce wings
- **Bring** a **reusable water bottle** and **coffee cup** to reduce waste

**Don't:**
- **Litter** or **pollute** the city's streets and waterways
- **Disrespect** the city's **indigenous peoples** or their cultural heritage
- **Ignore** the city's **traffic laws** and **bike lanes**
- **Be** **disrespectful** to the city's **LGBTQ+ community** or any other marginalized group
- **Fail** to **tip** your servers and bartenders
- **Overlook** the city's many **local artists** and **musicians**
