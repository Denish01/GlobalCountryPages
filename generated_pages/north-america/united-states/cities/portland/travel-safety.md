# Portland Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Portland is generally a safe city to visit, with low to moderate crime rates and a strong sense of community, but as with any major city, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Portland is a vibrant and eclectic city with a strong reputation for being welcoming and inclusive. The city has a low violent crime rate, and most visitors can feel safe walking around the city's many neighborhoods, including the downtown area, during the day and evening. However, as with any major city, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, bike theft, and vandalism can occur, and visitors should take common-sense precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The city also has a moderate risk of scams, particularly in areas with high tourist traffic, so visitors should be aware of their surroundings and be cautious of unsolicited offers or deals that seem too good to be true.

Overall, Portland is a safe city for visitors, with a strong sense of community and a low risk of violent crime. However, visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe, particularly at night or in areas with high crime rates. The city's police department is responsive and effective, and visitors can feel confident that they will receive help if they need it.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **panhandlers** and **aggressive solicitors** in areas with high tourist traffic, particularly in the Hawthorne District and Old Town/Chinatown.
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas, particularly in the downtown area and at popular tourist attractions.
- Be cautious of **scams** involving **unsolicited offers** or **deals that seem too good to be true**, particularly in areas with high tourist traffic.
- Be aware of **bike theft**, particularly in areas with high bike traffic, and take necessary precautions to secure your bike.
- Be cautious of **drunk drivers** and **impaired pedestrians**, particularly at night, and plan ahead for safe transportation.
- Be aware of **natural disasters**, such as earthquakes and floods, and have a plan in place in case of an emergency.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Portland, but visitors should be up to date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Portland, but bottled water is also widely available.
- **Medical Facilities:** Portland has a high-quality healthcare system, with numerous hospitals and medical facilities available, including **Oregon Health & Science University** and **Legacy Good Samaritan Medical Center**. Visitors can expect to receive excellent medical care, but may need to provide proof of insurance or pay out of pocket for services.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available in Portland, including **Walgreens** and **CVS**, and visitors can easily access prescription medications and other healthcare supplies.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas.
- Keep valuables secure and be cautious of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas.
- Use **public transportation** or **ride-sharing services** instead of walking alone at night.
- Be cautious of **scams** involving **unsolicited offers** or **deals that seem too good to be true**.
- Keep your **hotel room door** locked and secure, and use the hotel's safe to store valuables.
- Be aware of **natural disasters**, such as earthquakes and floods, and have a plan in place in case of an emergency.
- Stay informed about local events and **protests**, and avoid areas with high levels of activity.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas.
- Use **public transportation** or **ride-sharing services** instead of walking alone at night.
- Be cautious of **catcalls** and **harassment**, particularly in areas with high levels of foot traffic, and be prepared to respond assertively if necessary.
- Keep your **hotel room door** locked and secure, and use the hotel's safe to store valuables.
