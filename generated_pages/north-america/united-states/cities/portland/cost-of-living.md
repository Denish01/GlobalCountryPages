[SECTION] How Expensive Is Portland? [/SECTION]
Portland is considered one of the more expensive cities in the United States, with a cost of living that is higher than the national average. Compared to other major cities in the US, Portland's cost of living is similar to that of cities like San Francisco and New York, but lower than cities like Seattle and Los Angeles. The city's proximity to the Pacific Ocean, its vibrant cultural scene, and its strong economy all contribute to its relatively high cost of living. However, Portland also offers a unique blend of urban and outdoor activities, a thriving food and drink scene, and a high quality of life, making it an attractive destination for visitors and residents alike.

Portland's cost of living is also influenced by its location in the state of Oregon, which has a relatively high tax burden and a strong economy driven by the tech and healthcare industries. The city's popularity with young professionals and families has also driven up housing costs, making it one of the most expensive cities in the US for renters and homebuyers. Despite these costs, Portland remains a popular destination for people from all over the world, drawn by its unique culture, beautiful natural surroundings, and high quality of life.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting Portland during the shoulder season (April-May or September-October) to avoid peak tourist prices.
- Take advantage of the city's many free or low-cost attractions, such as the Japanese Garden, the Portland Art Museum, and the city's many parks and gardens.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transportation costs.
- Eat at food trucks or local eateries instead of mid-range or fine dining restaurants to save on food costs.
- Look for apartment rentals outside of the city center to save on housing costs.
- Take advantage of the city's many happy hour deals and discounts to save on entertainment costs.
