# Getting Around Miami — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Miami, you can use a variety of transportation options, including international and domestic flights, an extensive rail network, buses, and city transport systems such as metro, bus, taxi, and ride-hailing services.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Miami International Airport (MIA) is the primary airport serving the city, with numerous direct flights to destinations around the world, including major hubs in North America, South America, Europe, and the Caribbean. The airport is located about 8 miles northwest of downtown Miami and is easily accessible by taxi, ride-hailing services, or public transportation. If you're traveling from within the United States, you can also fly into Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL), which is about 30 miles north of Miami.

In addition to air travel, Miami can also be reached by car or bus from other cities in the United States. The city is connected to the rest of the country by several major highways, including I-75 and I-95, making it easily accessible by car. Several bus companies, including Greyhound and Megabus, also operate daily services to and from Miami, connecting the city to other major cities in the United States.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Miami has an extensive domestic air network, with numerous flights to destinations throughout the United States. Several major airlines, including American Airlines, Delta Air Lines, and United Airlines, operate flights from Miami International Airport to cities such as New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Houston. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through travel booking websites such as Expedia or Kayak. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the destination and time of year, but you can expect to pay between $200 and $500 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Miami has a moderate rail network, with several train lines connecting the city to other parts of the state and the country. The Brightline train service operates high-speed trains between Miami and other cities in Florida, including Fort Lauderdale and West Palm Beach. You can book train tickets online through the Brightline website or at the train station. The Tri-Rail commuter train service also operates between Miami and other cities in South Florida, including Fort Lauderdale and West Palm Beach.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Several intercity bus companies operate in Miami, connecting the city to other destinations in the United States. Greyhound and Megabus are two of the major bus companies that operate daily services to and from Miami, with routes to cities such as New York, Los Angeles, and Chicago. The bus companies operate from the Miami Central Station, which is located in downtown Miami. You can book bus tickets online through the companies' websites or at the bus station. Approximate costs for bus tickets vary depending on the destination and time of year, but you can expect to pay between $50 and $200 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Miami, you need a valid driver's license from your home country or state.
- The road quality in Miami is generally good, with well-maintained highways and streets.
- Fuel costs in Miami are relatively high, with gasoline prices averaging around $3 per gallon.
- Car rental companies such as Hertz, Avis, and Budget operate in Miami, with prices starting from around $40 per day.
- When driving in Miami, be aware of the city's busy streets and aggressive drivers.
- Parking in Miami can be challenging, especially in downtown areas, with prices ranging from $2 to $10 per hour.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of traffic congestion in Miami, especially during rush hour, and plan your route accordingly.
- Use a **GPS** or mapping app to navigate the city's streets and avoid getting lost.
- Be cautious when using ride-hailing services, and always check the driver's ID and license plate before getting in the car.
- Consider purchasing a **SunPass** or **E-Pass** for toll roads, which can save you time and money.
- Don't leave valuables in your car, especially in tourist areas, to avoid theft.
- Use the **Miami-Dade Transit** app to plan your route and track your bus or train in real-time.
- Be prepared for **traffic accidents**, and know what to do in case of an emergency.
