[SECTION] What Is Philadelphia? [/SECTION]
Philadelphia is a city with a long and storied history, dating back to 1682 when it was founded by William Penn. It is situated in the northeastern United States, in the state of Pennsylvania, and is the largest city in the state. Philadelphia is known for its significant role in American history, particularly during the American Revolution, and is home to many iconic landmarks such as Independence Hall, where the Declaration of Independence and the United States Constitution were signed. The city is also a major cultural and economic hub, with a diverse population and a thriving arts and entertainment scene.

Geographically, Philadelphia is located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, near the Delaware River. The city has a total area of approximately 134 square miles and is situated about 95 miles southwest of New York City. Philadelphia is a major transportation hub, with a large international airport and a comprehensive public transportation system.

Philadelphia is not a sovereign state, but rather a city within the United States, and as such, it does not have its own government or international recognition as a separate entity. However, it is a major urban center with a significant impact on the economy, culture, and politics of the United States.
