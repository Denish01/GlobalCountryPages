# Top Things to Do in Philadelphia — Attractions & Experiences

Philadelphia, the largest city in Pennsylvania, offers a diverse range of activities and attractions that showcase its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning architecture, making it a must-visit destination for any traveler.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Ghost Tour** of Philadelphia's haunted sites, including the Eastern State Penitentiary and the LaPierre House
- Visit the **Mutter Museum**, a medical museum with a collection of unusual and bizarre medical specimens
- Explore the **Street Art** scene in the Fishtown neighborhood, with vibrant murals and graffiti
- Attend a **Philadelphia Phillies** game at Citizens Bank Park, with stunning views of the city skyline
- Take a **Beer Tasting Tour** of Philadelphia's craft breweries, including Yards Brewing Company and Evil Genius Beer Company
- Visit the **Please Touch Museum**, a children's museum with interactive exhibits and play areas

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Fairmount Park, a large urban park with hiking trails, lakes, and scenic views
- Bartram's Garden, a historic botanical garden with a variety of plant species
- Schuylkill River Trail, a scenic trail for hiking and biking along the river
- Wissahickon Valley Park, a wilderness area with hiking trails and stunning views
- Morris Arboretum, a beautiful garden with a collection of trees and plant species

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- The **Philadelphia Orchestra**, a world-renowned symphony orchestra with performances at the Kimmel Center
- The **African American Museum in Philadelphia**, a museum showcasing the history and culture of African Americans
- The **Philadelphia Museum of Jewish Art**, a museum with a collection of Jewish art and artifacts
- The **Penn Museum**, a museum with a collection of artifacts from around the world, including ancient Egypt and Greece
- The **Kimmel Center**, a performing arts center with a variety of shows and concerts

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Shofuso Japanese House and Garden**, a beautiful and tranquil oasis in Fairmount Park
- The **Woodlands Cemetery**, a historic cemetery with stunning views of the city
- The **Betsy Ross House**, a historic home and museum showcasing the life and work of the famous seamstress
- The **Elfreth's Alley Association**, a small museum showcasing the history of the oldest continuously inhabited street in the United States

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- **New York City**, a 2-hour drive or train ride, with iconic landmarks like the Statue of Liberty and Central Park
- **Atlantic City**, a 2.5-hour drive, with casinos, beaches, and boardwalks
- **Lancaster**, a 2-hour drive, with Amish country and historic sites like the Landis Valley Farm Museum
- **Gettysburg**, a 2.5-hour drive, with historic sites like the Gettysburg National Military Park and the Gettysburg Museum of the Civil War
- **Brandywine Valley**, a 1-hour drive, with scenic countryside, historic sites, and museums like the Brandywine River Museum of Art
