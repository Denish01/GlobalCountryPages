[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
El Salvador has a well-developed transportation system, with a variety of options for getting around the country. The main modes of transportation are buses, taxis, and ride-hailing apps, with most people relying on buses and taxis to get around. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent bus services and affordable fares. However, traffic in major cities like San Salvador can be heavy, and roads in rural areas may be in poor condition.

The majority of people in El Salvador use buses and taxis to get around, as they are affordable and widely available. Buses are the most common mode of public transportation, with routes covering most areas of the country. Taxis are also widely available, and can be hailed on the street or booked in advance. Ride-hailing apps like Uber and Cabify are also popular, especially in major cities. For longer distances, there are also intercity buses and domestic flights available.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using public buses or shared taxis, which are often cheaper than taking a taxi or ride-hailing app.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps to ensure safety, especially at night.
- Always negotiate the fare before getting into a taxi, and make sure the meter is turned on.
- Be aware of traffic conditions and plan your route accordingly to avoid congestion.
- Consider renting a car for longer trips or to explore rural areas, but be aware of the costs and potential risks.
- Download transport apps like Moovit or Transit to help navigate the public transportation system and plan your route.
