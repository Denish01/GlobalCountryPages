# Education Costs in El Salvador

The cost of education in El Salvador varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in El Salvador is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being generally considered less competitive and of lower quality compared to private schools. The language of instruction is **Spanish**, and compulsory education lasts for 9 years, from primary to secondary school. While public schools are free or subsidized, private schools offer a higher quality of education, but at a higher cost. International schools are also available, offering a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **US-style** education.

The quality of education in El Salvador varies significantly between public and private institutions, with private schools generally offering better facilities, more qualified teachers, and a more comprehensive curriculum. However, the cost of private education can be prohibitively expensive for many Salvadorans, making public schools the only option for many families. Despite these challenges, El Salvador has made significant progress in increasing access to education, with a literacy rate of around 89% and a growing number of students pursuing higher education.
