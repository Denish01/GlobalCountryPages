[SECTION] How El Salvador Is Divided [/SECTION]
El Salvador is a sovereign nation divided into 14 departments, which are further subdivided into 262 municipalities. The departments are the primary administrative divisions of the country, and they are responsible for providing various public services to their citizens. The departments are also grouped into several regions, including the Western Region, the Central Region, and the Eastern Region, each with its own unique character and economic profile.

The administrative structure of El Salvador is designed to promote decentralization and local autonomy, with each department having its own governor and municipal council. This structure allows for more effective governance and decision-making at the local level, and it has helped to promote economic development and social progress in various parts of the country. The departments are also responsible for managing their own natural resources, including water, land, and forests, which are essential for the country's economic development and environmental sustainability.

[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
El Salvador has a well-developed transportation network, with several domestic airports, buses, and taxis available. The country's main airport is the Comalapa International Airport, which is located about 30 miles outside of San Salvador. From there, visitors can take buses or taxis to various parts of the country. The bus system is efficient and affordable, with several companies operating throughout the country. Taxis are also widely available, but visitors should be sure to use licensed taxis and agree on the fare before departing.

Driving in El Salvador can be challenging, especially for visitors who are not familiar with the country's roads and traffic laws. The country's roads are generally in good condition, but traffic can be heavy, especially in urban areas. Visitors who plan to drive should be sure to obtain a valid driver's license and purchase insurance before renting a car. Additionally, visitors should be aware of the country's traffic laws and regulations, including the use of seatbelts and headlights. Overall, getting around El Salvador is relatively easy, with several transportation options available to suit different budgets and preferences.
