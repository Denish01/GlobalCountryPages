[SECTION] Overview: El Salvador vs Guatemala [/SECTION]
El Salvador and Guatemala are two neighboring countries in Central America that share a similar cultural heritage, but they have distinct economic profiles. El Salvador has a more diversified economy, with a strong service sector and a growing manufacturing industry, while Guatemala's economy is more reliant on agriculture and remittances. In terms of cost of living, El Salvador is generally cheaper than Guatemala, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. However, Guatemala has a more vibrant cultural scene and a wider range of outdoor activities, which may appeal to tourists and expats.

The economic context of both countries is also worth considering. El Salvador has a more stable economy, with a lower inflation rate and a stronger currency (the US dollar is the official currency). Guatemala, on the other hand, has a more volatile economy, with a higher inflation rate and a currency that can fluctuate in value. Overall, El Salvador is a more affordable option for those looking to live or travel in Central America, but Guatemala offers a more authentic cultural experience and a wider range of outdoor activities.
