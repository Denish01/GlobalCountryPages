# El Salvador Tax Guide — Income, Corporate & VAT Rates

The tax rates in El Salvador range from 10% to 30% for individuals, with a corporate tax rate of 25%, and a value-added tax (VAT) of 13%, making it essential for expats and businesses to understand the tax system to ensure compliance and take advantage of available incentives.

[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
El Salvador has a **territorial taxation** system, where individuals and businesses are taxed only on income earned within the country, except for certain cases where worldwide income is considered. To be considered a tax resident in El Salvador, an individual must have a permanent home in the country, spend more than 183 days in a calendar year, or have a spouse or children who are tax residents. Foreigners who become tax residents are subject to taxation on their worldwide income, but may be eligible for **foreign tax credits** to avoid double taxation.

The tax system in El Salvador is relatively complex, with various laws and regulations governing income tax, corporate tax, VAT, and other taxes. The **General Directorate of Internal Revenue** (DGI) is responsible for administering and enforcing tax laws, and provides guidance and support to taxpayers through its website and local offices. Understanding the tax system and seeking professional advice can help individuals and businesses navigate the complexities and ensure compliance with tax laws and regulations.

[SECTION] Corporate & Business Tax [/SECTION]
- The corporate tax rate in El Salvador is 25%, applicable to companies with a permanent establishment in the country.
- Small businesses with annual revenues below $100,000 may be eligible for a reduced tax rate of 10%.
- Free zones and export processing zones offer **tax incentives** such as exemptions from income tax and VAT, as well as streamlined registration and regulatory procedures.
- Companies must register with the DGI and obtain a tax identification number to operate in El Salvador.
- Foreign companies with a presence in El Salvador may be subject to **branch profits tax** and other taxes.

[SECTION] VAT / Sales Tax [/SECTION]
- The standard VAT rate in El Salvador is 13%, applicable to most goods and services.
- Reduced rates of 5% and 10% apply to certain items such as food, medicine, and education services.
- Exemptions from VAT include basic necessities such as water, electricity, and healthcare services.
- Tourists may be eligible for a **VAT refund** on certain purchases, such as hotel stays and tourist activities.

[SECTION] For Expats & Foreign Workers [/SECTION]
- Expats who become tax residents in El Salvador are subject to taxation on their worldwide income, but may be eligible for **foreign tax credits** to avoid double taxation.
- El Salvador has double taxation treaties with 10 countries, including the United States, Spain, and Canada.
- Foreign workers may be required to contribute to the **social security system**, which provides benefits such as healthcare, pensions, and unemployment insurance.
- Expats may be subject to **remittance taxes** on funds transferred out of the country, but may be eligible for exemptions or reductions.
- Foreigners who own real estate in El Salvador may be subject to **property taxes** and other levies.
- Expats should consult with a tax professional to understand their tax obligations and take advantage of available incentives and exemptions.

[SECTION] Crypto & Investment Income [/SECTION]
- Investment income such as **dividends**, **interest**, and **capital gains** is subject to taxation in El Salvador, with rates ranging from 10% to 30%.
- **Cryptocurrency** transactions are subject to VAT and other taxes, with the DGI providing guidance on the tax treatment of digital assets.
- Foreign investors may be eligible for **tax incentives** such as exemptions from income tax and VAT, as well as streamlined registration and regulatory procedures.
- Individuals and businesses should consult with a tax professional to understand their tax obligations and take advantage of available incentives and exemptions.
