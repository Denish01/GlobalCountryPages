[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Copán Ruins**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a testament to the country's rich Mayan heritage, with intricate stone carvings and hieroglyphics.
- The **Garifuna Culture**, a unique blend of African, Caribbean, and Latin American influences, is reflected in the music, dance, and traditions of the Garifuna people.
- The **Lenca Pottery**, a traditional craft that dates back to pre-Columbian times, is still practiced today, with artisans creating beautiful and intricate ceramics.
- The **Punta Gorda**, a scenic coastal town, is known for its stunning natural beauty, with crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and vibrant marine life.
- The **Honduran Carnival**, a colorful and lively celebration, takes place in various towns and cities across the country, featuring music, dance, and traditional costumes.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Rigoberto Castillo**, a renowned Honduran writer, has made significant contributions to Latin American literature, with his works exploring themes of identity, culture, and social justice.
- **Honduran diaspora communities**, found in countries such as the United States, Spain, and Mexico, have made significant contributions to their adopted countries, in fields such as business, education, and the arts.
- **Francisco Morazán**, a national hero, played a key role in the country's struggle for independence and is remembered for his vision of a unified and democratic Central America.
- **The Garifuna community**, with their unique cultural heritage, has had a significant impact on the country's cultural identity and has been recognized by UNESCO for their contributions to intangible cultural heritage.
