The cost of utilities and internet in Honduras can vary depending on the location, usage, and provider, but on average, a monthly utility bill for an 85 sqm apartment can range from $100 to $200 USD.

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Honduras has a relatively developed utility infrastructure, with most households having access to electricity, water, and gas. However, the quality of these services can vary depending on the location, with urban areas generally having more reliable access to utilities than rural areas. The cost of utilities in Honduras can fluctuate due to seasonal variation, with higher demand for electricity and water during the dry season (December to May) leading to increased prices. Additionally, the country's inflation rate of 4.6% (2024) can also impact the cost of utilities, making it essential for consumers to regularly review their usage and adjust their budgets accordingly.

The Honduran government has implemented measures to stabilize the cost of utilities, including subsidies for low-income households and investments in renewable energy sources. However, the cost of utilities can still be a significant burden for many households, particularly those with limited incomes. As a result, it is essential for consumers to be mindful of their energy and water usage and take steps to reduce their consumption, such as using energy-efficient appliances and conserving water.
