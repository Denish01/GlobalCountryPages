[SECTION] What Is Honduras? [/SECTION]
Honduras is a **sovereign** country located in **North America**, bordered by **Guatemala**, **El Salvador**, and **Nicaragua**. With a population of 10.4 million people, Honduras is a significant player in the region. The country is known for its rich **cultural heritage**, which is a blend of **indigenous**, **Spanish**, and **African** influences. Honduras is also famous for its stunning **natural beauty**, with a diverse range of ecosystems, including **tropical rainforests**, **coral reefs**, and **mountain ranges**.

Honduras has a long and complex history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the **Maya civilization**. The country has been influenced by various cultures, including the **Spanish**, who colonized the region in the 16th century. Today, Honduras is a **democratic** country, with a **presidential system** of government. The country's economy is primarily driven by **agriculture**, **manufacturing**, and **tourism**, with a growing focus on **sustainable development**.

Honduras is a popular destination for **tourists**, who come to experience the country's **beautiful beaches**, **vibrant culture**, and **rich history**. The country is also home to a number of **protected areas**, including **national parks** and **wildlife refuges**, which are home to a wide range of **flora and fauna**. From the **cloud forests** of the **Mosquitia** region to the **coral reefs** of the **Caribbean Sea**, Honduras is a nature lover's paradise.
