[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Honduras is a **sovereign** country located in **North America**, specifically in the region of Central America. It is situated between the Caribbean Sea to the north and the Pacific Ocean to the south, with a long coastline along the **Gulf of Honduras**. The country shares borders with three neighboring countries: **Guatemala** to the west, **El Salvador** to the southwest, and **Nicaragua** to the southeast. This strategic location makes Honduras an important gateway between the Caribbean and the Pacific.

The country's geography is characterized by a mix of coastal lowlands, mountainous regions, and river valleys. The **Mesoamerican Reef**, the largest coral reef in the Caribbean, runs along the northern coast of Honduras, providing a rich marine ecosystem. The capital city, **Tegucigalpa**, is located in the central highlands, surrounded by mountains and valleys. The country's borders with its neighbors are largely defined by natural features such as rivers, mountains, and coastlines, which has helped shape the country's history, culture, and economy.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Honduras is a country of diverse terrain and landscape, with a range of ecosystems that support a wide variety of flora and fauna. The **mountain ranges**, including the **Cordillera de los Andes** and the **Cordillera de la Costa**, run along the western and central parts of the country, with peaks reaching elevations of over 2,800 meters. The **rivers**, such as the **Patuca River** and the **Ulúa River**, flow through the country, providing water and fertile soil for agriculture and supporting a range of aquatic life. The **coastal lowlands**, including the **Mosquitia** region, are characterized by mangrove forests, coral reefs, and sandy beaches.

The country's terrain is also home to several **volcanic regions**, including the **Golfo de Fonseca** and the **Lago de Yojoa**, which are popular tourist destinations. The **cloud forests**, found in the higher elevations, are home to a unique range of flora and fauna, including **orchids**, **quetzals**, and **monkeys**. The **Pico Bonito National Park**, located near the city of **La Ceiba**, is a popular destination for hiking and wildlife watching, with its stunning scenery and diverse wildlife.

The **islands** of Honduras, including **Roatán**, **Utila**, and **Guanaja**, are located off the northern coast and are known for their beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and vibrant marine life. These islands are a popular destination for **scuba diving**, **snorkeling**, and **fishing**, and are home to a range of marine species, including **sea turtles**, **rays**, and **groupers**.
