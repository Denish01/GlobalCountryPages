# Education Costs in Saint Martin

The cost of education in Saint Martin varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools can range from $2,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Saint Martin is overseen by the French government, and as such, it follows the French curriculum and educational standards. Public education is generally considered to be of good quality, with French being the primary language of instruction. However, there are also private schools and international schools that offer alternative curricula, such as the International Baccalaureate, and instruction in other languages, including English. Compulsory education in Saint Martin lasts for 10 years, from the age of 6 to 16, and is free for local students. International schools are available on the island, catering to the needs of expatriate families and offering a range of curricula, including British, American, and International Baccalaureate programs.

The quality of education in Saint Martin is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on core subjects such as mathematics, science, and language. However, some students may choose to pursue higher education abroad, particularly in France or other European countries, due to the limited availability of university programs on the island. Additionally, some private schools may offer specialized programs or courses, such as vocational training or language instruction, which can provide students with additional skills and qualifications.
