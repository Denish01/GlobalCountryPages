[SECTION] Overview: Saint Martin vs Sint Maarten [/SECTION]
Saint Martin and Sint Maarten are two separate territories on the same island, with distinct economic systems and cost structures. Saint Martin, as a French territory, uses the Euro as its official currency, while Sint Maarten, a Dutch territory, uses the Antillean Guilder, pegged to the US Dollar. The economic context of Saint Martin is more closely tied to the European Union, resulting in higher prices for goods and services. In contrast, Sint Maarten has a more tourism-driven economy, with a larger focus on affordability and competitiveness. Overall, Sint Maarten is generally cheaper than Saint Martin, with prices around 10-20% lower across various categories.

The cost difference between the two territories can be attributed to various factors, including taxes, labor costs, and transportation expenses. Saint Martin has a higher tax system, with a value-added tax (VAT) of 8.5%, compared to Sint Maarten's 5% turnover tax. Additionally, labor costs in Saint Martin are higher due to the territory's adherence to French labor laws and regulations. However, both territories offer a unique blend of French and Dutch cultures, stunning natural beauty, and a wide range of activities and attractions, making them popular destinations for tourists and expats alike.
