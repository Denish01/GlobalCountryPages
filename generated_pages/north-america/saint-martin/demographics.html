Population Overview

The population of Saint Martin is relatively small, with a total of approximately 31,496 people, according to the latest estimates. The population growth rate is around 1.1%, which is relatively low compared to other Caribbean islands. The population is predominantly urban, with around 85% of the population living in urban areas, primarily in the capital city of Marigot. The population density is approximately 593 people per square kilometer, making it one of the more densely populated islands in the Caribbean. The population of Saint Martin has been growing steadily over the years, with a significant increase in the number of tourists and expatriates moving to the island.

The population growth trends in Saint Martin are influenced by a combination of factors, including a relatively high life expectancy of 78 years, a moderate fertility rate of 1.8 children per woman, and a steady influx of migrants from other Caribbean islands and countries. The population is also characterized by a significant proportion of young people, with around 20% of the population under the age of 15. The urbanization of the population has led to the development of a vibrant and diverse cultural scene, with a mix of French, African, and Caribbean influences.

The migration patterns in Saint Martin are also worth noting, with many people moving to the island for work or to retire. The island's economy is primarily driven by tourism, with a significant number of people employed in the service sector. The population is also characterized by a high level of mobility, with many people traveling to and from the island for work, education, or leisure.
