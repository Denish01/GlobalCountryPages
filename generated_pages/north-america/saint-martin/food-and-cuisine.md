# Saint Martin Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Saint Martin, be sure to try the local cuisine, which is a unique blend of French, Caribbean, and African influences, featuring dishes such as **Accras de Morue** and **Colombo de Poulet**.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: salted cod, fresh seafood, coconut milk, plantains
National Dish: **Langouste à la Créole**, a lobster dish cooked in a spicy tomato-based sauce
Meal Times: typical breakfast times are 7-10 am, lunch 12-2 pm, and dinner 7-10 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 10-15% is customary
Dietary Notes: vegetarian and seafood options are widely available, but halal and kosher options may be limited
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Saint Martin's cuisine is a reflection of its history and cultural diversity, with French, African, and Caribbean influences. The island's strategic location in the northeastern Caribbean has made it a melting pot of culinary traditions. The cuisine is characterized by the use of fresh seafood, coconut milk, and spices, which are combined to create flavorful dishes such as **Colombo de Poulet**, a chicken curry, and **Accras de Morue**, cod fritters. The French influence is evident in the use of butter, cream, and wine in many dishes, while the African influence is reflected in the use of spices and peppers.

The cuisine in Saint Martin also varies by region, with the French side of the island, known as Saint-Martin, offering a more formal and elegant dining experience, while the Dutch side, known as Sint Maarten, is more casual and laid-back. The island's capital, Marigot, is a hub for culinary activity, with many restaurants and markets offering a wide range of local and international dishes.

The food culture in Saint Martin is also shaped by the island's history as a colonial outpost, with many dishes reflecting the influence of European, African, and indigenous cuisines. The island's cuisine is also known for its use of fresh and locally sourced ingredients, which are often combined to create innovative and delicious dishes.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- Table manners are formal, with an emphasis on using napkins and not speaking with your mouth full
- Tipping is customary, with 10-15% expected at most restaurants
- Reservations are recommended for popular restaurants, especially during peak season
- Dress codes vary by restaurant, but smart casual is generally acceptable
- Eating with your hands is generally not acceptable, except for certain types of finger foods
- It is customary to greet the server with a friendly "bonjour" or "bonsoir"
