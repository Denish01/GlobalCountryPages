[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
The healthcare system in Saint Martin is a mixed system, combining both public and private services. The public healthcare system is funded by the French government, as Saint Martin is an overseas collectivity of France. The quality of care in Saint Martin is generally good, with well-equipped hospitals and clinics, especially in the capital city of Marigot. However, the healthcare system can be overwhelmed during peak tourist season, and some specialized services may not be available. In urban areas, access to healthcare is generally good, with several hospitals and clinics available, while in rural areas, access can be more limited.

The public healthcare system in Saint Martin provides a range of services, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and prescriptions, which are generally free or low-cost for residents. However, the public system can be slow, and wait times can be long, which is why many residents and visitors opt for private healthcare services. Private healthcare services in Saint Martin offer a range of specialized services, including cardiology, oncology, and orthopedics, and are generally of high quality.

The French government has invested heavily in the healthcare system in Saint Martin, and the island has a number of well-equipped hospitals and clinics. However, the healthcare system can still be affected by the island's remote location, and some specialized services may not be available. Despite these challenges, the healthcare system in Saint Martin is generally well-organized, and visitors and residents can expect to receive high-quality care.

- **Rabies** vaccination is recommended for travelers who will be spending time outdoors or around animals.
- **Insect-borne illnesses** such as dengue fever and Zika virus are present in Saint Martin, and travelers should take precautions to prevent mosquito bites.

- **Food safety** is also generally good, but travelers should still take precautions to avoid eating undercooked or raw foods.
