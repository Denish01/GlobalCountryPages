Nova Scotia offers a diverse range of activities and attractions, from exploring the stunning natural beauty of Cape Breton Highlands National Park to discovering the rich history and culture of Halifax, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all interests.

Unique Experiences

  • Take a kayaking tour through the Bioluminescent Bay in Martinique Beach, where you can paddle through glowing waters filled with bioluminescent organisms
  • Go whale watching in the Atlantic Ocean, where you can spot humpback, finback, and minke whales, as well as dolphins and seabirds
  • Explore the Oak Island Money Pit, a mysterious site filled with hidden treasures and unexplained phenomena
  • Visit a lobster fisherman and learn about the industry, then enjoy a fresh lobster dinner at a local restaurant
  • Take a Ceilidh dance lesson and learn traditional Scottish and Irish dances
  • Go tide walking in the Bay of Fundy, where you can explore the ocean floor at low tide and discover hidden coves and marine life
  • Visit a Maple syrup farm and learn about the process of making maple syrup, then sample some of the local products

Outdoor & Nature

  • Explore the Cape Breton Highlands National Park, which features hiking trails, camping sites, and stunning scenic drives
  • Visit Martinique Beach, a 5km stretch of sandy beach perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and beachcombing
  • Hike the Skyline Trail, a 6.5km loop that offers stunning views of the coastline and surrounding landscape
  • Go birdwatching in the Shubenacadie River area, where you can spot over 200 species of birds, including eagles, osprey, and herons
  • Visit Kejimkujik National Park, which features hiking trails, camping sites, and stunning lakes and waterfalls

Cultural Experiences

  • Visit the Maritime Museum of the Atlantic, which features exhibits on Nova Scotia's maritime history, including the Titanic and the Halifax Explosion
  • Explore the Canadian Museum of Immigration at Pier 21, which tells the story of immigration to Canada and the role of Pier 21 in the country's history
  • Attend a Ceilidh at a local community center, where you can experience traditional Scottish and Irish music and dance
  • Visit the Art Gallery of Nova Scotia, which features a collection of Nova Scotian and Canadian art, including works by Alex Colville and Maud Lewis
  • Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Nova Scotian dishes, such as fish and brewis and rappie pie

Hidden Gems

  • Visit the Annapolis Royal Historic Gardens, a beautiful garden featuring a variety of plants and flowers, as well as a historic chapel and cemetery
  • Explore the Ovens Natural Park, a unique park featuring stunning coastal scenery and a series of sea caves and rock formations
  • Go fossil hunting in the Joggins Fossil Cliffs, where you can discover fossils of ancient plants and animals
  • Visit the Ross Farm Museum, a living history farm that showcases what life was like on a Nova Scotian farm in the 19th century
  • Take a stargazing tour at the Kejimkujik National Park, where you can learn about the night sky and see the stars in a dark sky preserve

Day Trips

  • Take a day trip from Halifax to Peggys Cove (45km, 1 hour), where you can visit the iconic lighthouse and explore the surrounding coastline
  • Visit Lunenburg (100km, 2 hours from Halifax), a charming town with a UNESCO World Heritage Site waterfront and a variety of shops and restaurants
  • Explore Cape Breton (350km, 4 hours from Halifax), where you can visit the Cape Breton Highlands National Park, the Fortress of Louisbourg, and the Alexander Graham Bell Museum
  • Take a day trip from Digby to Brier Island (30km, 1 hour), where you can go whale watching, visit the Brier Island Lighthouse, and explore the island's scenic coastline
  • Visit Wolfville (100km, 2 hours from Halifax), a charming town with a rich history, a variety of shops and restaurants, and a beautiful waterfront

Planning Tips

Suggested itineraries: 3-day (highlights of Halifax and surrounding area), 7-day (explore the province, including Cape Breton and the Annapolis Valley), 14-day (deep exploration of the province, including national parks, museums, and cultural events). Key tips for booking and prioritizing: book accommodations and tours in advance, especially during peak season, and prioritize the activities and attractions that are most important to you. Consider purchasing a Nova Scotia Museum Pass, which grants access to many of the province's museums and historic sites.
