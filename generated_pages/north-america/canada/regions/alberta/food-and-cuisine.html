Food Culture Overview

Alberta's cuisine is defined by its rich ranching history, with beef being a staple in many dishes. The region's indigenous communities, such as the Cree and Blackfoot, have also had a significant influence on the local cuisine, with dishes like Bannock and Pemmican being staples. The province's location in western Canada also means that it has been shaped by European settlement, with many restaurants serving traditional dishes like Roast Beef and Mashed Potatoes. Regional variations also exist, with the city of Calgary being known for its cowboy culture and the city of Edmonton being known for its indigenous cuisine.

The province's fertile soil and vast prairies also make it an ideal place for farming, with many local ingredients like wheat, canola, and saskatoon berries being used in many dishes. The region's many lakes and rivers also provide an abundance of fish and game, which are often featured on menus throughout the province. Overall, Alberta's cuisine is a unique blend of traditional and modern dishes, with a focus on local ingredients and bold flavors.

In recent years, Alberta's food scene has also become more diverse, with many restaurants serving international cuisine like Korean BBQ and Indian curries. This diversity has helped to create a vibrant and exciting food culture, with many options available for visitors to try. Whether you're in the mood for traditional Alberta Beef or something more exotic, there's something for everyone in Alberta.

Dining Customs

  • Tipping: it is customary to tip 15% to 20% of the total bill at restaurants
  • Reservations: it is recommended to make reservations at popular restaurants, especially on weekends
  • Dress Code: the dress code varies depending on the restaurant, but it is generally casual in most places
  • Table Manners: it is customary to keep your hands visible on the table and to not leave the table until everyone is finished eating
  • Food Sharing: it is common to share food with others at the table, especially in indigenous communities
  • Trying New Foods: it is encouraged to try new foods and flavors, especially when visiting local restaurants and cafes
