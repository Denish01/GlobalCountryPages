[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Old Quebec** historic district is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring cobblestone streets, historic buildings, and fortifications that date back to the 17th and 18th centuries.
- The **Quebec Winter Carnival** is a popular winter festival that showcases the region's rich cultural heritage, featuring parades, snow sculptures, and traditional music and dance.
- The **Musée national des beaux-arts du Québec** is a premier art museum that features a diverse collection of Quebec and Canadian art, including works by **Jean-Paul Riopelle** and **Emily Carr**.
- The **Festival d'Été de Québec** is a major music festival that takes place every summer, featuring a wide range of musical genres and performances by local and international artists.
- The **Cirque du Soleil** headquarters and training facility, **Le Monde de Cirque**, is a unique cultural attraction that offers tours and workshops, providing a glimpse into the world of circus arts.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Celine Dion** is a world-renowned singer and songwriter from Quebec, known for her powerful voice and iconic songs like **My Heart Will Go On**.
- The **Quebec diaspora** has made significant contributions to the cultural and economic development of other countries, particularly in the United States, where many Quebecers have settled and established successful businesses and communities.
- **Dr. Wilder Penfield** was a prominent neurosurgeon from Quebec who made groundbreaking discoveries in the field of neuroscience, including the development of the **Montreal Procedure** for treating epilepsy.
- The **Quebec values** of tolerance, diversity, and social solidarity have had a profound impact on the development of Canadian society, shaping the country's approach to issues like immigration, healthcare, and education.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **hospitality** of the Quebec people is legendary, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome and friendly service they receive in the region's hotels, restaurants, and shops.
- The **cuisine** of Quebec is a unique blend of French, British, and indigenous influences, featuring popular dishes like **poutine**, **tourtière**, and **sugar pie**.
- The **natural scenery** of Quebec is breathtaking, with the **Laurentian Mountains**, **Montmorency Falls**, and **Saint Lawrence River** offering ample opportunities for outdoor recreation and exploration.
- The **winter sports** infrastructure in Quebec is world-class, with **Mont-Tremblant** and **Le Massif** offering excellent skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities.
- The **festivals and events** in Quebec are a major draw for visitors, with the **Quebec Winter Carnival**, **Festival d'Été de Québec**, and **Just for Laughs** comedy festival being among the most popular.
- The **historic architecture** of Quebec is a major attraction, with the **Chateau Frontenac**, **Notre-Dame de Quebec Basilica-Cathedral**, and **Place Royale** being among the most iconic and historic buildings in the region.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Reford Gardens** in Grand-Métis are a hidden gem, featuring over 3,000 plant species and a stunning collection of modern and contemporary art.
- The **Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie** national park is a lesser-known destination, offering breathtaking scenery, hiking trails, and opportunities for kayaking and canoeing.
- The **Musée de la Civilization** in Quebec City is a unique museum that explores the history and culture of Quebec, featuring interactive exhibits and a vast collection of artifacts and artworks.
- The **Charlevoix region** is a picturesque area located northeast of Quebec City, known for its rolling hills, scenic coastline, and vibrant arts community.
