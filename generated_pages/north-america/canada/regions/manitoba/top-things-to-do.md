# Top Things to Do in Manitoba — Attractions & Experiences

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **polar bear tour** in Churchill, one of the best places in the world to see these majestic creatures in their natural habitat
- Learn about the history and culture of the **Inuit people** at the Itsanitaq Museum in Churchill
- Go **dog sledding** or **snowshoeing** in the winter, or **hiking** or **kayaking** in the summer, in the beautiful wilderness areas of Manitoba
- Take a **train tour** from Winnipeg to Churchill, passing through the Canadian Shield and boreal forest
- Visit the **Royal Canadian Mint** in Winnipeg, where you can learn about the history of coin-making and even strike your own coin
- Attend the **Winnipeg Folk Festival**, a popular music festival that features a range of local and international artists
- Go on a **beluga whale tour** in Hudson Bay, where you can see these friendly creatures up close in their natural habitat

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Riding Mountain National Park**: a vast wilderness area with hiking trails, lakes, and wildlife viewing opportunities
- **Wapusk National Park**: a remote wilderness area known for its polar bears, beluga whales, and northern lights
- **Assiniboine Forest**: a large urban forest in Winnipeg, offering hiking trails, picnic areas, and opportunities to see local wildlife
- **Lake Winnipeg**: a large lake with many beaches, parks, and recreational areas, offering swimming, fishing, and boating opportunities
- **Grand Beach**: a popular beach destination on the shores of Lake Winnipeg, offering swimming, fishing, and boating opportunities
- **Whiteshell Provincial Park**: a scenic park with hiking trails, lakes, and opportunities to see local wildlife, including beavers, otters, and deer

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Canadian Museum for Human Rights**: the first museum in the world dedicated solely to the subject of human rights
- **The Royal Winnipeg Ballet**: one of Canada's premier ballet companies, offering a range of productions throughout the year
- **Manitoba Museum**: a museum showcasing the natural and human history of Manitoba, with exhibits on dinosaurs, fossils, and the history of the province
- **Winnipeg Art Gallery**: a contemporary art museum featuring a range of local and international artists
- **Lower Fort Garry**: a restored 19th-century fur trading post that showcases the history of the Hudson's Bay Company
- **The Forks**: a historic site located at the confluence of the Red and Assiniboine rivers, offering shopping, dining, and entertainment options

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **Hecla Island**: a scenic island in Lake Winnipeg, offering hiking trails, beaches, and opportunities to see local wildlife
- **Gimli**: a charming town on the shores of Lake Winnipeg, known for its Icelandic heritage and vibrant arts scene
- **The Exchange District**: a historic neighborhood in Winnipeg, featuring beautiful architecture, boutique shops, and restaurants
- **The Manitoba Children's Museum**: a interactive museum featuring a range of exhibits and activities for kids
- **The Prairie Theatre Exchange**: a professional theatre company offering a range of productions throughout the year

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- **Winnipeg to Lower Fort Garry**: a 30-minute drive, offering a glimpse into the history of the Hudson's Bay Company
- **Winnipeg to Grand Beach**: a 1-hour drive, offering a relaxing day at the beach
- **Winnipeg to Riding Mountain National Park**: a 3-hour drive, offering hiking trails, lakes, and wildlife viewing opportunities
- **Winnipeg to Gimli**: a 1-hour drive, offering a charming town with Icelandic heritage and a vibrant arts scene
- **Churchill to Wapusk National Park**: a 1-hour drive, offering a remote wilderness area with polar bears, beluga whales, and northern lights
