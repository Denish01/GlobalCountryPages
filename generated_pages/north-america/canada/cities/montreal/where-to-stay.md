# Where to Stay in Montreal — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Montreal, the best areas to stay are in the downtown core, Old Montreal, and surrounding neighborhoods, which offer a mix of history, culture, and entertainment options. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top 7 areas/neighborhoods:

**Downtown Montreal** (Montreal)
Best For: business / luxury
Vibe: Downtown Montreal is a bustling area with a mix of modern and historic buildings, offering easy access to many corporate offices, restaurants, and entertainment venues. The area is vibrant and lively, with a wide range of amenities and services available.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: convenient location, many amenities, public transportation
Cons: can be noisy, expensive parking

**Old Montreal** (Montreal)
Best For: luxury / couples
Vibe: Old Montreal is a historic area with cobblestone streets, old buildings, and a romantic atmosphere. The area is perfect for those who want to experience the city's history and culture.
Price Range: $120-$250/night
Pros: historic charm, many restaurants, scenic views
Cons: can be crowded, limited parking

**Plateau Mont-Royal** (Montreal)
Best For: backpacker / families
Vibe: Plateau Mont-Royal is a trendy neighborhood with a mix of boutiques, restaurants, and cafes. The area is known for its vibrant atmosphere and eclectic mix of residents.
Price Range: $80-$180/night
Pros: affordable, lively atmosphere, many amenities
Cons: can be noisy, limited parking

**Rosemont-Petite-Patrie** (Montreal)
Best For: families / budget
Vibe: Rosemont-Petite-Patrie is a family-friendly neighborhood with a mix of residential streets, parks, and commercial areas. The area is known for its community atmosphere and affordable prices.
Price Range: $60-$120/night
Pros: affordable, family-friendly, many parks
Cons: limited amenities, not central location

**Mile End** (Montreal)
Best For: backpacker / artists
Vibe: Mile End is a trendy neighborhood with a mix of boutiques, art galleries, and cafes. The area is known for its vibrant atmosphere and eclectic mix of residents.
Price Range: $70-$150/night
Pros: affordable, lively atmosphere, many amenities
Cons: can be noisy, limited parking

**Hochelaga-Maisonneuve** (Montreal)
Best For: budget / families
Vibe: Hochelaga-Maisonneuve is a working-class neighborhood with a mix of residential streets, parks, and commercial areas. The area is known for its community atmosphere and affordable prices.
Price Range: $50-$100/night
Pros: affordable, family-friendly, many parks
Cons: limited amenities, not central location

**Westmount** (Montreal)
Best For: luxury / families
Vibe: Westmount is an affluent neighborhood with a mix of residential streets, parks, and commercial areas. The area is known for its upscale atmosphere and beautiful homes.
Price Range: $100-$200/night
Pros: safe, family-friendly, many amenities
Cons: expensive, limited public transportation
