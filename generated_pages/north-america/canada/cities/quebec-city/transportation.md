# Getting Around Quebec City — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Quebec City, you can use a variety of transportation options, including buses, taxis, ride-hailing services, and rental cars, as well as explore the city on foot or by bike, thanks to its pedestrian-friendly streets and scenic bike paths.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Quebec City is easily accessible by air, with Jean Lesage International Airport (YQB) serving as the main gateway to the city. The airport is located about 11 kilometers west of the city center and offers direct flights to several major hubs in Canada, the United States, and Europe, including Montreal, Toronto, New York, and Paris. You can also enter the city by car or bus, with several major highways and intercity bus routes connecting Quebec City to other cities in Canada and the northeastern United States.

If you prefer to travel by land, you can take a bus or drive from nearby cities like Montreal or Ottawa, or from major cities in the northeastern United States like New York or Boston. The city is also accessible by train, with VIA Rail offering regular services from Montreal and other major cities in Canada. Additionally, several cruise lines offer itineraries that include Quebec City as a port of call, allowing you to arrive in the city by sea.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
If you need to travel to other cities in Canada, you can use Quebec City's domestic air network, which is extensive and offers flights to many major cities across the country. Air Canada, WestJet, and Porter Airlines are some of the main domestic airlines operating in Quebec City, offering flights to destinations like Montreal, Toronto, Vancouver, and Calgary. You can book your flights online or through a travel agent, and prices vary depending on the route, time of year, and how far in advance you book. On average, a domestic flight from Quebec City to Montreal or Toronto can cost between $200 and $500 CAD, while flights to western Canada can cost $500 to $1,000 CAD or more.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Quebec City has a moderate rail network, with VIA Rail offering regular services to and from the city. The train station is located in the Saint-Roch neighborhood, just a short walk from the city center. You can take the train to Montreal, Ottawa, and other major cities in Canada, with prices varying depending on the route and class of service. High-speed rail options are limited in Canada, but you can take the VIA Rail Corridor service, which offers frequent departures and comfortable seating. You can book your tickets online or at the train station, and prices range from $50 to $200 CAD for a one-way ticket, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus companies like Orléans Express and Greyhound Canada offer services to and from Quebec City, connecting the city to other major cities in Canada and the northeastern United States. The bus station is located in the Saint-Roch neighborhood, just a short walk from the city center. You can book your tickets online or at the bus station, and prices vary depending on the route and time of year. On average, a one-way ticket from Quebec City to Montreal or Toronto can cost between $20 and $50 CAD, while longer routes can cost $50 to $100 CAD or more.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Quebec City, you need a valid driver's license from your home country or province, and car insurance that covers you in Canada.
- Road conditions in Quebec City are generally good, with well-maintained highways and streets, but be prepared for winter driving conditions during the colder months.
- Fuel costs in Canada are relatively high, with gas prices averaging around $1.20 per liter.
- If you plan to rent a car, book in advance to ensure availability, and consider renting from a reputable company like Enterprise or Hertz.
- Be aware of parking restrictions and fees in the city center, and consider using public parking lots or street parking with meters.
- Be prepared for traffic congestion during rush hour, especially on highways and major streets.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- **Plan ahead** and book your flights, trains, or buses in advance to ensure availability and get the best prices.
- **Use public transportation** to get around the city, as it is affordable and convenient.
- **Download the Transit app** to plan your route and track your bus or train in real-time.
- **Be prepared for winter driving conditions** if you plan to rent a car or drive in the city during the colder months.
- **Use a taxi or ride-hailing service** at night or if you are traveling with a group, as it can be more convenient and affordable.
- **Avoid scams** by only using licensed taxis or reputable ride-hailing services, and always check the meter or estimated fare before you start your journey.
- **Use a credit card or debit card** to pay for your transportation costs, as many companies accept electronic payments.
- **Be mindful of your belongings** and keep an eye on your luggage, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.
