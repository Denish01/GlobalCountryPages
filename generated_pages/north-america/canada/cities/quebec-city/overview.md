[SECTION] What Is Quebec City? [/SECTION]
Quebec City is a city located in the province of Quebec, Canada, and is the capital of the province. It is situated on the Saint Lawrence River, approximately 250 kilometers northeast of Montreal. Quebec City is known for its historic district, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and features cobblestone streets, historic buildings, and fortifications. The city is also famous for its winter festival, the Carnaval de Québec, which attracts millions of visitors each year.

Quebec City is a predominantly French-speaking city, with over 90% of the population speaking French as their first language. The city has a strong sense of history and culture, with many museums, galleries, and historic sites to explore. Visitors can walk along the city walls, visit the famous Chateau Frontenac, and explore the narrow streets of the Petit Champlain district.

The city is also an important economic and educational center, with several major universities and research institutions, including the Université Laval and the Université du Québec. Quebec City is a popular tourist destination, with over 4 million visitors each year, and is known for its friendly and welcoming atmosphere.
