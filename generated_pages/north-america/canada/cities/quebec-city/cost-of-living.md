# Cost of Living in Quebec City - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Quebec City is generally considered to be moderate, with daily expenses ranging from $80-150 USD per day for a mid-range traveler.

Currency: CAD
Tipping Custom: expected (10-15% in restaurants and bars)

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Quebec City? [/SECTION]
Quebec City is a popular tourist destination in Canada, and its cost of living is relatively high compared to other cities in North America. However, it is still considered to be more affordable than many major cities in the United States and Europe. The city's unique blend of French and Canadian culture, rich history, and stunning architecture make it an attractive destination for visitors from around the world. Compared to other Canadian cities, Quebec City is generally more expensive than cities like Montreal, but less expensive than cities like Toronto or Vancouver.

In terms of global averages, Quebec City is considered to be a moderately expensive city, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation falling somewhere in between those of Western Europe and the United States. However, the city's high standard of living, excellent healthcare system, and strong economy make it an attractive destination for expats and digital nomads.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting during the shoulder season (April-May or September-October) to avoid peak tourist prices.
- Take advantage of Quebec City's many free or low-cost attractions, such as the historic district and the Saint Lawrence River.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transportation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or try some of Quebec's famous street food, such as poutine or smoked meat sandwiches.
- Look for discounts or package deals on accommodations and attractions to save even more.
- Learn some basic French phrases to help you navigate the city and interact with locals, who may offer you a warmer welcome if you make an effort to speak their language.
