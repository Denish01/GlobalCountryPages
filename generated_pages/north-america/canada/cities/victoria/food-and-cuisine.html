Victoria, the capital of British Columbia, Canada, is a city that offers a diverse range of culinary delights, with popular dishes including seafood, especially salmon and halibut, as well as nanaimo bars and butter tarts.

Quick Facts

Staple Foodsseafood, particularly salmon and halibut, as well as locally-grown produce like berries and apples
National DishPoutine, a dish of French-Canadian origin consisting of French fries topped with cheese curds and gravy
Meal Timestypical breakfast times are between 7-10 am, lunch from 12-2 pm, and dinner from 5-8 pm
Tipping at Restaurantsa standard tip is 15% to 20% of the total bill before tax
Dietary NotesVictoria is relatively vegetarian-friendly, with many restaurants offering vegan and gluten-free options

Food Culture Overview

Victoria's cuisine is defined by its proximity to the ocean and its cultural influences from British, French, and Asian traditions. The city's food scene is characterized by an emphasis on local and sustainable ingredients, with many restaurants proudly showcasing their connections to local farmers and food producers. The city's cultural influences are also reflected in its many ethnic neighborhoods, such as Chinatown and Little Italy, which offer a wide range of international cuisine. Victoria is also known for its food trucks, which offer a variety of street food options, from tacos to barbecue.

Victoria's cuisine is also influenced by its indigenous heritage, with many restaurants incorporating traditional ingredients and cooking methods into their menus. The city is also home to a number of farmers' markets, which offer a wide range of local produce, meat, and dairy products. In addition, Victoria is known for its craft beer and wine scene, with many local breweries and wineries offering tastings and tours.

The city's food culture is also shaped by its seasonal produce, with many restaurants offering seasonal menus that showcase the freshest ingredients available. This emphasis on local and seasonal ingredients has helped to establish Victoria as a foodie destination, with many visitors coming to the city specifically to experience its unique culinary scene.

Drinks

  • Victoria Coffee: a popular coffee shop that serves a variety of coffee drinks, including lattes and cappuccinos, typically priced around $2-$5 CAD
  • BC Wine: a variety of wines produced in British Columbia, including Chardonnay and Merlot, typically priced around $10-$30 CAD per bottle
  • Craft Beer: a variety of beers produced by local breweries, including IPA and Pale Ale, typically priced around $5-$10 CAD per pint
  • Fresh Juice: a popular drink made from local and seasonal ingredients, typically priced around $5-$10 CAD
  • Tea: a popular drink that can be found at many cafes and restaurants, including Earl Grey and English Breakfast, typically priced around $2-$5 CAD

Dining Customs

  • Tipping: a standard tip is 15% to 20% of the total bill before tax
  • Reservations: reservations are recommended for popular restaurants, especially on weekends and holidays
  • Dress Code: dress codes vary by restaurant, but casual attire is generally acceptable
  • Eating Etiquette: it is customary to wait for the host to indicate where to sit, and to keep your hands visible on the table
  • Table Manners: it is customary to keep your elbows off the table and to not speak with your mouth full

Where to Eat

  • The Inner Harbour: a popular area with many restaurants and cafes, including The Fish Store and The Salmon House
  • Chinatown: a vibrant neighborhood with many Asian restaurants and shops, including Japadog and The Chinese Garden
  • Little Italy: a charming neighborhood with many Italian restaurants and cafes, including La Bella Vita and The Italian Kitchen
  • The Downtown Area: a bustling area with many restaurants and cafes, including The Victoria Coffee and The Fresh Juice Bar
  • The Waterfront: a scenic area with many restaurants and cafes, including The Seafood Restaurant and The Beachside Grill

Food Markets & Food Experiences

  • The Victoria Public Market: a popular market that features local produce, meat, and dairy products, as well as prepared foods and drinks
  • The Victoria Farmers' Market: a weekly market that features local produce, meat, and dairy products, as well as prepared foods and drinks
  • The Butchart Gardens: a popular tourist attraction that features beautiful gardens and a variety of food and drink options, including The Dining Room and The Tea Room
  • The Victoria Food Tours: a guided tour that takes you through the city's food scene, including restaurants, cafes, and markets
  • The Cook Street Village: a charming neighborhood with many restaurants, cafes, and shops, including The Cook Street Village Market and The Cook Street Village Bakery
