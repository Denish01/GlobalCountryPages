[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Calgary is a vibrant and welcoming city, with a strong sense of community and a low crime rate. Visitors can feel comfortable exploring the city's many attractions, from the famous Calgary Stampede to the beautiful Prince's Island Park. However, as with any city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Calgary is also prone to extreme weather conditions, such as thunderstorms and blizzards, which can pose a risk to travelers.

Overall, Calgary is a great destination for travelers, with a wide range of activities and attractions to suit all interests. With some basic precautions and an awareness of the local conditions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Calgary. It's worth noting that Calgary is a large city, and as such, it's not immune to crime. However, the city has a strong police presence, and most crimes are minor and opportunistic, such as purse snatching or vandalism.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Panhandling**: Aggressive panhandling is a common issue in some areas of Calgary, particularly in the downtown core.
- **Purse snatching**: Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and keep a close eye on their belongings, particularly in crowded areas.
- **Scams targeting tourists**: Scammers may target tourists with fake petitions or charity solicitations, so visitors should be cautious when approached by strangers.
- **Theft from vehicles**: Visitors should always lock their vehicles and keep valuables out of sight, as theft from vehicles is a common crime in Calgary.
- **ATM scams**: Visitors should be cautious when using ATMs, as scammers may use card skimmers or other devices to steal financial information.
- **Weather-related risks**: Calgary is prone to extreme weather conditions, such as thunderstorms and blizzards, which can pose a risk to travelers.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Calgary, but visitors should ensure they are up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is safe to drink in Calgary, but visitors may prefer to use bottled water.
- **Medical Facilities**: Calgary has a high-quality healthcare system, with many excellent hospitals and medical facilities.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacies are readily available in Calgary, with many locations throughout the city.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- **Be aware of your surroundings**: Visitors should always be mindful of their surroundings, particularly in crowded areas or at night.
- **Keep valuables secure**: Visitors should keep their valuables, such as wallets and phones, secure and out of sight.
- **Use reputable transportation**: Visitors should use reputable transportation services, such as taxis or ride-sharing services, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- **Stay informed**: Visitors should stay informed about local conditions, such as weather forecasts and crime alerts.
- **Follow local advice**: Visitors should follow local advice and instructions, particularly in emergency situations.
