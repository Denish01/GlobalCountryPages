[SECTION] What Is Toronto? [/SECTION]
Toronto is a city located in the province of Ontario, Canada, situated on the northwestern shore of Lake Ontario. As the largest city in Canada, Toronto is a major cultural and economic hub, known for its diverse population, vibrant arts and culture scene, and iconic landmarks such as the CN Tower and Toronto Islands. Toronto is also an important center for business, finance, and technology, with many major Canadian and international companies having operations in the city.

Geographically, Toronto is situated in the Greater Toronto Area (GTA), which includes the surrounding cities and municipalities. The city has a total area of approximately 630 square kilometers and is home to over 2.7 million people, making it one of the most populous cities in North America. Toronto is also a major transportation hub, with two international airports, a comprehensive public transit system, and a network of highways and roads connecting it to other cities in Canada and the United States.

Toronto is known for its diversity, with over 50% of the population born outside of Canada. The city is home to many different ethnic and cultural groups, and this diversity is reflected in its cuisine, arts, and cultural events. Toronto is also a popular tourist destination, with many attractions such as the Royal Ontario Museum, the Art Gallery of Ontario, and the Toronto Zoo.
