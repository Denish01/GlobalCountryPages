# Cost of Living in Vancouver - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Vancouver is relatively high, with a daily budget of $120-200 USD per day for a mid-range traveler, making it one of the more expensive cities in North America.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Expensive
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $80-120 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $120-200 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $250-350 USD
Currency: CAD
Tipping Custom: expected (15% in restaurants and bars)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Vancouver? [/SECTION]
Vancouver is considered one of the most expensive cities in Canada, with a high cost of living compared to other major cities in North America. The city's proximity to the ocean, mountains, and forests makes it a desirable place to live, which drives up the cost of housing and other living expenses. Compared to other global cities, Vancouver is more expensive than many cities in Europe and Asia, but less expensive than cities like New York or San Francisco.

The high cost of living in Vancouver is largely due to the city's strong economy, which is driven by industries such as technology, film production, and tourism. The city's mild climate and natural beauty also make it a popular destination for tourists and expats, which can drive up prices for accommodations and other services. Overall, Vancouver is a great place to live or visit, but it requires a significant budget to enjoy all that the city has to offer.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider sharing an apartment or house with roommates to reduce rent costs
- Take advantage of Vancouver's public transportation system, which is efficient and affordable
- Eat at local eateries or food trucks, which offer delicious and affordable meals
- Visit local markets, such as Granville Island, to buy fresh produce and seafood at lower prices
- Explore the city's many parks and outdoor spaces, which are free or low-cost
- Look for discounts and promotions at local attractions and museums to save on entertainment costs

Overall, Vancouver is a great city to visit or live in, but it requires a significant budget to enjoy all that the city has to offer. By being mindful of expenses and taking advantage of money-saving tips, visitors and residents can make the most of their time in Vancouver.
