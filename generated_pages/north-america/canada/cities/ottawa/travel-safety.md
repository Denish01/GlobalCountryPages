Ottawa is generally a very safe city to visit, with low crime rates and a strong emphasis on community policing, making it an ideal destination for tourists and travelers.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Ottawa is a vibrant and welcoming city, with a strong sense of community and a low crime rate. Visitors can feel safe walking around the city, even at night, and the city's many parks and green spaces are well-lit and well-patrolled. However, as with any city, there are some areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Ottawa is also a very accessible city, with a wide range of amenities and services available to visitors, including a comprehensive public transportation system and a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets.

Overall, Ottawa is a great destination for visitors of all ages and backgrounds, with a wide range of cultural attractions, historic landmarks, and outdoor activities to enjoy. The city is also very family-friendly, with many parks, museums, and other attractions that are specifically designed for children. With its low crime rate and strong sense of community, Ottawa is an ideal destination for solo travelers and women, who can feel safe and supported throughout their visit. The city's many neighborhoods, such as the Byward Market and Elgin Street, offer a wide range of shopping, dining, and entertainment options, and the city's vibrant arts and cultural scene is reflected in its many galleries, museums, and performance venues.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas, such as the Byward Market and Elgin Street
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake charity collectors or street performers
- Bike theft, particularly in areas with high foot traffic
- Overcharging by taxi drivers or restaurants
- Slippery sidewalks and roads during winter months
- Aggressive panhandling in some areas, such as downtown Ottawa

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Ottawa, but visitors should ensure they are up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Ottawa, but bottled water is also widely available
- **Medical Facilities:** Ottawa has a number of high-quality hospitals and medical facilities, including the Ottawa Hospital and the Children's Hospital of Eastern Ontario
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available throughout the city, and many are open 24 hours

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night
- Keep valuables secure and out of sight, and consider using a money belt or secure bag
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas, and stick to main roads and pedestrian paths
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel's safe if available
- Be cautious of aggressive panhandlers or scammers, and avoid engaging with them
- Follow local advice and instructions during winter months, and take necessary precautions to stay safe on snowy and icy roads and sidewalks
- Consider purchasing travel insurance to cover unexpected medical or travel-related expenses

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Ottawa is generally a very safe city for solo travelers and women, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community
- Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, such as the Byward Market or Elgin Street, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure, and consider using a money belt or secure bag
- Take advantage of Ottawa's many amenities and services, including public transportation and taxis, and consider using a reputable taxi company or ride-sharing service
