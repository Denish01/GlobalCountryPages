# Getting Around Edmonton — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Edmonton, you can use a variety of transportation options including buses, trains, taxis, ride-hailing services, and car rentals, which provide convenient and efficient ways to explore the city and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Edmonton is easily accessible by air, with two main airports serving the city: Edmonton International Airport (YEG) and the smaller Villeneuve Airport. Edmonton International Airport is the primary airport and offers direct flights to major Canadian cities, as well as international destinations in the United States, Europe, and Asia. The airport is located about 30 kilometers south of downtown Edmonton and is connected to the city center by highway and public transportation. Direct flight hubs include major Canadian cities such as Toronto, Vancouver, and Calgary, as well as US cities like Seattle and Minneapolis. Overland entry points include highways and intercity bus routes from neighboring cities and provinces.

For those traveling by land, Edmonton is situated at the intersection of several major highways, including the Trans-Canada Highway and Highway 2, which connect the city to other parts of Alberta and Canada. Intercity bus services also operate from Edmonton to other major cities in Canada, providing an affordable and convenient option for travelers. Additionally, Edmonton is a key stop on the VIA Rail network, with trains connecting the city to other major Canadian cities like Vancouver and Toronto.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights are a convenient way to get to Edmonton from other parts of Canada, with several airlines operating out of Edmonton International Airport. Airlines such as Air Canada, WestJet, and Flair Air offer multiple daily flights to major Canadian cities, including Toronto, Vancouver, and Calgary. Booking tips include comparing prices across different airlines and booking in advance to secure the best rates. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the route and time of year, but expect to pay around $200-$500 for a one-way ticket from a major Canadian city.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Edmonton has a moderate rail network, with VIA Rail operating trains to and from the city. The main rail route connects Edmonton to Vancouver and Toronto, with multiple departures per week. While there are no high-speed rail options in Edmonton, the trains offer a scenic and comfortable way to see the Canadian countryside. Booking can be done online or through the VIA Rail app, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service. Expect to pay around $100-$300 for a one-way ticket from a nearby city.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus companies such as Greyhound Canada and Red Arrow operate from Edmonton to other major cities in Canada, offering an affordable and convenient option for travelers. The buses are generally comfortable and well-maintained, with onboard amenities like Wi-Fi and restrooms. Routes and costs vary depending on the destination and time of year, but expect to pay around $20-$100 for a one-way ticket from a nearby city.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Edmonton, you will need a valid driver's license from your home country or a Canadian driver's license.
- Road conditions in Edmonton are generally good, with well-maintained highways and city streets.
- Fuel costs in Edmonton are around $1.20 per liter for gasoline, and $1.30 per liter for diesel.
- Car rental companies like Enterprise, Hertz, and Avis operate in Edmonton, with a wide range of vehicles available.
- Hazards to watch out for while driving in Edmonton include winter weather conditions like snow and ice, as well as construction and road closures.
- Parking in Edmonton can be challenging, especially in the downtown area, so be sure to plan ahead and research parking options before arriving.

- Be mindful of **winter weather** conditions, which can affect road and pedestrian travel.

- Keep an eye on your **belongings**, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.
- Respect **local traffic laws** and regulations, such as wearing a seatbelt and following speed limits.
