# Where to Stay in Edmonton — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Edmonton, the best areas to stay are in the downtown core, Old Strathcona, and surrounding neighborhoods, which offer a mix of entertainment, culture, and convenience. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas/neighborhoods in Edmonton, consider the following:
**Downtown Edmonton** (Edmonton)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: Downtown Edmonton is the central business district, with a vibrant atmosphere and easy access to amenities, including the Shaw Conference Centre and Sir Winston Churchill Square. The area is also home to many restaurants, bars, and entertainment venues.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Convenient location, access to public transportation, variety of dining and entertainment options
Cons: Can be noisy, limited parking options

**Old Strathcona** (Edmonton)
Best For: Backpacker / Family
Vibe: Old Strathcona is a historic neighborhood with a bohemian vibe, known for its eclectic shops, restaurants, and entertainment venues, including the famous Whyte Avenue. The area is also home to the Strathcona Farmers' Market and the Old Strathcona Antique Mall.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: Unique character, access to public transportation, variety of dining and shopping options
Cons: Can be crowded, limited parking options

**West Edmonton** (Edmonton)
Best For: Family / Luxury
Vibe: West Edmonton is a suburban area with a mix of residential and commercial developments, including the famous West Edmonton Mall. The area is also home to several parks and recreational facilities, including the West Edmonton Mall Ice Palace and the World Waterpark.
Price Range: $120-250/night
Pros: Convenient location, access to amenities, variety of dining and entertainment options
Cons: Can be busy, limited public transportation options

**River Valley** (Edmonton)
Best For: Nature Lover / Family
Vibe: The River Valley is a large park system that runs along the North Saskatchewan River, offering scenic views, hiking trails, and recreational facilities. The area is also home to several parks, including the Hawrelak Park and the Emily Murphy Park.
Price Range: $80-180/night
Pros: Scenic views, access to recreational facilities, peaceful atmosphere
Cons: Limited dining and entertainment options, can be isolated

**University Area** (Edmonton)
Best For: Student / Backpacker
Vibe: The University Area is a vibrant neighborhood surrounding the University of Alberta, with a mix of residential and commercial developments. The area is also home to several restaurants, bars, and entertainment venues, including the famous Garneau Theatre.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Convenient location, access to public transportation, variety of dining and entertainment options
Cons: Can be noisy, limited parking options
