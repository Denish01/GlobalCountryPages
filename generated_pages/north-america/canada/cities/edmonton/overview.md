[SECTION] What Is Edmonton? [/SECTION]
Edmonton is a city in the province of Alberta, Canada, and it is known for its vibrant arts and culture scene, as well as its stunning natural beauty. The city is situated in the central region of Alberta, at the intersection of the North Saskatchewan River and the Trans-Canada Highway. Edmonton is not an independent country, but rather a city within the country of Canada, and as such, it does not have its own sovereignty. The city has a diverse economy, with major industries in energy, technology, and healthcare.

Edmonton is also home to a number of festivals and events throughout the year, including the Edmonton Folk Music Festival and the Fringe Theatre Festival. The city has a strong sense of community, with a variety of neighborhoods and districts, each with its own unique character. From the historic Old Strathcona neighborhood to the trendy Ice District, Edmonton has something to offer for everyone. The city's geographic position, located near the center of the Alberta province, makes it an important hub for trade and commerce in western Canada.

In terms of its natural environment, Edmonton is surrounded by the beauty of the Canadian wilderness. The city has a number of parks and green spaces, including the North Saskatchewan River Valley, which runs through the heart of the city. The river valley is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, coyotes, and birds, and offers stunning views and hiking trails. Edmonton's location in the heart of the Canadian prairies also makes it an ideal base for exploring the surrounding countryside, including the nearby Rocky Mountains.
