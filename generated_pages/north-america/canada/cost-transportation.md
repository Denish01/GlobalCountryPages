[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Canada has a well-developed transportation network, with a variety of options available for getting around, including public transit, taxis, ride-hailing services, and personal vehicles. The quality of public transit varies by city, but most major cities have a comprehensive network of buses, subways, and commuter trains. In general, most people in Canada get around using their personal vehicles, but public transit is a popular option in urban areas.

The country's vast geography and dispersed population mean that transportation costs can be high, especially for those living in rural areas. However, many cities have invested in public transit infrastructure, making it a convenient and affordable option for residents and visitors. Additionally, the availability of ride-hailing services has increased in recent years, providing another option for getting around.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider purchasing a **Presto card** or other transit card for convenient travel on public transit systems.
- Use ride-hailing apps like **Uber** or **Lyft** for affordable and convenient transportation.
- Be aware of **traffic laws** and regulations, especially when driving in urban areas.
- Take advantage of **car-sharing services** or **bike-sharing programs** for a more affordable and environmentally friendly option.
- Always **negotiate the fare** before getting into a taxi, especially for longer trips.
- Use **public transit apps** like Transit or Moovit to plan your route and track your journey.
