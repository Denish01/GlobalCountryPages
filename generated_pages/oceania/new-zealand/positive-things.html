Cultural Treasures

  • The Whanganui National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its unique cultural and natural heritage.
  • The Kapa Haka is a traditional Maori performance art form, which combines singing, dancing, and storytelling.
  • The Ta Moko is a traditional Maori tattoo art form, which is highly regarded for its beauty and cultural significance.
  • The Auckland Art Gallery Toi o Tamaki is one of the largest and most respected art galleries in New Zealand, featuring a wide range of New Zealand and international art.
  • The Te Papa Museum is a world-class museum that showcases New Zealand's natural history, culture, and art.

People & Global Impact

  • Ernest Rutherford was a New Zealand-born physicist who made significant contributions to the field of nuclear physics and was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1908.
  • Jacinda Ardern is a notable New Zealand politician who has made a significant impact on the global stage, particularly in the areas of climate change and social justice.
  • The New Zealand diaspora has made significant contributions to the global community, with many New Zealanders working in fields such as medicine, education, and the arts.
  • Sir Edmund Hillary was a New Zealand mountaineer and explorer who was the first person to reach the summit of Mount Everest, and who also made significant contributions to the development of Nepal.

What Visitors Love

  • New Zealand's natural beauty is a major draw for visitors, with its stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and unique geological features.
  • The hospitality of the New Zealand people is widely praised, with visitors often commenting on the warm and welcoming nature of the locals.
  • Adventure activities such as bungee jumping, skydiving, and hiking are extremely popular in New Zealand, with the country offering a wide range of options for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • The food and wine in New Zealand is highly regarded, with the country producing a wide range of high-quality produce, including wine, seafood, and dairy products.
  • Maori cultural experiences are a highlight for many visitors, with opportunities to learn about Maori history, language, and customs.
  • The scenic drives in New Zealand are some of the most beautiful in the world, with routes such as the Milford Road and the Pacific Coast Highway offering stunning views and unique landscapes.

Hidden Gems

  • The Glowworm Caves in Waitomo are a unique and fascinating attraction, featuring thousands of glowworms that light up the caves like stars in the night sky.
  • The Hobbiton Movie Set is a must-visit for fans of the Lord of the Rings and Hobbit movies, with a detailed and immersive recreation of the Shire.
  • The Catlins Conservation Park is a lesser-known but highly scenic area, featuring stunning coastal landscapes, unique wildlife, and a range of outdoor activities.
  • The Waiheke Island is a hidden gem located just off the coast of Auckland, featuring beautiful beaches, vineyards, and a range of artistic and cultural attractions.
