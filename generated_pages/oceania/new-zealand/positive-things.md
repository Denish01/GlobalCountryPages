[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Whanganui National Park** is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its unique cultural and natural heritage.
- The **Kapa Haka** is a traditional Maori performance art form, which combines singing, dancing, and storytelling.
- The **Ta Moko** is a traditional Maori tattoo art form, which is highly regarded for its beauty and cultural significance.
- The **Auckland Art Gallery Toi o Tamaki** is one of the largest and most respected art galleries in New Zealand, featuring a wide range of New Zealand and international art.
- The **Te Papa Museum** is a world-class museum that showcases New Zealand's natural history, culture, and art.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Ernest Rutherford** was a New Zealand-born physicist who made significant contributions to the field of nuclear physics and was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1908.
- **Jacinda Ardern** is a notable New Zealand politician who has made a significant impact on the global stage, particularly in the areas of climate change and social justice.
- **The New Zealand diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global community, with many New Zealanders working in fields such as medicine, education, and the arts.
- **Sir Edmund Hillary** was a New Zealand mountaineer and explorer who was the first person to reach the summit of Mount Everest, and who also made significant contributions to the development of Nepal.
