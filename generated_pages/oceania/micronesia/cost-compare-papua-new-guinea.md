[SECTION] Overview: Micronesia vs Papua New Guinea [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Micronesia and Papua New Guinea varies significantly, despite both countries being located in the Pacific Islands. Micronesia, with its smaller population and more limited economy, tends to have lower prices for many everyday items, including housing, food, and transportation. In contrast, Papua New Guinea has a larger and more diverse economy, with a higher cost of living to match. However, the overall cost of living in Micronesia is still relatively high compared to other countries in the region, due to its reliance on imports and limited domestic production.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Micronesia has a relatively stable economy, with a strong focus on tourism and fishing, while Papua New Guinea has a more volatile economy, with a significant dependence on mining and agriculture. This volatility can lead to fluctuations in the cost of living, particularly in Papua New Guinea. Overall, Micronesia is generally cheaper than Papua New Guinea, with an overall cost of living that is around 15-20% lower. This makes Micronesia a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a affordable lifestyle in the Pacific Islands.
