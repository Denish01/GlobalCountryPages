# Education Costs in Palau

The cost of education in Palau varies depending on the type of institution and level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools charge tuition fees ranging from a few thousand to over $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Palau is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being generally considered to provide a good quality education, although some schools may lack resources and infrastructure. The language of instruction in public schools is primarily **Palauan**, while **English** is also widely used, especially in private and international schools. Compulsory education in Palau lasts for 12 years, from elementary to high school, and is free for all citizens. International schools are available in Palau, offering curricula such as the **International Baccalaureate** and **US-based** programs, catering to the needs of expatriate families and those seeking a more global education.

The quality of education in Palau is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on **mathematics**, **science**, and **language arts**. However, some schools may struggle with limited resources and outdated teaching methods. Private schools, on the other hand, often have more modern facilities and teaching methods, but can be expensive. International schools in Palau offer a more diverse and global education, with a focus on **critical thinking**, **problem-solving**, and **cultural awareness**. These schools often have a more diverse student body and faculty, providing a unique learning environment for students.
