Education System Overview

The education system in Palau is divided into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education, which is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 14, is provided by public and private schools, with a focus on basic subjects such as mathematics, science, and language. Secondary education, which is also compulsory, is provided by public and private high schools, with a focus on more advanced subjects such as social sciences, humanities, and vocational training. Tertiary education is provided by Palau Community College, which offers associate's and bachelor's degree programs in a range of fields, including business, education, and health sciences.

The quality of education in Palau is generally considered to be good, with a strong focus on preserving the country's unique culture and language. However, there are some challenges facing the education system, including a lack of resources and limited access to higher education opportunities. Public schools are generally well-funded and well-equipped, while private schools may offer more specialized programs and better facilities. Despite these challenges, the education system in Palau has made significant progress in recent years, with a growing number of students pursuing higher education and a increasing number of qualified teachers.

The education system in Palau is also influenced by the country's cultural and historical context. For example, the Palauan language is an official language and is taught in all schools, and students are also required to study Pacific Island history and culture. This emphasis on cultural preservation and awareness is an important part of the education system in Palau, and helps to promote a sense of national identity and pride.

International Schools

  • There are a limited number of international schools in Palau, with most schools following the American curriculum or the International Baccalaureate (IB) curriculum.
  • The Palau International School is a private school that offers an American-style education to students from preschool to grade 12.
  • The Koror International School is another private school that offers an IB curriculum to students from preschool to grade 12.
  • International schools in Palau are generally expensive, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $10,000 per year.
  • Most international schools in Palau are located in the capital city of Ngerulmud or in the largest town of Koror.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Palau as a foreigner, students must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of admission to a Palauan institution, proof of financial support, and proof of health insurance.
  • English language proficiency is required for most programs, and students may need to take an English language proficiency test such as the TOEFL or IELTS.
  • There are limited scholarship opportunities available for international students, but some institutions may offer tuition waivers or discounts.
  • Living costs in Palau are relatively high, with the cost of accommodation, food, and transportation ranging from $1,000 to $2,000 per month.
  • Foreign students may need to undergo a medical examination and obtain a health certificate before being allowed to study in Palau.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • One of the main challenges facing the education system in Palau is a lack of resources, including limited access to technology and educational materials.
  • The education system in Palau also faces challenges related to teacher training and retention, with many teachers leaving the profession due to low pay and limited career opportunities.
  • Despite these challenges, the education system in Palau has made significant progress in recent years, with a growing number of students pursuing higher education and a increasing number of qualified teachers.
  • The Palauan government has implemented several initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curriculum standards and the provision of professional development opportunities for teachers.
  • The education system in Palau also faces challenges related to cultural preservation and awareness, with a need to balance the preservation of traditional knowledge and practices with the need to provide students with a modern and relevant education.
