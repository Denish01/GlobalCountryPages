# Where to Stay in Palau — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Palau, consider the capital city of Ngerulmud or the more tourist-friendly area of Koror, which offer a range of accommodations catering to different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a detailed breakdown:
**Koror** (Koror)
Best For: luxury / family / business
Vibe: Koror is the commercial center of Palau and offers a lively atmosphere with numerous restaurants, shops, and entertainment options. The area is also close to several beaches and dive sites, making it an ideal location for water sports enthusiasts.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: easy access to amenities, proximity to beaches and dive sites, variety of accommodation options
Cons: can be crowded and noisy, limited budget options

**Ngerulmud** (Ngerulmud)
Best For: backpacker / business
Vibe: Ngerulmud is the capital city of Palau and has a more subdued atmosphere, with a focus on government buildings and administrative centers. The area is relatively quiet and offers a glimpse into the local culture.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: affordable accommodation options, opportunity to experience local culture, proximity to government offices
Cons: limited amenities and entertainment options

**Airai** (Airai)
Best For: family / luxury
Vibe: Airai is a scenic area located near the airport and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The area is relatively peaceful and ideal for families or those seeking a relaxing getaway.
Price Range: $250-600/night
Pros: beautiful scenery, proximity to the airport, range of accommodation options
Cons: limited amenities and entertainment options

**Melekeok** (Melekeok)
Best For: luxury / business
Vibe: Melekeok is a coastal area that offers a mix of modern and traditional architecture. The area is known for its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters, making it an ideal location for water sports enthusiasts.
Price Range: $300-700/night
Pros: beautiful beaches, modern amenities, proximity to dive sites
Cons: can be expensive, limited budget options

**Ngarchelong** (Ngarchelong)
Best For: backpacker / family
Vibe: Ngarchelong is a rural area located in the north of Palau and offers a glimpse into traditional Palauan culture. The area is relatively peaceful and ideal for those seeking a relaxing getaway.
Price Range: $80-250/night
Pros: affordable accommodation options, opportunity to experience local culture, stunning scenery
Cons: limited amenities and entertainment options, remote location

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (June to August)
- Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com or Expedia to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices for long-term stays or off-season bookings
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during peak season
- Read reviews and check the location of the accommodation before booking
- Look for packages that include activities or amenities, such as diving or snorkeling trips
- Be sure to check the cancellation policies and fees before booking
