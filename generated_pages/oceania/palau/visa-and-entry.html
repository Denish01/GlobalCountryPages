Visa Policy Overview

The visa policy of Palau is considered to be relatively liberal, with citizens from many countries eligible for visa-free entry or able to obtain a visa on arrival. Palau aims to promote tourism and facilitate travel to the country, while also ensuring the security and integrity of its borders. Citizens from over 30 countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, and many European nations, can enter Palau without a visa and stay for up to 30 days.

Palau's visa policy is designed to balance the need to facilitate travel and tourism with the need to maintain national security and protect the country's unique environment and culture. The government has implemented measures to ensure that visitors respect the country's laws and regulations, and to prevent the spread of diseases and other threats. Overall, Palau's visa policy is intended to promote responsible and sustainable tourism, while also supporting the country's economic development.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists can enter Palau without a visa and stay for up to 30 days. They must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay. Tourists can extend their stay for an additional 30 days, but must apply for an extension at the Bureau of Immigration in Koror.

Business Travelers: Business travelers may require a visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They must have a valid passport, a letter of invitation from a Palauan company, and proof of sufficient funds. Business travelers may be required to register with the Palauan government and obtain a business license.

Students: Students who wish to study in Palau must obtain a student visa. They must have a valid passport, proof of enrollment in a Palauan educational institution, and sufficient funds to support their stay. Students may be required to undergo a medical examination and provide proof of health insurance.

Workers: Workers who wish to work in Palau must obtain a work visa. They must have a valid passport, a job offer from a Palauan employer, and proof of qualifications and experience. Workers may be required to undergo a medical examination and provide proof of health insurance.

Entry Points & Procedures

The main entry point to Palau is the Roman Tmetuchl International Airport, which is located on the island of Babeldaob. Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their passport, visa (if required), and other documents. Visitors may be asked to provide information about their stay, including their accommodation and itinerary. The immigration officer will then stamp the visitor's passport and issue a visa (if required). Visitors must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country, including gifts and souvenirs.

Palau also has a number of border crossing points with neighboring countries, including Micronesia and the Philippines. However, these border crossings are limited, and visitors are advised to use the Roman Tmetuchl International Airport as their main entry point. Visitors who arrive by sea must also comply with Palau's customs and immigration regulations, and may be required to present their documents and undergo an inspection.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Failing to present a valid passport with at least six months' validity
  • Not having proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
  • Not declaring goods brought into the country, including gifts and souvenirs
  • Not registering with the Palauan government (if required)
  • Not obtaining the necessary visas or permits
  • Not respecting Palau's laws and regulations, including those related to the environment and culture
