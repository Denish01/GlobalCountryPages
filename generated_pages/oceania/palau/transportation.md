# Getting Around Palau — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Palau, you can use a combination of domestic flights, taxis, car rentals, and limited public transportation options, as the country has a relatively underdeveloped transportation infrastructure.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
The main gateway to Palau is the Roman Tmetuchl International Airport, which is located near the former capital, Koror. The airport receives direct flights from major hubs in Asia, such as Tokyo, Seoul, and Manila, as well as from neighboring Pacific island nations like Guam and the Marshall Islands. There are no overland entry points to Palau, as it is an island nation with no land borders. To get to Palau, you will need to fly into the international airport, which is served by several airlines, including Asiana Airlines, Japan Airlines, and China Airlines.

Travelers can also connect to Palau through major airports in the region, such as Guam International Airport or Manila Ninoy Aquino International Airport, which offer more frequent flights to Palau. It is recommended to book flights in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, to ensure availability and to get the best rates. Additionally, visitors should research any necessary visas or travel documents required for entry into Palau, as regulations can change frequently.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Palau has a limited domestic air network, with flights connecting the capital, Ngerulmud, to other parts of the country, including the Rock Islands and the outer islands. The main domestic airline is Palau Pacific Airways, which operates small propeller planes to various destinations within the country. Booking domestic flights can be done through the airline's website or through local travel agencies. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and availability. It is recommended to book domestic flights in advance, as schedules can be limited and subject to change.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Palau has no rail network, making trains and rail travel non-existent in the country. As a result, visitors will need to rely on other modes of transportation, such as domestic flights, taxis, and car rentals, to get around the country.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are no intercity bus companies in Palau, and public transportation is limited to a few routes in the capital, Ngerulmud, and the former capital, Koror. The quality of the buses is moderate, and routes are limited, making it more convenient to use taxis or car rentals to get around. Costs for bus fares are relatively low, ranging from $1 to $5, depending on the route.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Palau, visitors will need to obtain a local driver's license, which can be done through the Palau Division of Transportation.
- Road conditions in Palau are moderate, with some paved roads and many unpaved roads, especially in rural areas.
- Fuel costs are relatively high in Palau, due to the country's remote location and limited fuel supply.
- Car rental companies are available at the international airport and in Koror, with prices starting from around $40 per day.
- When driving in Palau, be aware of the country's unique road signs and traffic rules, which may differ from those in your home country.
- Hazards on the road include potholes, stray animals, and limited lighting, especially at night, so it is recommended to drive cautiously and slowly.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When taking a taxi, make sure to agree on the fare before starting your journey, as some taxis may not have meters.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
- Download local transportation apps, such as Palau Ride, to help you get around the country.
- Be prepared for limited public transportation options and plan your itinerary accordingly.
- Consider renting a car or hiring a private driver for the day to explore the country's scenic areas.
- Always wear a seatbelt and follow local traffic rules to ensure your safety on the road.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when interacting with local drivers and transportation providers.
- Keep some cash on hand, as not all transportation providers may accept credit or debit cards.
