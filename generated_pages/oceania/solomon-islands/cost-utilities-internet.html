Utility Costs Overview

The utility infrastructure in Solomon Islands is still developing, with some areas experiencing frequent power outages and water shortages. The cost of utilities can fluctuate due to seasonal variation, with higher demand for electricity and water during the dry season. The Solomon Islands government has implemented measures to improve the utility infrastructure, including investments in renewable energy and water supply systems. However, the cost of utilities remains a significant burden for many households, particularly those in rural areas.

The stability of utility costs in Solomon Islands is affected by various factors, including fuel prices, inflation, and government subsidies. The inflation rate in Solomon Islands is around 4.3%, which can impact the cost of utilities and other essential services. Additionally, the country's reliance on imported fuel and other resources can make utility costs vulnerable to fluctuations in global market prices. Despite these challenges, the government and utility providers are working to improve the efficiency and reliability of utility services, including the introduction of prepaid electricity meters and other initiatives to reduce energy consumption.

Utility Tips

  • To set up utilities in Solomon Islands, it is recommended to contact the relevant providers (e.g. Our Telekom, Bmobile, Solomon Power) and provide required documents, such as a copy of your passport and proof of address.
  • Prepaid electricity meters are available, which can help households budget and avoid unexpected bills.
  • The best internet providers in Solomon Islands are Our Telekom and Bmobile, which offer a range of plans and speeds.
  • To save on utility bills, households can take measures such as using energy-efficient appliances, conserving water, and reducing waste.
