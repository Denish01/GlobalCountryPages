[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Solomon Islands is divided into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education is compulsory for all children from ages 6 to 14 and is provided by government and church-run schools. Secondary education is available for students who complete primary school and wish to pursue further education, with a focus on academic and vocational training. Tertiary education is offered by a few institutions, including the Solomon Islands National University, which provides degree programs in various fields. The quality of education in Solomon Islands is improving, with a focus on increasing access and improving teacher training. However, there are still challenges, particularly in rural areas where resources are limited.

The public education system in Solomon Islands is the main provider of education, with the government responsible for funding and managing schools. However, there are also private schools, including international schools, that offer alternative education options for students. The private sector plays a significant role in providing education, particularly at the secondary and tertiary levels. Despite the challenges, the education system in Solomon Islands has made significant progress in recent years, with an increase in enrollment rates and a focus on improving the quality of education.

The Solomon Islands government has implemented various initiatives to improve the education system, including the introduction of new curriculum and assessment standards. The government has also increased funding for education, with a focus on improving infrastructure and providing resources for schools. However, there is still a need for more investment in education, particularly in rural areas where schools often lack basic resources such as textbooks and technology.
