# Where to Stay in Solomon Islands — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

The best places to stay in Solomon Islands are in the capital city of Honiara, the island of Guadalcanal, and the surrounding areas, which offer a range of accommodations for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, the following options are available:
**Honiara City** (Honiara)
Best For: Business, Luxury
Vibe: Honiara City is the hub of Solomon Islands, with a mix of traditional and modern architecture, and a vibrant market scene. The city has a laid-back atmosphere, with a blend of Melanesian and Western cultures.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to government offices, restaurants, and shops, easy access to public transportation, and a range of accommodation options.
Cons: Traffic congestion, limited nightlife options.

**Ranadi** (Honiara)
Best For: Backpackers, Families
Vibe: Ranadi is a coastal suburb of Honiara, known for its beautiful beaches, water sports, and outdoor activities. The area has a relaxed, small-town feel, with a strong sense of community.
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, beautiful beaches, and easy access to water sports and outdoor activities.
Cons: Limited dining options, and can be affected by coastal erosion.

**Tavanipupu** (Guadalcanal)
Best For: Luxury, Honeymooners
Vibe: Tavanipupu is a private island resort, located off the coast of Guadalcanal, known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and luxurious villas. The island has a secluded, romantic atmosphere, with a focus on relaxation and luxury.
Price Range: $300-500/night
Pros: Private island experience, luxurious villas, and stunning natural beauty.
Cons: Limited access to public transportation, and can be expensive.

**Gizo** (Gizo Island)
Best For: Divers, Adventure Seekers
Vibe: Gizo is the capital of the Western Province, located on the island of Gizo, known for its stunning diving spots, vibrant market scene, and outdoor activities. The town has a lively, adventurous atmosphere, with a strong sense of community.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: World-class diving spots, vibrant market scene, and easy access to outdoor activities.
Cons: Limited accommodation options, and can be affected by weather conditions.

**Munda** (New Georgia Island)
Best For: Families, Nature Lovers
Vibe: Munda is a small town located on the island of New Georgia, known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, and traditional villages. The town has a relaxed, peaceful atmosphere, with a strong sense of community.
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, stunning natural beauty, and easy access to outdoor activities.
Cons: Limited dining options, and can be affected by weather conditions.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September), to ensure availability and the best rates.
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb, to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider negotiating the price, especially for long-term stays or off-season bookings.
- Check the cancellation policies and fees before booking.
- Read reviews and check the ratings of the accommodation provider before booking.
- Look for packages that include meals, activities, or transportation to get the best value.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during peak season and lower during off-season.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas, and use reputable transportation services.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas, and keep an eye on your belongings.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting traditional villages or attending cultural events.
- Keep valuables secure, and use hotel safes or lockers when available.
- Stay informed about local weather conditions, and follow evacuation procedures in case of natural disasters.
- Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation, shops, and restaurants, to minimize the need for transportation and increase safety.
