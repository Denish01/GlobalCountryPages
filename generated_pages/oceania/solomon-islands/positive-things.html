Cultural Treasures

  • The Rennell and Bellona Province is home to a unique and diverse cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on traditional customs and practices.
  • The Solomon Islands National Museum showcases the country's rich cultural history, with exhibits on traditional art, archaeology, and natural history.
  • The Mataniko Falls are a popular cultural and tourist attraction, with a stunning waterfall and surrounding rainforest.
  • The Solomon Islands traditional panpipe music is a unique and distinctive art form, with a rich cultural significance and history.
  • The Tavanipupu Island is a culturally significant site, with ancient ruins and artifacts dating back to the Lapita period.

People & Global Impact

  • Sir Peter Kenilorea, the first Prime Minister of the Solomon Islands, played a key role in the country's transition to independence and was a respected regional leader.
  • The Solomon Islands diaspora community has made significant contributions to the economy and culture of countries such as Australia, New Zealand, and the United States.
  • Mary Mani is a prominent Solomon Islands artist, known for her vibrant and expressive paintings that reflect the country's unique cultural heritage.
  • The Solomon Islands values of hospitality and respect are highly regarded, with a strong emphasis on community and traditional customs.

What Visitors Love

  • The pristine beaches and coral reefs of the Solomon Islands are a major draw for tourists, with world-class snorkeling and diving opportunities.
  • The warm hospitality of the Solomon Islands people is widely praised, with visitors often commenting on the friendly and welcoming nature of the locals.
  • The unique and diverse wildlife of the Solomon Islands, including sea turtles, dugongs, and birds of paradise, is a major attraction for nature lovers and eco-tourists.
  • The traditional village stays offer a unique and immersive cultural experience, with visitors able to learn about traditional customs and way of life.
  • The Solomon Islands cuisine is a fusion of traditional and modern flavors, with popular dishes such as mud crab, fresh fish, and coconut rice.
  • The scenic boat trips and island hopping opportunities are highly recommended, with stunning views and a chance to explore the country's many islands and atolls.

Hidden Gems

  • The Tetepare Island is a conservation area, with a unique and diverse ecosystem, including rainforests, coral reefs, and sea grass beds.
  • The Solomon Islands have a rich and diverse traditional medicine**, with many plants and remedies still used today to treat a range of ailments.
  • The Kakaban Island is a unique cultural site, with ancient ruins and artifacts, and a rich history dating back to the Lapita period.
  • The Solomon Islands have a thriving community-based tourism** industry, with many local communities developing their own tourism initiatives and projects.
