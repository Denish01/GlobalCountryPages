# Getting Around Solomon Islands — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Solomon Islands, you can use a combination of domestic flights, buses, taxis, and rental cars, as the country has a limited but improving transportation network.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
The Solomon Islands are accessible by air, with the main international airport being Honiara International Airport, which receives flights from major airlines such as Solomon Airlines, Qantas, and Virgin Australia. Direct flights are available from cities like Brisbane, Nadi, and Port Moresby, making it relatively easy to reach the country. However, overland entry points are non-existent, as the Solomon Islands are an archipelago, and the only way to enter the country is by air or sea.

The country's strategic location in the Pacific Ocean makes it an attractive destination for travelers, with many cruise ships and yachts visiting the islands. The main ports of entry are Honiara, Gizo, and Noro, which receive cargo ships and passenger ferries from neighboring countries. Travelers can also enter the country by private boat or yacht, but they must follow the necessary customs and immigration procedures.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Domestic flights are the fastest way to travel between the islands, with Solomon Airlines operating regular flights to major destinations like Gizo, Munda, and Kirakira. The airline also offers charter flights to smaller islands and atolls. Booking domestic flights in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as the flights can be fully booked quickly. The approximate cost of a domestic flight can range from $100 to $500, depending on the route and time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
The Solomon Islands do not have a rail network, making domestic flights and sea travel the primary modes of transportation between the islands. However, some islands have scenic coastal roads and walking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Buses and coaches are available on the main islands, such as Guadalcanal and Malaita, and offer an affordable way to travel between towns and villages. The buses are often crowded and may not follow a fixed schedule, but they provide a convenient way to see the local scenery and meet the friendly islanders. The cost of a bus ride can range from $1 to $10, depending on the distance and route.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- A valid driver's license from your home country is required to rent a car in Solomon Islands, and you may need to obtain a local driver's license if you plan to stay for an extended period.
- Road conditions in Solomon Islands are generally poor, with many roads being unpaved and prone to flooding.
- Fuel costs can be high, and fuel availability may be limited on some islands.
- Car rental companies are available in Honiara and Gizo, but the selection of vehicles may be limited.
- Driving hazards include potholes, pedestrians, and livestock on the roads, and drivers should exercise caution when driving at night.
- It is recommended to rent a 4WD vehicle if you plan to explore the rural areas or travel on unpaved roads.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Always agree on the fare before taking a taxi or bus, and ensure that you have the correct change.
- Be prepared for delays and cancellations of flights and ferry services due to weather or mechanical issues.
- Download the Solomon Airlines app to book and manage your flights, and to receive updates on flight schedules and availability.
- Use reputable car rental companies and inspect the vehicle before renting.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and use licensed taxis or ride-hailing services.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, and dress modestly when visiting villages or attending cultural events.
- Learn some basic phrases in the local language, such as "kam na mauri" (hello) and "tanggio" (thank you), to show appreciation for the local culture.
- Stay informed about weather forecasts and sea conditions if you plan to travel by boat or ferry.
