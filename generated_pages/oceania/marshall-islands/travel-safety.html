Overall Safety Assessment

The Marshall Islands are generally a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to ensure your safety. The islands are prone to natural disasters, such as typhoons and flooding, which can pose a risk to tourists. Additionally, the healthcare system in the Marshall Islands is limited, and medical facilities may not be up to the standards of those in developed countries.

Despite these challenges, the Marshall Islands are a unique and fascinating destination, with a rich culture and stunning natural beauty. With some basic precautions and an understanding of the local environment, tourists can have a safe and enjoyable trip. It's crucial to stay informed about local conditions, respect the local culture, and take necessary precautions to minimize risks. By doing so, visitors can have a positive and rewarding experience in the Marshall Islands.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Petty theft and pickpocketing can occur in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations
  • Be cautious of scams involving fake goods or services, particularly in Ebeye
  • Credit card fraud is a risk, especially in tourist areas
  • Be wary of unfamiliar individuals approaching you with unsolicited offers or requests
  • Rip currents and strong waves can be hazardous for swimmers
  • Food poisoning is a risk, particularly when consuming raw or undercooked seafood
  • Limited access to medical care and emergency services can be a concern in remote areas

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to the Marshall Islands
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are limited, and serious medical cases may require evacuation to a neighboring country or the US
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacy access is limited, and it's recommended to bring essential medications and supplies

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
  • Keep valuables secure and out of sight
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in remote areas
  • Stay informed about local weather conditions and natural disaster warnings
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting remote villages
  • Bring essential medications and supplies, including sunscreen and insect repellent

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be cautious when interacting with local men, and avoid walking alone at night
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting remote villages or attending cultural events
  • Be prepared for limited access to medical care and emergency services
  • Stay in touch with family and friends back home, and consider registering with your government's travel advisory program
