[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
The Marshall Islands has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others require a visa on arrival or prior to arrival. The country allows visa-free access to citizens of around 30 countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, and Australia, for a limited period. The visa policy is designed to promote tourism and economic development while ensuring national security.

The Marshall Islands offers various types of visas, including **Tourist Visas**, **Business Visas**, and **Student Visas**, each with its own set of requirements and restrictions. The country also has a relatively straightforward visa application process, with most applications being processed within a few days. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements and regulations can change, and it is crucial to verify the current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Marshall Islands before traveling.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter the Marshall Islands visa-free for a maximum stay of 30 days. To qualify for a visa-free entry, tourists must have a return ticket, proof of sufficient funds, and proof of onward travel. Extensions are possible but require approval from the immigration authorities.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a **Business Visa**, which can be obtained on arrival or prior to arrival. They must have an invitation letter from a local company, proof of sufficient funds, and a return ticket. The maximum stay for business travelers is 30 days, and extensions are possible but require approval from the immigration authorities.
**Students:** Students require a **Student Visa**, which can be obtained prior to arrival. They must have proof of enrollment in a local educational institution, health insurance, and sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. The duration of the student visa is typically one year, and it can be extended with approval from the immigration authorities.
**Workers:** Workers require a **Work Visa**, which can be obtained prior to arrival. They must have a sponsorship letter from a local employer, proof of qualifications, and sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay. The duration of the work visa is typically one year, and it can be extended with approval from the immigration authorities.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The Marshall Islands has two main airports: Marshall Islands International Airport (MAJ) in Majuro and Kwajalein Airport (KWA) in Kwajalein. Upon arrival, visitors must go through immigration and customs procedures, which include presenting their passport, return ticket, and proof of sufficient funds. Visitors may also be required to complete a health declaration form and undergo a health screening. The immigration authorities may also ask questions about the purpose of the visit, accommodation, and onward travel plans.

The entry procedures are relatively straightforward, but it is essential to ensure that all required documents are in order to avoid any delays or issues. Visitors should also be prepared to provide additional information or documentation if required by the immigration authorities.
