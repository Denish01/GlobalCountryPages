[SECTION] Overview: Marshall Islands vs Palau [/SECTION]
The Marshall Islands and Palau are two island nations in the Pacific Ocean with distinct economic profiles. The Marshall Islands has a relatively low GDP per capita and a high reliance on foreign aid, which contributes to its lower cost of living. In contrast, Palau has a more developed economy with a higher GDP per capita, driven by tourism and fishing industries. As a result, Palau tends to have higher prices for goods and services, particularly in the areas of housing, food, and transportation. Overall, the Marshall Islands is a more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees, while Palau is better suited for those with higher incomes or a desire for a more luxurious lifestyle.

The cost of living difference between the two countries is significant, with the Marshall Islands offering savings of around 15-20% on average. This is particularly notable in the areas of housing and food, where prices in the Marshall Islands are often 20-30% lower than in Palau. However, it's worth noting that the Marshall Islands also has a more limited range of goods and services available, which can make it more difficult to find certain items or enjoy certain amenities. In contrast, Palau has a more developed infrastructure and a wider range of activities and attractions, which can make it a more desirable destination for some visitors.
