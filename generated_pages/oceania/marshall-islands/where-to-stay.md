# Where to Stay in Marshall Islands — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in the Marshall Islands, consider the capital city of Majuro, which offers a range of accommodations, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury resorts, as well as nearby islands like Ebeye and Arno, each with their own unique character and advantages.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, the following are recommended:
**Majuro** (Majuro)
Best For: business / luxury
Vibe: Majuro is the bustling capital city, with a mix of modern and traditional Marshallese culture, and a vibrant atmosphere, especially in the downtown area. The city offers a range of amenities, including restaurants, shops, and entertainment options.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: proximity to government offices, amenities, and entertainment options, as well as access to the city's harbor and waterfront, modern infrastructure, and a wide range of dining options
Cons: can be noisy and crowded, limited parking options

**Delap** (Majuro)
Best For: family / backpacker
Vibe: Delap is a residential area with a relaxed atmosphere, known for its beautiful beaches and scenic views, making it an ideal spot for families and those looking for a more laid-back experience. The area is also home to several parks and outdoor recreational spaces.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: affordable, close to beaches, and a more laid-back atmosphere, as well as access to outdoor recreational activities, and a sense of community
Cons: limited amenities and services

**Ebeye** (Kwajalein)
Best For: budget / adventure
Vibe: Ebeye is a small island with a vibrant community, known for its beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and outdoor activities, making it an ideal spot for adventure-seekers and budget travelers. The island has a strong sense of community, with many local events and festivals.
Price Range: $50-150/night
Pros: affordable, beautiful beaches, and outdoor activities, as well as a unique cultural experience, and access to the island's vibrant community
Cons: limited amenities and infrastructure

**Arno** (Arno)
Best For: luxury / relaxation
Vibe: Arno is a small, tranquil island with a luxurious atmosphere, known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and high-end resorts, making it an ideal spot for those looking for a relaxing getaway. The island is also home to several luxury villas and private residences.
Price Range: $300-500/night
Pros: luxurious, secluded, and beautiful beaches, as well as access to high-end amenities and services, and a sense of exclusivity
Cons: expensive, limited accessibility

**Laura** (Majuro)
Best For: family / business
Vibe: Laura is a residential area with a mix of modern and traditional Marshallese culture, known for its beautiful beaches, scenic views, and proximity to the city, making it an ideal spot for families and business travelers. The area is also home to several parks and outdoor recreational spaces.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: affordable, close to beaches, and modern infrastructure, as well as access to amenities and services, and a sense of community
Cons: limited nightlife options

**Rita** (Majuro)
Best For: backpacker / adventure
Vibe: Rita is a small, vibrant community with a laid-back atmosphere, known for its beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, and proximity to the city, making it an ideal spot for backpackers and adventure-seekers. The area is also home to several local shops and restaurants.
Price Range: $60-180/night
Pros: affordable, beautiful beaches, and outdoor activities, as well as access to local culture and community, and a sense of adventure
Cons: limited amenities and services

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September), to ensure availability and the best rates.
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb, to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for long-term stays or during the off-season.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with higher rates during peak season and lower rates during the off-season.
- Check for package deals, which can include accommodations, meals, and activities.
- Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before booking.
- Consider working with a local travel agent or tour operator to find the best options and deals.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of the local crime situation, with a higher risk of petty theft and burglary in some areas.
- Avoid walking alone at night, especially in isolated areas, and use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events.
- Stay informed about weather conditions, with a risk of typhoons and tropical storms during the wet season.
- Consider staying in areas with easy access to transportation, such as Majuro or Ebeye.
- Be mindful of the environment, with a focus on sustainable tourism and conservation efforts.
