# New Caledonia Tax Guide — Income, Corporate & VAT Rates

The tax rates in New Caledonia range from 5% to 40% for personal income, with a corporate tax rate of 30% and a value-added tax (VAT) rate of 5%.

[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
New Caledonia, as a territory of France, has a **territorial taxation** system, where individuals and companies are taxed on their income earned within the territory, regardless of their residency status. However, residents of New Caledonia are also subject to tax on their worldwide income, with **foreign tax credits** available to avoid double taxation. To be considered a resident for tax purposes, an individual must have their **permanent home** in New Caledonia, or spend more than **six months** in the territory during a calendar year.

The tax system in New Caledonia is designed to be relatively straightforward, with a focus on simplicity and ease of compliance. The territory has a **tax authority**, known as the Direction des Finances et de l'Économie, which is responsible for administering and collecting taxes. Taxpayers can access a range of services and resources online, including tax returns and payment facilities. New Caledonia's tax system is also designed to be **competitive**, with a range of incentives and exemptions available to attract businesses and investment to the territory.
