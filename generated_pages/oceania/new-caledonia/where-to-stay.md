# Where to Stay in New Caledonia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To find the best place to stay in New Caledonia, consider the top areas of Noumea, Isle of Pines, Bourail, Hienghene, and Poindimie, which offer a range of options for different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a brief overview:
**Noumea** (Noumea)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: Noumea is the capital city of New Caledonia and offers a modern and cosmopolitan vibe, with a mix of French and Melanesian cultures. The city has a vibrant atmosphere, with many restaurants, bars, and shops to explore.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Easy access to amenities, beautiful beaches, and a variety of accommodation options. The city is also home to many historical and cultural attractions, including the New Caledonia Museum and the Tjibaou Cultural Centre.
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and traffic can be heavy during peak hours.

**Isle of Pines** (Isle of Pines)
Best For: Family / Luxury
Vibe: The Isle of Pines is a tranquil and picturesque island with stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a relaxed atmosphere. The island is perfect for those looking to unwind and enjoy the natural beauty of New Caledonia.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Breathtaking scenery, beautiful beaches, and a range of outdoor activities such as snorkeling and diving. The island is also home to several luxury resorts and hotels.
Cons: Limited dining options, and can be quite isolated from the rest of New Caledonia.

**Bourail** (Bourail)
Best For: Backpacker / Adventure
Vibe: Bourail is a charming coastal town with a laid-back atmosphere, known for its beautiful beaches, waterfalls, and outdoor activities such as hiking and surfing. The town is a great base for exploring the surrounding countryside and enjoying the natural beauty of New Caledonia.
Price Range: $80-$150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, stunning natural scenery, and a range of outdoor activities. The town is also home to several restaurants and cafes serving local cuisine.
Cons: Limited amenities, and can be quite quiet during the off-season.

**Hienghene** (Hienghene)
Best For: Family / Nature
Vibe: Hienghene is a picturesque town surrounded by stunning natural scenery, including waterfalls, beaches, and mountains. The town has a relaxed atmosphere and is perfect for those looking to explore the great outdoors.
Price Range: $100-$200/night
Pros: Breathtaking scenery, outdoor activities such as hiking and fishing, and a range of accommodation options. The town is also home to several restaurants and cafes serving local cuisine.
Cons: Limited amenities, and can be quite isolated from the rest of New Caledonia.

**Poindimie** (Poindimie)
Best For: Luxury / Relaxation
Vibe: Poindimie is a tranquil and picturesque town with stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a relaxed atmosphere. The town is perfect for those looking to unwind and enjoy the natural beauty of New Caledonia.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, stunning scenery, and a range of outdoor activities such as snorkeling and diving. The town is also home to several luxury resorts and hotels.
Cons: Limited dining options, and can be quite isolated from the rest of New Caledonia.
