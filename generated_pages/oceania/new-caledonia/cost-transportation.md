[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
New Caledonia offers a range of transportation options, including public buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit is relatively good, with buses connecting major towns and cities. Most people in New Caledonia get around by using public buses or driving their own vehicles, as the territory has a well-developed road network. However, traffic congestion can be a problem in the capital city of Noumea, especially during peak hours.

The main transportation modes in New Caledonia include buses, taxis, and ferries, which connect the islands. The territory also has a domestic airport, which offers flights to other islands. The public transportation system is relatively affordable, with a single bus fare costing around $1.50 USD. However, the frequency and coverage of public transportation can be limited in some areas, making it necessary to plan ahead or use alternative modes of transport.
