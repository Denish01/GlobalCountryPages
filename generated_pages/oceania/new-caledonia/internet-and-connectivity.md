The internet in New Caledonia is generally reliable, with good speeds and coverage in urban areas, but can be limited in rural regions, making it essential to plan ahead and choose the right mobile carrier and data plan for your needs.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
New Caledonia's internet quality is relatively good, with an average speed of 10 Mbps download, which is sufficient for general browsing, email, and social media. However, the urban-rural divide is noticeable, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in the capital city of Noumea and other urban centers, while rural areas often experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. The reliability of the internet in New Caledonia is generally good, with occasional outages and maintenance work, but overall, it is possible to stay connected and productive during your stay.

The internet infrastructure in New Caledonia is relatively well-developed, with a mix of fiber-optic and satellite connections providing coverage to most parts of the territory. However, the lack of **5G** coverage and limited **mobile network coverage** in rural areas can be a challenge for those who need to stay connected on the go. Despite these limitations, the overall internet experience in New Caledonia is good, with many **WiFi hotspots** available in public areas, cafes, and restaurants, making it easy to stay connected and access the internet during your stay.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in New Caledonia, you can visit one of the main carriers' stores, such as **OPT** or **Mobilis**, and purchase a tourist SIM card, which typically costs around 20 USD. You will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and the SIM card will usually be activated immediately. The recommended carriers for tourists are OPT and Mobilis, which offer a range of data plans and packages, including prepaid and postpaid options. It is also possible to purchase a SIM card at the airport or from a local retailer, but be sure to check the prices and plans before making a purchase.

When choosing a data plan, consider your needs and budget, and opt for a plan that includes a sufficient amount of data and minutes. Some carriers also offer **data roaming** packages, which can be useful if you plan to travel to other Pacific islands or countries.
