[SECTION] Overview: New Caledonia vs Fiji [/SECTION]
New Caledonia and Fiji are both island nations in the Pacific, but they have distinct economic contexts that affect their cost of living. New Caledonia, as a territory of France, has a high standard of living and a strong economy, driven by its nickel mining industry and tourism. Fiji, on the other hand, has a more developing economy, with a mix of agriculture, tourism, and manufacturing. The cost of living in New Caledonia is generally higher, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being significantly more expensive than in Fiji. However, Fiji's lower costs come with some trade-offs, such as lower salaries and fewer job opportunities.

The overall cost of living in Fiji is around 20-30% lower than in New Caledonia, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees on a budget. However, New Caledonia's higher standard of living and better infrastructure may make it a more desirable choice for those who value comfort and convenience. In terms of specific costs, Fiji is generally cheaper for housing, food, and transportation, while New Caledonia is more expensive for these categories. However, both countries have their own unique advantages and disadvantages, and the best choice will depend on individual priorities and lifestyle.
