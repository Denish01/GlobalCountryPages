# Education Costs in New Caledonia

The cost of education in New Caledonia varies depending on the type of institution and the level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in New Caledonia is based on the French model, with French being the primary language of instruction. The system is divided into primary, secondary, and higher education, with 10 years of compulsory education. Public schools are generally considered to be of good quality, but private schools are also available for those who can afford them. International schools are also present in New Caledonia, offering a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate and the French baccalaureate.

The quality of education in New Caledonia is generally considered to be high, with a strong emphasis on core subjects such as mathematics, science, and language. However, the system can be quite rigid, with a focus on rote learning and standardized testing. Despite this, New Caledonia has a high literacy rate and a well-educated population. For expats, there are several international schools available, offering a range of curricula and teaching styles. These schools can provide a high-quality education, but can also be quite expensive.
