[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Kiribati is structured into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 14, and is provided free of charge by the government. The primary education system is divided into six years of education, with a focus on basic subjects such as **mathematics**, **science**, and **English**. Secondary education is provided by a number of government and private schools, and is typically attended by students between the ages of 14 and 18. The quality of education in Kiribati is generally considered to be improving, although there are still significant challenges related to limited resources and a lack of qualified teachers.

The public education system in Kiribati is the largest provider of education, with the majority of students attending government-funded schools. However, there are also a number of private schools, including **church-run schools** and **international schools**, which offer alternative education options for students. Despite the growth of the education system, there are still significant challenges related to access and quality, particularly in rural areas where resources are limited.
