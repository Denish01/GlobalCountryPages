[SECTION] Overview: Kiribati vs Tuvalu [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Kiribati and Tuvalu varies significantly, with Kiribati being the more affordable option. The economic context of both countries is largely driven by their remote locations and limited resources, resulting in higher prices for imported goods. However, Kiribati's larger population and more developed economy contribute to lower costs for certain goods and services. On average, Kiribati is around 15-20% cheaper than Tuvalu, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable Pacific island lifestyle.

The main difference in cost levels between the two countries lies in the prices of housing, food, and transportation. Kiribati's capital, Tarawa, offers a range of accommodation options, including apartments and houses, at lower prices compared to Tuvalu's capital, Funafuti. Additionally, the cost of food and dining out in Kiribati is generally lower, with meals at local restaurants and markets offering better value for money. However, Tuvalu's smaller size and more limited economy result in higher prices for certain imported goods and services, such as fuel and internet.
