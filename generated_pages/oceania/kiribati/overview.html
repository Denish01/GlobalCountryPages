What Is Kiribati?

Kiribati is a sovereign island nation located in the central Pacific Ocean, within the continent of Oceania. It is composed of 33 coral atolls, with a total land area of approximately 810 square kilometers. The country is known for its stunning natural beauty, with crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and an incredible array of marine life. Kiribati is also home to a unique and vibrant culture, with a strong emphasis on traditional practices and customs.

Geographically, Kiribati is situated about halfway between Hawaii and Australia, and is bordered by the Marshall Islands to the northwest and Nauru to the southwest. The country's capital, Tarawa, is the largest urban center and is located on the atoll of the same name. Kiribati is a member of the Commonwealth of Nations and has a parliamentary democracy system of government.

As a developing country, Kiribati faces a number of challenges, including limited economic resources, a lack of infrastructure, and the impacts of climate change. However, the country is also rich in natural resources, including fisheries and phosphates, and has a growing tourism industry. With its stunning natural beauty, unique culture, and warm hospitality, Kiribati is a fascinating destination for travelers and a significant player in the Pacific region.

Key History

  1. 1892 - The Gilbert Islands, which now form part of Kiribati, become a British protectorate.
  2. 1915 - The Gilbert and Ellice Islands are formed into a single British colony.
  3. 1975 - The Ellice Islands separate from the Gilbert Islands and become the British colony of Tuvalu.
  4. 1979 - Kiribati gains independence from the United Kingdom.
  5. 1983 - Kiribati signs a treaty of friendship with the United States, which recognizes Kiribati's sovereignty over certain islands.
  6. 1995 - Kiribati moves the International Date Line to the east of the country, in an effort to promote economic development.
  7. 2015 - Kiribati's president, Anote Tong, warns that the country may become the first nation to be displaced by climate change.

What Makes Kiribati Unique

  • Kiribati is home to a unique and diverse range of wildlife, including sea turtles, whales, and dolphins.
  • The country has a rich and vibrant culture, with a strong emphasis on traditional practices and customs.
  • Kiribati is one of the most remote countries in the world, with limited access to modern amenities and services.
  • The country is highly vulnerable to the impacts of climate change, including rising sea levels and increased weather events.
  • Kiribati has a growing renewable energy sector, with a focus on solar and wind power.

Quick Summary

Kiribati is a sovereign island nation in the central Pacific Ocean, known for its stunning natural beauty, unique culture, and significant challenges, including the impacts of climate change and limited economic resources.
