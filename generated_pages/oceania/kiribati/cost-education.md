# Education Costs in Kiribati

The cost of education in Kiribati varies depending on the type of institution and level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge fees ranging from $200 to $5,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Kiribati is primarily public, with a focus on providing free or subsidized education to all citizens. The **Ministry of Education** is responsible for overseeing the education sector, which includes both public and private institutions. The language of instruction is English, although **Gilbertese** is also widely spoken and taught in schools. Compulsory education in Kiribati spans 12 years, from primary to secondary level, with students typically starting school at the age of 6 and completing their secondary education at 18. While public schools are generally of good quality, private schools and international institutions tend to offer higher standards of education, although at a higher cost.

The availability of international schools in Kiribati is limited, with only a few institutions offering **International Baccalaureate** or **Cambridge** curricula. These schools cater primarily to expatriate families and wealthy locals, providing a more comprehensive and internationally recognized education. However, the cost of attending an international school in Kiribati can be prohibitively expensive, with fees ranging from $2,000 to $5,000 per year. Despite the challenges, the Kiribati government has made significant efforts to improve the quality of education, including investing in **teacher training** and **infrastructure development**.
