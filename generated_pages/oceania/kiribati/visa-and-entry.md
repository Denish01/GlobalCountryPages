# Kiribati Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Kiribati before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Kiribati has a relatively liberal visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival. The country's visa stance is designed to promote tourism and facilitate travel for business and other purposes. Citizens of over 70 countries can enter Kiribati without a visa, while others may obtain a visa on arrival or through prior application. The visa policy is subject to change, so it is essential to check with the official embassy or consulate of Kiribati for the latest requirements.

The visa-free access policy applies to citizens of countries such as the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, and many European nations. The maximum stay period for visa-free visitors is typically 30 days, but this can vary depending on the nationality and purpose of the visit. Kiribati's visa policy aims to strike a balance between promoting tourism and ensuring national security.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Kiribati visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival, depending on their nationality. The typical stay period for tourists is 30 days, but this can be extended for an additional 30 days through an application to the Kiribati Immigration Office. Tourists must provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds to support their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They must provide an invitation letter from a local company or organization and meet specific requirements, such as proof of business registration and tax clearance.
**Students:** Students must obtain a student visa prior to their arrival in Kiribati. They must provide proof of enrollment at a recognized educational institution and meet specific requirements, such as health insurance and sufficient funds to support their stay.
**Workers:** Workers must obtain a work visa prior to their arrival in Kiribati. They must provide proof of sponsorship from a local employer and meet specific requirements, such as a medical certificate and police clearance.

9. Medical certificate (for workers)

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Kiribati are the Bonriki International Airport (TRW) and the Tarawa Airport (TNW). Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and complete an arrival card. The immigration officer will verify the documents and grant entry to the country. Visitors must also declare any goods they are bringing into the country, including gifts, souvenirs, and food items.

The entry procedures in Kiribati are relatively straightforward, but visitors must ensure they have all the required documents and meet the necessary requirements. The immigration officers may ask questions about the purpose of the visit, the length of stay, and the accommodation arrangements. Visitors who are unsure about the entry requirements or procedures should contact the Kiribati Immigration Office or the official embassy or consulate of Kiribati for guidance.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Failing to check the visa requirements before traveling to Kiribati
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not providing proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
- Not declaring goods brought into the country, including gifts and souvenirs
- Not meeting the specific requirements for business travelers, students, or workers
- Not applying for a visa or extension in a timely manner, resulting in delays or penalties
