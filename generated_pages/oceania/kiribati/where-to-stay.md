# Where to Stay in Kiribati — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

The best places to stay in Kiribati are in the capital city of Tarawa, particularly in the neighborhoods of Betio, Bairiki, and Teinainano, which offer a range of accommodations and easy access to the city's amenities and attractions.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Kiribati, the following neighborhoods are recommended:
**Betio** (Tarawa)
Best For: backpacker / budget traveler
Vibe: Betio is a lively and bustling neighborhood with a mix of traditional and modern shops, restaurants, and entertainment options, offering a unique cultural experience for visitors. The area is also home to a number of historical landmarks and attractions, including the Betio Shipwreck and the Kiribati National Museum.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: affordable accommodations, close proximity to the city center, access to traditional markets and local cuisine
Cons: can be noisy and crowded, limited amenities and services

**Bairiki** (Tarawa)
Best For: luxury / business traveler
Vibe: Bairiki is a more upscale neighborhood with a range of modern amenities and services, including high-end restaurants, bars, and entertainment options. The area is also home to a number of government buildings and institutions, making it a hub for business and politics.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: modern amenities, close proximity to government buildings and institutions, access to high-end dining and entertainment options
Cons: can be expensive, limited traditional cultural experiences

**Teinainano** (Tarawa)
Best For: family / mid-range traveler
Vibe: Teinainano is a quiet and residential neighborhood with a range of mid-range accommodations and amenities, including restaurants, shops, and entertainment options. The area is also home to a number of parks and green spaces, making it a great option for families.
Price Range: $50-100/night
Pros: affordable accommodations, close proximity to parks and green spaces, access to mid-range amenities and services
Cons: can be limited in terms of traditional cultural experiences, limited high-end amenities and services

**Abaiang** (Abaiang)
Best For: luxury / relaxation
Vibe: Abaiang is a tranquil and picturesque island with a range of high-end resorts and amenities, including spas, restaurants, and water sports. The area is perfect for those looking to relax and unwind in a beautiful and peaceful environment.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: stunning natural beauty, access to high-end amenities and services, perfect for relaxation and water sports
Cons: can be expensive, limited access to traditional cultural experiences

**Tabiteuea** (Tabiteuea)
Best For: backpacker / adventure traveler
Vibe: Tabiteuea is a remote and rugged island with a range of budget-friendly accommodations and amenities, including guesthouses and traditional villages. The area is perfect for those looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure and cultural experience.
Price Range: $10-30/night
Pros: affordable accommodations, access to traditional villages and cultural experiences, perfect for adventure and exploration
Cons: can be limited in terms of modern amenities and services, limited access to high-end amenities and services

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (June to September)
- Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com or Expedia to compare prices and find deals
- Consider staying in guesthouses or traditional villages for a unique cultural experience
- Negotiate prices directly with hotel or guesthouse owners for long-term stays or off-season bookings
- Be aware of seasonal pricing and plan accordingly
- Read reviews and check the hotel or guesthouse's reputation before booking
- Consider booking a package deal that includes accommodations, meals, and activities for a hassle-free experience
