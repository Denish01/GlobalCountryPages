# Where to Stay in French Polynesia — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in French Polynesia, consider the islands of Tahiti, Moorea, and Bora Bora for a mix of culture, adventure, and luxury, with top areas including Papeete, Faaa, and Matira.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**Papeete** (Tahiti)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: As the capital city, Papeete offers a unique blend of French and Polynesian cultures, with a vibrant market and waterfront area. The city has a lively atmosphere, with many restaurants, shops, and cultural attractions.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: Access to amenities, cultural attractions, and transportation hubs, with many high-end hotels and resorts, proximity to the port for island-hopping, and a variety of dining and entertainment options.
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, with limited budget options.

**Faaa** (Tahiti)
Best For: Family / Backpacker
Vibe: Located near the airport, Faaa is a convenient and affordable area to stay, with a more laid-back atmosphere and easy access to the beach. The area has a mix of budget-friendly accommodations and family-friendly resorts.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Proximity to the airport, affordable accommodations, and a range of outdoor activities, including surfing and hiking, with many family-friendly resorts and a relaxed atmosphere.
Cons: Limited nightlife and cultural attractions.

**Matira** (Bora Bora)
Best For: Luxury / Honeymoon
Vibe: With its stunning white-sand beach and crystal-clear lagoon, Matira is a picturesque and romantic area, perfect for honeymooners and those seeking luxury. The area has a serene and peaceful atmosphere, with many high-end resorts and overwater bungalows.
Price Range: $500-1000/night
Pros: Breathtaking views, luxurious resorts, and a range of water sports and activities, including snorkeling and diving, with many resorts offering fine dining and spa facilities.
Cons: Expensive, with limited budget options and a remote location.

**Moorea** (Moorea)
Best For: Adventure / Nature
Vibe: With its lush green mountains and turquoise lagoon, Moorea is a nature lover's paradise, offering a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and horseback riding. The island has a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, with many eco-lodges and guesthouses.
Price Range: $150-400/night
Pros: Stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, and a range of accommodations, including eco-lodges and guesthouses, with a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere.
Cons: Limited nightlife and cultural attractions, with some areas being quite remote.

**Tahaa** (Tahaa)
Best For: Luxury / Relaxation
Vibe: Located in the Society Islands, Tahaa is a tranquil and secluded area, perfect for those seeking relaxation and luxury. The island has a serene and peaceful atmosphere, with many high-end resorts and spas.
Price Range: $300-600/night
Pros: Luxurious resorts, stunning natural beauty, and a range of water sports and activities, including snorkeling and diving, with many resorts offering fine dining and spa facilities.
Cons: Expensive, with limited budget options and a remote location.

**Huahine** (Huahine)
Best For: Adventure / Culture
Vibe: With its rich history and cultural attractions, Huahine is a great area for those interested in Polynesian culture and history. The island has a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, with many guesthouses and eco-lodges.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Rich cultural heritage, outdoor activities, and a range of accommodations, including guesthouses and eco-lodges, with a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere.
Cons: Limited nightlife and luxury options, with some areas being quite remote.
