[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Fiji Museum** in Suva is home to a vast collection of Fijian artifacts, including traditional canoes, war clubs, and ceremonial masks.
- The **Sigatoka Sand Dunes National Park** is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and features ancient archaeological sites and stunning natural scenery.
- The **Meke** traditional dance is an important part of Fijian culture and is often performed during ceremonies and celebrations.
- The **Tabua** ceremony is an ancient Fijian ritual in which a whale's tooth is presented as a symbol of respect and friendship.
- The **Bula Festival** is a popular annual event that showcases Fijian music, dance, and art.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Ratu Sir Kamisese Mara**, the first Prime Minister of Fiji, played a key role in the country's transition to independence in 1970.
- The **Fijian diaspora** has made significant contributions to the economies of countries such as Australia, New Zealand, and the United States, with many Fijians working in healthcare, education, and hospitality.
- **Vijay Singh**, a Fijian golfer, won the PGA Championship in 1998 and 2004 and was inducted into the World Golf Hall of Fame in 2006.
- The **Fijian values** of hospitality, respect, and community are highly prized and are often referred to as the "Fijian way of life".

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **clear waters and coral reefs** of the Mamanuca and Yasawa islands are a major draw for tourists, with many opportunities for snorkeling, diving, and swimming.
- The **local cuisine**, which features dishes such as kokoda (raw fish marinated in coconut milk) and lovo (meat cooked in an underground oven), is a highlight of many visitors' trips.
- The **Fijian hospitality** is legendary, with many visitors commenting on the warm welcome they receive from locals.
- The **scenic landscapes**, including the Coral Coast and the Kula National Park, offer breathtaking views and opportunities for hiking and exploration.
- The **cultural experiences**, such as visiting traditional villages and attending local ceremonies, are a major part of many visitors' itineraries.
- The **luxury resorts**, such as the Jean-Michel Cousteau Resort and the Warwick Fiji, offer high-end amenities and services.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Taveuni Island** is a lesser-known destination that offers stunning natural scenery, including waterfalls, hiking trails, and beautiful beaches.
- The **Fijian creole language**, also known as **Hindustani**, is a unique and fascinating language that reflects the country's cultural diversity.
- The **Navala Village**, which features traditional thatched-roof bures (huts), is a hidden gem that offers a glimpse into traditional Fijian village life.
- The **Koro Sea** is a remote and pristine area that is home to an incredible array of marine life, including dolphins, whales, and sea turtles.
