[SECTION] Overview: Fiji vs New Zealand [/SECTION]
Fiji and New Zealand are two island nations in the Pacific, but they have distinct economic profiles. Fiji has a smaller economy with a GDP per capita of $9,900, while New Zealand has a more developed economy with a GDP per capita of $43,600. The cost of living in Fiji is generally lower, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being significantly cheaper than in New Zealand. Fiji's economy is driven by tourism, agriculture, and remittances, while New Zealand's economy is more diversified, with a strong service sector and a significant manufacturing industry.

The overall cost of living in Fiji is approximately 30-40% lower than in New Zealand. This is due to a combination of factors, including lower housing costs, cheaper food, and lower transportation costs. However, it's worth noting that the cost of living in Fiji can vary depending on the location, with urban areas like Suva and Nadi tend to be more expensive than rural areas. In contrast, New Zealand has a more uniform cost of living across the country, with prices in cities like Auckland and Wellington being similar to those in smaller towns.
