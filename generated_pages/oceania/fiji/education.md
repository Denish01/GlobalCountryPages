[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Fiji is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 12, and is provided free of charge by the government. Secondary education is also compulsory, and students typically attend secondary school from ages 13 to 18. The quality of education in Fiji is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on **mathematics**, **science**, and **English language**. Public schools are the most common type of educational institution in Fiji, but there are also a number of private schools, including **faith-based schools** and **international schools**.

The public education system in Fiji is overseen by the Ministry of Education, which is responsible for setting curriculum standards, administering exams, and providing resources and support to schools. In recent years, the government has implemented a number of reforms aimed at improving the quality of education in Fiji, including the introduction of **new curriculum frameworks** and the **expansion of vocational training programs**. Despite these efforts, however, the education system in Fiji still faces a number of challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of resources in some schools.

In terms of access to education, Fiji has made significant progress in recent years, with **enrollment rates** increasing at all levels of education. However, there are still some disparities in access to education, particularly in rural areas where schools may be fewer and farther between. To address this issue, the government has implemented a number of initiatives aimed at improving access to education in rural areas, including the **construction of new schools** and the **introduction of distance learning programs**.
