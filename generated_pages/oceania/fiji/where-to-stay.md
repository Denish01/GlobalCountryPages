# Where to Stay in Fiji — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Fiji, consider the top areas of Nadi, Denarau Island, Coral Coast, Mamanuca Islands, Yasawa Islands, Suva, and Pacific Harbour, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Nadi** (Nadi)
Best For: Backpackers, families, and travelers looking for a mix of relaxation and activities
Vibe: Nadi has a lively atmosphere with a mix of local markets, restaurants, and bars, and is close to the international airport, making it a great base for exploring the surrounding islands. The town has a laid-back feel, with a variety of accommodation options and easy access to water sports and activities.
Price Range: $20-200/night
Pros: Close to the airport, variety of accommodation options, easy access to water sports and activities
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited high-end amenities

**Denarau Island** (Nadi)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families looking for a resort experience
Vibe: Denarau Island is a luxury resort area with a relaxed and upscale atmosphere, featuring several high-end resorts, a marina, and a golf course. The island has a tranquil and secluded feel, with plenty of opportunities for relaxation and leisure activities.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: Luxury amenities, beautiful beaches, and a golf course
Cons: Limited local culture and interaction, can be expensive

**Coral Coast** (Sigatoka)
Best For: Families, couples, and travelers looking for a relaxing beach experience
Vibe: The Coral Coast is a scenic stretch of coastline with a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, featuring several resorts, beaches, and water sports. The area has a laid-back and natural feel, with plenty of opportunities for snorkeling, diving, and other outdoor activities.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, water sports, and a relaxed atmosphere
Cons: Limited nightlife and entertainment options, can be affected by seasonal weather

**Mamanuca Islands** (Western Division)
Best For: Honeymooners, luxury travelers, and those looking for a secluded island experience
Vibe: The Mamanuca Islands are a group of small islands with a tranquil and picturesque atmosphere, featuring clear waters, white sandy beaches, and luxury resorts. The islands have a secluded and romantic feel, with plenty of opportunities for relaxation, snorkeling, and diving.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a secluded atmosphere
Cons: Limited amenities and activities, can be expensive to get to

**Yasawa Islands** (Northern Division)
Best For: Backpackers, budget travelers, and those looking for a more rustic island experience
Vibe: The Yasawa Islands are a group of islands with a laid-back and natural atmosphere, featuring clear waters, white sandy beaches, and budget-friendly resorts. The islands have a relaxed and peaceful feel, with plenty of opportunities for snorkeling, diving, and other outdoor activities.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable, beautiful beaches, and a relaxed atmosphere
Cons: Limited amenities and activities, can be affected by seasonal weather

**Suva** (Suva)
Best For: Business travelers, culture enthusiasts, and those looking for a urban experience
Vibe: Suva is the capital city of Fiji, with a vibrant and cosmopolitan atmosphere, featuring a mix of colonial architecture, local markets, and cultural attractions. The city has a lively and eclectic feel, with plenty of opportunities for shopping, dining, and entertainment.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Cultural attractions, local markets, and a vibrant atmosphere
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, limited beach activities

**Pacific Harbour** (Pacific Harbour)
Best For: Adventure seekers, families, and travelers looking for a mix of relaxation and activities
Vibe: Pacific Harbour is a coastal town with a relaxed and adventurous atmosphere, featuring a mix of water sports, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. The town has a laid-back and natural feel, with plenty of opportunities for snorkeling, diving, and other outdoor activities.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Water sports, outdoor activities, and a relaxed atmosphere
Cons: Limited high-end amenities, can be affected by seasonal weather
