Overall Safety Assessment

Fiji is generally a safe country to visit, with low levels of violent crime. The Fijian government has taken steps to improve the safety and security of tourists, including the establishment of a dedicated tourist police force. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to ensure your safety. Petty crime, such as theft and pickpocketing, can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, so it's crucial to be mindful of your belongings and surroundings. Additionally, Fiji is prone to natural disasters, such as cyclones and flooding, which can impact your travel plans.

Fiji's safety record is also influenced by its cultural and social context. The country has a strong tradition of hospitality and respect for visitors, which contributes to a welcoming and safe environment for tourists. However, as with any foreign travel, it's essential to be aware of local customs and traditions to avoid unintentionally offending locals. By taking the necessary precautions and being respectful of the local culture, you can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Fiji.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of taxi scams, where drivers may overcharge or take you to unwanted destinations
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or ATMs instead
  • Be mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Be wary of street vendors who may sell counterfeit goods or overcharge for services
  • Avoid drinking tap water, as it may not be safe for consumption
  • Be cautious of strong currents and riptides when swimming in the ocean
  • Be aware of cyclone seasons, which can impact your travel plans
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions to avoid unintentionally offending locals

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for travel to Fiji
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe for consumption, and bottled water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are generally good in major towns, but may be limited in rural areas. Travel insurance is recommended
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are available in major towns, but may have limited supplies of certain medications

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always lock your accommodation and use the safe to store valuables
  • Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Use reputable taxi companies or ride-sharing services
  • Avoid walking alone at night in rural areas or areas with high crime rates
  • Stay informed about local weather conditions and cyclone warnings
  • Respect local customs and traditions to avoid unintentionally offending locals
  • Learn some basic Fijian phrases, such as "hello" (bula) and "thank you" (vinaka)
  • Stay hydrated and wear sun protection when outdoors

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be cautious when accepting rides from strangers, and always use reputable taxi companies
  • Avoid walking alone at night in rural areas or areas with high crime rates
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
