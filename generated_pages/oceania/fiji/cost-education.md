# Education Costs in Fiji

The cost of education in Fiji varies greatly depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Fiji's education system is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being generally free or subsidized, but often lacking in quality and resources. The language of instruction is **English**, with **Fijian** and **Hindu** also being widely spoken. Compulsory education lasts for 13 years, from **primary school** to **secondary school**, and is overseen by the Ministry of Education. International schools are also available, particularly in the capital city of **Suva**, offering a range of curricula including **Cambridge** and **IB**.

The quality of education in Fiji's public schools is often a concern, with many schools lacking in resources and infrastructure. In contrast, private schools tend to have better facilities and more qualified teachers, but can be expensive. International schools, on the other hand, offer a high-quality education, but can be very costly. Expats and international students often opt for these schools due to their high standards and recognition globally.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Tips [/SECTION]
- The **University of the South Pacific** is a top-ranked institution in the region, offering a range of programs and scholarships.
- **International schools** are a good option for expats and international students, offering a high-quality education and recognized curricula.
- **Private tutoring** is widely available, but can be expensive, with prices ranging from $20 to $50 per hour.
- **Scholarships** are available for local and international students, particularly for those pursuing higher education.
- **Online learning options** are becoming increasingly popular, with many institutions offering online courses and degree programs.
- **Education quality** in Fiji is generally lower than in other developed countries in the region, but is improving, with the government investing in education infrastructure and resources.
