How Expensive Is Fiji?

Fiji is considered an affordable destination in the Pacific Islands, with a cost of living that is relatively lower compared to other island nations like Tonga and Vanuatu. The country offers a great balance of affordability and quality, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. Fiji's cost of living is influenced by its proximity to Australia and New Zealand, which drives up the cost of imported goods, but overall, it remains a great value destination.

Compared to its neighbors, Fiji is generally more affordable than Tonga, but slightly more expensive than Vanuatu. However, Fiji's unique cultural experiences, beautiful beaches, and lush forests make it a worthwhile destination for travelers. The cost of living in Fiji can vary depending on the location, with the capital city Suva being more expensive than other parts of the country. Overall, Fiji offers a great combination of affordability, natural beauty, and cultural richness, making it an excellent choice for travelers and expats.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs
  • Eat at local eateries and markets to experience traditional Fijian cuisine at a lower cost
  • Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transport costs
  • Avoid drinking bottled water and opt for filtered water instead to save money and reduce plastic waste
  • Take advantage of free activities such as visiting beaches, hiking, and exploring local markets
  • Consider visiting during the off-season (April-May or September-November) to get discounts on accommodation and tourist activities

Be sure to research and book accommodations and tours in advance to get the best prices and availability. Additionally, consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot to stay connected and avoid high roaming charges.

Overall, Fiji offers a great balance of affordability and quality, making it an excellent choice for travelers and expats. With some planning and research, visitors can have a comfortable and enjoyable experience in Fiji without breaking the bank.
