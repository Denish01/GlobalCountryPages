[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Fiji is an island nation located in the **Pacific Ocean**, northeast of Australia. It is situated in the **Melanesia** region of Oceania, lying between 15° and 22° south latitude and 175° east and 178° west longitude. The country is surrounded by several smaller islands, including the **Mamanuca Islands** and the **Yasawa Islands**. Fiji is not bordered by any countries, as it is an archipelago of more than 330 islands, with the two main islands being **Viti Levu** and **Vanua Levu**.

Fiji is strategically located near several countries, including **Tonga** to the east, **Vanuatu** to the west, and **New Zealand** to the southwest. The country's location in the Pacific Ocean makes it a popular destination for tourists and a significant player in regional trade and commerce. The **Fiji Strait**, which separates Viti Levu from Vanua Levu, is an important waterway that connects the **Koro Sea** to the **Bligh Water**. The country's unique location and geography have made it an attractive destination for travelers and a significant contributor to the regional economy.
