[SECTION] What Is Fiji? [/SECTION]
Fiji is a **sovereign** island nation located in the **Pacific Ocean**, within the continent of **Oceania**. It is an archipelago of more than 330 islands, with the two main islands being Viti Levu and Vanua Levu. Fiji is known for its clear lagoons, palm-lined beaches, and coral reefs, making it a popular destination for tourists and travelers. The country has a diverse culture, with a mix of indigenous Fijian, Indian, and European influences.

Fiji is situated northeast of Australia, and its geographic position makes it a significant location for trade and commerce in the Pacific region. The country has a total land area of approximately 18,274 square kilometers and a coastline of 1,129 kilometers. Fiji's economy is driven by tourism, agriculture, and mining, with sugar, gold, and timber being some of its main exports.

Fiji is also known for its rich cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on traditional customs and practices. The country has a diverse range of flora and fauna, including many species of birds, fish, and plants that are found nowhere else in the world. With its tropical climate and stunning natural beauty, Fiji is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience a unique and unforgettable island getaway.
