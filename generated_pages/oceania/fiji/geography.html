Fiji's terrain is characterized by mountainous islands, with the highest point being Mount Tomanivi, which is located on the island of Viti Levu. The interior of the main islands is covered with tropical rainforests, which are home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The Sigatoka Sand Dunes, located on the Coral Coast of Viti Levu, are a unique feature of Fiji's landscape, with sand dunes stretching for several kilometers along the coast.

The country's coastline is marked by white-sand beaches, coral reefs, and rocky shores. The Mamanuca Islands and the Yasawa Islands are popular destinations for tourists, with their crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and marine life. Fiji's terrain and landscape have made it a popular destination for hiking, surfing, and diving. The country's unique geography has also made it vulnerable to natural disasters, such as cyclones and tsunamis, which can have a significant impact on the country's infrastructure and economy.
