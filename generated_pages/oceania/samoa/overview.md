[SECTION] What Is Samoa? [/SECTION]
Samoa is a **sovereign** island nation located in the **Pacific Ocean**, within the region of **Oceania**. It is situated approximately halfway between **Hawaii** and **New Zealand**, and is composed of two main islands, **Upolu** and **Savai'i**. Samoa is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly locals. The country has a total land area of approximately 2,842 square kilometers and a population of around 0.22 million people.

Samoa is an independent nation, having gained its independence from **New Zealand** in 1962. The country has a **parliamentary democracy** system of government, with a prime minister as the head of government. The official languages of Samoa are **Samoan** and **English**, although Samoan is the most widely spoken language. The country has a diverse economy, with a mix of traditional industries such as **agriculture** and **fishing**, as well as a growing tourism sector.

Samoa is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world with its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush tropical landscapes. The country is also home to a number of unique cultural attractions, including traditional villages, historic sites, and vibrant markets. Samoa is a member of various international organizations, including the **United Nations** and the **Commonwealth of Nations**.
