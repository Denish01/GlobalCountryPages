Cultural Treasures

  • The Falealupo Beach is a stunning example of Samoa's natural beauty, with its white sand and crystal-clear waters.
  • The traditional Samoan Ava Ceremony is an important cultural ritual, where the chief of the village welcomes guests with a ceremonial cup of ava, a traditional Samoan drink.
  • The Robert Louis Stevenson Museum is a historic house museum showcasing the life and works of the famous Scottish author, who lived in Samoa from 1890 until his death in 1894.
  • The Samoan Pe'a is a traditional male tattoo that covers the body from the waist to the knees, and is considered an important part of Samoan cultural heritage.
  • The To Sua Ocean Trench is a unique natural wonder, a massive swimming hole surrounded by lush vegetation and filled with crystal-clear water.

People & Global Impact

  • Tupua Tamasese Meaole was a prominent Samoan chief and politician who played a key role in the country's independence movement.
  • The Samoan diaspora has made significant contributions to the National Rugby League in Australia and New Zealand, with many Samoan players competing at the top level.
  • Fiame Naomi Mata'afa is a prominent Samoan politician who has served as the country's first female Prime Minister, promoting women's rights and empowerment.
  • The Samoan values of Fa'aalo (respect) and Fa'amasino (humility) are highly valued in Samoan culture, and are considered essential for building strong relationships and community cohesion.

What Visitors Love

  • The warm hospitality of the Samoan people is consistently praised by visitors, who appreciate the welcoming and friendly nature of the locals.
  • The traditional Samoan cuisine is a highlight for many visitors, with popular dishes including Fa'ausi (steamed dumplings) and Palusami (steamed taro and coconut cream).
  • The stunning natural scenery of Samoa is a major draw for tourists, with its beautiful beaches, waterfalls, and lush rainforests.
  • The rich cultural heritage of Samoa is a major attraction, with many visitors attending traditional ceremonies and festivals, such as the Samoan Cultural Festival.
  • The relaxed and peaceful atmosphere of Samoa is a welcome respite for visitors looking to escape the stresses of modern life.
  • The opportunities for outdoor adventure are plentiful in Samoa, with popular activities including snorkeling, diving, and hiking.

Hidden Gems

  • The Savai'i Island is a lesser-known destination in Samoa, with its stunning natural beauty, including waterfalls, caves, and black sand beaches.
  • The Lake Lanoto'o is a unique and fascinating natural wonder, a freshwater lake that is home to a variety of birdlife and offers stunning views.
  • The Tafua Peninsula is a scenic and historic area, with its ancient fortifications and stunning coastal scenery.
  • The Salelologa Market is a bustling marketplace where visitors can find traditional Samoan handicrafts, fresh produce, and local cuisine.
