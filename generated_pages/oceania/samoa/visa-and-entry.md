# Samoa Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Samoa before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Samoa has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival. The country's visa stance is designed to promote tourism and facilitate travel for business and other purposes. Citizens of over 50 countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, and many European nations, can enter Samoa visa-free for a specified period, typically up to 60 days.

Samoa's visa policy is also geared towards encouraging investment and trade, with provisions for business travelers and investors. The country's immigration authorities aim to balance the need to facilitate travel with the need to ensure national security and protect the rights of Samoan citizens. As a result, visa requirements and regulations can change, and it is essential to check with the Samoan embassy or consulate for the latest information before planning a trip.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Samoa visa-free for up to 90 days, depending on their nationality. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, but this can be extended up to 180 days in some cases. To extend their stay, tourists must apply to the Samoan immigration authorities and provide proof of sufficient funds and onward travel.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must provide an invitation letter from a Samoan company or organization, as well as proof of their business activities and sufficient funds. Business travelers may be granted a stay of up to 30 days, which can be extended in some cases.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in Samoa must obtain a student visa before arriving in the country. They must provide proof of enrollment in a Samoan educational institution, as well as proof of sufficient funds and health insurance. The duration of a student visa is typically up to 12 months, depending on the course of study.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Samoa must obtain a work visa before arriving in the country. They must have a sponsorship from a Samoan employer and provide proof of their qualifications and experience. The work visa application process can take several weeks, and workers must also obtain a medical certificate and police clearance.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Samoa are Faleolo International Airport and Apia Harbor. Upon arrival, visitors must present their travel documents and complete an arrival card. The immigration officer will review the documents and may ask questions about the purpose of the visit, accommodation, and onward travel. Visitors may also be required to provide biometric data, such as fingerprints. The entry procedure typically takes around 30 minutes, but this can vary depending on the time of arrival and the number of passengers.

Samoa has a relatively straightforward entry procedure, but it is essential to ensure that all travel documents are in order before arriving in the country. Visitors who are unsure about the requirements or have any concerns should contact the Samoan embassy or consulate for guidance.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Failing to check the validity of your passport before traveling

- Not completing the arrival card correctly
- Not having the required documents, such as a visa or health insurance certificate
- Not declaring goods or currency in excess of the allowed limits
- Not respecting Samoan customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes when entering a church or traditional meeting house
