# Where to Stay in Samoa — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Samoa, consider the areas of Apia, Savai'i, and Upolu, which offer a range of accommodations and experiences for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas and neighborhoods in Samoa, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Apia** (Apia)
Best For: Luxury, Business
Vibe: Apia is the bustling capital city of Samoa, with a mix of modern and traditional architecture, and a vibrant cultural scene. The city has a laid-back atmosphere, with many restaurants, bars, and shops to explore.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to government offices, restaurants, and shops, easy access to public transportation, and a range of accommodation options.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, with traffic congestion during peak hours.

**Savai'i** (Savai'i)
Best For: Backpacker, Family
Vibe: Savai'i is a tranquil and picturesque island, with stunning natural beauty, including waterfalls, beaches, and rainforests. The island has a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, making it ideal for those seeking a quiet getaway.
Price Range: $50-150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, stunning natural beauty, and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and snorkeling.
Cons: Limited access to public transportation, and fewer amenities compared to Apia.

**Upolu** (Upolu)
Best For: Luxury, Family
Vibe: Upolu is a scenic island with a mix of modern and traditional villages, and a range of outdoor activities, including surfing and fishing. The island has a laid-back atmosphere, with many beaches and coves to explore.
Price Range: $80-250/night
Pros: Stunning natural beauty, range of outdoor activities, and a mix of modern and traditional villages to explore.
Cons: Can be crowded during peak season, and some areas may have limited access to public transportation.

**Manase** (Savai'i)
Best For: Backpacker, Family
Vibe: Manase is a tranquil and picturesque village, with stunning natural beauty, including beaches, waterfalls, and rainforests. The village has a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, making it ideal for those seeking a quiet getaway.
Price Range: $30-100/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, stunning natural beauty, and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and snorkeling.
Cons: Limited access to public transportation, and fewer amenities compared to Apia.

**Salelologa** (Savai'i)
Best For: Business, Luxury
Vibe: Salelologa is a bustling town, with a mix of modern and traditional architecture, and a vibrant cultural scene. The town has a laid-back atmosphere, with many restaurants, bars, and shops to explore.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Close proximity to government offices, restaurants, and shops, easy access to public transportation, and a range of accommodation options.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, with traffic congestion during peak hours.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]

- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Expedia, to compare prices and find deals.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas.
