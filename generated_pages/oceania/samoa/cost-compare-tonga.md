[SECTION] Overview: Samoa vs Tonga [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Samoa and Tonga can vary significantly, with Samoa being the more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees. Both countries have a relatively low cost of living compared to other Pacific Island nations, but Samoa's lower prices for housing, food, and transportation make it a more attractive choice for those on a budget. The economic context of both countries is similar, with a strong focus on agriculture, fishing, and tourism, but Samoa's more developed infrastructure and larger population contribute to its lower costs.

In terms of overall cost of living, Samoa is around 10-20% cheaper than Tonga, depending on the specific category and location. For example, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center of Apia, Samoa's capital, costs around $400-500 per month, while a similar apartment in Nuku'alofa, Tonga's capital, costs around $600-700 per month. This price difference is reflected in other categories, such as food, transportation, and utilities, making Samoa a more affordable option for those looking to live in the Pacific Islands.
