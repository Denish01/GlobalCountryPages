# Samoa Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Samoa, be sure to try the traditional dishes such as **Fa'ausi**, **Palusami**, and **Oka**, which showcase the country's unique blend of Polynesian and international flavors.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: coconut milk, taro, breadfruit, fish
National Dish: Palusami, a traditional Samoan dish made with taro and coconut cream
Meal Times: 7-9 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 6-8 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% in restaurants and bars
Dietary Notes: Vegetarian-friendly options available, but limited halal and kosher options
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Samoan cuisine is a unique blend of traditional Polynesian flavors and international influences, with a focus on fresh seafood, coconut milk, and root vegetables such as taro and breadfruit. The country's cuisine has been shaped by its history of colonization and trade, with influences from European, Asian, and American cultures. Samoan cuisine is known for its rich flavors and hearty portions, with a focus on community and sharing food with others. Traditional Samoan cuisine is often served at special occasions such as weddings and funerals, and is typically cooked in an **umu**, a traditional underground oven.

Samoan cuisine varies by region, with the coastal areas specializing in fresh seafood dishes such as **Oka** (raw fish marinated in coconut cream and lemon juice) and **Fa'ausi** (steamed fish with coconut cream and vegetables). The inland areas, on the other hand, focus on root vegetables and meats such as pork and chicken. Samoan cuisine is also known for its use of **coconut milk**, which is used in a variety of dishes including **Palusami** and **Fa'ausi**.

The use of **coconut milk** and **taro** is a common theme in Samoan cuisine, and these ingredients are often combined with fresh seafood and meats to create rich and flavorful dishes. Samoan cuisine is also known for its emphasis on community and sharing food with others, with traditional meals often served at special occasions such as weddings and funerals.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Eating with your hands** is common in traditional Samoan cuisine
- **Tipping** is not expected, but 5-10% is appreciated in restaurants and bars
- **Dress code** is casual, but modest clothing is recommended for traditional restaurants
- **Reservations** are not usually necessary, but recommended for large groups
- **Sharing food** is a common practice in Samoan culture, so be prepared to share your meal with others
- **Trying new foods** is encouraged, and you may be offered traditional Samoan dishes such as **Palusami** and **Fa'ausi**
