[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Wallis and Futuna is structured into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education, also known as **enseignement primaire**, is compulsory for all children between the ages of 5 and 11 and is provided free of charge by the government. Secondary education, or **collège**, is also compulsory and caters to students between the ages of 11 and 15. Tertiary education is available but limited, with most students pursuing higher education in France or other countries. The quality of education in Wallis and Futuna is generally good, with a strong emphasis on the French curriculum and language. Public schools are the most common, but there are also some private schools, mainly run by the Catholic Church.

The education system in Wallis and Futuna faces some challenges, including limited resources, a lack of qualified teachers, and a high dropout rate. However, the government has been working to improve the education sector, with a focus on increasing access to education, particularly in rural areas. The use of **technology** has also been introduced in some schools to enhance the learning experience and provide students with better access to educational resources.

Despite the challenges, the education system in Wallis and Futuna has some strengths, including a strong emphasis on **language** and **culture**. The Wallisian language is an important part of the curriculum, and students are encouraged to learn and appreciate their cultural heritage. The education system also provides students with a solid foundation in **mathematics** and **science**, which are essential skills for future careers.
