[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
The housing market in Wallis and Futuna is relatively small and limited, with most of the population living in traditional villages. The rental market is primarily driven by the demand from expatriates and government officials working in the territory. Typical lease terms range from 6 to 12 months, with a deposit requirement of one to two months' rent. Tenant rights are generally protected under French law, which applies in Wallis and Futuna as a territory of France. Compared to its regional neighbors, such as Samoa and Fiji, the housing costs in Wallis and Futuna are relatively moderate, with a lower cost of living but also limited availability of modern amenities.

The housing market in Wallis and Futuna is also influenced by the local culture and customs, with many residents living in traditional houses or **fales**. The government has implemented initiatives to improve the housing stock and provide affordable housing options for the local population. However, the market remains relatively underdeveloped, and prices can vary widely depending on the location, quality, and availability of housing. Overall, the housing market in Wallis and Futuna offers a unique and challenging environment for renters and buyers, with a mix of traditional and modern housing options available.
