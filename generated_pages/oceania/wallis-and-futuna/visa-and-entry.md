# Wallis and Futuna Visa & Entry Requirements

You will likely need a visa to enter Wallis and Futuna, a French territory in Oceania, depending on your nationality and purpose of visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Wallis and Futuna before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Wallis and Futuna has a moderate visa policy, with citizens of certain countries eligible for visa-free access or able to obtain a visa on arrival. The territory's visa policy is largely influenced by its parent country, France, and as such, many European Union citizens can enter without a visa. However, citizens of other countries may need to apply for a visa in advance. Currently, around 30 countries have visa-free access to Wallis and Futuna, including many European nations, Japan, and the United States.

The visa policy of Wallis and Futuna is designed to balance the need to control immigration with the desire to promote tourism and economic development. As a result, the territory offers a range of visa options, including tourist visas, business visas, and student visas. Visitors can stay in Wallis and Futuna for up to 90 days without a visa, depending on their nationality and purpose of visit.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can visit Wallis and Futuna without a visa for up to 90 days, depending on their nationality. They will need to show proof of onward travel and sufficient funds to support their stay. Extensions are possible but must be applied for in advance. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to apply for a business visa, which requires an invitation letter from a local company and proof of business activities. **Students:** Students must apply for a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a local educational institution and sufficient funds to support their stay. The duration of the visa will depend on the length of the study program. **Workers:** Workers must have a job offer from a local employer and apply for a work visa, which requires sponsorship from the employer. The application process can be lengthy, so it's essential to apply well in advance.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry point to Wallis and Futuna is the Hihifo Airport, which receives flights from Tahiti and other Pacific islands. There are no land borders, as the territory is an island nation. Upon arrival, visitors will need to go through immigration and customs, where they will be required to present their travel documents and complete a declaration form. It's essential to ensure that all documents are in order and that visitors have the necessary visas and permits before arriving in the territory.

The immigration process is relatively straightforward, but visitors should be prepared to answer questions about their purpose of visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements. It's also a good idea to have all necessary documents readily available, including proof of onward travel and sufficient funds.
