[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Vanuatu is situated in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, approximately 1,750 kilometers northeast of Australia. The country is an archipelago, consisting of 83 islands, with the largest islands being Espiritu Santo, Malakula, and Efate. Vanuatu is bordered by several bodies of water, including the Pacific Ocean to the north, east, and south, and the Coral Sea to the west. The country's nearest neighbors are Fiji to the west, New Caledonia to the south, and the Solomon Islands to the northwest.

The strategic location of Vanuatu in the Pacific Ocean has made it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region. The country's capital, Port Vila, is located on the island of Efate and is a major center for international trade and tourism. Vanuatu's unique location has also made it a popular destination for travelers and adventurers, with its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and diverse wildlife.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
The terrain of Vanuatu is characterized by volcanic islands, coral reefs, and rugged coastlines. The islands are mountainous, with several active volcanoes, including Mount Yasur on the island of Tanna, which is one of the most accessible active volcanoes in the world. The highest point in Vanuatu is Mount Tabwemasana, located on the island of Espiritu Santo, which rises to an elevation of 1,879 meters. The islands are also home to several rivers, including the Sarakata River and the Jordan River, which flow through the rugged terrain and provide a source of fresh water for the local population.

The landscape of Vanuatu is also characterized by dense forests, which cover much of the islands. The forests are home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including many species of birds, reptiles, and mammals. The coastlines of Vanuatu are also notable for their beauty, with white sandy beaches, coral reefs, and crystal-clear waters. The islands are also home to several natural harbors and bays, including the famous Mele Bay, which is a popular destination for sailors and yachtsmen.

The unique terrain and landscape of Vanuatu have also made it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with activities such as hiking, diving, and snorkeling being popular among visitors.
