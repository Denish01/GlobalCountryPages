[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Chief Roi Mata's Domain** is a UNESCO World Heritage site that showcases the country's rich cultural heritage and traditional way of life.
- The **Vanuatu Sand Drawing** tradition is a unique and intricate art form that tells stories and conveys messages through designs drawn on the sand.
- The **Vanuatu National Museum** is a treasure trove of cultural artifacts and historical exhibits that provide insights into the country's history and traditions.
- The **Ekasup Cultural Village** is a popular tourist destination that showcases the country's traditional music, dance, and art.
- The **Port Vila Market** is a bustling marketplace that sells local handicrafts, fresh produce, and traditional food.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Kalkot Mataskelekele**, a Vanuatuan politician, played a key role in the country's independence movement and served as the country's first prime minister.
- The **Vanuatu diaspora** community has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Vanuatuans working abroad as nurses, teachers, and entrepreneurs.
- **Father Walter Lini**, a Vanuatuan priest and politician, was a key figure in the country's independence movement and served as the country's first minister of foreign affairs.
- The **Vanuatu values** of community, respect, and reciprocity are highly regarded and have been recognized as an important part of the country's cultural heritage.

- The **hospitality** of the Vanuatuan people is legendary, with visitors often commenting on the warm and welcoming nature of the locals.
- The **local food** is a fusion of traditional and modern cuisine, with popular dishes including **laplap**, **tuluk**, and **kava**.
- The **scenery** is breathtaking, with stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush tropical forests.
- The **diving and snorkeling** opportunities are world-class, with numerous coral reefs and shipwrecks to explore.
- The **cultural festivals** are a highlight of the calendar, with the **Vanuatu Festival** and the **Port Vila Festival** showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage.
- The **eco-tourism** initiatives are highly regarded, with many visitors commenting on the country's commitment to sustainable tourism and environmental conservation.
