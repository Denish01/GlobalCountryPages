[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Vanuatu is divided into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 12, and is provided by both public and private schools. Secondary education is also available, although it is not compulsory, and is provided by a range of public and private schools, including **technical and vocational schools**. Tertiary education is available at the University of the South Pacific, which has a campus in Vanuatu, as well as at a range of other institutions, including **teacher training colleges** and **nursing schools**. The quality of education in Vanuatu is generally considered to be good, although there are some challenges, particularly in rural areas where access to education can be limited.

The education system in Vanuatu is overseen by the Ministry of Education, which is responsible for setting education policy and overseeing the implementation of education programs. The ministry works closely with a range of stakeholders, including schools, **community groups**, and **international organizations**, to ensure that education in Vanuatu is of high quality and meets the needs of all students. Despite the many strengths of the education system in Vanuatu, there are some challenges, particularly in terms of **access to education** and **teacher training**.

Overall, the education system in Vanuatu provides students with a range of opportunities to pursue their academic and professional goals, and is an important part of the country's social and economic development. The government of Vanuatu is committed to improving the education system, and has implemented a range of initiatives aimed at increasing access to education and improving the quality of education.
