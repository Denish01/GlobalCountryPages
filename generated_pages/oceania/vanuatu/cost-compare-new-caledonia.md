[SECTION] Overview: Vanuatu vs New Caledonia [/SECTION]
Vanuatu and New Caledonia are two Pacific island nations with distinct economic profiles. Vanuatu is a **sovereign state** with a relatively low GDP per capita, while New Caledonia is a **special collectivity** of France with a much higher GDP per capita. The cost of living in Vanuatu is generally lower due to its smaller economy and lower prices for goods and services. In contrast, New Caledonia has a more developed economy and a higher cost of living, driven by its strong ties to France and a more extensive range of amenities and services.

The overall cost of living in Vanuatu is around 20-30% lower than in New Caledonia, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a more affordable Pacific island lifestyle. However, it's essential to note that New Caledonia offers a more extensive range of amenities and services, including better healthcare and education facilities, which may be worth the extra cost for some individuals. The economic context of both countries is also influenced by their geography, with Vanuatu being more prone to natural disasters and New Caledonia having a more stable climate.
