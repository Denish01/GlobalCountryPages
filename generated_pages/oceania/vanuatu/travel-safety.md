# Vanuatu Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Vanuatu is generally a safe country to visit, with low crime rates and a strong focus on tourism, but as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Vanuatu is an archipelago of 83 islands in the Pacific Ocean, known for its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly people. The country has a relatively low crime rate, with most crimes being petty in nature, such as theft and pickpocketing. However, as with any tourist destination, it's essential to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety. The country is also prone to natural disasters, such as cyclones and earthquakes, which can pose a significant risk to travelers.

The Vanuatu government has taken steps to improve safety and security for tourists, including the establishment of a tourist police force and the implementation of various safety measures, such as CCTV cameras and improved lighting in public areas. Overall, Vanuatu is a safe and welcoming destination for travelers, but it's essential to be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas, such as markets and public transportation
- Be aware of **scams** targeting tourists, such as fake tour operators and **ATM scams**
- Be cautious of **cyclones** and **earthquakes**, which can pose a significant risk to travelers
- Be aware of **road safety** risks, such as rough roads and limited lighting
- Be cautious of **waterborne illnesses**, such as **dysentery** and **cholera**
- Be aware of **limited medical facilities** and **emergency services** in rural areas
- Be cautious of **coral reefs** and **strong currents** when swimming or snorkeling
- Be aware of **customs** and **traditions**, and respect local **culture** and **environment**

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, and Typhoid fever are recommended
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities are limited, especially in rural areas, and medical evacuation may be necessary in serious cases
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacy access is limited, especially in rural areas, and it's essential to bring necessary medications and supplies

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure
- Use reputable tour operators and transportation services
- Avoid traveling alone at night and use licensed taxis
- Keep important phone numbers and documents handy
- Be prepared for natural disasters, such as cyclones and earthquakes
- Respect local customs and traditions
- Learn some basic **Bislama** phrases, such as "hello" ( Halo ) and "thank you" ( Tank yu )
- Bring necessary medications and supplies, such as sunscreen and insect repellent

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **harassment** and **catcalling**, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid traveling alone at night and use licensed taxis
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure
- Respect local **customs** and **traditions**, and dress modestly
- Consider staying in **reputable** and **secure** accommodations, such as hotels or resorts
