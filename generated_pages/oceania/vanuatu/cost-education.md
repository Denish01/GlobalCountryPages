# Education Costs in Vanuatu

The cost of education in Vanuatu varies depending on the type of institution and level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Vanuatu is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the largest. Public schools in Vanuatu are generally free or subsidized, but the quality of education can vary depending on the location and resources available. The language of instruction in public schools is typically **Bislama**, **English**, or **French**, reflecting the country's linguistic diversity. Compulsory education in Vanuatu lasts for 6 years, from ages 6 to 12, and is designed to provide students with a basic education in core subjects such as mathematics, science, and language.

In contrast, private schools in Vanuatu often have better resources and higher-quality teaching, but can be expensive. Private schools may also offer a more internationalized curriculum, with instruction in **English** or **French**. International schools are also available in Vanuatu, particularly in the capital city of **Port Vila**, and offer a range of curricula such as the **International Baccalaureate** or **Cambridge International Examinations**. These schools cater primarily to expatriate families and wealthy locals, and can be very expensive.
