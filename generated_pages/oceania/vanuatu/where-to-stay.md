# Where to Stay in Vanuatu — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When planning your trip to Vanuatu, the best places to stay are in and around Port Vila, the capital, and on the island of Efate, which offer a range of accommodations and easy access to the country's main attractions.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas and neighborhoods, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Port Vila** (Port Vila)
Best For: Luxury, family, business
Vibe: Port Vila is the bustling capital of Vanuatu, with a mix of modern amenities, cultural attractions, and traditional markets, offering a unique blend of urban and island life. The area is known for its vibrant atmosphere, with a range of restaurants, bars, and shops to explore.
Price Range: $150-500/night
Pros: Easy access to amenities, cultural attractions, and water activities, a wide range of dining options, and a variety of accommodation types to suit all budgets. 
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, traffic in the city center can be a challenge.

**Mele** (Mele)
Best For: Backpacker, family
Vibe: Mele is a coastal village just outside Port Vila, known for its stunning beaches, waterfalls, and outdoor activities, offering a more relaxed and peaceful atmosphere. The area is popular with families and backpackers, with a range of budget-friendly accommodations and activities.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, outdoor activities like snorkeling and hiking, and a more laid-back atmosphere, with a range of affordable accommodation options. 
Cons: Limited amenities and dining options compared to Port Vila, can be affected by coastal erosion.

**Pango** (Pango)
Best For: Luxury, couples
Vibe: Pango is an upscale neighborhood on the outskirts of Port Vila, with luxurious resorts and stunning ocean views, offering a romantic and secluded atmosphere. The area is known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning sunsets.
Price Range: $200-600/night
Pros: Luxurious accommodations, stunning ocean views, and a romantic atmosphere, with easy access to Port Vila's amenities. 
Cons: Can be expensive, limited dining and entertainment options in the immediate area, and may require a car or taxi to get around.

**Efate Island** (Efate)
Best For: Family, adventure
Vibe: Efate Island is the main island of Vanuatu, with a range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and stunning natural beauty, offering a unique and authentic island experience. The area is popular with families and adventure seekers, with a range of activities like hiking, snorkeling, and diving.
Price Range: $50-250/night
Pros: Stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities like hiking and snorkeling, and a range of accommodation options, with easy access to Port Vila and other attractions. 
Cons: Can be affected by weather conditions, limited amenities and services in some areas, and may require a car or taxi to get around.

**Tanna Island** (Tanna)
Best For: Adventure, backpacker
Vibe: Tanna Island is a remote and rugged island in southern Vanuatu, known for its active volcano, stunning landscapes, and traditional culture, offering a unique and off-the-beaten-path experience. The area is popular with adventure seekers and backpackers, with a range of outdoor activities and cultural experiences.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Unique cultural experiences, stunning landscapes, and outdoor activities like hiking and volcano trekking, with a range of budget-friendly accommodation options. 
Cons: Limited amenities and services, can be challenging to get around, and may require a significant amount of planning and preparation.

**Luganville** (Luganville)
Best For: Family, business
Vibe: Luganville is the second-largest town in Vanuatu, located on the island of Espiritu Santo, with a range of amenities, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities, offering a more laid-back and peaceful atmosphere. The area is popular with families and business travelers, with a range of accommodation options and activities.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Range of amenities and services, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities like snorkeling and diving, with easy access to the island's main attractions. 
Cons: Can be affected by weather conditions, limited dining and entertainment options compared to Port Vila, and may require a car or taxi to get around.

**Santo** (Santo)
Best For: Luxury, couples
Vibe: Santo is a luxurious neighborhood on the island of Espiritu Santo, with stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and high-end resorts, offering a romantic and secluded atmosphere. The area is known for its beautiful beaches, stunning sunsets, and luxurious amenities.
Price Range: $250-800/night
Pros: Luxurious accommodations, stunning beaches, and a romantic atmosphere, with easy access to outdoor activities and cultural attractions. 
Cons: Can be expensive, limited dining and entertainment options in the immediate area, and may require a car or taxi to get around.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (June to September), to ensure availability and the best rates.
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider working with a local travel agent or tour operator to book accommodations and activities, as they often have insider knowledge and can provide personalized recommendations.
- Be sure to read reviews and check the cancellation policies before booking, as some properties may have strict cancellation policies.
- Negotiate the price, especially if you're booking directly with a property or staying for an extended period.
- Look for packages and special deals, which can include activities, meals, and other perks, and can often provide better value than booking individual components separately.
- Consider booking a room or package that includes breakfast, as this can be a convenient and cost-effective option.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Research the area and choose a safe and reputable property, with good reviews and a secure location.
- Consider the proximity to amenities, attractions, and transportation when selecting a property, as this can make a big difference in the convenience and enjoyment of your stay.
- Be aware of the local culture and customs, and respect the environment and local communities, as this can help to ensure a positive and respectful experience.
- Take necessary precautions to protect yourself and your belongings, such as using a hotel safe or locking your room, and being mindful of your surroundings.
- Check the weather forecast before traveling, as Vanuatu can be affected by cyclones and other natural disasters, and plan accordingly.
- Consider purchasing travel insurance, which can provide protection against unexpected events and help to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.
