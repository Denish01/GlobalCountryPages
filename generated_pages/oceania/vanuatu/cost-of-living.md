# Cost of Living in Vanuatu - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Vanuatu is generally moderate, with a daily budget of $80-150 USD per person for mid-range travelers, making it an affordable destination for tourists and expats alike.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $40-80 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $80-150 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $200-300 USD
Currency: VUV
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Vanuatu? [/SECTION]
Vanuatu is an island nation in the Pacific, known for its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and relatively low cost of living. Compared to other Pacific island nations, Vanuatu is moderately priced, offering a good balance between affordability and quality of life. The cost of living in Vanuatu is lower than in countries like Fiji and New Caledonia, but higher than in the Solomon Islands. Overall, Vanuatu offers a great value for travelers and expats looking for a unique and affordable island experience.

In terms of global averages, Vanuatu is generally more expensive than many countries in Southeast Asia and Central America, but less expensive than countries in Europe and North America. The cost of living in Vanuatu is also influenced by the country's remote location, which can drive up the cost of imported goods and transportation. However, with some planning and research, visitors can find affordable accommodation, food, and activities that fit their budget.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a luxury resort to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local markets or street food stalls to try traditional cuisine at a lower price.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transportation costs.
- Buy groceries and cook your own meals instead of eating out for every meal.
- Take advantage of free activities like visiting the local market, exploring the town, or going to the beach.
- Negotiate prices when shopping at local markets or with taxi drivers to get the best deal.

Overall, Vanuatu offers a unique and affordable island experience, with a range of accommodation options, delicious local cuisine, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and cultural exploration. With some planning and research, visitors can have a great time in Vanuatu without breaking the bank.
