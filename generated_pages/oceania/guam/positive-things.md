[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Latte of Freedom** is a historic monument that commemorates the island's liberation from Japanese occupation during World War II.
- The **Chamorro Village** is a popular cultural attraction that showcases traditional Chamorro huts, food, and handicrafts.
- The **Guam Museum** features exhibits on the island's history, including its colonial past and the impact of World War II.
- The **Merizo Bell Tower** is a historic landmark that dates back to the 19th century and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
- The **Inarajan Pools** are a natural wonder, featuring a series of crystal-clear pools and waterfalls that are perfect for swimming and relaxation.

- **Benjamin J. Cruz** is a notable Guamanian politician who has served as the island's governor and has been a vocal advocate for Guam's self-governance.
- The **Guamanian diaspora** has made significant contributions to the United States, particularly in the fields of military service, healthcare, and education.
- **Sioux Watson** is a renowned Guamanian artist who has exhibited her work internationally, showcasing the island's unique cultural heritage.

- The **hospitality of the Guamanian people** is consistently praised by visitors, who appreciate the warm welcome and friendly service they receive.
- The **local cuisine** is a fusion of Chamorro, Spanish, and Asian flavors, featuring popular dishes such as **kelaguen** and **finadene**.
- The **beaches and scenery** of Guam are stunning, with popular spots like **Tumon Bay** and **Gun Beach** offering crystal-clear waters and picturesque views.
- The **water sports and activities** available on the island, including snorkeling, diving, and paddleboarding, are a major draw for visitors.
- The **Hagatna Heritage Walking Trail** is a popular attraction that takes visitors on a historic tour of the island's capital city.
- The **Guam International Marathon** is a major sporting event that attracts runners from around the world.
