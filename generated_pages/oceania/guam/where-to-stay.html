When visiting Guam, the best areas to stay are in the central and northern parts of the island, particularly in the cities of Tamuning, Tumon, and Hagatna, which offer a range of accommodations and easy access to popular attractions.

Best Areas to Stay

For each of the top areas, here's a brief overview:

Tumon (Tamuning)

Best For: Luxury / Family / Business

Vibe: Tumon is known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife, making it an ideal spot for families, luxury travelers, and business visitors. The area is also home to many high-end resorts, restaurants, and shopping centers.

Price Range: $200-$500/night

Pros: Easy access to beaches, plenty of dining and shopping options, variety of accommodations

Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, especially at night

Tamuning (Tamuning)

Best For: Backpacker / Mid-range

Vibe: Tamuning is a bustling city with a mix of local culture, shopping, and dining options, making it a great spot for backpackers and mid-range travelers. The area is also close to the airport and has a variety of accommodations.

Price Range: $80-$200/night

Pros: Affordable, convenient location, plenty of local eateries

Cons: Traffic can be heavy, limited nightlife options

Hagatna (Hagatna)

Best For: Business / History

Vibe: Hagatna is the capital city of Guam and is steeped in history and culture, making it a great spot for business travelers and history buffs. The area is also home to many government buildings, museums, and historic landmarks.

Price Range: $100-$300/night

Pros: Rich history, convenient location, variety of cultural attractions

Cons: Limited nightlife options, can be quiet

Dededo (Dededo)

Best For: Family / Mid-range

Vibe: Dededo is a suburban area with a mix of residential and commercial properties, making it a great spot for families and mid-range travelers. The area is also close to many beaches and outdoor recreational activities.

Price Range: $80-$200/night

Pros: Affordable, convenient location, plenty of outdoor activities

Cons: Limited dining and shopping options, can be quiet

Mangilao (Mangilao)

Best For: Backpacker / Nature

Vibe: Mangilao is a rural area with a mix of natural beauty and outdoor recreational activities, making it a great spot for backpackers and nature lovers. The area is also close to many hiking trails and scenic viewpoints.

Price Range: $60-$150/night

Pros: Affordable, natural beauty, plenty of outdoor activities

Cons: Limited amenities, can be remote

Booking Tips

  • Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (December to April)
  • Use online booking platforms like Expedia, Booking.com, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals
  • Consider booking a package that includes flights and accommodations for discounts
  • Negotiate with hotel staff or property owners for long-term stays or off-season bookings
  • Be aware of seasonal pricing, with rates increasing during holidays and peak season
  • Check for any additional fees or taxes when booking accommodations
  • Read reviews and check the property's location before booking to ensure it meets your needs

Safety & Location Tips

  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night, especially in rural areas
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas like Tumon and Tamuning
  • Keep valuables secure and use hotel safes or lockers when available
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
  • Be mindful of Guam's unique cultural norms and customs, especially when visiting rural areas or attending local events
  • Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation, such as the Guam Public Transportation System (GPT) bus routes
