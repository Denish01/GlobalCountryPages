Guam is generally a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a strong emphasis on visitor safety, but as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Guam is a US territory in the Pacific Ocean, with a population of around 153,836 people, and its safety record is generally good. The island has a low crime rate compared to other destinations, and visitors can feel relatively safe walking around during the day and night. However, as with any popular tourist destination, there are some areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The local authorities take the safety of tourists seriously, and there are measures in place to prevent and respond to incidents.

The risk of natural disasters, such as typhoons and earthquakes, is higher in Guam due to its location in the Pacific Ocean. Visitors should be aware of the risks and take necessary precautions, such as staying informed about weather conditions and following evacuation instructions if necessary. In addition, the risk of scams and petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, is moderate, and visitors should be cautious when interacting with strangers or using public transportation.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Scams targeting tourists, such as **credit card skimming** and **ATM scams**, are common in popular tourist areas
- **Pickpocketing** and **theft** can occur in crowded areas, such as markets and public transportation
- **Drunk driving** is a risk, especially at night, and visitors should exercise caution when walking or driving
- **Typhoons** and **earthquakes** can occur, and visitors should stay informed about weather conditions and follow evacuation instructions if necessary
- **Rip currents** can be strong at some beaches, and visitors should swim at lifeguarded beaches and follow safety instructions
- **Sunburn** and **heat exhaustion** can be risks, especially for visitors who are not used to the tropical climate
- **Scams** targeting **online bookings** and **rental cars** are common, and visitors should be cautious when making online transactions

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Guam, but visitors should ensure they are up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink, but visitors may prefer to use **bottled water** as a precaution
- **Medical Facilities:** Guam has a well-equipped hospital and several medical clinics, with **quality medical care** available, but visitors may need to show **proof of insurance** or pay upfront for treatment
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are readily available, and **prescription medications** can be easily obtained with a valid prescription

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night
- Keep valuables secure and use a **hotel safe** or **lockbox** if possible
- Use **reputable taxi services** or **ride-sharing apps**, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use **credit cards** or **ATMs** instead
- Stay informed about local conditions, including weather and crime risks
- Follow **traffic rules** and **pedestrian safety guidelines**, and be cautious when driving or walking
- Keep **emergency contact numbers** handy, including the local police and your embassy

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Guam is generally a safe destination for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong emphasis on visitor safety
- However, visitors should still take normal precautions to protect themselves, such as avoiding walking alone at night and using reputable transportation services
- **Harassment** and **stalking** are rare, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and report any incidents to the local authorities
- **Women's health services** are available, including **contraceptives** and **emergency care**, and visitors can access these services at local hospitals and clinics
