# Cost of Living in Guam - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Guam is generally considered to be moderate to expensive, with a daily budget ranging from $100 to $300 per person, depending on individual lifestyle and accommodation choices.

Cost Level: Expensive

Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $150-300 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $300-500 USD
Currency: USD
Tipping Custom: expected (10-15%)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Guam? [/SECTION]
Guam is a United States territory in the Pacific Ocean, and its cost of living is influenced by its remote location and limited economy. Compared to other countries in the region, Guam is more expensive than the Northern Mariana Islands and Micronesia, but less expensive than Hawaii. The cost of living in Guam is also affected by the island's reliance on imported goods, which can drive up prices for food, fuel, and other essentials. Overall, Guam is a relatively expensive destination, but it offers a unique cultural experience and stunning natural beauty that makes it worth visiting.

The cost of living in Guam can vary depending on the location, with the capital city of Hagatna and the tourist area of Tumon Bay tend to be more expensive than other parts of the island. However, with some planning and research, visitors can find affordable accommodations, food, and activities that fit their budget. Guam is a popular destination for tourists from Japan, Korea, and other Asian countries, and it offers a range of amenities and services catering to these visitors.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel to save on accommodation costs
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls to try traditional Chamorro cuisine at affordable prices
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transport costs
- Take advantage of free or low-cost activities such as visiting the beach, hiking, or exploring the island's cultural attractions
- Shop at local markets or grocery stores to buy fresh produce and other essentials at lower prices
- Look for package deals or discounts on accommodations, flights, and activities to save on overall costs
