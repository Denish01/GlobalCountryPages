# Education Costs in Guam

The cost of education in Guam varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools can range from $4,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Guam is based on the American model, with 12 years of compulsory education. Public schools are free or subsidized for local students, while private schools offer a more specialized education with higher fees. The language of instruction is English, but Chamorro is also taught as a second language. Public schools in Guam generally have a good reputation, but some may lack resources and facilities compared to private schools. International schools are available in Guam, offering curricula such as the International Baccalaureate (IB) and the Advanced Placement (AP) program.

The quality of education in Guam is generally good, with a literacy rate of 99%. However, some public schools may face challenges such as limited resources and high student-teacher ratios. Private schools, on the other hand, often have smaller class sizes and more resources, but can be expensive. International schools in Guam offer a high-quality education, but often come with a higher price tag. Expats and international students may find that these schools offer a more familiar curriculum and teaching style.
