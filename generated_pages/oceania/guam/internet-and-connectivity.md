# Guam Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Guam is generally reliable and widespread, with several options for getting a SIM card or accessing WiFi, making it a suitable destination for tourists and remote workers alike.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Guam is of good quality, with an average internet speed of 25 Mbps download, which is sufficient for streaming and online browsing. However, there is a notable urban vs rural divide, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in urban areas such as Hagatna and Tamuning, while rural areas may experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Despite this, the overall reliability of the internet in Guam is good, with most providers offering stable connections and minimal downtime.

The internet infrastructure in Guam has improved significantly in recent years, with the introduction of 5G networks and the expansion of fiber-optic cables. This has enabled faster and more reliable connections, making it easier for residents and visitors to stay connected and access online services. Additionally, the **United States** territory status of Guam has facilitated the development of a modern and well-maintained telecommunications infrastructure, which has contributed to the country's good internet quality.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Guam is relatively easy, with several options available for tourists and visitors. The main carriers, Docomo Pacific, GTA, and iConnect, offer a range of plans and packages, including tourist-friendly options with data and voice minutes. To purchase a SIM card, visitors will need to provide a valid passport and may be required to register with the carrier. SIM cards can be purchased at carrier stores, as well as at some retail outlets and convenience stores.

It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and most reliable connections. Some popular plans for tourists include data-only packages, which can be a cost-effective option for those who only need to access the internet. The cost of a SIM card can vary, but tourists can expect to pay around 20 USD for a basic plan.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality in Guam is generally good, with most hotels and resorts offering free WiFi to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is also prevalent, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are available in major towns, offering reliable and fast WiFi connections, as well as other amenities such as meeting rooms and printing services.
- Public WiFi is available in some areas, including the airport and some public parks, but the quality and reliability can vary.
- Many restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi to their customers, making it easy to stay connected while dining out.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces such as The Hive and Guam Coworking Space offer reliable and fast WiFi connections, as well as other amenities such as meeting rooms and printing services.
- Reliable cafe chains such as Starbucks and The Coffee Bean & Tea Leaf offer free WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options such as portable WiFi hotspots and mobile networks can be useful in case of an outage or when working remotely.
- VPNs can be necessary for remote workers who need to access sensitive information or connect to company networks securely.
- Some hotels and resorts also offer coworking spaces and meeting rooms, making them a good option for remote workers who need to stay connected and productive.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major concern in Guam, but some websites may be blocked due to **US** regulations.
- Using a VPN can help to protect your online security and privacy, especially when using public WiFi networks.
- Roaming alternatives such as portable WiFi hotspots and local SIM cards can be more cost-effective than using international roaming services.
- eSIM options are available from some carriers, offering a convenient and flexible way to stay connected.
- It is recommended to check the coverage and reliability of your carrier before purchasing a SIM card or signing up for a plan.
- Be aware of data limits and speeds when using mobile networks, as excessive usage can result in extra charges or slowed speeds.
