The tax rates in Tuvalu range from 0% to 30% for individuals, with a corporate tax rate of 30%, and a Value-Added Tax (VAT) rate of 15%, making it essential for expats and businesses to understand the tax system to navigate their financial obligations.

[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
Tuvalu has a **territorial taxation system**, meaning that only income earned within the country is subject to tax, and foreign-earned income is generally exempt. To be considered a tax resident in Tuvalu, an individual must be physically present in the country for at least 183 days in a calendar year. The country's tax system is relatively simple, with a focus on income tax, corporate tax, and VAT.

The tax authorities in Tuvalu are responsible for administering and collecting taxes, and they provide guidance on tax-related matters to residents and businesses. The tax year in Tuvalu runs from January to December, and taxpayers are required to file their tax returns by the end of April of the following year. Understanding the tax system in Tuvalu is crucial for individuals and businesses to comply with tax laws and regulations.

[SECTION] Corporate & Business Tax [/SECTION]
- The **corporate tax rate** in Tuvalu is 30%, which applies to all businesses operating in the country.
- Small businesses in Tuvalu may be eligible for **incentives**, such as reduced tax rates or exemptions, depending on their size and industry.
- There are no **free zones** in Tuvalu, but the government has established several **economic development zones** to promote investment and economic growth.

- Companies in Tuvalu are also required to maintain **accounting records** and file annual tax returns with the tax authorities.

[SECTION] VAT / Sales Tax [/SECTION]
- The **standard VAT rate** in Tuvalu is 15%, which applies to most goods and services.
- There are **reduced VAT rates** for certain essential items, such as food and medicine, which are taxed at a rate of 5%.
- Some goods and services, such as **education** and **healthcare**, are **exempt** from VAT.
- Tuvalu has a **tourist refund scheme**, which allows international visitors to claim a refund of VAT paid on certain goods and services.

[SECTION] For Expats & Foreign Workers [/SECTION]
- **Tax residency rules** in Tuvalu are based on physical presence, and individuals who are present in the country for at least 183 days in a calendar year are considered tax residents.
- Tuvalu has no **double taxation treaties** with other countries, which means that foreign workers may be subject to taxation in both their home country and Tuvalu.
- Expats and foreign workers in Tuvalu are required to **register** with the tax authorities and obtain a tax identification number.
- Foreign workers in Tuvalu may be eligible for **social security** benefits, depending on their employment status and duration of stay.
- Expats and foreign workers in Tuvalu are subject to **remittance rules**, which govern the transfer of funds out of the country.
- Foreign workers in Tuvalu may also be subject to **withholding tax** on their income, which is deducted by their employer and remitted to the tax authorities.

[SECTION] Crypto & Investment Income [/SECTION]
- **Investment income**, such as dividends and interest, is subject to income tax in Tuvalu.
- **Capital gains** are included in taxable income and are subject to the same tax rates as ordinary income.
- **Cryptocurrency** is not specifically regulated in Tuvalu, but it is subject to the same tax rules as other forms of income.
- Investors in Tuvalu may be eligible for **tax incentives**, such as reduced tax rates or exemptions, depending on the type of investment and the location of the investment.
