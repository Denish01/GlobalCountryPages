# Education Costs in Tuvalu

The cost of education in Tuvalu is relatively low, with the government allocating 12.8% of its GDP towards education, making it largely subsidized for local students.

[FACTBOX]
Education Spend (% GDP): 12.8%
Literacy Rate: 95%
School System: 10 years of compulsory education
Academic Year: 12 months
Currency: AUD
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Tuvalu is largely public, with a few private schools available. The language of instruction is **Tuvaluan** and **English**, with an emphasis on bilingual education. The public education system is generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong focus on **primary education** and **secondary education**. Compulsory education lasts for 10 years, from age 5 to 15. International schools are not readily available in Tuvalu, but some schools may offer **International Baccalaureate** or **Cambridge International Examinations** programs.

The quality of education in Tuvalu is generally considered to be on par with other Pacific Island nations. However, there are some challenges, including a lack of resources and limited access to **higher education** opportunities. The government has implemented various initiatives to improve the education system, including **teacher training programs** and **infrastructure development**. Despite these challenges, Tuvalu has a relatively high **literacy rate**, with around 95% of the population able to read and write.
