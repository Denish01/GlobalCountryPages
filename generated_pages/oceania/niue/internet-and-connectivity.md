# Niue Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Niue is limited but improving, with a few options for getting a SIM card and accessing WiFi, although the overall quality and reliability can be variable.

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Niue is generally slow and unreliable, with an average download speed of 5 Mbps. The urban areas, such as the capital Alofi, tend to have better internet connectivity than the rural areas, where the signal can be weak and intermittent. The reliability of the internet is also a concern, with frequent outages and disconnections, especially during peak hours or bad weather. This can be frustrating for users who rely on the internet for work or communication, and it is essential to have a backup plan, such as a mobile hotspot or a satellite phone.

The internet infrastructure in Niue is still developing, and the government is working to improve the connectivity and speed. However, the remote location and limited resources of the island make it challenging to provide high-quality internet services. Despite these challenges, the internet has become an essential tool for the local community, and many residents and businesses rely on it for communication, education, and commerce. The internet has also opened up new opportunities for tourism and economic development, and it is expected to play a crucial role in the island's future growth and development.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Niue, you can visit the local telecom operator, Telecom Niue, or the international carrier, Digicel. You will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, and the SIM card will cost around 20 USD. The SIM card will give you access to the local mobile network, which covers most of the island, although the signal can be weak in some areas. You can also purchase a prepaid plan, which will give you a certain amount of data, voice minutes, and SMS. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable carrier, such as Telecom Niue or Digicel, to ensure that you get a reliable and high-quality service.

The documents needed to purchase a SIM card in Niue include a valid passport, proof of address, and a completed application form. The application form will require your personal details, such as name, address, and contact information, as well as your passport details. You will also need to provide a copy of your passport and proof of address, such as a utility bill or a lease agreement. The process of purchasing a SIM card is relatively straightforward, and the staff at the telecom operator will be able to assist you with the application and activation of your SIM card.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels offering free WiFi to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is limited, with only a few cafes offering WiFi, and the signal can be weak.
- There are no coworking spaces in Niue, but some hotels and resorts offer meeting rooms and conference facilities.
- Public WiFi is not widely available, but some restaurants and bars offer free WiFi to their customers.
- The local library also offers free WiFi, although the signal can be slow and unreliable.
