# Where to Stay in Niue — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Niue, consider the areas around Alofi, the capital, and the coastal regions for a mix of convenience, scenic views, and access to activities like snorkeling and diving. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Niue, the following are recommended:
**Alofi** (Alofi)
Best For: Business / Family
Vibe: Alofi has a laid-back, small-town feel with easy access to government offices, shops, and restaurants, making it ideal for both business travelers and families. The area is relatively quiet and safe.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Central location, access to amenities, easy to get around without a car.
Cons: Limited nightlife, can feel too quiet for some travelers.

**Tamakautoga** (Tamakautoga)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: This coastal area is known for its stunning scenery, including beaches and limestone cliffs, offering a serene and luxurious environment. It's perfect for those seeking relaxation and privacy.
Price Range: $250-450/night
Pros: Scenic views, private accommodations, access to outdoor activities.
Cons: Remote location, may require a car to get around.

**Avatele** (Avatele)
Best For: Backpacker / Family
Vibe: Avatele has a more rustic, village-like atmosphere with affordable accommodations and a beautiful beach, making it suitable for budget-conscious travelers and families looking for a laid-back experience.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Affordable, scenic beach, local culture.
Cons: Limited amenities, may feel too basic for some.

**Makefu** (Makefu)
Best For: Luxury / Couples
Vibe: Makefu offers a secluded and romantic setting, ideal for couples and luxury seekers, with its private villas and stunning views of the ocean.
Price Range: $300-500/night
Pros: Private, scenic, romantic.
Cons: Expensive, may feel isolated.

**Hikutavake** (Hikutavake)
Best For: Family / Business
Vibe: Hikutavake provides a balance between accessibility to Alofi and a more rural, peaceful environment, making it suitable for families and business travelers who want a quieter stay.
Price Range: $120-280/night
Pros: Quiet, accessible, family-friendly.
Cons: Limited dining options, may require a car.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations well in advance, especially during peak season (June to September), to ensure availability.
- Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or directly contact local hotels and guesthouses for the best rates.
- Consider negotiating the price, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with higher rates during peak season.
- Look for packages that include activities or car rentals for better value.
- Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before booking.
- Contact local tourism information for recommendations on accommodations and activities.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Niue is generally a safe destination, but take normal precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.
- Be mindful of the ocean conditions, as Niue's coastline can be rugged and unpredictable.
- Choose accommodations that are close to amenities or have easy access to transportation if you don't plan to rent a car.
- Areas around Alofi are well-lit and safe at night, but it's still advisable to walk in groups or use taxis for late-night travel.
- Respect local customs and the environment, as Niue is known for its pristine natural beauty and unique culture.
- Stay informed about weather conditions, as Niue can be affected by cyclones during the wet season.
