[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Niue is a small island nation with limited public transportation options. The most common modes of transportation are taxis, rental cars, and bicycles. The quality of public transit is relatively low, with no scheduled bus services available. Most people get around the island by renting a car or using a taxi service. The island's scenic coastal roads and lack of traffic make it an ideal destination for those who enjoy driving or cycling.

The island's small size and limited population mean that transportation options are limited, but the friendly locals and stunning scenery make it a great destination for those who enjoy exploring at their own pace. With no traffic lights or crowded streets, Niue is a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the island. The main road that circles the island is well-maintained, and the scenic views of the coastline and surrounding landscape make for a pleasant drive.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider renting a car for the duration of your stay, as this can be more cost-effective than using taxis or other services.
- Be sure to book your car rental in advance, as availability can be limited, especially during peak travel seasons.
- When using a taxi, be sure to agree on the fare before you start your journey, as some taxis may not have meters.
- Consider purchasing a **Niue SIM card** or portable Wi-Fi hotspot to stay connected and access important travel information during your stay.
- Always wear a helmet when cycling, as **road safety** is a concern in Niue, especially on the island's winding coastal roads.
- Be respectful of the island's environment and **wildlife**, and take care to avoid damaging the coral reefs or disturbing the local wildlife when snorkeling or diving.
