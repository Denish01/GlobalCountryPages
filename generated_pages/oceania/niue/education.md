The education system in Niue is based on the New Zealand curriculum, with some modifications to reflect the unique cultural and environmental context of the island. The system is divided into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education is compulsory for all children from the age of 5 to 11, and is provided by three primary schools on the island. Secondary education is also compulsory, and is provided by Niue High School, which offers a range of subjects including mathematics, science, English, and social studies. Tertiary education is not available on the island, but students can apply to study at universities in New Zealand or other countries through scholarships or other programs.

The quality of education in Niue is generally good, with a strong focus on providing students with a well-rounded education that includes academic, vocational, and extracurricular activities. However, the government is working to address these challenges through initiatives such as teacher training programs and the introduction of new technologies to support learning.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
The education system in Niue faces some challenges, including:
- Limited resources and infrastructure, which can make it difficult to provide students with access to modern technologies and educational materials.
- A lack of specialized teachers in certain subjects, which can limit the range of courses and programs available to students.
- The brain drain of skilled and educated Niueans who leave the island to pursue career opportunities abroad.
- The need for more vocational training and career guidance to help students prepare for the workforce.
- The importance of preserving and promoting the Niuean language and culture, which is an important part of the island's identity and heritage.
