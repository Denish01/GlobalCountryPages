# Getting Around Niue — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Niue, you can use a combination of flights, buses, taxis, and car rentals, as the island nation has a limited but reliable transportation network.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Niue is accessible by air, with the Hanan International Airport being the main entry point. The airport is located near the capital city of Alofi and receives direct flights from Auckland, New Zealand, which is the primary hub for international connections. You can fly to Auckland from major cities around the world and then take a connecting flight to Niue. There are no overland entry points to Niue, as it is a small island nation in the Pacific Ocean. 

The main airlines that operate flights to Niue include Air New Zealand, which offers weekly flights from Auckland. It is recommended to book your flights in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, to ensure availability and get the best rates. Additionally, it's essential to research any travel requirements, such as visas or health certificates, before planning your trip to Niue.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
There are no domestic flights within Niue, as the island is small and the airport is the main transportation hub. However, you can take a short flight from the airport to the nearby islands, but these are not typical domestic flights and are usually part of a tour or charter package. If you're looking to explore the surrounding islands, it's best to book a guided tour or charter a private boat.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Niue does not have a rail network, as the island is small and the transportation infrastructure is primarily focused on roads and air travel. As a result, there are no trains or rail services available for getting around the island.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are limited bus services available in Niue, and they mainly operate on a fixed schedule between the capital city of Alofi and other villages. The buses are not very frequent, and the routes are limited, but they can be a affordable way to get around the island. You can also hire a private coach or shuttle service for group tours or transportation.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Niue, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The road conditions in Niue are moderate, with some paved roads and some dirt tracks, especially in rural areas.
- Fuel costs are relatively high in Niue, so it's essential to factor this into your transportation budget.
- Car rental companies are available at the airport and in Alofi, and they offer a range of vehicles, including cars, scooters, and bikes.
- When driving in Niue, be aware of the **left-hand traffic** rule and watch out for **poor road conditions** and **wildlife** on the roads.
- It's also recommended to **rent a 4WD vehicle** if you plan to explore the island's rugged terrain and rural areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be sure to **book your flights and accommodations in advance**, especially during peak travel seasons, to avoid high prices and availability issues.
- **Research local transportation options** before arriving in Niue to get an idea of the available services and prices.
- **Download local apps**, such as maps and transportation guides, to help you navigate the island.
- **Be prepared for limited internet and phone coverage** in some areas of the island, and plan accordingly.
- **Respect local customs and traditions** when using public transportation or interacting with locals.
- **Bring cash**, as not all businesses in Niue accept credit or debit cards, and **have a backup plan** in case of an emergency or transportation disruption.
- **Consider purchasing travel insurance** that covers transportation and accommodation costs in case of unexpected changes or cancellations.
- **Stay informed** about local events and festivals that may affect transportation services and plan your itinerary accordingly.
