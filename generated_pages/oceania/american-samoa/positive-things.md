[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **National Park of American Samoa** is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and one of the most remote national parks in the US, featuring stunning scenery and diverse wildlife.
- The **Samoan Pe'a**, a traditional male tattoo, is an important part of American Samoan culture and is considered a rite of passage for young men.
- The **Faleomavaega National Park** is a historic site that preserves the traditional Samoan village of Faleomavaega and offers insights into the territory's rich cultural heritage.
- The **American Samoa Museum** showcases the history and culture of the territory, with exhibits on traditional Samoan art, fishing, and navigation.
- The **To'aga Beach** is a popular spot for traditional Samoan ceremonies and events, including the annual **American Samoa Festival**.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Eni Faleomavaega**, a former US Representative, was the first American Samoan to serve in the US Congress and played a key role in promoting the territory's interests in Washington.
- The **American Samoan diaspora** has made significant contributions to the US military, with many American Samoans serving in the US Armed Forces.
- **Tanya Edwards**, a Samoan-American artist, has gained international recognition for her work, which explores the experiences of Pacific Islander communities in the US.
- The **American Samoan community** values **fa'aalo**, or respect, which is an important part of the territory's cultural heritage and is reflected in the way people interact with each other and with the environment.
