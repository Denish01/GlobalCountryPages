# American Samoa Visa & Entry Requirements

As a general rule, citizens of the United States and certain other countries do not need a visa to enter American Samoa, but it is essential to verify the current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of American Samoa before traveling.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of American Samoa before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
American Samoa has a moderate visa policy, with citizens of around 50 countries eligible for visa-free access or visa on arrival. The territory's visa stance is designed to balance tourism and economic development with security and immigration concerns. Citizens of the United States, Australia, Canada, and the European Union, among others, can enter American Samoa without a visa, while citizens of other countries may need to obtain a visa in advance or on arrival.

The visa policy of American Samoa is influenced by its relationship with the United States, and citizens of the US are generally exempt from visa requirements. However, citizens of other countries should check the official government website or consult with the embassy or consulate of American Samoa to determine their specific visa requirements.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter American Samoa without a visa if they are citizens of eligible countries, and they can stay for up to 90 days. To extend their stay, tourists need to apply for an extension at the immigration office in Pago Pago. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers may need to provide additional documentation, such as an invitation letter or proof of business activities, to enter American Samoa. They may also need to obtain a special permit or license to conduct business in the territory. **Students:** Students who want to study in American Samoa need to provide proof of enrollment in an approved educational institution and may need to obtain a student visa. The duration of the student visa depends on the length of the study program. **Workers:** Workers who want to work in American Samoa need to have a valid job offer and sponsorship from a local employer. They need to apply for a work visa and undergo a medical examination before entering the territory.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry point to American Samoa is Pago Pago International Airport, which receives flights from Honolulu, Hawaii, and other Pacific island destinations. Upon arrival, visitors need to go through immigration and customs procedures, which include presenting their travel documents, completing a declaration form, and undergoing a baggage inspection. Visitors may also be asked questions about their purpose of visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements. The immigration office in Pago Pago is responsible for processing visa applications and extensions, and it is open from Monday to Friday during regular business hours.

In addition to the airport, American Samoa also has a seaport in Pago Pago, which receives cargo ships and cruise liners. Visitors arriving by sea need to follow the same immigration and customs procedures as air travelers. It is essential to note that American Samoa has strict regulations regarding the importation of certain goods, including firearms, narcotics, and protected species.
