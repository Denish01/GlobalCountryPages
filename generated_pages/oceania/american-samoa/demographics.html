Population Overview

American Samoa has a relatively small population, with a total area of 199 kmÂ², making it one of the most densely populated territories in the Pacific. The population has been growing steadily over the years, with a growth rate of 0.3%, although this growth is largely driven by natural increase rather than migration. The majority of the population, approximately 87%, resides in urban areas, with the capital city Pago Pago being the largest urban center. The population is relatively young, with a median age of 24.5 years, and the fertility rate is around 2.5 children per woman.

The population of American Samoa is also characterized by a high level of migration, both within the territory and to other countries, particularly the United States. Many American Samoans migrate to the US mainland for education, employment, or military service, and some eventually return to the territory, bringing with them new skills, ideas, and cultural practices. This migration has contributed to the unique cultural landscape of American Samoa, with a blend of traditional Samoan and American influences.

The population size of American Samoa is relatively small compared to other Pacific Island territories, and the government has implemented various measures to promote population growth and development, including investments in education, healthcare, and infrastructure. However, the territory still faces challenges related to population growth, including limited job opportunities, overcrowding, and pressure on natural resources.

The remaining population consists of Asian, Caucasian, and other Pacific Islander groups, who have migrated to the territory for various reasons, including economic opportunities, education, or family ties. The ethnic relations in American Samoa are generally harmonious, with a strong emphasis on traditional Samoan culture and values, although there are also efforts to promote diversity and inclusivity.

Languages

  • Samoan is the official language and the most widely spoken language in American Samoa, with approximately 90% of the population speaking it as their first language.
  • English is also an official language and is widely spoken, particularly in formal settings, such as government, education, and business.
  • Other languages spoken in American Samoa include Tongan, Tokelauan, and Fijian, which are spoken by immigrant communities from other Pacific Island countries.
  • Chamorro is also spoken by some people, particularly those with ancestral ties to Guam or the Northern Mariana Islands.
  • American Samoa has a language preservation program, which aims to promote the use of Samoan language and culture, particularly among young people.

  • The majority of the population, approximately 87%, resides in urban areas, with Pago Pago being the largest urban center.
