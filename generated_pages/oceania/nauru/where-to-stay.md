# Where to Stay in Nauru — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Nauru, consider the capital city of Yaren and its surrounding neighborhoods, which offer a range of accommodations for different types of travelers, from budget-friendly guesthouses to comfortable hotels.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, Nauru offers a unique experience, with the following being some of the most notable:
**Yaren** (Yaren)
Best For: business / government
Vibe: Yaren is the capital and commercial center of Nauru, with a bustling atmosphere and a mix of modern and traditional buildings. The area is home to many government offices, shops, and restaurants, making it a great place to experience the local culture.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: central location, easy access to amenities, close to the parliament building
Cons: can be noisy, limited entertainment options

**Anetan** (Anetan)
Best For: family / luxury
Vibe: Anetan is a coastal village with a beautiful beach and a relaxed atmosphere, making it perfect for families and those looking for a luxurious getaway. The area is known for its stunning sunsets and crystal-clear waters.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: beautiful beach, calm atmosphere, stunning sunsets
Cons: limited dining options, can be isolated

**Buada** (Buada)
Best For: backpacker / budget
Vibe: Buada is a small village with a lively atmosphere, known for its traditional Nauruan culture and affordable accommodations. The area is home to many local restaurants and shops, making it a great place to experience the local way of life.
Price Range: $50-100/night
Pros: affordable, rich cultural heritage, friendly locals
Cons: limited amenities, can be crowded

**Denigomodu** (Denigomodu)
Best For: luxury / business
Vibe: Denigomodu is a coastal village with a modern atmosphere, known for its high-end accommodations and stunning ocean views. The area is home to many restaurants and shops, making it a great place to relax and unwind.
Price Range: $200-400/night
Pros: modern amenities, stunning ocean views, close to the airport
Cons: can be expensive, limited cultural experiences

**Nibok** (Nibok)
Best For: family / budget
Vibe: Nibok is a small village with a relaxed atmosphere, known for its beautiful beach and affordable accommodations. The area is home to many local restaurants and shops, making it a great place to experience the local culture.
Price Range: $80-150/night
Pros: beautiful beach, affordable, friendly locals
Cons: limited amenities, can be isolated

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September)

- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
- Keep valuables secure, and use a hotel safe or a money belt

- Consider purchasing travel insurance, which can provide coverage in case of unexpected events
