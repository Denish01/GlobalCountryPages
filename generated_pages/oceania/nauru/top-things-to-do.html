Nauru, a small island nation in the Pacific Ocean, offers a unique blend of cultural, historical, and natural attractions that make it a fascinating destination to explore, with the top things to do including visiting the Command Ridge, exploring the Buada Lagoon, and experiencing the local culture at the Nauru National Museum.

Unique Experiences

  • Learn about the Nauruan language and culture through a traditional dance performance at the Nauru National Museum
  • Take a guided tour of the phosphate mining sites to understand the island's industrial history
  • Experience the local fishing industry by joining a fishing trip with a local guide
  • Visit a traditional Nauruan village to see the island's unique architecture and way of life
  • Attend a cultural festival, such as the Nauru Independence Day celebrations, to experience the island's vibrant culture
  • Go on a snorkeling or diving trip to explore the island's coral reefs and marine life
  • Take a cooking class to learn about traditional Nauruan cuisine and cooking techniques

Outdoor & Nature

  • Explore the Buada Lagoon and its surrounding tropical vegetation
  • Visit the Anetan Beach for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing
  • Go hiking on the Command Ridge for panoramic views of the island
  • Take a boat trip to the Nauru Marine Reserve to see the island's marine life
  • Visit the Topside area to see the remnants of the phosphate mining industry
  • Go birdwatching at the Anetan Harbour to see the island's unique bird species

Cultural Experiences

  • Visit the Nauru National Museum to learn about the island's history and culture
  • Attend a traditional Nauruan dance performance at the museum or a local village
  • Take a guided tour of the Parliament House to learn about the island's government and politics
  • Visit the Nauru Phosphate Corporation to learn about the island's phosphate mining industry
  • Attend a church service at one of the local churches to experience the island's Christian heritage
  • Take a cooking class to learn about traditional Nauruan cuisine and cooking techniques

Hidden Gems

  • Visit the Moqua Well, a historic well that played a significant role in the island's early settlement
  • Explore the Topside area, a former phosphate mining site with remnants of the island's industrial past
  • Take a walk along the Anetan Beach at sunset for stunning views of the ocean
  • Visit the Linkbelt Oval, a popular venue for sports events and cultural performances
  • Go on a fishing trip with a local guide to experience the island's fishing industry

Day Trips

  • Take a day trip to the Buada Lagoon (10 km, 30 minutes) to explore the lagoon and its surroundings
  • Visit the Anetan Beach (15 km, 45 minutes) for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing
  • Go on a hiking trip to the Command Ridge (5 km, 1 hour) for panoramic views of the island
  • Take a boat trip to the Nauru Marine Reserve (20 km, 1 hour) to see the island's marine life
  • Visit the Moqua Well (10 km, 30 minutes) to learn about the island's history and culture

Planning Tips

Suggested itineraries:

- 3-day itinerary: Visit the Command Ridge, Buada Lagoon, and Nauru National Museum

- 7-day itinerary: Explore the island's beaches, go hiking, and attend a cultural festival

- 14-day itinerary: Take a deep dive into the island's history, culture, and natural environment, and visit all the top attractions

Key tips: Book accommodations and tours in advance, prioritize the top attractions, and be respectful of the island's culture and environment

[RATING] 4/5: Nauru is a unique and fascinating destination that offers a blend of cultural, historical, and natural attractions, but may lack some modern amenities and infrastructure.
