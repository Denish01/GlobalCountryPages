Cultural Treasures

  • The Nauru National Museum showcases the country's history, culture, and traditions, and features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that highlight Nauru's unique cultural heritage.
  • The Anetan Catholic Church is a notable example of Nauru's cultural diversity, with a blend of traditional and modern architectural styles.
  • The Nauru Traditional Village is a cultural center that preserves and promotes Nauru's traditional way of life, including its customs, music, and dance.
  • The Buada Lagoon is a picturesque freshwater lagoon that is home to a variety of bird species and is considered a sacred site by the Nauruan people.
  • The Topside region is a historic area that features several World War II relics and memorials, including the Command Ridge lookout point.

People & Global Impact

  • Marcus Stephen, a former weightlifter and politician, served as the President of Nauru from 2007 to 2011 and was a key figure in promoting the country's development and international cooperation.
  • The Nauruan diaspora has made significant contributions to the economies and cultures of countries such as Australia, New Zealand, and the United States, with many Nauruans working in industries such as healthcare, education, and finance.
  • Nauru's community-based approach to development has been recognized internationally, with a focus on empowering local communities to take ownership of their development initiatives.
  • The Nauru Government has prioritized sustainable development and has implemented initiatives to promote environmental conservation, social justice, and economic growth.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Nauruan people, who are known for their warm welcomes and friendly nature, has been consistently praised by visitors.
  • The traditional Nauruan cuisine, which features dishes such as coconut crab and fresh fish, has been recognized for its unique flavors and cultural significance.
  • The beautiful beaches and coral reefs of Nauru, including the Anetan Beach and Buada Lagoon, offer a range of outdoor activities such as snorkeling, diving, and fishing.
  • The Topside region offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking and photography.
  • The Nauru National Festival, which is held annually, showcases the country's culture, music, and dance, and features a range of traditional performances and activities.
  • The local markets, which offer a range of traditional handicrafts and souvenirs, provide visitors with an opportunity to experience Nauru's vibrant culture and support local artisans.

Hidden Gems

  • The Moqua Well, a historic freshwater well that dates back to the 19th century, is a lesser-known attraction that offers insights into Nauru's history and cultural heritage.
  • The Nauru Conservation Area, which covers over 30% of the country's land area, is a protected region that is home to a variety of unique and endemic species.
  • The Nauru Pearl Farm, which is one of the few pearl farms in the Pacific, offers visitors the opportunity to learn about the process of pearl farming and to purchase high-quality pearls.
  • The Command Ridge lookout point, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, is a hidden gem that provides a unique perspective on Nauru's history and geography.
