Overall Safety Assessment

Nauru is generally a safe country to visit, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. The island nation has a small population, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. Nauru's remote location and limited resources can make it challenging to respond to emergencies, so it's crucial to be prepared and aware of potential risks.

Nauru's safety record is relatively good, with few reported incidents of crime or violence against tourists. The country has a strong police presence, and the community is generally law-abiding. However, the island's isolation and limited connectivity can make it difficult to access medical care or other essential services in the event of an emergency. Visitors should be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions to stay safe, such as staying informed about local conditions, being mindful of their surroundings, and taking steps to protect their health and well-being.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Petty theft and pickpocketing can occur in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations
  • Visitors should be aware of scams targeting tourists, such as fake tour operators or unauthorized guides
  • The island's remote location can make it challenging to access medical care or other essential services in the event of an emergency
  • Visitors should be cautious when swimming or snorkeling, as the coastline can be prone to strong currents and limited swimming areas
  • The risk of dengue fever and other mosquito-borne illnesses is present, and visitors should take necessary precautions to prevent mosquito bites
  • Visitors should be aware of the potential for cyclones and other natural disasters, and stay informed about local weather conditions
  • The island's limited resources can make it challenging to access ATM and other financial services, and visitors should be prepared with sufficient cash and credit

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to Nauru
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are limited, and visitors may need to be evacuated to another country for serious medical conditions
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacy access is limited, and visitors should bring any necessary medications with them

Practical Safety Tips

  • Stay informed about local conditions and weather forecasts
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and keep valuables secure
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit areas
  • Be cautious when swimming or snorkeling, and follow local advice
  • Bring necessary medications and supplies, as pharmacy access is limited
  • Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen, as the tropical sun can be intense
  • Respect local customs and traditions, and dress modestly when visiting villages or attending cultural events

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Solo female travelers should be aware of the potential for harassment or unwanted attention, and take necessary precautions to stay safe
  • Visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
  • Solo travelers should stay informed about local conditions and stay in touch with family and friends back home
  • Women travelers should be cautious when traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit areas and reputable transportation services
