[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Nauru has a moderate visa policy, with a limited number of countries having visa-free access. Citizens of Australia, New Zealand, and the United Kingdom, among a few others, can enter Nauru without a visa, while most other nationalities require a visa to enter the country. The visa policy is designed to balance the need to control immigration with the need to promote tourism and economic development. Nauru's visa requirements are relatively straightforward, but it's essential to ensure that all necessary documents are in order before traveling to the country.

The government of Nauru has implemented a visa system to manage the flow of visitors and ensure that all travelers meet the necessary requirements. The visa policy is subject to change, and it's crucial to stay up-to-date with the latest requirements to avoid any issues during travel. Nauru's visa policy is designed to be fair and efficient, with a focus on promoting tourism and economic development while maintaining national security and public safety.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can obtain a visa on arrival in Nauru, which is valid for a maximum stay of 30 days. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, and extensions can be granted in exceptional circumstances. Tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay in Nauru.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers require a visa, which must be obtained before traveling to Nauru. They must have an invitation letter from a Nauruan company, a valid passport, and proof of onward travel. Business travelers may be required to provide additional documentation, such as a business plan or proof of funding.
**Students:** Students require a visa, which must be obtained before traveling to Nauru. They must have proof of enrollment in a Nauruan educational institution, a valid passport, and proof of onward travel. Students may be required to provide additional documentation, such as a letter of acceptance or proof of funding.
**Workers:** Workers require a visa, which must be obtained before traveling to Nauru. They must have a sponsor in Nauru, a valid passport, and proof of onward travel. Workers may be required to provide additional documentation, such as a contract of employment or proof of qualifications.
