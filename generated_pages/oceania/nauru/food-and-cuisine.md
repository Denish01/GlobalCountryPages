# Nauru Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Nauru, you should try the local seafood dishes, coconut-based recipes, and traditional Pacific Islander cuisine, which are heavily influenced by the country's cultural heritage and geographic location.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: fish, coconuts, taro, breadfruit
National Dish: **Ekaw**, a traditional Nauruan dish made from raw fish marinated in coconut milk and mixed with onions, tomatoes, and chili peppers
Meal Times: 7-8 am for breakfast, 12-1 pm for lunch, 6-7 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: not expected, but rounding up the bill is appreciated
Dietary Notes: limited options for vegetarians and vegans, but most restaurants can accommodate special requests
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Nauru's cuisine is defined by its geographic location in the Pacific Ocean, with a strong emphasis on seafood, coconuts, and root vegetables like taro and breadfruit. The country's cultural heritage is also reflected in its cuisine, with influences from Pacific Islander, Asian, and European traditions. Traditional Nauruan cuisine is often simple, with a focus on fresh ingredients and minimal preparation, while modern Nauruan cuisine has adopted more complex flavors and cooking techniques. Regional variations in Nauru's cuisine are limited, but the country's coastal location means that seafood is a staple ingredient in most dishes.

Nauru's cuisine has been shaped by its history as a colonial outpost and its modern-day status as a sovereign nation. The country's culinary traditions have been influenced by its Pacific Islander neighbors, as well as by Asian and European traders and colonizers. As a result, Nauruan cuisine is a unique blend of traditional and modern flavors, with a focus on fresh ingredients and simple preparation methods. Despite its limited options for vegetarians and vegans, Nauruan cuisine is generally accommodating of special dietary requests, and most restaurants can provide alternatives or modifications to suit individual needs.

The traditional Nauruan diet is rich in protein, fiber, and healthy fats, with a focus on whole foods like fish, coconuts, and root vegetables. Modern Nauruan cuisine has adopted more processed ingredients and cooking methods, but the country's culinary traditions remain rooted in its Pacific Islander heritage. With its emphasis on fresh ingredients, simple preparation methods, and traditional flavors, Nauruan cuisine is a unique and flavorful reflection of the country's cultural identity.

- **Eating with locals**: try to eat with locals, as it is a great way to experience the culture and cuisine
