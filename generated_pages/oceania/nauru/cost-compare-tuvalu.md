[SECTION] Overview: Nauru vs Tuvalu [/SECTION]
Nauru and Tuvalu are two small island nations in the Pacific Ocean, with distinct economic profiles. Nauru has a relatively high GDP per capita, driven by its phosphate mining industry, while Tuvalu's economy is more diversified, with a focus on fishing and tourism. The cost of living in Nauru is higher due to its remote location and limited availability of goods and services, which drives up prices. In contrast, Tuvalu has a more competitive market, with a greater variety of products available at lower prices.

The overall cost of living in Nauru is around 15-20% higher than in Tuvalu, making Tuvalu a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable Pacific island lifestyle. However, Nauru's stronger economy and higher standard of living may make it a more desirable choice for those prioritizing comfort and convenience. The difference in cost of living between the two countries is most pronounced in the areas of housing, food, and transportation, with Nauru being significantly more expensive in these categories.
