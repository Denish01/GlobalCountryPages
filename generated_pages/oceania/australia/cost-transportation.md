[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Australia has a well-developed transportation network, with a range of options available for getting around, including public transportation, taxis, ride-hailing services, and car rentals. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with major cities like Sydney and Melbourne having comprehensive and efficient systems, while smaller cities and regional areas may have more limited options. Most people in Australia get around by driving, with car ownership rates being among the highest in the world, but public transportation is also widely used, particularly in urban areas.

The main transportation modes in Australia include buses, trains, and ferries, with many cities also having bike-sharing schemes and pedestrian-friendly infrastructure. The country's large size and relatively small population mean that air travel is also a common way to cover long distances, with many domestic flights available between major cities. Overall, Australia's transportation network is well-developed and efficient, making it easy to get around, whether by public transportation, driving, or flying.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save money and reduce your environmental impact.
- Consider purchasing a **Myki** or **Opal** card for convenient travel on public transportation in Melbourne and Sydney.
- Use ride-hailing apps like **Uber** or **Ola** for convenient and affordable door-to-door transportation.
- Be aware of **toll roads** and factor in the cost when driving between cities.
- Always wear a seatbelt and follow local traffic laws to ensure your safety on the road.
- Consider renting a car or booking a **Greyhound** bus for longer trips between cities.
