When deciding where to stay in Australia, consider popular areas like Sydney's Central Business District, Melbourne's Southbank, and the Gold Coast's Surfers Paradise, which offer a mix of city life, cultural attractions, and beach activities.

Best Areas to Stay

For each of the top areas, we've outlined the best fit for different types of travelers, the vibe, price range, pros, and cons.

Sydney Central Business District (Sydney)

Best For: Business, Luxury

Vibe: The Sydney Central Business District is a bustling area with iconic landmarks like the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge, offering a sophisticated and fast-paced atmosphere. The area is also home to many high-end restaurants and bars.

Price Range: $200-$500/night

Pros: Close proximity to major attractions, excellent public transportation, and a wide range of dining and entertainment options. The area is also within walking distance to the Royal Botanic Garden and the Museum of Contemporary Art.

Cons: Can be quite expensive, and the area can get crowded with tourists and commuters.

Melbourne Southbank (Melbourne)

Best For: Family, Luxury

Vibe: Melbourne's Southbank is a vibrant and cultural area, known for its street performers, restaurants, and bars, with a lively and entertaining atmosphere. The area is also home to the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre and the Crown Casino.

Price Range: $150-$400/night

Pros: Plenty of family-friendly activities, such as the Melbourne Aquarium and the Eureka Skydeck, and easy access to the city's main attractions. The area is also within walking distance to the Docklands and the Melbourne Cricket Ground.

Cons: Can be noisy, especially at night, and parking can be a challenge.

Surfers Paradise (Gold Coast)

Best For: Backpacker, Family

Vibe: Surfers Paradise is a lively and bustling area, known for its beautiful beaches, theme parks, and vibrant nightlife, with a fun and energetic atmosphere. The area is also home to many restaurants, bars, and shops.

Price Range: $100-$300/night

Pros: Affordable accommodation options, plenty of activities for families and backpackers, and a beautiful beach. The area is also within walking distance to the Gold Coast Convention and Exhibition Centre.

Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, especially during peak season, and some areas can be a bit run-down.

Cairns City (Cairns)

Best For: Adventure-seeker, Family

Vibe: Cairns City is a tropical and laid-back area, known for its proximity to the Great Barrier Reef and the Daintree Rainforest, with a relaxed and adventurous atmosphere. The area is also home to many restaurants, bars, and shops.

Price Range: $80-$250/night

Pros: Plenty of outdoor activities, such as snorkeling and hiking, and a more relaxed atmosphere than the major cities. The area is also within walking distance to the Cairns Esplanade and the Night Markets.

Cons: Can be quite hot and humid, and some areas can be a bit touristy.

Byron Bay (Byron Bay)

Best For: Backpacker, Family

Vibe: Byron Bay is a laid-back and alternative area, known for its beautiful beaches, surf culture, and vibrant arts scene, with a relaxed and bohemian atmosphere. The area is also home to many restaurants, bars, and shops.

Price Range: $60-$200/night

Pros: Affordable accommodation options, plenty of outdoor activities, and a unique and eclectic atmosphere. The area is also within walking distance to the Byron Bay Lighthouse and the Cape Byron State Conservation Area.

Cons: Can be quite crowded, especially during peak season, and some areas can be a bit run-down.

Perth City (Perth)

Best For: Business, Luxury

Vibe: Perth City is a modern and vibrant area, known for its beautiful parks, cultural attractions, and excellent dining scene, with a sophisticated and cosmopolitan atmosphere. The area is also home to many high-end restaurants and bars.

Price Range: $120-$350/night

Pros: Close proximity to major attractions, excellent public transportation, and a wide range of dining and entertainment options. The area is also within walking distance to the Perth Cultural Centre and the Kings Park and Botanic Garden.

Cons: Can be quite expensive, and the area can get crowded with tourists and commuters.

Booking Tips

  • Book accommodation in advance, especially during peak season (December to February) to avoid high prices and limited availability.
  • Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find deals.
  • Consider booking a package deal that includes accommodation, flights, and activities to save money.
  • Be flexible with your travel dates and accommodation options to find the best value.
  • Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before booking to ensure a smooth and hassle-free stay.
  • Negotiate prices, especially for long-term stays or off-peak season bookings.
  • Look for discounts and promotions, especially during the off-season (March to November) to save money.

Safety & Location Tips

  • Research the area before booking to ensure it's safe and convenient for your needs.
  • Avoid areas with high crime rates or limited public transportation, such as some suburbs in major cities.
  • Consider staying in areas with a high police presence, such as city centers or tourist areas.
  • Keep valuables secure and be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night.
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.
