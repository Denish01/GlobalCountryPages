[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Australia's infrastructure is considered one of the most developed in the world, with significant investments in recent years to improve the transportation network, energy sector, and telecommunications. The country has a strong economy, which has enabled it to invest heavily in infrastructure development, including roads, railways, airports, and seaports. However, there is still a significant gap between urban and rural areas, with many rural communities lacking access to basic infrastructure such as reliable internet and mobile phone coverage. The Australian government has recognized this gap and has implemented various initiatives to address it, including the rollout of the National Broadband Network (NBN) and investments in rural road and rail infrastructure.

Overall, Australia's infrastructure is well-suited to support the country's growing population and economy, with a strong focus on transportation, energy, and telecommunications. The country's infrastructure is also well-maintained, with regular upgrades and repairs to ensure that it remains in good condition. However, there are still challenges to be addressed, particularly in rural areas where access to basic infrastructure can be limited. The Australian government is working to address these challenges through various initiatives and investments, including the development of new infrastructure projects and the upgrade of existing ones.

Australia's infrastructure development is also driven by the country's strategic location, with many major cities and ports located along the coast. This has enabled the country to become a major hub for international trade and commerce, with many companies and industries relying on Australia's infrastructure to transport goods and services. The country's infrastructure is also well-integrated, with a strong focus on multimodal transport, including roads, railways, airports, and seaports.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]

- The country has a reliable and efficient electricity grid, with a low incidence of power outages and interruptions.
- Australia is also a major producer of **liquefied natural gas (LNG)**, with many major LNG projects located along the coast.
- The country has set a target of generating at least 23% of its electricity from renewable sources by 2020, and is on track to meet this target.
- Australia is also investing in new energy technologies, including **battery storage** and **smart grids**, to improve the efficiency and reliability of its energy sector.
