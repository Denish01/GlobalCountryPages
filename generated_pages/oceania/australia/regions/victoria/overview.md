[SECTION] What Is Victoria? [/SECTION]
Victoria is a state in the southeastern region of Australia, bordered by New South Wales to the north and east, South Australia to the west, and the Bass Strait to the south. The state is known for its diverse landscape, which includes mountains, forests, and coastlines along the Southern Ocean. Victoria is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including many unique and endemic species. The state's capital and largest city, Melbourne, is a cultural and economic hub, known for its vibrant arts and culture scene, world-class restaurants, and international sporting events.

Victoria is also known for its rich history, which dates back to the early 19th century when the state was first established as a British colony. The state has a strong **indigenous culture**, with many Aboriginal communities living in the region for thousands of years before European settlement. Today, Victoria is a popular tourist destination, with millions of visitors each year drawn to its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and world-class events.

The state's economy is diverse, with major industries including **agriculture**, **manufacturing**, and **tourism**. Victoria is also home to a number of world-class universities and research institutions, making it a hub for **education** and **innovation**. The state's population is approximately 6.7 million people, with the majority living in the Melbourne metropolitan area.

2. 1854 - The Eureka Stockade takes place, a rebellion by gold miners against the government's taxation policies.
3. 1901 - Victoria becomes a state of the Commonwealth of Australia, with Melbourne serving as the national capital until 1927.
4. 1956 - Melbourne hosts the Summer Olympics, a major international sporting event that puts the city on the world stage.
5. 2009 - Victoria experiences one of its worst natural disasters, the Black Saturday bushfires, which kill 173 people and destroy thousands of homes.
