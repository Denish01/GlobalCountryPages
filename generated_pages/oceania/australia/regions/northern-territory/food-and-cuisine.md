# Northern Territory Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting the Northern Territory, you should try the unique and flavorful dishes that blend traditional Australian cuisine with Asian and indigenous influences, such as **Kakadu Plum**-infused sauces and **Barramundi** fish.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: beef, seafood, bush tomatoes, Kakadu plums
National Dish: Barramundi with Kakadu Plum Sauce, a dish that showcases the region's fresh seafood and native ingredients
Meal Times: 7-9 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 6-8 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 10% is standard, but not expected
Dietary Notes: vegetarian and gluten-free options are available, but limited in rural areas
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
The Northern Territory's cuisine is defined by its unique blend of traditional Australian, Asian, and indigenous influences. The region's proximity to Southeast Asia and its history of cultural exchange have resulted in a distinct flavor profile that is both familiar and exotic. The use of native ingredients such as **bush tomatoes**, **Kakadu plums**, and ** Davidson's plum** is a key feature of Northern Territory cuisine, and many restaurants and chefs are now incorporating these ingredients into their dishes. The territory's cuisine also varies by region, with the tropical north offering a range of fresh seafood and the desert interior featuring more traditional bush foods.

The Northern Territory is home to a number of unique and exotic ingredients, including **crocodile meat**, **buffalo meat**, and **native herbs**. These ingredients are often combined with more traditional Australian ingredients such as beef and lamb to create a truly unique flavor profile. The territory's cuisine is also influenced by its Asian neighbors, with many restaurants serving dishes such as **Thai-style green curry** and **Vietnamese-style pho**.

The Northern Territory's food culture is also shaped by its outdoor lifestyle and the availability of fresh produce. Many restaurants and cafes offer outdoor seating and **barbecue** facilities, and the territory is home to a number of **food festivals** and **markets** throughout the year. These events showcase the region's unique cuisine and provide visitors with the opportunity to sample a range of local dishes and ingredients.
