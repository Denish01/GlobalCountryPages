# Getting Around Queensland — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Queensland, you can utilize a range of transportation options, including domestic flights, trains, buses, and city transport, as well as rent a car to explore the region at your own pace.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Queensland has several main airports that serve as entry points for international and domestic travelers. Brisbane Airport is the largest and busiest airport in the state, offering direct flights to major cities in Asia, Europe, and the Americas. Cairns Airport is another significant hub, with direct flights to many domestic and international destinations, particularly in the Asia-Pacific region. The Gold Coast Airport also receives international flights, mainly from New Zealand and Asia. Overland entry points are limited, but you can enter Queensland by car or bus from neighboring states, such as New South Wales.

If you're planning to enter Queensland overland, you can take the Pacific Highway or the Newell Highway from New South Wales. There are also several bus companies that operate services between Queensland and other states, including Greyhound Australia and Murrays Bus Service. These buses often stop at major towns and cities along the way, making it a convenient option for those who prefer to travel by land. Additionally, you can also take the train from other states, with Queensland Rail operating services from neighboring states.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Queensland has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights within the state and to other parts of Australia. The main domestic airlines in Queensland are Qantas, Virgin Australia, and Jetstar, which offer frequent flights to major cities and regional towns. Some of the main routes include Brisbane to Cairns, Brisbane to the Gold Coast, and Cairns to the Whitsundays. When booking domestic flights, it's a good idea to compare prices across different airlines and book in advance to get the best deals. Approximate costs for domestic flights in Queensland can range from $100 to $500, depending on the route and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Queensland has a moderate rail network, with several train lines operating within the state and to other parts of Australia. The main rail operator in Queensland is Queensland Rail, which offers services to regional towns and cities, including the Spirit of Queensland train that runs from Brisbane to Cairns. There are no high-speed train options in Queensland, but the trains are generally comfortable and offer scenic views of the countryside. You can book train tickets online or at train stations, and prices vary depending on the route and class of travel.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several bus and coach companies that operate in Queensland, offering services between cities and regional towns. Some of the main companies include Greyhound Australia, Murrays Bus Service, and Premier Motor Service. These buses often stop at major towns and cities along the way, making it a convenient option for those who prefer to travel by land. The quality of the buses varies, but most are modern and comfortable, with air conditioning and onboard amenities. Costs for bus travel in Queensland can range from $20 to $100, depending on the route and time of year.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Queensland, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driving permit.
- The road quality in Queensland is generally good, with well-maintained highways and roads.
- Fuel costs in Queensland are relatively high, with petrol prices ranging from $1.20 to $1.50 per liter.
- When renting a car, be sure to choose a reputable company and check the vehicle for any damage before you drive away.
- Be aware of the risks of driving in Queensland, including fatigue, wildlife on the roads, and extreme weather conditions such as cyclones and floods.
- It's a good idea to purchase car insurance when renting a vehicle, as this will protect you in case of an accident or theft.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of the risks of **driving fatigue**, particularly on long road trips, and take regular breaks to rest and stretch your legs.
- Use reputable **taxi** or **ride-hailing** services, and always check the driver's ID before you get in the vehicle.
- **Book your tickets** in advance, particularly during peak travel seasons, to avoid sold-out situations and high prices.
- Consider purchasing a **public transport card**, such as a Go Card, which can be used on buses, trains, and ferries throughout Queensland.
- Be mindful of your **personal belongings**, particularly in crowded areas such as train stations and airports, and keep a close eye on your luggage at all times.
- Download **transport apps**, such as the Queensland Rail app or the TransLink app, to help you plan your journey and track your progress.
- Be prepared for **extreme weather conditions**, such as cyclones or heatwaves, and follow the advice of local authorities to stay safe.
