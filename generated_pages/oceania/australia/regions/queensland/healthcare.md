[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
Queensland's healthcare system is a mix of public and private providers, with a strong emphasis on universal access to medical care. The public system is funded by the government and provides comprehensive coverage to all Australian citizens and permanent residents, including those living in Queensland. The private system, on the other hand, offers additional services and amenities to those who can afford them. The quality of care in Queensland is generally high, with well-trained medical professionals and modern facilities. However, there can be significant differences in access to healthcare between urban and rural areas, with rural areas often having fewer medical facilities and specialists.

The public healthcare system in Queensland is managed by the Queensland Health department, which oversees a network of hospitals, clinics, and community health services. The department also provides funding for various health programs and initiatives, including mental health services, cancer treatment, and preventive care. In contrast, the private healthcare system in Queensland is comprised of private hospitals, clinics, and medical centers, which offer a range of services, including surgical procedures, diagnostic testing, and specialized care.

Overall, Queensland's healthcare system is designed to provide comprehensive and equitable access to medical care, regardless of income or social status. However, the system can be complex, and navigating it can be challenging, especially for visitors or new residents. It is essential to have a basic understanding of how the system works and what to expect when seeking medical care in Queensland.

[SECTION] Health Insurance [/SECTION]
- **Private health insurance** is recommended for visitors and non-residents, as it can provide additional coverage and amenities not available through the public system.
- **Medicare** is Australia's public health insurance system, which provides comprehensive coverage to citizens and permanent residents, including those living in Queensland.
- **Private health insurance plans** are available from various providers, including Bupa, Medibank, and NIB, and can offer additional services, such as dental care, optical care, and physiotherapy.
- **Expats** and temporary residents may be eligible for private health insurance plans, which can provide coverage for medical expenses, including hospital stays and surgical procedures.
- **Costs** for private health insurance plans can vary widely, depending on the provider, level of coverage, and individual circumstances.
- **Waiting periods** may apply to some private health insurance plans, which can range from a few months to several years, depending on the provider and level of coverage.

[SECTION] Pharmacies & Medications [/SECTION]
- **Pharmacies** are widely available in Queensland, with many locations open extended hours, including weekends and public holidays.
- **Prescription medications** are available from pharmacies, hospitals, and some medical centers, and may require a prescription from a licensed medical professional.
- **Common medications** are widely available, including pain relievers, antihistamines, and antibiotics.
- **Costs** for prescription medications can vary, depending on the type of medication, dosage, and individual circumstances, but many medications are subsidized by the government.
- **Over-the-counter medications** are available from pharmacies, supermarkets, and some convenience stores, and can provide relief for minor ailments, such as headaches, colds, and flu.

[SECTION] Vaccinations & Health Risks [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations** are recommended for travelers to Queensland, including hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and influenza.
- **Infectious diseases**, such as influenza, gastroenteritis, and respiratory infections, can occur in Queensland, especially during peak travel seasons.
- **Mosquito-borne illnesses**, such as dengue fever and Zika virus, can occur in some areas of Queensland, particularly in the tropical north.
- **Water safety** is generally good in Queensland, but visitors should take precautions when swimming in rivers, lakes, and oceans, as strong currents and marine life can pose risks.
- **Food safety** is generally good in Queensland, but visitors should take precautions when eating at outdoor markets, food stalls, and restaurants, as food poisoning can occur.
- **Sun protection** is essential in Queensland, as the sun's UV rays can be strong, especially during peak summer months.

- **Accredited hospitals** and clinics in Queensland must meet strict standards for quality and safety, and many are recognized internationally for their expertise and facilities.
- **Cost savings** can be significant for medical tourists, as the cost of medical procedures in Queensland can be lower than in many other developed countries.
- **Popular procedures** for medical tourists in Queensland include cosmetic surgery, dental care, and orthopedic surgery.
