[SECTION] What Is Tasmania? [/SECTION]
Tasmania is a region and island state located 240 kilometers south of the Australian mainland, separated by the Bass Strait. It is part of the country of Australia and has a unique cultural and geographic identity. The island is known for its rugged wilderness, pristine lakes, and stunning coastline, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Tasmania is also home to a rich cultural heritage, with a strong sense of community and a thriving arts scene.

The island has a total area of approximately 68,401 square kilometers, with a diverse range of landscapes, including mountains, forests, and coastal plains. The capital city, Hobart, is located in the south of the island and is the largest city in Tasmania, with a population of around 250,000 people. Tasmania is also home to a number of smaller towns and villages, each with its own unique character and charm.

Tasmania has a strong sense of identity and a distinct cultural heritage, shaped by its history, geography, and indigenous heritage. The island has a rich and complex history, with evidence of human habitation dating back thousands of years. Today, Tasmania is a popular tourist destination, known for its natural beauty, unique wildlife, and rich cultural heritage.

1. 1803 - The British established a penal colony in Tasmania, which was initially known as Van Diemen's Land.
2. 1853 - The penal colony was closed, and the island was renamed Tasmania in honor of the Dutch explorer Abel Tasman.
3. 1856 - The University of Tasmania was established, making it one of the oldest universities in Australia.
4. 1901 - Tasmania became a state of Australia, with its own constitution and system of government.
5. 1989 - The Tasmanian Wilderness World Heritage Area was established, recognizing the island's unique and diverse natural heritage.
