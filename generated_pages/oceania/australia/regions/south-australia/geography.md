# South Australia Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

South Australia is a region located in the southern part of the country of Australia, covering a vast area of diverse landscapes and climates.

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
South Australia is situated in the southern part of the Australian continent, bordered by the Northern Territory to the north, Queensland to the northeast, New South Wales to the east, Victoria to the southeast, and Western Australia to the west. The region is surrounded by the **Indian Ocean** to the west and the **Southern Ocean** to the south, with the **Cooper Creek** and **Lake Eyre** forming part of its northern boundary. The capital city of South Australia is Adelaide, which is located on the **Torrens River** and is the main gateway to the region.

The location of South Australia makes it a unique and diverse region, with a range of different landscapes and climates. From the **Outback** in the north to the **Fleurieu Peninsula** in the south, the region is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including many **endemic species** that can be found nowhere else in the world. The region's borders are also home to many important **indigenous cultural sites**, which are a significant part of Australia's cultural heritage.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
The terrain of South Australia is incredibly diverse, ranging from the **arid deserts** of the north to the **fertile valleys** of the south. The region is home to several major mountain ranges, including the **Flinders Ranges** and the **Mount Lofty Ranges**, which offer stunning scenery and a range of outdoor activities such as hiking and rock climbing. The **River Murray**, which forms part of the border with New South Wales and Victoria, is a significant waterway that supports a wide range of agricultural and industrial activities.

The landscape of South Australia is also characterized by its many **coastal features**, including the **Fleurieu Peninsula** and **Kangaroo Island**, which are known for their stunning beaches, rugged coastlines, and diverse wildlife. The region's **deserts**, including the **Simpson Desert** and the **Stuart Highway**, are also an important part of its landscape, with their unique flora and fauna and rich **indigenous cultural heritage**. The **Lake Eyre Basin**, which is the lowest point in Australia, is a significant geographical feature that is home to a wide range of unique and **endangered species**.

The region's diverse landscape also supports a wide range of **agricultural activities**, including **wine production**, **sheep and cattle farming**, and **crop production**. The **Fleurieu Peninsula** is particularly famous for its **wine region**, which produces some of Australia's finest wines. The region's **coastal areas** are also an important part of its economy, with **fishing** and **tourism** being significant industries.
