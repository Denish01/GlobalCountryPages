[SECTION] What Is Sydney? [/SECTION]
Sydney is a major city located on the eastern coast of Australia, situated on the Tasman Sea. It is the largest city in Australia and the state capital of New South Wales. The city is known for its iconic landmarks such as the **Sydney Opera House** and the **Sydney Harbour Bridge**, as well as its stunning natural beauty, including **Bondi Beach** and the **Blue Mountains**. Sydney is a cultural and economic hub, with a diverse population and a thriving arts and culture scene.

The city is surrounded by the **Pacific Ocean** to the east, the **Blue Mountains** to the west, and the **Hawkesbury River** to the north. Sydney is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year with its mild climate, beautiful beaches, and world-class cultural institutions. The city is also an important economic center, with a strong finance sector and a major port.

Sydney is a significant cultural center, with a wide range of museums, galleries, and performance venues. The city is home to the **Australian Museum**, the **Art Gallery of New South Wales**, and the **Sydney Symphony Orchestra**, among other cultural institutions. The city also hosts a number of major festivals and events, including the **Sydney Festival** and the **Mardi Gras**.
