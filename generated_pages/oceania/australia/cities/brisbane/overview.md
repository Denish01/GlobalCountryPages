[SECTION] What Is Brisbane? [/SECTION]
Brisbane is a city located in the southeastern part of Queensland, Australia, and it serves as the state's capital. The city is situated on the Brisbane River and is known for its pleasant subtropical climate, with warm winters and hot, humid summers. Brisbane is a major cultural and economic hub in Australia, with a strong focus on arts, entertainment, and education. The city is home to many universities, museums, and galleries, and it hosts various festivals and events throughout the year, including the Brisbane Festival and the Queensland Music Festival.

Geographically, Brisbane is positioned near the coast, about 20 kilometers from the Pacific Ocean. The city is surrounded by several mountains and hills, including the Great Dividing Range, which provides a scenic backdrop to the city. Brisbane is also close to several popular tourist destinations, including the Gold Coast and the Sunshine Coast, which are known for their beautiful beaches, theme parks, and outdoor recreational activities.

Brisbane is a popular tourist destination, with many attractions and activities to offer visitors. The city has a rich history, dating back to the early 19th century, and it is home to many historic buildings, landmarks, and cultural institutions. Visitors to Brisbane can explore the city's many museums, galleries, and historic sites, or take a stroll along the Brisbane River and enjoy the city's vibrant atmosphere.
