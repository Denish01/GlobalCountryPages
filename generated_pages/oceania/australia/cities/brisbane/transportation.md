# Getting Around Brisbane — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Brisbane, you have a variety of transport options including buses, trains, ferries, and bike-share systems, making it easy to explore the city and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Brisbane is easily accessible by air, with Brisbane Airport (BNE) being the main entry point for international and domestic flights. The airport is located about 12 kilometers northeast of the city center and offers direct flights to many major hubs in Asia, Europe, and the Americas. If you're traveling from within Australia, you can also reach Brisbane by car or bus, with the city being connected to the rest of the country by a network of highways and intercity bus routes.

For those traveling overland, Brisbane is connected to the rest of Australia by the M1 highway, which runs north to the Sunshine Coast and south to the Gold Coast and Sydney. There are also several bus companies that operate daily services to and from Brisbane, including Greyhound Australia and Murrays Bus Service. If you're traveling from nearby cities, such as the Gold Coast or Sunshine Coast, you can also take a train or bus, with regular services operating throughout the day.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Brisbane has an extensive domestic air network, with flights to most major cities in Australia. The main domestic airlines operating out of Brisbane Airport are Qantas, Virgin Australia, and Jetstar, with flights to destinations such as Sydney, Melbourne, Cairns, and Perth. You can book flights online through the airlines' websites or through a travel agent, with prices varying depending on the route and time of year. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Brisbane can range from $100 to $500 or more, depending on the destination and class of travel.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Brisbane has a moderate rail network, with trains operating to nearby cities and regional centers. The main rail line runs north to the Sunshine Coast and south to the Gold Coast, with regular services operating throughout the day. There are also trains to other parts of Queensland, such as the Gold Coast, Cairns, and Longreach. The rail network is operated by Queensland Rail, with tickets available online or at train stations. While there are no high-speed rail options in Brisbane, the trains are generally comfortable and reliable, with food and drink available on board.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several bus and coach companies operating in Brisbane, including Greyhound Australia and Murrays Bus Service. These companies offer services to nearby cities and regional centers, as well as to other parts of Australia. The buses are generally comfortable and well-maintained, with amenities such as air conditioning and Wi-Fi. Prices vary depending on the route and class of travel, but you can expect to pay around $20 to $100 or more for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Brisbane, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The road quality in Brisbane is generally good, with well-maintained highways and city streets.
- Fuel costs in Brisbane are around $1.50 to $2.00 per liter, depending on the type of fuel and location.
- If you're planning to rent a car, book in advance to ensure availability, especially during peak travel seasons.
- Be aware of hazards such as pedestrians, cyclists, and roadwork, and always follow local traffic laws and regulations.
- It's a good idea to purchase car insurance when renting a vehicle, as it can provide peace of mind and financial protection in case of an accident.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Always check the schedule and plan your route in advance to avoid delays and congestion.
- Consider purchasing a Go Card, which is a reusable transit card that can be used on buses, trains, and ferries.
- Be mindful of peak hour traffic, which can cause significant delays, especially during weekdays.
- If you're using a ride-hailing app, make sure to check the estimated fare and route before you start your journey.
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas and public transport, to avoid theft or loss.
- Don't forget to validate your ticket or tap your Go Card when boarding public transport to avoid fines.
- Download the TransLink app to plan your journey and track your route in real-time.
- Be aware of any road closures or construction that may affect your journey and plan accordingly.
