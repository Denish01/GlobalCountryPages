[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Brisbane is situated in the southeastern part of Queensland, approximately 945 kilometers north of Sydney, the capital of New South Wales. The city is nestled in a curve of the Brisbane River, which flows into Moreton Bay, a large bay in the Coral Sea. To the east of Brisbane lies the Pacific Ocean, while the Great Dividing Range lies to the west. Brisbane is bordered by the state of New South Wales to the south, and the Sunshine Coast and Gold Coast regions to the north and south, respectively.

The city's location makes it an important hub for trade and commerce, with the Port of Brisbane being one of the busiest ports in Australia. Brisbane is also a major cultural and educational center, with several universities and institutions, including the University of Queensland and the Queensland University of Technology. The city's proximity to the Pacific Ocean and the Coral Sea also makes it a popular destination for tourists, with many beaches, islands, and marine parks within easy reach.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Brisbane's terrain is characterized by a mix of coastal lowlands, hills, and mountains. The city is situated in a valley, with the Brisbane River flowing through it, and is surrounded by hills and mountains, including the Taylor Range and the D'Aguilar Range. To the east of the city lies the Coral Sea, with several islands, including Moreton Island and North Stradbroke Island, which are popular tourist destinations. The terrain of Brisbane is also dotted with several parks and gardens, including the Brisbane Botanic Gardens and the Roma Street Parkland.

The landscape of Brisbane is also marked by several rivers and creeks, including the Brisbane River, the Nerang River, and the Pine River. The city's coastal location means that it is also home to several beaches, including the popular South Bank Parklands and the Wynnum Beach. The terrain of Brisbane is generally mild, with few extreme elevation changes, making it an ideal location for outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and water sports.

The surrounding countryside of Brisbane is also home to several national parks and forests, including the Lamington National Park and the Tamborine National Park. These parks offer a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and camping, and are home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several species of eucalyptus trees, mangroves, and rainforest plants.
