[SECTION] What Is Cairns? [/SECTION]
Cairns is a **city** located in the tropical region of Far North Queensland, Australia. It is situated about 1,700 kilometers northwest of Brisbane, the state capital, and is nestled between the Coral Sea and the Great Dividing Range. Cairns is not a sovereign nation but rather a part of Australia, and as such, it does not have its own government or international recognition as a separate entity. The city is known for its stunning natural beauty, with the Great Barrier Reef, the Daintree Rainforest, and the Atherton Tablelands all within easy reach.

Cairns is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year with its warm climate, beautiful beaches, and outdoor recreational activities such as snorkeling, diving, and hiking. The city has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong Indigenous Australian presence and a history of settlement dating back to the late 19th century. Cairns is also an important economic center, with a diverse range of industries including tourism, agriculture, and mining.

The city's proximity to the Great Barrier Reef, one of the most biologically diverse ecosystems on the planet, makes it an ideal base for exploring this natural wonder. The reef is home to an incredible array of marine life, including coral, fish, turtles, and whales, and offers a range of activities such as snorkeling, diving, and boat tours. Cairns is also close to the Daintree Rainforest, the oldest continuously surviving rainforest on earth, which is home to a vast array of plant and animal species.
