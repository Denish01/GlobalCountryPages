# Cost of Living in Cairns - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Cairns is generally moderate, with daily expenses ranging from $80-150 USD per day for a mid-range traveler, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike.

Currency: AUD

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Cairns? [/SECTION]
Cairns is a popular tourist destination in tropical North Queensland, Australia, known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities. Compared to other major cities in Australia, Cairns has a relatively moderate cost of living. The cost of accommodation, food, and activities in Cairns is generally lower than in cities like Sydney or Melbourne, but higher than in smaller regional towns. Overall, Cairns offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats.

In terms of global averages, Cairns is more expensive than many cities in Southeast Asia or South America, but more affordable than many cities in Western Europe or North America. The city's economy is driven by tourism, agriculture, and mining, which can affect the cost of living and the availability of certain goods and services. However, overall, Cairns is a great value destination for travelers who want to experience the beauty of tropical Australia without breaking the bank.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting Cairns during the shoulder season (April-May or September-November) to avoid peak tourist prices.
- Look for accommodation options outside of the city center, such as in nearby suburbs or towns, to save on rent.
- Take advantage of the city's many free or low-cost outdoor activities, such as visiting the Cairns Esplanade or hiking in the surrounding mountains.
- Eat at local eateries or food trucks, which often offer affordable and delicious meals.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on fuel and parking costs.
- Book tours and activities in advance to take advantage of early bird discounts or package deals.
