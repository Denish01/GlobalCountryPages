[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Cairns is situated in the tropical region of Far North Queensland, approximately 1,700 kilometers northwest of Brisbane, the state capital. The city is nestled between the Coral Sea to the east and the Great Dividing Range to the west, with the Atherton Tableland and the Daintree Rainforest nearby. The city's proximity to the Great Barrier Reef, one of the world's most famous coral reef systems, makes it a popular destination for tourists and snorkeling enthusiasts. The surrounding landscape is characterized by lush rainforests, rugged mountains, and scenic coastlines, providing a unique and diverse environment.

The city of Cairns is bordered by several smaller towns and suburbs, including Smithfield, Yorkeys Knob, and Trinity Beach. The nearby cities of Port Douglas and Mossman are also popular tourist destinations, known for their beautiful beaches, golf courses, and wildlife sanctuaries. The region is surrounded by several major roads, including the Captain Cook Highway and the Bruce Highway, which provide easy access to the city and its surroundings. The Cairns Airport is also a major transportation hub, with flights connecting to major cities in Australia and around the world.
