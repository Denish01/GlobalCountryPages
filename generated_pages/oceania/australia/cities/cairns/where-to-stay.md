# Where to Stay in Cairns — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Cairns, consider the city's central business district, northern beaches, and surrounding suburbs, which offer a range of accommodation options to suit different types of travelers.

For each of the top areas in Cairns, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Cairns City** (Cairns)
Best For: Backpackers, families, and business travelers
Vibe: Cairns City has a lively atmosphere with many restaurants, bars, and shops, and is within walking distance to the waterfront and marina. The city also hosts various events and festivals throughout the year, making it a great spot for those looking for entertainment.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Convenient location, easy access to public transportation, and a wide range of dining and entertainment options. The city is also close to the Cairns Esplanade, a scenic waterfront area with parks, walking trails, and picnic spots.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, especially during peak season.

**Palm Cove** (Cairns)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families
Vibe: Palm Cove is a picturesque beach town with a relaxed and sophisticated atmosphere, featuring high-end resorts, boutique shops, and fine dining restaurants. The town is surrounded by beautiful beaches, rainforests, and the Great Barrier Reef, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers and those seeking relaxation.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Stunning beach, upscale amenities, and easy access to the Great Barrier Reef. Palm Cove is also a great spot for water sports, such as snorkeling, diving, and kayaking.
Cons: Limited budget options and can be quite expensive.

**Trinity Beach** (Cairns)
Best For: Families and couples
Vibe: Trinity Beach is a laid-back and family-friendly suburb with a beautiful beach, parklands, and a range of restaurants and cafes. The suburb is also close to the Cairns Golf Course and the Great Barrier Reef, making it a great spot for golfers and nature lovers.
Price Range: $100-250/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, patrolled beach, and a relaxed atmosphere. Trinity Beach is also a great spot for swimming, surfing, and sunbathing.
Cons: Limited nightlife and can be quite quiet.

**Yorkeys Knob** (Cairns)
Best For: Budget travelers and families
Vibe: Yorkeys Knob is a quiet and affordable suburb with a beautiful beach, a boat club, and a range of accommodation options. The suburb is also close to the Cairns Marina and the Great Barrier Reef, making it a great spot for boat tours and water sports.
Price Range: $60-150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation, beautiful beach, and a relaxed atmosphere. Yorkeys Knob is also a great spot for fishing, boating, and other water activities.
Cons: Limited dining and entertainment options.

**Edge Hill** (Cairns)
Best For: Business travelers and couples
Vibe: Edge Hill is a leafy and upscale suburb with a range of accommodation options, restaurants, and cafes. The suburb is also close to the Cairns City and the airport, making it a great spot for business travelers and those with early flights.
Price Range: $120-280/night
Pros: Convenient location, upscale amenities, and easy access to the city. Edge Hill is also a great spot for shopping, dining, and entertainment.
Cons: Limited budget options and can be quite expensive.

**Manunda** (Cairns)
Best For: Budget travelers and backpackers
Vibe: Manunda is a vibrant and multicultural suburb with a range of accommodation options, restaurants, and shops. The suburb is also close to the Cairns City and the airport, making it a great spot for budget travelers and backpackers.
Price Range: $40-100/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation, multicultural atmosphere, and easy access to public transportation. Manunda is also a great spot for shopping, dining, and entertainment.
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, especially during peak season.
