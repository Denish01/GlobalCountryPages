[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Cairns is a popular tourist destination in tropical North Queensland, Australia, known for its stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and friendly locals. The city has a reputation for being safe, with low crime rates compared to other major cities in Australia. However, as with any tourist destination, there are potential risks and hazards to be aware of, such as petty crime, scams, and natural disasters. Visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as being mindful of their belongings, avoiding walking alone at night, and staying informed about local conditions.

Overall, Cairns is a great destination for travelers, with a strong focus on tourism and a wide range of activities and attractions to enjoy. The city has a well-developed infrastructure, with good transportation links, accommodation options, and medical facilities. With some basic precautions and awareness of potential risks, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Cairns. The city's tropical location and outdoor lifestyle mean that visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with sun exposure, heat, and water activities, and take necessary precautions to protect themselves.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **bag snatching** in crowded areas, such as markets and tourist hotspots
- Beware of **scams** targeting tourists, such as fake tour operators and accommodation providers
- Be cautious of **wildlife**, such as crocodiles and snakes, when visiting outdoor areas and participating in water activities
- Be aware of **sun exposure** and **heat stroke** risks, and take necessary precautions to protect yourself
- Be mindful of **riptide** risks when swimming at beaches, and follow local advice and warnings
- Be cautious of **drinking water** from unverified sources, and stick to bottled or filtered water
- Be aware of **road safety** risks, such as driving on unsealed roads and encountering wildlife

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Cairns, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Cairns, but bottled water is widely available
- **Medical Facilities:** Cairns has a well-equipped hospital and medical facilities, with access to specialist care and emergency services
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are readily available in Cairns, with a wide range of medications and health products available

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always **wear sunscreen** and a hat when outdoors, and stay hydrated to avoid heat stroke
- Be **mindful of your belongings**, especially in crowded areas, and keep valuables secure
- **Follow local advice** and warnings, especially when participating in water activities or visiting outdoor areas
- **Stay informed** about local conditions, such as weather forecasts and road closures
- **Respect wildlife**, and keep a safe distance when encountering animals in the wild
- **Use reputable tour operators**, and research providers before booking tours or activities
- **Stay in well-lit and populated areas** at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- **Keep your accommodation secure**, and use hotel safes or lockers to store valuables

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- **Be aware of your surroundings**, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- **Use reputable transportation services**, such as taxis or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- **Keep in touch with family and friends** back home, and let them know your itinerary and accommodation details
- **Consider joining a group tour** or using a reputable tour operator, especially when participating in outdoor activities or visiting remote areas
