[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Cairns is a major tourist destination in Australia, and as such, it has a well-developed transportation network. The city is served by Cairns Airport, which is located about 7 kilometers from the city center. The airport receives direct flights from major Australian cities, including Brisbane, Sydney, and Melbourne, as well as from some international destinations, such as New Zealand and Southeast Asia. If you're coming from outside of Australia, you may need to fly into a major hub like Brisbane or Sydney and then take a domestic flight to Cairns.

In addition to flying, Cairns can also be reached by car or bus. The city is located on the Bruce Highway, which connects it to other major cities in Queensland, such as Brisbane and Townsville. There are also several bus companies that operate daily services to and from Cairns, including Greyhound Australia and Premier Motor Service. If you're coming from nearby cities, such as Port Douglas or the Atherton Tablelands, you can also take a shuttle or taxi service.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
If you're traveling within Australia, you can use domestic airlines such as Qantas, Virgin Australia, and Jetstar to get to Cairns. These airlines offer frequent flights from major cities, and you can often find good deals if you book in advance. Some of the main routes to Cairns include flights from Brisbane, Sydney, and Melbourne, as well as from other regional cities in Queensland. When booking domestic flights, it's a good idea to compare prices across different airlines and to consider flying into a nearby airport, such as the Great Barrier Reef Airport in Hamilton Island, if you're planning to visit the surrounding area.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Cairns has a limited rail network, but you can take a train to the city from Brisbane on the Spirit of Queensland service, which is operated by Queensland Rail. The journey takes around 24 hours, depending on the route and the type of service you choose. There are also some tourist trains that operate in the area, such as the Kuranda Scenic Railway, which runs from Cairns to the nearby town of Kuranda. This train journey takes around 2 hours and offers stunning views of the surrounding rainforest and waterfalls.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Cairns, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an international driver's license.
- The road conditions in and around Cairns are generally good, with well-maintained highways and scenic drives.
- Fuel costs in Australia are relatively high, with petrol prices averaging around $1.50 per liter.
- If you're planning to rent a car, it's a good idea to book in advance and to compare prices across different companies.
- Some common hazards to watch out for when driving in Cairns include wildlife, such as kangaroos and wallabies, and heavy rainfall during the wet season.
- It's also a good idea to take regular breaks and to drive safely, especially on long journeys or in unfamiliar areas.
