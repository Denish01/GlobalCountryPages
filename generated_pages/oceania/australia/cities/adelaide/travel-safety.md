[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Adelaide is considered one of the safest cities in Australia, with a low crime rate compared to other major cities. The city has a strong focus on community safety, with a visible police presence and well-lit streets. However, as with any city, it's still important to take common-sense precautions to ensure your safety, such as being aware of your surroundings and keeping an eye on your belongings. Adelaide is a popular tourist destination, and the city takes steps to ensure that visitors feel welcome and safe.

Adelaide's safety rating is reflected in its low rates of violent crime and petty theft. The city has a strong and visible police presence, and there are many safe and well-lit areas to explore. However, as with any city, there are some areas that are considered safer than others. Overall, Adelaide is a great destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Australian culture and lifestyle in a safe and welcoming environment.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake charity collectors or street performers
- Drink spiking and alcohol-related violence in nightlife areas
- Overcharging by taxi drivers or tour operators
- Online scams and phishing attempts targeting tourists
- Opportunistic theft from cars or hotel rooms
- Aggressive begging or panhandling in some areas

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Adelaide, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Adelaide
- **Medical Facilities:** Adelaide has high-quality medical facilities and hospitals, including the Royal Adelaide Hospital and the Women's and Children's Hospital
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are easy to find and well-stocked, with many open late or 24 hours

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or lockbox
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas at night
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel's safe if available
- Be cautious of strangers approaching you or asking for money
- Follow local advice and instructions in the event of an emergency

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Adelaide is generally a very safe city for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong focus on community safety
- Be aware of your surroundings and take normal precautions to protect yourself and your belongings

- Keep in touch with family and friends back home and let them know your itinerary and plans
