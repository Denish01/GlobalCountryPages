# Getting Around Melbourne — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Melbourne, you can choose from a variety of transportation options including an extensive public transportation system, ride-hailing apps, and car rentals, making it easy to navigate the city and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Melbourne is easily accessible by air, with Melbourne Tullamarine Airport being the main international airport serving the city. The airport is located about 23 kilometers northwest of the city center and offers direct flights to many major cities around the world, including Asia, Europe, and the Americas. You can also enter Melbourne by car or bus from other parts of Australia, with the city being well-connected to the rest of the country via a network of highways and intercity bus routes. The city's proximity to the coast also makes it a popular destination for cruise ships, with the Port of Melbourne being a major stop for many cruise lines.

For those traveling from other parts of Australia, Melbourne is also accessible by train, with the city being a major stop on the country's rail network. The **Overland** train service connects Melbourne to Adelaide, while the **XPT** service connects the city to Sydney. There are also several bus companies that operate intercity routes to and from Melbourne, including **Greyhound Australia** and **Murrays Bus Service**. These services offer a convenient and affordable way to travel to Melbourne from other parts of the country.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Melbourne is well-connected to the rest of Australia by air, with several domestic airlines operating flights to and from the city. **Qantas**, **Virgin Australia**, and **Jetstar** are some of the major airlines that operate domestic flights to Melbourne, with routes connecting the city to most major cities in Australia. You can book flights online or through a travel agent, and it's a good idea to book in advance to get the best deals. Approximate costs for domestic flights to Melbourne can vary depending on the route and time of year, but you can expect to pay around $100-$300 for a one-way flight from a major city like Sydney or Brisbane.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Melbourne has an extensive rail network, with several train lines connecting the city to the rest of Australia. The **V/Line** service operates regional trains to destinations in Victoria, while the **Overland** and **XPT** services connect Melbourne to other states. The **Metro Trains Melbourne** network provides an efficient and convenient way to get around the city, with several train lines operating on a frequent schedule. You can book train tickets online or at a station, and it's a good idea to plan your route in advance to avoid peak-hour crowds.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Melbourne, you'll need a valid driver's license from your home country or an **International Driving Permit**.
- The city's roads are generally in good condition, but be aware of **peak-hour traffic** and **roadwork** in some areas.
- Fuel costs can vary depending on the type of vehicle and the location, but you can expect to pay around $1.50-$2.50 per liter for gasoline.
- Car rental companies such as **Avis**, **Hertz**, and **Europcar** operate in Melbourne, and it's a good idea to book in advance to get the best deals.
- Be aware of **toll roads** and **parking fees** in some areas, and make sure to follow local **traffic rules** and **road signs**.
- It's also a good idea to have a **GPS** or **map** to help navigate the city's streets.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be sure to purchase a **Myki** card for travel on public transportation, as **cash** is no longer accepted on trams and buses.
- Download the **PTV** app for journey planning and real-time information on public transportation.
- Be aware of **peak-hour surcharges** on taxis and ride-hailing services, and try to avoid traveling during these times if possible.
- Consider purchasing a **Myki Money** card for convenience and flexibility when traveling on public transportation.
- Always follow local **traffic rules** and **road signs**, and be aware of **pedestrian** and **cyclist** traffic in some areas.
- Be cautious of **scams** and **overcharging** by some taxi drivers, and always use a licensed and metered taxi service.
- Consider using a **pre-paid SIM card** or **portable Wi-Fi hotspot** to stay connected and navigate the city during your stay.
