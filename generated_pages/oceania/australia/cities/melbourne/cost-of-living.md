# Cost of Living in Melbourne - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Melbourne is generally considered to be moderate to expensive, with a daily budget ranging from $60-120 USD for backpackers to $200-350 USD for mid-range travelers.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Expensive
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $150-250 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $250-400 USD
Currency: AUD
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Melbourne? [/SECTION]
Melbourne is considered one of the most expensive cities in Australia, with a cost of living that is higher than the national average. Compared to other major cities in the world, Melbourne is more expensive than many cities in Asia and Europe, but less expensive than cities like New York or London. The city's high standard of living, strong economy, and limited housing supply all contribute to its relatively high cost of living. Overall, Melbourne is a great place to live or visit, but it does require a significant budget to enjoy all that the city has to offer.

In terms of specific costs, Melbourne is generally more expensive than other cities in Australia, such as Sydney or Brisbane. However, it is still possible to find affordable options for accommodation, food, and transportation, especially for those who are willing to look outside of the city center. With some planning and research, visitors and residents can make the most of their time in Melbourne without breaking the bank.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider living outside of the city center to save on accommodation costs
- Take advantage of Melbourne's public transportation system, which is efficient and affordable
- Eat at local eateries and street food stalls, which offer a range of affordable and delicious options
- Look for discounts and promotions at local restaurants and cafes
- Visit the city's many free museums and galleries, such as the National Gallery of Victoria
- Take a stroll along the Yarra River and enjoy the city's many parks and gardens, which are free to visit and offer a great way to spend time outdoors without spending money.
