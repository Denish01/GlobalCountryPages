[SECTION] What Is Hobart? [/SECTION]
Hobart is a city located on the southern coast of Tasmania, an island state of Australia. It is situated on the estuary of the Derwent River, which provides a stunning backdrop to the city's historic and modern architecture. As the capital of Tasmania, Hobart is the administrative and economic center of the state, with a strong focus on tourism, education, and the arts. The city has a rich history, dating back to its founding in 1804 as a British penal colony, and is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and stunning natural surroundings.

Hobart is nestled in a picturesque valley, surrounded by rugged mountains and waterways, making it an ideal base for exploring the natural beauty of Tasmania. The city has a mild climate, with four distinct seasons, and is known for its cool winters and mild summers. Hobart is also home to a thriving food and wine scene, with a strong focus on local produce, craft breweries, and artisanal products.

The city's compact size and easy-to-navigate streets make it an ideal destination for visitors, with many of its main attractions within walking distance of the city center. From its historic waterfront to its vibrant arts and cultural scene, Hobart has something to offer for every interest and age group.

1. 1804 - Hobart was founded as a British penal colony, with the arrival of the first European settlers.
2. 1825 - The city was officially named Hobart Town, after Lord Hobart, the British Secretary of State for the Colonies.
3. 1853 - The University of Tasmania was established, making it one of the oldest universities in Australia.
4. 1901 - Hobart became the capital of the newly formed state of Tasmania, following federation with the rest of Australia.
5. 1969 - The city hosted the first Sydney to Hobart Yacht Race, which has since become an annual event.
6. 1988 - The Tasmanian Museum and Art Gallery was established, showcasing the state's natural and cultural history.
7. 2011 - The city underwent significant redevelopment, with the construction of new waterfront precincts and public spaces.
