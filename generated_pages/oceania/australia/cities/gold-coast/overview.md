[SECTION] What Is Gold Coast? [/SECTION]
The Gold Coast is a city situated in the southeastern part of the state of Queensland, Australia, approximately 66 kilometers southeast of the state capital Brisbane. It is not an independent country but rather a part of Australia, with its own local government. The city is known for its stunning beaches, including Surfers Paradise, Main Beach, and Burleigh Heads, which attract millions of tourists every year. The Gold Coast is also famous for its theme parks, such as Dreamworld, Sea World, and Warner Bros. Movie World, making it a popular destination for families and thrill-seekers.

Geographically, the Gold Coast is bounded by the Pacific Ocean to the east, the Great Dividing Range to the west, and the New South Wales border to the south. The city has a subtropical climate, with mild winters and hot, humid summers. The Gold Coast is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including eucalyptus forests, mangrove swamps, and a variety of bird species.

The Gold Coast has a rich cultural scene, with numerous festivals and events held throughout the year, including the Gold Coast Marathon, the Magic Millions horse racing carnival, and the Bleach Festival. The city is also home to a number of museums, art galleries, and performance venues, showcasing the work of local and international artists.
