# Gold Coast Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

The culture of Gold Coast is a vibrant and laid-back fusion of Australian, Indigenous, and international influences, shaped by its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and thriving tourism industry.

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Gold Coast's culture is defined by its unique blend of urban and coastal lifestyles, with a strong emphasis on outdoor activities, entertainment, and community spirit. The city's cultural identity is influenced by its Indigenous heritage, with the Yugambeh people being the traditional owners of the land. The city's population is predominantly of European descent, with a significant proportion of residents born in the United Kingdom, New Zealand, and other parts of Australia. English is the primary language spoken, but the city's cultural diversity is reflected in its many restaurants, festivals, and cultural events celebrating international cuisines and traditions.

The Gold Coast's cultural landscape is also shaped by its history as a popular tourist destination, with a thriving hospitality industry and a wide range of attractions, theme parks, and events. The city's cultural identity is characterized by a relaxed, friendly, and welcoming atmosphere, with a strong sense of community and a deep connection to the natural environment. The city's cultural institutions, such as the Gold Coast Arts Centre and the Yugambeh Museum, showcase the region's history, art, and culture, and provide a platform for local artists and performers to share their work.

The Gold Coast's cultural diversity is also reflected in its many cultural festivals and events, which celebrate the city's Indigenous heritage, its history, and its international connections. The city's cultural identity is a dynamic and evolving entity, shaped by the interactions and contributions of its diverse population, and reflected in its vibrant arts scene, its thriving food culture, and its many community events and festivals.
