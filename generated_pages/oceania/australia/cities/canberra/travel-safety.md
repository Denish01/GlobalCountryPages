[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Canberra is the capital city of Australia and is known for its cleanliness, organization, and safety. The city has a strong police presence and a low crime rate, making it an ideal destination for tourists. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around the city, day or night, without worrying about their safety. However, as with any city, it's still important to take common-sense precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

The city's safety is also reflected in its low rate of violent crime, with most crimes being petty in nature, such as theft or vandalism. Additionally, the city has a strong focus on community safety, with many initiatives in place to prevent crime and support victims. The police force is also very responsive to tourists, with a dedicated tourist police unit that can provide assistance and support.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas, such as shopping centres and tourist attractions.
- Keep an eye out for **scams** targeting tourists, such as fake charity collectors or street performers asking for money.
- Be cautious when using **ATMs** and **credit card machines**, as there have been reports of card skimming and fraud.
- Avoid walking alone in **isolated areas** at night, such as parks or industrial areas.
- Be aware of **traffic** and follow local road rules, as Canberra has a number of roundabouts and one-way streets.
- Keep your **valuables secure**, such as your passport and phone, and be mindful of your surroundings.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Canberra, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Canberra, but bottled water is also widely available.
- **Medical Facilities:** Canberra has a number of high-quality hospitals and medical centres, including the Canberra Hospital and the Calvary John James Hospital.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are easy to find in Canberra, with many located in shopping centres and along main streets.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **mobile phone** with you, in case of emergencies.
- Be aware of your **surroundings**, especially in crowded areas or at night.
- Keep your **valuables secure**, such as your passport and phone.
- Use **reputable taxi services** or ride-sharing apps, such as Uber.
- Follow local **road rules** and be mindful of traffic.
- Keep an eye on your **drinks** when out at bars or clubs, to avoid drink spiking.
- Stay **hydrated** and wear **sunscreen**, as Canberra can be hot and sunny during the summer months.
