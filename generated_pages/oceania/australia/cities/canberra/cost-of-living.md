# Cost of Living in Canberra - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Canberra is generally considered to be moderate to expensive, with daily budgets ranging from $80-150 USD per day for backpackers to $250-400 USD per day for those seeking a more comfortable experience.

Currency: AUD
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Canberra? [/SECTION]
Canberra, the capital city of Australia, has a cost of living that is comparable to other major cities in the country, such as Sydney and Melbourne. However, it is generally considered to be slightly more affordable than these cities, with lower housing costs and a more laid-back atmosphere. Compared to global averages, Canberra is still a relatively expensive city, but it offers a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and a high standard of living. Overall, visitors and residents can expect to pay a premium for goods and services in Canberra, but the city's many free attractions and outdoor spaces help to offset these costs.

In terms of regional comparisons, Canberra is more expensive than many other cities in Australia, such as Adelaide and Hobart, but it is still more affordable than some of the larger cities, such as Sydney and Brisbane. The city's proximity to the Australian Alps and the coast also makes it an attractive destination for outdoor enthusiasts, who may be willing to pay a premium for the opportunity to enjoy the city's many natural attractions.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider visiting Canberra during the shoulder season (April-May or September-October) to avoid peak tourist prices and take advantage of more affordable accommodation options.
- Take advantage of the city's many free attractions, such as the National Museum of Australia and the Australian War Memorial.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transport costs.
- Eat at local eateries or food trucks instead of mid-range restaurants to save on food costs.
- Look for discounts or package deals on accommodation and tourist attractions to save even more.
- Consider sharing an apartment or house with others to split the cost of rent and utilities.
