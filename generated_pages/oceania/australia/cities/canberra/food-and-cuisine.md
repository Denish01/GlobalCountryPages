# Canberra Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Canberra, be sure to try the city's unique fusion of Australian, Asian, and European cuisine, which showcases a diverse range of flavors and ingredients, including popular dishes such as **Chiko Rolls**, **Meat Pies**, and **Lamington**.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: beef, lamb, seafood, vegetables
National Dish: **Meat Pie**, a filling pastry filled with minced meat and gravy
Meal Times: 7-9 am for breakfast, 12-2 pm for lunch, 6-8 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% in fine dining establishments, not expected in casual eateries
Dietary Notes: vegetarian and gluten-free options widely available, halal and kosher options also accessible
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Canberra's cuisine is a reflection of its cultural diversity, with a mix of traditional Australian dishes, Asian flavors, and European influences. The city's food scene is shaped by its proximity to the coast, which provides an abundance of fresh seafood, as well as its rural surroundings, which offer a wide range of meat and produce. Canberra's culinary identity is also defined by its vibrant cultural festivals and events, which celebrate the city's multicultural heritage. From the **National Multicultural Festival** to the **Canberra Food and Wine Festival**, these events showcase the city's diverse food culture and provide a platform for local chefs and producers to showcase their talents.

The city's culinary landscape is also influenced by its educational institutions, which attract students and academics from around the world. This has created a demand for international cuisine, with a wide range of restaurants and cafes serving everything from **Korean Bibimbap** to **Indian Curries**. Canberra's food culture is also characterized by its emphasis on local and seasonal produce, with many restaurants and cafes sourcing their ingredients from local farmers and suppliers.

Canberra's food scene is constantly evolving, with new restaurants and cafes opening up all the time. The city's culinary identity is also shaped by its history and geography, with many of its dishes reflecting its British and Irish heritage. From traditional **Sunday Roasts** to **Fish and Chips**, Canberra's cuisine is a unique blend of old and new, with a focus on fresh, local ingredients and creative flavor combinations.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- Table manners are generally informal, with an emphasis on casual, relaxed dining
- Tipping is not expected in casual eateries, but 5-10% is customary in fine dining establishments
- Reservations are recommended for popular restaurants, especially on weekends
- Dress codes are generally casual, but some fine dining establishments may require more formal attire
- It is customary to wait for the host to indicate where to sit, and to keep your hands visible on the table
- It is also customary to try a little of each dish, and to finish your meal before leaving the table
