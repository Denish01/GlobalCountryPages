# Education Costs in Australia

The cost of education in Australia varies widely, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private schools and international universities can range from $5,000 to $50,000 or more per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The Australian education system is highly regarded, with a strong emphasis on public education. Public schools in Australia are generally considered to be of high quality, with well-qualified teachers and modern facilities. However, private schools and international schools are also popular options, particularly among expatriate families. The language of instruction in Australian schools is English, and compulsory education lasts for 13 years, from age 5 to 18. International schools are also available, offering a range of curricula, including the International Baccalaureate (IB) and the Cambridge International Examinations (CIE).

The quality of education in Australia is generally considered to be high, with a strong focus on academic achievement, as well as extracurricular activities and personal development. Public schools are free or subsidized for local students, while private schools and international schools can be quite expensive. Despite the cost, many expatriate families choose to send their children to private or international schools, citing the high-quality education and facilities. Additionally, Australia is home to many world-class universities, which attract students from all over the world.
