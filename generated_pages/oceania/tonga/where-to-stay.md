# Where to Stay in Tonga — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in Tonga, consider the islands of Tongatapu, Ha'apai, and Vava'u, which offer a range of accommodations catering to different types of travelers, from budget-friendly options to luxury resorts.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Tonga, the following neighborhoods are recommended:
**Nukualofa** (Nukualofa)
Best For: business, luxury
Vibe: Nukualofa, the capital city, offers a mix of traditional Tongan culture and modern amenities, with a vibrant market and a variety of restaurants. The city has a lively atmosphere, especially around the Royal Palace and the waterfront.
Price Range: $100-300/night
Pros: convenient access to government offices, restaurants, and shops, proximity to the Royal Palace, and a range of accommodation options.
Cons: traffic can be heavy, and the city can be noisy.

**Kanokupolu** (Tongatapu)
Best For: family, backpacker
Vibe: Kanokupolu is a coastal village on the island of Tongatapu, known for its beautiful beach and laid-back atmosphere. The area is perfect for those seeking a relaxed, tropical getaway.
Price Range: $50-150/night
Pros: stunning beach, affordable accommodations, and a range of outdoor activities such as snorkeling and fishing.
Cons: limited dining options, and the area can be affected by coastal erosion.

**Neiafu** (Vava'u)
Best For: luxury, family
Vibe: Neiafu, the capital of the Vava'u island group, is a picturesque town surrounded by crystal-clear waters and coral reefs. The area is ideal for those seeking a luxurious, island-hopping experience.
Price Range: $150-400/night
Pros: stunning natural beauty, range of water sports, and a variety of high-end accommodations.
Cons: the area can be expensive, and some activities may require a boat trip.

**Ha'apai** (Ha'apai)
Best For: backpacker, family
Vibe: Ha'apai is a group of islands in central Tonga, known for their pristine beaches, coral reefs, and traditional Tongan villages. The area is perfect for those seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience.
Price Range: $30-100/night
Pros: unspoiled natural beauty, affordable accommodations, and a range of outdoor activities such as snorkeling and fishing.
Cons: limited amenities, and the area can be difficult to access.

**Fafa Island** (Tongatapu)
Best For: luxury, honeymoon
Vibe: Fafa Island is a small, private island located off the coast of Tongatapu, known for its stunning beach and luxurious resort. The area is ideal for those seeking a romantic, secluded getaway.
Price Range: $200-500/night
Pros: stunning natural beauty, luxurious accommodations, and a range of activities such as snorkeling and diving.
Cons: the area can be expensive, and the island may have limited amenities.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (June to September).
- Research and compare prices across different booking platforms, such as Booking.com and Airbnb.
- Consider negotiating the price, especially for longer stays or during the off-season.
- Be aware of any additional fees or taxes, such as the Tongan government's 15% tax on accommodations.
- Look for accommodations that offer a range of amenities, such as free Wi-Fi and breakfast.
- Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before booking.
- Consider booking a package deal that includes activities and transportation.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas such as markets and bus stations.
- Keep valuables secure, and consider using a safe or locker.
- Avoid walking alone at night, and use a reputable taxi service instead.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting villages or attending cultural events.
- Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation, such as Nukualofa and Neiafu.
- Research the area before booking, and look for accommodations with good reviews and a safe location.
