# Top Things to Do in Cook Islands — Attractions & Experiences

The Cook Islands offer a diverse range of activities and experiences, from snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters of **Muri Lagoon** to exploring the vibrant culture and history of **Avarua**, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a unique and unforgettable experience.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **vaka** (traditional canoe) tour to explore the islands' stunning lagoons and marine life
- Attend a **kapa** (tattoo) workshop to learn about the traditional art of Cook Islands tattooing
- Participate in a **umukai** (traditional feast) to experience the local cuisine and culture
- Learn to play the **ukulele** or **drums** with a local musician
- Take a **cooking class** to learn how to prepare traditional Cook Islands dishes
- Go on a **fishing charter** to catch some of the islands' famous seafood
- Attend a **traditional dance** performance to experience the vibrant culture and music of the Cook Islands

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Takitumu Conservation Area**, a protected area with unique flora and fauna
- Hike to the summit of **Te Manga**, the highest point on Rarotonga
- Snorkel or dive in the **Muri Lagoon**, known for its crystal-clear waters and diverse marine life
- Visit the **Aitutaki Lagoon**, a stunning lagoon with crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches
- Take a boat tour to **Moturakiti Island**, an uninhabited island with stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters
- Go **birdwatching** in the **Rarotonga** forest, home to a variety of unique and endemic species

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Cook Islands National Museum** to learn about the islands' history and culture
- Explore the **Punanga Nui Market**, a vibrant marketplace offering local produce, handicrafts, and souvenirs
- Attend a **church service** to experience the local culture and community
- Take a **traditional weaving** class to learn how to make local handicrafts
- Visit the **Taputapuatea Marae**, an ancient temple and marae, showcasing the islands' rich history and culture
- Participate in a **traditional ceremony** to experience the local customs and traditions

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Avana Harbour**, a picturesque harbour with stunning views and a historic wharf
- Explore the **Matavera** village, a traditional village with a rich history and culture
- Take a **sunset cruise** around the **Rarotonga** lagoon, offering breathtaking views of the island
- Go **swimming** in the **Wigmore's Waterfall**, a scenic waterfall with a natural swimming pool
- Visit the **Arorangi** village, a traditional village with a rich history and culture

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Aitutaki**, a stunning island with crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches (approx. 45 minutes by flight from Rarotonga)
- Visit the **Mauke** island, a traditional island with a rich history and culture (approx. 1 hour by flight from Rarotonga)
- Explore the **Atiu** island, a scenic island with unique flora and fauna (approx. 1.5 hours by flight from Rarotonga)
- Take a day trip to the **Moturakiti Island**, an uninhabited island with stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters (approx. 30 minutes by boat from Aitutaki)
- Visit the **Mitiaro** island, a traditional island with a rich history and culture (approx. 2 hours by flight from Rarotonga)
