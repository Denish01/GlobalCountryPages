# Where to Stay in Cook Islands — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When deciding where to stay in the Cook Islands, consider the main island of Rarotonga, which offers a range of accommodations from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury resorts, as well as the smaller island of Aitutaki, known for its stunning lagoon and high-end resorts.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, the following characteristics are worth considering:
**Muri Beach** (Avarua)
Best For: Families, couples, and luxury travelers
Vibe: Muri Beach is a picturesque and lively area with a range of restaurants, shops, and water sports, making it ideal for those seeking a vibrant atmosphere and easy access to amenities. The area is surrounded by a stunning lagoon and offers breathtaking views of the ocean.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Close proximity to Avarua, stunning beach, variety of dining options
Cons: Can be crowded, limited budget-friendly options

**Avarua** (Avarua)
Best For: Business travelers, backpackers, and those on a budget
Vibe: Avarua is the capital of the Cook Islands and offers a more urban experience, with a range of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions, making it ideal for those seeking a more local experience. The area has a lively atmosphere, with a mix of modern and traditional buildings.
Price Range: $80-$200/night
Pros: Affordable, close to amenities, cultural attractions
Cons: Limited luxury options, can be noisy

**Arorangi** (Avarua)
Best For: Families, couples, and those seeking a relaxing atmosphere
Vibe: Arorangi is a quieter area on the western coast of Rarotonga, known for its stunning sunsets and beautiful beaches, making it ideal for those seeking a peaceful and relaxing atmosphere. The area is surrounded by lush tropical gardens and offers breathtaking views of the ocean.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Peaceful atmosphere, stunning sunsets, affordable
Cons: Limited dining options, can be far from Avarua

**Titikaveka** (Avarua)
Best For: Couples, honeymooners, and luxury travelers
Vibe: Titikaveka is a picturesque area on the southern coast of Rarotonga, known for its stunning beaches and luxury resorts, making it ideal for those seeking a romantic and relaxing atmosphere. The area is surrounded by lush tropical gardens and offers breathtaking views of the ocean.
Price Range: $250-$500/night
Pros: Luxurious, peaceful, stunning beaches
Cons: Limited budget-friendly options, can be isolated

**Aitutaki** (Aitutaki)
Best For: Luxury travelers, couples, and those seeking a secluded atmosphere
Vibe: Aitutaki is a smaller island in the Cook Islands, known for its stunning lagoon and high-end resorts, making it ideal for those seeking a luxurious and secluded atmosphere. The area is surrounded by crystal-clear waters and offers breathtaking views of the ocean.
Price Range: $300-$600/night
Pros: Luxurious, secluded, stunning lagoon
Cons: Limited budget-friendly options, can be far from Rarotonga

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September)
- Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider package deals that include accommodation, meals, and activities
- Negotiate with local guesthouses or hotels for better rates, especially for long-term stays
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with higher rates during peak season and lower rates during the off-season
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs
- Look for eco-friendly or sustainable accommodations to support the local environment

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
- Keep valuables secure and use hotel safes or lockers when available
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting villages or attending cultural events
- Choose accommodations with good reviews and a safe location, close to amenities and transportation
- Consider renting a car or scooter to explore the island, but be aware of road conditions and drive safely
- Stay informed about local weather conditions and follow evacuation procedures in case of a cyclone or other natural disaster
