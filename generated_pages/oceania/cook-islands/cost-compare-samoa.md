[SECTION] Overview: Cook Islands vs Samoa [/SECTION]
The Cook Islands and Samoa are two island nations in the Pacific Ocean, known for their stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. However, when it comes to cost of living, there are significant differences between the two countries. The Cook Islands has a higher GDP per capita and a more developed economy, which translates to higher prices for goods and services. On the other hand, Samoa has a more laid-back and traditional way of life, with lower prices and a more affordable cost of living. Overall, Samoa is the cheaper option, with a difference of around 20-30% in overall cost of living.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in their cost of living. The Cook Islands has a more diversified economy, with a strong focus on tourism and financial services, which drives up prices. Samoa, on the other hand, has a more agriculture-based economy, which keeps prices lower. Additionally, the Cook Islands is a territory of New Zealand, which means that it has a more direct link to the global economy and is subject to external economic influences. This can also contribute to higher prices and a more volatile economy.
