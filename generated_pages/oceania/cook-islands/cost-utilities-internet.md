[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
The utility infrastructure in Cook Islands is relatively well-developed, with a reliable supply of **electricity**, **water**, and **waste management** services. However, the costs of these services can fluctuate due to seasonal variation in demand and changes in global fuel prices. During the peak tourist season, which runs from June to September, utility costs may increase due to higher demand for services. Additionally, the Cook Islands government has implemented measures to reduce the country's reliance on fossil fuels and increase the use of **renewable energy** sources, which may impact utility costs in the long term.

The quality of utility infrastructure in Cook Islands is generally good, with a high level of **service reliability** and **customer satisfaction**. However, there may be occasional disruptions to services due to maintenance or weather-related events. The government and utility providers are working to improve the resilience and sustainability of the utility infrastructure, which may lead to changes in costs and services over time. Overall, the utility costs in Cook Islands are relatively stable, but may be subject to fluctuations due to external factors.
