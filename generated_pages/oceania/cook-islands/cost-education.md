# Education Costs in Cook Islands

The cost of education in Cook Islands varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools charge tuition fees ranging from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Cook Islands is based on the New Zealand curriculum, with English and Cook Islands Maori being the primary languages of instruction. The system is divided into primary, secondary, and tertiary education, with 13 years of compulsory education. Public schools are generally considered to be of good quality, but private schools and international schools offer a more diverse range of academic programs and extracurricular activities. International schools in Cook Islands follow curricula such as the International Baccalaureate or the Cambridge International Examinations, and are popular among expatriate families.

The quality of education in Cook Islands is generally considered to be good, with a strong focus on literacy and numeracy skills. However, the education system faces challenges such as limited resources and a shortage of qualified teachers. Despite these challenges, the Cook Islands government has made significant investments in education, including the development of new schools and the introduction of new academic programs. The education system in Cook Islands is also supported by international organizations and donor countries, which provide funding and technical assistance to improve the quality of education.
