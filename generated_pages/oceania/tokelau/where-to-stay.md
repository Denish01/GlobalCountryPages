# Where to Stay in Tokelau — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To stay in Tokelau, consider the atolls of Atafu, Nukunonu, and Fakaofo, which offer a range of accommodations from guesthouses to family-run lodges, catering to different types of travelers and budgets.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Atafu Village** (Atafu)
Best For: Backpackers / Families
Vibe: Atafu Village offers a laid-back and rustic atmosphere, with a strong sense of community, where visitors can experience the traditional Tokelauan way of life. The village is surrounded by a stunning lagoon and coral reefs, perfect for snorkeling and diving.
Price Range: $20-$50/night
Pros: Affordable accommodations, opportunity to immerse in local culture, access to beautiful beaches and marine life. 
Cons: Limited amenities and infrastructure.

**Nukunonu Village** (Nukunonu)
Best For: Luxury / Couples
Vibe: Nukunonu Village provides a more upscale and secluded experience, with modern amenities and stunning ocean views. The village is known for its picturesque scenery and tranquil atmosphere, making it ideal for honeymooners and those seeking relaxation.
Price Range: $100-$200/night
Pros: High-end accommodations, breathtaking views, excellent fishing and diving opportunities. 
Cons: Steeper prices, limited dining options.

**Fakaofo Village** (Fakaofo)
Best For: Business / Solo Travelers
Vibe: Fakaofo Village offers a mix of traditional and modern amenities, with a range of accommodations and services catering to different needs. The village is the most populous of the three atolls and has a more vibrant atmosphere, with a variety of shops and restaurants.
Price Range: $50-$100/night
Pros: Convenient location, variety of amenities, access to government services and facilities. 
Cons: Less secluded than other areas, can be noisy at times.

**Lupilopi Village** (Atafu)
Best For: Families / Groups
Vibe: Lupilopi Village provides a warm and welcoming atmosphere, with a range of accommodations suitable for families and groups. The village is known for its beautiful beaches and outdoor activities, such as fishing and hiking.
Price Range: $30-$70/night
Pros: Spacious accommodations, family-friendly activities, stunning natural scenery. 
Cons: Limited dining options, can be crowded during peak season.

**Motufala Village** (Nukunonu)
Best For: Backpackers / Solo Travelers
Vibe: Motufala Village offers a budget-friendly and social atmosphere, with a range of affordable accommodations and activities. The village is surrounded by a stunning lagoon and coral reefs, perfect for snorkeling and diving.
Price Range: $20-$40/night
Pros: Affordable prices, social atmosphere, access to beautiful beaches and marine life. 
Cons: Basic amenities, limited infrastructure.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September), to secure availability and the best rates.
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Airbnb, to compare prices and find the best deals.
- Consider contacting local accommodation providers directly to negotiate prices or arrange for customized packages.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices tend to be higher during peak season and lower during the off-season.
- Look for packages that include meals, activities, and transportation to get the most value for your money.
- Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before making a booking.
- Be flexible with your travel dates and consider visiting during the shoulder season for better deals.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of the limited medical facilities and emergency services available in Tokelau, and take necessary precautions to stay safe and healthy.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting villages and interacting with the community.
- Keep in mind that Tokelau is a remote territory with limited infrastructure, and plan accordingly.
- Stay informed about weather conditions and sea states, especially if you plan to engage in water activities.
- Choose accommodations that are located in safe and convenient areas, with easy access to amenities and services.
- Consider hiring a local guide or joining a tour to explore the atolls and get the most out of your visit.
