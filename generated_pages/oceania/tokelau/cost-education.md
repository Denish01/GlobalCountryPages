# Education Costs in Tokelau

The cost of education in Tokelau is relatively low, with most schools being public and subsidized by the government, but specific fees and tuition costs vary depending on the type of institution and level of study.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Tokelau is overseen by the Tokelau Department of Education, which is responsible for ensuring that all citizens have access to quality education. The system is based on the New Zealand curriculum, with **Tokelauan** and **English** being the primary languages of instruction. Compulsory education in Tokelau lasts for 10 years, from primary to secondary level, and is free for all citizens. While there are no private schools in Tokelau, some students may choose to attend schools in neighboring countries, such as Samoa or New Zealand, for higher education or specialized training.

The quality of education in Tokelau is generally considered to be good, with a strong focus on **literacy** and **numeracy** skills. However, the limited resources and lack of specialized facilities can be a challenge for students who require additional support or want to pursue higher education. Despite these challenges, the education system in Tokelau has made significant progress in recent years, with a high **literacy rate** and a growing number of students pursuing higher education.
