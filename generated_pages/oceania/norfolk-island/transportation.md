To get around Norfolk Island, you can use a combination of taxis, buses, and rental cars, as the island has a limited public transportation system, but its small size makes it easy to navigate.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
The main airport on Norfolk Island is Norfolk Island Airport, which is located about 3.5 kilometers from the capital, Kingston. There are direct flights from Brisbane, Melbourne, and Sydney in Australia, as well as from Auckland in New Zealand. The airport is small, with only one terminal, but it has all the necessary facilities, including a cafe, a gift shop, and a currency exchange office. 

To get to Norfolk Island, you can fly into Norfolk Island Airport, and from there, you can take a taxi or rent a car to get to your accommodation. There are no overland entry points to the island, as it is located in the Pacific Ocean, about 1,400 kilometers northeast of Sydney. The airport is the only entry point to the island, and all visitors must arrive by air. 

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
There are no domestic flights within Norfolk Island, as the island is small and can be easily explored by car or on foot. However, there are flights from Norfolk Island to other destinations in Australia and New Zealand, operated by airlines such as Air New Zealand and Qantas. These flights can be booked online or through a travel agent, and prices vary depending on the time of year and availability. 

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
There is no rail network on Norfolk Island, as the island is small and does not require a train system. The island's roads are well-maintained and easy to navigate, and most people get around by car or on foot. 

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There is a limited bus service on Norfolk Island, which operates between the capital, Kingston, and other parts of the island. The buses are not frequent, but they can be a convenient way to get around, especially for those who do not have a car. The bus service is operated by a local company, and fares are relatively cheap.

- To drive on Norfolk Island, you need a valid driver's license from your home country, which must be held for at least one year.
- The roads on Norfolk Island are generally in good condition, but they can be narrow and winding, especially in rural areas.
- Fuel costs are relatively high on the island, as all fuel is imported from Australia.
- Car rental companies are available at the airport and in Kingston, and prices start from around $40 per day.
- When driving on Norfolk Island, be aware of the island's unique wildlife, such as the Norfolk Island parrot, and take care when driving at night, as there can be animals on the roads.
- It is also important to drive on the left-hand side of the road, as Norfolk Island is a territory of Australia.
