[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Theft**: Petty theft, such as pickpocketing or bag snatching, is rare, but visitors should still take precautions to secure their belongings.
- **Traffic accidents**: The island's narrow roads and lack of street lighting can make driving hazardous, particularly at night.
- **Natural disasters**: Norfolk Island is susceptible to cyclones, storms, and other natural disasters, and visitors should monitor weather forecasts and follow evacuation instructions if necessary.
- **Medical emergencies**: The island's remote location can make it difficult to access medical care, and visitors should take precautions to protect their health and well-being.
- **Rip currents**: The island's beaches can be hazardous, and visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from strong currents and riptides.
- **Wildlife**: The island is home to a number of unique and potentially hazardous wildlife species, including snakes, spiders, and centipedes.
- **Scams**: Scams are rare on Norfolk Island, but visitors should still be cautious when dealing with strangers or unfamiliar businesses.
