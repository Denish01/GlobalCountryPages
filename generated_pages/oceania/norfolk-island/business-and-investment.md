[SECTION] Business Environment [/SECTION]
The business environment in Norfolk Island is generally favorable, with a stable and secure economy, and a government that is open to foreign investment. The island's proximity to Australia and New Zealand makes it an attractive location for trade and investment. However, the island's small size and remote location can make it difficult to establish and operate a business, and the lack of infrastructure and resources can be a significant challenge. The government has implemented various initiatives to support businesses and attract investment, including providing financial assistance and streamlining regulatory processes.

The government's attitude towards foreign investment is positive, and it has introduced several measures to encourage foreign investment, including tax incentives and streamlined approval processes. However, the bureaucracy level can be high, and the process of obtaining necessary permits and licenses can be time-consuming. Corruption is not a significant issue in Norfolk Island, and the island has a reputation for being a safe and secure place to do business.

The island's economy is heavily reliant on tourism, and the government is working to diversify the economy and attract investment in other sectors, such as agriculture and renewable energy. The island's unique culture and stunning natural beauty make it an attractive location for tourists, and the government is investing in infrastructure and tourism facilities to support the growth of the tourism industry.
