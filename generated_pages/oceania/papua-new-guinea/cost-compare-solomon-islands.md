[SECTION] Overview: Papua New Guinea vs Solomon Islands [/SECTION]
Papua New Guinea and Solomon Islands are two neighboring countries in the Pacific Islands region, with distinct economic profiles. Papua New Guinea has a larger economy, driven by its natural resources, including oil, gas, and minerals, while Solomon Islands has a smaller, more agriculture-based economy. The cost of living in Papua New Guinea is generally higher, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being more expensive. However, the cost of living in Solomon Islands is relatively lower, making it an attractive option for expats and travelers on a budget.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Papua New Guinea has a more developed economy, with a higher GDP per capita, which contributes to its higher cost of living. In contrast, Solomon Islands has a smaller economy, with a lower GDP per capita, resulting in lower prices for goods and services. Overall, Solomon Islands is around 15-20% cheaper than Papua New Guinea, making it a more affordable option for those looking to live or travel in the region.
