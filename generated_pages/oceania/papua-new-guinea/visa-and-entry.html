Visa Policy Overview

Papua New Guinea has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Citizens of over 70 countries can enter Papua New Guinea without a visa, while others may need to obtain a visa on arrival or before their trip. The country aims to balance its security concerns with the need to promote tourism and economic development.

The visa policy is designed to facilitate travel for genuine visitors, while also ensuring that the country's borders are secure. Papua New Guinea has a unique cultural heritage and natural environment, and the government is keen to share these with the world. However, visitors must comply with the country's laws and regulations, and respect the local customs and traditions. With the right documentation and planning, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Papua New Guinea.

Tourists: Tourists can enter Papua New Guinea on a visa on arrival, which is valid for 60 days. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, but this can be extended for an additional 30 days. To extend their stay, tourists must apply to the Immigration and Citizenship Service Authority in Port Moresby.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may need to obtain a Business Visa, which requires an invitation letter from a local company or organization. The process for obtaining a business visa can take several weeks, so it's essential to plan ahead. Business travelers may also need to provide proof of their business activities and intentions.
Students: Students who wish to study in Papua New Guinea must obtain a Student Visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a recognized educational institution. The duration of the student visa will depend on the length of the course, but it can be extended if necessary.
Workers: Workers who wish to work in Papua New Guinea must obtain a Work Visa, which requires sponsorship from a local employer. The process for obtaining a work visa can be complex, so it's essential to seek advice from the Immigration and Citizenship Service Authority.

The main entry points for Papua New Guinea are the Jacksons International Airport in Port Moresby and the Nadzab Airport in Lae. Visitors can also enter the country by land through the border crossings with Indonesia. Upon arrival, visitors will need to present their travel documents and complete an arrival card. The immigration process can take some time, especially during peak travel seasons, so it's essential to be patient and prepared. Visitors may also be required to undergo a health screening or provide proof of vaccination against certain diseases.
