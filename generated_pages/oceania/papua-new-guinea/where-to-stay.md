# Where to Stay in Papua New Guinea — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Papua New Guinea, the best areas to stay include Port Moresby, Lae, and Madang, which offer a range of accommodations catering to different types of travelers and budgets. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, consider the following:
**Port Moresby** (Port Moresby)
Best For: Business/Luxury
Vibe: Port Moresby is the capital city and has a modern, urban vibe with a mix of traditional and contemporary architecture, offering a unique cultural experience. The city has a lively atmosphere with many restaurants, bars, and shops to explore.
Price Range: $150-500/night
Pros: Modern amenities, close proximity to government offices and businesses, variety of dining options
Cons: Traffic congestion, high crime rate in some areas

**Lae** (Lae)
Best For: Backpacker/Adventure
Vibe: Lae is a bustling city with a rich history and natural surroundings, offering a great base for exploring the Highlands and surrounding islands. The city has a laid-back atmosphere with many markets and cultural attractions to discover.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodations, access to outdoor activities, rich cultural heritage
Cons: Limited luxury options, traffic and noise pollution

**Madang** (Madang)
Best For: Family/Relaxation
Vibe: Madang is a coastal town with a relaxed, tropical atmosphere, surrounded by beautiful beaches and islands. The town has a peaceful ambiance with many opportunities for water sports and outdoor activities.
Price Range: $80-300/night
Pros: Scenic views, water sports, family-friendly resorts
Cons: Limited nightlife, seasonal availability of some amenities

**Goroka** (Goroka)
Best For: Backpacker/Culture
Vibe: Goroka is a town in the Eastern Highlands with a strong cultural identity and traditional practices, offering a unique insight into Papua New Guinean customs. The town has a lively atmosphere with many festivals and cultural events to experience.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Immersive cultural experience, affordable accommodations, access to traditional villages
Cons: Limited modern amenities, rugged terrain

**Kokoda** (Kokoda)
Best For: Adventure/Hiking
Vibe: Kokoda is a small town in the Oro Province, famous for its historic trekking trail and stunning natural scenery, providing an exciting adventure experience. The town has a remote, rugged atmosphere with many opportunities for hiking and exploration.
Price Range: $40-200/night
Pros: Access to the Kokoda Trail, scenic views, adventure activities
Cons: Basic amenities, limited accessibility

**Alotau** (Alotau)
Best For: Luxury/Relaxation
Vibe: Alotau is a coastal town in the Milne Bay Province, known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and luxurious resorts, offering a relaxing getaway. The town has a peaceful, tropical atmosphere with many opportunities for water sports and relaxation.
Price Range: $200-600/night
Pros: Luxurious resorts, scenic views, water sports
Cons: Limited cultural attractions, high prices

**Mount Hagen** (Mount Hagen)
Best For: Business/Adventure
Vibe: Mount Hagen is a town in the Western Highlands, surrounded by stunning natural scenery and offering a mix of business and adventure opportunities. The town has a lively atmosphere with many markets and cultural attractions to discover.
Price Range: $50-250/night
Pros: Access to business centers, outdoor activities, cultural experiences
Cons: Limited luxury options, traffic and noise pollution

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (June to September) to ensure availability.
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider negotiating rates, especially for long-term stays or off-season bookings.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with higher rates during festivals and holidays.
- Check the hotel's or resort's website for packages and promotions.
- Read reviews and check the hotel's or resort's ratings before booking.
- Be prepared for variable internet connectivity and limited payment options in some areas.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in urban areas, and use reputable taxi services or hotel shuttles.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded markets or public areas, and keep valuables secure.
- Choose accommodations with good security measures, such as 24-hour guards, CCTV cameras, and secure parking.
- Consider staying in areas with easy access to public transportation, such as bus stations or airports.
- Research local customs and traditions to avoid unintentionally offending locals.
- Stay informed about local events, festivals, and holidays, which may affect accommodation availability and prices.
