When deciding between Papua New Guinea and Indonesia, consider your priorities, as both countries offer unique experiences, from Papua New Guinea's untouched wilderness to Indonesia's vibrant culture.

Why People Compare Them

Papua New Guinea and Indonesia are often compared due to their proximity and cultural similarities, with both countries being located in the Asia-Pacific region and sharing a border on the island of New Guinea. This comparison is particularly relevant for travelers looking to explore the diverse cultures, landscapes, and ecosystems of the region, as both countries offer a wealth of exciting experiences, from trekking and diving to immersing oneself in traditional village life.

The comparison between Papua New Guinea and Indonesia is also driven by the fact that both countries are popular destinations for adventure seekers and culture enthusiasts, with many travelers considering visiting both countries as part of a larger trip to the region. Additionally, the two countries have distinct differences in terms of their history, language, and customs, making a comparison between them a fascinating and informative exercise.

Key Differences

  • Population: Papua New Guinea has a significantly smaller population than Indonesia, with approximately 11.8 million people compared to Indonesia's 273 million.
  • Size: Indonesia is roughly four times larger than Papua New Guinea, covering an area of approximately 1.9 million square kilometers.
  • Language: While both countries have English as an official language, Papua New Guinea has a more complex linguistic landscape, with over 800 languages spoken, including Tok Pisin and Hiri Motu.
  • Currency: The two countries have different currencies, with Papua New Guinea using the kina (PGK) and Indonesia using the rupiah (IDR).
  • Cost Level: Papua New Guinea is generally more expensive than Indonesia, particularly when it comes to accommodation and transportation.
  • Visa Ease: Indonesia is generally easier to visit, with a more streamlined visa process and fewer requirements for tourists.

Key Similarities

  • Climate: Both Papua New Guinea and Indonesia have a tropical climate, with high temperatures and high levels of humidity throughout the year.
  • Culture: Both countries have a rich cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on traditional customs and practices.
  • Nature: Both Papua New Guinea and Indonesia are home to incredible natural beauty, with lush rainforests, stunning coastlines, and diverse wildlife.
  • Hospitality: Both countries are known for their warm and welcoming hospitality, with a strong focus on community and family.

Bottom Line

Ultimately, the decision between Papua New Guinea and Indonesia depends on your individual priorities and interests. Papua New Guinea excels at offering a unique and untouched wilderness experience, with incredible opportunities for trekking, diving, and cultural immersion. Indonesia, on the other hand, is a more developed tourist destination, with a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant nightlife scene. By considering your goals and preferences, you can make an informed decision and choose the destination that best fits your needs.
