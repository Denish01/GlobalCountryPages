[SECTION] Overview: Northern Mariana Islands vs Palau [/SECTION]
The Northern Mariana Islands and Palau are two island territories in the Pacific Ocean with distinct economic contexts. The Northern Mariana Islands, a US territory, has a more developed economy with a strong tourism industry, while Palau, an independent nation, has a smaller economy with a growing tourism sector. The cost of living in the Northern Mariana Islands is generally higher due to its proximity to the US and the resulting higher demand for goods and services. In contrast, Palau has a more laid-back and affordable lifestyle, making it an attractive option for expats and travelers looking for a lower cost of living.

The economic context of the two territories also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. The Northern Mariana Islands has a more diversified economy with a strong focus on tourism, manufacturing, and finance, which drives up the cost of living. On the other hand, Palau's economy is primarily driven by tourism and fishing, resulting in lower costs for goods and services. Overall, Palau is around 15-20% cheaper than the Northern Mariana Islands, making it a more affordable option for those looking to live or visit the Pacific Islands.
