Education System Overview

The education system in Northern Mariana Islands is based on the United States' education model, with a Department of Education that oversees the public school system. The system consists of primary, secondary, and tertiary education, with 12 years of compulsory education. The public school system is free and open to all residents, while private schools offer alternative education options. The quality of education in the Northern Mariana Islands is considered to be good, with a high literacy rate and a strong emphasis on English language instruction. However, the system faces challenges such as limited resources and a brain drain of qualified teachers.

The public school system in Northern Mariana Islands is the largest provider of education, with a network of schools that offer primary, secondary, and tertiary education. The system is accredited by the US Department of Education, which ensures that the education provided meets the standards set by the United States. Private schools, on the other hand, offer alternative education options, including faith-based schools and international schools. These schools often have smaller class sizes and more resources, but may also have higher tuition fees.

The tertiary education system in Northern Mariana Islands is limited, with only a few institutions offering degree programs. The Northern Marianas College is the only public college in the territory, and offers associate's and bachelor's degree programs in a range of fields. Private colleges and universities also offer degree programs, but these may be more expensive and have limited accreditation.

International Schools

  • The Northern Mariana Islands have a number of international schools that offer IB and American curricula, including the Saipan International School and the Marianas International School.
  • These schools cater to the children of expatriates and locals who want an international education, and offer a range of extracurricular activities and sports programs.
  • The tuition fees for international schools in the Northern Mariana Islands vary, but can range from $5,000 to $15,000 per year.
  • International schools in the Northern Mariana Islands are located in Saipan and Tinian, and offer boarding facilities for students who want to live on campus.
  • The British curriculum is also available at some international schools, which offer A-levels and GCSEs to students.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in the Northern Mariana Islands, foreign students need to obtain a student visa, which can be applied for through the US Department of Homeland Security.
  • English language proficiency is a requirement for studying in the Northern Mariana Islands, and students may need to take an English language proficiency test such as TOEFL or IELTS.
  • There are scholarships available for international students, including the Northern Marianas College Scholarship and the University of Guam Scholarship.
  • The cost of living in the Northern Mariana Islands is relatively high, with rent and food costs ranging from $1,000 to $3,000 per month.
  • Foreign students can work part-time while studying in the Northern Mariana Islands, but need to obtain a work permit from the US Department of Labor.
  • The Northern Mariana Islands offer a unique island lifestyle and cultural experience for international students, with a range of outdoor activities and cultural events available.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • The education system in the Northern Mariana Islands faces challenges such as limited resources and a brain drain of qualified teachers.
  • The system is also heavily reliant on federal funding, which can be unpredictable and subject to change.
  • Despite these challenges, the education system in the Northern Mariana Islands has strengths such as a high literacy rate and a strong emphasis on English language instruction.
  • Recent reforms have aimed to improve the quality of education and increase access to education for all residents, including the establishment of a new curriculum and the introduction of online learning platforms.
  • The Northern Mariana Islands have also increased funding for education, which has helped to improve the quality of education and reduce class sizes.
