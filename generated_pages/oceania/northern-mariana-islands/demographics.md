# Northern Mariana Islands Demographics — Population, Ethnicity & Religion

The Northern Mariana Islands, a United States territory in Oceania, is home to a diverse population of approximately 47,329 people, with a unique blend of indigenous, Asian, and American influences.

[SECTION] Population Overview [/SECTION]
The population of the Northern Mariana Islands is relatively small, with a total area of 464 km². The population size has been steadily increasing over the years, with a growth rate of 1.3%. The median age of the population is 29.3 years, indicating a relatively young population. The majority of the population, approximately 91.2%, lives in urban areas, with the capital city of Saipan being the most populous. The population density is around 102 people per km², which is relatively high compared to other Pacific island nations.

The population growth trend in the Northern Mariana Islands is influenced by a combination of natural increase and net migration. The fertility rate is approximately 2.3 children per woman, which is higher than the replacement rate. However, the population growth rate is also affected by migration patterns, with many residents moving to the United States mainland for education, employment, or other opportunities. The life expectancy at birth is around 75.5 years, which is relatively high compared to other developing countries.

The urbanization of the population has significant implications for the economy, infrastructure, and social services. The government faces challenges in providing adequate housing, healthcare, and education to the growing urban population. However, the urbanization trend also presents opportunities for economic growth, innovation, and cultural exchange. The Northern Mariana Islands have a unique cultural identity shaped by their history, geography, and cultural exchange with other Pacific island nations.
