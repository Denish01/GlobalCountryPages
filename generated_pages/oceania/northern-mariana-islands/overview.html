What Is Northern Mariana Islands?

The Northern Mariana Islands is a territory of the United States located in the western Pacific Ocean. It is an unincorporated territory, meaning that it is not part of any state but is instead governed by the federal government. The islands are situated about 120 miles north of Guam, and their geographic position makes them a strategic location for trade and commerce. The Northern Mariana Islands are known for their stunning natural beauty, with crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and lush green forests.

The territory is composed of 14 islands, with Saipan being the largest and most populous. The Northern Mariana Islands have a unique cultural heritage, with a mix of Chamorro, Carolinian, and American influences. The islands have a rich history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to 1000 BC. Today, the Northern Mariana Islands are a popular tourist destination, with visitors drawn to their natural beauty, historic sites, and vibrant culture.

The Northern Mariana Islands have a commonwealth relationship with the United States, which means that they have a degree of autonomy in local affairs but rely on the US for defense and foreign policy. The territory has its own constitution, which was adopted in 1978, and a local government that is responsible for managing the islands' internal affairs.

Key History

  1. 1000 BC - The first human settlers arrive on the islands, likely from the Philippines or Indonesia.
  2. 1668 - The Spanish establish a colony on the islands, which they name the Mariana Islands.
  3. 1898 - The United States takes control of the islands from Spain following the Spanish-American War.
  4. 1947 - The United States establishes the Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands, which includes the Northern Mariana Islands.
  5. 1978 - The Northern Mariana Islands adopt a commonwealth constitution, which establishes their relationship with the United States.
  6. 1986 - The Northern Mariana Islands become a US territory, with the US taking responsibility for their defense and foreign policy.
  7. 2009 - The Northern Mariana Islands are affected by Typhoon Melor, which causes significant damage and disruption to the territory.
