# Northern Mariana Islands Visa & Entry Requirements

As a general rule, citizens of certain countries may enter the Northern Mariana Islands without a visa, while others require a visa or have specific requirements, so it's essential to check the current regulations before traveling.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Northern Mariana Islands before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
The Northern Mariana Islands have a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter visa-free, while others require a visa or have specific requirements. The islands are a territory of the United States, and as such, their visa policy is closely tied to that of the US. Citizens of countries with a strong relationship with the US, such as Canada, the UK, and Australia, are generally allowed to enter the Northern Mariana Islands without a visa, while citizens of other countries may require a visa or have specific requirements.

The Northern Mariana Islands offer visa-free access to citizens of around 40 countries, including most European countries, Japan, and South Korea. However, citizens of countries not on this list may require a visa, which can be obtained through the US embassy or consulate in their home country. The visa application process typically involves submitting required documents, paying a fee, and attending an interview at the embassy or consulate.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can typically stay in the Northern Mariana Islands for up to 90 days without a visa, as long as they meet the requirements of the Visa Waiver Program (VWP). To qualify for the VWP, tourists must have a valid passport, a return or onward ticket, and proof of sufficient funds. Extensions of stay are possible, but must be applied for through the US Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a non-immigrant visa, which can be obtained through the US embassy or consulate in their home country. They must provide documentation, such as a letter of invitation from a company in the Northern Mariana Islands, proof of business activities, and financial statements. The application process typically involves submitting required documents, paying a fee, and attending an interview at the embassy or consulate.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in the Northern Mariana Islands must obtain a student visa (F-1 visa) through the US embassy or consulate in their home country. They must provide proof of enrollment in an accredited institution, financial statements, and health insurance. The application process typically involves submitting required documents, paying a fee, and attending an interview at the embassy or consulate.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in the Northern Mariana Islands must obtain a work visa (H-2B visa) through the US embassy or consulate in their home country. They must have a job offer from a company in the Northern Mariana Islands and meet specific requirements, such as having the necessary qualifications and experience for the job. The application process typically involves submitting required documents, paying a fee, and attending an interview at the embassy or consulate.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry point to the Northern Mariana Islands is the Saipan International Airport (SPN), which receives flights from Guam, Japan, and other neighboring islands. Upon arrival, visitors must go through immigration and customs procedures, which involve presenting their passport, visa (if required), and completed customs declaration form. Visitors must also declare any restricted or prohibited items, such as firearms, drugs, or plant and animal products. The immigration officer may ask questions about the purpose of the visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements.

The Northern Mariana Islands also have a seaport, which receives cargo ships and private boats. However, entry by sea is subject to strict regulations and requires prior clearance from the US Customs and Border Protection (CBP). Visitors arriving by sea must meet specific requirements, such as having a valid passport, visa (if required), and proof of onward or return travel.
