# Bahrain Demographics — Population, Ethnicity & Religion

The population of Bahrain is comprised of a diverse mix of ethnic and religious groups, with approximately 1.5 million people living in the country, including citizens and expatriates.

[SECTION] Population Overview [/SECTION]
Bahrain's population has been steadily increasing over the years, with a growth rate of 4.5%, driven by a combination of natural increase and net migration. The country's population size is relatively small compared to its neighbors, but its urban population is significant, with approximately 88% of the population residing in urban areas. The population density is high, with an average of 2085 people per square kilometer. Bahrain's population is also relatively young, with a median age of 32 years. The country's population growth trends and urbanization patterns have significant implications for its economy, infrastructure, and social services.

The population of Bahrain is also characterized by a high degree of migration, with many expatriates living and working in the country. This has contributed to the country's cultural diversity and has had a significant impact on its economy. However, it also poses challenges for the government in terms of providing services and infrastructure to support the growing population. The government has implemented various initiatives to manage population growth and urbanization, including investments in housing, transportation, and healthcare.

Bahrain's population growth and urbanization trends are also closely linked to its economic development. The country's economy is driven by the oil and gas sector, but it is also diversifying into other areas such as finance, tourism, and manufacturing. This has created new job opportunities and attracted more people to the country, contributing to population growth. However, it also poses challenges in terms of managing the environmental and social impacts of rapid urbanization.
