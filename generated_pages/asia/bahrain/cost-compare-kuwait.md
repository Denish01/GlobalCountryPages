[SECTION] Overview: Bahrain vs Kuwait [/SECTION]
Bahrain and Kuwait are two neighboring countries in the Middle East with distinct economic profiles. Bahrain has a more diverse economy, with a strong focus on finance, tourism, and manufacturing, while Kuwait's economy is largely driven by oil exports. In terms of cost of living, Bahrain is generally cheaper than Kuwait, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. The cost of living in Bahrain is around 10-20% lower than in Kuwait, making it an attractive option for expats and travelers looking for a more affordable destination in the region.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in their cost of living. Bahrain has a more open economy, with a higher level of foreign investment and trade, which has helped to keep prices competitive. Kuwait, on the other hand, has a more closed economy, with a higher level of government control over key sectors, which can drive up prices. Additionally, Kuwait's high per capita income and limited housing supply contribute to higher prices for housing and other living expenses.
