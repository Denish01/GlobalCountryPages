# Bahrain Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Bahrain before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Bahrain has a moderate visa policy, offering visa-free access to citizens of over 100 countries. The country aims to facilitate travel and tourism, while maintaining security and regulatory requirements. Citizens of eligible countries can enter Bahrain without a visa and stay for a specified period, usually up to 90 days. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements and regulations can change, and travelers should always check with the official embassy or consulate of Bahrain for the latest information.

Bahrain's visa policy is designed to attract tourists, business travelers, and investors, while ensuring the country's security and integrity. The government has introduced various types of visas, including tourist visas, business visas, and e-visas, to cater to different categories of travelers. With its strategic location in the Middle East, Bahrain has become a hub for trade, commerce, and tourism, making it an attractive destination for travelers from around the world.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Bahrain with a visa on arrival, which is valid for 14 days and can be extended for an additional 14 days. To qualify for a tourist visa, travelers must have a valid passport, a return ticket, and proof of sufficient funds. The typical stay for tourists is 7-10 days, and extensions can be applied for at the Nationality, Passport, and Residence Affairs office.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a visa, depending on their nationality and purpose of visit. They must have an invitation letter from a Bahraini company, a valid passport, and proof of business activities. The requirements for business travelers differ from those for tourists, as they may need to provide additional documents, such as a letter of introduction from their employer.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in Bahrain must apply for a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a Bahraini educational institution, a valid passport, and proof of financial support. The student visa is usually valid for the duration of the study program, and students must register with the relevant authorities within a specified timeframe.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in Bahrain must have a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a Bahraini employer. The worker must have a valid passport, a medical certificate, and proof of qualifications and experience. The work visa is usually valid for a specific period, and workers must register with the relevant authorities within a specified timeframe.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Bahrain are Bahrain International Airport (BAH) and the King Fahd Causeway, which connects Bahrain to Saudi Arabia. Upon arrival, travelers must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and complete an arrival card. The immigration officer will stamp the traveler's passport and issue a visa on arrival (if required). Travelers must ensure that they have all the necessary documents and meet the entry requirements to avoid any delays or issues.

Travelers can also enter Bahrain by land or sea, but they must meet the same entry requirements as those arriving by air. The King Fahd Causeway is a popular entry point for travelers from Saudi Arabia, and there are also ferry services from other Gulf countries. Regardless of the entry point, travelers must be prepared to present their documents and answer questions from immigration officers.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not meeting the health insurance requirements
- Not having sufficient funds or proof of financial support
- Not registering with the relevant authorities within the specified timeframe
- Not having the necessary documents, such as a return ticket or hotel reservation
- Not complying with the terms and conditions of the visa, such as overstaying or working without a work visa
