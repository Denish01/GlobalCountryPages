Cultural Treasures

  • The Al Fateh Grand Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world and a prominent cultural landmark in Bahrain.
  • The Bahrain Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a historic fort that dates back to the 16th century and showcases the country's rich history.
  • The Bahrain National Museum features a collection of traditional Khaleeji music and dance performances, which are an important part of the country's cultural heritage.
  • The Arad Fort, a 15th-century fort, is a well-preserved example of traditional Bahraini architecture and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • The Muharraq city is a UNESCO-recognized site and features a range of traditional Bahraini houses, mosques, and other cultural landmarks.

People & Global Impact

  • Khalid bin Hamad Al Khalifa, a member of the Bahraini royal family, is a prominent figure in the country's sports scene and has played a key role in promoting mixed martial arts in the region.
  • A'ala Hubail, a Bahraini football player, is one of the most successful players in the country's history and has won numerous awards and accolades.
  • The Bahraini diaspora community has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Bahrainis working in finance, business, and other fields.
  • The Bahraini values of hospitality, respect, and generosity are highly regarded and reflected in the country's strong sense of community and social cohesion.

What Visitors Love

  • The warm hospitality of the Bahraini people is often cited as one of the highlights of visiting the country, with visitors praising the friendly and welcoming nature of the locals.
  • The traditional Bahraini cuisine, which includes dishes such as machboos and umu ali, is a major draw for visitors, with many restaurants and cafes serving delicious and authentic meals.
  • The scenic coastline of Bahrain, which features a range of beautiful beaches and coves, is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • The Bahrain Grand Prix is a major event that attracts thousands of visitors each year, with the country's world-class racing facilities and exciting atmosphere making it a must-visit for Formula 1 fans.
  • The historic forts and museums of Bahrain, such as the Bahrain Fort and Bahrain National Museum, offer a fascinating glimpse into the country's rich history and culture.
  • The vibrant souks of Manama, which sell everything from traditional clothing and jewelry to spices and perfumes, are a great place to experience the sights, sounds, and smells of Bahrain.

Hidden Gems

  • The Hawar Islands, a group of islands located off the coast of Bahrain, are a hidden gem for nature lovers and offer a range of activities, including snorkeling, diving, and birdwatching.
  • The Bahrain International Circuit, which hosts the Bahrain Grand Prix, also offers a range of other activities, including go-karting and driving experiences.
  • The Riffa Fort, a historic fort that dates back to the 18th century, is a lesser-known landmark that offers stunning views of the surrounding area and a glimpse into Bahrain's rich history.
  • The Bahrain Bay, a waterfront area in Manama, is a hidden gem for foodies and offers a range of restaurants and cafes serving delicious and authentic meals.
