# Education Costs in Bahrain

The cost of education in Bahrain varies depending on the type of institution and the level of education, with public schools being generally free or subsidized for Bahraini citizens, while private and international schools can range from $2,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Bahrain is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being free or subsidized for Bahraini citizens. The quality of education in public schools is generally good, but may not be on par with private schools, which often have better facilities and more experienced teachers. The language of instruction in public schools is Arabic, while private schools may offer instruction in English or other languages. Compulsory education in Bahrain lasts for 12 years, from the age of 6 to 18, and includes primary, intermediate, and secondary education. International schools are also available in Bahrain, offering a range of curricula, including British, American, and International Baccalaureate.

The education system in Bahrain is overseen by the Ministry of Education, which is responsible for setting curriculum standards, accrediting schools, and ensuring that schools meet certain standards. Private schools, on the other hand, are overseen by the Ministry of Education, but also have a degree of autonomy in terms of curriculum and teaching methods. International schools in Bahrain are popular among expatriates, and offer a range of curricula and teaching methods. Some of the most popular international schools in Bahrain include the British School of Bahrain, the American School of Bahrain, and the International School of Bahrain.
