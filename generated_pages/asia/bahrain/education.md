[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Bahrain is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, which is compulsory for all children, starts at the age of 6 and lasts for 6 years. Secondary education, which is also compulsory, lasts for 6 years and is divided into two stages: intermediate and secondary. Tertiary education, which includes universities and colleges, offers a range of undergraduate and graduate programs. The quality of education in Bahrain is generally good, with a strong focus on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) fields. Public schools are free for all citizens, while private schools offer a range of options for those who can afford it.

The Bahraini government has been working to improve the education system, with a focus on increasing access to quality education for all citizens. This includes initiatives to improve teacher training, upgrade school infrastructure, and introduce new technologies to enhance learning. Despite these efforts, there are still challenges to be addressed, including a shortage of skilled teachers and a need for more vocational training programs.

The education system in Bahrain is overseen by the Ministry of Education, which is responsible for setting curriculum standards, managing schools, and supervising teacher training. The ministry has also introduced a range of initiatives to promote education and improve student outcomes, including programs to encourage girls' education and support students with special needs.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- International schools are widely available in Bahrain, offering a range of curricula including **International Baccalaureate (IB)**, British, and American programs.
- These schools cater to the needs of expatriate families and Bahraini students who want to pursue international education.
- Tuition fees for international schools can range from 5,000 to 20,000 USD per year, depending on the school and curriculum.
- Many international schools are located in the capital city of Manama and surrounding areas, with some schools offering bus services to other parts of the country.
- Some popular international schools in Bahrain include the British School of Bahrain, the International School of Bahrain, and the Bahrain School.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Bahrain as a foreigner, students need to obtain a **student visa**, which can be sponsored by the university or college they are attending.
- English language proficiency is a requirement for many universities and colleges in Bahrain, and students may need to take a language proficiency test such as **IELTS** or **TOEFL**.
- Bahrain offers a range of scholarships to international students, including the **Bahrain Scholarship Program**, which covers tuition fees and living expenses.
- Living costs for students in Bahrain can range from 500 to 1,000 USD per month, depending on lifestyle and accommodation choices.
- Foreign students can work part-time while studying in Bahrain, but they need to obtain a **work permit** and meet certain requirements.
- Bahrain has a relatively low cost of living compared to other countries in the region, making it an attractive destination for international students.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the strengths of the education system in Bahrain is its focus on **STEM fields**, which has helped to drive economic growth and development in the country.
- However, there are still challenges to be addressed, including a shortage of skilled teachers and a need for more **vocational training programs**.
- The Bahraini government has introduced a range of initiatives to improve education quality, including programs to enhance **teacher training** and **school infrastructure**.
- Despite these efforts, there are still concerns about the **quality of education** in some schools, particularly in rural areas where resources may be limited.
- The education system in Bahrain has also faced challenges related to **equity and access**, with some students facing barriers to education due to their socioeconomic status or location.
