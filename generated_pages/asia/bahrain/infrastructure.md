[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Bahrain's infrastructure development level is high, with significant investments in recent years to upgrade and expand its roads, utilities, and telecommunications networks. The government has implemented various initiatives to bridge the urban-rural gap, ensuring that all areas of the country have access to basic services such as electricity, water, and sanitation. However, there is still a notable disparity between urban and rural areas, with the capital city Manama and other major towns enjoying better infrastructure facilities compared to smaller villages and rural areas. The government's efforts to develop the country's infrastructure have been driven by its **Economic Vision 2030**, which aims to diversify the economy and increase the private sector's contribution to GDP.

The country's infrastructure has undergone significant transformations in recent years, with a focus on **sustainability** and **innovation**. The government has invested heavily in renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, to reduce the country's dependence on fossil fuels and mitigate the impact of climate change. Additionally, Bahrain has implemented various initiatives to conserve water and reduce waste, recognizing the importance of protecting its limited natural resources. The government's efforts to develop the country's infrastructure have been supported by international organizations and foreign investors, who have provided funding and expertise to support various projects.

Bahrain's infrastructure development has also been driven by its strategic location, which makes it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region. The country's **logistics** and **transportation** infrastructure, including its roads, ports, and airport, are well-developed and efficient, facilitating the movement of goods and people in and out of the country. The government has also invested in developing the country's **tourism** infrastructure, including hotels, resorts, and cultural attractions, to promote tourism and diversify the economy.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]
- Bahrain's power generation is primarily based on **natural gas** and **oil**, with a total installed capacity of 3,923 MW.
- The country has made significant progress in increasing its **renewable energy** capacity, with a target of generating 10% of its electricity from renewable sources by 2030.
- The electricity grid is relatively **reliable**, with minimal outages and a high level of efficiency.
- The government has implemented various initiatives to reduce energy consumption and promote energy efficiency, including the use of **LED lighting** and **energy-efficient appliances**.
- Bahrain is also exploring the potential of **nuclear energy** as a future source of power, with plans to develop a nuclear power plant in the coming years.

[SECTION] Water & Sanitation [/SECTION]
- Bahrain's **tap water** is generally safe to drink, with a high level of quality and treatment.
- The country has a well-developed **sanitation** system, with a high level of coverage and treatment of wastewater.
- **Water access** is universal, with all areas of the country having access to clean and safe water.
- The government has implemented various initiatives to conserve water and reduce waste, including the use of **drip irrigation** and **water-efficient appliances**.
- Bahrain is also exploring the potential of **desalination** and **water recycling** to increase its water supply and reduce its dependence on groundwater.
