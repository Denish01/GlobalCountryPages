[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Bahrain is generally a safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other countries in the region. The Bahraini government has taken steps to ensure the safety and security of tourists, including the establishment of a dedicated tourist police force. However, as with any country, there are some areas that are considered safer than others, and tourists should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Bahrain has a relatively stable political environment, but there have been instances of protests and demonstrations in the past. These events are usually peaceful, but tourists should avoid areas where protests are taking place and follow local advice and instructions from authorities. The country is also susceptible to occasional sandstorms and heatwaves during the summer months, which can pose a risk to tourists, especially those who are not accustomed to the extreme heat. Overall, Bahrain is a safe and welcoming destination for tourists, with a rich culture and history to explore.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use **credit cards** or **ATMs** instead
- Be aware of **scams** targeting tourists, such as overpriced tours or fake **souvenirs**
- Be cautious of **traffic** accidents, especially when crossing the road or driving
- Avoid **drinking tap water**, and stick to **bottled water** instead
- Be aware of **heat exhaustion** and **dehydration**, especially during the summer months
- Be cautious of **crowded areas**, such as **mosques** and **markets**, where **pickpocketing** and **theft** can occur

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and Typhoid are recommended, while Meningitis and Rabies are required for certain travelers
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Bahrain has good quality medical facilities, with many hospitals and clinics available, but **health insurance** is recommended
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, with many open 24 hours, but **prescription medication** may be limited
