Overall Safety Assessment

Hong Kong is considered one of the safest cities in the world, with a strong police presence and low crime rates. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around the city, even at night, and the risk of violent crime is extremely low. However, as with any major city, there are some areas that are considered safer than others, and tourists should be aware of their surroundings and take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The city's low crime rate is due in part to its strict laws and efficient law enforcement, which help to maintain a safe and stable environment for residents and visitors.

Despite its overall safety, Hong Kong has experienced some social unrest and protests in recent years, which have occasionally turned violent. However, these events are relatively rare and usually do not affect tourists. The city's authorities are generally effective in maintaining order and minimizing disruptions to daily life. Visitors should stay informed about local conditions and follow the advice of local authorities to avoid any potential problems. With its excellent public transportation system, well-maintained infrastructure, and high standard of living, Hong Kong is an attractive destination for tourists and business travelers, offering a unique blend of East and West, traditional and modern.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Scams targeting tourists, such as fake teahouses or massage parlors
  • Overcharging by some taxi drivers or vendors
  • Counterfeit goods being sold in some markets or street stalls
  • Triad-related crime, although this is relatively rare and usually does not affect tourists
  • Petty theft in hotels or hostels, especially in shared rooms
  • ATM scams, such as card skimming or fake machines

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: No special vaccinations are required for travel to Hong Kong, but visitors should ensure they are up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
  • Water Safety: Tap water is safe to drink in Hong Kong, but bottled water is widely available for those who prefer it
  • Medical Facilities: Hong Kong has a high standard of medical care, with many excellent hospitals and clinics, but medical insurance is recommended to cover any potential costs
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available and well-stocked, with many open 24 hours

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots
  • Keep valuables secure and use hotel safes or lockers when available
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-hailing apps, and always check the meter is on
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or contactless payment instead
  • Be cautious of overly friendly strangers or people approaching you with unsolicited offers
  • Stay informed about local conditions and follow the advice of local authorities
  • Learn some basic Cantonese phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you", to show respect for the local culture

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Hong Kong is generally a very safe city for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong police presence
  • Take normal precautions to protect yourself and your belongings, such as avoiding walking alone in isolated areas at night
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
  • Be cautious of overly friendly strangers or people approaching you with unsolicited offers, and trust your instincts if you feel uncomfortable
