# Hong Kong Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

Hong Kong is located on the southeastern coast of China, surrounded by the South China Sea and the Pearl River Delta, with a unique blend of **urban** and **natural** landscapes that set it apart from other major cities in Asia.

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Hong Kong is situated on the southeastern coast of China, bordering the Guangdong province to the north and the South China Sea to the east, south, and west. The territory is made up of more than 200 islands, with the largest island being Lantau Island, where the **Hong Kong International Airport** is located. The Kowloon Peninsula and the New Territories are the main land areas, with the **Victoria Harbour** separating Hong Kong Island from Kowloon. The border with China is approximately 30 kilometers long, while the border with Macau is about 3 kilometers long, with both borders being land borders.

The location of Hong Kong has played a significant role in its development as a major **trade** and **financial** center, with its strategic position on the South China Sea allowing for easy access to the rest of Asia and the world. The territory is surrounded by several major cities, including Shenzhen and Guangzhou in China, and Macau, a special administrative region of China. The **Pearl River Delta** region, where Hong Kong is located, is one of the most densely populated and economically developed regions in the world.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
The terrain of Hong Kong is characterized by a mix of **mountains**, **hills**, and **coastal plains**, with the highest point being Tai Mo Shan, which is located in the New Territories. The landscape is dominated by the **Kowloon Hills** and the **Lantau Hills**, which are separated by the **Tolo Harbour** and the **Victoria Harbour**. The **coastline** of Hong Kong is highly irregular, with many **bays**, **inlets**, and **islands**, providing a unique and diverse landscape that is home to a wide range of **flora** and **fauna**.

The **geology** of Hong Kong is primarily composed of **granite**, **volcanic rock**, and **sedimentary rock**, with the territory being located on the **Eurasian Plate**. The **soil** in Hong Kong is generally poor in quality, with the **hills** and **mountains** being covered in a thin layer of **topsoil**. The **rivers** in Hong Kong are generally short and narrow, with the **Shing Mun River** and the **Lam Tsuen River** being two of the most notable rivers in the territory.

The **coastal areas** of Hong Kong are home to a wide range of **marine life**, including **coral reefs**, **mangroves**, and **seagrass beds**, which provide important **habitat** for many **marine species**. The **beaches** in Hong Kong are generally small and narrow, with the most popular beaches being located on the **southern coast** of Hong Kong Island and on the **Lantau Island**.

In addition to its natural beauty, Hong Kong's landscape has also been shaped by **human activity**, with the territory being one of the most **urbanized** and **densely populated** areas in the world. The **urban landscape** of Hong Kong is characterized by a mix of **high-rise buildings**, **apartment blocks**, and **commercial centers**, with the **Central District** being the main **financial** and **commercial** hub of the territory.
