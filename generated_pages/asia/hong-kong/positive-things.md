[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Big Buddha** on Lantau Island is a 34-meter-tall bronze statue that is one of the largest outdoor Buddha statues in the world.
- The **Man Mo Temple** is a historic temple that is dedicated to the gods of war and literature, and is one of the oldest and most famous temples in Hong Kong.
- The **Hong Kong Academy for Performing Arts** is a prestigious institution that offers training in music, dance, theater, and other performing arts.
- The **Cheung Chau Bun Festival** is a traditional festival that is held on the island of Cheung Chau, and features a parade, music, and traditional foods.
- The **Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade** is a waterfront area in Kowloon that offers stunning views of the Hong Kong skyline and is a popular spot for walking and sightseeing.

- **Bruce Lee** was a famous martial artist and actor who was born in Hong Kong and went on to become an international superstar.

- **Anita Mui** was a renowned Cantopop singer and actress who was known for her powerful voice and charismatic stage presence.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **hospitality** of the Hong Kong people is often commented on by visitors, who appreciate the friendly and welcoming nature of the locals.
- The **food** in Hong Kong is world-famous, with popular dishes such as **dim sum**, **roast goose**, and **congee**.
- The **scenery** of Hong Kong is stunning, with a unique blend of natural beauty and urban landscape, including the **Victoria Harbour** and the **Peak**.
- The **shopping** in Hong Kong is legendary, with everything from high-end fashion to traditional markets and street stalls.
- The **nightlife** in Hong Kong is vibrant and diverse, with everything from rooftop bars to live music venues and dance clubs.
- The **transportation system** in Hong Kong is efficient and convenient, with a comprehensive network of buses, trains, and ferries.
