[SECTION] How Hong Kong Is Divided [/SECTION]
Hong Kong is a special administrative region of China, with a high degree of autonomy in all matters except foreign affairs and defense. The territory is divided into 18 districts, each with its own district council. The districts are further divided into 460 constituency areas, which are used for electoral purposes. The government of Hong Kong is headed by a chief executive, who is advised by an executive council.

The administrative structure of Hong Kong is designed to provide a high degree of local autonomy, while also ensuring that the territory remains part of China. The 18 districts are responsible for providing a range of local services, including social welfare, recreation, and environmental protection. The government of Hong Kong also plays a key role in promoting the territory's economy, particularly in the areas of trade, finance, and tourism.

[SECTION] Regional Differences [/SECTION]
- The regions of Hong Kong differ significantly in terms of culture, with Hong Kong Island and Kowloon being more cosmopolitan and urban, while the New Territories and Outlying Islands are more rural and traditional.
- The economy of Hong Kong is driven by finance, trade, and tourism, with the financial sector being concentrated on Hong Kong Island.
- The climate of Hong Kong varies from region to region, with the urban areas being hotter and more humid than the rural areas.
- The language spoken in Hong Kong also varies, with Cantonese being the most widely spoken dialect, while English is widely used in business and tourism.

[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
Hong Kong has a well-developed public transportation system, with a comprehensive network of buses, trains, and ferries. The Mass Transit Railway (MTR) is the most popular mode of transportation, with a network of lines that cover most areas of the territory. Taxis are also widely available, while driving is not recommended due to the heavy traffic and limited parking. Domestic flights are not necessary, as the territory is relatively small and easily accessible by public transportation. The Octopus card is a convenient way to pay for public transportation, and can be used on buses, trains, and ferries. Overall, getting around Hong Kong is relatively easy, with a range of options available to suit different budgets and preferences.
