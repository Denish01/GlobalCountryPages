[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Singapore has a well-developed and efficient public transportation system, with a comprehensive network of buses and trains that cover most areas of the city-state. The quality of public transit in Singapore is high, with modern and clean vehicles, and a reliable schedule. Most people in Singapore get around using the public transportation system, which includes buses and trains, as it is convenient and affordable. The city-state's small size and grid-like road network also make it easy to navigate, with many people choosing to walk or cycle for short distances.

The public transportation system in Singapore is also integrated, with a single card, known as the EZ-Link card, that can be used to pay for fares on buses and trains. This makes it easy for commuters to switch between different modes of transport, and also provides a convenient way to pay for fares. In addition, the city-state has a number of taxi companies and ride-hailing services, such as Grab and Gojek, which provide an alternative mode of transport for those who prefer a more personalized service.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transport, consider purchasing a monthly transit pass, which can be used on buses and trains.
- Use the EZ-Link card to pay for fares on public transportation, as it is convenient and easy to use.
- Download the Grab or Gojek app to book a ride-hailing service, which can be more convenient than hailing a taxi on the street.
- Be aware of peak hour surcharges when using ride-hailing services, which can increase the cost of a ride.
- Consider walking or cycling for short distances, as Singapore is a relatively small city-state with a grid-like road network.
- Always use a licensed taxi or ride-hailing service, and agree on the fare before starting the journey to avoid any disputes.
