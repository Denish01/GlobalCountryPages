# Healthcare Costs in Singapore

In Singapore, the cost of healthcare can range from $20 to $500 or more per visit, depending on the type of service, provider, and individual's insurance coverage.

[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
Singapore has a well-regarded **mixed healthcare system**, which combines elements of public and private providers. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of high quality, with modern facilities and well-trained staff, while the private system offers more luxurious amenities and often shorter wait times. For locals, the system is generally affordable, with subsidized healthcare services available through the **Medisave** and **Medishield** programs. However, for foreigners, the cost of healthcare can be significantly higher, making it essential to have adequate insurance coverage. 

The healthcare system in Singapore is designed to provide a range of services, from basic primary care to complex specialist treatments. Public hospitals, such as the **Singapore General Hospital**, offer high-quality care at a lower cost, while private hospitals, like the **Gleneagles Hospital**, provide more personalized services at a higher price. Overall, the system is well-organized, and patients can easily access medical care, although the cost can vary significantly depending on the provider and individual's insurance coverage.

[SECTION] Pharmacy & Medication Costs [/SECTION]
- Common medications, such as **antibiotics** and **pain relievers**, can cost between $5-$20 per prescription.
- Prescription medication is generally available at pharmacies, such as **Guardian** and **Watsons**, with a valid prescription from a licensed doctor.
- Over-the-counter medications are widely available at pharmacies and convenience stores.
- Some medications, such as **controlled substances**, may require a special permit or license to purchase.
- Pharmacies are generally open from 9 am to 10 pm, although some may have extended hours.

[SECTION] Emergency Care [/SECTION]
In the event of a medical emergency, it is essential to call the **emergency number 995** or visit the nearest hospital. Singapore has a well-developed emergency services system, with **ambulances** and **emergency rooms** available at major hospitals. For foreigners, it is recommended to visit a private hospital, such as the **Mount Elizabeth Hospital**, which offers high-quality care and international-standard facilities. 

When visiting a hospital in Singapore, it is crucial to have adequate insurance coverage and to be prepared for potential out-of-pocket expenses. Patients should also be aware of the **hospital's billing policies** and **payment procedures** to avoid any unexpected costs. Additionally, it is a good idea to research the best hospitals for foreigners in Singapore, such as the **Raffles Hospital**, which offers a range of international-standard services and facilities.
