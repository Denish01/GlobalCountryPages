[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Singapore's infrastructure is characterized by its high level of development, with significant investments in recent years to enhance its efficiency and sustainability. The city-state has made notable strides in developing its transportation systems, energy sector, and water management, making it an attractive destination for businesses and tourists alike. However, despite its overall high standard of infrastructure, there is a negligible urban vs rural gap, as the entire country is essentially urbanized. The government has implemented various initiatives to ensure that all areas of the country have access to modern amenities and services.

The Singaporean government has been actively investing in infrastructure development, with a focus on smart and sustainable solutions. This includes the use of **smart grids** to manage energy distribution, **green buildings** to reduce environmental impact, and **intelligent transportation systems** to optimize traffic flow. These initiatives have contributed to Singapore's reputation as a hub for innovation and technology. The government has also implemented various programs to encourage private sector participation in infrastructure development, such as public-private partnerships (PPPs) and infrastructure financing initiatives.

Overall, Singapore's infrastructure is designed to support the country's economic growth, while also ensuring the well-being and quality of life of its citizens. The government's commitment to investing in infrastructure development has paid off, with Singapore consistently ranking high in various international indices, such as the World Bank's Ease of Doing Business index and the Mercer Quality of Living survey.

- The electricity grid in Singapore is highly reliable, with a **power outage rate** of less than 1 minute per customer per year.
- The government has set a target of increasing the share of renewable energy in the energy mix to **2% by 2030**, with a focus on solar power and other clean energy sources.
- Singapore is also exploring the use of **energy storage systems**, such as batteries, to enhance the efficiency and reliability of the electricity grid.
- The government has implemented various initiatives to promote energy efficiency, such as the **Energy Efficiency Fund** and the **Singapore Standard for Energy Efficiency**.
