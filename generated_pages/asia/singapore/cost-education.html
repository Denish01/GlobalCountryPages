Education System Overview

The education system in Singapore is highly regarded, with a strong emphasis on bilingualism and academic achievement. Public schools in Singapore offer high-quality education, with a focus on English as the primary language of instruction, while also teaching Mother Tongue languages such as Malay, Mandarin, and Tamil. Compulsory education in Singapore lasts for 6 years, from Primary 1 to Primary 6, after which students can choose to attend secondary school, junior college, or vocational training. Private education in Singapore is also available, with a range of options including international schools, which offer foreign curricula such as the International Baccalaureate or British A-levels.

The quality of education in Singapore is generally high, with public schools offering a rigorous and well-structured curriculum, while private schools offer more flexibility and customization. International schools in Singapore offer a range of curricula and teaching styles, catering to the needs of expat families and international students. However, the cost of private and international education in Singapore can be prohibitively expensive, with some schools charging upwards of $30,000 per year.

Education Quality & Tips

  • The National University of Singapore and Nanyang Technological University are among the top-ranked universities in Asia, offering high-quality education and research opportunities.
  • Scholarship opportunities are available for local and international students, including government-funded scholarships and private awards.
  • Online learning options are becoming increasingly popular in Singapore, with many institutions offering online courses and degree programs.
  • Education in Singapore is highly competitive, with a strong emphasis on academic achievement and extracurricular activities.
  • Expat families may find it helpful to research international schools and private institutions that cater to their specific needs and requirements.
  • Language support is available for international students, with many institutions offering language courses and support services to help students adapt to the local education system.
