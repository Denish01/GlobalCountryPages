[SECTION] What Is Georgia? [/SECTION]
Georgia is a **sovereign** country located in the continent of Asia, at the crossroads of Eastern Europe and Western Asia. It is situated in the Caucasus region, bordered by Russia to the north, Azerbaijan to the east, Armenia to the south, and Turkey to the southwest. With a population of 3.7 million people, Georgia is a relatively small country, but it has a rich history and culture that dates back thousands of years. The country is known for its stunning mountain landscapes, beautiful beaches, and vibrant cities, making it a popular tourist destination.

Georgia has a unique cultural identity, shaped by its strategic location at the intersection of Europe and Asia. The country has been influenced by various empires and civilizations throughout history, including the ancient Greeks, Romans, and Persians. Today, Georgia is a modern, democratic country with a growing economy and a strong sense of national identity. The capital city, Tbilisi, is a hub of cultural and economic activity, with a rich history, stunning architecture, and a vibrant atmosphere.

Georgia is also known for its natural beauty, with a diverse landscape that includes snow-capped mountains, fertile valleys, and a long coastline along the Black Sea. The country is home to several **UNESCO World Heritage Sites**, including the historic city of Mtskheta and the monastery of Gelati. With its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, Georgia is a fascinating country that has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

1. 1008 - The Kingdom of Georgia is formed, marking the beginning of a unified Georgian state.
2. 1236 - Georgia is invaded by the Mongols, who destroy many cities and towns, and the country becomes a vassal state.
3. 1810 - Georgia is annexed by the Russian Empire, and it remains under Russian rule for over a century.
4. 1918 - Georgia declares its independence from Russia, but it is short-lived, as the country is invaded by the Red Army in 1921.
5. 1991 - Georgia gains its independence from the Soviet Union, and it becomes a sovereign country once again.
6. 2008 - A brief war breaks out between Georgia and Russia, resulting in the occupation of two breakaway regions, Abkhazia and South Ossetia.
7. 2014 - Georgia signs an association agreement with the European Union, marking a significant step towards European integration.
