Visa Policy Overview

Georgia has a relatively liberal visa policy, allowing citizens of over 90 countries to enter the country without a visa or with a visa on arrival. The country aims to promote tourism and facilitate travel for business and other purposes. Citizens of countries that do not have a visa-free agreement with Georgia can usually obtain a visa on arrival or through an e-visa system. The Georgian government has been working to simplify the visa application process and make it more accessible to travelers from around the world.

The visa policy of Georgia is designed to balance the need to control the flow of visitors with the need to promote tourism and economic development. The country has a long history of cultural and trade exchange with its neighbors and other countries, and its visa policy reflects this. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and modern infrastructure, Georgia is an attractive destination for travelers from around the world.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists can enter Georgia without a visa if they are citizens of a visa-free country. They can stay in the country for up to one year, and can extend their stay if necessary. Tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support themselves during their stay.

Business Travelers: Business travelers may need to obtain a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must have an invitation letter from a Georgian company or organization, and must register with the relevant authorities. Business travelers can stay in the country for up to 30 days, and can extend their stay if necessary.

Students: Students who wish to study in Georgia must obtain a student visa. They must have an acceptance letter from a Georgian university, and must register with the relevant authorities. Students can stay in the country for the duration of their studies, and can work part-time to support themselves.

Workers: Workers who wish to work in Georgia must obtain a work visa. They must have a job offer from a Georgian company, and must register with the relevant authorities. Workers can stay in the country for the duration of their employment contract, and must have a valid work permit.

  3. Recent passport-sized photo
  4. Proof of onward travel

  6. Health insurance certificate
  7. Invitation letter (if required)
  8. Enrollment proof (if studying)

  10. Registration certificate (if staying for more than 30 days)

Entry Points & Procedures

The main entry points to Georgia are Tbilisi International Airport, Kutaisi International Airport, and the land borders with neighboring countries. When arriving at an airport, travelers must go through immigration and customs, and must have their documents checked by the relevant authorities. At land borders, travelers must stop at a border crossing point and have their documents checked by the relevant authorities. Travelers must also declare any goods they are bringing with them, and must pay any applicable duties or taxes.

When entering Georgia, travelers must have their documents in order, and must be prepared to answer questions from the immigration authorities. They must also have a valid passport, and must be able to prove their purpose for visiting the country. Travelers who are staying for more than 30 days must register with the relevant authorities, and must obtain a registration certificate.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
  • Not having sufficient funds to support oneself during the stay
  • Not having proof of onward travel
  • Not declaring goods brought into the country
  • Not registering with the relevant authorities if staying for more than 30 days
  • Not having a valid health insurance certificate

It is essential to avoid these common mistakes to ensure a smooth and hassle-free entry into Georgia. By being prepared and having all the necessary documents, travelers can enjoy their stay in this beautiful country without any issues.
