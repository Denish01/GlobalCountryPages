Cultural Treasures

  • The Gelati Monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a masterpiece of medieval Georgian architecture and a testament to the country's rich cultural heritage.
  • The Svaneti Region is home to a unique and ancient tradition of tower houses, which have been recognized by UNESCO as a remarkable example of traditional architecture.
  • The Kartvelian Language is a unique and ancient language that is spoken by the Georgian people and is an important part of the country's cultural identity.
  • The Tbilisi Opera and Ballet Theater is a world-renowned cultural institution that has hosted some of the most famous performers and productions in the world.
  • The Georgian Polyphonic Singing tradition is a unique and captivating form of music that has been recognized by UNESCO as a Masterpiece of the Intangible Heritage of Humanity.

People & Global Impact

  • Joseph Stalin, the former Soviet leader, was born in the Georgian city of Gori and had a significant impact on world history.
  • The Georgian Diaspora has made significant contributions to the cultural and economic development of countries around the world, with many notable Georgians achieving success in fields such as business, arts, and science.
  • Nikoloz Baratashvili, a 19th-century Georgian poet, is considered one of the greatest poets in the Georgian language and has had a lasting impact on the country's literary tradition.
  • The Georgian values of hospitality and respect for tradition are highly regarded and have been recognized as an important part of the country's cultural identity.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Georgian people is legendary, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome they receive in the country.
  • The food in Georgia is delicious and varied, with popular dishes such as khinkali (dumplings) and khachapuri (cheese bread) being favorites among visitors.
  • The scenery in Georgia is breathtaking, with the country's rugged mountains, beautiful beaches, and picturesque valleys making it a nature lover's paradise.
  • The wine in Georgia is some of the best in the world, with the country being home to some of the oldest and most traditional wine-making techniques.
  • The cities in Georgia, such as Tbilisi and Batumi, are vibrant and full of life, with a rich cultural scene and a wide range of historical and cultural attractions.
  • The festivals in Georgia, such as the Tbilisi International Festival and the Batumi Black Sea Music Festival, are world-renowned and attract visitors from around the globe.

Hidden Gems

  • The Tusheti Region is a remote and beautiful part of Georgia that is home to a unique and ancient culture, with many traditional villages and historic landmarks to explore.
  • The Vardzia Cave City is an ancient cave city that dates back to the 12th century and is a fascinating example of medieval Georgian architecture.
  • The Kazbegi Region is a stunningly beautiful part of Georgia that is home to some of the country's most impressive mountain scenery and traditional villages.
  • The Black Sea Coast in Georgia is a beautiful and scenic part of the country, with many popular beach resorts and historic towns to explore, such as Batumi and Kobuleti.
