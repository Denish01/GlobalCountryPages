[SECTION] Overview: Georgia vs Turkey [/SECTION]
Georgia and Turkey are two neighboring countries located in the Caucasus region, with distinct cultural and economic profiles. Georgia has a relatively low cost of living, with a GDP per capita of $10,700, while Turkey has a higher GDP per capita of $24,900. The inflation rate in Georgia is also lower, at 3.8%, compared to Turkey's 11.2%. In terms of overall cost of living, Georgia is generally cheaper than Turkey, with a difference of around 20-30%. This makes Georgia an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Georgia has a relatively small economy, with a strong focus on services and tourism, while Turkey has a larger and more diversified economy, with a strong manufacturing sector. This difference in economic structure contributes to the variation in cost of living between the two countries. Additionally, the exchange rate between the Georgian Lari (GEL) and the Turkish Lira (TRY) can also impact the cost of living for foreigners. Overall, Georgia's lower cost of living and more stable economy make it an attractive option for those looking for a affordable and relatively stable lifestyle.
