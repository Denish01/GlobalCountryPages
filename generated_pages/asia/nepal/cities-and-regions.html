How Nepal Is Divided

Nepal is divided into 7 provinces, each with its own provincial capital and government. The provinces are further divided into districts, with a total of 77 districts in the country. The provinces are: Province No. 1, Province No. 2, Bagmati Province, Gandaki Province, Lumbini Province, Karnali Province, and Sudurpashchim Province. This administrative structure is designed to promote decentralization and give more power to local governments.

The provinces in Nepal are based on the country's geography and cultural identity. For example, the Himalayan region is divided into several provinces, including Gandaki Province and Karnali Province, while the Terai region is divided into Province No. 1, Province No. 2, and Lumbini Province. This division allows for more effective governance and development of the country's diverse regions.

Major Cities

Kathmandu (845,000)

Role: capital, cultural center

Known For: its rich cultural heritage, historic temples, and vibrant festivals. The city is a mix of traditional and modern architecture, with ancient temples and palaces standing alongside modern buildings and shopping centers.

Best For: travelers interested in history, culture, and spirituality.

Pokhara (255,000)

Role: tourist hub, adventure sports center

Known For: its stunning natural beauty, with the Himalayas towering in the background and several lakes and rivers flowing through the city. The city is also known for its adventure sports, such as paragliding, rafting, and trekking.

Best For: travelers looking for outdoor activities, relaxation, and scenic views.

Lalitpur (220,000)

Role: cultural center, economic hub

Known For: its well-preserved ancient architecture, including the famous Patan Durbar Square. The city is also known for its vibrant arts scene, with numerous galleries and museums showcasing local and international art.

Best For: travelers interested in history, culture, and art.

Bharatpur (280,000)

Role: economic hub, transportation center

Known For: its strategic location, with the city serving as a major transportation hub for the country. The city is also known for its vibrant markets, with a wide range of goods and services available.

Best For: travelers looking for a convenient base for exploring the surrounding region.

Biratnagar (240,000)

Dharan (140,000)

Regional Differences

  • The Himalayan Region is known for its rugged terrain and scenic beauty, with numerous trekking and mountaineering opportunities available.
  • The Terai Region is characterized by its flat, fertile plains, with numerous agricultural farms and wildlife reserves located in the area.
  • The Kathmandu Valley is a cultural and urban hub, with numerous historic temples, vibrant festivals, and traditional architecture on display.
  • The Lumbini Zone is a cultural and historical region, with numerous ancient ruins and spiritual sites located in the area, including the birthplace of Buddha.

Getting Around

Nepal has a well-developed transportation network, with numerous domestic flights, buses, and taxis available. The country's major cities, including Kathmandu and Pokhara, are connected by regular flights, while buses and taxis provide a more affordable and accessible option for travelers. The country's road network is also well-developed, with numerous highways and roads connecting the major cities and towns. However, driving conditions can be challenging, particularly in the rural areas, due to the country's rugged terrain and limited infrastructure.

Domestic flights are a convenient option for travelers, with numerous airlines operating regular flights between the major cities. Buses and taxis are also a popular option, with numerous private and public operators providing services throughout the country. The country's train network is limited, with only a few routes available, but it provides a scenic and relaxing way to travel. Overall, Nepal has a well-developed transportation network, with numerous options available for travelers to get around the country.
