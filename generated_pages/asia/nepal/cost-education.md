# Education Costs in Nepal

The cost of education in Nepal can vary greatly depending on the type of institution and level of study, with annual fees ranging from free or subsidized for public primary and secondary schools to several thousand dollars for private and international schools.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Nepal's education system is divided into public and private institutions, with the public sector offering free or subsidized education to students. However, the quality of education in public schools can be inferior compared to private schools, which often have better infrastructure and more qualified teachers. The language of instruction in most schools is **Nepali**, but English is also widely used, especially in private schools. Compulsory education in Nepal lasts for 12 years, from primary to secondary level, and students can choose to pursue higher education at universities or colleges after completing their secondary education. International schools are also available in major cities like **Kathmandu** and **Pokhara**, offering curricula like the **International Baccalaureate** (IB) or **Cambridge International Examinations** (CIE).

The education system in Nepal has undergone significant reforms in recent years, with a focus on improving access to education, especially for disadvantaged groups. However, the country still faces challenges like **teacher shortages** and **infrastructure gaps**, which can affect the quality of education. Despite these challenges, Nepal has made significant progress in increasing **enrollment rates** and **literacy rates**, especially among girls and women. The government has also introduced initiatives like **scholarship programs** and **financial assistance** to support students from low-income backgrounds.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Tips [/SECTION]
- The best schools for expats in Nepal are often international schools, which offer high-quality facilities and curricula like the **International Baccalaureate** (IB) or **Cambridge International Examinations** (CIE).
- Scholarship availability is limited, but some universities and organizations offer **merit-based** or **need-based** scholarships to students.
- Online learning options are becoming increasingly popular in Nepal, with many universities and institutions offering **online courses** or **distance learning programs**.
- Education quality in Nepal can vary greatly depending on the institution and location, but overall, the country has made significant progress in improving access to education and **literacy rates**.
- For students who want to pursue higher education abroad, **language proficiency** is a major requirement, and many students take **English language courses** to prepare for exams like **TOEFL** or **IELTS**.
- The **Nepal government** offers various initiatives to support students, including **financial assistance** and **scholarship programs**, especially for disadvantaged groups like **girls and women**.
