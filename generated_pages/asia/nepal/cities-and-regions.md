[SECTION] How Nepal Is Divided [/SECTION]
Nepal is divided into 7 provinces, each with its own provincial capital and government. The provinces are further divided into districts, with a total of 77 districts in the country. The provinces are: Province No. 1, Province No. 2, Bagmati Province, Gandaki Province, Lumbini Province, Karnali Province, and Sudurpashchim Province. This administrative structure is designed to promote decentralization and give more power to local governments.

The provinces in Nepal are based on the country's geography and cultural identity. For example, the Himalayan region is divided into several provinces, including Gandaki Province and Karnali Province, while the Terai region is divided into Province No. 1, Province No. 2, and Lumbini Province. This division allows for more effective governance and development of the country's diverse regions.
