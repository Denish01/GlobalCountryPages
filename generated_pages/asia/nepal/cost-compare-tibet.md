[SECTION] Overview: Nepal vs Tibet [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Nepal and Tibet varies significantly, with Nepal being the more affordable option. Nepal's lower cost of living is due to its larger population, more developed economy, and greater availability of goods and services. In contrast, Tibet's remote location and limited infrastructure contribute to its higher costs. The economic context of both countries also plays a role, with Nepal having a more diverse economy and Tibet being heavily reliant on tourism and government subsidies.

The difference in cost of living between Nepal and Tibet is around 20-30%, with Nepal being the cheaper option. This is evident in the prices of everyday items such as food, housing, and transportation. For example, a meal at a mid-range restaurant in Nepal can cost around $3-5, while in Tibet it can cost $5-10. Similarly, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center of Nepal can cost around $200-300 per month, while in Tibet it can cost $300-500 per month. Overall, Nepal offers a more affordable lifestyle for expats, students, and retirees, while Tibet is more suitable for those with a higher budget and a desire for a unique cultural experience.
