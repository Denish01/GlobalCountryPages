[SECTION] Overview: Israel vs Jordan [/SECTION]
Israel and Jordan are two neighboring countries in the Middle East with distinct economic profiles. Israel has a high-income economy with a strong technology sector, while Jordan has a developing economy with a significant service sector. The cost of living in Israel is generally higher than in Jordan, mainly due to Israel's higher GDP per capita and stronger economy. The difference in living costs between the two countries is around 20-30%, with Jordan being the more affordable option. This makes Jordan an attractive destination for expats, students, and retirees looking for a lower cost of living.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Israel has a highly developed infrastructure, a strong education system, and a high standard of living, which contributes to its higher cost of living. In contrast, Jordan has a more modest economy, with a lower standard of living and less developed infrastructure. However, Jordan's economy is growing, and the country is investing in various sectors, including tourism and technology. This growth is expected to have a positive impact on the cost of living in Jordan, making it an even more attractive option for those looking for a affordable and comfortable lifestyle.
