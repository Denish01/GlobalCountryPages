[SECTION] Overview: Israel vs Lebanon [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Israel and Lebanon differs significantly due to the countries' economic contexts. Israel has a strong and diversified economy, with a high standard of living, which contributes to its relatively high cost of living. In contrast, Lebanon's economy has faced challenges in recent years, including a large public debt and a decline in the value of its currency, which has led to a lower cost of living. Overall, Lebanon is generally cheaper than Israel, with a difference of around 20-30% in overall cost of living. This is reflected in various aspects of daily life, including housing, food, transportation, and utilities.

The difference in cost of living between Israel and Lebanon is also influenced by the countries' different lifestyles and cultural norms. Israel is known for its modern and Westernized lifestyle, with a high demand for luxury goods and services, which drives up prices. In contrast, Lebanon has a more traditional and Middle Eastern lifestyle, with a stronger emphasis on community and family, which can lead to lower costs for certain goods and services. Additionally, Lebanon's economy is more informal, with a larger share of small businesses and street vendors, which can also contribute to lower prices.
