Location & Borders

The UAE is situated in the Middle East, bordering Saudi Arabia to the west and south, and Oman to the east. The country has a coastline along the Persian Gulf to the north and the Gulf of Oman to the east, with the Strait of Hormuz separating it from Iran. The UAE's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa has made it an important center for trade and commerce. The country's borders are marked by a diverse range of landscapes, from the sandy dunes of the Arabian Desert to the rocky mountains of the Hajar Mountains.

The UAE's location in the Arabian Peninsula has also made it a significant player in the region's politics and economy. The country's borders with Saudi Arabia and Oman are relatively long, with the Saudi border stretching for over 470 kilometers and the Omani border for over 530 kilometers. The UAE's coastline, on the other hand, is approximately 1,318 kilometers long, providing access to the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman. The country's unique geography has also made it an important hub for maritime trade, with several major ports, including the Port of Jebel Ali and the Port of Fujairah.

Terrain & Landscape

The UAE's terrain is characterized by a mix of sandy deserts, rocky mountains, and coastal plains. The Arabian Desert, which covers much of the country, is a vast expanse of sand dunes that stretch as far as the eye can see. The desert landscape is dotted with oases, such as the Liwa Oasis, which provide a haven for plants and animals in the harsh desert environment. The Hajar Mountains, which run along the eastern coast of the UAE, are a rugged range of mountains that rise to over 2,000 meters in some areas.

The UAE's coastline is marked by a series of beaches and creeks, which provide a habitat for a wide range of marine life. The Mangrove forests that line the coast are an important breeding ground for fish and other marine species. The country's wadis, or dry riverbeds, are also an important feature of the landscape, providing a source of water and a habitat for plants and animals during the rainy season. The UAE's unique terrain has made it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with activities such as desert safaris, hiking, and scuba diving.

The UAE's landscape has also been shaped by human activity, with the construction of cities and towns in the desert. The country's urban areas, such as Dubai and Abu Dhabi, are known for their modern architecture and luxurious amenities. The UAE's agricultural areas, such as the Al Ain Oasis, are also an important feature of the landscape, providing a source of fresh produce for the country's population.
