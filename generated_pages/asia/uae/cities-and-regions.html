How UAE Is Divided

The United Arab Emirates is a sovereign state divided into seven emirates, each with its own ruler and a degree of autonomy. The emirates are Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al Quwain, Ras Al Khaimah, and Fujairah. Abu Dhabi, being the capital, serves as the seat of the federal government and is the largest emirate in terms of land area. The other emirates have their own local governments, but the federal government has authority over matters such as defense, foreign affairs, and education.

The division of the UAE into emirates is based on historical and geographical factors. Each emirate has its own distinct character, with some being more urbanized and industrialized, while others are more rural and traditional. The emirates are also responsible for their own economic development, with some relying heavily on oil and gas, while others have diversified into tourism, finance, and other sectors.

Major Cities

Dubai (population over 3.3 million)

Role: economic hub

Known For: Dubai is known for its modern architecture, luxurious shopping malls, and vibrant nightlife, with iconic landmarks such as the Burj Khalifa and the Palm Jumeirah.

Best For: Dubai is best for business travelers, shoppers, and those looking for a luxurious and modern city experience.

Abu Dhabi (population over 1.4 million)

Role: capital

Known For: Abu Dhabi is known for its rich cultural heritage, with historic sites such as the Sheikh Zayed Mosque and the Al Husn Fort, as well as its modern attractions such as the Louvre Abu Dhabi and the Yas Marina Circuit.

Best For: Abu Dhabi is best for those interested in history, culture, and traditional Emirati experiences.

Sharjah (population over 1.4 million)

Role: cultural center

Known For: Sharjah is known for its rich cultural heritage, with historic sites such as the Sharjah Fort and the Al Hisn Fort, as well as its modern attractions such as the Sharjah Museum of Islamic Civilization and the Sharjah Art Museum.

Best For: Sharjah is best for those interested in history, culture, and traditional Emirati experiences.

Ajman (population over 340,000)

Role: port city

Known For: Ajman is known for its beautiful beaches, historic sites such as the Ajman Fort, and its traditional souks (markets).

Best For: Ajman is best for those looking for a relaxed and traditional Emirati experience, with easy access to the sea and outdoor activities.

Ras Al Khaimah (population over 170,000)

Role: tourist destination

Known For: Ras Al Khaimah is known for its natural beauty, with the Hajar Mountains and the Arabian Gulf coastline, as well as its historic sites such as the Dhayah Fort and the Al Hisn Fort.

Best For: Ras Al Khaimah is best for those looking for outdoor adventures, such as hiking and water sports, as well as those interested in history and culture.

Fujairah (population over 220,000)

Role: port city

Known For: Fujairah is known for its beautiful beaches, historic sites such as the Fujairah Fort, and its traditional souks (markets).

Best For: Fujairah is best for those looking for a relaxed and traditional Emirati experience, with easy access to the sea and outdoor activities.

Umm Al Quwain (population over 70,000)

Role: tourist destination

Known For: Umm Al Quwain is known for its natural beauty, with the Arabian Gulf coastline and the mangrove forests, as well as its historic sites such as the Umm Al Quwain Fort.

Best For: Umm Al Quwain is best for those looking for outdoor adventures, such as birdwatching and fishing, as well as those interested in history and culture.

Regional Differences

  • The northern emirates, such as Ras Al Khaimah and Fujairah, are known for their natural beauty and outdoor activities, with a more rural and traditional way of life.
  • The eastern region, including the Hajar Mountains, is known for its scenic landscapes and outdoor activities, such as hiking and camping.
  • The western region, including the Liwa Oasis, is known for its desert landscapes and traditional Bedouin culture, with opportunities for desert safaris and camping.
  • The coastal region, including cities such as Dubai and Ajman, is known for its urbanized and modern way of life, with a focus on tourism, trade, and commerce.

Getting Around

The UAE has a well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights connecting the major cities, as well as buses and taxis available for shorter journeys. The country is also investing heavily in its rail network, with plans to connect the major cities by train in the near future. For those who prefer to drive, the UAE has a modern and well-maintained road network, with car rental companies and taxi services widely available. However, driving in the UAE can be challenging, especially for those who are not used to the local driving style, and visitors are advised to exercise caution and follow local traffic laws.

Domestic flights are a convenient way to travel between the major cities, with several airlines operating frequent flights. Buses are also a popular mode of transportation, with companies such as Dubai's RTA and Abu Dhabi's DOT operating extensive networks of routes. Taxis are widely available, but can be more expensive than buses, and visitors are advised to agree on the fare before starting the journey. Overall, the UAE has a well-developed transportation network, making it easy to get around the country and explore its many attractions.
