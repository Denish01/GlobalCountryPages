# Cost of Living in UAE - Prices & Budget Guide

Living in or visiting the UAE can be expensive, with daily budgets ranging from $80-150 USD per day for backpackers to $500-1000 USD per day for those seeking comfort and luxury.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Expensive
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $80-150 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $200-400 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $500-1000 USD
Currency: AED
Tipping Custom: expected (10-15%)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is UAE? [/SECTION]
The UAE is considered one of the more expensive countries in the world, with a high cost of living, particularly in cities like Dubai and Abu Dhabi. Compared to its neighbors, Saudi Arabia and Oman, the UAE is significantly more expensive, with prices for accommodation, food, and transportation being among the highest in the region. The country's strong economy, driven by its oil and gas industry, and its popularity as a tourist destination, have contributed to the high cost of living. However, the UAE also offers a high standard of living, with modern amenities, world-class infrastructure, and a wide range of entertainment and leisure activities, making it an attractive destination for visitors and expatriates alike.

The cost of living in the UAE can vary greatly depending on the lifestyle and personal choices of an individual. While it is possible to live relatively affordably in the UAE, especially outside of the major cities, the cost of accommodation, food, and transportation can be high. Additionally, the UAE has a high tax-free salary environment, which can be a significant advantage for expatriates, but also contributes to the high cost of living. Overall, the UAE is a great destination for those who are willing to pay a premium for a high standard of living and world-class amenities.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider sharing an apartment with roommates to reduce rent costs
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to save on fuel and taxi costs
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls for affordable and delicious meals
- Take advantage of happy hour deals or discounts at restaurants and bars
- Shop at local markets or supermarkets for groceries and other essentials
- Avoid traveling during peak season (June to September) to save on accommodation and flight costs
