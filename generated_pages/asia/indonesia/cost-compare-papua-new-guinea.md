[SECTION] Overview: Indonesia vs Papua New Guinea [/SECTION]
Indonesia and Papua New Guinea are two neighboring countries in Southeast Asia and the Pacific, with vastly different economies and cost of living. Indonesia is the world's fourth most populous country, with a diverse economy and a growing middle class, while Papua New Guinea is a smaller, more rural country with a economy based on natural resources. The cost of living in Indonesia is generally lower than in Papua New Guinea, with cheaper accommodation, food, and transportation options. However, the cost of living in Papua New Guinea can vary greatly depending on the location, with cities like Port Moresby being more expensive than rural areas.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Indonesia has a more developed economy, with a larger manufacturing sector and a growing services industry, which has led to a higher standard of living and a more competitive job market. Papua New Guinea, on the other hand, has a smaller economy, with a greater reliance on natural resources, which can make it more vulnerable to fluctuations in global commodity prices. Overall, Indonesia is generally around 30-40% cheaper than Papua New Guinea, making it a more attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a affordable place to live.
