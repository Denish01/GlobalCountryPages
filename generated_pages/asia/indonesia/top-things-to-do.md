# Top Things to Do in Indonesia — Attractions & Experiences

Indonesia offers a diverse range of experiences, from exploring ancient temples and vibrant cities to relaxing on beautiful beaches and trekking through lush national parks, making it a paradise for travelers with its rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Attend a traditional **Wayang Kulit** shadow puppet show in Yogyakarta, a unique blend of art, music, and storytelling
- Take a **Surfing** lesson in Bali, with its world-class waves and stunning beaches
- Learn **Batik** making in a local workshop, a traditional Indonesian textile art form
- Experience a **Gamelan** music performance, a traditional Indonesian percussion ensemble
- Visit a **Kopi Luwak** coffee plantation, famous for its unique and expensive coffee production process
- Explore the **Tana Toraja** region, known for its unique funeral rituals and traditional villages
- Take a **Cooking Class** to learn about Indonesian cuisine and its diverse flavors and ingredients

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore **Komodo National Park**, home to the iconic Komodo dragon and stunning marine life
- Hike to the summit of **Mount Rinjani**, an active volcano with breathtaking views and a crater lake
- Relax on the beautiful beaches of **Bali**, such as Kuta, Seminyak, and Nusa Dua
- Visit **Kerinci Seblat National Park**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most biodiverse places on Earth
- Take a **Snorkeling** or **Diving** trip to the **Raja Ampat** islands, known for their incredible marine life and coral reefs

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum** in Jakarta, showcasing Indonesia's rich cultural and historical heritage
- Explore the **Prambanan Temple** complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest Hindu temple complexes in the world
- Attend a traditional **Legong** dance performance in Bali, a classical dance form known for its beauty and elegance
- Visit the **Taman Sari Water Castle** in Yogyakarta, a historic royal garden and water palace
- Learn about **Indonesian Batik** making at a local workshop, a traditional textile art form
- Explore the **Old Town** of Jakarta, known for its historic buildings, museums, and cultural attractions

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Kelimutu National Park** on the island of Flores, known for its stunning crater lakes and traditional villages
- Explore the **Sianok Canyon** in West Sumatra, a beautiful and rugged natural landscape
- Relax on the beautiful beaches of **Lombok**, a lesser-known island with stunning natural scenery
- Take a **Trekking** trip to the **Wae Rebo** village in Flores, known for its traditional architecture and stunning natural scenery
- Visit the **Bunaken National Park** in North Sulawesi, a marine park with incredible coral reefs and marine life

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to the **Borobudur Temple** from Yogyakarta, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest Buddhist temples in the world (distance: 40 km, travel time: 1 hour)
- Visit the **Uluwatu Temple** and **Kuta Beach** from Bali, a stunning temple and beach combination (distance: 30 km, travel time: 1 hour)
- Explore the **Taman Mini Indonesia Indah** cultural park from Jakarta, a showcase of Indonesia's diverse cultures and traditions (distance: 20 km, travel time: 30 minutes)
- Take a day trip to the **Krakatau** volcanic island from Jakarta, a historic and scenic island (distance: 50 km, travel time: 2 hours)
- Visit the **Prambanan Temple** complex from Yogyakarta, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest Hindu temple complexes in the world (distance: 15 km, travel time: 30 minutes)
