# Indonesia Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Indonesia before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Indonesia has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of over 160 countries to enter the country with varying requirements. The country offers visa-free access to citizens of a few countries, including those from the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) region, for a limited period. Indonesia's visa policy aims to promote tourism, trade, and cultural exchange while maintaining national security and immigration control. The country has introduced various visa schemes, including visa on arrival, e-visa, and free visa, to facilitate entry for foreign nationals.

The Indonesian government has been working to simplify and streamline its visa application process, making it easier for tourists, business travelers, and other visitors to enter the country. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it is crucial to check with the official embassy or consulate of Indonesia for the latest information before planning a trip.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Indonesia with a visa on arrival, which is valid for 30 days and can be extended once for an additional 30 days. The typical stay for tourists is 30-60 days, and they must meet certain requirements, such as having a return ticket and sufficient funds. Tourists can also apply for a **Social Visa**, which allows them to stay in Indonesia for up to six months.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a **Business Visa**, which can be obtained through a sponsorship from an Indonesian company. They may also need to provide an invitation letter from the company and meet specific requirements, such as having a valid passport and proof of financial means.
**Students:** Students must apply for a **Student Visa**, which requires enrollment proof from an Indonesian educational institution. The duration of the visa depends on the length of the study program.
**Workers:** Workers must obtain a **Work Visa**, which requires sponsorship from an Indonesian company. The process involves applying for a work permit and meeting specific requirements, such as having a valid passport and proof of qualifications.

10. Payment for visa fees

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
Indonesia has several main airports, including Soekarno-Hatta International Airport in Jakarta, Ngurah Rai International Airport in Bali, and Juanda International Airport in Surabaya. Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and complete an arrival/departure card. The immigration process typically takes around 30-60 minutes, depending on the airport and the number of passengers. Visitors can also enter Indonesia through land borders, such as the border crossing with Malaysia in West Kalimantan.

At immigration, visitors will be asked to provide their travel documents, including their passport, visa (if required), and onward ticket. They may also be asked questions about their purpose of visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements. It is essential to ensure that all travel documents are in order and that visitors have met the necessary requirements before proceeding to immigration.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not checking the validity of the passport before traveling
- Failing to obtain the required visa or documentation
- Not having sufficient funds or proof of onward travel
- Not completing the arrival/departure card correctly
- Not declaring goods or exceeding the duty-free allowance
- Not having health insurance that covers the duration of stay

It is crucial to avoid these common mistakes to ensure a smooth and hassle-free entry into Indonesia. Visitors should always verify the current visa requirements and regulations with the official embassy or consulate of Indonesia before traveling.
