# Rent & Housing Prices in East Timor

Rent in East Timor can range from $200 to $800 per month for a one-bedroom apartment, depending on the location and quality of the property.

[SECTION] Housing Market Overview [/SECTION]
The rental market in East Timor is relatively small, with a limited supply of quality housing, particularly in the capital city of Dili. This can lead to higher prices and competition for available properties. Typical lease terms are for one to two years, with a deposit requirement of one to two months' rent. Tenant rights are generally protected under East Timorese law, but it is still important for renters to carefully review their lease agreements and understand their obligations. Compared to regional neighbors, East Timor's housing costs are relatively high, particularly in Dili, due to the country's limited supply of housing and high demand from international organizations and government agencies.

The housing market in East Timor is also influenced by the country's growing economy and increasing demand for housing from both locals and expats. The government has implemented initiatives to increase the supply of affordable housing, but more needs to be done to address the shortage of quality housing in the country. Despite these challenges, East Timor remains an attractive destination for expats and investors, with its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and growing economy. The country's housing market is expected to continue to evolve in the coming years, with new developments and investments in the pipeline.
