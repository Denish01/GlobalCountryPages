[SECTION] How East Timor Is Divided [/SECTION]
East Timor is divided into 13 municipalities, which are further divided into 65 administrative posts, 442 sucos, and 2,233 aldeias. The municipalities are the primary administrative divisions of the country, and they are responsible for providing basic services such as healthcare, education, and infrastructure to the local population. The municipalities are also responsible for collecting taxes and managing local resources, which gives them a significant amount of autonomy in governing their respective areas.

The administrative divisions of East Timor are designed to promote decentralization and local governance, which is essential for the country's development. The 13 municipalities are Aileu, Ainaro, Baucau, Bobonaro, Cova Lima, Dili, Ermera, Lautem, Liquica, Manatuto, Manufahi, Oecusse, and Viqueque. Each municipality has its own unique characteristics, culture, and history, which are reflected in their administrative structures and local traditions.

[SECTION] Regional Differences [/SECTION]
- The coastal regions of East Timor, such as Dili and Baucau, have a more urban and modern character, with a stronger focus on trade and commerce.
- The mountainous regions, such as Aileu and Ermera, are more rural and traditional, with a focus on coffee production and traditional crafts.
- The eastern regions, such as Lautem and Viqueque, have a more rugged and remote character, with a stronger focus on fishing and traditional crafts.
- The western regions, such as Cova Lima and Bobonaro, have a more diverse character, with a mix of urban and rural areas, and a focus on trade, commerce, and traditional crafts.

[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
East Timor has a limited but improving transportation network, with domestic flights, buses, and taxis available. The country's roads are generally in poor condition, and driving can be challenging, especially in rural areas. However, the scenic routes and stunning landscapes make driving a rewarding experience for adventurous travelers. Domestic flights are available between Dili and other major cities, and buses and taxis are widely available for shorter trips. The country is also investing in improving its transportation infrastructure, including the construction of new roads and the expansion of the airport.
