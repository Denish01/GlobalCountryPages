# Positive Things About Bangladesh - Achievements & Culture

[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Baitul Mukarram National Mosque** in Dhaka is one of the largest mosques in the world and a prominent example of modern Islamic architecture.
- The **Lalbagh Fort** in Dhaka is a 17th-century Mughal fort and a popular tourist destination.
- The **Sundarbans**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the largest mangrove forest in the world and home to the **Bengal tiger**.
- The **Pahela Baishakh** festival, which marks the beginning of the Bengali New Year, is a significant cultural event in Bangladesh.
- The **Jamdani** textile, a traditional Bengali fabric, is a UNESCO-recognized intangible cultural heritage of humanity.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Muhammad Yunus**, a Bangladeshi economist and Nobel laureate, is known for his work in microfinance and social business.
- The **Bangladeshi diaspora** has made significant contributions to the economy and culture of countries such as the United States, the United Kingdom, and Canada.
- **Sheikh Mujibur Rahman**, the founding father of Bangladesh, played a key role in the country's independence movement and is revered as a national hero.
- The **Grameen Bank**, founded by Muhammad Yunus, has provided financial services to millions of people, especially women, in rural Bangladesh and has been recognized globally for its innovative approach to microfinance.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **hospitality** of the Bangladeshi people is often praised by visitors, who appreciate the warm welcome and generosity they receive.
- The **food** in Bangladesh is a fusion of traditional Bengali cuisine and international flavors, with popular dishes such as **biriyani** and **haleem**.
- The **scenery** of Bangladesh, with its lush green countryside, rivers, and beaches, is a major draw for tourists.
- The **Cox's Bazar Beach**, one of the longest natural sea beaches in the world, is a popular destination for visitors.
- The **Sundarbans** mangrove forest is a unique and fascinating ecosystem that attracts nature lovers and adventure seekers.
- The **Dhaka city**, with its rich history and cultural heritage, is a treasure trove of experiences, from visiting ancient forts and mosques to exploring the vibrant streets and markets.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Rangamati Hill District** is a lesser-known destination in Bangladesh, known for its scenic beauty, tribal villages, and traditional handicrafts.
- The **Puthia Temple Complex** is a collection of ancient temples and ruins that showcase the rich cultural heritage of Bangladesh.
- The **Lawachara National Park** is a protected area that is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including the **hoolock gibbon**, an endangered primate species.
- The **Bangladesh National Museum** in Dhaka is a treasure trove of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the country's history, culture, and natural heritage.
