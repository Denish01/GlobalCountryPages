How Bangladesh Is Divided

Bangladesh is divided into 8 administrative divisions, which are further subdivided into 64 districts. The divisions are Dhaka, Chittagong, Sylhet, Khulna, Barisal, Rajshahi, Rangpur, and Mymensingh. Each division has its own unique character and plays a significant role in the country's economy, culture, and politics. The divisions are also divided into upazilas or sub-districts, which are the second-tier administrative units.

The administrative structure of Bangladesh is designed to provide effective governance and delivery of public services to its large and diverse population. The divisions are responsible for implementing national policies and programs, while the districts and upazilas are responsible for providing basic services such as healthcare, education, and infrastructure. The administrative structure also provides a framework for decentralization and participation of local communities in the decision-making process.

Major Cities

Dhaka (20.0M)

Role: capital, economic hub

Known For: Dhaka is a bustling metropolis with a rich cultural heritage, known for its historic landmarks, vibrant markets, and delicious street food. The city is also home to many educational and research institutions, making it a hub for learning and innovation.

Best For: history buffs, foodies, shoppers

Chittagong (4.0M)

Role: port city, economic hub

Known For: Chittagong is a major port city and industrial center, known for its beautiful beaches, scenic hills, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is also home to many historical landmarks, including the Chittagong War Cemetery and the Ahsan Manzil.

Best For: beachgoers, history enthusiasts, business travelers

Sylhet (1.5M)

Role: cultural center, tourist hub

Known For: Sylhet is a city located in the northeastern part of Bangladesh, known for its beautiful tea gardens, scenic lakes, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is also home to many historical landmarks, including the Sylhet Shahi Eidgah and the Ratargul Swamp Forest.

Best For: nature lovers, culture enthusiasts, adventure seekers

Khulna (1.5M)

Role: industrial center, port city

Known For: Khulna is a major industrial center and port city, known for its shipbuilding industry, textile mills, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is also home to many historical landmarks, including the Khulna Museum and the Shait Gumbad Mosque.

Best For: business travelers, history enthusiasts, shoppers

Rajshahi (1.0M)

Role: educational hub, cultural center

Known For: Rajshahi is a city located in the northwestern part of Bangladesh, known for its beautiful gardens, scenic rivers, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is also home to many educational institutions, including the University of Rajshahi.

Best For: students, culture enthusiasts, nature lovers

Barisal (0.5M)

Role: port city, agricultural center

Known For: Barisal is a city located in the southern part of Bangladesh, known for its beautiful rivers, scenic countryside, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is also home to many historical landmarks, including the Barisal Museum and the Durga Sagor.

Best For: nature lovers, history enthusiasts, adventure seekers

Regional Differences

  • The regions of Bangladesh differ significantly in terms of their cultural and linguistic identities, with the northern regions having a distinct dialect and cultural practices.
  • The economic activities of the regions also vary, with the Dhaka and Chittagong divisions being the most industrialized, while the Rajshahi and Rangpur divisions are primarily agricultural.
  • The climate of the regions also differs, with the coastal regions being more prone to cyclones and floods, while the hilly regions are cooler and more scenic.
  • The language spoken in the regions also varies, with Bengali being the official language, but with distinct dialects and accents in different regions.

Getting Around

Bangladesh has a well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis available. The country has several international airports, including the Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka and the Shah Amanat International Airport in Chittagong. The train network is also well-developed, with trains connecting major cities and towns. Buses and taxis are also widely available, but the road conditions can be poor in some areas. The driving conditions in Bangladesh can be challenging, especially in the rural areas, due to the presence of pedestrians, cyclists, and animals on the roads. However, with proper planning and precautions, traveling in Bangladesh can be a rewarding experience.
