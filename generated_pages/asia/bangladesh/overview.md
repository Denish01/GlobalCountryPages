[SECTION] What Is Bangladesh? [/SECTION]
Bangladesh is a **sovereign** country located in **Asia**, bordered by **India** to the west, north, and east, and **Myanmar** to the southeast. The country is situated in the **Ganges-Brahmaputra delta**, which is the largest delta in the world and home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Bangladesh is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its vibrant festivals, delicious cuisine, and traditional crafts. The country has a long history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the **Stone Age**.

Bangladesh is a **densely populated** country, with a population of over 172 million people, making it one of the most populous countries in the world. The country has a **tropical monsoon climate**, with high temperatures and high humidity levels throughout the year. The capital city, **Dhaka**, is a bustling metropolis with a rich history and culture, and is home to many of the country's major landmarks and attractions. Bangladesh is also known for its **natural beauty**, with a long coastline along the **Bay of Bengal** and a range of **rivers** and **wetlands** that are home to a diverse range of wildlife.

Bangladesh has a **developing economy**, with a growing manufacturing sector and a significant agricultural sector. The country is a major producer of **textiles**, **jute**, and **tea**, and is also known for its **fisheries** and **livestock** industries. Bangladesh is a member of several international organizations, including the **United Nations**, the **Commonwealth of Nations**, and the **South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation**.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1947 - Bangladesh gains independence from British colonial rule as part of the Dominion of Pakistan, with the country being divided into two wings, East Pakistan and West Pakistan.
2. 1952 - The **Bengali Language Movement** begins, with protesters demanding equal status for the Bengali language, which eventually becomes a key factor in the country's struggle for independence.
3. 1971 - Bangladesh declares independence from Pakistan, following a long and bloody conflict, and becomes an independent sovereign state.
4. 1972 - **Sheikh Mujibur Rahman** becomes the country's first president, and plays a key role in shaping the country's early years.
5. 1991 - Bangladesh adopts a **parliamentary democracy**, with a prime minister as head of government and a president as head of state.
6. 2008 - The **Awami League** wins a landslide victory in the country's general election, with **Sheikh Hasina** becoming prime minister.
7. 2013 - The **Rana Plaza collapse** occurs, resulting in one of the deadliest industrial disasters in history, and highlighting the need for improved labor rights and safety standards in the country's garment industry.

[SECTION] What Makes Bangladesh Unique [/SECTION]
- Bangladesh is home to the **Sundarbans**, the largest mangrove forest in the world, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to a diverse range of wildlife, including the **Bengal tiger**.
- The country has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of **Islamic**, **Hindu**, and **Buddhist** influences, and is known for its vibrant festivals, including the **Eid al-Fitr** and **Durga Puja**.
- Bangladesh is a major producer of **jute**, which is used to make a range of products, including **burlap** and **ropes**.
- The country has a significant **garment industry**, with many international brands sourcing clothing from Bangladeshi manufacturers.
- Bangladesh is home to a diverse range of **rivers**, including the **Ganges**, **Brahmaputra**, and **Meghna**, which provide a source of freshwater and support a range of aquatic life.

Bangladesh is a sovereign country located in Asia, known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and growing economy, with a long history, diverse culture, and significant contributions to the global textile and garment industries.
