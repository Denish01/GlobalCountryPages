Overall Safety Assessment

Bangladesh is a densely populated country with a vibrant culture and rich history. While it can be a rewarding destination for travelers, there are certain safety concerns that visitors should be aware of. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, is a common problem in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, the country is prone to natural disasters, including cyclones, floods, and earthquakes, which can pose a significant risk to travelers. However, with proper planning and precautions, visitors can minimize their risk and have a safe and enjoyable trip.

Overall, Bangladesh is considered a relatively safe country for tourists, with low levels of violent crime. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, especially in crowded areas and at night. It's also important to be aware of local customs and traditions, and to respect the country's conservative culture. By taking a balanced and informed approach to safety, travelers can have a positive and enriching experience in Bangladesh.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Be aware of scams targeting tourists, such as fake tour guides and overpriced services
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
  • Be cautious of traffic accidents, especially when crossing the road or riding a rickshaw
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas
  • Be aware of food poisoning risks, especially when eating from street vendors
  • Be cautious of begging and aggressive solicitation, especially in tourist areas

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended for travelers to Bangladesh
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities in Bangladesh are limited, especially outside of major cities, and medical insurance is highly recommended
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available, but the quality of medication can vary

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep a low profile, especially in crowded areas
  • Avoid carrying valuables or large amounts of cash
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps instead of hailing taxis on the street
  • Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuables
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas
  • Stay informed about local conditions and events that may affect your safety
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or temples

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be prepared for staring and solicitation, especially in crowded areas
  • Dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing, especially when visiting mosques or temples
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas
  • Consider hiring a reputable guide or joining a group tour to minimize risks and stay safe
