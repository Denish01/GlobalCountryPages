# Bangladesh Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

To experience the authentic flavors of Bangladesh, you should try dishes like **Hilsa Fish**, **Biryani**, and **Mishti Doi**, which showcase the country's rich culinary heritage. 

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: rice, fish, lentils, vegetables
National Dish: Hilsa Fish, a national symbol of Bangladesh, often served in a mustard-based sauce
Meal Times: 8-10 am for breakfast, 1-3 pm for lunch, 8-10 pm for dinner
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% of the total bill
Dietary Notes: predominantly halal, with some vegetarian options available
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Bangladeshi cuisine is defined by its use of fresh ingredients, bold spices, and a blend of traditional and modern cooking techniques. The country's strategic location at the crossroads of South and Southeast Asia has influenced its cuisine, with many dishes bearing similarities to Indian, Pakistani, and Middle Eastern flavors. Regional variations also play a significant role, with different areas of the country specializing in unique dishes and ingredients. For example, the coastal regions are known for their fresh seafood, while the northern areas are famous for their **Khichuri**, a hearty rice and lentil dish.

The cuisine of Bangladesh is also shaped by its **Mughal** and **British** colonial past, with many dishes bearing the influence of these cultures. The use of **ghee** (clarified butter) and **mustard oil** is prevalent in Bangladeshi cooking, adding a rich and distinct flavor to many dishes. The country's love for sweets is also evident, with **Mishti Doi** (sweet yogurt) and **Rosogolla** (a sweet dumpling) being popular desserts.

Bangladeshi cuisine is often characterized by its bold flavors and spices, with many dishes featuring a combination of **turmeric**, **cumin**, **coriander**, and **chili peppers**. The use of fresh **herbs** like **cilantro** and **mint** is also common, adding a bright and fresh flavor to many dishes. With its rich culinary heritage and diverse regional flavors, Bangladesh offers a unique and exciting food experience for visitors.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Eating with the right hand**: it is customary to eat with the right hand, as the left hand is considered unclean
- **Respect for elders**: it is customary to show respect to elders by offering them the best seats and the first serving of food
- **Tipping**: tipping is not mandatory, but 5-10% of the total bill is considered polite
- **Dress code**: modest dress is recommended, especially when visiting rural areas or mosques
- **Reservation culture**: reservations are not always necessary, but it is recommended to book in advance for popular restaurants
- **Eating etiquette**: it is customary to wait for the host to start eating before beginning to eat
