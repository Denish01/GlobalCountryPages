# Getting Around Bangladesh — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Bangladesh, you can use a variety of transportation options, including domestic flights, trains, buses, and city transport such as taxis, ride-hailing services, and rickshaws.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Bangladesh is easily accessible by air, with two main international airports: Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka and Shah Amanat International Airport in Chittagong. Direct flights are available from major hubs in Asia, Europe, and the Middle East, including Singapore, Dubai, and London. If you prefer to enter the country by land, you can do so from India or Myanmar, with several overland entry points, including the Benapole-Petrapole border crossing with India and the Teknaf border crossing with Myanmar.

The main overland entry points from India are at Benapole-Petrapole, Gede-Darshana, and Burimari-Changrabandha, while the main entry point from Myanmar is at Teknaf. You can take buses or taxis from these border crossings to major cities in Bangladesh. Alternatively, you can take a ferry from India or other neighboring countries to reach Bangladesh by sea. However, this option is less common and may require more planning and preparation.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
For domestic travel, Bangladesh has a moderate air network, with several airlines operating flights to major cities, including Dhaka, Chittagong, Sylhet, and Cox's Bazar. The main domestic airlines are **Biman Bangladesh Airlines**, **Novoair**, and **US-Bangla Airlines**, which offer daily flights to various destinations. You can book tickets online or through travel agents, with approximate costs ranging from $50 to $200, depending on the route and airline. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, to ensure availability and get the best rates.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Bangladesh has a limited rail network, with most trains operating on meter-gauge tracks. The main routes connect Dhaka to other major cities, including Chittagong, Sylhet, and Khulna. While the rail network is not extensive, it's a relatively affordable and comfortable way to travel, with approximate costs ranging from $5 to $20, depending on the class and route. You can book tickets at train stations or through online portals, but it's recommended to book in advance, especially for popular routes. There are no high-speed train options available in Bangladesh, but some trains offer **first-class** and **air-conditioned** carriages for more comfort.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
For intercity travel, buses and coaches are a popular option, with several companies operating on various routes. The main bus companies are **Green Line**, **Shyamoli**, and **Hanif**, which offer a range of services, from **economy** to **VIP**. The quality of buses varies, but most are relatively comfortable and affordable, with approximate costs ranging from $5 to $20, depending on the route and class. You can book tickets at bus stations or through online portals, and it's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Bangladesh, you'll need a valid **international driving permit** or a local driving license.
- Road conditions in Bangladesh are generally poor, with many roads being narrow and congested.
- Fuel costs are relatively low, with **gasoline** and **diesel** available at most fuel stations.
- Car rental options are limited, but available in major cities, with **economy** and **sedan** options starting from $30 per day.
- Hazards to watch out for include **potholes**, **traffic congestion**, and **aggressive driving**.
- It's recommended to hire a driver or use a reputable car rental service to avoid the challenges of driving in Bangladesh.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **scams** and **overcharging** by taxi and rickshaw drivers, and always negotiate the fare before starting your journey.
- Use **reputable** bus and train companies to avoid **delays** and **cancellations**.
- Download **ride-hailing apps** like Uber or local alternatives to book convenient and affordable rides.
- Carry **small bills** and **change** to avoid **inconvenience** when paying for transport.
- Be **patient** and **flexible** when traveling in Bangladesh, as transport options can be **unpredictable**.
- Use **authorized** taxi and bus stands to avoid **scams** and **overcharging**.
- Keep your **valuables** safe and secure, especially in crowded transport areas.
- Learn some **basic Bengali phrases**, such as "hello" (হ্যালো) and "thank you" (ধন্যবাদ), to help you navigate transport options and interact with locals.
