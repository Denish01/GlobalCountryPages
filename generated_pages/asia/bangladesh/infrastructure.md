# Bangladesh Infrastructure — Roads, Airports, Electricity & Water

The infrastructure in Bangladesh is rapidly developing, with significant investments in recent years, but still faces challenges in terms of quality, accessibility, and reliability, particularly in rural areas.

Electricity Access: 90% of population

Rail Network: 2,835 km
Major Airports: 3
Major Ports: 2
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Bangladesh has made significant progress in developing its infrastructure in recent years, with a focus on improving the quality of life for its large and growing population. The government has invested heavily in various sectors, including transport, energy, and telecommunications, to support economic growth and reduce poverty. However, despite these efforts, the country still faces significant challenges in terms of infrastructure development, particularly in rural areas where access to basic services such as electricity, clean water, and sanitation remains limited. The urban-rural gap in infrastructure development is a major concern, with cities like Dhaka and Chittagong having relatively better infrastructure compared to rural areas.

The government has launched several initiatives to address these challenges, including the **Seventh Five-Year Plan**, which aims to improve infrastructure development and reduce poverty. The plan focuses on increasing investment in key sectors such as transport, energy, and telecommunications, and improving the business environment to attract private sector investment. Additionally, the government has also launched several **public-private partnership** initiatives to develop infrastructure projects, such as roads, bridges, and ports.

Despite these efforts, Bangladesh still faces significant challenges in terms of infrastructure development, including a lack of funding, corruption, and bureaucratic inefficiencies. The country needs to address these challenges to achieve its development goals and improve the quality of life for its citizens. The government has also recognized the importance of **sustainable development** and has launched several initiatives to promote green growth and reduce the country's carbon footprint.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]
- The power sector in Bangladesh is dominated by **fossil fuels**, with natural gas being the primary source of electricity generation.
- The country has made significant progress in increasing electricity access, with over 90% of the population having access to electricity.
- However, the power sector still faces challenges in terms of reliability, with frequent **power outages** and **load shedding** being common.
- The government has launched several initiatives to promote **renewable energy**, including solar and wind power, to reduce the country's dependence on fossil fuels.
- Bangladesh has set a target of generating 10% of its electricity from renewable sources by 2025.

[SECTION] Water & Sanitation [/SECTION]
- Access to **safe drinking water** is a major challenge in Bangladesh, particularly in rural areas where many people rely on **tube wells** and **ponds** for their water needs.
- The country has made significant progress in improving **sanitation coverage**, with over 90% of the population having access to improved sanitation facilities.
- However, **water pollution** remains a major concern, with many rivers and water bodies being polluted by industrial and agricultural waste.
- The government has launched several initiatives to improve **water management**, including the construction of new **water treatment plants** and **wastewater management systems**.
- Bangladesh has also made significant progress in reducing **open defecation**, with the number of people practicing open defecation reducing significantly in recent years.

[SECTION] Telecommunications [/SECTION]
- Bangladesh has made significant progress in improving **mobile phone coverage**, with over 90% of the population having access to mobile phone services.
- The country has also made significant progress in improving **internet penetration**, with over 20% of the population having access to the internet.
- The government has launched several initiatives to promote **broadband internet**, including the construction of new **fiber optic cables** and **4G networks**.
- Bangladesh has also made significant progress in improving **postal services**, with the introduction of new **postal codes** and **tracking systems**.
- The country has a well-developed **telecommunications regulatory framework**, with the **Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory Commission** responsible for regulating the sector.

[SECTION] Development Projects [/SECTION]
- The government has launched several major infrastructure development projects, including the construction of a new **bridge over the Padma River** and the **Dhaka-Chittagong highway**.
- The **Asian Development Bank** and the **World Bank** are providing significant funding for these projects, with a focus on improving the country's transport infrastructure.
- The government has also launched several initiatives to promote **public-private partnerships**, including the construction of new **ports** and **airports**.
- Bangladesh has also made significant progress in developing its **information technology** sector, with several major **IT parks** being constructed in Dhaka and other cities.
- The government has set a target of increasing the contribution of the **IT sector** to the country's GDP to 10% by 2025.
