# Education Costs in Bangladesh

The cost of education in Bangladesh varies depending on the type of institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international schools can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Bangladesh is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the largest. The **public education** system is generally considered to be of lower quality compared to the private sector, with limited resources and infrastructure. The language of instruction in public schools is **Bengali**, while private schools may offer English as a medium of instruction. Compulsory education in Bangladesh lasts for 12 years, from primary to higher secondary level. International schools are also available in major cities like Dhaka and Chittagong, offering curricula such as the **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Exams**.

The quality of education in Bangladesh is a major concern, with many students opting for private tuition and coaching centers to supplement their learning. The government has implemented various initiatives to improve the education system, including the introduction of **free textbooks** and **mid-day meals** for students. However, the education system still faces challenges such as **teacher shortages** and **infrastructure deficits**. Despite these challenges, Bangladesh has made significant progress in increasing **enrollment rates** and **literacy rates** in recent years.
