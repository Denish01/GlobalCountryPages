[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
The Philippines is an **archipelago** located in Southeast Asia, comprising over 7,000 islands. It is situated in the western Pacific Ocean, with the Philippine Sea to the east, the South China Sea to the west, and the Celebes Sea to the south. The country's strategic location has made it an important hub for trade and commerce in the region. The Philippines shares maritime borders with several countries, including Taiwan to the north, Malaysia and Indonesia to the south, and Vietnam to the west.

The Philippines is an island nation, with its islands divided into three main geographical divisions: Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. The capital city, Manila, is located on the island of Luzon, which is the largest and most populous island in the country. The Philippines' unique geography has created a diverse range of ecosystems, from **coral reefs** and **rainforests** to **volcanic mountains** and **white-sand beaches**. The country's location near the equator and its tropical climate make it prone to natural disasters such as typhoons, earthquakes, and volcanic eruptions.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
The Philippines is a country of striking natural beauty, with a diverse range of landscapes and geological features. The terrain is characterized by **mountain ranges**, **valleys**, and **coastal plains**, with many active and dormant volcanoes scattered throughout the islands. The highest peak, Mount Apo, is located on the island of Mindanao and is a popular destination for hikers and adventure seekers. The country's **rivers**, including the Pasig River and the Cagayan River, play an important role in shaping the landscape and supporting agriculture and industry.

The Philippines is also home to some of the most beautiful and diverse **beaches** in the world, with popular destinations such as Boracay, Palawan, and Cebu attracting millions of tourists each year. The country's **islands** are surrounded by **coral reefs**, which are home to an incredible array of **marine life**, including fish, turtles, and dolphins. The Philippines' unique geography has also created a range of **caves**, **waterfalls**, and **hot springs**, which are popular destinations for tourists and adventure seekers.

The country's **forests** are another important feature of its landscape, with many areas of **primary forest** still intact. The Philippines is home to some of the most biodiverse ecosystems in the world, with many **endemic species** found nowhere else on Earth. The country's **agricultural lands** are also an important part of its landscape, with many areas dedicated to crops such as rice, corn, and sugarcane.
