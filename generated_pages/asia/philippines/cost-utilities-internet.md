# Utility & Internet Costs in Philippines

The cost of utilities and internet in the Philippines can range from $60 to $150 per month for a medium-sized apartment, depending on the location and usage.

[FACTBOX]
Monthly Utilities (85 sqm apt): ($100-$120 USD)

Electricity Cost: ($0.12-$0.15 per kWh)
Currency: PHP
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
The Philippines has a relatively well-developed utility infrastructure, with most households having access to electricity, water, and gas. However, the quality of these services can vary depending on the location, with urban areas generally having more reliable and efficient utilities. The cost of utilities in the Philippines can fluctuate, with prices increasing during peak seasons such as summer and holidays. Additionally, the cost of electricity can vary depending on the region, with some areas having higher rates due to transmission and distribution costs.

The Philippines has a **tropical climate**, which means that the demand for electricity and water can be high during certain times of the year. As a result, utility costs can be higher during the summer months when air conditioning and cooling systems are in use. However, the cost of utilities can be managed by being mindful of energy consumption and taking advantage of **energy-efficient appliances** and **time-of-use pricing**.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities, it is recommended to contact the local utility providers, such as **Meralco** for electricity and **Maynilad** for water, to schedule an installation and meter reading.
- Prepaid electricity can be a cost-effective option for those who want to manage their energy consumption and avoid surprise bills.
- The best internet providers in the Philippines are **PLDT** and **Globe**, which offer a range of plans and packages to suit different needs and budgets.
- To save on utility bills, it is recommended to use **energy-efficient appliances**, turn off lights and electronics when not in use, and take advantage of **time-of-use pricing**.
- It is also recommended to regularly check and compare utility rates and plans to ensure that you are getting the best deal for your money.
