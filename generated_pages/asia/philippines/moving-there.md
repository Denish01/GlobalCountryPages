# Moving to Philippines — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to the Philippines, you should start by researching the various visa options and requirements, as well as understanding the cost of living, culture, and expat communities in the country.

[SECTION] Why People Move to Philippines [/SECTION]
The Philippines is a popular destination for expats and retirees due to its warm climate, beautiful beaches, and low cost of living. Many people are drawn to the country's **tropical lifestyle**, where they can enjoy a relaxed pace of life and a wide range of outdoor activities. The Philippines is also a great destination for **digital nomads**, with many coworking spaces and a growing startup scene. The country has a large and diverse expat community, with many foreigners choosing to settle in cities such as Manila, Cebu, and Davao.

The Philippines offers a high **quality of life** at an affordable price, with modern amenities and infrastructure available in many areas. The country has a well-developed healthcare system, with many high-quality hospitals and medical facilities, particularly in Manila. The Philippines is also a great destination for **foodies**, with a wide range of delicious and affordable cuisine available. From traditional Filipino dishes such as adobo and sinigang, to international cuisine and fresh seafood, there's something for every taste and budget.

The expat demographic in the Philippines is diverse, with people from all over the world choosing to settle in the country. Many expats are **retirees**, drawn by the country's low cost of living and warm climate. Others are **young professionals**, attracted by the country's growing economy and startup scene. The Philippines is also a popular destination for **families**, with many international schools and a wide range of outdoor activities available.

[SECTION] Expat Communities [/SECTION]
- Manila is a popular destination for expats, with many international schools, restaurants, and social clubs
- Cebu is a growing hub for digital nomads and startups, with many coworking spaces and networking events
- Davao is a great destination for retirees, with a low cost of living and a warm climate
- Online groups such as Facebook groups and forums are a great way to connect with other expats and get advice and support
- Social networks such as InterNations and Expats in the Philippines are also a great way to meet other foreigners and learn about local events and activities

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- Opening a bank account in the Philippines can be a straightforward process, with many banks offering services in English
- Money transfers can be made using services such as PayPal or TransferWise, or through a local bank
- The Philippine peso is the local currency, although US dollars are widely accepted
- Credit cards are widely accepted in major cities, although cash is still the preferred method of payment
- [CALLOUT] Be aware of the local tax laws and regulations, including income tax and social security contributions

[SECTION] Challenges & Honest Advice [/SECTION]

- Language barriers can be a challenge, particularly outside of major cities where English is not as widely spoken
