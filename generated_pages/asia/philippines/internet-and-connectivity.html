Internet Overview

The Philippines has made significant strides in improving its internet infrastructure over the years, with the average internet speed increasing to 7.4 Mbps. However, the country still faces an urban-rural divide in terms of internet quality, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in major cities like Manila and Cebu, while rural areas often experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Despite these challenges, the internet is generally reliable, with most users experiencing minimal downtime and few issues with connectivity.

The Philippine government has implemented initiatives to improve internet access and speed, particularly in rural areas, with the goal of increasing digital inclusion and promoting economic growth. As a result, internet penetration has increased, with more Filipinos having access to the internet and using it for various purposes, including communication, education, and entertainment. However, the country still lags behind its neighbors in terms of internet speed and quality, and there is room for improvement to meet the growing demands of its digital economy.

Getting a SIM Card

Getting a SIM card in the Philippines is relatively easy, with several options available for tourists and travelers. The main carriers, Globe, Smart, and Sun Cellular, offer a range of prepaid SIM cards and plans that can be purchased at airports, malls, and convenience stores. To buy a SIM card, you will need to provide a valid passport and fill out a registration form, which is a requirement under Philippine law to prevent fraud and crime. Recommended carriers include Globe and Smart, which offer a range of tourist-friendly plans with data, voice, and text services.

WiFi Availability

  • Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels and resorts offering free WiFi to their guests, although speeds can vary depending on the location and number of users.
  • Cafe WiFi culture is popular in the Philippines, with many cafes and coffee shops offering free WiFi to customers, making it easy to stay connected while enjoying a cup of coffee or snack.
  • Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular in major cities, offering reliable and fast WiFi, as well as other amenities like meeting rooms and printing services.
  • Public WiFi is available in some areas, such as airports, malls, and public plazas, although speeds can be slow and security can be a concern.
  • Some restaurants and bars also offer free WiFi to customers, although speeds can vary depending on the location and number of users.

For Remote Workers

  • Coworking spaces like WeWork, Regus, and The Office are popular among remote workers, offering reliable and fast WiFi, as well as other amenities like meeting rooms and printing services.
  • Reliable cafe chains like Starbucks, Coffee Bean, and Figaro offer free WiFi and a comfortable workspace, making them popular among remote workers and digital nomads.
  • Backup internet options like portable WiFi hotspots and prepaid SIM cards can be useful in case of emergencies or when working in areas with limited internet access.
  • VPNs are recommended for remote workers to ensure secure and private internet access, especially when working with sensitive data or using public WiFi.
  • Some hotels and resorts also offer coworking spaces and facilities specifically designed for remote workers and digital nomads, making them a great option for those looking for a productive and comfortable workspace.

Tips & Warnings

  • Internet censorship is not a major concern in the Philippines, although some websites and online services may be blocked due to copyright or licensing issues.
  • Some websites and online services may be restricted or blocked in certain areas, such as public schools or government offices, so it's a good idea to check with your carrier or internet service provider for more information.
  • VPNs are recommended to ensure secure and private internet access, especially when using public WiFi or working with sensitive data.
  • Roaming alternatives like portable WiFi hotspots and prepaid SIM cards can be a cost-effective and convenient option for travelers, especially those who need to stay connected for work or emergencies.
  • eSIM options are not widely available in the Philippines, although some carriers may offer eSIM plans for certain devices, so it's best to check with your carrier or internet service provider for more information.
  • Public WiFi can be a security risk, so it's a good idea to use a VPN and keep your devices and data secure when using public WiFi.
