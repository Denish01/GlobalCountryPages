# Healthcare Costs in Philippines

The cost of healthcare in the Philippines varies widely depending on the type of care and location, with public healthcare services generally being more affordable than private ones, and health expenditure per capita averaging $194 per year.

[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
The Philippines has a mixed healthcare system, with both public and private providers offering a range of services. Public healthcare services are generally more affordable, but the quality of care can vary, with some public hospitals being understaffed and underequipped. Private healthcare providers, on the other hand, offer higher-quality care, but at a significantly higher cost. For locals, the public healthcare system is often the only option, while foreigners and those who can afford it tend to opt for private healthcare. Having health insurance is highly recommended, as it can help cover the high costs of private healthcare and provide access to better-quality care.

The healthcare system in the Philippines is overseen by the Department of Health, which is responsible for setting healthcare policies and regulating healthcare providers. The country has a large number of healthcare professionals, including doctors, nurses, and midwives, but there is a shortage of specialists in certain fields. For foreigners, it is often possible to find English-speaking healthcare professionals, particularly in private hospitals and clinics. However, it is still a good idea to learn some basic Filipino phrases and to research healthcare options before arriving in the country.

[SECTION] Pharmacy & Medication Costs [/SECTION]
- Prescription medication can cost between $5-$20 per month, depending on the type and brand
- Over-the-counter medication is widely available and can cost between $2-$10 per packet
- Pharmacies are readily available in urban areas, but may be scarce in rural areas
- A prescription from a doctor is usually required to purchase medication
- Some medications may be available without a prescription, but it is still recommended to consult a doctor before taking any medication
- Prices for medication can vary depending on the location and type of pharmacy

[SECTION] Emergency Care [/SECTION]
In the event of a medical emergency, it is best to call the local emergency number, 911, or to go to the nearest hospital. Ambulance services are available, but may not always be prompt or reliable. For foreigners, it is recommended to research the best hospitals in the area and to have a plan in place in case of an emergency. Some of the best hospitals for foreigners in the Philippines include St. Luke's Medical Center in Manila and Chong Hua Hospital in Cebu. These hospitals have English-speaking staff and offer high-quality care, but can be expensive.

It is also a good idea to have a list of emergency contact numbers, including the embassy or consulate of your home country, as well as a list of local healthcare providers and hospitals. In addition, it is recommended to have a basic first aid kit and to know how to provide basic first aid, such as CPR and wound care. By being prepared and knowing what to do in case of an emergency, you can help ensure that you receive the best possible care and stay safe while in the Philippines.
