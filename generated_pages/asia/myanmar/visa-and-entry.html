Visa Policy Overview

Myanmar has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. The country has been opening up to tourism and foreign investment in recent years, but still maintains a relatively strict stance on immigration. Citizens of around 10 countries, including Japan, South Korea, and some Southeast Asian nations, enjoy visa-free access to Myanmar for a limited period. However, most nationalities require a visa to enter the country.

The Myanmar government has introduced an e-visa system, which allows travelers to apply for a visa online and receive an electronic visa approval letter. This has streamlined the application process and made it easier for tourists and business travelers to visit the country. However, it's essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it's always best to check with the official embassy or consulate of Myanmar for the latest information.

The main airports in Myanmar are Yangon International Airport, Mandalay International Airport, and Naypyidaw International Airport. Land borders are also open to tourists, with the most popular border crossings at Tachileik, Myawaddy, and Kawthaung. When arriving at immigration, travelers must present their passport, visa (if required), and completed arrival/departure card. They will also be required to provide fingerprints and undergo a brief interview with immigration officials.
Upon arrival, travelers can exchange currency, purchase a SIM card, and arrange for transportation to their destination. It's essential to note that immigration procedures can be slow, especially during peak travel seasons, so it's best to arrive prepared and patient. Travelers should also ensure that they have all the necessary documents and information to avoid any issues or delays during the immigration process.
- Not applying for an e-visa in advance, resulting in delays or rejection
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not providing proof of onward travel or return ticket
- Not having sufficient funds (at least $300 per person)
- Not meeting the specific requirements for business, student, or work visas
- Not arranging for transportation from the airport or border crossing in advance, resulting in higher costs or inconvenience
