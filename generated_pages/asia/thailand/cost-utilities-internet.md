# Utility & Internet Costs in Thailand

The cost of utilities and internet in Thailand can range from $50 to $150 per month for a small to medium-sized apartment, depending on usage and provider.

[FACTBOX]
Monthly Utilities (85 sqm apt): ($100-$120 USD)
Internet (60 Mbps): ($15-$25 USD/month)
Mobile Data (10GB): ($5-$10 USD/month)
Electricity Cost: ($0.15 per kWh)
Currency: THB
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Thailand has a well-developed utility infrastructure, with widespread access to electricity, water, and gas. The cost of utilities in Thailand is generally stable, with some seasonal variation. For example, electricity costs may be higher during the hot summer months when air conditioning usage is higher. The cost of utilities can also vary depending on the location, with urban areas tend to have higher costs than rural areas.

The quality of utility infrastructure in Thailand is generally good, with most areas having access to reliable electricity and water supply. However, there may be some power outages or water disruptions during heavy rainfall or other extreme weather conditions. The cost of utilities is also affected by the country's inflation rate, which is currently at 1.4% per annum. This means that the cost of utilities may increase over time, but the increase is likely to be gradual.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities in Thailand, you will typically need to provide identification and proof of address to the service provider.
- Prepaid electricity meters are available in some areas, which can help you save money by only paying for what you use.
- AIS and True are two of the largest and most reliable internet service providers in Thailand, offering a range of plans and speeds.
- To save on utility bills, consider using energy-efficient appliances and turning off lights and electronics when not in use.
- It's also a good idea to compare prices and plans from different providers to find the best deal for your needs and budget.
