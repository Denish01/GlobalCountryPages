[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
Thailand's internet infrastructure has improved significantly over the years, with **high-speed internet** available in most parts of the country. The urban areas, such as Bangkok and Chiang Mai, have excellent internet coverage with fast speeds and reliable connections. However, the rural areas still lag behind, with slower speeds and less reliable connections. Despite this, the overall internet quality in Thailand is good, with most users having access to **4G networks** and **fiber-optic connections**. The internet in Thailand is generally reliable, with minimal downtime and fast speeds, making it suitable for streaming, online gaming, and other bandwidth-intensive activities.

The Thai government has made significant investments in improving the country's internet infrastructure, with a focus on expanding **fiber-optic networks** and improving **mobile network coverage**. This has resulted in a significant increase in internet speeds and coverage, making Thailand one of the best-connected countries in Southeast Asia. However, the internet in Thailand is not without its challenges, with issues such as **internet censorship** and **blocked websites** still prevalent. Despite these challenges, Thailand remains a popular destination for digital nomads and travelers who need to stay connected.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Thailand is relatively easy and affordable, with several options available for tourists and residents. The main carriers, AIS, True Move, and DTAC, offer a range of **tourist SIM cards** with different data plans and prices. To buy a SIM card, you will need to provide your passport and a photocopy of your visa, if applicable. You can buy SIM cards at the airport, mobile phone shops, or convenience stores. The cost of a tourist SIM card is around 10-15 USD, with data plans starting from 5 USD per week. It is recommended to buy a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds.

- Some social media platforms and online services may be **restricted** or **blocked**.
- Using a **VPN** is recommended to access **blocked websites** and to ensure **online security**.
