# Laos Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Laos before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Laos has a moderate visa policy, allowing citizens of certain countries to enter without a visa, while others can obtain a visa on arrival or must apply in advance. The country offers visa-free access to citizens of several countries, including those from the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) and a few other nations. This liberal approach aims to promote tourism and economic development in the country. Currently, citizens of around 15 countries can enter Laos without a visa, while over 150 nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival.

The Lao government has been working to simplify its visa process and encourage more visitors to the country. As a result, the visa application process has become more streamlined, and the introduction of an e-visa system has made it easier for travelers to apply for and obtain a visa. However, it is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it is crucial to check with the official embassy or consulate of Laos for the most up-to-date information before planning a trip.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can typically stay in Laos for up to 30 days, and their visa can be extended for an additional 60 days. The requirements for tourists include a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds. Tourists can also apply for a visa on arrival at certain airports and border crossings.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require an invitation letter from a Lao company or organization, and they must apply for a business visa in advance. The requirements for business travelers include a valid passport, a completed application form, and supporting documents such as a letter of introduction and proof of business activities.
**Students:** Students who plan to study in Laos must apply for a student visa and provide proof of enrollment at a recognized institution. The requirements for students include a valid passport, a completed application form, and supporting documents such as a letter of acceptance and proof of financial support.
**Workers:** Workers who plan to work in Laos must apply for a work visa and provide proof of sponsorship from a Lao employer. The requirements for workers include a valid passport, a completed application form, and supporting documents such as a contract of employment and proof of qualifications.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airports in Laos include Wattay International Airport in Vientiane and Luang Prabang International Airport. The country also has several land border crossings with its neighboring countries, including Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia. When arriving in Laos, travelers must go through immigration and customs, where they will need to present their passport, visa (if required), and completed arrival/departure card. The immigration process typically takes around 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the time of day and the number of travelers.

Travelers can also enter Laos by boat or bus, and the entry procedures are similar to those at airports. However, it is essential to note that not all border crossings offer visa on arrival, so it is crucial to check the requirements in advance. Additionally, travelers should ensure that they have all the necessary documents, including a valid passport, visa (if required), and proof of onward travel.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not checking the validity of the passport before traveling
- Not having the required documents, such as proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
- Not applying for a visa in advance, when required
- Not having a return ticket or onward ticket
- Not declaring goods or currency at customs
- Not keeping a copy of the visa application fee receipt
