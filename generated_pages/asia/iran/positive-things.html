Cultural Treasures

  • The Persepolis archaeological site, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a testament to Iran's rich cultural heritage.
  • The Taq-e Bostan monument, a collection of ancient rock reliefs, showcases the country's historical significance.
  • The Persian Carpet is an iconic symbol of Iranian art and craftsmanship, with intricate designs and patterns.
  • The Nowruz celebration, a traditional Persian New Year festival, is a vibrant and colorful expression of Iranian culture.
  • The Golestan Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a stunning example of Iranian architecture and design.

People & Global Impact

  • Shirin Ebadi, an Iranian human rights activist, was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2003 for her efforts to promote democracy and human rights.
  • The Iranian diaspora has made significant contributions to various fields, including science, art, and literature, with notable figures such as Azar Nafisi and Marjane Satrapi.
  • Rumi, a 13th-century Persian poet, has had a profound impact on world literature, with his poetry and teachings still widely studied and admired today.
  • The Baha'i faith, founded in Iran in the 19th century, has spread to become a global religion, with a strong emphasis on unity, equality, and social justice.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Iranian people, who are known for their warm welcomes and generous hosting.
  • The delicious cuisine, which includes popular dishes such as kebabs, stews, and rice.
  • The breathtaking scenery, including the Alborz Mountains, the Caspian Sea, and the deserts of Kavir and Lut.
  • The rich cultural heritage, with numerous historical sites, museums, and festivals to explore.
  • The vibrant cities, such as Tehran, Isfahan, and Shiraz, which offer a unique blend of traditional and modern attractions.
  • The traditional markets, or bazaars, which offer a wide range of goods, including handicrafts, spices, and textiles.

Hidden Gems

  • The Hormoz Island, located in the Persian Gulf, is a stunning example of Iran's natural beauty, with its red beaches and salt caves.
  • The Kandovan village, located in East Azerbaijan, is a unique example of troglodyte architecture, with homes carved into the rock face.
  • The Shushtar Historical Hydraulic System, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an ancient irrigation system that showcases Iran's engineering prowess.
  • The Dasht-e Kavir desert, also known as the Great Salt Desert, is a vast and beautiful expanse of sand and salt, with unique geological formations and wildlife.
