[SECTION] Overview: Iran vs Turkey [/SECTION]
Iran and Turkey are two neighboring countries in the Middle East with distinct economic profiles. Iran has a largely state-controlled economy, while Turkey has a more diversified and open economy. In terms of cost of living, Iran is generally cheaper than Turkey, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. However, Turkey has a more developed tourism infrastructure and a wider range of consumer goods, which can drive up costs for expats and tourists.

The economic context of the two countries is also different, with Iran facing international sanctions and a more isolated economy, while Turkey is a member of the G20 and has a more integrated economy with Europe. Despite these differences, both countries offer a unique cultural and historical experience for visitors and expats, with a rich heritage and natural beauty. Iran's cost of living is around 20-30% lower than Turkey's, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious travelers and expats.
