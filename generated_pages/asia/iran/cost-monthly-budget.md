# Monthly Budget Guide for Iran

To live in Iran, you would need a monthly budget of around $400-$700 for a budget lifestyle, $700-$1,200 for a mid-range lifestyle, and $1,200-$2,000 for a comfortable lifestyle.

[FACTBOX]

Mid-Range Living (monthly): $700-$1,200
Comfortable Living (monthly): $1,200-$2,000
Average Local Salary: $250-$400/month, approximate
Currency: IRR
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Cost of Living Overview [/SECTION]
Iran is a relatively affordable country to live in, with a cost of living that is lower than many of its neighboring countries. The GDP per capita (PPP) of $19,874 indicates a relatively high standard of living, but the cost of living in Iran is still lower than in many other countries in the region. A budget of $400-$700 per month can go a long way in Iran, covering basic expenses such as rent, food, and transportation. However, for a more comfortable lifestyle, a budget of $1,200-$2,000 per month is more realistic. Compared to neighboring countries such as Turkey and Iraq, Iran is relatively affordable, but it is still more expensive than countries such as Pakistan and Afghanistan.

The cost of living in Iran can vary greatly depending on the city and lifestyle. Tehran, the capital city, is the most expensive city in Iran, with high prices for rent, food, and transportation. Other cities, such as Shiraz and Isfahan, are more affordable and offer a lower cost of living. Overall, Iran is a great destination for travelers and expats who are looking for an affordable and culturally rich experience. With a little planning and research, it is possible to live comfortably in Iran on a budget of $1,000-$1,500 per month.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Strategies [/SECTION]
- Learn some basic Persian phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or ride-hailing services
- Cook your meals instead of eating out
- Shop at local markets instead of supermarkets
- Avoid traveling during peak season (June to September)
- Consider sharing an apartment with others to reduce rent costs
- Use a local SIM card or prepaid phone plan to reduce communication costs
- Take advantage of free or low-cost cultural events and attractions
