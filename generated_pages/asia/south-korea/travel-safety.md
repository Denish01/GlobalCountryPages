[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
South Korea is generally a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other countries. The overall safety rating of 4/5 reflects the country's strong law and order, efficient emergency services, and low risk of violent crime. However, as with any foreign travel, it is essential to take normal precautions to ensure your safety, such as being aware of your surroundings, keeping valuables secure, and avoiding walking alone in dimly lit areas at night. The country has a robust tourism infrastructure, with many resources available for visitors, including a dedicated tourist police force and a 24-hour emergency hotline.

The biggest safety concerns for tourists in South Korea are likely to be petty scams, such as pickpocketing or overcharging by taxi drivers, and natural disasters, such as typhoons or earthquakes. However, these risks can be mitigated by taking sensible precautions, such as keeping a close eye on your belongings, using reputable taxi services, and staying informed about weather forecasts. Overall, South Korea is a wonderful and safe destination for travelers, with a unique culture, rich history, and stunning natural beauty to explore.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Pickpocketing** is a common scam in crowded areas, such as markets and public transportation.
- **Overcharging** by taxi drivers can occur, especially for tourists who do not speak Korean.
- **ATM scams** can happen, so it is essential to use reputable ATMs and cover the keypad when entering your PIN.
- **Street food scams** can occur, with some vendors overcharging or selling low-quality food.
- **Crowded areas** can be a target for **scammers** and **thieves**, so it is essential to stay alert and keep valuables secure.
- **Natural disasters**, such as typhoons and earthquakes, can occur, so it is crucial to stay informed about weather forecasts and emergency procedures.
- **Traffic accidents** can happen, especially for pedestrians and cyclists, so it is essential to follow traffic rules and be aware of your surroundings.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to South Korea, but it is recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended for sensitive stomachs.
- **Medical Facilities:** South Korea has high-quality medical facilities, with many hospitals and clinics offering excellent care.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, and many prescription medications can be purchased over-the-counter.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Learn some basic Korean phrases, such as "hello" (annyeonghaseyo) and "thank you" (gamsahamnida).
- Carry a **portable Wi-Fi hotspot** or purchase a local SIM card to stay connected.
- Use reputable **taxi services**, such as those found at official taxi stands or booked through a hotel.
- Keep **valuables secure**, especially in crowded areas, and consider using a money belt or a secure bag.
- Stay **informed about local events** and demonstrations, which can sometimes turn violent.
- Follow **traffic rules** and be aware of your surroundings, especially when crossing the road.
- Try to **avoid traveling alone at night**, especially in rural areas.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- **Dress modestly**, especially when visiting temples or attending cultural events.
- **Be prepared for stares**, as solo women travelers can sometimes attract attention.
- **Use reputable transportation services**, such as licensed taxis or ride-hailing apps.
- **Stay connected** with family and friends back home, and consider sharing your itinerary with them.
