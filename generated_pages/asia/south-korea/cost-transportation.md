[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
South Korea has a well-developed transportation network, with a variety of options available for getting around, including public transportation, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The country's public transportation system is known for its quality and efficiency, with a comprehensive network of buses, subways, and commuter trains. Most people in South Korea rely on public transportation to get around, with the subway being a particularly popular option, especially in major cities like Seoul.

The quality of public transit in South Korea is high, with modern and clean buses and trains, and a convenient payment system using rechargeable cards like T-Money. The country's transportation infrastructure is also well-integrated, making it easy to transfer between different modes of transport. For example, many subway stations have bus terminals and taxi stands nearby, making it easy to switch between modes of transport. Additionally, many cities in South Korea have bike-sharing systems and pedestrian-friendly streets, making it easy to get around without a car.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a rechargeable card like T-Money, which can be used on buses, subways, and taxis.
- Use ride-hailing apps like KakaoTaxi and Naver Taxi to get around, especially at night or in areas with limited public transportation.
- Always negotiate the fare before getting into a taxi, especially if you're not using a meter.
- Be aware of peak hour traffic and plan your route accordingly to avoid congestion.
- Use the subway or bus to get around during the day, as they are often the most convenient and affordable options.
- Consider purchasing a monthly transit pass if you plan to use public transportation frequently, as it can save you money in the long run.
