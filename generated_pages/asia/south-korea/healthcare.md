# South Korea Healthcare — Hospitals, Insurance & Medical Guide

Healthcare in South Korea is a well-organized and modern system that offers high-quality medical services, with a strong emphasis on technology and patient care, making it an attractive destination for medical tourism and a reliable option for expats and locals alike.

[SECTION] Healthcare Overview [/SECTION]
South Korea's healthcare system is a universal system that provides comprehensive coverage to all citizens, with a strong public sector and a growing private sector. The system is structured around the **National Health Insurance** (NHI) program, which is mandatory for all citizens and provides a wide range of medical services, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and prescriptions. The quality of care in South Korea is excellent, with well-trained medical professionals and state-of-the-art facilities, especially in urban areas like Seoul. However, there may be some disparities in access to healthcare services in rural areas, where medical facilities and specialists may be limited.

The public healthcare system in South Korea is well-organized and efficient, with a strong network of hospitals, clinics, and healthcare centers. The private sector is also growing, with many private hospitals and clinics offering high-quality services, often with a focus on specialized care and advanced technology. In general, the quality of care in South Korea is considered to be among the best in Asia, with high patient satisfaction rates and good health outcomes.

Overall, South Korea's healthcare system is well-suited to meet the needs of both locals and expats, with a wide range of medical services available, including traditional Korean medicine and alternative therapies. However, it's still important for visitors and expats to have some knowledge of the system and to take out adequate health insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

[SECTION] Health Insurance [/SECTION]
- **National Health Insurance** (NHI) is mandatory for all citizens and provides comprehensive coverage, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and prescriptions.
- Private health insurance is also available, often with a focus on specialized care and advanced technology, and can be purchased to supplement NHI coverage.
- Expats and foreigners are eligible to join the NHI program, but may also want to consider purchasing private health insurance to cover any additional expenses.
- The cost of health insurance in South Korea is relatively affordable, with premiums starting from around 100,000 KRW per month.
- It's recommended that visitors and expats have some form of health insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses, as medical costs can be high in South Korea.
- Some employers in South Korea may also offer health insurance as part of their employee benefits package.

[SECTION] Pharmacies & Medications [/SECTION]
- Pharmacies are widespread in South Korea, with many locations throughout urban and rural areas.
- A wide range of medications is available, including prescription and over-the-counter drugs, and many pharmacies are open late or 24 hours.
- Prescription requirements are generally similar to those in Western countries, with a doctor's prescription required for most medications.
- Common brands and generic medications are available, often at a lower cost than in Western countries.
- The cost of medications in South Korea is generally affordable, with many medications available for under 10,000 KRW per prescription.

[SECTION] Vaccinations & Health Risks [/SECTION]
- **Hepatitis A** and **Hepatitis B** vaccinations are recommended for all travelers to South Korea.
- **Japanese Encephalitis** vaccination is also recommended for travelers who will be spending time in rural areas.
- **Rabies** vaccination is recommended for travelers who will be spending time around animals.
- South Korea has a low risk of **malaria**, but travelers to rural areas may still want to take precautions.
- The water supply in South Korea is generally safe, but travelers may still want to take precautions when drinking tap water.
- Food safety is also generally good in South Korea, but travelers should still take precautions when eating from street vendors or unfamiliar restaurants.

[SECTION] Medical Tourism [/SECTION]
- South Korea is a popular destination for medical tourism, with many high-quality hospitals and clinics offering specialized care and advanced technology.
- **Cosmetic surgery** and **dental care** are popular procedures among medical tourists, with many clinics offering affordable and high-quality services.
- **Cancer treatment** and **cardiovascular care** are also areas of expertise in South Korea, with many hospitals and clinics offering advanced treatments and therapies.
- The cost of medical care in South Korea can be significantly lower than in Western countries, making it an attractive option for medical tourists.

[SECTION] Emergency Procedures [/SECTION]
- In the event of an emergency, dial 119 to reach the emergency services.
- Ambulance services are reliable and efficient, with many hospitals and clinics having their own emergency departments.
- The nearest hospital or clinic can be found by asking locals or using a mapping app.
- Insurance claims can be filed with the hospital or clinic, and many insurance companies have a 24-hour hotline for emergency assistance.
- It's recommended that visitors and expats carry a copy of their health insurance card and any relevant medical information with them at all times.
