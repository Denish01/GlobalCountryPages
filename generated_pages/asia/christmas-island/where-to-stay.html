To find the best place to stay in Christmas Island, consider the island's limited but unique accommodation options, which cater to various types of travelers, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious resorts.

Best Areas to Stay

For a small island like Christmas Island, the best areas to stay are limited, but each has its own charm and advantages. Here are the top areas to consider:

The Settlement (Flying Fish Cove)

Best For: Families / Business

Vibe: The Settlement is the main hub of Christmas Island, offering a range of amenities, including shops, restaurants, and a post office, with a laid-back and welcoming atmosphere. The area is surrounded by lush tropical forests and stunning coastline views.

Price Range: $150-300/night

Pros: Convenient location, access to amenities, stunning views

Cons: Limited nightlife options

Kampong (Flying Fish Cove)

Best For: Backpackers / Budget travelers

Vibe: Kampong is a small village area with a mix of old and new houses, offering a glimpse into the island's unique culture and history. The area is quiet and peaceful, with a strong sense of community.

Price Range: $80-150/night

Pros: Affordable, cultural experience, friendly locals

Cons: Limited amenities

Drummond Cove (Flying Fish Cove)

Best For: Luxury / Couples

Vibe: Drummond Cove is a picturesque coastal area with stunning views of the Indian Ocean, offering a romantic and relaxing atmosphere. The area is surrounded by lush tropical forests and features a beautiful beach.

Price Range: $250-400/night

Pros: Breathtaking views, luxurious amenities, secluded beach

Cons: Limited dining options

Poon Saan (Flying Fish Cove)

Best For: Families / Nature lovers

Vibe: Poon Saan is a scenic area with stunning views of the surrounding landscape, offering a peaceful and natural atmosphere. The area is surrounded by lush tropical forests and features several walking trails.

Price Range: $150-250/night

Pros: Stunning views, natural surroundings, peaceful atmosphere

Cons: Limited amenities

Flying Fish Cove (Flying Fish Cove)

Best For: Business / Luxury

Vibe: Flying Fish Cove is the main town area of Christmas Island, offering a range of amenities, including restaurants, shops, and a harbor, with a bustling and vibrant atmosphere. The area is surrounded by stunning coastline views and features a beautiful beach.

Price Range: $200-350/night

Pros: Convenient location, luxurious amenities, stunning views

Cons: Limited nightlife options

Booking Tips

  • Book in advance, especially during peak season (December to January)
  • Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Expedia, for a wide range of options
  • Consider negotiating prices for long-term stays or off-season bookings
  • Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during peak season
  • Check for package deals, including accommodation and activities
  • Read reviews and check the cancellation policies before booking
  • Contact local tourism operators for customized booking options

Safety & Location Tips

  • Be aware of the island's remote location and limited medical facilities
  • Take necessary precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas
  • Keep valuables secure, as petty theft can occur
  • Stay informed about weather conditions, especially during the monsoon season
  • Consider renting a car or scooter to explore the island, as public transportation is limited
