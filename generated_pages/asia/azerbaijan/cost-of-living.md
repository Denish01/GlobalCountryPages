[SECTION] How Expensive Is Azerbaijan? [/SECTION]
Azerbaijan is considered an affordable country to visit or live in, with a cost of living that is lower than many of its European and Asian neighbors. The country's economy has been growing rapidly in recent years, driven by its large oil and gas reserves, but this has not yet led to a significant increase in prices. In general, Azerbaijan offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. Compared to its neighbors, Azerbaijan is more expensive than Georgia and Armenia, but cheaper than Russia and Turkey.

The cost of living in Azerbaijan can vary depending on the location, with the capital city Baku being the most expensive. However, even in Baku, prices are generally lower than in many other major cities around the world. Outside of Baku, the cost of living is significantly lower, making it an attractive option for those looking to experience the country's culture and natural beauty without breaking the bank. Overall, Azerbaijan offers a unique blend of affordability, culture, and natural beauty, making it a great destination for travelers and expats.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying outside of Baku to save on accommodation costs
- Eat at local restaurants and try traditional Azerbaijani cuisine, which is often cheaper and more delicious than Western-style food
- Use public transport instead of taxis to get around
- Avoid traveling during peak season (June to August) to save on accommodation and tour prices
- Learn some basic Azerbaijani phrases, such as "merhaba" (hello) and "təşəkkür" (thank you), to show respect for the local culture and get better prices
- Haggling is expected at markets and bazaars, so be prepared to negotiate prices to get the best deal
