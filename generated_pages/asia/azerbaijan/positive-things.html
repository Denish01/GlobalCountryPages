Cultural Treasures

  • The Walled City of Baku, a UNESCO World Heritage site, features a unique blend of medieval and modern architecture.
  • The Azerbaijan Carpet Museum showcases the country's rich tradition of carpet-weaving, with a collection of over 10,000 carpets.
  • The Mugham Festival, held annually in Baku, celebrates Azerbaijan's traditional music and dance.
  • The Sheki Khan's Palace, a 18th-century palace in the city of Sheki, features stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • The Gobustan National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is home to over 6,000 ancient rock carvings and petroglyphs.

People & Global Impact

  • Nasimi, a 14th-century poet, is considered one of the greatest poets in Azerbaijani literature, and his works have been translated into many languages.
  • The Azerbaijani diaspora has made significant contributions to the fields of science, art, and politics, with notable figures including Mammad Amin Rasulzade, a key figure in the country's independence movement.
  • Heydar Aliyev, the former president of Azerbaijan, played a key role in the country's post-Soviet development and was a prominent figure in regional politics.
  • The Azerbaijani people place a strong emphasis on hospitality, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome they receive in the country.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Azerbaijani people, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome they receive.
  • The delicious cuisine, which features a mix of traditional dishes such as plov and kebabs, as well as modern fusion restaurants.
  • The stunning scenery, with the country's diverse landscape featuring everything from snow-capped mountains to deserts and coastal beaches.
  • The rich cultural heritage, with numerous museums, historical sites, and festivals to explore.
  • The vibrant city life, with Baku offering a range of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • The opportunities for outdoor adventure, with activities such as hiking, skiing, and rock climbing available in the country's beautiful national parks.

Hidden Gems

  • The Lahij village, a small village in the mountains, is known for its traditional crafts and stunning scenery, and is a great place to experience rural Azerbaijani life.
  • The Qobustan mud volcanoes, a unique natural phenomenon, are a must-see for anyone interested in geology or the natural world.
  • The Shirvan National Park, a protected area of steppe and semi-desert, is home to a wide range of wildlife, including gazelles and wolves.
  • The Nakhchivan region, a autonomous republic located in the southwest of the country, is home to a unique cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty, with attractions such as the Momuna Khatun Mausoleum and the Yusuf ibn Kuseyir Mausoleum.
