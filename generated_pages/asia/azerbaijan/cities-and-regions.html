How Azerbaijan Is Divided

Azerbaijan is divided into several administrative divisions, including 66 rayons, 11 cities, and 1 autonomous republic, known as the Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic. The country is further divided into 10 economic regions, which were established to promote economic development and investment in different parts of the country. The administrative structure of Azerbaijan is designed to provide a high level of autonomy to local governments, allowing them to manage their own affairs and make decisions that are in the best interests of their communities.

The division of Azerbaijan into different regions and cities is based on a combination of geographical, economic, and cultural factors. The country's mountainous terrain, for example, has played a significant role in shaping the development of its regions, with many cities and towns located in valleys and low-lying areas. The economy of Azerbaijan is also an important factor, with the oil and gas industry playing a major role in the development of the country's coastal regions.

Regional Differences

  • The regions of Azerbaijan differ significantly in terms of their geography, economy, and culture, with the coastal regions being more urban and industrial, while the mountainous regions are more rural and traditional.
  • The economy of Azerbaijan is also a major factor in regional differences, with the oil and gas industry playing a dominant role in the coastal regions, while agriculture and trade are more important in the rural areas.
  • The climate of Azerbaijan also varies significantly from region to region, with the coastal regions having a more temperate climate, while the mountainous regions are colder and more prone to snow.
  • The language and culture of Azerbaijan also differ from region to region, with the Azerbaijani language being spoken throughout the country, but with different dialects and cultural traditions in different regions.

Getting Around

Azerbaijan has a well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis available. The country's main airport is located in Baku, and there are also several smaller airports in other cities, including Ganja and Nakhchivan. The train network in Azerbaijan is also well-developed, with regular services connecting Baku to other major cities, including Ganja, Sumgait, and Lankaran. Buses and taxis are also widely available, and are a popular mode of transportation for both locals and tourists. The road network in Azerbaijan is generally in good condition, making it easy to get around the country by car. However, driving in Azerbaijan can be challenging, especially in the mountainous regions, where the roads are often narrow and winding.
