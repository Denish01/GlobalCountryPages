# Moving to Azerbaijan — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Azerbaijan, you will need to navigate the country's visa and residency options, understand the cost of living and culture, and prepare for the challenges of relocating to a new country with a unique blend of Eastern European and Middle Eastern influences.

[SECTION] Why People Move to Azerbaijan [/SECTION]
Azerbaijan has become an attractive destination for expats in recent years, drawn by its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and growing economy. The country offers a unique blend of traditional and modern culture, with a mix of Soviet-era architecture and modern skyscrapers in the capital city of Baku. Many expats are also attracted by the country's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, making it an important hub for trade and commerce. The expat community in Azerbaijan is relatively small, but growing, with many opportunities for socializing and networking.

The quality of life in Azerbaijan is generally good, with a low cost of living compared to other European countries. The country has a well-developed infrastructure, with modern amenities and services available in Baku and other major cities. However, the pace of life can be slow, and the bureaucracy can be challenging to navigate. Expats who are flexible, patient, and willing to learn about the local culture will find that Azerbaijan has a lot to offer.

Azerbaijan is a popular destination for **oil and gas industry** professionals, as well as those working in **finance**, **construction**, and **education**. The country is also a popular destination for **digital nomads**, with a growing number of co-working spaces and startup incubators. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and growing economy, Azerbaijan is an attractive destination for expats looking for a new adventure.

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- Opening a bank account in Azerbaijan can be challenging, but many banks offer services for expats
- Money transfers can be made through banks or online services such as **PayPal** or **TransferWise**
- The local currency is the **Azerbaijani manat (AZN)**, but US dollars and euros are widely accepted
- Credit cards are accepted in major cities, but cash is still the preferred method of payment
- Taxes in Azerbaijan are relatively low, with a flat rate of 14% for individuals

[SECTION] Challenges & Honest Advice [/SECTION]
- **Culture shock** can be a challenge for expats, especially those from Western countries
- **Bureaucracy** can be slow and challenging to navigate
- **Language barriers** can be a problem, as not many people speak English outside of Baku
- **Traffic** in Baku can be chaotic, and driving can be challenging
- **Air pollution** can be a problem in Baku, especially in the winter months
- Be prepared to be **patient** and **flexible**, and to **learn about the local culture** and customs.
