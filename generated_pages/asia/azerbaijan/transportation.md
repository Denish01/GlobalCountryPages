# Getting Around Azerbaijan — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Azerbaijan, you can use a variety of transportation options including taxis, buses, trains, and domestic flights, as well as rent a car or use ride-hailing apps to navigate the country's roads and cities.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Azerbaijan has several international airports, with the main one being Heydar Aliyev International Airport (GYD) located in Baku, the capital city. This airport receives direct flights from major hubs such as Istanbul, Dubai, and Moscow, making it a convenient entry point for travelers. Additionally, there are also international airports in Ganja and Nakhchivan, which receive flights from neighboring countries. Overland entry points are also available, with border crossings open to travelers from Russia, Georgia, Armenia, Iran, and Turkey, although some of these borders may have restrictions or requirements for special permits.

For those planning to enter Azerbaijan by land, it's essential to research the current border crossing requirements and any necessary documentation, such as visas or permits. The main overland entry points include the border crossings with Georgia, which is a popular route for travelers coming from Europe, and the border with Iran, which is a common entry point for those traveling from the Middle East or Central Asia. The border with Armenia is currently closed due to ongoing conflicts, and the border with Russia is open but may require special permits.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Azerbaijan has a moderate domestic air network, with flights connecting Baku to other major cities such as Ganja, Nakhchivan, and Lankaran. The main domestic airline is Azerbaijan Airlines, which operates regular flights to these destinations. Booking domestic flights can be done online or through a local travel agency, and prices are generally reasonable, with a one-way ticket costing around $50-$100. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, to ensure availability and get the best prices.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Azerbaijan's rail network is moderate, with trains connecting Baku to other major cities such as Ganja, Sumgait, and Lankaran. The trains are generally comfortable and reliable, with both daytime and overnight options available. The booking process can be done online or at the train station, and prices are generally affordable, with a one-way ticket costing around $10-$30. There are also high-speed train options available, such as the Baku-Subway train, which connects the capital city to the surrounding suburbs.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Azerbaijan has an extensive network of intercity buses and coaches, which connect major cities and towns. The buses are generally comfortable and affordable, with prices starting from around $5-$10 for a one-way ticket. The main bus companies include BakuBus and Azerbaijan Bus, which operate regular services to destinations such as Ganja, Sumgait, and Sheki. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, to ensure availability and get the best prices.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Azerbaijan, you'll need to have a valid driver's license from your home country, as well as a valid passport and car insurance.
- The road conditions in Azerbaijan can be challenging, especially outside of the major cities, with potholes and uneven surfaces common.
- Fuel costs are generally affordable, with a liter of gasoline costing around $0.50-$0.70.
- Car rental companies are available in Baku and other major cities, with prices starting from around $40-$60 per day.
- When driving in Azerbaijan, be aware of the local traffic laws and regulations, including the requirement to wear a seatbelt and use headlights during the day.
- Be cautious of aggressive drivers and pedestrians, especially in urban areas, and consider hiring a local driver or guide if you're not comfortable driving yourself.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When taking a taxi, make sure to agree on the price before you start your journey, or ensure the meter is turned on.
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots, and keep an eye on your belongings.
- Download the Bolt or Yandex.Taxi app to book a ride-hailing service, which can be a convenient and affordable option.
- Consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot to stay connected and navigate the country.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when traveling to rural areas or interacting with locals.
- Learn some basic Azerbaijani phrases, such as "hello" (salam) and "thank you" (təşəkkür), to show respect and appreciation for the local culture.
- Be prepared for occasional power outages or internet disruptions, especially outside of the major cities, and have a backup plan in place.
