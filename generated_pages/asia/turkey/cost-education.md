# Education Costs in Turkey

The cost of education in Turkey varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international universities can range from $1,000 to $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Turkey is divided into public and private institutions, with the public system being generally considered to be of high quality, but often overcrowded. The language of instruction is **Turkish**, and students are required to attend school for 12 years, starting from the age of 6. The public education system is free, but private schools and universities can be expensive. There are also international schools available in major cities like Istanbul and Ankara, which offer a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **British A-Levels**.

The quality of education in Turkey is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on **science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM)** subjects. However, the education system can be highly competitive, and students are often required to take additional **private tutoring** classes outside of school to prepare for exams. The government has also implemented various initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of **online learning platforms** and **digital textbooks**.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Tips [/SECTION]
- The **best schools for expats** are often international schools, which offer a range of curricula and languages.
- **Scholarships** are available for high-achieving students, both local and international, to study at Turkish universities.
- **Online learning options** are becoming increasingly popular, with many universities and institutions offering **distance learning programs**.
- The education system in Turkey is highly competitive, and students often require **private tutoring** to prepare for exams.
- **Language barriers** can be a challenge for international students, but many universities offer **language support services**.
- **Research opportunities** are available at top-ranked universities, with many institutions having **partnerships with international companies**.
