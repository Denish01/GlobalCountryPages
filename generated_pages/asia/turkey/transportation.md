# Getting Around Turkey — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Turkey, you can use a combination of domestic flights, trains, buses, and city transportation options, as well as rent a car or hire a taxi, depending on your budget and preferred mode of travel.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Turkey has several international airports, with Istanbul Airport (IST) being the main hub, offering direct flights to many destinations around the world. Other major airports include Sabiha Gökçen International Airport (SAW) in Istanbul, Ankara Esenboğa Airport (ESB) in the capital city, and Antalya Airport (AYT) in the southern resort town of Antalya. You can also enter Turkey by land from its neighboring countries, including Greece, Bulgaria, Georgia, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Iran, Iraq, and Syria, with several border crossings available.

If you prefer to travel by land, you can take a bus or drive from Europe or the Middle East, with several bus companies operating international routes. Alternatively, you can take a ferry from Greece or other nearby islands to reach Turkey's coastal towns. The country's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe and Asia makes it an important transportation hub, with many options available for travelers.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Turkey has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights between major cities and smaller towns. The main domestic airlines include Turkish Airlines, Pegasus Airlines, and SunExpress, offering frequent flights to destinations such as Istanbul, Ankara, Antalya, and Izmir. You can book flights online or through a travel agency, with prices varying depending on the route and time of year. A one-way ticket can cost anywhere from $20 to $100, depending on the distance and demand.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Turkey's rail network is moderate, with several train routes connecting major cities, including the capital Ankara, Istanbul, and Izmir. The country has invested in high-speed rail, with the Ankara-Istanbul line being one of the most popular routes. You can book train tickets online or at the station, with prices starting from around $10 for a one-way ticket. The train network is operated by the Turkish State Railways (TCDD), with several classes of service available, including high-speed and sleeper trains.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Turkey has an extensive network of intercity buses, with several companies operating routes between major cities and smaller towns. The main bus companies include Metro Turizm, Pamukkale Turizm, and Varan Turizm, offering comfortable and affordable travel options. You can book tickets online or at the bus station, with prices starting from around $10 for a one-way ticket. The bus network is a popular way to travel, especially for shorter distances, with frequent departures and a wide range of routes available.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Turkey, you need a valid international driving license, which can be obtained in your home country or rented with a car.
- The road quality in Turkey is generally good, with several highways and motorways connecting major cities.
- Fuel costs are relatively high, with gasoline and diesel prices around $1.50-2 per liter.
- Car rental companies are widely available, with prices starting from around $40 per day for a compact car.
- When driving in Turkey, be aware of the local traffic rules and customs, such as driving on the right-hand side of the road and using headlights during the day.
- Be cautious of aggressive drivers and pedestrians, especially in urban areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Always agree on the price before taking a taxi, and make sure the meter is turned on.
- Be cautious of scams, especially in tourist areas, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
- Download the **Istanbulkart** app for easy travel on public transportation in Istanbul.
- Use a reputable bus company for intercity travel, and book tickets in advance to avoid sold-out situations.
- Consider purchasing a **Turkey SIM card** for data and calls, as it can be more affordable than roaming charges.
- Be respectful of local customs and dress modestly when traveling to rural areas or visiting mosques.
- Learn some basic **Turkish phrases**, such as "merhaba" (hello) and "teşekkür" (thank you), to show respect and appreciation for the local culture.
