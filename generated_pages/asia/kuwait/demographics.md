[SECTION] Population Overview [/SECTION]
Kuwait's population has been steadily increasing over the years, with a growth rate of approximately 1.8% per annum. The country's population size is relatively small compared to its neighbors, but its strategic location and rich oil reserves have made it an attractive destination for expatriates and migrant workers. As a result, Kuwait has one of the highest population densities in the world, with around 273 people per square kilometer. The majority of the population resides in urban areas, with around 98% of the population living in cities. This has led to the development of modern infrastructure and amenities, making Kuwait an attractive place to live and work.

The population growth in Kuwait is largely driven by the influx of expatriates, who come to the country to work in various sectors, including oil and gas, construction, and services. The government has implemented various policies to manage the population growth, including stricter visa requirements and regulations for migrant workers. Despite these efforts, the population is expected to continue growing, driven by the country's strong economy and high standard of living.

Kuwait's population is also characterized by a high life expectancy, with an average life expectancy of around 77.6 years. This is due to the country's well-developed healthcare system and high standard of living. The fertility rate in Kuwait is around 2.1 children per woman, which is relatively low compared to other countries in the region. This is due to the increasing education and career aspirations of women, as well as the availability of family planning services.
