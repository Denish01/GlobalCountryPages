[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Kuwait is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, which is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 14, is free for all Kuwaiti citizens and provides a basic education in subjects such as **Arabic**, **mathematics**, and **science**. Secondary education, which is also compulsory, lasts for three years and provides students with a more specialized education in areas such as **humanities**, **science**, and **technology**. Tertiary education is provided by a number of universities and colleges, both public and private, and offers a wide range of academic programs in fields such as **business**, **engineering**, and **medicine**.

The quality of education in Kuwait is generally high, with a strong emphasis on **academic achievement** and **research**. The country has a well-developed public education system, with a large number of schools and universities that offer free or low-cost education to Kuwaiti citizens. However, the private education sector is also well-established, with a number of international schools and universities that offer high-quality education to students from around the world.

In recent years, the Kuwaiti government has implemented a number of reforms aimed at improving the quality and accessibility of education in the country. These reforms have included the introduction of new **curricula** and **teaching methods**, as well as investments in **educational infrastructure** and **technology**. As a result, the education system in Kuwait is now more diverse and dynamic than ever before, with a wide range of opportunities available to students of all backgrounds and interests.
