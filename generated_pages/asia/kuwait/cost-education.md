# Education Costs in Kuwait

The cost of education in Kuwait varies depending on the type of school and institution, with public education being generally free or subsidized for Kuwaiti citizens, while private and international schools can range from $5,000 to $20,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Kuwait is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being free or subsidized for Kuwaiti citizens. The **Ministry of Education** oversees the public education system, which consists of primary, intermediate, and secondary schools. The language of instruction is **Arabic**, but English is also widely taught as a second language. The public education system is generally considered to be of high quality, with a strong emphasis on science, mathematics, and technology. However, private schools and international schools are also available, offering a range of curricula and languages of instruction, including **English**, **French**, and **German**.

Private education in Kuwait is generally considered to be of higher quality than public education, with smaller class sizes and more resources available. However, it is also more expensive, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $20,000 per year. International schools are also available, offering a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **British A-levels**. These schools are popular with expats and Kuwaiti nationals who want to provide their children with a high-quality, international education.
