[SECTION] Overview: Kuwait vs Saudi Arabia [/SECTION]
Kuwait and Saudi Arabia are two neighboring countries in the Middle East with distinct economic profiles. Kuwait has a high-income economy, driven by its large oil reserves, while Saudi Arabia has a more diversified economy, with a growing non-oil sector. In terms of cost of living, Kuwait is generally more expensive than Saudi Arabia, particularly when it comes to housing and transportation. The cost of living in Kuwait is around 15-20% higher than in Saudi Arabia, making Saudi Arabia a more attractive option for expats and travelers on a budget.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Kuwait has a relatively small population and a high per capita income, which drives up the cost of living. In contrast, Saudi Arabia has a larger population and a more diverse economy, which helps to keep costs lower. Overall, while both countries offer a high standard of living, Saudi Arabia is generally the more affordable option.
