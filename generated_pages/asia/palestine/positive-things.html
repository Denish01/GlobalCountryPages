Cultural Treasures

  • The Church of the Nativity in Bethlehem is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the oldest continuously operating churches in the world.
  • The ancient city of Jericho is a significant archaeological site, with evidence of human habitation dating back over 10,000 years.
  • Palestinian embroidery is a traditional art form that reflects the country's rich cultural heritage and is often used to adorn traditional clothing and textiles.
  • The Hebron Glass Factory is a historic factory that produces handmade glass products, including intricate ornaments and decorative items.
  • The Palestinian Museum in Birzeit showcases the history and culture of Palestine, with a collection of artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays.

People & Global Impact

  • Edward Said was a prominent Palestinian-American scholar and cultural critic who made significant contributions to the fields of literature, music, and politics.
  • The Palestinian diaspora has made significant contributions to the global community, with many Palestinians achieving success in fields such as medicine, science, and the arts.
  • Hanadi Jaradat is a Palestinian woman who became the first female pilot in the Middle East, inspiring a new generation of women to pursue careers in aviation.
  • The Palestinian community has a strong tradition of hospitality and generosity, with many families welcoming visitors and strangers into their homes.

What Visitors Love

  • The scenic landscapes of Palestine, including the Jordan Valley and the hills of the West Bank, offer breathtaking views and opportunities for hiking and exploration.
  • Palestinian cuisine is a unique blend of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern flavors, with popular dishes including maqluba, musakhan, and kunafeh.
  • The historic city of Jerusalem is a major draw for tourists, with its rich history, cultural landmarks, and spiritual significance.
  • The warm hospitality of the Palestinian people is often cited as a highlight of visiting Palestine, with many visitors forming lasting connections with their hosts.
  • The vibrant markets of Palestine, such as the Old City Market in Hebron, offer a wide range of traditional goods, including handicrafts, spices, and textiles.
  • The beautiful beaches of the Gaza Strip, such as Al-Shuja'iyya Beach, provide a relaxing and scenic destination for visitors.

Hidden Gems

  • The Wadi Qelt nature reserve is a hidden gem, with its stunning scenery, hiking trails, and historic sites, including the St. George's Monastery.
  • The Palestinian village of Battir is a UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its ancient terracing system and traditional farming practices.
  • The Hebron Rehabilitation Committee is a local organization that works to preserve and restore the historic city of Hebron, including its ancient buildings and cultural landmarks.
  • The Palestine Marathon is an annual event that takes place in Bethlehem, promoting sports, health, and community development in Palestine.
