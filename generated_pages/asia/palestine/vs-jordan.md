# Palestine vs Jordan

When deciding between Palestine and Jordan, consider your priorities, as both destinations offer unique cultural, historical, and natural experiences. 

[SECTION] Why People Compare Them [/SECTION]
People often compare Palestine and Jordan due to their geographical proximity and shared cultural heritage. Both countries are located in the Middle East, with Jordan bordering Palestine to the east, and they share a rich history, with many historical sites and landmarks, such as the ancient city of Petra in Jordan and the Church of the Nativity in Bethlehem, Palestine.

The comparison between Palestine and Jordan is also driven by the fact that many travelers visit both countries as part of a larger Middle Eastern itinerary, taking advantage of their close proximity to explore the region's diverse culture, history, and natural beauty. Additionally, both countries have a significant tourism industry, with Palestine attracting visitors interested in its unique cultural and historical heritage, and Jordan drawing travelers with its stunning natural landscapes, including the Dead Sea and Wadi Rum.

[SECTION] Key Differences [/SECTION]
- **Population**: Palestine has a significantly smaller population than Jordan, with 5.4 million people compared to Jordan's 10.2 million.
- **Size**: Jordan is much larger than Palestine, with a total area of 89,342 km² compared to Palestine's 6,220 km².
- **Language**: While Arabic is the primary language spoken in both countries, English is more widely spoken in Jordan, making it easier for tourists to communicate.
- **Currency**: Palestine uses the Israeli Shekel (ILS), while Jordan has its own currency, the Jordanian Dinar (JOD).
- **Climate**: Palestine has a Mediterranean climate, while Jordan has a mix of desert and Mediterranean climates, with very hot summers and mild winters.
- **Safety**: Jordan is generally considered a safer destination than Palestine, with lower crime rates and a more stable political environment.

[SECTION] Key Similarities [/SECTION]
- **History**: Both Palestine and Jordan have a rich cultural and historical heritage, with many ancient sites and landmarks, such as the Roman ruins of Jerash in Jordan and the ancient city of Hebron in Palestine.
- **Cuisine**: The cuisine in both countries is similar, with popular dishes like falafel, shawarma, and mansaf, which are often served with rice, vegetables, and flatbread.
- **Hospitality**: Both Palestinians and Jordanians are known for their warm hospitality, with visitors often being greeted with traditional Arabic coffee and sweet treats.
- **Geography**: Both countries have a mix of mountainous and desert landscapes, with the Jordan River forming a natural border between Palestine and Jordan.
