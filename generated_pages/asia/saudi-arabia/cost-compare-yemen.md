[SECTION] Overview: Saudi Arabia vs Yemen [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Saudi Arabia and Yemen varies significantly, with Saudi Arabia being one of the wealthiest countries in the Middle East and Yemen being one of the poorest. Saudi Arabia has a high standard of living, with modern amenities and a strong economy, while Yemen has been affected by conflict and economic instability, leading to a lower cost of living. Overall, Yemen is generally cheaper than Saudi Arabia, with a cost of living that is approximately 30-40% lower. This is due to lower prices for housing, food, and transportation in Yemen.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in the cost of living. Saudi Arabia has a strong economy driven by oil exports, while Yemen's economy has been affected by conflict and instability. As a result, Saudi Arabia has a higher GDP per capita and a lower inflation rate than Yemen. However, despite the higher cost of living in Saudi Arabia, the country offers a high standard of living and modern amenities, making it an attractive destination for expats and tourists. In contrast, Yemen's lower cost of living may be offset by the challenges of living in a country affected by conflict and instability.
