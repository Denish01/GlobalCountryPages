Cultural Treasures

  • The At-Turaif District in Diriyah is a UNESCO World Heritage site and the historic capital of the Saudi state.
  • The Hejaz Railway is a historic railway that showcases the country's rich transportation history.
  • The Najd Village in Riyadh is a cultural heritage site that preserves traditional Saudi architecture.
  • The Janadriyah Cultural Festival is an annual festival that celebrates Saudi culture, music, and art.
  • The Al-'Ula archaeological site is a historic city that dates back to the 6th century BC and features ancient rock-cut tombs.

People & Global Impact

  • Prince Sultan bin Salman Al-Saud is a Saudi astronaut who became the first Arab and Muslim to travel to space in 1985.
  • Nawal El Moutawakel is a Saudi-born Moroccan athlete who won the first Olympic gold medal for a Muslim woman in 1984.
  • Dr. Hayat Sindi is a Saudi medical scientist who has made significant contributions to the field of biotechnology and has been recognized as one of the most influential women in the Arab world.
  • The Saudi diaspora has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Saudi expatriates holding key positions in international business and finance.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Saudi people is renowned for its warmth and generosity, with visitors often commenting on the welcoming nature of their hosts.
  • The cuisine is a unique blend of Middle Eastern and international flavors, with popular dishes such as kebabs, falafel, and shawarma.
  • The scenery of Saudi Arabia is diverse and breathtaking, with deserts, mountains, and coastlines offering a range of landscapes to explore.
  • The Masmak Fortress in Riyadh is a historic fort that dates back to the 19th century and offers a glimpse into the country's rich history.
  • The Red Sands dunes near Riyadh are a popular destination for desert safaris and offer stunning views of the Arabian Desert.
  • The Farasan Islands in the Red Sea are a protected marine reserve and offer a unique opportunity to see coral reefs and marine life.

Hidden Gems

  • The Edge of the World is a scenic spot near Riyadh that offers breathtaking views of the Acacia Valley and is a popular destination for hiking and camping.
  • The Al-Ahsa Oasis is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the largest oases in the world, featuring ancient forts, mosques, and traditional villages.
  • The Rijal Almaa village is a historic village in the Asir Region that is known for its traditional architecture and stunning mountain scenery.
  • The King Fahd Complex for the Printing of the Holy Quran in Medina is a unique facility that produces millions of copies of the Quran every year and offers guided tours.
