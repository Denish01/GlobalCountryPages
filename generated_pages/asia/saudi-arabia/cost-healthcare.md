# Healthcare Costs in Saudi Arabia

The cost of healthcare in Saudi Arabia varies significantly between public and private facilities, with the health expenditure per capita being around $1,825 and out-of-pocket spend being 14.1% of total healthcare expenditure.

[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
The healthcare system in Saudi Arabia is a mixed system, with both public and private facilities available. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of high quality, with well-equipped hospitals and qualified medical staff. However, the system can be overcrowded and waiting times can be long. In contrast, private healthcare facilities offer a more personalized and efficient service, but at a higher cost. For locals, the public healthcare system is generally the first point of contact, while foreigners often opt for private healthcare facilities. It is recommended that foreigners have health insurance to cover the cost of medical treatment, as the cost of healthcare in Saudi Arabia can be high.

The healthcare system in Saudi Arabia is well-organized, with a network of hospitals, clinics, and medical centers throughout the country. The Ministry of Health is responsible for overseeing the public healthcare system, while private healthcare facilities are regulated by the Saudi Arabian General Investment Authority. In general, the quality of healthcare in Saudi Arabia is high, with many medical facilities equipped with the latest technology and staffed by qualified medical professionals. However, the cost of healthcare can be a significant burden for those without insurance, and many people rely on out-of-pocket payments to cover the cost of medical treatment.

[SECTION] Emergency Care [/SECTION]
In the event of a medical emergency, it is recommended to call the emergency number 997 or visit the nearest hospital. Many hospitals in Saudi Arabia have emergency departments that are equipped to handle a wide range of medical emergencies. For foreigners, it is recommended to visit a private hospital, as they often have staff who speak English and are familiar with international medical protocols. Some of the best hospitals for foreigners in Saudi Arabia include the King Faisal Specialist Hospital in Riyadh and the King Abdulaziz University Hospital in Jeddah. These hospitals offer a high level of care and have a wide range of medical specialties available.

It is also recommended to have a plan in place in case of a medical emergency, including having a list of emergency contact numbers and knowing the location of the nearest hospital. Many health insurance plans also offer emergency evacuation and repatriation services, which can be lifesaving in the event of a serious medical emergency. By being prepared and having access to quality medical care, foreigners can minimize the risk of a medical emergency and ensure that they receive the best possible care if something does happen.
