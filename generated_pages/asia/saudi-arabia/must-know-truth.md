# The Truth About Saudi Arabia - History & Real Facts

[SECTION] Historical Context [/SECTION]
Saudi Arabia, officially known as the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, has a rich and complex history that spans thousands of years. The territory that is now Saudi Arabia was inhabited by various tribes and empires, including the ancient civilizations of Dilmun and Thamud. In the 7th century, the region was unified under the **Islamic Caliphate**, with the city of Medina serving as a major center of Islamic learning and culture. The modern Saudi state was founded in 1932 by **Abdulaziz bin Abdul Rahman**, also known as Ibn Saud, who united the various tribes and regions of the Arabian Peninsula under a single government.

The history of Saudi Arabia is closely tied to the history of **Islam**, with the country serving as the birthplace of the religion and home to the two holiest cities in Islam, Mecca and Medina. The country's early history was marked by a series of **Islamic empires**, including the Umayyad and Abbasid caliphates, which played a significant role in the development of Islamic law, culture, and science. In the 16th century, the **Ottoman Empire** gained control of the region, but the Saudi state was eventually established as an independent entity in the early 20th century.

The discovery of **oil** in 1938 transformed the Saudi economy and had a profound impact on the country's development. The oil industry brought significant wealth and investment to the country, allowing the government to modernize its infrastructure and develop its economy. Today, Saudi Arabia is one of the world's largest oil producers and a major player in the global energy market. The country's **monarchy** has been ruled by the House of Saud since its founding, with the king serving as the head of state and head of government.

The country's history has also been marked by a series of **border disputes** and **regional conflicts**, including the Gulf War and the Yemen Civil War. Despite these challenges, Saudi Arabia has maintained its independence and sovereignty, and has become a major player in regional and global affairs. The country's strategic location at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa has made it an important hub for trade and commerce, and its rich cultural heritage has made it a popular destination for tourists and travelers.
