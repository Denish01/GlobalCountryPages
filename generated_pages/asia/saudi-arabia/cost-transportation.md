[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Saudi Arabia has a well-developed transportation network, with various modes of transport available, including buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit varies between cities, with major cities like Riyadh and Jeddah having more comprehensive networks. Most people in Saudi Arabia rely on private vehicles for transportation, but the use of public transport is becoming increasingly popular, especially among expatriates and locals who prefer not to drive.

The country's transportation infrastructure is constantly being improved, with new projects being implemented to enhance the efficiency and safety of the transport network. The government has also invested heavily in the development of public transport systems, including the construction of new metro lines and bus networks. As a result, getting around Saudi Arabia is becoming easier and more convenient, with a range of options available to suit different budgets and preferences.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Using public transport or ride-hailing services can be more cost-effective than renting a car or taking taxis.
- The **Uber** and **Careem** ride-hailing apps are widely available in Saudi Arabia and can be a convenient option for getting around.
- When taking a taxi, it's a good idea to **negotiate the fare** before you start your journey to avoid any disagreements.
- **Traffic** in Saudi Arabia can be heavy, especially during rush hour, so it's a good idea to plan your journey accordingly.
- The **Saudi Transport App** can be a useful tool for planning your journey and getting around the country.
