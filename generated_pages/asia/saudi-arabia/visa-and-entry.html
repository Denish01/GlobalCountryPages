Visa Policy Overview

Saudi Arabia has a moderate to strict visa policy, with a limited number of countries eligible for visa-free access. Citizens of Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates can enter Saudi Arabia without a visa. However, citizens of other countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, and Australia, require a visa to enter the country. The visa policy is designed to balance the need to control immigration with the desire to promote tourism, business, and cultural exchange.

The Saudi government has introduced various types of visas, including tourist visas, business visas, and work visas, to cater to different categories of visitors. The visa application process typically involves submitting required documents, paying the applicable fee, and waiting for the visa to be processed. The processing time can vary depending on the type of visa and the nationality of the applicant.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists can apply for an eVisa, which is valid for 90 days. The typical stay for tourists is 30 days, but this can be extended for an additional 30 days. Tourists must provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds to support their stay.

Business Travelers: Business travelers require an invitation letter from a Saudi company and must apply for a business visa. The visa is typically valid for 30 days, but can be extended for an additional 30 days. Business travelers must provide proof of their business activities and may be required to attend an interview at the embassy or consulate.

Students: Students require a student visa, which is valid for the duration of their studies. Students must provide proof of enrollment at a Saudi university and may be required to undergo a medical examination.

Workers: Workers require a work visa, which is sponsored by their employer. The visa is typically valid for one year, but can be extended for an additional year. Workers must provide proof of their employment contract and may be required to undergo a medical examination.

Documents Checklist

  1. A valid passport with at least six months' validity
  2. A completed visa application form
  3. A recent passport-sized photograph
  4. Proof of onward travel
  5. Proof of sufficient funds to support your stay
  6. A medical certificate, if required
  7. A police certificate, if required
  8. Proof of enrollment, if applying for a student visa
  9. An invitation letter, if applying for a business visa
  10. A copy of your employment contract, if applying for a work visa

Entry Points & Procedures

The main airports in Saudi Arabia are King Khalid International Airport in Riyadh, King Abdulaziz International Airport in Jeddah, and King Fahd International Airport in Dammam. Visitors can also enter the country through land borders with neighboring countries. Upon arrival, visitors must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their passport, visa, and other supporting documents. The immigration officer will review the documents and may ask questions about the purpose of the visit and the length of stay.

Visitors can expect a relatively straightforward entry procedure, but it is essential to ensure that all required documents are in order to avoid any delays or issues. It is also a good idea to have a copy of your visa and other important documents, in case the originals are requested by the immigration authorities.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Failing to apply for the correct type of visa
  • Not providing all required documents
  • Not having a valid passport with sufficient validity
  • Not having proof of onward travel
  • Not having sufficient funds to support your stay
  • Not registering with the relevant authorities, if required
