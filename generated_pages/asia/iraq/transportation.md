# Getting Around Iraq — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Iraq, you can utilize a range of transportation options including domestic flights, trains, buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services, although the quality and availability of these options can vary greatly depending on the region and current security situation.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Iraq has several international airports, with Baghdad International Airport being the largest and most well-connected, offering direct flights to major hubs in the Middle East, Europe, and Asia. The other major international airports are Basra International Airport in the south and Erbil International Airport in the Kurdistan Region. Direct flight hubs include Istanbul, Dubai, and Amman, making it relatively easy to get to Iraq from other parts of the world. Overland entry points are also available, with border crossings from neighboring countries such as Turkey, Iran, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, and Syria, although these may be subject to certain restrictions and requirements.

Travelers can also enter Iraq by land, with the most common overland entry points being from Turkey, Iran, and Kuwait. However, it is essential to check the current security situation and any travel restrictions before attempting to cross the border by land. Additionally, it is recommended to research and comply with all necessary visa requirements and regulations before entering Iraq. The process of obtaining a visa can be complex and time-consuming, so it is crucial to plan ahead and allow sufficient time for the application process.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Iraq's domestic air network is limited, with only a few airlines operating within the country. Iraqi Airways is the national carrier and offers flights between major cities such as Baghdad, Basra, and Erbil. Other domestic airlines include FlyBaghdad and Kurdistan Airlines. Booking domestic flights can be challenging, and it is recommended to use a local travel agent or book through the airline's website. Approximate costs for domestic flights can range from $100 to $300, depending on the route and airline. It is also important to note that domestic flights may be subject to delays or cancellations due to security concerns or other factors, so it is essential to stay informed and flexible when traveling within Iraq.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Iraq's rail network is limited, with only a few routes available, including the Baghdad-Basra line. The trains are often slow and not very reliable, but they can be a cheap and scenic way to travel. There are no high-speed rail options available in Iraq. Booking train tickets can be challenging, and it is recommended to visit the train station in person or use a local travel agent. The train network is also subject to disruptions and closures due to security concerns, so it is essential to check the current situation before planning a train journey.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus companies operate in Iraq, offering connections between major cities. However, the quality of the buses and the roads can vary greatly, and journeys can be long and uncomfortable. Bus companies such as Al-Mansour and Al-Saqr operate on routes such as Baghdad-Erbil and Baghdad-Basra. Costs can range from $10 to $30, depending on the route and bus company. It is also important to note that bus travel can be affected by security concerns, and it is essential to stay informed and take necessary precautions when traveling by bus in Iraq.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Iraq, you will need to obtain a local driver's license or use an international driving permit.
- Road conditions in Iraq can be poor, with many roads damaged or under construction.
- Fuel costs can be relatively high, with prices ranging from $0.50 to $1 per liter.
- Car rental options are limited, and it is recommended to use a reputable company.
- Driving in Iraq can be hazardous, with many drivers ignoring traffic rules and regulations.
- It is essential to exercise caution when driving in Iraq, especially at night or in areas with poor security.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Always research and comply with the latest travel restrictions and security warnings before traveling to Iraq.
- Use reputable and licensed taxi or ride-hailing services to avoid scams and ensure safety.
- Be prepared for delays and disruptions to transportation services due to security concerns or other factors.
- Use cash instead of credit cards, as many businesses and services may not accept foreign cards.
- Download local apps such as Zain Cash and Asiacell for ride-hailing and other services.
- Stay informed about local conditions and events that may affect transportation, such as protests or demonstrations.
- Consider hiring a local guide or driver to help navigate the transportation system and ensure safety.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions when using public transportation or interacting with locals.
