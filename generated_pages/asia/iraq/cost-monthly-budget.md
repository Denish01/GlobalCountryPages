[SECTION] Budget Tier Lifestyles [/SECTION]
A budget lifestyle in Iraq can be challenging, but it is possible to get by with $400-700 per month. At this level, individuals would need to share an apartment outside of the city center, cook most of their meals, and use public transportation. Entertainment and social activities would be limited, and savings would be minimal. However, it is possible to find affordable options for food, clothing, and other necessities. For example, a meal at a local restaurant can cost around $3-5, and a kilogram of rice can cost around $1-2.

A mid-range lifestyle in Iraq with $800-1,200 per month can provide a relatively comfortable standard of living. At this level, individuals can afford a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, eat out occasionally, and use a mix of public and private transportation. Entertainment and social activities can include going out to restaurants and cafes, and savings can be around 10-20% of the monthly budget. However, healthcare and insurance costs can be a significant expense, and individuals may need to prioritize their spending accordingly.

A comfortable lifestyle in Iraq with $1,500-2,500 per month can provide a high standard of living. At this level, individuals can afford a nice two-bedroom apartment in the city center, eat out frequently, and use a car or taxi for transportation. Entertainment and social activities can include traveling, going to concerts and events, and savings can be around 20-30% of the monthly budget. However, individuals may need to be mindful of their spending habits, as the cost of living in Iraq can vary significantly depending on the location and lifestyle.

- Buy food and other necessities in bulk to save around 10-20% on costs.
- Use local markets and shops instead of supermarkets to save around 10-20% on costs.
- Avoid traveling during peak season to save around 20-30% on transportation costs.
