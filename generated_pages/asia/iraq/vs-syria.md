# Iraq vs Syria

When deciding between Iraq and Syria, consider factors such as safety, cultural attractions, and travel ease to make an informed decision that suits your needs.

[SECTION] Why People Compare Them [/SECTION]
Iraq and Syria are two neighboring countries in the Middle East that are often compared due to their shared history, cultural heritage, and geographical proximity. Both countries have experienced significant turmoil in recent years, with Iraq dealing with the aftermath of the US-led invasion and Syria embroiled in a devastating civil war, leading many to wonder which country is safer, more stable, and more suitable for travel.

The comparison between Iraq and Syria is also driven by the fact that both countries are home to numerous ancient historical sites, including Babylon, Ur, and Palmyra, which are of great interest to history buffs and archaeology enthusiasts. Additionally, the two countries have distinct cultural identities, with Iraq being known for its vibrant cities, such as Baghdad and Basra, and Syria being famous for its rich culinary traditions and stunning natural landscapes.

[SECTION] Key Differences [/SECTION]
- **Population**: Iraq has a significantly larger population than Syria, with over 46 million people compared to Syria's 18 million.
- **Size**: Iraq is more than twice as large as Syria, covering an area of 438,317 km² compared to Syria's 185,180 km².
- **Currency**: Iraq uses the Iraqi Dinar, while Syria uses the Syrian Pound, although both countries also accept US dollars.
- **Climate**: Iraq has a predominantly desert climate, while Syria has a mix of desert and Mediterranean climates.
- **Cost Level**: Iraq is considered a medium-cost country, while Syria is relatively low-cost, although prices can vary depending on the location and availability of goods.
- **Safety Level**: Both countries are considered unsafe for travel, but Syria is currently more unstable due to the ongoing civil war.

[SECTION] Key Similarities [/SECTION]
- **Language**: Both Iraq and Syria have Arabic as an official language, and Kurdish is also widely spoken in both countries.
- **History**: Both countries have a rich cultural heritage, with numerous ancient historical sites, including Babylon, Ur, and Palmyra.
- **Cuisine**: Both countries share similar culinary traditions, with popular dishes such as kebabs, falafel, and shawarma.
- **Hospitality**: Both Iraqis and Syrians are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature towards guests.
