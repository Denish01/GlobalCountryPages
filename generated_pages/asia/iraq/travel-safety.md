[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Iraq is a complex and challenging destination for travelers, with a range of safety concerns that need to be carefully considered. The country has faced significant security challenges in recent years, including terrorism, sectarian violence, and crime. While some areas of the country are considered safer than others, the overall safety situation is fragile and can change quickly. Travelers need to be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

Despite the challenges, Iraq is a country with a rich history and culture, and many travelers are drawn to its ancient cities, historical landmarks, and vibrant markets. With careful planning and research, it is possible to have a safe and rewarding experience in Iraq. However, it is essential to stay informed about local conditions, follow government travel advisories, and exercise caution when traveling to or within the country. Travelers should also be aware of the local customs and traditions, and respect the cultural and social norms of the country.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Terrorism**: Iraq has experienced numerous terrorist attacks in recent years, and the risk of terrorism remains high.
- **Kidnapping**: Kidnapping is a significant risk in Iraq, particularly in areas with high levels of crime and violence.
- **Scams**: Scams are common in Iraq, particularly in tourist areas, and can include scams related to money exchange, accommodation, and transportation.
- **Petty theft**: Petty theft is a moderate risk in Iraq, and travelers should take necessary precautions to protect their belongings.
- **Traffic accidents**: Traffic accidents are a significant risk in Iraq, and travelers should exercise caution when driving or walking.
- **Protests and demonstrations**: Protests and demonstrations can occur in Iraq, and travelers should avoid areas where these are taking place.
- **Corruption**: Corruption is a significant problem in Iraq, and travelers may encounter corrupt officials or individuals seeking bribes.
- **Mine and explosive remnants**: Iraq is still contaminated with mines and explosive remnants from previous conflicts, and travelers should exercise caution when traveling to rural areas.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: Travelers to Iraq should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations, including **Hepatitis A**, **Typhoid**, and **Rabies**.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is not safe to drink in Iraq, and travelers should stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities**: Medical facilities in Iraq are limited, and travelers may need to seek medical attention in neighboring countries in the event of a serious illness or injury. Travelers should ensure they have adequate **health insurance** that covers medical evacuation.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacy access is limited in Iraq, and travelers should bring any necessary medications with them.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always stay informed about local conditions and follow government travel advisories.
- Exercise caution when traveling to or within Iraq, and avoid traveling at night.
- Keep a low profile and avoid drawing attention to yourself.
- Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use secure methods of payment.
- Keep your valuables secure and consider using a hotel safe or a secure bag.
- Avoid traveling to areas with high levels of crime or violence.
- Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Solo and women travelers should exercise extra caution when traveling in Iraq, and consider traveling with a group or a reputable tour operator.
- Women travelers should dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.
- Solo and women travelers should avoid traveling at night and stick to well-lit and populated areas.
- Consider staying in a reputable hotel or guesthouse, and research the local area before arriving.
