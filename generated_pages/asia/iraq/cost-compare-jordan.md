[SECTION] Overview: Iraq vs Jordan [/SECTION]
The cost of living in Iraq and Jordan can vary significantly, with Iraq generally being the more affordable option. Iraq's economy has been growing steadily in recent years, driven by its oil exports, while Jordan's economy has been more diversified, with a strong focus on services and tourism. However, Iraq's cost of living is still lower, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being significantly cheaper than in Jordan. On average, Iraq is around 20-30% cheaper than Jordan, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for a more affordable lifestyle.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Iraq's economy has been impacted by years of conflict and instability, which has led to a shortage of certain goods and services, driving up prices. However, the country has been making efforts to rebuild and stabilize its economy, which has led to a decrease in prices for certain goods and services. Jordan, on the other hand, has a more stable economy, but it is also more prone to external shocks, such as changes in global oil prices, which can impact the cost of living.
