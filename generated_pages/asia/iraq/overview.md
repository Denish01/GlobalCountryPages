[SECTION] What Is Iraq? [/SECTION]
Iraq is a **sovereign** country located in the Middle East, in the continent of Asia. It is situated in a strategic geographic position, bordered by several countries including Turkey, Iran, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, and Syria. Iraq is known for its rich cultural heritage, with a history dating back to ancient civilizations such as the Sumerians, Babylonians, and Assyrians. The country has a diverse landscape, with deserts, mountains, and rivers, including the famous Tigris and Euphrates rivers.

Iraq has a long and complex history, with various empires and dynasties rising and falling over the centuries. The country has been influenced by different cultures, including Arabic, Kurdish, and Turkish, which has resulted in a unique blend of traditions and customs. Iraq is also home to several important cities, including Baghdad, the capital, as well as Basra, Mosul, and Kirkuk. These cities have played significant roles in the country's history, economy, and culture.

In recent years, Iraq has faced several challenges, including conflict, instability, and economic difficulties. However, the country is working to rebuild and recover, with a focus on developing its economy, improving its infrastructure, and promoting tourism. Iraq is a country with a rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural beauty, making it a fascinating destination for travelers and a significant player in the region.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1920 - The modern state of Iraq was established under British mandate, following the collapse of the Ottoman Empire.
2. 1932 - Iraq gained independence from Britain, with King Faisal II as its first monarch.
3. 1958 - A revolution led by Abdul Karim Qasim overthrew the monarchy, and Iraq became a republic.
4. 1979 - Saddam Hussein came to power, marking the beginning of a period of authoritarian rule and international isolation.
5. 2003 - The United States-led invasion of Iraq resulted in the overthrow of Saddam Hussein and the establishment of a new government.
6. 2011 - The United States withdrew its troops from Iraq, marking the end of the Iraq War.
7. 2014 - The Islamic State (IS) militant group seized control of large areas of Iraq, leading to a new wave of conflict and instability.
