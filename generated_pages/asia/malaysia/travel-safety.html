Overall Safety Assessment

Malaysia is generally a safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other countries in the region. The country has a strong economy and a high standard of living, which contributes to its overall safety. However, as with any country, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and tourists should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and snatch theft can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, while violent crimes are relatively rare. The Malaysian government has implemented various measures to ensure the safety of tourists, including the establishment of a tourist police force.

Malaysia is a multicultural and multiracial country, and tourists are generally welcomed with open arms. The country has a vibrant culture and a rich history, with many exciting places to visit and experiences to be had. From the bustling streets of Kuala Lumpur to the beautiful beaches of Penang and the lush rainforests of Borneo, Malaysia has something to offer for every kind of traveler. With some basic precautions and an understanding of the local culture, tourists can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Malaysia.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Be cautious of snatch theft, where thieves on motorcycles grab bags or jewelry from pedestrians
  • Be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
  • Be wary of scams targeting tourists, such as the "friendly local" who offers to show you around and then demands money
  • Watch out for traffic accidents, as Malaysia has a high rate of road fatalities
  • Be careful when using taxis, as some drivers may overcharge or take you on a longer route than necessary
  • Avoid eating from street vendors that do not have a good reputation, as food poisoning can be a risk

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to Malaysia
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Malaysia has high-quality medical facilities, particularly in major cities, but medical care can be expensive and may require insurance
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available, but some medications may require a prescription

Practical Safety Tips

  • Always carry a copy of your passport and leave the original in a safe place
  • Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps to avoid scams
  • Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas
  • Respect local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting mosques or temples
  • Stay hydrated and take regular breaks in the hot tropical sun
  • Learn some basic Malay phrases, such as "hello" (hello) and "terima kasih" (thank you)

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs, particularly when visiting mosques or temples
  • Use reputable transportation and avoid hitchhiking or accepting rides from strangers
  • Stay in touch with family and friends back home and let them know your itinerary and whereabouts
