[SECTION] Overview: Malaysia vs Singapore [/SECTION]
Malaysia and Singapore are two neighboring countries in Southeast Asia with distinct economic and cultural profiles. Malaysia is a **sovereign** country with a diverse economy, rich natural resources, and a relatively low cost of living. In contrast, Singapore is a **highly developed** city-state with a strong finance sector, high standard of living, and one of the highest costs of living in the world. The economic context of the two countries is also different, with Malaysia having a more **diversified economy** and Singapore having a more **service-oriented economy**. Overall, Malaysia is generally cheaper than Singapore, with an overall cost of living that is around 40-50% lower. This is due to various factors, including lower housing costs, food prices, and transportation expenses.

The cost of living difference between Malaysia and Singapore is significant, with Malaysia offering a more affordable lifestyle for expats, students, and retirees. For example, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center of Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia's capital, can cost around $200-300 per month, while a similar apartment in Singapore can cost around $1,500-2,000 per month. Additionally, meals at restaurants and groceries are also significantly cheaper in Malaysia, making it an attractive option for those looking to save money. However, Singapore offers a more **convenient** and **efficient** lifestyle, with a well-developed public transportation system and a wide range of amenities and services.
