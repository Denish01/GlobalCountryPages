[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Malaysia is generally of high quality, with fast speeds and reliable connections, especially in urban areas such as Kuala Lumpur and Penang. However, there is a noticeable urban-rural divide, with rural areas often experiencing slower speeds and less reliable connections. Despite this, the overall internet quality in Malaysia is good, with many **Internet Service Providers (ISPs)** offering a range of plans and packages to suit different needs and budgets. The Malaysian government has also invested heavily in improving the country's internet infrastructure, with a focus on expanding **4G** and **5G** coverage to more areas.

In terms of reliability, the internet in Malaysia is generally stable, with minimal downtime or disruptions. However, as with any country, there can be occasional outages or issues, especially during peak usage hours or in areas with high demand. Overall, Malaysia's internet is well-suited for both personal and business use, with many **coworking spaces** and **cafe chains** offering fast and reliable connections.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Malaysia, you can visit one of the many **mobile phone shops** or **convenience stores** that sell SIM cards from the major carriers, such as Maxis, Celcom, and Digi. You will typically need to provide your passport and fill out a registration form to purchase a SIM card. The cost of a tourist SIM card is around 10 USD, and data plans start from around 5 USD per month. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the major carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds. You can also buy SIM cards at airports, such as Kuala Lumpur International Airport, or at **7-Eleven** convenience stores.
