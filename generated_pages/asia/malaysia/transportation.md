# Getting Around Malaysia — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Malaysia, you can use a variety of transportation options including domestic flights, trains, buses, taxis, and ride-hailing apps, which provide a convenient and relatively affordable way to travel throughout the country.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Malaysia has several international airports, with Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KUL) being the main gateway to the country. The airport is a major hub for Malaysia Airlines and offers direct flights to numerous destinations in Asia, Europe, and Australia. Other notable airports include Penang International Airport (PEN) and Kota Kinabalu International Airport (BKI). If you're traveling overland, you can enter Malaysia from neighboring countries such as Thailand, Indonesia, and Singapore. The main overland entry points are at the border towns of Bukit Kayu Hitam and Padang Besar in the north, and Johor Bahru in the south.

If you prefer to travel by sea, you can take a ferry from neighboring countries or from within Malaysia. The main ports are located in Penang, Langkawi, and Kuala Lumpur. Malaysia is also a popular destination for cruise ships, with many international cruise lines offering itineraries that include stops in Malaysian ports. Additionally, you can take a bus or train from neighboring countries, with several bus and train companies operating cross-border services.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Malaysia has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to various destinations within the country. The main domestic airlines are Malaysia Airlines, AirAsia, and Malindo Air, which offer frequent flights to major cities such as Kuala Lumpur, Penang, and Kota Kinabalu. You can book domestic flights online or through a travel agent, and it's recommended to book in advance to secure the best rates. The cost of domestic flights varies depending on the route and time of booking, but you can expect to pay around $20-$100 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Malaysia has a moderate rail network, with train services operated by Keretapi Tanah Melayu (KTM). The rail network connects major cities such as Kuala Lumpur, Penang, and Johor Bahru, with frequent trains running throughout the day. The trains are generally comfortable and clean, with food and beverage services available on board. You can book train tickets online or at the train station, and it's recommended to book in advance to secure a seat. The cost of train tickets varies depending on the route and class of service, but you can expect to pay around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Malaysia has an extensive network of buses and coaches, with several companies operating intercity services. The main bus companies are Transnasional, Nice, and Aeroline, which offer frequent services to major cities and towns. The buses are generally comfortable and clean, with food and beverage services available on board. You can book bus tickets online or at the bus station, and it's recommended to book in advance to secure a seat. The cost of bus tickets varies depending on the route and class of service, but you can expect to pay around $5-$20 for a one-way ticket.
