# Malaysia Visa & Entry Requirements

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Malaysia before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Malaysia has a moderate visa policy, offering visa-free access to citizens of over 80 countries. The country aims to strike a balance between facilitating tourism and business travel while maintaining national security and controlling immigration. Citizens of eligible countries can enter Malaysia without a visa for a specified period, usually ranging from 30 to 90 days. However, it's essential to note that visa requirements can change, and travelers should always check with the Malaysian embassy or consulate for the latest information.

Malaysia's visa policy is designed to promote tourism, business, and cultural exchange while ensuring the country's security and integrity. The government has introduced various types of visas, including electronic visas (eVisas), to simplify the application process and attract more visitors. With its rich cultural heritage, diverse cuisine, and modern infrastructure, Malaysia is an attractive destination for travelers from around the world.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Malaysia without a visa for a maximum stay of 90 days, depending on their nationality. They must have a valid passport, return or onward ticket, and proof of sufficient funds. Tourists can extend their stay for an additional 30 days, but this must be done through the Malaysian Immigration Department.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They must provide an invitation letter from a Malaysian company, proof of business activities, and other relevant documents. Business travelers can apply for a **Multiple-Entry Visa**, which allows them to enter and exit Malaysia multiple times within a specified period.
**Students:** Students must apply for a **Student Visa**, which requires proof of enrollment in a Malaysian educational institution, a valid passport, and proof of sufficient funds. The student visa is usually valid for the duration of the course, and students must comply with the terms and conditions of their visa.
**Workers:** Workers must have a **Work Visa**, which requires sponsorship from a Malaysian employer. The employer must apply for a work permit on behalf of the worker, and the worker must meet the necessary qualifications and experience requirements. The work visa is usually valid for a specific period, and workers must comply with the terms and conditions of their visa.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
Malaysia has several main airports, including Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA), Penang International Airport (PEN), and Kota Kinabalu International Airport (BKI). When arriving at an airport, travelers must proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their passport, visa (if required), and other documents. Travelers must also fill out a **Disembarkation Card**, which will be provided by the airline or available at the airport. The immigration officer will inspect the traveler's documents and may ask questions about their purpose of visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements. Once cleared, travelers can collect their luggage and proceed to their destination.

Land borders are also available, particularly for travelers from neighboring countries such as Thailand and Singapore. Travelers must present their passport, visa (if required), and other documents at the border crossing. The immigration process may take longer at land borders, and travelers should be prepared for potential delays.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not having a return or onward ticket
- Not having proof of sufficient funds
- Not having the necessary documents, such as a visa or invitation letter
- Not filling out the Disembarkation Card correctly
- Not declaring goods or currency exceeding the allowed limit

By understanding the visa requirements and regulations, travelers can ensure a smooth and hassle-free entry into Malaysia. It's essential to stay informed about the latest visa rules and regulations to avoid any potential issues during their trip.
