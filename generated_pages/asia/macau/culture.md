[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Respect for elders is deeply ingrained in Macau's culture, so it's customary to greet older individuals with a bow or a handshake
- Learn a few basic Cantonese phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you," to show appreciation for the local culture
- Remove your shoes before entering a traditional Chinese home or temple
- Use both hands when giving or receiving something, as using one hand can be considered impolite
- Try the local cuisine and drinks, as food plays a significant role in Macau's social culture
- Be mindful of dress codes when visiting temples or attending cultural events

**Don't:**
- Avoid touching or pointing at Buddha statues or other religious artifacts, as this is considered disrespectful
- Don't finish a meal completely, as this suggests that the host did not provide enough food
- Avoid discussing politics or sensitive topics, as this can be considered impolite
- Don't tip at restaurants or bars, as this is not customary in Macau
- Avoid taking pictures of people without their permission, especially in rural areas
- Don't litter or throw trash on the streets, as Macau takes pride in its cleanliness
