Healthcare System Overview

Macau's healthcare system is a mixed model, with both public and private healthcare services available. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of high quality, but it can be overcrowded and have long wait times. Foreigners may face difficulties in accessing public healthcare services, and may be required to pay out-of-pocket for treatment. Private healthcare services, on the other hand, are generally of high quality and have shorter wait times, but can be more expensive. Many locals and foreigners choose to use private healthcare services, especially for non-emergency treatments.

The healthcare system in Macau is generally well-equipped to handle a wide range of medical conditions, with many hospitals and clinics having modern facilities and equipment. However, some specialized treatments may not be available in Macau, and patients may need to travel to Hong Kong or mainland China for treatment. Health insurance is not mandatory in Macau, but it is highly recommended, especially for foreigners who may not be eligible for public healthcare services. Many employers in Macau offer health insurance as part of their employee benefits package, but individuals can also purchase private health insurance plans.

Pharmacy & Medication Costs

  • Common medications such as antibiotics and painkillers can cost between $5-$20 per prescription
  • Prescription medications may require a doctor's prescription, and some medications may not be available in Macau
  • Pharmacies are widely available in Macau, with many locations open 24 hours
  • Over-the-counter medications such as cold and flu remedies can cost between $10-$30 per package
  • Some medications may be cheaper in Macau than in other countries, but prices can vary depending on the pharmacy and location
  • It is recommended to bring any prescription medications with you when traveling to Macau, as some medications may not be available or may require a special prescription

Emergency Care

In the event of a medical emergency, it is recommended to call the emergency number 999 or go to the nearest hospital. The public hospital system in Macau has a well-equipped emergency department, but wait times can be long. Private hospitals may have shorter wait times and more specialized services, but may be more expensive. For foreigners, it is recommended to have health insurance that covers emergency medical evacuation and repatriation, in case specialized treatment is not available in Macau. Some of the best hospitals for foreigners in Macau include the Conde S. Januario Hospital and the Kiang Wu Hospital, which have modern facilities and equipment, as well as English-speaking staff.
