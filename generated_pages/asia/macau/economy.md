[SECTION] Economic Overview [/SECTION]
Macau's economy is classified as a high-income economy, with a highly developed service sector and a strong focus on gaming and tourism. The territory has experienced rapid economic growth over the past few decades, driven by the liberalization of the gaming industry in 2002, which allowed foreign investment and led to the development of large-scale casino resorts. Today, Macau is the world's largest gaming hub, surpassing Las Vegas in terms of revenue. The economy is also characterized by a high level of government revenue, which is generated primarily from gaming taxes and other levies.

The development level of Macau's economy is highly advanced, with a strong financial system, a well-developed infrastructure, and a high standard of living. The territory has a highly skilled workforce, with a strong emphasis on education and training. The government has also implemented various initiatives to diversify the economy, including the development of the financial services sector, the promotion of small and medium-sized enterprises, and the encouragement of foreign investment.

Macau's economic trajectory is expected to continue its upward trend, driven by the ongoing growth of the gaming and tourism industries. However, the government is also aware of the need to diversify the economy and reduce its dependence on a single industry. To this end, the government has been actively promoting the development of other sectors, such as financial services, technology, and logistics.
