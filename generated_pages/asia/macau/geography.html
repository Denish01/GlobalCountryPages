Location & Borders

Macau is situated on the western edge of the Pearl River Delta, approximately 60 kilometers west of Hong Kong. The region is comprised of the Macau Peninsula, which is connected to mainland China, and the islands of Taipa and Coloane, which are connected to the peninsula by bridges and a causeway. Macau is bordered by the Guangdong province of China to the north, east, and west, and by Hong Kong to the east across the Pearl River Delta. The South China Sea lies to the south of Macau, providing a maritime border.

The strategic location of Macau has made it an important center for trade and commerce in the region. The city's proximity to Hong Kong and mainland China has facilitated the exchange of goods, services, and ideas, and has contributed to its growth and development as a major economic hub. The borders of Macau are well-defined, with a series of bridges, roads, and border crossings connecting the region to mainland China and Hong Kong. The maritime border is also well-defined, with a series of islands and reefs that mark the edge of Macau's territorial waters.
