[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Macau has a well-developed public transportation system, with buses and taxis being the primary modes of transportation. The city's compact size and grid-like street pattern make it easy to navigate, with most people getting around on foot or by using public transportation. The quality of public transit in Macau is generally good, with frequent bus services and a comprehensive network of routes covering most areas of the city.

The majority of residents in Macau use public transportation to get around, with buses being the most popular mode of transportation. The city's bus network is operated by several companies, including Transportas Companhia de Macau (TCM) and Reolian Public Transport Co., Ltd. Taxis are also widely available, but can be more expensive than using public transportation. Ride-hailing apps, such as Didi Chuxing and Grab, are also available in Macau, providing an alternative to traditional taxis.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a monthly transit pass, which can be used on buses and other public transportation.
- Use ride-hailing apps, such as Didi Chuxing and Grab, for a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.
- Be sure to agree on the fare with the taxi driver before starting your journey, as some drivers may not use the meter.
- Consider walking or cycling for short distances, as Macau is a compact city with a grid-like street pattern.
- Be aware of traffic rules and regulations, and always wear a seatbelt when driving or riding in a vehicle.
- Download the Macau Public Transportation app for a convenient way to plan your route and track bus schedules.
