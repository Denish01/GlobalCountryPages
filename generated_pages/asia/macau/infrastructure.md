[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Macau's infrastructure is highly developed, with a strong focus on tourism and gaming industries. The region has invested heavily in recent years to improve its roads, public transportation, and telecommunications. The urban areas of Macau have a high level of infrastructure development, with modern buildings, well-maintained roads, and a reliable public transportation system. However, there is a noticeable gap between the urban and rural areas, with some rural areas lacking access to basic infrastructure such as clean water and sanitation.

The Macau government has been actively working to address this gap, with investments in rural infrastructure development and social welfare programs. The region's infrastructure is also well-integrated with neighboring regions, including China and Hong Kong, making it an important hub for trade and tourism. Macau's infrastructure development is driven by its **special administrative region** status, which gives it a high degree of autonomy in managing its internal affairs.

Macau's infrastructure is also influenced by its **parent** country, China, which provides significant funding and support for major infrastructure projects. The region's **neighbors**, including China and Hong Kong, also play an important role in shaping its infrastructure development, with many joint projects and initiatives aimed at improving regional connectivity and cooperation.
