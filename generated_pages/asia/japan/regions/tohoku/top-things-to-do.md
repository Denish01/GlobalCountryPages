# Top Things to Do in Tohoku — Attractions & Experiences

Tohoku, a region in northern Japan, offers a wide range of exciting activities and experiences, from visiting historic temples and castles to exploring stunning natural landscapes and enjoying unique local cuisine.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Attend the **Aomori Nebuta Festival**, a vibrant summer festival with colorful floats and traditional music
- Visit a **sake brewery** in Sendai to learn about the production process and sample local varieties
- Take a **stargazing tour** in the Aomori countryside, known for its incredibly dark skies
- Experience **farm-to-table dining** at a local farm or restaurant, featuring fresh Tohoku produce
- Learn about **traditional Tohoku crafts**, such as woodblock printing or lacquerware, at a local workshop
- Participate in a **tea ceremony** at a historic temple or traditional tea house
- Go **snowshoeing** in the Japanese Alps, a unique winter adventure

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Bandai-Asahi National Park**, a stunning park with hiking trails, lakes, and waterfalls
- Hike the **Dewa Sanzan** trails, a network of sacred hiking trails with breathtaking views
- Visit the **Matsushima Bay**, a scenic bay with over 260 small islands and stunning sunsets
- Relax at the **Nyuto Onsen** hot springs, a remote resort with natural hot springs and stunning scenery
- Go **skiing** at the **Zao Onsen Ski Resort**, a popular resort with unique snow monsters and hot springs
- Explore the **Abukuma Cave**, a massive limestone cave with stunning rock formations

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Sendai City Museum**, a museum showcasing the city's history and culture
- Explore the **Hirosaki Castle** and its surrounding historic town, with many preserved buildings and shops
- Attend a **traditional festival**, such as the Sendai Tanabata Festival or the Aomori Nebuta Festival
- Learn about **Tohoku's samurai history** at the **Tsuruga Castle** museum
- Visit the **Yamagata Museum of Art**, a museum featuring local and regional art
- Take a **calligraphy class**, a traditional Japanese art form, at a local cultural center

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Goshogawara Tachineputa Museum**, a small museum showcasing the city's unique Tachineputa Festival floats
- Explore the **Shirakami-Sanchi National Park**, a lesser-known park with stunning mountain scenery and hiking trails
- Relax at the **Kuroyu Onsen**, a remote hot spring resort with natural hot springs and stunning scenery
- Visit the **Sakata City**, a charming coastal town with a rich history and cultural heritage
- Attend a **local market**, such as the Sendai Morning Market, to sample fresh produce and local specialties

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip from Sendai to **Matsushima Bay** (30 miles, 1 hour), with stunning scenery and historic temples
- Visit the **Dewa Sanzan** from Tsuruoka (20 miles, 30 minutes), with sacred hiking trails and stunning views
- Explore the **Hirosaki Castle** from Aomori (30 miles, 1 hour), with a historic castle and traditional town
- Go **hiking** in the **Bandai-Asahi National Park** from Fukushima (40 miles, 1.5 hours), with stunning scenery and wildlife
- Visit the **Ginzan Onsen** from Yamagata (30 miles, 1 hour), with traditional hot springs and historic architecture
