The Chubu region in Japan offers a diverse range of activities and attractions, from outdoor adventures in the Japanese Alps to cultural experiences in cities like Nagoya and Kanazawa, making it a must-visit destination for any traveler.

Unique Experiences

  • Visit the Nagoya TV Tower for panoramic views of the city and a unique dining experience at its revolving restaurant
  • Take a gold leaf class in Kanazawa to learn about the traditional craft of gold leaf production
  • Explore the Kiso Valley and hike along the Nakasendo Trail, a historic route that connects Tokyo and Kyoto
  • Attend a traditional Japanese tea ceremony in a historic teahouse in Kanazawa or Nagoya
  • Visit the Gifu Castle and try your hand at traditional Japanese archery
  • Take a stroll along the Asano River in Kanazawa and enjoy the beautiful scenery and historic bridges

Outdoor & Nature

  • Hike in the Japanese Alps, which offer stunning scenery and a range of trails for all levels
  • Visit the Shirakawa-go valley and enjoy the picturesque scenery and traditional villages
  • Explore the Noto Peninsula, known for its stunning coastal scenery and beautiful beaches
  • Take a boat ride on Lake Biwa, Japan's largest freshwater lake
  • Visit the Hida Mountains and enjoy outdoor activities such as skiing, hiking, and hot springs

Cultural Experiences

  • Visit the Nagoya City Art Museum, which features a collection of modern and contemporary art
  • Explore the Kanazawa 21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art, which showcases a range of contemporary art and installations
  • Attend a traditional Japanese festival, such as the Nagoya Festival or the Kanazawa Hyakumanben Festival
  • Visit the Hida Folk Village and learn about traditional Japanese crafts and customs
  • Take a stroll through the historic district of Takayama and visit traditional shops and cafes

Hidden Gems

  • Visit the Gujo Hachiman, a small town known for its traditional architecture and beautiful scenery
  • Explore the Kiso Valley and visit traditional villages such as Tsumago and Magome
  • Take a day trip to the Noto Peninsula, which offers stunning coastal scenery and beautiful beaches
  • Visit the Toyota City, which offers a range of attractions related to the automotive industry
  • Attend a traditional Japanese music performance, such as a shamisen or koto concert

Day Trips

  1. Take a day trip from Nagoya to Gujo Hachiman (distance: 120 km, travel time: 2 hours), which offers traditional architecture and beautiful scenery
  2. Visit Kanazawa from Nagoya (distance: 160 km, travel time: 2.5 hours), which features a range of cultural attractions and historic sites
  3. Take a day trip from Takayama to Shirakawa-go (distance: 30 km, travel time: 1 hour), which offers stunning scenery and traditional villages
  4. Visit Gifu from Nagoya (distance: 30 km, travel time: 30 minutes), which features a range of cultural attractions and historic sites
  5. Take a day trip from Kanazawa to the Noto Peninsula (distance: 100 km, travel time: 2 hours), which offers stunning coastal scenery and beautiful beaches

Planning Tips

Suggested itineraries: 3-day (highlights of Nagoya and Kanazawa), 7-day (comprehensive tour of Chubu), 14-day (deep exploration of the region). Key tips for booking and prioritizing: book accommodations and transportation in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, and prioritize cultural experiences and outdoor activities. Consider purchasing a Japan Rail Pass for convenient and affordable travel between cities.
