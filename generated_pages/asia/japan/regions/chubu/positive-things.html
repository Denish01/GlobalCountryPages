Chubu, a region in Japan, is renowned for its remarkable achievements in science, technology, and innovation, as well as its rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural scenery.

Cultural Treasures

  • The Nagoya Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a prominent cultural landmark and a testament to the region's rich history.
  • The Owari Tsushima Tenno Festival, a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage, showcases the region's vibrant traditions and customs.
  • The Kakamigahara Air and Space Museum is a unique cultural institution that highlights the region's contributions to the field of aviation and space exploration.
  • The Hida Folk Village, an open-air museum, preserves and showcases the region's traditional architecture and way of life.
  • The Noh Theatre, a traditional Japanese performing art form, is still widely practiced and appreciated in the Chubu region.

People & Global Impact

  • Akio Toyoda, the president of Toyota Motor Corporation, is a notable figure from the Chubu region who has played a significant role in shaping the global automotive industry.
  • Yoshio Tanaka, a renowned Japanese engineer, was born in the Chubu region and has made significant contributions to the development of high-speed rail technology.
  • The Japanese diaspora community in the Chubu region has made significant contributions to the local economy and culture, with many individuals playing important roles in international trade and cultural exchange.
  • The region's strong emphasis on community values and social cohesion has fostered a unique and supportive environment that encourages innovation and entrepreneurship.

What Visitors Love

  • The region's hospitality and warm welcome are consistently praised by visitors, who appreciate the local people's friendly and accommodating nature.
  • The local cuisine, including dishes such as hitsumabushi and tenmusu, is highly regarded for its unique flavors and ingredients.
  • The scenic beauty of the Chubu region, with its mountains, forests, and coastal areas, offers a wide range of outdoor activities and breathtaking views.
  • The traditional festivals and events, such as the Nagoya Festival and the Owari Tsushima Tenno Festival, provide a unique glimpse into the region's vibrant culture and customs.
  • The shopping districts, including the Sakae and Osu areas, offer a wide range of local products and souvenirs, from traditional crafts to modern electronics.
  • The onsen (hot springs) and public baths are highly popular among visitors, who appreciate the region's unique thermal waters and relaxation facilities.

Hidden Gems

  • The Kiso Valley, a scenic and historic area, is home to many traditional villages and hiking trails, offering a unique glimpse into the region's rural landscape and cultural heritage.
  • The Inuyama Castle, a lesser-known but equally impressive castle, is a hidden gem that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Kiso River and Mount Haku.
  • The Toyota Commemorative Museum of Industry and Technology, a unique museum showcasing the history of the Toyota Motor Corporation, provides a fascinating insight into the region's industrial heritage and innovative spirit.
  • The Nagoya/Boston Museum of Fine Arts, a collaboration between the Nagoya City Art Museum and the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, features a unique collection of Japanese and Western art, highlighting the region's cultural exchange and artistic appreciation.
