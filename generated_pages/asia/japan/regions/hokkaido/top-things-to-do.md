# Top Things to Do in Hokkaido — Attractions & Experiences

Hokkaido, the northernmost region of Japan, offers a diverse range of exciting experiences, from breathtaking natural landscapes to unique cultural attractions, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **sushi-making class** in Sapporo to learn the art of creating delicious sushi
- Explore the **Icebreaker Ship** in Otaru, a unique vessel that breaks through ice in the Sea of Japan
- Visit the **Sapporo Beer Museum** to learn about Japan's beer-brewing history and sample local beers
- Experience the **Ainu culture** at the Shiraoi Ainu Museum, showcasing the traditions and customs of Hokkaido's indigenous people
- Go **dog sledding** in the winter or **hiking** in the summer in the beautiful Daisetsuzan National Park
- Take a **stargazing tour** in the clear skies of Hokkaido, perfect for astronomy enthusiasts
- Learn about **Hokkaido's indigenous flora** at the Sapporo Maruyama Zoo's botanical garden

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Daisetsuzan National Park**, Japan's largest national park, featuring stunning mountains and hiking trails
- Visit the **Shikotsu-Toya National Park**, known for its picturesque lakes and hot springs
- Hike the **Rishiri-Rebun-Sarobetsu National Park** trails, offering breathtaking coastal views and diverse wildlife
- Relax in the **Noboribetsu Onsen** hot springs, surrounded by beautiful scenery
- Take a **whale-watching tour** in the Sea of Japan, spotting humpback, minke, and sperm whales
- Go **skiing** or **snowboarding** at one of Hokkaido's many world-class resorts, such as Niseko or Furano

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Hakodate Magistrate's Office**, a historic building showcasing the city's rich history
- Explore the **Sapporo Clock Tower**, a iconic landmark and symbol of the city
- Learn about **Hokkaido's history** at the Hokkaido Museum, featuring exhibits on the region's indigenous people and development
- Attend a **traditional Ainu dance performance** at the Shiraoi Ainu Museum
- Visit the **Otaru Museum of Art**, showcasing a collection of Japanese and Western art
- Take a **calligraphy class** in Sapporo, learning the art of Japanese writing

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Kamiyubetsu Tulip Park**, featuring over 1 million tulips in bloom
- Explore the **Cape Chikyu**, a scenic coastal area with stunning views of the Sea of Japan
- Relax in the **Toyako Onsen** hot springs, a lesser-known but beautiful spot
- Take a **scenic drive** along the **Furano Valley**, surrounded by picturesque countryside and rolling hills
- Visit the **Asahikawa Museum of Art**, showcasing a unique collection of modern and contemporary art

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip from Sapporo to **Otaru** (30 km, 30 minutes by train), exploring the city's charming canal and historic architecture
- Visit **Furano** (135 km, 2 hours by car) from Sapporo, enjoying the scenic countryside and world-class skiing
- Explore **Hakodate** (312 km, 3.5 hours by train) from Sapporo, visiting the city's historic landmarks and sampling fresh seafood
- Take a day trip from Asahikawa to **Biei** (25 km, 30 minutes by car), known for its picturesque countryside and scenic drives
- Visit **Rishiri Island** (40 km, 2 hours by ferry) from Wakkanai, exploring the island's stunning coastal scenery and wildlife
