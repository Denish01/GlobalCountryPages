# Top Things to Do in Okinawa — Attractions & Experiences

Okinawa, a region in southern Japan, offers a unique blend of traditional Ryukyu culture, stunning natural beauty, and modern attractions that make it a must-visit destination for travelers.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Ryukyu Glass-blowing**: Learn the traditional art of glass-blowing at a local workshop in Naha.
- **Okinawan Traditional Dance**: Watch a performance of traditional Okinawan dance, such as the **Shishimai Lion Dance**.
- **Star-gazing at Emerald Beach**: Spend an evening gazing at the stars on the beach, away from city lights.
- **Snorkeling with Manta Rays**: Take a guided tour to snorkel with manta rays in the crystal-clear waters of **Onna Village**.
- **Okinawan Karate Lesson**: Learn the traditional martial art of **Okinawan Karate** from a local master.
- **Traditional Okinawan Pottery**: Create your own traditional Okinawan pottery at a local workshop in **Naha**.
- **Okinawan Cuisine Cooking Class**: Learn how to prepare traditional Okinawan dishes, such as **Goya Champuru**, at a local cooking class.

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Kerama Islands**: A group of islands known for their stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse marine life.
- **Yanbaru National Park**: A national park featuring lush forests, waterfalls, and hiking trails.
- **Daisekirinzan**: A scenic hiking trail offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
- **Naminoue Beach**: A popular beach with clear waters and a variety of water sports.
- **Zanpa Cape**: A scenic spot offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape and the **Zanpa Lighthouse**.
- **Okinawa Kaichu National Monument**: A national monument featuring a stunning underwater cave system.

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Okinawa Prefectural Museum**: A museum showcasing the history, culture, and natural environment of Okinawa.
- **Shuri Castle Museum**: A museum featuring exhibits on the history and culture of the Ryukyu Kingdom.
- **Naminoue Shrine**: A historic Shinto shrine offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
- **Sogenji Temple**: A Buddhist temple featuring stunning gardens and traditional architecture.
- **Ryukyu Kingdom Festival**: A festival celebrating the culture and traditions of the Ryukyu Kingdom.
- **Okinawa Traditional Crafts Village**: A village featuring traditional Okinawan crafts, such as weaving, pottery, and glass-blowing.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **Tinabe Beach**: A secluded beach with crystal-clear waters and white sand, located in the **Kerama Islands**.
- **Hedo Point**: A scenic spot offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape and the **Pacific Ocean**.
- **Okinawa Rail Museum**: A small museum featuring exhibits on the history of railways in Okinawa.
- **Katsuren Castle**: A well-preserved castle ruin offering insights into Okinawa's medieval history.
- **Yachimun no Sato**: A village featuring traditional Okinawan pottery and ceramics.

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]

- **Onna to Emerald Beach**: A day trip to **Emerald Beach**, located in **Onna** (distance: 30 km, travel time: 1 hour).
- **Motobu to Churaumi Aquarium**: A day trip to the **Churaumi Aquarium**, located in **Motobu** (distance: 40 km, travel time: 1.5 hours).
- **Nanjo to Sefa Utaki**: A day trip to the sacred site of **Sefa Utaki**, located in **Nanjo** (distance: 20 km, travel time: 45 minutes).
- **Nago to Orion Happy Park**: A day trip to the **Orion Happy Park**, located in **Nago** (distance: 50 km, travel time: 2 hours).
