# Positive Things About Kyushu - Achievements & Culture

Kyushu, the southernmost major island of Japan, is renowned for its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and notable achievements in various fields, making it a unique and fascinating region to explore.

[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine**, a Shinto shrine dedicated to the god of learning, is a popular tourist destination and an important cultural landmark in Kyushu.
- The **Kawachi Fuji Garden**, a beautiful floral park featuring over 150 varieties of wisteria, is a must-visit attraction during the spring season.
- The **Sasebo Harbor**, a historic naval port, is now a popular tourist destination and a symbol of Kyushu's rich maritime history.
- The **Kunisaki Peninsula**, a scenic region known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, is home to several important archaeological sites and historical landmarks.
- The **Yakushima Island**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is known for its unique wildlife and ecosystems, including the **Jomon Sugi**, a 2,000-year-old cedar tree.

- **Dazai Osamu**, a famous Japanese novelist, was born in Kyushu and drew inspiration from the region's culture and landscapes in his works.
- **Son Masayoshi**, a successful entrepreneur and founder of **SoftBank**, was born in Kyushu and has made significant contributions to Japan's technology industry.
- **Ayumi Nakamura**, a renowned Japanese singer and songwriter, was born in Kyushu and has released several popular albums and singles.
- The **Kyushu diaspora**, a community of people from Kyushu who have migrated to other parts of the world, has made significant contributions to the cultural and economic development of their adopted countries.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **hospitality** of the Kyushu people, who are known for their warm and welcoming nature, is a major draw for tourists.
- The **local cuisine**, which includes popular dishes such as **tonkotsu ramen** and **mentaiko**, is a favorite among visitors.
- The **natural scenery**, including the **Sakurajima volcano** and the **Keramashoto National Park**, is a major attraction for nature lovers.
- The **hot springs**, or **onsen**, which are abundant in Kyushu, are a popular destination for those seeking relaxation and rejuvenation.
- The **traditional festivals**, such as the **Hakata Gion Yamakasa** festival, which showcases the region's unique culture and traditions.
- The **shopping**, including the **Tenjin district** in Fukuoka, which offers a wide range of local products and souvenirs.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Gunkanjima Island**, a former coal mining island that is now a popular tourist destination, offers a unique glimpse into Kyushu's industrial past.
- The **Kurokawa Onsen**, a secluded hot spring resort town, is a hidden gem that is off the beaten path but offers a unique and relaxing experience.
- The **Sagano Romantic Train**, a scenic train that runs through the **Sagano** region, offers a stunning view of the surrounding countryside and is a must-try for train enthusiasts.
- The **Fukuoka Tower**, a 234-meter-tall observation tower, offers a panoramic view of the city and is a great spot to watch the sunset.
