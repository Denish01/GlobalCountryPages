[SECTION] What Is Kyushu? [/SECTION]
Kyushu is a region located on the southernmost main island of Japan, comprising seven prefectures: Fukuoka, Saga, Nagasaki, Kumamoto, Oita, Miyazaki, and Kagoshima. The island is situated to the west of the main island of Honshu, separated by the Kanmon Strait, and is known for its stunning natural beauty, including active volcanoes, hot springs, and scenic coastlines. Kyushu is also home to a diverse range of cultural and historical attractions, including ancient temples, shrines, and castles.

Geographically, Kyushu is a mountainous region, with many active and dormant volcanoes, including the famous Mount Aso, which is one of the largest calderas in the world. The island is also home to several major cities, including Fukuoka, which is the largest city on the island and a major economic and cultural center. Kyushu is connected to the main island of Honshu by a network of bridges and tunnels, including the Kanmon Tunnel, which runs under the Kanmon Strait.

Kyushu has a unique cultural identity that is distinct from the rest of Japan, with a strong emphasis on traditional crafts, music, and cuisine. The island is famous for its delicious food, including tonkotsu ramen, mentaiko (spicy pollock roe), and horse meat, as well as its vibrant festivals and events, such as the Hakata Gion Yamakasa festival.
