# Getting Around Kanto — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Kanto, you can utilize a comprehensive network of transportation options, including **trains**, **buses**, **taxis**, and **ride-hailing services**, making it easy to navigate this populous region in Japan.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Kanto is served by two major international airports: **Narita International Airport (NRT)** and **Haneda Airport (HND)**, both of which offer direct flights to numerous destinations around the world. Narita Airport is located about 60 kilometers east of central Tokyo, while Haneda Airport is located about 15 kilometers south of central Tokyo. If you're traveling overland, you can enter Kanto by **bullet train** or **bus** from other regions in Japan. The region is well-connected to the rest of the country, with frequent departures from major cities like Osaka, Nagoya, and Sapporo.

The main entry points for Kanto are Tokyo, Yokohama, and Kawasaki, all of which have excellent transportation links to the rest of the region. From these cities, you can easily access other parts of Kanto, including the **Nikko** and **Hakone** national parks, the **Fuji Five Lakes** region, and the **Tokyo Disney Resort**. Whether you're traveling by air, land, or sea, getting to Kanto is relatively straightforward, with a wide range of options to suit all budgets and preferences.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Kanto has an extensive domestic air network, with frequent flights connecting Tokyo's two main airports to other cities across Japan. **Japan Airlines**, **All Nippon Airways**, and **Skymark Airlines** are some of the major domestic airlines operating in the region. You can fly from Haneda or Narita airports to destinations like Sapporo, Fukuoka, and Okinawa, with flight times ranging from about an hour to three hours. Booking domestic flights in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as prices can be relatively high. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $100 to $500, depending on the route and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Kanto has an extensive and efficient rail network, with **Japan Railways (JR)** operating many lines throughout the region. The **Tokyo Metro** and **Toei Subway** systems provide comprehensive coverage of the city, with frequent departures and convenient transfers. The **bullet train (Shinkansen)** network connects Tokyo to other major cities in Japan, including Osaka, Nagoya, and Sapporo. You can book train tickets online or at station ticket offices, with prices varying depending on the route and class of service. A **Japan Rail Pass** can be a cost-effective option for travelers planning to use the train network extensively.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Kanto has a moderate network of intercity buses and coaches, with companies like **JR Bus** and **Keisei Bus** operating routes to various destinations in the region. Buses are generally less expensive than trains, but may take longer to reach your destination. You can book bus tickets online or at bus terminals, with prices starting from around $10 for shorter routes. Some bus companies offer **highway bus** services, which can be a convenient and affordable way to travel longer distances.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Kanto, you'll need a valid **Japanese driver's license** or an **International Driving Permit**.
- Road conditions in Kanto are generally good, with well-maintained highways and urban roads.
- Fuel costs in Japan can be relatively high, with gasoline prices averaging around $1.50 per liter.
- Car rental companies like **Toyota Rent-a-Car** and **Nippon Rent-a-Car** have locations throughout the region.
- When driving in Kanto, be aware of **traffic congestion** and **parking challenges**, especially in urban areas.
- Be sure to follow all **traffic rules and regulations**, including wearing a seatbelt and using headlights at night.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When using public transportation, consider purchasing a **Suica** or **Pasmo** card for convenient travel on trains, buses, and subways.
- Be mindful of **rush hour** times (usually 7-9 am and 5-7 pm) when using public transportation, as trains and buses can be very crowded.
- Download the **Hyperdia** or **Google Maps** app for helpful route planning and navigation.
- Be aware of **scams targeting tourists**, such as overpriced taxi rides or fake train ticket sellers.
- When taking a taxi, make sure to check the **meter** is turned on and the **driver** is using a legitimate route.
- Consider purchasing **travel insurance** that covers transportation-related accidents or delays.
- Be respectful of **local customs** and **traffic rules** when driving or using public transportation in Kanto.
