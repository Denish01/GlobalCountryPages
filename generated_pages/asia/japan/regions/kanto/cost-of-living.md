# Cost of Living in Kanto - Prices & Budget Guide

Living in or visiting Kanto can be very expensive, with daily budgets ranging from $60-120 USD for backpackers to $200-500 USD for mid-range travelers and $500-1000 USD for those seeking comfort.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Very Expensive
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $150-300 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $300-600 USD
Currency: JPY
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Kanto? [/SECTION]
Kanto, being the most populous region in Japan, boasts a high standard of living, which contributes to its relatively high cost of living. Compared to global averages, Kanto is one of the more expensive regions, especially when considering accommodation, food, and transportation costs. However, the unique cultural experiences, rich history, and modern amenities make it a desirable destination for many travelers. The cost of living in Kanto is significantly higher than in other parts of Japan, with Tokyo, the capital city, being one of the most expensive cities in the world.

In comparison to its neighbors, Kanto's cost of living is higher than that of other regions in Japan, such as Kansai or Hokkaido. However, the region's economic prosperity, world-class infrastructure, and unparalleled entertainment options make it an attractive destination for both tourists and expatriates. The high cost of living in Kanto is largely due to the region's dense population, limited land availability, and strong demand for goods and services.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a **guesthouse** or **hostel** instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at **local eateries** or **street food stalls** to enjoy affordable and delicious meals.
- Use **public transportation** instead of taxis to get around the city.
- Take advantage of **discounted train passes** for long-distance travel.
- Shop at **local markets** or **convenience stores** for groceries and daily essentials.
- Look for **free admission days** or **discounted tickets** for museums and attractions to save on entertainment costs.

Overall, Kanto is a unique and fascinating region that offers a wide range of experiences for visitors.
