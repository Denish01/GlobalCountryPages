[SECTION] How Kanto Is Divided [/SECTION]
Kanto is a region located on the eastern coast of Honshu, the largest island of Japan, and it is divided into 7 prefectures, each with its own unique character and attractions. The prefectures are Tokyo, Kanagawa, Saitama, Chiba, Tochigi, Gunma, and Ibaraki, and they are further divided into cities, towns, and villages. The region is home to over 37 million people, with the majority living in the Tokyo metropolitan area, which is one of the largest urban agglomerations in the world.

The administrative structure of Kanto is complex, with each prefecture having its own government and responsibilities. The Tokyo metropolitan area is a special case, as it is not only the capital of Japan but also a major economic and cultural center. The other prefectures in Kanto also have their own unique characteristics, with some being more rural and others being more urbanized. For example, the prefecture of Tochigi is known for its natural beauty, with many national parks and mountains, while the prefecture of Chiba is known for its coastal cities and beaches.
