[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Japan has a well-developed and efficient transportation network, with a wide range of options available to get around, including **public transportation**, **taxis**, **ride-hailing services**, and **driving**. The quality of public transit in Japan is exceptionally high, with a comprehensive network of buses, trains, and subways that cover most areas of the country. Most people in Japan rely on public transportation to get around, with many cities having a well-developed and efficient system.

The main transportation modes in Japan include buses, trains, and subways, with many cities also having a network of bike lanes and walking paths. The country is also home to a number of **high-speed rail lines**, including the famous **Shinkansen**, which connects major cities such as Tokyo, Osaka, and Nagoya. Additionally, many cities have a **congestion charge** in place to reduce traffic congestion and encourage the use of public transportation.
