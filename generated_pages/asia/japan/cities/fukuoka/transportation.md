To get around Fukuoka, you can use a variety of transportation options, including an extensive rail network, buses, and taxis, as well as rent a car or use ride-hailing apps.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Fukuoka is easily accessible by air, with Fukuoka Airport (FUK) being the main international airport serving the city. The airport is located about 3 kilometers east of Fukuoka's city center and offers direct flights to many major cities in Asia, including Tokyo, Seoul, and Hong Kong, as well as some destinations in Europe and North America. Fukuoka can also be reached by bus or train from other cities in Japan, with the **Hakata** train station being a major hub for the **JR Kyushu** rail network.

In addition to flying, Fukuoka can be reached by bus or train from other cities in Japan, with several overland entry points, including the **Hakata** train station and the **Fukuoka** bus terminal. The city is also connected to the nearby city of **Sasebo** by a highway and to the city of **Kitakyushu** by a tunnel under the **Kanmon Strait**. Visitors from other parts of Asia can also reach Fukuoka by ferry, with several routes connecting the city to ports in South Korea and China.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Fukuoka is well-connected to other cities in Japan by domestic flights, with several airlines operating out of Fukuoka Airport, including **Japan Airlines**, **All Nippon Airways**, and **Skymark Airlines**. The main domestic routes from Fukuoka include flights to Tokyo, Osaka, and Sapporo, as well as several smaller cities on the island of Kyushu. Booking domestic flights in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, and can be done online or through a travel agent. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Fukuoka start from around $100 USD for a one-way ticket to Tokyo.

Fukuoka has an extensive rail network, with several train lines connecting the city to other parts of Kyushu and Japan. The **JR Kyushu** rail network is the main operator of train services in Fukuoka, with several lines radiating out from the **Hakata** train station, including the **Kyushu Shinkansen** line, which connects Fukuoka to **Shin-Osaka** and **Tokyo**. The **Nishitetsu** rail network also operates several lines in Fukuoka, including the **Nishitetsu Tenjin-Omuta Line** and the **Nishitetsu Dazaifu Line**. Booking train tickets in advance is recommended, especially for long-distance journeys, and can be done online or at train stations.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Fukuoka has an extensive network of buses and coaches, with several companies operating services within the city and to other parts of Kyushu. [SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Fukuoka, you will need to have a valid **Japanese driver's license** or an **International Driving Permit**.
- Road conditions in Fukuoka are generally good, with several highways and expressways connecting the city to other parts of Kyushu.
- Fuel costs in Fukuoka are generally high, with gasoline prices averaging around $1.50 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies in Fukuoka include **Toyota Rent-a-Car** and **Nissan Rent-a-Car**, with prices starting from around $40 USD per day.
- Hazards to watch out for when driving in Fukuoka include **narrow streets** and **aggressive drivers**.
- Parking in Fukuoka can be challenging, especially in the city center, with prices averaging around $5-$10 USD per hour.

- When using public transportation in Fukuoka, it is a good idea to **buy a prepaid IC card**, such as a **Sugoca** or **Nimoca** card, which can be used on buses and trains.
