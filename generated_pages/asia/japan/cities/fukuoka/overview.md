[SECTION] What Is Fukuoka? [/SECTION]
Fukuoka is a city located on the northern shore of Kyushu, the southernmost of Japan's four main islands. It is situated on the coast of the Genkai Sea and is the largest city on the island of Kyushu. Fukuoka is known for its rich history, cultural landmarks, and traditional Japanese cuisine, including its famous Tonkotsu Ramen and Hakata Gyoza. The city has a population of around 1.5 million people and is a major economic and cultural center in the region.

Fukuoka is a significant cultural and historical center in Japan, with a number of important landmarks and attractions, including the Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine, the Fukuoka Tower, and the Kyushu National Museum. The city is also home to a number of festivals and events throughout the year, including the Hakata Gion Yamakasa festival and the Fukuoka Asian Art Triennale. Fukuoka is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over Japan and around the world.

The city has a long history, dating back to the 6th century, and has been an important center of trade and commerce in the region for many centuries. Today, Fukuoka is a thriving city with a strong economy, a rich cultural scene, and a high quality of life. The city is well-connected to the rest of Japan and the world, with a major international airport and a comprehensive public transportation system.
