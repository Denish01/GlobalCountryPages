# Getting Around Nagoya — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Nagoya, you can use a variety of transportation options, including an extensive rail network, buses, and city subways, as well as taxis and ride-hailing services.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Nagoya is easily accessible by air, with Chubu Centrair International Airport (NGO) being the main entry point for international travelers. The airport is located about 30 miles south of Nagoya city center and offers direct flights to many major Asian cities, as well as some destinations in Europe and North America. You can also fly into Nagoya Airfield (NKM), which is a smaller airport that primarily serves domestic flights. If you prefer to travel by land, you can take a bus or train from other major Japanese cities, such as Tokyo or Osaka, to Nagoya. Japan has a well-developed highway system, and you can also drive to Nagoya from other parts of the country.

For those traveling from outside Japan, the most convenient way to get to Nagoya is by flying into Chubu Centrair International Airport (NGO). The airport is well-connected to the city center by public transportation, including buses and trains. You can also take a taxi or use a ride-hailing service to get to your destination. If you are already in Japan, you can take a bullet train, known as the **Shinkansen**, from other major cities to Nagoya. The trip from Tokyo to Nagoya takes about 1.5 hours, while the trip from Osaka takes about 1 hour.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Japan has a well-developed domestic air network, with many flights connecting Nagoya to other major cities. You can fly from Nagoya to destinations such as Sapporo, Fukuoka, and Okinawa, among others. The main domestic airlines operating in Japan are Japan Airlines (JAL) and All Nippon Airways (ANA), although there are also some smaller regional airlines. To book a domestic flight, you can visit the airline's website or use a travel booking website such as Expedia or Booking.com. The cost of a domestic flight in Japan can vary depending on the route and time of year, but you can expect to pay around $100-$200 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Nagoya has an extensive rail network, with many train lines connecting the city to other parts of Japan. The **Shinkansen** bullet train is the fastest way to travel, with trains running regularly to destinations such as Tokyo, Osaka, and Kyoto. You can also take a local train, known as a **JR train**, to travel to nearby cities and towns. To book a train ticket, you can visit a train station or use a ticket vending machine. You can also book tickets online through the Japan Railways website or a travel booking website. The cost of a train ticket in Japan can vary depending on the route and type of train, but you can expect to pay around $50-$100 for a one-way ticket on a **Shinkansen** train.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are many bus companies operating in Nagoya, offering routes to destinations such as Tokyo, Osaka, and Kyoto. The buses are a more affordable option than trains, with one-way tickets starting from around $20. However, the journey times are often longer than by train, and the buses may not be as frequent. Some of the main bus companies operating in Nagoya include Willer Bus and Japan Bus Lines. You can book a bus ticket online through the bus company's website or at a bus station.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Japan, you will need to obtain an **International Driving Permit** (IDP) if you do not have a Japanese driver's license.
- Japan has a well-developed highway system, with many roads in good condition.
- Fuel costs in Japan are relatively high, with gasoline prices starting from around $5 per liter.
- To rent a car in Nagoya, you can use a car rental company such as Toyota Rent-a-Car or Nissan Rent-a-Car.
- Be aware of the **road rules** in Japan, which include driving on the left-hand side of the road and wearing a seatbelt at all times.
- Be careful when driving in Japan, as the roads can be narrow and winding, especially in rural areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When using public transportation in Nagoya, it's a good idea to **buy a prepaid IC card**, such as a Suica or Pasmo card, which can be used to pay for train and bus fares.
- Be aware of **peak travel times**, such as rush hour, when trains and buses may be more crowded.
- When taking a taxi, make sure to **check the meter** to ensure you are not being overcharged.
- Consider **downloading a transportation app**, such as Google Maps or Hyperdia, to help you navigate the city's transportation system.
- Be careful of **scams** targeting tourists, such as overpriced taxi rides or fake train tickets.
- When using a ride-hailing service, make sure to **check the driver's ID** and license plate number before getting in the car.
- Consider **purchasing travel insurance** to cover any unexpected transportation costs or accidents.
