[SECTION] What Is Nagoya? [/SECTION]
Nagoya is a city located in the central part of Honshu, the largest island of Japan. It is situated in the Aichi Prefecture and serves as the prefecture's capital. With a population of over 2.3 million people, Nagoya is Japan's third-most populous city, after Tokyo and Yokohama. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its historic landmarks, museums, and traditional festivals. Nagoya is also a major industrial and economic center, with a strong focus on the automotive and aerospace sectors.

Geographically, Nagoya is positioned in a strategic location, with easy access to the Pacific Ocean and the Japanese mainland. The city is surrounded by mountains to the east and west, and the Ise Bay to the south. This unique geography has played a significant role in shaping the city's history and development. Nagoya has a humid subtropical climate, with hot summers and mild winters, making it an attractive destination for tourists and business travelers alike.

Nagoya is also known for its unique cultural identity, which is reflected in its cuisine, festivals, and traditions. The city is famous for its **Hitsumabushi**, a traditional dish made from grilled eel, and its **Tenmusu**, a type of tempura-filled rice ball. Nagoya is also home to several historic landmarks, including the **Nagoya Castle**, which dates back to the 17th century, and the **Atsuta Shrine**, which is one of the most important Shinto shrines in Japan.
