Kobe is situated in the Kansai region of Japan, on the southern coast of Honshu, the largest island in the Japanese archipelago. The city is bordered by the Osaka Bay to the south, and the Rokko Mountains to the north, with the Akashi Strait separating it from Awaji Island to the southwest. Kobe's strategic location has made it an important port city, with trade and cultural exchange playing a significant role in its history and development. The city is also close to other major urban centers, including Osaka and Kyoto, making it an attractive destination for tourists and business travelers alike.
