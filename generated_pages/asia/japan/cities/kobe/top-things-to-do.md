# Top Things to Do in Kobe — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Kobe, visit its iconic landmarks, indulge in unique experiences, and explore its rich cultural heritage, from historic temples to scenic hiking trails.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Kobe Wine Tour** to learn about the city's wine production and taste local wines
- Visit the **Kobe Earthquake Memorial Museum** to learn about the city's recovery from the 1995 earthquake
- Explore the **Kobe Harborland** area, a popular spot for shopping and entertainment
- Attend a **Kobe Jazz Festival** to enjoy live music performances
- Try **Kobe Beef** at a local restaurant, known for its high quality and unique flavor
- Take a **Stroll along the Kobe Waterfront**, a scenic area with beautiful views of the bay
- Visit the **Kobe Fashion Museum**, a unique museum showcasing fashion from around the world

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Hike the **Rokko Mountains**, which offer scenic views of the city and surrounding landscape
- Visit the **Kobe Nunobiki Herb Gardens**, which feature a variety of herbs and stunning views
- Explore the **Arima Onsen** hot spring resort, located in a beautiful natural setting
- Relax on **Suma Beach**, a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing
- Take a **Kobe Bay Cruise**, which offers scenic views of the city and bay

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Kobe City Museum**, which features exhibits on the city's history and culture
- Explore the **Kitano-cho** historic district, which features historic Western-style houses and museums
- Attend a **Traditional Japanese Tea Ceremony** at a local temple or cultural center
- Visit the **Kobe Fashion Museum**, which showcases fashion from around the world
- Explore the **Ikuta Shrine**, a historic Shinto shrine with beautiful gardens
- Take a **Calligraphy Class**, where you can learn about Japanese calligraphy and create your own artwork

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Kobe Brick Warehouse**, a historic warehouse that now features shops and restaurants
- Explore the **Harborland** area, which offers shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- Take a **Walk along the Kobe River**, which offers scenic views of the city and surrounding landscape
- Visit the **Kobe Nunobiki Falls**, a beautiful waterfall located in the Rokko Mountains
- Attend a **Local Festival**, such as the Kobe Festival or the Suma Beach Festival

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Osaka**, located about 30 minutes from Kobe by train, to visit its famous castle and entertainment district
- Visit **Himeji**, located about 1 hour from Kobe by train, to see its famous castle and historic district
- Explore **Nara**, located about 1.5 hours from Kobe by train, to visit its historic temples and see its famous deer
- Take a day trip to **Kyoto**, located about 1 hour from Kobe by train, to visit its famous temples and gardens
- Visit **Hiroshima**, located about 2.5 hours from Kobe by train, to visit its peace memorial park and museum

[SECTION] Planning Tips [/SECTION]

Key tips for booking and prioritizing: book accommodations and restaurants in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, and consider purchasing a **Kobe Tourist Card** for discounts and convenience.
