# Top Things to Do in Hiroshima — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Hiroshima, the top things to do include exploring the city's historic landmarks, experiencing its unique culture, and visiting nearby natural attractions, with must-see sites like the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum, and the famous Miyajima Island.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Hiroshima-style Okonomiyaki** cooking class to learn how to make the city's famous savory pancake
- Visit the **Hiroshima Toyo Carp** baseball stadium to watch a live game and experience the local sports culture
- Explore the **Hon-dori Shopping Arcade**, a historic shopping street filled with local shops and restaurants
- Attend a **Hiroshima Peace Memorial Ceremony** to pay respects to the victims of the atomic bombing
- Take a **Miyajima Island Sunset Cruise** to see the famous island from a different perspective
- Visit the **Hiroshima Orizuru Tower** at night to see the city's skyline illuminated
- Experience the **Hiroshima Food Culture** by trying local specialties like okonomiyaki, carp sashimi, and momiji manju

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Miyajima Island** to see the famous **Itsukushima Shrine** and enjoy the island's natural beauty
- Explore the **Shukkeien Garden**, a beautiful Japanese garden with a peaceful atmosphere
- Hike to the top of **Mount Misen**, the highest peak on Miyajima Island, for stunning views of the surrounding area
- Visit the **Hiroshima Botanical Garden**, a beautiful garden featuring a wide variety of plants and flowers
- Take a stroll along the **Kyobashi River**, a scenic river that runs through the heart of Hiroshima

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum** to learn about the history of the atomic bombing
- Explore the **Hiroshima Castle**, a historic castle that now serves as a museum
- Attend a **Traditional Japanese Tea Ceremony** at the **Hiroshima Castle** or the **Shukkeien Garden**
- Visit the **Mitaki-dera Temple**, a historic temple famous for its beautiful gardens and architecture
- Take a **Calligraphy Class** to learn about Japanese writing and art
- Explore the **Hiroshima Museum of Art**, a museum featuring a collection of modern and contemporary Japanese art

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Kamiyacho** district, a historic neighborhood filled with old shops and restaurants
- Explore the **Nakajima-chou** district, a charming neighborhood with a mix of old and new shops and restaurants
- Take a stroll along the **Honkawa River**, a scenic river that runs through the heart of Hiroshima
- Visit the **Hiroshima City Museum of History and Traditional Crafts**, a museum showcasing the city's history and traditional crafts
- Attend a **Local Festival**, such as the Hiroshima Flower Festival or the Miyajima Island Festival

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Miyajima Island** (10 km, 30 minutes by ferry), famous for its scenic beauty and historic landmarks
- Visit **Okayama** (120 km, 2 hours by train), a city known for its beautiful gardens and historic castle
- Explore **Kurashiki** (120 km, 2 hours by train), a charming town with a well-preserved historic district
- Take a day trip to **Onomichi** (100 km, 1.5 hours by train), a scenic town with a beautiful harbor and historic temples
- Visit **Iwakuni** (50 km, 1 hour by train), a city famous for its historic castle and scenic parks
