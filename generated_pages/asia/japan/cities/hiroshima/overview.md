[SECTION] What Is Hiroshima? [/SECTION]
Hiroshima is a city situated in the western part of Honshu, the largest island of Japan. It is the capital of Hiroshima Prefecture and has a population of approximately 1.2 million people. The city is located on the coast of the Seto Inland Sea and is known for its tragic history, particularly the atomic bombing in 1945. Today, Hiroshima is a thriving city that has risen from the ashes, with a strong focus on peace, culture, and tourism. The city is home to many historical landmarks, museums, and cultural attractions, including the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and the Atomic Bomb Dome, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Hiroshima is also known for its natural beauty, with numerous parks, gardens, and scenic spots along the coast. The city has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of traditional and modern Japanese culture. Visitors can experience the local cuisine, which includes popular dishes such as okonomiyaki and carp, as well as traditional Japanese festivals and events. Hiroshima is also an important economic center, with a strong focus on industry, technology, and innovation.

The city's somber history and vibrant culture make it a unique and fascinating destination for visitors. From its historic landmarks to its modern attractions, Hiroshima has something to offer for everyone. Whether you're interested in history, culture, food, or entertainment, Hiroshima is a city that will leave a lasting impression.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1589 - Hiroshima was founded as a castle town by the daimyo Mori Terumoto.
2. 1868 - The city became a major commercial center during the Meiji period.
3. 1945 - Hiroshima was devastated by the atomic bombing on August 6, which killed an estimated 70,000 to 80,000 people immediately.
4. 1949 - The Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park was established to commemorate the victims of the atomic bombing.
5. 1996 - The Atomic Bomb Dome was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
6. 2016 - The city hosted the G7 Summit, which brought international attention to Hiroshima's role in promoting peace and nuclear disarmament.
7. 2020 - Hiroshima celebrated the 75th anniversary of the atomic bombing, with various events and ceremonies held throughout the city.
