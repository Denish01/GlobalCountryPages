# Getting Around Yokohama — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Yokohama, you can use a combination of efficient public transportation options, including trains, buses, and subways, as well as convenient ride-hailing services and taxis.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Yokohama is located near two major international airports, Narita International Airport and Haneda Airport, both of which serve as hubs for numerous domestic and international flights. Narita International Airport is located about 60 kilometers away from Yokohama, while Haneda Airport is about 30 kilometers away. Direct flights to these airports are available from major cities around the world, making it relatively easy to get to Yokohama. From the airports, you can take trains, buses, or taxis to get to Yokohama.

Overland entry points to Yokohama include bus and train stations that connect the city to other parts of Japan. The **Tokaido Shinkansen** bullet train line, for example, connects Yokohama to other major cities in Japan, including Tokyo, Osaka, and Nagoya. Yokohama is also accessible by bus from other parts of Japan, with several bus companies operating routes to and from the city. Additionally, Yokohama has a major port that serves as a hub for international shipping and cruise lines, making it possible to enter the city by sea.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
While Yokohama does not have its own airport, the nearby Haneda Airport and Narita International Airport offer extensive domestic flight networks. Domestic airlines such as **Japan Airlines** and **All Nippon Airways** operate flights to and from these airports, connecting Yokohama to other parts of Japan. To book domestic flights, you can use the airlines' websites or visit a travel agency. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the route and time of year, but you can expect to pay around $100-$300 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Yokohama has an extensive rail network that connects the city to other parts of Japan. The **Tokaido Shinkansen** bullet train line is a key route that connects Yokohama to Tokyo, Osaka, and Nagoya, with frequent departures throughout the day. Other rail lines, such as the **JR Yokohama Line** and the **Minato Mirai Line**, connect Yokohama to surrounding cities and neighborhoods. To book train tickets, you can use ticket vending machines at train stations or visit a ticket counter. You can also use the **Japan Rail Pass** to travel on multiple trains and routes.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus companies such as **Odakyu Bus** and **Keikyu Bus** operate routes to and from Yokohama, connecting the city to other parts of Japan. These buses offer a more affordable alternative to trains, with approximate costs ranging from $10-$50 for a one-way ticket. The quality of the buses is generally good, with comfortable seating and amenities such as air conditioning and restrooms. However, the routes and schedules may be limited compared to trains, so it's a good idea to plan ahead and book your tickets in advance.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Yokohama, you will need to obtain an **International Driving Permit** or a Japanese driver's license.
- Road conditions in Yokohama are generally good, with well-maintained highways and roads.
- Fuel costs in Japan are relatively high, with prices ranging from $1.50-$2.50 per liter.
- Car rental companies such as **Toyota Rent-a-Car** and **Nippon Rent-a-Car** operate in Yokohama, with prices starting from around $50-$100 per day.
- When driving in Yokohama, be aware of the **left-hand traffic** rules and be prepared for congested roads and parking challenges.
- It's also a good idea to have a GPS or mapping app on your phone to help navigate the city's complex road network.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When using public transportation in Yokohama, it's a good idea to **buy a prepaid IC card** such as **Suica** or **Pasmo** to make travel more convenient.
- Be aware of **rush hour** times (usually 7-9 am and 5-7 pm) when using public transportation, as trains and buses can be very crowded.
- When taking a taxi, make sure to **check the meter** to ensure you are not being overcharged.
- Download the **Google Maps** or **Hyperdia** app to help navigate Yokohama's transportation network.
- Be prepared for **language barriers** when using public transportation, as not all signs and announcements may be in English.
- Consider **purchasing a Japan Rail Pass** if you plan to travel extensively by train.
- Be mindful of **pickpocketing** and **scams** in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.
