[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing in crowded areas, such as train stations and tourist hotspots
- Overcharging by some taxi drivers, so make sure to use a metered taxi
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake souvenir shops or street performers asking for money
- Crowded and noisy areas, such as Susukino, which can be overwhelming for some visitors
- Natural disasters, such as earthquakes and heavy snowfall, which can cause disruptions to travel plans
- Food poisoning from eating undercooked or raw food, especially from street vendors
- Getting lost in the city, especially for those who don't speak Japanese, so make sure to carry a map or use a GPS device

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Sapporo, but it's always a good idea to make sure your routine vaccinations are up to date
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is safe to drink in Sapporo, but bottled water is also widely available
- **Medical Facilities:** Sapporo has high-quality medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, and many medical professionals speak some English
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are easy to find and well-stocked, with many medications available over the counter

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas, and keep an eye on your belongings
- Use a money belt or a secure wallet to protect your valuables
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas at night
- Use a reputable taxi company or ride-sharing service, and always check the meter is on
- Learn some basic Japanese phrases, such as "konnichiwa" (hello) and "arigatou" (thank you)
- Carry a map or use a GPS device to navigate the city
- Be prepared for natural disasters, such as earthquakes and heavy snowfall, by having a plan and staying informed

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Sapporo is a very safe city for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit or deserted areas
- Use a reputable taxi company or ride-sharing service, and always check the meter is on
- Consider staying in a well-lit and populated area, such as the city center or a tourist district, for added safety and convenience
