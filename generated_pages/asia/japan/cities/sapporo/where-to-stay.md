# Where to Stay in Sapporo — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Sapporo, the best areas to stay are in the city center, including neighborhoods such as **Sapporo Station**, **Odori**, and **Susukino**, which offer a range of accommodations and easy access to public transportation.

For each of the top 5-7 areas/neighborhoods:

**Sapporo Station** (Sapporo)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: Located in the heart of the city, Sapporo Station is a bustling area with a wide range of amenities and easy access to public transportation. The area is surrounded by high-end hotels, restaurants, and shops.
Price Range: $150-$300/night
Pros: Convenient location, wide range of amenities, easy access to public transportation
Cons: Can be noisy, expensive

**Odori** (Sapporo)
Best For: Family / Backpacker
Vibe: Odori is a lively area in the city center, known for its beautiful park and vibrant atmosphere. The area is surrounded by restaurants, shops, and entertainment options.
Price Range: $80-$200/night
Pros: Central location, beautiful park, wide range of amenities
Cons: Can be crowded, limited budget options

**Susukino** (Sapporo)
Best For: Luxury / Nightlife
Vibe: Susukino is Sapporo's entertainment district, known for its vibrant nightlife, restaurants, and bars. The area is popular with tourists and locals alike.
Price Range: $100-$250/night
Pros: Vibrant nightlife, wide range of restaurants and bars, convenient location
Cons: Can be noisy, expensive

**Maruyama** (Sapporo)
Best For: Family / Nature
Vibe: Maruyama is a quiet residential area located near the Maruyama Zoo and the Sapporo Maruyama Ski Resort. The area is surrounded by beautiful parks and nature reserves.
Price Range: $60-$150/night
Pros: Quiet location, beautiful parks, easy access to nature reserves
Cons: Limited amenities, far from city center

**Kita** (Sapporo)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: Kita is a bustling commercial area located in the north of the city. The area is surrounded by high-end hotels, restaurants, and shops.
Price Range: $120-$280/night
Pros: Convenient location, wide range of amenities, easy access to public transportation
Cons: Can be noisy, expensive

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak travel seasons (December to March, July to August)
- Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals
- Consider staying in a guesthouse or hostel for a more local experience
- Negotiate prices directly with hotel staff or owners for long-term stays or off-season bookings
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with higher rates during peak travel seasons
- Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas at night, especially in the Susukino district
- Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas such as train stations and shopping districts
- Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in tourist areas
- Use reputable taxi companies or public transportation to get around the city
- Stay informed about local events and festivals, which can affect traffic and accommodation prices
- Consider purchasing a prepaid IC card for convenient travel on public transportation
