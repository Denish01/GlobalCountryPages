[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Osaka is situated in the Kansai region of Japan, on the island of Honshu, and is bordered by the prefectures of Kyoto, Nara, Hyogo, and Wakayama. The city is located on the Osaka Bay, which is connected to the Seto Inland Sea, and is approximately 550 kilometers west of Tokyo, the capital city of Japan. Osaka is a major urban center and is considered one of the most populous cities in Japan, with a population of over 2.7 million people.

The city's location on the Osaka Bay provides access to the sea and has played a significant role in its development as a major port city. The surrounding prefectures of Kyoto, Nara, and Hyogo are also known for their rich cultural heritage and historical landmarks, making the Kansai region a popular tourist destination. The city's proximity to these areas has contributed to its growth as a major economic and cultural center in western Japan.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Osaka is a relatively flat city, with most of its area consisting of low-lying plains and coastal regions. The city's terrain is characterized by the presence of several rivers, including the Dojima, Tosabori, and Okawa rivers, which flow through the city and empty into the Osaka Bay. The surrounding mountains, including the Rokko Mountains to the north and the Izumi Mountains to the south, provide a scenic backdrop to the city and offer opportunities for hiking and outdoor recreation.

The city's landscape is also dominated by its urban sprawl, with a mix of modern skyscrapers, historic temples, and traditional neighborhoods. The Osaka Castle, a major landmark and popular tourist destination, is situated in the center of the city and is surrounded by a large park and moat. The city's coastal regions are also home to several beaches and waterfront areas, which are popular with locals and tourists alike.

The urban landscape of Osaka is also characterized by its vibrant entertainment districts, including the Dotonbori and Shinsekai areas, which are known for their neon lights, street food, and lively atmosphere. The city's many parks and gardens, including the Osaka Castle Park and the Sumiyoshi Park, provide a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
