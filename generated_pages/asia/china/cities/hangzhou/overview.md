[SECTION] What Is Hangzhou? [/SECTION]
Hangzhou is a major city located in the Yangtze River Delta region of China, about 150 kilometers southwest of Shanghai. It is situated on the banks of the Qiantang River and is surrounded by rolling hills and scenic lakes, including the famous West Lake. As the capital of Zhejiang Province, Hangzhou is a hub of politics, economy, and culture, with a long history dating back over 2,200 years. The city is known for its stunning natural beauty, with numerous parks, gardens, and scenic areas, as well as its rich cultural heritage, including traditional Chinese architecture, art, and cuisine.

Hangzhou is also an important center of trade and commerce, with a strong focus on technology, manufacturing, and tourism. The city is home to many major companies, including Alibaba Group, one of the world's largest e-commerce companies, and is a popular destination for business travelers and tourists alike. With its unique blend of traditional and modern culture, stunning natural beauty, and thriving economy, Hangzhou is a city that has something to offer for everyone.

In terms of its sovereignty status, Hangzhou is a prefecture-level city under the administration of Zhejiang Province, with a certain degree of autonomy in terms of economic and social development. However, as a city in China, Hangzhou is subject to the overall governance and policies of the Chinese central government.
