[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Hangzhou is a popular tourist destination in eastern China, known for its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and modern infrastructure. The city has a strong focus on tourism, with many attractions and amenities catering to visitors from around the world. Overall, Hangzhou is considered a safe city, with low crime rates compared to other major cities in China. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. Visitors should be mindful of pickpocketing and scams, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, and take necessary measures to protect themselves and their belongings.

In general, Hangzhou is a welcoming and friendly city, with a strong emphasis on hospitality and customer service. The city has a well-developed public transportation system, and many locals are eager to help visitors navigate the city and its attractions. However, language barriers can sometimes be a challenge, and visitors may encounter difficulties communicating with locals, particularly outside of tourist areas. Despite these challenges, Hangzhou is generally a safe and enjoyable destination for tourists, with many opportunities to experience the city's unique culture, history, and natural beauty.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake taxis or tour operators
- Overcharging by vendors or service providers
- Traffic accidents, particularly for pedestrians and cyclists
- Food poisoning from eating at unlicensed or unsanitary restaurants
- ATM scams, such as card skimming or fake ATMs
- Online scams, such as fake hotel bookings or tour packages

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Recommended vaccinations include hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and typhoid fever
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not considered safe to drink, and visitors are advised to use bottled or filtered water
- **Medical Facilities:** Hangzhou has many high-quality medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, with some offering English-language services
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, with many offering a range of medications and healthcare products

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your belongings, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Use licensed taxis or ride-hailing services, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or mobile payments instead
- Be cautious when eating at street stalls or unlicensed restaurants
- Avoid traveling alone at night, particularly in unfamiliar areas
- Keep your hotel room door locked, and use the hotel's safe to store valuables
- Be prepared for crowds and long lines at popular attractions

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your surroundings, particularly at night, and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Avoid accepting drinks or invitations from strangers, and be cautious when interacting with locals
- Consider joining a group tour or using a reputable tour operator to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience
