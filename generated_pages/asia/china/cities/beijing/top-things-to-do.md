# Top Things to Do in Beijing — Attractions & Experiences

Beijing, the capital of China, offers a plethora of exciting activities and experiences that cater to different interests and preferences, making it a must-visit destination for travelers from around the world.

[SECTION] Top Attractions [/SECTION]
[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Forbidden City | Dongcheng District | Palace | The largest ancient palace complex in the world, a **UNESCO World Heritage Site** |
| Great Wall of China | Badaling, Mutianyu, and other locations | Fortification | One of the **Seven Wonders of the Medieval World**, a must-visit iconic landmark |
| Temple of Heaven | Dongcheng District | Temple | A beautiful example of **Chinese architecture**, where emperors once prayed for peace and prosperity |
| Summer Palace | Haidian District | Palace | A stunning complex of gardens, lakes, and palaces, a popular retreat for royalty and locals alike |
| Ming Tombs | Changping District | Mausoleum | The final resting place of 13 **Ming dynasty** emperors, a historic site with great cultural significance |
| Tiananmen Square | Dongcheng District | Square | A large public square in the heart of Beijing, surrounded by iconic landmarks like the **Mausoleum of Mao Zedong** |
| Beijing Zoo | Xicheng District | Zoo | Home to a wide range of animals, including the giant panda, a symbol of China |
| National Museum of China | Dongcheng District | Museum | A world-class museum showcasing China's history, art, and culture, with a vast collection of artifacts |
[/TABLE]

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **rickshaw tour** through the narrow alleys of the old city, known as hutongs, to experience the traditional way of life in Beijing
- Enjoy a **Peking opera performance** at the Liyuan Theater, a classic art form that combines music, dance, and acrobatics
- Visit a **traditional tea house** to learn about China's tea culture and sample some of the finest teas in the country
- Explore the **Panjiayuan Antique Market**, a vast marketplace where you can find rare and unique souvenirs
- Learn how to make **Chinese dumplings** at a local cooking class, a fun and interactive way to experience the local cuisine
- Take a **kung fu class** with a local master, a great way to learn about this ancient martial art and get some exercise

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Jingshan Park**, a beautiful park with stunning views of the Forbidden City and the surrounding cityscape
- Hike the **Great Wall of China** at Mutianyu, a less crowded and more scenic section of the wall
- Visit the **Beijing Botanical Garden**, a peaceful oasis in the heart of the city, home to a wide range of plant species
- Take a **boat ride** on the **Kunming Lake**, a serene and picturesque lake in the Summer Palace complex
- Go **skiing** at the **Nanshan Ski Village**, a popular ski resort with world-class facilities and stunning scenery

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum of China** to learn about China's rich history and culture
- Explore the **Temple of Confucius**, a historic temple dedicated to the great philosopher and educator
- Attend a **traditional Chinese music performance** at the **Beijing Concert Hall**, a great way to experience the local music scene
- Take a **calligraphy class** with a local artist, a unique way to learn about this ancient art form
- Visit the **Beijing Folklore Museum**, a museum showcasing the traditional customs and folk arts of Beijing

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Wudaoying Hutong**, a charming and historic alleyway with a mix of old and new shops and restaurants
- Visit the **Beijing 798 Art District**, a thriving arts community with galleries, studios, and street art
- Take a **stroll along the **Liulichang Street**, a historic street lined with traditional shops, tea houses, and restaurants
- Go **shopping** at the **Silk Street Market**, a popular marketplace with a wide range of goods, from silk to souvenirs
- Visit the **Dongyue Temple**, a historic temple with a unique blend of Taoist and Buddhist architecture

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to the **Great Wall of China** at Badaling, about 45 miles northwest of Beijing, a 1-2 hour drive
- Visit the **Ming Tombs**, about 30 miles northwest of Beijing, a 1-hour drive
- Explore the **Chengde Summer Resort**, about 150 miles northeast of Beijing, a 3-4 hour drive
- Go **hiking** at the **Jinshanling Great Wall**, about 75 miles northeast of Beijing, a 2-3 hour drive
- Visit the **Tianjin City**, about 75 miles southeast of Beijing, a 1-2 hour drive or train ride
