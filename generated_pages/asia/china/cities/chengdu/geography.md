# Chengdu Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Chengdu is situated in the center of the Sichuan Province, in the western part of China. The city is surrounded by the Sichuan Basin and the Tibetan Plateau, with the Jinsha River and the Min River flowing through it. Chengdu is a landlocked city, with no direct access to the ocean, but it is connected to the rest of China through a network of highways, railways, and waterways. The city's strategic location has made it an important hub for trade and commerce in western China, with connections to neighboring provinces such as Shaanxi, Gansu, and Yunnan.

The city's location in the Sichuan Basin has also made it prone to earthquakes, with several major seismic events occurring in the region over the centuries. However, Chengdu's unique geography has also created a fertile and productive agricultural region, with the city being known for its production of grains, vegetables, and fruits. The surrounding mountains and rivers have also made Chengdu a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with opportunities for hiking, skiing, and water sports.
