# Getting Around Chengdu — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Chengdu, you can utilize a variety of transportation options, including the city's international airport, domestic flights, trains, buses, and public transportation systems, as well as ride-hailing apps and taxis.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Chengdu is a major transportation hub in western China, with Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU) serving as the primary gateway to the city. The airport is located about 16 kilometers southwest of the city center and offers direct flights to numerous domestic and international destinations, including Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Hong Kong, and several cities in Southeast Asia, Europe, and North America. Travelers can also enter Chengdu by train or bus from neighboring cities and provinces, such as Chongqing, Xi'an, and Lhasa.

In addition to its airport, Chengdu has several major railway stations, including Chengdu East Railway Station and Chengdu South Railway Station, which provide convenient access to other parts of China. The city is also connected to the rest of the country by a network of highways and expressways, making it easily accessible by car or bus. For travelers entering Chengdu from neighboring countries, such as Nepal or Tibet, there are also several overland entry points, including the Chengdu-Kunming Highway and the Sichuan-Tibet Highway.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Chengdu has an extensive domestic air network, with flights connecting the city to most major cities in China. Domestic airlines such as China Eastern, China Southern, and Sichuan Airlines operate frequent flights to destinations like Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen. Travelers can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through a travel agency. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Chengdu to other major cities in China range from $100 to $500 USD, depending on the destination and time of year. It's a good idea to book in advance to secure the best prices, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Chengdu has an extensive rail network, with high-speed trains connecting the city to other major cities in China. The city is a key stop on the Beijing-Guangzhou-Shenzhen high-speed rail line, with frequent departures to destinations like Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. Travelers can book train tickets online through the China Railway Corporation's website or through a travel agency. The high-speed trains are a convenient and relatively affordable way to travel, with approximate costs ranging from $50 to $200 USD, depending on the destination and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Chengdu has a well-developed intercity bus network, with several major bus companies operating routes to neighboring cities and provinces. The buses are a relatively affordable way to travel, with approximate costs ranging from $10 to $50 USD, depending on the destination and class of service. However, the buses may not be as convenient as the trains, as they often make multiple stops along the way and may be subject to traffic delays.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Chengdu, you will need to obtain a Chinese driver's license, as international licenses are not recognized.
- The road conditions in Chengdu are generally good, with well-maintained highways and expressways.
- Fuel costs in Chengdu are relatively affordable, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies like Hertz and Avis have offices in Chengdu, but it's often more convenient to hire a driver or use public transportation.
- Hazards to watch out for when driving in Chengdu include aggressive drivers, pedestrians, and cyclists.
- It's also important to be aware of the local traffic laws and regulations, such as the requirement to wear a seatbelt and use headlights at night.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of scams targeting tourists, such as overpriced taxis or fake train tickets.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps to avoid being overcharged.
- Download the Didi Chuxing or AutoNavi app to hail a ride or book a taxi.
- Learn some basic Chinese phrases, such as "hello" (nǐ hǎo) and "thank you" (xiè xiè), to help you navigate.
- Be prepared for crowds and long lines at transportation hubs, especially during peak travel seasons.
- Consider purchasing a **China SIM card** or portable Wi-Fi hotspot to stay connected and access maps and transportation information.
- Always carry a **map or guidebook** to help you navigate the city and find your way around.
- Be mindful of your **personal belongings** and keep a close eye on your luggage, especially in crowded areas.
