[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan Province in China, is a popular tourist destination known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and delicious cuisine. The city has a relatively low crime rate, with most crimes being petty theft and scams. Visitors can feel safe walking around the city, even at night, as the streets are generally well-lit and populated. However, as with any city, it's essential to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

Chengdu is also prone to natural disasters, such as earthquakes and floods, due to its location in the Sichuan Basin. While the city has a well-developed emergency response system, it's crucial for visitors to be prepared and stay informed about weather conditions and other potential hazards. Additionally, the city's air quality can be a concern, especially during the winter months, so visitors with respiratory issues should take necessary precautions. Overall, Chengdu is a safe and welcoming city for tourists, but it's essential to be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Pickpocketing**: Be aware of your belongings in crowded areas, such as public transportation and tourist hotspots.
- **Scams**: Be cautious of overly friendly locals who may try to sell you fake goods or services.
- **Traffic accidents**: Be careful when crossing streets, as traffic rules are not always followed.
- **Food poisoning**: Eat at reputable restaurants and avoid undercooked or raw food.
- **ATM scams**: Use ATMs located in secure areas, such as banks or hotels, and be aware of your surroundings.
- **Overcharging**: Be aware of prices and negotiate when necessary, especially when using taxis or buying goods from street vendors.
- **Theft**: Keep a close eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or public transportation.
- **Natural disasters**: Stay informed about weather conditions and follow evacuation instructions if necessary.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: Recommended vaccinations include hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and typhoid fever.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities**: Chengdu has well-equipped hospitals and medical facilities, but medical care can be expensive, and insurance is recommended.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacies are widely available, and many medications can be purchased over-the-counter.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep a close eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or mobile payments instead.
- Stay informed about local conditions and follow local advice.
- Learn some basic Chinese phrases, such as "hello" (nǐ hǎo) and "thank you" (xiè xiè).
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuable items.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in poorly lit or deserted areas.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuable items.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites.
- Consider joining a group tour or finding a travel buddy to explore the city with.
