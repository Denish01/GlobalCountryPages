Getting There

Wuhan Tianhe International Airport is the main airport serving the city, with direct flights to many domestic and international destinations. The airport is located about 26 kilometers northwest of the city center and is connected to the city by a highway and several bus routes. You can also take a taxi or ride-hailing app from the airport to your destination. If you are coming from other parts of China, you can also take a train or bus to Wuhan, with several major bus stations and train stations located throughout the city.

For international travelers, Wuhan Tianhe International Airport offers direct flights to many major cities in Asia, Europe, and North America. You can also fly into other major airports in China, such as Beijing Capital International Airport or Shanghai Pudong International Airport, and then take a domestic flight or train to Wuhan. Overland entry points include several major highways and bus routes that connect Wuhan to other cities in China, as well as a few international border crossings with neighboring countries.

Domestic Flights

Domestic flights are a convenient way to get to Wuhan from other parts of China, with many major airlines operating flights to and from Wuhan Tianhe International Airport. Some of the main domestic airlines operating in China include China Eastern, China Southern, and Air China, and they offer flights to many major cities throughout the country. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the route and time of year. Approximate costs for domestic flights can range from $50 to $200 or more, depending on the distance and demand.

Trains & Rail

Wuhan has an extensive rail network, with several major train stations located throughout the city. The Wuhan Railway Station is the main train station, and it offers high-speed trains to many major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. You can book train tickets online through the China Railway Corporation's website or through a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service. High-speed trains are a convenient and relatively affordable way to get to Wuhan from other parts of China, with approximate costs ranging from $50 to $200 or more, depending on the distance and class of service.

Buses & Coaches

Intercity buses are another option for getting to Wuhan from other parts of China, with several major bus companies operating routes to and from the city. The main bus stations in Wuhan include the Wuhan Bus Station and the Hankou Bus Station, and they offer buses to many major cities throughout the country. You can book bus tickets online through the bus companies' websites or through a travel agency, and prices vary depending on the route and class of service. Approximate costs for intercity buses can range from $20 to $100 or more, depending on the distance and demand.

Driving

  • To drive in Wuhan, you will need to obtain a Chinese driver's license, which can be done through the Wuhan Traffic Police Department.
  • Road conditions in Wuhan are generally good, with many major highways and roads well-maintained.
  • Fuel costs in Wuhan are relatively affordable, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 per liter.
  • Car rental companies are available in Wuhan, including international companies like Hertz and Avis, as well as local companies.
  • When driving in Wuhan, be aware of the traffic rules and regulations, including the requirement to drive on the right-hand side of the road.
  • Be sure to check the road conditions and weather forecast before driving, especially during the winter months when roads can be slippery.

  • Be sure to download the Didi Chuxing ride-hailing app, which is widely used in Wuhan and can be a convenient way to get around the city.
  • When taking a taxi, be sure to use a meter and agree on the fare before you start your journey.
