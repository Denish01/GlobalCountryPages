Shenzhen is a major city in Guangdong Province, China, known for its rapid economic growth, technological innovation, and cultural diversity.

[SECTION] What Is Shenzhen? [/SECTION]
Shenzhen is a sub-provincial city located in the southern part of Guangdong Province, China. It is situated near the Pearl River Delta and borders the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region to the south. Shenzhen is known for its stunning economic growth, transforming from a small fishing village to a modern metropolis in just a few decades. The city is a major hub for technology, finance, and international trade, earning it the nickname "China's Silicon Valley." Shenzhen is also a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors with its theme parks, beaches, and cultural attractions.

Shenzhen's strategic location and business-friendly environment have made it an attractive location for foreign investment and entrepreneurship. The city is home to many top Chinese technology companies, including Huawei, Tencent, and DJI, and is a major center for innovation and startups. Shenzhen's population is diverse, with people from all over China and the world living and working in the city. The city's cultural scene is vibrant, with a mix of traditional Chinese and modern influences.

Shenzhen's history is closely tied to its location and economic development. The city's proximity to Hong Kong and its strategic position on the Pearl River Delta made it an attractive location for trade and commerce. In 1979, the Chinese government designated Shenzhen as one of the country's first Special Economic Zones (SEZs), which led to rapid economic growth and development.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 214 BC - The area now known as Shenzhen is first inhabited during the Qin Dynasty.
2. 1573 - The Ming Dynasty establishes a customs office in the area to collect taxes on trade with Hong Kong and Macau.
3. 1898 - The British lease the New Territories, including the area now known as Shenzhen, from China for 99 years.
4. 1979 - The Chinese government designates Shenzhen as one of the country's first Special Economic Zones (SEZs), leading to rapid economic growth and development.
5. 1980 - The Shenzhen Special Economic Zone is officially established, with a focus on export-oriented manufacturing and foreign investment.
6. 1997 - The British return the New Territories, including Hong Kong, to China, and Shenzhen becomes a major economic and cultural center in the region.
7. 2010 - The Chinese government launches the Qianhai Cooperation Zone, a new economic development project aimed at strengthening ties between Shenzhen and Hong Kong.

[SECTION] What Makes Shenzhen Unique [/SECTION]
- Shenzhen is home to the **Shenzhen Stock Exchange**, one of China's two main stock exchanges, and a major center for finance and investment.
- The city is a hub for **technology and innovation**, with many top Chinese technology companies, including Huawei and Tencent, headquartered there.
- Shenzhen is a major **manufacturing center**, with a focus on export-oriented production, particularly in the electronics and textiles sectors.
- The city is known for its **theme parks and tourist attractions**, including the popular **Window of the World** and **Splendid China Folk Village**.
- Shenzhen has a **diverse cultural scene**, with a mix of traditional Chinese and modern influences, and a vibrant arts and entertainment community.
