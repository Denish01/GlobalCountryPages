# Where to Stay in Shenzhen — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Shenzhen, the best areas to stay include Futian, Luohu, Nanshan, and Yantian, which offer a mix of convenience, amenities, and access to popular attractions. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Futian** (Shenzhen)
Best For: Business travelers
Vibe: Futian is a bustling commercial district with a mix of modern skyscrapers and traditional markets, offering a unique blend of old and new. The area is known for its vibrant nightlife and diverse dining options.
Price Range: $80-200/night
Pros: Conveniently located near the Futian Border Crossing, easy access to public transportation, and a wide range of dining and entertainment options.
Cons: Traffic can be heavy, and the area can be quite crowded.

**Luohu** (Shenzhen)
Best For: Luxury travelers
Vibe: Luohu is a popular area for shoppers and foodies, with a mix of high-end malls and traditional markets. The area is known for its vibrant atmosphere and diverse culinary options.
Price Range: $150-300/night
Pros: Close proximity to the Luohu Border Crossing, a wide range of luxury hotels and shopping options, and easy access to public transportation.
Cons: The area can be quite expensive, and traffic can be heavy.

**Nanshan** (Shenzhen)
Best For: Families and backpackers
Vibe: Nanshan is a coastal district with a mix of beaches, parks, and scenic hiking trails. The area is known for its relaxed atmosphere and stunning natural beauty.
Price Range: $50-150/night
Pros: Easy access to beaches and outdoor recreational activities, a wide range of accommodation options, and a more relaxed atmosphere.
Cons: The area can be quite far from the city center, and public transportation options may be limited.

**Yantian** (Shenzhen)
Best For: Budget travelers
Vibe: Yantian is a coastal district with a mix of beaches, parks, and scenic hiking trails. The area is known for its laid-back atmosphere and stunning natural beauty.
Price Range: $30-80/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, easy access to beaches and outdoor recreational activities, and a more relaxed atmosphere.
Cons: The area can be quite far from the city center, and public transportation options may be limited.

**Bao'an** (Shenzhen)
Best For: Business travelers
Vibe: Bao'an is a rapidly developing district with a mix of industrial and commercial areas. The area is known for its convenient location and easy access to the airport.
Price Range: $60-120/night
Pros: Close proximity to the airport, easy access to public transportation, and a wide range of accommodation options.
Cons: The area can be quite industrial, and traffic can be heavy.

**Longgang** (Shenzhen)
Best For: Families and backpackers
Vibe: Longgang is a suburban district with a mix of parks, scenic hiking trails, and traditional villages. The area is known for its relaxed atmosphere and stunning natural beauty.
Price Range: $40-100/night
Pros: Easy access to outdoor recreational activities, a wide range of accommodation options, and a more relaxed atmosphere.
Cons: The area can be quite far from the city center, and public transportation options may be limited.
