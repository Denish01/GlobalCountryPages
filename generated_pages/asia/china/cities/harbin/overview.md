[SECTION] What Is Harbin? [/SECTION]
Harbin is a sub-provincial city in China, situated in the southeastern part of Heilongjiang Province, in the northeastern region of the country. The city is nestled on the southern bank of the Songhua River and has a total area of approximately 53,069 square kilometers. Harbin is a significant cultural, economic, and transportation hub in northeastern China, with a population of around 10 million people. The city's strategic location has made it an important center for trade, commerce, and tourism in the region.

Geographically, Harbin is characterized by its cold, temperate climate, with long, harsh winters and short, mild summers. The city's landscape is dominated by flat plains, with some hills and mountains in the surrounding areas. Harbin is also known for its beautiful winter scenery, with numerous parks, gardens, and frozen lakes that attract millions of tourists each year. The city's unique blend of Chinese and Russian cultures is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and cultural events, making it a fascinating destination for visitors from around the world.

As the capital of Heilongjiang Province, Harbin plays a vital role in the region's economy, with major industries such as manufacturing, agriculture, and services. The city is also home to several prestigious universities and research institutions, making it a hub for education and innovation. Harbin's rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and unique blend of Chinese and Russian influences make it a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the diversity and complexity of China.
