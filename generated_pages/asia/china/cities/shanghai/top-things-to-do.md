# Top Things to Do in Shanghai — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Shanghai, visit iconic attractions like the Oriental Pearl Tower, explore the city's vibrant neighborhoods, and indulge in its unique cultural experiences.

[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Oriental Pearl Tower | Lujiazui, Pudong | Observation Deck | Panoramic views of the city skyline |
| The Bund | Huangpu District | Historical Waterfront | Iconic colonial-era architecture and nightlife |
| Shanghai Museum | People's Square, Huangpu District | Museum | Extensive collection of Chinese art and artifacts |
| Yu Garden | Jing'an District | Classical Chinese Garden | Serene oasis in the heart of the city |

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **Huangpu River Cruise** to see the city's skyline from a different perspective
- Explore the **Shanghai Propaganda Poster Art Centre** to learn about China's communist past
- Visit the **Shanghai Circus World** for a thrilling acrobatic performance
- Wander through the **Tianzifang** neighborhood to discover local artists and artisans
- Attend a **Chinese Calligraphy** class to learn about this ancient art form
- Enjoy a **Traditional Shanghai Dim Sum** meal at a local restaurant
- Take a **Street Food Tour** to sample the city's diverse culinary delights

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **People's Square** is a large public park in the heart of the city
- **Lujiazui Central Green** is a peaceful oasis in the financial district
- **Shanghai Botanical Garden** features a variety of plant species and a peaceful atmosphere
- **Zhujiajiao Water Town** is a nearby ancient town with canals and traditional architecture
- **Dianshan Lake** is a popular spot for hiking and outdoor activities

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **Shanghai Museum** is a must-visit for its extensive collection of Chinese art and artifacts
- **Longhua Temple** is a historic Buddhist temple with stunning architecture
- **Shanghai Grand Theater** hosts a variety of performances, including ballet and opera
- **China Art Museum** features a collection of modern and contemporary Chinese art
- **Jade Buddha Temple** is a peaceful oasis with two rare jade Buddha statues
- **Shanghai Confucius Temple** is a historic temple dedicated to the philosopher Confucius

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **The Shanghai French Concession's** hidden alleys and boutiques
- **The Propaganda Poster Art Centre** is a unique museum showcasing communist-era posters
- **The Shanghai City God Temple** is a historic temple dedicated to the city's patron deity
- **The Tianzifang neighborhood's** narrow alleys and local artisans
- **The Jing'an Temple** is a historic Buddhist temple with stunning architecture

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- **Hangzhou**: 150 km, 2 hours, famous for its **West Lake** and **Longjing Tea Plantations**
- **Suzhou**: 100 km, 1.5 hours, known for its **Classical Gardens** and **Canal Towns**
- **Nanjing**: 300 km, 3 hours, historic city with **Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum** and **Qinhuai River**
- **Zhujiajiao Water Town**: 50 km, 1 hour, ancient town with canals and traditional architecture
- **Chongming Island**: 60 km, 2 hours, peaceful island with **Dongtan Wetland Park** and **Chongming Museum**

To make the most of your trip, consider visiting during the **Shanghai Cherry Blossom Festival** or the **Shanghai International Arts Festival**. Be sure to try some of the local cuisine, including **Xiaolongbao** and **Jiaozi**, and don't forget to **haggle** when shopping at local markets. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and modern amenities, Shanghai is a city that has something to offer for every kind of traveler.
