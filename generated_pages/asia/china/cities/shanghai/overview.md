[SECTION] What Is Shanghai? [/SECTION]
Shanghai is a **municipality** under the direct administration of the central government of China, meaning it is a city with provincial-level status. Geographically, Shanghai is situated on the eastern coast of China, at the mouth of the Yangtze River, and is bordered by the provinces of Jiangsu and Zhejiang to the north and west. The city is known for its impressive skyline, which features some of the tallest buildings in the world, including the **Shanghai Tower**, as well as its rich cultural heritage, which includes a blend of traditional Chinese and Western influences.

Shanghai is also a major economic hub, with a strong focus on finance, trade, and industry. The city is home to the **Shanghai Stock Exchange**, one of the largest stock exchanges in the world, and is a key center for international trade and commerce. Shanghai's strategic location and well-developed infrastructure make it an important gateway for foreign investment and trade in China.

In addition to its economic and cultural significance, Shanghai is also known for its vibrant arts and entertainment scene, which includes a variety of museums, galleries, and performance venues. The city is home to the **Shanghai Museum**, which features a collection of Chinese art and artifacts, as well as the **Shanghai Grand Theater**, which hosts a variety of performances, including opera, ballet, and concerts.
