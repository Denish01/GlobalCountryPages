[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Shanghai is situated on the eastern coast of China, at the mouth of the Yangtze River, which is the longest river in Asia and the third-longest in the world. The city is bordered by Jiangsu Province to the north and west, and Zhejiang Province to the south, with the East China Sea to the east. This strategic location has made Shanghai a major trade and cultural center for centuries, with a unique blend of traditional Chinese and modern international influences.

The city's coastal location has also played a significant role in its development, with the Port of Shanghai being one of the busiest ports in the world. The city's proximity to the Yangtze River Delta has also made it an important center for trade and commerce, with many major industries, including textiles, steel, and electronics, located in the city. Shanghai's location has also made it a popular tourist destination, with many visitors drawn to its unique blend of traditional and modern culture, architecture, and natural scenery.
