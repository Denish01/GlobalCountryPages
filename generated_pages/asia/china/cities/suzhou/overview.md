[SECTION] What Is Suzhou? [/SECTION]
Suzhou is a city in eastern China, situated in the Jiangsu Province, about 100 kilometers west of Shanghai. It is a prefecture-level city, which means it is a major administrative division of the province. Suzhou is known for its stunning natural beauty, with numerous canals, lakes, and gardens that have earned it the nickname "Venice of the East". The city has a rich cultural heritage, with a history dating back over 2,500 years, and is home to many ancient temples, pagodas, and historical landmarks.

Geographically, Suzhou is located in the Yangtze River Delta, which is one of the most densely populated and economically developed regions in China. The city has a total area of approximately 8,488 square kilometers and a population of over 10 million people. Suzhou is a major economic center, with a strong focus on industries such as textiles, electronics, and biotechnology. The city is also a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year with its beautiful gardens, historic landmarks, and vibrant cultural scene.

Suzhou is also known for its unique blend of traditional and modern culture. The city is home to many ancient traditions, such as Chinese opera, calligraphy, and painting, which are still practiced and celebrated today. At the same time, Suzhou is a modern and cosmopolitan city, with a thriving arts and cultural scene, and a wide range of international restaurants, bars, and entertainment venues.
