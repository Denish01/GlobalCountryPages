[SECTION] What Is Guangzhou? [/SECTION]
Guangzhou is a sub-provincial city located in the Guangdong province of China, situated on the Pearl River about 120 kilometers northwest of Hong Kong. As the capital of Guangdong, Guangzhou is a major cultural, economic, and political center in southern China. The city has a long history dating back over 2,200 years, with a rich cultural heritage and a blend of traditional and modern architecture. Guangzhou is known for its vibrant economy, with major industries in manufacturing, logistics, and trade, as well as its historic landmarks, cultural festivals, and traditional cuisine.

Guangzhou is situated in the Pearl River Delta, one of the most densely populated and economically developed regions in China. The city's strategic location has made it an important hub for trade and commerce, with a major international airport, seaport, and transportation network. Guangzhou is also a major tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world with its historic temples, museums, and cultural attractions.

As a major city in China, Guangzhou is subject to the sovereignty of the Chinese government, with a sub-provincial administration that reports directly to the central government. The city has a high degree of autonomy in economic and social matters, but is still subject to the overall authority of the Chinese Communist Party.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 214 BC - Guangzhou was founded as a city during the Qin dynasty, with the name Panyu.
2. 111 BC - Guangzhou became a major center of trade and commerce during the Han dynasty, with the establishment of the Silk Road.
3. 1711 - The city was renamed Guangzhou, which means "excellent city" in Chinese.
4. 1842 - Guangzhou was occupied by British forces during the Opium Wars, leading to the forced opening of the city to foreign trade.
5. 1949 - Guangzhou was liberated by the Communist Party of China, becoming a major city in the new socialist state.
6. 1978 - Guangzhou was designated as one of the first cities in China to be opened to foreign investment and trade, leading to rapid economic growth and development.
7. 2010 - Guangzhou hosted the Asian Games, a major international sporting event that showcased the city's modern infrastructure and cultural attractions.

- The city is home to the **Temple of the Six Banyan Trees**, a historic Buddhist temple that dates back to the 3rd century AD.
- Guangzhou is a major center of **international trade and commerce**, with a large international airport, seaport, and transportation network.
- The city is famous for its **Cherry Blossom Festival**, which is held every spring and attracts millions of visitors.
