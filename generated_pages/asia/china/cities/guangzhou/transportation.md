# Getting Around Guangzhou — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Guangzhou, you can use a variety of transportation options, including the metro, buses, taxis, and ride-hailing apps, as well as take advantage of the city's extensive rail network and domestic air connections.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Guangzhou is a major transportation hub in southern China, with Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN) serving as the city's main international airport. The airport is located about 28 kilometers north of the city center and offers direct flights to many major cities in Asia, Europe, and North America, including Beijing, Shanghai, Hong Kong, Tokyo, Seoul, Bangkok, Singapore, London, and Los Angeles. Additionally, Guangzhou is connected to neighboring cities and provinces by a network of highways and expressways, making it easily accessible by car or bus. The city is also a key stop on the **Pearl River Delta** economic zone, with frequent bus and train services to nearby cities like Shenzhen, Dongguan, and Zhuhai.

For travelers entering Guangzhou overland, there are several options available. The city is connected to Hong Kong by a network of highways and bridges, with several border crossings open to pedestrians, cars, and buses. There are also frequent bus and train services to neighboring provinces, including Fujian, Jiangxi, and Hunan. Furthermore, Guangzhou is a major stop on the **China-Vietnam Railway**, with frequent train services to Hanoi and other cities in northern Vietnam.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Guangzhou has an extensive domestic air network, with flights to many major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, Chengdu, and Xi'an. Domestic airlines such as **China Southern Airlines**, **China Eastern Airlines**, and **Air China** operate from Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport, offering frequent flights to destinations across the country. Booking domestic flights in China can be done online through the airlines' websites or through travel agencies, and it is recommended to book in advance to secure the best prices. Approximate costs for domestic flights from Guangzhou to other major cities in China range from $100 to $500 USD, depending on the destination and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Guangzhou has an extensive rail network, with frequent train services to many major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Xi'an. The city is a key stop on the **Beijing-Guangzhou-Shenzhen-Hong Kong High-Speed Railway**, with frequent high-speed trains to destinations in the **Pearl River Delta** and beyond. The high-speed trains, also known as **G-trains** or **D-trains**, offer a convenient and relatively affordable way to travel, with journey times of around 8-10 hours to Beijing and 2-3 hours to Shenzhen. Booking train tickets in China can be done online through the China Railway Corporation website or through travel agencies, and it is recommended to book in advance to secure the best prices.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Guangzhou has a well-developed intercity bus network, with frequent services to neighboring cities and provinces. Several bus companies, including **Guangzhou Bus Company** and **Pearl River Delta Bus Company**, operate from the city's main bus stations, offering a range of services from budget-friendly options to more luxurious coaches. The quality of the buses varies, but many services offer comfortable seating, air conditioning, and onboard amenities such as Wi-Fi and food and drink vendors. Costs for intercity bus travel in Guangzhou range from $10 to $50 USD, depending on the destination and type of service.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Guangzhou, you will need a valid Chinese driver's license or an international driving permit.
- Road conditions in Guangzhou are generally good, with well-maintained highways and city streets.
- Fuel costs in Guangzhou are relatively low, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental is available in Guangzhou, with several major companies offering a range of vehicles, from economy cars to luxury SUVs.
- Hazards to watch out for when driving in Guangzhou include aggressive drivers, pedestrians, and cyclists, as well as frequent traffic jams and construction delays.
- It is recommended to avoid driving in Guangzhou during peak hours (7-9 am and 5-7 pm) and to use a GPS or mapping app to navigate the city's complex road network.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of scams targeting tourists, such as overpriced taxis or fake train tickets.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps to avoid being overcharged.
- Download the **Didi Chuxing** or **Alibaba's AutoNavi** apps to book taxis or ride-hailing services.
- Use cash or mobile payments such as **WeChat Pay** or **Alipay** to pay for transportation.
- Be prepared for language barriers, especially when using public transportation or interacting with locals.
- Use a money belt or secure bag to protect your valuables, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.
- Stay informed about local traffic conditions and plan your route accordingly to avoid delays.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when interacting with taxi drivers or other transportation providers.
