# Getting Around Kunming — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Kunming, you can use a variety of transportation options, including taxis, buses, trains, and ride-hailing apps, which provide a convenient and affordable way to explore the city and its surrounding areas.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Kunming Changshui International Airport is the main entry point for travelers visiting the city, with direct flights from major Chinese cities like Shanghai, Beijing, and Guangzhou, as well as international flights from Southeast Asia and other parts of the world. The airport is located about 25 kilometers northeast of the city center and is connected to the city by a highway and an expressway. You can take a taxi or bus from the airport to get to your destination.

In addition to flying, you can also enter Kunming by train or bus from other Chinese cities. The city has several train stations, including the Kunming Railway Station and the Kunming South Railway Station, which offer high-speed trains to nearby cities like Dali and Lijiang. There are also several bus stations in the city that offer bus services to nearby towns and cities. If you're coming from neighboring countries like Laos or Myanmar, you can also enter Kunming by land, although this may require more time and planning.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Kunming has an extensive domestic air network, with flights to most major cities in China. You can fly to Kunming from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and other major cities, with multiple flights per day. China Eastern, China Southern, and Air China are some of the major airlines that operate domestic flights to and from Kunming. When booking domestic flights, it's a good idea to book in advance to get the best prices, which can range from $50 to $200 USD per person, depending on the route and time of year.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Kunming has an extensive rail network, with high-speed trains connecting the city to nearby cities like Dali and Lijiang. The city has several train stations, including the Kunming Railway Station and the Kunming South Railway Station, which offer high-speed trains to nearby cities. You can book train tickets online or at the train station, and the cost will depend on the route and class of service. High-speed trains are a convenient and comfortable way to travel, with prices ranging from $20 to $100 USD per person, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Kunming has a well-developed bus network, with buses connecting the city to nearby towns and cities. You can take a bus from the city's several bus stations, including the Kunming West Bus Station and the Kunming South Bus Station. Bus companies like Fudou Bus and Yunnan Bus operate buses to nearby cities, with prices ranging from $10 to $50 USD per person, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Kunming, you'll need a valid Chinese driver's license or an international driving permit.
- The road quality in Kunming is generally good, with well-maintained highways and city streets.
- Fuel costs in Kunming are relatively low, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies like Hertz and Avis operate in Kunming, with prices ranging from $40 to $100 USD per day.
- When driving in Kunming, be aware of the city's traffic rules and regulations, which may be different from those in your home country.
- Hazards like traffic congestion and pedestrian traffic can be challenging, so it's a good idea to exercise caution when driving in the city.

- Be aware of **scams** targeting tourists, such as overpriced taxis or fake tour operators.
- Use **cash** or **mobile payments** like WeChat Pay or Alipay to pay for transportation, as not all vendors accept credit cards.
- Download **transport apps** like Didi Chuxing or Alibaba's AutoNavi to book taxis or ride-hailing services.
- Be prepared for **traffic congestion** during peak hours, especially during rush hour or holidays.
- Learn some **basic Chinese phrases**, such as "hello" (nǐ hǎo) and "thank you" (xiè xiè), to help you communicate with taxi drivers or vendors.
- Consider purchasing a **local SIM card** or **portable Wi-Fi hotspot** to stay connected and navigate the city.
- Be mindful of **safety** when using public transportation, especially at night or in crowded areas.
