# Cost of Living in Tianjin - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Tianjin is generally considered to be moderate, with a daily budget ranging from $30-100 USD per day for a backpacker, $50-200 USD per day for a mid-range traveler, and $150-500 USD per day for a comfortable lifestyle.

Daily Budget (Comfort): $150-500 USD
Currency: CNY
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Tianjin? [/SECTION]
Tianjin is a major city in China, and its cost of living is relatively moderate compared to other major cities in the country. The city's prices are generally lower than those in Beijing and Shanghai, but higher than those in smaller cities in China. Overall, Tianjin is a great destination for travelers who want to experience the excitement of a major Chinese city without breaking the bank. The cost of living in Tianjin is also influenced by its proximity to Beijing, which can drive up prices for certain goods and services.

In comparison to other cities in Asia, Tianjin is generally more expensive than cities in Southeast Asia, such as Hanoi or Bangkok, but less expensive than cities in Japan or South Korea. However, the cost of living in Tianjin can vary greatly depending on the individual's lifestyle and preferences. For example, a traveler who wants to experience the city's luxury shopping and dining scene may find that Tianjin is quite expensive, while a backpacker who is content with eating at street stalls and staying in hostels may find that the city is relatively affordable.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or apartment outside of the city center to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries and street food stalls to save on food costs.
- Use public transportation instead of taxis to get around the city.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (June to August) to save on accommodation and transportation costs.
- Take advantage of the city's many free or low-cost attractions, such as the Tianjin Museum and the Ancient Culture Street.
- Learn some basic Mandarin phrases to help you navigate the city and avoid getting overcharged by vendors and taxi drivers.
