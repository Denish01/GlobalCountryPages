# Getting Around Chongqing — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Chongqing, you can utilize a range of transportation options including buses, taxis, ride-hailing apps, and a well-developed metro system, as well as trains and domestic flights to explore the city and its surroundings.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Chongqing Jiangbei International Airport (CKG) is the main airport serving the city, with direct flights to many major Chinese cities, as well as international destinations in Asia and beyond. The airport is a significant hub for several Chinese airlines, including China Southern Airlines, China Eastern Airlines, and Sichuan Airlines. You can also enter Chongqing by train or bus from neighboring cities, with several major highways and railways connecting the city to the rest of China. The city's location in the southwest of China makes it a key transportation hub for the region, with many overland entry points from neighboring provinces such as Sichuan, Guizhou, and Hubei.

The city's transportation infrastructure is well-developed, with many options for getting around, including taxis, buses, and ride-hailing apps. For those traveling from other parts of China, there are also many domestic flights and trains available, making it easy to get to Chongqing from other major cities. Additionally, the city's airport is located about 20 kilometers north of the city center, and there are several options for getting to the city center, including taxis, buses, and the metro.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Chongqing has an extensive domestic air network, with flights to many major Chinese cities, including Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen. You can book domestic flights through the websites of Chinese airlines, such as China Southern Airlines, China Eastern Airlines, and Sichuan Airlines, or through online travel agencies like Ctrip or Qunar. Booking in advance can help you get the best prices, with approximate costs ranging from $50 to $200 USD for a one-way ticket, depending on the route and airline. It's also a good idea to check for any travel restrictions or requirements, such as health certificates or proof of onward travel, before booking your flight.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Chongqing has an extensive rail network, with high-speed trains connecting the city to many other major Chinese cities, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. The city's main train station is Chongqing West Railway Station, which is located in the Yubei District and offers high-speed trains to many destinations. You can book train tickets through the website of China Railway Corporation or through online travel agencies like Ctrip or Qunar. The booking process is relatively straightforward, and you can choose from a range of classes and prices, with approximate costs ranging from $20 to $100 USD for a one-way ticket, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are many intercity bus companies operating in Chongqing, with routes to neighboring cities and provinces. The city's main bus station is the Chongqing Long-Distance Bus Station, which is located in the Jiangbei District and offers buses to many destinations. The quality of the buses varies, but many companies offer comfortable and air-conditioned coaches. The costs are generally lower than trains or flights, with approximate costs ranging from $10 to $50 USD for a one-way ticket, depending on the route and company. You can book bus tickets through the websites of the bus companies or through online travel agencies like Ctrip or Qunar.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Chongqing, you will need a valid Chinese driver's license or an international driver's license, which can be obtained through the Chinese embassy or consulate in your home country.
- The road quality in Chongqing is generally moderate, with some roads being well-maintained and others being more rough, especially in rural areas.
- Fuel costs in Chongqing are relatively low, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies are available in Chongqing, including international chains like Hertz and Avis, as well as local companies, and you can book a car through their websites or at the airport.
- Driving in Chongqing can be challenging, especially for foreigners, due to the crowded roads and different driving customs, so it's a good idea to exercise caution and consider hiring a driver or using public transportation.
- Parking in Chongqing can be difficult, especially in the city center, so be prepared for limited parking options and be sure to follow local parking regulations.
