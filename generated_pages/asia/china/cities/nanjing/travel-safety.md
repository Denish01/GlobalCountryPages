[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Nanjing, the capital of Jiangsu Province in eastern China, is a city with a rich history and cultural heritage. With a population of over 8 million people, Nanjing is a bustling metropolis, but it remains a relatively safe city for tourists. The city has a low crime rate compared to other major cities in China, and visitors can feel comfortable walking around the city, even at night. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to take common-sense precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. This includes being aware of your surroundings, keeping an eye on your belongings, and avoiding traveling alone in unfamiliar areas at night.

Nanjing is a popular tourist destination, and the city has taken steps to improve safety and security for visitors. The city has a dedicated tourist police force, and many areas popular with tourists are well-patrolled and have CCTV cameras. Additionally, many locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and English is becoming more widely spoken, especially among younger people and those in the service industry. However, as with any city, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should take care to avoid areas that are known to be prone to crime or other safety issues.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and petty theft are common in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake taxi drivers or tour guides, are common
- Be wary of overly friendly locals who may be trying to scam or cheat you
- Be careful when using ATMs or exchanging money, as scams and theft can occur
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Avoid traveling alone in unfamiliar areas at night, especially in areas that are known to be prone to crime

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to China
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Nanjing has many high-quality hospitals and medical facilities, including some that cater specifically to foreigners
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but many do not speak English, so it's a good idea to bring a translation guide or ask a local for assistance

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or mobile payments instead
- Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuables
- Avoid traveling alone in unfamiliar areas at night, especially in areas that are known to be prone to crime
- Learn some basic Chinese phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you", to help you communicate with locals
- Bring a translation guide or use a translation app on your phone to help you navigate
