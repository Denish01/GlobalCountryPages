# Where to Stay in Nanjing — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To find the best place to stay in Nanjing, consider the city's top neighborhoods, including Xuanwu, Qinhuai, Jianye, Gulou, and Confucius Temple, which offer a range of accommodations for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Xuanwu** (Nanjing)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families
Vibe: This area is known for its upscale atmosphere, with many high-end hotels and shopping centers, and is surrounded by beautiful parks and gardens. It's a great place to experience the city's modern side.
Price Range: $100-500/night
Pros: Convenient location, modern amenities, and beautiful scenery. 
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and may lack the traditional charm of other areas.

**Qinhuai** (Nanjing)
Best For: Couples and history buffs
Vibe: This area is famous for its traditional Chinese architecture, canals, and historic sites, offering a romantic and culturally rich experience. It's a great place to explore the city's heritage.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Rich history, scenic canals, and traditional architecture. 
Cons: Can be quite crowded, and may have limited modern amenities.

**Jianye** (Nanjing)
Best For: Business travelers and shoppers
Vibe: This area is a major commercial center, with many office buildings, shopping malls, and entertainment venues, making it a great place to stay for business or shopping trips. It's a bustling area with a modern vibe.
Price Range: $80-300/night
Pros: Convenient location, modern amenities, and plenty of shopping and dining options. 
Cons: Can be quite busy, and may lack the charm of other areas.

**Gulou** (Nanjing)
Best For: Backpackers and students
Vibe: This area is known for its lively atmosphere, with many universities, bars, and restaurants, making it a great place to stay for young travelers. It's a vibrant area with a youthful energy.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable prices, lively atmosphere, and plenty of dining and entertainment options. 
Cons: Can be quite noisy, and may lack the amenities of other areas.

**Confucius Temple** (Nanjing)
Best For: Families and cultural travelers
Vibe: This area is a historic and cultural hub, with many traditional shops, restaurants, and attractions, offering a unique and immersive experience. It's a great place to learn about the city's heritage.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: Rich history, cultural attractions, and traditional architecture. 
Cons: Can be quite crowded, and may have limited modern amenities.
