Xi'an is a major city located in the northwest of China, known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and significance as a former capital of China.

[SECTION] What Is Xi'an? [/SECTION]
Xi'an is a city located in the Shaanxi province of China, with a long and storied history dating back over 3,000 years. As the capital of several Chinese dynasties, including the Qin, Han, Sui, and Tang, Xi'an has played a significant role in the country's development and growth. The city is situated in the northwest of China, near the Yellow River, and is known for its well-preserved ancient city walls, watchtowers, and historic landmarks such as the Terracotta Warriors and the Big Wild Goose Pagoda. Xi'an is also an important cultural and economic center in western China, with a diverse population and a unique blend of traditional and modern architecture.

Xi'an is a prefecture-level city, meaning it is a major urban center with a high degree of autonomy and self-governance. The city is divided into several districts, each with its own unique character and attractions. The city's rich history and cultural heritage have made it a popular tourist destination, with millions of visitors each year. Xi'an is also an important hub for education, research, and innovation, with several major universities and institutes located in the city.

The city's strategic location at the eastern end of the Silk Road, a major trade route that connected China with Central Asia and Europe, has also made it an important center for international trade and commerce. Today, Xi'an is a thriving metropolis with a strong economy, a rich cultural scene, and a unique blend of traditional and modern attractions.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 221-206 BCE - The Qin dynasty establishes its capital in Xi'an, marking the beginning of the city's long history as a major urban center.
2. 206 BCE-220 CE - The Han dynasty rules China from Xi'an, during which time the city becomes a major center of culture, learning, and trade.
3. 618-907 CE - The Tang dynasty establishes its capital in Xi'an, marking the beginning of the city's golden age as a center of art, literature, and culture.
4. 1368-1644 CE - The Ming dynasty rebuilds and expands the city walls and fortifications of Xi'an, which still stand today.
5. 1900 CE - The Boxer Rebellion leads to the occupation of Xi'an by foreign troops, marking the beginning of a period of foreign influence and modernization in the city.
6. 1949 CE - The Communist Party comes to power in China, and Xi'an becomes a major center of industry and commerce in the northwest.
7. 1974 CE - The discovery of the Terracotta Warriors in Xi'an marks a major turning point in the city's history, as it becomes a major tourist destination and cultural center.

[SECTION] What Makes Xi'an Unique [/SECTION]
- Xi'an is home to the famous Terracotta Warriors, a collection of over 8,000 life-sized clay soldiers that were buried with the first emperor of China.
- The city is surrounded by a well-preserved ancient city wall, which is one of the oldest and most intact in China.
- Xi'an is a major center of Islamic culture in China, with a large Muslim population and several important mosques, including the Great Mosque of Xi'an.
- The city is home to several major universities and research institutes, including Xi'an Jiaotong University and the Chinese Academy of Sciences.
- Xi'an is a major hub for traditional Chinese medicine, with several hospitals and research centers specializing in the subject.

Xi'an is a historic city in northwest China known for its rich cultural heritage, well-preserved ancient city walls, and significant landmarks such as the Terracotta Warriors, making it a unique and fascinating destination for visitors and a thriving center of commerce and innovation.
