# Zhejiang Cities & Regions Guide

Zhejiang is divided into eleven prefecture-level cities, with the capital being Hangzhou, and features a mix of coastal ports, scenic islands, and mountainous regions, making it a diverse and culturally rich province in eastern China.

[SECTION] How Zhejiang Is Divided [/SECTION]
Zhejiang is a province located in the eastern part of China, and its administrative structure is divided into eleven prefecture-level cities, which are further divided into districts, counties, and county-level cities. The eleven prefecture-level cities are Hangzhou, Ningbo, Wenzhou, Jiaxing, Huzhou, Shaoxing, Jinhua, Quzhou, Zhoushan, Taizhou, and Lishui. This division allows for a more localized governance and administration, catering to the unique needs and characteristics of each region.

The division of Zhejiang into these cities and regions is largely based on geographical and economic factors, with the coastal cities such as Ningbo and Wenzhou serving as major ports and economic hubs, while the inland cities like Hangzhou and Jinhua are known for their cultural and historical significance. This diversity in administrative divisions allows Zhejiang to maintain a balance between economic development, cultural preservation, and environmental conservation.

[SECTION] Regional Differences [/SECTION]
- The coastal regions of Zhejiang, such as Ningbo and Wenzhou, have a distinct cultural and economic identity shaped by their maritime history and trade activities.
- The inland regions, such as Hangzhou and Jinhua, are known for their rich cultural heritage, historic landmarks, and traditional crafts.
- The mountainous regions, such as Huzhou and Lishui, are characterized by their stunning natural scenery, traditional tea plantations, and rural way of life.
- The language and dialects spoken in different regions of Zhejiang also vary, with the Wu dialect being predominant in the coastal areas and the Mandarin dialect being more widely spoken in the inland regions.

Zhejiang has a well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights connecting major cities, high-speed trains linking the province to other parts of China, and an extensive network of buses and highways.
