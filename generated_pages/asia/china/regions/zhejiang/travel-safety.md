[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Zhejiang, a region in eastern China, is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant cities. The region has a strong focus on tourism, with many popular destinations such as Hangzhou, Ningbo, and Wuzhen. Overall, Zhejiang is considered a very safe region, with low crime rates compared to other major tourist destinations. Visitors can feel comfortable exploring the region's many attractions, from ancient temples and gardens to modern shopping malls and entertainment complexes. However, as with any travel destination, it's still important to take common-sense precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

While Zhejiang is generally a safe region, there are some areas that are considered safer than others. For example, the cities of Hangzhou and Ningbo are generally considered very safe, with low crime rates and a strong police presence. However, as with any major city, there are some areas that are best avoided, particularly at night. Additionally, visitors should be aware of the potential for natural disasters, such as typhoons and earthquakes, which can occur in the region. Overall, with some basic precautions and an awareness of the local conditions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Zhejiang.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas, such as train stations and tourist attractions
- Be cautious of **scams** targeting tourists, such as fake tour guides or overpriced souvenirs
- Watch out for **traffic accidents**, particularly when crossing the road or driving
- Be aware of **natural disasters**, such as typhoons and earthquakes, which can occur in the region
- Be cautious of **food poisoning**, particularly when eating at street stalls or from vendors
- Be aware of **crowd safety**, particularly during festivals and holidays when large crowds can gather
- Be cautious of **ATM scams**, such as card skimming or fake ATMs

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travelers to China
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not considered safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in Zhejiang are generally of high quality, particularly in major cities such as Hangzhou and Ningbo
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, particularly in urban areas, and many medications are available over-the-counter

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **map** or **guidebook** to help navigate the region
- Be aware of **local customs** and **traditions**, particularly when visiting temples or cultural attractions
- Keep **valuables secure**, particularly in crowded areas or tourist attractions
- Use **reputable taxi companies** or **ride-sharing services** to avoid scams
- Stay **informed** about local conditions, particularly during times of natural disasters or unrest
- Learn some **basic Chinese phrases**, such as "hello" and "thank you", to help communicate with locals
- Be prepared for **power outages** or **internet disruptions**, particularly in rural areas

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Consider **staying in well-lit** and **populated areas**, particularly at night
- Be aware of **local dress codes**, particularly when visiting temples or cultural attractions
- Keep **in touch with family and friends** back home, and consider **registering with your government's travel advisory program**
- Be cautious of **unsolicited attention** or **harassment**, particularly from strangers or vendors
- Consider **joining a guided tour** or **group tour** to meet other travelers and stay safe
