# Cost of Living in Zhejiang - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Zhejiang is generally moderate, with a daily budget of $30-60 USD for backpackers, $60-120 USD for mid-range travelers, and $120-250 USD for those seeking comfort.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $30-60 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $60-120 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $120-250 USD
Currency: CNY
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Zhejiang? [/SECTION]
Zhejiang is a region in eastern China known for its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and thriving economy. Compared to other regions in China, Zhejiang has a moderate cost of living, with prices slightly higher than in inland provinces but lower than in major cities like Shanghai and Beijing. Overall, Zhejiang offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike.

In terms of global comparison, Zhejiang's cost of living is relatively low, especially when it comes to accommodation and food. However, prices for transportation, entertainment, and utilities are closer to the global average. Visitors from Western countries may find that Zhejiang offers great value for money, while those from other parts of Asia may find it slightly more expensive than their home countries.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in guesthouses or hostels instead of hotels to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls to enjoy authentic cuisine at affordable prices.
- Use public transportation or walk/bike whenever possible to reduce transport expenses.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost cultural attractions, such as temples, parks, and museums.
- Learn some basic Mandarin phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (June to August) to avoid higher prices and tourist crowds.
