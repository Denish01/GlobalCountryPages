The best time to visit Zhejiang is during the spring and autumn seasons, from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Best months to visit Zhejiang: March to May and September to November. Peak season: June to August. Budget season: December to February. Avoid: June to August due to the hot and humid weather, and the crowds of tourists during the summer vacation.

Regional Variations

  • The coastal regions, such as Ningbo and Wenzhou, have a distinct maritime climate, with significant rainfall and fog throughout the year.
  • The mountainous regions, such as the Qingyuan Mountains, have a distinct alpine climate, with significant snowfall during the winter months.
  • The inland regions, such as Hangzhou and Jinhua, have a humid subtropical climate, with hot summers and cold winters.
  • The islands, such as Putuo Island and Dongshan Island, have a distinct island climate, with mild winters and warm summers.
  • The rural areas, such as Anji and Lin'an, have a distinct rural climate, with significant seasonal variations and limited access to amenities.

Practical Tips

  • Book your accommodations and transportation well in advance, especially during peak season.
  • Pack accordingly, with light clothing for summer and warm clothing for winter.
  • Bring an umbrella or raincoat, as rainfall can occur throughout the year.
  • Take advantage of the shoulder season, when prices are lower and crowds are smaller.
  • Avoid traveling during the Chinese New Year and National Day holidays, when prices are higher and crowds are larger.
  • Consider purchasing travel insurance, in case of unexpected events or cancellations.
