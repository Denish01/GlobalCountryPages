# Getting Around Shandong — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Shandong, you can use a combination of domestic flights, trains, buses, and city transportation options, including metro, city bus, taxi, and ride-hailing services, which provide convenient and efficient ways to explore the region.

[FACTBOX]
Main International Airport(s): Qingdao Liuting International Airport (TAO), Jinan Yaoqiang International Airport (TNA)
Domestic Air Network: extensive
Rail Network: extensive
Drives On: right
Road Quality: good
Ride-Hailing Apps: Didi Chuxing, Alibaba's AutoNavi
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Shandong has two main international airports, Qingdao Liuting International Airport (TAO) and Jinan Yaoqiang International Airport (TNA), which receive direct flights from major cities in Asia, Europe, and North America. Qingdao Liuting International Airport is a major hub with direct flights to cities like Seoul, Tokyo, and Hong Kong, while Jinan Yaoqiang International Airport has direct flights to cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. You can also enter Shandong by train or bus from neighboring provinces, such as Jiangsu, Anhui, and Henan.

If you prefer to travel by land, you can take a bus or train from nearby cities like Beijing, Tianjin, or Shanghai. The main overland entry points are located in the cities of Jinan, Qingdao, and Yantai, which have well-developed transportation infrastructure. From these cities, you can easily connect to other parts of Shandong by bus, train, or taxi. Additionally, the province has a well-developed highway system, making it convenient to drive into Shandong from neighboring provinces.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Shandong has an extensive domestic air network, with flights connecting major cities like Jinan, Qingdao, Yantai, and Weihai. Domestic airlines such as China Eastern, China Southern, and Shandong Airlines operate flights to and from Shandong. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through travel agencies. The cost of domestic flights varies depending on the route and time of booking, but you can expect to pay around $50-$200 USD for a one-way ticket. It's recommended to book in advance to get the best prices, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Shandong has an extensive rail network, with high-speed trains connecting major cities like Jinan, Qingdao, and Yantai. The high-speed trains, also known as **G-trains** or **D-trains**, can reach speeds of up to 300 km/h, making them a convenient and efficient way to travel. You can book train tickets online through the China Railway Corporation's website or through ticket machines at train stations. The cost of train tickets varies depending on the route and class of service, but you can expect to pay around $20-$100 USD for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Shandong has a well-developed intercity bus network, with bus companies like Shandong Bus and Qingdao Bus operating routes to and from major cities. The buses are comfortable and affordable, with fares ranging from $10-$50 USD for a one-way ticket. You can book bus tickets at the bus station or through online platforms like Alibaba's AutoNavi. The bus network is a good option for shorter distances or for traveling to smaller towns and villages.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Shandong, you need a valid Chinese driver's license or an international driving permit.
- The road conditions in Shandong are generally good, with well-maintained highways and urban roads.
- Fuel costs are relatively low, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50-$1.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies like Hertz and Avis have offices in major cities like Jinan and Qingdao, with prices starting from around $40-$60 USD per day.
- When driving in Shandong, be aware of the local traffic rules and customs, such as the **right-hand traffic rule** and the **priority to pedestrians**.
- Be prepared for potential hazards like traffic congestion, construction, and inclement weather.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- When taking a taxi, make sure to use a licensed taxi and agree on the fare before you start your journey.
- Be aware of **scams** targeting tourists, such as overcharging or taking a detour.
- Download the **Didi Chuxing** app to book ride-hailing services, which often have English support.
- Use **cash** or **mobile payments** like WeChat Pay or Alipay to pay for transportation, as not all services accept credit cards.
- Be prepared for **language barriers**, especially outside of major cities, and consider carrying a **translation app** or **phrasebook**.
- Respect local **traffic rules** and **customs**, such as yielding to pedestrians and cyclists.
- Consider purchasing a **China SIM card** or **portable Wi-Fi hotspot** to stay connected and navigate during your trip.
