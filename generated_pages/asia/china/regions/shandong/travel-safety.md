[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Shandong is a relatively safe region in China, with low rates of violent crime and a strong police presence. The region has a large number of tourists, and as a result, there are many measures in place to ensure visitor safety. However, as with any popular tourist destination, there are still some risks to be aware of, such as pickpocketing and scams. It's also important to be mindful of the local culture and customs, as Shandong is a conservative region and visitors are expected to respect local norms.

Overall, Shandong is a great destination for travelers, with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a unique cultural heritage. With some basic precautions and an understanding of the local environment, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip. It's worth noting that the Chinese government has implemented various safety measures, such as increased security checks and surveillance cameras, to ensure the safety of tourists. Additionally, many tourist areas have English-speaking staff and signs, making it easier for international visitors to navigate.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Pickpocketing**: A common crime in tourist areas, visitors should be mindful of their belongings, especially in crowded areas.
- **Scams**: Some scams target tourists, such as fake taxis or overpriced goods, so visitors should be cautious when using services or buying products.
- **Traffic accidents**: Shandong's roads can be chaotic, and traffic accidents are a common risk, so visitors should be careful when crossing the road or using public transportation.
- **Food poisoning**: While rare, food poisoning can occur, especially when eating at street food stalls or unlicensed restaurants, so visitors should be cautious when trying local cuisine.
- **Natural disasters**: Shandong is prone to natural disasters such as typhoons and earthquakes, so visitors should be aware of the weather forecast and follow local instructions in case of an emergency.
- **Petty theft**: Visitors should be mindful of their belongings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots, as petty theft can occur.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: Visitors should ensure they have all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Shandong, including hepatitis A and typhoid fever.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is not safe to drink in Shandong, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities**: Shandong has a well-developed healthcare system, with many high-quality hospitals and medical facilities, especially in larger cities like Jinan and Qingdao.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacies are widely available in Shandong, and many prescription medications can be purchased over-the-counter.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a hotel business card or a map with your accommodation's address, in case you get lost.
- Be cautious when using ATMs or exchanging money, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas, and stick to well-lit and populated areas.
- Learn some basic Chinese phrases, such as "hello" (nǐ hǎo) and "thank you" (xiè xiè), to help you communicate with locals.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Solo female travelers should be cautious when traveling alone at night, and consider using reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps.
- Women should dress modestly, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites, to avoid attracting unwanted attention.
- Solo travelers should stay in touch with family and friends back home, and consider sharing their itinerary with someone they trust.
- Women should be aware of their surroundings and trust their instincts, avoiding situations that make them feel uncomfortable or unsafe.
