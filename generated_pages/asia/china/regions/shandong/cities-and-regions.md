[SECTION] How Shandong Is Divided [/SECTION]
Shandong is a coastal province in eastern China, and its administrative structure is divided into 16 prefecture-level cities, which are further divided into districts, counties, and townships. The 16 prefecture-level cities in Shandong are: Jinan, Qingdao, Zibo, Zaozhuang, Dongying, Yantai, Weifang, Jining, Tai'an, Weihai, Rizhao, Linyi, Dezhou, Liaocheng, Binzhou, and Heze.

The administrative divisions of Shandong are designed to promote economic development, improve living standards, and provide effective governance. Each prefecture-level city has its own government, which is responsible for managing local affairs, providing public services, and implementing national policies. The cities are also divided into districts, which are further divided into subdistricts, towns, and townships. This hierarchical structure allows for efficient governance and administration of the province.
