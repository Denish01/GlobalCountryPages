# Cost of Living in Shandong - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Shandong is generally affordable, with a daily budget of $20-50 USD for backpackers, $50-100 USD for mid-range travelers, and $100-200 USD for comfort seekers. 

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $20-50 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $50-100 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $100-200 USD
Currency: CNY
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Shandong? [/SECTION]
Shandong is a region in eastern China that offers a unique blend of traditional culture, historic landmarks, and modern amenities. Compared to other major cities in China, Shandong is relatively affordable, with prices lower than those in Beijing or Shanghai. However, it is still more expensive than many other regions in China, particularly in terms of accommodation and transportation. Overall, Shandong is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience the best of China without breaking the bank.

In terms of global averages, Shandong is generally cheaper than many Western countries, but more expensive than some other Asian destinations. For example, a meal at a mid-range restaurant in Shandong might cost $10-20 USD, which is comparable to prices in many European cities. However, the cost of accommodation in Shandong is generally lower than in many Western countries, with hostel dorms starting from $10-20 USD per night.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in hostels or guesthouses instead of hotels to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls, which offer delicious and affordable meals.
- Use public transportation instead of taxis to get around the city.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (June to August) when prices are higher.
- Learn some basic Chinese phrases to negotiate prices and avoid tourist traps.
- Take advantage of free walking tours and cultural events to experience the local culture without spending money.
