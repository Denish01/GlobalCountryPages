[SECTION] How Jiangsu Is Divided [/SECTION]
Jiangsu is a province located in the eastern part of China, and it is divided into 13 prefecture-level cities, which are further divided into districts, county-level cities, and counties. The province is situated in the Yangtze River Delta, and its administrative structure is designed to facilitate economic development, urbanization, and regional coordination. The 13 prefecture-level cities in Jiangsu are Nanjing, Wuxi, Xuzhou, Changzhou, Suzhou, Nantong, Lianyungang, Huai'an, Yancheng, Yangzhou, Zhenjiang, Taizhou, and Suqian.

The administrative divisions in Jiangsu are designed to promote regional development and cooperation, with each city having its own unique characteristics and strengths. For example, Nanjing is the capital and a major cultural center, while Suzhou is known for its beautiful gardens and canals. The province's administrative structure also allows for coordination and cooperation between cities and regions, facilitating the development of the region as a whole.

[SECTION] Regional Differences [/SECTION]
- The Yangtze River Delta region is more economically developed and urbanized, with a strong focus on trade, industry, and tourism.
- The Jianghuai Plain region is characterized by its flat, fertile land and dense population, with a strong focus on agriculture and industry.
- The northern part of Jiangsu has a more maritime-oriented economy, with a strong focus on trade and industry.
- The southern part of Jiangsu is more mountainous and scenic, with a strong focus on tourism and agriculture.
- The regional differences in Jiangsu are also reflected in the local culture, with different regions having their own unique customs, traditions, and dialects.

[SECTION] Getting Around [/SECTION]
Jiangsu has a well-developed transportation network, with multiple domestic airports, high-speed rail lines, and highways connecting the province to other parts of China. The province is also served by a comprehensive bus network, with frequent services between cities and towns. For those who prefer to drive, the province has a well-maintained road network, with many highways and expressways. However, driving in Jiangsu can be challenging, especially in urban areas, due to heavy traffic and congestion.

The high-speed rail network in Jiangsu is particularly convenient, with frequent services between major cities like Nanjing, Suzhou, and Wuxi. The journey from Nanjing to Shanghai, for example, takes just over an hour by high-speed rail. The province is also served by a number of domestic airports, including Nanjing Lukou International Airport and Suzhou Guangfu Airport, which offer flights to destinations throughout China. Overall, getting around Jiangsu is relatively easy, with a range of transportation options available to suit different budgets and preferences.
