Getting There

Jiangsu is easily accessible by air, with several international airports, including Nanjing Lukou International Airport and Sunan Shuofang International Airport, which offer direct flights to major cities in Asia, Europe, and North America. The main entry points by air are Nanjing and Wuxi, with Nanjing being the primary hub. You can also enter Jiangsu by land or sea, with several major highways and railways connecting the region to neighboring provinces and countries. The main overland entry points are from Shanghai to the south and Shandong to the north.

If you are traveling from within China, you can take a domestic flight or train to Jiangsu. The region is well-connected to major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. You can also take a bus or drive to Jiangsu, with several major highways passing through the region. Jiangsu is also accessible by sea, with several major ports, including the Port of Nanjing and the Port of Lianyungang, which offer ferry services to nearby cities and countries.

Domestic Flights

Jiangsu has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to and from the region. The main domestic airlines serving Jiangsu are China Eastern Airlines, China Southern Airlines, and Shanghai Airlines. You can fly from Nanjing or Wuxi to major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. Booking domestic flights in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $50 to $200 USD, depending on the route and airline.

Trains & Rail

Jiangsu has an extensive rail network, with several high-speed lines connecting the region to major cities in China. The main rail lines in Jiangsu are the Beijing-Shanghai High-Speed Railway and the Shanghai-Nanjing Intercity Railway. You can take a high-speed train from Nanjing or Wuxi to Beijing, Shanghai, or other major cities in China. The rail network in Jiangsu is modern and efficient, with comfortable seats and convenient booking processes. You can book train tickets online or at the train station, and the cost ranges from $20 to $100 USD, depending on the route and class of service.

Buses & Coaches

Jiangsu has an extensive network of buses and coaches, connecting the region to major cities in China. The main bus companies in Jiangsu are Jiangsu Bus and China Bus, which operate routes to nearby provinces and cities. You can take a bus from Nanjing or Wuxi to Shanghai, Beijing, or other major cities in China. The bus network in Jiangsu is convenient and affordable, with approximate costs ranging from $10 to $50 USD, depending on the route and class of service.

Driving

  • To drive in Jiangsu, you need a valid Chinese driver's license or an international driving permit.
  • The road conditions in Jiangsu are generally good, with several major highways passing through the region.
  • Fuel costs in Jiangsu are relatively high, with prices ranging from $1.00 to $2.00 USD per liter.
  • Car rental services are available in major cities, with prices ranging from $40 to $100 USD per day.
  • Hazards to watch out for when driving in Jiangsu include traffic congestion, pedestrian and cyclist traffic, and construction zones.
  • It is recommended to hire a driver or use a ride-hailing service if you are not familiar with the local roads and traffic conditions.

Transport Tips

  • Always carry a valid form of identification and a map or guidebook when traveling in Jiangsu.
  • Be mindful of scams and touts, especially at airports and train stations.
  • Use reputable taxi or ride-hailing services to avoid overcharging.
  • Download the Didi Chuxing or AutoNavi app for convenient ride-hailing services.
