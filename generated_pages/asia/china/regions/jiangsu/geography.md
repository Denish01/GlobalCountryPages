[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Jiangsu is situated in the eastern part of China, bordering the Yellow Sea to the east and the East China Sea to the southeast. The region is bounded by the provinces of Shandong to the north, Anhui to the west, and Zhejiang to the south, as well as the municipality of Shanghai to the southeast. Jiangsu's strategic location has made it an important center for trade and commerce, with the Grand Canal, one of the oldest and longest canals in the world, running through the region.

The region's coastline along the Yellow Sea is approximately 1,000 kilometers long, providing access to the sea and facilitating the development of ports and shipping industries. Jiangsu's borders with neighboring provinces are largely defined by natural features such as rivers and mountains, with the Huai River forming part of the border with Anhui and the Yangtze River forming part of the border with Shanghai. The region's location in the eastern part of China has also made it an important hub for transportation and communication, with several major highways and railways passing through the region.

Jiangsu's terrain is characterized by a mix of plains, hills, and mountains, with the majority of the region consisting of flat or gently sloping plains. The North Jiangsu Plain, which covers much of the northern part of the region, is a flat and low-lying area that is prone to flooding. The region's highest point is Mount Yuntai, which is located in the southwest and has an elevation of 625 meters. The landscape of Jiangsu is also dotted with numerous lakes, rivers, and canals, including the famous Taihu Lake, which is one of the largest freshwater lakes in China.

The region's coastline along the Yellow Sea is marked by a series of sandy beaches, mudflats, and salt marshes, which provide important habitats for a wide range of marine and coastal species. Inland, the landscape is dominated by a mix of agricultural land, forests, and urban areas, with many of the region's cities and towns located along the banks of rivers and canals. The region's terrain has played an important role in shaping its economy and culture, with the fertile plains and extensive waterways making it an important center for agriculture, trade, and industry.

The region's natural beauty is also reflected in its many parks and nature reserves, which provide important habitats for a wide range of plant and animal species. The Jiangsu province is home to several **national parks**, including the Yuntai Mountain National Park and the Taihu Lake National Park, which attract millions of visitors each year. The region's natural beauty, combined with its rich cultural heritage and economic importance, make it a popular destination for tourists and business travelers alike.
