[SECTION] What Is Jiangsu? [/SECTION]
Jiangsu is a region in China that is situated on the eastern coast, bordering the Yellow Sea to the east and the provinces of Shandong to the north, Anhui to the west, and Zhejiang to the south. The province has a total area of approximately 102,600 square kilometers and a population of over 84 million people, making it one of the most populous provinces in China. Jiangsu is known for its stunning natural beauty, with the Yangtze River flowing through the southern part of the province and the Grand Canal, one of the oldest and longest canals in the world, running through the heart of the province.

The province has a long and rich history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Neolithic period. Jiangsu has been an important center of trade, culture, and learning for centuries, with many famous scholars, poets, and artists hailing from the region. Today, Jiangsu is a major economic hub, with a diverse range of industries, including textiles, electronics, and biotechnology. The province is also home to many famous tourist attractions, including the ancient city of Nanjing, the scenic town of Suzhou, and the beautiful Lake Tai.

Jiangsu is also known for its unique cultural heritage, with a blend of traditional and modern elements. The province is famous for its traditional Chinese gardens, which are renowned for their beauty and tranquility. Jiangsu is also home to many famous festivals and traditions, including the Qingming Festival, the Dragon Boat Festival, and the Mid-Autumn Festival. The province has a rich culinary tradition, with many famous dishes, including dumplings, noodles, and seafood.
