How Xinjiang Is Divided

Xinjiang is a vast and diverse region, covering over 1.6 million square kilometers in northwest China. The region is divided into 14 prefectures, which are further subdivided into counties, cities, and towns. The administrative structure of Xinjiang is complex, with a mix of prefecture-level cities, autonomous prefectures, and counties. The region is governed by the Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region People's Government, which is responsible for implementing national policies and overseeing the region's economic, social, and cultural development.

The division of Xinjiang into different prefectures and counties reflects the region's geographic and cultural diversity. The prefectures are often defined by their natural features, such as mountains, rivers, and deserts, as well as their cultural and ethnic characteristics. For example, the Kashgar Prefecture is known for its Uyghur culture and history, while the Altay Prefecture is home to a mix of Kazakh and Mongolian ethnic groups.

Regional Differences

  • The regions of Xinjiang differ significantly in terms of culture, with the south being predominantly Uyghur and the north being more diverse, with Kazakh, Mongolian, and Han Chinese populations.
  • The economy of Xinjiang also varies by region, with the north being more industrialized and the south being more agricultural.
  • The climate of Xinjiang ranges from arid desert in the south to mountainous and scenic in the north, with the Tian Shan Mountains and the Altay Mountains offering opportunities for hiking, skiing, and outdoor recreation.
  • The language spoken in Xinjiang also varies by region, with Mandarin being the official language, but Uyghur, Kazakh, and Mongolian also being widely spoken in different areas.

Getting Around

Xinjiang has a well-developed transportation network, with domestic flights connecting the region to major cities in China. The region is also connected by train, with the Lanxin Railway linking Xinjiang to the rest of China. Buses and taxis are also widely available, and many visitors choose to drive or take a private car service to get around. However, driving conditions can be challenging, especially in the rural areas, and visitors should be prepared for long distances and rough roads.

The transportation network in Xinjiang is constantly improving, with new airports, roads, and railways being built to connect the region to the rest of China and Central Asia. Visitors can also take advantage of the region's scenic beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities by hiking, skiing, or taking a horseback ride through the mountains and countryside. However, visitors should also be aware of the regional differences and cultural sensitivities, and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.
