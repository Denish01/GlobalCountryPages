# Best Time to Visit Fujian — Month-by-Month Guide

The best time to visit Fujian is during the spring and autumn months, from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild and comfortable, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. 

[CALLOUT] Best months to visit Fujian: March to May and September to November. Peak season: June to August. Budget season: December to February. Avoid: June to August due to hot and humid weather, and the risk of typhoons. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Seasons Overview [/SECTION]
Fujian, a southeastern province in China, has a subtropical monsoon climate with four distinct seasons. The region experiences a dry season from October to March, with minimal rainfall, and a wet season from April to September, with most of the annual rainfall occurring during these months. The climate in Fujian varies from north to south, with the northern part of the province having a more temperate climate and the southern part having a tropical climate. The mountainous regions in the west have a cooler climate than the coastal areas. The seasons in Fujian have a significant impact on travel, with the dry season being the best time to visit the province's many scenic spots, and the wet season being ideal for visiting the region's many waterfalls and lakes.

The spring season, from March to May, is a great time to visit Fujian, with mild temperatures and minimal rainfall, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and sightseeing. The autumn season, from September to November, is also a great time to visit, with comfortable temperatures and scenic landscapes. The summer months, from June to August, are hot and humid, with frequent rain showers and typhoons, making it the peak tourist season but not the most comfortable time to visit. The winter months, from December to February, are cool and dry, with minimal rainfall, making it a good time to visit the province's many museums and cultural attractions.

Fujian's climate is also influenced by its coastal location, with the ocean having a moderating effect on the temperature. The province's many islands, such as Gulangyu Island, have a unique climate, with a mix of subtropical and tropical characteristics. The region's geography, with its many mountains and valleys, also affects the climate, with the mountainous regions having a cooler climate than the coastal areas.
