# Fujian Cities & Regions Guide

Fujian is a coastal province in southeastern China, divided into nine prefecture-level cities and various counties, with its capital in Fuzhou, and featuring a range of cities and regions that offer unique cultural, economic, and natural attractions.

[SECTION] How Fujian Is Divided [/SECTION]
Fujian is divided into nine prefecture-level cities, which are further divided into counties, county-level cities, and districts. The nine prefecture-level cities are Fuzhou, Xiamen, Putian, Sanming, Quanzhou, Zhangzhou, Nanping, Longyan, and Ningde. Each of these cities has its own unique character and plays a distinct role in the province's economy and culture. The provincial government is responsible for overseeing the administration of these cities and counties, and for implementing policies and programs to promote economic development, education, and healthcare throughout the province.

The administrative structure of Fujian is designed to balance the needs of urban and rural areas, with a focus on promoting economic growth and improving living standards. The province has a number of special economic zones and development areas, which offer incentives and support for businesses and investors. The government also plays a key role in protecting the province's natural environment and cultural heritage, with a number of programs and initiatives aimed at preserving traditional crafts, customs, and historical sites.

- The **Mindong** region in northeastern Fujian is known for its mountainous landscapes, tea plantations, and rural areas, with a strong emphasis on agriculture and natural resources.
- The **Minbei** region in northern Fujian is known for its inland location, rural areas, and natural resources, with a focus on agriculture, forestry, and mining.
- The **Minzhong** region in central Fujian is known for its urban areas, transportation infrastructure, and logistics industry, with a strong emphasis on trade and commerce.

Fujian has a well-developed transportation network, with a number of domestic airports, train stations, and bus terminals. In terms of domestic flights, the province has a number of airports, including Fuzhou Changle International Airport and Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport, which offer regular flights to destinations throughout China. Overall, Fujian's transportation network is well-developed and convenient, making it easy for visitors to explore the province's many cities and regions.
