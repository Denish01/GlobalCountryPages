Getting There

Fujian is easily accessible by air, with several international airports, including Fuzhou Changle International Airport and Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport, which offer direct flights to major cities in Asia, as well as connections to Europe and North America. The region is also connected to neighboring provinces and cities by a network of highways and railways, making it possible to enter Fujian by bus or train from nearby cities. Additionally, Fujian has several major ports, including the Port of Fuzhou and the Port of Xiamen, which receive international cargo ships and passenger ferries.

The main entry points for Fujian include Fuzhou, the capital city, which is located in the northeastern part of the province, and Xiamen, a major port city located on the southeastern coast. Both cities have well-developed transportation infrastructure, including airports, train stations, and bus terminals, making it easy to get around and explore the surrounding areas. From these cities, you can take domestic flights, trains, or buses to other parts of Fujian, or rent a car and drive to your destination.

Domestic Flights

Fujian has an extensive domestic air network, with multiple flights per day connecting the region to major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. The main domestic airlines operating in Fujian include China Eastern, China Southern, and Xiamen Airlines, which offer a range of routes and schedules to suit different budgets and travel plans. To book domestic flights, you can use online travel agencies such as Ctrip or Booking.com, or visit the airport in person to purchase tickets. Approximate costs for domestic flights in Fujian range from $50 to $200 USD per person, depending on the route and time of year.

Trains & Rail

Fujian has a well-developed rail network, with high-speed trains connecting the region to major cities in China, including Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. The main rail lines in Fujian include the Fuzhou-Xiamen Railway and the Xiamen-Shenzhen Railway, which offer frequent departures and comfortable seating. To book train tickets, you can use the official website of China Railway Corporation or visit a train station in person. High-speed trains are a convenient and relatively affordable way to travel in Fujian, with approximate costs ranging from $20 to $100 USD per person, depending on the route and class of service.

Buses & Coaches

Fujian has a comprehensive network of buses and coaches, which connect the region to neighboring provinces and cities. The main bus companies operating in Fujian include Fuzhou Bus Company and Xiamen Bus Company, which offer a range of routes and schedules to suit different budgets and travel plans. Bus travel is a relatively affordable way to get around in Fujian, with approximate costs ranging from $10 to $50 USD per person, depending on the route and type of bus. However, buses may not be as comfortable or convenient as trains or flights, and may not offer the same level of service or amenities.

Driving

  • To drive in Fujian, you will need a valid Chinese driver's license or an international driving permit, which can be obtained through the Chinese government or a travel agency.
  • Road conditions in Fujian are generally good, with well-maintained highways and urban roads, but rural areas may have poorer road conditions.
  • Fuel costs in Fujian are relatively affordable, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 USD per liter.
  • Car rental companies in Fujian include Hertz, Avis, and local operators, which offer a range of vehicles and prices to suit different budgets and travel plans.
  • Hazards to watch out for when driving in Fujian include heavy traffic, pedestrian and cyclist traffic, and poor road conditions in rural areas.
  • It is recommended to drive defensively and follow local traffic rules and regulations to ensure a safe and enjoyable journey.

Transport Tips

  • Be aware of scams and overcharging when using taxis or ride-hailing apps, and always insist on using the meter or a fixed fare.
  • Download the Didi Chuxing or AutoNavi app to book taxis or ride-hailing services, which are widely available and convenient.
  • Use cash or mobile payment methods such as WeChat Pay or Alipay to pay for transportation, as not all operators accept credit cards.
  • Be prepared for crowds and long lines during peak travel periods, such as holidays and festivals.
  • Consider purchasing a China Rail Pass for convenient and affordable train travel, which can be booked online or at train stations.
  • Always follow local traffic rules and regulations, and be mindful of pedestrian and cyclist traffic when driving or walking.
