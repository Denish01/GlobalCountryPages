How Fujian Is Divided

Fujian is divided into nine prefecture-level cities, which are further divided into counties, county-level cities, and districts. The nine prefecture-level cities are Fuzhou, Xiamen, Putian, Sanming, Quanzhou, Zhangzhou, Nanping, Longyan, and Ningde. Each of these cities has its own unique character and plays a distinct role in the province's economy and culture. The provincial government is responsible for overseeing the administration of these cities and counties, and for implementing policies and programs to promote economic development, education, and healthcare throughout the province.

The administrative structure of Fujian is designed to balance the needs of urban and rural areas, with a focus on promoting economic growth and improving living standards. The province has a number of special economic zones and development areas, which offer incentives and support for businesses and investors. The government also plays a key role in protecting the province's natural environment and cultural heritage, with a number of programs and initiatives aimed at preserving traditional crafts, customs, and historical sites.

Major Cities

Fuzhou (7.1 million)

Role: capital and economic hub

Known For: Fuzhou is a major port city and commercial center, with a rich history and culture. The city is known for its traditional architecture, temples, and gardens, as well as its modern amenities and infrastructure.

Best For: business travelers, history buffs, and those interested in exploring the province's capital city.

Xiamen (3.5 million)

Role: economic hub and tourist center

Known For: Xiamen is a major port city and tourist destination, known for its beautiful beaches, scenic coastline, and colonial architecture. The city is a popular destination for domestic and international tourists, and is also a major center for trade and commerce.

Best For: tourists, beachgoers, and those interested in exploring the province's coastal scenery.

Quanzhou (8.6 million)

Role: economic hub and cultural center

Known For: Quanzhou is a major city in southern Fujian, known for its rich history and culture. The city is a center for trade and commerce, and is also home to a number of historical sites and cultural attractions.

Best For: business travelers, history buffs, and those interested in exploring the province's cultural heritage.

Putian (3.2 million)

Role: economic hub and agricultural center

Known For: Putian is a major city in central Fujian, known for its agriculture and natural resources. The city is a center for the production of fruits, vegetables, and other crops, and is also home to a number of industrial and manufacturing enterprises.

Best For: business travelers, nature lovers, and those interested in exploring the province's rural areas.

Zhangzhou (4.8 million)

Role: economic hub and transportation center

Known For: Zhangzhou is a major city in southern Fujian, known for its transportation infrastructure and logistics industry. The city is a center for trade and commerce, and is also home to a number of industrial and manufacturing enterprises.

Best For: business travelers, logistics professionals, and those interested in exploring the province's transportation networks.

Regional Differences

  • The Minnan region in southern Fujian is known for its coastal scenery, port cities, and trade and commerce, with a distinct cultural and linguistic identity shaped by its history of maritime trade and cultural exchange.
  • The Mindong region in northeastern Fujian is known for its mountainous landscapes, tea plantations, and rural areas, with a strong emphasis on agriculture and natural resources.
  • The Minbei region in northern Fujian is known for its inland location, rural areas, and natural resources, with a focus on agriculture, forestry, and mining.
  • The Minzhong region in central Fujian is known for its urban areas, transportation infrastructure, and logistics industry, with a strong emphasis on trade and commerce.

Getting Around

Fujian has a well-developed transportation network, with a number of domestic airports, train stations, and bus terminals. The province is connected to the rest of China by high-speed rail, and also has a number of highways and expressways that link the major cities and regions. The coastal cities of Xiamen and Fuzhou are also major ports, with regular ferry services to nearby islands and coastal cities. In terms of driving conditions, the province has a number of scenic roads and highways, but traffic can be heavy in urban areas, and road conditions can be poor in rural areas.

For visitors, the best way to get around Fujian is by high-speed rail or bus, which offer convenient and affordable transportation between the major cities and regions. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also widely available, but may not be as cost-effective for longer journeys. In terms of domestic flights, the province has a number of airports, including Fuzhou Changle International Airport and Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport, which offer regular flights to destinations throughout China. Overall, Fujian's transportation network is well-developed and convenient, making it easy for visitors to explore the province's many cities and regions.
