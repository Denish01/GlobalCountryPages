[SECTION] What Is Fujian? [/SECTION]
Fujian is a **province** located on the southeastern coast of China, bordering the provinces of Jiangxi to the west, Zhejiang to the north, and Guangdong to the southwest. It is situated on the Taiwan Strait, opposite the island of Taiwan. With a population of approximately 39 million people, Fujian is a significant economic and cultural hub in the region. The province is known for its breathtaking natural scenery, including rugged coastlines, scenic islands, and lush mountains.

Fujian's strategic location has made it an important center for trade and commerce throughout history. The province has a long and complex history, with various dynasties and empires vying for control over the region. Today, Fujian is a thriving **economic zone**, with major industries in manufacturing, logistics, and tourism. The province is also home to a diverse range of **cultural traditions**, including the Hakka and Minnan dialects, which are unique to the region.

Fujian's capital city, Fuzhou, is a major urban center with a rich history and culture. The city is known for its **historic architecture**, including the famous Three Lanes and Seven Alleys, a network of narrow streets and traditional buildings that date back to the Qing dynasty. The province is also home to several **UNESCO World Heritage sites**, including the ancient city of Quanzhou and the Fujian Tulou, a collection of traditional earthen buildings that are unique to the region.
