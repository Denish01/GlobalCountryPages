[SECTION] What Is Henan? [/SECTION]
Henan is a province in the central part of China, bordered by six other provinces, including Shaanxi to the west, Shanxi to the north, Shandong to the northeast, Anhui to the southeast, Hubei to the south, and Jiangsu to the east. It has a total area of approximately 167,000 square kilometers and a population of over 94 million people, making it one of the most populous provinces in China. Henan is known for its fertile plains, mountains, and rivers, including the famous Yellow River, which runs through the province and has played a significant role in shaping its history and culture.

Henan has a long and rich history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Paleolithic era. The province has been an important center of Chinese civilization, with many significant historical events and cultural movements originating from the region. Today, Henan is a major economic hub, with a diverse range of industries, including agriculture, manufacturing, and services. The province is also home to many significant cultural and historical sites, including the famous Longmen Grottoes, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The capital city of Henan is Zhengzhou, which is a major transportation hub and economic center. The city has a long history, dating back to the Shang Dynasty, and is known for its cultural attractions, including the Henan Museum and the Zhengzhou Confucius Temple. Other major cities in Henan include Luoyang, Kaifeng, and Pingdingshan, each with its own unique history, culture, and attractions.
