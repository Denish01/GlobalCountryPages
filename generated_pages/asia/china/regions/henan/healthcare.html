Healthcare Overview

The healthcare system in Henan is a mixed model, with both public and private providers offering a range of medical services. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of good quality, with well-equipped hospitals and clinics, especially in urban areas such as Zhengzhou, the capital city of Henan. However, the quality of care can vary significantly in rural areas, where medical facilities and personnel may be limited. The private healthcare sector is also well-developed, with many high-quality hospitals and clinics catering to the needs of affluent locals and foreigners. Overall, the quality of healthcare in Henan is considered to be good, with a rating of 3/5, although there is room for improvement, especially in rural areas.

The structure of the healthcare system in Henan is similar to that of the rest of China, with a strong emphasis on preventive care and public health programs. The government has implemented various initiatives to improve healthcare access and quality, including the expansion of health insurance coverage and the development of community health centers. Despite these efforts, there are still significant disparities in healthcare access and outcomes between urban and rural areas, with rural areas generally having poorer health indicators and less access to medical care.

In terms of access to healthcare, urban areas such as Zhengzhou and Luoyang have a wide range of medical facilities and services available, including high-quality hospitals, clinics, and specialty care centers. In contrast, rural areas may have limited access to medical care, with fewer hospitals and clinics, and less specialized care available. However, the government has been working to improve healthcare access in rural areas, including the development of telemedicine programs and the deployment of mobile health clinics.

Health Insurance

  • Foreigners living in Henan are recommended to have private health insurance that covers them globally, including in China.
  • Local health insurance options are available, including the China National Health Insurance Scheme, which provides coverage for basic medical services.
  • Expats can also purchase private health insurance from international insurance companies, such as Allianz or Bupa.
  • The cost of health insurance in Henan can vary widely, depending on the type and level of coverage, as well as the age and health status of the individual.
  • It is recommended that foreigners living in Henan have a minimum of $100,000 in annual health insurance coverage to ensure access to quality medical care.
  • Some employers in Henan may offer health insurance as a benefit to their employees, so it is worth checking with your employer to see if this is available.

Pharmacies & Medications

  • Pharmacies are widespread in Henan, with many locations throughout urban and rural areas.
  • A wide range of medications is available, including both Western and traditional Chinese medicines.
  • Prescription medications are generally available, although some medications may require a prescription from a Chinese doctor.
  • Common brands of medications are available, including both domestic and international brands.
  • The cost of medications in Henan can vary, depending on the type and brand of medication, as well as the location and type of pharmacy.

Vaccinations & Health Risks

  • Hepatitis A and hepatitis B vaccinations are recommended for all travelers to Henan.
  • Rabies vaccination is also recommended, especially for those who will be spending time outdoors or around animals.
  • Typhoid and cholera vaccinations may be recommended for certain individuals, such as those who will be working with food or water.
  • Henan is considered to be a low-risk area for malaria, although dengue fever and Japanese encephalitis can be present in some areas.
  • Food and water safety can be a concern in Henan, especially for foreigners who are not used to the local cuisine and water supply.
  • It is recommended that foreigners living in Henan take precautions to avoid foodborne illnesses, such as eating at reputable restaurants and avoiding undercooked meat and raw vegetables.

Medical Tourism

  • Henan is not a major destination for medical tourism, although some foreigners may travel to the region for traditional Chinese medicine treatments.
  • The Zhengzhou International Hospital is a popular destination for foreigners seeking medical care, with a wide range of specialties and English-speaking staff.
  • The cost of medical care in Henan can be lower than in other countries, making it an attractive option for some medical tourists.
  • However, the quality of care can vary, and foreigners should do their research and choose a reputable hospital or clinic.

Emergency Procedures

  • In the event of a medical emergency, foreigners in Henan should call the emergency number 120 for assistance.
  • Ambulance services are available, although they may not always be reliable or prompt.
  • Foreigners should have a plan in place for emergency medical care, including knowing the location of the nearest hospital or clinic.
  • It is recommended that foreigners living in Henan have a portable medical kit with them at all times, including basic first aid supplies and any prescription medications.
  • In the event of a serious medical emergency, foreigners may need to be evacuated to a larger city or another country for treatment, so it is essential to have a plan in place for emergency medical evacuation.
