[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Henan's cuisine is defined by its rich history, cultural diversity, and geographical location. As a province in central China, Henan has been influenced by various culinary traditions, including **Huaiyang cuisine** and **Shandong cuisine**. The province's cuisine is characterized by its use of fresh ingredients, simple cooking methods, and emphasis on preserving traditional techniques. Henan's food culture is also shaped by its agricultural abundance, with the province being a major producer of wheat, rice, and soybeans. Regional variations within Henan include the **Zhengzhou-Style cuisine**, known for its bold flavors and spicy dishes, and the **Kaifeng-Style cuisine**, which is famous for its delicate flavors and intricate cooking techniques.

The history of Henan's cuisine dates back to the **Shang Dynasty**, when the province was an important center for agriculture and trade. Over time, Henan's cuisine has been influenced by various dynasties, including the **Ming** and **Qing** dynasties, which introduced new ingredients and cooking techniques to the region. Today, Henan's cuisine is a unique blend of traditional and modern flavors, with a focus on using fresh, locally-sourced ingredients.

Henan's food culture is also closely tied to its cultural heritage, with many traditional dishes being served during festivals and special occasions. For example, **Dumplings** are a staple food during the **Chinese New Year**, while **Noodles** are typically served during the **Dragon Boat Festival**. The province's cuisine is also known for its **Buddhist vegetarian cuisine**, which is characterized by its use of tofu, vegetables, and other plant-based ingredients.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Table Manners**: it is customary to use chopsticks when eating, and to not leave them standing upright in your rice bowl
- **Tipping**: tipping is not expected, but 5-10% is appreciated for good service
- **Reservation Culture**: reservations are not always necessary, but it is recommended to make a reservation for large groups or special occasions
- **Dress Code**: dress codes vary depending on the restaurant, but it is generally recommended to dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing
- **Eating Etiquette**: it is customary to wait for the host to start eating before you begin, and to not finish a meal completely, as it implies that the host did not provide enough food
- **Gift-Giving**: it is customary to bring a gift, such as fruit or wine, when invited to a meal at someone's home
