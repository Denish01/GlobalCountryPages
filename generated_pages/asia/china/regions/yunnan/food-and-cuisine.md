# Yunnan Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

Yunnan's cuisine is a unique blend of Chinese, Tibetan, and Southeast Asian flavors, offering a diverse range of dishes that showcase the region's cultural and geographical diversity, with must-try dishes including **Crossing the Bridge Noodles**, **Steam Pot Chicken**, and **Xuanwei Ham**.

Meal Times: typical breakfast times are between 7-9 am, lunch between 11 am-1 pm, and dinner between 5-7 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: not expected, but 5-10% is appreciated for good service

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Yunnan's cuisine is shaped by its geographical location, with the region's mountainous terrain and proximity to Tibet and Southeast Asia influencing the types of ingredients and cooking techniques used. The cuisine is known for its bold flavors, with **Sichuan pepper**, **chili peppers**, and **fermented soybeans** being common ingredients. Yunnan's cuisine is also characterized by its use of **wild mushrooms**, **wild herbs**, and other **foraged ingredients**, which are often combined with **pork**, **chicken**, and **beef** to create hearty and flavorful dishes. The region's cuisine is also influenced by its cultural diversity, with **Tibetan**, **Bai**, and **Naxi** communities contributing their own unique cooking traditions and ingredients.

The cuisine in Yunnan varies by region, with different areas specializing in different types of dishes. The **Dali** region is known for its **Bai cuisine**, which features dishes such as **Xuanwei Ham** and **Steamed Fish with Pickled Chili Peppers**. The **Lijiang** region is known for its **Naxi cuisine**, which features dishes such as **Naxi Barbecue** and **Steamed Chicken with Wild Herbs**. The **Kunming** region is known for its **Yunnan cuisine**, which features dishes such as **Crossing the Bridge Noodles** and **Steam Pot Chicken**.

Yunnan's cuisine is also known for its **street food culture**, with many vendors and markets offering a wide range of dishes and snacks. The region is also home to many **food festivals**, which celebrate the local cuisine and offer visitors the opportunity to try a variety of dishes and drinks. Some popular food festivals in Yunnan include the **Kunming Food Festival** and the **Dali Food Festival**.
