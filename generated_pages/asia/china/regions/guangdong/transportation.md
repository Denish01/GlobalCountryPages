# Getting Around Guangdong — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Guangdong, you can utilize a comprehensive network of transportation options, including extensive air, rail, and road systems, as well as various local transport modes. 

[FACTBOX]
Main International Airport(s): Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN), Shenzhen Bao'an International Airport (SZX)
Domestic Air Network: extensive
Rail Network: extensive
Drives On: right
Road Quality: good
Ride-Hailing Apps: Didi Chuxing, Alibaba's AutoNavi
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Guangdong is easily accessible by air, with two main international airports: Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN) and Shenzhen Bao'an International Airport (SZX). Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport is a major hub with direct flights to numerous destinations worldwide, including Asia, Europe, and North America. Shenzhen Bao'an International Airport also offers direct flights to many international destinations, particularly in Asia. Both airports are well-connected to the surrounding cities and provinces by high-speed rail, buses, and taxis.

For those traveling overland, Guangdong shares borders with several provinces in China, including Fujian, Jiangxi, and Hunan, as well as the special administrative regions of Hong Kong and Macau. There are several border crossings, including the Luohu Port and the Futian Port, which connect Guangdong to Hong Kong. Additionally, there are bus and train services available from neighboring provinces and regions, making it convenient to enter Guangdong by land.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Guangdong has an extensive domestic air network, with flights connecting to major cities across China. Domestic airlines such as China Southern, China Eastern, and Shenzhen Airlines operate frequent flights from Guangzhou and Shenzhen to destinations like Beijing, Shanghai, and Xiamen. Booking domestic flights in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $50 to $200 USD, depending on the route and airline. It's also worth noting that some airports in Guangdong, such as Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport, offer convenient check-in and baggage drop-off services, making the travel process more efficient.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Guangdong has an extensive rail network, with high-speed trains connecting major cities like Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and Dongguan. The **Guangzhou-Shenzhen-Hong Kong High-Speed Rail** link is a notable example, offering frequent departures and reducing travel time between these cities to under an hour. Booking train tickets in advance is recommended, especially for high-speed trains, as they tend to sell out quickly. The booking process can be done online through the China Railway Corporation website or through mobile apps like **China Railway Corporation's** official app. Train travel is a convenient and relatively affordable option, with prices ranging from $10 to $50 USD for high-speed trains, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity bus services are readily available in Guangdong, connecting major cities and towns. Bus companies like **Guangzhou Bus** and **Shenzhen Bus** operate frequent services, with routes spanning the entire province. Bus travel is generally affordable, with prices ranging from $5 to $20 USD, depending on the route and type of bus. However, bus travel times can be longer than train or air travel, and the quality of buses can vary. It's recommended to opt for **high-speed buses** or **luxury coaches** for a more comfortable journey.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Guangdong, you'll need a valid Chinese driver's license or an international driving permit.
- Road conditions in Guangdong are generally good, with well-maintained highways and urban roads.
- Fuel costs are relatively affordable, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental services are available in major cities like Guangzhou and Shenzhen, with prices starting from $20 USD per day.
- Hazards to watch out for include heavy traffic, aggressive driving, and pedestrian and cyclist traffic.
- It's recommended to avoid driving during peak hours or in unfamiliar areas, and to always wear a seatbelt and follow local traffic laws.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **traffic scams**, where drivers may take you on a longer route to increase the fare.
- Use **mobile payment apps** like WeChat Pay or Alipay to pay for transportation, as many services do not accept cash.
- Download **transportation apps** like Didi Chuxing or AutoNavi to book taxis or ride-hailing services.
- Always **check the price** before starting your journey, and agree on the fare with the driver.
- Be mindful of **peak travel seasons**, when transportation prices and travel times may increase.
- Consider purchasing a **Guangdong transportation card**, which can be used on public transportation systems throughout the province.
- Stay **informed about local traffic conditions** and plan your route accordingly to avoid congestion.
- Learn some **basic Chinese phrases**, such as "hello" (nǐ hǎo) and "thank you" (xiè xiè), to help you communicate with drivers and locals.
