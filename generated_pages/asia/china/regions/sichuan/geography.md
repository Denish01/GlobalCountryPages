The region is landlocked, with no direct access to the ocean, but its proximity to the Tibetan Plateau and the Himalayan mountain range has a significant impact on its climate and geography. Sichuan is also home to several major rivers, including the Yangtze River, which flows through the region and provides water, transportation, and fertile soil for agriculture.

The location of Sichuan has played a crucial role in shaping its history and culture, with the region serving as a major hub for trade and cultural exchange between China and its neighboring countries. The region's borders have been influenced by its geography, with the Tibetan Plateau to the west and the Sichuan Basin to the east creating natural boundaries and dividing the region into distinct areas. The capital city of Sichuan is Chengdu, which is located in the Sichuan Basin and serves as the economic, cultural, and political center of the region.
