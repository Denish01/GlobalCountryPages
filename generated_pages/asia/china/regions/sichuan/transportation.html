To get around in Sichuan, you can utilize a variety of transportation options, including flights, trains, buses, and taxis, as well as explore the region on foot or by bike, with the most convenient option depending on your specific travel plans and budget.

Getting There

Sichuan can be accessed through several major airports, including Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport, which is the largest airport in the region and offers direct flights to many domestic and international destinations. Mianyang Nanjiao Airport is another significant airport in Sichuan, providing connections to major cities in China. If you prefer to enter Sichuan overland, you can take buses or trains from neighboring provinces, such as Qinghai, Gansu, Shaanxi, and Guizhou. The main entry points by road include the Sichuan-Tibet Highway and the Sichuan-Shaanxi Highway.

For international travelers, the most convenient way to get to Sichuan is by flying into Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport, which has direct connections to many major cities in Asia, Europe, and North America. You can also take domestic flights from other major cities in China to Sichuan. Additionally, Sichuan has several border crossings with neighboring countries, including Nepal and Tibet, although these are mainly used for trade and local travel.

Domestic Flights

Sichuan has an extensive domestic air network, with multiple flights per day connecting the region to other major cities in China. The main domestic airlines operating in Sichuan include China Southern Airlines, China Eastern Airlines, and Sichuan Airlines. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through travel agencies, and it's recommended to book in advance to secure the best prices. The cost of domestic flights in China can vary greatly depending on the route and time of year, but on average, a one-way ticket can cost anywhere from $50 to $200.

Trains & Rail

Sichuan has a well-developed rail network, with high-speed trains connecting the region to other major cities in China. The main rail lines in Sichuan include the Baoji-Chengdu Railway, the Chengdu-Kunming Railway, and the Chengdu-Lanzhou Railway. You can book train tickets online through the China Railway Corporation website or through ticket offices at train stations. High-speed trains, also known as G-trains or D-trains, are the fastest way to travel by rail, with speeds of up to 350 kilometers per hour. The cost of train tickets in China varies depending on the class of service and the route, but on average, a one-way ticket can cost anywhere from $10 to $100.

Buses & Coaches

Sichuan has an extensive network of buses and coaches connecting the region to other parts of China. The main bus companies operating in Sichuan include the Sichuan Bus Company and the China National Travel Service. You can book bus tickets online or through ticket offices at bus stations. The cost of bus tickets in China is generally lower than train or air travel, with prices starting from around $5 for short-distance routes. However, the journey time can be longer, and the comfort level may vary depending on the type of bus and road conditions.

Driving

  • To drive in Sichuan, you need to obtain a Chinese driver's license, which can be done through the Sichuan Public Security Bureau.
  • The road conditions in Sichuan can be challenging, especially in rural areas, with steep mountains and heavy traffic.
  • Fuel costs in China are generally lower than in Western countries, with gasoline prices starting from around $0.50 per liter.
  • Car rental companies are available in major cities, including Chengdu and Mianyang, with prices starting from around $20 per day.
  • When driving in Sichuan, be aware of the local traffic rules and customs, such as giving way to pedestrians and cyclists.
  • Be prepared for potential hazards, such as landslides and rockfalls, especially during the rainy season.

Transport Tips

  • When taking taxis, make sure to use a licensed taxi and agree on the fare before you start your journey.
  • Be aware of scams, such as overcharging or taking a longer route, and always check the meter before you pay.
  • Download the Didi Chuxing app to book ride-hailing services, which is widely available in Sichuan.
  • When using public transport, try to avoid peak hours, especially during rush hour, to avoid overcrowding.
  • Always carry a map or a guidebook, and try to learn some basic Chinese phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you".
  • Be prepared for potential delays or cancellations, especially during the rainy season or festivals, and plan your itinerary accordingly.
  • When driving, always wear a seatbelt and follow local traffic rules to ensure your safety.
  • Try to avoid traveling during the Chinese New Year or National Day holidays, when traffic and transport can be extremely busy.
