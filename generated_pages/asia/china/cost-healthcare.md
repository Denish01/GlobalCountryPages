# Healthcare Costs in China

The cost of healthcare in China can vary significantly depending on the type of care and location, with an average health expenditure per capita of $763, and out-of-pocket spend accounting for 32.2% of total health expenditure.

[SECTION] Healthcare System Overview [/SECTION]
China's healthcare system is a mixed model, combining elements of public and private care. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of lower quality compared to private hospitals, which often have better facilities and more experienced doctors. For locals, the healthcare system is often subsidized by the government, with many citizens having access to basic medical care. However, for foreigners, it is highly recommended to have health insurance, as the cost of medical care can be high, and many hospitals may not accept foreign insurance plans. In addition, the quality of care can vary significantly depending on the location, with major cities like Beijing and Shanghai generally having better-equipped hospitals and more experienced medical staff.

The healthcare system in China is also characterized by a significant disparity between urban and rural areas. In urban areas, there are often more private hospitals and clinics, which can provide high-quality care, but at a higher cost. In rural areas, the public healthcare system is often the only option, and the quality of care can be lower. For foreigners, it is often recommended to seek medical care in major cities, where there are more international-standard hospitals and medical facilities. Many hospitals in China also have international departments, which cater specifically to foreigners and offer higher-quality care, often with English-speaking staff.

[SECTION] Pharmacy & Medication Costs [/SECTION]
- Prescription medication can cost between $5-$20 per month, depending on the type and brand
- Over-the-counter medication is widely available, with prices starting from $1-$5
- Pharmacies are common in urban areas, but may be less accessible in rural areas
- Prescription rules vary, but generally require a doctor's prescription for certain medications
- Common medications like painkillers and antibiotics are widely available, but may require a prescription
- Some medications may be counterfeit or of poor quality, so it's essential to purchase from reputable pharmacies

[SECTION] Emergency Care [/SECTION]
In the event of a medical emergency, it's essential to call the local emergency number, 120, which will dispatch an ambulance to your location. Ambulance services are generally available in urban areas, but may be less accessible in rural areas. For foreigners, it's recommended to seek medical care at international-standard hospitals, which often have English-speaking staff and better-equipped facilities. Some of the best hospitals for foreigners in China include the Beijing United Family Hospital, the Shanghai United Family Hospital, and the Guangzhou United Family Hospital. These hospitals offer high-quality medical care, including emergency services, and often have international-standard facilities and equipment.
