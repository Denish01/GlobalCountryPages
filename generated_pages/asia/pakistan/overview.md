[SECTION] What Is Pakistan? [/SECTION]
Pakistan is a **sovereign** country located in South Asia, bordered by India, China, Afghanistan, and Iran. It is the world's fifth-most populous country, with a diverse geography that ranges from the **Himalayan** mountains in the north to the **Arabian Sea** in the south. Pakistan is known for its rich cultural heritage, which is a blend of **Islamic**, **Mughal**, and **British** influences. The country has a long history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the **Indus Valley Civilization**, which is considered one of the oldest civilizations in the world.

Pakistan is a federal parliamentary republic, with a president serving as the head of state and a prime minister as the head of government. The country has a diverse economy, with major industries including **textiles**, **agriculture**, and **manufacturing**. Pakistan is also home to several **UNESCO World Heritage Sites**, including the ancient city of **Mohenjo-Daro** and the **Lahore Fort**. The country has a vibrant culture, with a mix of traditional and modern music, dance, and art.

Pakistan's geographic position makes it a strategically important country, with several major trade routes passing through it. The country has a long coastline along the Arabian Sea, which provides access to major shipping lanes and trade routes. Pakistan's diverse geography and climate also make it a popular destination for tourists, with several **national parks** and **wildlife reserves** that are home to a wide range of flora and fauna.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1947 - Pakistan gains independence from British colonial rule, with Muhammad Ali Jinnah as its first governor-general.
2. 1956 - Pakistan becomes a republic, with the adoption of its first constitution.
3. 1971 - East Pakistan secedes from Pakistan and becomes the independent country of Bangladesh.
4. 1977 - General Muhammad Zia-ul-Haq seizes power in a military coup, beginning a period of military rule that lasts for over a decade.
5. 2008 - Pakistan transitions back to democratic rule, with the election of Asif Ali Zardari as president.
6. 2010 - Pakistan is affected by severe flooding, which kills over 1,700 people and displaces millions.
7. 2018 - Imran Khan becomes the prime minister of Pakistan, marking a new era in the country's politics.
