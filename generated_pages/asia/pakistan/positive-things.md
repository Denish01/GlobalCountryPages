[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Mohenjo-Daro** archaeological site, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the oldest and most well-preserved ancient cities in the world.
- The **Lahore Fort**, also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a stunning example of Mughal architecture and features intricate marble work and ornate gardens.
- The **Shalamar Gardens**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a beautiful example of Mughal garden design and features intricate water features and walking paths.
- The **Sufi Shrines** of Pakistan, such as the **Data Darbar** in Lahore, are important cultural and spiritual centers that attract millions of visitors each year.
- The **Pakistani Truck Art** tradition, which involves decorating trucks with intricate paintings and ornaments, is a unique and colorful aspect of Pakistani culture.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Malala Yousafzai**, the youngest Nobel Prize laureate, is a Pakistani activist for girls' education and has become a global symbol of courage and resilience.
- The **Pakistani diaspora** has made significant contributions to the economies and cultures of countries such as the United States, the United Kingdom, and Canada.
- **Abdus Salam**, a Pakistani physicist, was the first Muslim to win the Nobel Prize in Physics and made significant contributions to the field of particle physics.
- The **Pakistani community** in the United States, which numbers over 500,000, has made significant contributions to the fields of medicine, engineering, and business.
