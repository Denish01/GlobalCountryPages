Visa Policy Overview

Pakistan has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Currently, citizens of around 50 countries can enter Pakistan visa-free or obtain a visa on arrival, while others need to apply for a visa in advance. The country has been working to relax its visa rules to promote tourism and trade, especially with neighboring countries and strategic partners.

The government of Pakistan has introduced an electronic visa system, which allows citizens of eligible countries to apply for a visa online. This has streamlined the process and made it easier for tourists and business travelers to visit the country. However, the requirements and regulations can still be complex, and it's essential to check with the official authorities for the latest information.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: To visit Pakistan as a tourist, you'll typically need a tourist visa, which can be obtained online through the eVisa system or on arrival at certain airports. The typical stay is 30 days, but this can be extended for an additional 30 days. You'll need to provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds.

Business Travelers: Business travelers will need to apply for a business visa, which requires an invitation letter from a Pakistani company or organization. The requirements and process are similar to those for tourist visas, but you may need to provide additional documentation, such as a letter from your employer.

Students: International students need to apply for a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment at a Pakistani university or institution. The visa is usually valid for the duration of the course, and you'll need to provide documentation, such as a letter of acceptance and proof of payment.

Workers: Workers will need to apply for a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a Pakistani employer. The process can be complex, and you'll need to provide documentation, such as a contract and proof of qualifications.

  3. Recent passport-sized photos
  4. Proof of onward travel
  5. Proof of sufficient funds
  6. Invitation letter (for business or student visas)

  8. Contract and proof of qualifications (for work visas)

  10. Medical certificate (for certain nationalities)

Entry Points & Procedures

Pakistan has several international airports, including Islamabad International Airport, Lahore Allama Iqbal International Airport, and Karachi Jinnah International Airport. When arriving at an airport, you'll need to go through immigration and customs, where you'll be required to present your passport, visa (if required), and other documents. The process can take some time, especially during peak travel seasons. You may also be asked questions about your travel plans and the purpose of your visit.

In addition to airports, Pakistan has several land border crossings with neighboring countries, including India, China, Afghanistan, and Iran. When crossing the border by land, you'll need to present your passport, visa (if required), and other documents at the border crossing. The process can be more complex and time-consuming than at airports, and you may need to provide additional documentation.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not checking the latest visa requirements before traveling
  • Failing to register with the local authorities within 24 hours of arrival
  • Not providing required documentation, such as proof of onward travel
  • Overstaying your visa, which can result in fines and penalties
  • Not respecting local customs and laws, which can lead to difficulties with the authorities
  • Not making photocopies of important documents, such as your passport and visa, in case of loss or theft
