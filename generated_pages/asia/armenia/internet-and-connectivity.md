[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Armenia has undergone significant improvements in recent years, with the average internet speed increasing to 10 Mbps download. While there is still an urban-rural divide in terms of internet quality, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in cities like Yerevan, the overall quality of the internet is good. The internet is generally reliable, with minimal downtime and good connectivity, making it suitable for most users, including remote workers and tourists. However, the rural areas still lag behind, with slower speeds and less reliable connections, which can be a challenge for those who need to stay connected.

The Armenian government has made efforts to improve the country's internet infrastructure, including the rollout of 4G networks and the introduction of fiber-optic internet services. As a result, the internet has become an essential part of daily life in Armenia, with many businesses, schools, and government institutions relying on it to operate. The country's internet penetration rate is around 70%, which is relatively high compared to other countries in the region. Overall, the internet in Armenia is a valuable resource for both locals and visitors, providing access to a wealth of information, entertainment, and communication tools.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Armenia is relatively easy, with several mobile providers offering tourist-friendly plans. The main carriers, VivaCell-MTS, Beeline, and Ucom, offer a range of plans, including prepaid and postpaid options. To buy a SIM card, you will need to provide a valid passport and proof of address, such as a hotel reservation or a rental agreement. You can buy SIM cards at the airport, in mobile provider stores, or at authorized retailers. The cost of a tourist SIM card is around 5 USD, and data plans are relatively affordable, with prices starting from around 10 USD per month for 1 GB of data.

It's recommended to buy a SIM card from one of the main carriers, as they offer the best coverage and speeds. VivaCell-MTS and Beeline are the most popular carriers, and they offer a range of plans, including tourist-friendly options. Ucom is another popular carrier, and it offers competitive pricing and good coverage. When buying a SIM card, make sure to check the coverage and speeds in your area, as well as the cost of data and voice calls.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is not a major issue in Armenia, but some websites may be blocked due to copyright or other restrictions.
- Blocked sites include some torrent and file-sharing platforms, as well as some online gambling sites.
- VPNs are recommended for secure and private browsing, especially when working with sensitive data.
