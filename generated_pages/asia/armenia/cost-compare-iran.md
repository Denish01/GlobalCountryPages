[SECTION] Overview: Armenia vs Iran [/SECTION]
Armenia and Iran are two neighboring countries in the South Caucasus region, with distinct economic and cultural profiles. Armenia has a relatively small economy, with a GDP per capita of around $10,600, while Iran's economy is larger, with a GDP per capita of around $14,400. However, Iran's economy has been facing significant challenges in recent years, including high inflation and economic sanctions, which have driven up the cost of living. In contrast, Armenia has a more stable economy, with lower inflation and a more favorable business environment. As a result, Armenia is generally cheaper than Iran, with a lower cost of living and more affordable prices for everyday items.

The cost of living difference between Armenia and Iran is significant, with Armenia offering a more affordable lifestyle for expats, students, and retirees. Similarly, the cost of a meal at a mid-range restaurant in Armenia is around $5-10, while in Iran it can cost around $10-20. Overall, Armenia offers a more affordable lifestyle, with a lower cost of living and more affordable prices for everyday items.
