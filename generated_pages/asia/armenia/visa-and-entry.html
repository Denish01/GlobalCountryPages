Visa Policy Overview

Armenia has a relatively liberal visa policy, with citizens of over 50 countries eligible for visa-free entry. The country aims to promote tourism and facilitate travel for visitors from around the world. Citizens of countries that are not eligible for visa-free entry can obtain a visa on arrival or apply for an eVisa online. The visa policy is designed to be straightforward and efficient, making it easy for travelers to plan their trip to Armenia.

The Armenian government has introduced various measures to simplify the visa application process, including the introduction of an electronic visa system. This system allows citizens of eligible countries to apply for a visa online and receive a decision within a short period. Additionally, Armenia has signed visa waiver agreements with several countries, allowing citizens of these countries to enter Armenia without a visa. Overall, Armenia's visa policy is designed to be welcoming and accommodating to visitors from around the world.

Tourists: Tourists can enter Armenia visa-free for up to 120 days, depending on their nationality. They must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay. Tourists can extend their stay by registering with the authorities. Typical stay for tourists is 7-10 days, and extensions can be granted for up to 60 days.
Business Travelers: Business travelers may require a visa, depending on their nationality and the purpose of their visit. They must have an invitation letter from an Armenian company, a valid passport, and proof of onward travel. Business travelers can stay in Armenia for up to 120 days, and extensions can be granted for up to 60 days.
Students: Students must have a valid passport, proof of enrollment at an Armenian university, and a letter of acceptance from the university. They can stay in Armenia for the duration of their studies, and their visa can be extended as needed.
Workers: Workers must have a valid passport, a sponsorship letter from an Armenian employer, and a work permit. They can stay in Armenia for up to 12 months, and their visa can be extended for up to 24 months.

The main airport in Armenia is Zvartnots International Airport, located near the capital city of Yerevan. The airport is well-equipped with modern facilities, and visitors can expect a smooth and efficient arrival process. Upon arrival, visitors will need to present their passport, visa (if required), and complete a customs declaration form. The immigration process typically takes around 30 minutes, and visitors can then proceed to collect their luggage and exit the airport. Armenia also has several land borders with neighboring countries, including Georgia, Azerbaijan, Iran, and Turkey. Visitors can enter Armenia by land, but they must ensure that they have the necessary documents and visas (if required) before attempting to cross the border.
Upon arrival, visitors will need to register with the authorities if they plan to stay in Armenia for more than 120 days. This can be done at the local police station or at the Ministry of Internal Affairs. Visitors will need to provide their passport, visa (if required), and proof of address in Armenia. The registration process typically takes around 30 minutes, and visitors will receive a registration certificate that must be carried with them at all times.
