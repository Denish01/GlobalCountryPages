[SECTION] Overview: Armenia vs Turkey [/SECTION]
Armenia and Turkey are two neighboring countries with distinct economic profiles. Armenia has a smaller economy with a GDP per capita of around $10,610, while Turkey's GDP per capita is significantly higher at $28,340. Despite the economic differences, Armenia's cost of living is generally lower, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees. The country's lower cost of living is driven by factors such as lower housing costs, affordable food, and relatively low transportation expenses.

The economic context of both countries also plays a significant role in shaping their cost of living. Armenia's economy is largely driven by agriculture, mining, and manufacturing, while Turkey's economy is more diversified, with a strong focus on services, industry, and tourism. The difference in economic profiles contributes to the variation in cost of living between the two countries. Overall, Armenia is around 20-30% cheaper than Turkey, making it a more affordable option for those looking to relocate or travel.
