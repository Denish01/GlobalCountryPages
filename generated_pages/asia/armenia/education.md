# Armenia Education — Schools, Universities & Student Guide

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Armenia is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, also known as elementary education, is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 10 and provides a foundation in basic subjects such as **mathematics**, **science**, and **language**. Secondary education, which lasts for 7 years, builds on the foundation established during primary education and provides students with a more specialized education in areas such as **humanities** and **natural sciences**. Tertiary education, which includes universities and colleges, provides students with advanced education and training in specialized fields.

The quality of education in Armenia is generally considered to be high, with a strong emphasis on **academic achievement** and **critical thinking**. The education system is predominantly public, with the majority of schools and universities being state-funded. However, there are also a number of private schools and universities that offer alternative educational options for students.

The education system in Armenia has undergone significant reforms in recent years, with a focus on **modernizing** the curriculum and **improving** the quality of education. These reforms have included the introduction of new **teaching methods** and **technologies**, as well as an increased emphasis on **foreign language instruction** and **cultural exchange programs**.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Armenia, international students must obtain a **student visa**, which can be applied for through the Armenian embassy or consulate in their home country.
- **Language proficiency** in Armenian or English is typically required for admission to Armenian universities, although some programs may be taught in other languages.
- **Scholarships** are available to international students, including government-funded scholarships and private awards.
- The cost of living in Armenia is relatively low, with **accommodation** and **food** costs averaging around $200-300 per month.
- International students can work part-time while studying in Armenia, although **work permits** may be required.
- **Health insurance** is required for all international students, and can be purchased through a private provider or the university.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the major strengths of the Armenian education system is its emphasis on **academic achievement** and **critical thinking**.
- However, the system also faces challenges such as **limited resources** and **outdated infrastructure** in some schools.
- Recent reforms have aimed to **modernize** the curriculum and **improve** the quality of education, although more work is needed to address these challenges.
- The education system in Armenia also places a strong emphasis on **cultural heritage** and **national identity**, which can be both a strength and a challenge in terms of **internationalization** and **globalization**.
- Despite these challenges, Armenia has made significant progress in recent years in terms of **increasing access** to education and **improving educational outcomes**.
