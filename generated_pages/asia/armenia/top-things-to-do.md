# Top Things to Do in Armenia — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Armenia, you should not miss the opportunity to explore its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and historical landmarks, including the ancient Geghard Monastery, the picturesque Lake Sevan, and the vibrant capital city of Yerevan.

[TABLE]
| Attraction | Location | Type | Why Visit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Geghard Monastery | Goght, Kotayk Province | Medieval monastery | A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most famous tourist destinations in Armenia |
| Etchmiadzin Cathedral | Vagharshapat, Armavir Province | Cathedral | The mother church of the Armenian Apostolic Church and a significant historical landmark |
| Tatev Monastery | Tatev, Syunik Province | Medieval monastery | A stunning example of Armenian architecture and a popular spot for hiking and trekking |
| Yerevan Brandy Company | Yerevan | Factory | A renowned brandy producer that offers tours and tastings of its famous Ararat brandy |

| Matenadaran Museum | Yerevan | Museum | A unique museum that houses a vast collection of ancient manuscripts and books |

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **duduk**-making workshop, where you can learn to craft and play this traditional Armenian instrument
- Attend a **lavash**-baking masterclass, where you can learn to make this traditional Armenian bread
- Go on a **wine-tasting** tour in the Vayots Dzor Province, where you can sample some of Armenia's famous wines
- Take a **hiking** trip in the **Dilijan National Park**, where you can enjoy stunning views and scenic landscapes
- Visit a **traditional Armenian village**, where you can experience the local culture and way of life
- Attend a **traditional Armenian dance** performance, where you can enjoy the colorful and energetic dances of Armenia

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Explore the **Dilijan National Park**, which offers stunning scenery, hiking trails, and opportunities for birdwatching
- Visit the **Lake Sevan**, which is the largest lake in Armenia and a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating
- Go **hiking** in the **Armenian Highlands**, where you can enjoy stunning views and scenic landscapes
- Take a **horseback ride** through the **Ararat Valley**, where you can enjoy the scenic views and traditional villages
- Visit the **Jermuk Waterfall**, which is a stunning natural wonder and a popular spot for hiking and trekking

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **National Museum of Armenia**, which houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the country's history and culture
- Attend a **performance** at the **Yerevan Opera House**, which is a stunning example of Soviet architecture and a popular spot for cultural events
- Explore the **Etchmiadzin Cathedral**, which is the mother church of the Armenian Apostolic Church and a significant historical landmark
- Take a **tour** of the **Yerevan Brandy Company**, which is a renowned brandy producer and a popular spot for tastings and tours
- Visit the **Matenadaran Museum**, which houses a vast collection of ancient manuscripts and books and is a significant cultural landmark

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Tumo Center for Creative Technologies**, which is a unique hub for innovation and creativity
- Explore the **Vernissage Market**, which is a popular spot for shopping and browsing local handicrafts and souvenirs
- Take a **stroll** through the **Lovers' Park**, which is a beautiful green space in the heart of Yerevan
- Visit the **Children's Park**, which is a popular spot for families and offers a range of attractions and activities
- Go **off-the-beaten-path** and explore the **Armenian countryside**, where you can experience the local culture and way of life

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to **Garni Gorge** (30 km, 45 minutes from Yerevan), which offers stunning views and scenic landscapes
- Visit **Etchmiadzin Cathedral** (20 km, 30 minutes from Yerevan), which is the mother church of the Armenian Apostolic Church and a significant historical landmark
- Explore **Dilijan National Park** (100 km, 2 hours from Yerevan), which offers stunning scenery, hiking trails, and opportunities for birdwatching
- Go on a **wine-tasting** tour in the **Vayots Dzor Province** (120 km, 2.5 hours from Yerevan), where you can sample some of Armenia's famous wines
- Take a day trip to **Lake Sevan** (60 km, 1 hour from Yerevan), which is the largest lake in Armenia and a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating
