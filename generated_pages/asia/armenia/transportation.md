# Getting Around Armenia — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Armenia, you can use a combination of domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis, as well as drive or use ride-hailing services, with Yerevan, the capital city, serving as the main transportation hub.

Main International Airport(s): Zvartnots International Airport (EVN)

Rail Network: limited
Drives On: right
Road Quality: moderate
Ride-Hailing Apps: Yandex.Taxi, Bolt
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Armenia can be reached by air, land, or a combination of both, with the main international airport, Zvartnots International Airport (EVN), located about 10 kilometers west of Yerevan. Direct flights are available from major European cities, such as Moscow, Paris, and Amsterdam, as well as from neighboring countries like Georgia and Iran. Alternatively, you can enter Armenia overland from Georgia, Azerbaijan, Iran, or Turkey, with the most popular border crossing being the one with Georgia. 

The main overland entry points are located along the borders with neighboring countries, with the most popular ones being the Bagratashen border crossing with Georgia, the Meghri border crossing with Iran, and the Yeraskh border crossing with Azerbaijan. However, please note that the border with Turkey is currently closed, and the border with Azerbaijan is subject to certain restrictions due to the ongoing conflict in the region. It is essential to check the current entry requirements and any travel restrictions before planning your trip to Armenia.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Armenia has a limited domestic air network, with only a few flights available between Yerevan and other major cities like Gyumri and Vanadzor. The main domestic airline is **Armenia Airways**, which operates flights from Yerevan's Zvartnots International Airport to other parts of the country. You can book domestic flights online or through a local travel agency, with approximate costs ranging from $50 to $100 USD per one-way ticket, depending on the route and time of booking. It is recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons, to secure the best rates.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Armenia has a limited rail network, with only a few trains operating between Yerevan and other major cities like Gyumri and Vanadzor. The trains are relatively slow and not very frequent, but they can be a cheap and scenic way to travel. The main train operator is **South Caucasus Railway**, which offers both passenger and freight services. You can book train tickets at the train station or through a local travel agency, with approximate costs ranging from $5 to $20 USD per one-way ticket, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Armenia has an extensive network of buses and coaches, with many private companies operating between Yerevan and other major cities. The buses are generally modern and comfortable, and they can be a convenient and affordable way to travel. The main bus companies are **Yerevan Bus Factory** and **Gyumri Bus Factory**, which offer both intercity and international services. You can book bus tickets at the bus station or through a local travel agency, with approximate costs ranging from $5 to $20 USD per one-way ticket, depending on the route and class of service.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Armenia, you need to have a valid **driver's license** and a **car insurance** policy that covers you in Armenia.
- The road conditions in Armenia can be **moderate to poor**, especially outside of Yerevan, so it's essential to drive carefully and be prepared for unexpected hazards.
- Fuel costs in Armenia are relatively **low**, with gasoline prices ranging from $0.50 to $1.00 USD per liter.
- Car rental companies like **Europcar** and **Hertz** have offices in Yerevan, and you can rent a car for approximately $20-$50 USD per day.
- When driving in Armenia, be aware of **aggressive drivers** and **poor road conditions**, and always wear a seatbelt and follow local traffic rules.
- It's also essential to have a **basic knowledge of Armenian** and a **map or GPS device**, as not all road signs are translated into English.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Always **agree on the price** before taking a taxi or using a ride-hailing service.
- Be aware of **scams and overcharging**, especially when using taxis or buses.
- Download the **Yandex.Taxi** or **Bolt** app to book a ride-hailing service.
- Use **cash** when paying for transportation, as not all providers accept credit or debit cards.
- Be prepared for **traffic jams** and **road closures**, especially during peak travel seasons.
- Always **follow local traffic rules** and **wear a seatbelt** when driving or riding in a vehicle.
- Consider purchasing a **local SIM card** or **portable Wi-Fi hotspot** to stay connected and navigate Armenia's transportation system.
