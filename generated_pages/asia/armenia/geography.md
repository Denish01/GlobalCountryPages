[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Armenia is a **sovereign** country located in the **South Caucasus** region of Asia. It is situated in the mountainous area between the **Black Sea** and the **Caspian Sea**, but it is **landlocked**, meaning it does not have any direct access to the ocean. Armenia is bordered by four countries: Georgia to the north, Azerbaijan to the east, Iran to the south, and Turkey to the west. The country's strategic location has made it an important crossroads between Europe and Asia throughout history.

The borders of Armenia are marked by mountains and rivers, with the **Lesser Caucasus Mountains** forming the northern border with Georgia. The **Aragatsotn Province** in the west and the **Syunik Province** in the south are also mountainous regions, with peaks reaching elevations of over 3,000 meters. The **Araks River** forms part of the border with Turkey, while the **Debed River** marks the border with Georgia. Armenia's unique location has resulted in a diverse geography and climate, with different regions experiencing different conditions throughout the year.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Armenia's terrain is characterized by **mountains**, **valleys**, and **plateaus**. The country is home to several mountain ranges, including the **Geghama Mountains**, the **Vardenis Mountains**, and the **Zangezur Mountains**. The **Aragats** mountain, the highest peak in Armenia, is a popular destination for hikers and climbers. The **Sevan Lake**, the largest lake in Armenia, is situated in the **Geghama Mountains** and is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating.

The **Ararat Valley** in the west is a fertile region, with the **Araks River** flowing through it. The valley is home to several important cities, including the capital **Yerevan**, and is the country's main agricultural region. The **Dilijan National Park** in the north is a protected area, known for its **forests**, **rivers**, and **wildlife**. The park is a popular destination for hiking, birdwatching, and camping. Armenia's diverse terrain and landscape have resulted in a wide range of ecosystems, with different regions supporting different types of flora and fauna.

The **climate** and **geology** of Armenia have also played a significant role in shaping the country's landscape. The **volcanic** region of **Javakhk** in the north is known for its **hot springs** and **mineral water**. The **Sevan Lake** is also a popular spot for **mining**, with several **mineral deposits** found in the surrounding mountains. Armenia's unique geology has resulted in a diverse range of **minerals**, including **copper**, **gold**, and **molybdenum**.
