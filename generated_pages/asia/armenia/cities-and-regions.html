How Armenia Is Divided

Armenia is a sovereign country located in the South Caucasus region of Asia. The country is divided into 11 provinces, also known as marzes, which are further subdivided into cities, towns, and villages. The provinces of Armenia are Aragatsotn, Ararat, Armavir, Gegharkunik, Kotayk, Lori, Shirak, Syunik, Tavush, Vayots Dzor, and Yerevan. Each province has its own administrative center and is responsible for managing local affairs.

The administrative structure of Armenia is designed to provide a high level of autonomy to the provinces, allowing them to manage their own local affairs and make decisions that are in the best interest of their residents. The provinces are responsible for providing a range of services, including education, healthcare, and infrastructure development. The capital city, Yerevan, is a special case, as it is not part of any province and is instead governed directly by the central government.

Regional Differences

  • The regions of Armenia differ significantly in terms of their geography, economy, and culture. The mountainous regions of Lori, Syunik, and Vayots Dzor are known for their stunning natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities, while the fertile regions of Ararat and Armavir are known for their agricultural production and wine production.
  • The urban regions of Yerevan and Gyumri are known for their rich cultural heritage and historic architecture, while the rural regions of Aragatsotn and Tavush are known for their traditional way of life and historic sites.
  • The economy of Armenia is also varied by region, with the urban regions of Yerevan and Gyumri being the main drivers of economic growth, while the rural regions are more focused on agriculture and small-scale industry.
  • The language and culture of Armenia are also varied by region, with the urban regions of Yerevan and Gyumri being more cosmopolitan and Western-influenced, while the rural regions are more traditional and conservative.

Getting Around

Armenia has a well-developed transportation network, with a wide range of options available for getting around the country. The capital city, Yerevan, is served by an international airport, which offers flights to destinations in Europe, Asia, and the Middle East. There are also domestic flights available between Yerevan and other major cities in Armenia, including Gyumri and Vanadzor.

The road network in Armenia is also well-developed, with a wide range of roads and highways connecting the major cities and towns. The main highway, the M1, runs from Yerevan to the Georgian border, while the M2 runs from Yerevan to the Iranian border. There are also a wide range of buses and taxis available, which offer a convenient and affordable way to get around the country.

In addition to buses and taxis, Armenia also has a wide range of other transportation options available, including trains and rental cars. The train network in Armenia is limited, but it offers a scenic and relaxing way to see the countryside. Rental cars are also widely available, and offer a convenient and flexible way to get around the country.
