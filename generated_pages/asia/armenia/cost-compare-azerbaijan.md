[SECTION] Overview: Armenia vs Azerbaijan [/SECTION]
Armenia and Azerbaijan are two neighboring countries in the South Caucasus region, with distinct economic and cultural profiles. Armenia has a more **diversified economy** with a strong focus on services, while Azerbaijan's economy is largely driven by its **oil and gas sector**. In terms of cost of living, Armenia tends to be more affordable, with lower prices for accommodation, food, and transportation. However, Azerbaijan's stronger economy and higher average salaries mean that locals may have more purchasing power. Overall, Armenia is a more budget-friendly option for expats, students, and travelers, while Azerbaijan may be more suitable for those with higher incomes or a taste for luxury.

The cost of living difference between the two countries is significant, with Armenia offering savings of up to 20% on everyday expenses. This is largely due to the fact that Armenia has a more **competitive market** with a wider range of options for accommodation, food, and services. In contrast, Azerbaijan's economy is more **monopolized**, leading to higher prices for certain goods and services. However, Azerbaijan's **modern infrastructure** and **high standard of living** may make it a more attractive option for some, despite the higher costs.
