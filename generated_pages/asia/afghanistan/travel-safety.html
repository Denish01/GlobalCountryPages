Overall Safety Assessment

Afghanistan is considered a high-risk destination due to ongoing conflict, terrorism, and crime. The country has been plagued by decades of war and instability, which has led to a breakdown in law and order. As a result, the risk of violent crime, kidnapping, and terrorism is high. Additionally, the country's infrastructure and emergency services are underdeveloped, making it challenging for tourists to access medical care or assistance in case of an emergency. However, it is essential to note that many Afghans are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and with proper planning and precautions, it is possible to have a safe and enriching experience in the country.

Despite the challenges, Afghanistan is a country with a rich history and culture, and many tourists are drawn to its stunning landscapes, ancient monuments, and vibrant markets. However, it is crucial to exercise extreme caution when traveling to Afghanistan. The country's security situation can change rapidly, and tourists should be prepared for unexpected events, such as demonstrations, attacks, or natural disasters. It is also essential to respect local customs and traditions, as well as to follow local advice and guidance to minimize the risk of encountering problems.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Kidnapping: a high risk of kidnapping, particularly in areas with high levels of terrorism and insurgency
  • Terrorism: a high risk of terrorist attacks, particularly in urban areas
  • Scams: a high risk of scams, particularly in markets and bazaars
  • Theft: a high risk of theft, particularly in crowded areas
  • Corruption: a high risk of corruption, particularly when dealing with officials
  • Landmines: a risk of landmines and unexploded ordnance, particularly in rural areas
  • Demonstrations: a risk of demonstrations and protests, which can turn violent
  • Banditry: a risk of banditry and armed robbery, particularly on roads and highways

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: hepatitis A, hepatitis B, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended
  • Water Safety: tap water is not safe to drink, bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: medical facilities are limited and underdeveloped, particularly outside of urban areas
  • Pharmacy Access: pharmacy access is limited, particularly outside of urban areas

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep a low profile
  • Avoid traveling at night and use reputable transportation services
  • Keep valuables secure and be cautious of scams and theft
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Follow local advice and guidance
  • Stay informed about local conditions and security developments
  • Keep emergency contact numbers handy
  • Consider hiring a reputable guide or security service

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be extremely cautious when traveling alone, particularly at night
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions
  • Avoid traveling to areas with high levels of terrorism and insurgency
  • Consider hiring a reputable guide or security service
  • Stay in touch with family and friends back home and keep them informed of your itinerary and whereabouts
