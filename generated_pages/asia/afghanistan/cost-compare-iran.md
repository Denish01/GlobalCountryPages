[SECTION] Overview: Afghanistan vs Iran [/SECTION]
Afghanistan and Iran are two neighboring countries in Asia with distinct economic profiles. Afghanistan is a developing country with a relatively low GDP per capita, while Iran has a more diversified economy with a higher GDP per capita. The cost of living in Afghanistan is generally lower than in Iran, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being significantly cheaper. However, the economic context of both countries is complex, and prices can vary greatly depending on the location, with cities like Kabul and Tehran being more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas.

The overall cost of living difference between Afghanistan and Iran is around 20-30%, with Afghanistan being the cheaper option. This is due to several factors, including the lower cost of housing, food, and transportation in Afghanistan. However, it's worth noting that Iran has a more developed economy and infrastructure, which can provide a higher standard of living and better amenities. Expats and travelers should consider these factors when deciding which country to visit or live in.
