# Vietnam Economy - GDP, Industries & Trade

Vietnam's economy is a rapidly growing, mixed economy driven by a combination of state-owned enterprises, private sector growth, and foreign investment, with a nominal GDP of over $240 billion and a per capita GDP of around $2,400.

[SECTION] Economic Overview [/SECTION]
Vietnam's economy has experienced significant growth and transformation over the past few decades, transitioning from a centrally planned economy to a more market-oriented economy. The country has made considerable progress in reducing poverty and improving living standards, with the poverty rate declining from over 50% in the 1990s to around 5% today. Vietnam's economy is classified as a lower-middle-income economy, with a GDP per capita of around $2,400. The country has set ambitious targets to become an upper-middle-income economy by 2035 and a high-income economy by 2045.

Vietnam's economic growth has been driven by a combination of factors, including large-scale privatization, trade liberalization, and foreign investment. The country has become a popular destination for foreign investors, with many multinational corporations setting up operations in Vietnam to take advantage of its low labor costs, favorable business environment, and strategic location. The government has also implemented various policies to support the development of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), which account for a significant proportion of the country's economic output.

The Vietnamese government has also made efforts to diversify the country's economy, reducing its dependence on agriculture and increasing the share of industry and services in the GDP. The government has also invested heavily in infrastructure development, including the construction of new roads, ports, and airports, to support the growth of trade and commerce.

[SECTION] Economic Outlook [/SECTION]
Vietnam's economy is expected to continue growing at a rapid pace, driven by a combination of foreign investment, trade liberalization, and domestic consumption. The government has set ambitious targets to increase the share of industry and services in the GDP, while reducing the share of agriculture. The country is also expected to benefit from its participation in various free trade agreements, including the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership (CPTPP) and the EU-Vietnam Free Trade Agreement (EVFTA).

The Vietnamese government has also made efforts to improve the business environment, including simplifying regulatory procedures and reducing bureaucratic barriers. The government has also invested heavily in human capital development, including education and training programs, to support the growth of the economy. Major projects and reforms include the development of new industrial parks and economic zones, the expansion of the country's transport network, and the implementation of various policies to support the growth of SMEs. Overall, Vietnam's economy is expected to continue growing at a rapid pace, driven by a combination of domestic and external factors.
