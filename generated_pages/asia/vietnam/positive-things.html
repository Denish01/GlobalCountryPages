Vietnam is renowned for its remarkable achievements in various fields, including science, sports, arts, and development, making it a country with a rich cultural heritage and a strong presence on the global stage.

Notable Achievements

CategoryAchievementDetails
ScienceNASA CollaborationVietnam has collaborated with NASA on several projects, including the Vietnam Natural Resource Management Project, which aims to improve the country's natural resource management capabilities.
SportsOlympic MedalsVietnamese athletes have won several Olympic medals, including gold medals in shooting and weightlifting, demonstrating the country's growing prowess in international sports.
ArtsUNESCO RecognitionThe art of Bai Choi, a traditional Vietnamese folk game, has been recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, highlighting the country's rich cultural diversity.
DevelopmentPoverty ReductionVietnam has made significant strides in reducing poverty, with the poverty rate decreasing from 58% in 1993 to less than 5% in 2020, according to the World Bank.
InnovationStart-up EcosystemVietnam has a thriving start-up ecosystem, with major cities like Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City hosting numerous incubators, accelerators, and innovation hubs, such as the Vietnam Silicon Valley project.
Global ContributionsUN PeacekeepingVietnam has been an active participant in UN peacekeeping missions, with Vietnamese troops serving in several countries, including South Sudan and the Central African Republic.
EducationInternational RankingsVietnamese universities, such as the Vietnam National University, have been ranked among the top universities in Asia, reflecting the country's growing reputation for academic excellence.
Environmental ConservationNational ParksVietnam is home to several national parks, including Ha Long Bay and Phong Nha-Ke Bang, which are recognized for their unique biodiversity and natural beauty.

Cultural Treasures

  • The Imperial City of Thang Long in Hanoi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring ancient temples, palaces, and artifacts that showcase Vietnam's rich cultural heritage.
  • The Hoi An Ancient Town is a well-preserved example of a traditional Vietnamese trading port, with its narrow streets, ancient temples, and vibrant local markets.
  • The Ca Tru music, a traditional form of Vietnamese music, has been recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, highlighting the country's rich cultural diversity.
  • The Water Puppetry art form, which originated in Vietnam, is a unique and fascinating form of storytelling that combines puppetry, music, and water.
  • The Vietnamese Calligraphy art form is a traditional and highly respected form of artistic expression, with many Vietnamese calligraphers gaining international recognition for their work.

People & Global Impact

  • Nguyen Thi Minh Khai, a Vietnamese revolutionary and politician, played a key role in the country's fight for independence and is still revered as a national hero.
  • The Vietnamese diaspora has made significant contributions to the global community, with many Vietnamese individuals achieving success in fields such as science, technology, and the arts.
  • Ton Duc Thang, a Vietnamese revolutionary and statesman, was a close associate of Ho Chi Minh and played a key role in the country's early development.
  • The Vietnamese community is known for its strong family values, respect for tradition, and hospitality towards visitors, making it a welcoming and inclusive society.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Vietnamese people is consistently praised by visitors, with many commenting on the warm welcomes and generous nature of the locals.
  • The food in Vietnam is renowned for its freshness, variety, and flavor, with popular dishes such as Pho and Banh Mi gaining international recognition.
  • The scenery in Vietnam is breathtaking, with stunning natural beauty ranging from the Ha Long Bay to the Mekong Delta.
  • The experiences in Vietnam are diverse and exciting, with visitors able to explore ancient temples, try traditional crafts, and take part in vibrant festivals and celebrations.
  • The markets in Vietnam are a treasure trove of local goods, with visitors able to find everything from fresh produce to handmade crafts and souvenirs.
  • The cycling routes in Vietnam are popular among visitors, with many taking advantage of the country's scenic countryside and quiet roads to explore the local landscape.

Hidden Gems

  • The Con Dao Islands are a little-known archipelago off the coast of Vietnam, featuring stunning beaches, coral reefs, and a rich history.
  • The Ba Be National Park is a remote and pristine area of natural beauty, with visitors able to trek through lush forests, swim in crystal-clear lakes, and spot rare wildlife.
  • The Mai Chau Valley is a scenic and peaceful region, with visitors able to experience traditional Vietnamese rural life, try local cuisine, and take part in outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.
  • The Ly Son Island is a small and tranquil island off the coast of Vietnam, featuring stunning scenery, ancient temples, and a unique cultural heritage.
