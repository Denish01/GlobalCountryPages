# Vietnam Visa & Entry Requirements

To enter Vietnam, citizens of most countries will need a visa, although some nationalities are eligible for visa-free access or can obtain a visa on arrival. 

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Vietnam before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Vietnam has a moderate visa policy, with citizens of 24 countries eligible for visa-free access, including ASEAN member states, Japan, South Korea, and some European countries. The visa-free stay period ranges from 14 to 90 days, depending on the nationality. Citizens of other countries can apply for a visa on arrival or an e-visa, which is typically valid for a single entry and a stay of up to 30 days.

The Vietnamese government has been actively promoting tourism and has introduced various visa facilitation measures, including the e-visa system, which allows citizens of 80 countries to apply for a visa online. However, the visa requirements and regulations can change frequently, so it is essential to check with the official embassy or consulate of Vietnam for the latest information.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can apply for a single-entry or multiple-entry visa, which is typically valid for a stay of up to 30 days. The visa application process usually takes 2-3 working days, and tourists can extend their stay for an additional 30 days. **Business Travelers:** Business travelers will need to provide an invitation letter from a Vietnamese company and may need to apply for a different type of visa, such as a business visa or a work visa. **Students:** Students will need to provide proof of enrollment at a Vietnamese university and may need to apply for a student visa, which is typically valid for a stay of up to 12 months. **Workers:** Workers will need to have a sponsorship from a Vietnamese company and may need to apply for a work visa, which is typically valid for a stay of up to 2 years.

[SECTION] Documents Checklist [/SECTION]
1. A valid passport with at least 6 months' validity
2. A completed visa application form
3. One recent passport-sized photo
4. Proof of payment for the visa application fee
5. Proof of onward travel or return ticket
6. Hotel reservation or proof of accommodation
7. Travel itinerary or tour booking
8. Proof of sufficient funds, such as cash or credit cards
9. Health insurance certificate, if required
10. Any additional documents required by the embassy or consulate, such as a police certificate or birth certificate

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airports in Vietnam are Noi Bai International Airport in Hanoi, Tan Son Nhat International Airport in Ho Chi Minh City, and Da Nang International Airport in Da Nang. When arriving at the airport, visitors will need to go through immigration and customs, where they will need to present their passport, visa, and other required documents. The immigration process typically takes around 15-30 minutes, depending on the time of day and the number of passengers. Visitors can also enter Vietnam through land borders, such as the border with China, Laos, or Cambodia, where they will need to go through immigration and customs procedures.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not checking the visa requirements and regulations before traveling to Vietnam
- Not having a valid passport with at least 6 months' validity
- Not providing all the required documents for the visa application
- Not paying the correct visa application fee
- Not having proof of onward travel or return ticket
- Not having sufficient funds to support their stay in Vietnam
