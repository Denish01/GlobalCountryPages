# Vietnam Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Vietnam is generally a safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other popular tourist destinations, but visitors should still be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Vietnam is a popular tourist destination with a rich culture and history, attracting millions of visitors each year. The country has a relatively low crime rate, with most crimes being petty and opportunistic. However, as with any foreign travel, there are potential risks and hazards that visitors should be aware of. These include petty theft, scams, and traffic accidents, which can be mitigated by taking common-sense precautions and staying informed.

Overall, Vietnam is a safe country to visit, with most tourists experiencing no problems during their stay. The Vietnamese government has taken steps to improve tourist safety, including the establishment of a tourist police force and the implementation of safety measures in popular tourist areas. However, visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe, such as avoiding traveling alone at night and keeping valuables secure.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Pickpocketing** is a common problem in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations.
- **Scams** can occur, particularly in popular tourist areas, including scams involving **motorbike rentals** and **tour bookings**.
- **Traffic accidents** are a significant risk in Vietnam, particularly when crossing the road or riding a motorbike.
- **Food poisoning** can occur, particularly when eating from street vendors or unlicensed restaurants.
- **Theft** from hotels and guesthouses can occur, particularly if valuables are left unsecured.
- **Overcharging** by taxi drivers and other service providers can occur, particularly in tourist areas.
- **Snatch-and-grab** robberies can occur, particularly in crowded areas.
- **Cybercrime** is a growing problem in Vietnam, with some tourists reporting unauthorized transactions on their credit cards.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for travel to Vietnam.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in major cities are generally good, but can be limited in rural areas. Travelers should have **health insurance** that covers medical evacuation.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, particularly in major cities, but some medications may not be available.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas.
- Keep valuables secure, and use a hotel safe or locker if available.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, particularly in rural areas.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing services.
- Be cautious when crossing the road, and use pedestrian crossings where available.
- Avoid eating from unlicensed street vendors or restaurants.
- Keep your hotel room door locked, and use the hotel safe if available.
- Be cautious when using ATMs, and avoid using them in isolated areas.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Vietnam is generally a safe country for solo and women travelers, but some precautions should be taken.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, particularly in rural areas.
- Be cautious when interacting with strangers, particularly in crowded areas.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing services, and avoid hitchhiking.
- Dress modestly, particularly when visiting temples or rural areas.
