[SECTION] What Is Vietnam? [/SECTION]
Vietnam is a **sovereign** country located in Southeast Asia, bordered by China to the north, Laos to the northwest, and Cambodia to the southwest. The country has a long coastline along the South China Sea to the east, and its geographic position has played a significant role in shaping its history and culture. With a rich heritage and stunning natural beauty, Vietnam is known for its vibrant cities, delicious cuisine, and friendly people.

Vietnam's sovereignty status is that of a socialist republic, with a one-party system and a government that plays a significant role in the economy. The country has a diverse geography, with mountains, forests, and coastal plains, and its climate varies from tropical in the south to temperate in the north. Vietnam is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including many endangered species, and its natural beauty is a major draw for tourists.

Vietnam is also known for its complex history, with many different cultures and empires rising and falling over the centuries. The country has been influenced by Chinese, Indian, and European cultures, and its unique blend of traditions and customs is reflected in its cuisine, festivals, and art. From the bustling streets of Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City to the peaceful countryside and stunning beaches, Vietnam is a country that has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 111 BC - Vietnam is annexed by the Chinese Han Dynasty, marking the beginning of over 1,000 years of Chinese rule.
2. 938 AD - The Vietnamese defeat the Chinese at the Battle of Bach Dang, gaining independence and establishing the Dai Viet kingdom.
3. 1802 - The Nguyen Dynasty is established, marking the beginning of a new era of Vietnamese history.
4. 1954 - The First Indochina War ends with the signing of the Geneva Accords, recognizing Vietnam's independence from France.
5. 1976 - North and South Vietnam are reunified under communist rule, with Hanoi as the capital.
6. 1986 - The Vietnamese government introduces economic reforms, known as **Doi Moi**, which lead to rapid economic growth and transformation.
7. 2007 - Vietnam joins the World Trade Organization, further integrating itself into the global economy.
