# Education Costs in Kazakhstan

The cost of education in Kazakhstan varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Kazakhstan's education system is a mix of public and private institutions, with a strong emphasis on **bilingual education** in Kazakh and Russian. The country has a 12-year compulsory education system, which includes primary, secondary, and upper secondary education. Public education is generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong focus on **mathematics**, **science**, and **language skills**. However, private schools and international schools often offer a more **Western-style education** and may have additional resources and facilities. International schools are available in major cities like Astana and Almaty, offering a range of curricula including **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Exams**.

The language of instruction in public schools is typically Kazakh or Russian, while private and international schools may offer instruction in **English** or other languages. The education system in Kazakhstan is overseen by the **Ministry of Education and Science**, which is responsible for setting curriculum standards and overseeing the quality of education. In recent years, the government has implemented a number of reforms aimed at improving the quality of education, including the introduction of new curriculum standards and the development of **online learning platforms**.
