[SECTION] What Is Kazakhstan? [/SECTION]
Kazakhstan is a **sovereign** country located in Central Asia, bordered by Russia to the north, China to the east, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan to the south. With a vast territory of over 2.7 million square kilometers, Kazakhstan is the world's largest landlocked country and the ninth-largest country by land area. The country has a diverse geography, featuring vast steppes, mountains, and deserts, including the **Kyzylkum Desert** and the **Tien Shan mountain range**.

Kazakhstan is known for its rich natural resources, including oil, natural gas, and minerals such as uranium, copper, and gold. The country has a growing economy and is a member of several international organizations, including the **United Nations**, the **European Bank for Reconstruction and Development**, and the **Shanghai Cooperation Organization**. The capital city, Astana, is a modern and futuristic city, featuring iconic buildings such as the **Bayterek Tower** and the **Palace of Peace and Reconciliation**.

Kazakhstan has a unique cultural heritage, with a mix of traditional nomadic and modern urban influences. The country has a diverse population, with over 130 ethnic groups, including Kazakhs, Russians, Uzbeks, and Ukrainians. The official languages are Kazakh and Russian, and the currency is the **Kazakhstani tenge** (KZT). Kazakhstan is a popular tourist destination, with attractions such as the **Charyn Canyon**, the **Turgen Gorge**, and the **Altyn-Emel National Park**.

1. 1917 - The Russian Revolution leads to the establishment of the **Alash Orda** government in Kazakhstan, which declares independence from Russia.
2. 1920 - The **Russian Red Army** invades Kazakhstan, and the country becomes part of the **Soviet Union**.
3. 1930s - Kazakhstan experiences a period of rapid **industrialization** and **collectivization**, with the development of the country's oil and gas industry.
4. 1991 - Kazakhstan declares its independence from the Soviet Union and becomes a **sovereign state**.
5. 1997 - The capital city is moved from **Almaty** to **Astana**, which is renamed from **Akmola**.
6. 2015 - Kazakhstan joins the **Eurasian Economic Union**, a regional economic organization that aims to promote economic integration and cooperation.
7. 2019 - Kazakhstan's president, **Nursultan Nazarbayev**, resigns after 30 years in office, and **Kassym-Jomart Tokayev** takes over as the new president.

[SECTION] What Makes Kazakhstan Unique [/SECTION]
- Kazakhstan is home to the **Baikonur Cosmodrome**, the world's oldest and largest space launch facility, which is leased by Russia.
- The country has a unique tradition of **eagle hunting**, where trained eagles are used for hunting small game such as rabbits and foxes.
- Kazakhstan is rich in **fossil fuels**, with estimated oil reserves of over 30 billion barrels and natural gas reserves of over 1 trillion cubic meters.
- The country has a diverse **wildlife**, with species such as the **snow leopard**, the **Saiga antelope**, and the **Kazakhstani wild ass**.
- Kazakhstan is home to the **Silk Road**, a historic trade route that connected Europe and Asia, with several ancient cities and monuments such as **Turkestan** and **Otrar**.
