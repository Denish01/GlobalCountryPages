[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Kazakhstan is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, also known as **basic education**, lasts for four years and is compulsory for all children from the age of six. Secondary education, which includes **general secondary education** and **vocational education**, lasts for seven years and provides students with a range of academic and practical skills. Tertiary education, which includes **universities** and **colleges**, provides students with higher education qualifications and is highly valued in Kazakh society. The quality of education in Kazakhstan is generally high, with a strong emphasis on **mathematics**, **science**, and **language**.

The education system in Kazakhstan is primarily public, with the majority of schools and universities funded by the government. However, there are also a number of private schools and universities, which offer a range of academic programs and are often popular with international students. The public education system is generally considered to be of high quality, with well-qualified teachers and modern facilities. However, there are some challenges, including a lack of resources in some rural areas and a need for greater emphasis on **critical thinking** and **problem-solving skills**.

In recent years, the government has implemented a number of reforms aimed at improving the quality of education in Kazakhstan. These reforms have included the introduction of new **curricula**, the development of **teacher training programs**, and the establishment of **education standards**. As a result, the education system in Kazakhstan is becoming increasingly competitive and is attracting a growing number of international students.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- There are a number of international schools in Kazakhstan, which offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate (IB)**, **British**, and **American** curricula.
- These schools are generally located in the major cities, such as Almaty and Astana, and are popular with expatriate families and international students.
- The cost of attending an international school in Kazakhstan can be high, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per year.
- Some of the most popular international schools in Kazakhstan include the **Almaty International School**, the **Astana International School**, and the **Haileybury Astana**.
- International schools in Kazakhstan often have modern facilities and well-qualified teachers, and offer a range of extracurricular activities and sports programs.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Kazakhstan, international students must obtain a **student visa**, which can be obtained from the Kazakh embassy or consulate in their home country.
- International students must also demonstrate proficiency in **Kazakh** or **Russian**, although some universities offer programs in **English**.
- There are a number of **scholarships** available to international students, including the **Bolashak Scholarship**, which is offered by the government of Kazakhstan.
- The cost of living in Kazakhstan can be relatively low, with **accommodation** and **food** costs ranging from $200 to $500 per month.
- International students in Kazakhstan must also register with the **migration police** and obtain a **registration card**.
- Health insurance is also required for international students, and can be obtained from a range of providers.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the main strengths of the education system in Kazakhstan is its **emphasis on mathematics and science**, which has helped to produce a highly skilled and competitive workforce.
- However, there are also some challenges, including a lack of **critical thinking and problem-solving skills**, which can make it difficult for students to succeed in an increasingly complex and globalized world.
- The education system in Kazakhstan is also often criticized for its **emphasis on rote learning**, which can stifle creativity and innovation.
- In recent years, the government has implemented a number of reforms aimed at improving the quality of education, including the introduction of **new curricula** and **teacher training programs**.
- Despite these challenges, the education system in Kazakhstan is becoming increasingly competitive, and is attracting a growing number of international students and investors.
