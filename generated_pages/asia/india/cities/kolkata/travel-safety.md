# Kolkata Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Kolkata is generally a safe city to visit, but as with any major urban center, tourists should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Kolkata, formerly known as Calcutta, is a vibrant and culturally rich city in eastern India. While it is considered a safe city, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, scams, and harassment can occur, particularly in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Visitors should be mindful of their belongings, especially in busy markets and public transportation. Additionally, the city's chaotic traffic and lack of pedestrian infrastructure can be hazardous for tourists. However, the city has a strong police presence, and most areas are well-patrolled, making it a relatively safe destination for tourists.

As with any major city, there are areas that are considered safer than others. The city's main tourist areas, such as Park Street and Chowringhee, are generally well-lit and heavily patrolled, making them safe for visitors. However, some areas, such as the slums and certain neighborhoods in the north and east of the city, are best avoided due to higher crime rates and poverty. By taking necessary precautions and being aware of their surroundings, tourists can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Kolkata.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas and public transportation
- Avoid **scams** by street vendors and taxi drivers, who may try to overcharge or cheat tourists
- Be wary of **harassment** from street vendors and beggars, who can be persistent and aggressive
- Watch out for **traffic accidents**, which can occur due to chaotic traffic and lack of pedestrian infrastructure
- Be careful of **food poisoning**, which can occur from eating at street stalls or unhygienic restaurants
- Avoid **unlicensed taxis**, which can be unsafe and overpriced
- Be cautious of **crowd surges**, which can occur during festivals and public events

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended for travel to India
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Kolkata has many good-quality hospitals and medical facilities, but medical care can be expensive and may require insurance
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are readily available and well-stocked, but some medications may require a prescription

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **map** or use a GPS device to navigate the city
- Be **mindful of your belongings**, especially in crowded areas and public transportation
- Avoid **carrying large amounts of cash**, and use credit cards or traveler's checks instead
- Keep **important phone numbers** handy, such as the tourist police and your embassy
- Avoid **walking alone at night**, and use taxis or ride-sharing services instead
- Be **respectful of local customs** and traditions, especially when visiting temples or mosques
- Keep **informed about local events** and festivals, which can affect traffic and crowds

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be **cautious of harassment** from street vendors and men, who may make unwanted advances or comments
- Avoid **traveling alone at night**, and use taxis or ride-sharing services instead
- Dress **modestly** and avoid revealing clothing, especially when visiting temples or mosques
- Keep **in touch with family and friends** back home, and let them know your itinerary and whereabouts
