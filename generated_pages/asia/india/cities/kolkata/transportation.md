# Getting Around Kolkata — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Kolkata, you can use a variety of transportation options, including metro, buses, taxis, ride-hailing apps, and trains, which provide an extensive network of routes to help you navigate the city.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Kolkata is well-connected to the rest of the world through its main international airport, Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport, which is located about 17 kilometers from the city center. The airport receives direct flights from major hubs such as Dubai, Bangkok, and Singapore, making it a convenient entry point for international travelers. Additionally, the city is also connected to other parts of India through domestic flights, trains, and buses, making it easily accessible from anywhere in the country.

For those traveling overland, Kolkata is connected to other cities in India through a network of national highways and state roads. The city is also a major railway hub, with several train stations that receive trains from all over the country. The **Esplanade Bus Station** and the **Babughat Bus Station** are two of the main bus terminals in the city, which receive buses from other cities in India. The **Sealdah Railway Station** and the **Howrah Railway Station** are two of the main train stations in the city, which receive trains from all over the country.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Kolkata has an extensive domestic air network, with flights connecting the city to all major destinations in India. Domestic airlines such as **IndiGo**, **SpiceJet**, and **Air India** operate multiple flights a day to cities such as Delhi, Mumbai, and Chennai. Booking domestic flights in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as prices tend to rise closer to the departure date. Approximate costs for domestic flights can range from $50 to $200, depending on the destination and the time of booking.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Kolkata has an extensive rail network, with trains connecting the city to all parts of India. The **Eastern Railway** and the **South Eastern Railway** are two of the main railway zones that operate trains from Kolkata. The city is connected to other major cities in India through a network of express and passenger trains, including the **Rajdhani Express** and the **Shatabdi Express**. Booking train tickets in advance is recommended, especially during peak travel seasons, as seats tend to fill up quickly. The booking process can be done online through the **Indian Railways** website or through a travel agent.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Kolkata is connected to other cities in India through a network of intercity buses, which are operated by both government and private companies. The **West Bengal Surface Transport Corporation** operates buses to other cities in West Bengal, while private companies such as **Volvo** and **Mercedes** operate luxury buses to other cities in India. The quality of buses varies, with some offering air-conditioned seats and onboard amenities, while others are more basic. Costs can range from $10 to $50, depending on the destination and the type of bus.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Kolkata, you will need to have a valid **Indian driving license** or an **International Driving Permit**.
- Road conditions in Kolkata can be chaotic, with narrow streets and aggressive drivers, so be sure to drive defensively.
- Fuel costs can range from $1 to $2 per liter, depending on the type of fuel and the location.
- Car rental companies such as **Hertz** and **Avis** operate in Kolkata, offering a range of vehicles for rent.
- When driving in Kolkata, be sure to watch out for **pedestrians** and **cyclists**, who often share the road with cars.
- Parking can be a challenge in Kolkata, especially in the city center, so be sure to plan your route in advance and use a parking app to find a spot.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be sure to **agree on the fare** before you start your journey, whether you are taking a taxi or a tuk-tuk.
- Use a **reputable taxi company** or a ride-hailing app to avoid overcharging.
- **Avoid traveling during peak hours**, especially during rush hour, as traffic can be chaotic.
- **Download a transportation app**, such as Google Maps or Ola, to help you navigate the city.
- **Be mindful of your belongings**, especially in crowded areas, as pickpocketing and theft can be a problem.
- **Respect local customs** and traditions, especially when traveling to rural areas or visiting temples and mosques.
- **Stay hydrated** and bring **sunscreen** and a **hat** to protect yourself from the sun.
- **Learn some basic Bengali phrases**, such as "hello" (নমস্কার) and "thank you" (ধন্যবাদ), to help you communicate with locals.
