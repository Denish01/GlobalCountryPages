# Kolkata Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

Kolkata, the capital of West Bengal, is a city with a rich and diverse cultural heritage that reflects its history, geography, and the influences of various ethnic and linguistic groups.

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Kolkata's culture is defined by its unique blend of traditional and modern elements, with a strong emphasis on intellectual and artistic pursuits. The city has a long history of being a cultural and educational hub, and its people are known for their love of literature, music, and art. The key influences on Kolkata's culture come from its Bengali heritage, as well as the impact of British colonial rule, which has left a lasting legacy on the city's architecture, language, and customs. The ethnic and linguistic makeup of Kolkata is predominantly Bengali, but the city is also home to significant communities of other ethnic groups, including Muslims, Christians, and Sikhs.

The city's cultural identity is also shaped by its history of being a major center of learning and intellectual inquiry, with institutions such as the University of Calcutta and the Indian Statistical Institute being located here. Kolkata is also known for its vibrant street life, with its bustling markets, street food stalls, and cultural festivals, which reflect the city's love of celebration and community. The city's cultural diversity is also reflected in its cuisine, which is a unique blend of traditional Bengali dishes, as well as influences from other parts of India and the world.

Kolkata's cultural scene is also characterized by its strong sense of community and social responsibility, with many of its residents being actively involved in social and cultural organizations. The city is also home to a thriving arts scene, with numerous galleries, museums, and performance venues, which showcase the work of local and international artists. Overall, Kolkata's cultural identity is a rich and complex tapestry, woven from the threads of its history, geography, and the diverse influences of its people.

[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Respect the local customs and traditions, especially during festivals and celebrations
- Learn a few basic phrases in Bengali, such as "dhanyavad" (thank you) and "nomoskar" (hello)
- Try the local cuisine, including street food and traditional dishes
- Engage with the local people, who are known for their hospitality and warmth
- Visit the city's cultural attractions, such as museums and galleries
- Attend a traditional Bengali festival or celebration, such as Durga Puja or Poila Boishakh

**Don't:**
- Don't tip excessively, as it is not expected in Kolkata
- Avoid eating with your left hand, as it is considered impolite
- Don't point with your feet, as it is considered rude
- Avoid public displays of affection, as they are generally frowned upon
- Don't litter or throw trash on the streets, as it is considered disrespectful to the city and its people
- Don't ignore the local traffic rules and regulations, as they are in place for your safety
