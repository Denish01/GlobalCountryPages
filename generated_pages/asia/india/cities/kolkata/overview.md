[SECTION] What Is Kolkata? [/SECTION]
Kolkata, formerly known as Calcutta, is a **megacity** located in the eastern part of India, in the state of West Bengal. It is situated on the banks of the **Hooghly River** and is the third-most populous city in India, with a population of over 4.5 million people. Kolkata is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and significant contributions to the fields of art, literature, and science. The city has a unique blend of traditional and modern architecture, with numerous historical landmarks, museums, and cultural institutions.

Kolkata is also an important economic and commercial center, with a thriving **information technology** sector and a major **port city**. The city is home to several prestigious educational institutions, including the **University of Calcutta**, which is one of the oldest and most respected universities in India. Kolkata is also known for its vibrant festivals, including the **Durga Puja**, which is a major celebration in the city.

The city's strategic location has made it an important center for trade and commerce, with connections to the rest of India and the world. Kolkata is well-connected by air, rail, and road, with a major international airport and several railway stations. The city's **public transportation system** is also well-developed, with a comprehensive network of buses, trams, and metro lines.

1. 1690 - The British East India Company establishes a trading post in Kolkata, which becomes a major center for trade and commerce.
2. 1756 - The British build **Fort William**, which becomes the seat of British power in India.
3. 1857 - The **Indian Rebellion** breaks out in Kolkata, marking a significant turning point in the Indian independence movement.
4. 1911 - The British government moves the capital of India from Kolkata to **New Delhi**.
5. 1947 - India gains independence from British rule, and Kolkata becomes the capital of the state of West Bengal.
6. 2001 - The city's name is officially changed from Calcutta to **Kolkata**, to reflect its original Bengali name.
7. 2014 - The **Kolkata Metro** is expanded, with new lines and stations added to the network.

- Kolkata is home to the **Indian Museum**, which is one of the oldest and largest museums in India, with a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits.
- The city is known for its vibrant **street food scene**, with a wide range of traditional Bengali dishes and snacks available.
- Kolkata is the birthplace of the **Bengal Renaissance**, a cultural and intellectual movement that played a significant role in the Indian independence movement.
- The city is home to several prestigious **educational institutions**, including the University of Calcutta and the Indian Institute of Technology.
- Kolkata is a major center for **arts and culture**, with numerous theaters, galleries, and performance venues.
