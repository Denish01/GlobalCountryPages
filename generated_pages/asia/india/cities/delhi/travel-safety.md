[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Delhi, the capital of India, is a bustling metropolis with a rich history and culture. While it can be a fascinating destination for tourists, the city also poses some safety concerns. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing, scams, and street harassment are common, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Additionally, the city's chaotic traffic and poor road conditions can be hazardous for pedestrians and drivers alike. However, with some basic precautions and awareness, visitors can minimize their risk of encountering problems and have a safe and enjoyable trip.

It's essential to stay informed about local conditions, respect cultural norms, and be mindful of your surroundings to ensure a smooth and safe experience in Delhi. The city has a dedicated tourist police force that can provide assistance and support to visitors. Moreover, many popular tourist areas have a significant police presence, which helps to deter crime. By taking necessary precautions and being aware of potential risks, visitors can focus on exploring the city's many attractions, such as the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and India Gate, and experiencing the local cuisine and culture.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **touts** and **scammers** who may approach you with fake travel deals or try to sell you counterfeit goods.
- Beware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas, such as buses, trains, and markets.
- Be mindful of **street harassment**, especially for solo female travelers.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use **credit cards** or **traveler's checks** instead.
- Be wary of **unlicensed taxis** or **auto-rickshaws**, and always use **metered** or **prepaid** services.
- Don't drink **tap water**, and avoid eating from **street vendors** that may not follow proper food safety guidelines.
- Be cautious of **cybercafes** and **public Wi-Fi** networks, as they may not be secure.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended for travel to Delhi.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Delhi has many high-quality medical facilities, including private hospitals and clinics, but medical care can be expensive, and **travel insurance** is recommended.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, and many medications can be purchased over-the-counter, but it's essential to consult a doctor before taking any medication.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **map** or use a **GPS** device to navigate the city.
- Be mindful of **traffic** and follow local rules and regulations.
- Avoid traveling alone at **night**, especially for solo female travelers.
- Keep your **valuables** secure, and consider using a **money belt** or a **hotel safe**.
- Stay **hydrated** and take regular breaks in **shaded** areas to avoid heat exhaustion.
- Be respectful of local **customs** and **traditions**, and dress modestly when visiting temples or mosques.
- Learn some basic **Hindi phrases**, such as "hello" (namaste) and "thank you" (dhanyavad), to help you communicate with locals.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in **upscale neighborhoods**, such as Hauz Khas or South Delhi, which are generally safer and more secure.
- Avoid traveling alone at **night**, and use **prepaid taxis** or **auto-rickshaws** instead of hitchhiking or walking alone.
- Be mindful of **street harassment**, and avoid engaging with **touts** or **scammers** who may approach you.
- Consider joining a **group tour** or using a **reputable travel agency** to help you navigate the city and stay safe.
