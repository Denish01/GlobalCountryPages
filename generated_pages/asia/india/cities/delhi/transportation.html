Getting There

Delhi is well-connected to the rest of the world through its main international airport, Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL), which is a major hub for several airlines, including Air India, and offers direct flights to many destinations in Asia, Europe, and the Americas. The airport is located about 16 kilometers southwest of the city center and is easily accessible by taxi, metro, or bus. For those traveling overland, Delhi is connected to the rest of India by a network of highways and roads, and there are several bus companies that offer services to and from the city, including Volvo and Mercedes coaches. Additionally, Delhi is a major railway hub, with several train stations, including the New Delhi Railway Station and the Old Delhi Railway Station, which offer connections to other parts of India.

For international travelers, there are several direct flight hubs that offer convenient connections to Delhi, including Dubai, Singapore, and London. These hubs offer a range of airlines and flight schedules, making it easy to find a flight that suits your needs and budget. Alternatively, travelers can also enter Delhi by road or rail from neighboring countries, including Pakistan, Nepal, and Bhutan, although this may require additional documentation and planning. It's also worth noting that Delhi is a major cultural and economic hub, attracting visitors and business travelers from all over the world, and as such, it has a well-developed infrastructure to support the needs of travelers.

Domestic Flights

Delhi has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines offering flights to and from the city, including IndiGo, SpiceJet, and Air India. The main domestic airport is the Indira Gandhi International Airport, which is also the city's main international airport. Domestic flights are a convenient way to travel to other parts of India, with many destinations offering multiple daily flights. To book domestic flights, travelers can use online booking platforms, such as MakeMyTrip or Yatra, or visit the airport in person. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the route and airline, but expect to pay around $50-$200 for a one-way ticket. It's also worth noting that domestic flights can be a good option for traveling to nearby cities, such as Agra or Jaipur, which are popular tourist destinations.

Trains & Rail

Delhi has an extensive rail network, with several train stations offering connections to other parts of India. The main train station is the New Delhi Railway Station, which is a major hub for Indian Railways. The rail network in India is generally of good quality, with many trains offering comfortable seating and amenities, such as air conditioning and food service. Key routes from Delhi include trains to Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai, as well as to neighboring countries, such as Pakistan and Nepal. High-speed train options are also available, including the Shatabdi Express and the Duronto Express, which offer fast and comfortable travel to nearby cities. To book train tickets, travelers can use the Indian Railways website or visit a train station in person. It's also worth noting that train travel can be a good option for traveling to nearby cities, such as Varanasi or Amritsar, which are popular tourist destinations.

Buses & Coaches

Delhi has a moderate network of intercity buses and coaches, with several companies offering services to and from the city, including UPSRTC and HRTC. The quality of buses and coaches varies, but many offer comfortable seating and amenities, such as air conditioning and food service. Routes from Delhi include services to nearby cities, such as Agra, Jaipur, and Chandigarh, as well as to more distant destinations, such as Mumbai and Kolkata. Costs vary depending on the route and company, but expect to pay around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket. To book bus tickets, travelers can use online booking platforms, such as Redbus or MakeMyTrip, or visit a bus station in person.

Driving

  • To drive in Delhi, you will need to obtain an Indian driving license or an International Driving Permit.
  • Road conditions in Delhi can be poor, with many potholes and uneven surfaces, so it's essential to exercise caution when driving.
  • Fuel costs are generally low, with petrol and diesel available at many gas stations throughout the city.
  • Car rental companies, such as Hertz and Avis, are available in Delhi, but be sure to research and compare prices before booking.
  • Hazards to watch out for when driving in Delhi include aggressive drivers, pedestrians, and cattle, which can often be found on the roads.
  • It's also worth noting that driving in Delhi can be challenging, especially for those who are not used to driving in heavy traffic, so it's essential to be patient and cautious.

Transport Tips

  • Be aware of scams, such as overcharging or fake taxis, and always use reputable companies or services.
  • Payment methods, such as cash and credit cards, are widely accepted, but it's a good idea to have some local currency, Indian rupees, on hand.
  • Download transport apps, such as Ola or Uber, to make it easy to book rides and get around the city.
  • Be mindful of traffic rules and road safety, and always wear a seatbelt when driving or riding in a vehicle.
  • Consider using public transportation, such as the metro or bus, to avoid traffic and reduce your carbon footprint.
  • Be prepared for delays and cancellations, especially during peak travel seasons or holidays, and plan accordingly.
  • Always research and compare prices before booking any transportation services, and be sure to read reviews and check the safety record of the company or service.
  • Finally, be respectful of local customs and traditions, and avoid littering or polluting the environment, and always follow traffic rules and regulations.
