# Cost of Living in Delhi - Prices & Budget Guide

Delhi is a moderately priced city, offering a range of affordable options for accommodation, food, and transportation, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $20-40 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $40-80 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $80-150 USD
Currency: INR
Tipping Custom: expected (5-10% in restaurants and bars)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Delhi? [/SECTION]
Delhi, the capital city of India, is a bustling metropolis with a rich cultural heritage and a blend of traditional and modern amenities. Compared to other major cities in Asia, Delhi is relatively affordable, with a cost of living that is lower than cities like Tokyo, Seoul, or Hong Kong. However, it is more expensive than many other Indian cities, due to its status as the national capital and a major hub for business, politics, and tourism. Overall, Delhi offers a unique combination of affordability and accessibility, making it an attractive destination for visitors and expats.

In terms of global averages, Delhi's cost of living is generally lower than that of Western cities, but higher than many cities in Southeast Asia or Latin America. However, the city's diverse range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, and its vibrant food scene, with a wide range of affordable and high-end dining options, make it an attractive destination for travelers with varying budgets.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or budget hotel, which can be significantly cheaper than mid-range or luxury options.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls, which offer delicious and affordable meals.
- Use public transportation, such as buses or the metro, which are cheap and efficient.
- Avoid traveling during peak hours or seasons, when prices for accommodation and transportation tend to be higher.
- Take advantage of discounts or promotions offered by restaurants, bars, or shops, which can help reduce expenses.
- Learn some basic Hindi phrases, which can help you navigate the city and interact with locals, potentially leading to better deals or more affordable options.
