# Getting Around Ahmedabad — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Ahmedabad, you can use a combination of public transportation options such as buses, trains, and auto-rickshaws, as well as private transportation options like taxis and ride-hailing apps.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Ahmedabad is a major city in India and has a well-connected international airport, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport (AMD), which is located about 15 kilometers from the city center. The airport receives direct flights from major cities in the Middle East, Europe, and Asia, as well as domestic flights from all major cities in India. Some of the direct flight hubs to Ahmedabad include Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Chennai. If you are traveling overland, Ahmedabad is connected to major cities in India by national highways and state highways, and you can enter the city by bus or private vehicle.

If you are traveling from neighboring cities or states, you can also take trains or buses to reach Ahmedabad. The city has a major railway station, Ahmedabad Junction, which is connected to all major cities in India by rail. The railway station is located in the heart of the city and is well-connected to other parts of the city by public transportation. You can also take buses from neighboring cities to reach Ahmedabad, and the city has a number of bus stations, including the Ahmedabad Central Bus Station, which is located in the city center.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Ahmedabad is well-connected to other cities in India by domestic flights, and you can fly to major cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Chennai from the Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport. Some of the major domestic airlines that operate flights to and from Ahmedabad include **IndiGo**, **SpiceJet**, and **Air India**. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through online travel agencies like **MakeMyTrip** or **Yatra**. The cost of domestic flights can vary depending on the route, airline, and time of booking, but you can expect to pay around $50-$100 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Ahmedabad has an extensive rail network, and you can take trains to major cities in India from the Ahmedabad Junction railway station. The rail network is operated by **Indian Railways**, and you can book tickets online through the Indian Railways website or through authorized ticketing agents. Some of the key routes from Ahmedabad include Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Chennai, and you can take high-speed trains like the **Shatabdi Express** or the **Duronto Express** to reach these cities. The cost of train tickets can vary depending on the route, class, and availability, but you can expect to pay around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Ahmedabad is connected to neighboring cities and states by a network of buses and coaches, and you can take buses from the Ahmedabad Central Bus Station or other bus stations in the city. Some of the major bus operators that operate from Ahmedabad include **Gujarat State Road Transport Corporation (GSRTC)** and private operators like **Neeta Tours and Travels**. The quality of buses can vary depending on the operator and route, but you can expect to pay around $5-$20 for a one-way ticket.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Ahmedabad, you need to have a valid **Indian driving license** or an international driving permit.
- The road quality in Ahmedabad is moderate, with some roads being well-maintained and others being poorly maintained.
- Fuel costs can vary depending on the type of vehicle and fuel, but you can expect to pay around $1-$2 per liter of petrol.
- If you plan to rent a car, it's recommended to book in advance and choose a reputable rental company.
- Some of the hazards to watch out for while driving in Ahmedabad include **aggressive drivers**, **potholes**, and **traffic congestion**.
- It's also recommended to avoid driving during peak hours, especially during rush hour or festivals.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Always use **metered taxis** or **ride-hailing apps** to avoid being overcharged.
- Be careful of **scams** like overcharging or fake ticketing, and always verify the authenticity of tickets or receipts.
- Download **transport apps** like Uber or Ola to book rides and track your journey.
- Always carry **small change** and **local currency** to avoid any inconvenience.
- Be respectful of **local traffic rules** and **customs**, and avoid driving or walking in **restricted areas**.
- It's recommended to **avoid traveling alone** at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.
- Always keep your **valuables safe** and be aware of your surroundings to avoid any **pickpocketing** or **theft**.
- [CALLOUT] Be prepared for **traffic congestion** and **delays**, especially during peak hours or festivals, and plan your journey accordingly.
