Ahmedabad is generally a safe city to visit, with low to moderate crime rates, but as with any major city, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Ahmedabad, a city in the state of Gujarat, India, is known for its rich cultural heritage and historic landmarks. The city has a relatively low crime rate compared to other major cities in India. However, as with any urban area, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, bag snatching, and scams can occur. Visitors should be cautious of their belongings, especially in crowded areas and public transportation. The city also experiences occasional protests and demonstrations, which can cause disruptions to traffic and daily life.

Overall, Ahmedabad is a safe city for tourists, with a strong police presence and a relatively low incidence of violent crime. However, it is still important for visitors to take necessary precautions to stay safe, such as avoiding traveling alone at night, using reputable taxi services, and being mindful of their surroundings. The city's tourist police are also available to assist visitors with any issues or concerns they may have.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Pickpocketing**: Be cautious of your belongings in crowded areas and public transportation.
- **Scams**: Be wary of overly friendly strangers or people approaching you with unsolicited offers or deals.
- **Traffic accidents**: Ahmedabad's roads can be chaotic, be careful when crossing streets or using public transportation.
- **Food poisoning**: Eat at reputable restaurants and avoid street food that may not be properly cooked or handled.
- **Petty theft**: Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots.
- **Overcharging**: Be mindful of prices when shopping or using taxi services, and negotiate when necessary.
- **Crowd surges**: Be cautious in crowded areas, especially during festivals or events.
- **Begging**: Be prepared for beggars, especially in tourist areas, and be respectful but firm in your response.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: Visitors should consult their doctor about recommended vaccinations, such as hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities**: Ahmedabad has several good quality hospitals and medical facilities, including the Civil Hospital and the Apollo Hospital.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacies are widely available, but visitors may need to show a prescription for certain medications.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night.
- Use reputable taxi services, such as Ola or Uber, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Keep your valuables secure and be cautious of pickpocketing or scams.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit and crowded areas.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or mosques.
- Learn some basic Gujarati phrases, such as "kem chho" (hello) and "dhanyavad" (thank you).
- Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen, as Ahmedabad can be hot and sunny.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be prepared for attention or stares, especially in more conservative areas, and dress modestly to avoid drawing attention to yourself.
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit and crowded areas.
- Be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or tourist hotspots, and keep your valuables secure.
