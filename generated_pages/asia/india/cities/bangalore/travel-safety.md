# Bangalore Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Bangalore is generally a safe city to visit, with low to moderate crime rates and a strong police presence, but as with any major city, visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Bangalore, also known as Bengaluru, is a bustling metropolis with a population of over 12 million people. The city has a reputation for being one of the safest major cities in India, with a strong police presence and a low crime rate compared to other cities of similar size. However, as with any major city, there are still areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. The city has a vibrant atmosphere, with a mix of modern and traditional culture, and visitors can feel comfortable exploring the city's many attractions, including its historic temples, gardens, and bustling markets.

Despite its overall safety, Bangalore is not immune to crime, and visitors should still take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and bag snatching are common in crowded areas, and visitors should be mindful of their belongings, especially in busy markets and public transportation. Additionally, there have been reports of scams targeting tourists, including taxi scams and overpriced tours, so visitors should be cautious when dealing with strangers and always research reputable companies before booking any services.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of taxi scams, where drivers may take you on a longer route to increase the fare or claim that the meter is not working.
- Be aware of pickpocketing and bag snatching in crowded areas, such as markets and public transportation.
- Be cautious of overpriced tours and activities, and always research reputable companies before booking.
- Be aware of stray dogs and poor lighting in some areas, especially at night.
- Be cautious of drinking water from street vendors, as it may not be safe.
- Be aware of traffic rules and pedestrian safety, as traffic in Bangalore can be chaotic.
- Be cautious of scams targeting tourists, such as fake police officers or officials asking for bribes.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Recommended vaccinations include hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities:** Bangalore has a number of high-quality medical facilities, including private hospitals and clinics, but visitors may need to pay upfront for treatment.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but visitors may need to show a prescription for certain medications.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas, and keep valuables secure.
- Use reputable taxi services, such as Uber or Ola, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit and populated areas.
- Be cautious of strangers approaching you, and avoid engaging with anyone who seems suspicious.
- Keep your hotel room door locked and secure, and use the hotel's safe if available.
- Be aware of local customs and dress modestly, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites.
- Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen, as the sun can be strong in Bangalore.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of unwanted attention, and avoid engaging with strangers who seem overly friendly or aggressive.
- Dress modestly, and avoid revealing clothing, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites.
- Use reputable taxi services, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and stick to well-lit and populated areas.
- Consider staying in a hotel or hostel with good security and a 24-hour front desk.
