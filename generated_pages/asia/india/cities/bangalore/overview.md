[SECTION] What Is Bangalore? [/SECTION]
Bangalore, also known as Bengaluru, is the capital of the Indian state of Karnataka. It is situated on the Deccan Plateau, in the southeastern part of the state, and is known for its pleasant climate and natural beauty. The city is nestled in a valley surrounded by hills and is home to several lakes, parks, and gardens. Bangalore is a major hub for technology, entrepreneurship, and education, earning it the nickname "Silicon Valley of India." The city has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of traditional and modern influences, and is home to many historical landmarks, museums, and cultural institutions.

Bangalore is a significant economic center, with a strong presence of major industries such as information technology, biotechnology, and manufacturing. The city is also a major hub for startups and entrepreneurship, with many incubators, accelerators, and coworking spaces. Bangalore is well-connected to the rest of India and the world, with a major international airport and a comprehensive network of roads, railways, and public transportation.

The city has a diverse population, with people from various parts of India and the world living and working in Bangalore. The city is known for its vibrant cultural scene, with a wide range of festivals, events, and activities throughout the year. From traditional Indian festivals like Diwali and Ganesh Chaturthi to modern events like music concerts and food festivals, there is always something happening in Bangalore.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1537 - The city of Bangalore was founded by Kempe Gowda, a feudatory of the Vijayanagara Empire.
2. 1638 - The city was captured by the Marathas, who ruled it for over a century.
3. 1799 - The British East India Company defeated the Kingdom of Mysore, which included Bangalore, in the Fourth Anglo-Mysore War.
4. 1947 - India gained independence from British rule, and Bangalore became the capital of the state of Mysore.
5. 1973 - The city was renamed Bengaluru, which is the Kannada language version of the city's name.
6. 2000s - Bangalore experienced rapid growth and development, driven by the IT and biotechnology industries.
7. 2010s - The city continued to grow and evolve, with a focus on sustainability, innovation, and entrepreneurship.
