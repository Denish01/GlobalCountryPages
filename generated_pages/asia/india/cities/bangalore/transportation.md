# Getting Around Bangalore — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Bangalore, you can use a variety of transportation options, including the metro, city buses, taxis, ride-hailing apps, and auto-rickshaws, as well as domestic flights, trains, and buses for traveling to and from the city.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Bangalore is well-connected to the rest of the world through its main international airport, Kempegowda International Airport (BLR), which is located about 40 kilometers north of the city center. The airport serves as a major hub for several domestic and international airlines, including Air India, IndiGo, and SpiceJet, and offers direct flights to many cities in Asia, Europe, and the Middle East. Some of the major direct flight hubs to Bangalore include Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, and Hyderabad, as well as international destinations like Dubai, Singapore, and London.

In addition to flying, Bangalore can also be reached by road or rail from other parts of India. The city is connected to the national highway network, and several bus companies operate services to and from Bangalore, including the Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) and private operators like VRL and SRS. The city is also a major railway hub, with several trains connecting it to other parts of the country, including the Shatabdi Express and the Rajdhani Express.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Bangalore has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to and from the city. Some of the major domestic airlines that operate in Bangalore include IndiGo, SpiceJet, and Air India, which offer flights to many cities across the country, including Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, and Hyderabad. Booking domestic flights in India can be done online through the airlines' websites or through online travel agencies like MakeMyTrip or Cleartrip. Approximate costs for domestic flights can vary depending on the route and the time of booking, but a one-way ticket can cost anywhere from $50 to $200.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Bangalore has an extensive rail network, with several trains connecting it to other parts of the country. The city is a major railway hub, with several railway stations, including the Bangalore City railway station and the Yesvantpur railway station. Some of the key routes from Bangalore include trains to Chennai, Hyderabad, and Mumbai, as well as to other parts of Karnataka like Mysore and Hassan. The Indian Railways website is a good resource for booking train tickets, and it is recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons. The train network in India is generally reliable, but delays can occur, and it is a good idea to check the train schedule and status before traveling.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Bangalore is connected to other cities in India by a network of buses and coaches, operated by both government and private companies. The Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) operates bus services to many cities in Karnataka, as well as to neighboring states like Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh. Private bus companies like VRL and SRS also operate services to and from Bangalore, offering a range of options from budget-friendly to luxury buses. The quality of the buses can vary, but many private operators offer comfortable and clean buses with amenities like air conditioning and reclining seats. Costs can vary depending on the route and the type of bus, but a one-way ticket can cost anywhere from $10 to $50.

- The road quality in Bangalore can be poor, especially during the monsoon season, and it is recommended to exercise caution when driving.
- Fuel costs in India can be high, and it is recommended to budget accordingly.
- Car rental companies like Hertz and Avis operate in Bangalore, and offer a range of cars for rent.
- It is recommended to avoid driving in Bangalore during peak hours, as the traffic can be heavy and chaotic.
- It is also recommended to be aware of the local traffic rules and regulations, and to drive defensively to avoid accidents.
