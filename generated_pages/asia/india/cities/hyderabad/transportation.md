# Getting Around Hyderabad — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Hyderabad, you can use a variety of transportation options, including taxis, buses, trains, and ride-hailing apps, as well as the city's efficient metro system.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Hyderabad is well-connected to the rest of the world through the Rajiv Gandhi International Airport, which is located about 25 kilometers from the city center. The airport is a major hub for several domestic and international airlines, including Air India, IndiGo, and SpiceJet, and offers direct flights to many cities in Asia, Europe, and the Middle East. Some of the major direct flight hubs to Hyderabad include Dubai, Singapore, and London. If you prefer to travel by land, you can enter Hyderabad by bus or train from nearby cities like Mumbai, Bangalore, and Chennai.

For overland entry points, Hyderabad is connected to the rest of India through a network of national highways, including the NH 44 and NH 65. You can take a bus or drive from nearby cities to reach Hyderabad. The city is also a major railway hub, with several trains connecting it to other parts of the country. The Secunderabad Railway Station and the Hyderabad Railway Station are the two main railway stations in the city, and they offer connections to many cities across India.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Hyderabad has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to and from the city. Some of the major domestic airlines that operate in Hyderabad include IndiGo, SpiceJet, and Air India. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through online travel agencies like MakeMyTrip or Yatra. The approximate cost of a domestic flight from Hyderabad to other major cities in India can range from $50 to $200, depending on the destination and the time of booking. It's always a good idea to book in advance to get the best rates.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Hyderabad has an extensive rail network, with several trains connecting it to other parts of the country. The city is a major railway hub, with several trains operating from the Secunderabad Railway Station and the Hyderabad Railway Station. You can book train tickets online through the Indian Railways website or through online travel agencies. The rail network in Hyderabad is generally of good quality, with several high-speed trains operating on key routes. Some of the major train routes from Hyderabad include the Hyderabad-Mumbai Express, the Hyderabad-Delhi Express, and the Hyderabad-Chennai Express.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
There are several intercity bus companies that operate in Hyderabad, offering connections to nearby cities like Mumbai, Bangalore, and Chennai. Some of the major bus companies that operate in Hyderabad include the Telangana State Road Transport Corporation (TSRTC) and private operators like APSRTC and KSRTC. The quality of the buses can vary, but many operators offer comfortable and air-conditioned buses. The cost of a bus ticket can range from $10 to $50, depending on the destination and the type of bus.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Hyderabad, you need a valid Indian driver's license or an international driving permit.
- The road conditions in Hyderabad can be moderate, with some roads being well-maintained and others being poorly maintained.
- The fuel costs in Hyderabad can range from $0.70 to $1.00 per liter, depending on the type of fuel.
- If you plan to rent a car in Hyderabad, be sure to choose a reputable rental company and check the condition of the vehicle before you start driving.
- Some of the hazards to watch out for while driving in Hyderabad include potholes, traffic congestion, and aggressive drivers.
- It's always a good idea to drive defensively and follow the local traffic rules to avoid any accidents or fines.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be sure to use a metered taxi or a ride-hailing app to avoid any disputes over the fare.
- Always negotiate the fare before you start your journey if you're taking an auto-rickshaw.
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas like bus stations and train stations.
- Download the Hyderabad Metro app or the TSRTC app to plan your journey and track the schedules of the metro and buses.
- Use a reputable online travel agency to book your domestic flights or train tickets.
- Be prepared for traffic congestion and plan your journey accordingly to avoid any delays.
- Avoid traveling during peak hours or during festivals and holidays when the traffic can be especially heavy.
- Always carry a map or a guidebook with you to help you navigate the city.
