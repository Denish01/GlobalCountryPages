# Cost of Living in Agra - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Agra is relatively affordable, with a daily budget of $15-30 USD for backpackers, $30-60 USD for mid-range travelers, and $60-100 USD for comfort seekers.

Daily Budget (Comfort): $60-100 USD
Currency: INR
Tipping Custom: expected (5-10%)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Agra? [/SECTION]
Agra, a city in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, is known for its rich history and cultural heritage, including the iconic Taj Mahal. Compared to other major cities in India, Agra has a relatively low cost of living, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. The cost of living in Agra is significantly lower than in major cities like Mumbai or Delhi, but slightly higher than in smaller towns in India. Overall, Agra offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an excellent choice for those looking to experience the best of Indian culture without breaking the bank.

In terms of global averages, Agra is considered an affordable city, with prices comparable to those in Southeast Asia or Eastern Europe. However, prices can vary depending on the location, with areas closer to the Taj Mahal and other tourist attractions tend to be more expensive than those in the outskirts of the city. Additionally, prices can fluctuate depending on the time of year, with peak tourist season (October to March) tend to be more expensive than the off-season.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries and street food stalls to experience authentic Indian cuisine at a lower cost.
- Use public transport or walk/bike whenever possible to save on transport costs.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (October to March) to save on accommodation and tour prices.
- Learn some basic Hindi phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals.
- Consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot to save on internet costs.
