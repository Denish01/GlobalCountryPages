[SECTION] What Is Agra? [/SECTION]
Agra is a city in the northern part of India, situated on the banks of the Yamuna River. It is part of the state of Uttar Pradesh and is known for its significant cultural and historical importance. The city has a long and storied past, with various dynasties and empires having ruled over it, leaving behind a rich legacy of architecture, art, and literature. Agra is also a major tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year who come to marvel at its breathtaking monuments and landmarks.

Geographically, Agra is located in the Indo-Gangetic Plain, which is one of the most fertile and densely populated regions in the world. The city has a subtropical climate, with hot summers and mild winters, and is surrounded by several smaller towns and villages. Agra is also an important commercial center, with a thriving industry and a significant contribution to the country's economy.

Agra's sovereignty status is that of a city within the state of Uttar Pradesh, with its own municipal corporation governing the local affairs. The city is also part of the larger National Capital Region (NCR) of India, which includes the capital city of New Delhi and several other surrounding cities and towns. As a result, Agra is closely linked to the national capital and is an important part of the country's overall economic and cultural landscape.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1501 - The city of Agra was founded by Sultan Sikandar Lodi, who established it as the capital of the Lodi dynasty.
2. 1526 - The Mughal Empire, founded by Babur, conquered Agra and made it their capital, leading to a period of significant cultural and architectural development.
3. 1653 - The Taj Mahal, one of the most famous landmarks in Agra, was completed by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a mausoleum for his wife.
4. 1803 - The British East India Company conquered Agra, marking the beginning of British rule in the city.
5. 1947 - India gained independence from British rule, and Agra became part of the newly formed country.
6. 1983 - The Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural and historical significance.

[SECTION] What Makes Agra Unique [/SECTION]
- Agra is home to the Taj Mahal, one of the **Seven Wonders of the World** and a testament to Mughal architecture and craftsmanship.
- The city has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of **Mughal**, **Persian**, and **Indian** influences evident in its art, literature, and music.
- Agra is a major center for **handicrafts** and **textiles**, with a long tradition of skilled artisans and craftsmen producing intricate and beautiful works.
- The city is surrounded by several important historical and cultural sites, including the **Agra Fort** and the **Fatehpur Sikri**.
- Agra is a significant **pilgrimage site** for Hindus, with several important temples and shrines, including the **Mankameshwar Temple** and the **Kailash Temple**.

Agra is a city in northern India known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and iconic landmarks like the Taj Mahal, which make it a unique and fascinating destination for tourists and travelers from around the world.
