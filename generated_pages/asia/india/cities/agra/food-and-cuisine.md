# Agra Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Agra, be sure to try the local specialties, including the famous **Petha**, a sweet and savory dish made from white pumpkin, as well as other popular dishes like **Dalmoth**, **Bedai**, and **Mughlai** cuisine, which showcase the city's rich culinary heritage.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: wheat, rice, lentils, ghee
National Dish: Dal Baati Churma, a traditional Rajasthani dish made with lentils, wheat, and ghee
Meal Times: typical breakfast times are 7-10 am, lunch 12-3 pm, and dinner 7-10 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% of the total bill is customary
Dietary Notes: vegetarian-friendly, with many options available for halal and kosher diets
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Agra's cuisine is a unique blend of **Mughlai**, **Rajasthani**, and **North Indian** flavors, reflecting the city's rich history and cultural heritage. The city's proximity to the Taj Mahal and its status as a former Mughal capital have contributed to the development of a distinct culinary style, characterized by the use of rich spices, nuts, and dried fruits. Agra's cuisine is also influenced by its geographical location, with the city's chefs making use of local ingredients like **pumpkin**, **lentils**, and **wheat** to create a variety of delicious dishes. From street food to high-end restaurants, Agra's food scene is a reflection of the city's warm hospitality and love of good food.

The city's culinary landscape is also shaped by its cultural and religious diversity, with a significant **Muslim** population contributing to the popularity of **halal** cuisine, and a strong **Hindu** presence influencing the use of **vegetarian** ingredients. Agra's cuisine is also known for its **sweet** and **savory** flavors, with popular dishes like **Petha** and **Dalmoth** showcasing the city's love of sweet and spicy flavors.

Agra's food culture is also closely tied to its **festivals** and **celebrations**, with special dishes prepared for occasions like **Diwali**, **Eid**, and **Holi**. The city's chefs take great pride in their culinary traditions, and visitors can experience the rich flavors and hospitality of Agra's food culture by trying the local specialties and visiting the city's many markets and food stalls.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Eating with the right hand**: it is customary to eat with the right hand, as the left hand is considered unclean
- **Removing shoes**: it is customary to remove shoes before entering a restaurant or home
- **Tipping**: 5-10% of the total bill is customary
- **Reservations**: reservations are recommended for high-end restaurants, especially during peak season
- **Dress code**: a modest dress code is recommended, especially when visiting temples or mosques
- **Trying local cuisine**: visitors are encouraged to try local cuisine and drinks, as it is a great way to experience the culture and hospitality of Agra
