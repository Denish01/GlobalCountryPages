# Chennai - Overview & Essential Facts

Chennai is the capital city of the Indian state of Tamil Nadu, located on the Coromandel Coast off the Bay of Bengal, and is known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful beaches, and historic landmarks.

[SECTION] What Is Chennai? [/SECTION]
Chennai, formerly known as Madras, is a major cultural, economic, and educational center in South India. The city has a long and storied history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Paleolithic era. Chennai is situated on the Coromandel Coast, which has been an important hub for trade and commerce for centuries. The city is known for its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and vibrant cultural scene, which showcases the rich heritage of the region. Chennai is also a major center for the **Indian automobile industry**, with several major manufacturers having their headquarters and production facilities in the city.

Chennai is a major urban agglomeration, with a population of over 10 million people, making it one of the most populous cities in India. The city has a diverse population, with people from various ethnic, linguistic, and cultural backgrounds living and working together. Chennai is also a major center for education and research, with several prestigious universities and institutions, including the **Indian Institute of Technology Madras** and the **University of Madras**.

The city has a tropical wet and dry climate, with high temperatures and humidity levels throughout the year. Chennai is also prone to natural disasters, such as cyclones and floods, which can have a significant impact on the city's infrastructure and economy. Despite these challenges, Chennai remains a vibrant and dynamic city, with a strong sense of community and a deep connection to its rich cultural heritage.
