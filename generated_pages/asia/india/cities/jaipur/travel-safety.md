[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Jaipur, also known as the Pink City, is a popular tourist destination in India, known for its rich cultural heritage and historical landmarks. The city has a relatively low crime rate compared to other major cities in India, and most visitors have a safe and enjoyable experience. However, as with any travel destination, it's essential to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and scams are common in tourist areas, and visitors should be cautious when dealing with strangers or using public transportation.

Overall, Jaipur is a safe city to visit, and most tourists have a positive experience. The city has a strong police presence, and tourist areas are generally well-patrolled. However, it's still important to take necessary precautions to stay safe, such as avoiding traveling alone at night, using reputable taxi services, and being mindful of your belongings in crowded areas.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **touts** and **scammers** who may approach you in tourist areas, offering fake tours or services.
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations.
- Avoid using **unlicensed taxi services**, as they may overcharge or scam you.
- Be cautious of **street food**, as it may not be safe for consumption.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of **cash**, and use reputable **ATMs** instead.
- Be aware of **traffic scams**, where drivers may try to overcharge or scam you.
- Be cautious of **fake police officers**, who may try to scam or extort you.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and Typhoid are recommended for travel to Jaipur.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended.
- **Medical Facilities:** Jaipur has modern medical facilities, including private hospitals and clinics, but medical care may not be up to Western standards.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are readily available, but may not always have English-speaking staff.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always use **reputable taxi services**, such as those authorized by the government.
- Avoid traveling alone at **night**, and use well-lit and populated areas instead.
- Keep your **valuables secure**, and be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas.
- Avoid carrying large amounts of **cash**, and use **credit cards** or **ATMs** instead.
- Be cautious of **strangers** who approach you, and avoid engaging with them.
- Always **haggle** when shopping at markets, and be aware of **scams**.
- Stay **hydrated** and take regular breaks in **shaded areas** to avoid heat exhaustion.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be cautious of **harassment**, and avoid traveling alone at night.
- Dress **modestly**, and avoid revealing clothing.
- Use **reputable taxi services**, and avoid hitchhiking or using unlicensed taxis.
- Stay in **well-lit and populated areas**, and avoid isolated areas.
