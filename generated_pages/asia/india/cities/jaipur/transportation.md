# Getting Around Jaipur — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Jaipur, you can use a variety of transportation options, including taxis, buses, auto-rickshaws, and ride-hailing apps, as well as explore the city on foot or by bicycle.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Jaipur is well-connected to the rest of India and the world through its international airport, which receives flights from major cities in India and abroad. The main international airport serving Jaipur is the Jaipur International Airport, located about 13 kilometers from the city center. The airport is a major hub for domestic and international flights, with direct connections to cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore, as well as international destinations like Dubai, Singapore, and Bangkok. You can also reach Jaipur by road or rail, with several national highways and railway lines connecting the city to other parts of India.

If you are traveling from outside India, you can fly into Jaipur International Airport or into one of the major international airports in India, such as Delhi or Mumbai, and then take a domestic flight or train to Jaipur. The city is also accessible by road, with several bus companies operating services from nearby cities like Delhi, Agra, and Udaipur. The main overland entry points to Jaipur are the national highways NH 8, NH 11, and NH 12, which connect the city to other parts of India.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Jaipur has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to and from the city. The main domestic airlines serving Jaipur are Air India, IndiGo, SpiceJet, and Jet Airways, which offer flights to cities like Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, and Hyderabad. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through online travel agencies like MakeMyTrip or Yatra. The cost of domestic flights in India can vary depending on the route, airline, and time of booking, but you can expect to pay around $50-$100 for a one-way ticket from Jaipur to a nearby city.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Jaipur has an extensive rail network, with several railway lines connecting the city to other parts of India. The main railway station in Jaipur is the Jaipur Junction, which is a major hub for trains to and from the city. You can take trains to nearby cities like Delhi, Agra, and Udaipur, as well as to longer-distance destinations like Mumbai and Bangalore. The Indian Railways website allows you to book train tickets online, and you can also book through authorized ticketing agents. The cost of train travel in India can vary depending on the class of service and the route, but you can expect to pay around $10-$50 for a one-way ticket from Jaipur to a nearby city.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Jaipur has a well-developed bus network, with several private and government-owned bus companies operating services to and from the city. The main bus station in Jaipur is the Sindhi Camp Bus Station, which is located in the heart of the city. You can take buses to nearby cities like Delhi, Agra, and Udaipur, as well as to longer-distance destinations like Mumbai and Bangalore. The cost of bus travel in India can vary depending on the route and the class of service, but you can expect to pay around $10-$30 for a one-way ticket from Jaipur to a nearby city.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Jaipur, you need a valid Indian driver's license or an international driving permit.
- The road quality in Jaipur is moderate, with some roads being well-maintained and others being poorly maintained.
- The fuel cost in Jaipur is around $1.50 per liter for gasoline and $1.20 per liter for diesel.
- You can rent a car in Jaipur through companies like Hertz or Avis, with prices starting from around $40 per day.
- Some hazards to watch out for while driving in Jaipur include potholes, traffic congestion, and aggressive drivers.
- It is recommended to hire a driver or use a taxi service instead of driving yourself, especially if you are not familiar with the local roads and traffic conditions.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **scams** and overcharging by taxi and auto-rickshaw drivers, and always agree on the fare before you start your journey.
- Use **reputable taxi services** like Uber or Ola, which offer a safe and convenient way to get around the city.
- Download **transport apps** like Google Maps or Maps.me, which can help you navigate the city and find the best routes.
- Always **wear a helmet** when riding a motorcycle or scooter, and follow local traffic rules and regulations.
- Be **patient and polite** when dealing with traffic congestion or aggressive drivers, and avoid getting into arguments or confrontations.
- Use **cashless payment options** like credit cards or mobile wallets, which are widely accepted in Jaipur.
- **Respect local customs and traditions**, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites, and dress modestly and remove your shoes when required.
