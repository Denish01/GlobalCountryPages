# Cost of Living in Mumbai - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Mumbai can vary greatly depending on lifestyle and accommodations, but on average, a daily budget for a backpacker can range from $20-40 USD, while a mid-range traveler can expect to spend around $50-100 USD per day.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Moderate
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $20-40 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $50-100 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $150-250 USD
Currency: INR
Tipping Custom: expected (5-10% in restaurants and bars)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Mumbai? [/SECTION]
Mumbai is one of the most populous cities in the world, and as such, it has a unique blend of affordable and expensive options. Compared to other major cities in the world, Mumbai is relatively affordable, but it is still one of the most expensive cities in India. The cost of living in Mumbai is influenced by factors such as the high demand for housing, the growing economy, and the influx of tourists and business travelers. Overall, Mumbai is a moderate to expensive city, with prices for accommodations, food, and transportation ranging from affordable to very expensive.

In comparison to other cities in India, Mumbai is more expensive than many smaller cities, but it is still relatively affordable compared to other major cities such as Delhi and Bangalore. The cost of living in Mumbai is also influenced by the neighborhood, with areas such as Colaba and Bandra being more expensive than others such as Andheri and Borivali. Overall, Mumbai offers a unique blend of affordability and luxury, making it a great destination for travelers and expats alike.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs
- Eat at local eateries and street food stalls instead of restaurants to save on food costs
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or rental cars to save on transport costs
- Avoid traveling during peak season (December to February) to save on accommodation and flight costs
- Consider buying a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot instead of paying for international roaming charges
- Take advantage of free attractions and activities in Mumbai, such as visiting the beaches and exploring the street markets, to save on entertainment costs
