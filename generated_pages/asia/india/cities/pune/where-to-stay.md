# Where to Stay in Pune — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Pune, the best areas to stay are in neighborhoods that offer a mix of comfort, convenience, and cultural experiences, such as Koregaon Park, Shivajinagar, and Kalyani Nagar. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas/neighborhoods in Pune, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Koregaon Park** (Pune)
Best For: Luxury travelers and families
Vibe: Koregaon Park is known for its upscale vibe, with tree-lined streets, trendy restaurants, and boutique shops, making it a great area to experience the city's cosmopolitan side. The area is also home to the famous Osho International Meditation Resort, which attracts spiritual seekers from around the world.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Upscale amenities, proximity to major attractions, and a variety of dining options
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and traffic in the area can be heavy at times

**Shivajinagar** (Pune)
Best For: Business travelers and solo backpackers
Vibe: Shivajinagar is a bustling commercial area with a mix of old and new Pune, offering a glimpse into the city's rich history and cultural heritage. The area is home to many street food stalls, local markets, and historic landmarks like the Shivajinagar Bus Stand and the Pune Railway Station.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Convenient location, affordable options, and a vibrant atmosphere
Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, with limited parking options

**Kalyani Nagar** (Pune)
Best For: Young travelers and couples
Vibe: Kalyani Nagar is a trendy and vibrant area, known for its nightlife, restaurants, and cafes, making it a great spot for those looking to experience the city's youthful energy. The area is also close to the Mutha River and the Kalyani Nagar Park, offering scenic views and outdoor activities.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: Lively atmosphere, variety of dining options, and proximity to major attractions
Cons: Can be noisy at night, and traffic in the area can be heavy

**Viman Nagar** (Pune)
Best For: Families and business travelers
Vibe: Viman Nagar is a quiet and upscale area, located close to the Pune Airport, making it a convenient option for those with early morning flights. The area is also home to many restaurants, cafes, and shops, offering a range of amenities and services.
Price Range: $40-180/night
Pros: Convenient location, quiet atmosphere, and a range of amenities
Cons: Limited nightlife options, and can be quite expensive

**Baner** (Pune)
Best For: Families and long-stay travelers
Vibe: Baner is a residential area located in the western part of Pune, known for its peaceful atmosphere and scenic views of the surrounding hills. The area is also close to many IT parks and business centers, making it a great option for those working in the area.
Price Range: $25-120/night
Pros: Affordable options, peaceful atmosphere, and proximity to major IT parks
Cons: Limited dining options, and can be quite far from the city center

**Hinjewadi** (Pune)
Best For: Business travelers and IT professionals
Vibe: Hinjewadi is a major IT hub in Pune, known for its modern infrastructure and amenities, making it a great spot for those working in the tech industry. The area is also home to many restaurants, cafes, and entertainment options, offering a range of activities and services.
Price Range: $30-150/night
Pros: Convenient location, modern amenities, and a range of dining options
Cons: Can be quite crowded, and traffic in the area can be heavy

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance to get the best rates, especially during peak season
- Use online booking platforms like Booking.com, Agoda, and Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Negotiate with hotel owners or property managers to get a better rate, especially for long-stay bookings
- Consider booking a room with a flexible cancellation policy, in case of changes to your travel plans
- Check for any seasonal discounts or promotions, especially during the off-season
- Read reviews and check the location of the property before making a booking
- Use a credit card or PayPal to make a secure payment, and avoid paying in cash

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid staying in areas with high crime rates, such as the Pune Railway Station area
- Be cautious when walking alone at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash
- Keep your valuables safe, and use a hotel safe or a secure locker
- Stay in areas with good transportation links, such as Koregaon Park and Shivajinagar
- Consider staying in a hotel or guesthouse with 24-hour security and reception
- Be aware of your surroundings, and avoid traveling alone in unfamiliar areas
- Keep a copy of your important documents, such as your passport and visa, and leave a copy with a trusted friend or family member back home
