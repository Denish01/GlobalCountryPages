# Pune Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

Pune, a city in the state of Maharashtra, India, is known for its rich cultural heritage, which is a unique blend of traditional and modern influences.

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Pune's culture is defined by its history as a center of learning and intellectual pursuits, with a strong presence of educational institutions and research centers. The city has a diverse population, with a mix of ethnic and linguistic groups, including Marathis, Gujaratis, and Punjabis, among others. The key influences on Pune's culture are its Marathi heritage, its history as a center of learning, and its proximity to Mumbai, the financial capital of India. The city's cultural identity is also shaped by its many historical landmarks, including the Shaniwar Wada and the Aga Khan Palace, which reflect its rich history and architectural heritage.

Pune's cultural identity is also reflected in its many festivals and traditions, which are an integral part of the city's social fabric. The city celebrates many festivals throughout the year, including the Pune Festival, which showcases the city's cultural heritage through music, dance, and theater performances. The city is also home to many cultural institutions, including museums, art galleries, and libraries, which promote the city's rich cultural heritage.

The city's cultural identity is also influenced by its many educational institutions, which attract students from all over India and abroad. The city is home to many prestigious universities and research institutions, including the University of Pune and the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, which have contributed to the city's reputation as a center of learning and intellectual pursuits.

[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Respect the city's cultural heritage and traditions
- Learn a few basic phrases in Marathi, the local language
- Try the local cuisine, including street food and traditional dishes
- Visit the city's many historical landmarks and cultural institutions
- Participate in the city's festivals and celebrations
- Dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting temples and other cultural institutions

**Don't:**
- Do not litter or throw trash on the streets
- Do not touch or point at people, especially in a crowded area
- Do not eat with your left hand, as it is considered impolite
- Do not take pictures of people without their permission
- Do not enter temples or other cultural institutions without removing your shoes
- Do not speak loudly or use profanity in public
