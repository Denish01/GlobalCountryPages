[SECTION] What Is Pune? [/SECTION]
Pune is a city situated in the western part of India, in the state of Maharashtra. It is located about 150 kilometers southeast of Mumbai, the financial capital of India. Pune is known for its pleasant climate, educational institutions, and cultural activities, earning it the nickname "Oxford of the East". The city has a long history dating back to the 8th century and has been ruled by various dynasties, including the Maratha Empire and the British. Today, Pune is a thriving metropolis with a strong economy, rich cultural heritage, and a high standard of living.

Pune is situated in the Sahyadri mountain range, near the Western Ghats, and is surrounded by hills, forests, and rivers. The city has a diverse geography, with a mix of urban and rural areas, and is home to several lakes, dams, and waterfalls. Pune is also a major hub for the Indian film industry, with many film production studios and acting schools located in the city. The city's cultural scene is vibrant, with numerous festivals, concerts, and events taking place throughout the year.

Pune is also known for its historical landmarks, including the Shaniwar Wada, a 18th-century fort that was once the seat of the Maratha Empire, and the Aga Khan Palace, a beautiful palace built in the 19th century. The city is also home to several museums, galleries, and cultural institutions, including the Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum, which showcases a collection of Indian artifacts and art.
