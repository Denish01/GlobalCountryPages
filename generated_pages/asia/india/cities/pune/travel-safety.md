[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Pune is a bustling city in the state of Maharashtra, known for its rich cultural heritage, educational institutions, and IT hubs. The city has a relatively low crime rate compared to other major cities in India, with most crimes being petty in nature, such as pickpocketing, theft, and street scams. However, as with any popular tourist destination, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. The city has a strong police presence, and tourist areas are generally well-patrolled, making it a relatively safe destination for visitors.

Overall, Pune is a great destination for travelers, with a vibrant atmosphere, rich history, and friendly locals. While it's essential to be mindful of potential risks, the city's safety record is relatively good, and visitors can feel comfortable exploring the city's many attractions, from historical landmarks to modern shopping malls. With some basic precautions and awareness of local conditions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Pune.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Be aware of **pickpocketing** and **theft** in crowded areas, such as bus stations and markets.
- Beware of **street scams**, such as people approaching you with fake stories or trying to sell you counterfeit goods.
- Be cautious of **overcharging** by taxi drivers or autorickshaw drivers, always use a meter or agree on a fare beforehand.
- Be aware of **traffic risks**, such as reckless driving and poor road conditions.
- Be cautious of **food safety**, always eat at reputable restaurants and avoid street food from unhygienic vendors.
- Be aware of **monsoon flooding**, which can occur during the rainy season, and take necessary precautions.
- Beware of **credit card scams**, always use reputable ATMs and be cautious when using your credit card at restaurants or shops.
- Be aware of **local festivals and events**, which can attract large crowds and increase the risk of petty crime.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, Typhoid, and Rabies vaccinations are recommended for travel to India.
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities:** Pune has good quality medical facilities, with many private hospitals and clinics, but medical insurance is recommended.
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, and many medications are available over-the-counter, but it's always best to consult a doctor before taking any medication.

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Always carry a **copy of your passport** and **important phone numbers** with you.
- Be aware of your **surroundings**, especially in crowded areas, and keep an eye on your belongings.
- Use **reputable taxi services** or **ride-hailing apps**, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Avoid **carrying large amounts of cash**, and use **credit cards or debit cards** instead.
- Be **respectful of local customs** and **dress modestly**, especially when visiting temples or mosques.
- Stay **hydrated** and **take breaks** in the heat, especially during the summer months.
- Learn some **basic Hindi phrases**, such as "hello" (namaste) and "thank you" (dhanyavad), to help you communicate with locals.

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Be **aware of your surroundings**, especially at night, and avoid traveling alone in isolated areas.
- Dress **modestly** and **respect local customs**, especially when visiting temples or mosques.
- Use **reputable taxi services** or **ride-hailing apps**, and always check the driver's ID before getting in.
- Stay in **well-lit and populated areas**, and avoid walking alone in dimly lit streets.
