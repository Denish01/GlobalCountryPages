# Lucknow Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Lucknow, you should try the city's signature dishes like kebabs, biryani, and chaat items, which showcase the city's rich culinary heritage and blend of Mughal, Persian, and local influences. 

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: wheat, rice, lentils, meat (especially lamb and chicken)
National Dish: Biryani, a mixed rice dish made with basmati rice, meat or vegetables, and a blend of spices
Meal Times: typical breakfast time is 8-10 am, lunch is 12-2 pm, and dinner is 7-10 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% of the total bill is customary
Dietary Notes: many restaurants offer halal options, and some are vegetarian-friendly, but it's best to check ahead of time
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Lucknow's cuisine is known for its rich and diverse flavors, which reflect the city's history as a cultural crossroads. The city's culinary scene is heavily influenced by Mughal and Persian traditions, which is evident in the use of rich spices, nuts, and dried fruits in many dishes. Lucknow is particularly famous for its **kebabs**, which are marinated meat dishes that are grilled or fried to perfection. The city is also home to a variety of **chaat items**, which are savory snacks made with a combination of ingredients like potatoes, chickpeas, and yogurt. Regional variations in Lucknow's cuisine include the use of local ingredients like **dal** (lentils) and **atta** (whole wheat flour), which are staples in many Indian households.

The city's culinary scene is also shaped by its Muslim heritage, which is reflected in the prevalence of **halal** meat and the use of spices like **cumin**, **coriander**, and **cardamom**. Lucknow's cuisine is often described as **Nawabi**, which refers to the city's history as the capital of the Nawabs of Awadh. This cuisine is characterized by its rich and elaborate dishes, which are often served at special occasions like weddings and festivals. Overall, Lucknow's food culture is a unique blend of traditional and modern influences, which makes it a fascinating destination for food lovers.

Lucknow's cuisine is not just about the food itself, but also about the cultural and social context in which it is consumed. Mealtimes are often seen as opportunities to socialize and bond with family and friends, and the city's many **dhabas** (small eateries) and **food stalls** are popular gathering places for locals and visitors alike. The city's culinary scene is also influenced by its **street food culture**, which is characterized by the presence of many small vendors and stalls that sell a variety of snacks and drinks. From **kebabs** and **chaat items** to **falooda** and **kulfi**, Lucknow's street food scene has something to offer every kind of food lover.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Eating with your hands**: it is common to eat with your hands in many Indian restaurants and households, especially when eating traditional dishes like **kebabs** and **biryani**
- **Using a napkin**: it is customary to use a napkin to wipe your hands and mouth while eating
- **Tipping**: 5-10% of the total bill is customary in most restaurants
- **Reservation culture**: it is a good idea to make reservations in advance, especially for popular restaurants
- **Dress code**: dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing, especially when visiting traditional restaurants or eating at someone's home
- **Trying local cuisine**: be adventurous and try local dishes, even if they are unfamiliar to you
