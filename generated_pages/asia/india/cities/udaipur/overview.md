[SECTION] What Is Udaipur? [/SECTION]
Udaipur is a city in the Indian state of Rajasthan, situated in the southern part of the state. It is known for its stunning architecture, beautiful lakes, and rich cultural heritage. The city is often referred to as the "City of Lakes" due to the presence of several lakes, including Lake Pichola, Lake Fateh Sagar, and Lake Udai Sagar. Udaipur is also known for its historic monuments, such as the City Palace, Jag Mandir, and Saheliyon-ki-Bari, which reflect the city's rich history and cultural significance.

Udaipur is located in the Aravalli mountain range, which provides a picturesque backdrop to the city. The city has a population of around 451,000 people, according to the 2011 census, and is a popular tourist destination. Udaipur is also an important economic center, with a strong focus on tourism, textiles, and handicrafts. The city is well-connected to other parts of India, with a airport, railway station, and bus station.

The city's history dates back to the 16th century, when it was founded by Maharana Udai Singh II, the ruler of the Mewar kingdom. Over the centuries, Udaipur has been ruled by various dynasties, including the Mewar kingdom, the Mughal Empire, and the British East India Company. Today, Udaipur is a thriving city that offers a unique blend of traditional and modern culture, making it a popular destination for tourists and travelers.
