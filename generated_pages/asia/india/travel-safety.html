Overall Safety Assessment

India is a vast and diverse country, and safety can vary greatly depending on the region, city, and even neighborhood. While India has a reputation for being a challenging destination, most visitors have a safe and enjoyable experience. Petty crimes like pickpocketing and scams are common, especially in tourist areas, but violent crimes against tourists are relatively rare. However, visitors should be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions to stay safe. With some basic knowledge and planning, travelers can minimize their risks and have a rewarding experience in India.

India's safety landscape is also influenced by its geography, with natural disasters like earthquakes, floods, and heatwaves posing a threat to travelers. Additionally, the country's large population and crowded cities can be overwhelming, and visitors may need to take extra care to avoid accidents and stay healthy. Despite these challenges, India's rich culture, warm hospitality, and stunning natural beauty make it a fascinating destination for travelers. By being aware of the potential risks and taking necessary precautions, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in India.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and bag snatching are common in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Scams like the "gemstone scam" and "hotel scam" target tourists, especially in major cities
  • Traffic accidents are a major risk, especially for pedestrians and motorcyclists
  • Food and waterborne illnesses can be a problem, especially for travelers who are not accustomed to local cuisine
  • Crowd surges and stampedes can occur during festivals and events
  • Theft from hotel rooms and guesthouses is not uncommon
  • Cybercrime and credit card scams are on the rise in India
  • Begging and aggressive touting can be a nuisance in tourist areas

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended for travelers to India
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Quality medical facilities are available in major cities, but may be limited in rural areas
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available, but the quality of medications can vary

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or debit cards instead
  • Be cautious when using ATMs and avoid using them at night
  • Avoid eating from street vendors or consuming undercooked food
  • Stay hydrated and take regular breaks in hot weather
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or mosques
  • Learn some basic Hindi phrases, such as "hello" (namaste) and "thank you" (dhanyavad)

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Research your destination thoroughly and stay informed about local conditions
  • Dress modestly and avoid drawing attention to yourself, especially in rural areas
  • Avoid traveling alone at night and use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps
  • Be cautious when interacting with strangers, especially men, and avoid accepting rides or gifts from unknown individuals
  • Consider joining a group tour or using a reputable travel company to minimize risks
