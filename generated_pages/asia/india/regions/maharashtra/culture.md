# Maharashtra Culture - Food, Traditions & Etiquette

Maharashtra's culture is a vibrant and diverse blend of traditional and modern elements, shaped by its rich history, geographic location, and the influences of various ethnic and linguistic groups. 

[SECTION] Cultural Identity [/SECTION]
Maharashtra's culture is defined by its unique blend of **Marathi** traditions, **Hindu** and **Buddhist** influences, and its strategic location on the western coast of India. The state is home to a diverse population of over 112 million people, with the majority speaking **Marathi**, the official language. The cultural identity of Maharashtra is also shaped by its history, including the rule of the **Maratha Empire** and the **Peshwa** dynasty, which left a lasting legacy in the state's architecture, art, and literature. The state's cultural landscape is further enriched by the presence of various ethnic and linguistic groups, including **Gujaratis**, **Sindhis**, and **Konkanis**, each contributing their unique customs and traditions to the state's cultural tapestry.

The cultural identity of Maharashtra is also reflected in its traditional attire, with men wearing the **dhoti** and **kurta**, while women wear the **saree** or **salwar kameez**. The state is also known for its vibrant **folk music** and **dance** traditions, including the **Lavani** and **Tamasha** performances, which are an integral part of Maharashtra's cultural heritage. The state's cultural identity is also influenced by its **cuisine**, which is a unique blend of **vegetarian** and **non-vegetarian** dishes, with a focus on **local ingredients** and **traditional cooking methods**.

Maharashtra's cultural identity is also shaped by its **festival calendar**, which is filled with vibrant celebrations and festivities throughout the year. The state is home to several **UNESCO World Heritage Sites**, including the **Ajanta** and **Ellora Caves**, which are a testament to its rich cultural and historical heritage. The state's cultural landscape is also influenced by its **urban** and **rural** divide, with the cities of **Mumbai** and **Pune** being the hubs of modern culture and entertainment, while the rural areas are known for their traditional way of life and cultural practices.

[SECTION] Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts [/SECTION]
**Do:**
- Remove your shoes before entering a temple or home
- Use your right hand when eating or giving or receiving something
- Respect the local customs and traditions
- Learn a few basic **Marathi** phrases to interact with the locals
- Try the local cuisine and drinks
- Respect the environment and conserve water and energy

**Don't:**
- Touch or point at someone with your feet
- Eat with your left hand or walk around with your shoes on in a temple or home
- Litter or pollute the environment
- Take pictures of people or temples without permission
- Show public displays of affection
- Disrespect the local customs and traditions

[SECTION] Arts & Music [/SECTION]
- **Lavani** is a traditional folk music and dance form that is popular in Maharashtra
- **Tamasha** is a traditional form of theater and dance that is known for its vibrant performances
- **Bharud** is a traditional form of folk music that is popular in rural Maharashtra
- **Marathi cinema** is a thriving film industry that produces a range of movies, from romantic comedies to historical dramas
- **Marathi literature** is known for its rich and diverse range of authors and poets, including the famous **Sant Tukaram**
