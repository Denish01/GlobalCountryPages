[SECTION] What Is Goa? [/SECTION]
Goa is a region located on the western coast of India, bordered by the states of Maharashtra to the north and Karnataka to the south. It has a long coastline along the Arabian Sea, with a total area of approximately 3,702 square kilometers. Goa is known for its beautiful beaches, such as Palolem, Baga, and Calangute, which attract millions of tourists every year. The region has a rich cultural heritage, with a unique blend of Indian and Portuguese influences, reflecting its history as a Portuguese colony from 1510 to 1961.

Geographically, Goa is divided into two districts: North Goa and South Goa. The region is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, with several national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, including the Bhagwan Mahaveer Sanctuary and Mollem National Park. Goa's economy is primarily driven by tourism, with a significant contribution from the mining and manufacturing sectors. The region has a population of approximately 1.8 million people, with a mix of Hindus, Christians, and Muslims.

Goa's cultural landscape is characterized by its vibrant festivals, such as the Goa Carnival and the Feast of St. Francis Xavier, which reflect its rich cultural heritage. The region is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes a range of seafood dishes, such as fish curry and prawn balchao. Goa's unique cultural identity is a result of its complex history, which has been shaped by various influences, including Portuguese, Indian, and Islamic traditions.

2. 1624 - The Dutch attempt to capture Goa from the Portuguese, but are unsuccessful.
3. 1752 - The Maratha Empire, led by the Peshwa, attacks Goa, but the Portuguese are able to defend the territory.
4. 1843 - The Portuguese introduce the **Latin alphabet** in Goa, which replaces the traditional **Devanagari script**.
5. 1961 - India annexes Goa, along with the neighboring territories of Daman and Diu, after a brief military conflict with Portugal.
6. 1987 - Goa is granted statehood, becoming the 25th state of India.
7. 2012 - The **National Green Tribunal** orders the closure of all iron ore mines in Goa, citing environmental concerns.
