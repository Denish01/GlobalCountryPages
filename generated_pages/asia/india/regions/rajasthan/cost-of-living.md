# Cost of Living in Rajasthan - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Rajasthan can vary greatly depending on lifestyle and location, but overall it is considered to be an affordable destination with a daily budget of $15-30 USD for backpackers, $30-60 USD for mid-range travelers, and $60-100 USD for comfort seekers.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $15-30 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $30-60 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $60-100 USD
Currency: INR
Tipping Custom: expected (5-10% in restaurants and bars)
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Rajasthan? [/SECTION]
Rajasthan is a region in northwest India known for its rich cultural heritage, historic forts, and vibrant cities. Compared to other major tourist destinations in India, Rajasthan is relatively affordable, with prices lower than in cities like Delhi or Mumbai. However, prices can vary greatly depending on the location, with popular tourist spots like Jaipur and Udaipur tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. Overall, Rajasthan offers a great balance of affordability and experience, making it an attractive destination for travelers from around the world.

In terms of global averages, Rajasthan is considered to be a relatively inexpensive destination, with prices comparable to other popular tourist spots in Southeast Asia. However, prices can fluctuate depending on the time of year, with peak season (October to February) tend to be more expensive than the off-season. Additionally, prices can vary depending on the type of experience, with luxury resorts and high-end restaurants tend to be more expensive than budget-friendly options.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in budget-friendly accommodations like hostels or guesthouses, which can save you up to 50% on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls, which offer delicious and affordable meals for under $3.
- Use public transport or walk/bike whenever possible, as taxis and private cars can be expensive.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (October to February) when prices tend to be higher.
- Bargain at local markets and shops, as prices are often negotiable.
- Consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot, which can save you money on international roaming charges.
