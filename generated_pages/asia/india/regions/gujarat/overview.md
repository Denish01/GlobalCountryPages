[SECTION] What Is Gujarat? [/SECTION]
Gujarat is a **region** located in the western part of India, bordering the Arabian Sea to the west, Pakistan to the northwest, and the Indian states of Rajasthan to the north, Madhya Pradesh to the east, and Maharashtra to the southeast. The state has a diverse geography, with a long coastline, mountains, and a vast desert landscape. Gujarat is home to a population of over 60 million people, with a mix of urban and rural communities. The state is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its vibrant festivals, traditional cuisine, and historic landmarks.

Gujarat has a long history dating back to the ancient Indus Valley Civilization, with many archaeological sites and monuments scattered throughout the state. The state has been ruled by various dynasties and empires, including the Mauryan, Gupta, and Mughal empires. Today, Gujarat is a thriving economy, with major industries in textiles, pharmaceuticals, and petrochemicals. The state is also a popular tourist destination, with its beautiful beaches, historic cities, and vibrant culture.

Gujarat is also known for its unique cultural practices and traditions, including its vibrant festivals, such as the Navratri and Uttarayan. The state is home to many important pilgrimage sites, including the Dwarkadhish temple and the Somnath temple. Gujarat is also famous for its traditional cuisine, which includes popular dishes such as dhokla, khakhra, and undhiyu.
