To get around in Gujarat, you can use a variety of transportation options, including airports, domestic flights, rail networks, buses, and city transport, as well as driving or hiring a vehicle.

Getting There

Gujarat has several major airports that connect to domestic and international destinations. The Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad is the largest airport in the state and offers direct flights to many major cities in India, as well as international flights to destinations in the Middle East, Europe, and Asia. Other major airports in Gujarat include the Vadodara Airport and the Surat Airport, which offer connections to domestic destinations. If you're traveling overland, Gujarat has several entry points along its borders with neighboring states, including Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, and Rajasthan.

For travelers coming from outside India, the most convenient way to get to Gujarat is by flying into a major airport such as Mumbai or Delhi and then taking a domestic flight or train to Gujarat. Alternatively, you can take a bus or drive from a neighboring state. Gujarat is well-connected to the rest of India by road and rail, making it easy to get to from most major cities.

Domestic Flights

Gujarat has an extensive network of domestic flights that connect to major cities across India. Domestic airlines such as IndiGo, SpiceJet, and Air India operate frequent flights from Ahmedabad, Vadodara, and Surat to destinations such as Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Hyderabad. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through online travel agencies such as MakeMyTrip or Cleartrip. Approximate costs for domestic flights vary depending on the route and time of year, but you can expect to pay between $50 and $200 for a one-way ticket.

Trains & Rail

Gujarat has an extensive rail network that connects to major cities across India. The Indian Railways network is one of the largest in the world and offers a convenient and affordable way to get around. Key routes in Gujarat include the Ahmedabad-Mumbai line, the Ahmedabad-Delhi line, and the Vadodara-Surat line. You can book train tickets online through the Indian Railways website or through online travel agencies. High-speed trains such as the Shatabdi Express and the Gatimaan Express operate on certain routes, offering faster travel times and more comfortable seating.

Buses & Coaches

Gujarat has a well-developed network of buses and coaches that connect to major cities across the state and neighboring states. Intercity bus companies such as Gujarat State Road Transport Corporation (GSRTC) and private operators offer frequent services from cities such as Ahmedabad, Vadodara, and Surat to destinations such as Mumbai, Delhi, and Jaipur. Bus travel is a convenient and affordable option, with approximate costs ranging from $10 to $50 for a one-way ticket, depending on the route and type of bus.

Driving

  • To drive in Gujarat, you'll need a valid Indian driving license or an International Driving Permit.
  • Road conditions in Gujarat vary from good to poor, with some highways and city roads being well-maintained, while others may be congested or poorly maintained.
  • Fuel costs in Gujarat are relatively affordable, with petrol and diesel prices ranging from $0.70 to $1.00 per liter.
  • Car rental companies such as Hertz and Avis operate in major cities, offering a range of vehicles for hire.
  • When driving in Gujarat, be aware of traffic congestion, poor road conditions, and aggressive driving.
  • It's also a good idea to hire a local driver or take a guided tour to avoid navigating unfamiliar roads and traffic conditions.

Transport Tips

  • When taking a taxi or ride-hailing service, always check the meter or agree on a fare before you start your journey.
  • Be aware of scams and overcharging, especially when using unofficial taxi services.
  • Download transport apps such as Ola or Uber to book rides and track your journey.
  • Always carry a map or use a GPS device to navigate unfamiliar areas.
  • Be prepared for traffic congestion and delays, especially during peak hours or festivals.
  • Consider hiring a local guide or joining a guided tour to get a deeper understanding of the local culture and history.
  • When using public transport, be mindful of crowds and personal belongings, and avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Don't forget to tip your driver or guide for good service, as this is expected in Indian culture.
