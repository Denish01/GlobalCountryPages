Overall Safety Assessment

Punjab, a region in the northwestern part of India, is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and vibrant cities. The region is generally considered safe for tourists, with low crime rates compared to other major cities in India. However, as with any popular tourist destination, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, scams, and theft can occur, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. It's essential to take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

The Punjabi people are known for their hospitality, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms. However, it's crucial to respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting rural areas or attending cultural events. The region has a strong police presence, and tourist police are available to assist visitors in case of any emergencies. Overall, Punjab is a wonderful destination to explore, with its rich history, delicious cuisine, and warm people, but it's essential to be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Scams targeting tourists, such as overpriced taxi fares and fake tour guides
  • Petty crimes, such as bag snatching and mobile phone theft
  • Traffic accidents, due to reckless driving and poor road conditions
  • Food poisoning, due to unhygienic food and water
  • Scams related to accommodation, such as overpriced hotels and guesthouses
  • Fake police officers or officials, targeting tourists for bribes or scams

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are recommended for travel to Punjab
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Good quality medical facilities are available in major cities, but may be limited in rural areas
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available, but it's essential to check the expiration dates and authenticity of medications

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or a secure bag
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in rural areas
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or attending cultural events
  • Stay hydrated and take regular breaks, especially during hot summer months
  • Learn some basic Punjabi phrases, such as "hello" (sat sri akaal) and "thank you" (dhanyavad)

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be mindful of your dress and behavior, especially when visiting temples or attending cultural events
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and consider using reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps
  • Stay in touch with family and friends back home, and consider sharing your itinerary with them
  • Be cautious when interacting with strangers, and avoid accepting rides or accommodation from unknown individuals
