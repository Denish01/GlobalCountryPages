[SECTION] What Is Punjab? [/SECTION]
Punjab is a region in the northern part of India, bordering the countries of Pakistan to the west and the Indian states of Himachal Pradesh to the northeast, Haryana to the south and southeast, and Rajasthan to the southwest. The region is divided into two parts: the Indian state of Punjab and the Pakistani province of Punjab. The Indian state of Punjab is the 20th largest state in India, covering an area of approximately 50,362 square kilometers. Punjab is known for its fertile plains, which make it one of the most productive agricultural regions in the world. The region is home to a diverse population, with a mix of urban and rural areas, and a rich cultural heritage that reflects its history, traditions, and values.

The geographic position of Punjab has played a significant role in shaping its history and culture. The region has been an important center of trade and commerce, with the ancient Silk Road passing through it. The Indus Valley Civilization, one of the oldest civilizations in the world, flourished in the region, leaving behind a rich legacy of art, architecture, and literature. Punjab is also home to several major rivers, including the Indus, Ravi, and Sutlej, which have supported agriculture and industry in the region.

Punjab is known for its vibrant traditions, including its music, dance, and festivals. The region is home to several major festivals, including the Baisakhi, Lohri, and Diwali, which are celebrated with great enthusiasm and fervor. The region is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes popular dishes such as sarson ka saag, makki di roti, and tandoori chicken.
