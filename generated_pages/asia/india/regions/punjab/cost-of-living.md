# Cost of Living in Punjab - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Punjab is generally affordable, with a daily budget of $15-30 USD per person for basic expenses, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike.

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $15-25 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $30-50 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $60-100 USD
Currency: INR
Tipping Custom: not expected, but 5-10% in high-end establishments
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Punjab? [/SECTION]
Punjab, a region in northern India, offers a unique blend of traditional and modern culture, rich history, and natural beauty. Compared to other major cities in India, Punjab is relatively affordable, with costs lower than in metropolitan areas like Mumbai or Delhi. However, prices can vary depending on the location, with cities like Chandigarh and Amritsar tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. Overall, Punjab provides a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an excellent choice for travelers and expats.

In terms of global averages, Punjab's cost of living is lower than in many Western countries, but slightly higher than in some other parts of Asia. The region's economy is primarily driven by agriculture, manufacturing, and services, which contributes to its relatively stable and affordable cost of living. As a result, visitors can enjoy a comfortable stay in Punjab without breaking the bank, with plenty of options for accommodation, food, and entertainment to suit various budgets.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in smaller towns or outside city centers for more affordable accommodation options.
- Eat at local eateries and street food stalls to enjoy traditional cuisine at lower prices.
- Use public transport or walk/bike whenever possible to save on fuel and taxi costs.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (June to August) to get better deals on flights and hotels.
- Learn some basic Punjabi phrases to negotiate prices and interact with locals.
- Take advantage of free or low-cost attractions, such as visiting local markets, temples, and historical sites.
