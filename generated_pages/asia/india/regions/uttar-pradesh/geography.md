# Uttar Pradesh Geography - Location, Terrain & Climate

Uttar Pradesh is a vast and populous region located in the northern part of India, bordered by several states and countries, and featuring a diverse range of terrain and climate.

[SECTION] Location & Borders [/SECTION]
Uttar Pradesh is situated in the northern part of India, and it is the most populous state in the country. It shares international borders with Nepal to the north and is bounded by several Indian states, including Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh to the north, Haryana and Delhi to the west, Rajasthan to the southwest, Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh to the south, Jharkhand to the southeast, and Bihar to the east. The region is landlocked, with no direct access to the sea, but it is drained by several major rivers, including the Ganges, Yamuna, and Ghaghara.

The location of Uttar Pradesh has played a significant role in shaping its history, culture, and economy. The region has been an important center of trade and commerce for centuries, with the Ganges River providing a major transportation route. The state's strategic location has also made it an important hub for politics and governance, with several major cities, including Lucknow, the state capital, and Kanpur, a major industrial center. The region's borders with several states and countries have also contributed to its cultural diversity, with influences from various parts of India and beyond.

[SECTION] Terrain & Landscape [/SECTION]
Uttar Pradesh is a region of diverse terrain and landscape, with a range of mountains, rivers, and plains. The northern part of the state is home to the **Himalayan foothills**, with several major mountain ranges, including the **Shivalik Hills** and the **Siwalik Range**. The southern part of the state is characterized by the **Ganges-Yamuna Doab**, a fertile alluvial plain that is drained by the Ganges and Yamuna rivers. The eastern part of the state is home to the **Ghaghara River** and its tributaries, which flow through a region of rugged terrain and dense forests.

The state's terrain is also characterized by several major rivers, including the Ganges, Yamuna, and Ghaghara, which provide water and support agriculture and industry. The region's landscape is also dotted with several major lakes and wetlands, including the **Ramsar Sites**, which are recognized as important habitats for migratory birds and other wildlife. The state's diverse terrain and landscape have made it an important center for agriculture, with several major crops, including wheat, rice, and sugarcane, being grown in the region.

The region's terrain has also played a significant role in shaping its climate, with the Himalayan foothills and the Ganges-Yamuna Doab contributing to a subtropical climate with hot summers and cool winters. The state's landscape is also characterized by several major forests, including the **Dudhwa National Park**, which is home to several endangered species, including the **Bengal tiger** and the **Asian elephant**.
