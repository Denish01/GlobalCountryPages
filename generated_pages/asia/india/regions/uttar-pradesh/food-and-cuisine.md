# Uttar Pradesh Food Guide — Dishes, Street Food & Dining

When visiting Uttar Pradesh, be sure to try the region's signature dishes, such as **Galouti Kebab**, **Lucknowi Biryani**, and **Malaiyyo**, which showcase the unique blend of Mughal, Persian, and local flavors that define the state's cuisine.

[FACTBOX]
Staple Foods: wheat, rice, lentils, ghee
National Dish: Lucknowi Biryani, a fragrant and flavorful rice-based dish made with aromatic spices, basmati rice, and marinated meat or vegetables
Meal Times: typical breakfast time is 8-10 am, lunch is 12-2 pm, and dinner is 7-10 pm
Tipping at Restaurants: 5-10% of the total bill is customary
Dietary Notes: Uttar Pradesh is a vegetarian-friendly region, with many options available for vegetarians and vegans, and halal meat is widely available

[SECTION] Food Culture Overview [/SECTION]
Uttar Pradesh's cuisine is a unique blend of Mughal, Persian, and local flavors, with a strong emphasis on rich and fragrant spices, such as **cardamom**, **cinnamon**, and **saffron**. The region's culinary history is deeply rooted in its cultural and historical heritage, with many dishes dating back to the Mughal era. The city of Lucknow, in particular, is renowned for its **Awadhi** cuisine, which is characterized by its use of **dum** (steaming) and **bhunao** (sauteing) cooking techniques. Regional variations are also evident, with the eastern part of the state being known for its **Bhojpuri** cuisine, which features dishes such as **Litti Chokha** and **Jhal Muri**.

The influence of **Mughal** and **Persian** cuisine is evident in the use of **kebabs**, **biryani**, and **korma** dishes, which are staples of Uttar Pradesh's cuisine. The region's cuisine is also known for its rich and creamy **sweets**, such as **Gulab Jamun** and **Jalebi**, which are often served at special occasions and festivals. Overall, Uttar Pradesh's cuisine is a vibrant and diverse reflection of the region's cultural and historical heritage.

Uttar Pradesh's food culture is also deeply rooted in its **street food** tradition, with many popular dishes being sold by vendors and hawkers on the streets. The state is home to a wide range of **local markets**, such as the famous **Chowk** market in Lucknow, which offer a wide variety of fresh produce, spices, and street food. The region's cuisine is also known for its **seasonal variations**, with many dishes being prepared specifically for certain festivals and occasions, such as **Holi** and **Diwali**.

[SECTION] Dining Customs [/SECTION]
- **Eating with the right hand**: it is customary to eat with the right hand, as the left hand is considered unclean
- **Respecting elders**: it is customary to respect elders and guests by offering them food and drink first
- **Trying local cuisine**: it is customary to try local cuisine and drinks, as they are an integral part of the region's culture and heritage
- **Tipping**: tipping is customary in restaurants and bars, with 5-10% of the total bill being the norm
- **Dress code**: dress code varies depending on the restaurant and occasion, but it is generally recommended to dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing
- **Reservation culture**: reservation culture is not very prevalent in Uttar Pradesh, but it is recommended to make reservations in advance for popular restaurants and during peak season
