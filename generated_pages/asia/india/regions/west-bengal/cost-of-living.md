# Cost of Living in West Bengal - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in West Bengal is generally affordable, with a daily budget of $10-30 USD for backpackers, $30-60 USD for mid-range travelers, and $60-100 USD for comfort seekers. 

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable
Daily Budget (Backpacker): $10-30 USD
Daily Budget (Mid-Range): $30-60 USD
Daily Budget (Comfort): $60-100 USD
Currency: INR
Tipping Custom: not expected
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is West Bengal? [/SECTION]
West Bengal is a region in eastern India known for its rich cultural heritage and diverse geography. The cost of living in West Bengal is relatively low compared to other parts of India, making it an attractive destination for tourists and expats alike. The region's affordability is due to its lower cost of accommodation, food, and transportation compared to other major cities in India. However, prices can vary depending on the location, with cities like Kolkata being more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas.

In comparison to its neighbors, West Bengal is more affordable than states like Delhi and Maharashtra, but more expensive than states like Bihar and Odisha. The region's economy is driven by industries such as textiles, steel, and agriculture, which contributes to its relatively low cost of living. Overall, West Bengal offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an excellent choice for those looking to experience the best of India without breaking the bank.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in budget-friendly accommodations like hostels or guesthouses, which can save you up to 50% on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local eateries or street food stalls, which offer delicious and affordable meals.
- Use public transport like buses or metro, which is cheap and efficient.
- Avoid traveling during peak season (October to February) when prices are higher.
- Learn some basic Bengali phrases, which can help you negotiate prices and interact with locals.
- Take advantage of the region's many free or low-cost attractions, such as visiting the Victoria Memorial or taking a stroll along the Hooghly River.
