# Getting Around West Bengal — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in West Bengal, you can use a variety of transportation options including flights, trains, buses, and taxis, as well as ride-hailing apps and local transportation like metro, city buses, and tuk-tuks.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
West Bengal is easily accessible by air, with the Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport (CCU) in Kolkata being the main international airport. The airport is well-connected to major cities in India and abroad, with direct flights to destinations like Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, and Bangkok. You can also enter West Bengal by road or rail from neighboring states like Bihar, Odisha, and Assam. The state has several major railway stations, including Howrah and Sealdah in Kolkata, which are connected to major cities in India. The main overland entry points into West Bengal are the borders with Nepal and Bhutan, as well as the states of Bihar, Odisha, and Assam.

If you are traveling from outside India, you can fly into Kolkata or other major cities in India and then take a domestic flight or train to West Bengal. The state is also connected to Bangladesh by road and rail, with several border crossings along the international border. The main border crossings are at Petrapole, Gede, and Changrabandha, which are connected to major cities in Bangladesh. You can take a bus or train from Bangladesh to West Bengal, or hire a taxi or private vehicle for the journey.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
West Bengal has an extensive domestic air network, with several airlines operating flights to and from the state. The main domestic airlines operating in West Bengal are **IndiGo**, **SpiceJet**, and **Air India**, which offer flights to major cities in India like Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore. You can book domestic flights online through the airlines' websites or through travel portals like **MakeMyTrip** or **Yatra**. The approximate cost of a domestic flight from Kolkata to Delhi is around $100-$200, depending on the airline and time of booking. It is recommended to book flights in advance to get the best deals, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
West Bengal has an extensive rail network, with several major railway stations in the state. The main railway stations in West Bengal are Howrah and Sealdah in Kolkata, which are connected to major cities in India. You can take a train from Kolkata to other parts of the state, or to neighboring states like Bihar, Odisha, and Assam. The **Indian Railways** website allows you to book train tickets online, and you can also book tickets at railway stations or through travel agents. The rail network in West Bengal is generally well-maintained, with several high-speed trains operating on major routes. The approximate cost of a train ticket from Kolkata to Delhi is around $50-$100, depending on the class of travel and availability.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
West Bengal has a well-developed bus network, with several private and government bus operators offering services to major cities in the state. The main bus operators in West Bengal are **West Bengal Surface Transport Corporation** and **South Bengal State Transport Corporation**, which offer bus services to cities like Kolkata, Siliguri, and Darjeeling. You can book bus tickets online through the operators' websites or at bus stations. The approximate cost of a bus ticket from Kolkata to Siliguri is around $10-$20, depending on the operator and class of travel.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in West Bengal, you need to have a valid **Indian driving license** or an **International Driving Permit**.
- The road quality in West Bengal is generally moderate, with some roads being well-maintained and others being poorly maintained.
- Fuel costs in West Bengal are around $1.00-$1.50 per liter, depending on the type of fuel.
- Car rental services are available in major cities like Kolkata and Siliguri, with prices starting from around $20-$50 per day.
- When driving in West Bengal, be aware of **cattle and pedestrians on the road**, as well as **potholes and poor road conditions**.
- It is recommended to hire a driver or take a taxi if you are not familiar with the roads and traffic conditions in West Bengal.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Always **check the meter** before starting your journey in a taxi or auto-rickshaw.
- Be aware of **scams and overcharging** by taxi and auto-rickshaw drivers, and always agree on the fare before starting your journey.
- Download **ride-hailing apps** like Uber and Ola to get around major cities.
- **Carry change** with you, as not all taxi and auto-rickshaw drivers may have change for large bills.
- Be **patient and courteous** when dealing with traffic and other road users, as the roads in West Bengal can be chaotic.
- Always **wear a helmet** when riding a motorcycle or scooter, and follow traffic rules to avoid accidents.
- Be aware of **local festivals and events**, which can cause traffic disruptions and road closures.
- **Learn some basic Bengali phrases**, such as "hello" (নমস্কার) and "thank you" (ধন্যবাদ), to help you communicate with locals.
