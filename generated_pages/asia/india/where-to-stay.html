When planning a trip to India, the best places to stay depend on the city, with popular options including Connaught Place in New Delhi, Colaba in Mumbai, M G Road in Bangalore, Park Street in Kolkata, Fort Kochi in Kochi, Hauz Khas in New Delhi, and Pondicherry Town in Pondicherry, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers.

Best Areas to Stay

For each of the top 7 areas/neighborhoods:

Connaught Place (New Delhi)

Best For: Luxury/Business

Vibe: This central business district is known for its colonial architecture, upscale shopping, and vibrant nightlife, making it an ideal location for business travelers and those seeking a luxurious experience. The area is well-connected to the rest of the city and offers a wide range of dining and entertainment options.

Price Range: $100-500/night

Pros: Central location, easy access to public transport, variety of restaurants and bars

Cons: Traffic congestion, high prices

Colaba (Mumbai)

Best For: Backpacker/Luxury

Vibe: This historic neighborhood is a popular destination for travelers, with its iconic Gateway of India, lively streets, and eclectic mix of budget-friendly and high-end accommodations. The area is known for its vibrant atmosphere, street food, and shopping.

Price Range: $20-200/night

Pros: Affordable options, vibrant atmosphere, proximity to major attractions

Cons: Crowded and noisy

M G Road (Bangalore)

Best For: Business/Family

Vibe: This major commercial hub is home to many shops, restaurants, and entertainment venues, making it an ideal location for families and business travelers. The area is well-connected to the rest of the city and offers a range of accommodation options.

Price Range: $50-200/night

Pros: Central location, variety of dining and entertainment options, easy access to public transport

Cons: Traffic congestion, limited budget options

Park Street (Kolkata)

Best For: Luxury/Family

Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is known for its colonial architecture, trendy restaurants, and vibrant nightlife, making it a popular destination for families and luxury travelers. The area is well-connected to the rest of the city and offers a range of accommodation options.

Price Range: $50-200/night

Pros: Historic architecture, variety of dining options, central location

Cons: Limited budget options, traffic congestion

Fort Kochi (Kochi)

Best For: Backpacker/Luxury

Vibe: This historic neighborhood is a popular destination for travelers, with its colonial architecture, lively streets, and eclectic mix of budget-friendly and high-end accommodations. The area is known for its vibrant atmosphere, street food, and shopping.

Price Range: $10-100/night

Pros: Affordable options, vibrant atmosphere, proximity to major attractions

Cons: Limited high-end options, crowded and noisy

Hauz Khas (New Delhi)

Best For: Luxury/Business

Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is known for its trendy restaurants, bars, and boutiques, making it a popular destination for business travelers and those seeking a luxurious experience. The area is well-connected to the rest of the city and offers a range of accommodation options.

Price Range: $50-200/night

Pros: Central location, variety of dining and entertainment options, easy access to public transport

Cons: Limited budget options, traffic congestion

Pondicherry Town (Pondicherry)

Best For: Backpacker/Luxury

Vibe: This historic town is a popular destination for travelers, with its colonial architecture, lively streets, and eclectic mix of budget-friendly and high-end accommodations. The area is known for its vibrant atmosphere, street food, and shopping.

Price Range: $10-50/night

Pros: Affordable options, vibrant atmosphere, proximity to major attractions

Cons: Limited high-end options, crowded and noisy

Booking Tips

  • Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak travel seasons
  • Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com, Agoda, or Airbnb to compare prices and options
  • Negotiate prices, especially when booking directly with hotels or guesthouses
  • Consider budget-friendly options like hostels or guesthouses for affordable stays
  • Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices often higher during peak travel seasons
  • Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it meets your needs
  • Use travel apps like Google Maps or Rome2rio to research transportation options and plan your itinerary

Safety & Location Tips

  • Research the area before booking, considering factors like safety, proximity to attractions, and transportation options
  • Avoid areas with high crime rates or known safety concerns
  • Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
  • Keep valuables secure and be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
  • Consider staying in areas with 24-hour security or a reception desk for added safety and convenience
