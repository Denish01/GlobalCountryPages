Overall Safety Assessment

Lebanon is a country with a rich history and culture, but it also faces various safety challenges. The country has experienced periods of instability and conflict, particularly in recent years, which can impact the safety of visitors. However, with proper planning and precautions, tourists can still have a safe and enjoyable experience in Lebanon. The capital city, Beirut, is generally considered to be a safe and welcoming place for tourists, with a vibrant nightlife and a wide range of cultural attractions. However, as with any country, there are areas that are considered safer than others, and visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

The Lebanese government has taken steps to improve the safety and security of tourists, including the establishment of a tourist police force. Additionally, many hotels and tourist attractions have increased their security measures to protect visitors. Despite these efforts, visitors should still be aware of the potential risks and take necessary precautions, such as avoiding certain areas, being mindful of their belongings, and staying informed about local conditions. By taking a balanced and informed approach, tourists can minimize their risks and have a safe and enjoyable experience in Lebanon.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and petty theft are common in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Scams targeting tourists, such as overcharging for goods and services, are common in some areas
  • Credit card skimming and ATM scams have been reported in some areas
  • Demonstrations and protests can occur in Beirut and other cities, and tourists should avoid these areas
  • Traffic accidents are common in Lebanon, and tourists should be cautious when crossing the road or driving
  • Some areas of Lebanon are still contaminated with unexploded ordnance from past conflicts, and tourists should be aware of this risk

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for all travelers to Lebanon
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not considered safe to drink, and bottled water is widely available
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities in Beirut and other major cities are generally of a high standard, but may be limited in rural areas
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available in major cities, but may be limited in rural areas

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use credit cards or debit cards instead
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Stay informed about local conditions and demonstrations, and avoid these areas
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases, such as "merhaba" (hello) and "shukraan" (thank you)
  • Keep your hotel room door locked and use the hotel safe to store valuable items

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be prepared for some stares and attention from locals, especially in more conservative areas
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps
  • Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, and avoiding isolated or deserted areas
