[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Baalbek** temple complex, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is one of the largest and best-preserved Roman ruins in the world.
- The **Byblos** archaeological site, also a UNESCO World Heritage site, showcases the city's rich history dating back to the Neolithic period.
- **Khalil Gibran**'s literary and artistic works are a significant part of Lebanon's cultural heritage, with his book **The Prophet** being a classic of world literature.
- The traditional **Dabke** folk dance is a popular expression of Lebanese culture, often performed at social gatherings and celebrations.
- The **National Museum of Beirut** houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing Lebanon's history and cultural heritage.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Fairouz**, a renowned Lebanese singer, is considered a cultural icon in the Arab world, with a career spanning over six decades.
- The **Lebanese diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global community, with notable figures like **Carlos Slim**, **Steve Jobs**, and **Hassan Kamel Al-Sabbah**.
- **Rafic Hariri**, a former Lebanese prime minister, played a key role in the country's post-civil war reconstruction efforts and was a prominent businessman and philanthropist.
- The values of **hospitality** and **generosity** are deeply ingrained in Lebanese culture, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome they receive from the locals.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **hospitality** of the Lebanese people, with visitors often commenting on the warm and welcoming nature of the locals.
- The **cuisine**, which is a unique blend of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern flavors, with popular dishes like **tabbouleh**, **kibbeh**, and **falafel**.
- The **scenic beauty** of the country, with its rugged coastline, mountainous terrain, and picturesque villages like **Bcharre** and **Kfardebian**.
- The **vibrant nightlife** of **Beirut**, with its many bars, clubs, and restaurants, making it a popular destination for young travelers.
- The **rich history** and **cultural heritage** of the country, with its many historical sites, museums, and cultural events.
- The **ski resorts**, like **Faraya** and **Mzaar**, which offer world-class skiing and snowboarding facilities in the winter months.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Jeita Grotto**, a stunning limestone cave system, is a lesser-known natural wonder that is definitely worth visiting.
- The **Qadisha Valley**, a scenic and historic valley, is home to many ancient monasteries and churches, and offers breathtaking views and hiking trails.
- The **Batroun** region, with its beautiful coastline, historic sites, and vibrant cultural scene, is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered.
- The **Lebanese wine** industry, which produces some excellent wines, including the famous **Ksara** and **Massaya** labels, is a lesser-known aspect of the country's culture and economy.
