# Education Costs in Lebanon

The cost of education in Lebanon varies significantly depending on the type of institution, with public schools offering free or subsidized education, while private and international schools can range from $1,000 to $10,000 or more per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Lebanon's education system is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector offering free or subsidized education to all citizens. However, the quality of public education is often lower compared to private schools, which are generally considered to be of higher quality. The language of instruction in public schools is **Arabic**, while private schools often teach in **English** or **French**. Compulsory education in Lebanon lasts for 12 years, from age 6 to 18, and includes primary and secondary education. International schools are also available in Lebanon, offering a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **British A-levels**.

The quality of education in Lebanon is generally considered to be good, with many private schools and universities offering high-quality programs. However, the public education sector faces challenges, including limited resources and outdated curricula. Despite these challenges, Lebanon has a high literacy rate, with over 95% of the population able to read and write. International schools in Lebanon offer a range of programs and curricula, making it a popular destination for expat families.
