[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Lebanon has improved significantly over the past few years, with average download speeds of 20 Mbps, making it possible to stream videos, browse the web, and stay connected with family and friends. However, there is a noticeable urban vs rural divide, with cities like Beirut and Tripoli enjoying faster and more reliable internet connections, while rural areas often experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Despite these challenges, the internet in Lebanon is generally reliable, with most users experiencing minimal downtime and few issues with connectivity.

In terms of reliability, the internet in Lebanon is relatively stable, with most providers offering **unlimited data plans** and **24/7 customer support**. However, during peak hours or in areas with high demand, speeds can slow down significantly, making it frustrating to get work done or stream videos. Additionally, **power outages** are common in some areas, which can affect internet connectivity, especially in rural areas where backup power systems may not be available.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Lebanon, tourists can visit one of the many mobile provider stores, such as Touch or Alfa, and purchase a **tourist SIM card** for around 10 USD. To buy a SIM card, you will need to provide a **passport** and fill out a simple registration form. The process is relatively straightforward, and most providers offer **English language support**, making it easy for tourists to get connected. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from one of the main carriers, such as Touch or Alfa, as they offer the best coverage and data plans.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with most hotels offering **free WiFi** to their guests.
- Cafe WiFi culture is widespread, with many cafes and restaurants offering **free WiFi** to customers.
- Coworking spaces are becoming increasingly popular, with many offering **high-speed internet** and **comfortable workspaces**.
- Public WiFi is limited, but many cities have **free public WiFi** in popular areas, such as city centers and tourist attractions.
- Many malls and shopping centers also offer **free WiFi**, making it easy to stay connected while shopping.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces, such as **Beirut Digital District**, offer comfortable workspaces and high-speed internet, making them ideal for remote workers.
- Reliable cafe chains, such as **Cafe du Liban**, offer free WiFi and a comfortable work environment, making them a great alternative to coworking spaces.
- Backup internet options, such as **mobile hotspots**, are available for purchase, providing a reliable backup in case of internet outages.
- VPNs are recommended for remote workers, as they provide an additional layer of security and **data encryption**.
- Many cafes and restaurants also offer **power outlets** and **comfortable seating**, making them ideal for remote workers who need to work on the go.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is limited in Lebanon, but some websites may be blocked, so it's recommended to use a **VPN** to access restricted content.
- Blocked sites include some **pornography** and **gambling** websites, but most social media and news websites are accessible.
- VPN recommendations include **ExpressVPN** and **NordVPN**, which offer reliable and secure connections.
- Roaming alternatives, such as **eSIMs**, are available for purchase, providing a convenient and cost-effective way to stay connected while traveling.
- It's recommended to purchase a **local SIM card** instead of relying on roaming, as it's often cheaper and offers better coverage.
