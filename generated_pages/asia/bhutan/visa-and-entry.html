Visa Policy Overview

Bhutan has a moderate visa policy, with a focus on sustainable tourism and cultural preservation. The country aims to strike a balance between welcoming visitors and protecting its unique environment and traditions. Citizens of only a few countries, including India, can enter Bhutan without a visa, while others need to obtain a visa in advance or on arrival. The government has introduced various measures to regulate tourism, including a daily tariff and a requirement for travelers to book their tours through a licensed tour operator.

Bhutan's visa policy is designed to promote responsible tourism and ensure that visitors respect the country's customs and environment. The government has set a limit on the number of tourists allowed to enter the country each year, and visitors are required to pay a daily tariff that includes a sustainable development fee. This fee is used to fund various development projects, including education, healthcare, and infrastructure development. Overall, Bhutan's visa policy is aimed at preserving the country's unique culture and environment while still allowing visitors to experience its beauty and hospitality.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists are required to book their tour through a licensed tour operator and pay a daily tariff, which includes a sustainable development fee, accommodation, and food. The typical stay for tourists is 14-30 days, and extensions are possible but subject to approval. Tourists are also required to have a minimum of $200 per day for their stay in Bhutan.

Business Travelers: Business travelers need to obtain a visa in advance and provide an invitation letter from a Bhutanese company or organization. They may also need to provide additional documentation, such as a letter from their employer and proof of business activities in Bhutan.

Students: Students who wish to study in Bhutan need to obtain a student visa and provide proof of enrollment in a Bhutanese educational institution. The duration of the student visa is typically one year, but can be extended subject to approval.

Workers: Workers who wish to work in Bhutan need to obtain a work visa and provide a sponsorship letter from a Bhutanese employer. They may also need to provide additional documentation, such as a contract of employment and proof of qualifications.

Entry Points & Procedures

The main entry points to Bhutan are through the Paro International Airport, which is located about 60 kilometers from the capital city Thimphu. Visitors can also enter Bhutan by land through the border towns of Phuentsholing and Gelephu. Upon arrival, visitors need to present their visa and travel documents to the immigration officer and complete the necessary formalities. The immigration process typically takes about 30 minutes to an hour, and visitors are required to provide biometric data, including fingerprints and a photograph. Visitors are also required to declare any restricted or prohibited items, such as tobacco and narcotics.

Upon arrival, visitors are also required to pay the daily tariff and sustainable development fee, which includes accommodation, food, and other services. The tariff is set by the government and is non-negotiable. Visitors are also required to provide proof of sufficient funds and health insurance, and may be subject to a medical examination if they are staying in Bhutan for an extended period.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not booking a tour through a licensed operator
  • Not paying the daily tariff and sustainable development fee in advance
  • Not declaring restricted or prohibited items
  • Not having sufficient funds or health insurance
  • Not providing required documentation, such as proof of enrollment or invitation letter
  • Not respecting Bhutan's unique culture and environment, including dress code and etiquette requirements
