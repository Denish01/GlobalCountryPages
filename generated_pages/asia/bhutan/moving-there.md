# Moving to Bhutan — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Bhutan, you will need to carefully consider the country's unique cultural and environmental requirements, as well as its strict regulations and limited opportunities for foreigners.

[SECTION] Why People Move to Bhutan [/SECTION]
Bhutan is a country like no other, with its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and commitment to **Gross National Happiness**. Many people are drawn to Bhutan's unique approach to development, which prioritizes the well-being of its citizens and the environment. Expats who move to Bhutan are often drawn to the country's **peaceful and serene atmosphere**, as well as its opportunities for **hiking and outdoor adventures**. The majority of expats in Bhutan are professionals and entrepreneurs who are working on development projects or starting their own businesses. The quality of life in Bhutan is generally high, with a strong emphasis on community and social connections.

Despite its many attractions, Bhutan is not a typical expat destination. The country has a **limited number of jobs available for foreigners**, and the cost of living can be relatively high. However, for those who are willing to adapt to the local way of life, Bhutan can be a incredibly rewarding place to live. The country has a **strong sense of community**, and expats are often welcomed with open arms. Bhutan is also a great place for families, with a **high quality of education** and a safe and healthy environment.

Bhutan's expat community is small but diverse, with people from all over the world drawn to the country's unique culture and way of life. The majority of expats live in the capital city of Thimphu, where there are a range of **amenities and services** available. However, the cost of living in Thimphu can be high, and some expats prefer to live in smaller towns or rural areas where the cost of living is lower.

[SECTION] Expat Communities [/SECTION]

- Online groups, such as Facebook and LinkedIn, are a great way to connect with other expats and get advice and support.
- Social networks, such as expat forums and blogs, can provide valuable information and insights into expat life in Bhutan.

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- Opening a bank account in Bhutan can be a straightforward process, but it is essential to have all the necessary documents.
- Money transfers can be made through a range of channels, including banks and online services.
- The **Bhutanese currency**, the Ngultrum, is pegged to the Indian Rupee, and it is essential to understand the exchange rates and fees.
- Credit cards are not widely accepted in Bhutan, and it is essential to have cash and other forms of payment.
- The **cost of living** in Bhutan can be relatively high, especially in Thimphu, and it is essential to budget carefully and plan ahead.

[SECTION] Challenges & Honest Advice [/SECTION]
- **Culture shock** can be a significant challenge for expats in Bhutan, and it is essential to be patient and flexible.
- The **bureaucracy** in Bhutan can be slow and frustrating, and it is essential to be prepared for delays and setbacks.
- **Language barriers** can be a challenge, especially outside of Thimphu, and it is essential to learn some basic Dzongkha phrases.
- The **limited job opportunities** and **high cost of living** can be a challenge for expats, and it is essential to have a clear plan and budget in place.
- The **unique cultural norms** and **environmental regulations** in Bhutan can take some getting used to, and it is essential to be respectful and mindful of the local way of life.
- **Healthcare** can be limited in Bhutan, especially outside of Thimphu, and it is essential to have a plan in place for medical emergencies.
