Economic Overview

Bhutan's economy is a unique blend of traditional and modern elements, with a strong focus on sustainable development and gross national happiness. As a Lower-Middle income country, Bhutan has made significant progress in recent years, with a steady growth rate and a decline in poverty levels. The country's economy is driven by a combination of agriculture, hydroelectric power, and tourism, with a growing services sector. Bhutan's development level is categorized as a developing country, with a strong emphasis on human development and environmental conservation.

Bhutan's economic trajectory is shaped by its commitment to sustainable development, which is reflected in its development philosophy of Gross National Happiness (GNH). This approach prioritizes the well-being of citizens and the environment, alongside economic growth. As a result, Bhutan has implemented policies to promote sustainable agriculture, renewable energy, and eco-tourism, which have contributed to its economic growth and development. The country has also made efforts to diversify its economy, with a focus on developing its services sector, including finance, education, and healthcare.

Despite its progress, Bhutan's economy still faces challenges, including a narrow economic base, limited infrastructure, and a reliance on imports. The country is also vulnerable to external shocks, including changes in global commodity prices and climate change. However, with its unique approach to development and a strong commitment to sustainability, Bhutan is well-positioned to continue its economic growth and development in the years to come.

Trade Profile

Top Exports:

  • Hydroelectric Power: Bhutan exports electricity to India, which is a significant contributor to its economy
  • Agricultural Products: Fruits, vegetables, and spices are exported to India and other countries
  • Minerals: Bhutan exports minerals, including limestone, dolomite, and gypsum
  • Handicrafts: Traditional handicrafts, including textiles and woodcarvings, are exported to countries around the world
  • Timber: Bhutan exports timber and wood products to India and other countries

Top Imports:

  • Machinery and Equipment: Bhutan imports machinery and equipment, including vehicles and construction equipment
  • Fuel: The country imports fuel, including diesel and petrol, to meet its energy needs
  • Electronics: Bhutan imports electronics, including computers and mobile phones
  • Food Products: The country imports food products, including rice, wheat, and sugar
  • Pharmaceuticals: Bhutan imports pharmaceuticals and medical equipment to meet its healthcare needs

Key Trading Partners:

  • India: Bhutan's largest trading partner, with significant trade in hydroelectric power, agricultural products, and minerals
  • China: Bhutan has a growing trade relationship with China, with imports of machinery and equipment
  • Thailand: Bhutan imports goods, including electronics and food products, from Thailand
