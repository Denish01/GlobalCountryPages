[SECTION] What Is Bhutan? [/SECTION]
Bhutan is a **sovereign** country located in the **Himalayan** mountain range in Asia. It is situated between two giant neighbors, **China** to the north and **India** to the south, east, and west. With a population of approximately 0.78 million people, Bhutan is one of the smallest countries in the world. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, unique culture, and commitment to **sustainable development**. The country is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several endangered species, and its pristine environment is a major draw for tourists and nature lovers.

Bhutan's geography is characterized by steep mountains, deep valleys, and dense forests. The country has a varied climate, ranging from tropical in the south to alpine in the north. The capital city, **Thimphu**, is the largest city in the country and is home to many important institutions, including the **Royal Palace** and the **National Assembly**. Bhutan is a **constitutional monarchy**, with the **King** serving as the head of state and the **Prime Minister** as the head of government.

Bhutan is also known for its unique approach to development, which emphasizes **gross national happiness** (GNH) over **gross domestic product** (GDP). This approach prioritizes the well-being of citizens and the environment, and has made Bhutan a model for sustainable development. The country has also made significant progress in recent years, with improvements in healthcare, education, and infrastructure.

[SECTION] Key History [/SECTION]
1. 1616 - The **Zhabdrung Ngawang Namgyal** unified the warring factions of Bhutan and established the **dual system of government**, with the **Je Khenpo** (Chief Abbot) and the **Druk Desi** (Temporal Ruler) sharing power.
2. 1907 - The **Treaty of Punakha** was signed, making Bhutan a **British protectorate** and giving the British control over Bhutan's foreign affairs.
3. 1949 - Bhutan signed the **Treaty of Perpetual Peace and Friendship** with India, which recognized Bhutan's sovereignty and provided for Indian support in the event of a threat to Bhutan's security.
4. 1971 - Bhutan joined the **United Nations**, marking its entry into the global community.
5. 2008 - Bhutan transitioned from an **absolute monarchy** to a **constitutional monarchy**, with the adoption of a new constitution.
6. 2013 - The **Royal Government of Bhutan** launched the **Bhutanese Economic Development Policy**, which aimed to promote economic growth and reduce poverty.
7. 2018 - Bhutan celebrated the **50th anniversary of the coronation** of the **Fourth King**, who played a key role in modernizing the country.

[SECTION] What Makes Bhutan Unique [/SECTION]
- Bhutan is the only **carbon-negative** country in the world, meaning that it absorbs more carbon dioxide than it emits.
- The country has a unique approach to **tourism**, with a focus on **high-value, low-impact** tourism that promotes cultural exchange and environmental conservation.
- Bhutan is home to a rich and diverse **cultural heritage**, with a strong emphasis on **Buddhism** and traditional **arts and crafts**.
- The country has made significant progress in **human development**, with improvements in healthcare, education, and poverty reduction.
- Bhutan is committed to **sustainable development**, with a focus on **renewable energy**, **organic farming**, and **environmental conservation**.
