# Education Costs in Bhutan

The cost of education in Bhutan varies depending on the type of school and institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private and international schools can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Bhutan is divided into public and private schools, with the public schools being the primary provider of education. The language of instruction in public schools is Dzongkha, while private schools may use English as a medium of instruction. The school system consists of 12 years of compulsory education, which includes primary, middle, and secondary education. International schools are also available in Bhutan, mainly in the capital city of Thimphu, which offer a range of curricula including the International Baccalaureate (IB) and the Cambridge International Examinations (CIE).

The quality of education in public schools is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on traditional Bhutanese values and culture. However, private schools are often considered to be of higher quality, with better infrastructure and more qualified teachers. International schools in Bhutan offer a high-quality education with a focus on international curricula and are popular among expatriates and wealthy Bhutanese families.
