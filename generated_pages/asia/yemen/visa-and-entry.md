# Yemen Visa & Entry Requirements

To enter Yemen, most foreign nationals require a visa, which can be obtained through various types and requirements depending on their nationality and purpose of visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Yemen before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Yemen has a relatively strict visa policy, with only a few countries having visa-free access or eligibility for visa on arrival. The country requires visitors to obtain a visa prior to their arrival, with some exceptions for diplomatic or special cases. Citizens of neighboring countries, such as Saudi Arabia and Oman, may have different requirements or restrictions. Yemen's visa policy is designed to regulate and monitor the flow of foreign visitors, ensuring national security and public safety.

The Yemeni government has been working to streamline its visa application process, introducing new types of visas and simplifying requirements for certain nationalities. However, the country's visa policy remains largely restrictive, with a focus on controlling the influx of foreign visitors. As a result, travelers should carefully review the requirements and regulations before planning their trip to Yemen.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** To visit Yemen as a tourist, travelers typically need to obtain a tourist visa, which is usually valid for a single entry and a maximum stay of 30 days. The visa application process typically requires a completed application form, a valid passport, and proof of onward travel. Tourists may also need to provide proof of sufficient funds and health insurance. Extensions of stay are possible, but must be applied for through the relevant authorities.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers to Yemen require a business visa, which can be obtained through the Yemeni embassy or consulate. The application process typically requires a letter of invitation from a Yemeni company, as well as proof of the traveler's business activities and intentions. Business visas are usually valid for a single entry and a maximum stay of 30 days, with the possibility of extension.
**Students:** Students traveling to Yemen for educational purposes require a student visa, which can be obtained through the Yemeni embassy or consulate. The application process typically requires proof of enrollment in a Yemeni educational institution, as well as proof of sufficient funds and health insurance. Student visas are usually valid for a single entry and a maximum stay of one year, with the possibility of extension.
**Workers:** Workers traveling to Yemen for employment purposes require a work visa, which can be obtained through the Yemeni embassy or consulate. The application process typically requires a letter of sponsorship from a Yemeni employer, as well as proof of the traveler's qualifications and experience. Work visas are usually valid for a single entry and a maximum stay of one year, with the possibility of extension.

3. One recent passport-sized photograph
4. Proof of onward travel
5. Proof of sufficient funds
6. Health insurance certificate
7. Letter of invitation or sponsorship (for business, work, or student visas)
8. Proof of enrollment in a Yemeni educational institution (for student visas)
9. Proof of qualifications and experience (for work visas)
10. Police certificate or background check (for certain types of visas)

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Yemen are Sanaa International Airport and Aden International Airport, as well as several land border crossings with Saudi Arabia and Oman. Upon arrival, travelers will need to present their visa, passport, and other required documents to immigration officials. The entry process typically involves a brief interview and inspection of the traveler's documents and luggage. Travelers should be prepared to provide information about their purpose of visit, accommodation, and onward travel plans.

Immigration procedures in Yemen can be slow and bureaucratic, and travelers should be patient and prepared for delays. It is also recommended to have all required documents and information readily available, as well as to be respectful and cooperative with immigration officials. In some cases, travelers may be required to undergo additional screening or questioning, and should be prepared to provide additional information or documentation as needed.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Failing to obtain a visa prior to arrival, when required
- Not having all required documents and information readily available
- Not being respectful and cooperative with immigration officials
- Not having a clear understanding of the purpose of visit and onward travel plans
- Not being prepared for delays and bureaucratic procedures
- Not having sufficient funds or health insurance to cover the duration of stay
