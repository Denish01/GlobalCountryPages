[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Yemen has a limited public transportation system, with most people relying on taxis, minibuses, and shared taxis to get around. The quality of public transit varies greatly between cities, with Sanaa having a more developed system than other parts of the country. In rural areas, transportation options are often limited, and people may have to rely on walking or using donkeys and other animals to get around. The lack of a comprehensive public transportation system makes it difficult for people to move around, especially for those who cannot afford private transportation.

The main transportation modes in Yemen are taxis, minibuses, and shared taxis, which are often overcrowded and uncomfortable. The roads in Yemen are often in poor condition, which can make traveling by car or bus a challenging and time-consuming experience. Despite these challenges, there are many scenic routes in Yemen, and traveling by car or bus can be a great way to see the country's beautiful landscapes. However, it is essential to be aware of the security situation and take necessary precautions when traveling.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using shared taxis or minibuses, which are often cheaper than taking a private taxi.
- When taking a taxi, it is essential to negotiate the fare before starting the journey to avoid being overcharged.
- Be aware of the security situation in Yemen and take necessary precautions when traveling, especially at night.
- Consider hiring a car and driver for long-distance travel, as this can be a more comfortable and safer option.
- Use reputable taxi companies or ride-hailing apps when available, and always check the driver's ID before starting the journey.
- Be mindful of the **local currency**, YER, and have some cash with you, as not all places accept credit or debit cards.
