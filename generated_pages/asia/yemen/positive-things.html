Cultural Treasures

  • The Old City of Sana'a is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its ancient architecture and historic significance.
  • The Socotra Archipelago is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its unique biodiversity and natural beauty.
  • The Great Mosque of Sana'a is one of the oldest and most important mosques in Yemen, featuring a mix of Islamic and Ottoman architectural styles.
  • The Taqwah Mosque is a historic mosque in Zabid, known for its unique architecture and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Dar al-Hajar palace is a historic palace in Sana'a, featuring a mix of Islamic and Ottoman architectural styles.

People & Global Impact

  • Abdul Rahman Ibn Khaldun was a renowned Yemeni historian and scholar, known for his contributions to the field of Islamic history and sociology.
  • The Yemeni diaspora has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Yemenis living and working abroad, particularly in countries such as Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates.
  • Tawakkol Karman is a Yemeni journalist and activist, known for her advocacy on issues such as women's rights and freedom of speech.
  • The Yemeni community is known for its strong sense of hospitality and tradition, with many Yemenis placing a high value on family and social relationships.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Yemeni people is often cited as one of the country's greatest strengths, with visitors frequently commenting on the warm welcome they receive.
  • The natural scenery of Yemen is stunning, with popular destinations including the Socotra Archipelago and the Haraz Mountains.
  • The traditional cuisine of Yemen is unique and flavorful, with popular dishes including saltah, mandhi, and fahsa.
  • The historic architecture of Yemen is a major draw for tourists, with popular destinations including the Old City of Sana'a and the Great Mosque of Sana'a.
  • The cultural festivals of Yemen are colorful and vibrant, with popular events including the Sana'a Summer Festival and the Socotra Cultural Festival.
  • The traditional markets of Yemen are a great place to pick up unique souvenirs, with popular destinations including the Bab al-Yemen market in Sana'a.

Hidden Gems

  • The Wadi Hadhramaut valley is a historic and cultural treasure, featuring a range of ancient towns and villages, including the city of Shibam.
  • The Socotra Island is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its unique biodiversity and natural beauty, and is home to a range of unique flora and fauna, including the dragon's blood tree.
