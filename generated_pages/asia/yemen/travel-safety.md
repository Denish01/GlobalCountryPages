[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Yemen has been embroiled in a complex and multifaceted conflict since 2015, which has led to a significant deterioration in the country's security situation. The conflict has resulted in a rise in terrorism, kidnapping, and violent crime, making it extremely challenging for tourists to visit the country safely. Additionally, the country's infrastructure, including healthcare and emergency services, has been severely impacted, further exacerbating the risks faced by visitors.

The security situation in Yemen is highly unpredictable, and the risk of being caught in the crossfire or becoming a target of terrorism is high. Furthermore, the country's economy has been severely impacted, leading to widespread poverty and a rise in petty crime. As a result, tourists are advised to exercise extreme caution when traveling to Yemen, and to carefully consider the risks before making any travel plans. It is essential to stay informed about the latest developments and to follow official travel advisories to minimize the risks.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Kidnapping**: There is a high risk of kidnapping in Yemen, particularly in areas controlled by terrorist groups.
- **Terrorism**: Terrorist attacks are common in Yemen, and tourists are advised to avoid crowded areas and public gatherings.
- **Armed robbery**: Armed robbery is a significant risk in Yemen, particularly in areas with high crime rates.
- **Scams**: Scams are common in Yemen, particularly in tourist areas, and visitors are advised to be cautious when dealing with strangers.
- **Roadblocks**: Roadblocks are common in Yemen, and tourists are advised to exercise caution when traveling by road.
- **Piracy**: Piracy is a risk in Yemen's coastal areas, and tourists are advised to avoid traveling by sea.
- **Landmines**: Landmines are a significant risk in Yemen, particularly in areas that have been affected by conflict.
- **Crowd violence**: Crowd violence is a risk in Yemen, particularly in areas with high levels of unrest.

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: Vaccinations against **hepatitis A**, **hepatitis B**, **typhoid**, and **rabies** are recommended for travel to Yemen.
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is not safe to drink in Yemen, and tourists are advised to stick to bottled or filtered water.
- **Medical Facilities**: Medical facilities in Yemen are limited, and tourists are advised to have comprehensive medical insurance that includes evacuation coverage.
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacy access is limited in Yemen, and tourists are advised to bring any necessary medications with them.
