# Education Costs in Oman

The cost of education in Oman varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education being generally free or subsidized for local students, while private and international schools can range from $2,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Oman's education system is divided into two main sectors: public and private. The public education sector is generally considered to be of high quality, with a strong emphasis on **Arabic language** and **Islamic studies**. However, private schools often offer a more **international curriculum**, such as the **British** or **American** system, which can be appealing to expatriate families. Compulsory education in Oman lasts for 12 years, starting from the age of 6 and ending at the age of 18. International schools are also available in Oman, particularly in the capital city of Muscat, which offer a range of curricula and cater to students from diverse backgrounds.

The language of instruction in public schools is **Arabic**, while private schools may offer instruction in **English** or other languages. The Omani government has implemented various initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of **technology** and **vocational training** programs. Despite these efforts, some challenges remain, such as the need for more **specialized teachers** and **infrastructure development**. Overall, Oman's education system is well-regarded in the region, and the country has made significant progress in increasing access to education for all citizens.
