[SECTION] Overview: Oman vs Saudi Arabia [/SECTION]
Oman and Saudi Arabia are two neighboring countries in the Middle East with distinct economies and cost of living profiles. Oman has a more diverse economy, with a strong focus on tourism, manufacturing, and logistics, while Saudi Arabia's economy is largely driven by oil exports. In terms of cost of living, Oman tends to be cheaper than Saudi Arabia, particularly when it comes to housing, food, and transportation. However, the cost difference between the two countries can vary depending on the specific location, lifestyle, and personal preferences.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in determining the cost of living. Oman has a relatively small population and a more relaxed pace of life, which can contribute to lower costs, while Saudi Arabia has a larger population and a more fast-paced economy, which can drive up costs. Additionally, the exchange rates and inflation rates in the two countries can also impact the cost of living. Overall, Oman's more diverse economy and relaxed pace of life make it a more affordable option for expats and tourists, while Saudi Arabia's larger economy and more extensive infrastructure make it a more attractive option for business and investment.
