[SECTION] Overview: Oman vs Yemen [/SECTION]
Oman and Yemen are two neighboring countries in the Arabian Peninsula with distinct economic profiles. Oman has a high-income economy, driven by its oil and gas sector, while Yemen is a low-income country with a fragile economy. The cost of living in Oman is significantly higher than in Yemen, reflecting the country's stronger economy and higher standards of living. In general, Oman is more expensive than Yemen, with prices for housing, food, and transportation being substantially higher. However, Yemen's economy has been severely impacted by conflict and instability, which has led to a significant decline in the standard of living and a rise in poverty.

The economic context of the two countries is also reflected in their GDP per capita and inflation rates. Oman has a GDP per capita of over $29,000, while Yemen's GDP per capita is less than $2,500. Additionally, Oman's inflation rate is relatively low, at 2.5%, while Yemen's inflation rate is significantly higher, at 10.2%. These differences in economic indicators contribute to the overall cost of living in each country, with Oman being more expensive than Yemen. On average, Yemen is approximately 30-40% cheaper than Oman, making it a more affordable option for expats, students, and retirees.

[SECTION] Bottom Line [/SECTION]
When considering the cost of living in Oman and Yemen, it is essential to weigh the pros and cons of each country. Oman offers a high standard of living, modern amenities, and a stable economy, but at a significantly higher cost. Yemen, on the other hand, provides a more affordable lifestyle, but with limitations in terms of modern amenities and economic stability. For expats, students, and retirees, Yemen may be a more attractive option due to its lower cost of living, but it is crucial to consider the country's current situation and potential risks. Ultimately, the choice between Oman and Yemen depends on individual priorities and circumstances.
