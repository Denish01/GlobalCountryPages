Cultural Treasures

  • The Bahla Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a well-preserved example of medieval Islamic architecture in Oman.
  • The Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque is a stunning example of modern Islamic architecture, featuring a 50-meter-high dome and intricate stone carvings.
  • The Omani Traditional Dress is a unique and colorful aspect of Omani culture, with men wearing the dishdasha and women wearing the abaya.
  • The Omani Folk Music is a vibrant and expressive art form, featuring traditional instruments such as the oud and qraqeb.
  • The Muscat Festival is an annual celebration of Omani culture, featuring traditional music, dance, and food.

People & Global Impact

  • Qaboos bin Said Al Said, the former Sultan of Oman, played a significant role in modernizing the country and promoting international cooperation.
  • Omani expatriates have made significant contributions to the development of various countries, including the United Arab Emirates and Saudi Arabia.
  • Omani business leaders, such as Mohammed Al Barwani, have played a key role in promoting economic development and investment in the region.
  • The Omani diaspora has a strong presence in countries such as the United Kingdom, United States, and Canada, with many Omanis contributing to their adopted countries in fields such as medicine, engineering, and education.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Omani people is renowned, with visitors often commenting on the warm and welcoming nature of the locals.
  • The cuisine of Oman is a unique blend of Arabic, Indian, and African flavors, with popular dishes such as shuwa and luqaimat.
  • The scenery of Oman is breathtaking, with its rugged mountains, pristine beaches, and stunning deserts.
  • The Wadi Shab is a popular destination for hiking and swimming, with its crystal-clear waters and stunning rock formations.
  • The Muttrah Souk is a bustling marketplace where visitors can find traditional Omani goods, such as frankincense, perfumes, and handicrafts.
  • The Dhow Cruises in Muscat are a popular way to explore the city's coastline and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hidden Gems

  • The Jebel Shams mountain is a lesser-known destination that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking and camping.
  • The Ras Al Jinz Turtle Reserve is a protected area that is home to a large population of green turtles and is a great spot for nature lovers and conservationists.
  • The Nizwa Fort is a historic fort that dates back to the 17th century and offers a glimpse into Oman's rich history and culture.
  • The Al Ansab Wetlands is a protected area that is home to a wide variety of bird species and is a great spot for birdwatching and nature photography.
