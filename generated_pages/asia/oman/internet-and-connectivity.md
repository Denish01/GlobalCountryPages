[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Oman is generally of good quality, with average download speeds of 20 Mbps, making it suitable for streaming, browsing, and other online activities. However, there is a noticeable urban-rural divide, with faster speeds and more reliable connections available in cities like Muscat, while rural areas may experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Overall, the internet infrastructure in Oman is relatively reliable, with minimal downtime and a wide range of internet service providers to choose from.

In terms of internet quality, Oman has made significant strides in recent years, with the government investing heavily in the development of its telecommunications sector. As a result, the country now boasts a modern and efficient internet infrastructure, with a range of providers offering competitive packages and promotions. However, as with many countries in the region, there are some restrictions on internet access, with certain websites and services blocked or censored, which may be a concern for some travelers.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Oman is a relatively straightforward process, with a range of providers offering tourist-friendly plans and packages. The two main carriers, Omantel and Ooredoo, offer a range of plans, including prepaid and postpaid options, with data allowances starting from 1GB. To purchase a SIM card, you will need to provide a valid passport and visa, as well as a copy of your hotel reservation or proof of address. SIM cards can be purchased at airports, malls, and other retail outlets, as well as from street vendors and small shops.
