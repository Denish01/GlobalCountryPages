Overall Safety Assessment

Oman is generally considered a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other countries in the region. The Omani government has implemented various measures to ensure the safety and security of tourists, including the presence of tourist police in major areas. However, as with any travel destination, it is essential to take common-sense precautions to minimize the risk of encountering problems. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, keep valuables secure, and avoid displaying signs of wealth. Additionally, Oman has strict laws and customs, and visitors should respect these to avoid any issues during their stay.

Overall, Oman offers a welcoming and secure environment for tourists, with a strong emphasis on hospitality and respect for visitors. The country's low crime rate, combined with its rich culture and stunning natural beauty, makes it an attractive destination for travelers. However, it is crucial to remain informed about local conditions and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. By doing so, visitors can focus on exploring Oman's many wonders, from its historic forts and souks to its breathtaking landscapes and beautiful beaches.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and petty theft can occur in crowded areas, such as souks and markets
  • Scams targeting tourists, such as overcharging for goods and services, can occur in some areas
  • Visitors should be cautious when using ATMs and credit cards, as fraud can occur
  • Oman has strict laws regarding drugs, and visitors should avoid carrying any substances that could be considered illegal
  • The country also has strict laws regarding dress code and public behavior, and visitors should respect these to avoid any issues
  • Visitors should be aware of the risk of heat exhaustion and dehydration, particularly when traveling in the desert
  • Some areas, such as the Musandam Peninsula, can be prone to piracy and smuggling, and visitors should exercise caution when traveling by boat

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations are recommended for travel to Oman
  • Water Safety: Tap water is generally not safe to drink, and visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water
  • Medical Facilities: Oman has high-quality medical facilities, particularly in Muscat, but visitors may need to pay upfront for treatment
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available, particularly in urban areas, and many medications are available over-the-counter

Practical Safety Tips

  • Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly, particularly when visiting mosques or other cultural sites
  • It is essential to stay hydrated, particularly when traveling in the desert, and visitors should bring plenty of water and sun protection
  • Visitors should be cautious when crossing the road, as traffic laws can be unpredictable
  • It is recommended to use reputable taxi services, such as those found at airports or hotels, rather than hitchhiking or using unlicensed taxis
  • Visitors should keep valuables secure and be mindful of their surroundings, particularly in crowded areas
  • It is essential to respect the local environment and wildlife, particularly when visiting protected areas

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Solo female travelers should exercise extra caution, particularly at night, and avoid traveling alone in remote areas
  • Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly, particularly when visiting cultural sites or interacting with locals
  • It is recommended to stay in well-lit and populated areas, particularly at night, and to avoid walking alone in isolated areas
  • Women should be prepared for some attention or harassment, particularly in crowded areas, and should be confident and firm in their interactions with locals
