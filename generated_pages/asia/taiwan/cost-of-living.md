# Cost of Living in Taiwan - Prices & Budget Guide

The cost of living in Taiwan can range from affordable to moderate, with daily expenses for travelers and expats varying depending on lifestyle and location. 

[FACTBOX]
Cost Level: Affordable

Currency: TWD

[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] How Expensive Is Taiwan? [/SECTION]
Taiwan is generally considered to be an affordable country, with a lower cost of living compared to many other developed countries in Asia, such as Japan and South Korea. However, prices in Taiwan can vary depending on the location, with cities like Taipei and Kaohsiung tend to be more expensive than smaller towns and rural areas. Compared to its neighbors, Taiwan is more expensive than the Philippines, but less expensive than Japan. Overall, Taiwan offers a great balance of quality of life and affordability, making it an attractive destination for travelers and expats.

In terms of global averages, Taiwan's cost of living is slightly below the midpoint, making it an attractive option for those looking for a balance between affordability and modern amenities. The country's unique blend of traditional and modern culture, combined with its stunning natural beauty and vibrant cities, make it an excellent choice for travelers and expats looking to experience the best of Asia without breaking the bank.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Tips [/SECTION]
- Consider staying in a hostel or guesthouse instead of a hotel to save on accommodation costs.
- Eat at local night markets or street food stalls to try delicious and affordable Taiwanese cuisine.
- Use public transportation, such as buses and metro, to get around instead of taxis.
- Buy a prepaid SIM card or rent a portable Wi-Fi hotspot to stay connected without breaking the bank.
- Take advantage of free admission to many museums and cultural attractions on certain days of the week or month.
- Learn some basic Mandarin phrases to navigate everyday situations and avoid relying on expensive tourist services.
