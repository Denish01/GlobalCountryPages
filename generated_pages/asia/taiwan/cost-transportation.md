[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Taiwan has a well-developed public transportation system, with **buses**, **metros**, and **trains** being the primary modes of transport. The quality of public transit is generally high, with frequent and reliable services connecting major cities and towns. Most people in Taiwan get around using public transportation, with the **Easycard** being a popular prepaid card for convenient travel. The Easycard can be used on buses, metros, and trains, making it easy to navigate the transportation system.

The transportation network in Taiwan is comprehensive, with major cities like Taipei, Kaohsiung, and Taichung having their own metro systems. The **High-Speed Rail** connects major cities, reducing travel time significantly. For shorter distances, **buses** and **taxis** are readily available. With the **YouBike** public bike-sharing system, cycling is also a convenient option for short trips. The bike-sharing system is available in major cities, with rental fees starting from $0.10 USD per 30 minutes.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider purchasing a **monthly transit pass** or using **ride-hailing apps**.
- The **Easycard** is a convenient prepaid card for public transportation, and can also be used at convenience stores and restaurants.
- When taking a **taxi**, make sure to use the meter to avoid overcharging.
- **Negotiate the fare** before starting your journey, especially for longer trips.
- Be aware of **traffic rules** and **road conditions**, especially when driving or cycling.
- Consider using **public bicycles** or **walking** for short distances to reduce costs and stay healthy.
