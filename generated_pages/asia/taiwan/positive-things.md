# Positive Things About Taiwan - Achievements & Culture

Taiwan is a country with a plethora of positive achievements and highlights, ranging from its remarkable scientific advancements to its rich cultural heritage, making it a fascinating destination for travelers and a significant player on the global stage.

[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **National Palace Museum** in Taipei is home to one of the largest collections of Chinese art and artifacts in the world, including the famous **Jadeite Cabbage** sculpture.
- The **Sun Moon Lake** scenic area is a popular destination for its natural beauty and cultural significance, with numerous temples, gardens, and traditional villages to explore.
- The **Taipei 101** skyscraper is an iconic landmark and a testament to Taiwan's engineering prowess, offering stunning views of the city from its observation deck.
- The **Lukang Folk Arts Museum** showcases the traditional crafts and folk arts of Taiwan, with exhibits on woodcarving, pottery, and textiles.
- The **Dadaocheng** neighborhood in Taipei is a historic district known for its well-preserved traditional architecture, street food, and cultural heritage.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Taiwanese diaspora** communities around the world, particularly in the US, have made significant contributions to fields like technology, medicine, and academia, with notable figures like **Jerry Yang**, co-founder of Yahoo!, and **Steve Chen**, co-founder of YouTube.
- **Tsai Ing-wen**, the first female President of Taiwan, has been a vocal advocate for women's rights and LGBTQ+ rights, and has played a key role in promoting Taiwan's international relations and global presence.
- The **Buddhist Tzu Chi Foundation**, founded by **Master Cheng Yen**, is a global humanitarian organization that provides disaster relief, medical care, and education to people in need around the world.
- **Taiwanese values** like hard work, education, and community service are highly regarded and have contributed to the country's social cohesion and economic success.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- Taiwan's **hospitality** is renowned, with visitors often commenting on the warm welcome and friendly service they receive from locals.
- The **food** in Taiwan is incredibly diverse and delicious, with popular dishes like **beef noodle soup**, **stinky tofu**, and **bubble tea**.
- The **scenery** in Taiwan is breathtaking, with stunning natural landscapes like **Taroko National Park**, **Sun Moon Lake**, and the **East Coast National Scenic Area**.
- The **night markets** in Taiwan, like **Shilin Night Market** in Taipei and **Liuhe Night Market** in Kaohsiung, offer a unique and exciting shopping and dining experience.
- The **hot springs** in Taiwan, particularly in the **Beitou** district of Taipei, are a popular destination for relaxation and rejuvenation.
- The **festivals** in Taiwan, like the **Lantern Festival** and the **Dragon Boat Festival**, are colorful and lively, showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Wufeng Lin Family Mansion** in Taichung is a stunning example of traditional Taiwanese architecture, with intricately carved wooden decorations and beautiful gardens.
- The **Yehliu Geopark** on the northern coast of Taiwan is a unique geological formation, featuring stunning rock formations and scenic coastal views.
- The **Taiwan Indigenous Peoples Cultural Park** in Sun Moon Lake is a museum and cultural center that showcases the history and traditions of Taiwan's indigenous tribes.
- The **Daxi Old Street** in Taoyuan is a charming historic district, with traditional shops, restaurants, and tea houses, offering a glimpse into Taiwan's past.
