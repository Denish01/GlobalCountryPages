# Utility & Internet Costs in Taiwan

The cost of utilities and internet in Taiwan can vary depending on factors such as location, usage, and provider, but on average, a monthly utility bill for an 85 sqm apartment can range from $150 to $300 USD.

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Taiwan has a relatively well-developed utility infrastructure, with a high standard of electricity, water, and gas supply. However, the costs of these utilities can fluctuate depending on the season, with higher electricity costs during the summer months due to increased air conditioning usage. The Taiwan government has implemented measures to stabilize utility prices, but prices can still vary depending on the provider and location. Overall, the utility costs in Taiwan are relatively stable, with some seasonal variation.

The quality of utility infrastructure in Taiwan is generally good, with a reliable supply of electricity, water, and gas. However, some areas may experience occasional power outages or water supply disruptions, especially during typhoons or other natural disasters. The government has invested heavily in upgrading the utility infrastructure, including the implementation of smart grids and water management systems.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities in Taiwan, you will typically need to provide identification and proof of address to the utility provider.
- Prepaid electricity plans are available, but postpaid plans are more common and often offer better rates.
- Chunghwa Telecom and Taiwan Mobile are two of the largest internet and phone providers in Taiwan, offering a range of plans and services.
- To save on utility bills, consider using energy-efficient appliances and turning off lights and electronics when not in use.
- Some utility providers offer discounts for long-term contracts or bundle deals, so it's worth shopping around to find the best option for your needs.
