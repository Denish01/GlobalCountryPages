# Taiwan Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Taiwan is generally a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates and a high level of respect for tourists, making it an excellent destination for travelers from around the world.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Taiwan is considered one of the safest countries in Asia, with a strong emphasis on law and order. The country has a low crime rate, and violent crime is rare. Tourists can feel comfortable walking around at night, and the streets are generally well-lit and safe. However, as with any country, there are some areas that are considered safer than others, and tourists should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. The Taiwanese government takes the safety of tourists very seriously, and there are many resources available to help visitors stay safe.

Overall, Taiwan is a great destination for tourists, with a unique blend of traditional and modern culture, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant food scene. With some basic precautions and an understanding of the local culture, tourists can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Taiwan. The country's low crime rate and strong emphasis on safety make it an excellent choice for solo travelers, women, and families. Additionally, the Taiwanese people are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature, making visitors feel at home.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Pickpocketing and petty theft can occur in crowded areas, such as night markets and train stations
- Scams targeting tourists, such as fake taxi drivers and overpriced souvenirs, can occur in popular tourist areas
- Traffic accidents can occur, especially when crossing the road, tourists should be careful and follow local traffic rules
- Natural disasters, such as typhoons and earthquakes, can occur, tourists should check the weather forecast and follow local advice
- Food poisoning can occur, especially when eating from street vendors, tourists should take precautions and choose reputable restaurants
- Credit card scams can occur, tourists should be careful when using ATMs and credit cards

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended for travel to Taiwan
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, bottled water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Taiwan has high-quality medical facilities, with many hospitals and clinics available, especially in urban areas
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, especially in urban areas, and many pharmacies have English-speaking staff

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
- Keep valuables secure, and use hotel safes or lockers when available
- Use reputable taxi companies, or ride-hailing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Follow local traffic rules, and be careful when crossing the road
- Stay hydrated, especially in hot weather, and take regular breaks
- Learn some basic Chinese phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you", to help you communicate with locals
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Taiwan is generally a very safe country for solo and women travelers, with low crime rates and a high level of respect for tourists
- Solo travelers should take normal precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables secure
- Women travelers should be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites
- Solo and women travelers should consider joining a guided tour or using a reputable tour company to help them navigate the country and stay safe.
