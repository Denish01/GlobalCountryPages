# Cocos Islands Visa & Entry Requirements

You do not need a visa to enter the Cocos Islands if you are an Australian citizen or a citizen of a country that does not require a visa to enter Australia, but you must meet certain requirements and follow specific procedures.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Cocos Islands before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
The Cocos Islands have a relatively liberal visa policy, with citizens of many countries able to enter the territory without a visa. As a territory of Australia, the Cocos Islands' visa policy is closely tied to that of Australia, and visitors who are eligible to enter Australia without a visa are also eligible to enter the Cocos Islands. Citizens of over 30 countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, and Canada, can enter the Cocos Islands without a visa for tourism or business purposes.

The Cocos Islands' visa policy is designed to facilitate travel to the territory while maintaining the security and integrity of the Australian border. Visitors who are not eligible for visa-free entry may need to apply for a visa at an Australian embassy or consulate before traveling to the Cocos Islands. It is essential to note that visa requirements can change, and it is crucial to verify the current requirements before planning a trip to the Cocos Islands.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter the Cocos Islands without a visa if they are eligible for visa-free entry. The typical stay for tourists is up to 90 days, and extensions are possible in some cases. Tourists must have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers who are eligible for visa-free entry can enter the Cocos Islands without a visa. However, they may need to provide additional documentation, such as a letter of invitation from a Cocos Islands-based company or organization. Business travelers should also be aware of any specific requirements or regulations related to their business activities.
**Students:** Students who wish to study in the Cocos Islands must apply for a student visa at an Australian embassy or consulate. Students must provide proof of enrollment in a Cocos Islands-based educational institution and meet specific requirements related to their course of study.
**Workers:** Workers who wish to work in the Cocos Islands must apply for a work visa at an Australian embassy or consulate. Workers must have a job offer from a Cocos Islands-based employer and meet specific requirements related to their employment.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The Cocos Islands have a small airport, Cocos (Keeling) Islands Airport, which receives flights from Perth, Australia. Visitors arriving by air will need to clear immigration and customs before proceeding to their destination. The immigration process typically involves presenting a valid passport, completed arrival/departure card, and any required visas or documentation. Visitors may also be asked questions about their purpose of stay, accommodation arrangements, and onward travel plans.

Upon arrival, visitors will also need to declare any goods they are bringing with them, including food, plant, and animal products. The Cocos Islands have strict customs regulations to protect the territory's unique environment and ecosystem. Visitors should be aware of these regulations and declare any prohibited or restricted items to avoid fines or penalties.
