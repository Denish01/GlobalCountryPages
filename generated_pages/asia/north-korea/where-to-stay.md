# Where to Stay in North Korea — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting North Korea, the best places to stay are in the capital city of Pyongyang, with top areas including the Central District, Potonggang District, and Rangnang District, offering a range of accommodations and experiences for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas, here's a breakdown of what to expect:
**Central District** (Pyongyang)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: The Central District is the heart of Pyongyang, with a vibrant atmosphere and stunning views of the city's iconic landmarks, such as the Juche Tower and the Pyongyang Grand Theatre. This area is known for its impressive architecture and lively streets.
Price Range: $100-500/night
Pros: Close proximity to major attractions, high-end amenities, and excellent dining options. The area is also well-patrolled, ensuring a safe and secure environment for visitors.
Cons: Limited budget-friendly options, and some areas may be restricted to foreigners.

**Potonggang District** (Pyongyang)
Best For: Family / Mid-range
Vibe: The Potonggang District is a charming area with a mix of old and new Pyongyang, offering a glimpse into the city's history and culture. This area is known for its beautiful parks and gardens, making it an ideal spot for families.
Price Range: $50-200/night
Pros: Affordable accommodations, scenic views, and easy access to public transportation. The area is also home to several local markets and restaurants, offering a taste of authentic North Korean cuisine.
Cons: Limited luxury options, and some areas may be under construction.

**Rangnang District** (Pyongyang)
Best For: Backpacker / Budget
Vibe: The Rangnang District is a lively and eclectic area, with a mix of old and new buildings, and a vibrant atmosphere. This area is known for its affordable accommodations and lively nightlife, making it a great spot for backpackers.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Budget-friendly options, close proximity to major attractions, and a lively nightlife. The area is also home to several local eateries and bars, offering a taste of North Korean street food.
Cons: Limited amenities, and some areas may be noisy.

**Hyongjesan District** (Pyongyang)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: The Hyongjesan District is a modern and upscale area, with sleek skyscrapers and a sophisticated atmosphere. This area is known for its high-end amenities and excellent dining options, making it an ideal spot for business travelers.
Price Range: $150-600/night
Pros: High-end amenities, excellent dining options, and close proximity to major business centers. The area is also well-patrolled, ensuring a safe and secure environment for visitors.
Cons: Limited budget-friendly options, and some areas may be restricted to foreigners.

**Kaeson District** (Pyongyang)
Best For: Family / Mid-range
Vibe: The Kaeson District is a charming area with a mix of old and new Pyongyang, offering a glimpse into the city's history and culture. This area is known for its beautiful parks and gardens, making it an ideal spot for families.
Price Range: $40-150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodations, scenic views, and easy access to public transportation. The area is also home to several local markets and restaurants, offering a taste of authentic North Korean cuisine.
Cons: Limited luxury options, and some areas may be under construction.
