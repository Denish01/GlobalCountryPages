[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Mansudae Grand Monument**, a large bronze statue of former leaders Kim Il-sung and Kim Jong-il, is a notable example of North Korean art and architecture.
- The **Kaesong Folk Custom Museum** showcases the country's rich cultural heritage, with exhibits on traditional music, dance, and crafts.
- The **Pyongyang Folklore Park** is a popular tourist destination, featuring traditional music and dance performances, as well as exhibits on Korean folklore.
- The **Myohyangsan Mountain** is a sacred site in North Korean culture, with several temples and shrines dedicated to the worship of the mountain spirits.
- The **Koguryo Tombs**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a collection of ancient tombs that date back to the Koguryo Kingdom, which existed from 37 BC to 668 AD.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Kim Il-sung**, the founder of North Korea, played a significant role in the country's development and is still revered as a national hero.
- The **North Korean diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global community, with many North Koreans living and working abroad, particularly in **China**, **Russia**, and **South Korea**.
- **Pak Doo-ik**, a North Korean football player, is a notable example of the country's athletic talent, having played for several international teams, including the **North Korean national team**.
- The **Juche** ideology, developed by Kim Il-sung, emphasizes the importance of self-reliance and independence, and has had a significant impact on North Korean society and politics.
