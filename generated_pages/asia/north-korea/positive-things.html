Cultural Treasures

  • The Mansudae Grand Monument, a large bronze statue of former leaders Kim Il-sung and Kim Jong-il, is a notable example of North Korean art and architecture.
  • The Kaesong Folk Custom Museum showcases the country's rich cultural heritage, with exhibits on traditional music, dance, and crafts.
  • The Pyongyang Folklore Park is a popular tourist destination, featuring traditional music and dance performances, as well as exhibits on Korean folklore.
  • The Myohyangsan Mountain is a sacred site in North Korean culture, with several temples and shrines dedicated to the worship of the mountain spirits.
  • The Koguryo Tombs, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a collection of ancient tombs that date back to the Koguryo Kingdom, which existed from 37 BC to 668 AD.

People & Global Impact

  • Kim Il-sung, the founder of North Korea, played a significant role in the country's development and is still revered as a national hero.
  • The North Korean diaspora has made significant contributions to the global community, with many North Koreans living and working abroad, particularly in China, Russia, and South Korea.
  • Pak Doo-ik, a North Korean football player, is a notable example of the country's athletic talent, having played for several international teams, including the North Korean national team.
  • The Juche ideology, developed by Kim Il-sung, emphasizes the importance of self-reliance and independence, and has had a significant impact on North Korean society and politics.

What Visitors Love

  • The Pyongyang Metro is a popular tourist attraction, known for its ornate decorations and efficient service.
  • The Korean Central Zoo is home to a wide range of animals, including tigers, bears, and monkeys, and is a popular destination for families.
  • The Myohyangsan Mountain offers stunning scenery and hiking trails, and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • The Pyongyang Restaurant serves traditional North Korean cuisine, including naengmyeon (cold noodles) and bibimbap (mixed rice bowl).
  • The Kaeson Youth Park is a popular destination for families, with several amusement park rides and attractions.
  • The Pyongyang Marathon is an annual event that attracts runners from around the world, and offers a unique opportunity to experience North Korean culture.

Hidden Gems

  • The Ryongwang Pavilion, located in the heart of Pyongyang, is a beautiful example of traditional North Korean architecture, with stunning views of the city.
  • The Kumgangsan Mountain is a lesser-known destination, but offers stunning scenery and hiking trails, and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • The Changgwangsan Hotel is a historic hotel that dates back to the 1950s, and offers a unique glimpse into North Korea's past.
  • The Pyongyang University of Science and Technology is a leading institution of higher learning in North Korea, and offers a range of academic programs, including engineering and computer science.
