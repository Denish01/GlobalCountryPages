# Moving to North Korea — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to North Korea, you will need to carefully consider the country's unique restrictions and requirements, as well as the challenges that come with living in a country with limited international connections and a tightly controlled society.

[SECTION] Why People Move to North Korea [/SECTION]
North Korea, also known as the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, is a country that is not typically considered a popular expat destination due to its restrictive policies and limited economic opportunities. However, some people may still be drawn to the country's unique culture and history, as well as its natural beauty. Expats who do move to North Korea are often diplomats, aid workers, or journalists, and they typically live in the capital city of Pyongyang. The quality of life in North Korea can be challenging, with limited access to basic necessities like food, water, and healthcare, as well as restricted freedom of movement and communication.

Despite these challenges, some expats may find that the experience of living in North Korea is rewarding and eye-opening, providing a unique perspective on a country that is often misunderstood by the outside world. However, it is essential to be aware of the potential risks and difficulties involved in living in a country with such a restrictive and controlled environment. The demographics of expats in North Korea are typically limited to those who are affiliated with the government, international organizations, or NGOs, and they often have limited interaction with the local population.

The overall quality of life in North Korea is generally considered to be low, with limited access to basic necessities and a restricted range of activities and entertainment options. However, for those who are willing to adapt to the challenges and restrictions of living in North Korea, the experience can be a unique and rewarding one.
