Visa Policy Overview

North Korea has a strict visa policy, with only a few countries granted visa-free access. The country's visa stance is tightly controlled, and all visitors must adhere to the rules and regulations set by the government. Currently, only a handful of countries, including China and a few other neighboring nations, are eligible for visa-free entry. All other nationalities require a visa to enter the country, which must be obtained prior to arrival.

The visa application process for North Korea can be complex and time-consuming, and it is essential to apply well in advance of the intended travel date. The government has strict requirements and regulations in place for all visitors, including tourists, business travelers, students, and workers. It is crucial to understand the specific requirements for your nationality and purpose of visit to ensure a smooth entry into the country.

By Visitor Type

Tourists: Tourists must apply for a tourist visa, which is typically valid for 30 days. The visa application process requires a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and payment of the relevant fees. Tourists must also be part of an organized tour group, which can be arranged through a licensed tour operator. Extensions of stay are possible, but must be approved by the relevant authorities.

Business Travelers: Business travelers must apply for a business visa, which requires an invitation letter from a North Korean company or organization. The visa application process also requires a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and payment of the relevant fees. Business travelers must also register with the relevant authorities and obtain any necessary permits or licenses.

Students: Students must apply for a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment at a North Korean university or institution. The visa application process also requires a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and payment of the relevant fees. Students must also register with the relevant authorities and obtain any necessary permits or licenses.

Workers: Workers must apply for a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a North Korean employer. The visa application process also requires a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and payment of the relevant fees. Workers must also register with the relevant authorities and obtain any necessary permits or licenses.

Entry Points & Procedures

The main entry point for North Korea is Pyongyang International Airport, which receives flights from a limited number of countries, including China and Russia. Land borders are also open, but crossing by land can be complex and requires special permission. Upon arrival, visitors must go through immigration and customs, where they will be required to present their visa, passport, and other relevant documents. It is essential to have all necessary documents in order to avoid any issues or delays. Visitors will also be required to declare any electronic devices, including cameras and phones, and may be subject to searches or inspections.

Upon arrival, visitors will be met by their tour guide or representative, who will assist with the immigration and customs process. It is essential to stay with the tour group at all times and follow the instructions of the guide to avoid any issues or problems. Visitors who are not part of an organized tour group may be subject to additional requirements or restrictions.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Not applying for a visa in advance of travel
  • Not having all necessary documents, including a valid passport and proof of onward travel
  • Not declaring electronic devices or other restricted items
  • Not staying with the tour group or following the instructions of the guide
  • Not registering with the relevant authorities or obtaining necessary permits or licenses
  • Not respecting local customs and regulations, including dress codes and photography restrictions
