# Turkmenistan Infrastructure — Roads, Airports, Electricity & Water

The infrastructure in Turkmenistan is characterized by significant investments in recent years, particularly in the energy and transportation sectors, although the country still faces challenges in terms of rural-urban disparities and overall development level.

Rail Network: 3,440 km
Major Airports: 5
Major Ports: 1 major port on the Caspian Sea

[SECTION] Infrastructure Overview [/SECTION]
Turkmenistan has been focusing on developing its infrastructure to support its growing economy, which is largely driven by its vast natural gas reserves. The government has invested heavily in constructing modern roads, railways, airports, and seaports to improve connectivity and facilitate trade. However, despite these efforts, there is still a notable gap between urban and rural areas in terms of infrastructure development. While cities like Ashgabat boast modern amenities and well-maintained roads, rural areas often lack access to basic services like reliable electricity and clean water. Recent investments have also been aimed at diversifying the economy and reducing dependence on hydrocarbons, which could lead to more balanced infrastructure development across the country.

The country's infrastructure development has been supported by both domestic and international funding. Turkmenistan has been actively engaging with international organizations and neighboring countries to secure investments and expertise for its large-scale infrastructure projects. This includes collaborations in the energy sector, transportation networks, and telecommunications. However, the pace and effectiveness of these projects can be influenced by various factors, including geopolitical relationships and economic conditions.

Turkmenistan's unique geopolitical position, sandwiched between major powers and rich in natural resources, makes its infrastructure development strategic not only for internal growth but also for regional connectivity. The country is part of several regional initiatives aimed at enhancing economic integration and cooperation, which could further boost its infrastructure development in the coming years.

[SECTION] Energy & Electricity [/SECTION]
- The primary source of **power generation** is natural gas, which is abundant in Turkmenistan.
- The country has made significant strides in increasing its **electricity access**, with 100% of the population now having access to electricity.
- There have been efforts to improve the **reliability** of the power supply, including the construction of new power plants and the renovation of existing infrastructure.
- **Renewable energy** sources, such as solar and wind power, are being explored as part of the country's strategy to diversify its energy sector.
- Despite advancements, **outages** can still occur, particularly in rural areas where the infrastructure may not be as robust.

[SECTION] Water & Sanitation [/SECTION]
- **Tap water safety** is a concern in some areas, particularly in rural regions where water treatment facilities may not be as advanced.
- The coverage of **sanitation** facilities is relatively high in urban areas but can be limited in rural settings.
- **Water access** in rural areas is often dependent on wells or other traditional sources, which may not always be reliable or safe.
- The government has been investing in **water infrastructure** projects, including the construction of new water treatment plants and the expansion of piped water systems.
- **Conservation efforts** are also being promoted, given the country's arid climate and the importance of preserving water resources.

[SECTION] Telecommunications [/SECTION]
- **Mobile coverage** is widespread, with most of the population having access to mobile phone services.
- **Fiber and broadband** services are available in major cities, offering high-speed internet access to residents and businesses.
- The **4G/5G rollout** is underway, with several telecom operators investing in the upgrade of their networks to provide faster and more reliable internet services.
- **Postal services** are provided by the state-owned postal company, which offers a range of services including mail delivery and parcel shipping.
- **Internet penetration** is growing, although it still lags behind some of its regional peers, with about 21.3% of the population having internet access.

[SECTION] Development Projects [/SECTION]
- One of the major ongoing projects is the **construction of the Turkmenistan-Afghanistan-Tajikistan (TAT) railway**, which aims to enhance regional connectivity and trade.
- The **development of the Caspian Sea port** in Turkmenbashi is another significant project, expected to increase the country's maritime trade capabilities.
- **Renewable energy projects**, including solar and wind farms, are being developed with the support of international organizations and investors.
- The **upgrade of the national road network** continues, with a focus on improving the quality and safety of roads, particularly in rural areas.
- **Funding** for these projects comes from a combination of domestic sources and international loans and grants, highlighting the collaborative approach to infrastructure development in Turkmenistan.
