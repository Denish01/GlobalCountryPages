# Where to Stay in Turkmenistan — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When visiting Turkmenistan, the best places to stay are in the capital city of Ashgabat, with its modern amenities and access to major attractions, as well as in the historic city of Mary, which offers a glimpse into the country's rich cultural heritage.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Turkmenistan, the following neighborhoods are recommended:
**Ashgabat City Center** (Ashgabat)
Best For: Luxury/Business
Vibe: The city center is known for its modern architecture, marble-clad buildings, and well-manicured parks, offering a unique blend of traditional and contemporary styles. The area is bustling with activity, from government offices to shopping centers and restaurants.
Price Range: $100-500/night
Pros: Convenient location, access to major attractions, modern amenities
Cons: Traffic can be heavy, and the area can feel overly formal

**Berzengi** (Ashgabat)
Best For: Family/Luxury
Vibe: This upscale neighborhood is located just outside the city center and offers a more relaxed atmosphere, with tree-lined streets and modern villas. The area is popular with expats and wealthy locals, and features several parks and shopping centers.
Price Range: $150-600/night
Pros: Quiet and safe, modern amenities, convenient location
Cons: Limited dining options, can feel isolated from the city center

**Mary City Center** (Mary)
Best For: Budget/Backpacker
Vibe: The city center of Mary is a historic area with a rich cultural heritage, featuring ancient ruins, museums, and traditional bazaars. The area is bustling with activity, from locals selling handicrafts to tourists exploring the city's attractions.
Price Range: $20-100/night
Pros: Affordable prices, rich cultural heritage, friendly locals
Cons: Limited modern amenities, can feel crowded and noisy

**Avaza** (Turkmenbashi)
Best For: Luxury/Relaxation
Vibe: This coastal resort town is located on the Caspian Sea and offers a relaxing atmosphere, with modern hotels, beaches, and water sports. The area is popular with wealthy locals and tourists looking to escape the city.
Price Range: $200-1000/night
Pros: Beautiful beaches, modern amenities, relaxing atmosphere
Cons: Limited cultural attractions, can feel isolated from the rest of the country

**Kopet Dag** (Ashgabat)
Best For: Nature/Luxury
Vibe: This mountainous region is located just outside Ashgabat and offers a scenic atmosphere, with hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views. The area is popular with nature lovers and those looking to escape the city.
Price Range: $100-500/night
Pros: Scenic views, outdoor activities, convenient location
Cons: Limited amenities, can be crowded on weekends

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season (June to September)
- Use online booking platforms, such as Booking.com or Expedia, to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with prices increasing during peak season and decreasing during the off-season
- Read reviews and check the location before booking, to ensure a safe and convenient stay
- Use a reputable travel agency or tour operator to book accommodations and tours
- Be prepared for limited options and availability, especially outside of Ashgabat

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid traveling to areas near the border with Afghanistan, due to security concerns
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and markets
- Keep valuables secure, and use a hotel safe or a money belt
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or debit cards instead
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
- Use a reputable taxi service or ride-sharing app, to ensure a safe and convenient ride
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting cultural attractions or interacting with locals
