# Turkmenistan Internet & SIM Cards — Connectivity Guide

The internet in Turkmenistan is relatively limited and slow, with an average speed of 5 Mbps, but it is possible to get a SIM card and access mobile data and WiFi in major cities like Ashgabat.

5G Available: No

[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Turkmenistan is not as developed as in other countries, with a significant urban vs rural divide in terms of internet quality and availability. In major cities like Ashgabat, the capital, internet speed and reliability are relatively better, with some areas having **fiber optic** connections. However, in rural areas, internet access is often limited, and speeds can be very slow. The internet infrastructure in Turkmenistan is mostly state-controlled, which can lead to **censorship** and **restrictions** on online content.

The reliability of the internet in Turkmenistan can be a concern, with frequent **outages** and **disruptions** reported, especially during peak hours or special events. The government has been working to improve the internet infrastructure, but progress has been slow. Despite these challenges, many locals and tourists rely on the internet for communication, entertainment, and information, and there are ways to access **mobile data** and **WiFi** in major cities.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
To get a SIM card in Turkmenistan, tourists can visit the offices of the main carriers, such as TM Cell, MTS, or Altyn Asyr, and provide their **passport** and **registration** documents. The process can take some time, but it is possible to get a SIM card with a **prepaid plan** that includes data, voice, and text services. It is recommended to purchase a SIM card from a authorized dealer or the carrier's office to avoid any issues with **activation** or **registration**.

- Cafe WiFi culture is not very common in Turkmenistan, but some cafes and restaurants in major cities may offer **free WiFi** to their customers.
- Coworking spaces are limited in Turkmenistan, but some **business centers** and **incubators** may offer **WiFi** and other amenities to remote workers and entrepreneurs.
- Public WiFi is not widely available in Turkmenistan, but some **public libraries** and **internet cafes** may offer **WiFi** access to the public.
- Some **shopping malls** and **airports** may also offer **free WiFi** to visitors and travelers.
