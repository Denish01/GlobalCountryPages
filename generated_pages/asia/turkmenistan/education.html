Education System Overview

The education system in Turkmenistan is divided into three main stages: primary education, which lasts for six years, secondary education, which lasts for six years, and tertiary education, which typically lasts for four to five years. The system is highly centralized, with the Ministry of Education responsible for setting the curriculum, textbooks, and educational standards. The quality of education in Turkmenistan is considered to be relatively high, with a strong emphasis on mathematics, science, and language. However, the system has been criticized for being overly rigid and lacking in creativity. Public education is free and compulsory for all citizens, while private education is limited and expensive.

The education system in Turkmenistan has undergone significant reforms in recent years, with a focus on modernizing the curriculum and improving the quality of teaching. However, the system still faces significant challenges, including a lack of resources and infrastructure, particularly in rural areas. Despite these challenges, the education system in Turkmenistan has made significant progress in recent years, with a high literacy rate and a growing number of students pursuing higher education.

The government of Turkmenistan has also placed a strong emphasis on vocational training and technical education, with a focus on developing the country's economy and industry. This has led to the establishment of a number of vocational schools and technical colleges, which offer training in areas such as engineering, agriculture, and tourism.

International Schools

  • The International School of Ashgabat offers a British-style curriculum and is accredited by the Council of International Schools.
  • The Turkmen-Turkish School offers a Turkish-style curriculum and is popular with Turkmen and Turkish students.
  • The Ashgabat International School offers an American-style curriculum and is accredited by the Western Association of Schools and Colleges.
  • International schools in Turkmenistan are limited and expensive, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $10,000 per year.
  • International schools are mainly located in the capital city, Ashgabat, and offer a range of curricula, including IB, British, and American.

Studying as a Foreigner

  • To study in Turkmenistan, international students must obtain a student visa, which can be obtained through the Turkmen embassy or consulate in their home country.
  • International students must also demonstrate proficiency in Turkmen language, although some universities offer English-language programs.
  • Scholarships are available for international students, although they are competitive and limited.
  • The cost of living in Turkmenistan is relatively low, with rent, food, and transportation costs comparable to those in other Central Asian countries.
  • International students must also register with the local authorities and obtain a residence permit.

Education Quality & Challenges

  • The education system in Turkmenistan is facing challenges in terms of quality, with a lack of resources and infrastructure, particularly in rural areas.
  • The system is also overly centralized, with a lack of autonomy for universities and schools.
  • Despite these challenges, the education system in Turkmenistan has made significant progress in recent years, with a high literacy rate and a growing number of students pursuing higher education.
  • The government has also implemented reforms aimed at improving the quality of education, including the introduction of new curricula and teaching methods.
  • However, the system still lacks in terms of international recognition, with limited opportunities for international collaboration and exchange programs.
