# Getting Around Tajikistan — Transport & Travel Guide

To get around in Tajikistan, you can use a combination of domestic flights, trains, buses, and taxis, as well as rent a car or hire a driver, depending on your budget and preferred mode of transportation.

[SECTION] Getting There [/SECTION]
Tajikistan has several international airports, with Dushanbe International Airport (DYU) being the main gateway to the country. There are direct flights to Dushanbe from major cities such as Moscow, Dubai, and Istanbul. You can also enter Tajikistan overland from neighboring countries, including China, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Afghanistan. The most popular overland entry points are the border crossings with Kyrgyzstan and Uzbekistan, which are relatively easy to navigate.

If you plan to enter Tajikistan by land, it's essential to research the current border crossing requirements and regulations, as they can change frequently. Additionally, consider hiring a guide or joining a tour group to help navigate the border crossing process. For those flying into Dushanbe, there are several transportation options available from the airport, including taxis, buses, and rental cars. It's recommended to book your transportation in advance to avoid any issues or delays.

[SECTION] Domestic Flights [/SECTION]
Tajikistan has a limited domestic air network, with flights operated by **Tajik Air** and **Somoni Air**. The main domestic routes connect Dushanbe to regional cities such as Khorugh, Kulyab, and Khujand. Booking domestic flights can be challenging, as online booking systems are not always available, and it's recommended to book through a local travel agency or at the airport. Approximate costs for domestic flights range from $50 to $200, depending on the route and airline.

[SECTION] Trains & Rail [/SECTION]
Tajikistan's rail network is limited, with only a few routes connecting the capital Dushanbe to regional cities. The **Tajik Railway** operates trains on these routes, but the service is not frequent, and the quality of the trains is relatively poor. There are no high-speed rail options available in Tajikistan. Booking train tickets can be done at the train station or through a local travel agency. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.

[SECTION] Buses & Coaches [/SECTION]
Intercity buses and coaches are a popular mode of transportation in Tajikistan, with several companies operating on various routes. The **KamAZ** bus company is one of the most reliable options, with daily departures from Dushanbe to regional cities. The quality of the buses varies, but most are relatively comfortable and affordable, with costs ranging from $5 to $20, depending on the route and company.

[SECTION] Driving [/SECTION]
- To drive in Tajikistan, you'll need to obtain a local driver's license, as international licenses are not recognized.
- Road conditions in Tajikistan are generally poor, with many unpaved roads and limited infrastructure.
- Fuel costs are relatively high, with gasoline prices ranging from $1 to $2 per liter.
- Car rental companies are limited, and it's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.
- Hazards on the road include potholes, landslides, and aggressive drivers, so it's essential to exercise caution and drive defensively.
- Consider hiring a driver or joining a guided tour to avoid the challenges of driving in Tajikistan.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Be prepared for delays and cancellations, especially when traveling by bus or train.
- Always negotiate the price before hiring a taxi or ride-hailing service.
- Download local ride-hailing apps, such as Taxi Maxim and Yandex.Taxi, to book transportation in advance.
- Be aware of scams, such as overcharging or fake taxi services, and always use reputable companies.
- Learn some basic **Tajik** phrases, such as "hello" (salom) and "thank you" (tashakur), to help navigate transportation situations.
- Consider purchasing a local SIM card or portable Wi-Fi hotspot to stay connected and access transportation information on the go.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when interacting with drivers or transportation providers.
