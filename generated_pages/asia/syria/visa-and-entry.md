# Syria Visa & Entry Requirements

To enter Syria, most foreign nationals need a visa, which can be obtained through various types, including tourist, business, and student visas, and the requirements vary depending on the nationality and purpose of the visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Syria before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Syria has a moderate to strict visa policy, with only a few countries having visa-free access. Citizens of countries such as Egypt, Jordan, and Lebanon can enter Syria without a visa, while others need to obtain a visa in advance or on arrival. The Syrian government has been tightening its visa regulations in recent years, making it more challenging for tourists and business travelers to enter the country.

The visa policy in Syria is designed to control the flow of foreigners into the country, and the requirements can vary depending on the purpose of the visit. Tourists, business travelers, students, and workers all have different requirements, and the application process can be complex. It is essential to understand the visa requirements and regulations before planning a trip to Syria.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can obtain a visa on arrival at Damascus International Airport or through the Syrian embassy or consulate in their home country. The typical stay for tourists is 15 days, and extensions are possible but require approval from the Syrian authorities. Tourists must provide proof of onward travel and sufficient funds for their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers need to obtain a visa in advance through the Syrian embassy or consulate. They must provide an invitation letter from a Syrian company, proof of business activities, and other relevant documents. Business travelers can stay in Syria for up to 30 days, and extensions are possible but require approval from the Syrian authorities.
**Students:** Students need to obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment in a Syrian university, a valid passport, and other relevant documents. Students can stay in Syria for the duration of their studies, and their visa can be extended as needed.
**Workers:** Workers need to obtain a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a Syrian employer. Workers must provide proof of employment, a valid passport, and other relevant documents. Workers can stay in Syria for the duration of their employment contract, and their visa can be extended as needed.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Syria are Damascus International Airport, Aleppo International Airport, and the land borders with neighboring countries such as Jordan, Lebanon, and Turkey. Upon arrival, travelers must go through immigration and customs procedures, which can be time-consuming. Travelers must provide their passport, visa (if required), and other relevant documents to the immigration officer. The officer will review the documents and ask questions about the purpose of the visit, accommodation, and onward travel plans.

Travelers can also enter Syria through land borders, but the procedures can be more complex. Travelers must provide their passport, visa (if required), and other relevant documents to the border authorities. The authorities will review the documents and ask questions about the purpose of the visit, accommodation, and onward travel plans. It is essential to ensure that all documents are in order and that the traveler has the necessary visas and permits before attempting to enter Syria through a land border.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not checking the visa requirements before traveling to Syria
- Not providing all the necessary documents for the visa application
- Not having a valid passport with at least six months' validity
- Not having proof of onward travel
- Not having sufficient funds for the stay
- Not registering with the Syrian authorities within the required timeframe after arrival
