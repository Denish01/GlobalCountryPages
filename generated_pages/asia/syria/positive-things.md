[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The ancient city of **Palmyra** is a UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its impressive ruins and historical significance.
- The **Umayyad Mosque** in Damascus is one of the largest and most beautiful mosques in the world, featuring intricate stone carvings and mosaics.
- The **Khan As'ad Pasha** in Damascus is a well-preserved example of traditional Syrian architecture, dating back to the 16th century.
- The **Syrian National Museum** in Damascus showcases the country's rich cultural heritage, with a collection of artifacts and exhibits spanning thousands of years.
- The **Aleppo Citadel** is a historic fortress and UNESCO World Heritage site, offering stunning views of the surrounding city.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Omar Sharif**, a renowned Syrian-Egyptian actor, is known for his iconic roles in films like **Lawrence of Arabia** and **Doctor Zhivago**.
- The **Syrian diaspora** has made significant contributions to fields like medicine, engineering, and finance, with many successful entrepreneurs and professionals around the world.
- **Ribal Al-Assad**, a Syrian businessman and philanthropist, has worked to promote cultural exchange and understanding between Syria and other countries.
- **Maya Zbib**, a Syrian-Canadian writer and activist, has written extensively on issues like women's rights and social justice in the Middle East.

- The **Lake Assad** is a large artificial lake, created by the construction of the **Tishrin Dam**, and offers opportunities for boating and fishing.
