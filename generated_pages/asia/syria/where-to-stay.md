# Where to Stay in Syria — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

When traveling to Syria, the best areas to stay are in the capital city of Damascus and other major cities like Aleppo and Latakia, which offer a range of accommodations and amenities for different types of travelers.

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas/neighborhoods in Syria, here's what you can expect:
**Old City** (Damascus)
Best For: history buffs, cultural enthusiasts
Vibe: The Old City is a historic and culturally rich area with narrow streets, ancient mosques, and traditional markets, offering a unique and immersive experience for visitors.
Price Range: $20-50/night
Pros: proximity to historic landmarks, authentic local experience, affordable prices
Cons: narrow streets can be confusing, limited modern amenities

**Ummayad Square** (Damascus)
Best For: luxury travelers, business travelers
Vibe: Ummayad Square is a modern and upscale area with high-end hotels, restaurants, and shops, offering a comfortable and convenient stay for visitors.
Price Range: $100-200/night
Pros: modern amenities, easy access to transportation, high-end dining options
Cons: expensive prices, limited cultural experience

**Aziziyah** (Aleppo)
Best For: families, mid-range travelers
Vibe: Aziziyah is a charming and historic neighborhood with a mix of old and new buildings, offering a warm and welcoming atmosphere for visitors.
Price Range: $30-70/night
Pros: historic architecture, family-friendly atmosphere, affordable prices
Cons: limited modern amenities, some areas are still recovering from conflict

**Qalamun** (Damascus)
Best For: nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts
Vibe: Qalamun is a scenic and mountainous area with stunning views, hiking trails, and natural beauty, offering a peaceful and relaxing escape from the city.
Price Range: $40-80/night
Pros: natural beauty, outdoor activities, peaceful atmosphere
Cons: limited amenities, remote location

**Latakia City** (Latakia)
Best For: beachgoers, relaxation seekers
Vibe: Latakia City is a coastal city with beautiful beaches, a vibrant waterfront, and a relaxed atmosphere, offering a perfect spot for relaxation and leisure.
Price Range: $50-100/night
Pros: beautiful beaches, waterfront promenade, relaxed atmosphere
Cons: limited cultural experience, some areas are still developing

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to September)
- Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, and Airbnb to compare prices and find deals
- Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings
- Be flexible with your travel dates and accommodation options to get the best value
- Research and read reviews to ensure the accommodation meets your needs and expectations
- Look for accommodations with good security and safety features, especially in areas with limited tourist infrastructure
- Check the cancellation policies and fees before booking

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Avoid areas near the Turkish and Iraqi borders due to ongoing conflict and instability
- Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on local news and developments
- Stay in well-lit and populated areas, especially at night
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-hailing apps to get around
- Keep your valuables secure and be mindful of pickpocketing and petty theft
- Consider staying in areas with good access to transportation, such as airports, bus stations, and taxi hubs
