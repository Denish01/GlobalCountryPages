[SECTION] What Is Brunei? [/SECTION]
Brunei is a **sovereign** country located on the island of Borneo in Southeast Asia. It is a small, oil-rich nation surrounded by the Malaysian state of Sarawak to the east, south, and west, and the South China Sea to the north. Brunei is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning mosques, and pristine rainforests. The country has a unique blend of traditional and modern elements, with a strong emphasis on **Islam** and the Malay culture.

Geographically, Brunei is a relatively small country, with a total area of approximately 5,765 square kilometers. The country's terrain is characterized by dense rainforests, mangrove swamps, and rugged hills. The climate is tropical, with high temperatures and high humidity levels throughout the year. Brunei's strategic location on the South China Sea has made it an important center for trade and commerce in the region.

Brunei gained its independence from the United Kingdom in 1984 and has since maintained a **monarchical** system of government. The country is ruled by the Sultan, who serves as the head of state and head of government. Brunei is a member of various international organizations, including the United Nations, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), and the Commonwealth of Nations.

1. 1888 - Brunei becomes a British protectorate, with the United Kingdom responsible for the country's foreign affairs.
2. 1906 - The Anglo-Brunei Treaty is signed, establishing Brunei as a British protectorate and paving the way for British involvement in the country's internal affairs.
3. 1941 - Brunei is occupied by Japan during World War II, and the country suffers significant damage and loss of life.
4. 1959 - The Brunei Constitution is introduced, establishing the country as a constitutional monarchy.
5. 1984 - Brunei gains its independence from the United Kingdom, with Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah becoming the country's first head of state.
6. 1992 - Brunei introduces its national philosophy, **Melayu Islam Beraja** (Malay Islamic Monarchy), which emphasizes the importance of Malay culture, Islam, and the monarchy.
7. 2014 - Brunei introduces **Sharia law**, making it one of the few countries in the world to adopt Islamic law as its official legal system.

- Brunei is home to the **Ulu Temburong National Park**, one of the most pristine and untouched rainforests in Borneo.
- The country has a unique **cultural heritage**, with a blend of Malay, Islamic, and indigenous influences.
- Brunei is one of the **richest countries in the world**, with a high standard of living and a strong economy based on oil and gas production.
- The country is home to the **Sultan Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque**, one of the most beautiful and iconic mosques in the world.
