Visa Policy Overview

Brunei has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Citizens of around 60 countries can enter Brunei visa-free, while others may need to obtain a visa on arrival or through the embassy. The country aims to balance its tourism and economic growth with security and immigration control. As a result, visa requirements and regulations are subject to change, and it is essential to check with the official authorities for the latest information.

Brunei's visa policy is designed to facilitate travel for tourists, business travelers, and other visitors, while ensuring the country's security and immigration rules are respected. The government has introduced various types of visas, including tourist visas, business visas, and student visas, each with its own set of requirements and regulations. With its strategic location in Southeast Asia, Brunei is an attractive destination for travelers and businesspeople alike, and its visa policy reflects this.

Tourists: Tourists can typically stay in Brunei for up to 90 days, depending on their nationality. They may need to show proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and a valid passport. Visa extensions are possible, but subject to certain conditions. Business Travelers: Business travelers may need to provide an invitation letter from a Brunei-based company, as well as proof of their business activities and intentions. They may be eligible for a multiple-entry visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. Students: Students must provide proof of enrollment in a Brunei-based educational institution, as well as a valid passport and sufficient funds. They may be eligible for a student visa, which is typically valid for the duration of their studies. Workers: Workers must have a valid work permit and sponsorship from a Brunei-based employer. They may need to undergo medical tests and provide proof of their qualifications and experience.

The main entry points to Brunei are Brunei International Airport (BWN) and the land borders with Malaysia. Upon arrival, visitors will need to proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and answer questions about their stay. Visitors may also be asked to provide biometric data, such as fingerprints. The immigration process is generally efficient, but it is essential to ensure that all required documents are in order to avoid any delays or issues.
Brunei has a relatively straightforward entry procedure, with most visitors able to clear immigration quickly. However, it is essential to be aware of the required documents and procedures to avoid any issues. Visitors may also be subject to customs inspections, and it is essential to declare any restricted or prohibited items.
- Not checking the visa requirements for their nationality before traveling
- Not having a valid passport with sufficient validity
- Not providing proof of onward travel or sufficient funds
- Not declaring restricted or prohibited items at customs
- Not having the required documents for their visitor type (e.g., invitation letter for business travelers)
- Not registering with the local authorities if staying in Brunei for an extended period
