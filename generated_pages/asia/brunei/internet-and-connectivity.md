[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Brunei is considered to be of high quality, with average speeds of 25 Mbps download, making it suitable for streaming, online gaming, and other data-intensive activities. The urban areas, such as the capital city Bandar Seri Begawan, have excellent internet coverage, with most homes and businesses having access to high-speed internet. However, the rural areas may experience slower speeds and more limited coverage, although the government has been working to improve the infrastructure in these areas. The reliability of the internet in Brunei is also generally good, with minimal downtime and fast repair times in case of outages.

The internet infrastructure in Brunei is well-developed, with a number of internet service providers (ISPs) offering a range of plans and services. The main carriers, DST, Progresif, and TelBru, offer a range of plans, including prepaid and postpaid options, as well as data-only plans for tourists and visitors. The prices for these plans are generally competitive, with tourists able to purchase a SIM card with a data plan for around 10 USD.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Brunei is relatively easy, with a number of options available for tourists and visitors. The main carriers, DST, Progresif, and TelBru, all offer prepaid SIM cards with data plans, which can be purchased at their retail stores or at the airport. To purchase a SIM card, visitors will need to provide their passport and may need to register their SIM card with the carrier. The recommended carriers for tourists are DST and Progresif, which offer a range of data plans, including a 1GB data plan for around 10 USD.
