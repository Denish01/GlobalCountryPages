# Where to Stay in Brunei — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

For travelers visiting Brunei, the best areas to stay are in and around the capital city of Bandar Seri Begawan, which offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For each of the top areas in Brunei, here's a brief overview:
**Gadong** (Bandar Seri Begawan)
Best For: Luxury / Family
Vibe: Gadong is a popular area known for its vibrant nightlife, shopping centers, and restaurants, making it an ideal spot for families and those seeking a luxurious stay. The area is also close to many tourist attractions, including the Gadong Night Market.
Price Range: $100-$500/night
Pros: Close proximity to shopping centers, restaurants, and nightlife, easy access to public transportation, wide range of accommodation options.
Cons: Can be noisy at night due to the nightlife, traffic can be heavy during peak hours.

**Kiulap** (Bandar Seri Begawan)
Best For: Business / Luxury
Vibe: Kiulap is a central business district with a mix of commercial and residential areas, offering a convenient and comfortable stay for business travelers and those seeking luxury. The area is also home to many restaurants, cafes, and shops.
Price Range: $150-$600/night
Pros: Close to major business centers, easy access to public transportation, wide range of dining and entertainment options.
Cons: Can be expensive, limited budget-friendly options.

**Seri Complex** (Bandar Seri Begawan)
Best For: Backpacker / Budget
Vibe: Seri Complex is a budget-friendly area with a range of affordable accommodation options, including hostels and guesthouses. The area is also close to many tourist attractions, including the Sultan Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque.
Price Range: $20-$100/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, close to tourist attractions, easy access to public transportation.
Cons: Limited luxury options, can be noisy at night.

**Manggis** (Bandar Seri Begawan)
Best For: Family / Budget
Vibe: Manggis is a residential area with a range of affordable accommodation options, including guesthouses and apartments. The area is also close to many parks and green spaces, making it an ideal spot for families.
Price Range: $30-$150/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, close to parks and green spaces, easy access to public transportation.
Cons: Limited luxury options, can be far from city center.

**Kuala Belait** (Kuala Belait)
Best For: Adventure / Nature
Vibe: Kuala Belait is a town located in the western part of Brunei, known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities, such as hiking and birdwatching. The area is ideal for adventure seekers and nature lovers.
Price Range: $50-$200/night
Pros: Close to natural attractions, outdoor activities, peaceful and serene environment.
Cons: Limited accommodation options, can be far from city center.
