# Brunei Education — Schools, Universities & Student Guide

The education system in Brunei is highly regarded and well-structured, with a strong emphasis on **Islamic values** and **bilingualism**, providing students with a comprehensive and competitive education that prepares them for success in an increasingly globalized world.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Brunei is divided into three main stages: **primary education**, which lasts for six years, **secondary education**, which lasts for five years, and **tertiary education**, which includes higher education institutions such as universities and colleges. The system is overseen by the Ministry of Education, which ensures that all schools adhere to a standardized curriculum that emphasizes **academic excellence**, **moral values**, and **physical education**. Public schools are free for all citizens, while private schools offer an alternative for those who can afford it. The quality of education in Brunei is generally high, with a strong focus on **science**, **technology**, **engineering**, and **mathematics** (STEM) subjects, as well as **language** and **literature**.

The education system in Brunei is also known for its **bilingual approach**, where both Malay and English are used as languages of instruction. This approach has been successful in producing students who are proficient in both languages and have a strong foundation in **Islamic studies**. The government has also implemented various initiatives to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of **information and communication technology** (ICT) in schools and the provision of **teacher training** programs.

The public education system in Brunei is generally considered to be of high quality, with well-equipped schools and qualified teachers. However, there are some challenges that the system faces, including a lack of **special needs education** and a **limited range of extracurricular activities**. Despite these challenges, the education system in Brunei is highly regarded and has produced many successful students who have gone on to study at top universities around the world.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- International schools in Brunei offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** (IB), **British A-Levels**, and **American High School Diploma**.
- Some popular international schools in Brunei include the **International School of Brunei**, **Jerudong International School**, and **Seri Omega International School**.
- Tuition fees for international schools in Brunei can range from $5,000 to $15,000 per year, depending on the school and the level of study.
- International schools in Brunei are generally located in the capital city, Bandar Seri Begawan, and offer a range of extracurricular activities and facilities, including sports fields, swimming pools, and libraries.
- Many international schools in Brunei also offer **boarding facilities** for students who are not from Brunei, providing a safe and supportive environment for students to study and live.

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Brunei, foreign students need to obtain a **student visa**, which can be applied for through the Brunei Embassy or Consulate in their home country.
- English language proficiency is a requirement for most universities in Brunei, and students may need to take an **English language proficiency test**, such as IELTS or TOEFL, to demonstrate their language skills.
- The Brunei government offers **scholarships** to international students, including the **Brunei Darussalam Government Scholarship**, which covers tuition fees, living expenses, and other costs.
- Living costs in Brunei are generally relatively low, with **accommodation**, **food**, and **transportation** costing around $500 to $1,000 per month.
- Foreign students in Brunei can also apply for **part-time work** to help support themselves while they study, although this is subject to certain restrictions and requirements.
- The Brunei government also provides **health insurance** for international students, which covers medical expenses and other health-related costs.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the strengths of the education system in Brunei is its **emphasis on Islamic values** and **moral education**, which helps to promote **social cohesion** and **community values**.
- However, one of the challenges facing the education system in Brunei is a **lack of special needs education**, which can make it difficult for students with disabilities to access education.
- The education system in Brunei is also **highly centralized**, which can make it difficult for schools to innovate and respond to local needs.
- Despite these challenges, the education system in Brunei has undergone significant **reforms** in recent years, including the introduction of **new curricula** and **teacher training programs**.
- The government has also invested in **education infrastructure**, including the construction of new schools and the upgrade of existing facilities, which has helped to improve the overall quality of education in Brunei.
