[SECTION] Overview: Brunei vs Singapore [/SECTION]
Brunei and Singapore are two neighboring countries in Southeast Asia with distinct economic profiles. Brunei is a small, oil-rich nation with a high standard of living, while Singapore is a highly developed, cosmopolitan city-state with a strong finance and trade sector. In terms of cost of living, Brunei is generally cheaper than Singapore, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation. However, Singapore offers a more vibrant and diverse lifestyle, with a wider range of entertainment, cultural, and educational opportunities.

The economic context of the two countries also plays a significant role in their cost of living. Brunei's economy is heavily reliant on oil and gas exports, which has led to a relatively high standard of living and a low inflation rate. Singapore, on the other hand, has a more diversified economy with a strong focus on finance, trade, and technology, which has driven up costs and inflation. Overall, Brunei's lower cost of living and high standard of living make it an attractive option for expats and retirees, while Singapore's vibrant lifestyle and economic opportunities make it a hub for entrepreneurs and young professionals.
