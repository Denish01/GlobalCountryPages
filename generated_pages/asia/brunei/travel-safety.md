[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Brunei is generally a very safe country to visit, with low crime rates compared to other countries in the region. The country has a strong emphasis on Islamic values and a high standard of living, which contributes to its safe and stable environment. Visitors can feel comfortable walking around the capital city, Bandar Seri Begawan, and other tourist areas, with minimal risk of petty crime or violent incidents. However, as with any country, it's still important to take normal precautions to ensure your safety, such as being aware of your surroundings and keeping valuables secure.

Brunei is also a relatively conservative country, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. This includes dressing modestly, especially when visiting mosques or other religious sites, and avoiding public displays of affection. The country also has strict laws against certain activities, such as drinking alcohol or engaging in public indecency, so it's essential to be aware of these laws and regulations before traveling to Brunei. Overall, with some basic precautions and an understanding of local customs, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable trip to Brunei.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- **Pickpocketing**: A common crime in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations
- **Scams**: Some scams target tourists, such as fake tour guides or overpriced souvenirs
- **Traffic accidents**: Brunei has a high rate of traffic accidents, so be careful when crossing roads or driving
- **Petty theft**: There is a low risk of petty theft, such as theft of valuables from hotel rooms or public areas
- **Credit card scams**: Some businesses may not accept credit cards, or may have outdated payment systems, so be cautious when using credit cards
- **Food poisoning**: There is a low risk of food poisoning, but still be aware of food safety and hygiene when eating at local restaurants

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations**: No specific vaccinations are required for travel to Brunei, but it's recommended to be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations
- **Water Safety**: Tap water is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is still recommended
- **Medical Facilities**: Brunei has high-quality medical facilities, including the Raja Isteri Pengiran Anak Saleha Hospital in Bandar Seri Begawan
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacies are widely available, and many medications can be purchased over-the-counter
