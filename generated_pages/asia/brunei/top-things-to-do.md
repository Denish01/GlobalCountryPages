# Top Things to Do in Brunei — Attractions & Experiences

To experience the best of Brunei, visit its stunning mosques, explore the lush rainforests, and immerse yourself in the local culture, which seamlessly blends traditional and modern elements.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **proboscis monkey** tour in the Ulu Temburong National Park to spot these unique creatures in their natural habitat
- Experience the **Sultan's birthday celebrations**, a grand event showcasing Brunei's culture and traditions
- Go on a **longboat ride** through the water villages of Kampong Ayer to see traditional stilt houses and local life
- Visit the **Gadong Night Market** to try local street food and shop for souvenirs
- Attend a **traditional Brunei wedding**, a vibrant and colorful celebration of local customs
- Take a **cooking class** to learn about Brunei's unique cuisine, which combines Malay, Chinese, and Indian flavors
- Explore the **caves of the Peradayan Forest Reserve**, featuring stunning rock formations and diverse wildlife

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- Hike through the **Ulu Temburong National Park**, with its canopy walks and stunning rainforest scenery
- Visit the **Tasek Merimbun Heritage Park**, a former lake bed with unique rock formations and archaeological sites
- Explore the **Peradayan Forest Reserve**, home to diverse wildlife, including proboscis monkeys and hornbills
- Relax on the **Pantai Seri Kenangan beach**, a scenic spot with crystal-clear waters and powdery sand
- Take a **boat tour** of the **Brunei River**, offering stunning views of the water villages and surrounding landscape
- Go **birdwatching** in the **Tutong District**, known for its diverse avifauna, including the rare **argus pheasant**

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Brunei Museum**, featuring exhibits on Brunei's history, culture, and natural environment
- Explore the **Sultan Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque**, a stunning example of Islamic architecture
- Attend a **traditional Brunei dance performance**, showcasing local customs and music
- Take a **tour of the Istana Nurul Iman**, the world's largest residential palace
- Visit the **Kampong Ayer Cultural Centre**, showcasing the history and traditions of the water villages
- Attend a **local festival**, such as the **Brunei International Film Festival** or the **Brunei Darussalam International Music Festival**

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- Visit the **Tutong Thursday Market**, a local market selling traditional handicrafts and souvenirs
- Explore the **Labi Forest Reserve**, a lesser-known nature reserve with diverse wildlife and hiking trails
- Take a **tour of the Brunei Shell Petroleum oil rig**, offering insights into the country's oil and gas industry
- Relax at the **Pantai Seri Kenangan beach**, a scenic spot with crystal-clear waters and powdery sand
- Visit the **Kampong Perdayan**, a traditional water village with stilt houses and local workshops

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- Take a day trip to the **Ulu Temburong National Park** (distance: 45 km, travel time: 1 hour), featuring stunning rainforest scenery and canopy walks
- Visit the **Tasek Merimbun Heritage Park** (distance: 30 km, travel time: 45 minutes), a former lake bed with unique rock formations and archaeological sites
- Explore the **Peradayan Forest Reserve** (distance: 20 km, travel time: 30 minutes), home to diverse wildlife, including proboscis monkeys and hornbills
- Take a day trip to the **Tutong District** (distance: 40 km, travel time: 1 hour), known for its traditional water villages and local markets
- Visit the **Seria oil field** (distance: 100 km, travel time: 2 hours), offering insights into Brunei's oil and gas industry
