[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Uzbekistan has a well-developed transportation system, with a variety of options available for getting around, including buses, taxis, ride-hailing services, and trains. The quality of public transit is generally good, with frequent and reliable services, especially in major cities like Tashkent and Samarkand. Most people in Uzbekistan get around using public transportation, with buses and minibuses being the most popular options, while taxis and ride-hailing services are also widely used.

The country's transportation network is relatively affordable, with prices comparable to other countries in the region. However, prices can vary depending on the city and region, with Tashkent tend to be more expensive than other cities. Additionally, the cost of transportation can be affected by the time of day and the availability of services, with prices tend to be higher during peak hours and lower during off-peak hours.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Using public transportation can be a cost-effective way to get around, with monthly passes available for frequent travelers.
- Ride-hailing apps like Yandex.Taxi and MyTaxi can be a convenient option, especially for shorter trips.
- **Negotiating taxi fares** can be necessary, especially for longer trips or when using metered taxis.
- **Traffic in major cities** can be heavy, especially during peak hours, so it's a good idea to plan ahead and allow extra time for travel.
- Using a **reliable taxi service** or ride-hailing app can be safer than hailing a taxi on the street.
- **Respecting local customs** and traditions when using public transportation can be important, especially in more conservative areas.
