[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet in Uzbekistan has undergone significant improvements in recent years, with the average internet speed increasing to 10 Mbps download. However, there is still a notable urban-rural divide, with faster and more reliable connections available in cities like Tashkent, Samarkand, and Bukhara, while rural areas often experience slower speeds and more frequent outages. Despite these challenges, the overall internet quality in Uzbekistan is relatively good, with many cafes, restaurants, and hotels offering **WiFi** connections.

The reliability of the internet in Uzbekistan can vary depending on the location and time of day. In general, the internet is more stable during the daytime, but can be slower and more prone to outages during peak hours or in areas with high demand. Additionally, the government has been known to restrict access to certain websites and online services, which can impact the overall internet experience. However, for most tourists and travelers, the internet in Uzbekistan is sufficient for basic needs like staying in touch with family and friends, accessing maps and travel information, and sharing experiences on social media.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Uzbekistan is a relatively straightforward process, with many mobile providers offering **tourist SIMs** that can be purchased at airports, phone shops, and other retail locations. To buy a SIM card, tourists will typically need to provide their passport and fill out a registration form, which can be done in a matter of minutes. The main carriers in Uzbekistan, including Ucell, Beeline, and Uzmobile, offer a range of prepaid plans that include data, voice, and text services. These plans can be topped up at many locations throughout the country, including convenience stores, pharmacies, and phone shops.

For tourists and travelers, Ucell and Beeline are recommended carriers, as they offer a range of affordable plans and have good coverage in most areas of the country. Uzmobile is also a good option, especially for those who plan to visit rural areas, as it has a strong network in these regions.

[SECTION] WiFi Availability [/SECTION]
- Hotel WiFi quality is generally good, with many hotels offering fast and reliable connections in their rooms and public areas.
- Cafe WiFi culture is common in Uzbekistan, with many cafes and restaurants offering free WiFi to customers.
- Coworking spaces are available in major cities like Tashkent and Samarkand, providing a productive environment for remote workers and digital nomads.
- Public WiFi is limited in Uzbekistan, but can be found in some parks, museums, and other public areas.
- Many restaurants and bars also offer WiFi, making it easy to stay connected while enjoying local cuisine and drinks.

[SECTION] For Remote Workers [/SECTION]
- Coworking spaces like Tashkent Coworking and Samarkand Coworking offer a range of amenities, including fast WiFi, desks, and meeting rooms.
- Reliable cafe chains like Coffee Shop and Cafe Europa offer good WiFi and a comfortable working environment.
- Backup internet options like portable **WiFi hotspots** and **mobile routers** can be useful for remote workers who need a reliable connection.
- VPNs are recommended for remote workers who need to access restricted websites or services, as the government has been known to block certain online content.
- Many hotels and guesthouses also offer coworking spaces and other facilities for remote workers, making it easy to find a productive and comfortable environment.

[SECTION] Tips & Warnings [/SECTION]
- Internet censorship is a significant issue in Uzbekistan, with many websites and online services blocked by the government.
- Blocked sites include social media platforms, news websites, and other online content that is deemed sensitive or restricted.
- VPNs are recommended for anyone who needs to access restricted websites or services, as they can help to bypass government blocks and ensure a secure connection.
- Roaming alternatives like **eSIMs** and **portable WiFi hotspots** can be useful for tourists and travelers who need to stay connected while avoiding high roaming charges.
- When using public WiFi or other unsecured connections, it's essential to take precautions like using a VPN and avoiding sensitive online activities to minimize the risk of **cyber attacks** and **data breaches**.
