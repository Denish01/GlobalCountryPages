Infrastructure Overview

Uzbekistan's infrastructure has undergone significant development in recent years, with the government investing heavily in modernizing the country's roads, energy systems, and telecommunications networks. However, there is still a significant urban-rural gap, with cities like Tashkent and Samarkand having better infrastructure than rural areas. The government has implemented various initiatives to address this gap, including the construction of new roads, bridges, and energy facilities. Despite these efforts, the country still faces challenges in providing reliable and efficient infrastructure services, particularly in rural areas.

The government has also been working to attract foreign investment to support the development of the country's infrastructure. This has led to the implementation of several major projects, including the construction of new highways, railways, and energy facilities. The government has also been investing in the development of the country's tourism infrastructure, including the construction of new hotels, restaurants, and other facilities.

Uzbekistan's infrastructure development is also closely tied to its regional relationships, with the country playing a key role in several regional initiatives, including the Belt and Road Initiative. This has led to significant investment in the country's transport infrastructure, including the construction of new roads and railways. The government has also been working to improve the country's energy security, including the development of new energy sources and the modernization of the country's energy grid.
