The cost of utilities and internet in Uzbekistan can vary depending on factors such as location, usage, and provider, but on average, a monthly utility bill for an 85 sqm apartment can range from $50 to $150 USD.

[SECTION] Utility Costs Overview [/SECTION]
Uzbekistan's utility infrastructure has undergone significant improvements in recent years, with a focus on increasing access to reliable and affordable services. However, the costs of utilities can fluctuate depending on the season, with higher prices during the winter months due to increased demand for heating. The government has implemented measures to stabilize prices, but the overall cost of utilities remains a significant expense for many households. The quality of utility infrastructure varies across different regions, with urban areas generally having better access to reliable services than rural areas.

The cost of utilities in Uzbekistan is also influenced by the country's economic conditions, including inflation and exchange rates. With an inflation rate of 9.6% in 2024, the cost of utilities is likely to increase, making it essential for households to budget accordingly. Additionally, the government's efforts to increase energy efficiency and reduce waste can help mitigate the impact of rising utility costs. Overall, understanding the factors that affect utility costs in Uzbekistan can help households make informed decisions about their energy usage and budgeting.

[SECTION] Utility Tips [/SECTION]
- To set up utilities in Uzbekistan, it is recommended to contact the local utility providers, such as Uztelecom or Telia, and provide the necessary documentation, including a passport and proof of address.
- Prepaid electricity plans can be a cost-effective option for households with variable energy usage, but postpaid plans may offer more flexibility and convenience.
- Uztelecom and Telia are two of the most popular internet providers in Uzbekistan, offering a range of plans and speeds to suit different needs and budgets.
- To save on utility bills, households can consider energy-efficient appliances, turning off lights and electronics when not in use, and using public transportation or carpooling to reduce fuel consumption.
- It is also essential to monitor utility usage regularly and adjust consumption habits accordingly to avoid unexpected high bills.
