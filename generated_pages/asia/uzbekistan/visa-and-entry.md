# Uzbekistan Visa & Entry Requirements

You will likely need a visa to enter Uzbekistan, but the requirements vary depending on your nationality and the purpose of your visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Uzbekistan before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Uzbekistan has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and required visas for different nationalities. Citizens of over 60 countries can enter Uzbekistan visa-free for a certain period, while others can obtain a visa on arrival or through an e-visa system. The country has been gradually liberalizing its visa policy to attract more tourists and business travelers.

The visa policy of Uzbekistan is designed to balance the need to control the flow of visitors with the goal of promoting tourism and economic development. The government has introduced various measures to simplify the visa application process, including the introduction of an e-visa system and the expansion of visa-free access to citizens of certain countries. However, the requirements and regulations can still be complex, and it is essential to verify the current requirements before planning a trip to Uzbekistan.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can enter Uzbekistan visa-free for up to 30 days, but they must register with the authorities within 3 days of arrival. The typical stay for tourists is 7-14 days, and extensions are possible but require additional documentation. Tourists must also have a valid passport, proof of onward travel, and sufficient funds to support their stay.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers may require a visa, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They must provide an invitation letter from a Uzbek company or organization, as well as proof of their business activities. The requirements and regulations for business travelers can be complex, and it is recommended to consult with the Uzbek embassy or consulate for specific guidance.
**Students:** Students who plan to study in Uzbekistan must obtain a student visa, which requires proof of enrollment at a Uzbek university or institution. The duration of the visa will depend on the length of the study program, and students must also register with the authorities within 3 days of arrival.
**Workers:** Workers who plan to work in Uzbekistan must obtain a work visa, which requires sponsorship from a Uzbek employer. The process can be complex and requires additional documentation, including a contract and proof of qualifications.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main airports in Uzbekistan are Tashkent International Airport, Samarkand International Airport, and Bukhara International Airport. Land borders are also open, with major crossings at the borders with Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Afghanistan, and Turkmenistan. Upon arrival, visitors must go through immigration and customs, where they will need to present their passport, visa (if required), and other documents. The process can be slow, especially during peak travel seasons, and visitors should be prepared to wait in line and answer questions from immigration officials.

At immigration, visitors will be required to fill out a customs declaration form and declare any goods they are bringing into the country. They will also need to register with the authorities within 3 days of arrival, which can be done at a local police station or through a hotel. The registration process requires proof of identity, proof of address, and payment of a small fee.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Failing to register with the authorities within 3 days of arrival
- Not having a valid passport with at least 6 months' validity
- Not having proof of onward travel
- Not having sufficient funds to support stay
- Not having the required visa or documentation
- Not declaring goods at customs, which can result in fines or confiscation of goods

It is essential to carefully review the visa requirements and regulations before traveling to Uzbekistan to avoid any issues or delays. By understanding the visa policy and requirements, visitors can ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to this beautiful and historic country.
