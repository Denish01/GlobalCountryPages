# Education Costs in Uzbekistan

The cost of education in Uzbekistan varies depending on the type of institution and level of study, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
Uzbekistan's education system is overseen by the Ministry of Public Education, with a focus on providing free and compulsory education to all citizens. The system is divided into primary, secondary, and higher education, with the language of instruction being **Uzbek**. While public schools are generally considered to be of good quality, private schools and international institutions often offer a more **Western-style** education, with English as a primary language of instruction. Compulsory education in Uzbekistan lasts for 12 years, from age 6 to 18, with students typically attending primary school for 4 years, followed by 5 years of secondary school, and finally 3 years of **lyceum** or **college**.

The quality of education in Uzbekistan's public schools can vary, with some schools in urban areas being better equipped and staffed than those in rural areas. However, in recent years, the government has made efforts to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new **curricula** and **teaching methods**. Private schools and international institutions, on the other hand, often have more resources and better facilities, but can be expensive. International schools in Uzbekistan offer a range of **curricula**, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Examinations**, and are popular among **expat** families.
