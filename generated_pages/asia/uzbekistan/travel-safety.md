# Uzbekistan Travel Safety - Advisories & Tips

Uzbekistan is generally considered a safe country to visit, with low crime rates and a strong emphasis on tourism, but as with any foreign travel, it's essential to be aware of local conditions and take necessary precautions.

[SECTION] Overall Safety Assessment [/SECTION]
Uzbekistan has undergone significant transformations in recent years, opening up to tourism and implementing various safety measures to ensure a pleasant experience for visitors. The country's low crime rate, combined with its rich cultural heritage and historical landmarks, makes it an attractive destination for travelers. However, as with any foreign travel, it's essential to be mindful of local customs, respect traditions, and take necessary precautions to stay safe. Petty crimes, such as pickpocketing and theft, can occur in crowded areas and tourist hotspots, but these are relatively rare and can be easily avoided by taking basic precautions.

Uzbekistan's government has taken steps to improve safety and security, including increasing police presence in tourist areas and introducing a dedicated tourist police force. Additionally, the country has invested in infrastructure development, making it easier for visitors to navigate and access various attractions. Despite these efforts, it's crucial to remain vigilant and aware of local conditions, particularly in areas near the borders with neighboring countries. By taking necessary precautions and staying informed, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Uzbekistan.

[SECTION] Common Risks & Scams [/SECTION]
- Petty theft and pickpocketing in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
- Scams targeting tourists, such as overcharging for services or selling counterfeit goods
- Traffic accidents, particularly when traveling by car or taxi
- Natural disasters, such as earthquakes and landslides, in mountainous regions
- Border crossings, which can be unpredictable and may require additional documentation
- **ATM scams**, where thieves use skimming devices to steal card information
- **Overcharging** by taxi drivers or vendors, who may take advantage of tourists

[SECTION] Health & Medical [/SECTION]
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A and typhoid fever vaccinations are recommended, while rabies and COVID-19 vaccinations may be required depending on the time of year and local conditions
- **Water Safety:** Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled or filtered water is recommended
- **Medical Facilities:** Medical facilities in major cities are generally good, but may be limited in rural areas, and health insurance is recommended
- **Pharmacy Access:** Pharmacies are widely available, but may not always have a wide range of medications, and it's recommended to bring any necessary medications with you

[SECTION] Practical Safety Tips [/SECTION]
- Be mindful of local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting mosques or other cultural sites
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and use reputable exchange services
- Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt or safe
- Avoid traveling alone at night, particularly in rural areas
- Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always check the driver's ID before getting in
- Stay hydrated and bring sun protection, as the sun can be intense in Uzbekistan
- Learn some basic Uzbek phrases, such as "hello" and "thank you", to show respect for the local culture

[SECTION] For Solo & Women Travelers [/SECTION]
- Uzbekistan is generally a safe country for solo and women travelers, but it's still important to take necessary precautions and be mindful of local customs
- Dress modestly and respectfully, particularly when visiting cultural sites or attending local events
- Avoid traveling alone at night, and consider joining a guided tour or using a reputable transportation service
- Be prepared for occasional stares or attention, but this is generally not malicious and can be easily ignored
