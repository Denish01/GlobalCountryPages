Education System Overview

The education system in Cambodia is overseen by the Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport, and is divided into primary, secondary, and higher education. Public schools are generally free or low-cost, but the quality of education can vary greatly depending on the location and resources available. Private schools, on the other hand, offer a higher quality of education, but at a significantly higher cost. The language of instruction in public schools is Khmer, while private and international schools often teach in English or other foreign languages. Compulsory education in Cambodia lasts for 12 years, from age 6 to 18.

International schools are available in major cities like Phnom Penh and Siem Reap, offering a range of curricula, including International Baccalaureate, Cambridge International Examinations, and American and British curricula. These schools cater to expatriate families and wealthy locals, and offer a high-quality education, but at a premium price. The quality of education in Cambodia is generally considered to be lower than in other countries in the region, but there are efforts being made to improve the system, particularly in the areas of teacher training and curriculum development.

Education Quality & Tips

  • The International School of Phnom Penh and Northbridge International School are highly regarded international schools in Cambodia, offering a range of curricula and extracurricular activities.
  • Scholarships are available for local students, particularly those from disadvantaged backgrounds, but competition is fierce and funding is limited.
  • Online learning options are becoming increasingly popular in Cambodia, with many universities and schools offering online courses and degree programs.
  • Education quality in Cambodia is generally considered to be lower than in other countries in the region, but there are efforts being made to improve the system, particularly in the areas of teacher training and curriculum development.
  • Expatriate families often choose to send their children to international schools, which offer a high-quality education and a range of extracurricular activities.
  • [CALLOUT] It is essential to research and visit schools before making a decision, as the quality of education and facilities can vary greatly between institutions.
