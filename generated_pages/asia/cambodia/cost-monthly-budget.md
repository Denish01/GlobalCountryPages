# Monthly Budget Guide for Cambodia

To live in Cambodia, you would need around $400-1,200 per month, depending on your lifestyle and personal preferences.

[FACTBOX]

Average Local Salary: $200 USD/month, approximate
Currency: KHR
[/FACTBOX]

[SECTION] Cost of Living Overview [/SECTION]
Cambodia is considered one of the most affordable countries in Southeast Asia, with a low cost of living compared to its neighboring countries. The GDP per capita (PPP) of $7,967 (2024) indicates a relatively low-to-middle-income economy. A budget of $400 per month can provide a basic but comfortable life, while $700 per month can offer a more mid-range lifestyle with some luxuries. In comparison, neighboring countries like Thailand and Vietnam have higher costs of living, making Cambodia an attractive option for expats and digital nomads. However, the cost of living in Cambodia can vary greatly depending on the location, with Phnom Penh being the most expensive city.

The affordability of Cambodia is also reflected in the prices of everyday items, such as food, transportation, and accommodation. A meal at a local restaurant can cost as little as $3, while a one-bedroom apartment in the city center can cost around $200 per month. However, prices can increase significantly in tourist areas and major cities. Overall, Cambodia offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for people looking to live abroad.

[SECTION] Budget Tier Lifestyles [/SECTION]
At the budget level of $400 per month, daily life in Cambodia can be quite basic but still comfortable. You can afford to share a one-bedroom apartment outside of the city center, cook most of your meals, and use public transportation. You can also afford to go out and socialize occasionally, but you may have to be careful with your expenses. For example, you can eat at local restaurants for around $3 per meal, but you may not be able to afford to eat at mid-range restaurants. You can also afford to take the occasional taxi ride, but you may have to walk or use public transportation most of the time.

At the mid-range level of $700 per month, daily life in Cambodia can be quite comfortable. You can afford to rent a one-bedroom apartment in the city center, eat out at mid-range restaurants, and use a mix of public and private transportation. You can also afford to go out and socialize more frequently, and you may be able to afford to take the occasional weekend trip. For example, you can eat at mid-range restaurants for around $10 per meal, and you can afford to take a taxi ride whenever you need to. You can also afford to join a gym or take a cooking class, which can be a great way to meet new people.

At the comfortable level of $1,200 per month, daily life in Cambodia can be quite luxurious. You can afford to rent a nice two-bedroom apartment in the city center, eat out at high-end restaurants, and use private transportation. You can also afford to go out and socialize as much as you want, and you may be able to afford to take regular weekend trips. For example, you can eat at high-end restaurants for around $20 per meal, and you can afford to take a taxi ride whenever you need to. You can also afford to join a private gym or take a private cooking class, which can be a great way to meet new people.

[SECTION] Money-Saving Strategies [/SECTION]
- Learn to cook local dishes to save money on food
- Use public transportation instead of taxis or private cars
- Rent an apartment outside of the city center to save on rent
- Avoid eating at tourist restaurants and instead opt for local eateries
- Use cash instead of credit cards to avoid foreign transaction fees
- Learn some basic Khmer phrases to negotiate prices and avoid tourist traps
- Consider buying a local SIM card instead of using international roaming
- Shop at local markets instead of supermarkets to save on groceries

[SECTION] Is Cambodia Affordable? [/SECTION]
Cambodia can be a very affordable destination for digital nomads, retirees, and students. The low cost of living, combined with the relatively low cost of accommodation and food, makes it an attractive option for people looking to live abroad. Additionally, the country has a growing expat community, which can make it easy to meet new people and find like-minded individuals. However, Cambodia may not be the most affordable destination for families, as the cost of living can add up quickly, especially when it comes to education and healthcare.

Overall, Cambodia is a great option for people who are looking for a low-cost, high-quality lifestyle. The country offers a great balance of affordability and quality of life, making it an attractive destination for people from all over the world. With its rich culture, beautiful landscapes, and friendly people, Cambodia is a great place to live, work, and retire. However, it's worth noting that the cost of living in Cambodia can vary greatly depending on the location and lifestyle, so it's essential to do your research and plan carefully before making the move.
