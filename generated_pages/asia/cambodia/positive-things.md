[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Angkor Wat** temple complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the largest and most impressive archaeological sites in the world.
- The **Royal Ballet of Cambodia**, a traditional dance troupe, has been recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.
- The **Khmer New Year**, also known as **Choul Chnam Thmey**, is a significant cultural celebration, marked by traditional festivities and ceremonies.
- The **National Museum of Cambodia** in Phnom Penh houses a vast collection of Khmer art and artifacts, including sculptures, ceramics, and textiles.
- The **Silver Pagoda**, located within the Royal Palace complex in Phnom Penh, is a stunning example of Khmer architecture and craftsmanship.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **King Norodom Sihanouk**, the former king of Cambodia, played a significant role in the country's independence movement and was a prominent figure in Cambodian politics.
- The **Cambodian diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global community, with many Cambodians achieving success in fields such as business, politics, and the arts.
- **Somaly Mam**, a Cambodian human rights activist, has worked tirelessly to combat human trafficking and promote women's rights in Cambodia.
- The **Khmer Rouge Tribunal**, established to try former leaders of the Khmer Rouge regime, has provided a measure of justice and accountability for the victims of the regime's atrocities.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The warm **hospitality** of the Cambodian people, who are known for their friendly and welcoming nature.
- The delicious **street food**, with popular dishes such as **Fish Amok** and **Nom Banh Chok**.
- The stunning **natural scenery**, including the **Tonle Sap Lake** and the **Cardamom Mountains**.
- The vibrant **markets**, such as the **Central Market** in Phnom Penh, where visitors can find unique souvenirs and try local cuisine.
- The **temples and ruins**, including **Angkor Wat** and **Bayon**, which offer a glimpse into Cambodia's rich history and culture.
- The **festivals and celebrations**, such as the **Water Festival**, which showcase the country's vibrant culture and traditions.

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- The **Koh Tonsay Island**, also known as **Rabbit Island**, is a peaceful and secluded island off the coast of Kep, known for its beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters.
- The **Banteay Chhmar** temple, a remote and lesser-visited temple complex, offers a unique and authentic experience for travelers.
- The **Prek Toal Bird Sanctuary**, located on the **Tonle Sap Lake**, is a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts, with over 150 species of birds documented in the area.
- The **Phnom Penh's Street Art**, which has become a vibrant and dynamic aspect of the city's cultural landscape, with many local and international artists contributing to the scene.
