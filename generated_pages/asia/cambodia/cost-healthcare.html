Healthcare System Overview

Cambodia's healthcare system is a mixed model, with both public and private providers offering a range of services. Public healthcare is generally more affordable, but the quality can vary greatly depending on the location and facility. In rural areas, public healthcare facilities may be understaffed and under-resourced, while in urban areas like Phnom Penh, the quality of public healthcare is generally better. For foreigners, private healthcare is often the preferred option, as it offers better facilities, more English-speaking staff, and a wider range of services. However, private healthcare can be expensive, and it is recommended that foreigners have health insurance to cover the costs.

The healthcare system in Cambodia is also affected by the country's limited resources and infrastructure. Many public healthcare facilities lack modern equipment and trained staff, which can make it difficult to access quality care. Additionally, the country's healthcare system is often overwhelmed by the demand for services, particularly in urban areas. Despite these challenges, the Cambodian government has made efforts to improve the healthcare system, including increasing funding for public healthcare and implementing programs to improve access to healthcare for rural communities.

Pharmacy & Medication Costs

  • Common medications like antibiotics and pain relievers can cost between $5-10 per prescription
  • Prescription rules vary, but many medications are available over-the-counter
  • Pharmacies are widely available, particularly in urban areas
  • Many pharmacies offer a range of services, including vaccinations and health checks
  • Some pharmacies may not always have the latest or most effective medications
  • It is recommended to check the expiration dates and authenticity of medications before purchasing

Emergency Care

In the event of a medical emergency, it is recommended to call the emergency number 119 or go to the nearest hospital. Private hospitals like the Royal Phnom Penh Hospital and International SOS often have better emergency facilities and more experienced staff. However, these hospitals can be expensive, and it is recommended to have health insurance to cover the costs. For foreigners, it is also recommended to have a list of emergency contact numbers, including the embassy and insurance provider. Additionally, it is a good idea to research the best hospitals and medical facilities in the area before an emergency arises, to ensure that you can access quality care quickly and easily.

In the case of a serious medical emergency, it may be necessary to evacuate to a neighboring country or to a country with more advanced medical facilities. This can be a complex and expensive process, and it is recommended to have a plan in place before an emergency arises. Many health insurance providers offer emergency evacuation services, and it is recommended to check the policy details before purchasing. Additionally, it is a good idea to research the emergency evacuation procedures and protocols in place in Cambodia, to ensure that you are prepared in the event of an emergency.
