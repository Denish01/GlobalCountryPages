Overall Safety Assessment

Cambodia is generally a safe country to visit, with low levels of violent crime. However, petty crimes such as pickpocketing, bag snatching, and scams are common, particularly in tourist areas. The country has made significant progress in recent years in terms of security, but visitors should still exercise caution, especially when traveling at night or in crowded areas. With some basic precautions and an understanding of the local culture, visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience in Cambodia.

As with any country, there are areas that are considered safer than others. Major tourist destinations such as Siem Reap, Phnom Penh, and Sihanoukville are generally well-patrolled and safe, but visitors should still be mindful of their belongings and avoid traveling alone at night. The country's roads can be hazardous, especially for motorcyclists, and visitors should exercise extreme caution when driving or crossing the road. Additionally, Cambodia is prone to natural disasters such as floods and droughts, which can affect travel plans and safety.

Common Risks & Scams

  • Pickpocketing and bag snatching: Common in crowded areas and tourist hotspots
  • Scams: Be wary of overly friendly locals who offer to guide you or sell you something
  • Motorcycle scams: Be cautious when renting motorcycles, and always wear a helmet
  • ATM scams: Be aware of your surroundings when using ATMs, and cover the keypad when entering your PIN
  • Gemstone scams: Be wary of locals who offer to sell you gemstones or other valuable items
  • Traffic accidents: Be extremely cautious when driving or crossing the road, as traffic laws are often ignored
  • Food and water poisoning: Be careful what you eat and drink, and avoid undercooked meat and untreated water
  • Moto-dups: Be aware of moto-dups, who may offer to take you to a destination, but then demand more money

Health & Medical

  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A, hepatitis B, and typhoid are recommended
  • Water Safety: Tap water is not safe to drink, and bottled water is recommended
  • Medical Facilities: Medical facilities are limited, especially outside of major cities, and medical insurance is highly recommended
  • Pharmacy Access: Pharmacies are widely available, but the quality of medication can vary

Practical Safety Tips

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas
  • Keep valuables secure and consider using a money belt
  • Avoid traveling alone at night, and use reputable taxi services
  • Learn some basic Khmer phrases, such as "hello" (chum reap suor) and "thank you" (akun)
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use credit cards or ATMs instead
  • Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen, as the sun can be intense
  • Consider hiring a reputable guide or joining a tour group

For Solo & Women Travelers

  • Be cautious when interacting with locals, especially men, and avoid traveling alone at night
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples or rural areas
  • Consider staying in well-lit and populated areas, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas
  • Research your accommodation thoroughly, and choose a reputable and safe hotel or guesthouse
