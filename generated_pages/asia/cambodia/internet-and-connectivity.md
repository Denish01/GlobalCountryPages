[SECTION] Internet Overview [/SECTION]
The internet quality in Cambodia has improved significantly over the years, with most urban areas having access to fast and reliable connections. However, there is still a noticeable **urban vs rural divide**, with rural areas often experiencing slower speeds and less reliable connections. The overall internet infrastructure in Cambodia is relatively well-developed, with a good range of internet service providers offering a variety of plans to suit different needs and budgets. Despite some occasional outages and slow speeds, the internet in Cambodia is generally **reliable**, with most users experiencing minimal disruptions to their online activities.

In recent years, the Cambodian government has invested heavily in improving the country's internet infrastructure, with a focus on expanding coverage to rural areas and increasing the overall speed and reliability of connections. As a result, internet penetration has increased significantly, with more and more Cambodians going online to access information, communicate with friends and family, and conduct business. The internet has also become an essential tool for tourists and remote workers, with many cafes, restaurants, and hotels offering **free WiFi** to their customers.

[SECTION] Getting a SIM Card [/SECTION]
Getting a SIM card in Cambodia is a relatively straightforward process, with many mobile providers offering **tourist-friendly plans** that include data, voice minutes, and text messages. The main carriers, such as Cellcard, Metfone, and Smart Axiata, have outlets at major airports and in urban areas, where you can purchase a SIM card and register for a plan. To buy a SIM card, you will need to provide your passport and fill out a registration form, which usually takes just a few minutes to complete. It's recommended to purchase a SIM card from a reputable provider, as this will ensure that you get a reliable and fast connection.
