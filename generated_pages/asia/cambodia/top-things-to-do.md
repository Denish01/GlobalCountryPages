# Top Things to Do in Cambodia — Attractions & Experiences

When visiting Cambodia, the best things to do include exploring the ancient temples of Angkor Wat, experiencing the vibrant culture of Phnom Penh, and relaxing on the beautiful beaches of Sihanoukville.

[SECTION] Unique Experiences [/SECTION]
- Take a **traditional Khmer cooking class** in Siem Reap, where you can learn to prepare local dishes such as fish amok and nom banh chok
- Explore the **floating villages** of Tonle Sap Lake, where you can see traditional stilt houses and learn about the local way of life
- Attend a **traditional Khmer dance performance** in Phnom Penh, featuring classical dances such as the Apsara and the Ramayana
- Visit the **Kampong Thom** countryside, where you can see traditional villages and learn about the local agriculture
- Take a **street food tour** of Phnom Penh, where you can sample local delicacies such as fried insects and nom banh chok
- Go on a **motorbike tour** of the countryside, where you can see rural villages and scenic landscapes
- Visit the **Phnom Tamao Wildlife Rescue Center**, where you can see rescued animals such as elephants, tigers, and bears

[SECTION] Outdoor & Nature [/SECTION]
- **Kirirom National Park**, a protected area with forests, waterfalls, and hiking trails
- **Ream National Park**, a coastal park with mangrove forests, beaches, and coral reefs
- **Tonle Sap Lake**, a large freshwater lake with floating villages and diverse wildlife
- **Cardamom Mountains**, a mountain range with forests, waterfalls, and trekking trails
- **Sihanoukville Beach**, a popular beach destination with crystal-clear waters and powdery white sand
- **Koh Rong Island**, a tropical island with pristine beaches, coral reefs, and a vibrant party scene

[SECTION] Cultural Experiences [/SECTION]
- **National Museum of Cambodia**, a museum showcasing the country's rich history and culture
- **Royal Palace**, the official residence of the King of Cambodia, featuring stunning architecture and gardens
- **Silver Pagoda**, a beautiful temple featuring a stunning silver floor and intricate carvings
- **Angkor Wat Temple Complex**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest temple complexes in the world
- **Wat Phnom**, a historic temple in Phnom Penh, featuring a stunning stupa and beautiful gardens
- **Banteay Srei Temple**, a small but beautifully carved temple, known for its intricate carvings and red sandstone

[SECTION] Hidden Gems [/SECTION]
- **Battambang**, a charming town with a rich cultural heritage, known for its colonial architecture and vibrant arts scene
- **Kampong Thom**, a rural town with traditional villages and a relaxed atmosphere
- **Koh Tonsay Island**, a peaceful island with pristine beaches and a laid-back atmosphere
- **Phnom Kulen National Park**, a protected area with forests, waterfalls, and ancient temples
- **Siem Reap's Art Market**, a bustling market featuring local artwork, handicrafts, and souvenirs

[SECTION] Day Trips [/SECTION]
- **Tonle Sap Lake** from Siem Reap, a 30-minute drive, featuring floating villages and diverse wildlife
- **Banteay Srei Temple** from Siem Reap, a 30-minute drive, featuring intricate carvings and red sandstone
- **Kirirom National Park** from Phnom Penh, a 2-hour drive, featuring forests, waterfalls, and hiking trails
- **Sihanoukville Beach** from Phnom Penh, a 4-hour drive, featuring crystal-clear waters and powdery white sand
- **Koh Rong Island** from Sihanoukville, a 2-hour boat ride, featuring pristine beaches and a vibrant party scene
