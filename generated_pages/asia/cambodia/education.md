[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Cambodia is structured into three main levels: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, which is compulsory for all children, lasts for six years, followed by three years of lower secondary education and three years of upper secondary education. Tertiary education is provided by universities and other higher education institutions, with a strong focus on fields such as **business**, **engineering**, and **medicine**. The quality of education in Cambodia varies, with public schools often struggling with limited resources and outdated teaching methods, while private schools and international institutions tend to offer higher-quality education.

In recent years, the Cambodian government has made significant efforts to improve the education system, including increasing access to education for disadvantaged groups and implementing reforms to improve the quality of teaching and learning. Despite these efforts, the education system in Cambodia still faces significant challenges, including a shortage of qualified teachers and limited resources. The government has also introduced initiatives to promote **vocational training** and **technical education**, in order to provide students with practical skills that can be applied in the workforce.

The education system in Cambodia is also characterized by a strong emphasis on **public education**, with the government providing free education to all citizens. However, many students also attend private schools, which offer a range of programs and curricula, including **international curricula** such as the International Baccalaureate (IB) and the Cambridge International Examinations (CIE). The cost of attending private schools in Cambodia can be relatively high, but many students and families see it as a worthwhile investment in their future.

[SECTION] International Schools [/SECTION]
- International schools are widely available in Cambodia, particularly in Phnom Penh and other major cities, offering a range of curricula, including the IB, British, and American curricula.
- The cost of attending an international school in Cambodia can be relatively high, with tuition fees ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per year.
- Many international schools in Cambodia offer a range of extracurricular activities and sports programs, as well as **language support** for students who are non-native English speakers.
- Some popular international schools in Cambodia include the International School of Phnom Penh, the Northbridge International School, and the Australian International School.
- International schools in Cambodia are often accredited by international organizations, such as the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC) or the Council of International Schools (CIS).

[SECTION] Studying as a Foreigner [/SECTION]
- To study in Cambodia, foreign students must obtain a **student visa**, which can be applied for through the Cambodian embassy or consulate in their home country.
- Foreign students must also meet the **language requirements** for their chosen program, which may include proficiency in Khmer or English.
- There are several **scholarships** available for foreign students to study in Cambodia, including the Cambodian Government Scholarship and the ASEAN Scholarship.
- The cost of living in Cambodia can be relatively low, with **accommodation** options starting from $200 per month and **food** options starting from $3 per meal.
- Foreign students can also take advantage of **health insurance** options, which can provide coverage for medical expenses while studying in Cambodia.
- Many universities in Cambodia offer **support services** for foreign students, including language support, academic advising, and cultural orientation.

[SECTION] Education Quality & Challenges [/SECTION]
- One of the main challenges facing the education system in Cambodia is the shortage of **qualified teachers**, particularly in rural areas.
- The education system in Cambodia also struggles with **limited resources**, including outdated textbooks and inadequate infrastructure.
- Despite these challenges, the education system in Cambodia has made significant progress in recent years, with **enrollment rates** increasing and **dropout rates** decreasing.
- The government has also implemented **reforms** to improve the quality of education, including the introduction of new curricula and teaching methods.
- However, more needs to be done to address the **disparities** in education between urban and rural areas, as well as between different socioeconomic groups.
