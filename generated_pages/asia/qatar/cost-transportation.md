[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Qatar has a well-developed transportation system, with a range of options available for getting around the country. The most common modes of transport are **taxis**, **ride-hailing services**, and **private cars**, with many residents and visitors also using **public buses** and the **metro system**. The quality of public transit in Qatar is generally good, with modern and air-conditioned buses and trains, and a comprehensive network of routes covering most areas of the country.

The majority of people in Qatar get around by car, with many residents owning their own vehicles or using ride-hailing services. However, the government has been investing heavily in public transportation in recent years, with the aim of reducing congestion and promoting more sustainable modes of transport. The metro system, which was launched in 2019, has been particularly popular, with its efficient and convenient service making it a popular choice for commuters.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transport, consider using public buses or the metro system, which offer affordable and convenient options for getting around the city.
- Ride-hailing apps like Uber and Careem are widely available and offer competitive pricing, but be sure to check the estimated fare before you start your journey.
- When taking a taxi, make sure to agree on the fare before you start your journey, and be aware that some drivers may not speak English.
- If you plan to drive in Qatar, be aware that the roads can be congested, especially during rush hour, and that parking fees can add up quickly.
- Consider using a **transportation app** like Karwa or Qatar Taxi to book and pay for your journeys, which can help you avoid queues and save time.
- Always wear a seatbelt and follow local traffic laws, which are strictly enforced in Qatar.
