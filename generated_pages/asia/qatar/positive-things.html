Cultural Treasures

  • The Museum of Islamic Art, designed by I.M. Pei, is a iconic landmark and a center for Islamic art and culture, featuring a collection of over 14,000 objects.
  • The Souq Waqif, a traditional market, offers a glimpse into Qatar's rich cultural heritage, with its vibrant atmosphere and traditional goods.
  • The Al Zubarah Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a historic fort that dates back to the 18th century, showcasing the country's history and architecture.
  • The Qatari thobe and abaya**, traditional garments, are an integral part of the country's cultural identity, reflecting its rich heritage and traditions.
  • The Henna Night, a traditional celebration, is an essential part of Qatari weddings, where the bride's hands and feet are decorated with intricate henna designs.

People & Global Impact

  • Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani, the Emir of Qatar, has played a key role in promoting the country's foreign policy and economic development, with a focus on sustainability and innovation.
  • Qatari expatriates have made significant contributions to the global economy, with many holding key positions in international organizations and companies.
  • Mohammed bin Abdulrahman Al Thani, the Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, has been instrumental in promoting Qatar's diplomatic efforts and international relationships.
  • The Qatari diaspora community has been actively involved in promoting the country's culture and values, with many community organizations and events celebrating Qatari heritage.

What Visitors Love

  • The hospitality of the Qatari people, known for their warm welcome and generosity, making visitors feel at home.
  • The traditional Qatari cuisine, featuring dishes such as machboos and ummaliora, which offer a unique and flavorful experience.
  • The stunning architecture of Doha, with its blend of traditional and modern buildings, including the iconic Burj Doha.
  • The vibrant souks, such as the Souq Waqif, which offer a glimpse into the country's rich cultural heritage.
  • The beautiful beaches, such as the Katara Beach, which provide a relaxing and enjoyable experience.
  • The world-class museums, such as the Museum of Islamic Art, which offer a unique and educational experience.

Hidden Gems

  • The Al Thakira Mangroves, a protected area featuring a unique ecosystem and a variety of wildlife, including the dugong and green turtle.
  • The Qatar National Library, a state-of-the-art library featuring a vast collection of books and resources, with a focus on Arabic and Islamic studies.
  • The Katara Cultural Village, a cultural hub featuring a variety of events and activities, including concerts, exhibitions, and workshops.
  • The Aspire Park, a large public park featuring walking and cycling trails, gardens, and a lake, offering a peaceful escape from the city.
