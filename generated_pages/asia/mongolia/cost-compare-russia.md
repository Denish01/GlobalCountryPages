[SECTION] Overview: Mongolia vs Russia [/SECTION]
Mongolia and Russia are two neighboring countries with distinct economic profiles. Mongolia, a smaller and less populous country, has a more limited economy, which contributes to its lower cost of living. The country's GDP per capita is significantly lower than Russia's, and its inflation rate is slightly higher. In contrast, Russia has a more diversified economy, with a strong energy sector, which drives up prices. Overall, Mongolia offers a more affordable lifestyle, with lower prices for housing, food, and transportation.

The cost difference between Mongolia and Russia is significant, with Mongolia being around 20-30% cheaper. This is especially noticeable in the cost of housing, where a one-bedroom apartment in Ulaanbaatar, the capital of Mongolia, can cost around $200-300 per month, compared to $500-700 per month in Moscow, the capital of Russia. Additionally, the cost of food and transportation in Mongolia is also lower, making it an attractive option for expats, students, and retirees looking for an affordable lifestyle.
