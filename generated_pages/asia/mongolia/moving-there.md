# Moving to Mongolia — Visas, Residency & Expat Guide

To move to Mongolia, you will need to research and understand the country's visa requirements, cost of living, and cultural nuances, and then plan your relocation carefully, considering factors such as housing, employment, and healthcare. 

[SECTION] Why People Move to Mongolia [/SECTION]
Mongolia has become an attractive destination for expats and adventure-seekers alike, drawn by its vast, untouched landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and growing economy. The country offers a unique blend of traditional and modern lifestyles, with a mix of **nomadic** and urban experiences. Expats in Mongolia are a diverse group, including professionals, entrepreneurs, and retirees, with many drawn by the country's **mining industry**, as well as its growing **tourism sector**.

The quality of life in Mongolia is generally good, with a relatively low cost of living compared to other Asian countries. However, the country still faces challenges related to **infrastructure** and **bureaucracy**, which can make everyday life more difficult for expats. Despite these challenges, many expats find that the country's warm and welcoming people, stunning natural beauty, and sense of community make it a rewarding and enriching place to live.

Mongolia's expat demographics are diverse, with many coming from countries such as the United States, Canada, Australia, and Europe. The majority of expats live in the capital city, **Ulaanbaatar**, which offers a range of amenities and services, including international schools, hospitals, and shopping centers. However, some expats also choose to live in other parts of the country, such as the **Gobi Desert** or the **Altai Mountains**, which offer a more rural and peaceful way of life.

6. Arrange for health insurance that covers you in Mongolia
7. Research and choose a neighborhood to live in
8. Find a place to live, either by renting an apartment or buying a house

- The **Mongolia Expats** online forum is another useful resource for expats, with discussions on topics such as housing, jobs, and travel

- The **Ulaanbaatar Hash House Harriers** is a social club that organizes regular events and activities for expats and locals alike

[SECTION] Banking & Finances [/SECTION]
- Opening a bank account in Mongolia can be a straightforward process, with many banks offering services in English
- The **Mongolian Tugrik** is the local currency, although US dollars and euros are also widely accepted
- Credit cards, such as **Visa** and **Mastercard**, are becoming more widely accepted in Ulaanbaatar, but cash is still the preferred method of payment
- Money transfers can be made through services such as **Western Union** and **MoneyGram**
- It is a good idea to have a **local bank account** to receive and send money, as well as to pay bills and utilities

[SECTION] Challenges & Honest Advice [/SECTION]
- **Culture shock** can be a significant challenge for expats in Mongolia, particularly in rural areas where traditional customs and ways of life prevail
- **Bureaucracy** can be frustrating, with slow and sometimes confusing administrative processes
- **Language barriers** can be a problem, particularly outside of Ulaanbaatar where English is not widely spoken
- **Harsh climate** can be a challenge, with very cold winters and hot summers
- **Infrastructure** can be limited, particularly in rural areas where roads and amenities may be poor
- **Healthcare** can be a concern, with limited medical facilities and services available outside of Ulaanbaatar, and it is essential to have good health insurance that covers you in Mongolia.
