The education system in Kyrgyzstan is divided into three main stages: primary, secondary, and tertiary education. Primary education, also known as **basic education**, is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 14 and lasts for 9 years. Secondary education, also known as **upper secondary education**, is optional and lasts for 2 years, after which students can take the national entrance exams to gain admission to universities. The quality of education in Kyrgyzstan varies, with public schools often underfunded and overcrowded, while private schools and international schools offer higher quality education at a higher cost. The **public education system** is free and accessible to all citizens, but the **private education sector** is growing rapidly, with many private schools and universities offering a range of academic programs.
