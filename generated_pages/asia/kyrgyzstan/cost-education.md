# Education Costs in Kyrgyzstan

Education costs in Kyrgyzstan vary depending on the type of institution and the level of education, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions charge tuition fees ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Kyrgyzstan is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the dominant provider of education. Public schools are generally considered to provide a good quality education, with a strong emphasis on **mathematics**, **science**, and **language**. The language of instruction is primarily **Kyrgyz**, although **Russian** is also widely used, particularly in urban areas. Compulsory education in Kyrgyzstan lasts for 11 years, from age 7 to 18, and is divided into primary, secondary, and vocational education. International schools are available in major cities, such as Bishkek, and offer a range of curricula, including the **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Examinations**.

The quality of education in Kyrgyzstan is generally considered to be good, although there are some variations between urban and rural areas. Public schools in urban areas tend to have better facilities and more qualified teachers, while rural schools may struggle with limited resources and a shortage of qualified staff. Private schools, on the other hand, often have higher standards and more modern facilities, but can be expensive and may not be accessible to all students. International schools in Kyrgyzstan offer a high-quality education, but are generally very expensive and may not be an option for many families.
