# Kyrgyzstan Tax Guide — Income, Corporate & VAT Rates

The tax rates in Kyrgyzstan range from 5% to 20% for personal income tax, with a corporate tax rate of 10% and a value-added tax (VAT) rate of 12%. 

[CALLOUT] Tax laws change frequently. Always consult a qualified tax professional for advice specific to your situation. This is a general guide only. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Tax System Overview [/SECTION]
Kyrgyzstan has a **territorial taxation** system, where residents are taxed on their income earned within the country, regardless of where it is paid. Non-residents are taxed only on their income derived from sources in Kyrgyzstan. To be considered a tax resident in Kyrgyzstan, an individual must have a permanent home in the country or spend more than 183 days in a calendar year. The tax system is relatively straightforward, with a focus on simplicity and ease of compliance.

The tax authorities in Kyrgyzstan are responsible for administering the tax system, including collecting taxes, conducting audits, and providing tax guidance to taxpayers. The tax year in Kyrgyzstan runs from January to December, and taxpayers are required to file their tax returns by April 15th of the following year. The tax treaty network in Kyrgyzstan includes 11 countries, which helps to prevent double taxation and facilitate international trade and investment.

Personal income tax rates in Kyrgyzstan are progressive, with five brackets ranging from 5% to 20%. Taxpayers are allowed to claim deductions for **charitable donations**, **mortgage interest**, and **education expenses**, among others. Tax returns must be filed annually, and taxpayers can choose to file electronically or by mail. The tax authorities also provide a **tax credit** for taxpayers who have paid taxes in other countries.

[SECTION] Corporate & Business Tax [/SECTION]
- The corporate tax rate in Kyrgyzstan is 10%, which is relatively low compared to other countries in the region.
- Small businesses with annual turnover of less than 3 million KGS are eligible for a **simplified tax regime**, which requires less paperwork and compliance.
- Kyrgyzstan has several **free economic zones**, which offer tax incentives and other benefits to businesses that operate within these zones.
- Foreign companies that wish to operate in Kyrgyzstan must register with the tax authorities and obtain a **tax identification number**.
- Companies are required to file their tax returns quarterly, and must also submit an annual tax return by April 15th of the following year.

[SECTION] VAT / Sales Tax [/SECTION]
- The standard VAT rate in Kyrgyzstan is 12%, which applies to most goods and services.
- A reduced VAT rate of 5% applies to **basic foodstuffs**, **medicines**, and **educational materials**.
- Certain goods and services, such as **financial services** and **healthcare**, are exempt from VAT.
- Tourists who purchase goods in Kyrgyzstan may be eligible for a **tax refund** when they leave the country, provided they meet certain conditions.

[SECTION] For Expats & Foreign Workers [/SECTION]
- Expats who live and work in Kyrgyzstan are considered tax residents and are subject to the same tax rules as locals.
- Kyrgyzstan has **double taxation treaties** with 11 countries, which helps to prevent double taxation and fiscal evasion.
- Foreign workers who are employed by a Kyrgyz company must contribute to the **social security system**, which provides benefits such as pension, healthcare, and unemployment insurance.
- Expats who earn income from foreign sources may be subject to **withholding tax**, which can be reduced or eliminated under a double taxation treaty.
- Foreign workers who are self-employed or have their own business must register with the tax authorities and obtain a **tax identification number**.
- Expats who leave Kyrgyzstan must file a **departure tax return** and obtain a **tax clearance certificate** before departing the country.

[SECTION] Crypto & Investment Income [/SECTION]
- **Investment income**, such as dividends, interest, and capital gains, is subject to income tax in Kyrgyzstan.
- **Cryptocurrency** is considered a **digital asset** and is subject to capital gains tax, which is 10% for residents and 15% for non-residents.
- Foreign investment income is subject to **withholding tax**, which can be reduced or eliminated under a double taxation treaty.
- Taxpayers who earn investment income must file an annual tax return and report their income from all sources, including foreign sources.
