# Transportation Costs in Sri Lanka

The cost of transportation in Sri Lanka can vary depending on the mode of transport and location, but on average, a monthly transit pass can cost around $10-$20 USD, while a taxi start rate can range from $0.50-$1.50 USD.

[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Sri Lanka has a well-developed transportation network, with buses, trains, and taxis being the most common modes of transport. The quality of public transit is generally good, with buses and trains connecting major cities and towns. However, the road network can be congested, especially in urban areas like Colombo. Most people in Sri Lanka get around by using public transportation, with buses being the most popular mode of transport. The bus network is extensive, covering almost all parts of the country, and is relatively affordable.

The train network in Sri Lanka is also well-developed, with several lines connecting major cities and towns. The trains are generally clean and comfortable, and offer a scenic way to see the country. Taxis are also widely available, and can be hired for short or long distances. Ride-hailing apps like Uber and PickMe are also available in major cities, making it easy to get around without having to negotiate with taxi drivers. Overall, getting around in Sri Lanka is relatively easy and affordable, with a range of transportation options available to suit different budgets and preferences.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- Use public transportation whenever possible, as it is affordable and convenient.

- Be aware of traffic rules and regulations, especially when driving.
