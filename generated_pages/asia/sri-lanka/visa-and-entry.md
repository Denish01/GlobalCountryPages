# Sri Lanka Visa & Entry Requirements

To enter Sri Lanka, citizens of most countries require a visa, which can be obtained on arrival or prior to travel, depending on their nationality and purpose of visit.

[CALLOUT] Visa rules change frequently. Always verify current requirements with the official embassy or consulate of Sri Lanka before traveling. [/CALLOUT]

[SECTION] Visa Policy Overview [/SECTION]
Sri Lanka has a moderate visa policy, with a mix of visa-free access, visa on arrival, and prior approval requirements for different nationalities. Citizens of around 20 countries can enter Sri Lanka visa-free, while many others can obtain a visa on arrival or through an electronic visa system. The country aims to promote tourism and business travel, while also maintaining security and immigration controls.

The visa policy is designed to facilitate travel for legitimate purposes, such as tourism, business, and education, while preventing unauthorized immigration and ensuring national security. The Sri Lankan government has introduced various visa schemes and programs to attract foreign visitors, investors, and workers, while also streamlining the application process and reducing processing times.

[SECTION] By Visitor Type [/SECTION]
**Tourists:** Tourists can obtain a visa on arrival, which is valid for 30 days and can be extended up to 6 months. The typical stay for tourists is 2-4 weeks, and they can apply for an extension at the Department of Immigration and Emigration in Colombo. Tourists are required to have a return or onward ticket, sufficient funds, and proof of accommodation.
**Business Travelers:** Business travelers require a visa, which can be obtained on arrival or prior to travel, depending on the purpose and duration of their stay. They may need to provide an invitation letter from a Sri Lankan company, proof of business activities, and other relevant documents.
**Students:** Students require a student visa, which can be obtained prior to travel. They need to provide proof of enrollment at a Sri Lankan educational institution, a letter of acceptance, and other relevant documents. The student visa is typically valid for the duration of the course, which can range from a few months to several years.
**Workers:** Workers require a work visa, which can be obtained prior to travel. They need to provide a sponsorship letter from a Sri Lankan employer, proof of qualifications and experience, and other relevant documents. The work visa is typically valid for the duration of the employment contract, which can range from a few months to several years.

[SECTION] Entry Points & Procedures [/SECTION]
The main entry points to Sri Lanka are the Bandaranaike International Airport (BIA) in Colombo, the Mattala Rajapaksa International Airport (MRIA) in Hambantota, and the Colombo Port. Upon arrival, travelers need to proceed to the immigration counter, where they will be required to present their travel documents and complete a declaration form. The immigration officer will then stamp the passport and issue a visa, if required. Travelers can also enter Sri Lanka by land or sea, through designated border crossings and ports.

At the immigration counter, travelers may be asked questions about their purpose of visit, length of stay, and accommodation arrangements. They may also be required to provide biometric data, such as fingerprints, and undergo a security screening. It is essential to ensure that all travel documents are in order and that travelers have the required visas and permits before entering Sri Lanka.

[SECTION] Common Mistakes To Avoid [/SECTION]
- Not having a valid passport with at least 6 months' validity
- Not completing the visa application form correctly
- Not providing required documents, such as proof of onward travel or hotel reservation
- Not declaring goods or currency exceeding the allowed limits
- Not having the required vaccinations or health insurance
- Not respecting Sri Lankan customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly and removing shoes when visiting temples or mosques
