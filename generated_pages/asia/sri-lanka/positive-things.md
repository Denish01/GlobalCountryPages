[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The **Temple of the Tooth** in Kandy is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in Sri Lanka, attracting millions of pilgrims and tourists every year.
- The **Esala Perahera** festival in Kandy is a spectacular cultural event that showcases traditional Sri Lankan music, dance, and costumes, and is considered one of the most important cultural festivals in the country.
- The **Sigiriya Rock Fortress** is an ancient rock fortress and palace complex that is a masterpiece of urban planning and architecture, and is considered one of the most impressive archaeological sites in Sri Lanka.
- The **Kandyan Art** form is a unique and intricate style of art that originated in the Kandyan Kingdom, characterized by its use of vibrant colors and intricate patterns, and is still practiced by many Sri Lankan artists today.
- The **Vesak Festival** is a significant Buddhist holiday that is celebrated in Sri Lanka with great fervor, and is marked by the illumination of lanterns, the distribution of food and drinks, and the performance of traditional music and dance.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **Mahinda Rajapaksa**, the former President of Sri Lanka, played a crucial role in ending the country's civil war and has been a key figure in shaping the country's foreign policy.
- The **Sri Lankan diaspora** has made significant contributions to the global economy, with many Sri Lankans holding prominent positions in fields like medicine, engineering, and finance, and have been instrumental in promoting Sri Lankan culture and values abroad.
- **Aruna Shantha**, a Sri Lankan entrepreneur, has been recognized for his innovative approach to sustainable energy and has been awarded numerous international awards for his contributions to the field of renewable energy.
- The **Buddhist philosophy** of **Ahimsa**, or non-violence, has had a profound impact on the world, inspiring leaders like **Mahatma Gandhi** and **Martin Luther King Jr.** to adopt non-violent methods of protest and resistance.

[SECTION] What Visitors Love [/SECTION]
- The **hospitality** of the Sri Lankan people is legendary, with visitors often commenting on the warm and welcoming nature of the locals, who are always eager to share their culture and traditions with foreign guests.
- The **food** in Sri Lanka is a fusion of different flavors and cuisines, with popular dishes like **hoppers**, **string hoppers**, and **lamprais** being must-try items for any visitor to the country.
- The **scenery** in Sri Lanka is breathtaking, with the country's diverse landscapes ranging from **beaches** and **forests** to **mountains** and **rivers**, offering a wide range of opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking, surfing, and wildlife watching.
- The **cultural experiences** in Sri Lanka are unparalleled, with visitors able to explore ancient **temples**, **palaces**, and **fortresses**, and experience the country's vibrant **festivals** and **traditions** firsthand.
- The **wildlife** in Sri Lanka is incredible, with the country being home to a wide range of **elephants**, **leopards**, ** sloth bears**, and **birds**, and offering many opportunities for **safari** and **wildlife watching**.
- The **beaches** in Sri Lanka are stunning, with popular destinations like **Mirissa**, **Weligama**, and **Trincomalee** offering a range of activities like **surfing**, **snorkeling**, and **whale watching**, and providing a relaxing and peaceful atmosphere for visitors to unwind.
