Visa Policy Overview

The Maldives has a relatively liberal visa policy, allowing citizens of many countries to enter without a pre-arranged visa. The country offers visa-free access to citizens of over 100 countries, making it a popular tourist destination. Citizens of eligible countries can enter the Maldives for a maximum stay of 30 days, which can be extended for an additional 60 days. The visa policy is designed to promote tourism, which is a significant contributor to the country's economy.

The Maldives' visa policy is based on a system of visa exemptions, where citizens of certain countries are allowed to enter the country without a visa. The list of exempt countries includes most European countries, as well as countries in North and South America, Asia, and Africa. Citizens of countries that are not exempt must obtain a visa prior to their arrival in the Maldives. The country also offers a range of visa types, including Tourist, Business, and Student visas, each with its own set of requirements and restrictions.
