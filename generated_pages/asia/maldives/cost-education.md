# Education Costs in Maldives

The cost of education in Maldives varies depending on the type of institution and the student's residency status, with public education being largely subsidized by the government, while private and international schools offering a range of tuition fees.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Maldives is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the largest provider of education. Public schools in Maldives offer free education to all citizens, with the government covering the costs of tuition, textbooks, and other educational materials. The language of instruction in public schools is **Dhivehi**, with English being taught as a second language. The public education system is generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on **Islamic studies** and **Maldivian culture**. However, private schools in Maldives offer a more diverse range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **Cambridge International Examinations**, and are often preferred by expatriate families and those seeking a more Western-style education.

The compulsory education years in Maldives are from age 6 to 16, with students attending primary school from age 6 to 11 and secondary school from age 12 to 16. After completing secondary school, students can pursue higher education at one of the several **public universities** or **private colleges** in the country. International schools are also available in Maldives, particularly in the capital city of **Male**, and offer a range of curricula and programs for students from preschool to high school. These schools are often accredited by international organizations and offer a high-quality education that is recognized globally.
