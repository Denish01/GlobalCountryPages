Healthcare Overview

The Maldivian healthcare system is a mixed model, with both public and private providers offering a range of services. The public healthcare system is generally considered to be of good quality, with well-equipped hospitals and clinics, especially in the capital city of Male. However, there can be significant differences in the quality of care between urban and rural areas, with rural areas often having more limited access to specialized services. The private healthcare sector is also well-developed, with several high-quality hospitals and clinics catering to tourists and expatriates.

The public healthcare system is primarily funded by the government, with some services also provided by non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and private providers. The Ministry of Health is responsible for overseeing the healthcare system, including the provision of healthcare services, health promotion, and disease prevention. Despite the overall good quality of care, there are some challenges facing the healthcare system in Maldives, including a shortage of specialized healthcare professionals and limited access to certain services, such as mental health care.

The healthcare system in Maldives is also influenced by the country's unique geography, with many islands having limited access to healthcare services. To address this, the government has implemented a number of initiatives, including the use of telemedicine and the deployment of healthcare professionals to remote islands.

Health Insurance

  • Travel insurance is highly recommended for visitors to Maldives, as it can help cover the cost of medical care in the event of an emergency.
  • Private health insurance is also available for expatriates and locals, with several international insurance companies offering coverage in Maldives.
  • Local insurance options are also available, including the Maldives Social Health Insurance Scheme, which provides coverage for citizens and permanent residents.
  • Expatriate health insurance is often required by employers, and can help cover the cost of medical care, including evacuation to other countries if necessary.
  • Costs can vary widely, depending on the type and level of coverage, as well as the individual's age and health status.
  • Pre-existing conditions may be excluded from coverage, or may require additional premiums, so it's essential to carefully review insurance policies before purchasing.

Pharmacies & Medications

  • Medications are widely available in Maldives, including many common prescription and over-the-counter medications.
  • Prescription requirements vary, but many medications can be obtained without a prescription, especially in private pharmacies.
  • Common brands include international companies such as Pfizer and GlaxoSmithKline, as well as local generics.
  • Costs can vary, but many medications are relatively affordable, especially when compared to prices in other developed countries.
  • Availability can be limited in rural areas, where pharmacies may not always have a wide range of medications in stock.

Vaccinations & Health Risks

  • Recommended vaccinations include hepatitis A, hepatitis B, typhoid, and rabies, as well as routine vaccinations such as MMR and DTaP.
  • Endemic diseases include dengue fever, chikungunya, and influenza, which can be prevalent during certain times of the year.
  • Water safety is generally good, but it's still essential to take precautions, such as drinking bottled or filtered water and avoiding untreated water.
  • Food safety is also generally good, but it's essential to take precautions, such as eating at reputable restaurants and avoiding undercooked or raw foods.
  • Sun protection is essential, as the sun can be intense in Maldives, especially during peak hours.
  • Insect-borne illnesses can be prevalent, especially during certain times of the year, so it's essential to take precautions, such as using insect repellent and wearing protective clothing.

Medical Tourism

  • Maldives is not typically considered a medical tourism destination, but it does offer some specialized services, such as cosmetic surgery and dental care.
  • Popular procedures include cosmetic surgery, dental care, and orthopedic surgery.
  • Accredited hospitals include the ADK Hospital and the Tree Top Hospital, which offer a range of specialized services.
  • Cost savings can be significant, especially when compared to prices in other developed countries, but it's essential to carefully research and compare prices before making a decision.

Emergency Procedures

  • Emergency number is 102, which can be used to contact emergency services, including ambulance and police.
  • Ambulance reliability can vary, especially in rural areas, where services may not always be available.
  • Nearest hospitals can be contacted directly in the event of an emergency, and many hospitals have 24-hour emergency departments.
  • Insurance claims can be complex, so it's essential to carefully review insurance policies and contact the insurance company directly in the event of an emergency.
  • Language can be a barrier, especially in rural areas, where English may not always be spoken, so it's essential to have a plan in place, such as a translation service or a local contact.
