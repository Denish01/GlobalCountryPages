[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Maldives is divided into three main stages: **primary education**, **secondary education**, and **tertiary education**. Primary education is compulsory for all children between the ages of 6 and 12, and is provided free of charge by the government. Secondary education is also compulsory, and is divided into two stages: lower secondary (ages 13-15) and upper secondary (ages 16-18). Tertiary education is provided by a number of institutions, including the **Maldives National University**, which offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. The quality of education in Maldives is generally considered to be good, with a strong emphasis on **Islamic studies** and **Dhivehi language**. However, there are some challenges facing the education system, including a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of resources in some schools.

The education system in Maldives is mostly public, with the government providing funding for the majority of schools. However, there are also a number of **private schools** and **international schools** that offer alternative educational options. These schools often have higher fees than public schools, but may offer additional resources and facilities. In recent years, the government has implemented a number of reforms aimed at improving the quality of education in Maldives, including the introduction of new **curricula** and **teaching methods**.

Overall, the education system in Maldives is well-established and provides a range of opportunities for students to pursue their educational goals. However, there are still some challenges that need to be addressed, including the need for more qualified teachers and better resources. With the government's ongoing efforts to improve the education system, Maldives is likely to continue to develop and grow as a hub for education in the region.
