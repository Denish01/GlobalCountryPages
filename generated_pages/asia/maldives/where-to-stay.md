# Where to Stay in Maldives — Neighborhoods & Accommodation

To find the best place to stay in Maldives, consider the top areas of Male, Hulhumale, Villingili, Thulusdhoo, and the various atolls, each offering a unique experience for different types of travelers. 

[SECTION] Best Areas to Stay [/SECTION]
For a unique and unforgettable experience in Maldives, the following areas are highly recommended:
**Male** (Male)
Best For: Luxury / Business
Vibe: Male, the capital city, offers a bustling and vibrant atmosphere, with a mix of traditional and modern culture, and is ideal for those looking to experience the local way of life. The city is filled with historic landmarks, markets, and restaurants.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: Access to a wide range of amenities, including restaurants, shops, and entertainment options, proximity to the airport, and a variety of accommodation options. 
Cons: Can be crowded and noisy, and may not offer the same level of relaxation as other areas.

**Hulhumale** (Hulhumale)
Best For: Family / Budget
Vibe: Hulhumale is a newly developed island with a more laid-back and peaceful atmosphere, making it an ideal destination for families and those on a budget. The island offers a beautiful beach and a range of water sports.
Price Range: $100-$300/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, beautiful beach, and a range of water sports and activities. 
Cons: Limited dining and entertainment options, and can be quite far from the airport.

**Villingili** (Villingili)
Best For: Luxury / Relaxation
Vibe: Villingili is a tranquil and secluded island with a luxurious and relaxing atmosphere, making it perfect for those looking to unwind and escape the crowds. The island offers stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters.
Price Range: $300-$600/night
Pros: Pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a range of luxury resorts and amenities. 
Cons: Can be quite expensive, and limited dining and entertainment options.

**Thulusdhoo** (Thulusdhoo)
Best For: Backpacker / Surfer
Vibe: Thulusdhoo is a small and laid-back island with a surfer's paradise atmosphere, making it an ideal destination for backpackers and surfers. The island offers a range of surf breaks and a relaxed atmosphere.
Price Range: $50-$200/night
Pros: Affordable accommodation options, world-class surf breaks, and a lively and social atmosphere. 
Cons: Limited amenities and services, and can be quite crowded during peak season.

**Ari Atoll** (Ari Atoll)
Best For: Diver / Nature Lover
Vibe: Ari Atoll is a stunning and diverse atoll with a relaxed and natural atmosphere, making it perfect for divers and nature lovers. The atoll offers a range of dive sites and snorkeling opportunities.
Price Range: $200-$500/night
Pros: World-class dive sites, stunning natural beauty, and a range of accommodation options. 
Cons: Can be quite far from the airport, and limited dining and entertainment options.

[SECTION] Booking Tips [/SECTION]
- Book in advance, especially during peak season, to ensure availability and the best rates.
- Use online booking platforms such as Booking.com, Expedia, or Agoda to compare prices and find deals.
- Consider negotiating with the hotel or resort directly for a better rate, especially for long stays.
- Look for package deals that include accommodation, meals, and activities for a more convenient and cost-effective option.
- Be aware of seasonal pricing, with peak season typically from December to April.
- Read reviews and check the hotel's or resort's policies before booking.
- Consider booking a hotel or resort that offers a range of amenities and activities to enhance your stay.

[SECTION] Safety & Location Tips [/SECTION]
- Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas like Male, and keep an eye on your belongings.
- Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in isolated areas.
- Choose a hotel or resort that is located in a safe and convenient area, with easy access to amenities and activities.
- Consider booking a hotel or resort that offers a range of services, including airport transfers and water sports.
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or other cultural sites.
- Keep in mind that the Maldives is a Muslim country, and dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting local areas.
