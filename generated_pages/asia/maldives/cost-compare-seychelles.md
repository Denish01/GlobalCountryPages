[SECTION] Overview: Maldives vs Seychelles [/SECTION]
The Maldives and Seychelles are two island nations in the Indian Ocean, known for their stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and coral reefs. While both countries offer a unique and luxurious experience, they differ significantly in terms of cost. The Maldives is a popular destination for high-end tourism, with many luxury resorts and hotels, which drives up costs. Seychelles, on the other hand, has a more diverse economy and a wider range of accommodation options, making it a more affordable choice. The economic context of the two countries also plays a role in their cost levels, with the Maldives having a smaller and more specialized economy, and Seychelles having a larger and more diversified economy.

The cost difference between the two countries is significant, with Seychelles being around 15-20% cheaper than the Maldives. This is reflected in the prices of accommodation, food, transportation, and other living expenses. For example, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center of Male, the capital of the Maldives, can cost around $1,500-2,000 per month, while a similar apartment in Victoria, the capital of Seychelles, can cost around $1,000-1,500 per month. Similarly, a meal at a mid-range restaurant in the Maldives can cost around $20-30 per person, while a similar meal in Seychelles can cost around $15-25 per person.
