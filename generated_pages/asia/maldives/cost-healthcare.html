Healthcare System Overview

The healthcare system in Maldives is a mixed system, with both public and private providers offering a range of services. Public healthcare is generally more affordable, with lower costs for doctor visits and hospital stays, but the quality of care can vary and wait times can be long. Private healthcare, on the other hand, offers higher quality care and shorter wait times, but at a significantly higher cost. For locals, the healthcare system is generally accessible, with many public healthcare facilities offering free or low-cost services. For foreigners, however, private healthcare is often the preferred option, as it offers higher quality care and more comprehensive services.

In terms of insurance, it is not mandatory to have health insurance in Maldives, but it is highly recommended, especially for foreigners. Many private healthcare providers require proof of insurance before providing treatment, and having insurance can help to cover the high costs of medical care. Additionally, some employers in Maldives may offer health insurance as part of their employee benefits package, so it's worth checking with your employer to see if this is an option.

Pharmacy & Medication Costs

  • Common medications such as antibiotics and pain relievers can cost between $5-$20 per prescription
  • Prescription medications may require a doctor's note or prescription from a licensed healthcare provider
  • Pharmacies are widely available in Male and other major cities, but may be less accessible in rural areas
  • Many pharmacies offer over-the-counter medications without a prescription, but it's always best to consult with a doctor or pharmacist before taking any medication
  • Some pharmacies may offer discounts or promotions for certain medications or services

Emergency Care

In the event of a medical emergency, it's best to call the emergency number, 102, or seek immediate attention at the nearest hospital. Many private hospitals in Male and other major cities offer 24-hour emergency services, including ambulance transportation and emergency room care. For foreigners, it's often best to seek care at a private hospital, as they may offer more comprehensive and specialized services. Some of the best hospitals for foreigners in Maldives include the ADK Hospital and the Cocoa Hospital, both of which offer high-quality medical care and amenities.

It's also a good idea to research and prepare for any potential medical emergencies before they happen. This can include purchasing travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, researching local healthcare providers and emergency services, and carrying a list of important phone numbers and medical contacts. By being prepared and knowing what to do in case of a medical emergency, you can help to ensure a safe and healthy trip to Maldives.
