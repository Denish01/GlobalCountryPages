When deciding where to stay in Jordan, consider the country's diverse range of accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts, and choose a location that suits your travel style, whether you're a backpacker, luxury traveler, or family on vacation.

Best Areas to Stay

For each of the top areas in Jordan, there's a unique character and set of advantages that cater to different types of travelers. Here are some of the best areas to stay:

Downtown Amman (Amman)

Best For: Backpackers / Business travelers

Vibe: Downtown Amman is a bustling area with a mix of old and new, offering easy access to historic sites, restaurants, and nightlife. The area is always lively, with a vibrant atmosphere that's perfect for those who want to be in the heart of the action.

Price Range: $20-100/night

Pros: Central location, easy access to public transportation, variety of dining and entertainment options

Cons: Can be noisy and crowded, limited parking options

Abdoun (Amman)

Best For: Luxury travelers / Families

Vibe: Abdoun is an upscale neighborhood in Amman, known for its modern amenities, beautiful parks, and stunning views of the city. The area is quiet and safe, making it an ideal choice for families or those seeking a more relaxed atmosphere.

Price Range: $100-300/night

Pros: Modern amenities, safe and secure, beautiful parks and views

Cons: Limited access to historic sites, can be expensive

Aqaba (Aqaba)

Best For: Beachgoers / Families

Vibe: Aqaba is a coastal city in southern Jordan, offering a relaxed and laid-back atmosphere, with beautiful beaches, water sports, and a vibrant nightlife. The area is perfect for those who want to unwind and enjoy the sun, sea, and sand.

Price Range: $30-150/night

Pros: Beautiful beaches, water sports, vibrant nightlife

Cons: Can be crowded during peak season, limited cultural attractions

Wadi Rum (Wadi Rum)

Best For: Adventure seekers / Nature lovers

Vibe: Wadi Rum is a stunning desert valley in southern Jordan, known for its breathtaking landscapes, hiking trails, and Bedouin camps. The area is perfect for those who want to experience the great outdoors and connect with nature.

Price Range: $20-100/night

Pros: Breathtaking landscapes, hiking trails, unique cultural experiences

Cons: Limited amenities, can be hot and dusty

Petra (Wadi Musa)

Best For: History buffs / Adventure seekers

Vibe: Petra is an ancient city in southern Jordan, famous for its rock-cut architecture and stunning natural surroundings. The area is perfect for those who want to explore history and culture, with a range of accommodations available to suit different budgets.

Price Range: $20-100/night

Pros: Historic site, stunning natural surroundings, variety of accommodations

Cons: Can be crowded, limited amenities outside of the historic site

Dead Sea (Dead Sea)

Best For: Relaxation / Wellness

Vibe: The Dead Sea is a unique natural phenomenon in western Jordan, offering a range of spa and wellness facilities, as well as stunning scenery and tranquil atmosphere. The area is perfect for those who want to relax and rejuvenate, with a range of accommodations available to suit different budgets.

Price Range: $50-200/night

Pros: Unique natural phenomenon, spa and wellness facilities, tranquil atmosphere

Cons: Limited cultural attractions, can be expensive

Booking Tips

  • Book in advance, especially during peak season (June to August)
  • Use online platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or Hotels.com to compare prices and find deals
  • Consider negotiating prices, especially for longer stays or off-season bookings
  • Look for packages that include meals, tours, or other amenities to save money
  • Read reviews and check the location before booking to ensure it suits your needs
  • Be aware of any additional fees or taxes, such as the Jordanian tourism tax
  • Use travel agencies or local tour operators to book accommodations and tours, especially for more remote areas

Safety & Location Tips

  • Avoid areas near the Syrian or Iraqi borders, due to security concerns
  • Be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or at night
  • Use reputable taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and always agree on the fare before starting your journey
  • Keep valuables secure and be aware of pickpocketing in tourist areas
  • Stay hydrated and protected from the sun, especially in desert areas
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or other cultural sites
