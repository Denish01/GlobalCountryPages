[SECTION] Getting Around Overview [/SECTION]
Getting around Jordan is relatively easy, with a variety of transportation modes available, including taxis, buses, and ride-hailing services. The quality of public transit in Jordan is generally good, with most cities having a well-developed network of buses and taxis. However, the quality of roads and traffic conditions can vary, especially in rural areas. Most people in Jordan get around using taxis or private cars, as the public transportation system is not as comprehensive as in some other countries. 
The main transportation modes in Jordan include **taxis**, **buses**, and **ride-hailing services**, with **private cars** also being a popular option for those who can afford it. The government is also investing in improving the public transportation system, with new bus routes and **train** services being introduced in some areas.

[SECTION] Transport Tips [/SECTION]
- To save money on transportation, consider using public buses or shared taxis for short distances.
- Use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Careem for convenient and affordable rides.
- Always negotiate the fare with taxi drivers before starting your journey, and make sure to use the meter.
- Be aware of traffic conditions and road closures, especially during peak hours or construction periods.
- Consider renting a car for longer trips or for more flexibility in your travel plans.
- Download the **Moovit** app for helpful public transportation routes and schedules in major Jordanian cities.
