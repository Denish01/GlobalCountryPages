The best time to visit Jordan is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and pleasant, making it ideal for exploring the country's many historical and natural attractions.

Best months to visit Jordan: March to May and September to November. Peak season: June to August. Budget season: December to February. Avoid: July and August due to the extreme heat and crowds, and January due to the cold winter weather.

Seasons Overview

Jordan has a dry climate, with very little rainfall throughout the year. The country can be divided into three main climate zones: the Mediterranean climate in the northwest, the desert climate in the east and south, and the highland climate in the central and western regions. The dry season, which runs from May to October, is characterized by hot temperatures and very little rainfall, while the wet season, which runs from November to April, is mild and wet. The seasons can greatly affect travel in Jordan, with the summer months being the peak tourist season, but also the hottest and most crowded. The winter months, on the other hand, are the coolest and wettest, but also the best time to see the country's flora and fauna.

The seasons can also affect the prices of accommodations and tourist services, with the peak season being the most expensive. However, the budget season, which runs from December to February, can be a great time to visit Jordan, as prices are lower and the crowds are smaller. It's worth noting that the Ramadan month, which usually falls in May or June, can also affect travel in Jordan, as some tourist attractions and services may be closed or operate on reduced hours.

Regional Variations

  • The north of Jordan, including the cities of Amman and Jerash, has a Mediterranean climate, with mild winters and hot summers.
  • The east and south of Jordan, including the deserts of Wadi Rum and the city of Aqaba, have a desert climate, with very hot summers and mild winters.
  • The central and western regions of Jordan, including the cities of Petra and Wadi Musa, have a highland climate, with cool winters and mild summers.
  • The Dead Sea region has a unique climate, with very hot summers and mild winters, and is a great place to visit year-round.
  • The mountainous regions of Jordan, including the cities of Ajloun and Jerash, have a cooler climate, with cold winters and mild summers.

Practical Tips

  • Book your accommodations and tourist services well in advance, especially during the peak season.
  • Consider visiting during the shoulder season, when prices are lower and the crowds are smaller.
  • Pack accordingly, with light clothing for the summer months and warm clothing for the winter months.
  • Don't forget to bring sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Avoid traveling during the Ramadan month, when some tourist attractions and services may be closed or operate on reduced hours.
  • Consider hiring a guide or joining a tour group to get the most out of your visit to Jordan.
