# Education Costs in Jordan

The cost of education in Jordan varies depending on the type of school and institution, with public schools being generally free or subsidized, while private schools and international institutions can range from $1,000 to $10,000 per year.

[SECTION] Education System Overview [/SECTION]
The education system in Jordan is divided into public and private sectors, with the public sector being the largest. Public schools in Jordan are generally considered to be of good quality, with a strong emphasis on **Arabic language** and **Islamic studies**. However, private schools are often preferred by parents who can afford them, as they are seen as offering higher-quality education and better facilities. The language of instruction in Jordan is Arabic, but many private schools and universities also offer instruction in **English**. Compulsory education in Jordan lasts for 12 years, from age 6 to 18, and is divided into primary, preparatory, and secondary stages. International schools are also available in Jordan, particularly in Amman, and offer a range of curricula, including **International Baccalaureate** and **British GCSE**.

The quality of education in Jordan is generally considered to be high, with a strong emphasis on **science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM)** subjects. However, the education system is also highly competitive, and students are often under a lot of pressure to perform well in exams. Despite this, Jordan has a highly educated population, with a literacy rate of over 98%. The country is also home to a number of reputable universities, including the **University of Jordan** and **Jordan University of Science and Technology**, which offer a range of undergraduate and graduate programs.
