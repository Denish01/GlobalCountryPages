Market Overview

The Jordanian property market has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by government initiatives to attract foreign investment and a growing demand for housing. The market is characterized by a mix of modern and traditional properties, with a focus on luxury developments in urban areas. The current market conditions are favorable for buyers, with a wide range of properties available at competitive prices. However, the market is also subject to fluctuations in the regional economy and global events. The price trajectory has been steadily increasing, with a focus on urban areas such as Amman, where prices are higher compared to rural areas.

The urban-rural divide is significant in Jordan, with urban areas offering a wider range of amenities and services. The capital city, Amman, is the hub of the property market, with a high demand for luxury apartments and villas. In contrast, rural areas offer a more traditional and affordable lifestyle, with a focus on agricultural land and smaller properties. The government has implemented initiatives to encourage development in rural areas, including tax incentives and infrastructure projects.

The market trends in Jordan are influenced by the regional economy, with a focus on tourism, industry, and agriculture. The country's strategic location and stable economy make it an attractive destination for foreign investors, with a growing demand for commercial and residential properties. However, the market is also subject to risks, including fluctuations in the global economy and regional instability.

Foreign Ownership Rules

  • Foreigners can buy property in Jordan, but they must obtain a license from the Ministry of Interior.
  • The license is typically granted for a period of one year, and it can be renewed.
  • Foreigners can own property in their own name, but they must register the property with the Ministry of Interior.
  • Foreign companies can also own property in Jordan, but they must be registered with the Ministry of Industry and Trade.
  • Foreigners can lease property in Jordan for a period of up to 50 years, with the option to renew.

Buying Process

  1. Research the market and find a property that meets your needs and budget.
  2. Contact a real estate agent or lawyer to assist with the purchase process.
  3. Obtain a pre-approval for a mortgage, if necessary.
  4. Make an offer on the property and negotiate the terms of the sale.
  5. Sign a purchase agreement and pay a deposit.
  6. Obtain a license from the Ministry of Interior, if necessary.
  7. Register the property with the Ministry of Interior.
  8. Pay the transfer tax and other fees.
  9. Obtain a title deed and register the property in your name.
  10. Complete any necessary renovations or repairs.

Rental Market

  • The rental market in Jordan is competitive, with a wide range of properties available.
  • Tenant rights are protected by law, with a focus on fair rent and secure tenure.
  • Typical lease terms are one to three years, with the option to renew.
  • The deposit is typically one to two months' rent.
  • Properties are often furnished, but unfurnished properties are also available.
