[SECTION] Cultural Treasures [/SECTION]
- The ancient city of **Petra**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a testament to Jordan's rich history and cultural heritage.
- The **Dead Sea Scrolls**, discovered in Qumran, are a significant archaeological find, providing valuable insights into the region's history and religion.
- The traditional **henna night** is an important part of Jordanian weddings, where the bride's hands and feet are decorated with intricate henna designs.
- The **Jordan Museum** in Amman showcases the country's history and culture, with a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Jordan's past.
- The **Aqaba Fortress**, a 16th-century Ottoman fort, is a historic landmark that reflects Jordan's strategic importance in the region.

[SECTION] People & Global Impact [/SECTION]
- **King Hussein**, the former monarch of Jordan, played a key role in promoting peace and stability in the Middle East, and was a respected leader on the world stage.
- **Queen Rania**, the current queen consort, is a prominent advocate for education and women's rights, and has been recognized for her philanthropic work.
- The **Jordanian diaspora** has made significant contributions to various fields, including medicine, engineering, and business, with many Jordanians holding prominent positions in companies and organizations around the world.
- **Ahmad Al-Shugairi**, a Jordanian TV presenter, is known for his social and humanitarian work, and has been recognized for his efforts to promote tolerance and understanding.
